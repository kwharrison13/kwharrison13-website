*Narrator:*

Over the last three years, we've started seeing YC companies land actual multi-million dollar deals within their first year, if not during the actual YC batch. It's not unusual at all to see a YC startup's first customer be one of the largest companies in the world today.

In the past, startups had a hard time selling to huge companies. It's just always been too hard to get a hold of the right people there, too hard to build a product with the right depth of features for a big company to use quickly, and too low ROI for these companies to take on the risk of working with an early-stage startup. But AI has changed the score on all three of these fronts.

First, the leaders of these massive enterprises are out there looking for teams that can bend AI to solve key problems for them. The buyers of these giant companies, the biggest ones in the world, are awake and they want to talk.

Secondly, coding agents have made it possible for the first time for small teams to ship incredibly thoughtful, nuanced products to large companies in months, not years. The meme where some startups want to operate in stealth for three years until they reach feature parity with incumbents before launching is dead. For the first time, a two-to-three person team can ship something a Fortune 10 company can find useful.

Finally, leaders of these companies understand where they need to create value internally, what they should outsource, and what will happen to their companies if they don't adapt to AI.

We want to back more teams that want to go after deals with the biggest companies in the world.
