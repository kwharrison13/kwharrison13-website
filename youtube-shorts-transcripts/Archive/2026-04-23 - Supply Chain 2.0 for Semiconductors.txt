A single advanced AI chip goes through about 1400 process steps of manufacturing, crosses
a dozen countries and takes 5 months to build. This supply chain is managed with spreadsheets,
SAP, and phone calls. In 2021, a $300 chip held up a $50,000 car and $200 billion in vehicles
did not get built. Companies could see their direct suppliers but had zero visibility
into the second and third tiers. It has gotten worse. TSMC Advanced Packaging is the single
biggest bottleneck in AI compute right now. An NVIDIA has locked up over 60% of it.
High bandwidth memory is booked through 2026. Export controls change quarterly. At the same time,
the CHIPS Act is standing up new American fabs in Arizona, Texas, Ohio, and New York.
Each needing a supply chain built nearly from scratch.
Almost none of the tooling you would expect exist.
Real-time allocation tracking, multi-tier risk monitoring, export compliance.
You need to understand wafer allocation and packaging constraints at a deep level to build this.
Which is exactly why it's a startup opportunity and not a feature just inside SAP.
