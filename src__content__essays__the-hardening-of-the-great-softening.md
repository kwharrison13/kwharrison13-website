For some reason, right around summertime each year I find myself reflecting on the nature of work and my relationship with it. In August 2022, I wrote "Toil We Must." In April 2023, I wrote "The Renaissance of Rise and Grind."

This essay continues that thread — examining how the cultural pendulum on work ethic is swinging back again.

## The Cycle

Every few years the discourse around work resets. We oscillate between hustle culture and burnout culture, rarely landing on something sustainable.

> The question isn't whether to work hard. It's whether your work compounds.

The best investors and builders I know have an almost monastic relationship with their craft. Not because they glorify suffering, but because they've found the thing worth suffering *for*.

## What Changed

The post-COVID recalibration created a genuine softening — remote work, four-day weeks, quiet quitting. Some of it was healthy. Some of it was overcorrection.

Now the pendulum is hardening again. Not back to toxic hustle culture, but toward a more intentional intensity.
