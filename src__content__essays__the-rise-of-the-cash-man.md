I think it will surprise no one to find that the best place to start writing about a venture-backed Adam Neumann fever dream is with Disneyland.

This is the story of how charisma, capital, and conviction created one of the most fascinating case studies in modern venture history.

## The Setup

Every generation of tech gets its mythological founder figure. Someone who bends reality so hard that the line between vision and delusion becomes impossible to draw in real time.

> The best founders are the ones you can't quite tell if they're geniuses or crazy — until the outcome resolves the ambiguity.

Adam Neumann was that figure for the late 2010s. Not because WeWork succeeded, but because the *mechanism* by which he raised capital revealed something deep about how venture markets actually function.
