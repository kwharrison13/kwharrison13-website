# Richard Branson

In [Talent (Book)](/books/talent), Branson is quoted as an example of how a perceived deficit can function as an advantage. Describing his own dyslexia, he says it *"helped me think big but keep our messages simple,"* and contrasts the business world's tendency to get caught up in facts and figures with *"the ability to dream, conceptualise, and innovate"* that he argues separates the successful from the unsuccessful. The book uses him to make the point that an inability to focus on every detail can, for some people, reallocate attention toward a more important bigger picture.

**Context:** Richard Branson is a British entrepreneur and founder of the Virgin Group, which spans airlines, music, telecoms, and Virgin Galactic. He has spoken publicly about how his dyslexia shaped his approach to business.
