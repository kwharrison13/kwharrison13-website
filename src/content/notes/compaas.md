# Compaas

Compensation-data/comp-planning startup (compa.as), founded by [Bethanye Blount](/notes/bethanye-blount), that appears in Kyle's Pave Diligence — 2021 as one of Pave's direct competitors. The dossier lists its customers as [Benchling](/notes/benchling), [GitLab](/notes/gitlab), [Reddit](/notes/reddit), Viz.ai, [Alation](/notes/alation), and [Algolia](/notes/algolia), and notes a co-published "Best Practices for Remote Compensation" resource with GitLab ("We are no longer in spreadsheets, thanks to Compaas") (Pave Diligence — 2021).

A customer-side view came from [Marie Szuts](/notes/marie-szuts) at [Figma](/notes/figma) during the Pave diligence: she thought very highly of Bethanye Blount and called Compaas "one of the first tools to show you data," but reported that Figma tried Compaas's comp-planning-cycle tool and it "really failed" — in some ways the company was too small to fully deliver its core product, core functionality was missing, and they couldn't make admin changes despite a year of product feedback (November 1st, 2021 — Marie Szuts (Figma) — Pave Diligence). That experience is part of the broader picture Szuts painted of a fragmented comp-planning market (Compaas → ChartHop → spreadsheets) with no clear winner.

**Context:** Compaas (compa.as) is an early compensation-management and pay-data startup founded by Bethanye McGregor Blount, aimed at helping companies benchmark and plan employee compensation.

## Where this appears

- Pave Diligence — 2021 — listed among Pave's direct competitors (customers: Benchling, GitLab, Reddit, Viz.ai, Alation, Algolia); co-authored a remote-comp resource with GitLab.
- November 1st, 2021 — Marie Szuts (Figma) — Pave Diligence — customer perspective: praised as an early data tool, but its comp-planning cycle tool "really failed" for Figma.
