# Acts 5

A New Testament chapter cited in Kyle's 8 Points of the Gospel topical study, in the section on consecration as "the yielding up of oneself to God." [Neal A. Maxwell](/notes/neal-a-maxwell) invokes [Acts 5](/notes/acts-5):1–11 for the point that "the last portion is the hardest to yield" — that so many cling tenaciously to a particular "part," treating obsessions like possessions, and that holding back this part deprives God's work of some of mankind's very best intellects. In this reading the chapter is a cautionary image of incomplete, withheld consecration.

**Context:** Acts 5:1–11 recounts Ananias and Sapphira, who sold a possession but kept back part of the price while pretending to lay all of it at the apostles' feet, and died for the deceit. Maxwell uses it as a figure for partial consecration — giving much to God while flinching from yielding the last and hardest part.
