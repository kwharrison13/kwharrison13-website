# VP of Sales Engineering

In Kyle's notes the "VP of Sales Engineering" surfaces as the right *champion / buyer persona* for a deal-management tool like [Prelay](/notes/prelay). In the September 2021 call, [Steve Malouf](/notes/steve-malouf) (advising [Prelay](/notes/prelay) as an [Datadog ($DDOG)](/notes/datadog) mentor) said he introduced founder [Gabriella DeFlorio](/notes/gabriella-deflorio) to the VP of Sales Engineering at Datadog to evaluate the product. The go-to-market lesson Kyle pulled out: when a product touches many lines of business and risks a long, multi-stakeholder sales cycle, "if you focus on a use case you can find the right person (e.g. VP of Sales Engineering) and that helps the deal move more quickly" — i.e. anchor on a specific use case (like POC management) and the use case points you to the executive owner who can drive the deal.

**Context:** A VP of Sales Engineering leads the sales-engineering / pre-sales solutions function — the technical team that runs demos, proofs-of-concept, and technical evaluations supporting the sales org. They own exactly the cross-functional deal complexity that tools like Prelay target.
