# Affliction

In Kyle's The Process of the Book of Mormon topical study, affliction is the fifth of the six steps in the gospel pattern that unfolds across the Book of Mormon's first sixty pages (first vision → revelation → opposition → gathering → **affliction** → [Atonement](/notes/atonement)). The study illustrates it through [Nephi](/notes/nephi)'s broken bow and starving in the wilderness — "having seen many afflictions." Its hinge is the word **"NEVERTHELESS"**: Nephi "drank the bitter cup without BECOMING bitter." The lesson Kyle draws is that the right response to affliction is to turn *toward* God rather than away — affliction is the trial that precedes and prepares the soul for the Atonement.
