# Matthew 18

In Kyle's gospel study, Matthew 18 carries two distinct lessons. The chapter gives its name to The Little Ones, The Lost Ones, where he reads it as making **little children the greatest in the Kingdom of Heaven** — "to accept children is to accept Christ, and to cause the little ones to stumble invites a millstone." The theme is anti-self-righteousness: we cannot sort the sinners from the righteous because we are all sinners, and the disciple is to be childlike, not childish.

In Knowledge — Topical Study he draws on the **parable of the unmerciful servant** (Matt 18:23–34) — the king who forgave a debt of ten thousand talents and the servant who then refused to forgive a fellow servant a hundred pence — as part of the "Atonement: Revelation or Experimentation" thread on gratitude and how we learn from experience without being condemned by it.

**Context:** Matthew 18 is Christ's discourse on humility and forgiveness in the church — becoming as a little child, the warning against offending "these little ones," the parable of the lost sheep, the instruction to reconcile with an offending brother, the "seventy times seven" answer to Peter, and the parable of the unmerciful servant.
