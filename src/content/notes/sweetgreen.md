# Sweetgreen

Sweetgreen recurs in Kyle's 2021 [Watershed](/notes/watershed-climate) diligence as the canonical example of a customer with a supply-chain-heavy carbon footprint. In the Watershed Climate Diligence — 2021 notes, Sweetgreen is one of Watershed's flagship customers (alongside Stripe, Shopify, Doordash, Square, and Airbnb) and is repeatedly used to illustrate how the product works: it "started by adding a list of its food purchases," then used Watershed across procurement, marketing, and finance — a multi-persona, cross-functional deployment. It anchors the Scope 1/2/3 framing too: Scope 1 is what's used in the restaurants, Scope 2 is purchased energy, and Scope 3 — "everything else in the supply chain," the carbon of producing and transporting the food — is where Sweetgreen's footprint and cost concentrate ("the carbon is where the cost is... Sweetgreen → supply chain"). Kyle also emailed [Kevin Quandt](/notes/kevin-quandt) at Sweetgreen as a reference customer during the evaluation, part of the outreach [Teesha Chowdhury](/notes/teesha-chowdhury)'s page documents.

**Context:** Sweetgreen is a U.S. fast-casual salad and bowl restaurant chain founded in 2007; it went public in 2021 and emphasizes locally-sourced, seasonal ingredients.
