# Resilience

A recurring frame across Kyle's reading: resilience is **made, not born** — a capacity built through experience rather than a fixed trait. [The Boy Who Was Raised as a Dog](/books/the-boy-who-was-raised-as-a-dog) states the thesis most directly, pushing back on the prevailing view that "children are resilient" — Bruce Perry argues children are in fact *more* vulnerable to trauma than adults, and that "resilient children are made, not born," forged by "the patterns of stress and of nurturing that they experience early on in life." [The Self-Driven Child](/books/the-self-driven-child) supplies the mechanism: *controlled, age-appropriate stress* (sleep training as the canonical example) builds resilience, while an unconditionally loving "safe base" at home lets kids recover and take hits from all directions. The aim is a brain that is "powerful, but also happy and resilient" — a Kyle-note on the book pairs the idea explicitly with Grit.

The same idea recurs outside the parenting books. [What It Takes](/books/what-it-takes) quotes Stephen Schwarzman: "The resilience you exhibit in the face of adversity — rather than the adversity itself — will be what defines you as a person." [Becoming](/books/becoming) frames a softer, simultaneous version — "grief and resilience live together" — where loss and the insistence to "control what you can" coexist rather than follow one another. Across all four, resilience connects to [Parenting](/notes/parenting), Stress, and the broader theme that hardship handled well is formative rather than merely survivable.

## Where this appears

- [The Boy Who Was Raised as a Dog](/books/the-boy-who-was-raised-as-a-dog) — "resilient children are made, not born"; resilience as a product of early stress-and-nurture patterns, not an innate buffer
- [The Self-Driven Child](/books/the-self-driven-child) — controlled stress builds resilience; a loving home base as the recovery ground; paired with grit
- [What It Takes](/books/what-it-takes) — Schwarzman: resilience in the face of adversity, not the adversity itself, defines a person
- [Becoming](/books/becoming) — "grief and resilience live together"; the two as simultaneous, not sequential
