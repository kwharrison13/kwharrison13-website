# Joshua 24

Joshua 24:15 — "choose you this day whom ye will serve... but as for me and my house, we will serve the LORD" — is the through-line in Kyle's notes, used as the scriptural anchor for [Agency](/notes/agency) and decisive commitment. In the July 6–12, 2020. Alma 30–31: "The Virtue of the Word of God" study it is cross-referenced with [Alma 30](/notes/alma-30):8 ("Choose ye this day, whom ye will serve") and tied to a mission recording in which Kyle examined what was holding him back — [Anger](/notes/anger), [Pride](/notes/pride), [Envy](/notes/envy), and [lust](/notes/lust) — alongside the two-wolves story attributed to [John Bytheway](/notes/john-bytheway).

In Revelation — Topical Study, the same verse caps a sequence on acting on revelation: the lesson that one must develop the intention to act on whatever answer is received, that demanding a witness before faith is [Sign Seeking](/notes/sign-seeking) ([Korihor](/notes/korihor)), and that it is in trying one's faith that the witness comes ([Ether 12](/notes/ether-12):6). The note ends emphatically — "SO ACT NOW. CHOOSE YE THIS DAY (Joshua 24:15)."

**Context:** Joshua 24 records Joshua's farewell address at Shechem, renewing Israel's covenant with the LORD; verse 15's "choose you this day whom ye will serve" is one of the Old Testament's most-cited calls to decision.

## Where this appears

- Revelation — Topical Study — the verse as the closing call to act on revelation rather than wait for a sign
