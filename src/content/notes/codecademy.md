# Codecademy

Codecademy appears in Kyle's corpus almost entirely as the prior employer of [Joseph Ambeault](/notes/joseph-ambeault), its former VP of Product (reporting to the CEO over product design/management; left July 2021), who served as an expert reference in the Sprig fka UserLeap - Diligence. Through Ambeault's account, Codecademy becomes a useful data point on the product-research tooling stack: in-app micro-surveys via [Sprig](/notes/sprig) drove **20–30% response rates vs. ~1% on [SurveyMonkey](/notes/surveymonkey)**; [FullStory](/notes/fullstory) session replay was rolled out company-wide (even to customer service), with Ambeault calling its pricing "outrageous" but still increasing spend; and [UserTesting](/notes/usertesting) "won every evaluation" he ran (Codecademy, Verizon, Discovery).

Codecademy also illustrates Ambeault's thesis that scaled (9-figure) companies start treating product-research functions as strategic rather than afterthoughts — Codecademy built its own A/B testing in-house (not trusting LaunchDarkly in production) and negotiated carve-outs in its FullStory contract. The product was primarily web-based (hard to write software on a phone), which shaped its tooling choices.

**Context:** Codecademy is an online interactive learning platform for coding and technical skills, founded in 2011; it was acquired by Skillsoft in 2022.
