# Sonos

In the Watershed Climate Diligence — 2021, Sonos appears as a real-world reference customer that [Deji Olukotun](/notes/deji-olukotun) (a Sonos sustainability/policy lead) used to pressure-test Watershed's product. Olukotun's December 2021 feedback frames Sonos as a hard case for Watershed's automated emissions mapping: it is described as "a 20 year-old company with highly distributed teams" whose data partly lives in non-scannable PDFs, especially around Bill of Materials (BOM). His conclusion was that Watershed's data-ingestion tooling would work best for startups, companies with standardized data, or those with an established Chief Sustainability Officer structure — not (yet) for a company like Sonos that still needs to "get our own house in order on the data side." He saw more value in Watershed's climate-plan and offset/removal-purchasing modules than in pure automation. Sonos was also noted earlier as a potential customer Watershed was introduced to (alongside Peloton, Okta, Robinhood, Otrium, Confluent, and Deliveroo), and a Sonos Carbon Footprint Report appears in the diligence appendix.

**Context:** Sonos is a publicly traded American audio-hardware company (NASDAQ: SONO), known for its multi-room wireless smart speakers and soundbars, founded in 2002 and headquartered in Santa Barbara, California.
