# Flow (Company)

**One-line:** *[Adam Neumann](/notes/adam-neumann)'s post-[WeWork](/notes/wework) residential real-estate venture — [a16z](/notes/a16z)'s largest single check ($350M at a $1B+ valuation, pre-operations, Aug 2022) — and the case study at the center of Kyle's essay [The Rise of The Cash Man](/essays/the-rise-of-the-cash-man).*

## What we know

- Residential real-estate company founded by [Adam Neumann](/notes/adam-neumann) after [WeWork](/notes/wework). [a16z's *Investing in Flow*](https://a16z.com/2022/08/15/investing-in-flow/) post announced a **$350M** check "before it even commences operations" at "over a billion dollar valuation" — the firm's **largest single check**. Pitched by [Marc Andreessen](/notes/marc-andreessen) as community + brand brought to residential, "so renters receive the benefits of owners."
- Neumann had [purchased 3,000+ apartment units](https://www.nytimes.com/2022/08/15/business/dealbook/adam-neumann-flow-new-company-wework-real-estate.html) in Miami, Fort Lauderdale, Atlanta, and Nashville (portfolio ~$1B). Flow operates Neumann's own properties and intends to serve "new developments and other third parties" — an owner + property-manager + branded-subscription-community hybrid. The "Neumann's assets, managed by his company" structure echoes the conflict-of-interest dynamic that dogged WeWork's S-1.
- Most ambiguous business-model amalgam in Kyle's corpus: per NYU's Arpit Gupta, likely a combination of **timeshares (flexibility) + co-ops (equity) + layaway financing (access) + luxury buildings**, plus optional crypto/rent-to-own upside — "a number of existing things marketed as one" to justify a tech valuation.

## In Kyle's analysis

[The Rise of The Cash Man](/essays/the-rise-of-the-cash-man) uses Flow not to endorse it (Kyle is explicitly **not a bull**) but to reverse-engineer "the venture process" — how a firm "makes the numbers work" on a [storyteller](/notes/storytelling) founder by stacking component businesses (RRE hold-co · property management · subscription community · data) into a comp sheet (~6x forward revenue, comps AppFolio / CoStar / Greystar) and a WeWork-scale forward model (~$30B / 10x if it works; "WeWork 2.0" at 1–2x revenue if it raises $20B and falls short). The pragmatic floor: "strike out all the fluff and you have a fancy property management company," whose best-in-class comp (Greystar) already puts Flow's valuation underwater. See the full notes in The Rise of The Cash Man - Research.
