# X.com

X.com was the financial-services company [Elon Musk](/notes/elon-musk) founded in 1999, pitched as "the Amazon of financial services" — a single entity meant to unify all of a person's financial life. As recounted in [The Founders](/books/the-founders), X.com and Confinity (founded by [Peter Thiel](/notes/peter-thiel) and [Max Levchin](/notes/max-levchin)) cannibalized each other competing for eBay sellers, then merged in 2000; the merged entity became [PayPal](/notes/paypal). Musk was ousted as CEO of the combined company while on his honeymoon in a coordinated board move and replaced by Thiel as interim CEO — a coup he later called "well-executed" and even "slightly complimentary."

The X.com pitch is the connective tissue between Musk and [Jeff Bezos](/notes/jeff-bezos) in the wiki: Bezos's "general store" / "one site offering everything" model is cited as the direct echo behind Musk's "Amazon of financial services" framing for X.com. The internship under [Peter Nicholson](/notes/peter-nicholson) at Scotiabank, which taught Musk "how lame banks are," is noted as the formative experience underwriting his confidence to attack the financial space.

**Context:** X.com, founded by Elon Musk in 1999, merged with Confinity in 2000 and was renamed PayPal in 2001; eBay acquired PayPal in 2002. Musk later reacquired the x.com domain and repurposed the name for his Twitter rebrand in 2023.
