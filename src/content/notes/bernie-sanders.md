# Bernie Sanders

The U.S. senator and presidential candidate appears in Kyle's notes as a case study in media and political communication rather than as a policy subject. The richest treatment is [David Perell](/notes/david-perell)'s 2016 long-read ["Do You #FeelTheBern"](/notes/do-you-feelthebern-behind-bernie-sanders-quest-to-take-over-the-white-house), which reads the Sanders campaign through [Marshall McLuhan](/notes/marshall-mcluhan)'s "the medium is the message": Sanders won young voters' trust by being unfiltered, direct, and authentic across social platforms, tuning his rhetoric to each medium — ephemeral 10-second Snapchat clips, combative Reddit AMAs, more careful and viral-built YouTube/Facebook content. Perell's argument is that direct, unfiltered access — a scarce resource in the mass-media age — is what made Sanders credible. He also surfaces in [The Death of Expertise](/books/the-death-of-expertise) alongside Newt Gingrich's "I'll go with how people feel," in the book's thread on the decline of deference to expertise in public life.

**Context:** Bernie Sanders is a U.S. Senator from Vermont, a self-described democratic socialist who ran high-profile campaigns for the Democratic presidential nomination in 2016 and 2020, both noted for grassroots small-dollar fundraising and strong appeal among younger voters.
