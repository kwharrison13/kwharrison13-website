# Moroni 7

Mormon's sermon on [Faith](/notes/faith), [Hope](/notes/hope), and [Charity](/notes/charity) — recorded by his son [Moroni](/notes/moroni) — and the Book of Mormon's central chapter on how good enters a life. In the December 7–13, 2020 Come Follow Me study Kyle traces a chain of doctrine through it: **[Real Intent](/notes/real-intent) is what gives an act value** ("except he shall do it with real intent it profiteth him nothing," [Moroni 7](/notes/moroni-7):6–10) — the same test of sincerity that gates [Moroni 10](/notes/moroni-10):4. The **[Light of Christ](/notes/light-of-christ)** lets every person judge good from evil ("everything which inviteth and enticeth to do good… is inspired of God," 7:13–16). **[Faith](/notes/faith)** is how you "lay hold upon every good thing," since "all things which are good come of Christ" (7:20–25). **Miracles** have not ceased — "if these things have ceased… it is because of unbelief" (7:35–37) — a striking comfort from Mormon, who wrote it after watching his nation destroyed. And **[Hope](/notes/hope)** is inseparable from faith (7:40–42).

The chapter is also Kyle's primary text on charity. In Charity & Love he leans on [Moroni 7](/notes/moroni-7):47 — "charity is the pure love of Christ" — to argue that charity is not a deed but an *attribute* of Christ; verse 45 defines it as His own character (suffereth long, is kind), and verse 48 commands praying "with all the energy of heart" to be filled with it. In Spiritual Gifts, [Moroni 7](/notes/moroni-7) is named alongside [Moroni 10](/notes/moroni-10) and [1 Corinthians 12](/notes/1-corinthians-12) as a list of gifts to seek — "can't think of anything? Ask for charity."

**Context:** Moroni 7 records a sermon by the prophet-historian Mormon, preserved by his son Moroni, and is among the most frequently cited Book of Mormon chapters in Latter-day Saint teaching on faith, hope, and charity.
