# Preach My Gospel

Across Kyle's gospel-study notes, [Preach My Gospel](/notes/preach-my-gospel) functions as the operating manual for [Missionary Work](/notes/missionary-work) — cited not as theory but as a page-numbered field guide. His Teaching Repentance study leans on the "commitment-extending pattern" (PMG pg. 195–196): missionaries "rarely teach without extending a commitment, because commitment is an essential part of repentance," and the purpose is always to *invite* people to come unto Christ rather than to drag or push. His Interviewing study draws the baptismal-interview pattern from PMG pg. 206 — assessing humility, desire for baptism, a broken heart and contrite spirit, witnessed repentance, and determination to serve. The Olive Tree Allegory notes cite PMG 137 on "what kind of effort the Lord requires of His servants."

The framing principle Kyle keeps returning to comes from [Faith is Not Blind](/books/faith-is-not-blind): missionaries don't "bring people into the church" — they "invite others to come unto Christ," so "the impetus is on the learner." [Chad's Big Quote Book](/notes/chad-s-big-quote-book) anchors the manual in scripture ("unto what were ye ordained? To preach my gospel by the Spirit") and in [M. Russell Ballard](/notes/m-russell-ballard)'s charge to use Preach My Gospel in members' homes, calling a mission "the greatest education in the world."

**Context:** Preach My Gospel: A Guide to Missionary Service is the LDS Church's standard missionary training manual, introduced in 2004. It organizes teaching around a set of lessons and the doctrine that missionaries should teach "by the Spirit," adapting to the learner rather than reciting fixed scripts.
