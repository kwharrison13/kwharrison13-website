# Utah Tech Ecosystem

The Utah Tech Ecosystem appears in Kyle's November 2021 sourcing call with [Serene Papenfuss](/notes/serene-papenfuss) of [Kickstart Seed Fund](/notes/kickstart-seed-fund) as a talent-flow map. The notes track which scaled Utah companies were producing founders and operators, with quality judgments attached: a lot of people were leaving [Divvy](/notes/divvy) and [Pluralsight](/notes/pluralsight), but Divvy alumni were "not as good a signal." Operators coming out of sales-led companies like [Qualtrics](/notes/qualtrics) tend to over-index on go-to-market, arriving thinking "they're going to hire 30 sales reps in the first year." The strongest signal in the notes: **product and engineering people coming out of [Lucid](/notes/lucid) and [Pluralsight](/notes/pluralsight) are typically better than average.** The framing is practical — using the ecosystem's company genealogy as a heuristic for evaluating Utah-based founders.

**Context:** Utah's technology hub, often branded "Silicon Slopes," is anchored along the Wasatch Front (Salt Lake City to Provo) and produced companies such as Qualtrics, Pluralsight, Lucid, Divvy, and Domo. Kickstart Seed Fund is one of the region's longstanding early-stage venture firms.
