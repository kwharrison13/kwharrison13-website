# Anaplan

Anaplan recurs across Kyle's notes as the reference point for connected planning and FP&A — the "$20B+ performance planning and management space" (cited at a ~$9–10B market cap, $500M+ revenue) (CaptivateIQ Diligence — 2021, November 8th, 2021 — Global Deal Flow). It anchors two arguments. First, it's the model for the "Excel in the Cloud" approach: the CaptivateIQ thesis notes that giving users Excel-like flexibility with better data integrations "has worked successfully for both Anaplan and Smartsheet," explaining why users revert to spreadsheets when overwhelmed by overly opinionated platforms. Second, it's the long-term competitive target — CaptivateIQ's big vision is to extend from sales-commission management into forecasting and broader operational planning to compete with Anaplan, a framing repeated in the Global Deal Flow review.

Anaplan also shows up from the expert/customer side: a Vectra AI finance manager who has used Anaplan judged CaptivateIQ's calculation engine "much more sophisticated" (CaptivateIQ Diligence — 2021), and Ashton Grewal of Dorsal Capital Management mentioned holding Anaplan in the public markets (October 16th, 2023 — Ashton Grewal (Dorsal Capital Management)).

**Context:** Anaplan is an enterprise cloud planning platform (FP&A, sales planning, supply chain, workforce) built on its proprietary in-memory "Hyperblock" calculation engine. Founded in 2006, it IPO'd on the NYSE in 2018 and was taken private by Thoma Bravo in 2022 for roughly $10.7B.
