# Smart Government

"Smart government" is the framing [Jeff Friedman](/notes/jeff-friedman) uses to reposition the better-known "smart cities" idea, anchored in his work at [New York University](/notes/new-york-university)'s center on the topic. Per Kyle's notes, Friedman argues "there's no such thing as a smart city because there's no such thing as a city; a city is a construct" — and prefers to organize the work around the *citizen and community* rather than around the city as a technological object. The move is deliberately deflationary: it pulls the emphasis away from sensors, dashboards, and urban tech toward the human and institutional layer of governing.

The concept surfaces from a single networking call in Kyle's wiki, where Friedman — a former Microsoft government-sales executive and turnaround consultant for small cities — discussed smart government and the use of data in urban government. It remains thinly sourced here, grounded in that one conversation.
