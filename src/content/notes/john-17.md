# John 17

The seventeenth chapter of the Gospel of John — Christ's great Intercessory Prayer — recurs across Kyle's gospel-study notes through two anchors. The first is **John 17:3**, "this is life eternal, that they might know thee the only true God, and Jesus Christ whom thou hast sent." In 10 Gifts To Take Home From Your Mission, that verse defines the first gift: a knowledge and love of God, "where that knowledge is 'life eternal.'" Knowledge — Topical Study uses the same verse to argue "no man can be saved in ignorance," and The Godhead cites it to insist that "if it is a matter of eternal life to know God the Father and His Son… then it is important enough to make sure that we've got the right idea."

The second anchor is John 17 as the model **prayer of the mediator**. Prayer — Topical Study reads it (alongside [3 Nephi 19](/notes/3-nephi-19)) as the pattern for how to pray: grateful, "unselfishly pleading for the benefit of others" (John 17:9, 15–19), giving "a faithful accounting of the blessings of God," and wholly submissive — "I have finished the work which thou gavest me to do" (John 17:4). Christ "gives an accounting of those individuals who have chosen to follow Him… He pleads for us!"

**Context:** John 17 records Jesus's prayer to the Father on the night before the Crucifixion, in which he prays for himself, for his apostles, and for all future believers — petitioning "that they all may be one." It is traditionally called the High Priestly or Intercessory Prayer.

## Where this appears

- 10 Gifts To Take Home From Your Mission — John 17:3, knowing God as "life eternal," the first gift of a mission.
- Knowledge — Topical Study — John 17:3 as the basis for "no man can be saved in ignorance."
- Prayer — Topical Study — John 17 as Christ's mediator prayer and the model pattern of how to pray.
