# Martin Kuppinger

Co-founder of [KuppingerCole](/notes/kuppingercole), a ~50-person European analyst firm specializing in identity and cybersecurity (market research, events, and advisory). Kyle drew on Kuppinger as an external analyst voice during his 2021 diligence on [Salt Security](/notes/salt-security): in a joint analyst session with [Alexei Balaganski](/notes/alexei-balaganski) on October 12, 2021, the two framed API security as a fast-growing space (one vendor when they began covering it in 2014, now 31 vendors) tightly coupled to data protection — "Why would you want to protect individual components of your infrastructure when you can just protect your data at the point of creation?" Their characterization of legacy network-security players as "the dinosaurs in the market" — having "a lot of money and potential" but scrambling to find a new place — is cited on the [Imperva](/notes/imperva) page as the analyst read on incumbents under pressure from API-native entrants.

Per the session notes, KuppingerCole offers research access and individual calls to investors at roughly $25K per week but does not perform due diligence itself, instead working alongside firms like PwC and Accenture.

**Context:** Martin Kuppinger is a German cybersecurity analyst and founder of KuppingerCole Analysts AG, a research and advisory firm focused on identity and access management (IAM), cybersecurity, and digital transformation.
