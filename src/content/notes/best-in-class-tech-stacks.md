# Best-in-Class Tech Stacks

"Best-in-Class Tech Stacks" is a sourcing/investing lens in Kyle's notes: the idea that modern companies assemble a stack of point solutions, each the best in its category, and that mapping those categories is a way to source deals. It became an explicit 2022 deal-flow goal in December 13th, 2021 — US Deal Flow (2022 Goals) — Kyle's third goal was to map "best-in-class tech stacks" onto the "Index Family" of experts, naming a slot per function: **Pave in HR, CaptivateIQ in sales, Ada Support in customer support, [Sprig](/notes/sprig) in product.** The underlying economic logic surfaces in the CaptivateIQ Diligence — 2021 notes via Amit Bendov of [Gong](/notes/gong): "if you provide the best product then even if you have 40% market share you're going to have 80% of the profit in the market" — i.e., quality concentrates the profit pool, which is what makes a best-in-class point solution a defensible bet.

As a tag, it collects Kyle's product-craft reading and sits alongside [Product](/notes/product), [Product Management](/notes/product-management), and [Playbook — Product](/notes/playbook-product). The saved sources under it include Vedika Jain's essay "The Rise of Superpower Tech" and Ken Norton's bringthedonuts.com product-management writing. It is also one of the concept tags on [Product Led Growth — The End User Era](/books/product-led-growth-the-end-user-era).
