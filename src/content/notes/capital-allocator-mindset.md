# Capital Allocator Mindset

**One-line definition:** *Treating attention/interest like scarce capital — forcing the question "what would you put dollars towards?" as a discipline that surfaces real priorities.*

## How sources describe it

- From Kyle's note ([raw/inbox/Capital Allocator Mindset.md](../../raw/inbox/Capital%20Allocator%20Mindset.md)), verbatim:

  > Being forced to say "what would you put dollars towards" will ensure that you radically prioritize what is interesting vs. when you FEEL like you have endless amounts of "interest" you can have in things.

- The core move: a budget constraint (dollars) is a forcing function for prioritization. Stated "interest" is cheap and feels unlimited; *allocated* interest is revealed preference. Asking where you'd actually deploy capital collapses the gap between felt interest and real priority.
