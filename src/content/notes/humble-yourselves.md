# Humble Yourselves

A gospel-study theme Kyle draws principally from [Ether 12](/notes/ether-12):27 — "I give unto men weakness that they may be humble… for if they humble themselves before me, and have faith in me, then will I make weak things become strong unto them." Kyle's distinctive reading inverts the usual emphasis: the *stronger* you are, the more confident you feel that you are smart enough or capable enough to handle things on your own, so "the trick is to humble yourself even (and especially) in your strength." He files this directly against [The Pride Cycle](/notes/the-pride-cycle) and pairs it with [Faith](/notes/faith) as the two-step ordering — humility and faith first, the miracle after.

The theme sits inside Moroni's discourse on weakness made strong, and Kyle ties it to the [Brother of Jared](/notes/brother-of-jared), whose faith moved the mountain Zerin: faith comes first, the miracle follows ("thou workest after men have faith," [Ether 12](/notes/ether-12):30). It surfaces again in his notes on [Russell M. Nelson](/notes/russell-m-nelson)'s talk on faith moving mountains.
