# Perfection

In Kyle's Sermon on the Mount topical study, "perfection" is read against Matthew 5:48 ("Be ye therefore perfect, even as your Father which is in heaven is perfect") as meaning *complete, finished, fully developed* — not flawlessness achieved instantly. The study frames the purpose of life as **progression, not change in the twinkling of an eye**: humanity as a whole had to progress from the natural man, through the law of Moses, to the law of Christ. Strikingly, the note argues that even Christ, sinless as He was, could not include Himself in the Father's perfection at the moment He spoke those words because He "wasn't complete" yet — "But He is now, and we can be also."

Perfection here sits alongside the chapter's other [Agency](/notes/agency)-and-[Judgment](/notes/judgment) themes: weighing the worth of our desires against the eternal perspective (Matthew 6:19–21), and judging on righteous principles rather than rendering final verdicts.
