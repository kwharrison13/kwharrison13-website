# Family Culture

In [How Will You Measure Your Life](/books/how-will-you-measure-your-life), [Clayton Christensen](/notes/clayton-christensen) frames a family's culture as something that accretes from repeated small decisions rather than being declared once. The book's central claim: "Allowing your children to get away with lazy or disrespectful behavior a few times will begin the process of making it your family's culture. So will telling them that you're proud of them when they work hard to solve a problem." Culture, in this view, is the cumulative record of what behaviors a family tolerates and what it praises — each instance either reinforcing or eroding the norm.

The idea ports Christensen's organizational theory of culture onto the home: just as a company's culture is the set of problem-solving methods that have worked often enough to become the default, a family's culture is built from which responses get rewarded and which get let slide. The implication is that parents shape culture less through stated values than through the consistency of their reactions in ordinary moments.

## Where this appears

- [How Will You Measure Your Life](/books/how-will-you-measure-your-life) — argues a family's culture forms from repeated small decisions: what lazy or disrespectful behavior gets allowed versus what hard problem-solving gets praised.
