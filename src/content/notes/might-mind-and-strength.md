# Might, Mind, and Strength

"Might, mind, and strength" is the scriptural phrase Kyle drew out of [Alma 39](/notes/alma-39):13, where [Alma the Younger](/notes/alma-the-younger) counsels his son [Corianton](/notes/corianton) to "turn to the Lord with all your mind, might, and strength." In Kyle's study of [Alma 39](/notes/alma-39)–[Alma 42](/notes/alma-42), the phrase becomes a shorthand for whole-self devotion — the call to give everything one has back to the Lord rather than holding any part in reserve.

Kyle explicitly ties the phrase to the [Law of Consecration](/notes/law-of-consecration), glossing it as "time, talents, energy; might, mind, and strength" — i.e., the full inventory of what a person can dedicate. It sits alongside the chapter's other threads (self-mastery, parenting, teaching toward Christ) as the affirmative counterpart to Corianton's failure: where Corianton boasted in his own strength and let his mind go idle, the remedy is to redirect that same might, mind, and strength toward God.

**Context:** "Might, mind, and strength" echoes the great commandment ("love the Lord thy God with all thy heart, might, mind, and strength," Mark 12:30 / D&C 59:5) and recurs across Latter-day Saint scripture as a formula for total consecration of self.
