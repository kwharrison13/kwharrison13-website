# Mark Sellers

An investor whose Harvard MBA address — "So You Want To Be The Next Warren Buffett? How's Your Writing?" — Kyle uses as a load-bearing source in The Storytelling of Investing - Research. Sellers ties [Warren Buffett](/notes/warren-buffett)'s investing directly to his writing: "If you can't write clearly… you don't think very clearly. And if you don't think clearly, you're in trouble." His address makes the harder argument that the usual inputs are not edge: "You have almost no chance of being a great investor… like 2% or less," reading is "table stakes, not edge," and experience is "required for admission… not a source of an economic moat."

In Kyle's framework this places Sellers among the citations supporting the [Wordsmiths](/notes/wordsmiths) thesis — that clear writing is the rarest and most compounding input to great investing — and connects him to the [Berkshire Hathaway](/notes/berkshire-hathaway) page as a commentator on Buffett-as-writer.
