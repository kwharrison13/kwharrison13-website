# Bullshit Jobs

**One-line:** *[David Graeber](/notes/david-graeber)'s thesis (originally the essay "On the Phenomenon of Bullshit Jobs") that a large and growing share of jobs are ones even the people doing them consider pointless.*

In [Utopia For Realists](/books/utopia-for-realists) the concept anchors a value-vs-pay inversion: the jobs that shift money around create little tangible value yet pay the most, while the "agents of prosperity" — teachers, nurses, garbage collectors — are paid poorly. Bregman pairs it with the claim that "for every dollar a bank earns, ~60 cents is destroyed elsewhere," whereas a dollar to a researcher returns at least $5 — i.e., it has become increasingly profitable *not* to innovate.
