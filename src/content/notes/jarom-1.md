# Jarom 1

Jarom 1 is the short Book of Mormon chapter Kyle cites for one of the recurring promised-land covenants: "Inasmuch as ye will keep my commandments ye shall prosper in the land" ([Jarom 1](/notes/jarom-1):9). In his July 20–26, 2020. Alma 36–38: "Look to God and Live" study it appears in that prosper-in-the-land formulation, and it shows up again in the scripture cross-references for that week.

In The Process of the Book of Mormon, Jarom 1:9 is grouped with the same "keep my commandments / prosper in the land" promise as it recurs across [2 Nephi 1](/notes/2-nephi-1), [Omni 1](/notes/omni-1), [Mosiah 1](/notes/mosiah-1), [Alma 9](/notes/alma-9), and [Alma 36](/notes/alma-36) — Kyle tracing how a single covenant phrase threads through the book's many authors.

**Context:** The Book of Jarom is a single, brief chapter near the start of the Book of Mormon, written by Jarom (a descendant of Nephi keeping the small plates) and notable for reaffirming the land-prosperity covenant given to Lehi's posterity.
