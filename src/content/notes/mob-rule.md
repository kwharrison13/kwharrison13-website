# Mob Rule

The failure mode of democratic legitimacy: what happens when [Voice of the People](/notes/voice-of-the-people) sheds restraint and popular will becomes raw majority coercion. In Kyle's reading, mob rule is the dark twin of "the voice of the people" — the same principle that grounds democratic authority is also "democracy's greatest vulnerability to mob rule and the tyranny of the uninformed majority." [Voice of the People](/notes/voice-of-the-people)

The anchoring case is the Mormon expulsions in [Joseph Smith — Rough Stone Rolling](/books/joseph-smith-rough-stone-rolling), read as an instance where "in the name of democracy" a majority deprived a minority of its democratic rights — the through-line tying "vox populi is the vox Dei" to its abuse. The structural counterweight Kyle keeps returning to is an [Educated Electorate](/notes/educated-electorate): the precondition that keeps popular will from collapsing into mob rule.

**Context:** "Mob rule" (ochlocracy) is the classical political-theory label for governance by the unchecked mob — distinct from lawful democracy — and is the danger the U.S. founders sought to temper through representative institutions and minority-rights protections (the "tyranny of the majority" Tocqueville and Madison warned against).
