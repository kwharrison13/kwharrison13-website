# Roam Brainstorm

"Roam Brainstorm" is one of Kyle's personal capture tags inside [Roam Research](/notes/roam-research) — a #[Roam Brainstorm](/notes/roam-brainstorm) marker he attaches to highlights and notes that contain a kernel worth coming back to and developing further. It functions as a self-curated idea queue rather than a topic: when Kyle reads something that sparks a half-formed thought, he tags it so the relational graph surfaces it later. The tag clusters heavily around organizational design, communication, and vision-vs-execution themes.

In practice the tag appears most densely on Kyle's marginalia in the Amazon books. In [Amazon Unbound](/books/amazon-unbound) he flags "creative wandering" (trusting an engineering team to go execute and return with increments), the balance between "inventing for the customer" and "building with the customer in mind," "vision vs. details," and "culture of reading vs. writing." In [The Everything Store](/books/the-everything-store) he tags the note that employees, like customers, want to understand vision ("[Roadmaps are fake news](/notes/roadmaps-are-fake-news) but if you're not using a road map then how are you communicating vision?"). The same "culture of reading vs. writing" thread connects to [Six-Page Narratives](/notes/six-page-narratives) (the [Bezos](/notes/jeff-bezos) memo culture) and recurs in [How To Read a Book](/books/how-to-read-a-book), where Kyle ties it to "learning, no greater responsibility." In the June 22nd, 2021 — Conor White-Sullivan (Roam Research) call he tags [Bridgewater Associates](/notes/bridgewater-associates)' "individual baseball card" practice — the same idea that resurfaces in The Storytelling of Investing - Research. The tag thus sits adjacent to [RoamCult](/notes/roamcult) in Kyle's tools-for-thought vocabulary.
