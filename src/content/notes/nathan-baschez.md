# Nathan Baschez

**One-line:** *Writer and product thinker (Every / Divinations) whose essay "What good is strategy right now?" framed the tension Kyle leans on between strategic value-creation and basic human dignity.*

## What we know

- Author of *"What good is strategy right now?"* (June 2020, written around the George Floyd murder), which contrasts the imperative to create business value with the need to enable quality humanity despite the business implications. (source: Unions, Unions Everywhere - Research)
- Argues that limiting the "bargaining power of suppliers" (à la Michael Porter) reads very differently when the suppliers are workers — that decimating the labor movement cost workers "compounding effects over time" and a "much more equitable society," with disproportionate impact on Black Americans.
- Honest about union drawbacks (e.g. police unions as an obstacle to police-brutality reform) while noting that US business owners take a "principled" stand against labor's anti-competitive behavior "while happily glossing over their own."
