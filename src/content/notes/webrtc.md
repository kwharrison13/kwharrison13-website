# WebRTC

The real-time audio/video technology layer at the center of [Kelly Toole](/notes/kelly-toole)'s 2021 diligence on [LiveKit](/notes/livekit) (see August 18th, 2021 — LiveKit Questions (Kelly Toole)). In working through the diligence questions, Kelly described "digging deep in WebRTC land," and the meeting maps the competitive landscape for developers building realtime experiences across three tiers: build it all in-house (hard, flexible, scalable), leverage open-source ([LiveKit](/notes/livekit), Janus, Jitsi — medium-hard, flexible, scalable), or use CPaaS providers (Twilio, Daily.co, Agora — easy, but inflexible with scalability limits). LiveKit positions itself as "open source infrastructure for building and scaling realtime audio and video experiences," aiming to fill the gap between those options with an easy, flexible, scalable solution plus a planned cloud product.

**Context:** WebRTC (Web Real-Time Communication) is an open standard and set of browser APIs enabling peer-to-peer audio, video, and data streaming directly between clients without plugins.
