# Permanent Capital

**One-line definition:** *Investor capital that can't be redeemed at will, giving a manager the freedom to act long-term.*

## How it shows up

- [Morgan Housel](/notes/morgan-housel)'s flywheel: content → trust → permanent capital → durable advantage. "[Buffett](/notes/warren-buffett) and [Marks](/notes/howard-marks) used content to instill trust, trust gave them permanent capital, and permanent capital gave them a massive financial advantage."

## Related concepts

- [Morgan Housel](/notes/morgan-housel) — frames permanent capital as the payoff of the content → trust flywheel.
- [Warren Buffett](/notes/warren-buffett) — cited as an investor whose content-built trust secured permanent capital and a durable advantage.
- [Howard Marks](/notes/howard-marks) — cited alongside Buffett as using content to convert trust into permanent capital.
- [Capital Allocation](/notes/capital-allocation) — the broader discipline under which permanent capital gives a manager freedom to act long-term.
