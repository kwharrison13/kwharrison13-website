# COVID

In Kyle's wiki, COVID functions mainly as a forcing function for two of his recurring interests: the geography of cities and the adoption of software. Reading [The New Urban Crisis](/books/the-new-urban-crisis), Kyle uses COVID to push on Richard Florida's argument that the affluent return to the urban core for high-paying jobs, short commutes, and amenities. [Remote Work](/notes/remote-work), Kyle notes, removes the commute and proximity requirements — leaving amenities as the only question — and "in a COVID world, those amenities look different (back yards, larger houses, more open space)," pointing *away* from the dense core. This is the seed of his [COVID pushes us towards something like Prosperable](/notes/covid-pushes-us-towards-something-like-prosperable) / [Prosperable](/notes/prosperable) thesis.

On the software side, the [SignEasy](/notes/signeasy) page cites COVID as a tailwind for e-signature: the company saw its SMB e-signature market as a "blue-ocean $20–30B opportunity growing 30%+ post-COVID," with business revenue growing 80–100% as document workflows went remote.

**Context:** COVID-19 is the disease caused by the SARS-CoV-2 coronavirus; the global pandemic beginning in early 2020 triggered widespread lockdowns and an accelerated, durable shift toward remote work and digital workflows.
