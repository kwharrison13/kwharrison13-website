# Stripe

The payments-infrastructure company ([Patrick Collison](/notes/patrick-collison) and [John Collison](/notes/john-collison)) that recurs in Kyle's notes as the **canonical developer-first / product-led-growth archetype**. [Product Led Growth — The End User Era](/books/product-led-growth-the-end-user-era) quotes Stripe directly as the model: "Stripe is a company built by developers, for developers… developer sales isn't about selling in a traditional sense. The hard pitch, the feature list, rehearsed objection handling… none of these things matter when the buyer of a product is the person that builds with it, rather than a procurement professional." Kyle's takeaway from that book — that PLG sales turns into [Customer Success](/notes/customer-success) — is anchored on the Stripe developer-sales motion.

Stripe also functions as a **diligence reference node**: in Identity & Access Management Diligence — 2021 and [SendGrid](/notes/sendgrid)'s notes it's grouped with [Twilio](/notes/twilio) and [PagerDuty](/notes/pagerduty) as proof "developers are the new kingmakers"; in [Plaid](/notes/plaid) and Salt Security diligence it's named among the heavily-connected fintech endpoints (with [Modern Treasury](/notes/modern-treasury)); and it appears in The Talent Vortex - Research as one of the high-growth fintech operators Kyle sampled alongside Plaid and [Dropbox](/notes/dropbox). Its sharpest cautionary appearance is via [Fast](/notes/fast): Stripe led the one-click-checkout startup's $20M Series A and led its later rounds, so Fast's collapse carried reputational spillover onto Stripe as a corporate VC. Separately, Stripe surfaces in the Watershed Climate Diligence — 2021 notes as a Watershed customer and Stripe Climate channel partner whose founders are Watershed investors.

**Context:** Stripe is a privately held payments-infrastructure company founded in 2010 by brothers Patrick and John Collison, providing APIs that let businesses accept and manage online payments; it became one of the most valuable venture-backed startups of its era.
