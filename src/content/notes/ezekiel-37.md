# Ezekiel 37

A chapter of Ezekiel cited in the Abrahamic Covenant study for its "two sticks" prophecy of the [Gathering of Israel](/notes/gathering-of-israel). The study reads it as the Restoration: "Take thee a stick and write upon it for Judah, write for Joseph, the stick of Ephraim" — the stick of Judah (the Bible) and the stick of Joseph (the Book of Mormon) become one in the hand ([Ezekiel 37](/notes/ezekiel-37):15–17). It is paired with [Daniel 2](/notes/daniel-2)'s image prophecy of the empires of history down to the European nations and the stone "cut without hands" (the restored church, 1830). As mostly holders of the birthright through [Ephraim](/notes/ephraim), the seed of Abraham bear the responsibility to gather Israel and bless the nations through the ordinances of salvation.

**Context:** Ezekiel 37 contains two of the prophet's best-known visions: the valley of dry bones brought back to life, and the joining of the two sticks of Judah and Joseph into one — the passage Latter-day Saints read as foretelling the coming together of the Bible and the Book of Mormon.
