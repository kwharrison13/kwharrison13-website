# Oil & Gas Service Business 2.0

A capture from Kyle's June 29th, 2021 Roam "Quick Capture" reading log (an Index Ventures-era reading session) that frames an angle on the [Oil & Gas](/notes/oil-and-gas) industry: not the commodity or the producers, but a *modernized* take on the **oilfield-services** layer — the businesses that supply drilling, equipment, and technical services to producers rather than owning the reserves. The "2.0" framing implies a rebuild or next-generation version of those service companies. The saved sources point squarely at this layer: a Wikipedia list of oilfield service companies and an Investopedia oil & gas industry overview, alongside two private Google Drive documents. Beyond the saved links the capture carries no written synthesis, so the specific thesis behind "2.0" is not attested in the source.

**Context:** The oilfield-services sector (Schlumberger, Halliburton, Baker Hughes, and many smaller specialists) provides the drilling, completion, and technical services that exploration-and-production companies need — a distinct, often more asset-light slice of the energy value chain than reserve ownership. A "2.0" thesis in this space typically points at modernizing legacy service businesses with software, automation, or new operating models.
