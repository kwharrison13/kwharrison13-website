# Civil-Military Fusion

China's strategic doctrine of deliberately integrating commercial and military technology development — the deliberate fusion the US fragments by design. In [Chip War](/books/chip-war), Kyle flags it as one of the book's load-bearing facts: the People's Liberation Army can simply buy cutting-edge US chips off-the-shelf and plug them into military systems (a Georgetown study of 343 PLA AI-procurement contracts found fewer than 20% involved export-controlled firms), so "Civil Military Fusion" looks like it's working. Kyle's note on that passage reads simply "#Anduril - Civil Military Fusion."

The concept is one of the most-recurring annotations across Kyle's defense/industrial reading, where it functions as evidence for a verticalized defense-tech investment thesis around [Anduril](/notes/anduril) — civil-military fusion sits alongside [Sensor Fusion](/notes/sensor-fusion) and attritable/expendable assets as the pillars of that thesis. [The Man From The Future](/books/the-man-from-the-future) supplies the deep historical precedent: [John von Neumann](/notes/john-von-neumann)'s [RAND](/notes/rand-corporation)-style applied research is read as scientists self-organizing around national-security problems ahead of the bureaucracy — proto-civil-military fusion, and a model for what the US once did and Anduril aims to revive.

**Context:** "Military-Civil Fusion" (军民融合) is an official Chinese national strategy, elevated under Xi Jinping, that aims to erase barriers between China's civilian commercial/research sector and its military so that advances in one flow freely to the other.
