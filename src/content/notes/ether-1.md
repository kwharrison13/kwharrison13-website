# Ether 1

Ether 1 opens the Book of Ether and the Jaredite narrative that Kyle studies in November 9–15, 2020. Ether 1–5: "Rend That Veil of Unbelief". It places [Jared](/notes/jared) and his brother "from the great tower, at the time the Lord confounded the language of the people" (Ether 1:33), with the [Brother of Jared](/notes/brother-of-jared) described as "a large and mighty man, and a man highly favored of the Lord" — the one Jared repeatedly sends to inquire of the Lord, first to spare their language, then to learn where they should go (Ether 1:34–38).

Kyle's reading of Ether 1 turns on *why* the Brother of Jared was protected when the languages were confounded: he was *already* righteous and "highly favored of the Lord." The relationship with God that saved him was built before the crisis arrived, not in response to it — Kyle frames this through [Thomas S. Monson](/notes/thomas-s-monson)'s line that "when the time for performance arrives, the time for preparation is past." This makes Ether 1 the entry point into his [Spiritual Complacency](/notes/spiritual-complacency) theme: readiness is a position you must already be standing in. He pairs it with [Russell M. Nelson](/notes/russell-m-nelson)'s warning that in coming days survival will require "the guiding, directing, comforting, and constant influence of the Holy Ghost" — the constant relationship, not the emergency appeal, is what carries you.

**Context:** Ether 1 is the first chapter of the Book of Ether in the Book of Mormon, abridged by Moroni from the record of the prophet Ether. It introduces the Jaredites, who left the Tower of Babel when the languages were confounded, and the Brother of Jared, whose prayers spared his family's language and set the migration in motion.
