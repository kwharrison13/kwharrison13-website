# Restoration

In Kyle's gospel-study corpus, the Restoration is the founding event of Latter-day Saint theology — the re-establishment of the gospel, priesthood, and ordinances after the Great Apostasy. Christian Reformation, Zion, and Seekers sets it against the religious landscape it entered: the early Saints were **seekers** awaiting "a restoration of all things," and "the Restoration was built to attract seekers rather than reformers." Mormonism is framed there not as a replacement for Christianity but as an attempt to "save it… to bring it out of the woods and out of obscurity," with the "Christian reformation… the purification of the apostasy to prepare the world for the Restoration."

The Olive Tree Allegory (Jacob 5) maps the same arc — scattering, apostasy, and the grafting-in of the House of Ephraim ([Brigham Young](/notes/brigham-young) taught [Joseph Smith](/notes/joseph-smith) was a pure Ephraimite) as "the preparation for the restoration." Joseph Smith Lecture 1 — The First Vision and Its Aftermath treats the First Vision as "the foundational theophany of the Restoration," noting that "at crucial turning points in the Restoration" the adversary "has made his power felt." [The Life of John Taylor](/books/the-life-of-john-taylor) shows the doctrine in practice: Taylor's Liverpool lectures covered "the restoration of the gospel through the ministration of angels… and the powers and authority of the Holy Priesthood," with the Restoration and the [Book of Mormon](/notes/book-of-mormon) named as "the doctrinal core of Taylor's preaching."

**Context:** In Latter-day Saint belief, the Restoration refers to the founding events beginning with Joseph Smith's First Vision (c. 1820), including the coming forth of the Book of Mormon and the restoration of priesthood authority — held to restore the original church established by Jesus Christ after a period of apostasy.

## Where this appears

- Olive Tree Allegory — Jacob 5 read as scattering, apostasy, and the grafting-in that prepares the Restoration.

- [The Life of John Taylor](/books/the-life-of-john-taylor) — the Restoration and Book of Mormon as the doctrinal core of Taylor's preaching.
