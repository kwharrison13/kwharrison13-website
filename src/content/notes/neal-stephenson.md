# Neal Stephenson

Science-fiction novelist who recurs across Kyle's corpus as a [historical-futurism](/notes/historical-futurism) touchstone — sci-fi as a tool for inventing (and warning about) the future. In [Boom — Bubbles & The End of Stagnation](/books/boom-bubbles-and-the-end-of-stagnation) he's quoted from "Innovation Starvation": "believing we have all the technology we'll ever need, we seek to draw attention to its destructive side effects" — the lament that anchors the book's stagnation thesis. In [Amazon Unbound](/books/amazon-unbound) he appears as one of the science-fiction influences behind [Jeff Bezos](/notes/jeff-bezos)'s [Blue Origin](/notes/blue-origin): in its early years Blue "resembled a club more than a company," a think tank including "novelist Neal Stephenson and science historian George Dyson" brainstorming radical ways to travel into space.

His novels surface as cultural reference points too: *[Snow Crash](/books/snow-crash)* (1992) is cited in Books 2.0 - Research as the cyberpunk classic of virtual-reality metaverse and neural-interface hacking, and *Fall, Or Dodge In Hell* appears in Historical Futurism - Research via [Dan Egan](/notes/dan-egan), who reads science fiction precisely because it "closely models reality."

**Context:** Neal Stephenson is an American speculative-fiction author known for *Snow Crash*, *Cryptonomicon*, *The Diamond Age*, and *Seveneves*; he coined the term "metaverse" and has worked with real-world space and tech ventures including Blue Origin and Magic Leap.
