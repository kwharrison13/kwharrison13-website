# Morality

A recurring theme in the [Why You're Christian](/notes/why-you-re-christian) long-read, framed around the question of whether morality can exist without an eternal construct. The argument annotated there: "Without an eternal construct, morality is just consensus — and consensus shifts," so "today's virtues can become tomorrow's vices… Without the word of God, all we have are opinions." Kyle flags the open question — *does morality exist without an eternal construct?* — as a gospel topic to explore.

## Where this appears

- [Why You're Christian](/notes/why-you-re-christian) — the source of the "morality without God is just shifting consensus" argument, and Kyle's annotated question about whether objective morality requires an eternal construct.
