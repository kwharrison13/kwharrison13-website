# Sendhil Mullainathan

Co-author (with Eldar Shafir) of the behavioral-economics research on scarcity that anchors the [Scarcity Mentality](/notes/scarcity-mentality) concept page, as cited in [Rutger Bregman](/notes/rutger-bregman)'s [Utopia For Realists](/books/utopia-for-realists). The Mullainathan & Shafir finding reframes the "why do the poor make dumb decisions?" question: people aren't poor because they make bad decisions; they make bad decisions because scarcity itself consumes cognitive bandwidth, collapsing the long-term perspective. The wiki uses this research to explain why financial-education programs barely move outcomes — "like teaching someone to swim and then throwing them in a stormy sea" — and why an unconditional [Universal Basic Income](/notes/universal-basic-income), which removes the scarcity itself, outperforms behavioral nudges.

**Context:** Sendhil Mullainathan (b. 1972) is an American economist, currently a professor at the University of Chicago Booth School of Business. With psychologist Eldar Shafir he co-authored *Scarcity: Why Having Too Little Means So Much* (2013), the work behind the cognitive-bandwidth findings referenced here. He is also a recipient of a MacArthur "genius" Fellowship.
