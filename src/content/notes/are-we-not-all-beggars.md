# Are We Not All Beggars?

"Are We Not All Beggars?" surfaces in Kyle's Fasting study (September 5, 2021) as a "See also" reference attached to a discussion of why we care for others. The note sits alongside the Matthew 25 "least of these" passage and a tiered model of [Motivation](/notes/motivation) — telestial (because we have to), terrestrial (because we want to), celestial (because we love God) — framed as "obedience to the unenforceable": doing right out of love for God rather than compulsion. The beggars idea reinforces that frame: it situates charity and the fast offering as a recognition of shared dependence rather than a transaction.

**Context:** "Are We Not All Beggars?" is a 2014 General Conference address by Elder Jeffrey R. Holland of the LDS Church's Quorum of the Twelve Apostles, drawing on King Benjamin's sermon in Mosiah 4 ("For are we not all beggars? Do we not all depend upon the same Being...?"). It teaches that because all people are spiritually indebted to God, caring for the poor is a fundamental Christian obligation.
