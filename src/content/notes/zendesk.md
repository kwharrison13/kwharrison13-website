# Zendesk

Zendesk, the customer-service / support platform, appears across Kyle's notes as an incumbent fixture of the contact-center tech stack. In the Cresta.ai - Diligence, it shows up as the support layer a luxury (Neiman Marcus) buyer ran alongside [LivePerson](/notes/liveperson) chat — and the same expert noted that at [Groupon](/notes/groupon) he had been "one of Zendesk's largest customers," using that scale as a reference point for how thoughtfully Cresta should size the Neiman Marcus opportunity. In the November 2nd, 2021 — Stephen Case (Facebook) call, Zendesk surfaces as one of the channels (with [Twilio ($TWLO)](/notes/twilio)) through which larger enterprises can push FB Messenger / WhatsApp message APIs.

Zendesk also anchors a sales-leadership lineage in Kyle's people notes: [Jim Cyb](/notes/jim-cyb) is logged as the former CRO of both Duo and Zendesk, part of the "Urlocker/Cyb school of sales ops" framing the diligence values for analytically rigorous, ops-driven enterprise sales leadership.

**Context:** Zendesk is a customer-service and support-ticketing software company founded in 2007 (Copenhagen, now San Francisco); it went public in 2014 and was taken private in 2022.
