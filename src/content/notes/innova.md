# Innova

**One-line:** *A first/last-mile electric mobility-as-a-service company (Innova EV) whose core thesis is heavy IoT data collection from instrumented vehicles.*

## What we know

- Core business is first/last-mile mobility-as-a-service: electric low/medium on-highway vehicles instrumented with an IoT data-analytics platform (sensors, cameras, air-quality sensing, ground radar) gathering data every ~10ms; serves commuter (train station → office park) and campus carshare/rideshare use cases, and is testing autonomous driving at Ohio State for the Smart City Challenge (May 16th, 2018 — Roman (Innova)).
- The real thesis is data ([Data in Investing](/notes/data-in-investing)): aims to be the most-instrumented high-use mobility option and sells a data-aggregation platform/dashboards to cities (Chicago), utilities (ComEd), transit (Metra), municipalities, and employers — often around infrastructure, asset utilization, and CSR reporting (May 16th, 2018 — Roman (Innova)).
- Four monetization layers: rides (~$3/ride, with intent to make them free to capture more data), on-vehicle advertising, an in-vehicle media/coupon platform, and the data-analytics platform (May 16th, 2018 — Roman (Innova)).
- Built over ~6 years, largely self-funded by the founder; drew strong Chinese investor interest including a term sheet after ~9 months of diligence (May 16th, 2018 — Roman (Innova)).
- A separate entity, "Inverom," buys the cars (one deal with an insurance company; also Nissan leases) and leases them to the service over a 60-month term (May 16th, 2018 — Roman (Innova)).
- Traction: ~40 cars deployed (targeting 150 by year-end), sub-$1M ARR (targeting ~$2M by Q1 '19), raising $5–7M at a ~$18M pre-money discussed late the prior year (May 16th, 2018 — Roman (Innova)).
- Introduced to Kyle via [David Fidalgo](/notes/david-fidalgo) of [Y-Mobility](/notes/y-mobility) (May 16th, 2018 — David Fidalgo (Y-Mobility)).
