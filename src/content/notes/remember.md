# Remember

In Kyle's topical study of Moroni's Exhortations ([Moroni 10](/notes/moroni-10)), "remember" is identified as the rhetorical hinge of [Moroni](/notes/moroni)'s eight closing invitations. The first exhortation is to **remember** how merciful the Lord has been and to **ponder** how good God has been ([Moroni 10](/notes/moroni-10):3–4), and many of the later charges — remember the source of every gift, "remember these things" ([Moroni 10](/notes/moroni-10):27–29) — likewise begin with the same word, "as if we already knew." Scripture, on this reading, exists precisely so we can see the history of God's goodness and remember it.

The note records [Boyd K. Packer](/notes/boyd-k-packer)'s claim that "remember" is the most important word in the English language. "Remember" sits alongside [Tender Mercies](/notes/tender-mercies), Testimony, Spiritual Gifts, and [Come Unto Christ](/notes/come-unto-christ) as one of the core themes Kyle pulls out of Moroni's final chapter — the move from recalling God's past mercy to coming unto Christ and being made perfect in Him.
