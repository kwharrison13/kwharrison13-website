# Ten Principles for How to Run a Company

**Author:** [Niko Canner](/notes/niko-canner)
**URL:** http://www.onhumanenterprise.com/ten-principles-run-company
**One-line:** *The one page you'd write to describe what it means for a company to be managed well — a standard the board can hold the company to.* #[Leadership](/notes/leadership) #Playbook: Leadership

## The framing

> You've invented something that will change the world. But you have weeks left to live. She asks you one thing: **would you write one page that describes what you believe it means for this company to be managed well, which I and the board can then use as a standard to which we hold the company and ensure is always maintained?**

## The ten principles

1. **Articulate a destination** that's deeply compelling and far beyond our current grasp.
2. **Define an agenda** of what the company will actively execute that stretches it beyond current capabilities, while ensuring must-dos are achieved and leaving enough slack to preserve time for reflection.
3. For each part of the agenda (except where there's an explicit choice to defer or pause), **ensure that there is a sponsor** who can formulate a brief — what outcomes, by when, subject to what constraints.
4. **Give owners room** to shape their path to deliver on the brief. #Accountability
5. Ensure sponsors and owners **regularly and actively review their degree of confidence** that the brief will be achieved (green, yellow, or red) and the reasoning behind their assessments. #KPI
   > **Kyle:** *"When performance is measured, performance improves. When performance is measured and reported, the rate of improvement accelerates." — [Thomas S. Monson](/notes/thomas-s-monson)*
6. Acknowledge explicitly **where there needs to be tension** between different objectives.
   > **Kyle:** *"The test of a first-rate intelligence is the ability to hold two opposed ideas in mind at the same time and still retain the ability to function." — [F. Scott Fitzgerald](/notes/f-scott-fitzgerald)*
7. **Articulate the patterns** of how to operate well that will most strengthen and preserve the company over the long term — principles and practices.
   > **Kyle:** *People create systems and systems create companies.* #[Systems Thinking](/notes/systems-thinking) #[Find the Quote](/notes/find-the-quote)
8. Conceptually **separate the management of people and their careers from the role of sponsor**, whether played by the same or different managers. #[mentorship](/notes/mentorship) #[My Mentors](/notes/my-mentors)
9. Maintain a strong discipline of **observing when outcomes diverge from expectations**.
10. Give every member the context, information, and voice to raise the issues and opportunities they see — a right to share perspectives and reasoning, but not to make the decisions that belong to sponsors and owners. #Default to transparency
