# The Fabric of Reality

**Why it's on the list:** *[David Deutsch](/notes/david-deutsch)'s case that the path to real understanding is the **unification** of knowledge, not endless specialization — the idea [Chris Anderson](/notes/chris-anderson) credits with convincing him to take over TED.*

**Where I saw it:** Cited at length in [TED Talks: The Official TED Guide to Public Speaking](/books/ted-talks-the-official-ted-guide-to-public-speaking). Anderson recounts reading it while deciding whether to buy the TED conference: Deutsch distinguishes *knowledge* (which inevitably specializes) from *understanding* (which requires moving in the opposite direction — pulling the camera back to see how strands of a "vast spiderweb of knowledge" connect). That spiderweb metaphor became part of Anderson's thesis for why live idea-sharing matters more, not less, in a machine age.
