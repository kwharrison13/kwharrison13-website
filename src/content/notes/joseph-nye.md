# Joseph Nye

The political scientist who coined "[Soft Power](/notes/soft-power)" — defined in The Storytelling of Investing - Research as "the ability to attract, and attraction often leads to acquiescence… in behavioral terms, soft power is attractive power." Kyle picks up Nye's vocabulary via [Mario Gabriele](/notes/mario-gabriele)'s "Whose Story Wins?", which carries the concept into the startup/investing frame: soft power "acts more gently, like a story… enforcing values and norms through narrative." Nye's line "Politics in an information age may ultimately be about whose story wins" supplies the title and the central frame for that piece.

In Kyle's usage, Nye anchors the external-facing half of the storytelling thesis — the attraction-rather-than-coercion mechanism that pairs with the internal writing cultures (the [Wordsmiths](/notes/wordsmiths)).

**Context:** Joseph Nye (1937–2025) was an American political scientist at Harvard who developed the concepts of "soft power" and "smart power" in international relations.
