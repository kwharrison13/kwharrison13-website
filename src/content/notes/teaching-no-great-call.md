# Teaching, No Great Call

In Kyle's Alma 17–22 — I Will Make an Instrument of Thee study, this tag is paired with [How To Begin Teaching](/notes/how-to-begin-teaching) and [Learning, No Greater Responsibility](/notes/learning-no-greater-responsibility) to organize a practical model of gospel teaching drawn from [Ammon](/notes/ammon)'s conversion of King [Lamoni](/notes/lamoni) in Alma 18. The phrase points to the title of the Church's teaching handbook ("Teaching, No Greater Call") and is used here as a heading for the teaching skills the chapter dramatizes: let silence work, let the Spirit guide your questions, be relatable and set expectations, keep answers clear and concise, ask for commitment, teach for understanding, teach from the scriptures, make it relevant to the learner, and end with prayer.

The grouping treats Ammon as a case study in good pedagogy — reading the room (Alma 18:12), perceiving the king's unspoken thoughts, and building from belief in "a Great Spirit" up to the full [Plan of Salvation](/notes/plan-of-salvation) — rather than as a one-off miracle story.

**Context:** *Teaching, No Greater Call* is a long-running resource guide published by The Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-day Saints for teachers in its lessons and auxiliaries; its companion phrase "no greater responsibility" runs through Church instruction on teaching and learning.

## Where this appears

- Alma 17–22 — I Will Make an Instrument of Thee — heading that frames Ammon's interaction with King Lamoni as a step-by-step model of how to begin teaching.
