# Joon Suh

**Joon Suh** leads finance / FP&A at [Ada Support](/notes/ada-support), which he joined ~2021. He stood up the company's first FP&A function and ran a multi-year tool evaluation — surveying [Planful](/notes/planful), [Pigment](/notes/pigment), [Mosaic.Tech](/notes/mosaic-tech), [Cube Software](/notes/cube-software), and [Adaptive Planning](/notes/adaptive-planning) before landing on [Vareto](/notes/vareto).

Kyle spoke with him on July 5th, 2023 as a [Vareto](/notes/vareto) customer reference. His read:

- **Vareto won on time-to-value and interface** — day-one mission-critical features, dashboards that cut hours of close/review work to ~30 minutes, and an "explore" drill-down (table → journal entries) so useful he "haven't logged into NetSuite once" since adopting it.
- **Pricing:** ~$20–25K all-in for Vareto vs. ~$100K for [Adaptive](/notes/adaptive-planning) with services, and [Cube](/notes/cube-software) at ~$30K but priced per-integration. The buy is always weighed against hiring another analyst.
- **Competitive candor:** Adaptive is proven-at-scale but outdated and heavy ("no one gets fired for using Adaptive"); Cube is a marginal step up from spreadsheets; [Pigment](/notes/pigment) is powerful but less streamlined.
- **Team praise:** rates [Kat Orekhova](/notes/kat-orekhova) highly; values Vareto's responsive support and direct PM access.
