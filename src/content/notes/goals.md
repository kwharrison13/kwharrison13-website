# Goals

A concept Kyle tags on material about aiming at the right outcome rather than a proxy for it. The anchor is a [James Clear](/notes/james-clear) quote saved in July 2nd, 2020: "The real goal is not to 'beat the market.' The goal is to build wealth. The real goal is not to read more books. The goal is to understand what you read. **Don't let a proxy become the target. Don't optimize for the wrong outcome.**" — the [Investing](/notes/investing) and [Reading](/notes/reading) examples make the point that an easily-measured metric quietly substitutes for the harder thing it was meant to track.

[The Pursuit of Meaning](/notes/the-pursuit-of-meaning) extends the theme from optimization to life-direction: it notes that Americans name happiness their #1 goal — fueling an $11B self-help industry, with 45% setting New Year's resolutions around being happier — yet argues [Happiness](/notes/happiness) is transitory while meaning is what we carry to the grave. The essay's reflective exercise (revisit which childhood projects, hobbies, and subjects satisfied you most) is a way of locating real goals, and it closes on the forward-looking fear/pain questions that point at what one is actually aiming for.
