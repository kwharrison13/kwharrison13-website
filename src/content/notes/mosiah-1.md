# Mosiah 1

A Book of Mormon chapter Kyle cites for King Benjamin's charge to his sons about the scriptures and the land-prosperity promise. The anchoring verse in his notes is Mosiah 1:7: *"I would that ye should remember to search them diligently, that ye may profit thereby; and I would that ye should keep the commandments of God, that ye may prosper in the land according to the promises which the Lord made unto our fathers."*

In both his Come Follow Me study of Alma 36–38 and his The Process of the Book of Mormon study, Mosiah 1:7 appears in a recurring chain of cross-references ([2 Nephi 1](/notes/2-nephi-1):20, [Jarom 1](/notes/jarom-1):9, [Omni 1](/notes/omni-1):6, [Alma 9](/notes/alma-9):13–14, [Alma 36](/notes/alma-36):30) that restate the Book of Mormon's central conditional covenant: keep the commandments and prosper in the land; fail and be cut off. It functions in Kyle's notes as one node in that "prosper in the land" promise thread.

## Where this appears

- Come Follow Me — Alma 36–38 — Mosiah 1:7 ("search them diligently... prosper in the land") as part of the prosperity-covenant cross-reference chain.
- The Process of the Book of Mormon — Mosiah 1:7 grouped with the other "prosper in the land" promise verses.
