# Lock You Heart

A missionary address by [Spencer W. Kimball](/notes/spencer-w-kimball), cited in Kyle's Alma 17–22 — I Will Make an Instrument of Thee study as a source on what it means to "show forth good examples." Kyle pulls three takeaways from it: that a missionary's example matters because poor conduct "can do irreparable damage to the reputation of the Church and your Mission"; that "your whole thought should be missionary work"; and that the two mission years are a period of giving oneself "to the Lord, totally, to teach the Gospel to the world." The talk is grouped under Alma 17:11's promise that those who are patient in long-suffering and show forth good examples will be made "an instrument of thee in my hands unto the salvation of many souls."

**Context:** "Lock Your Heart" is a widely circulated missionary teaching attributed to Spencer W. Kimball (12th President of the Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-day Saints), urging full-time missionaries to set aside romantic distraction and devote their entire focus to missionary work.

## Where this appears
