# Acts 10

A New Testament chapter cited in Kyle's Testimony — Topical Study under "Testimony of Faith vs. Desire of Hope." Kyle reads **Acts 10:34–44** as Peter declaring that he and his brethren "are witnesses of Christ and his resurrection." The takeaway Kyle drew: "Our ultimate responsibility to the world is to bear testimony to the world of what we know to be true. As Peter did, we must never cease to invite people to repent and come unto Christ because we love them." It sits alongside Acts 2 in the study as an example of an Apostle reciting the words of the prophets so that the [Holy Ghost](/notes/holy-ghost) can prick the hearts of hearers.

**Context:** Acts 10 records the conversion of Cornelius, a Roman centurion — the moment the early Christian gospel opens to the Gentiles. Peter's vision of the sheet of unclean animals ("what God hath cleansed, that call not thou common") and his sermon at Cornelius's house are the chapter's pivotal events.
