# American Decline

"American decline" is the recurring question — is the United States hollowing out? — that runs through Kyle's The Hardening Of The Great Softening - Research thread. The grounded evidence sits in the [Jamie Raskin](/notes/jamie-raskin) page, which records the surrounding decline-weighted material: demographic analysis of the [January 6th](/notes/january-6th) marchers, data on uneducated whites as the one postwar group that hasn't advanced, and worry over how many Americans believe the 2020 election was stolen. Raskin appears in that thread as a counterweight — "the way he speaks about America makes me hopeful" — a note of hope inside otherwise decline-leaning notes. The theme pairs with [American Exceptionalism](/notes/american-exceptionalism) and [One Billion Americans](/notes/one-billion-americans) as the two poles Kyle weighs: whether America is exceptional and still ascendant, or in retreat.

**Context:** "American decline" is a long-running debate in political and economic commentary over whether the United States is losing its relative global, economic, demographic, or institutional standing — a framing invoked across the political spectrum and frequently set against arguments for continued American exceptionalism or renewal.
