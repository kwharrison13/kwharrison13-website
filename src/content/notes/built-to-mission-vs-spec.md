# Built to Mission vs Spec

"Built to mission vs. built to spec" is one of the recurring threads Kyle pulls out of [Skunk Works](/books/skunk-works) (Ben R. Rich), and the book's single most-repeated principle: **"Trust the customer to know the problem but not the solution"** — i.e., build to the *mission*, not to the *spec*. Building to spec rewards adherence to bureaucracy and produces "exquisite platforms": impressive, expensive single-points-of-failure that get invalidated once warfare evolves around them. Kyle's inline notes restate this twice — "Build to the mission" against the automated stealth-fighter program, and "Built to spec vs built to missions — again, focused on exquisite platforms" against the B-2 contest.

The thread connects directly to the book's other arcs: the [Founders Mentality](/notes/founders-mentality) / missionary-vs-mercenary drift (Kelly Johnson's founder-led innovation vs. "build for the buck rather than the mission"), [Managed Monopoly](/notes/managed-monopoly) (anti-Darwinian primes rewarded for adherence rather than results), and the modern thesis that [Anduril](/notes/anduril) is the skunk-works answer — buying off-the-shelf where possible and building custom only where the mission requires it, rather than gold-plating to a spec.
