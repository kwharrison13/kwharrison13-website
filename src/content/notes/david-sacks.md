# David Sacks

PayPal's product chief and later a venture investor, surfacing in Kyle's corpus across two distinct eras. In [The Founders](/books/the-founders) (Jimmy Soni's PayPal history), Sacks is the "Dr. No" product lead — [Peter Thiel](/notes/peter-thiel) pulled rank to hire him over the team's objections precisely because Sacks came into his interview "guns blazing against their prospective employer's flagship product" ("Peter said, 'I need people here I can scream at,'" Sacks remembered). As product manager he "became, like, 'Dr. No,'" saying no to "everyone's stupid ideas" to keep precious engineering bandwidth aligned with long-term strategy, and pushed revenue-and-incentive priorities against Bill Harris's process-and-cost-cutting instincts.

Sacks is the wiki's canonical example of the [Pre-Mortem](/notes/pre-mortem): at PayPal he ran the mirror-image thought experiment "If I was running eBay Payments, what would I do to kill PayPal?" — a move Kyle tags directly as running the rival's playbook against yourself to surface [Platform Risk](/notes/platform-risk) before it materializes (the [Post-Mortem](/notes/post-mortems) and Platform Risk pages both cite it).

In his second act, Sacks co-founded [Craft Ventures](/notes/craft-ventures) and authored [The Burn Multiple](/notes/the-burn-multiple) (Craft Ventures, April 2020) — the framework defining Net Burn / Net New ARR as the single most revealing metric of a startup's capital efficiency, which Kyle treats as the original definition of the [Burn Multiple](/notes/burn-multiple) concept.

**Context:** David Sacks is a South-African-born American entrepreneur and investor — early PayPal COO, founder of Yammer (acquired by Microsoft), and co-founder of Craft Ventures with Bill Lee.
