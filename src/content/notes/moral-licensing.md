# Moral Licensing

The psychological tendency to use a sense of one's own goodness or prior virtue as permission to act badly. Kyle tags it in [Charlie Munger — The Psychology of Human Misjudgment](/notes/charlie-munger-the-psychology-of-human-misjudgment), connecting it to Raskolnikov's belief in his "moral right to kill" in [Crime & Punishment](/books/crime-and-punishment).

**Context:** Moral licensing is a documented effect in social psychology in which past good behavior (or even the feeling of being a moral person) loosens self-restraint and makes subsequent ethically questionable behavior feel justified.
