# Africa

Africa shows up across Kyle's notes along two threads: as a frontier for grassroots [Entrepreneurship](/notes/entrepreneurship) and community-driven finance, and as a place of perspective-shifting travel. On the finance side, the Shara pre-brief from [Julia Andre](/notes/julia-andre) and [Martin Mignot](/notes/martin-mignot) frames the continent's structural credit gap: roughly 95% of commerce runs through SMEs, who are themselves the largest source of consumer credit because there are no credit cards or credit bureaus — leaving a financing gap estimated north of $1tn. Shara's thesis is to productize the trust embedded in existing community bonds to underwrite otherwise-unserved populations (Kyle compares it to [Grameen Bank](/notes/grameen) and his own Go Solar experience of needing "infrastructure imbued with cultural trust"). On the entrepreneurship side, Kyle's June 2026 reading capture saved an Irish Times piece on entrepreneurs in Uganda "taking control of their own destiny," extending the wiki's [Entrepreneurship](/notes/entrepreneurship) theme geographically, alongside a MarketWatch Africa markets piece queued to read.

Africa also appears in a personal register: in Kyle's note on [Meaningful Travel](/notes/meaningful-travel), he cites [Tim Fewkes](/notes/tim-fewkes) and [Karen Fewkes](/notes/karen-fewkes) taking their kids to Africa each summer to pull them out of the reality they're accustomed to — a lived example of travel-as-transformation.
