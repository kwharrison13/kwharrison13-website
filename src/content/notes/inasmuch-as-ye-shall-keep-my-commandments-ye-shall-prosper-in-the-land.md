# Inasmuch as ye shall keep my commandments ye shall prosper in the land

This is the first-person ("my commandments") wording of the Book of Mormon's central covenant promise, spoken by the Lord. Kyle catalogs it in his Alma 36–38 study as one of several near-identical phrasings of the same promise — what he treats as the [Central Theme of the Book of Mormon](/notes/central-theme-of-the-book-of-mormon), "one of perseverant obedience." This exact form appears at 2 Nephi 4:4 ("Inasmuch as ye shall keep my commandments ye shall prosper in the land; and inasmuch as ye will not keep my commandments ye shall be cut off from my presence") and is echoed when the promise is recalled back to Lehi. It sits alongside the ["commandments of God"](/notes/inasmuch-as-ye-shall-keep-the-commandments-of-god-ye-shall-prosper-in-the-land) variant Alma uses in Alma 36/38 and the "ye will keep" form found in Jarom 1.

Kyle's note deliberately tracks these wording variants as separate entries to follow how the same covenant is restated across different authors and generations of the record.
