# EHR

In Kyle's notes the EHR (electronic health record) recurs as the practice-management backbone inside [Behavioral Health](/notes/behavioral-health) provider-enablement bundles. On the [Grow Therapy](/notes/grow-therapy) page, EHR sits alongside marketing, insurance credentialing, billing, consent, and scheduling as part of the "one-stop-shop" Grow runs for mental-health therapists in exchange for a per-session cut. [Spring Health](/notes/spring-health) uses [Kareo](/notes/kareo) as its EHR and is "not planning to build its own EHR soon" — and [Kareo](/notes/kareo) itself is described as a practice-management / EHR vendor for behavioral-health providers, with Spring as a large customer. The pattern across these pages: EHR is a buy-not-build infrastructure layer, and owning (or wrapping) it is part of how provider-enablement platforms create stickiness with therapists and clinics.

**Context:** An EHR (electronic health record) is the digital system of record for patient charts, clinical notes, scheduling, and billing in a medical practice. In behavioral health, practice-management/EHR vendors (Kareo/Tebra, SimplePractice, etc.) supply the back-office software that smaller providers can't build themselves.

## Where this appears

- [Grow Therapy](/notes/grow-therapy) — EHR is one piece of its provider-enablement bundle (marketing + credentialing + billing + EHR + scheduling) for therapists.
- [Spring Health](/notes/spring-health) — uses [Kareo](/notes/kareo) as its EHR; not planning to build its own soon.
- [Kareo](/notes/kareo) — practice-management / EHR vendor for behavioral-health providers, with Spring Health a large customer.
