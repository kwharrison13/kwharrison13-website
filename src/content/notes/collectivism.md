# Collectivism

In Kyle's wiki, collectivism is the explicit antagonist of [Ayn Rand](/notes/ayn-rand)'s [Anthem](/books/anthem) — the dystopia where the individual will has been abolished in favor of "the will of all men together." The novella renders it as a society whose creed is "WE ARE ONE IN ALL AND ALL IN ONE. THERE ARE NO MEN BUT ONLY THE GREAT WE, ONE, INDIVISIBLE AND FOREVER," where the word "alone" is "the great transgression and the root of all evil" and "there is no transgression blacker than to do or think alone." (source: [Anthem](/books/anthem))

Anthem dramatizes the practical cost of collectivism through suppressed [Innovation](/notes/innovation): Equality 7-2521's rediscovered electric light is condemned by the Council because "what is not thought by all men cannot be true" and because the device "would bring ruin to the Department of Candles" — invention blocked to preserve collective consensus and existing plans. Rand's reply is the individualist ethic, which Anthem closes on with the single sacred word "EGO." Anthem sits alongside the wiki's broader dystopia cluster ([Brave New World](/books/brave-new-world), [Animal Farm](/books/animal-farm), [1984](/books/1984)) of engineered or coerced collectivism. (source: [Anthem](/books/anthem))

**Context:** Collectivism is the political and ethical doctrine that the group — class, nation, society — has moral priority over the individual; in liberal and Objectivist thought it is the named opposite of individualism, and a recurring frame in the 20th-century dystopia tradition Rand wrote alongside.

## Where this appears

- [Anthem](/books/anthem) — the novella's central evil: the abolished "I," the worship of the "Great We," and the Council's suppression of the rediscovered electric light to protect collective consensus.
