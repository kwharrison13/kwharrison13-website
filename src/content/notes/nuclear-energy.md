# Nuclear Energy

The recurring carbon-free baseload thesis across Kyle's climate and progress reading. In [How To Avoid a Climate Disaster](/books/how-to-avoid-a-climate-disaster) it is the most-tagged concept (7 of 19 notes) — [Bill Gates](/notes/bill-gates)'s central bet, instantiated by [TerraPower](/notes/terrapower): "the only carbon-free energy source that can reliably deliver power day and night, through every season, almost anywhere on earth, that has been proven to work on a large scale," with far fewer deaths than coal pollution. Kyle reads the book's implicit conclusion as that political opposition to nuclear is "mostly emotional rather than empirical" (only two US plants broken ground since Three Mile Island and Chernobyl, "even though more people die from coal pollution in a single year than have died in all nuclear accidents combined"). [Marc Andreessen](/notes/marc-andreessen)'s [It's Time To Build](/notes/it-s-time-to-build) makes nuclear the literal answer to climate — "build a few thousand zero-emission nuclear reactors, start with 10, then 100, then the rest."

In [Boom: Bubbles & The End of Stagnation](/books/boom-bubbles-and-the-end-of-stagnation) by [Byrne Hobart](/notes/byrne-hobart) and [Tobias Huber](/notes/tobias-huber), nuclear is the "purest 'apocalyptic technology' case study" — the Manhattan Project and nuclear power as an innovation-accelerating bubble — and the book diagnoses "pseudo-environmental policy reactions against nuclear energy" as an instance of safetyism inhibiting progress, with **nuclear fusion** repeatedly invoked as the kind of radically novel energy source decarbonization actually needs. That fusion thread runs into Kyle's deal work: the Helion sourcing note covers the Everett-based fusion startup's $500M Series E led by [Sam Altman](/notes/sam-altman), and the Watershed Climate Diligence — 2021 page uses "expected shifts in the economy around us (e.g. nuclear energy)" as an example forecasting variable.

**Context:** Nuclear energy comprises fission (today's commercial reactors) and fusion (the still-experimental approach pursued by firms like Helion and Commonwealth Fusion). It is the densest scalable low-carbon power source, and its role in decarbonization is a defining debate in climate and "abundance"/progress-studies circles.
