# Troy Jollimore

Poet, philosopher, and Cal State Chico ethics professor who is a recurring voice in Kyle's [We Need a Renaissance of Rhetoric](/essays/we-need-a-renaissance-of-rhetoric) research and his [AI Cheating in Education](/notes/ai-cheating-in-education) notes. In the New York Magazine "Everyone Is Cheating Their Way Through College" reporting, Jollimore — after two years grading AI-generated papers and more than two decades teaching writing — warns that "massive numbers of students are going to emerge from university with degrees, and into the workforce, who are essentially illiterate," both literally and in the sense of knowing nothing of their own culture. He frames writing as becoming "an anachronistic art elective like basket-weaving," and describes the mood among faculty as a full-blown existential crisis where colleagues mostly talk about retirement.

Within Kyle's argument, Jollimore anchors the **humanist defense** of writing (alongside Orin Starn and [W.H. Auden](/notes/w-h-auden)): that writing is how thinking actually happens — "language is the mother, not the handmaiden, of thought" — so off-loading it to LLMs means losing the underlying worldview, not just a skill. This sits in tension with the [Tyler Cowen](/notes/tyler-cowen) "AI literacy is the new skill" rebuttal that Kyle treats as valid-but-incomplete.

**Context:** Troy Jollimore is a Canadian-American poet and philosopher, professor of philosophy at California State University, Chico; his poetry collection *Tom Thomson in Purgatory* won the National Book Critics Circle Award.
