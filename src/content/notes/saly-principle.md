# SALY Principle

SALY — "Same As Last Year" — is a recurring tag in Kyle's notes for the default assumption that "how the world is, is how it always was," and the discipline of refusing to let that default go unexamined. It carries two complementary readings across the corpus. In [The Great Mental Models Volume 1 — General Thinking Concepts](/books/the-great-mental-models-volume-1-general-thinking-concepts) it appears as a caution that *trend is not destiny* — the world is not fixed just because it has been a certain way. In [Discipline Is Destiny](/books/discipline-is-destiny) Kyle reads it more constructively, alongside the Stoic insistence on the daily over the dramatic: "everything you do is cumulative," a [Continuous Improvement](/notes/kaizen) frame of deciding today and doing it tomorrow and the day after.

The same tag marks a leadership move in two very different settings. In the June 22nd, 2021 — Conor White-Sullivan (Roam Research) meeting, Conor's hiring philosophy — wanting people who ask whether the team is "asking the right questions" and has "a system that is learning" — is flagged SALY precisely because it means *not* doing things the way they've always been done. And in [Gordon B. Hinckley — Go Forward With Faith](/books/gordon-b-hinckley-go-forward-with-faith), Hinckley is the embodiment of the principle: "not necessarily bound by convention," refusing to treat "always done a certain way" as an argument for continuance, while still knowing the "built-in liberties" and "built-in limits" of his office.

- [The Great Mental Models Volume 1 — General Thinking Concepts](/books/the-great-mental-models-volume-1-general-thinking-concepts) — "how the world is, is how it always was"; trend is not destiny.
