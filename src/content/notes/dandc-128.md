# D&C 128

A section of the Doctrine and Covenants that surfaces in Kyle's study mainly around temple work, family history, and the binding/recording power of the priesthood — plus one recurring rallying verse. The most-quoted line is **D&C 128:22** — "Brethren, shall we not go on in so great a cause? Go forward and not backward. Courage, brethren; and on, on to the victory!" — which General Conference — October 2021 flags as "the recurring rallying scripture," tied to redeeming the dead "out of their prison." Kyle's Abrahamic Covenant notes pull the section's central genealogical doctrine: "we without them cannot be made perfect" — the obligation to work on one's own lineage, with one's inheritance increasing as you trace and redeem your literal family back as far as you can.

The section's doctrine of binding and recording recurs too. October 19–25, 2020. 3 Nephi 27–4 Nephi: "There Could Not Be a Happier People" cites **128:8** — the ordinance power whereby "whatsoever you record on earth shall be recorded in heaven," and "out of the books shall your dead be judged." Virtue quotes **128:19**'s "voice of gladness… glad tidings for the dead" and the promise that "the knowledge of God [shall] descend upon them." The Lehonti Principle uses **128:1** for a quieter point about revelation — the Spirit speaks to what we are already pondering.

**Context:** Doctrine and Covenants Section 128 is an 1842 epistle from Joseph Smith on baptism for the dead, instructing the early Church on record-keeping, the sealing/binding power, and the work of redeeming ancestors — the doctrinal basis for Latter-day Saint temple and family-history work.
