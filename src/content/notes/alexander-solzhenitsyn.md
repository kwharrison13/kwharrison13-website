# Alexander Solzhenitsyn

**One-line:** *Soviet dissident writer whose "Live Not by Lies" serves as a recurring moral touchstone in [The Invention of Russia](/books/the-invention-of-russia).*

## What we know

- "Knew there was only one way to defeat" the Soviet system: "'Live not by lies,' he wrote on the day of his arrest" (source: [The Invention of Russia](/books/the-invention-of-russia)).
- After *Gulag Archipelago* was published in France in 1974, he was stripped of Soviet citizenship and expelled. As a parting shot he wrote "Live Not by Lies" to the Soviet intelligentsia, ending with the commandment "DON'T LIE! DON'T PARTICIPATE IN A LIE! DON'T SUPPORT A LIE!" (source: [The Invention of Russia](/books/the-invention-of-russia)).
- His framing of the daily lie as "incorporated into the state system as the vital link holding everything together, with billions of tiny fasteners" recurs as the book's diagnosis of Soviet and post-Soviet media (source: [The Invention of Russia](/books/the-invention-of-russia)).

## Mentioned in

- [The Invention of Russia](/books/the-invention-of-russia) — "Live Not by Lies" as the book's moral spine; later editorials echo his appeal to journalists.
