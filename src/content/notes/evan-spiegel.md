# Evan Spiegel

Snapchat co-founder and CEO, quoted in [How To Be a Capitalist Without Any Capital](/books/how-to-be-a-capitalist-without-any-capital) on the inevitability of imitation: "People are going to copy your product if you build great stuff. Just because Yahoo has a search box doesn't make it Google." The book deploys the line to argue that defensibility comes from execution quality rather than from any single feature. In Kyle's reading the principle is sound but the speaker is "the wrong avatar for it" — he notes the irony that Facebook's copying of Snapchat's Stories sent Snap "into a bit of a death spiral," making Spiegel a cautionary example rather than a vindication of his own maxim.

**Context:** Evan Spiegel (b. 1990) co-founded Snap Inc. (Snapchat) in 2011 and took the company public in 2017. He is among the youngest self-made billionaires associated with the social-media era.
