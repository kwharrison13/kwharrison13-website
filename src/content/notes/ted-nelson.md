# Ted Nelson

Ted Nelson appears in Kyle's notes as a foundational figure in the prehistory of the hyperlinked web — directly relevant to the wiki Kyle is building. In [Hyper Publishing: The Wiki Strategy](/notes/hyper-publishing-the-wiki-strategy) ([David Perell](/notes/david-perell)'s essay), Nelson is credited with coining the term "hyperlink" in 1965 as part of Project Xanadu, inspired by Vannevar Bush's 1945 essay *As We May Think* — establishing that the hyperlink predates the internet itself. He surfaces again in the August 18th, 2021 — Valued Empyrean (Conor White-Sullivan & Darwin Harrison) conversation, where [Conor White-Sullivan](/notes/conor-white-sullivan) shared Nelson's eulogy for [Douglas Engelbart](/notes/douglas-engelbart) as a [Tools For Thought](/notes/tools-for-thought) resource, placing Nelson in the lineage of computing pioneers (Engelbart's mouse and hypertext, the "Mother of All Demos," [Xerox PARC](/notes/xerox-parc)) that Conor draws on.

**Context:** Ted Nelson (b. 1937) is an American information-technology pioneer who coined the terms "hypertext" and "hyperlink" and founded Project Xanadu in 1960, an envisioned hypertext system that long predated and influenced the World Wide Web.
