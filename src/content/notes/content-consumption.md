# Content Consumption

A recurring theme across David Perell's notes that passive consumption is a trap: it "feels so good — too good," giving a thin, two-dimensional view, while only writing about an idea yields a three-dimensional perspective ([Riding the Writing Wave](/notes/riding-the-writing-wave)). The same discipline-over-access thesis carries a warning against obsessive news consumption ([News in the Age of Abundance](/notes/news-in-the-age-of-abundance)).

## Reflection — July 14th, 2020

From the July 14th, 2020 daily log, a question Kyle posed tying consumption to creation — the link between [Content Consumption](/notes/content-consumption) and [Writing](/notes/writing):

> Can't remember where I heard the quote but it was something like "people who spend their time on Facebook will never build something like Facebook"
>
> *mutes political statements about Facebook being evil*
>
> Is it true of people who try and read too much stuff? They'll never write the great stuff? Or do you just need to be really good at reading effectively?
