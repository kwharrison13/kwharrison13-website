> Address by [Jeffrey R. Holland](/notes/jeffrey-r-holland) to departing missionaries at the Provo Missionary Training Center, January 2011. Sources: [YouTube](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Kq5gvc7p0RM), [Google Drive recording](https://drive.google.com/file/d/1NL7pI-Yqxn7FG689VRnkTL1PN9qy_c54/view?usp=sharing), and a [transcript](https://laytreasuresinheaven.com/feed-my-sheep-elder-holland/). Full text preserved verbatim below; Kyle's bolded/underscored emphasis is kept as-is.

## Key Takeaways

- **Convert the missionary first.** The fundamental shift [Preach My Gospel](/notes/preach-my-gospel) made — born when [Gordon B. Hinckley](/notes/gordon-b-hinckley) told prospective mission presidents "something's wrong with our missionary program" after seeing missionaries return and fall away — was to stop leaping straight to the investigator. "We need to convert *you*, and then *you* figure out how to convert investigators." The gospel has to get "down here in the heart of the missionary" before there's any hope an investigator can receive it.
- **Meet the investigator where they are.** For a century the Church taught memorized, uniform discussions ("Sit down! I've got a message"). Hired actors posing as investigators reported they'd have thrown the missionaries out if not paid — because the missionaries didn't *hear* the dying father or the expelled son. "You have to go where the investigator is, before you can get the investigator to come where you are."
- **The Wallace Toronto / Moroni 8 story.** A grieving Czech mother, told by her minister that her dead baptized-less child was in hell, vowed never to speak to a minister again — until Wallace Toronto, in halting Czech in 1928, asked "Would you like us to tell you where your daughter is?" and read her [Moroni 8](/notes/moroni-8) on infant baptism and the grace of Christ. The lesson the planned discussions could never have scripted: "that's why it's got to be in the hearts of the missionaries."
- **Three things missionaries do poorly — study, prepare, teach for commitment.** Study now means deep preparation, not just reading pages ("you want Elder Nelson because he's practiced"). Preparing means knowing what *each* family needs, not just which street to tract. And teaching for commitment is the hardest and most serious — delivering the goods "knee-to-knee, face-to-face" so people make and keep covenants.
- **Obedience predicts everything.** "Tell me how your obedience is, and I'll tell you right now how your mission is going to be." The early-rising and discipline exist to make study and companionship study possible — companionship study is the first thing lost to a wasted morning.
- **The Spirit, not a credential, confers authority.** Unlike the surgeon analogy, a missionary can be a "first-grade surgeon the first day" — the Spirit of the Lord itself "will pronounce that gift upon your head and endow that mantel of authority on your shoulders from the first day," if the missionary is honest in study, planning, and teaching.
- **"Feed my sheep" — forever.** Holland closes on the risen Christ's threefold question to Peter on the Galilee shore. The point of the three "Do you love me?"s is total: leaving the nets "was forever." A mission marks an hour you can't come back from — "you've left your nets, and you're going to feed sheep" — and the first and great commandment, "Do you love Him?", is the only foundation that makes any of it possible.
