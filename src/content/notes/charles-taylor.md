# Charles Taylor

Charles Taylor is the philosopher Kyle's notes track as the author of [A Secular Age](/notes/a-secular-age), the book [Zach Edwards](/notes/zach-edwards) recommended during the September 17th, 2021 call (September 17th, 2021 — Zach Edwards (Clearstone Associates)) in a thread on secularism. Kyle filed the recommendation into his anti-library, framing the book as Taylor's "landmark account of how the West shifted from a world in which belief in God was axiomatic to one in which it is one option among many." In the same conversation it surfaced alongside [Truth and Tolerance](/notes/truth-and-tolerance) as part of a discussion about secularism and free speech.

**Context:** Charles Taylor (b. 1931) is a Canadian philosopher known for work on the self, modernity, and secularization. His 2007 book *A Secular Age* is a sweeping account of how Western societies moved from near-universal religious belief to a condition in which faith is one contested option among many.
