# Plan of Redemption

A Book of Mormon doctrinal phrase Kyle tracks through his [Come, Follow Me](/notes/come-follow-me) study in [Alma](/notes/alma). In Alma 13–16 it is the doctrine that makes [Resurrection](/notes/resurrection) possible: "if it had not been for the plan of redemption, which was laid from the foundation of the world, there could have been no resurrection of the dead" (Alma 12:25), and had Adam and Eve partaken of the tree of life before "a preparatory state," the plan "would have been frustrated."

In Alma 17–22 Kyle raises the explicit question — **what is the difference between the [Plan of Salvation](/notes/plan-of-salvation) and the plan of redemption?** — drawing on the sons of [Mosiah](/notes/mosiah) undertaking missionary work so others "might know of the plan of redemption" (Alma 17:16) and Aaron expounding "the plan of redemption, which was prepared from the foundation of the world" alongside "the coming of Christ" (Alma 18:39). The plan is grouped there with [Atonement](/notes/atonement) and missionary work as a core theme.

**Context:** In Latter-day Saint scripture, "plan of redemption" is one of several terms (with "plan of salvation," "plan of happiness," "plan of mercy") for God's plan, centered on the Atonement of Jesus Christ, by which fallen mankind can be redeemed from death and sin.
