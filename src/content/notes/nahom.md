# Nahom

Nahom is a place-name from [1 Nephi 16](/notes/1-nephi-16):34 — "Ishmael died, and was buried in the place which was called Nahom" — encountered during Lehi's family's journey through the wilderness toward the promised land. In Kyle's study of 1 Nephi 16–22 — I Will Prepare the Way before You, Nahom appears in the "Nephi's Journey" section as one of the named waypoints (alongside the valley of Lemuel and Shazer) on the southward route. Kyle pairs the verse with an image and a [Book of Mormon Central](https://bookofmormoncentral.org/blog/archaeological-evidence-for-7-locations-on-lehi-s-journey-to-the-promised-land) source on archaeological evidence for locations on Lehi's journey — flagging Nahom as a point of real-world geographic corroboration for the narrative.

**Context:** Nahom is widely cited in Latter-day Saint apologetics because the tribal name "NHM" appears on altars excavated in Yemen dating to roughly Lehi's era, in a location consistent with the Book of Mormon's southward-then-eastward travel description — one of the more-discussed external evidences for the text.
