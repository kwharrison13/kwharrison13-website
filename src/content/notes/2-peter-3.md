# 2 Peter 3

A chapter of the New Testament cited in Kyle's Obedience — Topical Study. There, 2 Peter 3:10–11, 14 grounds the definition of obedience as "the utilization of one's agency to follow God, and keep his commandments." The verse — "the day of the Lord will come as a thief in the night… what manner of persons ought ye to be in all holy conversation and godliness… be diligent that ye may be found of him in peace, without spot, and blameless" — frames obedience as the diligent, godly readiness that prepares one to meet the Lord.

**Context:** 2 Peter 3 is the final chapter of the second epistle attributed to the apostle Peter, addressing scoffers who doubt Christ's return and exhorting believers to holy living in anticipation of the day of the Lord and a new heaven and earth.
