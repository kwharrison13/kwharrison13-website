# Blackstone

Alternative-asset manager (>$800B AUM) that Kyle uses as the central analogy for the future of venture capital. The firm's founding, near-death (the Edgcomb loss), and culture are narrated in [Stephen A. Schwarzman](/notes/stephen-a-schwarzman)'s memoir [What It Takes](/books/what-it-takes), from which Kyle draws the operating model: Blackstone is "basically a holding company for financial asset classes" — private equity, real estate, hedge funds, credit — that figured out the ~80% of any business that is shared infrastructure (network, resources, brand) and then **went to find the person with the magic 20% to scale a new asset class to ultimate efficiency**. Schwarzman's "Why not?" — the open-ended refusal to define the firm too narrowly — anchors Kyle's [Bifurcation in Investing](/notes/bifurcation-in-investing) concept and recurs across his venture-structure essays.

That model is the seed of Kyle's [The Blackstone of Innovation](/essays/the-blackstone-of-innovation) essay (subtitle: *Venture Capital Is The Name, AUM Collection Is The Game*), which he raised as a concern in his September 13th, 2022 — Harry Stebbings Podcast appearance: the danger of large venture firms becoming "the Blackstone of Innovation" — an AUM-aggregation / fees-as-the-product machine rather than a returns engine. The "Agglomerator playbook" recurs in The Puritans of Venture Capital - Research and The Productization of Venture Capital - Research. On the people side, Ramzi Ramsey joined Blackstone Growth in a senior leadership role in 2021 (June 22nd, 2021 — Ramzi Ramsey Joins Blackstone Growth). [What It Takes](/books/what-it-takes) also surfaces Blackstone's [Culture](/notes/culture) — meritocracy, open Monday meetings, "Don't. Lose. Money.," and intellectual integrity as its backbone.

**Context:** Blackstone Inc. is a New York-based global alternative-asset manager founded in 1985 by Stephen A. Schwarzman and Peter Peterson. It is one of the world's largest investment firms by assets under management, spanning private equity, real estate, credit, and hedge-fund solutions.
