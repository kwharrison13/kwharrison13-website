# Mark 9

Mark 9 is the New Testament chapter Kyle's [gospel-study](/notes/gospel-study) notes return to for two distinct teachings of Christ on [Faith](/notes/faith) and self-mastery. The most cited verses are the father of the afflicted child crying "Lord, I believe; help thou mine unbelief" (Mark 9:24) and Jesus' answer "all things are possible to him that believeth" (Mark 9:23) — quoted in Faith — Topical Study as the model for the "little things" that show God we believe, and again in Christ Is Risen; Faith in Him Will Move Mountains — Russell M. Nelson and Temporal Wealth and the Kingdom of God — Brigham Young.

The chapter also supplies two other strands. In Prayer — Topical Study, Mark 9:29–30 grounds the counsel to join prayer with fasting ("sometimes use fasting AND prayer"). In That All Men May Come Unto Him, a study on self-mastery and discipleship, Mark 9:43 ("if thy foot offend thee, cut it off") frames discipleship as sacrificing the good for the better and the better for the best.

**Context:** Mark 9 is the ninth chapter of the Gospel of Mark in the New Testament. It contains the Transfiguration, Jesus healing a boy with an unclean spirit after the disciples could not (including the "help thou mine unbelief" exchange), the teaching on prayer and fasting, and warnings about offense and discipleship.
