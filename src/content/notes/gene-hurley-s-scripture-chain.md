# Gene Hurley's Scripture Chain

A scripture-study method that recurs as a tag throughout Kyle's 2020 Come Follow Me notes. In practice it marks a passage where Kyle builds a **chain of cross-referenced scriptures around a single theme** — gathering verses from across the standard works that illuminate one idea, then layering personal reflection on top. In August 24–30, 2020. Helaman 7–12: "Remember the Lord" the tag heads chains on themes like "God will give you strength" (linking Helaman 7:22–23, Philippians 4:13, and Mosiah 7:29) and "Knowledge and [Accountability](/notes/accountability)" (chaining Helaman 7:24 to 3 Nephi 28:34–35 and Isaiah 24:5).

The same tag organizes thematic chains in August 31–September 6, 2020. Helaman 13–16: "Glad Tidings of Great Joy" (e.g. on broken covenants and accountability) and October 26–November 1, 2020. Mormon 1–6: "I Would That I Could Persuade All … to Repent" (on the pride cycle and repentance), and it reappears in Teachings of Presidents of the Church: Joseph Smith. It functions less as a doctrine than as a named technique in Kyle's study practice — likely learned from a person named Gene Hurley — for moving from a single verse to a web of mutually reinforcing scripture.

## Where this appears

- August 24–30, 2020. Helaman 7–12: "Remember the Lord" — tags chains on God's strength and on knowledge/accountability
- August 31–September 6, 2020. Helaman 13–16: "Glad Tidings of Great Joy" — tags chains on broken covenants and accountability
- October 26–November 1, 2020. Mormon 1–6: "I Would That I Could Persuade All … to Repent" — tags chains around the pride cycle and repentance
- Teachings of Presidents of the Church: Joseph Smith — tags a chain on unrighteous acts and the sanctification of the Church
