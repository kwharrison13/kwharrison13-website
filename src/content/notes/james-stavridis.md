# James Stavridis

Admiral James Stavridis is the co-author, with [Elliot Ackerman](/notes/elliot-ackerman), of [2034 — A Novel of the Next World War](/books/2034-a-novel-of-the-next-world-war) and its direct sequel [2054](/books/2054). In Kyle's notes on 2034, the retired admiral's operational experience is what grounds the novel's Pacific naval-war and cyber-conflict scenarios — the credibility of the war-game-as-fiction comes from Stavridis having actually run such commands. The book sits in Kyle's defense-scenario cluster alongside [The Kill Chain](/books/the-kill-chain) and [Nuclear War — a Scenario](/books/nuclear-war-a-scenario) as fiction-as-strategic-foresight.

2054 carries the same authorial pairing forward, projecting the geopolitical and technological trajectory further into the future (touching [Quantum Computing](/notes/quantum-computing) and the Kurzweil-style singularity).

**Context:** James Stavridis is a retired four-star U.S. Navy admiral who served as Supreme Allied Commander Europe (NATO) and dean of the Fletcher School at Tufts; since retiring he has become a prolific author and commentator on national security and naval strategy.
