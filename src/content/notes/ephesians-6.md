# Ephesians 6

Ephesians 6 is the source text for Kyle's The Whole Armor of God topical study (Topical Journal 1.2–1.4). It supplies the "whole armor of God" analogy that the study works through piece by piece: the loins (virtue) protected by truth, the breastplate of righteousness over heart and conduct, the feet shod with the gospel of peace, the helmet of the [Plan of Salvation](/notes/plan-of-salvation) guarding the mind, the shield of [Faith](/notes/faith), and the sword of the word of God. Kyle pairs the chapter with [D&C 27](/notes/dandc-27) (which restates the armor in latter-day terms) and [2 Nephi 1](/notes/2-nephi-1):23 ("put on the armor of righteousness"), and reads it alongside President [Ezra Taft Benson](/notes/ezra-taft-benson)'s *Stand In Holy Places*, with a source quote from [Mark E. Peterson](/notes/mark-e-peterson).

**Context:** Ephesians 6:10–18 is the passage in which the Apostle Paul exhorts believers to "put on the whole armour of God" so as to "stand against the wiles of the devil" — enumerating the belt of truth, breastplate of righteousness, gospel-shod feet, shield of faith, helmet of salvation, and the sword of the Spirit (the word of God). It is one of the best-known extended metaphors in the New Testament.

## Where this appears

- The Whole Armor of God — the topical study built directly on this chapter, mapping each piece of armor to what it protects and why (truth, righteousness, peace, the Plan of Salvation, faith, the word of God).
