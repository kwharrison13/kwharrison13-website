# Chenmark Capital Management

Chenmark is the lead lived example in [On the Nature of Long-Term Holds](/notes/on-the-nature-of-long-term-holds) — a Portland, Maine operating investment vehicle that acquires and builds premier small and medium enterprises (SMEs) with an *indefinite* hold period. It is owned and operated by [Trish Higgins](/notes/trish-higgins) (HBS 2013), her husband James, and brother-in-law Palmer, all formerly Manhattan-based finance professionals who "grew weary of the buy-trade churn mindset that permeates Wall Street." Per the Yale case, Chenmark operates businesses in lawn care, landscaping, frozen dough, and tourism, employs ~450 people, and generates ~$60M in annual revenue.

Chenmark embodies the case's core thesis: that wealth is built by holding and [compounding](/notes/compounding) a good business for decades rather than flipping it every five years. Higgins frames the firm explicitly as [Permanent Capital](/notes/permanent-capital) — "we own and operate businesses with a forever mindset" — explaining that the very best operators and wealth-accumulators do so "over extraordinarily long periods of time measured in decades," precisely because doing so avoids taxes, transaction costs, idle time, and redeployment risk. The case notes Higgins shares her thinking in a weekly email blast, and positions Chenmark alongside [Will Thorndike](/notes/will-thorndike)'s "outsiders" worldview as the operating manual for the long-term [Holding Company](/notes/holding-company) model.

**Context:** Chenmark is a permanent-capital holding company founded in 2015 by James and Trish Higgins (and Palmer Higgins), known in the search-fund / "holdco" community for its long-term-hold approach to acquiring small businesses and its widely-read weekly newsletter.
