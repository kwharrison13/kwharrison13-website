# Solo Capitalists

A recurring node in Kyle's thesis on the unbundling of venture capital — the rise of individual investors who write large checks and operate like single-GP funds, drawing on a personal following rather than a partnership brand. The concept appears as a dedicated section heading in the unbundling research (next to Angel Investing), and is named as one of the "millionaire machines" reshaping early-stage investing alongside Rolling Funds and IPOs in the Vedika Jain call, framed there as new groups of founders and investors "popping up outside of Sand Hill Road."

In the Morgan Schwanke call, solo capitalists are tied to the same shift toward community-first, follower-driven capital ("Solo Capitalists pulling their own following will also be powerful"), sitting alongside Rolling Funds, [DAO](/notes/dao)s, and [Contrary Capital](/notes/contrary) as modes of Fund Differentiation. Across these sources Kyle treats the solo capitalist as a symptom of venture's disaggregation — distribution and brand moving from firms to individuals.

**Context:** "Solo capitalist" is a term popularized around 2020 (notably by Nikhil Basu Trivedi) for individual investors who lead priced rounds with fund-scale checks — operating without a traditional partnership — enabled by vehicles like AngelList rolling funds and SPVs.
