# Robert D. Hales

Robert D. Hales appears across Kyle's notes as a Latter-day Saint leader cited both as a first-hand witness and as a source of doctrinal quotes. In [Gordon B. Hinckley — Go Forward With Faith](/books/gordon-b-hinckley-go-forward-with-faith), Hales is identified as the Presiding Bishop who "worked closely with President Hinckley on many projects relative to the Church's temporal affairs," and he supplies two of the book's most quoted character observations: that the decisions reaching the First Presidency are "Solomon-like" — "He measures before he cuts" — and that "I have never met an individual who can become so well informed through reading and through contact with people. When he spends an evening at dinner with someone, he leaves knowing something about that individual's expertise."

In Kyle's gospel-study notes he is quoted as a teacher of doctrine. Patience — Topical Study builds on his framing of the "universal question of mortality" — "Why is it that the Son of God and His holy prophets… have trials and tribulations even when they are trying to do Heavenly Father's will? Why is it so hard, especially for them?" — and on his point that "tests and trials… allow us and our Heavenly Father to see whether we will exercise our agency to follow His son." Humility — Topical Study cites him on baptismal covenants ("Do you know what your baptismal covenants mean?").

**Context:** Robert D. Hales (1932–2017) was an American leader in The Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-day Saints, serving as Presiding Bishop and, from 1994 until his death, as a member of the Quorum of the Twelve Apostles.
