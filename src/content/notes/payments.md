# Payments

Payments is the fintech sector that recurs across Kyle's company notes and reading logs, usually as a reminder of how unforgivingly hard the category is. The load-bearing line comes from April 5th, 2022 — Reading Frederik Gieschen's Writing, where [Max Levchin](/notes/max-levchin) "always says they're very careful when they invest in anything around payments because they know **exactly** how hard it is" — a [Paypal](/notes/paypal)-founder's earned caution toward the space. The same notes trace [Paypal](/notes/paypal)'s own arc as a payments company "that started off as a product with no use case," then a use case with no business model, before becoming a sustainable business.

In Kyle's company pages the sector frames two contrasting case studies. [Adyen](/notes/adyen) appears as a payments platform whose investment-review-style pitch was cited as the model of "what good growth-thesis forming looks like." [Venmo](/notes/venmo) appears on the risk side: in Kyle's API-security notes it's the case where ~200 million transactions were scraped via its API — "data leakage," used to illustrate that securing the front-end leaves the payments API interface exposed.

**Context:** "Payments" spans card networks, processors, gateways, and consumer apps — a sector defined by razor-thin margins, heavy regulation, fraud risk, and entrenched network effects, which is why operators like Levchin treat it as one of the hardest categories to build in.
