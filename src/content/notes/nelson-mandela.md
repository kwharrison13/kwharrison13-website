# Nelson Mandela

Referenced in [Michelle Obama](/notes/michelle-obama)'s memoir [Becoming](/books/becoming) as the figure who reframed her sense of the timescale of change. Obama writes that "meeting [Nelson Mandela](/notes/nelson-mandela) gave me the perspective I needed a couple of years into our White House journey — that real change happens slowly, not just over months and years but over decades and lifetimes." In the book's argument this reinforces [Barack Obama](/notes/barack-obama)'s community-organizing frame of "the world as it is" vs. "the world as it should be," where progress means "planting seeds … the fruit of which we might never see" — patience cast as both a political and a personal virtue.

**Context:** Nelson Mandela (1918–2013) was a South African anti-apartheid leader who, after 27 years' imprisonment, became South Africa's first democratically elected president (1994–1999) and a global symbol of reconciliation.

## Where this appears

- [Becoming](/books/becoming) — quoted as the source of Obama's lesson that "real change happens slowly … over decades and lifetimes," reinforcing the book's theme of patient, generational change.
