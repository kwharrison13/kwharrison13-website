# Adam Cohen

**One-line:** *Shared-mobility researcher and consultant at [UC Berkeley](/notes/uc-berkeley), focused on the future of mobility, autonomous vehicles, land use, and parking.*

## What we know

- Does policy research and impact studies across public and private sectors, including some high-speed-rail work; led the national evaluation of the USDOT's $8M Mobility-on-Demand "sandbox" grant program (11 projects).
- Contrarian on AV hype — argues legal/institutional challenges will slow adoption, and that land use, walkable communities, and policy (not the vehicles) are the real levers.
- Thinks deeply about the future of parking: structures have ~60-year lifespans, so cities should be planning adaptable/repurposable parking now even if AVs are decades away.
