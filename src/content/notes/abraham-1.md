# Abraham 1

Abraham 1 is the opening chapter of the Book of Abraham (Pearl of Great Price), which Kyle leans on heavily in his Abrahamic Covenant topical study to establish the doctrine of the **right of the firstborn**. He cites Abraham 1:2 (Abraham's desire "to receive the blessings of the fathers" and "the right to administer the same") and Abraham 1:3 (priesthood authority as "the right of the firstborn") to distinguish the general priesthood, held by all worthy descendants, from the directing keys held by one — the same structure by which all brethren today hold the priesthood but the prophet holds the keys. He also points to Abraham 1:4, reading "the appointment of God unto the Fathers concerning the seed" as the promise to Enoch that a remnant of his seed would always remain on the earth, and to Abraham 1:28 as a charter for documentation — patriarchal blessings as "a legal record of inheritance."

In The Curse of Cain, Kyle uses Abraham 1:26–27 to illustrate a **broken lineage**: Pharaoh stands on a line cut off from the birthright, so that even a righteous ruler could not hold the priesthood by descent. This grounds the study's larger claim that loss of a forefather's inheritance damages a whole posterity — "that's how Satan can damage your posterity" — until temple ordinances restore the severed line.

**Context:** Abraham 1 is part of the Book of Abraham in the Latter-day Saint Pearl of Great Price, presenting Abraham's first-person account of his life in Ur, his deliverance from sacrifice, and his receipt of the priesthood and covenant blessings.

## Where this appears

- Abrahamic Covenant — cited (1:2–4, 1:28) for the desires of Abraham, the right of the firstborn, the promise of a remnant seed, and patriarchal blessings as a legal record of inheritance.
- The Curse of Cain — cited (1:26–27) for Pharaoh standing on a broken lineage cut off from the birthright priesthood.
