# Operational Discipline

The engineering-and-operating ethos that several of Kyle's books frame as the casualty of [Financialization](/notes/financialization). In [The Man Who Broke Capitalism](/books/the-man-who-broke-capitalism), it's the "engineering-over-finance ethos Welchism displaced" — [Jack Welch](/notes/jack-welch) redirected GE's profits to investors over R&D, wages, and reinvention, with his own stated creed that "what we have to sell as an enterprise to the equity investor is consistent, above-average earnings growth." [Flying Blind](/books/flying-blind) reads Boeing the same way: the airplane-engineer-first culture, where "functional fathers" of structures, flight controls, and propulsion had wide authority and were encouraged to "fight loudly for their views in the name of a better, safer airplane," was abandoned for shareholder metrics (RONA, buybacks). Kyle's reading lens treats Boeing as the cautionary case for a hollowed-out industrial culture, with [Anduril](/notes/anduril) as the counter-image.

[Catastrophic Care](/books/catastrophic-care) supplies a different but parallel sense: discipline as a *market feedback loop* rather than a corporate culture. David Goldhill argues that on the "Mainland," consumers "exercise highly effective discipline over the industries that produce the goods and services we consume" — punishing the expensive, careless, and wasteful — and that health care's dysfunction stems from "Surrogates" (insurers, Medicare) severing that disciplining role. The page cross-links to [The Man Who Broke Capitalism](/books/the-man-who-broke-capitalism) on the financialization-replacing-operational-discipline parallel. All three trace dysfunction to misaligned incentives at the system level, and stand in direct opposition to the [Berkshire Hathaway Annual Letters](/books/berkshire-hathaway-annual-letters), whose entire frame is operational discipline over financial engineering.

## Where this appears

- [The Man Who Broke Capitalism](/books/the-man-who-broke-capitalism) — the engineering-over-finance ethos Welchism displaced; Welch's Wall-Street-first creed.
- [Flying Blind](/books/flying-blind) — Boeing's engineer-first culture ("functional fathers") abandoned for shareholder metrics; Kyle's hollowed-out-industrial-culture lens.
- [Catastrophic Care](/books/catastrophic-care) — the consumer-as-disciplinarian as the missing market feedback loop health care lacks.
