# Rex Woodbury

Rex Woodbury appears in Kyle's notes as a venture investor at [Index Ventures](/notes/index-ventures) and author of the **Digital Native** newsletter (digitalnative.tech), whose cultural-trend writing Kyle mines repeatedly for essay research. He shows up in The Death of a Venture Fund - Research and The Productization of Venture Capital - Research among the long roster of investors Kyle canvassed; in the former, Woodbury offers a read on why a fund might wind down — generational transition (partners retiring), insufficient differentiation, and "too many career investors competing against fancy ex-founders" (e.g., Dylan Field as a Sequoia partner) — and notes Index's fondness for the phrase "crucible moment." His essay "Play It Cool: Chasing Heat vs. Being Contrarian in Venture Capital" is cited directly.

Two of his recurring "cultural barometer" observations anchor other research threads: "Gen Z hates [Leslie Knope](/notes/leslie-knope)" (the generational read of the *Parks and Rec* workaholic) feeds Toil We Must - Research, and "TikTok comments are the greatest window into the soul of society" appears in What's In a Post-Mortem - Research. Separately, in the Watershed Climate Diligence — 2021 dossier, Woodbury (then at Index) joined a May 10, 2021 reference call about [Imperfect Foods](/notes/imperfect-foods), a portfolio company.

**Context:** Rex Woodbury is a venture investor (partner at Daybreak Ventures; formerly Index Ventures) known for the widely-read "Digital Native" newsletter on consumer technology and cultural trends.
