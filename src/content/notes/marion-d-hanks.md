# Marion D. Hanks

A Latter-day Saint general authority quoted in Kyle's Knowledge — Topical Study on gospel learning as active effort rather than passive exposure. Hanks's central point: "no one knows anything about Christ's work simply by being born a member of the church," nor from "years of unmotivated exposure in meetings or classes" — "he must learn," and "learning involves self-investment and effort." He frames the gospel as something to "be studied as carefully as any science," with literature "acquired and read" and learning "increased in our spare time day by day," so that the disciple becomes "literate in the most important field of knowledge in the universe."

**Context:** Marion D. Hanks (1921–2011) was an American religious leader who served as a General Authority (a President of the First Quorum of the Seventy) in The Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-day Saints, known for his emphasis on service and personal scripture study.
