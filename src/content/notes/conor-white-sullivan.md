# Conor White-Sullivan

Founder of [Roam Research](/notes/roam-research) and a recurring figure in Kyle's [Tools For Thought](/notes/tools-for-thought) thinking. Kyle met with him repeatedly in 2021–2022 (June 22 and 24, 2021; January 31, 2022) during his time sourcing and diligencing tools-for-thought companies. White-Sullivan supplies one of the load-bearing frames in [How To Take Smart Notes](/books/how-to-take-smart-notes): that you need "to be able to get compound interest on your thoughts," because "good ideas come from when ideas have sex: the intersection of different things that you've been reading" — the [Idea Sex](/notes/idea-sex) thread that anchors the book's argument about the slip-box surfacing unsought connections.

In [The Notetaking Cold War](/notes/the-notetaking-cold-war) (Dan Shipper), White-Sullivan is one pole of the era's defining note-taking debate: against [Tiago Forte](/notes/tiago-forte)'s view that "notes should be organized by actionability" and live in one place, Conor "thinks that notes should go everywhere, and that there's no single top-down structure that can encapsulate all note-taking" — a divide Shipper maps onto the two-thousand-year-old essentialist-vs-pragmatist split in philosophy. He also appears in the Valued Empyrean conversation with [Darwin Harrison](/notes/darwin-harrison) — a discussion of a philosophy and organizational structure for creating "Value" — and turns up across Kyle's Open-Source Knowledge - Research, Networked Conviction — Roam + Investing - Research, and Historical Futurism - Research essay-research threads.

**Context:** Conor White-Sullivan is the co-founder and CEO of Roam Research, a networked note-taking application built around bidirectional links and outliner-style blocks that became prominent in the "tools for thought" movement around 2020.
