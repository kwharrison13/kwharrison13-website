# Immigration

In Kyle's notes, immigration appears chiefly as the central lever of [Matt Yglesias](/notes/matt-yglesias)'s [One Billion Americans](/notes/one-billion-americans) thesis — the argument that the U.S. should roughly triple its population, largely through immigration, to sustain its standing and dynamism. In Kyle's Great Softening research, this surfaces as the "increase immigration to 1b Americans" idea, slotted into a thread weighing whether America is in decline: the framing that immigrants may be "the most aspirational people," set against the observation that great powers (Romans → British) average roughly 200 years.

It also sits within the broader concept of [Migration](/notes/migration) (population movement across geographies driven by economic, political, or social factors), which the wiki links to the [California Diaspora](/notes/california-diaspora) and the urban economics of [City Building](/notes/city-building). Across these notes immigration is treated less as a partisan policy debate and more as a demographic input to national vitality and growth.

**Context:** Immigration is the movement of people into a country to take up residence. In the U.S. policy debate it spans economic arguments (labor supply, innovation, demographic growth) and political ones; Yglesias's *One Billion Americans* (2020) makes the pro-growth case for substantially expanding it.
