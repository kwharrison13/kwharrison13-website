# Mosiah 29

The Book of Mormon chapter in which King Mosiah dissolves the monarchy and institutes government by the [Voice of the People](/notes/voice-of-the-people). Across Kyle's notes it functions as the canonical proof-text for two opposed ideas held in tension. On the affirming side, Mosiah 29:2-3 records that the people wanted Aaron (next in line for the throne) to be king, but neither Aaron nor any of the sons of [Mosiah](/notes/mosiah) would take the kingdom — the abdication that forces the shift to judges (June 22–28, 2020. Alma 17–22: "I Will Make an Instrument of Thee"). On the cautionary side, verses 26-27 — "it is not common that the voice of the people desireth anything contrary to that which is right… but if the time comes that the voice of the people doth choose iniquity, then is the time that the judgments of God will come upon you" — recur as the warning that majority rule is legitimate only while it tracks righteousness. Both Helaman studies pair this passage with the Gadianton robbers "filling the judgment-seats" as the case study of the people's voice turning corrupt (August 17–23, 2020. Helaman 1–6: "The Rock of Our Redeemer", August 24–30, 2020. Helaman 7–12: "Remember the Lord").

The same passage anchors the political through-line of [Joseph Smith — Rough Stone Rolling](/books/joseph-smith-rough-stone-rolling), where Joseph invokes Mosiah 29:26-27 to argue that when the people's voice is unjust "Aristarchy should be the governing principle… the wisest & best Laws should be made" — the prophet-vs-democracy, vox-populi-vs-[Mob Rule](/notes/mob-rule) tension at the heart of the book. In Spiritual Gifts, Mosiah 29:18-20 is cited alongside D&C 50:35 and Alma 37:33 for the idea that there is nothing ordained of God that one cannot overcome.

## Where this appears

- [Joseph Smith — Rough Stone Rolling](/books/joseph-smith-rough-stone-rolling) — Joseph cites Mosiah 29:26-27 in the church-vs-democracy debate over majority rule and "aristarchy"
- June 22–28, 2020. Alma 17–22: "I Will Make an Instrument of Thee" — Mosiah 29:2-3, the sons of Mosiah refusing the kingdom
- August 17–23, 2020. Helaman 1–6: "The Rock of Our Redeemer" — verses 26-27 paired with Helaman's corrupt government as the voice-of-the-people warning
- August 24–30, 2020. Helaman 7–12: "Remember the Lord" — same warning, paired with the Gadianton robbers usurping the judgment-seats
- Spiritual Gifts — Mosiah 29:18-20 cited on overcoming what is ordained of God
