# Ronald Syme

In [To Save America, Restore Our Frontier](/notes/to-save-america-restore-our-frontier), Ronald Syme is quoted as a historian of Rome to ground the article's central analogy: "The strength and vitality of an empire is frequently due to the new aristocracy from the periphery." The line anchors the piece's argument that renewal flows from the frontier and its outsiders rather than from an established center — a pattern the article carries forward from Rome to America.

**Context:** Sir Ronald Syme (1903–1989) was a New Zealand–born historian of ancient Rome, best known for *The Roman Revolution* (1939), which reframed the rise of Augustus around the emergence of a new governing elite drawn from outside the old Roman aristocracy.

## Where this appears

- [To Save America, Restore Our Frontier](/notes/to-save-america-restore-our-frontier) — quoted on Rome's "new aristocracy from the periphery" to support the article's frontier-renewal thesis.
