# Hiram Page

Hiram Page appears once in Kyle's Early Mormonism topical study, in the section on the Missouri persecutions and what enraged the Jacksonians: "[William McLellin](/notes/william-mclellin) interviews [Hiram Page](/notes/hiram-page) in regards to his beatings at the hands of the mob." In context, Page stands as one of the early Saints who suffered violence during the Jackson County expulsions — the persecution the study attributes not to any crime of the Mormons but, in [Alexander Majors](/notes/alexander-majors)'s later words, "solely from the claim that they had a new revelation from the almighty."

**Context:** Hiram Page (1800–1852) was an early Latter-day Saint and one of the Eight Witnesses to the Book of Mormon. He married into the Whitmer family and was among the Saints driven out of Jackson County, Missouri, during the 1833 persecutions.
