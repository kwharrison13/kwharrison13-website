# Vaughn J. Featherstone

Vaughn J. Featherstone is cited in Kyle's Humility — Topical Study as the source of a teaching illustration on identity and destiny: the story of the imprisoned son of King Louis XVI, where the throne's enemies sought to "destroy him morally" so he "would never realize the great and grand destiny that life had bestowed upon him." The anecdote is used to underscore the study's theme of remembering one's true worth and divine potential. He appears in the study's people index alongside [Dieter F. Uchtdorf](/notes/dieter-f-uchtdorf), [Robert D. Hales](/notes/robert-d-hales), [C.S. Lewis](/notes/c-s-lewis), and [Tad R. Callister](/notes/tad-r-callister).

**Context:** Vaughn J. Featherstone (1931–2014) was a General Authority of The Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-day Saints, serving as a member of the Presiding Bishopric and later in the Quorums of the Seventy, and was known for his teaching and writing on youth, service, and character.
