# Isaiah 63

A chapter of the Old Testament book of Isaiah. In Kyle's Charity & Love topical study, [Isaiah 63](/notes/isaiah-63):9 ("In all their affliction, he was afflicted… in his love and in his pity he redeemed them") is cited to show that the [Atonement](/notes/atonement) was suffered because of Christ's love. It supports the study's core argument that [Charity](/notes/charity) is the *prompting motive* — the pure love of Christ that moves the redemptive work — rather than a deed or act in itself.

**Context:** Isaiah 63 opens with the imagery of the Lord returning from Edom with garments stained red, recounting His redemption of Israel and the mercy He showed them in their affliction.

## Where this appears

- Charity & Love — Isaiah 63:9 quoted (Topical Journal 2.17) to tie the Atonement to Christ's love and undergird charity as "the pure love of Christ."
