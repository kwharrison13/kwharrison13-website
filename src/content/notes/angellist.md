# AngelList

AngelList recurs in Kyle's corpus as the canonical infrastructure layer for syndicates, SPVs, and emerging-manager fund products — and as [Naval Ravikant](/notes/naval-ravikant)'s defining company. Naval co-founded it, and his "portfolio-of-options" investing system is described as "AngelList in practice" in [Naval Ravikant — The Angel Philosopher](/notes/naval-ravikant-the-angel-philosopher). In Kyle's manager meetings it shows up as the rails new GPs build on: Morgan Schwanke started Mana Ventures as an AngelList syndicate (where most of his capital sat), launched a Rolling Funds vehicle a year later, and saw his AngelList product experience as an edge for building a better one; Arielle Kogut at Tribe used AngelList's "2% + 10% carry" terms as the competitive benchmark for SPV economics.

It also appears as a product/API reference and a competitive frame. Andreas Klinger — who worked at AngelList — cited their caution about deprecating API fields as a lesson in platform design. The WeFunder memo names AngelList (alongside Party Round and Stonks) as the "great for insiders" accredited-SPV model — capped at 250 investors per SPV (100 above $10M) — that WeFunder's retail Reg CF approach is positioned against.

**Context:** AngelList is a platform for startups and investors founded in 2010 by Naval Ravikant and Babak Nivi; it pioneered online angel syndicates and SPVs and later Rolling Funds, becoming core back-office infrastructure for a large share of emerging venture managers.
