# Packy McCormick

Packy McCormick is the writer behind [Not Boring](/notes/not-boring), and one of the "bards of company building and investing" Kyle repeatedly cites — a model of the **open-source-knowledge layer** forming around investing, where long, entertaining company narratives replace traditional dry analysis. In Open-Source Knowledge - Research, Kyle leans on two of his pieces: "Idea Legos," which quotes [Tim Urban](/notes/tim-urban) on how "language made ideas composable... ideas got bigger and better faster and faster" (the compounding-ideas passage Kyle uses for the "Interconnectivity" section), and "Business is the New Sports," the source of Kyle's framing that "people are desperate for the play-by-play" and that "intrinsic motivation is the currency of the commons."

Kyle has annotated two of McCormick's essays as full long-reads: [The AppetiZIRP](/notes/the-appetizirp) (ZIRP-era phenomena as previews of an [Automation](/notes/automation)-driven, UBI-style abundance future) and [The Enchanted Notebook](/notes/the-enchanted-notebook) (on [Ray Kurzweil](/notes/ray-kurzweil), the Singularity, and "innovation is half storytelling," with the Amazon Future Press Release as a writing discipline). McCormick's analysis also shaped Kyle's company work directly: his Replit deep dive was built partly from a close read of McCormick's [Not Boring](/notes/not-boring) memo on [Replit](/notes/replit), from which Kyle drew the "world's first massively distributed and collaborative operating system" endgame framing.

**Context:** Packy McCormick is a writer and investor — author of the widely read business/tech newsletter Not Boring and founder of Not Boring Capital, an early-stage venture fund. His essays are known for blending narrative storytelling with company strategy analysis.
