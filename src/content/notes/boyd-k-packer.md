# Boyd K. Packer

An Apostle of The Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-day Saints — a member, and later President, of the Quorum of the Twelve. In the corpus he appears as the author of "The Candle of the Lord", a talk to new mission presidents on how spiritual knowledge is received, why it cannot be conveyed in words alone, and how "a testimony is to be *found* in the *bearing* of it."

Across Kyle's reading he is also the recurring institutional figure in the LDS Church's tension between ecclesiastical authority and intellectual/scholarly inquiry. In [Leonard Arrington and the Writing of Mormon History](/books/leonard-arrington-and-the-writing-of-mormon-history) he is [Leonard Arrington](/notes/leonard-arrington)'s "chief antagonist," remembered for the candid maxim "Some things that are true are not very useful" — the view that "dogma would always trump data," and that members in academic research wrongly begin to "judge the Church... by the principles of their own profession" rather than the reverse. [Stretching The Heavens](/books/stretching-the-heavens) casts him in the same role against [Eugene England](/notes/eugene-england): his talk "The Mantle Is Far, Far Greater than the Intellect" and his counsel to England to resign from Dialogue "crystallized England's conflict with institutional authority," along with his line that "the doctrines of the gospel are revealed through the Spirit to prophets, not through the intellect to scholars." He also figures in the Lester Bush priesthood-ban manuscript episode.

Beyond the intellectual-authority debates, Packer appears in [The Crucible of Doubt](/books/the-crucible-of-doubt) cited (alongside [George Albert Smith](/notes/george-albert-smith)) on revelation and over-programming, and in [The Priesthood Power of Women](/books/the-priesthood-power-of-women) recounting [David O. McKay](/notes/david-o-mckay) "finally beginning to understand" temple ordinances late in life — Packer's point that even a prophet of fifty years "was learning, he was growing." He is also a frequent source of quotations across Kyle's gospel-study and Come Follow Me notes and in [Chad's Big Quote Book](/notes/chad-s-big-quote-book).

**Context:** Boyd K. Packer (1924–2015) was a Latter-day Saint apostle who served in the Quorum of the Twelve from 1970 and as its President from 2008 until his death; he was known for forceful defenses of ecclesiastical authority, traditional doctrine, and the priority of revelation over academic scholarship.

- [Leonard Arrington and the Writing of Mormon History](/books/leonard-arrington-and-the-writing-of-mormon-history) — Arrington's chief antagonist; "some things that are true are not very useful"; dogma over data.
- [Stretching The Heavens](/books/stretching-the-heavens) — his conflict with [Eugene England](/notes/eugene-england); "The Mantle Is Far, Far Greater than the Intellect"; counsel to resign Dialogue.

## Connections

- [Holy Ghost](/notes/holy-ghost) — the central subject of "The Candle of the Lord": how the gift is received and recognized.
- Still Small Voice — the Spirit whispers gently rather than shouting.
- [Revelation](/notes/revelation) — "pure intelligence flowing into you… sudden strokes of ideas."
- [Testimony](/notes/testimony) — found in the bearing of it; grows by increments, often "and they knew it not."
