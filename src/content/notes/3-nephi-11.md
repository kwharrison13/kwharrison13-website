# 3 Nephi 11

3 Nephi 11 — the chapter where the resurrected Christ descends to minister among the Nephites at the temple in Bountiful — appears in Kyle's gospel study as a chapter about Christ's persistence and the call to become as a little child. The September 14–20, 2020. 3 Nephi 8–11: "Arise and Come Forth unto Me" Come Follow Me notes treat it as the climax of the 3 Nephi 8–11 arc — after the three hours of darkness and the voice of Christ calling survivors to repentance — under the heading "Christ never gives up."

The Revelation — Topical Study cites 3 Nephi 11:38 — God's invitation to "become as little children" — read with [Clayton M. Christensen](/notes/clayton-christensen)'s observation that this requires abandoning much of what we've learned about the impossible and trusting God "as innocently as little children do," so that "when we are doing all we can and our leaders ask us to do more, miracles are the only option."

**Context:** 3 Nephi 11 records the resurrected Jesus Christ descending from heaven to the gathered Nephites, inviting them one by one to feel the prints in His hands and feet, declaring the doctrine of baptism, forbidding contention, and commanding all to "become as a little child" to enter the kingdom of God.
