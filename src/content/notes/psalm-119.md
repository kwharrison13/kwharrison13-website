# Psalm 119

A psalm from the Old Testament book of Psalms, used in Kyle's scripture study as a cross-reference on turning inward and reflecting on one's ways. In the September 7–13, 2020. 3 Nephi 1–7: "Lift Up Your Head and Be of Good Cheer" study, Psalm 119:59 is cited verbatim — "I thought on my ways, and turned my feet unto thy testimonies." The study sets this in contrast with the selfishness of [the rising generation](/essays/the-rising-generation) who "became for themselves" ([3 Nephi 1](/notes/3-nephi-1):29), connecting it to the prodigal son who "came to himself" ([Luke 15](/notes/luke-15):17) as a model of reflective repentance opposed to [Pride](/notes/pride).

**Context:** Psalm 119 is the longest chapter in the Bible, an acrostic poem of 176 verses meditating throughout on the value of God's law, word, and testimonies.

## Where this appears

- September 7–13, 2020. 3 Nephi 1–7: "Lift Up Your Head and Be of Good Cheer" — verse 59 cited as a cross-reference on reflecting on one's ways and turning toward God, contrasted with selfishness and pride
