# Microbes

A topic Kyle saved into his June 29th, 2021 Roam "Quick Capture" reading log while at [Index Ventures](/notes/index-ventures). The capture pairs two Jessica Green TED talks ("Are we filtering the wrong microbes?" and "We're covered in germs — let's design for that") with [Phylagen](/notes/phylagen), the company whose page is the lone backlink here. The thread reads as Kyle exploring the microbiome / built-environment angle — the idea that the microbes around us (in buildings, on surfaces, in air) carry signal worth measuring — with Phylagen as the commercial expression of it.

**Context:** Microbes are microscopic organisms (bacteria, fungi, viruses, archaea). Jessica Green is a biologist known for work on the "built-environment microbiome" — the microbial communities inside the spaces humans occupy. Phylagen is a company that analyzes environmental microbial samples (e.g. dust) to trace where products and materials come from.
