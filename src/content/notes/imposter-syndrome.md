# Imposter Syndrome

Named in passing in *How to Take Smart Notes*: high achievers who've glimpsed the vast amount of knowledge out there are prone to Imposter Syndrome — feeling not up to the job despite evidence to the contrary — because good students keep raising the bar for themselves by focusing on what they haven't yet mastered (citing Clance and Imes 1978; Brems et al. 1994).
