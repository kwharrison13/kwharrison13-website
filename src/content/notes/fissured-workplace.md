# Fissured Workplace

**One-line definition:** *Economists' term for the arrangement where companies outsource specialized forms of work to subcontractors — blamed for eroding labor standards and creating a legalized form of wage discrimination that exacerbates inequality.*

## How sources describe it

- From [Amazon Unbound](/books/amazon-unbound) (Brad Stone), on Amazon's last-mile delivery: *"Economists called this kind of arrangement, where companies outsourced specialized forms of work to subcontractors, 'the fissured workplace,' and blamed it for eroding labor standards and fomenting a legalized form of wage discrimination that exacerbated inequality."*
- A decades-long trend propagated not just by FedEx and Uber but by hotels, cable providers, and tech companies like Apple. Amazon's scale "spread it that much further, and policymakers… were caught flat-footed."
- The mechanism in Amazon's case: "arm's-length deals" with non-union DSP (delivery service partner) drivers, letting it pay lower rates and avoid "the prospective nightmare of drivers bargaining collectively." Cross-pressure on this model surfaced in the YouTube Music / Cognizant joint-employer fight.
