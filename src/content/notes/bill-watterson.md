# Bill Watterson

Cartoonist Naval Ravikant names as an example of authenticity as a competitive moat: in "Escape competition through authenticity," he asks who could compete with Watterson by creating a better *Calvin and Hobbes* — the point being that no one could, because Watterson's work was an inseparable extension of who he was. It's a passing name-drop illustrating the point, not a discussion of Watterson himself.
