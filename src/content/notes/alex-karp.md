# Alex Karp

**One-line:** *Co-founder and CEO of Palantir; author of the essay "The Disapproval of the Crowd," a defense of conflict and creative output over corporate agreeableness.*

## What we know

- Wrote ["Likability Is a Jail" / "The Disapproval of the Crowd"](https://www.founderstribune.org/p/the-disapproval-of-the-crowd-by-alex-karp), arguing that a "culture of agreeableness" moves institutions *away* from creative output, and that "the act of rebellion that involves building something from nothing... by definition requires a rejection of what has come before."
- Borrowed John Mulaney's line that "likability is a jail" and invokes [Emerson](/notes/ralph-waldo-emerson)'s *Self-Reliance* ("for nonconformity, the world whips you with its displeasure").
- Argues that most "innovation" in Silicon Valley is mimicry — "derivative and retrograde" — and that the best founders resist the urge to build "imperfect imitations of prior successes."
