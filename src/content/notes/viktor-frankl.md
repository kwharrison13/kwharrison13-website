# Viktor Frankl

Austrian psychiatrist invoked in [Faith is Not Blind](/books/faith-is-not-blind) as a witness to the twentieth-century collapse of inherited meaning. The Hafens quote Frankl, writing from his years in a Nazi concentration camp, that "the traditions which buttressed [man's] behavior are now rapidly diminishing. No instinct tells him what he has to do, and no tradition tells him what he ought to do" — soon he will not even know "what he wishes to do," and will instead be governed by "what other people wish him to do." In the book's "life / my life" frame, Frankl marks the moment the outer "life" box dissolves alongside [Samuel Beckett](/notes/samuel-beckett)'s *Waiting for Godot* and relativism. The Hafens credit Frankl with the constructive response — that the modern vacuum of meaning is an opportunity for each person to define a meaningful life — while arguing it is "difficult, perhaps impossible, to infer general cosmic meaning from purely personal preferences," the gap the Restoration's "big box" is meant to fill.

**Context:** Viktor Frankl (1905–1997) was an Austrian neurologist, psychiatrist, and Holocaust survivor, founder of logotherapy and author of *Man's Search for Meaning*, which argues that the primary human drive is the search for meaning.
