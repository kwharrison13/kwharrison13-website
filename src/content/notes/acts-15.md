# Acts 15

A chapter in the New Testament book of Acts, cited in Kyle's Christ Defines His Gospel study under the "Grace" heading. There [Acts 15](/notes/acts-15):11 sits alongside John 1:17 and Ephesians 2:8 as a grace proof-text, paired against a parallel "Works" list (Matthew 5:16, Ephesians 2:10, James 2:14–17) — part of the study's larger faith-and-works thread, where grace is real but discipleship is costly ([Dietrich Bonhoeffer](/notes/dietrich-bonhoeffer)'s "cheap grace" vs. "costly grace") and salvation is conditional until endured to the end ([Dallin H. Oaks](/notes/dallin-h-oaks)).

**Context:** Acts 15 records the Jerusalem Council, where the early church debated whether Gentile converts must be circumcised and keep the law of Moses; Peter's speech (v. 11) — "we believe that through the grace of the Lord Jesus Christ we shall be saved, even as they" — is the grace verse Kyle's notes draw on.
