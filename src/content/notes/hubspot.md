# Hubspot

SaaS marketing/CRM company that recurs in Kyle's diligence notes mainly as a **GTM archetype** — "the Hubspot playbook" of using free tools and content to drive bottom-up awareness and inbound. The Pave Diligence — 2021 dossier notes Pave is "using the Hubspot growth playbook around collecting information and offering access to benchmarking data," and the Salt Security Diligence — 2021 page asks whether Salt could "run the Hubspot playbook to drive bottom-up awareness" via a free API-vulnerability assessment tool.

It also appears as a market-positioning comp and as a customer logo. In the Rob Koyfman (Koyfin) call, Hubspot is invoked as the prosumer-segment template — "like Hubspot or [Canva](/notes/canva) in their respective markets" — for Koyfin's positioning between retail day traders and sophisticated professionals (November 15th, 2021 — Rob Koyfman (Koyfin)). And in the Airplane.dev read, Hubspot is listed (with [Square](/notes/square) and Coupa) among the businesses that had adopted Airplane after its March 2021 Series A (January 31st, 2022 — Read up on Airplane.dev).

**Context:** HubSpot ($HUBS) is a Boston-based public company offering CRM, marketing, sales, and service software, widely credited with popularizing "inbound marketing" — free tools and content as a top-of-funnel acquisition engine.

## Where this appears

- Pave Diligence — 2021 — Pave is described as running "the Hubspot growth playbook" of free benchmarking access to collect data.
- Salt Security Diligence — 2021 — raised as a GTM idea: run the Hubspot playbook via a free API-risk assessment tool.
- November 15th, 2021 — Rob Koyfman (Koyfin) — used as a prosumer-positioning comp (Hubspot/Canva) for Koyfin.
- January 31st, 2022 — Read up on Airplane.dev — listed as an Airplane.dev customer alongside Square and Coupa.
