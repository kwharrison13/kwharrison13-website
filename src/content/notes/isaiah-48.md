# Isaiah 48

A chapter of the Old Testament book of Isaiah that recurs in Kyle's [gospel-study](/notes/gospel-study) notes for a single verse — Isaiah 48:10, "I have chosen thee in the furnace of affliction" — paired each time with its Book of Mormon parallel [1 Nephi 20](/notes/1-nephi-20):10. In Chosen Leaders, the verse anchors the study's core distinction between the *called* and the *chosen*: "Some think that they should be spared from any adversity if they keep God's commandments, but it is in the 'furnace of affliction' that we are chosen" ([D. Todd Christofferson](/notes/d-todd-christofferson)). In The Reasons The Spirit Withdraws, the same verse supports the affliction-as-purification theme — "the furnace of affliction helps purify even the very best of saints by burning away the dross in their lives and leaving behind pure gold" ([Paul V. Johnson](/notes/paul-v-johnson)).

**Context:** Isaiah 48 is a chapter in the biblical book of Isaiah, addressing a rebellious house of Jacob; verse 10 ("Behold, I have refined thee, but not with silver; I have chosen thee in the furnace of affliction") is paralleled in the Book of Mormon at 1 Nephi 20, where Nephi quotes Isaiah, and is a frequently cited LDS proof-text for the redemptive purpose of trials.

## Where this appears

- Chosen Leaders — 48:10's "furnace of affliction" frames how the called become the chosen through adversity
- The Reasons The Spirit Withdraws — 48:10 supports the purifying-furnace theme that "no pain we suffer is wasted"
