# Roy Disney

Roy Disney appears in Kyle's notes on [The Everything Store](/books/the-everything-store) as a recurring shorthand for the operating partner who "makes the money happen" so that the visionary's dreams can be built. In a Kyle note responding to a highlight about [Jeff Bezos](/notes/jeff-bezos) turning imagination into physical reality (alongside Walt Disney, Henry Ford, and Steve Jobs), Kyle writes: "Connect to Roy Disney always making the money happen to build Walt's dreams." Kyle uses the Walt-and-Roy pairing as a portfolio cross-reference — a frame for the complementary roles of the dreamer ([Walt Disney](/notes/walt-disney)) and the financial/operational partner who funds and executes the vision across his own portfolio companies.

**Context:** Roy O. Disney was Walt Disney's older brother and co-founder of The Walt Disney Company. He ran the studio's business and finances while Walt drove the creative vision — the classic visionary-plus-operator partnership Kyle invokes here.

## Where this appears

- [The Everything Store](/books/the-everything-store) — invoked as the "make the money happen for Walt's dreams" archetype, both in a Kyle note on the [Jeff Bezos](/notes/jeff-bezos) / great-entrepreneur highlight and as a portfolio operating-philosophy cross-reference.
