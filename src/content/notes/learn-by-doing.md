# Learn by Doing

The career-development framing that recurs in [Julie Chen](/notes/julie-chen)'s thesis: surround yourself with smart people to "learn by doing," find your "spike," don't rush your learning, and don't start something just for the sake of it. It is the reasoning she gives for joining an early-stage company ([Retool](/notes/retool)) after leaving [Contrary](/notes/contrary) — "the largest check of my life" — choosing proximity to talented people and hands-on work over moving faster on her own. The idea sits inside her broader "[Build a Better On Ramp For Life](/notes/build-a-better-on-ramp-for-life)" career framework (June 6th, 2022 — Julie Chen (Retool)).
