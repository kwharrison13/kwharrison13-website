# Matt Schulman

Founder and CEO of [Pave](/notes/pave) (formerly Trove), the real-time compensation system-of-record at the center of Kyle's 2021 diligence for [Index Ventures](/notes/index-ventures). Schulman, who spent a couple of years at [Facebook](/notes/facebook) before founding the company, was described as having impressed the deal team — Mark Goldberg was "super impressed" with him, and [Dominic Jacquesson](/notes/dominic-jacquesson) noted "Matt continues to impress" in their November 2021 meeting. His thesis, captured in his "Compensation Is Stuck In The Dark Ages" essay, was that benchmarking surveys are broken because of manual spreadsheet upload, and that Pave's wedge is real-time integrations with a company's HRIS, ATS, and cap table.

Under Schulman, Pave scaled from ~$300K to ~$5M ARR in 2021 on the back of a give-to-get data network (1,300+ companies benchmarking, ~140 paying customers at ~$40K ACV), fast-following [YC Continuity](/notes/yc-continuity)'s $46M Series B done that August at $400M post. The defining strategic risk he discussed was the dependency on [Carta](/notes/carta) (which could throttle API access), with Schulman acknowledging an "uneasy relationship" with Carta CEO [Henry Ward](/notes/henry-ward) but believing Pave was near "escape velocity." He told the Index team he wasn't looking to raise "until Q1 or Q2," and Index's plan was a pre-emptive Series C — a ~$50M lead in a $100M round at a ~$1B valuation — anchored by a December 14, 2021 dinner with the US partners.

**Context:** Matt Schulman is the founder and CEO of Pave, a compensation-management and benchmarking startup. He previously worked as an engineer at Facebook.
