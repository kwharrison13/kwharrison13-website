# Conversation

In [Stop Asking Questions](/books/stop-asking-questions), conversation is reframed from a passive back-and-forth into something the host actively *leads*. The book's craft is to take charge from the start (telling — not asking — a guest to find a quiet room), to skip small talk and "fillers and distractions" in favor of what you actually care about, and to interrupt when needed with the exonerating phrase "I'm sorry to interrupt, but…" before naming a higher purpose. The guide-on-a-hike analogy captures the stance: you don't keep asking "left or right?", you know the paths and lead. A related caution from the book is that good conversation technique can leave the *other* person feeling vulnerable and alone in their vulnerability if you never reveal anything about yourself — drawing people out carries a reciprocity cost.

A second, very different model of conversation appears in Books 2.0 - Research, drawn from the biography [Gordon B. Hinckley — Go Forward With Faith](/books/gordon-b-hinckley-go-forward-with-faith): conversation as a learning instrument. Hinckley is described leaving "an evening at dinner with someone… knowing something about that individual's expertise" — grilling a security officer for an hour on police procedure, speaking builders' language about finials and mullions — using ordinary conversation to compound knowledge across domains. Here conversation is tagged alongside [Spirit of Humility](/notes/spirit-of-humility) as a vehicle for an "insatiable appetite for [Learning](/notes/learning)."

## Where this appears

- [Stop Asking Questions](/books/stop-asking-questions) — taking charge of a conversation, the "sorry to interrupt" move, and avoiding small talk; conversation as a led, directive craft
- Books 2.0 - Research — conversation as a learning tool (Hinckley extracting expertise from everyone he meets), via [Gordon B. Hinckley — Go Forward With Faith](/books/gordon-b-hinckley-go-forward-with-faith)
