# Agents Unto Themselves

A scriptural phrase and gospel-study theme drawn from **D&C 58:26–28** — "it is not meet that I should command in all things… for the power is in them, wherein they are agents unto themselves." It anchors Kyle's "We Are Responsible for Our Own Learning" study, the introductory week of the 2019 New Testament [Come, Follow Me](/notes/come-follow-me) curriculum on taking personal responsibility for one's own learning. Kyle frames it as the charge to "be agents unto yourselves" who are "anxiously engaged in a good cause" and who "seek learning by study and also by faith" (D&C 88:118) rather than waiting to be acted upon.

The concept sits inside a cluster Kyle builds around [Agency](/notes/agency), [Learning, No Greater Responsibility](/notes/learning-no-greater-responsibility), and self-reliance in conversion — that "we cannot borrow conversion from anyone else" and must each gain and nurture our own testimony. It links agency directly to the obligation of self-directed gospel learning.

**Context:** In Latter-day Saint doctrine, "agency" is the God-given capacity to choose, and being "agents unto themselves" (D&C 58) is a frequently-cited proof-text for personal initiative — that disciples should act for themselves and do much good of their own free will rather than be commanded in all things.
