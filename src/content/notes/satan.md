# Satan

Across Kyle's gospel-study notes, Satan is treated less as an abstract figure than as a **practical model of how temptation operates** — and how to counter it. The dominant theme is *strategy*. The Lehonti Principle reads Alma 47 as a template: Amalickiah is Satan and Lehonti is you; the invitation to "come down" is repeated not because it grows more attractive but because Satan never gives up and the constant reminder keeps the temptation in mind. When he can't move you to the line, "he brings the line to you," then "administers poison by degrees." He doesn't want to ride shotgun — he wants to drive ("he leadeth them by the neck with a flaxen cord," [2 Nephi 26](/notes/2-nephi-26):22). Using Satan Against Satan inverts the dynamic: treat each temptation as a *catalyst* to do something righteous instead, becoming "a master rather than a victim" of Satan, going looking for "Goliaths" until the fight becomes fun, with quick [Repentance](/notes/repentance) keeping you clean.

A second thread is **generational reach**: The Curse of Cain argues that when a forefather loses his inheritance his posterity loses theirs — "that's how Satan can damage your posterity" — which the temple ordinances and [Malachi 4](/notes/malachi-4) are meant to heal. A third is **vigilance**: How Do We Know Where To Protect Ourselves notes Satan "attacks hardest when you're tired," making sleep a spiritual defense. [Henry B. Eyring](/notes/henry-b-eyring)'s counsel recurs — Satan expands "the space that is NOT safe" and pushes you to come ever closer to the dividing line, but you bring heaven's protection down simply by turning toward the Savior. Satan appears throughout the broader Plan of Salvation — Topical Study, Faith — Topical Study, and [Atonement](/notes/atonement)-related studies as the adversary against which agency, faith, and the Atonement are exercised.

**Context:** In Latter-day Saint and broader Christian theology, Satan (the devil, Lucifer) is the adversary who opposes God's plan and tempts mankind to sin, cast out of heaven for rebellion against God.

## Where this appears

- Using Satan Against Satan — turning temptation into a catalyst; being a master, not a victim; hunting "Goliaths"; quick repentance.
- The Lehonti Principle — Amalickiah-as-Satan model: never gives up, brings the line to you, poisons "by degrees," wants to drive.
- The Curse of Cain — generational reach: a lost inheritance damages posterity; the temple as the fix.
- How Do We Know Where To Protect Ourselves — Satan attacks hardest when you're tired; sleep as spiritual defense.
- Plan of Salvation — Topical Study — Satan as the adversary in the broader plan.
- Faith — Topical Study — Satan opposed by faith.
- Christ Defines His Gospel, Grace For Grace, How To Apply The Atonement, Everyone Is Human — Satan referenced in the atonement/repentance studies.
