# Old Testament Christology

A recurring tag in Kyle's [Book of Mormon](/notes/book-of-mormon) study, always paired with [Unapologetically Christian](/notes/unapologetically-christian). It captures the idea — drawn out repeatedly in his Alma and Helaman notes — that the Nephites knew Christ and looked forward to His coming long before His mortal ministry. They kept the [Law of Moses](/notes/law-of-moses) as a "type of his coming" ([Alma 25](/notes/alma-25):15) while still believing in and rejoicing in "his day which is to come" ([Helaman 8](/notes/helaman-8):22), rather than treating the law as an end in itself the way Kyle observes the Old World Jews did with "tradition."

The throughline Kyle pulls (citing [Joseph Smith — Rough Stone Rolling](/books/joseph-smith-rough-stone-rolling)) is that there was "no sharp drop after the Fall, followed by gradual spiritual enlightenment" — "theologically, the ancient patriarchs were the equals of later Christians," and "the problem of history was to hold on to the Gospel, not to prepare for its coming." In his reading, the Nephites' ability to "know of Christ and see past the [Law of Moses](/notes/law-of-moses)" was simply a matter of spiritual preparation, not a later doctrinal development.

**Context:** "Old Testament Christology" generally refers to readings of pre-Christian scripture that find Christ already present or anticipated within it. In the Latter-day Saint frame Kyle is working in, this is heightened: Book of Mormon prophets are depicted as having explicit foreknowledge of Christ centuries before His birth.

## Where this appears

- August 24–30, 2020. Helaman 7–12: "Remember the Lord" — tagged where Nephi's people are shown looking forward to Christ's coming while keeping the Law of Moses without the Old World fixation on tradition.
- July 6–12, 2020. Alma 30–31: "The Virtue of the Word of God" — tagged on Alma 25:15, the law as a "type" of Christ's coming, with the *Rough Stone Rolling* "patriarchs were the equals of later Christians" note.
- June 29–July 5, 2020. Alma 23–29: "They Never Did Fall Away" — same Alma 25:15 / *Rough Stone Rolling* pairing, framing the Anti-Nephi-Lehies' steadfast looking-forward to Christ.
