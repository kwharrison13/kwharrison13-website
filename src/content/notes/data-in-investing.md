# Data in Investing

Kyle's running thread on using data, analytics, and quantitative systems as a source of edge in both venture and public-market investing — and a catalog of how firms try (and sometimes fail) to operationalize it. The cautionary anchor is [Inside the Rise and Fall of Coatue's Quant Fund](/notes/inside-the-rise-and-fall-of-coatue-s-quant-fund): [Coatue](/notes/coatue) raised >$350M for a quant fund that "died in 15 months," undone less by bad models than by [Philippe Laffont](/notes/philippe-laffont)'s refusal "to let a quant fund be a quant fund" — second-guessing signals and scaling strategies on his wishes rather than a quantitative rationale. The lesson Kyle draws is that data infrastructure fails without genuine buy-in at the top.

On the practitioner side, the concept maps two contrasting data-driven shops. Tribe Capital (via Arielle Kogut) runs a "very data driven approach," pulling transactional data through a "Magic 8 Ball" report built by Jonathan Hsu (an early Facebook data scientist) across 1,000+ companies since 2013 for benchmarking. Inside [Contrary Capital](/notes/contrary), the Seth Kimmel session details building the PRISM data architecture — a Snowflake-backed pipeline enriching VP/Fellow data with Harmonic.ai and Proxycurl into "one massive observable table of the Contrary universe" to score people by quality and network strength; Kimmel frames the data-science side of investing as "a good exercise in [First Principles](/notes/first-principles-thinking) thinking." The thesis also shows up in operating companies ([Innova](/notes/innova)'s instrumented-vehicle data play) and in [Jeet Raut](/notes/jeet-raut)'s proposed scraped-plus-questionnaire municipal data product, and connects to [Equity Research](/notes/equity-research) (public disclosure as the data substrate) and [Investing](/notes/investing) more broadly.

**Context:** "Data in investing" broadly refers to the use of alternative data, quantitative models, and analytics platforms to source, underwrite, and monitor investments — a fast-growing practice across both venture capital and hedge funds over the past decade.
