# Sebastian Duesterhoeft

Sebastian Duesterhoeft is a venture-investor colleague of Kyle's — a former Index Ventures teammate who later appears at [Coatue](/notes/coatue) (and is listed among [Lightspeed Venture Partners](/notes/lightspeed-venture-partners) contacts in Kyle's outreach notes). He recurs as both a reference *on* Kyle and a source *for* Kyle's essay research. In the September 13th, 2022 — Harry Stebbings Podcast prep, Sebastian supplies reference-style answers about Kyle: his "tenacity & resourcefulness" ("he'll find a way to the heart of any founder" — cold-calling customers and candidates, running product AMAs on Twitter), his knack for building "special founder relationships" (founders inviting Kyle to invest personally even when his firm lost the deal), and a candid not-quite-weakness — Kyle "doesn't work as well in bigger, more rigid structures," where boundaries blunt his creativity. His favorite memory is Kyle first discovering [Roam Research](/notes/roam-research) and showing it internally ("this is friggin awesome"). Dave Yuan gives a separate reference call on Sebastian himself (January 11th, 2022 — Dave Yuan (Tidemark)).

As a research source, Sebastian's framings seed several of Kyle's essays. For [Cash—Kingmaker or Killer](/essays/cashkingmaker-or-killer), his "experiments where companies burn like crazy to win a market" line anchors the capital-abundance angle: "some companies are insanely efficient because they leverage OSS/PLG extremely well, but the abundance of capital has also led to a few 'experiments' where companies decided they were just going to burn like crazy to win a market." He is also quoted (at Coatue) in [The Productization of Venture Capital](/essays/productization) survey of VC platform differentiation, and appears in [Competitive Moats](/essays/competitive-moats) research and the January 24th, 2024 — Outreach for SF Trip list.
