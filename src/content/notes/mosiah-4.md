# Mosiah 4

Mosiah 4 is a recurring proof-text across Kyle's topical gospel studies, drawn on most often for its linked themes of **faith, humility, and growth in knowledge**. In the Faith — Topical Study, Kyle cites Mosiah 4:6–7 ("the goodness of God, and his matchless power, this is the man who receiveth salvation") to argue that faith follows from knowing who the Savior really is, and Mosiah 4:11–12 (to humble yourself and call "on the name of the Lord daily, ye shall grow in the knowledge of the glory of Him that created you") to frame faith as cultivated through daily devotion rather than logical proof.

The same verses anchor the Humility — Topical Study, where Mosiah 4:11–12 is grouped with [Mosiah 2](/notes/mosiah-2):34, [Alma 13](/notes/alma-13):10–12, and [D&C 20](/notes/dandc-20):30–31 as scriptures on "what is just and true" and the rendering of all one has to God. In The Lehonti Principle, Mosiah 4:30 is cited alongside [Dallin H. Oaks](/notes/dallin-h-oaks) on the chain from desires to thoughts to actions, tying the chapter to vigilance over one's inner life.

**Context:** Mosiah 4 in the Book of Mormon records King Benjamin's address to his people after they are overcome by the Spirit — on receiving a remission of sins, retaining it through humility and service, and watching one's thoughts, words, and deeds.

## Where this appears

- Faith — Topical Study — Mosiah 4:6–7 and 4:11–12 as the basis for faith growing out of love and daily devotion.
- Humility — Topical Study — Mosiah 4:11–12 grouped with other scriptures on rendering all to God and humbling oneself.
- The Lehonti Principle — Mosiah 4:30 on guarding desires, thoughts, and actions.
