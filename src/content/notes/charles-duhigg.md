# Charles Duhigg

Charles Duhigg is the author of *[The Power of Habit](/notes/the-power-of-habit)*, cited across two books in the wiki as the popularizer of the habit loop. In [Atomic Habits](/books/atomic-habits), [James Clear](/notes/james-clear) credits Duhigg with bringing the "cue, routine, reward" framing to a wide audience — a more recent popularization of what B. F. Skinner first proposed as "stimulus, response, reward" — which Clear then builds on with his own four-step model (cue, craving, response, reward). [Essentialism](/books/essentialism) quotes Duhigg directly on the mechanics of the loop: a cue triggers the brain into automatic mode, a routine is the behavior itself, and a reward tells the brain whether the habit is worth remembering, so that the loop becomes more automatic as cue and reward grow neurologically intertwined.

**Context:** Charles Duhigg is a Pulitzer Prize–winning journalist and author of *The Power of Habit* (2012) and *Smarter Faster Better*, known for translating behavioral-science research into popular frameworks.
