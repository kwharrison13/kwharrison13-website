# CalPERS

CalPERS — the California Public Employees' Retirement System, the ~$380B public pension fund — shows up in Kyle's notes both as a target LP and as a case study in manager selection. In the May 31st, 2018 — Jon Powers (CleanCapital) call, CalPERS is named as the kind of large institutional investor [CleanCapital](/notes/cleancapital) hoped to bring into its clean-energy projects (alongside Carval and John Hancock Energy).

The richer thread is the 2019 episode covered in [Almost $14 Billion Pulled From CalPERS Equity Managers](/notes/almost-14-billion-pulled-from-calpers-equity-managers): under CIO [Ben Meng](/notes/ben-meng), CalPERS terminated most of its external equity managers, cutting their allocation from $33.6 billion to $5.5 billion and reallocating toward internal index strategies, justified on long-term-underperformance grounds and a "renewed focus on performance and our ability to achieve our 7% assumed rate." CEO [Marcie Frost](/notes/marcie-frost) disclosed the move in an October letter to the board, quantifying it: "over the last five years, traditional managers have underperformed their benchmarks by 48 bps and emerging managers by 126 [bps]." CalPERS sits beside [UC Investments](/notes/uc-investments) as the companion case in Kyle's notes on California public-fund manager performance and emerging-manager / diversity policy ([Assembly Bill 2041 — UC Office of the Chief Investment Officer](/notes/assembly-bill-2041-uc-office-of-the-chief-investment-officer)).

**Context:** CalPERS is the largest public pension fund in the United States, managing retirement and health benefits for California state and local public employees, with assets on the order of $400–500B in recent years. Its scale makes it a closely watched LP whose allocation decisions move markets and set norms for public-pension investing.
