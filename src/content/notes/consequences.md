# Consequences

"Consequences" is tagged in [Charlie Munger — A Lesson on Elementary Worldly Wisdom, Revisited](/notes/charlie-munger-a-lesson-on-elementary-worldly-wisdom-revisited) as the operative term in the piece's summary of operant conditioning — one of the psychological models [Charlie Munger](/notes/charlie-munger) argues every thinker should hold. The definition Kyle flags: "A behavior is followed by a consequence, and the nature of the consequence modifies the organism's tendency to repeat the behavior in the future." In Munger's worldly-wisdom framework, consequences are the lever behind incentives — the mechanism by which rewards and punishments shape future behavior — which is why understanding them is foundational to predicting how people and systems actually act.

**Context:** In operant conditioning (B.F. Skinner), a consequence is the outcome that follows a behavior — reinforcement (which increases the behavior) or punishment (which decreases it). It is the core building block of incentive-driven behavior models.
