# Ginger

Ginger recurs across Kyle's [Behavioral Health](/notes/behavioral-health) diligence as one of the employer-benefit / EAP-replacement mental-health platforms, consistently grouped with [Lyra](/notes/lyra), [Spring Health](/notes/spring-health), and Modern Health (and contrasted against insurance-network marketplaces like [SonderMind](/notes/sondermind) / [Headway](/notes/headway) / [Alma](/notes/alma) and D2C teletherapy like [Talkspace](/notes/talkspace) / [BetterHelp](/notes/betterhelp)). In the October 4th, 2021 expert transcripts, Ginger and Lyra are characterized as the "1.0" generation relative to Modern Health's "2.0": Ginger reportedly had to pivot from measurement/monitoring into telepsychiatry, while Lyra locked into expensive high-license clinicians. Its margin advantage comes from stepped care (app → coach → therapist → MD), which lets it resolve cases at near-zero COGS.

The diligence repeatedly frames payer integration as Ginger's most credible path to scale — Kaiser's investment in Ginger is cited as the model, against the threat that a payer like UnitedHealthcare can onboard 200,000 covered lives overnight, something a startup takes a decade of BD to match. Ginger's clinicians are largely contract workers who multi-home (e.g., working Ginger and Modern Health simultaneously) at roughly half private-pay rates. By the 2020 covered-lives data, Ginger sat among the employer-market leaders (Lyra, Ginger, Spring, Modern Health) that all at least doubled covered lives that year.

**Context:** *Ginger was a virtual behavioral-health company offering on-demand coaching, therapy, and psychiatry; in 2021 it merged with meditation app Headspace to form Headspace Health, combining Ginger's clinical care with Headspace's consumer mindfulness product.*
