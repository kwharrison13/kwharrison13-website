# 1 Corinthians 5

Paul's chapter on discipline within the Corinthian church. In Kyle's Christ Defines His Gospel study it appears in a chain of grace-and-salvation proof texts (alongside [Galatians 2](/notes/galatians-2), [Acts 2](/notes/acts-2), [Romans 10](/notes/romans-10), and [Mark 13](/notes/mark-13)), cited at 5:5 for the idea that "the spirit may be saved at [the] 2nd coming" — that a person's spirit can yet be saved in the day of the Lord.

**Context:** 1 Corinthians 5 is the New Testament chapter where the Apostle Paul addresses a case of immorality in the Corinthian congregation, instructing the church to deliver the offender "unto Satan for the destruction of the flesh, that the spirit may be saved in the day of the Lord Jesus" (5:5).
