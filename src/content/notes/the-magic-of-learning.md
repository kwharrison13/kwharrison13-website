# The Magic of Learning

**Author:** [David Perell](/notes/david-perell)
**URL:** https://www.perell.com/blog/magic-of-learning
**One-line:** *A short meditation on [Learning](/notes/learning) for its own sake — that we only come to love learning once it stops being mandatory, that doubt and questions must precede truth, and that the drive to understand is what binds and fulfills us as a species.*

## Key claims

- **We learn to love learning only when it's no longer required.** In youth, learning is a chore; freed from grades and external approval, we come to love it for its intrinsic value — "learning for the sake of learning" ([Learning](/notes/learning)).
- **Doubt and questions come first.** "Doubt before truth. Questions come before answers." Answers can enter our minds only when questions make space for the journey of exploration. Quoting [Dieter F. Uchtdorf](/notes/dieter-f-uchtdorf): "First doubt your doubts before you doubt your faith."
- **Discovery is a unifying human drive.** "Our undirected quest to explore the universe, invent technology, and ask deep philosophical questions binds humanity." We are seekers on an infinite, iterative journey.
- **To grow the mind is to be human.** Intellectual endeavor binds us as a species and endows life with purpose — "learning is the evolving magic that fulfills us."

## Notable quotes

> "First doubt your doubts before you doubt your faith." — [Dieter F. Uchtdorf](/notes/dieter-f-uchtdorf)

> "And so, my fellow Americans: ask not what your country can do for you — ask what you can do for your country." — John F. Kennedy

> "To grow our mind is to be human… Learning is the evolving magic that fulfills us."
