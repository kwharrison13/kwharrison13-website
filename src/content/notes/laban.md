# Laban

Book of Mormon figure who appears in Kyle's The Process of the Book of Mormon notes as the embodiment of "opposition in all things." In the narrative pattern Kyle maps onto the early chapters, [Lehi](/notes/lehi) receives revelation to obtain the plates, and the resulting conflict — [Lehi](/notes/lehi) vs. [Laban](/notes/laban), paralleling [Nephi](/notes/nephi) vs. [Laman](/notes/laman) and [Lemuel](/notes/lemuel), and Jerusalem vs. Babylon — represents the obstacle stage in a recurring process of revelation, opposition, affliction, and deliverance.

**Context:** In the Book of Mormon, Laban is a powerful man in Jerusalem who keeps the brass plates (a record of scripture and genealogy) that Lehi's sons are commanded to retrieve. His refusal and the ensuing confrontation form one of the narrative's foundational episodes.
