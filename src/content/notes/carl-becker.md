# Carl Becker

Carl Becker appears in [American Sphinx](/books/american-sphinx) as the source of an epigraph-style judgment on Thomas Jefferson's thought. The book quotes Becker (1944): "Jefferson's ideas had also this felicity, and also perhaps a little too much of it. They come to birth a little too easily, and rest a little precariously on the aspirations and ideals of good men, and not sufficiently on the brute concrete facts of the world as it is." The line crystallizes the book's recurring tension — Jefferson's idealism and its uneasy contact with reality.

**Context:** Carl L. Becker (1873–1945) was an American historian known for *The Declaration of Independence: A Study in the History of Political Ideas* and *The Heavenly City of the Eighteenth-Century Philosophers*, a leading figure in the relativist and "intellectual history" currents of early-20th-century American historiography.

## Where this appears
