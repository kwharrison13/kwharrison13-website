# Lectures on Faith

The single text Kyle returns to most when he reasons about [Faith](/notes/faith). He leans on **Lecture 3** for its claim that exercising faith unto salvation requires three things: (1) that God exists, (2) "a correct idea of his character, perfections and attributes," and (3) "an actual knowledge that one's course of life accords with his will" (Faith — Topical Study, The First Principles of Faithful Pragmatism). That second requirement — a *correct idea of God's character* — is the load-bearing one for Kyle: it powers his whole God-as-Parent / Nature of God framework, where trusting God's will is only possible if you first understand who He is and that He is reliable.

In Kyle's reading the Lectures supply the structural backbone of The First Principles of Faithful Pragmatism, whose argument tracks the three requirements directly. He also pairs the text with "but if not…" faith — the resolve to serve God *whether or not* He delivers (Alma 13–16 — Enter into the Rest of the Lord): people mistake God's inaction for inability because they can't see the eternal picture, so confidence in His promises depends on knowing His character. The Lectures sit inside Kyle's belief → faith → [Perfect Knowledge](/notes/perfect-knowledge) ladder, where faith unto salvation is more than belief that something *can* happen.

**Context:** *Lectures on Faith* is a series of seven theological lectures on the doctrine of faith, prepared in the early 1830s for the School of the Prophets in Kirtland (associated with Joseph Smith) and published in the 1835 Doctrine and Covenants. Lecture 3 — the one Kyle cites — lays out the conditions necessary for faith unto life and salvation.
