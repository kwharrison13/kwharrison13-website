# Microsoft

Microsoft recurs across Kyle's corpus mostly as the **incumbent gorilla** — the deep-pocketed platform that either anchors a category or is the foil a startup must displace. In identity, it is the central antagonist: the Identity & Access Management Diligence — 2021 note treats Microsoft Active Directory as the dominant on-prem standard (90% user penetration, ~45% paid market share), the benchmark against which [Okta](/notes/okta)'s opportunity is measured, and the partner Microsoft offers SailPoint against "common enemies." [Todd McKinnon — Creating and Defining a New Market Category](/notes/todd-mckinnon-creating-and-defining-a-new-market-category) tells the same story from Okta's side: Active Directory as the incumbent Okta displaced, and Microsoft's 2014 "Okta killer" announcement that paradoxically *validated* the category ("the biggest software company in the world wants to do it — it must be pretty good").

Elsewhere Microsoft is the archetype of scale and resourcefulness. [The Start-Up of You](/books/the-start-up-of-you) invokes it as the incumbent whose lack of hustle "still counts billions in the bank," illustrating constraint-driven resourcefulness by contrast. [The Kill Chain](/books/the-kill-chain) names it among the top-five AI R&D spenders ($70.5B across the big five in 2018) that dwarf the entire defense industry. In climate, [How To Avoid a Climate Disaster](/books/how-to-avoid-a-climate-disaster) praises Microsoft's standalone research group "as an engine for the supply of innovations," and the Watershed Climate Diligence — 2021 note cites its 2030 net-zero pledge (ahead of Amazon's 2040). In code tooling, February 9th, 2022 — Reading Up on Replit calls Microsoft "the 800 lb gorilla in programming" via GitHub, VS Code, and Azure; [The Market Is The Greatest Critic](/notes/the-market-is-the-greatest-critic) lists it among the diversified platforms (alongside [Google](/notes/google), [Apple](/notes/apple), [Facebook](/notes/meta-company)) spared in the downturn. Microsoft also surfaces as biographical backdrop — where [Joel Kwartler](/notes/joel-kwartler) interned and discovered [Figma](/notes/figma) (June 6th, 2022 — Joel Kwartler (Sourcegraph)), and as a customer (NCX's 200K-ton carbon deal, December 13th, 2021 — NCX Partner Presentation).

**Context:** Microsoft Corporation, founded in 1975 by Bill Gates and Paul Allen, is one of the world's largest technology companies. Its business spans Windows and Office, the Azure cloud platform, GitHub and developer tools, Xbox, and (since 2016) LinkedIn, with a major position in AI through its partnership with OpenAI.
