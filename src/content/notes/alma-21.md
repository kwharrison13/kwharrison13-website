# Alma 21

A chapter in the Book of Mormon (book of Alma), studied in Kyle's [Come, Follow Me](/notes/come-follow-me) week on Alma 17–22 — I Will Make an Instrument of Thee. Kyle uses Alma 21 as the contrast to [Ammon](/notes/ammon)'s approach in his **[How To Begin Teaching](/notes/how-to-begin-teaching)** note: where Ammon enters as a servant before teaching, Aaron jumps immediately to calling people to repentance (Alma 21:5-6) and asks doctrinal questions of an audience he has already alienated (Alma 21:7-8). Kyle is careful to note this is illustrative, not a verdict on Aaron as a teacher — outcomes also turned on a more hardened audience. The chapter also grounds Kyle's note on **contention**: Aaron leaves a synagogue where they "contended with many about the word" (Alma 21:11), which Kyle pairs with [3 Nephi 11](/notes/3-nephi-11):29 ("he that hath the spirit of contention is not of me").

**Context:** Alma chapter 21 follows Aaron and his brethren preaching among the Amalekites and Amulonites, their rejection and imprisonment, and Aaron's eventual departure to preach elsewhere.
