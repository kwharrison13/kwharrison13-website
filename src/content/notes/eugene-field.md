# Eugene Field

A poet credited in [Chad's Big Quote Book](/notes/chad-s-big-quote-book) for "Little Boy Blue," the sentimental poem about a child's toys left waiting in the dust after his death ("they wonder, as waiting these long years through... what has become of our little Boy Blue / Since he kissed them and put them there").

**Context:** Eugene Field (1850–1895) was an American writer and poet, often called "the poet of childhood," best known for sentimental children's verse including "Little Boy Blue" and "Wynken, Blynken, and Nod."
