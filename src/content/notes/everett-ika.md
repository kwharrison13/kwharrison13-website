# Everett Ika

Everett Ika is an LDS missionary leader ("Elder Everett Ika") whom Kyle quotes in his gospel study on the nature of faith and the Lord's way of doing missionary work. The line Kyle preserves: **"When you start doubting, you're not trusting Heavenly Father. You can only CLAIM to have faith. Heavenly Father doesn't say no, he just promises us something better."** Kyle uses it to ground the charge to "do the Lord's work in his way. Doubt not, fear not," and immediately pairs it with the shepherd/sheepherder distinction — a sheepherder *drives* sheep forward, but a SHEPHERD *leads*, allowing them to follow.

The quote recurs across two studies. In Missionary Work — Topical Study it sits among the section on doing the Lord's work in his own way (Jeremiah 16:16's "fishers" and "hunters"), and in The Good Shepherd it appears in the summary alongside [Jake Smith](/notes/jake-smith)'s companion line ("a shepherd knows every sheep by name, but the sheep all know him by name as well"). Both studies list Everett Ika among the people cited.

## Where this appears

- Missionary Work — Topical Study — quoted on faith vs. doubt ("Heavenly Father doesn't say no, he just promises us something better") in the section on doing the Lord's work his way.
- The Good Shepherd — the same quote anchors the "doubt not, fear not" theme, paired with the shepherd-leads-vs-sheepherder-drives distinction.
