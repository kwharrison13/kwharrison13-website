# How To Begin Teaching

A teaching framework from [Preach My Gospel](/notes/preach-my-gospel) that Kyle traced through the example of [Ammon](/notes/ammon) in the [Alma 17](/notes/alma-17)–22 study. Reading Ammon's conversion of King Lamoni as a case study, Kyle pulled out the sequence the chapter models: **let silence work** (the king "answered him not for the space of an hour," Alma 18:14), **let the Spirit guide your questions** (Ammon "perceived the thoughts of the king," 18:16), **be relatable and set expectations** (18:17), give clear, concise answers (18:19), **ask for commitment** ("Wilt thou hearken unto my words," 18:22), **teach for understanding** by meeting people where they are (18:24-27), teach from the scriptures and the [Plan of Salvation](/notes/plan-of-salvation) (18:36), make it relevant to them (18:38, e.g. [Family History](/notes/family-history)), and **end with prayer** toward repentance (18:41). It connects directly to [Teach for Understanding](/notes/teach-for-understanding).

In Kyle's read of [Charlie Munger](/notes/charlie-munger)'s "Psychology of Human Misjudgment," he notes alongside Munger's material that "How To Begin Teaching in Preach My Gospel is meant to establish [Empathy](/notes/empathy) and understanding" — linking the missionary teaching pattern to the persuasion and human-nature themes Munger lays out.

**Context:** "How to Begin Teaching" is a section in *Preach My Gospel*, the missionary guide of The Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-day Saints, offering principles for opening a gospel discussion in a way that builds trust and understanding.
