# Tom Perkins

Tom Perkins is a co-founder of [Kleiner Perkins](/notes/kleiner-perkins) (1972) and, across Kyle's reading, the archetype of the activist, "entrepreneurs ourselves" venture capitalist. The line Kyle returns to most — quoted in both [The Power Law — Venture Capital & the Making of the New Future](/books/the-power-law-venture-capital-and-the-making-of-the-new-future) and [What Is An Investor](/essays/what-is-an-investor) — is Perkins's framing of the firm: *"We distinguished ourselves right from the beginning by saying: We are not investors. We're not Wall-Street, stock-picker, investor people. We are entrepreneurs ourselves, and we will work with entrepreneurs in an entrepreneurial way… We will be in it up to our elbows."* In What Is An Investor - Research this anchors the essay's "VCs who reject the label" thread, and [Ho Nam](/notes/ho-nam)'s own stance is noted as an echo of it.

[The Power Law — Venture Capital & the Making of the New Future](/books/the-power-law-venture-capital-and-the-making-of-the-new-future) paints Perkins as "a Ferrari-driving, yacht-owning, self-regarding dandy" — the first venture capitalist "to revel unashamedly in the role of promoter and front man." It credits him with [Perkins's law](/notes/perkins-s-law): *"market risk is inversely proportional to technical risk,"* because solving a truly hard technical problem leaves you facing minimal competition (drawn from the Tandem and [Genentech](/notes/genentech) bets). Mallaby uses Perkins, alongside Don Valentine, as evidence that great VCs "stamp their will" on portfolio companies rather than passively showing up. In [VC — An American History](/books/vc), Perkins is one of three archetypal figures — with Arthur Rock and Don Valentine — whose styles defined the industry; Perkins is the one who "emphasized investments in technology."

**Context:** Thomas J. Perkins (1932–2016) co-founded the venture firm Kleiner Perkins in 1972 and was an early backer of Genentech and Tandem Computers, among the most storied names in Silicon Valley venture history.
