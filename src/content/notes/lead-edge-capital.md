# Lead Edge Capital

Lead Edge Capital recurs in Kyle's investing-network notes as a growth-equity firm known for two distinctive traits: a disciplined, capital-efficiency-first investing checklist, and an LP base built from Fortune 500 CEOs and operators. In Networked Conviction — Roam + Investing - Research Kyle catalogues their "8 Criteria" alongside the deal screens of [Sequoia](/notes/sequoia), Future Ventures, BOND, and G Squared: $10M+ revenue growing 50%+/yr, 70%+ gross margins, recurring revenue, 90%+ gross retention, profitability/break-even, a diversified customer base, and above all **capital efficiency** — their stated preference for "companies like Duo Security that got to $200 million+ in revenue and burned about $40 million," on the logic that capital-efficient founders can "float on their burn rate" through a downturn.

The LP/network angle surfaces in the John Burke call, where Lead Edge's model of building relationships with Fortune 500 CEOs is cited as a precedent for what Merit Holdings and Contrary do with younger talent (likened to Village Global and On Deck). The firm is also [Zach Edwards](/notes/zach-edwards)'s affiliation in the March 2020 reference call about Kyle leaving Coatue for Teamshares — though the Zach Edwards page flags this may be a *different* Zach Edwards than the Clearstone/RBC contact (verify before merging).

**Context:** Lead Edge Capital is a growth-equity / late-stage investment firm founded in 2009 by Mitchell Green; it is notable for an LP base of roughly 700 entrepreneurs and executives who help with sourcing and due diligence, and for investments in companies like Alibaba, Spotify, Duo Security, and Bumble.
