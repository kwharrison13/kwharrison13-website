# BetterHelp

D2C teletherapy service that recurs across Kyle's [Behavioral Health](/notes/behavioral-health) diligence as the dominant consumer / cash-pay player, usually grouped with [Talkspace](/notes/talkspace) in the "telehealth/cash" bucket — highest brand recognition, low therapist payout, and (per multiple expert transcripts) "most hated by providers." It is owned by Teladoc, and the October 4th, 2021 — Behavioral Health Expert Transcripts paint it as the engine of Teladoc's growth: acquired in January 2015 for under $10M on roughly $500K–$1M of revenue, it grew to ~$300–350M by 2020 (~25% of Teladoc's total, ~70% of its top-line growth over four years), which one former executive called "the best deal of his 35-year career."

The transcripts give unusually granular unit economics: ~65% gross margins, profitable at scale after ~$100M ARR, CAC ~$250 against LTV ~3–4x CAC ($750–$1,000), English-speaking markets only, ~98% DTC with only thin B2B staff (focused on colleges/universities on a per-student-per-month basis). Experts noted its demand-first dynamic — latent consumer demand outpacing therapist supply — is the inverse of legacy Teladoc, and that BetterHelp is run completely independently (separate platform, zero interoperability) deliberately, to manage state-by-state telehealth regulatory risk. On the supply side, [Nicole Kleiman-Reck](/notes/nicole-kleiman-reck) (a Grow Therapy provider Kyle interviewed) was also an online therapist at BetterHelp, illustrating how providers multi-home across these platforms.

**Context:** BetterHelp is one of the largest online therapy platforms, matching consumers to licensed therapists for text, audio, and video sessions; it has been a subsidiary of Teladoc Health since 2015.
