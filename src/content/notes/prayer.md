# Prayer

The concept hub for prayer across Kyle's gospel study; the rich topical-journal deep-dive lives at Prayer — Topical Study (the suffix avoids a link-resolver collision with this stub). That study frames prayer as "the fastest way to open our hearts and understand in our hearts," cataloging *how* to pray — humbly, gratefully, with great desire and faith, avoiding vain repetitions, and joined with fasting — and names [Jesus Christ](/notes/jesus) as the perfect example ([John 17](/notes/john-17), [3 Nephi 19](/notes/3-nephi-19)). Its central question, "Answers to prayers: yours or His?", turns on the Spirit: a true answer motivates action, enlightens the mind, and gives peace, and "either you or the adversary can negate the mind or the heart, but no one can do both" — that double witness being the spirit of revelation. The aim is "not to pull heaven down to our will but to align our will with God's"; "God can educate our desires" ([Neal A. Maxwell](/notes/neal-a-maxwell)).

Related studies extend the theme. 3 Answers To Every Prayer reads the Brother of Jared's three problems in [Ether 2](/notes/ether-2)–[Ether 3](/notes/ether-3) — Breathing, Light, and Steering — as a pattern for the answers God gives. 5 P's Never To Forget makes Prayer the first of Kyle's five keys for studying the [Book of Mormon](/notes/book-of-mormon) ("written by men filled with the power of prophecy and revelation; we must pray for that power"). In the Come Follow Me study Alma 17–22, the sons of Mosiah "had given themselves to much prayer, and fasting," so they "taught with power and authority of God" — spiritual preparation preceding spiritual power. The theme also appears in How Do We Know Where To Protect Ourselves and the Alma 30–31 study.
