# Product

"Product" is a broad tagging hub in Kyle's notes — the umbrella under which his product-craft reading and the more specific [Product Management](/notes/product-management), [Playbook — Product](/notes/playbook-product), and [Best-in-Class Tech Stacks](/notes/best-in-class-tech-stacks) pages hang. The most grounded synthesis attached to it comes from [Product Led Growth — The End User Era](/books/product-led-growth-the-end-user-era), where the product itself becomes the center of gravity: "the first interaction with your company or product is typically through the product itself," sales reorganizes into [Customer Success](/notes/customer-success), and even the website and signup flow are treated as "part of the product." [Oh Snap, It's Here](/notes/oh-snap-it-s-here) supplies the consumer-side angle — the "product-vs-experience distinction" and what makes a consumer app feel indispensable.

The page also collects a "Quick Capture" reading log from Kyle's June 29th, 2021 Roam daily-note — a cluster of product-craft sources (Ken Norton's "bring the donuts" essays, Paul Graham's maker's-schedule and startup-ideas pieces, Joel Spolsky, *The Innovator's Solution*, *The Hard Thing About Hard Things*) saved to explore the discipline.
