# Basecamp

Basecamp (the project-management product and the company, formerly named after parent 37signals) recurs in Kyle's notes in two registers. First, as a tool: in [Remote — Office Not Required](/books/remote-office-not-required), by 37signals co-founders [Jason Fried](/notes/jason-fried) and [David Heinemeier Hansson](/notes/david-heinemeier-hansson), Basecamp is the running example of how remote accountability works — "central online repositories for tracking tasks and reporting progress, like Basecamp, create an irrefutable [record] showing what everyone is getting done and how long it's taking." It is the mechanism that lets remote work shift the question from "when did they get in?" to "show me what you did today."

Second, as a worldview: in [What Is An Extraordinary Man](/essays/what-is-an-extraordinary-man), Kyle holds up DHH (founder of Basecamp) as "the biggest champion" of the philosophy of putting your dent in the universe without needing to "fucking own the universe" — a decade-long critic of Silicon Valley's domination-and-grind ethos. Kyle cites the counter-argument that DHH "has made outstanding amounts of money and pays his employees super well and provides an amazing balanced life," using Basecamp as the proof case that calm, profitable, bootstrapped company-building is a legitimate alternative to growth-at-all-costs.

**Context:** Basecamp is a web-based project-management and team-communication tool first launched by 37signals in 2004; the company later renamed itself Basecamp before reverting to 37signals, and is known for its bootstrapped, anti-hypergrowth stance. This is general background.

## Where this appears

- [Remote — Office Not Required](/books/remote-office-not-required) — Basecamp is the example of a task/progress repository that makes remote work visible and "irrefutable."
- [What Is An Extraordinary Man](/essays/what-is-an-extraordinary-man) — DHH's Basecamp held up as the model of "put your dent in the universe" company-building over domination.
