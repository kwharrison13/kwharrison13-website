# Heber C. Kimball

An early Latter-day Saint leader who appears in two of Kyle's sources as a witness to [Joseph Smith](/notes/joseph-smith)'s inner life and as a case in Joseph's own spiritual growth. In [The Priesthood Power of Women](/books/the-priesthood-power-of-women), Kimball is quoted on Joseph's deepest frustration: that "the greatest torment [the Prophet Joseph] had... was because this people would not live up to their privileges," that Joseph "felt as though he were pent up in an acorn shell" because the Saints "would not prepare themselves to receive the rich treasures of wisdom and knowledge that he had to impart." The quote anchors that book's argument about higher knowledge the people lacked the diligence to receive.

In [Joseph Smith Lecture 2 — Joseph's Personality and Character](/notes/joseph-smith-lecture-2-joseph-s-personality-and-character), Kimball appears in a different light — as an illustration that early diligence is not destiny. The lecture notes that at the Kirtland Hebrew school taught by Joshua Seixas, Kimball was "the worst" student, and that though he "saw himself as 'illiterate and unlearned, weak and feeble,'" he "became one of the most powerful and fruitful missionaries in modern history."

**Context:** Heber C. Kimball (1801–1868) was an apostle of The Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-day Saints, a member of the original Quorum of the Twelve, a leader of the early British mission, and first counselor to Brigham Young in the First Presidency.

## Where this appears

- [The Priesthood Power of Women](/books/the-priesthood-power-of-women) — quoted on Joseph Smith's anguish that the Saints would not prepare to receive the higher knowledge he had to impart.
- [Joseph Smith Lecture 2 — Joseph's Personality and Character](/notes/joseph-smith-lecture-2-joseph-s-personality-and-character) — cited as the worst student in the Kirtland Hebrew school who nonetheless became one of the most fruitful missionaries in modern history.
