# Gratitude

In Kyle's corpus, gratitude recurs as the antidote to perfectionism and worry. In [The Magnolia Story](/books/the-magnolia-story) it is framed as Chip and Joanna Gaines's *"thankfulness and contentment"* — the book's closing argument against perfectionism: *"it's up to us to choose contentment and thankfulness now—and to stop imagining that we have to have everything perfect before we'll be happy."* The same posture runs through the book's "what's the point in worrying?" attitude.

The theme also surfaces in Kyle's mission-era gospel study, where gratitude appears as one of the [Christlike Attributes](/notes/christlike-attributes) worth carrying home — listed in 10 Gifts To Take Home From Your Mission among virtue, humility, and missionary work as a core theme.

## Where this appears

- [The Magnolia Story](/books/the-magnolia-story) — "thankfulness and contentment" as the closing argument against perfectionism; choosing contentment now rather than waiting for everything to be perfect.
- 10 Gifts To Take Home From Your Mission — gratitude tagged as one of the core Christlike attributes a missionary brings home.
- [Berkshire Hathaway Annual Letters](/books/berkshire-hathaway-annual-letters) — original `#gratitude` hashtag source.
