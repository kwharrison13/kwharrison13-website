# PayScale

Legacy compensation-data and management company that recurs in Kyle's [Pave](/notes/pave) diligence as an incumbent competitor. In the Pave Diligence — 2021 dossier, PayScale is grouped with [Shareworks](/notes/shareworks) as the legacy players in comp planning (versus earlier-stage startups like [Assemble](/notes/assemble) and [Welcome](/notes/welcome-comp-planning)), with the diligence narrative arguing customers are "super critical of the incumbents" and that Pave has started to break away — the only viewed real threat being [Carta](/notes/carta). The notes capture PayScale's profile (founded 2002, two-portal give-to-get model, ~8,000 subscribing companies, data from ~60 million users, a 2019 raise at a $325M valuation) and former CFO [Mark Klebanoff](/notes/mark-klebanoff), alongside a damning customer quote that PayScale's data was "absolutely garbage… nowhere near accurate for most jobs."

PayScale also appears as a competitor in two customer expert calls: [Abhishek Mahajan](/notes/abhishek-mahajan) at Flipkart evaluated PayScale but found it "only handling bits and pieces of the process" and "definitely not helping you benchmark to other companies" — one of several incumbents ([Zenefits](/notes/zenefits), Cass Information Systems) weighed before choosing Pave.

**Context:** PayScale is a Seattle-based compensation-data and software company founded in 2002, providing salary benchmarking and compensation-management tools built on a large crowdsourced pay dataset.

## Where this appears

- Pave Diligence — 2021 — positioned as a legacy incumbent (with [Shareworks](/notes/shareworks)) that Pave is displacing; profile and "data is garbage" customer critique captured.
- October 4th, 2021 — Abhishek Mahajan (Flipkart) on Pave — PayScale evaluated but found to handle only "bits and pieces" without cross-company benchmarking.
- [Abhishek Mahajan](/notes/abhishek-mahajan) — one of the comp tools ([PayScale](/notes/payscale), [Zenefits](/notes/zenefits), Cass) Flipkart evaluated before selecting Pave.
