# Intercession of Christ

A gospel-study theme Kyle traces through 3 Nephi, focused on the idea that the Father acts toward His people *for Christ's sake* — Christ as our advocate with the Father. He gathers the recurring "[For My Sake](/notes/for-my-sake)" phrasing: the Father will work "a great and a marvelous work… for my sake" ([3 Nephi 21](/notes/3-nephi-21):9, linked to the [Marvelous Work](/notes/marvelous-work) theme), and "ye shall call upon the Father in my name that he will bless the church for my sake" ([3 Nephi 27](/notes/3-nephi-27):7). In his [3 Nephi 27](/notes/3-nephi-27) notes Kyle states the principle plainly: "The things that Heavenly Father do for us he does because of Jesus Christ; he is our advocate with the Father" — "if ye call upon the Father, for the church, if it be in my name the Father will hear you" (27:9). He connects it to [John 14](/notes/john-14):16 (the Father giving another Comforter) and flags it as a [Gospel Topics to Explore](/notes/gospel-topics-to-explore).

## Where this appears

- October 19–25, 2020. 3 Nephi 27–4 Nephi: "There Could Not Be a Happier People" — states the core principle: the Father acts for us because of Christ, our advocate (3 Nephi 27:9)
