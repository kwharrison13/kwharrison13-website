# Disney

Disney is a recurring investment thesis and case study across Kyle's corpus, approached through several lenses. As a personal thesis: in [Networked Conviction — Roam + Investing](/essays/networked-conviction-roam-investing) Kyle describes building a [Disney ($DIS)](/notes/disney) thesis by reading [Walt Disney and the Promise of Progress City](/books/walt-disney-and-the-promise-of-progress-city) — a book about Walt's EPCOT city-building vision — and capturing the key takeaways in Roam. His conviction note on that book is pointed: "[Disney ($DIS)](/notes/disney) needs a [Walt Disney](/notes/walt-disney)-caliber CEO, and then they need to partner with [Tesla ($TSLA)](/notes/tesla). I used to say Disney should buy Tesla — but maybe Tesla should buy Disney."

As a business model, Disney appears as the canonical "hits business" and brand story. The The Hits Business - Research notes hold up Disney ("the House of Mouse") as the exemplar [A24](/notes/a24) is compared to — a studio that extends its consumer relationship "beyond the screen" into TV, music, publishing, physical experiences, with A24 framed as "a redux of Walt's strategy designed for the 21st century." In [The Greatest Thing Since Sliced Bread](/notes/the-greatest-thing-since-sliced-bread), Disney is one of the three canonical brands (with [Apple ($AAPL)](/notes/apple) and [Nike](/notes/nike)) that sell a feeling rather than a feature — Disney makes us feel "happy and like a kid again."

Disney is also a textbook value-investing example: the [Charlie Munger — A Lesson on Elementary Worldly Wisdom, Revisited](/notes/charlie-munger-a-lesson-on-elementary-worldly-wisdom-revisited) notes cite Disney as "an amazing example of autocatalysis" — sitting on a library of owned-copyright films in the can, then reaping "billions of dollars worth of tail wind" when the videocassette let parents and grandparents replay them at home for free.

**Context:** The Walt Disney Company is a diversified media and entertainment conglomerate founded in 1923, spanning film studios, theme parks, streaming (Disney+), and a deep library of owned IP. Its franchise-and-library model is a frequent reference point in discussions of durable brands and content economics.
