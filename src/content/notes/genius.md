# Genius

In Kyle's corpus "genius" is almost always invoked to be **dethroned in favor of perseverance and broad learning**. The clearest statement is in [John Quincy Adams](/books/john-quincy-adams): cured by Harvard and a stalling career "of the delusion of his own genius," Adams resolved he "would depend not on genius, but on perseverance." Kyle pairs that highlight directly with the [Gordon B. Hinckley — Go Forward With Faith](/books/gordon-b-hinckley-go-forward-with-faith) family creed: "We do not belong to a family of geniuses… Each of us must pay the price of hard work if we achieve" — and Hinckley's own warning that "the penitentiary [is] full of geniuses who were too smart for their own good." The two books form a deliberate cross-reference: native brilliance is a temptation; honest, persistent work is the real engine.

A second, more admiring sense appears in the Hinckley biography and recurs in the [Charlie Munger — USC Gould School of Law Commencement Address](/notes/charlie-munger-usc-gould-school-of-law-commencement-address) notes and the Books 2.0 - Research doc: Hinckley as a "multifaceted genius" — a [Polymath](/notes/polymath) with "an insatiable appetite for [Learning](/notes/learning)" who could speak the specialized language of physicians, lawyers, builders, and police, "grasps things quickly, has an amazing breadth of knowledge, and can apply what he knows." Here genius is not innate spark but accumulated, applied breadth — closer to Munger's latticework-of-models ideal than to romantic inspiration. [The Geography of Genius](/notes/the-geography-of-genius) (anti-library) extends the theme outward, asking why genius clusters in particular places and eras rather than particular people.

## Where this appears

- [John Quincy Adams](/books/john-quincy-adams) — Adams renounces "the delusion of his own genius," choosing perseverance; tagged with the Hinckley hard-work passage
- [Gordon B. Hinckley — Go Forward With Faith](/books/gordon-b-hinckley-go-forward-with-faith) — "no royalty like the royalty of high endeavor," and Hinckley as a "multifaceted genius" of applied breadth
- [Charlie Munger — USC Gould School of Law Commencement Address](/notes/charlie-munger-usc-gould-school-of-law-commencement-address) — quotes the "multifaceted genius" / breadth-of-knowledge portrait of Hinckley
- [The Geography of Genius](/notes/the-geography-of-genius) — anti-library book on why genius clusters by place and era, not person
