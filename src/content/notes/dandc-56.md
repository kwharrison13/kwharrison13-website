# D&C 56

Section 56 of the [Doctrine and Covenants](/notes/doctrine-and-covenants) anchors a key point in Kyle's The Good Shepherd study. Verses 26–28 — "it is not meet that I should command in all things; for he that is compelled in all things, the same is a slothful and not a wise servant" — are read as the scriptural basis for doing the Lord's work in the Lord's way: a sheepherder *drives* sheep forward, but a SHEPHERD *leads* and allows them to follow. The notes use the section to distinguish leadership by love from leadership by compulsion, reinforcing the broader argument that God and Christ are "shepherds, not sheepherders" ([Neal A. Maxwell](/notes/neal-a-maxwell)).

**Context:** Doctrine and Covenants Section 56 is a revelation given to Joseph Smith in June 1831 in Kirtland, Ohio. It addresses disobedience among several elders and contains the well-known teaching against being "compelled in all things," counseling that the willing and wise servant acts from agency rather than coercion.

## Where this appears

- The Good Shepherd — verses 26–28 cited for the shepherd-vs-sheepherder distinction: leading by love, not compelling.
