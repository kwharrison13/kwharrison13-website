# Free Knowledge and Innovation

"Free Knowledge and Innovation" is [Matt Clancy](/notes/matt-clancy)'s signature Substack essay and the source for Kyle's claim that access to knowledge drives innovation. Its thesis, quoted directly on the Clancy page: "If you want to encourage more innovation, give people better access to knowledge." Clancy, an innovation economist working in the [Progress Studies](/notes/progress-studies) tradition, synthesizes empirical evidence that new access to knowledge produces measurable downstream effects — citing Berkes & Nencka on the effect of public libraries and Thompson & Hanley on Wikipedia, and pointing to patent depositories as another access channel. The common pattern: when knowledge becomes more freely available, patenting and citation activity measurably rise.

The essay anchors the [Open-Source Knowledge](/essays/open-source-knowledge) concept in Kyle's wiki — it is the access-drives-innovation evidence base that underpins arguments connecting [Knowledge](/notes/knowledge), [Innovation](/notes/innovation), and open-access institutions.
