# James Traub

Author of [John Quincy Adams](/books/john-quincy-adams) (subtitle: *Militant Spirit*), the biography Kyle read. Kyle's notes credit Traub with two interpretive throughlines. First, on Adams's lifelong [Depression](/notes/depression) — the "blue devils" he battled from college onward — Traub traces how Adams "summoned the will to become the person his parents had told him he must be," carrying the crushing weight of [expectations](/notes/expectations) that his brothers and sons did not survive. Second, Traub names Adams the "fountainhead" of realist foreign policy — skeptical of America's capacity to do good abroad — a [Realism](/notes/realism) thread Kyle pairs with [Henry Kissinger](/notes/henry-kissinger)'s observation that Americans won't accept a self-described "realist" president.

**Context:** James Traub is an American journalist and author, a longtime contributor to *Foreign Policy* and *The New York Times Magazine*, who has written biographies and books on foreign affairs; *John Quincy Adams: Militant Spirit* was published in 2016.
