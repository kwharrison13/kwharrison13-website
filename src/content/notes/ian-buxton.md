# Ian Buxton

Ian Buxton is a senior security leader at [Vail Resorts](/notes/vail-resorts) (21 years in security and privacy, starting at a Big Four consulting firm, then rising through Vail's security org from SOX compliance). He appears in Kyle's notes as one of the eight reference calls in the [Noname Security](/notes/noname-security) diligence (November 29, 2021) — the buyer-side voice on the [API Security](/notes/api-security) landscape. His framing is that [Application Security](/notes/application-security) has grown complex as APIs became central, and that a good API-security program needs both prevention (secure code, an API inventory) and detection plus *prioritization* — because big organizations have limited resources and "just knowing a risk exists doesn't necessarily fix it."

On vendor selection, Buxton's team was running a Noname POC / risk-assessment to gather data before committing, and praised Noname as far more flexible than competitors — willing to do a professional-services "risk assessment" to build a business case rather than a "take it or leave it" sale. He compared it against Imperva (established but seemed less user-friendly in discovery calls) and [Salt Security](/notes/salt-security) (a good-enough solution they lost contact with after client-management turnover). His selection criteria were ease of management ("asking for a new tool is also asking for new headcount"), ease of integration with their existing F5/WAF stack, and performance during peak web cycles. He also supplies the recurring analogy Kyle files under [CrowdStrike](/notes/crowdstrike): API security "feels like Endpoint Security 5-6 years ago," and the winning move is packaging indicators into an actionable report rather than dumping raw signal.

**Context:** Vail Resorts (NYSE: MTN) is a mountain-resort and hospitality operator; Buxton's title in the notes is Senior Director of Information Security, Risk, Compliance & Privacy. (General role context; the substance below is from the diligence call.)
