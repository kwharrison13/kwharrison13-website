# Nephi

Nephi is the opening prophet-narrator of the Book of Mormon and a recurring anchor across Kyle's gospel study. In The Process of the Book of Mormon, Nephi is the hinge of the book's first 60 pages: the whole gospel pattern — [Lehi](/notes/lehi)'s vision, revelation, opposition, restoration, and the [Atonement](/notes/atonement) — turns on the word "**NEVERTHELESS**," because Nephi "drank the bitter cup without *becoming* bitter," turning toward God in affliction rather than away. His plain statement of the [Doctrine of Christ](/notes/doctrine-of-christ) in 2 Nephi 31 — faith in Christ, repentance, baptism, the gift of the Holy Ghost, and enduring to the end — is the structural spine of [M. Russell Ballard](/notes/m-russell-ballard)'s devotional Follow the Doctrine and Gospel of Christ, cited as the model of "plainness."

Nephi also recurs as an exemplar of *how to teach and testify*. In Teaching Repentance he stands (alongside [Ammon](/notes/ammon)) as a master teacher of "sweet, inviting boldness." In Moroni's Exhortations, Moroni's charge to ask God whether "these things" are true echoes Nephi's stated purpose in writing — to show God's "tender mercies" — and his model of faithful humility: "I know that [God] loveth His children; nevertheless, I do not know the meaning of all things" (1 Nephi 11). And in The Candle of the Lord, Nephi supplies the key texts on how the Spirit communicates: angels "speak the words of Christ," so we must "feast upon the words of Christ" (2 Nephi 32), while Laman and Lemuel were "past *feeling*" his words (1 Nephi 17).

**Context:** Nephi is the son of the prophet Lehi and the principal author of the first two books of the Book of Mormon (1 and 2 Nephi), a foundational text of the Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-day Saints. The name is also borne by later figures, including Nephi son of Helaman, whom the Teaching Repentance notes reference separately.

## Where this appears

- Follow the Doctrine and Gospel of Christ — M. Russell Ballard — Nephi's 2 Nephi 31 statement of the [Doctrine of Christ](/notes/doctrine-of-christ) is the structural spine of Ballard's "keep it simple" devotional.
- The Process of the Book of Mormon — Nephi as the "NEVERTHELESS" hinge: drinking the bitter cup without becoming bitter.
- Teaching Repentance — Nephi (son of Helaman) as a master teacher of "sweet boldness," alongside [Ammon](/notes/ammon).
- Moroni's Exhortations — Moroni's "ask God" charge echoing Nephi's purpose of showing God's "tender mercies."
- The Candle of the Lord — Boyd K. Packer — Nephi's texts on feasting upon the words of Christ and Laman/Lemuel being "past feeling."
