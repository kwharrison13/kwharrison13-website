# Hyrum Smith

Older brother of [Joseph Smith](/notes/joseph-smith) and a recurring figure in Kyle's church-history notes, most often invoked as a foil that sharpens who Joseph was. Across [Truman G. Madsen](/notes/truman-g-madsen)'s Joseph Smith lectures, Hyrum is the steady, "sedate, sober, serious" Smith — the one converts expected "a prophet should look like" — set against Joseph's playful, "hail-fellow-well-met" temperament; some converts who wanted Hyrum-like gravity left the Church over Joseph's frolicking. [Brigham Young](/notes/brigham-young) is quoted distinguishing the two: Hyrum's "integrity was of the highest order, but his ability was not equal to Joseph's… Hyrum was a positive man; Joseph was a comparative man." His loyalty is the keynote — in the [first-vision lecture](/notes/joseph-smith-lecture-2-joseph-s-personality-and-character) notes, "William's instability is set against Hyrum's unyielding loyalty," and the Smith family's "total familial endurance" held the early Church together.

Hyrum also appears as a witness to faith and to martyrdom. In Kyle's General Conference- October 2021 notes, Brent H. Nielsen tells the story (via [Truman G. Madsen](/notes/truman-g-madsen)) of Hyrum being one in a circle of brethren who each tried to bless someone "until finally he was able to" — Kyle flags it as faith "as a skill, not just a binary." In [The Life of John Taylor](/books/the-life-of-john-taylor), Hyrum is martyred alongside Joseph at Carthage; the morning of his death he read and turned down the leaf at Ether 12, "a silent witness that he, too, knew he was going 'like a lamb to the slaughter.'"

**Context:** Hyrum Smith (1800–1844) was the elder brother of Joseph Smith, an early leader and Patriarch of The Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-day Saints, and was killed with Joseph by a mob at Carthage Jail, Illinois.
