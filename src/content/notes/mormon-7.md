# Mormon 7

[Mormon](/notes/mormon)'s final exhortation, addressed directly to a future remnant — studied in Kyle's Come Follow Me notes on [Mormon 7](/notes/mormon-7)–[Mormon 9](/notes/mormon-9). Kyle organizes the chapter around its anaphora of *"Know ye"* (7:2–5): know ye that ye are of the house of Israel, that ye must come to repentance, that ye must lay down your weapons of war, that ye must believe in Jesus Christ. He glosses "Know ye" as *"You know better"* — Mormon speaking out of years of watching his people fail to repent.

The hinge is verse 8's *"Therefore repent"*, which Kyle ties to his recurring [Therefore, What?](/notes/therefore-what) frame: [Boyd K. Packer](/notes/boyd-k-packer)'s habit of asking "Therefore, what?" after any exhortation, quoted via [Jeffrey R. Holland](/notes/jeffrey-r-holland) — the idea that teaching is "to no avail if the actual lives of His disciples did not change." The chapter closes on a minimal creed (7:9): if you can't believe anything else, believe in Christ; believing "that" leads to believing "this also."
