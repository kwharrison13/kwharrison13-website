# Provider Enablement

The business-model category for platforms that take the back-office burden off an individual healthcare provider — marketing, credentialing, billing, EHR, consent, scheduling — in exchange for a cut of each session. The defining example in Kyle's notes is [Grow Therapy](/notes/grow-therapy), surfaced through an [Index Ventures](/notes/index-ventures) [Behavioral Health](/notes/behavioral-health) diligence call with a Grow contractor. Grow is described as a "one-stop-shop" / "lead provider" platform for mental-health therapists: it handles insurance contracting, billing, consent, EHR, and scheduling, and even pays for the provider's [Psychology Today](/notes/psychology-today) profile, supplying a steady flow of insurance-based clients so the therapist never has to advertise.

The economics run on a per-session take rather than a subscription: the therapist "pays Grow nothing," and Grow earns roughly 5–20% of a typical $125–150 insurance-paid session, with the provider netting ~$70/hour. A structural feature is that **credentialing follows the provider** even if they leave — which seeds a disintermediation dynamic: once a therapist builds a cash-pay book, the platform's value narrows to insurance handling and calendar control. Comparable provider-enablement platforms named in the same screen include [SonderMind](/notes/sondermind), [Headway](/notes/headway), [Spring Health](/notes/spring-health), Alma, Nirvana, Lyra, and Modern Health.

## Where this appears

- [Grow Therapy](/notes/grow-therapy) — the canonical provider-enablement platform: marketing + credentialing + billing + EHR for therapists, monetized as a per-session take rate.
