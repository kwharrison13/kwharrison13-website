# Moroni 5

The Book of Mormon chapter recording the sacramental prayer on the wine (water) — the companion to [Moroni 4](/notes/moroni-4)'s blessing on the bread. In Kyle's 3 Answers To Every Prayer topical study, [Moroni 4](/notes/moroni-4) and Moroni 5 are read together as a "two-part symbolism of physical and spiritual sacrifice, death, and Atonement": the bread where we witness we are *willing* to take Christ's name, and the water (Moroni 5:2) where we affirm we *do* always remember Him. Kyle draws the contrast sharply — in the blessing on the bread the willingness is what we promise; in the blessing on the water the promise becomes active and ongoing, that we "must actually and actively remember Christ and keep his commandments in order to have the spirit." He reads the bread as physical sacrifice/suffering → physical death → the body, and the water as spiritual sacrifice/suffering → spiritual death → [Atonement](/notes/atonement).

The chapter also appears in the Come Follow Me study November 30–December 6, 2020. Moroni 1–6: "To Keep Them in the Right Way", where Moroni records "the plain things" of the Church — ordinations, the sacrament prayers, [Baptism](/notes/baptism), record-keeping — because these are what "keep them in the right way" ([Moroni 6](/notes/moroni-6):4), the small structural things he sets down while writing alone after the destruction of his nation.

**Context:** Moroni 5 is the final chapter of the Book of Mormon's penultimate book, a single verse giving the prescribed prayer for blessing the sacramental wine. With [Moroni 4](/notes/moroni-4) (the bread) it provides the wording Latter-day Saints use weekly in the ordinance of the sacrament.

## Where this appears

- 3 Answers To Every Prayer — paired with [Moroni 4](/notes/moroni-4) as the two-part sacrament symbolism (water = spiritual sacrifice, the active "do always remember him").
- November 30–December 6, 2020. Moroni 1–6: "To Keep Them in the Right Way" — Moroni recording the sacrament prayers among the plain, structural things that keep the people "in the right way."
