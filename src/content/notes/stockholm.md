# Stockholm

In [Walt Disney and the Promise of Progress City](/books/walt-disney-and-the-promise-of-progress-city), Stockholm is one of [Walt Disney](/notes/walt-disney)'s key real-world models for EPCOT. Per Disney Legend Ray Watson, Walt was a big fan of the city and kept a booklet about it in his office. Between 1950 and 1970, Stockholm's planners modernized the city by building four new satellite towns, each with its own high-density center clustered around a transit node and a mix of shopping, commercial, and residential use. These functional "pods" connected to the town center by a radial subway system and highways — a design the book argues Walt clearly borrowed for EPCOT's pod-and-transit layout. The book uses it as a prime example of Walt's habit of synthesizing the best of others' ideas into something new: "Expose yourself to the best ideas available."

**Context:** Stockholm's post-war "ABC towns" (named for the Swedish words for work, housing, and center) — Vällingby and Farsta the best known — were internationally studied models of transit-oriented satellite development, built around the city's expanding metro (Tunnelbana).
