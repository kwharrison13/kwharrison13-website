# Alan Kay

Alan Kay is the pioneering computer scientist whose aphorisms recur across Kyle's notes as compact statements about perspective, prediction, and creation. In [The Everything Store](/books/the-everything-store) (and Perell's [synthesis of it](/notes/amazon-the-everything-store-article)), Kay is the thinker [Jeff Bezos](/notes/jeff-bezos) "revered" and "often quoted" — most of all **"point of view is worth 80 IQ points"** (a reminder that new ways of looking enhance understanding) and **"it's easier to invent the future than to predict it"** (the maxim Kyle marks as "the quote that drove Bezos").

The "perspective is worth 80 IQ points" line reappears as the anchor of [How to Maximize Creativity](/notes/how-to-maximize-creativity), where Kay frames how absorbing new experiences shifts perception "like fragments of colored glass in a kaleidoscope" — creativity cultivated through recombination, not forced thinking. Kay also surfaces in a different, technical register in Kyle's February 9th, 2022 — Reading Up on Replit notes: [Replit](/notes/replit) is described as realizing "Alan Kay's original vision for object-oriented programming — a biological-like system of live machines communicating via message-passing," with Replit as "a network of live code" against GitHub's "network of dead code."

**Context:** Alan Kay is an American computer scientist and Turing Award winner, a key figure at Xerox PARC, known for pioneering object-oriented programming (Smalltalk), graphical user interfaces, and the Dynabook concept that prefigured the personal computer and tablet.
