# SendGrid

SendGrid recurs across Kyle's notes as a canonical example of a developer-first, product-led-growth (PLG) email-API business. [Product Led Growth — The End User Era](/books/product-led-growth-the-end-user-era) uses SendGrid as a primary case study for how PLG reshapes the org chart and budget: because ~98% of SendGrid's customers came in through a self-service engine, the company viewed "the website and signup flow as part of the product," freed spend from sales to put 25%+ of revenue into R&D (vs. 15% at [CA](/notes/ca)), ran a 6:1 engineer-to-PM ratio (vs. 20:1 at CA), and built a dedicated [Pricing](/notes/pricing) function staffed by professionals "whose job it is to think about pricing all day every day." (The author drew directly on operating experience across CA and SendGrid.)

Elsewhere SendGrid is invoked as a developer-distribution archetype. The Identity & Access Management Diligence — 2021 dossier groups SendGrid with [Twilio](/notes/twilio), [Stripe](/notes/stripe), and [PagerDuty](/notes/pagerduty) as proof that "developers are the new kingmakers." Riley Hughes of [Trinsic](/notes/trinsic-id) modeled a $22/month usage-throttled pricing tier "after [Twilio ($TWLO)](/notes/twilio) or SendGrid." And in the November 7th, 2022 — Cleared Email note, SendGrid appears as the prior transactional-email vendor a company migrated away from (to Customer.io) for a better email experience.

**Context:** SendGrid is a cloud-based transactional and marketing email delivery API for developers, founded in 2009, IPO'd in 2017, and acquired by Twilio in 2019.
