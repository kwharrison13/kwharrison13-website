# Bradley Miles

Author of [Breaking into VC](/notes/breaking-into-vc), former [CoinDesk](/notes/coindesk) research lead, and founder of a crypto-native creator-economy startup ([FanCoin](/notes/fancoin)). His throughline, in Kyle's May 2018 conversation, is rejecting the "permission-based" economy — "most people wait for a banker to say 'yeah, you can do banking' before they pursue it" — in favor of becoming a recognized expert through a "very research-driven thesis." He coached people not to ask "can I be a VC?" but to become so good at and known for a specific domain that they get hired.

After the book, Miles leaned on his bitcoin/cryptocurrency expertise to lead research for [CoinDesk](/notes/coindesk), publishing ~100-page quarterly "State of Blockchain" reports that made him known in the space. Because writing the book had him talking to many VCs, his crypto knowledge became valuable — he had offers to start a fund or join earlier funds as a partner — but he "really wanted to build something." He got close to a managing partner at [Techstars](/notes/techstars), became an EIR, and raised capital "off their expertise" for a stealth venture, initially framed as a Goldman-Sachs-style bank to help businesses create blockchain-based protocols (advising on ICOs, security, game theory, mechanism design).

That stealth work converged on [FanCoin](/notes/fancoin) and the "engagement economy" — the relationship between creators/artists and their fans. The thesis: fans crave access artists find hard to monetize; a network where creators issue their own fan currencies lets the artist "be the network and unlock the value of their own engagement" (rather than using Facebook to engage fans for free). After cycling through coin designs, the play settled on an underlying base currency that can issue creator-based currencies plus provide an exchange, with a first cohort of 3–5 creators ([Creator Economy](/notes/creator-economy), [Crypto](/notes/crypto)). His recurring instinct: "turning complicated things — VC, crypto — into something understandable."
