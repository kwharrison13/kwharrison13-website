# Paul the Apostle

Paul the Apostle surfaces in two of Kyle's Come Follow Me studies as a scriptural touchstone. In the August 24–30, 2020. Helaman 7–12: "Remember the Lord" study, Kyle is working through an "interesting example of people not believing, seeing, and then believing" (the crowd at the judgment-seat) and asks whether there are other examples of seeing pushing people to believe — listing [Alma the Younger](/notes/alma-the-younger) and Paul the Apostle as candidate cases ("#Gospel Topics to Explore"). Paul's Damascus-road conversion is the implicit parallel: a witness that turns disbelief into faith.

In the December 31–January 6, 2019. "We Are Responsible for Our Own Learning" study, Paul appears through Acts 17:10–12, where Paul and Silas go to Berea and the Bereans are praised as "more noble" for receiving the word "with all readiness of mind, and searched the scriptures daily." Here Paul anchors the week's theme of personal responsibility for spiritual learning. Both appearances treat him as an exemplar — of conversion and of teaching people to search out truth for themselves.

**Context:** Paul the Apostle (originally Saul of Tarsus) was a first-century missionary and author of many New Testament epistles, known for his conversion on the road to Damascus and his missionary journeys across the Mediterranean.
