# Ben Horowitz

Co-founder (with [Marc Andreessen](/notes/marc-andreessen)) of the venture firm [a16z](/notes/a16z) and a recurring source of operating maxims across Kyle's corpus. Two quotes do most of the work. First, on [Pricing](/notes/pricing): **"There is no single decision you will make that will impact your company value more than the pricing"** — surfaced in [The Power Law — Venture Capital & the Making of the New Future](/books/the-power-law-venture-capital-and-the-making-of-the-new-future) and elevated to the lead claim on Kyle's [Pricing](/notes/pricing) concept page as the highest-leverage decision a company makes. Second, on the [Scarcity Mindset](/notes/scarcity-mindset): "with a scarcity mindset it's hard to be happy no matter how rich or good-looking you are, because there's always somebody richer or better looking" — the line that gives Kyle's [Scarcity Mindset](/notes/scarcity-mindset) page its one-line definition, drawn from a Horowitz interview Kyle read while reading Frederik Gieschen's writing.

In [The Power Law](/books/the-power-law-venture-capital-and-the-making-of-the-new-future), Horowitz also figures as half of the a16z "in-house consultancy" differentiation — the operating-partner model that distinguished Andreessen Horowitz when it launched. He recurs as a credited contributor in [Talent](/books/talent) (by Tyler Cowen and Daniel Gross) and as the author of the [Ben Horowitz](/notes/ben-horowitz) quote about Okta's near-death stretch that anchors the early-years narrative in the [Todd McKinnon](/notes/todd-mckinnon-creating-and-defining-a-new-market-category) long-read.

**Context:** Ben Horowitz is an American tech entrepreneur and investor — co-founder of Loudcloud/Opsware and, in 2009, of the venture firm Andreessen Horowitz (a16z); author of *The Hard Thing About Hard Things*.
