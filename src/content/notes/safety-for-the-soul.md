# Safety For The Soul

"Safety for the Soul" is [Jeffrey R. Holland](/notes/jeffrey-r-holland)'s October 2009 General Conference address on the endurance of the Book of Mormon, which Kyle treats as a load-bearing piece of his own testimony. The talk's central argument — cited repeatedly in Christ Is Risen; Faith in Him Will Move Mountains — Russell M. Nelson — is that Joseph and Hyrum Smith "would not die quoting from a book they had fabricated": in their last hour of need they took solace in the Book of Mormon, which they would not have done had it been a fiction they invented "out of whole cloth."

In The First Principles of Faithful Pragmatism, Kyle names it as one of two texts (alongside Bob Bennett's [Leap of Faith](/books/leap-of-faith)) that solidified his belief in the book, summarized as Holland's "crawling over or under or around the Book of Mormon" defense — the claim that any honest reckoning must confront the book itself.

**Context:** "Safety for the Soul" is a widely cited talk by Elder Jeffrey R. Holland of the Quorum of the Twelve Apostles, delivered at the October 2009 General Conference of The Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-day Saints, defending the divine origin of the Book of Mormon.
