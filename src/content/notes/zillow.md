# Zillow

Zillow is the dominant US real-estate search portal, and in Kyle's notes it functions mainly as the reference point against which [Aryeo](/notes/aryeo)'s data moat is measured. Through [Branick Weix](/notes/branick-weix)'s 2022 Aryeo portfolio updates, the recurring claim is that a typical property is created on Aryeo **6.82 days before** it's listed on the MLS, Zillow, or any other real-estate search site — Aryeo holds the listing media (and knows the original copyright holder) before it reaches Zillow. By spring 2022, more than 5% of all US home sales were passing through Aryeo first (April 5th, 2022 — Aryeo Q1 Update (Branick Weix), March 30th, 2022 — Aryeo February Update (Branick Weix)).

A later Aryeo update (read Nov 2022) sketches the competitive backdrop: the major real-estate portals — Zillow, Realtor.com, CoStar — had for years focused on iBuying, mortgage lending, and optimizing lead fees, and were now spending hundreds of millions of dollars to differentiate their sites and data inputs. Zillow also surfaces once as a throwaway line in Kyle's Lowercarbon Capital research — "get your hands on some Zillow stock because it has never been clearer that being in the traditional startup cities just doesn't matter like it used to" — i.e., remote work eroding the geographic premium of startup hubs.

**Context:** Zillow Group is a publicly traded ($Z) US online real-estate marketplace headquartered in Seattle, best known for home-search listings and the "Zestimate" valuation; it wound down its Zillow Offers iBuying business in late 2021.
