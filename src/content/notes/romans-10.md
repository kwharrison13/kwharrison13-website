# Romans 10

Romans 10 is a chapter of Paul's New Testament epistle to the Romans, cited in Kyle's gospel-study note Christ Defines His Gospel. There it appears under the "Works" cluster of scripture references — Romans 10:9–10, glossed as "Confess with your mouths and be saved" — supporting the note's exploration of how confession, baptism, grace, and enduring to the end fit together in the doctrine of salvation, alongside passages like [Acts 2](/notes/acts-2):37–38, [Galatians 2](/notes/galatians-2):17–21, and [Mark 13](/notes/mark-13):13.

**Context:** Romans 10:9–10 is one of the New Testament's most-quoted statements on salvation: "That if thou shalt confess with thy mouth the Lord Jesus, and shalt believe in thine heart that God hath raised him from the dead, thou shalt be saved."
