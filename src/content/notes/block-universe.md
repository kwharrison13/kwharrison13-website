# Block Universe

The block universe is the view that past, present, and future all exist equally — that the "open" future we experience is an artifact of our limited access to reality, not a feature of reality itself. In the wiki it is grounded in [Story of Your Life](/books/story-of-your-life), [Ted Chiang](/notes/ted-chiang)'s novella, where it carries the central physics argument: the fundamental laws of physics are [time-symmetric](/notes/time-symmetry), and the arrow of time is a statistical feature of [entropy](/notes/entropy) and initial conditions rather than of the laws themselves. That points toward [Eternalism](/notes/eternalism) / the block universe, summarized by Einstein's line that "the distinction between past, present and future is only a stubbornly persistent illusion."

In the story, the block universe is the metaphysical backdrop for Chiang's reframing of [Free Will](/notes/free-will) and [Determinism](/notes/determinism). Because the time-symmetric laws imply the future is "already there," knowing the future and freely living it stop being opposed — they become complementary, the way specifying boundary conditions and computing the action are complementary in physics. The protagonist Louise knows her daughter will die before the daughter is conceived, yet lives the whole arc anyway, because in the block-universe frame a life becomes a *completed shape* — like a sentence or a ray of light following [Fermat's Principle](/notes/fermat-s-principle) — not a chain of contingencies racing toward an unknown end.

**Context:** "Block universe" (or eternalism) is a position in the philosophy of time, associated with the spacetime of special relativity, holding that all moments in time are equally real and that temporal passage is not an objective feature of the universe.
