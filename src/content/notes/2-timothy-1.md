# 2 Timothy 1

Paul's second epistle to Timothy, chapter 1, surfaces in Kyle's gospel study chiefly through verses 7–9 and the "spirit of promise." In Find To Teach, it anchors the idea that the calling to teach is foreordained — every minister was ordained to that calling in the grand council before the earth was ([Joseph Smith](/notes/joseph-smith)) — and that "we have the spirit of promise because of our calling" ([2 Timothy 1](/notes/2-timothy-1):7–9). In Prayer — Topical Study, the same chapter is read against the discernment of prayer: "God hath not given us the spirit of fear," used to argue that God never employs fear, doubt, or a stupor of thought as a negative answer to prayer ([2 Timothy 1](/notes/2-timothy-1):7; cf. [D&C 9](/notes/dandc-9):8–9).

**Context:** 2 Timothy is one of the New Testament Pastoral Epistles, traditionally attributed to the Apostle Paul writing to Timothy. 2 Timothy 1:7 — "For God hath not given us the spirit of fear; but of power, and of love, and of a sound mind" — is among its most-cited verses.

## Where this appears

- Find To Teach — verses 7–9 tie the foreordained calling to teach to the "spirit of promise"
- Prayer — Topical Study — 1:7 used to argue God never answers prayer with fear or doubt
