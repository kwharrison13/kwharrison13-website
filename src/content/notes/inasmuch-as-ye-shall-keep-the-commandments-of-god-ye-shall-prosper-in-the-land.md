# Inasmuch as ye shall keep the commandments of God ye shall prosper in the land

This is the wording of the Book of Mormon's central covenant promise as Alma swears it to his sons. In Kyle's Alma 36–38 study he flags it as "the central promise" that "recurs across the whole record" — what he calls the [Central Theme of the Book of Mormon](/notes/central-theme-of-the-book-of-mormon), "one of perseverant obedience." Alma opens both Alma 36:1 and Alma 38:1 with it ("I swear unto you, that inasmuch as ye shall keep the commandments of God ye shall prosper in the land"), restates it at Alma 36:30 with its inverse ("inasmuch as ye will not keep the commandments of God ye shall be cut off from his presence"), and the same promise threads back through 2 Nephi 4, Jarom 1, and Omni 1.

Kyle's study collects several near-identical phrasings of this promise as distinct entries — this "of God" variant alongside [Inasmuch as ye shall keep my commandments ye shall prosper in the land](/notes/inasmuch-as-ye-shall-keep-my-commandments-ye-shall-prosper-in-the-land) (the 2 Nephi 4:4 wording) and the "ye will keep" form — tracking how the covenant is restated across authors and generations.
