# Jacob Riis

In [Evicted](/books/evicted), [Matthew Desmond](/notes/matthew-desmond) quotes Jacob Riis on the economics of housing the poor: "'The business of housing the poor,' Jacob Riis wrote 125 years ago, 'if it is to amount to anything, must be a business, as it was business with our fathers to put them where they are. As charity, pastime, or fad, it will miserably fail, always and everywhere.'" Desmond enlists Riis alongside [Jane Jacobs](/notes/jane-jacobs), [Alexis de Tocqueville](/notes/alexis-de-tocqueville), and [Franklin D. Roosevelt](/notes/franklin-d-roosevelt) as part of the book's intellectual frame for thinking about poverty, profit, and the housing of the poor — the line foreshadows Desmond's own argument about how the rental market profits from the poor.

**Context:** Jacob Riis (1849–1914) was a Danish-American journalist and social-documentary photographer whose 1890 book *How the Other Half Lives* exposed slum conditions in New York City and helped drive housing and tenement reform during the Progressive Era.

## Where this appears

- [Evicted](/books/evicted) — quoted on housing the poor as necessarily a *business* (not charity) if it is to succeed, anchoring Desmond's argument about profit and poverty
