# Alma 20

A chapter in the Book of Mormon (book of Alma), studied in Kyle's [Come, Follow Me](/notes/come-follow-me) week on Alma 17–22 — I Will Make an Instrument of Thee. Kyle's notes center on [Ammon](/notes/ammon)'s encounter with Lamoni's father, the over-king. Ammon intervenes when the father, angry at Lamoni for befriending the Nephites (Alma 20:13), seeks to slay his son; Ammon tells him "thou shalt not slay thy son" (Alma 20:17). The father, astonished at the great love Ammon shows for Lamoni (Alma 20:26), relents and grants Lamoni his kingdom. Kyle also flags Alma 20:30 — that Aaron and his brethren had fallen among a "more hardened and a more stiffnecked people," underscoring his note that differing missionary outcomes reflect the audience, not just the teacher's skill.

**Context:** Alma chapter 20 recounts Ammon delivering his brethren from prison and his confrontation with the father of King Lamoni, who is converted from anger to astonishment by Ammon's love and restraint.
