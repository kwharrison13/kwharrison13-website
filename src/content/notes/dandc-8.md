# D&C 8

Across Kyle's [gospel-study](/notes/gospel-study) notes, **D&C 8** is the touchstone for the **spirit of revelation** — specifically **8:2–3**, the Lord telling Oliver Cowdery, "I will tell you in your mind and in your heart, by the Holy Ghost." Kyle treats this dual witness (mind *and* heart) as the defining signature of true revelation. In the Helaman 13–16 study, prophets "speak whatsoever things should come into their hearts," which Kyle ties to D&C 8:2–3 and to being "led by the Spirit, not knowing beforehand the things which I should do." In the Moroni 10 study, confirmation comes "as a still, small voice that pierces the soul, told to the mind and the heart."

The verse also appears in Spiritual Gifts (8:2 — "it will reveal the answer to our questions") and Scripture Study — Topical Study (under "Understanding Revelation"). Miscellaneous Scriptures explicitly contrasts other revelation passages against 8:2–3's "tell you in your heart AND mind" double witness, and That All Men May Come Unto Him cites **8:10** ("do not ask for what you ought not").

**Context:** Doctrine and Covenants section 8 is an April 1829 revelation given through Joseph Smith for Oliver Cowdery, granting him the gift to translate and describing how the Lord communicates revelation through the mind and heart by the Holy Ghost.
