# FormFn

FormFn (styled "formf(n)") is a creator-first 1/1 NFT marketplace on [Solana](/notes/solana) that Index Origin evaluated at a December 13th, 2021 seed investment committee — a $3M SAFE at a $25M cap, briefed by [Cat Wu](/notes/cat-wu). Its thesis: the NFT space was dominated by large generative collections (e.g. CryptoPunks), leaving the millions of less-technical, financially-constrained independent artists with bad options — either mass-produce collections or pay hundreds of dollars to list on curated Ethereum marketplaces like [SuperRare](/notes/superrare) and [Foundation](/books/foundation). FormFn's wedge was to let artists mint and list 1/1 pieces on Solana for fractions of a penny (vs. >$100 on Ethereum) while keeping royalties, with the longer-term vision pitched as "Kickstarter but web3."

The founding team was [Matt Lim](/notes/matt-lim) (full-stack engineer; Caltech, Facebook, Oculus) and [Katherine Liu](/notes/katherine-liu) (designer/NFT artist; Stanford, IDEO, Instagram), who had known each other for seven years and already shipped fast-moving NFT tools (Mycoverse, Nifty Pixels, Nifty Generator). FormFn positioned itself as "Etsy" against [OpenSea](/notes/opensea)'s "Amazon" and SuperRare/Foundation's "Sotheby's/Christie's," planning a 10% primary / 5% secondary fee. The note's closest-competitor flag was [Holaplex](/notes/holaplex) (individual creator storefronts on Solana; raised a $6M CoinFund seed the prior week), with FormFn betting on superior discovery and UX. Deal dynamics noted competition from Li Jin at Variant and likely a16z, with support from Pear VC.
