# Valar Atomics

**One-line:** *Nuclear reactor company building a Utah facility that powers a chemical process producing diesel fuel — behind-the-meter energy that is fully fungible with the existing diesel supply chain.*

## What we know

- Building a nuclear reactor in Utah; the reactor is not designed to power a data center directly — it powers a **chemical process that produces diesel fuel**, and generators run on that diesel (source: June 26th, 2026 — Contrary Research Office Hours).
- This structure sidesteps nuclear intermittency and reliance problems: the chemical process can build up reserves, and in a worst case the facility can simply purchase diesel from the open market (source: June 26th, 2026 — Contrary Research Office Hours).
- The diesel output is fully fungible with the existing diesel supply chain — no new generator type required, nothing exotic to retrofit (source: June 26th, 2026 — Contrary Research Office Hours).
- Kyle drew an analogy to [Anduril](/notes/anduril)'s commercial-components thesis: "make it as simple as possible to retrofit into an existing supply chain" (source: June 26th, 2026 — Contrary Research Office Hours).
- Part of [Contrary](/notes/contrary)'s on-site / behind-the-meter energy portfolio alongside Hestia, Base Power, and Voltra (source: June 26th, 2026 — Contrary Research Office Hours).
