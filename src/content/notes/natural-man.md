# Natural Man

The recurring scriptural figure of the unredeemed self — "the enemy to God" of [Mosiah 3](/notes/mosiah-3):19 — that runs through several of Kyle's topical gospel studies. The governing text is constant: we must "yield to the enticings of the Holy Spirit, and [put] off the natural man and becometh a Saint… through the atonement of Christ" — but each study works a different facet. In Diligence, the natural man is the slow pull of complacency, "a slow-leaking cancer": [Satan](/notes/satan) leads "by the neck with a flaxen cord," pacifying souls into carnal security ("All is well in Zion") until the natural man "carefully leads them down to hell." In Virtue, Kyle draws the distinction at the center of the concept: **putting off the natural man is an internal *subjection* of desire** (a demand we alone may righteously make of ourselves), as opposed to **becoming a saint, which is humble *submission*** — "a demand requires subjection; a command allows submission," with [David A. Bednar](/notes/david-a-bednar)'s line that "the journey from bad to good is the process of putting off the natural man in each of us."

[The Broken Heart](/books/the-broken-heart) by [Bruce C. Hafen](/notes/bruce-c-hafen) supplies the sharpest doctrinal statement Kyle records: the natural man "will remain an enemy to God forever — even after paying for his own sins — unless he also becometh a saint through the atonement of Christ the Lord, and becometh as a child." The same enemy-to-God thread reappears as a core theme in Humility — Topical Study, Obedience — Topical Study, and Patience — Topical Study.

**Context:** "The natural man is an enemy to God" comes from King Benjamin's address in Mosiah 3:19, a foundational Book of Mormon passage on the fallen state of humanity and the change of nature wrought through Christ's Atonement.

## Where this appears

- Diligence — the natural man as complacency, "a slow-leaking cancer," led by Satan's "flaxen cord."
- Virtue — subjection of desire (putting off) vs. submission (becoming a saint).
- [The Broken Heart](/books/the-broken-heart) — Hafen's statement that the natural man stays an enemy to God even after paying for his own sins, absent the Atonement.
- Humility — Topical Study / Obedience — Topical Study / Patience — Topical Study — listed as a core theme tying these studies to the Atonement.
