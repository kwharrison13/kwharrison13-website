# Al Gore

Author of [The Assault on Reason](/books/the-assault-on-reason), the book Kyle read as a critique of the decline of public reason in [Democracy](/notes/democracy). The wiki treats Gore as a former U.S. vice president writing on how a "well-informed citizenry" and the rule of reason erode when electronic broadcasting displaces print. Kyle's notes engage Gore adversarially: they push back on his nostalgia for a more rational pre-television public ("the pre-television public wasn't actually that reasonable"), and on his framing of psychological manipulation as a recent, broadcast-driven phenomenon — arguing instead that it "has been part of it all along." Gore's account traces the [Propaganda](/notes/propaganda) tradition of engineered "consent" forward into modern media.

Gore surfaces a second time in [Double Entry](/books/double-entry), quoted in 2006 that "the old way of measuring value is becoming irrelevant" — invoked in the context of how double-entry accounting shaped (and may be outliving) our sense of what counts as value.

**Context:** Al Gore is a former U.S. senator and the 45th Vice President of the United States (1993–2001). After narrowly losing the 2000 presidential election, he became a prominent climate-change advocate, known for the documentary *An Inconvenient Truth*. *The Assault on Reason* (2007) is his book on the deterioration of rational public discourse.
