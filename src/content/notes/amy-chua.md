# Amy Chua

Amy Chua enters Kyle's wiki through his notes on [The Courage To Be Disliked](/books/the-courage-to-be-disliked), where she supplies the parenting/identity frame that connects to the book's Adlerian argument. Kyle's note pins down the idea: Chua's account is of *"simultaneously feeling chosen (having our sense of superiority lifted) while also being rejected and having a chip on our shoulder (inflames our sense of inferiority)."* In the Key Takeaways synthesis this maps cleanly onto Alfred Adler's pairing of inferiority and superiority — the Adlerian point that the feeling of inferiority is a stimulant to growth rather than a disease. Kyle links her work to [Battle Hymn of The Tiger Mother](/books/battle-hymn-of-the-tiger-mother), reading the "chosen and rejected" tension as the same dual pressure that drives identity formation.

**Context:** Amy Chua is a Yale Law School professor and author best known for the memoir *Battle Hymn of the Tiger Mother* (2011) on demanding, high-expectation parenting, and for *World on Fire* and (with Jed Rubenfeld) *The Triple Package*, which examine group success and the psychology of feeling both superior and insecure.
