# Chasten

In Kyle's [Come, Follow Me](/notes/come-follow-me) study of Helaman 13–Helaman 16, "chasten" names the idea that **God corrects His people because He loves them**, not as punishment for its own sake. The anchor verse is Helaman 15:3 — "the people of Nephi hath he loved, and also hath he chastened them; yea, in the days of their iniquities hath he chastened them because he loveth them." The study gathers this as a recurring scriptural pattern, chaining it across Ether 2:14, Helaman 12:3, 1 Nephi 16:39, Proverbs 19:18, Proverbs 3:11, Job 5:17, and Revelation 3:19 ("As many as I love, I rebuke and chasten").

The common thread in those passages is that chastening — afflictions, correction, even terror and famine — is what turns people back toward God when prosperity makes them forget Him; the response it asks for is repentance and a [Spirit of Humility](/notes/spirit-of-humility) rather than resentment. In the Helaman study the theme sits alongside [Accountability](/notes/accountability) and [Agency](/notes/agency) (each soul is responsible for what it does with the prophets' warnings) and the truth that [Wickedness Never Was Happiness](/notes/wickedness-never-was-happiness). One Proverbs instance is tagged to parenting, extending the "correct because you love" logic from God to family.
