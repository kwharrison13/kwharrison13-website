# Moroni 4

The Book of Mormon chapter recording the **sacrament prayer over the bread** — the focus of Kyle's study in 3 Answers To Every Prayer. There he reads [Moroni 4](/notes/moroni-4) (bread) against [Moroni 5](/notes/moroni-5) (water) as a two-part symbolism: the bread is where we witness we are *willing* to take Christ's name, and the water is where we affirm we *do* always remember Him — a pairing of physical and spiritual sacrifice, death, and [Atonement](/notes/atonement). The blessing on the bread ([Moroni 4](/notes/moroni-4):3) promises His spirit will be with us if we (1) take upon us the name of Christ, (2) always remember Him, and (3) keep His commandments.

In the Come Follow Me study November 30–December 6, 2020. Moroni 1–6: "To Keep Them in the Right Way", the chapter appears among the "plain things" of the Church that [Moroni](/notes/moroni) records — ordinations, the sacrament prayers, [Baptism](/notes/baptism), record-keeping — precisely because these structural simplicities are what "keep them in the right way."

**Context:** Moroni 4 is a short chapter in the Book of Mormon giving the exact wording of the sacramental prayer blessing the bread, used in Latter-day Saint worship.
