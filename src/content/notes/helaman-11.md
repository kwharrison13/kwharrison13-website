# Helaman 11

A chapter in the Book of Mormon's book of Helaman, studied in Kyle's Come Follow Me study of Helaman 7–12. Kyle reads it primarily as a chapter about being humbled. A famine drives the people to "remember the Lord their God" and "the words of Nephi" ([Helaman 11](/notes/helaman-11):7), which Kyle pairs with [Alma 32](/notes/alma-32):15-16 on how it is better to humble ourselves than to be compelled to be humble — a key text for the [Spirit of Humility](/notes/spirit-of-humility).

Kyle pulls two further threads from the chapter. First, "Brothers in the Gospel": Lehi "was not a whit behind" Nephi as to righteousness ([Helaman 11](/notes/helaman-11):19), which Kyle compares to other prophetic sibling pairs (Nephi and Sam, Joseph and Hyrum) who play a uniquely humble supporting role. Second, an early manifestation of [Pride](/notes/pride) as contentious arguing — even in a year of peace there were "a few contentions concerning the points of doctrine" ([Helaman 11](/notes/helaman-11):22) — leading Kyle to note that when people can't argue while maintaining respect for each other, it reflects a serious lack of [Empathy](/notes/empathy).

**Context:** Helaman 11 (Book of Mormon) recounts Nephi son of Helaman calling down a famine to end years of war and Gadianton-robber violence, the people's repentance under that affliction, and the return of relative peace before pride begins to creep back in.
