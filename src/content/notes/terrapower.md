# TerraPower

The specific next-generation [Nuclear Energy](/notes/nuclear-energy) bet inside [Bill Gates](/notes/bill-gates)'s [How To Avoid a Climate Disaster](/books/how-to-avoid-a-climate-disaster) — the company Gates says he founded in 2008, "bringing together some of the best minds in nuclear physics and computer modeling to design a next-generation nuclear reactor." In the book it's the concrete embodiment of Gates's argument that nuclear is the only carbon-free source that can reliably deliver power day and night, almost anywhere, at scale.

Per the book, TerraPower's reactor is designed to run on many different types of fuel, including the waste from other nuclear facilities; to produce far less waste than today's plants; to be fully automated (eliminating human error); and to be built underground (protecting it from attack). Gates also describes a molten-salt approach: store heat generated during the day, then convert it to electricity at night when cheap solar isn't available — so the plant doesn't have to compete head-on with solar during daylight hours. Gates frames TerraPower (one of "a handful of companies" working on advanced reactors) as the kind of technology that goes nowhere without the right policies and markets.

**Context:** TerraPower is a nuclear reactor design and development company founded in 2006 (chaired by Bill Gates), known for its traveling-wave reactor concept and the sodium-cooled Natrium reactor with molten-salt thermal storage, with a demonstration plant underway in Kemmerer, Wyoming.
