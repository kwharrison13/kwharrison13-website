# Falsifiability

The criterion that a claim counts as real knowledge only if it can in principle be proven wrong by experience. In [To Save America, Restore Our Frontier](/notes/to-save-america-restore-our-frontier), [Joe Lonsdale](/notes/joe-lonsdale) makes falsifiability the organizing metaphor: the Frontier is "a falsifiable environment" — "an environment of true accountability" — and the ideas, systems, and people that emerge from it are more resilient because they can be tested and broken. He cites [Karl Popper](/notes/karl-popper) directly ("any real science has to be falsifiable") and contrasts the Frontier with the Core, where bad ideas and bad bureaucracies "don't just survive in Washington — they prosper there. It's an anti-Frontier." The prescription that follows — [Charter Cities](/notes/city-building), "bold governance experiments (even on Mars or ocean)" — is framed as the institutional form of restoring falsifiable, accountable conditions.

**Context:** Falsifiability is Karl Popper's demarcation criterion separating empirical science from non-science: a theory is scientific only if it forbids certain observations and could thus be refuted by them. See the closely related [Falsification](/notes/falsification) for the testing process itself.
