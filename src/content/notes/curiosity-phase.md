# Curiosity Phase

The "Curiosity Phase" is [Michael Dempsey](/notes/michael-dempsey)'s named stage of AI company-building, referenced in [The Enchanted Notebook](/notes/the-enchanted-notebook). Mapped onto the Gartner hype-cycle, Dempsey places the present moment *past* the Peak of Inflated Expectations and the Trough of Disillusionment — a spot "exciting enough to lure people in while still being a really difficult spot in which to build an AI-powered company." The note invokes it to characterize where AI startups sit: enticing but treacherous, where software is likely an easier target for AI than mechanical engineering (more public code exists than public CAD), making it the more fertile near-term ground.

**Context:** The term comes from Michael Dempsey's 2022 essay "Company Building in the Curiosity Phase of AI" (michaeldempsey.me). Dempsey, a venture investor, uses it to describe a window where AI capabilities are advanced enough to attract founders and capital but not yet mature or defensible enough to make durable company-building straightforward.
