# Customer Obsession

The discipline of building backward from the customer rather than reacting to competitors. In [The Everything Store](/books/the-everything-store) and [Amazon Unbound](/books/amazon-unbound) it is the founding value of Amazon — the first of [Jeff Bezos](/notes/jeff-bezos)'s leadership principles ("working backwards" from the customer), captured in his recurring Jeffism: *"when given the choice of obsessing over competitors or obsessing over customers, we always obsess over customers."* Bezos made employees apply the principle daily, even to trivial product decisions. The contrast case in [The Everything Store](/books/the-everything-store) is Borders, which had "a narrow operating philosophy" obsessively focused on opening new stores and meeting Wall Street's quarterly expectations while "repeatedly missing the changing tastes of consumers." Kyle flags this as the central open question the book leaves unanswered for investors: *"what is customer obsession to a venture capitalist?"* — which he marks as the single most important paragraph for building a differentiated long-term venture fund strategy.

[Wisdom From Walmart](/notes/wisdom-from-walmart) (David Perell on [Sam Walton](/notes/sam-walton)) reframes the same idea as **frugality as customer obsession**: "We exist to provide value to our customers... every time Wal-Mart spends one dollar foolishly, it comes right out of our customers' pockets." This connects it directly to [Pricing](/notes/pricing) — low, disciplined pricing as the visible expression of customer obsession (the dominant thread Kyle pulls across Bezos, Walmart, and Costco). [Playing To Win](/books/playing-to-win) gives the strategy-textbook version: "the most important stakeholder is the end user," deep consumer immersion as the heart of strategy. [Product Led Growth: The End User Era](/books/product-led-growth-the-end-user-era) extends it to software — never losing sight of who you're here to serve as a core pillar of success.

## Where this appears

- [The Everything Store](/books/the-everything-store) — one of Amazon's five founding values ("obsess over customers, not competitors"); the source of Kyle's "customer obsession for a VC" question.
- [Amazon Unbound](/books/amazon-unbound) — leadership principle #1, "working backwards" from the customer; Bezos asks for the "most customer obsessed thing I had ever done in my career."
- [Jeff Bezos](/notes/jeff-bezos) — the lead principle in his fourteen leadership principles and the flywheel he debated cannibalizing.
- [Wisdom From Walmart](/notes/wisdom-from-walmart) — frugality reframed as saving the customer money (the Walton quotes).
- [Playing To Win](/books/playing-to-win) — "the most important stakeholder is the end user"; deep consumer immersion as the heart of strategy.
- [Product Led Growth: The End User Era](/books/product-led-growth-the-end-user-era) — never losing sight of who you serve as a core pillar of growth.
