# Postman

Postman appears in Kyle's notes in two distinct registers. First, as the **reference API platform** running through his [API Security](/notes/api-security) diligence: in the Salt Security diligence it is the testing/dev tool whose limits define what security players must add — "APIs are handled differently in development vs. production; not something Postman can just do" (Salt Security Diligence — 2021) — and the API-security transcript frames the landscape as "Postman vs. Salt Security." [David O'Neill](/notes/david-o-neill) of [APIMetrics](/notes/apimetrics) notes APIMetrics "started around the same time as Postman, Runscope, API Science," and that Ops teams "do the bare minimum with Postman" (November 8th, 2021 — David O'Neill (APIMetrics)). It also surfaces as a possible integration target alongside [Swagger](/notes/swagger) in the Noname diligence.

Second, and separately, Postman is one of Kyle's **org-design exemplars**. Reading [The Everything Store](/books/the-everything-store) he flags Postman's "product squads" as a model for forming product teams around "primitives," and lists it as a portfolio cross-reference for operating philosophy. In the Trust Lab call Postman is cited approvingly as a company that "has been great at hub + pod" team structure.

**Context:** Postman is an API development and collaboration platform (founded 2014, San Francisco) used by developers to design, test, document, and monitor APIs; co-founded by Abhinav Asthana.
