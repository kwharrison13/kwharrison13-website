# Brian Eno

Brian Eno enters Kyle's corpus through a single load-bearing idea: **"scenius."** [The Founders](/books/the-founders) quotes Eno recalling that, as a visual-art student, he was taught artistic revolution came from solitary geniuses — Picasso, Kandinsky, Rembrandt — but on closer inspection found them to be products of "very fertile scenes involving lots and lots of people… all sorts of people who created a kind of ecology of talent." Eno called this "scenius": "the intelligence of a whole… operation or group of people." [Jimmy Soni](/notes/jimmy-soni) uses the term to frame [The Founders](/books/the-founders) less as a payments-company history than as a study of the talent constellation that produced the PayPal Mafia.

Kyle annotated the scenius passage twice, making it the conceptual seed of his [Talent Vortex](/notes/talent-vortex) page — his own phrasing for what Eno calls scenius and Soni calls "Bell-like constellations." So Eno functions in the wiki as the originator of the vocabulary for collective rather than individual genius, the central lens of [The Founders](/books/the-founders).

**Context:** Brian Eno (b. 1948) is a British musician, producer, and visual artist — a founding member of Roxy Music and a pioneer of ambient music — who coined "scenius" to describe the communal, scene-based nature of creative breakthroughs.
