# Marie K. Hafen

Marie K. Hafen is the co-author, with her husband [Bruce C. Hafen](/notes/bruce-c-hafen), of [Faith is Not Blind](/books/faith-is-not-blind) — the book whose central argument, in Kyle's synthesis, is that mature faith runs *through* complexity rather than around it. The book's first-person narration is shared between the two authors; one passage recounts how "Dillon, Marie, and I and our classmates" lived out what Catholic sociologist [Thomas O'Dea](/notes/thomas-o-dea) called "Mormonism's most significant problem" — the collision between a literalistic, authoritative religion and the skepticism fostered by university study. Her authorial voice is part of the book's lived "ideal vs. real" framing of belief.

**Context:** Marie K. Hafen is a Latter-day Saint author and educator who has written and spoken on women, faith, and gospel scholarship, often in collaboration with her husband Bruce C. Hafen, an emeritus General Authority Seventy.
