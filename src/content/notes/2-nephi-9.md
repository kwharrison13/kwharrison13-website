# 2 Nephi 9

Jacob's discourse on the [Atonement](/notes/atonement) and the plan of salvation, which Kyle mines across many of his topical studies. It is a core text in How To Apply The Atonement (vv. 6–7 and vv. 21–22, where Christ "suffereth the pains of all men"). The chapter's account of post-mortal knowledge feeds both Faith — Topical Study (vv. 12–14, that life promises "a perfect knowledge") and Born Again (v. 14, "a knowledge of our guilt and nakedness"). (How To Apply The Atonement, Faith — Topical Study, Born Again)

Beyond the Atonement, Kyle pulls discrete verses for several themes: Plan of Salvation — Topical Study cites vv. 28–29 on the "natural man" (echoing [Mosiah 3](/notes/mosiah-3):19); Prayer — Topical Study cites v. 43 as the proof-text that humble prayer is most effective; Missionary Work — Topical Study contrasts vv. 13 vs. 28 as "good and bad planning"; and Obedience — Topical Study reads v. 35 ("wo unto the murderer who deliberately killeth") against Nephi's constrained, undesired act. (Plan of Salvation — Topical Study, Prayer — Topical Study, Missionary Work — Topical Study, Obedience — Topical Study)

**Context:** 2 Nephi 9 is a chapter in the Second Book of Nephi (Book of Mormon) recording the prophet Jacob's sermon on the resurrection, the Atonement of Christ, and the escape from death and hell — among the most doctrinally dense chapters in the book.

## Where this appears

- Faith — Topical Study — vv. 12–14, life's promise of perfect knowledge.

- Prayer — Topical Study — v. 43, humble prayer as most effective.
- Missionary Work — Topical Study — vv. 13 vs. 28, good vs. bad planning.
- Obedience — Topical Study — v. 35, deliberate vs. constrained action.
