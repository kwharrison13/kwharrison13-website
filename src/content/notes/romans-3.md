# Romans 3

Paul's epistle chapter that recurs across Kyle's gospel study as a proof-text on two linked ideas: universal sinfulness and salvation by grace. Two verses do the work. **[Romans 3](/notes/romans-3):23** — "all have sinned, and come short of the glory of God" — is cited in the August 31–September 6, 2020. Helaman 13–16: "Glad Tidings of Great Joy" study as the answer to whether God could "hate" any of His children (all fall short, so condemnation is universal absent the [Atonement](/notes/atonement)) and recurs in the Plan of Salvation — Topical Study under the "Hotel Hell" expansion alongside [2 Nephi 2](/notes/2-nephi-2):5 and [Moses 6](/notes/moses-6):57. **[Romans 3](/notes/romans-3):24–25** anchors the grace thread in Miscellaneous Scriptures — "saved by Grace of God" — sitting inside Kyle's recurring formulation that salvation comes by grace *after* our own righteous effort ([Moroni 10](/notes/moroni-10):32).

**Context:** Romans 3 is Paul's argument that both Jew and Gentile are "under sin" (3:9–23) and are "justified freely by his grace through the redemption that is in Christ Jesus" (3:24) — a cornerstone New Testament passage on the universality of sin and justification by faith/grace.

## Where this appears

- August 31–September 6, 2020. Helaman 13–16: "Glad Tidings of Great Joy" — 3:23 ("all have sinned") used to argue God's condemnation is universal, framing the need for mercy.

- Miscellaneous Scriptures — 3:24–25 logged under the "saved by Grace of God" / grace-after-effort cross-reference web.
