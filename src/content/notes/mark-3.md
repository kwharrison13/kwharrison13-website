# Mark 3

A chapter in the New Testament Gospel of Mark, cited in Kyle's gospel study. In the Abrahamic Covenant page, Mark 3:13–15 is grouped with [Luke 10](/notes/luke-10), [Luke 6](/notes/luke-6):13, and [John 15](/notes/john-15):16 as scriptural evidence that specific followers of Christ were chosen as Apostles — chosen by Christ himself — supporting the argument about priesthood authority and divine calling.

**Context:** Mark 3 records Jesus healing on the Sabbath, the growing crowds, and his ordaining of the twelve Apostles (Mark 3:13–19), whom he chose and sent forth to preach.

## Where this appears

- Abrahamic Covenant — Mark 3:13–15 cited (with Luke 10, Luke 6:13, John 15:16) for Christ choosing specific followers as Apostles.
