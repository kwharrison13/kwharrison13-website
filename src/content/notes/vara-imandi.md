# Vara Imandi

A customer-reference expert in Kyle's Noname Security - Diligence dossier (October 11, 2021), interviewed as a buyer of API-security tooling. Imandi leads Solutions Engineering for HLS at [MuleSoft](/notes/mulesoft) (since April 2020) and self-identifies as the final decision-maker for tools in MuleSoft's security landscape, with budget ownership over the department; he also writes frequently on LinkedIn about API security. Per the diligence biography, he was previously Director of Software Development at NTT DATA Services (2014–2020), and earlier worked at Deloitte and Oracle.

His call is one of the strongest signals in the dossier: MuleSoft, an API-first company, evaluated the landscape and signed a 3-year contract with [Noname Security](/notes/noname-security) at $1M+ per year — a buy Imandi attributes to Noname's roadmap fit (auto-cataloguing of APIs regardless of build tooling, scalability to customers like McDonald's at 30B+ daily transactions, and an agent-less approach). He drew a sharp distinction between Noname and pure API-gateway players: [Kong](/notes/kong) (cloud-native, microservices-focused) he saw as "augmentative than replacing" rather than a security threat, while gateways like MuleSoft's own are sold as a platform, not piecemeal. He framed [DevSecOps](/notes/devsecops) as a rising tide as APIs proliferate, and speculated Noname could be an attractive acquisition for someone like Microsoft.
