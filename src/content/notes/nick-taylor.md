# Nick Taylor

Nick Taylor is Director of [Customer Success](/notes/customer-success) at Onfido (ID verification / biometrics); he was previously CS Lead at Tradeshift, Senior CSM at Judopay, and CSM at PlayMob, with ~8 years in CS and two buying processes behind him. He appears in Kyle's Planhat - Diligence research as an expert call covering the broader CS-tooling landscape.

His through-line: "tooling requires GOOD DATA underneath it" — [Gainsight](/notes/gainsight) is only valuable with clean, hygienic data, and his first question of any vendor is how much works out of the box, since custom-built connectors can require a full-time hire just to maintain on top of an already expensive tool. Across roles he evaluated [ChurnZero](/notes/churnzero), [ToTango](/notes/totango), Gainsight, [Catalyst](/notes/catalyst), and [Custify](/notes/custify) — at Onfido he went far with Gainsight and Catalyst but ultimately chose [Custify](/notes/custify) for being the easiest to talk to and most flexible. He was bullish on Catalyst ("the new, cool software... once they've got more time in the market, they will be taking customers from Gainsight"). On [Planhat](/notes/planhat) he was more reserved: he hadn't run a buying process with them, recalled them as onboarding-focused with "super powerful" reporting tools but lacking the connections Onfido wanted at the time, and admitted "I don't have the best view of Planhat to make a view." His call provides a useful counterweight in the diligence — a knowledgeable CS leader who did *not* pick Planhat.
