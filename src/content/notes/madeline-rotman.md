# Madeline Rotman

Head of Sustainability at [Imperfect Foods](/notes/imperfect-foods) and a [Watershed](/notes/watershed-climate) customer reference during Kyle's 2021 Watershed Series B diligence. She joined Imperfect in August 2020 (after ~5 years in eCommerce grocery, including a produce-manager role at FreshDirect) and inherited an existing Watershed contract, which she re-bid and chose to renew — citing Watershed's ability to scale with the company and its all-in-one combination of carbon accounting, a reduction-planning tool, and offset purchasing (vs. point solutions like Pachama and [Patch](/notes/patch)).

Across two reference calls (with [Rex Woodbury](/notes/rex-woodbury) on May 10, 2021, and a follow-up Oct 18, 2021) Rotman gave Kyle some of the diligence's most useful operator color: Imperfect was "one of the more high-touch customers," meeting their dedicated Watershed rep weekly and moving from annual to quarterly footprints. Her recurring theme was that **the bottleneck is the data, not the software** — "most companies are the problem, not the software," because no company has its emissions data sorted into a clean, API-accessible format. She also flagged that there was no regulatory requirement forcing food businesses to report (treating it instead as a competitive advantage and a 10–15 year planning input), and offered the framing that "100 companies control 71% of greenhouse gases," so tools that help large organizations make decisions are what matter. Watershed's diligence notes rated her a strong expert worth returning to.
