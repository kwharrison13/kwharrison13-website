# Eric S. Raymond

In [Working in Public](/books/working-in-public), Eric S. Raymond is cited as an early articulator of the case for open source. His 1997 book-length essay, "The Cathedral and the Bazaar: Musings on Linux and Open Source by an Accidental Revolutionary," is presented as a compelling argument for the benefits of [Open Source](/notes/open-source) software. The book also attributes to him the aphorism "Given enough eyeballs, all bugs are shallow" — the claim that open source holds an advantage over closed source because more people inspecting the code increases the chance that more bugs get found.

**Context:** Eric S. Raymond (b. 1957) is an American programmer and writer, author of *The Cathedral and the Bazaar* and an early advocate and theorist of the open source software movement.
