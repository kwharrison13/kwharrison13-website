# Identity & Access Management

A software/security category Kyle has done investment diligence on and uses as a worked example of how he tracks an investing theme across his notes. The Identity & Access Management Diligence — 2021 page lays out his key questions: why identity is becoming more important, the market math (workers × machines × apps that all need identity), and a central puzzle — "Why isn't [Identity & Access Management](/notes/identity-and-access-management) a competitive advantage? How could it be?" He drills into sub-segments — [Customer Identity & Access Management (CIAM)](/notes/customer-identity-and-access-management-ciam) vs. Workforce Identity & Access Management (WIAM), Single-Sign On (SSO), Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA), Passwordless, and the standards SAML vs. OAuth — and probes the stickiness question through [Okta](/notes/okta): a SailPoint CEO's line that "my customers tell me they could replace Okta in a weekend if they had to," and the bull case where Okta could displace Microsoft Active Directory.

In his essay [Networked Conviction — Roam + Investing](/essays/networked-conviction-roam-investing), IAM appears as the canonical tag illustrating his Roam-as-CRM workflow: for every call he tags the person and company, plus relevant topic pages like [Identity & Access Management](/notes/identity-and-access-management), so that over time he can see which people keep recurring around a category and identify the specialists in his network.

**Context:** Identity and Access Management (IAM) is the security discipline of governing digital identities and what resources they can access — spanning authentication, single sign-on, multi-factor authentication, and identity governance. Public-market leaders include Okta, Microsoft (Entra/Azure AD), Ping Identity, and SailPoint.
