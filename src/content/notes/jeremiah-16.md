# Jeremiah 16

Old Testament chapter Kyle returns to for one verse — Jeremiah 16:16, "Behold, I will send for many fishers, saith the Lord, and they shall fish them; and after will I send for many hunters, and they shall hunt them from every mountain, and from every hill, and out of the holes of rocks." In both Missionary Work — Topical Study and The Good Shepherd, Kyle uses this verse to make the point that missionary work has been carried out very differently at different times and places, but always as the Lord's work done in the Lord's way — the disciple's task is to "doubt not, fear not." The verse sits alongside the shepherd-versus-sheepherder distinction ([Neal A. Maxwell](/notes/neal-a-maxwell): God leads by love) and the principle that the Lord does not compel "in all things" ([D&C 50](/notes/dandc-50):26–28).

**Context:** Jeremiah 16 is a chapter of the book of Jeremiah in the Hebrew Bible / Old Testament. Verse 16's "fishers and hunters" imagery is widely read in Latter-day Saint settings as a prophecy of the latter-day gathering of Israel.

## Where this appears

- Missionary Work — Topical Study — 16:16 framing missionary work as the Lord's work done in the Lord's way ("doubt not, fear not")
- The Good Shepherd — 16:16 cited in "in every decision, know who you follow," paired with leading-by-love over driving
