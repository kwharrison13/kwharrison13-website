# 3 Pillars of Eternity

A doctrinal framework Kyle used in his Alma 17–22 study to organize the gospel's foundational events into three pillars: the **Creation**, the **Fall of Man**, and the **[Atonement](/notes/atonement)**. The frame appears where Ammon teaches king Lamoni "all the things concerning the fall of man" and expounds "the plan of redemption, which was prepared from the foundation of the world" (Alma 18:36, 39), prompting Kyle to lay out the three pillars as the structure behind that teaching.

Under each pillar Kyle gathered the doctrine: the Creation raises the purpose of the earth and whether Adam and Eve were "created to fail" (the two commandments of the first and second estates); the Fall pairs "Adam fell that men might be; and men are, that they might have joy" (2 Nephi 2:25) with the Cowley & Whitney note that the Fall moved man "downward, yet forward"; and the Atonement is framed by the "Lamb slain from the foundation of the world" and the resolution of physical and spiritual death. Kyle treats the fall, atonement, judgment, and redemption as pieces of the broader [Plan of Salvation](/notes/plan-of-salvation) "that extends from eternity to eternity," distinguishing it from the narrower [plan of redemption](/notes/plan-of-redemption).

**Context:** In Latter-day Saint theology, the "three pillars of eternity" is a common teaching frame (associated with apostle Bruce R. McConkie) holding that the Creation, the Fall, and the Atonement are interdependent foundations of the plan of salvation — remove any one and the others collapse.
