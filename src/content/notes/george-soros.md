# George Soros

George Soros appears across two of Kyle's read books as a source of investing ideas. In [Boom — Bubbles & The End of Stagnation](/books/boom-bubbles-and-the-end-of-stagnation) he is credited with popularizing **reflexivity** — the positive feedback loop between expectations and prices that drives market dynamics — which the book treats as a load-bearing mechanism in its bubble model. The book traces the idea's intellectual roots to Soros's professor Karl Popper and to Robert K. Merton's "self-fulfilling prophecy."

In [The Psychology of Money](/books/the-psychology-of-money), Soros supplies a maxim on asymmetry over accuracy: "It's not whether you're right or wrong that's important, but how much money you make when you're right and how much you lose when you're wrong." Together the two references frame Soros as a thinker on market feedback loops and on sizing rather than win-rate.

**Context:** George Soros is a Hungarian-American investor and philanthropist who built one of the most successful hedge funds in history (Quantum Fund) and developed the theory of reflexivity in markets.

## Where this appears

- [Boom — Bubbles & The End of Stagnation](/books/boom-bubbles-and-the-end-of-stagnation) — credited with popularizing reflexivity, the expectations-prices feedback loop central to the book's bubble model.
- [The Psychology of Money](/books/the-psychology-of-money) — quoted on asymmetry: what matters is how much you make when right vs. lose when wrong.
