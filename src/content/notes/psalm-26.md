# Psalm 26

Psalm 26 appears in Kyle's Temporal Wealth and the Kingdom of God — Brigham Young study, where verses 2–3 — "Examine me, O LORD, and prove me; try my reins and my heart… I have walked in thy truth" — are grouped with [Abraham 3](/notes/abraham-3):25 ("we will prove them herewith") and [D&C 98](/notes/dandc-98):14 ("I will prove you in all things") under the theme of mortal life as a proving ground. Kyle's note ties the cluster together: "everything is about proving what we will do" (see [We Will Prove Them Herewith](/notes/we-will-prove-them-herewith)). The verse is read as the psalmist inviting God's testing — the same proving logic that runs through the Brigham Young material on whether wealth and means will be used righteously.

**Context:** Psalm 26 is a psalm of David, a plea of integrity in which the speaker asks God to examine and vindicate him because he has walked in faithfulness and avoided the company of the wicked.
