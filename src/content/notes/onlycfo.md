# OnlyCFO

OnlyCFO is the pseudonymous SaaS-finance writer behind the Substack long-read [How to Trick Investors and VCs](/notes/how-to-trick-investors-and-vcs). In that piece they walk through the accounting "shenanigans" that let startups flatter their numbers — capitalizing the bulk of SaaS sales commissions over 3–5 years, minimizing internal-use software capitalization to protect gross-margin optics, and gaming the Burn Multiple (Cash Burn / Net New ARR) by varying how each component is defined. The throughline of their argument is that [venture capital](/notes/venture-capital) investors are easier to deceive than public-market investors, because management controls what gets reported, many VCs lack a finance background, and many don't dig past topline metrics.
