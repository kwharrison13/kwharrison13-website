# Truth

Truth is one of the load-bearing threads running through Kyle's reading, and the corpus approaches it from several distinct angles. The **religious-intellectual** strand insists that truth is unitary and self-consistent: [The Faith of a Scientist](/books/the-faith-of-a-scientist) makes it the load-bearing premise — "the Gospel commits only to the truth, and no truth can conflict with another truth," so true science and true religion must always be in complete harmony. [Leonard Arrington and the Writing of Mormon History](/books/leonard-arrington-and-the-writing-of-mormon-history) extends this into a posture of fearless inquiry — "God does not need our lies" — and Arrington's Santayana-derived idea that truth can be expressed symbolically (myth, narrative, testimony) as well as literally, which freed him from anxiety over the historicity of founding events. Moroni 10 supplies the devotional version: by the power of the [Holy Ghost](/notes/holy-ghost) "you may know the [Truth](/notes/truth) of all things" (Moroni's Promise).

The **epistemic / civic** strand treats truth as a fragile public good. [21 Lessons for the 21st Century](/books/21-lessons-for-the-21st-century) warns that "humans prefer power to truth" and prizes "questions you cannot answer over answers you cannot question," tying the cherishing of scientific truth to freedom and the refusal to invest supreme authority in any text or leader. [Braving The Wilderness](/books/braving-the-wilderness) (via Harry Frankfurt) frames bullshit as a *greater enemy of truth than lies* — the wholesale abandonment of "truth as a shared starting point." And the **knowledge-architecture** strand makes truth procedural: [How To Take Smart Notes](/books/how-to-take-smart-notes) (via Luhmann and Humboldt) holds that "truth does not belong to anyone… it is the outcome of the scientific exchange of written ideas," so "the moment the author can be removed from the scene, the written piece is a public claim on truth." [The Notetaking Cold War](/notes/the-notetaking-cold-war) sharpens this into competing theories of truth itself — the essentialist's *representational* theory (a statement is true when it accurately represents reality) versus the pragmatist's *instrumental* theory (true when acting on it makes the world react as expected).

**Context:** Theories of truth are a core topic in philosophy — the correspondence theory (truth as accurate representation of reality), the pragmatic theory (truth as what works), and the coherence theory (truth as fit within a consistent system) being the principal families. The notes above map roughly onto correspondence (essentialist) vs. pragmatic poles.
