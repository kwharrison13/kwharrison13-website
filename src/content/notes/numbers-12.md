# Numbers 12

Numbers 12 is cited in Humility — Topical Study for its verse 3 — "Moses was very meek, above all the men which were upon the face of the earth" — which Kyle uses as a scriptural anchor for the distinction between *meekness* and *humility*. In the study, meekness is the first step (seeing yourself as God sees you, recognizing your weakness), and Moses serves as the model: his meekness did not make him weak, and likely underlies his own self-description, "I am not eloquent... I am slow of speech" (Exodus 4:10). The verse sits alongside [Moroni 7](/notes/moroni-7):44 and the [Dieter F. Uchtdorf](/notes/dieter-f-uchtdorf) line that "humility is not thinking less of yourself, but less ABOUT yourself."

**Context:** Numbers 12 is the chapter of the Hebrew Bible in which Miriam and Aaron speak against Moses; verse 3 contains the well-known description of Moses' meekness, after which the Lord defends Moses and Miriam is briefly struck with leprosy.
