# Nicholas Stern

Nicholas Stern appears in Kyle's notes through a single citation in [Marc Andreessen](/notes/marc-andreessen)'s essay [It's Time To Build](/notes/it-s-time-to-build). Andreessen invokes him for the line "capitalism is how we take care of people we don't know," using it to argue that the West's failure to build housing, schools, and hospitals is *not* a failure of capitalism — those fields are already lucrative — but rather a failure of desire, will, inertia, and regulatory capture. In the essay's structure, Stern's framing is one of three "it's not the obvious culprit" points (alongside "not money" and "not technical competence") that set up Andreessen's real diagnosis.

**Context:** Nicholas Stern (Baron Stern of Brentford) is a British economist, former World Bank chief economist, and author of the 2006 *Stern Review on the Economics of Climate Change*. He is widely known for work on the economics of climate change and public goods.

## Where this appears
