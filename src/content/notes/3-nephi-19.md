# 3 Nephi 19

A Book of Mormon chapter that recurs across [Kyle Harrison](/notes/kyle-harrison)'s gospel study as the **model prayer of [Jesus Christ](/notes/jesus)** — the "prayer of sanctification" the risen Christ offers for his Nephite disciples. It is the backbone of his Prayer — Topical Study, where Kyle pairs it with Christ's mediator prayer in [John 17](/notes/john-17) to extract a pattern of how to pray: grateful (19:20, 28), unselfishly pleading for others (19:21, 23, 29 / "I pray not for the world, but for those whom thou hast given me"), faithfully accounting God's blessings (19:22), and wholly submissive. He names Christ as "the perfect example of prayer in all things" and points to 19:33 — "their hearts were open and they did understand in their hearts" — as the proof that prayer opens the windows of heaven.

That same verse 33 anchors his Testimony — Topical Study (the multitude heard, bore record, and understood by the power of the Holy Ghost). The chapter also appears in Spiritual Gifts (19:24, the Spirit teaching us what to pray for) and in his November 9–15, 2020 Come Follow Me study (19:35, "so great faith have I never seen... because of their unbelief").

**Context:** 3 Nephi 19 records the night following the resurrected Christ's first day among the Nephites — the twelve disciples praying, Christ praying for them three times that they might be sanctified and granted the Holy Ghost, and the people being filled with the Spirit so that "their hearts were open and they did understand."

## Where this appears

- Prayer — Topical Study — the central model prayer; the four-part pattern (grateful, unselfish, faithful accounting, submissive); 19:33 on hearts opened and understood.
- Testimony — Topical Study — 19:33 on the multitude bearing record by the power of the Holy Ghost.
- Spiritual Gifts — 19:24, the Spirit teaching us what to pray for.
- Ether 1–5 — Rend That Veil of Unbelief — 19:35 on faith and unbelief.
