# Meredith Willson

Meredith Willson is the source Kyle credits for the line "the idle brain is the devil's playground" — the lyric from his musical [The Music Man (musical)](/notes/the-music-man-musical) — which Kyle uses in his Alma 39–42 study to illustrate the danger of idleness. He pairs it with Alma's reproof of [Corianton](/notes/corianton) ("Thou shouldst have tended to the ministry wherewith thou wast entrusted," Alma 39:4) and with [Geoffrey Chaucer](/notes/geoffrey-chaucer)'s "idleness is the root of mischief," under the heading "idleness is the devil's playground." The cluster supports Kyle's note that a wandering, unoccupied mind is what leaves room for sin.

**Context:** Meredith Willson (1902–1984) was an American composer, songwriter, and playwright best known for writing the 1957 Broadway musical *The Music Man*, including songs like "Seventy-Six Trombones" and "Till There Was You."
