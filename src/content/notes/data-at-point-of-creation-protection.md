# Data-at-point-of-creation protection

This is [Alexei Balaganski](/notes/alexei-balaganski)'s central thesis in the [Salt Security](/notes/salt-security) diligence: *"Why would you want to protect individual components of your infrastructure when you can just protect your data at the point of creation?"* It reframes security as data-centric rather than component-centric — protect the data itself as it is created, instead of fortifying each piece of infrastructure (APIs, networks, endpoints) separately. Balaganski, a [KuppingerCole](/notes/kuppingercole) analyst, pairs this with the view that network-security vendors are "dinosaurs" and that the whole cybersecurity market is migrating toward data protection. The thesis is the conceptual backbone of [DSPM](/notes/data-security-posture-management) platforms like [Laminar](/notes/laminar), which aim to coexist with and eventually subsume [API-security](/notes/api-security) tooling.

## Where this appears

- [Alexei Balaganski](/notes/alexei-balaganski) — originates the "protect data at the point of creation" argument over component-level security.
- [Laminar](/notes/laminar) — the DSPM platform whose strategy embodies this data-centric framing.
