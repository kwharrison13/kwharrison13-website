# Self-Replicating Systems

In [The Man From The Future](/books/the-man-from-the-future), self-replicating systems are the load-bearing "forgotten idea" of the book's final third. The lineage runs from [John von Neumann](/notes/john-von-neumann)'s *Theory of Self-Reproducing Automata* — first unveiled in his 1948 Hixon Symposium lecture and published posthumously in 1966 — through Philip K. Dick's "Autofac" story (machines that consume the Earth to make more of themselves), to the 1980 NASA SRS workshop that produced two detailed designs for fully self-replicating lunar factories. Modern descendants in the book include the RepRap, a 3D printer that prints ~80% of its own parts, and "von Neumann probes," self-replicating craft sent into space precisely to avoid swamping Earth.

Kyle's notes tag this thread #[Forgotten Ideas](/notes/forgotten-ideas) — work that was rigorous in its time and dropped not because it was wrong but because it ran ahead of execution capability. The page records that 13 of 25 of Kyle's notes on the book tag it #[Anduril](/notes/anduril): von Neumann's RAND-style applied research, civil-military fusion, and self-replicating systems read as deep historical precedent for the Anduril portfolio thesis (3D-printing and space-warfare manufacturing at scale).

**Context:** A self-replicating system is a machine or process able to produce copies of itself, often from raw materials in its environment. Von Neumann's mid-century work on self-reproducing automata is the canonical theoretical foundation; the concept recurs in proposals for autonomous space manufacturing and molecular nanotechnology.
