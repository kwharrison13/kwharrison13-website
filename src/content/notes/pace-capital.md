# Pace Capital

The venture firm where [Chris Paik](/notes/chris-paik) is an investor. In the wiki, Pace Capital appears as Paik's affiliation in the context of his "right AND clever" observation — that many public investors avoided [FAANG](/notes/faang) despite it driving the majority of market returns because owning it felt "too obvious," and "they wanted to be right AND clever." Kyle uses Paik's point in his [Conviction-Led Contrarianism](/essays/conviction-led-contrarianism) thinking as a caution against reflexive [Contrarianism](/notes/contrarianism): conviction sometimes lands on a widely held belief that simply happens to be true, and an idea shouldn't be rejected just because it's obvious.

**Context:** Pace Capital is an early-stage venture-capital firm co-founded by Chris Paik (previously of Thrive Capital).
