# Patch

Patch is an API-first carbon-offset / marketplace company that lets developers and sustainability teams launch climate programs "in days, not months," embedding offset purchasing into a checkout flow or app. In the [Watershed Climate](/notes/watershed-climate) competitive landscape it is mapped as a **marketplace** player (alongside Pachama, Climate Care) — companies that monetize by connecting buyers and sellers of carbon credits — as distinct from accounting-only tools ([Persefoni](/notes/persefoni), Emitwise, Metric) and strategy tools. The Watershed diligence repeatedly contrasts the two models: Patch "just piggy-backs on someone else's calculations" and can link into a website to do backward-looking carbon footprinting, but is seen as lacking the depth to actually build a footprint the way Watershed claims to. A defensibility note from that diligence: once a customer has built Patch into their checkout flow, they are much less likely to switch away.

From the November 2021 Patch investor update Kyle read (logged Jan 11, 2022, under Contrary - Portfolio Updates (Pre-Jan 2022)): first big customer live was Afterpay ($AFTPY), with EasyPost and Farfetch coming; 51 deals signed on the demand side and 240K tonnes of supply; burn jumped in October/November because they had to front cash for carbon inventory. Patch appears on Rob Toews' Fortune list of potential climate unicorns (with Pachama, [Watershed Climate](/notes/watershed-climate), Persefoni, etc.). [Claudine Emeott](/notes/claudine-emeott) of Salesforce Ventures flagged liking Patch as a possible partnership/investment fit on the climate side.

**Context:** Patch is a San Francisco-based climate startup (founded 2020) offering APIs for carbon credit purchasing, removal, and carbon accounting, used by companies to embed climate action into their products.
