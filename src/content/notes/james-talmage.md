# James Talmage

In [Stretching The Heavens](/books/stretching-the-heavens), Talmage appears as an apostle-scientist central to the book's science-and-faith threads. He is cited for "The Earth and Man," a speech insisting on "the harmony of true science and true gospel understanding," offered as a counterweight to anti-evolution remarks. The book counts him among the church's "most erudite scholars" called to produce teaching manuals: his *The Great Apostasy* (1910) began as a Young Women's manual, and *Jesus the Christ* was the official Melchizedek Priesthood manual in 1916 — situating him in a generation (with Roberts, Widtsoe, and Eyring) when "the church valued these men of science."

He is also the source the book uses for the doctrine of eternal progression. Described as "virtually the only apostle to produce a theological treatise… under official imprimatur," Talmage wrote in the first edition of his *Articles of Faith* that "Advancement from grade to grade within any kingdom, and from kingdom to kingdom, will be provided for. … Eternity is progressive."

**Context:** James E. Talmage (1862–1933) was a British-born geologist and an apostle of The Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-day Saints, author of *Jesus the Christ*, *The Articles of Faith*, and *The Great Apostasy*.
