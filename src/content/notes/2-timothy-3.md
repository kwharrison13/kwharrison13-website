# 2 Timothy 3

Paul's second epistle to Timothy, chapter 3, appears in Kyle's gospel study through two warning verses. In General Conference — October 2021, 3:7 — "Ever learning, and never able to come to the knowledge of the truth" — is quoted (alongside [Christoffel Golden Jr.](/notes/christoffel-golden-jr) and [Boyd K. Packer](/notes/boyd-k-packer)) to contrast worldly knowledge and skill against the unstable foundation they can camouflage, tying into the [Spirit of Humility](/notes/spirit-of-humility). In Plan of Salvation — Topical Study, 3:13 — "Evil men will get worse and worse" — sits in a chain of verses on hope and the resurrection.

**Context:** 2 Timothy 3 is a New Testament Pastoral Epistle chapter, traditionally attributed to the Apostle Paul. It opens by warning that "in the last days perilous times shall come" and catalogs the moral decline of the latter days.

## Where this appears

- General Conference — October 2021 — 3:7 ("ever learning, never able to come to the knowledge of the truth") on worldly knowledge vs. humility
- Plan of Salvation — Topical Study — 3:13 ("evil men will get worse and worse") in a hope-and-resurrection chain
