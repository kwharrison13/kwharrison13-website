# Luke 11

Luke 11 appears in Kyle's [Come, Follow Me](/notes/come-follow-me) study on being responsible for one's own learning, cited specifically as Luke 11:9–13 — the "ask, seek, knock" promise. In the note it grounds the principle of nurturing your own testimony: ask, seek, knock; search the scriptures; and act in faith while keeping an eternal perspective as questions come. It sits in a chain of cross-references on personal revelation and learning that includes [John 5](/notes/john-5):39, [Acts 17](/notes/acts-17):10-12, [Alma 5](/notes/alma-5):45-46, and [Alma 32](/notes/alma-32):27.

**Context:** Luke 11 is a chapter of the New Testament Gospel of Luke containing the Lord's Prayer, the parable of the friend at midnight, and the "ask and it shall be given you / seek and ye shall find / knock and it shall be opened" teaching (verses 9–13).
