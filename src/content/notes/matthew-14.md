# Matthew 14

A New Testament chapter Kyle cites in his notes on General Conference- October 2021 for the account of Peter walking on the water (Matthew 14:28–31). The takeaway he records, from Alvin F. Meredith's remarks: "Focusing on the things that are most important, especially those things that are down the road, the eternal things, is the key to maneuvering this life." While Peter kept his focus on the Savior he was able to walk on the water; when he got distracted by the wind ("boisterous"), he was afraid, began to sink, and was caught — "O thou of little faith, wherefore didst thou doubt?" The chapter functions as a parable of focus versus distraction in [Discipleship](/notes/discipleship).

## Where this appears

- General Conference- October 2021 — Peter walking on the water as an illustration of staying focused on eternal things rather than distractions
