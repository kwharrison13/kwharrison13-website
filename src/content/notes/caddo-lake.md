# Caddo Lake

Caddo Lake appears in [Our Towns](/books/our-towns) as an example of a place defined by what it has refused to become. The book quotes Texas author Joe Nick Patoski (writing in The Texas Observer in 2005): "Caddo is almost as significant for what it is *not* — no condos, no high-rises, no chain motels or restaurants, no resorts, no gated, planned communities, no margarita bars, no chains, no pretension, none of the trappings of modern Texas Lake Culture." Though it sits in northeast Texas relatively close to urban centers, those who "get Caddo" treat it as a natural jewel worth protecting. Kyle's note distills the point: Caddo Lake is "significant for what it is *not*" — a counter-model to development-by-default.

**Context:** Caddo Lake is a large bald-cypress wetland straddling the Texas–Louisiana border near Uncertain, Texas, known for its maze of bayous and its status as a protected natural area.
