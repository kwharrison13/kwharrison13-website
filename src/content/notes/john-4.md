# John 4

John 4 is the New Testament chapter recording Jesus' encounter with the Samaritan woman at the well and His teaching to the disciples afterward — the recurring "living water" passage in Kyle's [gospel-study](/notes/gospel-study) notes. In Diligence it anchors the idea that Christ takes a limited understanding and raises it to infinite capacity: to the Samaritan woman He offers "living water springing up into everlasting life" (John 4:14) rather than water that quenches only for a moment (John 4:13), and to the disciples He says "my meat is to do the will of him that sent me" (John 4:34) and "look on the fields, for they are white already to harvest" (John 4:35) — a call not to wait on the sustenance of the [Natural Man](/notes/natural-man) but to do the Father's will now.

The chapter recurs as a cross-reference across multiple studies. In the Alma 32–35 Come Follow Me study it grounds "worship in spirit and truth" — the hour when true worshippers worship "in spirit and in truth," heart and mind, not mountain or temple (John 4:20-23). In Teaching Repentance the Samaritan-woman dialogue (John 4:5–10) and the disciples "going shopping" (John 4:31–35) model how Christ teaches by contrasting normal water and meat with His living water and His meat. In the Olive Tree Allegory notes the Samaritans of John 4 are cited as evidence that Christ labored among non-Israelites (the wild olive branches), and in the 3 Nephi 20–26 study the living-bread/[Sacrament](/notes/sacrament) theme connects back to John 4. (Distinct from 1 John 4, the "God is love" epistle chapter, which appears separately in Kyle's notes.)

**Context:** John 4 is the fourth chapter of the Gospel of John in the New Testament, containing Jesus' conversation with a Samaritan woman at Jacob's well in Sychar — a passage notable for crossing the social barrier between Jews and Samaritans and for the "living water" discourse.
