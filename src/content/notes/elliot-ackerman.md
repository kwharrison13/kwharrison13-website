# Elliot Ackerman

Elliot Ackerman is the novelist and co-author, with retired Admiral [James Stavridis](/notes/james-stavridis), of the near-future defense-scenario novels Kyle read: [2034: A Novel of the Next World War](/books/2034-a-novel-of-the-next-world-war) and its direct sequel [2054](/books/2054). Across both books the pairing fuses Stavridis's operational naval experience — which grounds the Pacific war and cyber scenarios in [2034](/books/2034-a-novel-of-the-next-world-war) — with Ackerman's fiction craft, in the tradition of [Science Fiction](/notes/science-fiction) as strategic foresight.

The first book dramatizes American overreach, miscalculation, and a Sino-Iranian-Russian alignment, arguing that the U.S.'s deregulated, disaggregated tech ecosystem became a cyber vulnerability against China's centralized infrastructure. The sequel [2054](/books/2054) shifts to a [Singularity](/notes/singularity) race, featuring [Ray Kurzweil](/notes/ray-kurzweil) as a character clinging to life to witness "bio-integrated quantum computing" fuse biological and technological evolution, and closing on a quoted line from [Alexis de Tocqueville](/notes/alexis-de-tocqueville)'s _Democracy in America_.

**Context:** Elliot Ackerman is an American author and former Marine Corps officer who served five tours in Iraq and Afghanistan, receiving the Silver Star and Bronze Star for Valor. His co-author James Stavridis is a retired four-star admiral and former NATO Supreme Allied Commander.
