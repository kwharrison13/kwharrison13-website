# D. Michael Quinn

Mormon historian who recurs throughout [Stretching The Heavens](/books/stretching-the-heavens) as a parallel to [Eugene England](/notes/eugene-england) — a thinker caught in the same institutional tension between faithful belief and unvarnished history. Quinn publicly challenged Boyd K. Packer's demand that historians feed Latter-day Saints only a "diet of milk," writing that "a diet of milk alone will stunt the growth of, if not kill, any child," and protesting a "siege mentality" approach to Church history. He insisted his aims were the same as Packer's and Ezra Taft Benson's — only his prescription differed: a "faith-promoting" history that conceals controversy, he argued, "actually undermines the faith."

In words the book notes "sound remarkably like England's self-appraisal," Quinn saw himself "hunting for ways to justify contradictions between simplified official accounts and messy human history," believing the believing and critical halves of his personality were complementary. He resigned from BYU in 1988, thereafter referring to his former employer with bitter overstatement as "an Auschwitz of the mind."

**Context:** D. Michael Quinn (1944–2021) was a prominent historian of Mormonism and one of the **September Six**, the group of intellectuals disciplined or excommunicated by the LDS Church in 1993. His scholarship on early Mormon history and Joseph Smith's magic worldview was influential and controversial.
