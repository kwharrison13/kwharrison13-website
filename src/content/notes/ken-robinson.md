# Ken Robinson

Education thinker invoked in [The Self-Driven Child](/books/the-self-driven-child) as one of the leading voices on finding one's passion. The book cites his work *The Element: How Finding Your Passion Changes Everything*, which argues that the magic of life and work happens at the intersection where natural talent and personal passion converge. Robinson is described there as a "visionary education consultant," and his framing is distilled into two questions a parent can teach a child to ask: *What do I truly love to do?* and *What can I do better than most people?* The book recommends sharing *The Element*'s stories — Simpsons creator Matt Groening, who hated school but was fanatical about drawing, and choreographer Gillian Lynne, who couldn't sit still in class but thrived in dance — to spark discussion about wanting something and connecting the dots to get there.

**Context:** Sir Ken Robinson (1950–2020) was a British author and education advisor best known for his 2006 TED talk "Do Schools Kill Creativity?", one of the most-viewed TED talks ever, and for books on creativity and education including *The Element* and *Out of Our Minds*.
