# Roam Research

The networked-thought / tools-for-thought app ([Conor White-Sullivan](/notes/conor-white-sullivan)) that is, more than any other company, **the operating substrate of Kyle's own wiki practice**. His essay [Networked Conviction — Roam + Investing](/essays/networked-conviction-roam-investing) makes the case directly: Kyle uses Roam to "collect ideas, question them, find companies around those ideas, and ask the same sets of questions. And then I share what I learn," with a public [Investing 101 2.0](/notes/investing-101-2-0) Sandbox of templates. [How To Read a Book](/books/how-to-read-a-book) surfaces the conceptual reason it fits him — Roam's *relational* (rather than hierarchical) ordering of ideas, where block references serve as "the modern equivalent of scriptural verse references." [Note-taking](/notes/note-taking) and the broader [Tools For Thought](/notes/tools-for-thought) thread place it in the late-2010s networked-note revival alongside [Zettelkasten](/notes/zettelkasten) and "second brain" methods.

Roam also appears as a **company Kyle was close to via Conor White-Sullivan**, whom he met with repeatedly in 2021–2022 (June 22nd, June 24th, January 31st). Those conversations — split into wiki/meetings — covered hiring, leadership, communication and inferential distance, Conor's "[Roadmaps are Fake News](/notes/roadmaps-are-fake-news)" view, "fire your most demanding customers," metrics, and a proposed multi-test framework for evaluating fit (including a possible $5M investment test). The company recurs as a reference point in others' projects too: [Ideaflow](/notes/ideaflow) is logged as "similar to Roam Research," and [David Vargas](/notes/david-vargas)'s vision of a [Decentralized Community](/notes/decentralized-community) / [Decentralized Brain](/notes/decentralized-brain) (discussed in [The Death of Expertise](/books/the-death-of-expertise)) is built on Roam.

**Context:** Roam Research is a note-taking web app launched around 2019–2020 built on bidirectional links and a graph database of "blocks," which became a flagship product of the "tools for thought" movement; Conor White-Sullivan co-founded it.
