# Jason Schenker

Author of [Recession Proof](/books/recession-proof), where (per Kyle's reading) he argues as an economist that recession-proofing "comes down to keeping options open." The book's core prescriptions, which Kyle catalogs: [Continuous Learning](/notes/continuous-learning) ("always be learning… if you're not sharpening your tools, they're going to get rusty," building skills for the job you want), [Networking](/notes/networking) (the best time to build a network is during good years, not during a recession), and a [Margin of Safety](/notes/margin-of-safety) investing rule of "buy low, buy in bust times" while only investing in what you understand. He frames empathy and creative problem-solving as the "soft" skills least exposed to [Automation](/notes/automation), and education as "a great equalizer." Kyle marks it a light-engagement read.

**Context:** Jason Schenker is an American economist and futurist, founder of Prestige Economics and the Futurist Institute, and a prolific author of short business and forecasting books.
