# Ed Corno

Ed Corno is a 20+-year CTO/CSO, currently Chief/Client Technology Leader at IBM (Missouri-based), who appears in Kyle's wiki as an expert-call source in the Planhat - Diligence customer-success landscape diligence. He drove HP's US Postal account from $22M to $100M+ over five years and now has a role in IBM's CSM push. His framing of the market: the need has **shifted from churn to upsell** — v1.0 customer-success tooling was about preventing churn; now it's about adding services, a better customer-service experience, and moving customers onto higher-value products. His key decision criteria are getting the right data to understand the customer across the project lifecycle and then monitoring and guiding that relationship.

On vendor differentiation, Corno ranks [Gainsight](/notes/gainsight) first by working experience (he knows the CEO; strong R&D) but slots [Planhat](/notes/planhat) at #2 on product feature set — citing its vision/roadmap, customer-facing team, automation focus, and modules like its customer data platform and "success-inbox" — with [Medallia](/notes/medallia) (which IBM uses) last given its 20–25 years in the space. "Their technology is very good," he says of Planhat.
