# Henry B. Eyring

An LDS apostle and First Presidency leader whose teaching threads through Kyle's scripture study on the relationship between **faith and scholarship**, **pondering as an invitation to revelation**, and **staying on the safe side of temptation's line**. In Spiritual Gifts, Eyring grounds the "Defender" / faithful-scholar figure: a brilliant mind can reassure you by defending gospel truths with fact and logic, but "even the most brilliant and faithful person may defend the truth [with an] argument or fact that later proves false" — the scholar's faith was never *based* in the logic; it was the other way around. He also supplies the "Peacemakers" frame: the gift of helping people see another's words as "a contribution, rather than a correction."

His most-quoted line in the corpus is on revelation: "When we ponder, we invite revelation by the Spirit" — pondering being "the thinking and the praying I do after reading and studying in the scriptures carefully" (August 24–30, 2020. Helaman 7–12: "Remember the Lord"). The Lehonti Principle uses Eyring's counsel that Satan keeps pressing you to "come closer to the line" and that you "bring the protective powers of Heaven down… by simply declaring to go toward the Savior." How To Apply The Atonement cites his teaching that the Savior "chose to learn by His own personal experience" how to succor us; Find To Teach and Diligence draw on his counsel that obedience precedes spiritual sight and that the Spirit always "lets us feel an urging to DO things."

**Context:** Henry B. Eyring (b. 1933) is a member of the Quorum of the Twelve Apostles of The Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-day Saints and a longtime member of its First Presidency; he previously served as Commissioner of Church Education and president of Ricks College.
