# Forgiveness

Across Kyle's gospel-study notes, forgiveness runs along two axes — being forgiven by God, and forgiving others — both tied to the [Atonement](/notes/atonement) and [Repentance](/notes/repentance). Everyone Is Human grounds it in D&C 58:42–43 ("he who has repented of his sins, the same is forgiven, and I, the Lord, remember them no more"), framing forgiveness as the product of confessing *and forsaking* sin — confession is internal and voluntary, and you must "dump the whole bucket rather than a little at a time." How To Apply The Atonement handles the harder, relational case: the Lord "will forgive whom I will forgive," but *we* must forgive everyone — and once Christ has forgiven you, "the details don't matter." Its central image (from Kyle's 2013 testimony) reworks the old nail-in-the-board lesson: the Atonement does not just remove the nails and leave holes, it throws away the worn board and gives a new piece of wood — so the forgiven person becomes a new creature and should "FORGET THE OLD ONE."

Mighty Repentance extends forgiveness outward as an expanding desire — Enos moves from seeking his own remission, to his family's, to his enemies' — and warns that "holding a grudge is like drinking poison and waiting for the other person to die." Sermon on the Mount names forgiveness a "companion principle" to righteous judgment ([Dallin H. Oaks](/notes/dallin-h-oaks)'s distinction between forbidden final judgments and directed intermediate ones). [The Broken Heart](/books/the-broken-heart) supplies the counterweight: [Bruce C. Hafen](/notes/bruce-c-hafen) warns that *constant emphasis on the availability of forgiveness can be counterproductive*, tempting some to "live it up now and repent easily later" — understating the Atonement is its own risk.

## Where this appears

- Everyone Is Human — forgiveness as confess-and-forsake, built on D&C 58:42–43.
- How To Apply The Atonement — forgiving others vs. being forgiven; the new-board metaphor for becoming a new creature.
- Mighty Repentance — forgiveness as expanding desire (self → family → enemies); the grudge-as-poison line.
- Sermon on the Mount — forgiveness as a companion principle to righteous judgment.
- [The Broken Heart](/books/the-broken-heart) — the warning that over-emphasizing the availability of forgiveness can be counterproductive.
