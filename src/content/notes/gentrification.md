# Gentrification

A recurring theme in Kyle's [City Building](/notes/city-building) reading, where the two books that anchor it pull in different directions. In [The New Urban Crisis](/books/the-new-urban-crisis), [Richard Florida](/notes/richard-florida) (via the scholar [Lance Freeman](/notes/lance-freeman), "the leading student of gentrification and displacement") argues gentrification "actually displaces far fewer people than is commonly thought," is concentrated in a handful of superstar cities and even in particular neighborhoods within them, and is heavily *socially constructed* — "when it comes to how neighborhoods are defined, the gentrifiers ultimately win out," with race shaping perception. Florida's sobering counterpoint: the great bulk of poor Black neighborhoods remain immune to gentrification, their residents "trapped in chronic and persistent poverty." Kyle's marginal notes probe whether perception can be changed (could something like [Nextdoor](/notes/nextdoor) help?) and why scaled solutions so often fail.

In [What I Found in a Thousand Towns](/books/what-i-found-in-a-thousand-towns), [Dar Williams](/notes/dar-williams) frames gentrification as **two-level financial displacement** — pushing out the residents who built a town's identity "event by event, layer by layer," and "abandoning the soul" of an eclectic, inclusive place when "commodification and consumerism steamroll through the sense of positive proximity." Her counterweight is *positive proximity*: valuing what neighbors built together rather than what "pure profiteers" buy and inhabit, with affordable-housing mandates as the practical defense.

**Context:** Gentrification refers to the influx of higher-income residents and capital into previously lower-income urban neighborhoods, raising property values and rents and often changing the area's character — a central, contested topic in urban-planning and housing-policy debates.

## Where this appears

- [The New Urban Crisis](/books/the-new-urban-crisis) — Florida/Freeman's case that gentrification displaces fewer than assumed and is socially constructed; the trapped-poverty counterpoint
- [What I Found in a Thousand Towns](/books/what-i-found-in-a-thousand-towns) — Dar Williams's two-level financial-and-soul displacement, countered by "positive proximity"
