# The Crucible

Arthur Miller's play, which Kyle invokes in his Helaman 5 study on "the importance of [Names](/notes/names)." Against Helaman 5:6 — Helaman gives his sons the names of their first parents so that remembering their names triggers remembering their works — Kyle quotes [John Proctor](/notes/john-proctor)'s climactic refusal to sign a false confession: "Because it is my name! ... How may I live without my name? I have given you my soul; leave me my name!" The juxtaposition treats a name as inseparable from one's integrity and works, a theme that recurs in his notes on [The Importance of a Name](/notes/the-importance-of-a-name).

**Context:** *The Crucible* (1953) is Arthur Miller's dramatization of the Salem witch trials, written as an allegory for McCarthy-era accusations; John Proctor is the protagonist who chooses death over a signed lie.
