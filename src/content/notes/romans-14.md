# Romans 14

Across Kyle's gospel study, Romans 14 is invoked as the scripture on judging the actions of others and on doing all things unto the Lord. In Physical Appearance of Missionaries it is paired with [1 Corinthians 10](/notes/1-corinthians-10):21–33 to make the case that a disciple should "stand above the standard" and never let a "personal word of wisdom" offend those they hope to help — because, while people shouldn't judge, they will, and your conduct can drive them from the gospel.

In Sermon on the Mount, Romans 14 is cited alongside [Philippians 1](/notes/philippians-1):9–10 to reframe Christ's teaching on judgment ([Matthew 7](/notes/matthew-7):1, [3 Nephi 14](/notes/3-nephi-14)). Drawing on [Dallin H. Oaks](/notes/dallin-h-oaks)'s distinction between forbidden *final* judgments and permitted *intermediate* judgments, Kyle's note reads the chapter as teaching not "do not judge" but "use your agency righteously" — judging guided by the Spirit, within one's stewardship, with forgiveness as a companion principle.

**Context:** Romans 14 is a chapter in Paul's Epistle to the Romans addressing how believers should treat one another over disputable matters (eating, drinking, observing days), urging them not to judge or despise a brother and to act according to conscience "unto the Lord."

## Where this appears

- Physical Appearance of Missionaries — judging the actions of others; basis for standing above the standard rather than offending with a "personal word of wisdom."
- Sermon on the Mount — "do all things unto the Lord"; reframes judgment as righteous use of agency, paired with [Dallin H. Oaks](/notes/dallin-h-oaks)'s final-vs-intermediate distinction.
