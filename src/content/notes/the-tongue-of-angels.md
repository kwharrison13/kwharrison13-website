# The Tongue of Angels

A [Jeffrey R. Holland](/notes/jeffrey-r-holland) sermon on the power and discipline of speech — specifically the call to "speak hopefully" and to refuse the habit of murmuring. Kyle draws on it in his study of [1 Nephi 16](/notes/1-nephi-16), where [Nephi](/notes/nephi)'s broken bow becomes a contrast between deliberate, constructive response and the constant complaining of Laman and Lemuel. The maxim Kyle pulls forward: *"No misfortune is so bad that whining about it won't make it worse."* Holland frames it as the difference between Nephi (who built a new bow rather than murmur) and his brothers, and pairs it with [Orson F. Whitney](/notes/orson-f-whitney)'s line that *"the spirit of the gospel is optimistic… the opposite or pessimistic spirit drags men down and away from God, looks on the dark side, murmurs, complains, and is slow to yield obedience."*

The talk reads the Savior's command to "be of good cheer" as a commandment we may break more often than almost any other, and asks for encouraging speech "including about yourself." Within Kyle's notes it sits alongside the [Anxiously Engaged](/notes/anxiously-engaged) / free-will reading of Nephi's initiative — speech and disposition as deliberate choices rather than natural consequences.

**Context:** "The Tongue of Angels" is a 2007 General Conference address by Elder Jeffrey R. Holland of the Quorum of the Twelve Apostles of the Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-day Saints, on the spiritual stewardship of how we speak to and about one another.
