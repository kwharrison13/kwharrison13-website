# Isaiah 1

Isaiah 1 appears in Kyle's gospel study on the Abrahamic Covenant as scriptural support for **remnant theology** — the idea that God always preserves a remnant of the more righteous. Kyle cites Isaiah 1:9 ("Except the Lord of hosts had left unto us a very small remnant, we should have been as Sodom") within a discussion of chosen lineage and inheritance: God has always prepared ways to protect the covenant lineage, and a remnant of the faithful is consistently preserved across generations.

**Context:** Isaiah 1 is the opening chapter of the Book of Isaiah in the Hebrew Bible / Old Testament — a prophetic indictment of Judah's rebellion and empty ritual, ending with a call to repentance ("though your sins be as scarlet, they shall be as white as snow"). Verse 9, which Kyle cites, is the source of the remnant motif quoted in the New Testament (Romans 9:29).
