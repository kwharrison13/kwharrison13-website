# Moroni 8

A chapter near the close of the Book of Mormon — Mormon's epistle written to his son Moroni "soon after [Moroni's] calling to the ministry," reproduced in Kyle's Come Follow Me study of Moroni 7–9. Kyle reads two threads out of it. First, on the salvation of little children: "the whole need no physician, but they that are sick" (Moroni 8:8) — children are whole because they cannot sin, so the curse of Adam has no power over them. Kyle extends this into a principle of **prioritization by need** — "the Church is a hospital for sinners, not a museum for saints; different people need different things from God" (linked to The Laborers in the Vineyard). Second, on enduring to the end: Mormon prays not for Moroni's *safety* in a dangerous time but for his "endurance of faith on his name to the end" (Moroni 8:3), which Kyle connects to [M. Russell Ballard](/notes/m-russell-ballard)'s patriarch in Follow the Doctrine and Gospel of Christ.

In Spiritual Gifts, Kyle cites Moroni 8:26 — that those "known of the Lord" and "of value to the Lord are filled with hope and perfect love" — within his study of [Charity](/notes/charity) and the Fruits of the Spirit.

**Context:** Moroni 8 is the second of two chapters in the Book of Mormon (the other being part of Mormon's broader sermon) that frame the doctrine on infant baptism; Mormon condemns the baptism of little children as a denial of the mercy of Christ and the Atonement.
