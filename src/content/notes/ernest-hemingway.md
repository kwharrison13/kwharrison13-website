# Ernest Hemingway

Brief anecdote in *How to Take Smart Notes*: asked how often he rewrote his first draft, Hemingway said he rewrote the ending of *A Farewell to Arms* thirty-nine times before he was satisfied — when asked what the technical problem was, he replied simply, "Getting the words right" (Paris Review, 1956).
