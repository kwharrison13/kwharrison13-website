# Spiritual Self-Reliance

Spiritual self-reliance is the best-attested theme in Kyle's wiki page on [Russell M. Nelson](/notes/russell-m-nelson) — the idea that a person must obtain their *own* revelation, because borrowed faith is not enough. The signature claim attached to it is Nelson's warning that without one's own personal revelation "it will be impossible to survive spiritually." The thread surfaces alongside [The Crucible of Doubt](/books/the-crucible-of-doubt), where personal revelation is framed as necessary for spiritual survival rather than a supplement to received belief.

The concept is thinly sourced here — Nelson's references across the corpus are mostly brief or contextual rather than full treatments — but the consistent emphasis is that spiritual durability depends on a first-hand, individual relationship with revelation rather than on inherited or institutional faith.

**Context:** "Spiritual self-reliance" is language used within The Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-day Saints, of which Russell M. Nelson is president, emphasizing each member's responsibility to seek personal revelation directly.
