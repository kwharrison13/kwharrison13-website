# Tim Ferriss

Tim Ferriss anchors the framing of [How To Be a Capitalist Without Any Capital](/books/how-to-be-a-capitalist-without-any-capital) through his concept of the "New Rich." The book opens by borrowing Ferriss's term for the segment of people who have "figured out how to maximize everything in their life — even if it's not much — so it becomes an asset that works for them": resourceful with time, money, and energy, they get what they want when they want it, travel freely, keep "blank calendars," and carry very few expenses. Ferriss's book [The 4-Hour Workweek](/notes/the-4-hour-workweek) also closes the book's "Top Seven Books on Systems" list, recommended as the guide to systematizing your business and life "to save time, energy, and money."

In Kyle's concept note for [The 4-Hour Workweek](/notes/the-4-hour-workweek), Kyle reads [Brigham Young](/notes/brigham-young)'s counsel — that "one-third or one-fourth of the time… would be sufficient, if your labor were rightly directed" — as anticipating Ferriss's thesis a century early: wealth comes from working *smart*, not from grinding the clock.

**Context:** Tim Ferriss (b. 1977) is an author, entrepreneur, and podcaster, best known for *The 4-Hour Workweek* (2007) and the long-running *Tim Ferriss Show* podcast; his work popularized lifestyle design, the "New Rich," and outsourcing.
