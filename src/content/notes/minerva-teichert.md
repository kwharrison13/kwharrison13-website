# Minerva Teichert

LDS painter referenced in Elder Gilbert's "Come Home" talk, where her painting of a pioneer mother entering the Salt Lake Valley — beckoning others to follow — is cited among the talk's passages and visual references. In Kyle's gospel-study note the image sits alongside Russell M. Nelson, Dallin H. Oaks, the hymn "Softly and Tenderly Jesus Is Calling," and a Shakespeare epigraph as part of the talk's invitation to return home to the covenant path.

**Context:** Minerva Teichert (1888–1976) was an American painter trained at the Art Institute of Chicago and the Art Students League of New York, known for large-scale murals and her many depictions of Book of Mormon scenes and Latter-day Saint pioneer history.
