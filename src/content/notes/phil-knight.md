# Phil Knight

Phil Knight appears in [Stephen Schwarzman](/notes/stephen-schwarzman)'s [What It Takes](/books/what-it-takes) as a short illustration of hiring for grit. The memoir notes that when Knight was building [Nike](/notes/nike), he hired other distance runners to work with him — reasoning that whatever they lacked in business knowledge they made up for in stamina: they would never give up, would "take the pain and make it to the end of the race." Schwarzman uses the anecdote to underscore the book's talent-and-resilience theme.

**Context:** Phil Knight co-founded Nike (originally Blue Ribbon Sports) in 1964 with his University of Oregon track coach Bill Bowerman, and he was himself a middle-distance runner under Bowerman. His memoir *Shoe Dog* recounts the company's founding.
