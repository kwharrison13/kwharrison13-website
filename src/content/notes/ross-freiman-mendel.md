# Ross Freiman-Mendel

Ross Freiman-Mendel is a [Dropbox](/notes/dropbox) alum (Strategic Finance and EPD) who later went to [Persona](/notes/persona). He appears in Kyle's corpus as one of the operators surveyed for [The Talent Vortex — Mafias and Magnets](/essays/talent-vortex), answering Kyle's question about why Dropbox became a talent vortex (The Talent Vortex - Research).

His answer was deflationary in a useful way: "Nothing that felt extraordinary, honestly. Dropbox set a very high bar for recruiting, and then a lot of it was success breeds success." He described how very high-quality top-of-funnel candidates created a flywheel in EPD ("an objectively very high bar… almost to a fault"), how working in the orbit of strong leaders set people up for success afterward, and how candidate caliber attenuated over time. He also pushed back on the perks narrative — noting that Dropbox had "legendary perks" but that [Persona](/notes/persona), with a higher caliber bar and far lesser perks, proved people care about culture, work, and prospects rather than perks. His "talent != willingness to work hard, business intuition, hunger" line fed the essay's argument that raw selectivity is necessary but not sufficient.
