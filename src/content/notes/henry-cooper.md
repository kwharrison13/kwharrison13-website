# Henry Cooper

Ambassador and former director of the U.S. Missile Defense Agency, cited in [Annie Jacobsen](/notes/annie-jacobsen)'s [Nuclear War — a Scenario](/books/nuclear-war-a-scenario) (which Kyle read) for his on-record worst-case assessment of a high-altitude electromagnetic pulse (EMP) detonated over the United States: "The result could be to shut down the U.S. electric power grid for an indefinite period, leading to the death within a year of up to 90 percent of all Americans." In the book's people index Kyle flags Cooper's quote as relevant to defense-tech diligence — the 90%-casualty grid-collapse scenario as a reference point for that threat model.

**Context:** Henry F. "Hank" Cooper is an American defense official who served as director of the Strategic Defense Initiative Organization (the predecessor of the Missile Defense Agency) under President George H. W. Bush and has been a prominent advocate for missile defense and EMP-hardening of the electric grid.
