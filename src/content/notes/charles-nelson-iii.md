# Charles Nelson III

Cited in Kyle's [The Science of Learning](/notes/the-science-of-learning) notes for the claim that early environment shapes cognition: *"Children who grow up in cognitively stimulating and linguistically rich environments tend to be more sophisticated in their knowledge of the world and their ability to grasp things."* In those notes the quote sits under the [Parenting](/notes/parenting) heading, alongside research on project-based learning, [Montessori](/notes/montessori) education, and [Growth Mindset](/notes/growth-mindset) — used to support the thread that a child's surroundings, not just innate ability, drive how readily they learn.

**Context:** Charles A. Nelson III is a developmental neuroscientist at Harvard Medical School and Boston Children's Hospital, known for research on early brain development, the effects of early adversity and deprivation (including the Bucharest Early Intervention Project), and how enriched environments shape children's cognitive outcomes.
