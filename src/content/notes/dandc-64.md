# D&C 64

Section 64 of the Doctrine and Covenants appears in Kyle's Using Satan Against Satan study as a **standard-of-the-heart** text. He cites D&C 64:2 — "overcome the world, I will have compassion... Be perfect, nevertheless be of good cheer, for I shall lead you along" — paired with [Isaiah 59](/notes/isaiah-59):2, to make the point that when you slip below the standard you feel God's disappointment, and at or above it you feel His approval. It anchors his larger argument about quick repentance keeping a person clean and treating temptation as a catalyst to do good instead.
