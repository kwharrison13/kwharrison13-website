# Jacob 4

A chapter of the Book of Mormon (the book of Jacob) cited across Kyle's [gospel-study](/notes/gospel-study) notes. In Patience — Topical Study, the key verse is Jacob 4:14 — the Jews who "despised the words of plainness… by looking beyond the mark" — used to teach that we must accept generalities we cannot grasp and glory in the plainness of the gospel rather than seeking after things we are not meant to understand. In Spiritual Gifts, Jacob 4 appears twice: Jacob 4:13 ("the Spirit speaketh of things as they really are, and of things as they really will be") supports the function of the [Holy Ghost](/notes/holy-ghost) to reveal truth and future events, and Jacob 4:14 reappears in the study's appendix on personal callings.

**Context:** Jacob 4 is a chapter in the Book of Mormon's book of Jacob (a small-plates record). Verse 14's phrase "looking beyond the mark" is a widely cited LDS warning against over-complicating plain gospel truths; verse 13 is often quoted for its description of prophecy and the Spirit speaking of things "as they really are."

## Where this appears

- Patience — Topical Study — 4:14's "looking beyond the mark" teaches acceptance of gospel plainness over seeking what we cannot understand
- Spiritual Gifts — 4:13 grounds the Spirit's gift of revealing truth and future events; 4:14 recurs in the personal-callings study
