# Matter

In Kyle's notes, "Matter" tags a specific Latter-day Saint doctrine of the physical and the spiritual: that there is no immaterial reality. It traces to the [Doctrine and Covenants](/notes/doctrine-and-covenants) passage Kyle flagged in his 3 Nephi–[4 Nephi](/notes/4-nephi) study — "There is no such thing as immaterial matter. All spirit is matter, but it is more fine or pure, and can only be discerned by purer eyes... We cannot see it; but when our bodies are purified we shall see that it is all matter" (D&C 131:7–8). The point is that spirit is not a different substance from matter, only a finer, purer form of it.

The same statement anchors the discussion of [Metaphysics](/notes/metaphysics) in [Joseph Smith — Rough Stone Rolling](/books/joseph-smith-rough-stone-rolling), where Bushman frames "there is no such thing as immaterial matter" as a radical revision of Christian metaphysics that Joseph Smith dropped as a casual aside to a Methodist preacher — paired with eternal uncreated intelligences and the rejection of [Creation Ex Nihilo](/notes/creation-ex-nihilo) in favor of ordering chaos.
