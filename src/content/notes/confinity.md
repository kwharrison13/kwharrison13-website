# Confinity

Confinity is the company [Peter Thiel](/notes/peter-thiel) and [Max Levchin](/notes/max-levchin) co-founded — Thiel as the strategy/CEO figure, Levchin as CTO — that became [PayPal](/notes/paypal) via its 2000 merger with [Elon Musk](/notes/elon-musk)'s [X.com](/notes/x-com). Across Kyle's [The Founders](/books/the-founders) notes it is the origin point of the [PayPal Mafia](/notes/paypal-mafia): Levchin's "As hire As, Bs hire Cs" hiring bar and all-nighter engineering culture, and Thiel's contrarian, friends-as-cofounders recruiting, both trace to the Confinity team.

Confinity and X.com competed head-to-head for eBay sellers and cannibalized each other before merging in 2000; the combined entity became PayPal. During the merger, Musk's decision to rewind Levchin's stack from Unix to Windows nearly drove Levchin to quit and fed the boardroom coup that ousted Musk as CEO, leaving Thiel to run the company through its IPO. Earlier, Thiel had sought a ~$100M buyout of Confinity from BeFree, which BeFree's board declined.

**Context:** Confinity was founded in 1998, initially building security software for handheld devices before pivoting to PayPal's email-payments product.
