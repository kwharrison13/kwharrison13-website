# Catalyst

A US [Customer Success](/notes/customer-success) software vendor that appears throughout Kyle's Planhat - Diligence as the most-cited up-and-comer and the company experts most often weighed head-to-head against [Planhat](/notes/planhat). References describe Catalyst as the "new, cool" US player that "shot to the top pretty quickly," distinguished above all by **marketing and community**: it hired a CS community manager / social influencer as a marketing play, invested heavily in training and thought-leadership, and runs a modern LinkedIn presence. Experts repeatedly noted a strong, mission-driven team and very low churn (~1% cited), summarizing that "these tools are more than tools — they are the people running them." Its founder reaching out personally on LinkedIn was enough to make at least one buyer lean toward Catalyst.

On product, Catalyst is treated as feature-comparable to peers on the table-stakes axes (CTAs, health scores, [playbooks](/notes/playbook), integrations), but several references flagged it as behind [Gainsight](/notes/gainsight) on feature depth when evaluated ~6 months prior — notably lacking built-out email automation and deeper workflow complexity. On price it sits mid-tier (one buyer quoted Catalyst at $20K vs. Planhat $30K and [Vitally](/notes/vitally) $40K). One reference who bought Catalyst used it as the central operating system for all of CSM. The recurring verdict: very close to Planhat, with Catalyst winning on marketing/GTM and Planhat winning on product polish — and Catalyst notably not focused on Europe.

**Context:** Catalyst Software was a New York-based customer-success platform founded in 2017; it later merged with sales-engagement company Totango in 2024 to form Totango + Catalyst.
