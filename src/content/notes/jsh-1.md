# JSH 1

Joseph Smith—History, chapter 1 — the Pearl of Great Price account of the First Vision — recurs across Kyle's gospel study as the canonical text on seeking wisdom from God and on Joseph Smith's founding question. The most-cited verse is [JSH 1](/notes/jsh-1):12, quoted in full three times in the General Conference — October 2021 notes: "Never did any passage of scripture come with more power to the heart of man than this did at this time to mine… for the teachers of religion of the different sects understood the same passages of scripture so differently as to destroy all confidence in settling the question by an appeal to the Bible." This is Joseph's reaction to James 1:5 and the impetus for the First Vision.

The Alma 17–22 study cites [JSH 1](/notes/jsh-1):18 — Joseph's object "to know which of all the sects was right, that I might know which to join" — fitting the week's instrument-of-God theme. The Fulfilling the Measure of Our Creation - September 5, 2021 talk references [JSH 1](/notes/jsh-1):15-16, the verses describing the appearance of the Father and Son in the Sacred Grove.

**Context:** "Joseph Smith—History" is a canonized excerpt from Joseph Smith's 1838 history, included in the Latter-day Saint Pearl of Great Price. Chapter 1 narrates his boyhood religious confusion, his reading of James 1:5, the First Vision, and the visits of the angel Moroni.

## Where this appears

- General Conference — October 2021 — JSH 1:12 quoted three times: scripture coming "with power to the heart" and the sects' conflicting interpretations.
- Alma 17–22 — JSH 1:18, Joseph asking which sect was right, tied to the instrument-of-God theme.
- Fulfilling the Measure of Our Creation - September 5, 2021 — references JSH 1:15-16, the First Vision appearance.
