# Walter Isaacson

Walter Isaacson is the biographer behind several books in Kyle's library, and a recurring lens on genius and craft. In [Leonardo Da Vinci](/books/leonardo-da-vinci), Isaacson refuses the "lightning-strike" theory of genius — *"Slapping the 'genius' label on Leonardo oddly minimizes him … Leonardo's genius was a human one, wrought by his own will and ambition"* — reading the biography as a how-to-think manual built on curiosity and observation. In [Benjamin Franklin — An American Life](/books/benjamin-franklin-an-american-life) his central thesis is that clear writing was itself part of Franklin's importance as a scientist (a method Isaacson practices himself), and he repeatedly separates the man from the mythologized Poor Richard persona — redirecting [Max Weber](/notes/max-weber)'s "utilitarianism" charge at the character rather than the man.

Isaacson also anchors [The Greatest Sentence Ever Written](/books/the-greatest-sentence-ever-written), his short treatment of [Thomas Jefferson](/notes/thomas-jefferson)'s Declaration line and [Benjamin Franklin](/notes/benjamin-franklin) as the model of "true merit." Elsewhere he surfaces secondhand: [The Everything Store](/books/the-everything-store) cites him as Steve Jobs's biographer (the Apple agency-model "aikido move"; the collaboration-over-lone-genius thread Kyle flags as worth researching), and The Meme Economy - Research quotes his "Reality Distortion Field" description of Jobs.

**Context:** Walter Isaacson (b. 1952) is an American author and journalist, former CEO of the Aspen Institute and editor of *Time*, best known for biographies of Benjamin Franklin, Albert Einstein, Steve Jobs, Leonardo da Vinci, and Elon Musk.
