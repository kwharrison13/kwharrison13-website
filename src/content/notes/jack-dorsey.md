# Jack Dorsey

Jack Dorsey shows up in Kyle's notes along two threads. In [Essentialism](/books/essentialism), [Greg McKeown](/notes/greg-mckeown) holds up Dorsey — "best known as the creator of Twitter and as the founder and CEO of Square" — as a rare Essentialist leader, citing his model of the CEO as "chief editor of the company": out of a thousand things the company could do, the editor's job is to take the flood of inputs from engineers, support, and designers and decide "the one, or intersection of a few, that make sense for what we are doing."

The second thread is Bluesky: in the June 8th, 2023 — Amir Shevat (Darkmode Ventures) call, Dorsey is referenced as the figure behind "a new social protocol that you can disconnect your identity curation / moderation content from the social network" — framed as doing to social what an open protocol did to email.

**Context:** Jack Dorsey co-founded Twitter (serving as its CEO twice) and founded Square (now Block), the payments company. He helped initiate Bluesky as a decentralized social-protocol project that later spun out as an independent company.
