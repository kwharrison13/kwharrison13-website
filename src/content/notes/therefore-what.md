# Therefore, What?

Kyle's recurring "so what should I do about it?" prompt — the demand that knowledge convert into action. It surfaces in two registers across the corpus. In [How To Read a Book](/books/how-to-read-a-book) it maps onto Mortimer Adler's fourth active-reading question, *"What of it?"* — that after asking what a book says and whether it is true, the serious reader must ask what follows and what to do: *"it is not enough to know, we must do."* Kyle tags it repeatedly against Adler's distinction between knowing-that and knowing-how, and against the move from understanding a book to judging and acting on it.

The phrase also has a gospel lineage Kyle traces to [Boyd K. Packer](/notes/boyd-k-packer) via [Jeffrey R. Holland](/notes/jeffrey-r-holland): Packer's habit, after any presentation in the Quorum of the Twelve, of looking up and asking *"Therefore, what?"* — Holland reads this as what the Savior answered "day in and day out," since "these sermons and exhortations were to no avail if the actual lives of His disciples did not change." Kyle applies it to [Mormon 7](/notes/mormon-7)'s "therefore repent": *I know that you know all these things to be true, THEREFORE you must repent.* The unifying idea is the same in both registers — the discipline isn't taking in more information, it's interrogating what you've taken in for the obligation it imposes.

## Where this appears

- [How To Read a Book](/books/how-to-read-a-book) — Adler's fourth question "What of it?"; Kyle's "it is not enough to know, we must do" notes on the knowing-that / knowing-how distinction.
- November 2–8, 2020. Mormon 7–9: "I Speak unto You As If Ye Were Present" — Packer's "Therefore, what?" via Holland, applied to Mormon's "therefore repent."
