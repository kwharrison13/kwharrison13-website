# The Intelligent Investor

*The Intelligent Investor* is [Benjamin Graham](/notes/ben-graham)'s value-investing classic, and across Kyle's reading it surfaces as the canonical text both praised and qualified. [Warren Buffett](/notes/warren-buffett) calls it "still the best book on investing," crediting it with "the only three ideas you really need" — Mr. Market, treating a stock as a piece of a business, and [Margin of Safety](/notes/margin-of-safety) — a verdict repeated in both [Poor Charlie's Almanack](/books/poor-charlie-s-almanack) ([Charlie Munger](/notes/charlie-munger) echoing the line) and the [Munger](/notes/charlie-munger-a-lesson-on-elementary-worldly-wisdom-revisited) long-read.

[Morgan Housel](/notes/morgan-housel) in [The Psychology of Money](/books/the-psychology-of-money) supplies the qualification: the book is "full of wisdom — perhaps more than any other investment book ever published," but "as a how-to guide, it's questionable at best." Housel notes that Graham himself kept discarding his own published formulas edition to edition — "those do not work anymore... these are the formulas that seem to work better now" — making the book's enduring value its principles and temperament rather than its mechanical screens. It is the original source of the [Margin of Safety](/notes/margin-of-safety) concept that recurs across Kyle's investing notes.

**Context:** *The Intelligent Investor* (first published 1949) is Benjamin Graham's foundational work on value investing; later editions were annotated by Jason Zweig. Warren Buffett, Graham's student at Columbia, has long called it the best investing book ever written.

## Where this appears

- [The Psychology of Money](/books/the-psychology-of-money) — Housel praises its wisdom but calls it questionable as a how-to guide; source of Graham's Margin of Safety concept.
- [Charlie Munger — A Lesson on Elementary Worldly Wisdom, Revisited](/notes/charlie-munger-a-lesson-on-elementary-worldly-wisdom-revisited) — Buffett quoted on its three core ideas and the simplicity ethos.
