# Neil Postman

The media-ecologist whose print-vs-television argument drives [Amusing Ourselves To Death](/books/amusing-ourselves-to-death) — the book Kyle reads as a live update of print culture vs. social-media culture. Postman's central thesis, as Kyle's notes frame it, is that [Aldous Huxley](/notes/aldous-huxley)'s *Brave New World* (control through pleasure and distraction), not [George Orwell](/notes/george-orwell)'s *1984* (control through pain and surveillance), was the accurate prophecy for the modern West. Kyle's reading extends Postman's claim that print discipline structures public discourse around logic and coherence: strip that away and discourse reorganizes around loudness and conflict, where lying and self-contradiction become *ideal* because agreement and disagreement both amplify reach.

In the [Educated Electorate](/notes/educated-electorate) concept page, Postman appears as the diagnostician of how an electorate trained by print culture gets eroded by entertainment media — the structural defense of an informed citizenry against media-driven triviality.

**Context:** Neil Postman (1931–2003) was an American author, educator, and media theorist, best known for *Amusing Ourselves to Death* (1985) and his work in the field of media ecology.
