# John Mulaney

A comedian whose one-line — "Likability is a jail" — anchors a small but load-bearing node in Kyle's wiki. The line is the compact statement of the agreeableness-vs-creativity thesis: optimizing for the approval of others imprisons you. In Conviction-Led Contrarianism - Research, [Alex Karp](/notes/alex-karp) borrows Mulaney's phrase directly in his essay "Likability Is a Jail," extending it into an argument that a "culture of agreeableness… can move institutions away, not toward, creative output."

Across the [Likability](/notes/likability) concept and the [Alex Karp](/notes/alex-karp) page, Mulaney functions purely as the origin of that line — the comedian's framing that Karp elevated into a business-and-creativity argument.

**Context:** John Mulaney (b. 1982) is an American stand-up comedian and writer, formerly of *Saturday Night Live*, known for his polished narrative storytelling.
