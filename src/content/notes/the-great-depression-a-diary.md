# The Great Depression: A Diary

The real-time diary of Benjamin Roth, a young Youngstown, Ohio lawyer who, two years into the Great Depression, began recording what he saw happening to the economy and the people around him. Edited posthumously by his son Daniel B. Roth and James Ledbetter (PublicAffairs, 2009), it covers roughly 1931–1941 — a ground-level, contemporaneous account rather than a retrospective history.

**Why it's on the list:** *A primary-source ledger of how an ordinary observer experienced a financial collapse as it unfolded — the kind of contemporaneous record [Morgan Housel](/notes/morgan-housel) prizes (he also loves reading old newspapers for the same reason).*

**Where I saw it:** Recommended by [Morgan Housel](/notes/morgan-housel) on [Thinking and Walking — Morgan Housel](/notes/thinking-and-walking-morgan-housel) (Patrick O'Shaughnessy's Invest Like the Best, Ep. 4); tagged `#Journals` in the source.
