# Helaman 4

Chapter 4 of the Book of Helaman in the Book of Mormon. In Kyle's [Come, Follow Me](/notes/come-follow-me) study of [Helaman 1](/notes/helaman-1)–[Helaman 6](/notes/helaman-6), this chapter carries two of the lesson's central threads. First, the Nephites' "great loss" and "great slaughter" came because of "their wickedness and their abomination... yea, and it was among those also who professed to belong to the church of God" ([Helaman 4](/notes/helaman-4):11) — read alongside the parable of the Ten Virgins ([Matthew 25](/notes/matthew-25)) to suggest that even active, professing members can be led astray, an interpretation Kyle ties to [Bruce D. Porter](/notes/bruce-d-porter). Second, [Helaman 4](/notes/helaman-4):23 — "because of their iniquity the church had begun to dwindle; and they began to disbelieve in the spirit of prophecy and in the spirit of revelation" — grounds the lesson's **"written for our day"** theme: that as a people sin more, the church shrinks, members read that shrinkage as proof of its inadequacy, then begin to disbelieve in revelation itself. Kyle links this self-fulfilling dynamic to [Ezra Taft Benson](/notes/ezra-taft-benson) (the Book of Mormon "was written for our day") and a [Brigham Young](/notes/brigham-young) warning about cutting "the thread that bound them to the Prophet and to God."

**Context:** Helaman 4 recounts Nephite military losses and the church beginning to dwindle in unbelief, before Moronihah, Nephi, and Lehi preach the people back toward repentance.
