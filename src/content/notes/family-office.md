# Family Office

A private investment vehicle managing a wealthy family's capital, appearing in Kyle's notes mainly through his [Teamshares](/notes/teamshares) / private-deals conversations with [Kyle Gay](/notes/kyle-gay) at [Kensington Permanent Capital](/notes/kensington-permanent-capital). In the March 5th, 2020 — Kyle Gay (Kensington Permanent Capital) call, Gay's read was that family offices "DO want more access to direct deals" as the landscape becomes more capable — no one likes fees, but the space is murky, and some offices have so much deal flow they charge fees to other family offices. His caution: "Not sure that family offices should be doing a lot of direct buyouts because it's a lot of freaking work." He also flagged the deal-flow gap — "A lot of family offices don't have deal flow, it's mostly people who don't understand investing" — which is why the [Teamshares](/notes/teamshares) ESOP-conglomerate model could be too complicated for them, and described a contact who brokers family offices into 2–25% passive stakes in substantial family-owned businesses for the dividends and cash flow. By the March 30th, 2022 — Kyle Gay (Kensington Permanent Capital) note, Gay's brother Dan was doing "more family office stuff."

The concept connects directly to [Co-Investments](/notes/co-investments) — both pages carry the same saved ai-CIO link on investors seeking support for private-equity co-investments, the structural appetite that family offices increasingly bring to direct deals.

**Context:** A family office is a private wealth-management firm set up to handle the investments, estate planning, and affairs of a single (single-family office) or several (multi-family office) high-net-worth families, often investing directly in private companies alongside or instead of through funds.
