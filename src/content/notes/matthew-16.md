# Matthew 16

In Kyle's gospel study, Matthew 16 supplies two recurring threads. The first is the **paradox of discipleship** in verses 24–25: "let him deny himself and take up his cross and follow me" (16:24) and "whosoever will save his life shall lose it: and whosoever will lose his life for my sake shall find it" (16:25). In Self-Mastery — Topical Study he holds 16:24 in deliberate tension with the "easy yoke" of [Matthew 11](/notes/matthew-11):29–30, framing the gospel paradox between the hard cross and the light burden; he also reads 16:25 in his Alma study June 22–28, 2020. Alma 17–22: "I Will Make an Instrument of Thee".

The second thread is **priesthood authority and revelation** — Matthew 16:19 ("the keys of the kingdom") appears in his lists of New Testament proof-texts for ecclesiastical office in Abrahamic Covenant and again in Miscellaneous Scriptures, grouped with passages on revelation given "in your heart AND mind."

**Context:** Matthew 16 contains Peter's confession that Jesus is "the Christ, the Son of the living God," Christ's promise of "the keys of the kingdom of heaven" (16:19), the first prophecy of his death and resurrection, and the call to take up one's cross.
