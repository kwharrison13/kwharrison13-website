# Public-Private Partnership

In Kyle's corpus the public-private partnership appears as one of [DigitalTown](/notes/digitaltown)'s named monetization/governance models. Per the DigitalTown company page, the firm pitches several models to cities: an upfront platform payment where DigitalTown owns the data, volume-based transaction fees, or a "free public-private partnership where the city dedicates resources" — explicitly framed as a way past the traditional RFP procurement process. It fits DigitalTown's "people-direction" approach to smart cities, pitching governments, chambers, and institutions on running their own cloud-hosted local-commerce platform rather than buying top-down.

For the broader city-revitalization sense of the term — state/city governments taking "active steering roles for corporate and philanthropic efforts" in comeback towns — see [Public-Private Partnerships](/notes/public-private-partnerships).

**Context:** A public-private partnership (PPP / P3) is a cooperative arrangement between a government body and a private firm to deliver a service or build infrastructure, sharing resources, risk, and reward — often used to sidestep slower conventional procurement.

## Where this appears

- [DigitalTown](/notes/digitaltown) — public-private partnership listed as one of DigitalTown's monetization/governance models, where the city dedicates resources in lieu of the traditional RFP process.
