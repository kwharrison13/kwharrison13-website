# Abundance

In Kyle's wiki, "abundance" is the economic thesis that productivity growth and globalization are pushing more and more people past basic survival needs — and the open question of what humans do with that surplus. In [The AppetiZIRP](/notes/the-appetizirp) (Packy McCormick), it is the underlying claim behind the post-work future: as [Automation](/notes/automation) shrinks the share of people who must work (farming's collapse from 83% of the US population in 1800 to 1% today), [Universal Basic Income](/notes/universal-basic-income)-style abundance reshapes what humans do with their time. In [Future of Work](/notes/future-of-work), abundance is the optimistic pole — [Sam Altman](/notes/sam-altman) arguing AI will displace jobs while "likely creating massive abundance."

The same idea has a supply-side, build-it framing in [The Build-Nothing Country](/notes/the-build-nothing-country) (Noah Smith), where abundance is the pro-build stance that opposes the stasis of a society that restricts housing and blocks energy projects. Across these sources, "abundance" sits at the intersection of automation, post-scarcity economics, and the politics of whether a wealthy country can still build.

**Context:** "Abundance" in contemporary tech/policy discourse names a cluster of ideas — supply-side progressivism, post-scarcity from automation, and the pro-building agenda — associated with writers like Packy McCormick, Noah Smith, and the broader Progress Studies movement.
