# John Bingham on Human Capital

A recurring personal thread Kyle attaches to material on treating people — rather than capital or process — as a company's true differentiator. It surfaces in his notes on [The Expertise Economy](/books/the-expertise-economy), linked from "Kyle's threads" alongside [My Mentors](/notes/my-mentors), and tagged as "Related" beneath the book's argument that "if you're going to employ people anyways, why not make them the differentiator? They're not a commodity" (the [Quantifying Human Capital](/notes/quantifying-human-capital) / [People Capital](/notes/people-capital) passage). The thread frames human capital as something to be invested in and counted, not commoditized.

## Where this appears

- [The Expertise Economy](/books/the-expertise-economy) — listed among "Kyle's threads" and flagged as "Related" to the passage on employees-as-differentiator and not treating people as a commodity.
