# Reusable Rockets

This stub was flagged as the SpaceX rocketry thread inside [Elon Musk — Tesla, SpaceX, and the Quest for a Fantastic Future](/books/elon-musk-tesla-spacex-and-the-quest-for-a-fantastic-future) (Vance). The book describes how [Elon Musk](/notes/elon-musk), who had run software companies at Zip2 and PayPal, "had to pick things up on the job" at [SpaceX](/books/elon-musk-tesla-spacex-and-the-quest-for-a-fantastic-future) — relying first on textbooks for his rocketry knowledge, then "grilling" the brilliant engineers he hired about specific valves and materials. The Vance highlights captured on the book page emphasize this first-principles, learn-the-physics approach to building rockets rather than the reusability program specifically.

**Context:** Reusable rockets are launch vehicles whose stages return and land intact to be flown again, rather than being discarded after a single use — the approach SpaceX pioneered at scale with the Falcon 9 first-stage landings. Recovering and reflying the most expensive hardware is the central lever for driving down the per-launch cost of reaching orbit.
