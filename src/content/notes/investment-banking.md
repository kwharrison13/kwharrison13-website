# Investment Banking

Across Kyle's reading, investment banking appears mostly as a foil. In the [Berkshire Hathaway Annual Letters](/books/berkshire-hathaway-annual-letters), [Warren Buffett](/notes/warren-buffett) is repeatedly skeptical of bankers: "friendly investment bankers will reassure" a manager about a value-destroying stock issuance ("don't ask the barber whether you need a haircut"); their deal "books" project earnings "moving faster than a speeding bullet" while the banker won't forecast his own firm's next month; and most major acquisitions are "a honey pot for the investment bankers… on both sides." Buffett's standard is that bankers should act as a "responsible bartender" / gatekeeper guarding investors against promoter excess — a "bartender morality" he says many firms find "intolerably restrictive." His exception is Byron Trott of Goldman Sachs, who "understands Berkshire far better than any investment banker… and earns his fee."

In [Managers Not MBAs](/books/managers-not-mbas), [Henry Mintzberg](/notes/henry-mintzberg) names investment banking (with consulting) as the destination that pulls MBAs *away* from learning the practice of [Management](/notes/management) — the field where "you might double your salary" by ignoring your experience. On the [MBA Degrees](/notes/mba-degrees) page, "[Investment Banking](/notes/investment-banking) → [Private Equity](/notes/private-equity)" is the target of the archetypal signal-maximizing applicant who treats the MBA as pure status arbitrage. The one practitioner among the sources, [John Hoffmire](/notes/john-hoffmire), ran his own ESOP-focused investment-banking firm — helping employees buy and manage ~$2.2B of [ESOP](/notes/esop) stock before selling it to American Capital — a use of the trade aimed at employee ownership rather than dealmaking for its own sake.

**Context:** Investment banking is the financial-services business of advising on and underwriting securities issuance, mergers, and acquisitions; banks earn fees for raising capital and brokering deals between buyers and sellers.
