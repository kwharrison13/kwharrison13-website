# Ephesians 2

Ephesians 2 is the New Testament chapter Kyle returns to as the scriptural anchor for the relationship between grace and works. In Christ Defines His Gospel it appears on both sides of that pairing: **Ephesians 2:8** ("by grace are ye saved through faith") is filed under *Grace*, while **Ephesians 2:10** is filed under *Works* — the two verses placed side by side to hold the tension Kyle is studying between being saved by grace and being created "unto good works."

In Obedience — Topical Study, the same passage drives the chapter's central apparent conflict. Kyle quotes **Ephesians 2:8–9** ("by grace are ye saved through faith; and that not of yourself; it is the gift of God: not of works, lest any man should boast") against [John 3](/notes/john-3):5 ("except a man be born of water, and of the spirit, he cannot ENTER the kingdom of God") and asks, "So where is the obedience here?" His resolution is that obedience is not a price paid but "the appreciation we show a magnanimous God" — the means by which He asks us to become like Him. The chapter thus functions as a hinge text for Kyle's larger themes of [Grace](/notes/grace), the [Atonement](/notes/atonement), and Faith — Topical Study.

**Context:** Ephesians is a Pauline epistle to the church at Ephesus. Chapter 2 is best known for verses 8–10, a cornerstone of Christian teaching on salvation by grace through faith — "not of works" (2:8–9), yet "created in Christ Jesus unto good works" (2:10) — and is widely cited across Christian traditions in debates over the relationship of faith and works.

## Where this appears

- Christ Defines His Gospel — 2:8 cited under *Grace* and 2:10 under *Works*, holding the grace-vs-works pairing.
- Obedience — Topical Study — 2:8–9 set against John 3:5 to frame the "where is the obedience?" question, resolved by obedience as gratitude rather than payment.
