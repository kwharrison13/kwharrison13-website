# Moroni 9

The Book of Mormon chapter containing Mormon's second epistle to his son Moroni — written amid the wickedness of the Nephites and Lamanites, charging Moroni to "labor on" regardless, with the closing benediction "may Christ lift thee up." In the Come Follow Me study December 7–13, 2020. Moroni 7–9: "May Christ Lift Thee Up", Kyle captured no highlights for this chapter specifically, noting only its place at the end of [Mormon](/notes/mormon)'s preserved teaching on [Faith](/notes/faith), [Hope](/notes/hope), and [Charity](/notes/charity) (delivered through [Moroni 7](/notes/moroni-7)) and the epistle on the salvation of little children ([Moroni 8](/notes/moroni-8)).

The chapter surfaces in Kyle's Plan of Salvation — Topical Study through one verse — Moroni 9:6 — read as the reason mortal life is described as a *field* rather than a waste: "we have a labor to perform whilst in this tabernacle of clay." Set against Lehi's [Tree of Life](/notes/tree-of-life) vision, the mists of Satan swirl constantly around us, so we are invited to "walk by faith, not by sight," and the labor of mortality is what distinguishes the "large and spacious field" of earth from the "dark and dreary waste" of premortal life.

**Context:** Moroni 9 is one of two epistles from Mormon to Moroni preserved at the end of the Book of Moroni. It describes the depths of Nephite and Lamanite depravity in the final wars, yet ends with a father's plea that his son endure and be lifted up by Christ.
