# Terraform

Terraform is [HashiCorp](/notes/hashicorp)'s infrastructure-as-code tool. It appears in Kyle's wiki in the Noname Security - Diligence dossier, where [Dave Thompson](/notes/dave-thompson) of [Kong](/notes/kong) — describing his Linux-background, "command-line geeks" platform team running site reliability and DevOps — says they've adopted Terraform from HashiCorp, while noting that [Nomad](/notes/nomad) (HashiCorp's workload orchestrator) "isn't a good fit for their needs." In context it's a one-line stack signal: the kind of tooling a cloud-native, command-line-oriented engineering org reaches for, in the same breath where Thompson flags AWS bloat and other ripe-for-disruption middleware ([Jira](/notes/jira), [Confluence](/notes/confluence), [Jenkins](/notes/jenkins)).

**Context:** Terraform is an open-source-origin infrastructure-as-code tool from HashiCorp that lets engineers define and provision cloud infrastructure declaratively across providers (AWS, GCP, Azure, etc.). HashiCorp went public in 2021; IBM announced its acquisition of HashiCorp in 2024.
