# Carbon Credits

Carbon credits are the central unit of value in Kyle's diligence on [NCX](/notes/ncx) (Natural Capital Exchange), a forest-carbon marketplace that pays landowners to defer timber harvests one year at a time and sells the resulting credits to corporate buyers like Microsoft. The NCX - Diligence notes frame the key mechanic: "the carbon credit is in the delta between what you would do vs. what you could do better" — e.g. extending a 20-year harvest rotation to 25 years (the additionality principle). NCX positions itself as a tech-enabled *originator* of credits, distinct from resellers like Pachama that "just buy and resell credits without creating things."

The expert calls and the partner presentation surface the bull/bear tension around these credits: at roughly $25/ton, carbon income can level the field for small landowners against large TIMOs, but the value erodes the more it disrupts a rotation. Skeptics in the diligence (and [Mike Volpi](/notes/mike-volpi) in the partner meeting) flag verification/additionality risk and that carbon and timber markets likely reach a price equilibrium where net demand for harvested wood doesn't actually fall — captured in the meeting's line, "He who controls the carbon controls the universe."

**Context:** A carbon credit is a tradable permit representing the reduction, avoidance, or removal of one metric ton of CO2-equivalent. Voluntary and compliance carbon markets use them to let emitters offset emissions, with credit quality hinging on additionality, permanence, and verification.
