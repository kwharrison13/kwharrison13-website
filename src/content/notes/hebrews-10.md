# Hebrews 10

A chapter in the New Testament epistle to the Hebrews, cited in Kyle's Faith — Topical Study. Kyle draws on [Hebrews 10](/notes/hebrews-10):26 — "if we sin willfully after that we have received the knowledge of the truth, there remaineth no more sacrifice for sins" — to make a point about [Faith](/notes/faith), knowledge, and accountability. In his reading, just as there comes a point when a person is held accountable for their own mistakes, there is a point where one's knowledge becomes sufficient that they are no longer covered "under the umbrella of the [Atonement](/notes/atonement)" in the same way, but must stand on the knowledge they have. He pairs it with [2 Nephi 31](/notes/2-nephi-31):14 ("after this should deny me, it would have been better for you that ye had not known me").

**Context:** Hebrews 10 (New Testament) contrasts the repeated animal sacrifices of the Mosaic law with the single, sufficient sacrifice of Christ, then warns its readers against willful sin after receiving the truth and exhorts them to endurance and faith.

## Where this appears

- Faith — Topical Study — Hebrews 10:26 supports Kyle's argument about the relationship between received knowledge, accountability, and the limits of the Atonement's covering.
