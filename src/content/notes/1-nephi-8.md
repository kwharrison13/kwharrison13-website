# 1 Nephi 8

The chapter recording Lehi's vision of the [Tree of Life](/notes/tree-of-life), which Kyle's gospel study treats as one of the richest allegories in [The Book of Mormon](/books/the-book-of-mormon). His Plan of Salvation — Topical Study reads the whole vision as a map of human existence: a man in a white robe invites us into creation ([1 Nephi 8](/notes/1-nephi-8):5–6); the dark and dreary waste (premortal life) plus God's mercy becomes the large and spacious field of earth ([1 Nephi 8](/notes/1-nephi-8):7–9); the mists of darkness are "the temptations of the devil" ([1 Nephi 8](/notes/1-nephi-8):23); holding "continually" to the rod of iron is the only path to salvation ([1 Nephi 8](/notes/1-nephi-8):30); and the great and spacious building stands "in the air, high above the earth," having no foundation ([1 Nephi 8](/notes/1-nephi-8):26).

The same chapter grounds Kyle's study of Charity & Love: when Lehi tastes the love of God and finds it fills his soul "with exceedingly great joy," his first desire is that his "family should partake of it also" ([1 Nephi 8](/notes/1-nephi-8):12), and he later exhorts Laman and Lemuel "with all the feeling of a tender parent" ([1 Nephi 8](/notes/1-nephi-8):37) — charity as the love that moves the act, not the act itself. In Anti-Christ — Topical Study, the foundationless great-and-spacious building is the visual for ideology with no footing, contrasted with the rock of the Redeemer.

**Context:** 1 Nephi 8 is the chapter of the Book of Mormon containing Lehi's dream/vision of the tree of life, the iron rod, the mist of darkness, the river of filthy water, and the great and spacious building — one of the most-taught allegories in the text.

## Where this appears

- Plan of Salvation — Topical Study — the tree-of-life vision read verse-by-verse as a map of premortal, mortal, and eternal life
- Charity & Love — Lehi tasting the love of God and desiring to share it (vv. 12, 37) as the prompting motive of charity
- Anti-Christ — Topical Study — the great and spacious building with no foundation contrasted against the rock of the Redeemer
