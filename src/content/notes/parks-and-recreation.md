# Parks and Recreation

In Kyle's notes, *Parks and Recreation* matters as the source of [Leslie Knope](/notes/leslie-knope) — the [Amy Poehler](/notes/amy-poehler) character who serves as a cultural barometer for generational attitudes toward earnest, relentless work. The show appears via [Rex Woodbury](/notes/rex-woodbury)'s observation that "Gen Z hates Leslie Knope": the shift from celebrating Knope's earnest civic overwork to recoiling from it is read in The Hardening Of The Great Softening - Research and Toil We Must - Research as a marker of how the rising generation re-evaluates [Work Ethic](/notes/work-ethic), [Ambition](/notes/ambition), and [Hard Work](/notes/hard-work). The show itself is the carrier; the analytic weight sits on the Knope character.

**Context:** *Parks and Recreation* is an NBC mockumentary sitcom (2009–2015) set in the fictional town of Pawnee, Indiana, centered on Amy Poehler's exuberantly diligent mid-level civil servant Leslie Knope.
