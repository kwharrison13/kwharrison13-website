# Lyft

Lyft recurs across Kyle's notes in three distinct roles. In the Cash—Kingmaker or Killer - Research, the **[Uber ($UBER)](/notes/uber) vs. Lyft** matchup is one of the recurring case studies for the essay's central question — whether a large cash pile is itself a competitive advantage or whether outcomes track underlying market dynamics and user economics; the same cash-conversion thought experiment is reused in the [Competitive Moats](/essays/competitive-moats) research. In the Watershed Climate Diligence — 2021, Lyft surfaces via its sustainability lead [Sam Arons](/notes/sam-arons), one of the corporate sustainability leaders Kyle's Index team emailed as a customer/expert reference target during the Watershed carbon-accounting Series B diligence. And in the [Tom Schenk](/notes/tom-schenk) notes, Lyft appears in the [City of Chicago](/notes/chicago) govtech context — Schenk co-wrote the legislation legalizing ride shares in Chicago that required per-trip data.

**Context:** Lyft is a US ride-sharing company founded in 2012, the principal domestic rival to Uber in rideshare; it went public in 2019 and has pursued fleet electrification and carbon-neutrality commitments.
