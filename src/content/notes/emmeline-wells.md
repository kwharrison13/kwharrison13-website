# Emmeline Wells

In [Brigham Young — Pioneer Prophet](/books/brigham-young-pioneer-prophet), Emmeline Wells appears in the book's account of women's leadership in early Mormonism. [Brigham Young](/notes/brigham-young) remarked in 1875 that [Eliza Snow](/notes/eliza-snow) and Emmeline Wells "have given as good exhortations as any can give." The book uses this praise to illustrate Young's evolving appreciation of Mormon women's talents — not, the author argues, as a step toward equality, but because Young needed their abilities for the all-consuming task of building up the Kingdom of God, and came in the process to value those abilities in new ways.

**Context:** Emmeline B. Wells (1828–1921) was a Latter-day Saint writer, longtime editor of the *Woman's Exponent*, suffragist, and the fifth general president of the Relief Society of The Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-day Saints.
