# Robert Millet

Robert Millet appears in Kyle's gospel-study note Christ Defines His Gospel as the source of a line capturing the proper balance between grace and works: "It is just as bad to do nothing and say 'save me' as it is to say 'step back, I'll save myself!'" The quote anchors the note's argument that salvation is neither passive helplessness nor self-reliant striving, but a cooperation between the disciple's effort and Christ's saving power.

**Context:** Robert L. Millet is a Latter-day Saint scholar and emeritus professor of religious education at Brigham Young University, known for his writing on grace, the Atonement, and LDS-evangelical dialogue (e.g. *Grace Works*).

## Where this appears

- Christ Defines His Gospel — quoted on the grace-and-works balance: doing nothing while saying "save me" is as wrong as insisting "I'll save myself."
