# 1 Nephi 4

In Kyle's gospel study, **1 Nephi 4** is anchored almost entirely on verse 6 — Nephi being "led by the Spirit, not knowing beforehand the things which [he] should do." Both Missionary Work — Topical Study and Preach My Gospel Missionary cite [1 Nephi 4](/notes/1-nephi-4):6 as the scriptural type for what Kyle calls "[The Flow](/notes/the-flow)": following the promptings of the Spirit to go forward even without knowing what to do next. The condition for enjoying that flow is being ready, desirous, and bold — where boldness is defined as "a love filled risk taken out of eternal concern for someone else."

The chapter also appears in the scripture cross-references for August 31–September 6, 2020. Helaman 13–16: "Glad Tidings of Great Joy", grouping it with the broader Book of Mormon narrative arc rather than a specific verse-level note there.

**Context:** 1 Nephi 4 is the chapter in the Book of Mormon where Nephi returns to Jerusalem, slays Laban under the constraint of the Spirit, and obtains the brass plates — the source of the "led by the Spirit, not knowing beforehand" language.
