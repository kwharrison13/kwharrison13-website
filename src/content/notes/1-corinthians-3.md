# 1 Corinthians 3

Paul's chapter on the church as God's husbandry/building. In Kyle's notes on General Conference- October 2021 it appears as the "milk before meat" proof text — 1 Corinthians 3:2 ("I have fed you with milk, and not with meat: for hitherto ye were not able to bear it") — illustrating that the Lord teaches each person sequentially, according to their capacity to learn. Kyle glosses it as "Milk before meat. Walk before you run. These are sequential," tying it to the idea that gospel growth follows an instructive order.

**Context:** 1 Corinthians 3 is the New Testament chapter where the Apostle Paul rebukes the Corinthians' divisions, describes himself and Apollos as fellow laborers planting and watering while God gives the increase, and frames the church as God's building founded on Christ. The "milk, not meat" line (3:2) is among its best-known phrases.

## Where this appears

- General Conference- October 2021 — cited (3:2, "milk before meat") for the principle that the Lord teaches sequentially, according to each person's capacity.
