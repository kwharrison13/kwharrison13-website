# Wittgenstein

Wittgenstein appears in [The Notetaking Cold War](/notes/the-notetaking-cold-war) as one of the philosophers grouped under the [Pragmatists](/notes/pragmatists) label — alongside William James and [Rorty](/notes/rorty) — used to anchor the pragmatist side of the essay's note-organization-as-philosophy framing.

**Context:** Ludwig Wittgenstein (1889–1951) was an Austrian-British philosopher whose work on language, meaning, and "language games" (especially the later *Philosophical Investigations*) influenced ordinary-language philosophy and pragmatist thought.

## Where this appears

- [The Notetaking Cold War](/notes/the-notetaking-cold-war) — cited as an exemplar pragmatist thinker, illustrating the view that categories are useful tools rather than reflections of a fixed essence.
