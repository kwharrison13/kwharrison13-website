# Roadmaps are fake news

"Roadmaps are fake news" is one of Kyle's recurring product-building maxims: that a fixed, linear product plan misrepresents how breakthrough products actually get built. He traces it most directly to [Conor White-Sullivan](/notes/conor-white-sullivan) of [Roam Research](/notes/roam-research), whose view Kyle captured in the June 24th, 2021 — Conor White-Sullivan (Roam Research) conversation — "many paths you can take where you can get to," and that a "proper road map" should be a *Bayesian* one that "unlocks what comes next even if there are multiple possible ways to approach problems and unlock different steps."

Kyle annotates two Amazon histories with the same tag. In [Amazon Unbound](/books/amazon-unbound) he flags it against Rohit Prasad's admission after Alexa's launch: "I knew the playbook to the launch of Alexa and Echo. The playbook for the next five years, I didn't have" — success opened a path no roadmap had charted. In [The Everything Store](/books/the-everything-store) Kyle wrestles with the tension head-on in his own note: employees and customers want to understand vision, so "roadmaps are fake news but if you're not using a road map then how are you communicating vision?" The maxim, in his usage, is not anti-planning so much as a rejection of false certainty — a Bayesian, vision-led path-finding over a deterministic Gantt chart.
