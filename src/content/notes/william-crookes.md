# William Crookes

Chemist and physicist referenced in Kyle's notes on [Charlie Munger — A Lesson on Elementary Worldly Wisdom, Revisited](/notes/charlie-munger-a-lesson-on-elementary-worldly-wisdom-revisited) as the young scientist to whom [Michael Faraday](/notes/michael-faraday) gave the advice "Work. Finish. Publish."

**Context:** Sir William Crookes (1832–1919) was an English chemist and physicist who discovered the element thallium, invented the Crookes tube (foundational to the study of cathode rays), and served as president of the Royal Society.

## Where this appears

- [Charlie Munger — A Lesson on Elementary Worldly Wisdom, Revisited](/notes/charlie-munger-a-lesson-on-elementary-worldly-wisdom-revisited) — named as the recipient of Faraday's "Work. Finish. Publish." advice.
