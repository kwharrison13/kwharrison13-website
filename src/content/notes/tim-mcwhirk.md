# Tim McWhirk

A customer-success expert in Kyle's Planhat - Diligence dossier — VP of Customer Success at Grin, formerly Director of CS Operations at Pendo.io and VP of Global Support Services at Act-On. In his reference call he framed CS tools through two lenses: improving day-to-day visibility (health score, risk) and leveling up the team. He rated [Gainsight](/notes/gainsight) the standout — longest-tenured (with [ToTango](/notes/totango)), thought leaders, doing M&A — though flagged a slight red flag from private equity coming in. He'd evaluated [Catalyst](/notes/catalyst) (popular in CS forums, modern presence, but ~6 months prior lacked built-out email automation he needs), [ChurnZero](/notes/churnzero), ToTango, and [Strikedeck](/notes/strikedeck).

On Planhat specifically, he'd heard of it and seen its campaigns but hadn't dug into differentiators — and noted that for a small company, not going with the category leader "can raise questions" (the reason his team went with Gainsight). His broader points: the category is growing because SaaS renewal revenue eventually exceeds new-acquisition growth, demanding a tech stack for visibility; switching cost is "only as hard as your systems," so he'd re-evaluate every 18–24 months and switch readily with good migration infrastructure; and willingness to pay tops out around $100K.
