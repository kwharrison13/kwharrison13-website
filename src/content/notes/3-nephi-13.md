# 3 Nephi 13

The Book of Mormon's rendering of the Sermon on the Mount (the parallel to Matthew 6), and Kyle draws on several of its verses across his gospel study. Verse 22 — "if therefore thine eye be single, thy whole body shall be full of light" — is the proof-text behind Eye single to the glory of God, framing motivation that flows from the light one has received, and it recurs in Sermon on the Mount under "judge righteously" ("before you judge, make sure you can see straight"). The chapter's teaching on prayer supplies Prayer — Topical Study with verse 7, "avoid vain repetitions," and its fasting instruction (vv. 16–18) anchors the Fasting - September 5, 2021 study. (Eye single to the glory of God, Sermon on the Mount, Prayer — Topical Study)

The chapter also surfaces outside gospel study: The Hardening Of The Great Softening - Research invokes "Consider the lilies → seek kingdom of god" as a touchstone, linking the Sermon's call to seek God's kingdom first. (The Hardening Of The Great Softening - Research)

**Context:** 3 Nephi 13 is a chapter in the Third Book of Nephi (Book of Mormon) recording the resurrected Christ's delivery of the Sermon on the Mount to the people in the Americas, closely paralleling Matthew 6 (alms, prayer, fasting, treasures in heaven, "consider the lilies").

## Where this appears

- Eye single to the glory of God — v. 22, the single eye / body full of light.
- Sermon on the Mount — vv. 22 and surrounding, judging righteously / seeing straight.
- Prayer — Topical Study — v. 7, avoiding vain repetitions.
- Fasting - September 5, 2021 — vv. 16–18 on fasting.
- The Hardening Of The Great Softening - Research — "consider the lilies / seek the kingdom of God."
