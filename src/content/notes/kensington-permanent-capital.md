# Kensington Permanent Capital

Kensington Permanent Capital is [Kyle Gay](/notes/kyle-gay)'s firm, surfacing in Kyle's notes through two reference calls with Gay. In the March 5th, 2020 — Kyle Gay (Kensington Permanent Capital) call, Gay — formerly of [Norwest](/notes/norwest) — helped Kyle diligence whether to leave Coatue for [Teamshares](/notes/teamshares), walking through SMB-buyout economics, ESOP roll-ups, fundless sponsors, and platform strategy. Gay noted that Kensington had closed its first buyout, that a full proprietary process ran ~2.5 years and cost ~$500K in diligence expenses, and that the investors "who think correctly" are the ones building scalable platforms with a repeatable playbook (a first CFO, digital-marketing turnaround). He was impressed Teamshares had raised from [USV](/notes/usv) and rated the ESOP-conglomerate idea highly among reasons to leave a big firm.

In the later March 30th, 2022 — Kyle Gay (Kensington Permanent Capital) call, Gay had moved deeper into permanent capital in public equities, bought a minority stake in a hedge fund, and led a seed round for a growth-buyout vehicle. He discussed [Peter McCoy](/notes/peter-mccoy) at [Baton](/notes/baton)'s thesis of institutionalizing early-employee access to follow-on rounds, and the strained state of [LP](/notes/lp) fundraising — "everyone is doubling AUM, raising funds faster," so LPs increasingly want differentiation more than determined returns.

**Context:** "Permanent capital" refers to investment vehicles without a fixed fund life or forced exit timeline (e.g. holding companies, public equities, or evergreen structures), letting an investor hold assets indefinitely rather than returning capital on a 10-year fund clock. (The specific firm here is private to Kyle's network and not independently verifiable.)
