# John 3

John 3 is, in Kyle's notes, the **scriptural home of spiritual rebirth** — Christ's night conversation with Nicodemus, "except a man be born again, he cannot see the kingdom of God" (3:3) and "born of water and of the Spirit" to *enter* it (3:5). His Born Again study leans on [Spencer W. Kimball](/notes/spencer-w-kimball)'s "many births" frame to split the doctrine in two: **Part A**, the mighty change of heart and the *desire* of the kingdom (3:3), and **Part B**, baptism and the Holy Ghost, *entering* the kingdom — the point being that many Christians have done Part A but not B. Teaching Repentance reads 3:1–12 as the contrast between spiritual and physical birth.

The chapter also anchors a pattern Kyle traces across Diligence: in [John 3](/notes/john-3) and [John 4](/notes/john-4) Christ takes a limited understanding and raises it to infinite capacity three times — to Nicodemus (born of water and Spirit), to the Samaritan woman (living water springing to everlasting life), and to the disciples ("my meat is to do the will of him that sent me"). And 3:16–17 — "for God so loved the world" — appears in his Plan of Salvation — Topical Study as the proof that all would have had to suffer but Christ saved us. 8 Points of the Gospel pulls 3:30 ("he must increase, but I must decrease") as John the Baptist's consecration for Christ's elevation.

**Context:** John 3 is a chapter in the Gospel of John containing Jesus' dialogue with the Pharisee Nicodemus on being "born again," and the verse John 3:16, one of the most widely cited in the New Testament.

## Where this appears

- Born Again — 3:3, 3:5 split into Kimball's Part A (mighty change / desire) and Part B (baptism / entering the kingdom)
- Teaching Repentance — 3:1–12 (Nicodemus) as spiritual vs. physical birth
- Diligence — 3:3,5 in the three-fold pattern (with [John 4](/notes/john-4)) of Christ raising limited understanding to infinite capacity
- Plan of Salvation — Topical Study — 3:16–17, "God so loved the world," as the saving act
- 8 Points of the Gospel — 3:30, John the Baptist decreasing for Christ to increase
