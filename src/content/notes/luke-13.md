# Luke 13

Luke 13 appears in Kyle's Chosen Leaders study, cited as Luke 13:27 — "depart from me all ye workers of iniquity." In the note it is paired with [Matthew 25](/notes/matthew-25):21 ("well done thou good and faithful servant") to frame the final accounting before God: the leader hopes to hear the words of commendation and forever fears the words of dismissal. It reinforces the study's central contrast between the *called* and the *chosen* ([D&C 121](/notes/dandc-121):34) and the parable-of-the-laborers theme that "the last shall be first, and the first last" ([Matthew 20](/notes/matthew-20):16).

**Context:** Luke 13 is a chapter of the Gospel of Luke; verse 27 records the master telling those who claim acquaintance, "I know you not whence ye are; depart from me, all ye workers of iniquity."

## Where this appears

- Chosen Leaders — cites Luke 13:27 ("depart from me") alongside Matthew 25:21 as the two outcomes of the final accounting for leaders
