# Cal Newport

The author of [Digital Minimalism](/books/digital-minimalism), which Kyle read in 2019. In that book Newport frames digital minimalism as a values-rooted philosophy of technology use, reading modern attention through [Henry David Thoreau](/notes/henry-david-thoreau)'s "new economics" (the cost of a thing measured in life-hours exchanged) and building toward "solitude deprivation" — the idea that constant input from other minds degrades clarity, emotional regulation, and moral courage ([Digital Minimalism](/books/digital-minimalism)). His earlier book [Deep Work](/books/deep-work) is the direct precursor, sharing the premise that attention is a scarce resource to protect from low-value digital interruption.

Newport also recurs in Kyle's learning material as a voice on concentration: his line that learning "requires stretching your brain past the point of what's familiar or comfortable — and that stretch requires unbroken concentration" is quoted in both [The Science of Learning](/notes/the-science-of-learning) and Books 2.0 - Research, tying Newport's [Focus](/notes/focus) thesis to the conditions for real learning.

**Context:** Cal Newport is a computer-science professor at Georgetown and a popular author/blogger on focus, productivity, and technology's effect on attention. His books include "Deep Work," "Digital Minimalism," "So Good They Can't Ignore You," and "A World Without Email."
