# Seven Samurai

Across the wiki, *Seven Samurai* surfaces as [Max Levchin](/notes/max-levchin)'s self-described management education. His page records that he watched Akira Kurosawa's film "at least twenty-five times" one summer, has since watched it 100+ times, and calls it his "sole source of management training." The [Storytelling as Leadership](/notes/storytelling-as-leadership) page uses this as an example of a narrative lens on leadership — leaders learning to lead through story rather than doctrine — and ties it to the [The Founders](/books/the-founders) account of the PayPal recruiting playbook (Levchin's response to Musk's X.com pitching, the "tell a story about cogs" approach to assembling a team).

**Context:** *Seven Samurai* (1954) is Akira Kurosawa's epic about a village that hires seven masterless samurai to defend it against bandits — widely studied as a model of team assembly, complementary skills, and leadership under constraint, which is why it recurs as a management parable.
