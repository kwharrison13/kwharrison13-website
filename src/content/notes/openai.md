# OpenAI

OpenAI recurs across Kyle's notes as the central institution of the AGI era — read through four distinct lenses. As **author of its own mission statement**, [Sam Altman](/notes/sam-altman)'s *[Planning for AGI and Beyond](/notes/planning-for-agi-and-beyond)* is logged as "OpenAI's mission statement for stewarding AGI into existence" — a gradual, iteratively-deployed transition, the claim that safety and capabilities are "a false dichotomy," and a pivot away from "release everything" openness. As an **incumbency/closed-source debate**, *The Tail That Wags The Dog - Research* foregrounds John Luttig's "future of foundation models is closed-source" post and flags the cap-table incentive — [Founders Fund](/notes/founders-fund) is an OpenAI investor — as the bias Kyle wants the reader to notice.

The **darker register** runs through *The Cogsec Chronicles - Research*, which collects reporting on OpenAI's sycophantic GPT-4o updates (Kelsey Piper, Zvi Mowshowitz) as evidence of "intentionally predatory AIs" optimized for engagement. And the **knowledge-concentration** worry appears in the October 16th, 2023 — Peter Nixey (Intentional.io) meeting: if "OpenAI becomes the source of information and kills the market for information, then they control what the world thinks." On the **market side**, the Heard From LPs This Week... (tweet) note captures the "fever pitch to access rounds of OpenAI and Anthropic" dominating LP conversations — a Hype Cycles data point. OpenAI also surfaces operationally as a November 7th, 2022 — Cleared Email vendor (GPT-3) and a January 24th, 2024 — Outreach for SF Trip outreach target (Blake Samic).

**Context:** OpenAI is the AI research company founded in 2015, creator of GPT models and ChatGPT, led by Sam Altman; it operates as a capped-profit entity governed by a nonprofit board.
