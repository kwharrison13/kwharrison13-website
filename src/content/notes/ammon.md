# Ammon

One of the sons of Mosiah and the central missionary figure in Kyle's notes on Alma 17–22. Ammon's "Nay, but I will be thy servant" ([Alma 17](/notes/alma-17):25) — declining to be made a wife's husband and instead becoming a servant watching King Lamoni's flocks — is "the hinge of the whole narrative" in Kyle's reading: **service IS missionary work**, work and service open hearts where argument cannot ("one of the greatest secrets of missionary work is work!" — [Ezra Taft Benson](/notes/ezra-taft-benson)). Ammon defends the flocks, wins the hearts of his fellow servants, and is taken by Lamoni to be "the Great Spirit." Kyle's notes treat his teaching of Lamoni as a master case study in how to begin teaching: let silence work (Ammon waits an hour while the king says nothing), be "wise, yet harmless," perceive the thoughts of the listener by the Spirit, and begin "at the creation of the world" before expounding the plan of redemption ([Alma 18](/notes/alma-18):36, 39; see [How To Begin Teaching](/notes/how-to-begin-teaching) · [Teach for Understanding](/notes/teach-for-understanding)).

In the following week's chapters Ammon's converts (the Anti-Nephi-Lehies) "never did fall away," and Ammon refuses to boast except "in my God" — "I do not boast in my own strength... but I will rejoice in my God" ([Alma 26](/notes/alma-26)) — a refusal that anchors Kyle's notes on humility versus boasting. The Teaching Repentance topical study holds Ammon up (alongside [Nephi](/notes/nephi), son of Helaman) as a **master teacher** of "sweet boldness": he created "the perfect teaching situation in showing forth his power," let the king "smell the bacon" enough to develop curiosity, then "with boldness declared all these things unto Lamoni." His example is contrasted with his brother [Aaron](/notes/aaron), who called to repentance immediately — different outcomes owed as much to "a more hardened and a more stiffnecked people" as to method.

**Context:** Ammon is a missionary in the Book of Mormon, one of the four sons of King Mosiah, famous for his mission to the Lamanite king Lamoni.
