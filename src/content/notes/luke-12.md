# Luke 12

Luke 12 is cited across two of Kyle's gospel studies, each leaning on a different verse. In Knowledge — Topical Study it is the central proof-text for accountability: "he that knew not… shall be beaten with a few stripes" and "unto whomsoever much is given, of him shall be much required" (Luke 12:47–48). Kyle uses these verses to frame the responsibility that comes with knowledge — why feelings of inadequacy are common among Latter-day Saints who "know their Lord's will," and why the great purpose of life is to gain as much knowledge and wisdom as one is capable of, given how much is then expected.

In Christian Reformation, Zion, and Seekers, the chapter is cited for a different line — the Savior's warning to "take heed, beware of the leaven of the Pharisees" (Luke 12:1, paired with Mark 8:15).

**Context:** Luke 12 is a chapter of the New Testament Gospel of Luke containing several of Jesus's teachings, including the warning against the leaven of the Pharisees and the parable of the faithful and wise steward — the source of the principle "to whom much is given, much is required."

## Where this appears

- Knowledge — Topical Study — Luke 12:47–48 as the core accountability text: those who know the Lord's will are held to a higher standard, and "of whom much is given, much is required"
- Christian Reformation, Zion, and Seekers — Luke 12:1, the warning to "beware of the leaven of the Pharisees"
