# Susa Young Gates

Susa Young Gates appears in Kyle's book notes as a daughter of [Brigham Young](/notes/brigham-young) and a writer who anchors the "women's agency, record-keeping, and home literature" thread. In [Saints — No Unhallowed Hand](/books/saints-no-unhallowed-hand), she is the figure "writing before six a.m. with three children underfoot," held up alongside [Eliza R. Snow](/notes/eliza-snow) under the discipline of careful record-keeping (Kyle's margin: *note taking*). The counsel she tried to live by — Snow's charge to "never write a line or a word that is not calculated to help and benefit this kingdom" — Kyle reads "as a personal note to a writer," connecting it to his own [Note-taking](/notes/note-taking) / [Writing](/notes/writing) and [Stewardship](/notes/stewardship) themes.

In [Brigham Young — Pioneer Prophet](/books/brigham-young-pioneer-prophet) she surfaces as the source cited on Eliza Snow's role in the temple — Young's daughter who recorded that "Aunt Eliza... was the high priestess in the temporary House of the Lord," documenting how Snow and other of Young's wives gained official ecclesiastical status through temple ritual work.

**Context:** Susa Young Gates (1856–1933) was a daughter of Brigham Young, a prolific Latter-day Saint author, editor, and genealogist, founder of the Young Woman's Journal, and a prominent figure in the church's early women's organizations.
