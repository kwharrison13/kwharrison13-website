# Acts 2

The chapter of Acts where Peter preaches at Pentecost. In Kyle's gospel study it functions as a model of how the scriptures invite the [Holy Ghost](/notes/holy-ghost) and lead to baptism. In Testimony — Topical Study, Peter (Acts 2:14–38) is cited as the case study for the most powerful tool of testimony: he recited the words of the prophets of old — even before those who had crucified Christ — and the Holy Ghost pricked their hearts and led them to the waters of baptism. Many people desire a witness from the Spirit, and Peter uses the thing that invites that Spirit most strongly: the scriptures themselves.

In Christ Defines His Gospel, Acts 2:37–38 ("Men and brethren… be baptized!") is listed among the grace-and-works proof texts, alongside [Romans 10](/notes/romans-10) and [Ephesians 2](/notes/ephesians-2), as the call to act on belief through baptism.

**Context:** Acts 2 narrates the Day of Pentecost — the outpouring of the Holy Spirit on the apostles, the speaking in tongues, and Peter's sermon to the gathered crowd in Jerusalem, after which about three thousand were baptized. It is foundational to Christian accounts of the birth of the church.
