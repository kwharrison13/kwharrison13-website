# Young Entrepreneurs Academy

In Kyle's [Your Passion, Your Solution (YPYS)](/notes/your-passion-your-solution-ypys) business plan, the Young Entrepreneurs Academy (YEA!) is studied in detail (Appendix D) as the closest comparable reference model — the existing program YPYS benchmarked itself against. The plan's stated key difference: YEA! is school-embedded (run inside schools as part of their structure), whereas YPYS is designed as a standalone nonprofit that partners with high schools and colleges from the outside. YEA! sits in the competitive/comparable landscape that YPYS used to define its own positioning around social-entrepreneurship education and youth-led venture creation.

**Context:** The Young Entrepreneurs Academy (YEA!) is a U.S. program, started in 2004 at the University of Rochester, that guides middle- and high-school students through starting and running real businesses or social enterprises over a school year; it later affiliated with the U.S. Chamber of Commerce Foundation.
