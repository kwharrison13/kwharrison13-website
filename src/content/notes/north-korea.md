# North Korea

In Annie Jacobsen's [Nuclear War — a Scenario](/books/nuclear-war-a-scenario), North Korea is the initiating antagonist — the regime whose "Bolt out of the Blue" first strike sets the book's minute-by-minute apocalypse in motion. The book singles it out as the one exception to the diplomatic norm of announcing significant missile tests: between January 2022 and May 2023 it test-launched more than 100 missiles, including nuclear-capable weapons able to reach the continental U.S., none announced. The narrative also notes that the CIA long believed North Korean ICBMs lacked reentry capability until that assessment quietly changed in 2020.

Three threats from the book are the ones Kyle tagged for his defense-tech thesis (the surface areas he tracks across [Skunk Works](/books/skunk-works), [The Kill Chain](/books/the-kill-chain), and [Chip War](/books/chip-war)). First, **underground facilities (UGFs)**: per a 2021 Defense Intelligence Agency report, North Korea's UGF program is "the largest and most fortified in the world," thousands of bunkers built to withstand U.S. bunker-busters, dating to Kim Il Sung's 1963 call to "dig into the ground." Second, the **EMP-satellite "Doomsday Scenario"**: analyst James Oberg, initially skeptical, came to believe a North Korean satellite could carry a small nuclear warhead into an unusual south-to-north orbit passing over the U.S. (a 2016 launch did exactly this orbit), to be detonated for an electromagnetic-pulse strike. Third, **chemical warfare**: the DIA estimated up to several thousand metric tons of CW agents, with one analyst's "Sea of Sarin" model projecting 650,000–2.5 million civilian deaths in a Seoul attack — a fact Kyle flagged as under-attended relative to its severity.

**Context:** North Korea (the Democratic People's Republic of Korea) is a one-party state under the Kim dynasty, internationally sanctioned for its nuclear-weapons and ballistic-missile programs developed in defiance of UN resolutions.
