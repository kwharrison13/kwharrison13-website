# D&C 50

A section of the Doctrine and Covenants that recurs across Kyle's [gospel-study](/notes/gospel-study) notes as the scriptural anchor for two themes: **teaching by the Spirit** and **eternal progression in light**. On the teaching side, it is the proof-text that conversion comes through the Holy Ghost and not through the teacher — "people receive through the Holy Ghost, not us!" ([D&C 50](/notes/dandc-50):13–22). The Teaching Repentance and Preach My Gospel Missionary studies both lean on verses 18 and 21: "he that received the word of the spirit of truth receiveth it as it is preached by the spirit of truth… both are edified and rejoice together" — a missionary teaches "in the Lord's way" only by the power of the Spirit, and any "other way it is not of God."

On the progression side, [D&C 50](/notes/dandc-50):24 is cited as the "brighter and brighter until the perfect day" verse — "he that receiveth light, and continueth in God, receiveth more light" — and verse 40 ("ye are little children and ye cannot bear all things now; ye must grow in grace and in the knowledge of the truth") is the keystone of the Eye single to the glory of God study's argument that the **veil is a medium of mercy**, parceling out only as much light as we can yet bear. The Spiritual Gifts study pulls verse 35 ("we can overcome all things not ordained of God") as the promise that "there isn't anything you can't overcome," and folds the chapter into the larger gifts-of-the-Spirit canon alongside [Moroni 10](/notes/moroni-10), [1 Corinthians 12](/notes/1-corinthians-12), and [D&C 46](/notes/dandc-46).

## Where this appears

- Spiritual Gifts — v.35 ("overcome all things not ordained of God"), vv.21–22 (enlightenment by teaching truth); grouped with the gifts canon
- Teaching Repentance — vv.13–22: people receive through the Holy Ghost, not the missionary
- Preach My Gospel Missionary — vv.18, 21: teaching "in the Lord's way" only by the Spirit; "if it be by some other way it is not of God"
- Eye single to the glory of God — v.40: the veil as a medium of mercy, parceling out bearable light
- July 6–12, 2020. Alma 30–31: "The Virtue of the Word of God" — vv.24, 40 cross-referenced on growing "brighter and brighter" and not bearing all things now
