# Rhett Reese

In Kyle's notes, Reese appears as one of the writers who — with [Ryan Reynolds](/notes/ryan-reynolds) and [Paul Wernick](/notes/paul-wernick) — *"acted the role of auteurs"* in shaping Deadpool as a character. He is part of the evidence for [The Hits Business](/essays/the-hits-business)'s thesis that big [Intellectual Property](/notes/intellectual-property) can still produce genuinely good work: *Deadpool & Wolverine* ($200M budget, 78% Rotten Tomatoes) is held up against *Batman v Superman* ($325M, 29%) to make the point that *"Bigger does not mean better. But good does not mean small."*

**Context:** Rhett Reese is a screenwriter who, with longtime writing partner Paul Wernick, wrote the *Deadpool* films, *Zombieland*, and other features.
