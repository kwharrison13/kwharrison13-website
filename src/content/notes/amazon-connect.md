# Amazon Connect

Amazon Connect is AWS's cloud contact-center platform, grouped in Kyle's notes with [Five9](/notes/five9) and [Genesys](/notes/genesys) as one of the [CCaaS](/notes/ccaas) platforms that frame the consolidation risk over the agent-assist category. In the Cresta.ai - Diligence, multiple experts expect a CCaaS platform to absorb real-time agent coaching by buying or building over Cresta / [Balto.ai](/notes/balto-ai) / [Observe.ai](/notes/observe-ai) — and Amazon Connect is repeatedly the name attached to the high end of that bet. The recurring framing is the [All-in-One vs. Best-in-Class](/notes/all-in-one-vs-best-in-class) tension: "the larger you are the more you want customization (makes Amazon Connect attractive)," while mid-market favors a best-of-breed point solution like Cresta and the smallest buyers want "one throat to choke." The diligence's open question is whether Cresta survives if a CCaaS platform like Amazon Connect ships native agent-assist.

**Context:** Amazon Connect is a pay-as-you-go cloud contact-center service from AWS, offering omnichannel routing, IVR, and built-in analytics/AI features, used as an alternative to legacy on-prem contact-center systems.

## Where this appears

- Cresta.ai - Diligence — named as a CCaaS consolidator favored by large, customization-hungry buyers; a key threat to standalone agent-assist.
- [CCaaS](/notes/ccaas) — listed among the cloud contact-center platforms positioned to buy/build into agent assist.
- [All-in-One vs. Best-in-Class](/notes/all-in-one-vs-best-in-class) — the customization-vs-point-solution axis where Amazon Connect anchors the all-in-one end.
