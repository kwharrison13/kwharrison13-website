# Ether 13

A chapter in the Book of Ether (Book of Mormon) that Kyle studies as part of the Ether 12–15 Come Follow Me block. Kyle's notes focus on the prophet Ether's repeated warnings to King Coriantumr: the Lord tells Ether that if Coriantumr and his household will repent, He "would give unto him his kingdom and spare the people," but Coriantumr "repented not," and the prophesied destruction of the Jaredites was fulfilled. Kyle reads this against [Ether 12](/notes/ether-12):27 and #[The Pride Cycle](/notes/the-pride-cycle) — the theme that the stronger and more self-reliant you feel, the harder it is to humble yourself; the discipline is to humble yourself precisely *in* your strength rather than trusting your own arts of war and "cunning of the world."

The chapter is also a recurring cross-reference for the "the first shall be last, and the last first" pattern — [Ether 13](/notes/ether-13):12 is cited alongside Matthew 19:30, Mark 10:31, and Jacob 5:63 in the Chosen Leaders study. More broadly, Ether himself appears in [Russell M. Nelson](/notes/russell-m-nelson)'s Easter address Christ Is Risen as one of the scriptural exemplars of mountain-moving faith.

**Context:** Ether is the last book in the Book of Mormon's Jaredite record, an abridgment by Moroni of the prophet Ether's account of a people who came to the Americas at the time of the Tower of Babel. Chapter 13 includes Ether's prophecy of a New Jerusalem and the final wars that destroy the Jaredite nation.
