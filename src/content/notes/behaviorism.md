# Behaviorism

In [Brave New World Revisited](/books/brave-new-world-revisited), behaviorism is the school of thought [Aldous Huxley](/notes/aldous-huxley) runs a sustained argument against — the determinist scientism associated with [B.F. Skinner](/notes/b-f-skinner) and Watson that treats human behavior as fully explicable by environmental conditioning. Huxley's objection, recurring across the book's "crime and punishment" notes, is that the genetic and individual contribution to behavior can't be wished away: he quotes Skinner's claim that "as scientific explanation becomes more and more comprehensive, the contribution which may be claimed by the individual himself appears to approach zero," and rejects it, invoking [William James](/notes/william-james) and [Bertrand Russell](/notes/bertrand-russell) to insist that in real life "the individual can never be explained away." The book also notes the political stakes — behaviorist technique favors "control through the reinforcement of desirable behavior by rewards" and "non-violent manipulation of the environment" over punishment, the soft machinery of the *Brave New World* dystopia. Kyle's marginal note inverts the lens, reading it as "the anti-Christ version of 'a study of doctrines of the gospel will change behavior quicker than a study of behavior will change behavior.'" The thread ties directly into [Free Will](/notes/free-will).

**Context:** Behaviorism is a school of psychology (J.B. Watson, B.F. Skinner) holding that behavior is best understood through observable stimulus–response conditioning rather than internal mental states, with environment as the primary determinant of action. Skinner's *Walden Two* and *Beyond Freedom and Dignity* extended this into a deterministic social philosophy that many critics, Huxley among them, attacked as a denial of human freedom and individuality.

## Where this appears

- [Brave New World Revisited](/books/brave-new-world-revisited) — Huxley's running takedown of Skinner/Watson behaviorist determinism in defense of individual freedom and biological variability.
