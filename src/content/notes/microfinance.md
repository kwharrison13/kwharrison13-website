# Microfinance

In Kyle's corpus, microfinance appears through [John Hoffmire](/notes/john-hoffmire), the Saïd Business School (Oxford) faculty member focused on employee ownership and impact investing. Hoffmire led the **first sale of shares for a microfinance bank to an [ESOP](/notes/esop)** — at **K-REP in Kenya** — making microfinance one of the threads in his market-driven, anti-poverty work. It is also flagged as a tag on his page alongside his family microfinance projects referenced in the prep notes for Kyle's 2014 Oxford conversation with him.

**Context:** Microfinance is the provision of small loans, savings, and other financial services to low-income individuals and entrepreneurs who lack access to conventional banking — a model popularized by Muhammad Yunus and Grameen Bank in Bangladesh. Notably, Hoffmire's K-REP example combines microfinance with [Employee Ownership](/notes/employee-ownership), applying an ESOP structure to a microfinance institution itself.
