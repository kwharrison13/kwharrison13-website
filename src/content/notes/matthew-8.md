# Matthew 8

The New Testament chapter Kyle's notes use for the **Centurion who showed "so great faith"** — [Matthew 8](/notes/matthew-8):8, 10, 13. The centurion tells Christ, "speak the word only, and my servant shall be healed," and Christ marvels: "I have not found so great faith, no, not in Israel." In the Faith — Topical Study Kyle uses this episode to separate *belief* from *faith*: the centurion's belief that Christ could heal by word alone "certainly has great power" and "mere belief can wrought miracles," but it is filed among the references on "no greater faith" alongside the [Brother of Jared](/notes/brother-of-jared).

This is one of the moments Kyle clusters under [Never have I seen such great faith](/notes/never-have-i-seen-such-great-faith) — the scriptural instances where Christ ranks one person's faith above all others. On the [Brother of Jared](/notes/brother-of-jared) page it sits with the Nephites of [3 Nephi 19](/notes/3-nephi-19) as a parallel to the Brother of Jared's vision-granting faith. In the Olive Tree Allegory it carries a different weight: the Gentile centurion ([Matthew 8](/notes/matthew-8):10–12) is read as an instance of Christ laboring among the non-Israelites — the "wild trees" — alongside the Samaritans ([John 4](/notes/john-4)) and the Gentile woman ([Matthew 15](/notes/matthew-15)), foreshadowing the gospel going to all the Gentiles. The chapter also recurs in the November 9–15, 2020. Ether 1–5: "Rend That Veil of Unbelief" Come Follow Me study, cross-referenced with [Luke 7](/notes/luke-7)'s account of the same centurion.

**Context:** Matthew 8 is a chapter in the Gospel of Matthew recounting a series of healing miracles, including the healing of a leper, the centurion's servant (praised for the centurion's faith), and Peter's mother-in-law, along with the calming of the storm.
