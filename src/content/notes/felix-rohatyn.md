# Felix Rohatyn

In [What It Takes](/books/what-it-takes), Felix Rohatyn is the Lazard partner whose advice delivers the book's defining "masters vs. slaves" lesson. Interviewing a young [Stephen A. Schwarzman](/notes/stephen-a-schwarzman), Rohatyn offered him a job on the spot but advised him not to take it: "at Lazard, there are two types of people: masters like me and slaves like you would be... You should go work at Lehman Brothers, let them train you, and then come here to Lazard as a master." Schwarzman took the advice, going to Lehman to be "trained" rather than starting out subordinate at Lazard. The episode anchors the [Theory of Masters](/notes/theory-of-masters) takeaway — that mastery in finance comes from a training ground before an arena, like the Beatles in Hamburg or Gates on the Washington computers.

**Context:** Felix Rohatyn (1928–2019) was an Austrian-born American investment banker, a longtime partner at Lazard Frères, architect of New York City's 1970s fiscal rescue, and later U.S. Ambassador to France.
