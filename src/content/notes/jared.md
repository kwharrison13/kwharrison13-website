# Jared

Jared is the Book of Mormon figure who, with his brother, came "from the great tower, at the time the Lord confounded the language of the people" (Ether 1:33), leading their families and friends to a promised land. In Kyle's Come Follow Me study of Ether 1–Ether 5, Jared is the one who repeatedly directs his brother — the [Brother of Jared](/notes/brother-of-jared) — to go and inquire of the Lord: first that their language not be confounded, then to learn whether they will be driven from the land and where they should go (Ether 1:34–38). It is Jared's instinct to keep asking the right questions ("Cry again unto the Lord," "Go and inquire of the Lord") that frames Kyle's recurring lesson to "counsel with the Lord in all thy doings" (Alma 37).

Across the study Jared functions mostly as the foil and prompter to his more famous brother, whose unparalleled faith pierces the veil. He recurs in the people page for the [Brother of Jared](/notes/brother-of-jared), where the brother is described as "a large and mighty man, and a man highly favored of the Lord" whom Jared sends to the Lord.

**Context:** In the Book of Mormon's Book of Ether, Jared and his brother lead the Jaredites away from the Tower of Babel across the ocean to the Americas; the brother of Jared (unnamed in the text) is the central faith figure, while Jared is the one who initiates appeals to the Lord.
