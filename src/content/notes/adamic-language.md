# Adamic Language

In Kyle's Come Follow Me study of 3 Nephi 20–26, the Adamic language surfaces as the answer to a meditation on the limits of communication. Reflecting on 3 Nephi 21:20 and [Unity of the Faith](/notes/unity-of-the-faith), Kyle notes that "we are never communicating perfectly" — we hold an idea in our minds but listeners take away different perspectives — and imagines instead "a language that could perfectly capture your feelings and perspective." That ideal points to the Adamic tongue.

Per the study's gloss, scripture describes the language written and spoken by Adam and his children as "pure and undefiled" (Moses 6:5–6). [Brigham Young](/notes/brigham-young) taught it continued from Adam to Babel, where the Lord caused the people to forget their mother tongue and scattered them — except possibly Jared and his family, who (the study says, reflecting widely held Mormon belief) preserved the Adamic language at their immigration to the new world. This is why the brother of Jared's apocalyptic vision was rendered "linguistically inaccessible without divine interpretive help," since the language had been confounded (Ether 3:21–28).
