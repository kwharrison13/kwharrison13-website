# John 7

Gospel chapter Kyle returns to for one promise: John 7:17 — "If any man will do his will, he shall know of the doctrine, whether it be of God, or whether I speak of myself." In the Come Follow Me study December 31–January 6, 2019. "We Are Responsible for Our Own Learning", the verse anchors the lesson that we are responsible for our own learning: knowledge of doctrine comes through *doing*, paired with [John 5](/notes/john-5):39 ("search the scriptures") and the fuller context of 7:14–17, where Jesus teaches in the temple and the Jews marvel "how knoweth this man letters, having never learned?" In Scripture Study — Topical Study, Kyle quotes [Henry B. Eyring](/notes/henry-b-eyring) — that sincere prayer and careful study "ALWAYS feel an urging to DO things" — and ties it back to 7:17 as "a gospel truth and an eternal promise" that obedience yields spiritual sight.

**Context:** John 7 records Jesus teaching at the Feast of Tabernacles in Jerusalem amid division over his identity; verse 17's "do his will… know of the doctrine" is a frequently cited LDS proof-text for learning by obedience.
