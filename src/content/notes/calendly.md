# Calendly

**One-line:** *Bottoms-up scheduling tool, recurring in Kyle's notes as the canonical product-led-growth / virality comparable — it attracts decision-makers by serving their own individual productivity.*

## What we know

- [Sunil Patro](/notes/sunil-patro) benchmarked [SignEasy](/notes/signeasy)'s go-to-market against Calendly and [Loom](/notes/loom) — products that attract many decision-makers by serving individual productivity. (September 22nd, 2021 — Sunil Patro (SignEasy))
- Cited in [Product Led Growth — The End User Era](/books/product-led-growth-the-end-user-era) as an example of "consumerizing the enterprise": viral, network-effect products like Calendly are "really just targeting consumers inside an enterprise," so consumer growth tactics (invitations → conversion → viral coefficient) apply. ([Product Led Growth — The End User Era](/books/product-led-growth-the-end-user-era))
- The book also quotes a Calendly internal practice: a weekly process to collect "almost every piece of customer input and triage it to zero" to stay on top of customer pain points. ([Product Led Growth — The End User Era](/books/product-led-growth-the-end-user-era))
