# Buy Now, Pay Later (BNPL)

Buy Now, Pay Later (BNPL) recurs across Kyle's fall-2021 [Index Ventures](/notes/index-ventures) fintech sourcing notes as a financing primitive being applied well beyond its consumer-checkout origins. In the August 18th, 2021 — Point Card Pre-Brief (Mark Fiorentino), BNPL appears in a diligence question about a premium-tier "BNPL/debit card hybrid" launching that November and whether it would "eliminate 'graduation risk'" for users who would otherwise age into a traditional credit card — i.e. BNPL as a structural alternative to the consumer-credit ladder.

The notes show Kyle tracking BNPL spreading into new verticals. The September 7th, 2021 — PAYE Brief (Julia Andre) frames a company as "effectively B2B Buy Now, Pay Later" — the same deferred-payment mechanic applied to business payments. And the September 7th, 2021 — Tess Michaels (Stride Funding) call describes Stride Funding launching "installment contracts, growing super fast (e.g. BNPL for education)," pairing BNPL with Income Share Agreements in student-first education financing. Across all three, BNPL is treated as a flexible installment/credit construct whose interest lies in which adjacent markets — credit-card replacement, B2B payments, education finance — it can disrupt.

**Context:** Buy Now, Pay Later is a financing model that lets a buyer split a purchase into several interest-free or low-interest installments at the point of sale, with the provider paying the merchant up front and collecting from the consumer over time. It rose to prominence in consumer e-commerce (Affirm, Klarna, Afterpay) and has since been extended into B2B payments and other verticals, as Kyle's notes track.
