# Grow Therapy

**One-line:** *Provider-enablement / "lead provider" platform for mental-health therapists — it handles marketing, insurance credentialing and billing, EHR, consent, and scheduling, and takes a cut of each session.*

## What we know

- A "one-stop-shop" for therapists: does marketing (including paying for the provider's Psychology Today profile), insurance contracting, billing, consent, EHR, and scheduling. Providers can keep their own notes, and Grow supplies templates. (September 22nd, 2021 — Nicole Kleiman-Reck (Grow Therapy))
- Economics, per provider [Nicole Kleiman-Reck](/notes/nicole-kleiman-reck): the therapist pays Grow nothing; Grow is paid per hour. She cited the take as "only 5% which is nothing," and separately estimated ~20% of a typical $125–150 insurance-paid session goes to Grow. Each therapist contracts with insurers individually (not through Grow), so rates vary; she nets ~$70/hour on average. (September 22nd, 2021 — Nicole Kleiman-Reck (Grow Therapy))
- Mostly serves insurance-based clients, not cash pay; provides a steady flow of clients with no need for the therapist to advertise. Credentialing is done for the provider and follows them even if they leave Grow. (September 22nd, 2021 — Nicole Kleiman-Reck (Grow Therapy))
- Disintermediation view: for cash clients Grow eventually stops making sense, but its insurance handling is the core convenience; the therapist holds onto Grow partly for calendar control. (September 22nd, 2021 — Nicole Kleiman-Reck (Grow Therapy))
- Surfaced via an [Index Ventures](/notes/index-ventures) [Behavioral Health](/notes/behavioral-health) expert call with a current Grow Therapy contractor. Competitors named in the same diligence screener include [SonderMind](/notes/sondermind), [Headway](/notes/headway), [Spring Health](/notes/spring-health), Alma, Nirvana, Lyra, and Modern Health. (September 22nd, 2021 — Nicole Kleiman-Reck (Grow Therapy))
