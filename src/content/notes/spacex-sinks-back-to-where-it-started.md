# SpaceX Sinks Back to Where It Started

**Author:** David Rovella
**URL:** https://www.bloomberg.com/news/newsletters/2026-07-15/spacex-sinks-back-to-where-it-started-evening-briefing-americas?srnd=undefined
**One-line:** *[SpaceX](/books/elon-musk-tesla-spacex-and-the-quest-for-a-fantastic-future) shares fell to their lowest level since going public — briefly dipping below the IPO price — as the post-IPO hype around [Elon Musk](/notes/elon-musk)'s "rocket-satellite-AI venture" fades.*

## Key claims

- **SpaceX shares slumped to their lowest level since the company went public**, framed by the author against the "breathless" Wall Street and media hype that accompanied the IPO.
- **The stock briefly fell below its initial offering price on Wednesday**, a notable reversal from the frenzy that surrounded the listing.
- **The piece frames SpaceX as a combined "rocket-satellite-AI venture,"** situating the stock's slide within broader skepticism about IPO hype cycles ("Don't Believe the Hype").

## Notable quotes

> "This is especially the case for investors in Elon Musk's rocket-satellite-AI venture SpaceX."

> "The stock on Wednesday even briefly fell below its initial offering price."

## How it connects

- [SpaceX](/books/elon-musk-tesla-spacex-and-the-quest-for-a-fantastic-future) — subject; documents the stock's slide back toward (and briefly below) its IPO price.
- [Elon Musk](/notes/elon-musk) — described as the figure behind the venture; the piece's skepticism is aimed at the hype built around his company.
