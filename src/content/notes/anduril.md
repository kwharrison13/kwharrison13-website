# Anduril

**One-line:** *Defense-technology company founded by [Palmer Luckey](/notes/palmer-luckey) (Lattice software + autonomous systems) that recurs throughout Kyle's reading as the lens for a single thesis — that the future of military power belongs to autonomous, networked, attritable systems built at startup speed, not to a handful of exquisite legacy platforms.*

> This page reads Anduril the way Kyle's notes do: as the connective frame he projects onto defense and industrial histories. The supporting claims below come from those books and research notes; the company-specific positioning is Kyle's recurring annotation, not a claim the books make on their own.

## What we know

- Recurs as the dominant annotation across Kyle's defense/industrial reading — he reads semiconductor history as evidence for a defense-tech investment thesis (civil-military fusion, sensor fusion, treating platforms as expendable). ([Chip War](/books/chip-war))
- The Skunk Works era is the historical precedent Anduril is read as reviving: founder-led innovation ([Kelly Johnson](/notes/kelly-johnson)) works, with a failure mode of drift into building "for the buck rather than the mission," and **speed of delivery** as the competitive edge today's primes cannot match. ([Skunk Works](/books/skunk-works))
- The exquisite-platform paradigm (carriers, bombers, the F-35) is framed as the wrong shape for the next conflict; the right shape is many cheaper, more autonomous nodes networked into a "kill chain" lattice. ([The Kill Chain](/books/the-kill-chain))
- Boeing's collapse is the cautionary anti-pattern — what happens when an industrial culture is hollowed out by financial engineering — read alongside the thesis. ([Flying Blind](/books/flying-blind))
- Deep precedent for civil-military fusion and applied research sits in the von Neumann / RAND lineage. ([The Man From The Future](/books/the-man-from-the-future))
- Tagged as adjacent within the broader defense-tech frame. ([Nuclear War — a Scenario](/books/nuclear-war-a-scenario))
- Used as a case study in navigating "Layer 3" (unavoidable) [Dependencies](/notes/dependencies) — DoD buy-in, warfighter trust, Lattice integration, scaling 1K→50K employees; solicit true believers and care about the 1% "ride or die"; countercyclical in 2017, later riding [American Dynamism](/notes/american-dynamism). (source: wiki/essays-research/Conviction-Led Contrarianism - Research.md)
