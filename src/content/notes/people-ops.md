# People Ops

The organizational discipline of how a company structures, staffs, and manages its people — and, in the [Berkshire Hathaway Annual Letters](/books/berkshire-hathaway-annual-letters), the case for keeping that overhead radically thin. [Warren Buffett](/notes/warren-buffett) tags this idea to his description of Berkshire as "a compact organization" that "lets all of us spend our time managing the business rather than managing each other." His partnership with [Charlie Munger](/notes/charlie-munger) is the model: the two operate from different cities (Munger from Los Angeles), treat themselves as "interchangeable in business decisions," and find "a telephone call to be more productive than a half-day committee meeting."

The takeaway carried in the source is that distance and lean structure are features, not bugs — minimizing internal coordination cost frees leadership to focus outward on the business itself rather than on managing each other.
