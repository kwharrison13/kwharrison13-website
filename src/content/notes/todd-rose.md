# Todd Rose

In [The Expertise Economy](/books/the-expertise-economy), Todd Rose supplies the book's anchoring claim against standardized corporate training: "there is no such thing as an average person, an average education, or even a standard way that people learn." The book draws on Rose's thesis to argue that companies must abandon the one-size-fits-all, lecture-and-exam training model — which mirrors the university format and which sees over $130B spent in the US with little to show — in favor of [Continuous Learning](/notes/continuous-learning) built into the work itself, self-directed and personalized to the individual.

**Context:** Todd Rose is an American author and former director of the Mind, Brain, and Education program at the Harvard Graduate School of Education, and co-founder of the think tank Populace. His book *The End of Average* argues that designing systems around a statistical "average person" systematically fails real individuals.
