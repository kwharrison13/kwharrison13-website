# Testimony

In Kyle's gospel-study notes, testimony is the personal, spiritual knowledge of God and the gospel — and the recurring teaching is that it is *found in the bearing of it*, not received whole before it is spoken. [Boyd K. Packer](/notes/boyd-k-packer)'s talk The Candle of the Lord supplies the central image: "Somewhere in your quest for spiritual knowledge, there is that 'leap of faith'… the moment when you have gone to the edge of the light and stepped into the darkness to discover that the way is lighted ahead for just a footstep or two." A testimony grows like physical stature, almost unnoticed, and "it is by giving it away freely that it becomes yours" — protected, Packer argues, from the insincere, the intellectual, and the merely curious because it requires faith, humility, and obedience. [What I Want My Son to Know before He Leaves on His Mission](/notes/what-i-want-my-son-to-know-before-he-leaves-on-his-mission) echoes the missionary application: "you cannot convert people beyond your own conversion," and your own testimony is "the strongest arrow in your quiver."

[Faith is Not Blind](/books/faith-is-not-blind) frames testimony as compatible with — not threatened by — honest analysis: a true testimony "has nothing to fear from honest analysis," and faith welcomes data, harmonizing facts and conviction rather than walling them off. The same book records the father's counsel to a son unsettled about his testimony: keep the commandments so the search isn't biased by the heart's attachments. From the sociological side, [The Next Mormons](/books/the-next-mormons) treats testimony as the *belief* leg of a belief-behavior-experience model of religious transmission, noting a "stubbornly constant 17% doubt rate" across generations and that many former Mormons already had "significant cracks in their testimony" in adolescence — belief and behavior reinforcing one another in both directions.
