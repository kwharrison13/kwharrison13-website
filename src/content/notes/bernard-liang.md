# Bernard Liang

Author of a draft "Community Building Thesis" Kyle read in November 2021. Liang's thesis argues that the most talented people are simultaneously the most underestimated, underutilized, and misunderstood by broader society, and lays out a model for building a global, independent talent [Community](/notes/community) — generalized beyond engineers (he cites Dave Fontenot of Backend Capital as a more engineer-focused analog) to technical and non-technical talent as well as investors, writers, and community builders.

The thesis emphasizes "informal chats" as the core building block, a giver mentality (per Adam Grant's *Give and Take*), and a distinction between **content** (create once, consume indefinitely — broadly distributable at near-zero marginal cost) and **community** (a network of people for cases where content alone is insufficient). A recurring concern he names is the "Focus Paradox": community leaders are usually incentivized to optimize for their own community's success, so an independent agent free of those constraints is best positioned to optimize across the whole ecosystem — raising the question of how to build a global community without falling into that same trap. His tentative roadmap drew on his DoorDash alumni network for angel-investing and talent-matching efforts.
