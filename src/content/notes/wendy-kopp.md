# Wendy Kopp

Appears in Kyle's [MBA Degrees](/notes/mba-degrees) research dossier as an inspiration for the "Social MBA" thread — the counter-current of business-school graduates using the degree as a springboard into the social sector. Alongside [Muhammad Yunus](/notes/muhammad-yunus)'s Grameen Bank, Kopp's Teach for America is named as the model that helped legitimize social entrepreneurship as a destination for MBAs, feeding programs like Haas/REDF and the wave of schools institutionalizing social-enterprise tracks.

**Context:** Wendy Kopp founded Teach for America in 1990 — an idea she first proposed in her Princeton undergraduate thesis — recruiting top college graduates to teach in under-resourced US schools. She later co-founded and leads Teach for All, its global network.
