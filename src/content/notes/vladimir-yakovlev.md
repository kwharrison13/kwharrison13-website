# Vladimir Yakovlev

Vladimir Yakovlev is one of the firsthand sources for [The Invention of Russia](/books/the-invention-of-russia) — the [Arkady Ostrovsky](/notes/arkady-ostrovsky) page lists him in its open questions as a figure Ostrovsky interviewed directly while reporting the book. In the book itself, Yakovlev is identified as the founder of *Kommersant* (the influential post-Soviet business newspaper) and as the son of Yegor Yakovlev, the Soviet-era editor and Ostankino executive.

The book quotes a public appeal Yakovlev made after the murder of Boris Nemtsov, speaking both for himself and on behalf of his father: "Stop teaching people how to hate. Because hatred is already tearing the country to pieces… The information war is first and foremost destroying ourselves." The plea fits the book's larger argument (see [Arkady Ostrovsky](/notes/arkady-ostrovsky)) about Russian media's role in manufacturing the country's "meaning."

(Note: distinct from Yegor Yakovlev, his father, and from Alexander Yakovlev, the perestroika-era reformer — both also referenced in the book.)
