# 1 Million Cups

1 Million Cups appears in the wiki as a reference point, not a subject in its own right: [William Tewelow](/notes/william-tewelow) describes [Smart Cities Group](/notes/smart-cities-group) as "like a 1 Million Cups for smart city companies." It is invoked as the recognizable template for what he is building — a recurring, community-driven gathering where entrepreneurs network, collaborate, and present to peers — applied here to the niche of new-technology entrants in the smart-city space.

**Context:** 1 Million Cups is a free, weekly program created by the Kauffman Foundation in 2012 in which local entrepreneurs present their startups to a community audience over coffee. It runs in cities across the United States as a grassroots founder-support network.

## Where this appears

- [William Tewelow](/notes/william-tewelow) — uses 1 Million Cups as the analogy for his Smart Cities Group community model.
- [Smart Cities Group](/notes/smart-cities-group) — explicitly framed as "a 1 Million Cups for smart city companies."
