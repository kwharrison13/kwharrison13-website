# Zip2

Zip2 was [Elon Musk](/notes/elon-musk)'s first company — an online maps and business-directories venture — sold for hundreds of millions of dollars in 1999. As recounted in [The Founders](/books/the-founders), Musk came away from it unsatisfied, feeling the technology's potential was unrealized: "Zip2's products had not exhibited the awesome potential of the internet." That dissatisfaction is framed as the bridge to his next move, founding [X.com](/notes/x-com) the same year with a far larger ambition.

**Context:** Zip2 provided online city-guide and mapping software to newspapers in the mid-1990s. Compaq acquired it in 1999 for roughly $300 million, the deal that gave Musk his first major liquidity.
