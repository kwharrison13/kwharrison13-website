# Personal Revelation

Personal revelation is a recurring doctrinal frame in Kyle's gospel-study and reading notes (mis-filed under `companies` during a connection sweep; it is a religious concept). In [The Priesthood Power of Women](/books/the-priesthood-power-of-women) it is the means by which the doctrine of the priesthood is learned at all: Bruce R. McConkie taught that this doctrine "cannot be learned out of the scriptures alone… [it] is known only by personal revelation… line upon line and precept upon precept, by the power of the Holy Ghost." Sister Julie B. Beck pushes the claim to its strongest form — "the ability to qualify for, receive, and act on personal revelation is the single most important skill that can be acquired in this life."

[Russell M. Nelson](/notes/russell-m-nelson) is the figure who most carries this theme through Kyle's wiki. The Priesthood book quotes his admonition to "stretch beyond your current spiritual ability to receive personal revelation," and Nelson's own page frames his best-attested doctrine as spiritual self-reliance — that without one's own revelation "it will be impossible to survive spiritually," i.e. borrowed faith is not enough. Personal revelation thus sits at the center of how these sources treat both priesthood knowledge and individual spiritual survival.

## Where this appears

- [The Priesthood Power of Women](/books/the-priesthood-power-of-women) — McConkie, Nelson, and Beck quotes make personal revelation the only way the doctrine of priesthood is learned ("line upon line").
- [Russell M. Nelson](/notes/russell-m-nelson) — his emphasis on spiritual self-reliance: without one's own revelation, surviving spiritually is impossible.
