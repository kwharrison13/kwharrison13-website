# 1 Nephi 1

1 Nephi 1 — the opening chapter of the Book of Mormon — functions in Kyle's gospel study as the **starting point of the Book of Mormon's gospel pattern**. In The Process of the Book of Mormon, it is "Step 1: [Lehi](/notes/lehi)'s first vision… the story of the prophets," where Lehi prays, sees a vision, and sees Christ — the hinge that opens the full pattern (revelation → opposition → restoration → affliction → [Atonement](/notes/atonement)) the study traces across the book's first sixty pages.

The chapter is also read as evidence in two narrower studies. Diligence cites 1 Nephi 1:1 — Nephi's declaration that his record stands on his "knowledge of the goodness and the mysteries of God" — to ground the theme of God's goodness toward His people. Pattern of Prophetic Callings cites 1 Nephi 1:9 (with JSH 1:16) for the principle that "Christ always calls a prophet himself or through another prophet," using Lehi as a parallel to [Joseph Smith](/notes/joseph-smith). It is also listed among the Spiritual Gifts study's references.

**Context:** 1 Nephi 1 opens the Book of Mormon with the prophet Lehi in Jerusalem, his vision of God on His throne and of one descending in brightness (understood as Christ), and his call to prophesy of the city's destruction — beginning Nephi's record of "the goodness and the mysteries of God."
