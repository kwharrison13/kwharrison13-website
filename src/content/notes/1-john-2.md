# 1 John 2

A chapter of the first epistle of John, cited in Kyle's Anti-Christ — Topical Study as a scriptural definition of antichrist. The key verse is 1 John 2:22 — "He who denies Jesus is the Christ is a liar." Kyle works through the nuance: an antichrist is one who would assume the guise of Christ but is in reality opposed to Christ, yet he raises the question of whether one can deny something he does not know — concluding that those who never had the chance to learn of Christ do not lie but are simply left ignorant. The verse pairs with [1 John 4](/notes/1-john-4):3–6 (those who refuse to confess Christ are "not of God" and filled with the "spirit of antichrist") to frame the study's treatment of the Book of Mormon anti-Christs Sherem, Nehor, and Korihor.

**Context:** 1 John 2 is part of the New Testament's first epistle of John, traditionally attributed to the Apostle John, warning believers against antichrists and exhorting them to abide in Christ.
