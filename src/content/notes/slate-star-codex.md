# Slate Star Codex

In Kyle's notes, Slate Star Codex surfaces as the source for "Meditations on Moloch" — cited in the August 18th, 2021 — Valued Empyrean (Conor White-Sullivan & Darwin Harrison) conversation to make a point about why "people are more interested in personal wealth than common good." The note paraphrases the essay's multi-polar-trap argument: "People put more resources into their defensive posture, make greater sacrifices, and end up no better off." It was flagged `#look-it-up`, with the original link recorded (slatestarcodex.com/2014/07/30/meditations-on-moloch/). The reference framed a discussion of how groups that share values can coordinate toward impact rather than getting trapped in zero-sum competition.

**Context:** Slate Star Codex was a long-running blog by the pseudonymous writer Scott Alexander, central to the rationalist community. Its 2014 essay "Meditations on Moloch" personifies "Moloch" as the force of coordination failures and incentive traps that drive rational actors toward collectively bad outcomes (arms races, races to the bottom).
