# Frederick Terman

In [The Idea Factory](/books/the-idea-factory), Frederick Terman is the Stanford provost who served as a "booster" for [William Shockley](/notes/william-shockley) and helped draw him to Palo Alto. With little technology yet out in the Valley — "mostly apricot orchards and undeveloped land" — Terman told Shockley to come and offered to help him find an office in a new industrial park for young, innovative companies. The book notes that Terman is often credited as the father of Silicon Valley (drawing a contrast with Shockley, sometimes called its "Moses," whose failures kept him from the Valley's "promised land of wealth and influence").

**Context:** Frederick Terman (1900–1982) was an American engineer and Stanford University provost whose mentorship of students and recruitment of technology companies to the area around Stanford earned him a lasting reputation as a father of Silicon Valley.
