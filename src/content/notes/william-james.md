# William James

The American philosopher and psychologist surfaces across three of Kyle's books in distinct registers. In [Brave New World Revisited](/books/brave-new-world-revisited), [Aldous Huxley](/notes/aldous-huxley) invokes James's essay "Great Men and Their Environment" to defend the outstanding individual against Herbert Spencer's (and later [B.F. Skinner](/notes/b-f-skinner)'s) determinism — Huxley notes that [Bertrand Russell](/notes/bertrand-russell) sides with James in holding that individuals, not just impersonal social forces, drive historical change, and that Skinner's behaviorism ignores the "physiological forces" James insisted on.

In the two LDS-faith books he is cited for his philosophy of religion. [An Insider's View of Mormon Origins](/books/an-insider-s-view-of-mormon-origins) invokes James via *The Varieties of Religious Experience* on the nature of religious experience. [The Crucible of Doubt](/books/the-crucible-of-doubt) quotes James's famous rejoinder on the "will to believe" — that the person who says "Better to go without belief forever than believe a lie!" merely betrays "his own preponderant private horror of becoming a dupe," and that "worse things than being duped may happen to a man." James's image of the over-cautious general who keeps his soldiers out of battle forever to avoid a single wound becomes, in that book, an argument for a "certain lightness of heart" over excessive intellectual caution about belief.

**Context:** William James (1842–1910) was a founding figure of American psychology and pragmatist philosophy, best known for *The Principles of Psychology* (1890) and *The Varieties of Religious Experience* (1902); his essay "The Will to Believe" (1896) defended the rationality of religious faith under conditions of uncertainty.

## Where this appears

- [Brave New World Revisited](/books/brave-new-world-revisited) — Huxley uses James's "Great Men and Their Environment" to argue (with Russell) against Spencer/Skinner determinism and for the causal weight of individuals.
- [An Insider's View of Mormon Origins](/books/an-insider-s-view-of-mormon-origins) — cited via *The Varieties of Religious Experience* on religious experience.
- [The Crucible of Doubt](/books/the-crucible-of-doubt) — quoted on the "horror of being duped" and the will to believe.
