# Charlie Callinan

Co-founder, with [Dryden Brown](/notes/dryden-brown), of Bluebook Cities — the 2019–2020 effort to build a charter city in West Africa that anchors the [City Building](/notes/city-building) case study. In March 2019 Callinan and Brown were working together at a NYC hedge fund, both having just left athletics and school to learn public-markets investing; per Brown's telling, Callinan played football at Boston College and was briefly signed to the Buffalo Bills before the two quit that April to "build the next great city." Callinan is woven through the bootstrap details of the story: a $2,000 prize he won at a golf tournament (taking their balance to $2,047), plus a small grant from the [Charter Cities](/notes/city-building) Institute, funded their first flights to Nigeria. They built a city thesis near Callinan's university (Elizade) in Ondo State and landed a meeting with the Ondo State Government, before the thread shifted to Ghana — where, after Callinan flew home, Brown continued alone and ultimately met the Vice President.

**Context:** Beyond what Kyle's notes record, public detail on Charlie Callinan is limited; he is documented here primarily as Dryden Brown's Bluebook Cities co-founder.
