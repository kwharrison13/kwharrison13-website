# Cisco

[Cisco](/notes/cisco) surfaces across two very different corners of Kyle's corpus. In **security diligence**, it appears as the slow-moving incumbent: in the [Salt Security](/notes/salt-security) and [Palo Alto Networks ($PANW)](/notes/palo-alto-networks) notes it's cited to explain why startups win — "if you look at Cisco, Palo Alto Networks, etc., their propensity to innovate is much slower," and Cisco has acquired many security companies but tends to let products operate independently rather than integrate them. At the same time Cisco's own CISO (former CISO of Duo) is a marquee reference for [Wiz.io](/notes/wiz-io), reportedly calling Wiz's pitch "the best vendor pitch he has heard in 2 years" because at Cisco he ran 3,500+ cloud instances with no consistent way to reason about risk across siloed orgs (September 17th, 2021 — Wiz.io Pre-Brief (Shardul Shah)).

In **city-building and social-innovation** notes (from Kyle's 2018 calls), Cisco recurs as a corporate-philanthropy and smart-city actor. It is the anchor funder of [Destination: Home](/notes/destination-home) — its $50M grant seeded Santa Clara County's Supportive Housing and Innovation Fund, with ~90% directed to acquiring housing stock; [Chad Bojorquez](/notes/chad-bojorquez) spent roughly a year securing that grant, and [Social Innovation](/notes/social-innovation) frames the gift as a community-partnership necessity rather than a tax. On the smart-city side, [Venture Smarter](/notes/venture-smarter) is a Cisco partner (and the source of Cisco's "~75% of IoT projects fail" study); [UrbanLeap](/notes/urbanleap) ran a pilot where Cisco would pay and provide the platform free to cities; and [David Pickeral](/notes/david-pickeral) critiques Cisco (with Microsoft, IBM, Intel) for wanting to *sell to* cities rather than invest in or partner with them. [Hasan Luongo](/notes/hasan-luongo) spent a few months at Cisco post-acquisition.

**Context:** Cisco Systems is an American multinational networking-hardware and enterprise-technology company headquartered in San Jose, with a large acquired security portfolio and a longstanding corporate-social-responsibility / smart-cities program.
