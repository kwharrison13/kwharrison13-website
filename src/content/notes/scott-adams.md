# Scott Adams

Quoted at length in [Atomic Habits](/books/atomic-habits) by [James Clear](/notes/james-clear) as an illustration of how to "create a game where the odds are stacked in your favor." Adams, the cartoonist behind *Dilbert*, describes how he isn't elite at any single skill — he can draw better than most people but is "hardly an artist," and he's "not any funnier than the average standup comedian who never makes it big." The insight Clear draws from him is combinatorial: "few people can draw well and write jokes," and adding a business background gave Adams a topic few cartoonists could understand. The "stack top-25% skills into a rare combination" framing supports Atomic Habits' argument about playing to your strengths and choosing the right field of competition.

**Context:** Scott Adams (b. 1957) is an American cartoonist and author best known for creating the *Dilbert* comic strip, which satirizes white-collar office culture. He has also written business and self-help books, including *How to Fail at Almost Everything and Still Win Big*, the source of the "talent stack" idea quoted here.
