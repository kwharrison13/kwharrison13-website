# Crunchbase

In Kyle's notes, Crunchbase surfaces almost entirely as the **data backbone of [SignalRank](/notes/signalrank)**. On the June 6th, 2022 call, [Rob Hodgkinson](/notes/rob-hodgkinson) explained that SignalRank's founder, Keith, *created* Crunchbase (after building Europe's first ISP, an early unicorn, and RealNames) and sold it. SignalRank "productizes pro rata" by leveraging Crunchbase data to rank every investor into every unicorn ever — angels, incubators, seed managers, VCs, family offices — by how many unicorns they backed and how early, then partnering with the best-ranked seed managers to buy into their breakout portfolios at the Series B. (June 6th, 2022 — Rob Hodgkinson (SignalRank))

Crunchbase is thus treated as the canonical dataset for mapping the venture-investor landscape — the raw material that lets SignalRank turn investor track records into an algorithmic sourcing engine. It also appears in [Knowledge Economy](/notes/knowledge-economy) as part of that page's landscape of reference/data platforms, named alongside Wikipedia.

**Context:** Crunchbase is a commercial database of companies, funding rounds, investors, and people, originally launched in 2007 as a project inside TechCrunch and later spun out as an independent company. It is widely used by investors, salespeople, and researchers to track startup financing and company information.
