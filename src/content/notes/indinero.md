# InDinero

An accounting-and-tax automation company led by CEO [Jessica Mah](/notes/jessica-mah), captured in Kyle's July 2018 call with her. Mah framed the product as **augmenting accountants rather than replacing them** — *"if someone comes in and says they have a magic formula to automate accounting / tax, they're probably lying"* — by observing accountants, pulling in customer data, and automating around their service. She positioned InDinero as complementing rather than competing with [Xero](/notes/xero) and [Quickbooks](/notes/quickbooks).

On financials, the notes record north of $10M (sounding like ~$30M) in revenue, EBITDA-positive and growing without burning capital. The company was raising a $15–30M round, preferred working with insiders, wanted to cap dilution at ~10%, and expected to raise again in 18 months. Mah was guarded — sharing little detail and looking only for an investor able to write a $10M check — which reads in the notes as a politely closed door on Index's interest.

**Context:** inDinero is a financial-operations company offering outsourced accounting, tax, and bookkeeping services to small and mid-sized businesses, co-founded by Jessica Mah and Andy Su after going through Y Combinator.
