# History of the Church

A tag Kyle uses for his study of [LDS](/notes/the-church-of-jesus-christ-of-latter-day-saints) institutional history. It surfaces on [Saints — No Unhallowed Hand](/books/saints-no-unhallowed-hand), the second volume of the Church's official narrative history, which he read in 2021 and tagged `#Faith` #[History of the Church](/notes/history-of-the-church) #[Family History](/notes/family-history). In Kyle's reading the interest is less in the events of the pioneer trek than in the *interior* problem of **building an institution out of fallible people** — Brigham Young's fear that prosperity, not persecution, would ruin the Saints; the candid admission that "a prophet… may be fallible" while "the work… emanated from God"; Wilford Woodruff's Manifesto and his theology of revelation; and the governance mechanics of the Quorum of the Twelve acting "unanimously or not at all." History-of-the-church, for Kyle, is a case study in [Institution Building](/notes/institution-building), [Stewardship](/notes/stewardship), and [Living Revelation](/notes/living-revelation).
