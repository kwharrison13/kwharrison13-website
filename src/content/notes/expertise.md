# Expertise

Kyle's notes on expertise pull in two directions that resolve into a single picture: how real mastery is actually built, and where its authority breaks down. On the *building* side, [How To Take Smart Notes](/books/how-to-take-smart-notes) frames expertise as intuition — "sedimented experience, not rules" — where "real experts don't make plans" and memory works by retrieval strength, attaching information to a framework so the right cues fire (closing on [Charlie Munger](/notes/charlie-munger)'s latticework). [Range](/books/range) complicates the deliberate-practice story: experience breeds skill only in "kind" learning environments, and in "wicked" domains where patterns don't repeat, narrow experts are out-predicted by broad, self-doubting "foxes." [The Science of Learning](/notes/the-science-of-learning) attacks the "10,000-hour myth" head-on — practice may make a surgeon or mechanic awfully good, but "it takes more than that to become a compassionate minister, an intuitive psychotherapist, or even a good parent." [Leonardo Da Vinci](/books/leonardo-da-vinci) models a transferable, replicable kind of mastery — think visually, learn the rules then break them, "consult experience first and then with reasoning show why."

On the *authority* side, expertise is the eroding institution at the center of declining public trust. [Conspiracy Theories](/notes/conspiracy-theories) places the "death of expertise" alongside falling institutional trust and cable news as a symptom of decline — conspiracy theories appeal because they "simplify a complicated problem" once expert authority weakens. [Voice of the People](/notes/voice-of-the-people) draws on [The Death of Expertise](/books/the-death-of-expertise) for the corrective: "a people who mean to be their own Governors must arm themselves with the power which knowledge gives" (Madison), and "the wisdom of crowds is not the same as the crowds are wise." The strand connects to [Educated Electorate](/notes/educated-electorate) (the informed-citizenry defense) and to [Breaking into VC](/notes/breaking-into-vc), whose thesis is the constructive version — become the recognized expert on a person or topic "until you have to be hired."

**Context:** Expertise is deep, domain-specific competence built through extended experience and practice. Debates in cognitive science distinguish genuine expert intuition (in stable, feedback-rich domains) from overconfident pattern-matching in uncertain ones — and contemporary cultural debate concerns the erosion of public deference to expert authority.
