# Andrew Wilkinson

Andrew Wilkinson appears in Kyle's [Berkshire Hathaway Annual Letters](/books/berkshire-hathaway-annual-letters) page among the Private Connections people — grouped with [Warren Buffett](/notes/warren-buffett), [Charlie Munger](/notes/charlie-munger), [Howard Marks](/notes/howard-marks), [Morgan Housel](/notes/morgan-housel), and other capital-allocation and holding-company figures. The grouping situates him in the same constellation of ideas the Berkshire letters anchor: [Capital Allocation](/notes/capital-allocation), building a [Family of Companies](/notes/family-of-companies) / [Holding Company](/notes/holding-company), and "[Buy and Never Sell](/notes/buy-and-never-sell)" long-term ownership. The page itself carries no further commentary on him.

**Context:** Andrew Wilkinson is a Canadian entrepreneur and investor, co-founder of Tiny (Tiny Capital), a holding company that acquires and holds profitable internet and software businesses for the long term — explicitly modeled on the Berkshire Hathaway "buy good companies and never sell" approach. He co-hosts the *Two Bored Guys* podcast and is an active writer on business and acquisitions.
