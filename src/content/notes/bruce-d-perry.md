# Bruce D. Perry

Bruce D. Perry is the lead author of [The Boy Who Was Raised as a Dog](/books/the-boy-who-was-raised-as-a-dog) (co-written with science journalist [Maia Szalavitz](/notes/maia-szalavitz)). In Kyle's notes he is described as the **child psychiatrist whose clinical cases anchor the book's neurodevelopmental argument** — the book builds its case about childhood trauma, memory, empathy, and relationships out of his real patient stories. The page links his work to Kyle's broader threads on [Memory](/notes/memory), [Relationships](/notes/relationships), [Empathy](/notes/empathy), and [Family](/notes/family), and to related parenting reads like [The Self-Driven Child](/books/the-self-driven-child) and [The Parents' Guide to Boys](/books/the-parents-guide-to-boys).

**Context:** Bruce D. Perry is an American psychiatrist and senior fellow of the ChildTrauma Academy, known for research on how early trauma and neglect shape the developing brain. His later book *What Happened to You?* was co-authored with Oprah Winfrey.
