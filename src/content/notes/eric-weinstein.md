# Eric Weinstein

Cited in [Bet On Humanity](/notes/bet-on-humanity) for the claim that the future of work won't return as stable jobs — opportunities will be "many and lavishly rewarded" for a few creatives but "deeply troubling for a majority who depend on stable and cyclical work to feed families."

**Context:** Eric Weinstein is an American economist and mathematician, the former managing director of Thiel Capital, and host of *The Portal* podcast. He coined the term "Intellectual Dark Web" and writes/speaks on labor markets, stagnation, and the structure of modern work.

## Where this appears

- [Bet On Humanity](/notes/bet-on-humanity) — quoted on the disappearance of stable jobs and the bifurcation of future opportunity
