# Mosiah 27

The Book of Mormon chapter recounting [Alma the Younger](/notes/alma-the-younger)'s conversion, which Kyle reads for two complementary verses. The first is [Mosiah 27](/notes/mosiah-27):25–26 — becoming "a new creature in Christ." In the How To Apply The Atonement topical study this verse anchors Kyle's central image: the [Atonement](/notes/atonement) does not pull nails from a board and leave holes; it throws away the tired, worn board and gives you a new one, "generally better than the first." The same verse appears in Mighty Repentance, where conversion changes the natural man into "a sanctified, born again, purified person." So Mosiah 27 is Kyle's scriptural home for the doctrine that the Atonement makes you new rather than merely patching the old.

The second use is the surrounding narrative: in the Fasting - September 5, 2021 study, Kyle cites [Mosiah 27](/notes/mosiah-27):22–23, where the priests fast and pray for two days and nights that God would "open the mouth of Alma" and restore his limbs — an example of fasting for a special purpose at a time other than fast Sunday.

## Where this appears

- How To Apply The Atonement — Mosiah 27:25–26 as "become a new creature," the new-board metaphor for the Atonement
- Mighty Repentance — the same verse on conversion making one born again
- Fasting - September 5, 2021 — Mosiah 27:22–23 on fasting and praying for Alma's recovery
