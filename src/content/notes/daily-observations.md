# Daily Observations

Daily Observations is [Bridgewater Associates](/notes/bridgewater-associates)' published research letter — the central artifact Kyle points to when he holds Bridgewater up as a [Wordsmith](/notes/wordsmiths) culture, an organization that builds itself around writing. In his storytelling research he uses it as a concrete example of "show, don't tell": *"A cardinal rule of [Storytelling](/notes/storytelling) is to 'show, don't tell.' … You think Bridgewater publishes their Daily Observations for fun? Find a way to show, not tell."* The letter, in this framing, is how the firm institutionalizes written reasoning rather than merely asserting its views.

It sits alongside [Amazon](/notes/amazon)'s six-page narratives, [Stripe](/notes/stripe)'s documentation, and [Berkshire Hathaway](/notes/berkshire-hathaway)'s Buffett letters as one of the four exemplars Kyle planned to profile in a "Wordsmith Spotlight" series.

**Context:** Daily Observations is Bridgewater's long-running daily market-and-economy research note, written for clients and considered a core piece of the firm's intellectual culture under founder Ray Dalio.
