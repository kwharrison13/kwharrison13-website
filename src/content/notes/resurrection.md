# Resurrection

A core doctrine threaded through several of Kyle's gospel-study notes. In Russell M. Nelson's Easter address it is paired with the [Atonement](/notes/atonement) as the event that "changed each of our lives forever," and tied to [Hope](/notes/hope) via Moroni 7:40–42 — hope comes "through the atonement of Christ and the power of his resurrection."

In Alma 30–31, resurrection is one of the truths Korihor the [Anti-Christ](/notes/anti-christ) argues against — alongside [prophecy](/notes/prophecy), the [Atonement](/notes/atonement), [Agency](/notes/agency), ordinances, and priesthood — marking it as a load-bearing doctrine the wicked seek to deny. In The King Follett Sermon it appears in [Joseph Smith](/notes/joseph-smith)'s expansive teaching on man's eternal destiny: Christ had power "to lay down his body and take it up again," and the resurrection is bound up with [Exaltation](/notes/exaltation) and [Eternal Progression](/notes/eternal-progression) — rising "from one small degree to another… until you attain to the resurrection of the dead."

**Context:** In Christian and Latter-day Saint doctrine, the Resurrection is the reuniting of body and spirit in an immortal state, made possible by the resurrection of Jesus Christ, and is foundational to the plan of salvation and the hope of eternal life.
