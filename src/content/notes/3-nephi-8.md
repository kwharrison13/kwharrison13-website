# 3 Nephi 8

A Book of Mormon chapter opening the Come Follow Me study of 3 Nephi 8–11 — the great destruction at the death of Christ, with three hours of darkness across the land. Kyle draws two threads from it. First, [Sign Seeking](/notes/sign-seeking) hardens hearts over time: despite "so many signs had been given," the people fall into "great doubtings and disputations" (8:4), echoing [3 Nephi 2](/notes/3-nephi-2) where they grow "less astonished at a sign." Second, [Regret](/notes/regret) is "the cry of the destroyed" — "O that we had repented before this great and terrible day" (8:24–25) — prompting Kyle's note that there must be a way to study regret to help avoid the things we know we will later regret.

**Context:** 3 Nephi 8 records the cataclysm at Christ's crucifixion in the Book of Mormon — tempests, earthquakes, cities sunk and burned, and three days of thick darkness over the land of the Nephites.
