# Joseph Stalin

Stalin appears in the wiki only as the historical benchmark in [The Invention of Russia](/books/the-invention-of-russia)'s closing claim about [Vladimir Putin](/notes/vladimir-putin): "From Chechnya to Syria, Russia under Putin has killed more people than the Soviet Union did under any leader since Stalin." He functions here as the high-water mark of Soviet lethality against which Putin's record is measured — the implication being that one has to reach back to Stalin to find a comparably deadly Russian leadership.

**Context:** Joseph Stalin (1878–1953) led the Soviet Union from the mid-1920s until his death; his rule is associated with forced collectivization, the Great Purge, and famines that together caused millions of deaths.
