# Mark Lutter

Mark Lutter is the founder of the [Charter Cities Institute](/notes/charter-cities-institute) (CCI) and, in Kyle's notes, the primary node into the Silicon Valley new-cities world. He sits at the center of the [Charter Cities](/notes/city-building) / [City Building](/notes/city-building) thesis: when [Dryden Brown](/notes/dryden-brown) and [Charlie Callinan](/notes/charlie-callinan) went into research mode in 2019 to build a charter city in West Africa, Lutter and [Tamara Winter](/notes/tamara-winter) (then both at CCI) were among the new-cities crowd they consulted — alongside [Iyinoluwa Aboyeji](/notes/iyinoluwa-aboyeji), [Patri Friedman](/notes/patri-friedman), [Mike Solana](/notes/mike-solana), and [Jason Crawford](/notes/jason-crawford). CCI also gave Brown and Callinan their first small grant, which (together with Callinan's golf-tournament winnings) funded their flights to Nigeria.

He recurs as the institutional-experimentation wing of [Progress Studies](/notes/progress-studies): Kyle files the charter-cities / city-building strand under Progress Studies as its "institutional-experimentation" arm, with Lutter as its representative figure. The saved SoundCloud talk pairs him explicitly with Progress Studies and charter cities. [Tamara Winter](/notes/tamara-winter), by contrast, threads from CCI into the [Stripe](/notes/stripe) / Stripe Press orbit (she later became managing editor of Stripe Press).

**Context:** Mark Lutter is an economist who founded the Charter Cities Institute, a nonprofit advancing the "charter city" idea — new cities granted a distinct set of governing rules, a concept popularized by economist [Paul Romer](/notes/paul-romer) — as a way to import better institutions into a region.
