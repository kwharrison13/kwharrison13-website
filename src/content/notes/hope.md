# Hope

In Kyle's gospel-study notes hope is treated as **inseparable from [Faith](/notes/faith)** — the middle term of the faith-hope-charity triad. The defining citation is [Moroni 7](/notes/moroni-7):40–42: "if a man have faith he must needs have hope; for without faith there cannot be any hope." This pairing recurs in the Moroni 7–9 study, where hope is one of the three pillars of Mormon's sermon, and in Christ Is Risen; Faith in Him Will Move Mountains — Russell M. Nelson. The 1 Nephi 16–22 study turns hope into a posture of perseverance — "never give up hope," Nephi's "joy and great hopes" when his brethren humble themselves ([1 Nephi 16](/notes/1-nephi-16):5), and Joseph Smith's counsel (via George A. Smith) to "hang on, exercise faith, and keep up good courage" even when "sunk in the lowest pit." Kyle's mission-era topical treatment lives in Hope — Topical Study.

Hope also surfaces in Kyle's reading. [Wicked](/books/wicked) supplies a darker register: Elphaba wondering "whether it was immoral to raise children in the habit of hope," a question Kyle flags as part of the book's moral seriousness. [Becoming](/books/becoming) lists hope among the human concepts Michelle Obama's memoir threads (alongside resilience, optimism, and mentorship).

**Context:** Hope is one of the three theological virtues (faith, hope, charity) in Christian tradition. In Latter-day Saint usage it is specifically defined through Moroni and Ether as confident expectation of resurrection and eternal life through Christ — distinct from ordinary wishful optimism.
