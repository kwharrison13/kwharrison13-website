# Doug Leone

Doug Leone is the [Sequoia](/notes/sequoia) leader whose performance-first maxim Kyle treats as a load-bearing quote across his venture writing. The line — quoted from his September 2021 appearance with [Harry Stebbings](/notes/harry-stebbings) — is: "The two most important things are (1) performance, and (2) teamwork. But if you don't have number 1 then nothing else matters." Kyle reuses it both in his September 13th, 2022 — Harry Stebbings Podcast prep (to define a "so-so venture firm") and as the performance anchor of The Death of a Venture Fund - Research.

Leone is also a centerpiece of the **succession-planning** thread in The Death of a Venture Fund - Research, which Kyle flags (alongside performance) as the only consistently cited cause of a venture fund's death — 75%+ of his pre-mortem interview responses. Sequoia is one of the only firms to survive multiple regime changes, and Leone embodies the long-runway pipeline that makes that possible: he spent 24 years at the firm before taking over, and [Roelof Botha](/notes/roelof-botha) was there 9 years before Leone handed off. The lesson Kyle draws: no long runway for leaders means you're building a team, not a durable institution.

**Context:** Doug Leone is an Italian-American venture capitalist who led Sequoia Capital as Global Managing Partner from 1996 until handing leadership to Roelof Botha around 2022. He oversaw Sequoia's global expansion and its growth into a multi-stage, multi-geography franchise.
