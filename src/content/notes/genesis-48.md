# Genesis 48

**Full text:** Genesis 48 (KJV)


A chapter of the Old Testament book of Genesis, central to Kyle's Abrahamic Covenant gospel-study notes as the scriptural account of the birthright passing to [Ephraim](/notes/ephraim). The notes cite Genesis 48:17–20: Joseph's two sons are Manasseh and Ephraim, and though Manasseh is the firstborn, Israel (Jacob) crosses his hands to give the birthright blessing to the younger Ephraim "because he knows his seed will grow." This anchors the larger argument that the covenant birthright — a double portion and patriarchal status — passed from the unrighteous or unfavored firstborn to a chosen younger line, with most holders of the birthright today being of Ephraim and bearing the responsibility to gather Israel.

**Context:** Genesis 48 records the dying Jacob adopting and blessing Joseph's sons Ephraim and Manasseh, deliberately favoring the younger Ephraim.

## Where this appears

- Abrahamic Covenant — quoted (Gen. 48:17–20) as the scriptural basis for the birthright passing to Ephraim over Manasseh.
