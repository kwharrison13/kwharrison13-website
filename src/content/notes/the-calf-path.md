# The Calf-Path

"The Calf-Path" is the Sam Walter Foss poem that [David Perell](/notes/david-perell) quotes in his Amazon write-up as a warning about the danger of blindly following well-worn tracks — the frame for how [Jeff Bezos](/notes/jeff-bezos) trained veteran retailers to think differently.

**Context:** "The Calf-Path" (1895) by Sam Walter Foss is a poem about how a crooked path first trodden by a calf becomes, over generations, a road and then a city street that everyone follows without question — a parable about inherited convention and path dependence.

## Where this appears

- [Amazon- The Everything Store (article)](/notes/amazon-the-everything-store-article) — quoted as the Foss poem on the danger of following beaten tracks, applied to Bezos teaching retailers to think differently.
