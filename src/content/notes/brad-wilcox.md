# Brad Wilcox

A teacher in The Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-day Saints whose recurring theme in Kyle's gospel-study notes is grace, change, and the [Atonement](/notes/atonement). In his October 2021 General Conference address (Saturday Evening Session), Wilcox argued that [Repentance](/notes/repentance) and change are *essential*, not optional — "Christ's Atonement is all about change," and "he loves us too much to leave us as we are." His framing of worthiness anchors Kyle's notes: "Worthiness is not flawlessness. It is being honest about trying," and "Trying over and over again to take steps to be better is not being a hypocrite. It is being a disciple." His most-cited line in Kyle's notes: "Spend less time hating yourself for what you have done and more time loving Christ for what he has done."

His book [His Grace is Sufficient](/notes/his-grace-is-sufficient) is cited in Kyle's Alma 13–16 study for the same idea — that the miracle of the [Atonement](/notes/atonement) is not just that we can return home but that we can feel at home there, and that those who live without faith and repentance "are trying to abandon God and become comfortable with sin" rather than the reverse. Kyle uses Wilcox's grace-and-change message as a counterweight to [Universalism](/notes/universalism), which "comes close to the truth but perverts it."

**Context:** Brad Wilcox is a religious educator and, since 2021, a member of the Young Men General Presidency of The Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-day Saints; he is widely known among Latter-day Saints for his talk and book "His Grace Is Sufficient" on the relationship between grace and works.
