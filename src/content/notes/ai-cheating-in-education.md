# AI Cheating in Education

**One-line:** *The thread running through [We Need a Renaissance of Rhetoric](/essays/we-need-a-renaissance-of-rhetoric): students are off-loading writing and thinking onto LLMs, and the deeper diagnosis is that a factory-farmed, "codifiable" education had already hollowed out before AI arrived to expose it.*

## The shape of the argument (per Kyle's research)

- **The recurring student rationale:** "this isn't relevant to life, so why do it?" → just cheat. (Kyle's note on the NY Mag reporting.)
- **The cognitive cost:** early studies link AI off-loading to deteriorating memory, problem-solving, and critical thinking, more pronounced in younger users — echoed by the slowing/reversing [Flynn Effect](/notes/flynn-effect) ([Robert Sternberg](/notes/robert-sternberg)).
- **The skills rebuttal ([Tyler Cowen](/notes/tyler-cowen)):** education was already in crisis from "ideological capture, political monoculture, and extreme conformism"; AI literacy is itself the skill the future demands. Kyle treats this as valid-but-incomplete — skills serve ethos, but rhetoric and [thymos](/notes/thymos) are about more than marketable skill.
- **The humanist defense ([Troy Jollimore](/notes/troy-jollimore), Orin Starn, [W.H. Auden](/notes/w-h-auden)):** writing is how thinking happens — "language is the mother, not the handmaiden, of thought." Losing the practice means losing the worldview.
