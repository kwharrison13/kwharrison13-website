# Pessimism

_Stub — created on ingest of The Coward's Conviction - Research. In [The Coward's Conviction](/essays/the-cowards-conviction), Kyle frames pessimism as the intellectual default of *not thinking* — "people don't want to take the time to think about what could happen ([Optimism](/notes/optimism)), so they default to not thinking at all." Pessimism "always sounds smarter" and plays into the hand of anyone trying to avoid failure, but the durable wins come from the harder work of optimism. He endorses Arthur Koestler's "fraternity of (short-term) pessimists": basic optimists can afford to face facts and be pessimistic in their short-term predictions, while critics serve as a necessary balancing force (Roy vs. Walt [Disney](/notes/roy-o-disney))._
