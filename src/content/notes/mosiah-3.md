# Mosiah 3

In Kyle's gospel-study notes, Mosiah 3 is almost always shorthand for a single verse — **Mosiah 3:19**, King Benjamin's teaching that "the natural man is an enemy to God… unless he yields to the enticings of the Holy Spirit, and putteth off the natural man and becometh a saint through the atonement of Christ the Lord, and becometh as a child, submissive, meek, humble, patient, full of love." It is one of the load-bearing verses across his topical work, cited in General Conference — October 2021, Christ Is Risen; Faith in Him Will Move Mountains — Russell M. Nelson, and the July 6–12, 2020. Alma 30–31: "The Virtue of the Word of God" Come Follow Me notes.

Kyle uses the verse to structure a repeated argument about spiritual change. In Diligence, the [Natural Man](/notes/natural-man) is the "enemy to God" who, lulled into carnal security ("All is well in Zion"), is led "by the neck with a flaxen cord" — and the remedy is to yield to the Spirit and put off the natural man through the [Atonement](/notes/atonement). Virtue draws his sharpest distinction from the verse: putting off the natural man is an *internal subjection* (a power struggle over the soul), while becoming a saint comes only through *submission* — "yielding to the enticings of the holy spirit." Patience — Topical Study reads the same verse as the call to accept what we cannot understand, and Born Again pairs it with the Fall ([Moses 4](/notes/moses-4)) — taking off the "figs" of the natural man and putting on Christ.

**Context:** Mosiah 3 records the angel-delivered portion of King Benjamin's address in the Book of Mormon, prophesying of Jesus Christ and the Atonement. Verse 19, on putting off "the natural man," is among the most frequently cited verses in Latter-day Saint teaching.

## Where this appears

- Diligence — 3:19, the natural man led "by the neck with a flaxen cord"; yielding to the Spirit as the remedy
- Virtue — 3:19, the subjection (putting off) vs. submission (becoming a saint) distinction
- Born Again — 3:19 with Moses 4; taking off the natural man and putting on Christ
- Patience — Topical Study — 3:19 as the call to submit to what we cannot understand
- Christ Is Risen; Faith in Him Will Move Mountains — Russell M. Nelson — 3:19 quoted on becoming a saint through the Atonement
- General Conference — October 2021 — 3:19 cited in the conference notes
- July 6–12, 2020. Alma 30–31: "The Virtue of the Word of God" — 3:19 as a cross-reference
