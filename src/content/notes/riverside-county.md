# Riverside County

**One-line definition:** *The 10th largest county in the US — referenced for its Chief Data Officer's work on transparency, data infrastructure, and a stalled multi-city fiber build-out.*

## How sources describe it

- 10th largest US county (~2.5M residents, 28 cities); created a Chief Data Officer role ~3 years prior around transparency and citizen exposure into county operations. (June 28th, 2018 — Tom Mullen (Riverside County))
- Attempted a single coordinated fiber build-out across its 28 cities, but private-sector investment would have run ~$2B — too much even at the county's scale; getting all the cities and officials aligned was itself a feat.
- Manages 125 systems of record (healthcare, financial, spatial) with no single tool; rolling out Microsoft Power BI county-wide and exploring infrastructure-access monetization (light poles, roadway sensors, fleet telematics) as an alternative to selling to government.
