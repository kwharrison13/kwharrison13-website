# Every knee shall bow

A theme Kyle develops in his October 2020 Come Follow Me study of 3 Nephi 20–26, where he ties the phrase to [Unity of the Faith](/notes/unity-of-the-faith) and the limits of language. Reading [3 Nephi 21](/notes/3-nephi-21):20 (those who will not repent and come unto Christ are "cut off from among my people"), he frames "every knee shall bow" as "the ultimate sign of unity when no one can deny Jesus Christ." He pairs it with a reflection on imperfect communication — that we never communicate perfectly, holding ideas in our minds that come across with different takeaways — and points toward the [Adamic Language](/notes/adamic-language) that was "pure and undefiled" as the eventual resolution. He also raises it as an open question: even in the millennium, will alternative religions persist?

**Context:** "Every knee shall bow, and every tongue confess" is a scriptural phrase appearing in Isaiah 45:23 and quoted by Paul in Romans 14:11 and Philippians 2:10, traditionally read as the universal acknowledgment of God at the last day.
