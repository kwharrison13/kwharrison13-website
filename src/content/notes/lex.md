# LEX

A commercial real estate (CRE) securities marketplace that surfaced via an August 2021 intro during Kyle's [Index Ventures](/notes/index-ventures) sourcing. Per the intro materials, LEX lets **anyone — including non-accredited investors — buy and sell shares of individual buildings for $250/share with no minimums**, earning quarterly income from the building's tenants while holding shares. Its differentiator was an **uncompromising approach to democratizing CRE**: investors buy into *individual* buildings (the original owner retains equity, keeping LEX asset-light and scalable as a marketplace), and LEX operates an **SEC-registered ATS** allowing 9:30am-4:00pm trading like ordinary stocks, with no holding-period restrictions.

Founded three years prior with a mission to solve real estate's access and liquidity problems, LEX had at that point **raised $12.5M in seed financing from Greycroft and others**, secured all regulatory approvals, and taken its **first building public in March 2021** (trading under ticker $GWYGU). At the time of the intro it was raising a **~$20M Series A** to accelerate its real estate pipeline and grow the marketplace.

**Context:** LEX Markets was a New York fintech startup building a public exchange for fractional shares of individual commercial properties; it wound down operations in 2023.
