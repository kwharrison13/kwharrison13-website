# Cambridge Analytica

Cambridge Analytica appears in Kyle's notes via [Bonfire of the Consultancies](/notes/bonfire-of-the-consultancies), Lionel Barber's review of *When McKinsey Comes to Town* and *The Big Con*. It is named as part of the most damning episode in the consulting critique: in McKinsey's Saudi Arabia work on Vision 2030, McKinsey — working with Bain and Cambridge Analytica — appears to have promoted "sentiment analysis" to track public attitudes, and the mined social-media posts were subsequently used by the Saudi regime to track political opponents. In the note it is a load-bearing example of how the gap between consulting's high-minded self-image and its "grubby pursuit of profit" turned into work for despotic regimes.

**Context:** Cambridge Analytica was a British political-consulting and data-analytics firm, best known for the 2018 scandal over its harvesting of Facebook user data to build psychographic profiles for political ad targeting (including the Trump 2016 campaign). The fallout led to its bankruptcy and closure in 2018.
