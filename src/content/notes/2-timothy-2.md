# 2 Timothy 2

A chapter of the New Testament cited in Kyle's Humility — Topical Study. There, 2 Timothy 2:21 supplies the list of what a person becomes when "purged" of impurity — "a vessel unto honor, sanctified, meet for the master's use, and prepared unto every good work." Kyle uses the verse to describe the fruit of being cleansed and sanctified through Christ, alongside the parable of the imprisoned prince ("I was born to be a king") as an image of recognizing one's true potential as a child of God.

**Context:** 2 Timothy 2 is part of the apostle Paul's final epistle to Timothy, exhorting him to endure hardship as a soldier of Christ and to be a "vessel unto honor" by departing from iniquity.
