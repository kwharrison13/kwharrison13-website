# Remote.com

Appears in the Pave Diligence — 2021 dossier as a customer-reference contact during [Index Ventures](/notes/index-ventures)' compensation-tooling diligence. Nadia Vatalidis at Remote.com responded to outreach with a detailed account of how Remote evaluated and ultimately *passed* on Pave: "We reviewed multiple Vendors during Q2 (pave included) and settled on Kamsa." Her rationale is a useful negative data point on Pave's gaps as of 2021 — she chose Kamsa because it was globally aligned and could support the 50–85 countries Remote was scaling into, offered quarterly data refreshes (vs. annual), aligned to "modernHR/ModernTech" with nothing manual day-to-day (a "huge improvement from Mercer, AON ([Radford](/notes/radford))"), and had job titles and career levels that weren't stuck "from the '90s." Her noted downside of Kamsa: data couldn't be shared with customers, and the USD exchange rate was a bi-annual rolling average.

The reference underscores a core Pave risk the diligence flagged — whether the product could serve companies operating well beyond US-centric tech (Remote's globally-distributed workforce was exactly the case Kamsa won on).

**Context:** Remote.com (Remote) is a global HR platform for hiring, payroll, benefits, and compliance across dozens of countries, used by distributed and remote-first companies.
