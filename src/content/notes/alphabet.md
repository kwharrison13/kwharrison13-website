# Alphabet

Alphabet (Google) recurs across Kyle's notes as one of the handful of mega-cap technology winners that anchor his arguments about market structure and outlier returns. It is named as a [FAANG](/notes/faang) constituent — the "G" in the acronym for the cluster of stocks that drove an outsized share of public-market returns through the 2010s. In [The Hits Business](/essays/the-hits-business), Kyle uses Alphabet (alongside Apple and Amazon) as evidence of how dramatically tech climbed the Fortune 500 between 2012 and today; in [The Puritans of Venture Capital](/essays/the-puritans-of-venture-capital), it appears in his count of the ~528 public tech companies worth more than $1B — his case that truly generational companies are rare and most "unicorns" will be "de-horned."

Alphabet also operates [CapitalG](/notes/capitalg), described in Kyle's notes as Alphabet's independent growth fund — the corporate-VC vehicle that, per the September 22nd, 2021 — Ganesh Pai (Uptycs) sourcing call, had a term sheet ready for Uptycs that was "shut down by the [CrowdStrike ($CRWD)](/notes/crowdstrike) team."

**Context:** Alphabet Inc. is the Mountain View holding company created in 2015 to house Google and its "Other Bets," led for years by former CEO and executive chairman [Eric Schmidt](/notes/eric-schmidt). It is one of the most valuable public companies in the world. This is general background.
