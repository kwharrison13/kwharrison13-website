# Bryant S. Hinckley

Bryant S. Hinckley is the father of [Gordon B. Hinckley](/notes/gordon-b-hinckley), and in [Gordon B. Hinckley — Go Forward With Faith](/books/gordon-b-hinckley-go-forward-with-faith) he is the formative influence on Gordon's character and outlook. He and his wife Ada built a home library of more than a thousand volumes — a complete set of Shakespeare, numerous Lincoln titles, the fifty-volume Harvard Classics, and *The World's Best Histories* — cultivating in their children a love of literature, history, and learning that Gordon carried for life. Bryant rarely lectured but repeated maxims that communicated his expectations: "Try to live up to the high-water mark of your possibilities," "Be somebody. Stand up for something," and "Cynics do not contribute; skeptics do not create; doubters do not achieve."

His most quoted line in the book is a working-class creed: "There is no royalty like the royalty of high endeavor. We do not belong to a family of geniuses… Each of us must pay the price of hard work if we achieve." The decisive moment comes when a discouraged Gordon, struggling on his mission, writes home suggesting he quit; serving as both father and stake president, Bryant replies in a single brief letter — "Dear Gordon, I have your recent letter. I have only one suggestion: forget yourself and go to work." That counsel, the book frames, redirected Gordon's life.
