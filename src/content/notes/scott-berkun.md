# Scott Berkun

**One-line:** *Author and speaker on creativity, innovation, and management; wrote [The Myths of Innovation](/books/the-myths-of-innovation).*

## What we know

- Author of [The Myths of Innovation](/books/the-myths-of-innovation) (2007), a debunking of the romantic story of how ideas get made — the epiphany, the lone genius, the magic moment — in favor of innovation as long sequences of ordinary, unglamorous work.
- Ex-Microsoft: worked on Internet Explorer and other products, shipping a new version "every three or four months," before quitting in 2003 to write books.
- Frames himself as "an Occam's razor kind of guy" — arguing the real barriers to innovation are simple cultural things we overlook (lack of trust, risk-aversion, obsession with epiphany), not missing budgets or gadgets. See [Occam's Razor](/notes/occam-s-razor).
- Popularized/relayed the practitioner canon around [Brainstorming](/notes/brainstorming): recovering Alex Osborn's original fact/idea/solution method against the "abused and bastardized" modern usage.
