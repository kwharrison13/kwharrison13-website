# Webflow

Webflow shows up in Kyle's notes as the front-end / visual web-builder layer of the no-code stack. In the August 18th, 2021 — Rob @ Cartooli on WhaleSync customer-reference call, Rob describes Webflow as the attractive presentation layer paired against [Airtable](/notes/airtable)'s data layer: Webflow is "good at" building beautiful pages "but isn't set up to really take in data," and its CMS "makes it possible but forces you to troubleshoot all the time to make sure things are synced." That sync pain is precisely the gap [WhaleSync](/notes/whalesync) fills — letting builders push Airtable data into a Webflow front end without endless [Zapier](/notes/zapier) zaps or manual monitoring.

Webflow also appears in Kyle's [College Students- Focus On This](/notes/college-students-focus-on-this) long-read as a present-day example of a dominant [Platform](/notes/platform) to build a career around. Kyle's own note there reads: "True of any [Platform](/notes/platform); today it's Webflow or [Shopify ($SHOP)](/notes/shopify). Build your career where people are."

**Context:** Webflow is a visual web-design and CMS platform that lets users build responsive, production websites without hand-coding; it became a flagship tool of the no-code movement.
