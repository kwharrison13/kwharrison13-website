# Alma the Younger

A Book of Mormon prophet who recurs throughout Kyle's [Come, Follow Me](/notes/come-follow-me) gospel study as a figure of dramatic conversion and as a father teaching his sons. In the Alma 39–42 study, Kyle's central insight about Alma's counsel to his wayward son [Corianton](/notes/corianton) is a model of [Parenting](/notes/parenting): Alma spends only 14 verses on Corianton's sins but 74 verses teaching about [Jesus Christ](/notes/jesus) before relating it back to the boy — knowing your children well enough to speak to what stirs their hearts. He teaches the [Plan of Salvation](/notes/plan-of-salvation) and the Atonement directly (Alma 12).

His conversion is treated as a touchstone but also a caution. The Alma 30–31 study cites [David A. Bednar](/notes/david-a-bednar)'s point that the spiritually dramatic conversions of [Joseph Smith](/notes/joseph-smith), Paul, and Alma are "more the exception than the rule" — most revelation comes in the still small voice, not the road-to-Damascus moment. The General Conference — April 2020 notes draw on Alma's reflection that, upon being saved by Christ's [Atonement](/notes/atonement), his joy was not for himself alone but also for those he had harmed.

**Context:** Alma the Younger is a prophet and chief judge in the Book of Mormon (scripture in the Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-day Saints), known for his conversion after an angel stopped him while persecuting the church, and for the books of Alma that bear his record.
