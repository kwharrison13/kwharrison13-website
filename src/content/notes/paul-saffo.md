# Paul Saffo

A forecaster quoted in Kyle's wiki for a line on the economics of abundance: "In a world of hyper-abundant content, a point of view will become the scarcest of resources." In [When Java Meets Jive](/notes/when-java-meets-jive) he is paired with [Tiago Forte](/notes/tiago-forte) as a voice on why point-of-view, not content, is the scarce resource.

**Context:** Paul Saffo is a Silicon Valley technology forecaster and longtime Stanford lecturer, formerly director of the Institute for the Future, known for writing on long-term technological and social change.

- [When Java Meets Jive](/notes/when-java-meets-jive) — quoted on point-of-view as the scarce resource in an age of abundant content, alongside [Tiago Forte](/notes/tiago-forte).
