# Chris Paik

**One-line:** *Investor (Pace Capital) whose "right AND clever" critique explains why obvious truths get ignored.*

## What we know

- [Made the point](https://every.to/p/to-beat-the-market-you-have-to-outsmart-yourself) that many public investors avoided FAANG despite it driving the majority of market returns — because owning it felt "too obvious." "They wanted to be right AND clever."
- Kyle uses this to argue conviction won't always lead to contrarianism: sometimes it leads to a widely held belief that happens to be true, and you shouldn't reject it just because it's obvious.
