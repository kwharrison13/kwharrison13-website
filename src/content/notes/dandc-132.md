# D&C 132

In Kyle's gospel-study notes, Doctrine and Covenants section 132 is the scripture cited for **eternal marriage and the new and everlasting covenant**. The most-used verse is D&C 132:7: in 8 Points of the Gospel, "the gate to the celestial kingdom is **eternal marriage**, sealed through the Holy Spirit of Promise (D&C 132:7)," and a covenant is "made, entered into and sealed by the Holy Spirit of Promise" (Spiritual Gifts). Kyle's notes draw a practical worthiness test from it — "you can know you're living worthily through the feelings given through the Spirit of Promise… you would forget yourself and walk out of the temple with the desire of building up your spouse" (8 Points of the Gospel).

Miscellaneous Scriptures files D&C 132 under "eternal marriage (New and everlasting covenants)" and pairs it with a [Jeffrey R. Holland](/notes/jeffrey-r-holland) reassurance: "Don't worry. Your covenants are stronger than his transgressions." The Early Mormonism notes list D&C 132 among the sections (with D&C 52 and 103) that surface the harder historical questions of the early church.

**Context:** Doctrine and Covenants 132 is a revelation recorded by [Joseph Smith](/notes/joseph-smith) (dated 1843) on the "new and everlasting covenant" of marriage and eternal sealing; it is also the section that historically introduced the doctrine of plural marriage, which is why it features in discussions of early-church history.

## Where this appears
