# The Process of Faith

The Process of Faith is one of Kyle's Topical Journal studies — a recurring topical thread he cross-references rather than a single page of doctrine. In his study of 3 Answers To Every Prayer (the [Brother of Jared](/notes/brother-of-jared)'s three barge problems in Ether 2–Ether 3), Kyle connects to it at the moment prayer meets silence: "when you ask questions and there is silence, there is perhaps sin or we already have something we need to do first" — God's answer to the lighting problem was to send the Brother of Jared to "study it out" and act first. The recurring lesson is that faith is not passive waiting but disciplined work: "we don't just elope with reckless abandon. It needs to be our best work that we bring before the Lord. Something created by faith."

The arc the topic tracks runs through the [Brother of Jared](/notes/brother-of-jared) as its model case — from being chastened for not praying, to molding the sixteen stones himself, to faith becoming "perfect knowledge" so that "he could not be kept from within the veil." Faith built through struggle and action, then crystallized into knowing, is the throughline.

## Where this appears

- 3 Answers To Every Prayer — Kyle ties the topic to silence-after-prayer meaning "do something first," and to faith as one's best work brought before the Lord.
- [Brother of Jared](/notes/brother-of-jared) — the recurring case study: faith built through chastening and effort, ending in perfect knowledge that pierces the veil.
