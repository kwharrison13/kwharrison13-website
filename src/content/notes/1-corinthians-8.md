# 1 Corinthians 8

Paul's chapter on meat offered to idols, which opens with a statement about the oneness of God. In Kyle's The Godhead study it is a key proof text (8:6): "But to us there is but one God, the Father OF WHOM are all things… and one Lord Jesus Christ, BY WHOM are all things." Kyle reads this as one of the few places scripture declares one God while carrying an "obvious deeper meaning" — asking whether it is the Father or Jesus Christ who is God, and answering "Yes," in support of a distinct-but-unified Godhead.

**Context:** 1 Corinthians 8 is the New Testament chapter where the Apostle Paul addresses food sacrificed to idols, arguing that "an idol is nothing" and that for believers there is one God the Father and one Lord Jesus Christ (8:6), while urging that knowledge be tempered by charity for weaker consciences.
