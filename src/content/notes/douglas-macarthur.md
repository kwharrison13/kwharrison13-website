# Douglas MacArthur

Appears once in [Chad's Big Quote Book](/notes/chad-s-big-quote-book), credited as "General Douglas MacArthur" for the prose-poem "A Father's Prayer" ("Build me a son, O Lord, who will be strong enough to know when he is weak and brave enough to face himself when he is afraid…"). In the collection the piece sits among entries on character, fatherhood, and example — a prayer that a son learn strength, humility, compassion, and self-mastery under "the stress and spur of difficulties and challenges" rather than ease.

**Context:** Douglas MacArthur (1880–1964) was a five-star U.S. Army general who commanded Allied forces in the Pacific during World War II and led the postwar occupation of Japan. "A Father's Prayer" (also called "Build Me a Son") is widely attributed to him.
