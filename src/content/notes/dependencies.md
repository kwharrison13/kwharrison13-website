# Dependencies

**One-line definition:** *The web of external forces whose acceptance your "being right" depends on — "any truth that requires people's belief in it is, by definition, defined by its dependencies."*

## How sources describe it

- Kyle's central framework in [Conviction-Led Contrarianism](/essays/conviction-led-contrarianism): being contrarian *and right* often requires the powers that be to eventually accept that you're right. A hurricane has no dependencies on you; markets, publishers, regulators, and customers do.
- [John Maynard Keynes](/notes/john-maynard-keynes): "markets can remain irrational longer than you can remain solvent" — the quintessential dependency.
- **The three layers:**
  - **Layer 1 (phantom):** the interpersonal black hole — caring what *everyone* thinks. The only layer that isn't real; "believing that it matters is what gives it power over you." Must be conquered first.
  - **Layer 2 (legitimate, escapable):** specific naysayers (investors, publishers, academics). The answer is [Deviance](/notes/deviance) — bootstrap, self-publish, open-source.
  - **Layer 3 (unavoidable):** real institutional dependencies once you've had success — partners, customers, regulators. Answer: radical prioritization toward your "ride or die" 1% ([Palmer Luckey](/notes/palmer-luckey) / [Anduril](/notes/anduril)).
