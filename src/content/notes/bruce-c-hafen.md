# Bruce C. Hafen

Bruce C. Hafen is a Latter-day Saint author and General Authority whose writing anchors several threads in Kyle's gospel study. In [The Broken Heart](/books/the-broken-heart) he develops a rehabilitative theology of the [Atonement](/notes/atonement) — arguing it is a source of strength to deal with mortal heartbreak rather than a shield against sorrow, distinguishing the unconditional "free gifts of unmerited divine grace" from the conditional part requiring [Repentance](/notes/repentance), and warning that constant emphasis on the *availability* of forgiveness can be counterproductive. With his wife [Marie K. Hafen](/notes/marie-k-hafen) he co-authored [Faith is Not Blind](/books/faith-is-not-blind) (read 2019), whose argument is that mature faith runs *through* complexity toward "mature simplicity" rather than into disillusionment, and that faithful questioning is valuable.

A Hafen line recurs as a touchstone in Kyle's topical studies: through the Atonement we can "learn from our experience without being condemned by that experience," quoted in Knowledge — Topical Study as the resolution to the "Atonement: Revelation or Experimentation" quandary.

**Context:** Bruce C. Hafen (b. 1940) is a Latter-day Saint religious educator, former provost of Brigham Young University and dean of its law school, and an emeritus General Authority Seventy. He is known for writing on grace, the Atonement, and faith.
