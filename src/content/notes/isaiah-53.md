# Isaiah 53

Isaiah's "suffering servant" chapter, which Kyle's scripture notes read as a direct prophecy of Christ's [Atonement](/notes/atonement). In the Born Again study, he weaves [Isaiah 53](/notes/isaiah-53):3–5 ("wounded for our transgressions; bruised for our iniquities… he was despised and we esteemed him not") into a reading of the Parable of the Good Samaritan — the traveler stripped of his raiment as the soul ravished by mortality, and Christ as the Samaritan through whom "our garments, our covenants, are purified" (alongside [Luke 10](/notes/luke-10):25–37, [D&C 35](/notes/dandc-35):13, and [Alma 5](/notes/alma-5):21).

In Miscellaneous Scriptures, the chapter surfaces through its Book-of-Mormon quotation: [Mosiah 14](/notes/mosiah-14):5 — "bruised for our iniquities" — is flagged explicitly as [Isaiah 53](/notes/isaiah-53):5, part of Kyle's tracking of how the key prophets all testify of Christ. Across both notes, Isaiah 53 functions as the Old-Testament anchor for the doctrine of vicarious suffering.

## Where this appears

- Born Again — [Isaiah 53](/notes/isaiah-53):3–5 read into the Good Samaritan parable, with Christ purifying the covenant "garments."
- Miscellaneous Scriptures — [Mosiah 14](/notes/mosiah-14):5 ("bruised for our iniquities") identified as a quotation of [Isaiah 53](/notes/isaiah-53):5.
