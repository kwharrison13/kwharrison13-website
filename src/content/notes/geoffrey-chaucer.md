# Geoffrey Chaucer

The medieval English poet, cited in Kyle's gospel study of Alma 39–42 as the source of the maxim "idleness is the root of mischief." Kyle invokes it to gloss Alma's counsel to his son Corianton — who "shouldst have tended to the ministry" — under the theme that "idleness is the devil's playground" (a line he also attributes to [Meredith Willson](/notes/meredith-willson) in [The Music Man (musical)](/notes/the-music-man-musical)). The note traces the proverb specifically to Chaucer's *Tale of Melibee* (c. 1386).

**Context:** Geoffrey Chaucer (c. 1343–1400) was an English poet and author best known for *The Canterbury Tales*, a frame-narrative collection that includes the prose *Tale of Melibee*.
