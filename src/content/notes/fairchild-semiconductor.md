# Fairchild Semiconductor

Fairchild Semiconductor is a canonical example in Kyle's [Talent Vortex](/notes/talent-vortex) concept — one of the "Bell-like constellations of talent" [Jimmy Soni](/notes/jimmy-soni) enumerates in [The Founders](/books/the-founders) (alongside PayPal and General Magic) as a cohort that concentrated and then dispersed exceptional people. Its origin ties directly to [Bell Labs](/notes/bell-labs): per the [Bell Labs](/notes/bell-labs) page, [William Shockley](/notes/william-shockley)'s "uncanny ability to spot talent" was undone by his toxic management, which drove the "traitorous eight" out of Shockley Semiconductor to found Fairchild Semiconductor — the move that seeded Silicon Valley. Fairchild is thus the hinge in the corpus's talent-identification narrative: the moat was spotting the talent, but "whether you benefit from spotting talent depends on whether they go on to hate you or revere you."

**Context:** Fairchild Semiconductor was founded in 1957 in Mountain View, California by the "traitorous eight" (including Robert Noyce and Gordon Moore) after they left William Shockley's lab. Fairchild pioneered the commercial silicon integrated circuit and spawned dozens of "Fairchildren" spinoffs, including Intel, making it a foundational institution of Silicon Valley.
