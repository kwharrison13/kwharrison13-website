# Habits

Habits is the recurring theme of behaviors that compound quietly until they govern us — captured in [Charlie Munger](/notes/charlie-munger)'s gloss on Marley's ghost ("chains of habits that were too light to be felt before they became too strong to be broken") and in [Naval Ravikant](/notes/naval-ravikant)'s framing of daily reading as an exercise-like discipline.

## Where this appears

- [Charlie Munger — The Psychology of Human Misjudgment](/notes/charlie-munger-the-psychology-of-human-misjudgment) — the Marley's-ghost image of habit-chains that grow unbreakable.
- [Naval Ravikant — The Angel Philosopher](/notes/naval-ravikant-the-angel-philosopher) — reading as a daily habit, like exercise: the important thing is to do it every day.
