# Zeezrom

A Book of Mormon figure whose conversion is a centerpiece of Kyle's Alma 13–16 study. Zeezrom begins as a lawyer at Ammonihah who tries to ensnare [Alma](/notes/alma-the-younger) and [Amulek](/notes/amulek) in their words; the crowd's anger "because of the plainness of [Alma's] words unto Zeezrom" ([Alma 14](/notes/alma-14):2) becomes one of Kyle's examples of how [The Wicked Take the Truth to be Hard](/notes/the-wicked-take-the-truth-to-be-hard).

The arc Kyle dwells on is Zeezrom's collapse and healing in [Alma 15](/notes/alma-15). Stricken with "a burning fever, which was caused by the great tribulations of his mind on account of his wickedness" — supposing he had caused Alma and Amulek's deaths — Zeezrom sends for them and asks to be healed. Alma's response ties healing to belief: "Believest thou in the power of Christ unto salvation?" and "If thou believest in the redemption of Christ thou canst be healed." Zeezrom leaps to his feet, is baptized, and "began from that time forth to preach unto the people." In Kyle's reading the episode dramatizes faith unto action and the way sin "harrows up" the mind until it produces physical anguish — the inverse of [Wickedness Never Was Happiness](/notes/wickedness-never-was-happiness).
