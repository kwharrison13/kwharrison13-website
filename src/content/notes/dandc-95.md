# D&C 95

A section of the Doctrine and Covenants that recurs in Kyle's gospel-study notes as a key text on being **"chosen"** and on magnifying one's calling. In Miscellaneous Scriptures it anchors a cross-reference chain on the priesthood: [D&C 121](/notes/dandc-121):34 ("many are called, but few are chosen") leads into D&C 95:5 ("many are ordained, and there are none of them that are chosen") and D&C 95:6 (those not chosen "walk in darkness at noon-day"), which loops back to D&C 121:35–37 — the warning that "any degree of unrighteousness" forfeits the priesthood and means a man "is not chosen." The thread reads the two sections together as a single doctrine: ordination is not the same as being chosen, and being chosen is conditioned on righteousness.

In the Abrahamic Covenant notes, D&C 95:5–6 is paired again with D&C 121:33–40 under the heading of **magnifying our callings** — the idea that priesthood power is unlocked by making and keeping covenants rather than by office alone.

**Context:** Doctrine and Covenants Section 95 is an 1833 revelation to [Joseph Smith](/notes/joseph-smith) chastening the early Saints for delaying construction of the Kirtland Temple; its language about the called, the ordained, and the chosen is widely cross-referenced with D&C 121 in Latter-day Saint priesthood study.

## Where this appears

- Miscellaneous Scriptures — central to the "chosen" / magnifying-the-priesthood cross-reference chain, read alongside [D&C 121](/notes/dandc-121) and D&C 84.
- Abrahamic Covenant — cited (D&C 95:5–6 with D&C 121:33–40) under magnifying one's calling and unlocking priesthood power through covenants.
