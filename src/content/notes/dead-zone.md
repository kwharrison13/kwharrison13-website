# Dead Zone

[Everett Randle](/notes/everett-randle)'s metaphor for where undifferentiated venture firms get stuck — the middle ground occupied by the "long tail of so-so venture firms" that can't answer a founder's question of "what is the job-to-be-done that I'm hiring this firm for?" In Kyle's [Differentiation in Venture](/notes/differentiation-in-venture) thesis, the Dead Zone is the trap that swallows firms lacking a distinct character or a real product, as the market culls (per [Josh Wolfe](/notes/josh-wolfe)'s prediction) 50–75% of active private-market investors. Firms with a genuine differentiator — Insight's roll-up product, Ribbit's fintech product, Paradigm's crypto product — escape the Dead Zone; firms without one do not.
