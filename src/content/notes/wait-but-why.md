# Wait But Why

[Tim Urban](/notes/tim-urban)'s blog, and the specific source of a passage that recurs across Kyle's corpus on compounding ideas. Urban's 2017 Wait But Why essay on [Neuralink](/notes/neuralink) is where the cited line "Language made ideas composable... Knowledge, when shared, becomes like a grand, collective, inter-generational collaboration" originates — quoted by [Packy McCormick](/notes/packy-mccormick) as the bridge into Kyle's [Open-Source Knowledge](/essays/open-source-knowledge) and [Idea Sex](/notes/idea-sex) arguments. Kyle's January 11th, 2022 daily log captures the related Urban passage on the "Human Colossus": macroeconomic forces drive it to create value, so it "tends to want to invent newer and better technology... which means it can invent new stuff even faster."

So in this wiki, Wait But Why functions less as a publication-to-track and more as a single high-leverage citation — the long-form Neuralink piece that gave Kyle a quotable formulation of why shared knowledge compounds.

**Context:** Wait But Why is a long-form blog founded by Tim Urban (with Andrew Finn) in 2013, known for deeply researched, stick-figure-illustrated explainers on topics like artificial intelligence, the Fermi paradox, procrastination, and Elon Musk's companies (Tesla, SpaceX, Neuralink). Urban's 2016 TED talk "Inside the Mind of a Master Procrastinator" drew from the blog.
