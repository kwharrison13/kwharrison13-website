# Christian Brose

Author of [The Kill Chain](/books/the-kill-chain), the present-tense companion to [Skunk Works](/books/skunk-works) that diagnoses how the US lost its military speed advantage and what regaining it would require. In Kyle's notes Brose argues the "exquisite-platform" paradigm (carriers, bombers, the F-35) is the wrong shape for the next conflict — that the right answer is many cheaper, more autonomous networked nodes, which Kyle reads as [Anduril](/notes/anduril)'s whole thesis treated as the obvious endpoint. Brose connects directly back to [Skunk Works](/books/skunk-works) (the end of the founder-led innovation era with McNamara cost-cutting and the "Last Supper" consolidation), frames Anduril as a modern [PayPal Mafia](/notes/paypal-mafia), and invokes Sun Tzu's "win without fighting" ([The Art of War](/books/the-art-of-war)) as China's strategic goal. The book's implicit thesis: this is an incentive-structure problem, not a budget problem.

**Context:** Christian Brose served as staff director of the Senate Armed Services Committee under John McCain and later became head of strategy at Anduril Industries; *The Kill Chain: Defending America in the Future of High-Tech Warfare* was published in 2020.
