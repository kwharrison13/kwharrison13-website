# 1 Nephi 10

A Book of Mormon chapter Kyle returns to chiefly for two passages. Verses 17–19 are the load-bearing reference across his Diligence study — Nephi's desire to "see, and hear, and know of these things, by the power of the Holy Ghost, which is the gift of God unto all those who diligently seek him" (v. 17), and the promise that "he that diligently seeketh shall find; and the mysteries of God shall be unfolded unto them… for the course of the Lord is one eternal round" (v. 19). These verses anchor the framing of diligence as the all-encompassing hunger of those who "diligently seek" God, and they recur in Spiritual Gifts ("seek diligently and find") and the "We Are Responsible for Our Own Learning" week. (Diligence, Spiritual Gifts)

The chapter's second thread is verse 21 — "no unclean thing can enter into the presence of God" — which Kyle cites in Obedience — Topical Study (raising the question of whether faith, baptism, or circumcision of heart does the cleansing) and in Plan of Salvation — Topical Study (the judgment of God leading to freedom or captivity, since no unclean thing can dwell with God). It also appears in the Ether 12–15 ("By Faith All Things Are Fulfilled") week's cross-references on faith and finding. (Obedience — Topical Study, Plan of Salvation — Topical Study)

**Context:** 1 Nephi 10 is a chapter in the First Book of Nephi (Book of Mormon) in which Nephi recounts his father Lehi's prophecies of the Messiah and expresses his own desire to know spiritual things by the power of the Holy Ghost.
