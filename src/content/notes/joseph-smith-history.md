# Joseph Smith History

Joseph Smith History (JSH) is the canonical first-person account of [Joseph Smith](/notes/joseph-smith)'s early visions and the coming forth of the [Book of Mormon](/notes/book-of-mormon), published in the [Pearl of Great Price](/notes/pearl-of-great-price). Across Kyle's topical studies it serves as the primary source text. The First Vision — Topical Study reads JSH against its setting in the religious fervor of Palmyra, weighs its dating ("early spring of 1820") against the four firsthand accounts, and cites JSH 1:8 — Joseph's stated preference for the Methodist (Arminian) sect and his comfort with its emphasis on agency. The Joseph Smith — A Young Seer study draws on JSH 1:27–29 for the difficulties of Joseph's youth, his temptations, and the questionable friends he found himself among.

The Articles of Faith — Topical Study uses Joseph Smith History as the source for the thirteen [Articles of Faith](/notes/articles-of-faith) themselves — drawn from the [Wentworth Letter](/notes/wentworth-letter) and treated as a compact outline of the entire restored gospel, which Kyle re-reads as five movements (the plan of salvation, church structure, the resources, the performance, and the goal).

**Context:** "Joseph Smith—History" is an excerpt from Joseph Smith's 1838–39 history, canonized in the Pearl of Great Price (a standard work of The Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-day Saints). It is the most widely cited of Smith's several First Vision accounts and the official scriptural narrative of the Restoration's founding events.
