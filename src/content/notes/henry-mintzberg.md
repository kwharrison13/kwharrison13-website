# Henry Mintzberg

Henry Mintzberg is the author of [Managers Not MBAs](/books/managers-not-mbas), the structural critique of business education that anchors Kyle's [MBA Degrees](/notes/mba-degrees) research. His central argument, as Kyle records it: [Management](/notes/management) is a *practice* — a blend of craft, art, and science learned in context — not a science or profession that can be conferred by a degree taught out of context. He holds that MBA programs choose the wrong people (self-selected to "want to get ahead" and to compete rather than cooperate), educate them the wrong way ("chalk and talk" case-method instruction divorced from doing), and produce the wrong results. The book argues management is actually learned bottom-up through [Apprenticeship](/notes/apprenticeship) — "moving, mentoring, and monitoring" on the job (the Japanese OJT maxim, the Red Cross "counterpart" model) — and that rankings and numerical scores "get rid of judgment," substituting measurement for the [Judgment](/notes/judgment) management requires.

Mintzberg also features in Kyle's exploration-before-exploitation thread: "without exploration, there is nothing to exploit," and "the explorers can easily find exploiters, but the exploiters cannot simply hire explorers" — observations that connect [Managers Not MBAs](/books/managers-not-mbas) to [The Power Law](/books/the-power-law-venture-capital-and-the-making-of-the-new-future) (VC structure) and [Working in Public](/books/working-in-public) (creation vs. maintenance). He blames [Milton Friedman](/notes/milton-friedman)'s "economic immorality" position for hardening MBA students toward shareholder value, and cites [Malcolm Gladwell](/notes/malcolm-gladwell)'s "The Talent Myth" on Enron's MBA-stuffed "star system."

**Context:** Henry Mintzberg is a Canadian management scholar, the Cleghorn Professor of Management Studies at McGill University, known for his work on the nature of managerial work, organizational structure, strategy formation, and his sustained critique of conventional MBA education.
