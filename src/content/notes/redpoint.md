# Redpoint

In Kyle's notes "Redpoint" appears in two distinct senses. The dominant one is **Redpoint Ventures**, the venture-capital firm: in the Pave Diligence — 2021 dossier, [a16z](/notes/a16z) and Redpoint are both cited as having "50%+ penetration of Pave among their companies" (with a dedicated benchmarking tab across their portfolios) — i.e. Redpoint as an early customer/champion of [Pave fka Trove](/notes/pave). In [The Productization of Venture Capital](/essays/productization) research, Logan Bartlett at Redpoint is logged as offering talent and customer intros — a data point in Kyle's survey of how firms productize platform value. And in January 24th, 2024 — Outreach for SF Trip, Jordan Segall and Erica Brescia at Redpoint are on Kyle's SF-trip outreach list.

A second, unrelated entity — **Redpoint Global**, the customer-data platform (CDP) — surfaces in mParticle — Diligence (2018), where Redpoint is mapped as a competitor in the CDP category alongside Lytics, SessionM, [Segment](/notes/segment), and mParticle. Per AgilOne's CEO it was "the biggest competitor with the best product," though Segment claimed never to see the classic CDP players in deals (they read as CMO-direct marketing tools). These two Redpoints are different companies that happen to share a name.

**Context:** Redpoint Ventures is a Menlo Park-based venture firm (founded 1999) that invests across early and growth stages in enterprise and consumer software. Redpoint Global is a separate Massachusetts-based customer-data-platform company; the two are unaffiliated.
