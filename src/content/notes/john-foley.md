Founder and former CEO of [Peloton](/notes/peloton), which he started in 2012 (raising $307K on Kickstarter) and grew to a ~$50B market cap by January 2021 before its dramatic fall. Stepped down as CEO in February 2022. His personal fortune fell from ~$1.9B to ~$225M with Peloton's collapse.

His post-Peloton "second act" is [Ernesta](/notes/ernesta), a direct-to-consumer custom rug startup — pitched as a more disciplined, profitability-focused, PE-style business ("I am hungry, I am humbled... I want to show discipline... and have a real focus on unit economics"). Foley is the central case study in [Stewards of Capital](/essays/stewards-of-capital), Kyle's essay on capital stewardship and whether founders who destroy large amounts of capital deserve easy second acts.
