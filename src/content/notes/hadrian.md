# Hadrian

**One-line:** *Automated-manufacturing company founded by [Chris Power](/notes/chris-power) that builds software and machine automation to replace the aging, family-run machine shops supplying aerospace and defense primes — named for the Roman emperor as a nod to civilizational complacency ("someone needs to pave the roads").*

## What we know

- Builds a software platform ("Opus") with six components: AI-driven extraction/standardization of manufacturing-spec PDFs; automated production-inspection programming per material/alloy; software that effectively "drives" CNC and other machines (the operator pilots the machine rather than just loading/unloading it); coordination/scheduling/inventory/compliance software; and workforce development that trains people with no manufacturing background to be productive in about 30 days.
- Operates three US facilities as of mid-2026: Factory 2 (CNC, at max capacity, scaled in 18 months), Factory X (R&D — welding, additive, sheet metal, foundry/large-format castings), and a new Arizona facility (four times the size of Factory 2, ramped in six months after signing the lease). A further facility (~2 million square feet, described as the biggest US factory build-out outside Tesla's Gigafactory) was announced for later in the year.
- Estimated to be on a billion-dollar-plus revenue run rate heading into the following year, per [Chris Power](/notes/chris-power) in the interview.
- Competes/partners in the broader automated-manufacturing space alongside companies like Atomic Industries and Machina Labs, integrating their capabilities into larger government programs rather than treating them purely as competitors.
- SpaceX and Tesla are cited by Power as the only other companies that "know how to do production hell."
