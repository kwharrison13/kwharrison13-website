# Covenants vs. Commandments

This concept gathers Kyle's question, raised while reading [Joseph Smith — Rough Stone Rolling](/books/joseph-smith-rough-stone-rolling), about how covenants relate to commandments in Latter-day Saint theology. It arises in the book's treatment of plural marriage as "covenant-bound rule under revelation." Bushman writes that to Joseph's mind "revelation functioned like law" — the early revelations were literally called "commandments" and "required obedience," while the marriage revelation bound "partners to each other by covenant," making adultery a breach of the [New and Everlasting Covenant](/notes/new-and-everlasting-covenant) rather than merely of a moral code. The same strict standard held under plural marriage as under monogamy, "except that revelation set the standard."

Kyle's annotation pushes on the distinction in practical terms: *"Are non-temple marriages considered lesser than temple marriages? The [New and Everlasting Covenant](/notes/new-and-everlasting-covenant) includes ordinances sealed by the Priesthood — so is a civil marriage still considered adultery?"* The book frames the marriage covenant as preparing the Saints "less for wedded bliss than for heavenly rule" — covenant as the binding instrument of God's government, distinct from a commandment as a directive to obey.
