# Shashank Samala

Co-founder of [Heirloom](/notes/heirloom), the direct-air-capture carbon-removal startup, who surfaces in Kyle's Watershed Climate Diligence — 2021 dossier. Samala (alongside Heirloom's [Max Scholten](/notes/max-scholten)) is listed as a participant in Watershed's 2021 Climate Week event, where the founders of Heirloom and [Running Tide](/notes/running-tide) — "two cutting-edge carbon removal startups" — spoke with the climate leads at [Stripe](/notes/stripe) and [Shopify ($SHOP)](/notes/shopify), buyers who had "collectively kickstarted the market with $10m+ of purchasing commitments." The diligence notes frame these as "tech companies with customers, investors, deliverables—and competitors" rather than speculative non-profits, with energy efficiency (power burned per tonne removed) as the key metric and the expectation that removal costs could fall below $100/tonne within the decade.

**Context:** Shashank Samala is the co-founder and CEO of Heirloom Carbon Technologies, a US company using accelerated limestone mineralization for low-energy atmospheric carbon removal. He previously co-founded the hardware-prototyping company Tempo Automation.
