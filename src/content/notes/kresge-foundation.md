# Kresge Foundation

Surfaced through a July 2018 call with [Aaron Seybert](/notes/aaron-seybert), who joined Kresge to build its **central social-investment practice**. Per his account, Kresge tries to put its endowment to work while keeping the core of the business in **charitable investments that can still generate a return on capital**, aligned tightly with the foundation's program areas (Aaron covered the Detroit program and the American Cities practice). They do **direct investing across equity and debt** on behalf of those programs and carry balance-sheet capacity to de-risk transactions — aiming to get roughly **20-to-1 leverage** on their dollars by working through intermediaries, fund managers, and on-the-ground partners. About **80% of the work is debt-like structures** (subordinate/equity-like investments via an intermediary) and **20% is equity and guarantee work** (higher risk, higher touch).

A theme of the call was the deliberate **program-officer/finance tension**: every deal needs a program officer attesting "this fits the strategy" alongside the finance side attesting "this is structured correctly." Aaron also described a **"valley of death"** between what Kresge would deploy capital into and what its endowment would invest in — many self-described impact funds end up "too watered down" for the charitable side yet "not competitive enough" for the endowment; they screen 100+ impact funds and maybe one passes.

**Context:** The Kresge Foundation is a Troy, Michigan-based private philanthropy (endowed by hardware retailer S.S. Kresge, founder of what became Kmart) known for its social-investment and community-development work, including significant investment in Detroit.
