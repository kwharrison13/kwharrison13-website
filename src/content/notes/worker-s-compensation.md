# Worker's Compensation

Appears in Munger's worldly-wisdom lecture as his illustration of incentive-driven system design: he argues that, if he were running civilization, compensation for stress in workers' comp would be zero — not because work-caused stress isn't real, but because the net social damage of allowing it to be compensated at all outweighs the harm of leaving a few genuine cases uncompensated. A concrete case of the [Let Life Be Hard](/notes/let-life-be-hard) principle.

**Context:** Workers' compensation is a form of insurance that pays wage replacement and medical benefits to employees injured in the course of employment, in exchange for relinquishing the right to sue the employer.
