# Quake Concepts

A hub for the works that have fundamentally shifted how Kyle thinks — the "quake" inputs. This page primarily references pages stored elsewhere: the **quake books** (also surfaced on the public bookshelf) and the **quake talks** that have had a comparable impact.

## Quake Books

The eight books flagged as fundamentally shifting Kyle's thinking. Mirrors the quake set on the site bookshelf.

- [Leap of Faith](/books/leap-of-faith) by Bob Bennett (2018, re-read 2024)
- [John Quincy Adams](/books/john-quincy-adams) by [James Traub](/notes/james-traub) (2019)
- [Red Rising](/books/red-rising) by Pierce Brown (2019, re-read 2025)
- [Poor Charlie's Almanack](/books/poor-charlie-s-almanack) by [Charles T. Munger](/notes/charlie-munger) (2020)
- [Reinventing Knowledge: From Alexandria To The Internet](/books/reinventing-knowledge-from-alexandria-to-the-internet) by Ian F. McNeely & Lisa Wolverton (2021)
- [The Man Who Broke Capitalism](/books/the-man-who-broke-capitalism) by [David Gelles](/notes/david-gelles) (2025)
- [Crime & Punishment](/books/crime-and-punishment) by [Fyodor Dostoevsky](/notes/fyodor-dostoevsky) (2025)
- [The Courage To Be Disliked](/books/the-courage-to-be-disliked) by [Ichiro Kishimi](/notes/ichiro-kishimi) & [Fumitake Koga](/notes/fumitake-koga) (2025)
