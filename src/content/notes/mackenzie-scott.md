# MacKenzie Scott

In [Amazon Unbound](/books/amazon-unbound) and on the [Jeff Bezos](/notes/jeff-bezos) page, MacKenzie Scott (then MacKenzie Bezos) appears as an early co-founder of [Amazon](/notes/amazon): in 1994, when Bezos quit D. E. Shaw to start an online bookstore, the twenty-four-year-old MacKenzie took the wheel of the family's '88 Chevy Blazer and drove northwest from Fort Worth while Bezos sat in the passenger seat tapping financial projections into a spreadsheet. The book frames her as the partner present at the company's literal launch — "early co-founder driving the '88 Blazer west" — who later became "a $6B philanthropist."

**Context:** MacKenzie Scott is an American novelist and philanthropist who was married to Jeff Bezos during Amazon's founding and early decades. After their 2019 divorce she became one of the world's largest individual donors, giving away billions through rapid, unrestricted grants to nonprofits.

## Where this appears

- [Amazon Unbound](/books/amazon-unbound) — named among the book's cast as Amazon's early co-founder and, later, a major philanthropist
- [Jeff Bezos](/notes/jeff-bezos) — credited with driving the cross-country relocation when Amazon was founded
