# David McCullough

Pulitzer-winning historian whose books shaped [Larry H. Miller](/notes/larry-h-miller) in [Driven](/books/driven). After reading McCullough's biography of [John Adams](/books/john-adams), Miller declared Adams a kindred spirit — "His story is my story" — and marked passages in his copy, including Adams's late-life line ("work hard, conscience is neat and easy… hoping to do a 'little good'") and McCullough's own observation that "through all his life Adams would be happiest when there was clear purpose to his days," which Miller read as a description of himself. Miller credits *1776* and *John Adams* (both McCullough) with making "John Adams and history come alive," and the book recounts McCullough trading a signed copy of *1776* with [Gordon B. Hinckley](/notes/gordon-b-hinckley) — McCullough lamenting that history was no longer required for graduation at many universities.

**Context:** David McCullough (1933–2022) was an American historian and two-time Pulitzer Prize winner (*Truman*, *John Adams*), also author of *1776*, *The Wright Brothers*, and *The Path Between the Seas*, and a long-time narrator known for accessible, narrative-driven popular history.

- [Driven](/books/driven) — the biography of [Larry H. Miller](/notes/larry-h-miller) cites McCullough's *John Adams* and *1776* repeatedly as touchstones that made Miller call Adams a hero ("His story is my story").
