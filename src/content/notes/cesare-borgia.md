# Cesare Borgia

Cesare Borgia appears in Kyle's notes only as one of the people in [Walter Isaacson](/notes/walter-isaacson)'s biography of [Leonardo Da Vinci](/books/leonardo-da-vinci), where he is listed among the figures who cross Leonardo's life. The biography places Leonardo alongside a cast that includes [Luca Pacioli](/notes/luca-pacioli), [Albert Einstein](/notes/albert-einstein), [Steve Jobs](/notes/steve-jobs), [Charles Darwin](/notes/charles-darwin), [Alexander the Great](/notes/alexander-the-great), and [Aristotle](/notes/aristotle) — Cesare Borgia among them as a patron-era contemporary. Kyle has not recorded substantive notes on Borgia beyond this association.

**Context:** Cesare Borgia (c. 1475–1507) was an Italian Renaissance nobleman, condottiero, and cardinal, the illegitimate son of Pope Alexander VI. In 1502 he employed Leonardo da Vinci as a military architect and engineer. His ruthless statecraft was a central model for Machiavelli's *The Prince*.
