# Apprenticeship

In Kyle's corpus, apprenticeship names the idea that real competence — especially in [Management](/notes/management) — is learned in context, on the job, rather than conferred by a degree or taught out of context. The thesis is most explicit in [Managers Not MBAs](/books/managers-not-mbas) ([Henry Mintzberg](/notes/henry-mintzberg)): "If management is a practice, then some sort of apprenticeship has to figure prominently in how it is learned" — formalized as "**moving, mentoring, and monitoring**," with development coming "from the inside" through rotation across stretching jobs (a start-up, a turnaround, a strategic assignment). This sits beside the [Mentorship](/notes/mentorship) and [Operational Skills](/notes/operational-skills) threads: managers should earn their stripes "from the bottom up" rather than stepping in at the top, the same on-the-job logic Kyle tracks in [Reinventing Knowledge — From Alexandria To The Internet](/books/reinventing-knowledge-from-alexandria-to-the-internet) under "learning by teaching" and "apprenticeship and active engagement against the death of expertise."

The concept also recurs as a historical and biographical pattern. In [Benjamin Franklin — An American Life](/books/benjamin-franklin-an-american-life), Franklin — himself helped up "as a struggling artisan" — left a trust funding loans to "young married artificers" who had served apprenticeships, so they could establish their own businesses. The [Leonardo Da Vinci](/books/leonardo-da-vinci) notes list apprenticeship among the habits (alongside curiosity, observation, and learning in public) behind his self-taught, workshop-trained genius. Across these sources the through-line is the same: craft and judgment are transmitted by doing under guidance, not by classroom abstraction.

**Context:** Apprenticeship is the traditional model of skill transmission — a novice learns a trade by working under a master over time — historically central to the guild system and now echoed in modern forms like mentorship, on-the-job training, and incubators.
