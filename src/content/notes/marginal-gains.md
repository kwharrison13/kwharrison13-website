# Marginal Gains

The load-bearing opening example of [Atomic Habits](/books/atomic-habits): Dave Brailsford's "aggregation of marginal gains," the philosophy of "searching for a tiny margin of improvement in everything you do." Brailsford, hired to put British Cycling on a new trajectory, described it this way: "if you broke down everything you could think of that goes into riding a bike, and then improve it by 1 percent, you will get a significant increase when you put them all together." Kyle's margin note ties it to scripture — *"By small and simple things…"*

The principle sets up the book's central counter to the bias that "massive success requires massive action." Instead, James Clear argues, outcomes compound from accumulated 1% improvements — the habits version of [Compound Knowledge](/notes/compound-knowledge), where small gains accrete over time into outsized results.

**Context:** Sir Dave Brailsford was performance director of British Cycling (and later Team Sky); his "aggregation of marginal gains" approach is credited with the program's dominance at the 2008 and 2012 Olympics and in the Tour de France.
