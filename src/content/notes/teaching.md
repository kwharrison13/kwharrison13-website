# Teaching

Teaching appears in Kyle's notes from two angles: the gospel-study sources treat it as a spiritual act, and the long-reads treat it as a cognitive technology. On the gospel side, Find To Teach frames teaching as the purpose finding serves and insists it "is meant to be done by the Spirit" ([D&C 42](/notes/dandc-42):14), distinguishing an interior, Spirit-driven **Process** from the outward **Pattern** of a teaching visit (rapport → teach → testify → invite → promise → follow up). Therefore, What — Jeffrey R. Holland sharpens the standard: [Jeffrey R. Holland](/notes/jeffrey-r-holland) brackets the Savior's ministry with "teaching… preaching… and healing," calling for "a teacher who in the process heals," and borrows [Boyd K. Packer](/notes/boyd-k-packer)'s assessment question — "Therefore, what?" — to insist that teaching must produce changed lives, "not just delivered lessons" ("these are God's students, not yours").

The secular sources converge on teaching as a forcing function for the teacher's own understanding. [Forget More To Learn More](/notes/forget-more-to-learn-more) argues "compression is why teaching forces learning" — to communicate anything you must compress it, and distilling what you know forces you to grapple with the ideas. [Charlie Munger — Practical Thought About Practical Thought](/notes/charlie-munger-practical-thought-about-practical-thought) supplies the cautionary case: Munger's candid post-mortem that his talk "failed to get through to almost all people," a "folly as an amateur teacher" because he "attempted too much" — quoting the proverb "Teachers open the door, but you must enter by yourself." [The Science of Learning](/notes/the-science-of-learning) reads teaching as a craft with measurable methods, citing [Samuel Reed Hall](/notes/samuel-reed-hall)'s impediments to teaching and the research on what lets a teacher "boost student learning."
