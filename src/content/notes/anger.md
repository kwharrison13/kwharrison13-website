# Anger

A theme Kyle works on across two registers — therapeutic and spiritual. In therapy with [Kristina Kardanow](/notes/kristina-kardanow) ([Personal Therapist](/notes/personal-therapist)), the framing is that "anger is a secondary emotion. There is always something underneath it, usually a very vulnerable emotion." The November 2021 session covered *displacement* — we offload anger onto the people closest to us because they are the safest to be angry with ("kick the dog") — with the image that anger is "like a trash bag; if you never empty it then it's just going to overflow onto someone or something that doesn't deserve it." The healthy outlet discussed was expression through "I" statements.

The same theme recurs in Kyle's [Come, Follow Me](/notes/come-follow-me) study. In the Alma 30–31 study, anger appears among the four things Kyle felt were holding him back — [Anger](/notes/anger), [Pride](/notes/pride), [Envy](/notes/envy), and [lust](/notes/lust) — paired with the two-wolves parable ("the one you feed"). The Alma 23–29 study draws a sharp observation from Alma 27, where the Amalekites, unable to seek revenge, "stir up the people in anger": "When your anger is decoupled from what you're actually upset about, you know that you're looking for an excuse to be angry."
