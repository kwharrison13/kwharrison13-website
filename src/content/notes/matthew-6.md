# Matthew 6

Matthew 6 — part of the Sermon on the Mount — appears in two of Kyle's gospel-study notes. In Fasting - September 5, 2021, **Matthew 6:1–4, 16–18** is studied as Christ's teaching on private, sincere fasting and almsgiving done before God rather than for the praise of others. In the Sermon on the Mount note, **Matthew 6:19–21** ("lay up for yourselves treasures in heaven... for where your treasure is, there will your heart be also") is flagged as the "eternal perspective passage" — weighing the worth of our desires against the eternal view.

**Context:** Matthew 6 sits at the heart of the Sermon on the Mount, covering teachings on almsgiving, the Lord's Prayer, fasting, storing up treasures in heaven, and not being anxious for the things of the world.

## Where this appears

- Fasting - September 5, 2021 — Matthew 6:1–4, 16–18 on private, God-directed fasting and almsgiving
- Sermon on the Mount — Matthew 6:19–21 as the "eternal perspective" passage
