# Missionary Work

For Kyle, missionary work is the throughline tying together his own mission reflections, gospel-study, and the sociological literature on the LDS Church. The recurring frame — drawn from [James E. Faust](/notes/james-e-faust)'s "What I Want My Son to Know before He Leaves on His Mission" and the Missionary Work — Topical Study — is that it is "the Lord's work done in the Lord's way": hard work, lost-self service, love for the people, and personal testimony matter more than intellect or results. [Ezra Taft Benson](/notes/ezra-taft-benson)'s "if a missionary works, he will get the Spirit… that's the secret — work, work, work" sits alongside [Jeffrey R. Holland](/notes/jeffrey-r-holland)'s practical "ABC's of Missionary Work" (live the gospel, ask and answer questions, talk about the benefits, issue a call to action), and the conviction that boldness is "a love-filled risk taken out of eternal concern for someone else."

The reading also carries a hard, data-driven critique. [Law of the Harvest](/books/law-of-the-harvest) (David G. Stewart, recommended by [Erik Hansen](/notes/erik-hansen)) argues the Church baptizes far more than it retains, and that the gap is a methodology problem, not an inevitability of growth — with a [Boyd K. Packer](/notes/boyd-k-packer)-flavored warning that over-programming crowds out the core mission. [The Next Mormons](/books/the-next-mormons) supplies survey data: full-term missionaries are retained far more than non-servers or early returnees, and a "missionary effect" gives returned missionaries (especially those who learned a foreign language) markedly more positive views of immigrants. [Gordon B. Hinckley — Go Forward With Faith](/books/gordon-b-hinckley-go-forward-with-faith) frames the institutional side — the "raise the bar" and [Preach My Gospel](/notes/preach-my-gospel) curriculum era, missionary work as the Church serving as "an anchor in a world of shifting values."

**Context:** Missionary work is the LDS Church's proselytizing program — typically 18–24 month full-time missions — central to its growth and to the personal formation of those who serve. The [Preach My Gospel](/notes/preach-my-gospel) manual (introduced 2004) is its standard teaching guide.
