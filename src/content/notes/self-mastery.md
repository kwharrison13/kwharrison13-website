# Self-Mastery

A theme that runs from secular biography into Kyle's gospel study, where **self-mastery is the slow, incremental discipline of governing the "natural man" through small repeated choices** rather than one heroic effort. The richest treatment is the gospel-study page Self-Mastery — Topical Study, which frames mortality not as a test meant to perfect us but to "develop the ability to *try*" — we become like God "grace for grace," winning self-mastery "by suppression of injurious passions" and "a few good decisions at a time," the "very small helm" that turns a great ship in a storm. The companion Patience — Topical Study and Godly Characteristics of the Master treat patience as another form of self-control: composure under stress, postponing gratification, bearing with the faults of loved ones without fault-finding.

The Lehonti Principle is the inverse warning — Satan's incremental seduction of Lehonti (bringing the line ever closer, then poisoning "by degrees") models exactly the gradual erosion that self-mastery must resist, with resolution and desire as the "first steps to success in overcoming the temptations of Satan." [John Quincy Adams](/books/john-quincy-adams) supplies the secular anchor: Adams's mother's "perpetual evangelizing on the virtues of self-mastery had made the young man proof against distraction," the habit of disciplined work that let him buckle down.
