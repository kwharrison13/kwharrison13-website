# Estonia

Estonia appears in [The Future of Work (Article)](/notes/the-future-of-work-article) as the positive half of [Alec Ross](/notes/alec-ross)'s West Virginia vs. Estonia parable (from [Industries of the Future](/notes/industries-of-the-future)): the country that embraced a digital, future-facing economy, contrasted against a region that clung to declining coal and chemical industries.

**Context:** Estonia is a Baltic nation widely cited as a model of digital governance — e-residency, near-universal digital public services, and a startup-dense economy that emerged after independence from the Soviet Union in 1991.

## Where this appears

- [The Future of Work (Article)](/notes/the-future-of-work-article) — the future-embracing counterpoint to West Virginia in [Alec Ross](/notes/alec-ross)'s parable on automation and offshoring.
