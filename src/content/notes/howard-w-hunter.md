# Howard W. Hunter

An apostle and later president of the LDS Church whose appearances across Kyle's wiki cluster around two themes: missionary/teaching conviction and institutional honesty about church history. In [Chad's Big Quote Book](/notes/chad-s-big-quote-book) he is quoted on the link between the atonement and missionary work — "A great indicator of one's conversion is the desire to share the gospel with others" — and in Scripture Study — Topical Study on teaching with conviction: "That is how we build faith… with the power of the word of God taught in purity and with conviction."

His most consequential role in Kyle's notes comes from [Leonard Arrington and the Writing of Mormon History](/books/leonard-arrington-and-the-writing-of-mormon-history), where Hunter — as Church Historian — "opened the archives completely… in a stroke," ending a who-you-know access regime that had never been fully open. The biography casts him as the institutional ally of honest history: he held that "the Church was mature enough that our history should be honest," opposed suppressing or concealing documents, and privately told [Leonard Arrington](/notes/leonard-arrington) he was "in complete agreement" with a balanced historiography even while it put him "at odds with certain of my brethren" — positioning Hunter against the more guarded [Ezra Taft Benson](/notes/ezra-taft-benson) / [Boyd K. Packer](/notes/boyd-k-packer) camp in the Quorum of the Twelve.

**Context:** Howard W. Hunter (1907–1995) was the 14th President of The Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-day Saints, serving briefly in 1994–1995 after decades as an apostle. He had earlier been Church Historian.
