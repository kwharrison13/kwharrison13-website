# SmugMug

SmugMug is Kyle's central worked example of building an enduring, owner-controlled company instead of optimizing for an exit. In [On the Nature of Long-Term Holds](/notes/on-the-nature-of-long-term-holds), founder [Don MacAskill](/notes/don-macaskill) is quoted at length: "When an exit is always in your mind, it is harder to be operational and customer-centric — it becomes all about financial performance and not even operating financial performance, but investment performance." Founded in 2002 and bootstrapped without outside capital, SmugMug ($66M revenue, 210 employees, a 2020 *Forbes* Small Giant) built its model on customers paying for services — "an anomalistic strategy in Silicon Valley" — so MacAskill could keep control and a long-term timeline. His view was shaped by watching his father Chris (founder of Fatbrain.com) endure the pressures of going public: "the Wizard of Oz — everything is different behind the curtain." On the [Alternative Company Buying](/notes/alternative-company-buying) concept page, SmugMug is the named template for that whole philosophy: the enduring, owner-controlled, customer-centric company that deliberately avoids outside capital.

**Context:** SmugMug is a photo- and video-hosting and sharing platform founded by Don and Chris MacAskill in 2002; it remained privately held and bootstrapped, and acquired Flickr from Verizon/Oath in 2018.
