# Boston

Boston enters Kyle's wiki through the City of Boston, via his 2018 [networking call](/notes/networking-calls) with [Jascha Franklin-Hodge](/notes/jascha-franklin-hodge), who served as the city's CIO from summer 2014 to January 2018. The case study is a govtech / [City Building](/notes/city-building) one: building in-house digital-services and data teams inside city government rather than outsourcing them. Under Franklin-Hodge, Boston invested in constituent-facing digital touchpoints (the city website, parking-meter and property-tax payments), stood up an in-house product studio, and built a data-and-analytics team by gathering scattered "smart people with scripts on their desktops" under one roof — using it for predictive modeling on health-code risk, road-safety (Vision Zero), and fire-department hazard mapping. The note connects Boston's experience to Kyle's [The New City](/notes/the-new-city) interest.

**Context:** Boston is the capital and largest city of Massachusetts, a hub for higher education, healthcare, and technology.
