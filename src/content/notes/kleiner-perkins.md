# Kleiner Perkins

Kleiner Perkins appears in Kyle's corpus on two registers: as a foundational firm in the history of venture capital, and as an active co-investor in deals Kyle diligenced. In [VC — An American History](/books/vc), the firm is profiled as a node in "the family tree of venture funds," with co-founder [Tom Perkins](/notes/tom-perkins) (Kleiner Perkins, 1972) named — alongside Arthur Rock and Don Valentine ([Sequoia](/notes/sequoia)) — as one of three archetypal figures whose styles defined the industry; Perkins represents the VC who "emphasized investments in technology." The [Tom Perkins](/notes/tom-perkins) page captures the firm's self-conception as an "entrepreneurs ourselves" partnership ("we are not investors… we are entrepreneurs ourselves").

On the deal side, Kleiner Perkins shows up as a Series A co-lead in Kyle's [Watershed Climate](/notes/watershed-climate) diligence: Watershed raised a ~$14.7M Series A at ~$57M post co-led by [Sequoia](/notes/sequoia) and Kleiner Perkins (with [Laurene Powell Jobs](/notes/laurene-powell-jobs)) in July 2020, the round preceding the proprietary $50–100M Series B at ~$1B post that [Index Ventures](/notes/index-ventures) was courting in 2021. The firm also appears in Kyle's outreach notes (Bucky Moore at Kleiner Perkins) and in climate/VC research (Renegade Spotlight — Lowercarbon - Research).

**Context:** Kleiner Perkins (founded 1972 by Eugene Kleiner and Tom Perkins) is one of Silicon Valley's most storied venture firms, an early backer of Genentech, Amazon, Google, and many others, and a central figure in the canon of VC history.
