# Manuel Harnisch

VP of Customer Success at People Data Labs (ex-Head of CS at Kentik, Senior Director Strategic CS at SevOne), running a ~75-FTE team, and an expert caller in Kyle's Planhat diligence. He had recently bought [Catalyst](/notes/catalyst) and was a former Gainsight customer (which he called "legacy"), so his read on [Planhat](/notes/planhat) came from a single demo taken largely to learn.

Harnisch's contribution to the diligence was a sharp framing of what actually differentiates CS tools: UI, responsiveness, feature sets, and data-source integrations are **table stakes**, not differentiators — the real differentiator is "how much effort the team puts into the pre-sale phase" to make a buyer comfortable through closing, which is why Catalyst won him over while "everyone else — ToTango, Vitally — says they do that, then sends a 30–40-slide deck." On Planhat specifically he was lukewarm: the demo was high-level and "nothing in particular stood out above and beyond," and he wanted a tool that could also serve enterprise customers. He placed Planhat's pricing at mid-to-possibly-low-end (Gainsight high, Catalyst mid), prized **price predictability** (no 30–40% jump from doubling the customer base), and framed CS ROI around the idea that "churn is a result of not delivering value."
