# Bob Bodily

A Master's student in instructional design who Kyle spoke with in 2018 about the literature-review workflow and where a tool like [ZorHub](/notes/zorhub) could fit. He had done several literature reviews — finding everything written on a specific topic, then crunching it into interpretable analysis — and walked Kyle through the painful end-to-end process: identifying what a research area is even called (his "learning dashboards" review was variously labeled "educational recommender systems," "open-learner models," etc.), wrangling PDFs through reference managers like Mendeley and Zotero, searching across Google Scholar, ERIC, IEEE, EBSCO Host and ProQuest, and chasing down hard-to-find papers via author emails and interlibrary loan (for one review he gathered 100–200 PDFs).

He coded his findings in a hand-built Excel matrix and saw an opening for a tool that blended a reference manager with a qualitative-coding program — one that could clip quotes, figures, and references out of articles, support open/collaborative peer review, and let professors run article discussions. Bodily was personally interested in crowd-sourcing (Wikipedia, learning resources) and found the idea of crowd-sourced research "playlists" intriguing, while noting the hard problem of qualifying or endorsing them beyond mere popularity.
