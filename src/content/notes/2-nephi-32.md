# 2 Nephi 32

A Book of Mormon chapter Kyle leans on for **two practices: feasting on the words of Christ, and praying always**. The first comes from **2 Nephi 32:3** ("feast upon the words of Christ; for behold, the words of Christ will tell you all things what ye should do"), quoted in the General Conference — October 2021 notes alongside [Dallin H. Oaks](/notes/dallin-h-oaks)'s teaching that the Final Judgment evaluates what we have *become*, not just what we have done.

The chapter's larger role in Kyle's corpus is as the **prayer-always proof text**. **2 Nephi 32:8** ("the spirit… teacheth a man to pray") recurs as the baseline assumption underpinning several diagnostic studies: in How Do We Know Where To Protect Ourselves it is the spiritual checklist item ("never exclude God… never forget to pray"), and in How to Overcome Depression daily prayer per 32:8 is the stated precondition for the 10-step plan against non-clinical depression. Prayer — Topical Study uses it to argue prayer itself is Spirit-inspired. Spiritual Gifts mines the surrounding verses heavily — 32:5 (the Spirit "prompts us what to do and how to act"), 32:7 (those who "cry a lot for others"), and 32:8 (the Spirit teaches us what to pray for). 8 Points of the Gospel cites 32:9 ("pray and He will consecrate thy performance").

**Context:** 2 Nephi 32 is a chapter in the Book of Mormon, near the close of Nephi's writings, urging readers to "feast upon the words of Christ," to pray continually, and emphasizing that the Holy Ghost teaches what one should do.

## Where this appears

- General Conference — October 2021 — 32:3, "feast upon the words of Christ," with Oaks on becoming vs. doing.
- 8 Points of the Gospel — 32:9, "pray and He will consecrate thy performance."
- How Do We Know Where To Protect Ourselves — 32:8 as the "never forget to pray" checklist item.
- How to Overcome Depression — daily prayer per 32:8 as a precondition of the 10-step plan.
- Prayer — Topical Study — 32:8, the Spirit "teacheth a man to pray."
- Spiritual Gifts — 32:5, 7, 8 on recognizing promptings and what to pray for.
