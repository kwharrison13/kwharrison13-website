# Peter Drucker

The management thinker quoted across Kyle's library as an authority on leadership, focus, and self-management. [Essentialism](/books/essentialism) calls him "the father of modern management thinking" and the master of the "graceful no" (the wastepaper-basket letter to [Mihaly Csikszentmihalyi](/notes/mihaly-csikszentmihalyi)); it also cites his observation that the most important event of our era is "an unprecedented change in the human condition" — that for the first time large numbers of people "have choices… they will have to manage themselves," and "society is totally unprepared for it."

His definition of [Leadership](/notes/leadership) as results, not popularity, recurs twice: [Law of the Harvest](/books/law-of-the-harvest) quotes "Effective leaders are not those who are loved or admired. They are those whose followers do the right things. Popularity is not leadership. Results are," using it to frame missionary outreach leadership as execution and example. [The Expertise Economy](/books/the-expertise-economy) cites his line "Follow effective action with quiet reflection. From the quiet reflection will come even more effective action," tying him to the [Self-Reflection](/notes/self-reflection) thread in adult learning. In [The Idea Factory](/books/the-idea-factory), Drucker is invoked analytically rather than aphoristically: he argued that Bell Labs' own discoveries "had largely created modern electronics" and in doing so made the Labs' continued existence untenable, prescribing the "far bolder, riskier" course of going into business for itself.

**Context:** Peter Drucker (1909–2005) was an Austrian-American management consultant, educator, and author widely regarded as the founder of modern management theory. His best-known works include *The Effective Executive*, *The Practice of Management*, and *Management: Tasks, Responsibilities, Practices*.
