# Luke 9

Luke 9 surfaces across three of Kyle's gospel-study notes, each citing a different verse on the cost and commitment of discipleship. In Diligence, **Luke 9:23** — "deny [yourselves], and take up [your] cross daily and follow [Christ]" — frames diligent discipleship as a daily, deliberate burden taken up in light of the weight of our shortcomings and our effect on the growth of the kingdom. In General Conference- October 2021, **Luke 9:62** — "No man, having put his hand to the plough, and looking back, is fit for the kingdom of God" — appears as a caution against looking back once committed. In Miscellaneous Scriptures, **Luke 9:41** is read as teaching that some things come only "through prayer and fasting" — not prayer alone — making fasting an exercise of great faith.

**Context:** Luke 9 records the sending of the Twelve, the feeding of the five thousand, Peter's confession of Christ, the Transfiguration, and a series of sayings on the demands of following Jesus.

## Where this appears

- Diligence — Luke 9:23 ("take up your cross daily") on diligent discipleship as a daily burden
- General Conference- October 2021 — Luke 9:62 ("hand to the plough") as a caution against looking back
- Miscellaneous Scriptures — Luke 9:41 on fasting (not prayer alone) as an exercise of faith
