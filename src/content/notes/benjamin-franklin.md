# Benjamin Franklin

Founder, printer, scientist, and diplomat whose [Autobiography](/books/the-autobiography-of-benjamin-franklin) is read here mostly as a manual of deliberate self-improvement. Franklin treated character as an engineering problem: form a good plan, cut off distractions, make execution "his sole study and business."

## What we know

- Author of his own [Autobiography](/books/the-autobiography-of-benjamin-franklin) (read by Kyle in 2018, on the recommendation of the City of Boston), rated 5/5.
- Architect of the [Thirteen Virtues](/notes/thirteen-virtues) — a personal scheme for tracking and improving conduct that prefigures modern habit-tracking ([Atomic Habits](/books/atomic-habits)).
- His humility virtue's entire instruction was: "Imitate [Jesus](/notes/jesus) and [Socrates](/notes/socrates)."
- Namesake of the [Ben Franklin Effect](/notes/ben-franklin-effect) — the observation that asking someone for a favor can make them like you more.
- Wary of disputation ("they get victory sometimes, but they never get good will") and of partnerships without clear terms ([Partnership](/notes/partnership)).
- A candid self-observer of his own [Motivated Reasoning](/notes/motivated-reasoning) (the cod-fishing rationalization) and of perfectionism's limits ([The Speckled Ax](/notes/the-speckled-ax)).
- Appears in [American Sphinx](/books/american-sphinx), soothing Jefferson during the editing of the Declaration with the parable of the sign painter and the hat.

## Connections

- [Thirteen Virtues](/notes/thirteen-virtues) — Franklin's personal scheme for tracking and improving conduct.
- [The Speckled Ax](/notes/the-speckled-ax) — Franklin's parable on the limits of perfectionism, drawn from his pursuit of the virtues.
- [Motivated Reasoning](/notes/motivated-reasoning) — exemplified by Franklin's cod-fishing rationalization, which he flags as self-aware about his own reasoning.
- [Atomic Habits](/books/atomic-habits) — modern habit-tracking that the Thirteen Virtues scheme prefigures.
- [Ben Franklin Effect](/notes/ben-franklin-effect) — the social-psychology observation named for him: doing someone a favor warms them to you.
- [Junto](/notes/junto) — the mutual-improvement club Franklin founded, central to the Isaacson biography.
- [Walter Isaacson](/notes/walter-isaacson) — biographer whose [Benjamin Franklin: An American Life](/books/benjamin-franklin-an-american-life) is a primary source here.
- [Thomas Jefferson](/notes/thomas-jefferson) — co-drafter of the Declaration whom Franklin consoles in [American Sphinx](/books/american-sphinx).
- [Socrates](/notes/socrates) — paired with [Jesus](/notes/jesus) in the humility virtue's instruction: "Imitate Jesus and Socrates."
- [Jesus](/notes/jesus) — the other model Franklin names for the humility virtue.
