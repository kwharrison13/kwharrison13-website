# Priesthood

Across Kyle's Latter-day Saint reading, priesthood is examined less as a settled doctrine than as a fault line — the authority claim that binds family, succession, and Church government, and the locus of the tradition's hardest tensions. [The Priesthood Power of Women](/books/the-priesthood-power-of-women) makes the most affirmative argument: its central thesis distinguishes "God's total priesthood power from the delegated authority of ordained offices," holding that women receive priesthood power and authority through [Temple](/notes/temple) ordinances rather than ordination, and that [Covenant](/notes/covenant)-making "starts every man toward becoming a patriarch and every woman a matriarch." [American Zion](/books/american-zion) roots the concept in [Joseph Smith](/notes/joseph-smith)'s "ever-growing conception of priesthood authority" — the anchor he offered converts hungry for "biblical roots," "familial certainty," and "elusive knowledge" — and traces its darker history through Brigham Young's 1852 anti-Black ordination sermon.

The doubt-literature reads priesthood through the gap between divine authority and human fallibility. [The Crucible of Doubt](/books/the-crucible-of-doubt) quotes the line that "Mormons frequently describe priesthood as the authority to act in God's name" but "fail to plumb" its vexing implication — "delegation involves humans, humans entail error, and error in the context of authority creates conflict" — invoking Austin Farrer (a favorite of [Neal A. Maxwell](/notes/neal-a-maxwell)): "Authority can make law to be law; authority cannot make facts to be facts." [The Next Mormons](/books/the-next-mormons) surveys priesthood sociologically as one of the doctrines (alongside [Polygamy](/notes/polygamy)) the Church "jettisoned" or recast — the gendered "men have priesthood and women have motherhood" division, and the priesthood/temple ban as a flashpoint in members' faith. The concept also surfaces as a bare hashtag across gospel-study pages and in Godly Characteristics of the Master (the [Oath and Covenant of the Priesthood](/notes/oath-and-covenant-of-the-priesthood)).

**Context:** In The Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-day Saints, "priesthood" denotes the authority to act in God's name, conferred through ordination; its history includes the pre-1978 restriction barring Black men from ordination and ongoing discussion about women's relationship to priesthood authority.
