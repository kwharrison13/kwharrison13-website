# Nat Keohane

Nat Keohane is president of the [Center for Climate & Energy Solutions](/notes/center-for-climate-and-energy-solutions) (C2ES) and a former White House climate advisor. He appears in the [Watershed Climate](/notes/watershed-climate) Series B diligence (Watershed Climate Diligence — 2021) as a participant in Watershed's COP26-tied Climate Week 2021 event, where Watershed co-founder [Taylor Francis](/notes/taylor-francis) interviewed him in a "Policy: COP26 and Beyond" breakout. His central message there was a communications point: when talking about climate policy, don't lead with technical discussion — "set the stage by talking about how the policy is going to make people's lives better, in concrete ways they can see in the real world," and only then get into the how. In the diligence framing he sits in the policy-and-ecosystem layer around Watershed's carbon-measurement platform, alongside figures like [Christiana Figueres](/notes/christiana-figueres) ([Global Optimism](/notes/global-optimism)) and [Rebecca Dell](/notes/rebecca-dell) ([ClimateWorks](/notes/climateworks)).

**Context:** Nathaniel (Nat) Keohane became president of the Center for Climate and Energy Solutions (C2ES) in 2020. He previously served as a special assistant to President Obama on energy and environment, and earlier was an economist and senior vice president at the Environmental Defense Fund.
