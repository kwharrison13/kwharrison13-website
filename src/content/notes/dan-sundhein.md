# Dan Sundhein

Founder of the investment firm D1, quoted in Kyle's reading layer on [Charlie Munger — Investment Practices of Leading Charitable Foundations](/notes/charlie-munger-investment-practices-of-leading-charitable-foundations). The quote sits alongside [Chamath Palihapitiya](/notes/chamath-palihapitiya)'s "investing is an individual sport, company-building is a team sport" annotation, and offers a complementary framing of how an investing team coheres: *"We're not a basketball team. We're a track and field team. We're on the same team but we're running different things and have different strengths. But we're wearing the same team name. We all do better if the team wins."* In Kyle's notes the line functions as a metaphor for team structure in investing — individuals running their own events under one shared banner.

**Context:** Dan Sundheim is an American hedge-fund manager who founded D1 Capital Partners in 2018 after serving as chief investment officer of Viking Global Investors. (Note: Kyle's notes spell the name "Sundhein"; the commonly used spelling is "Sundheim.")
