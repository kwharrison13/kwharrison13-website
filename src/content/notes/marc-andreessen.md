# Marc Andreessen

Marc Andreessen is the co-founder of a16z (Andreessen Horowitz) and, in Kyle's corpus, both the catalyst and the foil for the "Build What?" thread. Kyle's essay-research It's Time To Build... But Build What - Research is written in direct response to Andreessen's 2020 a16z manifesto *It's Time To Build* — quoting its diagnosis that the failure is "a failure of action, and specifically our widespread inability to *build*," and its call that "we need to *want* these things… even if incumbents don't like it." Kyle's "but build what?" reframe accepts the energy while pressing on the missing object and mechanism. The same posture extends to Andreessen's 2023 *Techno-Optimist Manifesto*: Kyle's [Techno-Optimism](/notes/techno-optimism) page explicitly distinguishes "Andreessen's accelerationist version" from [Noah Smith](/notes/noah-smith)'s "normative / humanist variety," and lands closer to the latter, noting that Andreessen brands "sustainability" as an enemy while Kyle argues sustainability is a core part of techno-optimism. Andreessen is likewise the named anchor of [Effective Accelerationism](/notes/effective-accelerationism) (e/acc), where Kyle's critique is that the movement "offers no mechanism for purposeful growth and chokes out nuance."

Andreessen also appears as a venture-history figure. [The Power Law: Venture Capital & the Making of the New Future](/books/the-power-law-venture-capital-and-the-making-of-the-new-future) traces his arc from inventing the Mosaic browser at the University of Illinois ("the government was good at basic science… not good at turning breakthroughs into products") through founding a16z with [Ben Horowitz](/notes/ben-horowitz) on the differentiating bet to "smooth the learning curve for scientists who wanted to be chief executives" via an in-house consultancy. In Conviction-Led Contrarianism - Research Kyle cites Andreessen's "curse of the entrepreneur is being too early" and uses it as a mirror for the "important companies are often started *out* of the hype cycle" idea. [Building American Dynamism](/notes/building-american-dynamism) ([Katherine Boyle](/notes/katherine-boyle)'s essay) extends his "build" argument into a defense/dynamism frame.

**Context:** Marc Andreessen (b. 1971) co-created the Mosaic and Netscape web browsers, then co-founded the venture firm Andreessen Horowitz (a16z) with Ben Horowitz in 2009. He is a prominent and polarizing tech commentator, known for the essays "Why Software Is Eating the World" (2011), "It's Time to Build" (2020), and "The Techno-Optimist Manifesto" (2023).
