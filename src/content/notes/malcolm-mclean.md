# Malcolm McLean

The trucking-company owner credited in [David vs. Goliath- A History of New York Trade](/notes/david-vs-goliath-a-history-of-new-york-trade) with inventing the [shipping container](/notes/shipping-container) — frustrated by the inefficiency of transferring goods from truck to ship, his next company, Sea-Land, was first to embrace it and shipped faster and cheaper than rivals. The long read frames his invention as the one that reset the geography of trade.

**Context:** Malcolm McLean (1913–2001) was an American transportation entrepreneur whose standardized intermodal shipping container, debuting in 1956, transformed global trade and logistics by drastically lowering the cost of moving freight.
