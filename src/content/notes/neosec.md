# NeoSec

**One-line:** *An Israeli [API Security](/notes/api-security) startup that analyzes logs (rather than mirroring live traffic) and ships an XDR model — evaluated as a Salt/Noname alternative and described as feeling "geared towards being acquired by [CrowdStrike ($CRWD)](/notes/crowdstrike)."*

## What we know

- Differentiated approach: analyzes logs rather than doing live traffic analysis, parsing for anomalies with their own queries, and presents an XDR model where they identify risks themselves instead of relying on a human threat hunter. ([Nir Rothenberg](/notes/nir-rothenberg), Salt Security Diligence — 2021)
- "Super low friction" to evaluate; founders had already sold a prior startup to [Palo Alto Networks ($PANW)](/notes/palo-alto-networks) and raised from Atlas Ventures and other Israeli VCs. (Salt Security Diligence — 2021)
- Tradeoff of the log-analysis approach: baselines take longer to build than with live traffic. (Salt Security Diligence — 2021)
- [Nir Rothenberg](/notes/nir-rothenberg) (CISO, [Rapyd](/notes/rapyd)) said the product "felt like it was geared towards being acquired by CrowdStrike, like 'CrowdStrike for API Security'" — noting he's "always skeptical of founders that just want a quick exit," though the vision was interesting. (Salt Security Diligence — 2021)
- Rapyd might have chosen NeoSec but was already in POC with [Noname Security](/notes/noname-security) before testing Neo, and was too impressed with Noname — so they went with Noname. (Salt Security Diligence — 2021)
