# Regret

A theme Kyle draws out of his Come Follow Me study of [3 Nephi 8](/notes/3-nephi-8)–[3 Nephi 11](/notes/3-nephi-11) — the great destruction at the death of Christ and the voice calling survivors to repentance. Kyle frames regret as "the cry of the destroyed," anchored in the survivors' lament: "O that we had repented before this great and terrible day" ([3 Nephi 8](/notes/3-nephi-8):24–25). The takeaway he records is forward-looking and practical rather than mournful: "There must be a way to study regret to help us avoid the things we know we will later regret." Regret sits alongside [Sign Seeking](/notes/sign-seeking) and the [Broken heart and a contrite spirit](/notes/broken-heart-and-a-contrite-spirit) as one of the chapter block's key ideas, all in service of the claim that Christ never gives up on us.
