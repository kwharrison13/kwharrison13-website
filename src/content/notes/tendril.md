# Tendril

Tendril is a vertical-software company serving the utilities sector, described by CEO [Adrian Tuck](/notes/adrian-tuck) in his July 10th, 2018 — Adrian Tuck (Tendril) call with Kyle. Its product helps utilities own and control their customer relationships against new market entrants, activate customers and cross-sell new energy products, and (via a newer line) provide smart-home energy management. The mature business is the engagement/analytics piece; the newer business is energy management. Pricing ran ~$8/house/year on engagement and ~$50/house/year on energy management, sold through multi-year contracts with utilities — Tuck cited a $58M deal being signed over 36 months. As a B2B2C model riding utilities' very low customer churn, Tendril reported under 2% cumulative churn over two years and 95% recurring revenue.

On the financials Tuck shared, the core business did ~$36M in 2018 revenue (targeting $40M in 2019) and was profitable at ~$6M EBITDA, while the energy-management line did $3M in 2018 / $9M projected in 2019 and was burning ~$6M but expected to turn profitable by end of 2019. The company had raised ~$150M over the prior decade. The call's purpose was a contemplated transaction: Tuck wanted to look at a deal in September, ideally a majority sale with some rollover and $15–20M of primary capital — a roughly $100M check ($75M secondary, $25M primary) — valuing the whole company at $150–200M (3.5x LTM / 4x NTM revenue). Its main competitor, Tuck said, was Oracle's Opower, from which Tendril was picking up churning customers. Kyle reviewed this during his investing work.

**Context:** Tendril Inc. was a Boulder, Colorado-based energy/utilities software company; it later rebranded as Uplight after merging with Simple Energy and other firms.
