# Pete Buttigieg

Author of [Shortest Way Home](/books/shortest-way-home), the character-driven political memoir whose spine is revitalizing South Bend, Indiana — "College Town 2.0," the "Beta City" framework, and a focus on "quality of place" over "smokestack chasing." The book recounts his crash course in [New Urbanism](/notes/new-urbanism) and his frame for justice drawn from [John Rawls](/notes/john-rawls)'s [Veil of Ignorance](/notes/veil-of-ignorance) ("a society is fair if it looks like something we would design before knowing how we would come into the world"). His core lesson — that "great families, great cities, and even great nations are built through attention to the everyday" — makes the book a [City Building](/notes/city-building) touchstone in Kyle's corpus, alongside its Midwestern recovery counterpart [Janesville](/books/janesville).

He recurs across Kyle's notes as a model of tone-setting leadership: in [Where Do We Go Next?](/notes/where-do-we-go-next), Buttigieg is quoted that "a big part of why the presidency matters is the tone that it sets." In [Romney — A Reckoning](/books/romney-a-reckoning) he surfaces as a 2020 Democratic contender Oprah Winfrey doubted could beat Trump, and as the "adjacent character-driven political memoir" lineage that [Shortest Way Home](/books/shortest-way-home) belongs to.

**Context:** Pete Buttigieg was the mayor of South Bend, Indiana (2012–2020), a 2020 U.S. Democratic presidential candidate, and U.S. Secretary of Transportation (2021–2025).
