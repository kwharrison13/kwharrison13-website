# John Bunyan

English writer referenced in [Charlie Munger — The Psychology of Human Misjudgment](/notes/charlie-munger-the-psychology-of-human-misjudgment), where Munger says he wants, before he dies, to imitate to some extent an author he admires — invoking Bunyan as a model for laying out a body of practical wisdom.

**Context:** John Bunyan (1628–1688) was an English writer and Puritan preacher, best known for the allegory *The Pilgrim's Progress* (1678), one of the most widely read works in English.

## Where this appears

- [Charlie Munger — The Psychology of Human Misjudgment](/notes/charlie-munger-the-psychology-of-human-misjudgment) — Munger names Bunyan as a literary model while explaining why he loves his own way of laying out psychology.
