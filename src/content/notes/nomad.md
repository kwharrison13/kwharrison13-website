# Nomad

[HashiCorp](/notes/hashicorp)'s workload orchestrator. It surfaces in Kyle's wiki as a small but concrete data point about engineering-team tooling choices: in the Noname Security - Diligence notes, [Dave Thompson](/notes/dave-thompson) of [Kong](/notes/kong) — describing his Linux-background, "command-line geeks" platform team — says they've adopted [Terraform](/notes/terraform) from HashiCorp but that **Nomad "isn't a good fit for their needs."** It's used in the diligence as a texture detail of how modern, CLI-oriented infra teams pick and choose among HashiCorp's stack rather than adopting it wholesale.

The same name also appears as a concept thread in [Remote — Office Not Required](/books/remote-office-not-required) — but there it's the *nomadic-lifestyle* sense (leaving the city to work near your passion), not HashiCorp's product. That linkage is a wikilink-name collision, not a reference to the orchestrator.

**Context:** Nomad is HashiCorp's open-source workload orchestrator and scheduler — a lighter-weight alternative to Kubernetes that can schedule containers, VMs, and standalone binaries across a cluster. HashiCorp's better-known products include Terraform, Vault, and Consul.
