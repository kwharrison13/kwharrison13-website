# TikTok

TikTok appears in the wiki not as a deal or company study but as shorthand for a new educational medium. In Kyle's key takeaways on [Why Books Don't Work](/notes/why-books-don-t-work) (Andy Matuschak), he flags that "[TikTok](/notes/tiktok)-style education could be a revolutionary form of learning" — a concrete instance of Matuschak's argument that there is an opportunity to invent new mediums "as impactful as books originally were." The example Kyle attaches is Camden following [sharonsayso](/notes/sharonsayso) (Sharon McMahon's short-form civics/history account) on Instagram, treating TikTok-style short video as a candidate medium whose "grain" might bend toward how people actually learn.

**Context:** TikTok is a short-form video app owned by ByteDance, launched internationally in 2017; its algorithmic feed of short vertical videos made it one of the most-used social platforms globally and a dominant format for short-form content, including informal education.
