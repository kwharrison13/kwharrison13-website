# Luke 2

In both gospel studies that cite it, Luke 2 surfaces through a single verse — Luke 2:52, "Jesus increased in wisdom and stature, and in favour with God and man." In Self-Mastery — Topical Study, Kyle uses it to argue that even Christ, "though free from sin," experienced mortality as a process of growth and development — a model for self-mastery as something earned over time rather than possessed at once. In The Reasons The Spirit Withdraws, the same verse becomes a model of continual learning: paired with the line "when you stop learning, you die," it frames the Christ who kept increasing in wisdom as the pattern for the continual repentance and learning that keeps the Spirit present.

**Context:** Luke 2 is the New Testament chapter of the Gospel of Luke containing the nativity of Jesus and the boy Jesus teaching in the temple, closing with the summary verse that he "increased in wisdom and stature, and in favour with God and man."

## Where this appears

- Self-Mastery — Topical Study — Luke 2:52 as evidence that even the sinless Christ grew through mortality, a model for self-mastery as development over time
- The Reasons The Spirit Withdraws — Luke 2:52 read as a model of continual learning ("when you stop learning, you die") that keeps the Spirit near
