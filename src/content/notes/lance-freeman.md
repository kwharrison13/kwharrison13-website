# Lance Freeman

Urban scholar cited in [The New Urban Crisis](/books/the-new-urban-crisis) as "the leading student of [gentrification](/notes/gentrification) and displacement." [Richard Florida](/notes/richard-florida) draws on Freeman's research for one of the book's more counterintuitive claims: that gentrification "actually displaces far fewer people than is commonly thought," is concentrated in a handful of superstar cities and even particular neighborhoods within them, and is heavily *socially constructed* — "when it comes to how neighborhoods are defined, the gentrifiers ultimately win out," with race shaping perception. In Kyle's [Gentrification](/notes/gentrification) concept note, Freeman's finding is the pivot point against which Florida's sobering counterpoint is set: the bulk of poor Black neighborhoods remain immune to gentrification, their residents trapped in chronic, persistent poverty.

**Context:** Lance Freeman is a professor of urban planning (Columbia University, later the University of Pennsylvania) whose book *There Goes the 'Hood* challenged conventional assumptions about gentrification-driven displacement.
