# Progress Through Business

Progress Through Business is the non-profit that [John Hoffmire](/notes/john-hoffmire) helped create and chairs — a 14-office organization that assists low-income people through market-driven approaches, tied to the Center on Business and Poverty he directs at the University of Wisconsin–Madison. It is the institutional vehicle for Hoffmire's career-long thesis that business and markets, not aid alone, are the lever for lifting people out of poverty (the same conviction behind his work on [Employee Ownership](/notes/employee-ownership) and ESOPs).

In Kyle's August 2nd, 2014 — John Hoffmire Conversation at Oxford notes, Hoffmire framed one of the most valuable things the organization does as making its contributors **better writers** — he spends half-hours on the phone editing people's work, and ties writing decline to a broader complacency where people do "enough to get by." The notes also surface a telling detail on its compensation model: contributors who *could* be paid (after a third article) often choose not to finish it — partly kindness toward the non-profit, and partly, in Kyle's reading, fear of the obligation that compensation would entail. It connects to Hoffmire's broader interest in [Financial Literacy](/notes/financial-literacy) as a long, unglamorous sell.
