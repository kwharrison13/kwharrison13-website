# Customer Success

The post-sales discipline focused on customer health, retention, onboarding, and expansion — a maturing software category as SaaS renewal revenue outgrows new bookings. In Kyle's reading it is also the destination that *sales itself* migrates toward in a [Product Led Growth (PLG)](/notes/product-led-growth-plg) world: the most-repeated claim in his [Product Led Growth — The End User Era](/books/product-led-growth-the-end-user-era) highlights is that PLG is "not anti-sales," but that sales "takes a consultative approach that looks and feels more like customer success." When the buyer is the person who builds with the product (the [Stripe](/notes/stripe) developer-sales model), the hard pitch stops mattering and every customer-facing role must become fluent in the product itself.

As a software category, Customer Success is the subject of Kyle's Planhat - Diligence expert-call work, which maps a crowded tooling landscape. The omnipresent US enterprise incumbents are [Gainsight](/notes/gainsight) (category-creator, deepest features, but clunky and costly) and [ToTango](/notes/totango); the up-and-comers include [Catalyst](/notes/catalyst) (strong community/influencer marketing), [Vitally](/notes/vitally) (a16z-backed, usability-led, EMEA-leaning), [ChurnZero](/notes/churnzero) (mid-market pure-play), and the older [Strikedeck](/notes/strikedeck). [Planhat](/notes/planhat) is positioned in that field as a more configurable, data-mapping-heavy platform. The through-line across these calls is that CS tooling competes on data integration, configurability, and reducing [Churn](/notes/churn) / lifting [Net Revenue Retention](/notes/net-revenue-retention).

**Context:** "Customer Success" emerged as a distinct SaaS function in the 2010s — distinct from traditional support — built on the insight that subscription economics make retention and expansion (net revenue retention) the dominant driver of long-run growth, so proactively driving customer outcomes is a revenue function, not a cost center.
