# Romans 13

A chapter of Paul's epistle to the Romans cited in Kyle's Charity & Love gospel study as evidence that love of neighbor is the gathering point of all the commandments. The verse he leans on is Romans 13:9 — "And if there be any other commandment, it is briefly comprehended in this saying, namely, Thou shalt love thy neighbor as thyself." Kyle reads this alongside [1 Timothy 1](/notes/1-timothy-1):5 ("the end of the commandment is charity") and the great commandment of [Matthew 22](/notes/matthew-22):37–40, concluding that "all the commandments, all the requirements of christlike transformation all come within our obedience in loving one another."

**Context:** Romans 13 is a chapter of the Apostle Paul's letter to the church in Rome, best known for its teaching on submission to governing authorities ("the powers that be are ordained of God") and, in its closing verses, for summing up the law in the single command to love one's neighbor.
