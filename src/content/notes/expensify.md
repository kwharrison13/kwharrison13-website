# Expensify

In Kyle's corpus, Expensify surfaces in the Watershed Climate Diligence — 2021 dossier in two roles. It is listed among [Watershed Climate](/notes/watershed-climate)'s named customers, but more substantively it comes up in [Madeline Rotman](/notes/madeline-rotman)'s ([Imperfect Foods](/notes/imperfect-foods)) reference call as an example of the *data-access bottleneck* in carbon accounting: Imperfect's expense data lived in Expensify, and Rotman noted that vendors like Expensify "are definitely getting pressured to make their data more accessible," but that she "tried to get Expensify to do this but they're not ready." The anecdote illustrates the diligence's recurring theme — that the hardest part of carbon accounting is extracting clean structured data from the tools companies already use, since "no bill is made equal" and each must be managed individually.

**Context:** Expensify is a publicly traded expense-management and receipt-tracking software company founded in 2008, used by businesses to automate expense reports and reimbursements.
