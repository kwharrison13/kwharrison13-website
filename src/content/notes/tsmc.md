# TSMC

TSMC (Taiwan Semiconductor Manufacturing Company) is the central institution in [Chip War](/books/chip-war) and the anchor of several of Kyle's takeaways from the book. [Morris Chang](/notes/morris-chang) founded it after being passed over for CEO at [Texas Instruments](/notes/texas-instruments) — Chip War frames this as the canonical [Innovator's Dilemma](/notes/innovator-s-dilemma) departure: TI binned Chang's pure-play foundry idea in 1976, and the model walked away to become TSMC. Kyle's note "Could TI have become TSMC if he stayed?" sits right on that thread.

The book casts TSMC as Kyle's "monopolies as innovation engines" case study: a single dominant private actor with deep capital and technical patience can do what fragmented competitors can't. Chang's structural insight was the pure-play foundry model — TSMC promised never to design chips, only to build them, so it never competed with its customers and instead succeeded when they did ("the Grand Alliance"). That neutrality, plus relentless training, best-in-class tools, and Taiwanese government subsidy, let it out-invest rivals: by manufacturing for many designers it wrings out volume efficiencies a vertically integrated rival like [Intel](/notes/intel) can't match, which Chip War uses to explain Intel's foundry failure and decline.

Chip War also stresses TSMC's strategic weight. From day one it "wasn't really a private business: it was a project of the Taiwanese state" (Taiwan's government provided 48% of startup capital; Philips put up the rest for a 27.5% stake). Today it fabricates ~37% of the world's logic chips, and [Apple](/notes/apple) — designer of the world's most advanced processors — can only have them made by TSMC, giving Tim Cook more influence over the industry than any Pentagon official. The book treats Taiwan's chip concentration, and the un-replicable nature of TSMC's fabs and trained personnel, as the core geopolitical choke point in any US–China conflict.

**Context:** TSMC is the world's largest dedicated independent (pure-play) semiconductor foundry, headquartered in Hsinchu, Taiwan, and the leading-edge manufacturer for fabless designers including Apple, Nvidia, and Qualcomm.
