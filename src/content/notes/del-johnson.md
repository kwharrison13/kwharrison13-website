# Del Johnson

An investor/writer whose piece [Venture Capital Isn't the Problem—It's Venture Capitalists](https://www.theinformation.com/articles/venture-capital-isnt-the-problem-its-venture-capitalists) in [The Information](/notes/the-information) feeds Kyle's essay [What Is An Investor](/essays/what-is-an-investor) (per What Is An Investor - Research). Johnson's core argument is that venture's recent underperformance — the drought in new era-defining behemoths like Facebook or Google — is a fixable **practitioner** problem, not a structural flaw in the model. His analogy, which Kyle quotes: if a restaurant chain were underperforming, "would we say it's because the restaurant model is flawed? Or would we not first guess that the restaurant needed to serve better food?" He marshals research that VCs over-weight founder background and physical attributes over critical business evaluation, and that many VC managers are overconfident and would benefit from adhering to mathematical models.

In the wiki this puts Johnson on one side of a debate that also runs through [Noah Smith](/notes/noah-smith): whether VC's slump is a temporary end to easy money (Johnson's practitioner view) or a permanent structural shift (Smith's "fewer home runs" thesis). On the [Venture Capital](/notes/venture-capital) page he is paired with Smith as the two voices framing that structural-vs-temporary question.
