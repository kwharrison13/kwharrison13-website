# D&C 10

Section 10 of the [Doctrine and Covenants](/notes/doctrine-and-covenants) appears in Kyle's gospel study around two threads. In Publication of the Book of Mormon he reads [D&C 5](/notes/dandc-5) and [10](/notes/dandc-10) together as the revelations in which [Martin Harris](/notes/martin-harris) is called a "wicked man" and rebuked harshly for the ultimatum he gave the Lord (over the lost manuscript pages), then commanded to repent if he is ever to see the plates. In Christian Reformation, Zion, and Seekers Kyle notes that "[D&C 10](/notes/dandc-10) refers to this pattern" — the section connects to the Seekers / [Primitivism](/notes/primitivism) reformation context, where the Lord describes how the design to destroy the work of translation will be turned back.

**Context:** Doctrine and Covenants Section 10 is a revelation given to Joseph Smith (circa 1828–1829) concerning the 116 lost manuscript pages of the Book of Mormon translation, warning him not to retranslate the lost portion and instructing him to translate the plates of Nephi instead; it closes with the Lord's definition that "whosoever repenteth and cometh unto me, the same is my church" (D&C 10:67).

## Where this appears

- Publication of the Book of Mormon — paired with [D&C 5](/notes/dandc-5); Martin Harris rebuked as a "wicked man," commanded to repent before seeing the plates
- Christian Reformation, Zion, and Seekers — cited as referring to the seeker/primitivist reformation pattern
