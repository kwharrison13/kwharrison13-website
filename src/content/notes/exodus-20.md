# Exodus 20

A scripture reference cited in Kyle's Obedience — Topical Study. The study uses Exodus 20:13, "thou shalt not kill," to set up an apparent contradiction it then works through — the commandment against killing alongside the instances where God's servants are commanded to kill (e.g. Nephi and Laban). It also cites Exodus 20:3–6 (the commandment against idolatry and false gods) in the story of Shadrach, Meshach, and Abed-nego, as an example of "trusting obedience" that holds even when the outcome is uncertain.

**Context:** Exodus 20 is the chapter containing the Ten Commandments, given to Moses at Mount Sinai.

## Where this appears

- Obedience — Topical Study — cites Exodus 20:13 ("thou shalt not kill") and 20:3–6 (against idolatry) in discussions of obedience, the sin/transgression distinction, and the example of Shadrach, Meshach, and Abed-nego.
