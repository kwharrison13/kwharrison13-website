# The Law of Chastity

In Kyle's gospel study, the Law of Chastity appears within The Whole Armor of God as the meaning of the "loins" girt about with truth — "your virtue, protected by truth," where the truth in question is "a knowledge of things as they were, as they are, and as they will become," and "the test of Godhood is overcoming your physical desires." Kyle's notes push past the common "chocolate bar with the chocolate licked off" analogy as inadequate: that image conveys the bitterness over a partner's lost virginity but misframes the doctrine. His correction is that "virtue is not taken when someone engages in intercourse" — rather "it is the disobedient use of our God-given procreative powers that robs us of virtue," and crucially that "this virtue can be returned when repentance occurs."

The throughline is that the law is not primarily about an irreversible loss but about agency, obedience, and the availability of repentance — "repentance is available to all who honestly seek it."

**Context:** In Latter-day Saint teaching, the law of chastity is the standard of sexual purity — abstinence outside of marriage and fidelity within it.

## Where this appears

- The Whole Armor of God — the "loins girt with truth" represents virtue/chastity; Kyle reframes it around disobedient use of procreative power and the restorative power of repentance rather than the "licked chocolate bar" analogy.
