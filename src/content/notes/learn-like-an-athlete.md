# Learn Like An Athlete

The idea that learning should be treated with the same deliberate, effortful discipline athletes apply to their sport. In [How To Take Smart Notes](/books/how-to-take-smart-notes), the analogy lands on the principle that "the one who does the work does the learning" — a coach exists to direct effort, not to do the heavy lifting, just as no one would hire a coach to work out for them. Real learning, like athletic training, requires demanding deliberate practice rather than shortcuts; trying to skip the effort makes as little sense as going to the gym to lift as little as possible.

The Open-Source Knowledge - Research notes extend the metaphor toward self-review: professional athletes routinely study tapes of their own gameplay, so why don't knowledge workers? This frames "learning like an athlete" as a practice of reviewing one's own past performance — a form of [Spirit of Humility](/notes/spirit-of-humility) and inoculation against unexamined "consensus conclusions" ("being wrong" as a culture-alchemy discipline).
