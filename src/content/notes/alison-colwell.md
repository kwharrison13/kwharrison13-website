# Alison Colwell

Climate/sustainability practitioner interviewed as an expert customer reference in Kyle's Watershed Climate Diligence — 2021 dossier (call dated November 1, 2021). She joined [Okta ($OKTA)](/notes/okta) in January 2021 as its **first Director of Sustainability**, having come from [Sephora](/notes/sephora). At Okta she sat on an ESG committee (IR, legal, her boss, and the VP of "Okta for Good"), and before her arrival the company had run a greenhouse-gas evaluation and begun buying offsets.

Her account frames Okta's climate program around a "reduce first, always" philosophy — "easier to keep carbon out of the air rather than going to get it" — aligning to the TCFD disclosure framework. She walked through five focus areas: travel evaluations, electrification (working with landlords to electrify leased buildings), renewable electricity (achieved for offices and employee homes, partnering with Anthesis on work-from-home usage), vendor partnerships (verifying suppliers have science-based targets without creating "vendor fatigue"), and [Carbon Offsets](/notes/carbon-offsets) as the last resort once reduction is exhausted. As a Watershed-adjacent customer voice, her testimony fed [Index Ventures](/notes/index-ventures)' diligence on the climate-accounting category.
