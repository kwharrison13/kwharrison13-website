# Conversion

Conversion runs through Kyle's notes as both a missionary outcome and a personal threshold. In his Alma 17–22 study it is the conversion of [Ammon](/notes/ammon)'s convert king Lamoni, his household, and his father — the sons of Mosiah made "instruments in the hands of God." [What I Want My Son to Know before He Leaves on His Mission](/notes/what-i-want-my-son-to-know-before-he-leaves-on-his-mission) sharpens the personal stakes: "You cannot convert people beyond your own conversion" — the Book of Mormon plus a personal [Testimony](/notes/testimony) of it being "powerful instruments of conversion." Mighty Repentance adds the equation that full repentance *is* conversion.

The concept also shows up in Kyle's reading on the demographics and history of his faith. [The Next Mormons](/books/the-next-mormons) frames the "retention-vs-conversion math behind the slowing of Rodney Stark's 'next world faith' projection." [The Life of John Taylor](/books/the-life-of-john-taylor) supplies a biographical case: Taylor's pre-conversion path through Methodism and "successful" preaching, which Kyle reads as evidence for "taking seekers seriously rather than dismissing their pre-conversion lives as preparation-for-the-real-thing."
