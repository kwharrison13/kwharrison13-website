# China

In Kyle's wiki, China appears most concretely as a market lever in the movie business. In the essay [The Hits Business](/essays/the-hits-business), the Chinese market is the specific tool [Thomas Tull](/notes/thomas-tull) used to "weaponize globalization" and scale [Legendary Entertainment](/notes/legendary-entertainment) to blockbuster outcomes — Kyle flags it as a whole separate piece he could write, the foil to [A24](/notes/a24)'s small-and-opinionated model. The logic: Tull and other big studios realized they could get "a better shot at bigger outcomes" by making "fairly generalized, un-opinionated, mass appeal movies that can succeed in every international market," China chief among them. The [Thomas Tull](/notes/thomas-tull) page records the same point, citing the Forbes "global mogul" formula and a Washington Post piece on China's growing influence over Hollywood.

**Context:** China is the world's most populous nation (until recently) and second-largest economy. In the 2010s its booming box office made the Chinese market a decisive factor in Hollywood blockbuster economics — the period this wiki's references draw on.

## Where this appears

- [The Hits Business](/essays/the-hits-business) — the Chinese market as the lever Thomas Tull used to weaponize globalization for blockbuster scale, contrasted with A24.
- [Thomas Tull](/notes/thomas-tull) — built Legendary Entertainment on a formula leaning on globalization and the Chinese market.
