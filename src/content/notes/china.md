# China

In Kyle's wiki, China appears most concretely as a market lever in the movie business. In the essay [The Hits Business](/essays/the-hits-business), the Chinese market is the specific tool [Thomas Tull](/notes/thomas-tull) used to "weaponize globalization" and scale [Legendary Entertainment](/notes/legendary-entertainment) to blockbuster outcomes — Kyle flags it as a whole separate piece he could write, the foil to [A24](/notes/a24)'s small-and-opinionated model. The logic: Tull and other big studios realized they could get "a better shot at bigger outcomes" by making "fairly generalized, un-opinionated, mass appeal movies that can succeed in every international market," China chief among them. The [Thomas Tull](/notes/thomas-tull) page records the same point, citing the Forbes "global mogul" formula and a Washington Post piece on China's growing influence over Hollywood.

A second cluster of sources (July 2026) treats China as the leading source of open-weight AI models — Qwen, Kimi, GLM, DeepSeek — and the geopolitical strategy behind releasing them openly. [America’s Open-Model Paradox](/notes/america-s-open-model-paradox) argues Chinese open weights have become the default base/teacher model for Western AI builders via [Distillation (AI)](/notes/distillation-ai). [When China’s Open-Source AI Is a Trap](/notes/when-china-s-open-source-ai-is-a-trap) covers Xi Jinping's framing of open-source AI as a "shared asset for all humanity" ahead of the World AI Conference in Shanghai. [Who’s Afraid of Chinese Models?](/notes/who-s-afraid-of-chinese-models) frames China's strategy as "commoditize your complements," tied explicitly to China's lead in robotics and the physical-world application of AI.

**Context:** China is the world's most populous nation (until recently) and second-largest economy. In the 2010s its booming box office made the Chinese market a decisive factor in Hollywood blockbuster economics — the period this wiki's earlier references draw on; more recent (2026) sources track its open-weight AI model strategy.
