# Twilio Flex

[Twilio](/notes/twilio)'s programmable cloud contact-center product, which appears in Kyle's notes as the platform layer underneath an AI customer-support deployment. In the Cresta.ai diligence, a Neiman Marcus operator describes migrating their stack onto Twilio Flex: chat/email/SMS moved from "Power Front" to Twilio Flex in April (Power Front couldn't do Apple Business Chat), and voice was converted off legacy [Avaya](/notes/avaya) onto Twilio Flex as well. They chose Twilio Flex partly for "AI/ML flexibility," then ran [Cresta.ai](/notes/cresta-ai) on top of it to coach agents — integrating Cresta with Twilio Flex as their chat provider.

On the [Twilio](/notes/twilio) entity page, Flex is noted as Twilio's contact-center product and the reason it warrants its own concept page. The picture across these sources: Flex is the programmable CPaaS-based replacement for legacy contact-center vendors like Avaya and NICE, and the substrate that AI-agent-assist tools like Cresta plug into.

**Context:** Twilio Flex, launched in 2018, is a fully programmable cloud contact-center platform — Twilio's move up the stack from raw communications APIs to a customizable, application-layer call-center product that competes with incumbents like Genesys, NICE, and Avaya.
