# Shuli Song

Shuli Song runs the RevOps team at [Plaid](/notes/plaid) — managing reps, quota setting, plan administration, commission calculation, and processing — sitting within BizOps as the broader umbrella covering anything around variable compensation (CaptivateIQ Diligence — 2021). She did banking/consulting and then GitHub before Plaid, and as she crossed the three-year mark started looking for something with more P&L exposure or a customer-facing role; she's drawn to B2B software companies with strong fundamentals and varied product lines, and open to productivity tools like [Notion](/notes/notion) or [Airtable](/notes/airtable) at a 50–200 person company (January 31st, 2022 — Shuli Song (Plaid)). She flagged Usage-Based Pricing as a particularly interesting problem — how to incentivize reps and think about reps carrying multiple SKUs versus verticalization.

Kyle drew on her in two ways: as a [Plaid](/notes/plaid) operator surveyed for the Talent Vortex essay (one of the Plaid respondents alongside [Sundeep Peechu](/notes/sundeep-peechu) and [Hayden Pirkle](/notes/hayden-pirkle)), and as a sales-comp expert reference during the CaptivateIQ Diligence — 2021 dossier given her RevOps vantage at Plaid.
