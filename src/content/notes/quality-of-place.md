# Quality of Place

"Quality of place" is Richard Florida's term for the vibrant amenities — "great restaurants and cafes, a music scene, and lots of other things to do" — that attract the creative class. In [The New Urban Crisis](/books/the-new-urban-crisis), Florida names it as one of the three forces (alongside high-paying jobs and short commutes) pulling the affluent and educated back to the urban core. Kyle's marginal note pushes on this: [Remote Work](/notes/remote-work) dissolves the commute and proximity constraints, "leaving amenities as the only question" — and in a [COVID](/notes/covid) world the desired amenities (backyards, space, the outdoors) point *away* from the dense core (see [COVID pushes us towards something like Prosperable](/notes/covid-pushes-us-towards-something-like-prosperable) and [Prosperable](/notes/prosperable)).

The idea recurs as a [City Building](/notes/city-building) / [Economic Development](/notes/economic-development) framework. In [Shortest Way Home](/books/shortest-way-home), [Pete Buttigieg](/notes/pete-buttigieg) frames the modern shift in economic development: it "is no longer just a game of luring factories" with tax incentives ("smokestack chasing"), but increasingly recruiting *people* by enhancing a community's "quality of place." Kyle draws the same throughline on the [Economic Development](/notes/economic-development) page — recruit people, not just employers, by making the place itself appealing.

**Context:** "Quality of place" is a concept popularized by urbanist Richard Florida (notably in [The Rise of the Creative Class](/notes/the-rise-of-the-creative-class)) describing the lifestyle amenities, culture, and built environment that make a city attractive to talented workers — a counterpoint to recruiting firms via tax incentives.

## Where this appears

- [The New Urban Crisis](/books/the-new-urban-crisis) — Florida names quality of place (amenities) as one of three forces drawing the affluent back to the urban core; Kyle's note reframes it through remote work and COVID.
- [Shortest Way Home](/books/shortest-way-home) — Buttigieg's "recruit people, not factories" reframing of economic development around quality of place over "smokestack chasing."
- [Economic Development](/notes/economic-development) — the discipline's modern shift toward enhancing a community's quality of place to attract talent.
