# Crypto

In Kyle's corpus, crypto shows up less as a settled investment thesis than as a lens on bubbles, belief, and market fickleness. The richest treatment is in [Boom — Bubbles & The End of Stagnation](/books/boom-bubbles-and-the-end-of-stagnation), where [Byrne Hobart](/notes/byrne-hobart) and [Tobias Huber](/notes/tobias-huber) analyze Bitcoin as a "complex, multi-layered, and self-referential bubble" — at the code level "a bubble machine" whose value, security, and network effects compound with adoption, and whose every survived crash is "a testament to the network's robustness." They frame Bitcoin as "as much a social bubble as it is a financial bubble or a technological achievement," with Satoshi Nakamoto cast as a sacrificing, messianic founder (forgoing ~1.15M Bitcoin and departing early to cement his ideas as canonical) — an "innovation-accelerating bubble" of the same family as canals, railways, and the dot-com boom.

That bubble-skepticism recurs elsewhere. [Steve Case](/notes/steve-case) views blockchain as "foundational" but counsels caution on the Bitcoin/ICO mania, explicitly echoing the Dot Com bubble. And in [We're Selling Entrepreneurship Short](/notes/we-re-selling-entrepreneurship-short), crypto is the stand-in for venture fashion: "one day everyone is chasing crypto then they're hunting AI the next" — the point being that funding-dependent founders are exposed to those waves while founders who control their destiny are not. On the company side it threads through several of Kyle's diligence notes: [Bradley Miles](/notes/bradley-miles) (former CoinDesk research lead building the blockchain-based FanCoin), [Standard.re](/notes/standard-re) (on-chain life insurance pursuing a Bermuda-first regulatory path), [Hook.xyz](/notes/hook-xyz) (a Contrary NFT-ecosystem company), and [DigitalTown](/notes/digitaltown) (blockchain "City Shares" smart contracts).
