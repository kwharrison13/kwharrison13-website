# Playbook

"Playbook" appears in two distinct senses across Kyle's notes. In [Working in Public](/books/working-in-public) it is Kyle's **open strategic question**: *"What is the playbook for creating a commons-based project?"* — the repeatable recipe (if one exists) for spinning up the kind of open-source commons the book studies.

In the Planhat diligence it is a **concrete product feature** in the [Customer Success](/notes/customer-success) software category: playbooks are the baked-in best-practices and automation workflows (CTAs / action items across the customer lifecycle — e.g. a uniform first-90-days delivery for a segment) that let CS teams scale low-touch, one-to-many relationships without building everything themselves. The diligence treats playbooks as a core evaluation axis: [Planhat](/notes/planhat)'s are flagged as **basic** ("basic triggers, no smart rules… we can already hack this together with Intercom & Salesforce"), while [Catalyst](/notes/catalyst), [ToTango](/notes/totango), ChurnZero, Vitally and others are noted for stronger playbook + CTA automation. The most concrete product gap surfaced is the upgrade path on playbook automation / smart rules.
