# Matthew 22

The New Testament chapter Kyle's notes return to for the **two great commandments** — specifically [Matthew 22](/notes/matthew-22):37–40, "Thou shalt love the Lord thy God with all thy heart, and with all thy soul, and with all thy mind." In General Conference- October 2021 it is cited (via [Jeffrey R. Holland](/notes/jeffrey-r-holland)) as "the first great commandment in the Universe," paired with the corollary that God already loves us that way "now, wholeheartedly without reservation."

Across Kyle's gospel study the verse functions as the *root of all the commandments*. In Charity & Love he argues that "every direction we are given within the gospel directly correlates to the great and last commandment given by the Savior in [Matthew 22](/notes/matthew-22):37–40" — the reason charity, "the pure love of Christ," is the greatest of faith, hope, and charity. In 8 Points of the Gospel, [Neal A. Maxwell](/notes/neal-a-maxwell) reads the "heart, soul, and mind" language as the encompassing words of *consecration*: "the first commandment, which is constantly, not periodically, operative." Miscellaneous Scriptures cross-references it against [2 Nephi 31](/notes/2-nephi-31):20 ("press forward in Christ, endure to the end"), reinforcing that loving God with all one's being is the through-line of enduring discipleship.

**Context:** Matthew 22 is a chapter in the Gospel of Matthew containing the Parable of the Wedding Feast and Jesus' answer to the question of the greatest commandment — to love God with all one's heart, soul, and mind, and one's neighbor as oneself.
