# Wassily Leontief

Leontief appears in Kyle's notes as the sharpest statement of the automation-displacement thread. [Utopia For Realists](/books/utopia-for-realists) quotes his 1983 line that "the role of humans as the most important factor of production is bound to diminish, in the same way that the role of horses in agricultural production was first diminished and then eliminated by the introduction of tractors." [The Precariat](/notes/the-precariat) folds that "humans as horses displaced by tractors" image into the book's broader argument cluster — [Automation](/notes/automation) and the [Second Machine Age](/notes/second-machine-age) displacing workers, [Bullshit Jobs](/notes/bullshit-jobs) ([David Graeber](/notes/david-graeber)), and [Universal Basic Income](/notes/universal-basic-income) as the policy response — where the precariat names the human cost that those proposals are built to address.

**Context:** Wassily Leontief (1905–1999) was a Russian-American economist who won the 1973 Nobel Memorial Prize in Economic Sciences for developing input-output analysis, a method for modeling how the output of one industry feeds into others across an economy.

- [The Precariat](/notes/the-precariat) — his horse-and-tractor image cited within the automation/UBI argument cluster the book sits inside.
