# Lollapalooza

The lollapalooza effect is [Charlie Munger](/notes/charlie-munger)'s term for the way **really big effects come only from large combinations of factors acting in the same direction.** It is the Fifth Notion in his talk [Charlie Munger — Practical Thought About Practical Thought](/notes/charlie-munger-practical-thought-about-practical-thought) (Talk 4 of [Poor Charlie's Almanack](/books/poor-charlie-s-almanack)), the capstone of his five problem-solving notions: decide the big no-brainer questions first, apply numerical fluency, invert, think in a [multidisciplinary](/notes/multidisciplinary-thinking) manner — and finally watch for the lollapalooza, where several models or forces converge and compound rather than merely add. Munger's later [Charlie Munger — Harvard Law School Fiftieth Reunion Address](/notes/charlie-munger-harvard-law-school-fiftieth-reunion-address) lists it among his "ultra-simple, general problem-solving notions" as: "Watch for combinations of factors — the Lollapalooza effect."

The concept is central to Munger's broader case for carrying a full latticework of mental models: the biggest outcomes (in business, in human misjudgment, in markets) are typically multi-causal, so single-discipline thinking systematically under-predicts them.

**Context:** Munger coined "lollapalooza effect" to describe the confluence of multiple psychological tendencies or causal factors producing outsized, often nonlinear results; it became a signature idea of his mental-models framework as collected in *Poor Charlie's Almanack*.
