# Line upon line, precept upon precept

This phrase — from [2 Nephi 28](/notes/2-nephi-28):30, "I will give unto the children of men line upon line, precept upon precept, here a little and there a little" — is Kyle's recurring frame for how revelation and spiritual knowledge actually arrive: incrementally, not as a single Sacred-Grove vision. The Alma 30–31 study develops it at length through [David A. Bednar](/notes/david-a-bednar)'s teaching that "what we initially believe is the answer may be but one part of a 'line upon line' ongoing, incremental, and unfolding pattern of small answers," reinforced by [Joseph F. Smith](/notes/joseph-f-smith) ("the Lord withheld marvels from me, and showed me the truth, line upon line") and [Neal A. Maxwell](/notes/neal-a-maxwell) ("paced progress… is recommended by Him"). Kyle ties it to [D&C 98](/notes/dandc-98):12 and to the restoration itself growing "line upon line" from Joseph's first small inkling to temples worldwide.

The phrase also marks the live tension in [Saints — No Unhallowed Hand](/books/saints-no-unhallowed-hand): [Wilford Woodruff](/notes/wilford-woodruff)'s Manifesto and his theology that "inspiration *is* revelation," which sits right on the question of whether a commandment can ever be irrevocable. Kyle's margin note — "Can we ever say this about anything? We don't know the ways of God. Line upon line." — makes the principle a check against doctrinal certainty. In the General Conference- October 2021 notes it appears as a frame for "micro-betterments," incremental improvement as the spiritual default.

**Context:** "Line upon line, precept upon precept" appears in Isaiah 28:10 and is quoted in 2 Nephi 28:30 (Book of Mormon). In Latter-day Saint teaching it describes the gradual, incremental nature of personal and institutional revelation — knowledge given a little at a time as one is ready to receive it.
