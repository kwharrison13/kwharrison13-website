# Climate Tech

Climate tech is the investment category Kyle's notes anchor to [Watershed Climate](/notes/watershed-climate) and the broader move toward [Decarbonization](/notes/decarbonization) — "the economy-wide shift away from carbon-emitting processes," framed by Watershed as the defining economic transition of the decade. The most developed grounding is the 2021 Watershed Series B diligence at [Index Ventures](/notes/index-ventures), where the thesis calls Watershed "the best-referenced product and team we've seen in climate tech" and predicts a generational company "as the world tackles climate change." The diligence sketches the category's structure: an end-to-end platform that lets companies measure their carbon footprint (carbon accounting), build emission-reduction plans, and purchase offsets — set against a market where European reporting requirements begin in 2022, the SEC signals US guidance, and net-zero commitments create a $100B+ pool of demand for carbon offsets.

Within climate tech the diligence maps adjacent and competitive players: complementary supply-side projects ([NCX](/notes/ncx)), competing carbon-credit marketplaces ([Patch](/notes/patch), Pachama), and carbon-accounting rivals focused on heavy emitters ([Persefoni](/notes/persefoni), [Emitwise](/notes/emitwise), [Planetly](/notes/planetly)). The throughline of Kyle's interest is that climate tech becomes investable once measurement turns sustainability into "a math problem" — making decarbonization tractable for companies under regulatory, investor, customer, and employee pressure to act.

**Context:** "Climate tech" is the 2020s framing of the venture category formerly called [Cleantech](/notes/cleantech), reoriented around decarbonization and net-zero — spanning carbon accounting/measurement, energy, carbon removal and offsets, industrial decarbonization, and climate-risk software.
