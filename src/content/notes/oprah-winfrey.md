# Oprah Winfrey

In [Stop Asking Questions](/books/stop-asking-questions), Winfrey appears as an exemplar of the power of one-on-one audience intelligence. The book recounts that in her early years she signed autographs after live shows as fast as she could — "I [would] do all the autographs and never look up, trying to get through 350" — until she decided she would rather talk with the audience: "I want to find out who they are, where they come from. That became my favorite part of the day." She began talking with members of her live audience after each show to understand why they spent hundreds of dollars and hours of their time to see her, and considered it her greatest resource: "It's the reason why we were number one for 25 years."

The anecdote anchors the book's argument that genuine, curious conversation — rather than a checklist of questions — is how you actually understand people.

**Context:** Oprah Winfrey is an American talk-show host, producer, and media executive whose nationally syndicated *The Oprah Winfrey Show* ran from 1986 to 2011.
