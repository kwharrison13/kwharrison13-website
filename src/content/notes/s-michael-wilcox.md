# S. Michael Wilcox

A teacher Kyle quotes repeatedly across his gospel-study journals for memorable, image-rich one-liners. In Obedience — Topical Study, Wilcox frames "intelligent obedience" as the accumulation of repeated simple acts rather than a price paid — "How many times does a husband tell his wife, 'I love you' before they become a celestial marriage? The accumulation of repeated simple acts brings God's blessings into our lives." In Original 12 Apostles, Kyle uses Wilcox's lines on Thomas and marriage — "An eternity with someone at their best is worth a few times of seeing them at their worst," and the resurrection quip "God runs quite the beauty parlor. It's called the resurrection." In The Lehonti Principle, Wilcox supplies the framing for "the Jonathan Factor": "There are so many Jonathan's among us who will never be king, but will display some of the deepest measures of godly character."

**Context:** S. Michael Wilcox is a Latter-day Saint author and educator who taught at the LDS Institute of Religion at the University of Utah and has written widely on scripture and discipleship.
