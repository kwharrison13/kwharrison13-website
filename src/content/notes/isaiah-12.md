# Isaiah 12

Isaiah 12 appears in Kyle's gospel study on Diligence for its image of **"drawing water out of the wells of salvation"** (Isaiah 12:3, also quoted in 2 Nephi 22:3). Kyle uses the verse to illustrate that diligently seeking after righteousness requires faith in the reward available: "When you go about the sometimes difficult task of drawing water out of a well, you exercise the faith that water really lies below." The promised joy — "with joy ye shall draw water" — is the assurance that sustains the effort. The note flows from there into a discussion of spiritual rebirth and the "living water" theme.

**Context:** Isaiah 12 is a short chapter of the Book of Isaiah — a song of thanksgiving and praise ("Behold, God is my salvation") that the Book of Mormon quotes in 2 Nephi 22 as part of Nephi's extended Isaiah block. Verse 3, on drawing water from the wells of salvation, is its best-known line.

## Where this appears

- Diligence — Isaiah 12:3 ("with joy ye shall draw water out of the wells of salvation") cited as an image for diligently seeking righteousness in faith of the reward
