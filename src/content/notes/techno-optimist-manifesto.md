# Techno-Optimist Manifesto

The Techno-Optimist Manifesto is [Marc Andreessen](/notes/marc-andreessen)'s 2023 a16z essay, which recurs across Kyle's writing as the crystallizing document of the effective-accelerationism (e/acc) worldview. In [It's Time To Build... But Build What](/essays/its-time-to-build-but-build-what), Kyle treats it as the sequel to Andreessen's "It's Time To Build," arguing it "crystalized a growing dichotomy between effective altruism (aka EA) and effective accelerationism (aka e/acc)" and that much of accelerationism traces its roots back to the earlier "Time To Build" piece. In [Venture Capital's Fourth Turning](/essays/venture-capitals-fourth-turning), Kyle uses it as the sharpest contrast between old-guard "make the world a better place" values and the new-age stance — noting that the manifesto not only makes the techno-optimist case but "explicitly articulates 'the enemy.'"

The manifesto leans on two intellectual anchors Kyle has mapped. It cites [Ray Kurzweil](/notes/ray-kurzweil)'s [Law of Accelerating Returns](/notes/law-of-accelerating-returns) as a key claim — Kurzweil being Kyle's canonical case of hyper-optimistic exponential-curve futurism — and it closes with [David Deutsch](/notes/david-deutsch)'s line: "We have a duty to be optimistic. Because the future is open, not predetermined... it is our duty to fight for a better world." Kyle uses Deutsch's line as the moral grounding for the e/acc / [Techno-Optimism](/notes/techno-optimism) section of his research. In the satirical [Dr. Tokens or — How I Learned to Stop Worrying and Love the AI Bubble](/essays/dr-tokens-or-how-i-learned-to-stop), the manifesto is invoked as the "canonical gospel" perma-bears should revisit.

**Context:** "The Techno-Optimist Manifesto" (October 2023) is Andreessen's polemical statement of faith in technology, markets, and growth, framing a named list of "enemies" (stagnation, risk-aversion, certain strands of safetyism) — a foundational text for the e/acc movement.
