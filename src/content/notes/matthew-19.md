# Matthew 19

The New Testament chapter Kyle associates above all with the **rich young ruler** (Matt 19:16–22). His reading: the man kept the commandments yet "went away sorrowful" — so [Discipleship](/notes/discipleship) means "identifying what we lack, changing, and more fully following the Father and the Son," giving your whole soul rather than just clean compliance (December 31–January 6, 2019. "We Are Responsible for Our Own Learning"). The same passage recurs in his covenant study (October 12–18, 2020. 3 Nephi 20–26 — Ye Are the Children of the Covenant) and underwrites his notes on Temporal Wealth and the Kingdom of God — Brigham Young (citing Matt 19:16–24, "it is easier for a camel…").

Kyle also files Matthew 19:30 — "the first shall be last, and the last shall be first" — in his cross-reference web on leadership and reversal (Chosen Leaders).

**Context:** Matthew 19 contains Jesus' teachings on marriage and divorce, blessing the little children, and the encounter with the rich young ruler — where Christ tells him to sell his possessions, then observes how hard it is for the rich to enter the kingdom of heaven (vv. 16–24), closing with "many that are first shall be last; and the last shall be first" (v. 30).
