# D&C 58

A Doctrine and Covenants section Kyle returns to for three distinct teachings. The most-cited is the **"anxiously engaged" / agency** passage (58:26–28): "it is not meet that I should command in all things… for the power is in them, wherein they are agents unto themselves." Kyle uses it in 1 Nephi 16–22 — I Will Prepare the Way before You to read Nephi's broken bow — building a new one on his own initiative is the deliberate, self-directed response a disciple is meant to make — and in December 31–January 6, 2019. "We Are Responsible for Our Own Learning" as the scriptural ground for taking ownership of one's own learning. The verses tie directly to his [Anxiously Engaged](/notes/anxiously-engaged) and [Agents Unto Themselves](/notes/agents-unto-themselves) notes.

The second thread is **[Forgiveness](/notes/forgiveness)** (58:42–43): "he who has repented of his sins, the same is forgiven, and I, the Lord, remember them no more." In Everyone Is Human this is the heart of a topical study on confession and forsaking sin — why you must "dump the whole bucket" rather than a little at a time — and it reappears in Humility — Topical Study and Excommunication — Topical Study. The third thread is the **reasons for sadness** (58:2–5), which Kyle cites in How to Overcome Depression as a caution against being too quick to erase sorrow, since there can be divine purpose in difficulty.

**Context:** Doctrine and Covenants Section 58 is a revelation given to Joseph Smith in Zion (Jackson County, Missouri) in August 1831. It counsels the Saints on tribulation and its purposes, the principle of being "anxiously engaged in a good cause" of one's own free will, and the conditions of repentance and forgiveness.

## Where this appears

- 1 Nephi 16–22 — I Will Prepare the Way before You — 58:26–27, deliberate self-direction (Nephi's new bow) vs. murmuring
- December 31–January 6, 2019. "We Are Responsible for Our Own Learning" — 58:26–28, agents unto ourselves; owning our own learning
- Everyone Is Human — 58:42–43, the Lord "remembers no more"; dumping the whole bucket in confession
- Humility — Topical Study — 58:43, confessing and forsaking as the mark of repentance
- Excommunication — Topical Study — 58:16–24, Bishop Partridge
- How to Overcome Depression — 58:2–5, there are often reasons for sadness; don't rush to erase it
