# 1 Nephi 17

A Book of Mormon chapter (Nephi builds the ship and crosses to the promised land) that recurs in Kyle's notes through **two memorable verses**. The first, **1 Nephi 17:41**, describes the fiery flying serpents and the brass-serpent healing where "the labor which they had to perform was to look; and because of the simpleness of the way, or the easiness of it, there were many who perished." Kyle pairs this — in both [How To Take Smart Notes](/books/how-to-take-smart-notes) and the July 20–26, 2020. Alma 36–38: "Look to God and Live" study — with [Alma 37](/notes/alma-37):6 ("by small and simple things are great things brought to pass") to make the point that **the simplicity of an idea is what makes it powerful and easy to overlook** (the shipping-container example in How To Take Smart Notes; the "easiness of the way" / [Entropy](/notes/entropy) thread in the Alma study).

The second, **1 Nephi 17:45** ("ye were past feeling, that ye could not feel his words" / God spoke "in a still small voice"), anchors the recurring theme of spiritual numbness and revelation. It appears in the August 24–30, 2020. Helaman 7–12: "Remember the Lord" study and is quoted at length in Christ Is Risen; Faith in Him Will Move Mountains — Russell M. Nelson, where it is set beside [Dieter F. Uchtdorf](/notes/dieter-f-uchtdorf)'s counsel to "doubt your doubts before you doubt your faith."

**Context:** 1 Nephi 17 is a chapter in the Book of Mormon in which the prophet Nephi is commanded to build a ship to carry his family across the ocean to the promised land, recounting the Israelites' wilderness journey and the brass-serpent episode as he rebukes his rebellious brothers.
