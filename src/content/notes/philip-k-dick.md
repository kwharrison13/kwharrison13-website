# Philip K. Dick

Philip K. Dick appears in Kyle's notes as a touchstone [Science Fiction](/notes/science-fiction) author across two strands. In the Historical Futurism - Research doc, his short story "Second Variety" is catalogued in the working media list of near-future / speculative works that feed the "literature of dreaming" — raw material for the essay's argument that imagining the future is a discipline worth practicing. In [The Man From The Future](/books/the-man-from-the-future) (Ananyo Bhattacharya's biography of [John von Neumann](/notes/john-von-neumann)), Dick's "Autofac" is cited as having dramatized von Neumann's self-replicating automata — fiction serving as a cultural mirror of the replicating machines von Neumann formalized mathematically.

**Context:** Philip K. Dick (1928–1982) was an American science-fiction writer whose paranoid, reality-bending novels and stories — including *Do Androids Dream of Electric Sheep?*, *The Man in the High Castle*, and "Minority Report" — became the basis for films like *Blade Runner* and *Total Recall*.
