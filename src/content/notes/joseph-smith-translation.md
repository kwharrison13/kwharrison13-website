# Joseph Smith Translation

The [Joseph Smith](/notes/joseph-smith) Translation (JST) is a revision of the Bible that Kyle's gospel study draws on to sharpen the meaning of disputed passages. In the October 19–25, 2020. 3 Nephi 27–4 Nephi: "There Could Not Be a Happier People" notes, the JST is used to expand [Matthew 7](/notes/matthew-7):21–23 — where Christ tells those who prophesied in his name "I never knew you" — into the sharper reading **"Ye never knew me"** (JST Matt. 7:33). The shift reframes the warning: the failure is not that Christ failed to recognize them, but that they never truly knew him, tying into the study's theme of being built upon Christ's gospel in deed rather than in name only.

**Context:** The Joseph Smith Translation is a revision of the King James Bible made by Joseph Smith, founder of the Latter-day Saint movement, between 1830 and 1833. Latter-day Saints regard it as inspired clarification and restoration of biblical text; selections appear in the LDS edition of the scriptures.
