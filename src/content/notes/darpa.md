# DARPA

The U.S. Defense Advanced Research Projects Agency recurs across Kyle's corpus as both a defense-tech node and a culture-building exemplar. In [Skunk Works](/books/skunk-works), DARPA appears as the agency that funded the early stealth-vessel work: it authorized a study contract for the Sea Shadow catamaran model under Dr. Perry's direction, putting up $100,000 while Lockheed kicked in another $150,000 — the example Kyle uses to flag the pattern of "DARPA does its job to investigate viability; Skunk Works believes in it enough to help fund its own R&D." The book also draws Kyle's note that "secrecy led to internal inefficiency."

In Kyle's essay research, DARPA is one of the ambitious, strong-culture organizations he studied. Toil We Must - Research lists it alongside [Bell Labs](/notes/bell-labs) (and Apple, Amazon, the 49ers) as an organization "known for having strong cultures," and the [Ambition](/notes/ambition) concept page cites DARPA and Bell Labs as the culture-builders the "more ambitious than we are" argument leans on. It also surfaces in sourcing notes: in the June 24th, 2021 — Eric Menees (TradeUp) call, founder Eric Menees described starting in research "working with DARPA" before seeing entrepreneurship as a bigger multiplier on his impact.

**Context:** DARPA is the U.S. Department of Defense agency founded in 1958 (after Sputnik) to fund high-risk, high-reward breakthrough research; it is credited with seeding ARPANET, GPS, and stealth technology, and is a frequent reference point in discussions of high-ambition R&D culture.
