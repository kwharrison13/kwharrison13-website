# George A. Smith

George A. Smith appears in Kyle's 1 Nephi 16–22 — I Will Prepare the Way before You study as the source who preserved one of [Joseph Smith](/notes/joseph-smith)'s most-quoted encouragements about endurance. Under the week's "Never give up [Hope](/notes/hope)" thread, Kyle records George A. Smith quoting Joseph: that even "sunk in the lowest pit of Nova Scotia and all the Rocky Mountains piled on top of me, I ought not to be discouraged but hang on, exercise faith, and keep up good courage" — and would "come out on the top of the heap at last." The quote anchors the study's reflection on resilience and persistence under prolonged hardship.

**Context:** George A. Smith (1817–1875) was an early apostle of The Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-day Saints, a cousin of Joseph Smith, and later a counselor in the First Presidency under [Brigham Young](/notes/brigham-young). He was a prolific preserver of Joseph Smith's sayings and teachings.
