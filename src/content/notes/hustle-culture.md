# Hustle Culture

Hustle culture is the valorization of relentless work and the grind — a recurring subject in two of Kyle's essay-research threads where he probes both its appeal and its failure modes. In The Renaissance of Rise and Grind - Research (research behind the essay *Working Hard, Or Hardly Working?*), the cultural arc Kyle traces is "Greed is good" → "Rise and grind" → "It's just a job," with hustle culture's subversion and an emerging "Hatred of [Hard Work](/notes/hard-work)" / "Anti-Hustle Culture" as the counter-movement. He's careful to flag the danger inside it — [Hustle Porn](/notes/hustle-porn) — noting it's the same danger as "endlessly optimizing a note-taking system without doing anything noteworthy," performing the aesthetics of work as a vanity metric standing in for real output.

Toil We Must - Research (behind the essay *Toil We Must — But for what purpose?*) pushes on the distinction that matters most to Kyle: hard work is not the same as toxic culture. "Toxic is screaming, personal attacks, threats over funerals/weddings/births — *not* 'this will be demanding.'" Hustle culture, in his framing, is one pole of a spectrum running opposite a hatred of hard work, with most people somewhere in the middle balancing satisfaction against effort. The tag cluster (`hard-work`, `ambition`, `company-building`, `language-of-discourse`) places it squarely inside Kyle's broader [Hard Work](/notes/hard-work) theme.

**Context:** "Hustle culture" is the popular, often-criticized celebration of overwork and the "rise and grind" ethos that spread through startup and entrepreneurial circles in the 2010s, frequently associated with figures like Gary Vaynerchuk. The backlash term "hustle porn" was popularized by Alexis Ohanian to name the performative, image-driven version of it.
