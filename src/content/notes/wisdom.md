# Wisdom

The endpoint of a ladder Kyle traces in Where Is Wisdom? — Russell M. Nelson: education is the pursuit of [Knowledge](/notes/knowledge); knowledge is the *prerequisite* to wisdom; and wisdom is to "hearken unto the counsels of God" ([2 Nephi 9](/notes/2-nephi-9):29) and seek to become like Him. [Nelson](/notes/russell-m-nelson) answers Job's question — "Where shall wisdom be found?" ([Job 28](/notes/job-28):12) — by insisting wisdom is not the same as accumulated learning: "when they are learned they think they are wise, and they hearken not unto the counsel of God" ([2 Nephi 9](/notes/2-nephi-9):28). Wisdom "emanates from the Lord"; it is gained by asking ("if any of you lack wisdom, let him ask of God," [James 1](/notes/james-1):5) and grows "line upon line" only as one is "prepared to receive" it.

Distinct from [Knowledge](/notes/knowledge) (information attested and held) and [Intelligence](/notes/intelligence) ("the glory of God," the everlasting light of truth), wisdom is the *applied, God-aligned* use of knowledge — knowing what to do with what you know, and submitting that judgment to revelation.
