# Hebbia

A [Neural Search](/notes/neural-search) startup founded by [George Sivulka](/notes/george-sivulka), which Kyle deep-dived in a November 2021 sourcing call. The product lets users upload large volumes of unstructured documents (SEC filings, data rooms, legal contracts, even recipes or medical textbooks) and ask open-ended questions like *"what are the risks to the business?"* — using semantic search to populate pre-existing fields and surface the relevant passages, rather than relying on keyword matching.

Per Sivulka, the differentiator is generality: his PhD work was aimed at making neural search *general* across use cases without per-domain training, so the same engine already serves finance analysts, legal review, and even medical study with no retraining. The core buyer is anyone synthesizing across many documents — Kyle's notes flag PE firms, debt providers, and insurers spending 3+ weeks in data rooms (one customer had 1M LP agreements to model waterfalls across). At the time, the team was working to make the product *less* general / more specific, and weighing whether to package it as an API in the mold of [Elastic](/notes/elastic). The call also touched on user-research tooling, comparing it to [Tegus](/notes/tegus) clones and [UserTesting](/notes/usertesting).

**Context:** Hebbia is a New York-based enterprise AI search company founded by George Sivulka (Stanford), known for its "Matrix" agentic document-analysis product widely adopted in finance and legal; it raised a large Series B led by Andreessen Horowitz in 2024.
