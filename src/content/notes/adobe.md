# Adobe

Adobe recurs across Kyle's diligence work in three roles. As a **customer/reference account**, its Head of Global Customer Support [Klasey Gage](/notes/klasey-gage) served as an expert reference in the Cresta.ai - Diligence — describing how Adobe shared chat and "best agent" data to benchmark the top quartile of support agents, the trust hurdle agents face with AI-suggested responses ("like Tesla's driverless car"), and the scale of Adobe's support (8M conversations/yr, 300M+ visits to HelpX, contacts down 20% via digital self-service). The diligence frames handling true enterprise customers like Adobe as "a big lift" for Cresta. As a **security-org reference**, [Prabhath Karanth](/notes/prabhath-karanth) spent nine years building Adobe's security efforts through its cloud transition before [TripActions](/notes/tripactions).

Adobe also appears as the **disrupted incumbent** in the design/collaboration thread. The Sprig fka UserLeap - Diligence notes the now-canonical pattern: [Figma](/notes/figma) "blew up Sketch, [Invision](/notes/invision), and eventually Adobe… by putting collaboration first but connecting it tightly to the creation workflow," and asks who will "do to Jira what Figma did to Adobe." Separately, an "Adobe product" is named among the e-signature competitors to [SignEasy](/notes/signeasy) (alongside [HelloSign](/notes/hellosign), versus incumbent [DocuSign ($DOCU)](/notes/docusign)) in the SignEasy call.

**Context:** Adobe Inc. ($ADBE) is a US software company best known for Creative Cloud (Photoshop, Illustrator, Acrobat/PDF), Document Cloud (including e-signature via Adobe Sign), and Experience Cloud marketing/analytics products.
