# Fundacion Paraguaya

Fundacion Paraguaya appears in [The New Urban Crisis](/books/the-new-urban-crisis) as an example of bottom-up, entrepreneurship-driven anti-poverty work. The book pairs it with [Poverty Stoplight](/notes/poverty-stoplight) (the self-assessment tool the organization is known for) while making the point that the poor often have creative business ideas but lack the financial resources to tweak and refine their plans, or the time to wait for markets to mature — a constraint that matters because entrepreneurship and self-employment are critical alternatives where conventional jobs are scarce and the poor face hiring discrimination. In Kyle's reading notes it is one of the book's threads on grassroots, agency-respecting approaches to poverty.

**Context:** Fundación Paraguaya is a Paraguay-based nonprofit founded in 1985 that pioneers microfinance, entrepreneurship education, and self-sustaining models for poverty elimination; it developed the Poverty Stoplight, a multidimensional self-diagnostic tool families use to map and address their own poverty.
