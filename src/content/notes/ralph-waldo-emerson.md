# Ralph Waldo Emerson

A recurring touchstone across Kyle's reading, surfacing in three distinct threads. As a **transcendentalist source**, he is paired with [Henry David Thoreau](/notes/henry-david-thoreau) in [Digital Minimalism](/books/digital-minimalism) (the writings [Cal Newport](/notes/cal-newport) first discovered on the banks of the Charles) and underwrites [Walden](/books/walden)'s self-reliance ethic — the conviction that "what a man thinks of himself… determines his fate." As an **apostle of self-reliance and nonconformity**, Emerson is the spine of Kyle's conviction-led contrarianism research: [Alex Karp](/notes/alex-karp) invokes *Self-Reliance* ("for nonconformity, the world whips you with its displeasure") and extends Emerson's "Why drag about this corpse of your memory… leave your theory" against the internet's permanent record that confines us in "a straitjacket of our former selves."

He also appears as a **prophet of reading and individualism**. [Gordon B. Hinckley — Go Forward With Faith](/books/gordon-b-hinckley-go-forward-with-faith) records the young Hinckley concurring with Emerson, who, asked which books most affected his life, said "he could no more remember the books he had read than the meals he had eaten, but that they had made him" — and quotes Emerson's "That which we persist in doing becomes easier to do… our power to do has increased" (a line that recurs in [How To Take Smart Notes](/books/how-to-take-smart-notes)). [American Zion](/books/american-zion) casts Emerson as the era's competing "prophet" of severance and individual liberty — "This is the age of severance, of dissociation, of freedom" — the cultural backdrop to Joseph Smith's revelations. Other quoted fragments: "Burn the libraries, for their value is in this book" ([The Greatest Minds and Ideas of All Time](/books/the-greatest-minds-and-ideas-of-all-time)), "I pay the schoolmaster, but 'tis the schoolboys that educate my son" ([The Parents' Guide to Boys](/books/the-parents-guide-to-boys)), and "Never read a book that is not a year old" ([David Perell](/notes/not-so-lazy-and-entitled-millennials-david-perell)).

**Context:** Ralph Waldo Emerson (1803–1882) was an American essayist, lecturer, and poet who led the New England Transcendentalist movement; his essays "Self-Reliance" and "Nature" and his mentorship of [Henry David Thoreau](/notes/henry-david-thoreau) made him a central figure in 19th-century American thought.
