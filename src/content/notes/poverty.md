# Poverty

A recurring economic and moral theme across Kyle's reading, approached from several angles. The most developed is [Matthew Desmond](/notes/matthew-desmond)'s [Evicted: Poverty and Profit in the American City](/books/evicted), which reframes [Eviction](/notes/eviction) as "a cause, not just a condition, of poverty" — losing a home actively manufactures poverty, costing renters their jobs, belongings, neighborhoods, and children's stability, with job loss and eviction each triggering the other. Desmond's central charge is that poverty research has fixated on what the poor *lack* while ignoring how much money is *extracted* from them ("there is a lot of money to be made off the poor. The 'hood is good"), and he rejects both the liberal "structural forces" and conservative "individual deficiency" framings as too narrow — calling instead for "a serious study of exploitation and extractive markets" ([Exploitation](/notes/exploitation), [Income Inequality](/notes/income-inequality)). [Patrick Sharkey](/notes/patrick-sharkey) extends this in [Stuck in Place](/notes/stuck-in-place), arguing neighborhood poverty is multigenerational, "passed down from parents to children in the same way that genetic background and financial wealth are transmitted across generations."

The theme also appears in Kyle's broader reading. [China in Ten Words](/books/china-in-ten-words) documents the "polarization of rich and poor" produced by China's growth model ("spend 100 yuan to gain 10 yuan in increased GDP"), and the 30M-to-100M poverty figures depending on where the income line is drawn. [Working Toward Zion](/books/working-toward-zion) frames poverty theologically — the "hyper poor," inner-city, reservation, and Third World deprivation set against Brigham Young's ideal of a community with "no rich and no poor" ([Zion](/notes/zion)). The Quick Capture links below (Gates Foundation goalkeepers, a U.S. anti-poverty pledge) are sources Kyle saved to explore the topic but has not yet synthesized.
