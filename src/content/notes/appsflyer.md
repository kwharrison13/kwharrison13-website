# Appsflyer

Mobile marketing-analytics platform that appears in Kyle's corpus as a **customer reference** in his Salt Security Diligence — 2021 dossier. [David Mark](/notes/david-mark), Appsflyer's DevOps Team Lead and Architect, was one of six expert/customer reference calls in the diligence (October 4, 2021). Appsflyer was a Salt Security design partner — approached by Salt rather than the other way around — and Mark was the primary evaluator and decision-maker for the deployment. He noted that, unlike his previous companies which "only had few APIs," Appsflyer "has hundreds of APIs because it's a very different use case," making it a high-surface-area test of an API-security product.

Mark's feedback through Appsflyer fed two recurring threads in Kyle's security-tooling notes: the [DevOps](/notes/devops)-buyer critique that Salt "shows you the data but doesn't solve the problem" and was "built for security teams more than DevOps," and the observation (catalogued on the [Cloudflare](/notes/cloudflare) page) that API-security vendors could integrate with edge players like Cloudflare for active remediation.

**Context:** AppsFlyer is an Israel-founded mobile attribution and marketing-analytics company that measures app installs, in-app events, and ad-campaign performance across channels.
