# Camden Harrison

Camden Harrison appears in Kyle's gospel study as a source of remembered insight, most often tied to missionary teaching and the limits of accountability. In the 3 Nephi 20–26 study, he is credited with the definition of [Graven Images](/notes/graven-images) that frames Kyle's note: a graven image is "anything you put before God," not just literal golden statues — the things "we build up unto ourselves" and end up worshipping.

In Kyle's notes on [Faith is Not Blind](/books/faith-is-not-blind) (the Hafens), Camden recurs around the idea that "we value what we discover more than we value what we are told." Kyle ties this to "Camden Harrison's experience teaching in the MTC and dealing with the level of accountability we have for what people ultimately decide to do" — the conviction that missionaries don't "bring people into the church" but *invite* others to come unto Christ, leaving the impetus on the learner ([Preach My Gospel](/notes/preach-my-gospel)). The connection lands against the book's argument that the Lord "really can't save us without our freely chosen initiative." The reference is private and idiosyncratic to Kyle's notes; the shared surname and the MTC teaching reference suggest someone close to Kyle, though the sources do not state the relationship.
