# Thomas McGannon

A conversation partner whose jam session with Kyle seeded two of the load-bearing ideas in the [Historical Futurism](/notes/historical-futurism) essay. From a recorded conversation in Historical Futurism - Research, McGannon is credited with the "50-year news" device that became the essay's opening: if instead of 24-hour news you had a publication that came out only every 50 years, you wouldn't fill the pages with gossip or tragedy — "you would see that the curve of history bends toward progress." The same conversation produced the framework that the inventions that stick are the ones that are both (1) incentivized and (2) ubiquitous — Kyle's two recurring concepts for why an invention persists, where Coca-Cola, Google, and Band-Aids endure through [Ubiquity](/notes/ubiquity) while Google Glass and VR remain hard to predict because they haven't achieved it.
