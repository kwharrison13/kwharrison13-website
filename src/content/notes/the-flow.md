# The Flow

"The Flow" appears in Kyle's notes in two distinct but resonant senses. In [How To Take Smart Notes](/books/how-to-take-smart-notes), it is [Mihaly Csikszentmihalyi](/notes/mihaly-csikszentmihalyi)'s **flow** — "the state in which being highly focused becomes effortless." Ahrens uses an exergonic/endergonic-reaction analogy: a good workflow, "once triggered, continues by itself and even releases energy," pulling the worker along instead of draining willpower. The book ties this to "let the work carry you" (since [Willpower is a Finite Resource](/notes/willpower-is-a-finite-resource)) and to [Deep Work](/books/deep-work) — structuring the workflow so momentum, not effort, sustains the work, creating a "virtuous circle" rather than a vicious one.

In Kyle's Missionary Work — Topical Study and Teaching Repentance, "The Flow" is a missionary concept (drawn from *The Book of Life*): "a power… that will carry you from one appointment to another… from one miracle to another." It is entered through **boldness**, defined as "a love filled risk taken out of eternal concern for someone else" — following promptings "not knowing beforehand the things which you should do" (1 Nephi 4:6). Boldness grants access to the Flow because the missionary begins to feel for strangers the love Christ has for them. The shared thread across both senses: a self-sustaining, effortless momentum that comes from getting out of one's own way — whether through workflow design or through love-driven risk.

**Context:** Flow as a psychological state was named and studied by Mihaly Csikszentmihalyi (described from the 1970s onward), denoting effortless, fully absorbed focus on a challenging-but-tractable task.

## Where this appears

- [How To Take Smart Notes](/books/how-to-take-smart-notes) — Csikszentmihalyi's flow as effortless focus; a good workflow gains its own momentum and "carries you," paired with [Deep Work](/books/deep-work).
- Missionary Work — Topical Study — "The Flow" as a power that carries a missionary "from one miracle to another," entered through love-filled boldness.
- Teaching Repentance — boldness as an expression of love that opens the Flow; each invitation as an act of love.
