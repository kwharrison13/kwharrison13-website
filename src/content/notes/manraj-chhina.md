# Manraj Chhina

Head of Customer Success EMEA & APAC at Unibuddy (with ~6 years in CS and prior roles at Hibob, Claimable, Klikin, and Helpling), and one of the expert callers in Kyle's Planhat diligence. At the time of the call he was actively evaluating a CS tool — running on a stitched-together Intercom + Salesforce stack and finding [Gainsight](/notes/gainsight) both too expensive and too implementation-heavy to justify for a small team.

Chhina was evaluating [Vitally](/notes/vitally), [Catalyst](/notes/catalyst), and [Planhat](/notes/planhat) as "the front-runners," which he saw as broadly similar in functionality. His specific data points anchor several of the diligence's conclusions: the per-tool pricing quotes (**Vitally $40K, Planhat $30K, Catalyst $20K**); the critique that Planhat's playbooks are "fairly basic" (basic triggers, no smart rules, "we can already hack this together with Intercom & Salesforce"); his initial lean toward Catalyst because their founder reached out personally on LinkedIn; and — most cited — his observation that **Planhat is "furthest along," supporting Series C–D scaleups** while Catalyst and Vitally references skewed Series A–B. That upmarket evidence became one of Kyle's bull-case bullets for Planhat.
