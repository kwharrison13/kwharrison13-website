# Isaiah 59

A chapter of Isaiah Kyle returns to for its account of how sin separates a person from God. The anchor verse is [Isaiah 59](/notes/isaiah-59):2 — "But your iniquities have separated between you and your God, and your sins have hid his face from you, that he will not hear." In Using Satan Against Satan this verse is paired with [D&C 64](/notes/dandc-64):2 to describe the felt experience of the spiritual standard: "when you slip below the standard you feel God's disappointment; at or above it you feel His approval." The same source reads 59:2 as a warning mechanism — "we are always warned before we do something, but the more we refuse to listen, the harder it will be to hear in the future" — making the verse a description of how disobedience progressively deadens spiritual hearing. It is cross-listed there with [D&C 64](/notes/dandc-64), [D&C 111](/notes/dandc-111), and [D&C 11](/notes/dandc-11), and connected to Godly Sorrow and The Reasons The Spirit Withdraws.

The verse appears again in the notes from General Conference — October 2021, cited in the same separated-from-God sense.

**Context:** Isaiah 59 is a chapter in which the prophet declares that it is not the LORD's arm that is too short to save, but the people's iniquities that have created a barrier between them and God; it moves from confession of sin to the promise of a Redeemer coming to Zion.

## Where this appears

- Using Satan Against Satan — 59:2 paired with [D&C 64](/notes/dandc-64):2 on feeling God's approval/disappointment, and as a warning that refusing to listen deadens future spiritual hearing.
- General Conference — October 2021 — quotes 59:2 on iniquities separating us from God and hiding His face.
