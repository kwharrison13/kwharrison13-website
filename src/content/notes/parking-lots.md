# Parking Lots

The other canonical under-utilized asset in Kyle's [Alternative Real Estate](/notes/alternative-real-estate) thesis — paired with [Malls](/notes/malls) as the built space worth reimagining rather than writing off. The framing is the same arbitrage logic crystallized in [The New Urban Crisis](/books/the-new-urban-crisis): under-used or distressed footprint becomes "a huge buying opportunity… if you have a formula / strategy for increasing the value of that land." Parking lots are the everyday example of land whose surface use vastly under-prices its potential.

Kyle's June 18, 2026 link capture tagged the BBC Future piece The Massive Farms Emerging Beneath Our Cities here — reading the repurposing of tunnels, basements, and dead retail/parking footprint into food production as a concrete reinvention of exactly this kind of under-used space.

**Context:** Surface parking lots consume a large share of land in many U.S. cities and are increasingly seen by urbanists and developers as prime infill-redevelopment opportunities — convertible to housing, mixed-use, solar, or other higher-value uses as car dependence and parking minimums are reconsidered.
