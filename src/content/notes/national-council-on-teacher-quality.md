# National Council on Teacher Quality

The National Council on Teacher Quality is referenced in [The Science of Learning](/notes/the-science-of-learning) in the context of research on teacher effectiveness and the gap between established learning research and teacher-education practice — the essay's argument that there are evidence-based methods that work for the large majority of students.

**Context:** The National Council on Teacher Quality (NCTQ) is a U.S. nonprofit research and advocacy organization, founded in 2000, focused on teacher preparation, quality, and education policy.

## Where this appears

- [The Science of Learning](/notes/the-science-of-learning) — surfaces alongside the claim that decades of research establish how teachers can boost student learning, even as many teacher educators reject broadly effective methods.
