# Lloyd's

The London insurance market. Per the [Berkshire Hathaway Annual Letters](/books/berkshire-hathaway-annual-letters), it traces to ~1688, when Edward Lloyd opened a small London coffee house whose clientele — shipowners, merchants, and venturesome capitalists — began writing contracts transferring the risk of disaster at sea. Those capitalists became known as "underwriters at Lloyd's." Buffett cites it as a 322-year-old institution; [Warren Buffett](/notes/warren-buffett) also recounts a turnaround in which huge liabilities were taken over from Lloyd's to clean up its relationship with 27,972 "names" who had written problem-ridden policies.

A standing example of [Clark's Coffeehouse](/notes/clark-s-coffeehouse) — an institution that grew, almost spontaneously, out of a community gathered in a coffeehouse.
