# Alma 26

Book of Mormon chapter Kyle reads as [Ammon](/notes/ammon)'s rejoicing after the missions to the Lamanites — and his most-cited proof-text for humility. The key verse across his notes is 26:12: "I know that I am nothing; as to my strength I am weak; therefore I will not boast of myself, but I will boast of my God." In his June 29–July 5, 2020. Alma 23–29: "They Never Did Fall Away" study he frames the whole chapter as "Boast in my God" — Ammon refusing to glory in his own strength because "in his strength I can do all things," acknowledging God's hand because it is His work ([Jeffrey R. Holland](/notes/jeffrey-r-holland)).

That same verse anchors his Humility — Topical Study (Ammon listed alongside Nephi and Alma as spiritual giants who were great *because* they recognized their inadequacy) and his Revelation — Topical Study (paired with Nephi's "O wretched man that I am"). A second verse, 26:22, recurs in his Patience — Topical Study ("you have failed to make yourself worthy") and his Prayer — Topical Study (the Spirit reveals "the mysteries of God" to those who qualify).

**Context:** Alma chapter 26 is in the Book of Mormon (Latter-day Saint scripture). It records Ammon's hymn of rejoicing and thanksgiving over the success of the sons of Mosiah's mission to the Lamanites.
