# Luke 18

Luke 18 anchors Kyle's study of Brigham Young on temporal wealth through the account of the rich young ruler. Kyle quotes Luke 18:23 — "And when he heard this, he was very sorrowful: for he was very rich." The study uses [Brigham Young](/notes/brigham-young)'s reading of the episode to push back on a common assumption: Young argued that Jesus did *not* in fact require the young man to give away everything, but only to "distribute to the poor," because "if the poor had all the surplus property of the rich many of them would waste it… and destroy themselves in using it." The chapter thus becomes the scriptural seat for a nuanced doctrine of stewardship over wealth rather than wholesale renunciation.

**Context:** Luke 18 is a New Testament chapter of the Gospel of Luke that includes the parables of the persistent widow and the Pharisee and the publican, and the account of the rich young ruler whom Jesus invites to sell all he has and follow him.
