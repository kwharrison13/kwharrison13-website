# One-Person Billion Dollar Company

**One-line definition:** *The post-ChatGPT prophecy — popularized by [Sam Altman](/notes/sam-altman) — that AI tools let a single person (or two) build a billion-dollar company; [MEDVI](/notes/medvi) was hailed as its first fulfillment.*

## How sources describe it

- The framing everyone "talking about one-person billion-dollar companies since ChatGPT launched" saw fulfilled in the [MEDVI](/notes/medvi) headline — "their promised messiah." (source: The $1B Rorschach Test - Research)
- Kyle's pushback: *a billion dollars of what?* Revenue, not value. With 3–5 margin "takers" (rented infrastructure, ungodly [CAC](/notes/customer-acquisition-cost), churn, lawsuit costs) the headline number is a vanity metric, not an anchor for value — "lucky if this thing COULD trade at 1x revenue." See [Durable](/notes/durable). (source: The $1B Rorschach Test - Research)
- "Solo" doesn't mean what people think in 2026 — the "one person" runs on rented [Telehealth](/notes/telehealth) infrastructure ([OpenLoop Health](/notes/openloop-health), [CareValidate](/notes/carevalidate)) and a 100%-human, 100%-regulated supply chain. (source: The $1B Rorschach Test - Research)
