# Alma 14

A Book of Mormon chapter — the violent rejection of Alma and Amulek at Ammonihah — that recurs across [Kyle Harrison](/notes/kyle-harrison)'s gospel study as his central case study in **faith that submits to God's will**. In his Faith — Topical Study, Kyle draws the pivotal distinction that faith becomes power only when you know God's will and *then* follow it: Alma and Amulek accepted that the Lord's will might be to burn or to save (14:12–13, "Be it according to the will of the Lord. But behold, our work is not finished; therefore they burn us not"), and Alma's restraint — "the Spirit constraineth me that I must not stretch forth my hand" (14:11) — becomes Kyle's model of acting only on revealed will. He pairs it with the prayer "O Lord, give us strength according to our faith" (14:26). The same restraint verse (14:11) recurs in The Lehonti Principle and in his Alma 17–22 study.

The chapter is studied verse-by-verse in Kyle's Alma 13–16 — Enter into the Rest of the Lord Come Follow Me notes, which trace the people's anger "because of the plainness of [Alma's] words unto [Zeezrom](/notes/zeezrom)" (14:2), the burning of the believers, the taunt "if ye have such great power why do ye not deliver yourselves?" (14:20), and the eventual deliverance.

**Context:** Alma 14 recounts the aftermath of Alma and Amulek's preaching in the wicked city of Ammonihah — the people imprison the two missionaries and burn the believing women and children, with Alma constrained by the Spirit not to intervene; the prison later collapses and the two are delivered while their persecutors are slain.

## Where this appears

- Faith — Topical Study — 14:11–13, 26 as the model of faith that submits to God's will and acts only on revealed will.
- Alma 13–16 — Enter into the Rest of the Lord — verse-by-verse study of Ammonihah's rejection, the burning of believers, and the deliverance.
- Alma 17–22 — I Will Make an Instrument of Thee — 14:11 cross-referenced (the Spirit constraining Alma).
- The Lehonti Principle — 14:11 cited on restraint and following the Spirit.
