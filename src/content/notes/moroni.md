# Moroni

Moroni appears in Kyle's gospel-study notes in two roles. As the **final Book of Mormon prophet**, he closes the record with the eight exhortations of Moroni 10 — strong invitations to *remember* the Lord's mercy, *ponder* God's goodness, and *ask* God whether the things are true with "a sincere heart, with real intent, having faith in Christ" (Moroni's Promise, Moroni 10:3–4). Kyle treats this as a pattern: a testimony "rarely arrives as a light-switch experience" ([David A. Bednar](/notes/david-a-bednar)) but grows line upon line, and Moroni's repeated "remember" is the through-line ([Boyd K. Packer](/notes/boyd-k-packer) called *remember* the most important word in English). The same passage anchors his 5 P's Never To Forget study, where "Ponder" is justified by the fact that "[Moroni](/notes/moroni) himself suggested you do this" — the difference between reading and pondering being "like night and day."

As an **angelic messenger**, Moroni is the figure who appeared to [Joseph Smith](/notes/joseph-smith) in 1823 to reveal the golden plates. In Joseph Smith — A Young Seer, Kyle records the repeated visitations across 1823–1826: Joseph first only learns where the plates are, fails when Satan tempts him toward financial gain (Lucy Mack Smith's "first failure"), and is chastened — "Moroni is NOT going to put the plates in Joseph's unprepared hands." Joseph Smith Lecture 1 — The First Vision and Its Aftermath notes that the 1823 Moroni encounters, like the First Vision, left Joseph physically weak. The angel and the ancient prophet are the same person — a figure Kyle frames as both the writer who invites every reader to come unto Christ and be perfected in Him, and the guardian who prepared Joseph to receive the record.

**Context:** In Latter-day Saint scripture, Moroni was the last Nephite prophet, who finished and buried the gold plates of the Book of Mormon around AD 421 and, as a resurrected angel, delivered them to Joseph Smith beginning in 1823. The angel Moroni is the figure depicted atop many LDS temples.
