# Extending Commitments

A missionary-work principle worked through in Kyle's Teaching Repentance gospel study (drawn from his mission Topical Journal). In the framing there, "commitment is an ESSENTIAL part of repentance" — "the act of obligating oneself to a course of action and then diligently following through" ([PMG](/notes/preach-my-gospel) pg. 195–196). Rather than "drag" or "push" people, missionaries are to *invite*: "Rarely, if ever, should you talk to people or teach them without extending an invitation to do something that will strengthen their faith in Christ." The concrete commitments include invitations to be baptized, to attend church, to pray, and to read the Book of Mormon, plus the discipline of asking for referrals at every opportunity.

The study ties extending commitments to [Agency](/notes/agency) and accountability: by inviting rather than coercing, missionaries let God's children exercise their agency and so be held accountable — which the notes call "the key to Heavenly Father's plan." The power to extend commitments is described as coming "from testimony, from personal experience" — the principle "teach how you learned; testify how you lived."

## Where this appears

- Teaching Repentance — a core theme of the study; framed as inviting (not dragging) people to commit, with referrals, baptism, church, prayer, and scripture reading as the specific commitments, all tied to agency and accountability.
