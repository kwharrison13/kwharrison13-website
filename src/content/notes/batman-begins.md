# Batman Begins

In Kyle's notes, *Batman Begins* (2005) is the gateway film to his interest in directors as authors of their films. It's the [Christopher Nolan](/notes/christopher-nolan) picture that felt "different... unique... almost intimidating" to Kyle, and reading up on Nolan afterward is where he first encountered the word "[auteur](/notes/auteur-theory)." It functions less as a film entry in its own right than as the origin point of a personal thread about directorial authorship — Nolan sits alongside the Coen Brothers, [Quentin Tarantino](/notes/quentin-tarantino), [Martin Scorsese](/notes/martin-scorsese), and Rian Johnson among the directors Kyle "voraciously consumed" growing up.

**Context:** *Batman Begins* is a 2005 superhero film directed by Christopher Nolan, the first installment of his Dark Knight trilogy, widely credited with rebooting the Batman franchise with a more grounded, psychologically realistic tone.
