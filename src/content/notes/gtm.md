# GTM

GTM (go-to-market) is the strategy and motion by which a company reaches and sells to customers. In Kyle's corpus it shows up as a central lens of [Product Led Growth — The End User Era](/books/product-led-growth-the-end-user-era), which argues that [Product Led Growth (PLG)](/notes/product-led-growth-plg) is "more than just a cost effective GTM motion for startups" — it's a strategy aligned with how consumers want to buy software today, giving functional experts the power to choose best-of-breed tools without layers of approval. The book also ties GTM strategy to org structure: the engineer-to-PM ratio differed sharply between a top-down enterprise GTM (20:1 at CA) and a bottoms-up product-led one (6:1 at SendGrid), producing two very different product-development experiences.

The concept recurs in company diligence as a comparable: [Calendly](/notes/calendly) (alongside [Loom](/notes/loom)) is invoked by [Sunil Patro](/notes/sunil-patro) when benchmarking [SignEasy](/notes/signeasy)'s GTM — products that attract many decision-makers by first serving individual productivity, the classic PLG go-to-market shape.

**Context:** "Go-to-market" refers to a company's plan for delivering a product to end customers — encompassing sales motion, marketing, pricing, distribution channels, and the sequence in which it acquires and expands accounts.
