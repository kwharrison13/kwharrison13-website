# Videos

In [The Expertise Economy](/books/the-expertise-economy), video appears as one of the everyday, informal channels through which modern [Learning](/notes/learning) actually happens — listed alongside articles, books, and podcasts as the kind of content people consume constantly without categorizing it as "learning." The book frames a culture of [Continuous Learning](/notes/continuous-learning) as one where employees can pick up skills "in their own time and their own way" through all of these formats, formal and informal. Video clips also show up in the book's anxious counter-thesis about [Content Overload](/notes/content-overload): the "never-ending stream of websites, apps, and video clips" is part of what overwhelms workers and leaves only a fraction of the week for deliberate learning.

## Where this appears

- [The Expertise Economy](/books/the-expertise-economy) — video listed as an informal continuous-learning channel (alongside articles, books, podcasts), and as one source of the modern "content overload" that crowds out focused learning
