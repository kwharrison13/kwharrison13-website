# Marketing

In Kyle's sources, marketing is consistently framed as **story and resonance rather than ad spend** — the thing that makes an invention iconic, and increasingly something the customer does for the brand rather than something the brand buys. [The Greatest Thing Since Sliced Bread](/notes/the-greatest-thing-since-sliced-bread) (an early [David Perell](/notes/david-perell) piece) is the thesis statement: sliced bread became iconic not because it was a great invention but because of great marketing, and "we buy the story as much as the product." [The Everything Store](/books/the-everything-store) sharpens the point into Kyle's most-tagged marketing idea — **pricing *is* a marketing strategy**: Bezos's contrarian low-price stance positioned the brand, and Walmart's Scott put it flatly, "our marketing strategy is our pricing strategy," pouring ad dollars into price cuts rather than advertising. That overlap is captured in [Pricing](/notes/pricing), the concept Kyle notes pricing "largely *is*."

A second cluster of [Perell](/notes/david-perell)-adjacent long-reads tracks marketing's shift toward **transparency and the customer-as-marketer**. [Naked Brands](/notes/naked-brands) argues mass-marketing trust is no longer sustainable and [Influencers](/notes/influencers) win because they read as genuine; [America Runs On Instagram](/notes/america-runs-on-instagram) shows restaurants designed as Instagram bait, turning customers into the distribution channel; and [Wearable Brands](/notes/wearable-brands) frames branding as identity and self-expression. The darker counterweight is [Brave New World Revisited](/books/brave-new-world-revisited), where [Aldous Huxley](/notes/aldous-huxley)'s dissection of commercial propaganda and "motivational research" (cosmetics sell hope, not lanolin) is the cautionary version of the same persuasion machinery.
