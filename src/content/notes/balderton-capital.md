# Balderton Capital

Balderton Capital is a European venture firm that appears in Kyle's notes through two threads. [Francesco Corea](/notes/francesco-corea) built its in-house research function — a data tech stack for the investment team plus a two-year analyst program modeled on investment banking, where analysts run 4–8 week deep dives so any investor can "jump on a call with a founder not knowing anything about the space but with 30 minutes of prep" (April 5th, 2022 — Francesco Corea (Balderton Capital)). Corea contrasted this with [Contrary Research](/essays/contrary-research) and detailed Balderton's "T-Approach" coverage model and the hard problem of taxonomy. Separately, Balderton surfaces as a competing investor: an [Index Ventures](/notes/index-ventures) pre-brief on [Sylvera](/notes/sylvera) noted that Balderton (alongside [Coatue](/notes/coatue)) had made pre-emptive Series A offers, prompting Index to move fast (November 5th, 2021 — Sylvera Pre-Brief (Julia Andre)).

**Context:** Balderton Capital is a London-based venture firm founded in 2000 (originally as Benchmark Capital Europe before splitting off independently). It is one of Europe's largest early-stage VCs, investing primarily at Series A across European technology startups.
