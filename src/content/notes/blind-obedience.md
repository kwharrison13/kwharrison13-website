# Blind Obedience

In Kyle's Come Follow Me study of Helaman 13–16, "blind obedience" is invoked as the thing true gospel obedience is *not*. The page anchors the distinction to [Boyd K. Packer](/notes/boyd-k-packer)'s formulation: "We are not obedient because we are blind, we are obedient because we can see." Set within a study of Samuel the Lamanite's call to repentance, the concept ties obedience to [Agency](/notes/agency), knowledge, and accountability rather than to unthinking compliance — obedience flows from understanding and choice, not from being unable to see.
