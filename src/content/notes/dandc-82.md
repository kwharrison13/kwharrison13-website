# D&C 82

Section 82 of the [Doctrine and Covenants](/notes/doctrine-and-covenants) appears in Kyle's The Process of the Book of Mormon notes, where verse 10 — "I, the Lord, am bound when ye do what I say" — is cited as the scriptural statement of the covenant chain that runs through the Book of Mormon: keep the commandments and prosper, keep them not and be cut off. The notes connect this to the definition of a covenant in [Gospel Principles](/notes/gospel-principles) as "a sacred, terms-set agreement between God and His people," and treat the Lord's being *bound by obedience* as the engine of the book's recurring promise-and-consequence pattern.

**Context:** Doctrine and Covenants Section 82 is a revelation given to Joseph Smith in April 1832 in Jackson County, Missouri. Verse 10 ("I, the Lord, am bound when ye do what I say; but when ye do not what I say, ye have no promise") is among the most-cited statements in LDS scripture on the conditional nature of divine blessings.

## Where this appears

- The Process of the Book of Mormon — verse 10 cited for the covenant chain: the Lord bound by obedience, blessings conditioned on keeping the commandments.
