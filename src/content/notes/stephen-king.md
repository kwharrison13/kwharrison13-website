# Stephen King

Stephen King appears in Kyle's Historical Futurism - Research as the author of "The Jaunt," listed in his short-story reading set on speculative and cautionary fiction — alongside "The Machine Stops" ([E.M. Forster](/notes/e-m-forster)), "The Last Question" ([Isaac Asimov](/notes/isaac-asimov)), "Story of Your Life" ([Ted Chiang](/notes/ted-chiang)), "Second Variety" ([Philip K. Dick](/notes/philip-k-dick)), and "The Lottery" ([Shirley Jackson](/notes/shirley-jackson)). The grouping treats these stories as source material for imagining the future and the ways such visions turn dark.

**Context:** Stephen King is a prolific American author of horror, supernatural, and speculative fiction, known for novels including *The Shining*, *It*, and *The Stand*; "The Jaunt" is a 1981 science-fiction short story about teleportation.
