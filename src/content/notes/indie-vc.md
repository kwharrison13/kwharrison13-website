# Indie.vc

Indie.vc is the profitable-founder-controlled fund and model associated with [Bryce Roberts](/notes/bryce-roberts), which anchors the alternative path argued for in [We're Selling Entrepreneurship Short](/notes/we-re-selling-entrepreneurship-short). Against the venture orthodoxy the article critiques — where ~15 companies a year (≈0.004%) drive 95% of the industry's value and large funds need $50B+ public outcomes to clear their math — Indie.vc represents a deliberate counter-model: backing founders who stay profitable and keep control of their destiny rather than chasing outlier exits. The piece notes that "indie" companies that stayed profitable retained real optionality — "repurchasing equity, merging with VC-backed companies, getting acquired, or raising growth capital from traditional VCs" — and are insulated from the market's fickleness ("one day everyone is chasing crypto then they're hunting AI the next").

**Context:** Indie.vc was an investment program founded by Bryce Roberts (also of O'Reilly AlphaTech Ventures) that pioneered revenue-based / founder-friendly financing for companies building durable, profitable businesses rather than pursuing the blitzscaling venture path. The program wound down in 2021.
