# Luxury Beliefs

"Luxury beliefs" is the concept most associated with [Rob Henderson](/notes/rob-henderson) — ideas and opinions that confer status on the upper class while imposing costs on the lower class. In the wiki it attaches to Henderson through his appearance in Kyle's decline-vs-vitality research (The Hardening Of The Great Softening - Research), the same is-America-declining cluster that anchors [American Exceptionalism on Trial](/notes/american-exceptionalism-on-trial) (the January 6th demographic analysis, postwar data on uneducated whites, the hollowed-out middle class) set against America's entrepreneurialism and immigrant aspiration. The term functions as Henderson's signature contribution and the natural hub for that strand of his thought.

**Context:** Rob Henderson, a writer and psychologist, coined "luxury beliefs" to describe status-signaling ideas (e.g. about family structure, drugs, or policing) that are cheap for the affluent to hold but carry real costs for those lower down the social ladder — an analogue, in the realm of ideas, to luxury goods that once signaled class.
