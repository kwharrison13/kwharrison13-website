# 1914 Christmas Truce

A historical episode Kyle tags in [Charlie Munger — The Psychology of Human Misjudgment](/notes/charlie-munger-the-psychology-of-human-misjudgment) under the theme of "acknowledging humanity" — the idea that everyone remains a person even when you are shooting at them, a counterweight to the dehumanizing tendencies Munger catalogs.

**Context:** The Christmas Truce was a series of unofficial ceasefires along the Western Front around Christmas 1914 during World War I, when German and British soldiers crossed no-man's-land to exchange greetings, bury the dead, and in some places play football.

## Where this appears

- [Charlie Munger — The Psychology of Human Misjudgment](/notes/charlie-munger-the-psychology-of-human-misjudgment) — Kyle's annotation on recognizing the humanity of an adversary, against Munger's tendencies toward dehumanization.
