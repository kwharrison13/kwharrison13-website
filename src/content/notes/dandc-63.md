# D&C 63

Section 63 of the Doctrine and Covenants appears in Kyle's gospel study through two distinct verses. **D&C 63:9** is cited in the Helaman 7–12 study on [Sign Seeking](/notes/sign-seeking): "Faith cometh not by signs, but signs follow those that believe." The note frames sign-seeking as a mark of weak faith — the faithful receive signs, but signs do not produce faith.

**D&C 63:16** carries the section's other recurring use. In The Whole Armor of God, the verse identifies "the sword is the word of God," developing a metaphor where losing the Spirit through adultery (or pornography) means "you've just thrown away your sword" — losing all offense and defense and becoming spiritually useless. The same verse appears in Christian Reformation, Zion, and Seekers paired with D&C 84: "If people deny the faith, they are wrapped in sin. He who commits adultery WILL NOT HAVE THE SPIRIT." Across these sources, Section 63 functions as a proof text on faith-versus-signs and on the word of God as spiritual armor.

**Context:** Doctrine and Covenants Section 63 is a revelation given to Joseph Smith in August 1831 at Kirtland, Ohio, addressing the gathering to Zion in Missouri and warning against sign-seeking and unchastity.

## Where this appears

- Helaman 7–12 — "Remember the Lord" — cites 63:9 on faith not coming by signs ([Sign Seeking](/notes/sign-seeking))
- The Whole Armor of God — uses 63:16 ("the word of God") as the sword in the armor metaphor
- Christian Reformation, Zion, and Seekers — pairs 63:16 with D&C 84 on losing the Spirit through adultery
