# 4 Nephi 1

The single chapter of Fourth Nephi, which Kyle's gospel study treats as the [Book of Mormon](/notes/book-of-mormon)'s portrait of [Zion](/notes/zion) and its loss. His October 19–25, 2020. 3 Nephi 27–4 Nephi: "There Could Not Be a Happier People" Come Follow Me study reads the chapter closely: after Christ's visit, two nations known for dissension become "one, the children of Christ" ([4 Nephi 1](/notes/4-nephi-1):17) with "all things common among them" so "there were not rich and poor, bond and free" ([4 Nephi 1](/notes/4-nephi-1):3), the love of God dwelling in their hearts such that "there could not be a happier people among all the people who had been created by the hand of God" ([4 Nephi 1](/notes/4-nephi-1):15–16). Kyle is struck by how a peace that lasted nearly 300 years sprang from only two or three days of the Savior's presence — and equally by how quickly [The Pride Cycle](/notes/the-pride-cycle) reclaims them, with pride and "costly apparel" creeping back in the two-hundred-and-first year ([4 Nephi 1](/notes/4-nephi-1):24–25), a meditation on how the devil "eases" people carefully down to hell over time.

The chapter also surfaces briefly in Spiritual Gifts ([4 Nephi 1](/notes/4-nephi-1):4, on "partakers… of the heavenly gift") and in his notes on General Conference — October 2021.

**Context:** 4 Nephi is the shortest book in the Book of Mormon, a single chapter compressing nearly four centuries — the Zion society of peace and equality after Christ's New World ministry, followed by its gradual collapse into pride and division.
