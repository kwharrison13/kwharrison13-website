# John Proctor

John Proctor appears in Kyle's [gospel-study](/notes/gospel-study) of Helaman 1–6 as a literary cross-reference on the theme of [Names](/notes/names). Studying Helaman 5:6 — Helaman charging his sons to remember the names of their first parents so that remembering the names means remembering their works — Kyle pairs the verse with Proctor's anguished cry from [The Crucible](/notes/the-crucible): "Because it is my name! Because I cannot have another in my life! ... How may I live without my name? I have given you my soul; leave me my name!" The juxtaposition draws out the weight a name carries as an inheritance of identity and integrity.

**Context:** John Proctor is the central character of Arthur Miller's 1953 play *The Crucible*, a dramatization of the Salem witch trials. The quoted lines are his refusal, at the cost of his life, to sign a false confession that would destroy his name — Miller's allegory for integrity under the pressure of McCarthy-era accusation.
