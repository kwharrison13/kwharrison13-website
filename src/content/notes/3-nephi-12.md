# 3 Nephi 12

A Book of Mormon chapter — the resurrected Christ's "Sermon at the Temple" to the Nephites, paralleling the Sermon on the Mount ([Matthew 5](/notes/matthew-5)) — that recurs across [Kyle Harrison](/notes/kyle-harrison)'s gospel study through **verse 6**: "blessed are all they who do hunger and thirst after righteousness, for they shall be filled **with the Holy Ghost**." Kyle repeatedly draws the distinction that this beatitude blesses not the already-righteous but those who *desire* righteousness, and notes the one way the Nephite version differs from [Matthew 5](/notes/matthew-5):6 — it names what fills us, "even the Holy Ghost." This anchors his Diligence study (true diligence is the "all-encompassing hunger" of those who diligently seek God), his Mighty Repentance study (Enos models repentance as expanding desire; the beatitude blesses those who hunger), and his Revelation — Topical Study (the same hunger that, once satisfied into complacency, cuts off the flow of living water).

The chapter also supplies other touchpoints: **12:48** ("be perfect even as I, or your Father who is in heaven is perfect") in his October 19–25, 2020 Come Follow Me study; **12:16** ("let your light shine") in Revelation — Topical Study; and **12:9** (peacemakers) in Spiritual Gifts.

**Context:** 3 Nephi 12 records the risen Jesus Christ delivering to the Nephites a sermon closely paralleling the Sermon on the Mount — the Beatitudes, the call to be the salt of the earth and light of the world, the fulfilling (not destroying) of the law, and the command to be perfect — with distinctive wording, including the promise that those who hunger and thirst after righteousness shall be filled with the Holy Ghost.

## Where this appears

- Diligence — 12:6 on diligence as the hunger and thirst after righteousness that yields the Holy Ghost.
- Mighty Repentance — 12:6 on the beatitude blessing those who *desire* righteousness, not the already-righteous.
- Revelation — Topical Study — 12:6 (complacency cutting off living water) and 12:16 ("let your light shine").
- 3 Nephi 27–4 Nephi — There Could Not Be a Happier People — 12:48 on being perfect as the Father is perfect.
- Spiritual Gifts — 12:9, peacemakers, as a personal calling.
