# TM Forum Smart City Maturity Model

The TM Forum Smart City Maturity Model is one of the two named frameworks [Michael Mulquin](/notes/michael-mulquin) — a smart-city standards expert active across ISO, IEC, ITU, and the TM Forum — points to as key reference points for how cities should approach smart-city development. As Mulquin describes it, the model defines four maturity levels organized around: leadership & governance; citizen & partnerships; effective use of data; and IT infrastructure. He cites it alongside the "City as a Platform" manifesto as the frameworks that matter, within his broader argument that the market needs to move away from highly tailored per-city solutions toward massively customizable, standards-conformant ones.

**Context:** TM Forum is a global industry association for digital service providers; its Smart City Maturity & Benchmark Model is a published assessment framework that scores municipalities across governance, data, technology, and partnership dimensions to gauge readiness and progress.
