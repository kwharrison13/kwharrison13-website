# Tiago Forte

Tiago Forte is a recurring figure in Kyle's tools-for-thought and knowledge-management notes, appearing as one pole of the central debate about how to organize a personal knowledge base. In [The Notetaking Cold War](/notes/the-notetaking-cold-war), Forte is set against [Conor White-Sullivan](/notes/conor-white-sullivan) of [Roam Research](/notes/roam-research): Forte thinks notes should be organized by *actionability*, with each note living "in one and only one place" across his **PARA** categories — Projects, Areas, Resources, Archives — the "predictable structure" pole, versus Conor's "infinite flexibility." The piece reframes their disagreement as a two-thousand-year-old philosophy debate between essentialists and pragmatists about how the world itself is organized. Forte also surfaces in Kyle's biography of the scene: per Networked Conviction — Roam + Investing - Research, Kyle met Conor White-Sullivan "at a Tiago Forte meetup in SF in 2019."

Beyond note-taking, Forte recurs as a source of frames Kyle has adopted. In [When Java Meets Jive](/notes/when-java-meets-jive) he supplies the "perspective era" thesis — a shift from scarcity to abundance where point-of-view becomes "perspectives-as-a-service," the new scarce resource. [Working in Public](/books/working-in-public) quotes him on the same idea from the writer's side: moving from public to paywalled content trades reach for reader quality because "what people are paying for is not a bunch of text… [it's] the perspective the writer brings." And in [Just Flow With It](/notes/just-flow-with-it), Forte is cited calling flow "the holy grail of productivity." He is also listed as the person who recommended [How To Take Smart Notes](/books/how-to-take-smart-notes) to Kyle.

**Context:** Tiago Forte is the founder of Forte Labs, creator of the PARA method and the "Building a Second Brain" methodology and course, and author of the 2022 book *Building a Second Brain*. He is one of the most prominent figures in the modern "tools for thought" / personal knowledge management movement.
