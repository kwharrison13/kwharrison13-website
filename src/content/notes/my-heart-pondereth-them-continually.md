# My Heart Pondereth Them Continually

A pondering theme Kyle carries through his [gospel-study](/notes/gospel-study) notes, surfaced in his study of [Helaman 7](/notes/helaman-7)–12 ("Remember the Lord"). The grounded idea there is that pondering invites revelation: Nephi is blessed for things he had done while "pondering upon the things which the Lord had shown unto him" ([Helaman 10](/notes/helaman-10):2-4), and Kyle pairs this with [Henry B. Eyring](/notes/henry-b-eyring)'s teaching that "when we ponder, we invite revelation by the Spirit." The same note connects it to [Sometimes doing nothing is the most important something](/notes/sometimes-doing-nothing-is-the-most-important-something) — pondering as deliberate, unhurried reflection rather than idleness.

**Context:** The phrase echoes 2 Nephi 4 (the "Psalm of Nephi"), where Nephi declares "my heart pondereth continually upon the things which I have seen and heard" — a Book of Mormon passage on dwelling on spiritual things even amid personal struggle.
