# Zenefits

Zenefits surfaces across [Kyle Harrison](/notes/kyle-harrison)'s 2021 [Pave](/notes/pave) (fka Trove) compensation-platform diligence as a small-and-medium-business HRIS that doubles as an incumbent reference point in the comp-tooling landscape. In the Pave Diligence — 2021 dossier it appears in two roles: (1) as a legacy/adjacent compensation offering — Zenefits launched a Compensation Management module in 2018 priced at $14/user/month, built on "anonymized salary data from the hundreds of thousands of U.S. small and medium-sized businesses managed on the Zenefits platform," explicitly positioned for organizations under 500 employees, but judged in the notes as "really focus[ing] on benchmarking without much of a robust compensation planning tool across departments, budgets, etc."; and (2) as the system-of-record that integrates with Pave at customer accounts (e.g. [Ro](/notes/ro) uses [Carta](/notes/carta) for equity and Zenefits as its HRIS, both of which feed into Pave).

In the customer-reference calls, Zenefits shows up as the broad HRIS layer companies build on. [Mark Seidel](/notes/mark-seidel) of Relativity Space described Zenefits as "their information system to collect employee data" — title and the rest — and ranked it among the non-negotiables: "You NEED Zenefits, Workday, or Carta. Everything is a nice to have." [Abhishek Mahajan](/notes/abhishek-mahajan) of Flipkart listed Zenefits among the vendors evaluated before selecting Pave, noting the "product was good, but that wasn't the only factor" and that it "wasn't a terrible UI." Across the diligence, Zenefits reads as an established but benchmarking-light incumbent that Pave neither directly replaces nor competes head-on with — more a data source/HRIS that Pave's comp-planning tooling sits alongside.

**Context:** Zenefits is a cloud HR software company (later rebranded TriNet Zenefits after TriNet's 2022 acquisition) founded in 2013 by Parker Conrad, offering benefits administration, payroll, and HRIS tools aimed at small and mid-sized businesses. It was an early high-flying SaaS startup that faced a well-publicized compliance scandal and CEO turnover (Conrad departing in 2016, succeeded by Jay Fulcher, who is quoted in the diligence notes).
