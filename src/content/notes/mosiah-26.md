# Mosiah 26

A Book of Mormon chapter Kyle cites for the episode in which [Alma the Elder](/notes/alma-the-elder) receives counsel directly from God about how to handle transgressors within his newly gathered church. In both his Come Follow Me study of Alma 13–16 and of Alma 23–29, he points to Mosiah 26:13–32 as the passage where Alma, uncertain what to do with members who had sinned, takes the matter to the Lord and is given the pattern for judgment, repentance, and forgiveness within the church. It serves in Kyle's notes as the founding precedent for church discipline and the question of who judges whom.

## Where this appears

- Come Follow Me — Alma 13–16 — Mosiah 26:13–32 cited for Alma the Elder receiving God's counsel on handling his church.
- Come Follow Me — Alma 23–29 — same passage referenced on Alma the Elder being counseled by God about his church.
