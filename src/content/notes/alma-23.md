# Alma 23

The chapter where the Lamanites converted through [Ammon](/notes/ammon) and his brethren "never did fall away." In June 29–July 5, 2020. Alma 23–29: "They Never Did Fall Away", Kyle anchors this study on Alma 23:6 — those brought to the knowledge of the truth through the preaching of Ammon, by the spirit of revelation and prophecy, never fell away — raising his recurring question of what makes conversion stick: the teaching, the experience, or clinging to what you recognized you didn't have. The chapter also notes the limit of that harvest: the Amalekites and Amulonites were not converted "save only one" (Alma 23:14).

The chapter recurs in Excommunication — Topical Study, where Kyle reads it to make the point that the Anti-Nephi-Lehies were forgiven of their past murders because they were not members under covenant when they committed them — a doctrinal contrast with the heightened accountability that follows baptism.

**Context:** Alma 23 (in the Book of Mormon) records the conversion of many Lamanites under the missionary labors of the sons of Mosiah, who take the name Anti-Nephi-Lehi and become known for never falling away from their newfound faith.
