# Real Estate

Real estate runs through Kyle's reading along several tensions. As a **wealth-building vehicle**, [How To Be a Capitalist Without Any Capital](/books/how-to-be-a-capitalist-without-any-capital) lays out the most operational playbook — door-knocking and pocket listings to find deals, the "rent ≥ 1% of deal value" screen, no-money-down financing, hands-off property management, and buying near colleges for recession-proof rental markets ([Rental Properties](/notes/rental-properties)). [On the Nature of Long-Term Holds](/notes/on-the-nature-of-long-term-holds) reinforces this in Kyle's own note: "people make real money by getting involved in real estate and then buying a business and owning it for a long time." [Recession Proof](/books/recession-proof) adds the contrarian timing argument — buy a house *during* a recession, when it shifts from a seller's market — and [Berkshire Hathaway Annual Letters](/books/berkshire-hathaway-annual-letters) has Buffett calling his home his "third best investment" emotionally, while noting he'd have made more money renting and buying stocks.

As an **engine of inequality**, [Evicted](/books/evicted) (by [Matthew Desmond](/notes/matthew-desmond)) flips the lens: real estate is the extraction machine at the bottom of the housing market. Sherrena's "Why real estate? — long-term residuals, property being the best investment out there" exposes how landlords maximize return on the poorest tenants by neglecting repairs and limiting appliances; the ghetto is "social design, not accident," and Desmond's fix is a universal housing voucher. [What I Found in a Thousand Towns](/books/what-i-found-in-a-thousand-towns) frames affordable housing (mandated affordable units, evening out demand spikes) as the counterweight to displacing the committed, creative residents who build a town's soul. [John F.C. Turner](/notes/john-f-c-turner)'s "housing as a noun vs. housing as a verb" argues the poor are better served building their own housing near opportunity than receiving top-down projects, while [The Build-Nothing Country](/notes/the-build-nothing-country) (Noah Smith) blames America's housing shortfall on broken permitting and land-use systems. [Opendoor](/notes/opendoor) sits in this cluster as a tech-driven approach to real-estate transactions.
