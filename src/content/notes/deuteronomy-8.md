# Deuteronomy 8

An Old Testament chapter Kyle lists under "The Mustang Principle" in his topical study The Lehonti Principle. He cites [Deuteronomy 8](/notes/deuteronomy-8):17 (alongside [Judges 7](/notes/judges-7):2, [Alma 14](/notes/alma-14):11, [D&C 59](/notes/dandc-59):2, and the Topical Guide entry "Not trusting in the arm of flesh") to support the theme of acknowledging God's hand in all things rather than crediting one's own power — "there are no coincidences, you have to look for God's purposes."

**Context:** Deuteronomy 8 is Moses' warning to Israel as they near the promised land: remember the Lord through the wilderness, and beware lest prosperity tempt you to say in your heart (v. 17) "My power and the might of mine hand hath gotten me this wealth," forgetting that it is God who gives the power to get wealth.

## Where this appears
