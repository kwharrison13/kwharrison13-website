# Henry Gordon-Smith

Henry Gordon-Smith runs Agritecture Consulting, an urban-agriculture advisory firm Kyle spoke with on July 10th, 2018 (July 10th, 2018 — Henry Gordon-Smith (Agritecture Consulting)). At the time the firm had five full-time employees and had done ~60 projects across 10 countries: they gather data on the industry and its technologies, then consult for startups building business plans (recommending technology, building 3D and economic models) and for cities on local food-system strategy. Gordon-Smith's recurring theme in the call was profitability — four years in, he saw plenty of energy around urban agriculture but a long road to genuinely viable models, and argued for a "bottoms-up retraining" of the next generation of entrepreneurs to treat agriculture as a broken system ripe for change. He positioned Agritecture as the rare interdisciplinary player (no large consulting firm competitors in so niche a space) with global reach and a strong reputation, and saw their "most viable agriculture model" as an IP asset likely to matter in an acquisition.

**Context:** Henry Gordon-Smith is the founder and CEO of Agritecture, a New York-based consulting and education company focused on urban and controlled-environment agriculture (vertical farming, hydroponics, rooftop farms).
