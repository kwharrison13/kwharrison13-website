# Benchmark

The venture firm Kyle treats as the archetypal small, partnership-only "Puritan" of the industry. In his Puritans of Venture Capital research, Benchmark is the named exemplar of the cottage-scale model — focused on finding the very best companies, with each partner making roughly one investment per year — set against the AUM-maximizing "Capital Agglomerators," the thesis being that venture is "not one game but (at least) three" and the medium-fund middle gets squeezed out. [The Power Law — Venture Capital & the Making of the New Future](/books/the-power-law-venture-capital-and-the-making-of-the-new-future) supplies the supporting lore: Benchmark as "the anti-Softbank," its geographically concentrated, local-rather-than-global strength, its prospectus line ("God is not on the side of the big arsenals, but on the side of those who shoot best"), [Bill Gurley](/notes/bill-gurley)'s "grand-slam business" framing and unicorn-governance critiques (Uber, WeWork, liquidation preferences), and its fifty-fifty conviction that coaching entrepreneurs matters as much as deal selection ("I'm so far down, I can't see much sky").

In Kyle's sourcing notes Benchmark functions as a quality signal in the deal-flow graph. The Global Deal Flow log tracks Pave raising a $25M Series A from Benchmark after an [Accel](/notes/accel) seed; the Airplane.dev notes flag its $8.5M Series A led by Benchmark with Eric Vishria joining the board; and Benchmark recurs in the lead-firm sourcing maps alongside [a16z](/notes/a16z), [Sequoia](/notes/sequoia), and [Tiger Global Management](/notes/tiger-global-management) (e.g. the "Benchmark → a16z → Tiger" progression). The SF-trip outreach list names Chetan Puttagunta and Sarah Tavel at Benchmark as contacts.

**Context:** Benchmark is a Silicon Valley venture capital firm founded in 1995, known for its equal-partnership structure (no associate/seniority hierarchy, equal economics), small fund sizes, and early investments in eBay, Uber, Twitter, Snap, Discord, and Dropbox.
