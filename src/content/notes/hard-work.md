# Hard Work

Hard work is one of Kyle's most-linked virtues, and his sources frame it as the substitute for genius. [Driven](/books/driven) is the purest expression — [Larry H. Miller](/notes/larry-h-miller)'s thesis of 90-hour weeks for 20 years, "overpower problems with work," outworking everyone to be undeniable, with hard work the first item in his ten-point definition of an entrepreneur. [Gordon B. Hinckley — Go Forward With Faith](/books/gordon-b-hinckley-go-forward-with-faith) supplies the religious version through Hinckley's father Bryant: "We do not belong to a family of geniuses… Each of us must pay the price of hard work if we achieve… Only workers are found among the chosen people." [John Quincy Adams](/books/john-quincy-adams) makes the same renunciation explicit — Adams "would depend not on genius, but on perseverance" — and Kyle pairs the two passages directly, quoting the Hinckley "no royalty like the royalty of high endeavor" line against the Adams biography.

The concept also has a critical, generational edge. [Hillbilly Elegy](/books/hillbilly-elegy) reads hard work through [Cognitive Dissonance](/notes/cognitive-dissonance) — "we talk about the value of hard work but tell ourselves that the reason we're not working is some perceived unfairness," the lie that solves the broken connection between professed values and reality. The Renaissance of Rise and Grind - Research is where Kyle works through the cultural backlash — the "hatred of hard work" and anti-[Hustle Culture](/notes/hustle-culture) mood — while distinguishing exploitation (McDonald's underpaying workers) from people who "signed up for a battle with a 90% failure rate." [Leslie Knope](/notes/leslie-knope) serves as the barometer ("Gen Z hates Leslie Knope"), her shifting reception read as a sign of the "great softening." Related framings: the [Protestant Work Ethic](/notes/protestant-work-ethic) (diligence as a sign of God's favor) and [Golden Handcuffs](/notes/golden-handcuffs) (the comfort that blunts the drive).

## Where this appears

- [Driven](/books/driven) — Larry H. Miller's "overpower problems with work" as the book's central thesis.
- [Gordon B. Hinckley — Go Forward With Faith](/books/gordon-b-hinckley-go-forward-with-faith) — "Each of us must pay the price of hard work if we achieve."
- [John Quincy Adams](/books/john-quincy-adams) — Adams's choice of perseverance over genius, paired with the Hinckley passage.
- [Hillbilly Elegy](/books/hillbilly-elegy) — hard work invoked and betrayed; the cognitive dissonance of preaching it while not working.
- The Renaissance of Rise and Grind - Research — the cultural backlash against hard work / hustle culture.
- [Leslie Knope](/notes/leslie-knope) — "Gen Z hates Leslie Knope" as a barometer of diminished appetite for hard work.
- [Protestant Work Ethic](/notes/protestant-work-ethic) — diligence as a demonstration of divine favor.
- [Golden Handcuffs](/notes/golden-handcuffs) — the comfort that blunts the pull toward hard work.
