# Platform

The recurring idea that the most durable companies build infrastructure others build *on top of*, rather than products alone. In [The Everything Store](/books/the-everything-store), Kyle flags the line: **"It is the ambition of every technology company to be worth more than the sum of its parts… It wants to become, in the parlance of the industry, a platform."** He reads [Amazon](/notes/amazon)'s AWS as primitives that let others build, and notes [Bezos](/notes/jeff-bezos) preaching the importance of becoming a platform — "even well meaning gatekeepers slow innovation… when a platform is self-service, even the improbable ideas can get tried." Kyle's own gloss: *"more important than the products you launch is the platform that empowers many more people."*

The same concept reappears as career advice in [College Students: Focus On This](/notes/college-students-focus-on-this): **"build where the people are"** — find businesses leveraging the dominant platform in interesting ways and build your career where attention already is. Kyle updates the example to the present: "today it's [Webflow](/notes/webflow) or [Shopify ($SHOP)](/notes/shopify)."

**Context:** In technology and platform-economics literature, a "platform" is a product or infrastructure that creates value primarily by enabling third parties (developers, sellers, creators) to build and transact on top of it — a model whose returns scale with the ecosystem rather than the platform owner's own output.

## Where this appears

- [The Everything Store](/books/the-everything-store) — Amazon/AWS as the canonical example; Bezos's "every technology company seeks to become a platform" and the self-service-vs-gatekeeper argument.
- [College Students- Focus On This](/notes/college-students-focus-on-this) — "build where the people are"; picking a dominant platform (Webflow, Shopify) to launch a career.
