# Moses 1

The opening chapter of the Book of Moses (Pearl of Great Price), where Moses, having been shown the works of God, is confronted by [Satan](/notes/satan) demanding worship — and answers from a settled sense of identity: "Who art thou? For behold, I am a son of God, in the similitude of his Only Begotten; and where is thy glory, that I should worship thee?" ([Moses 1](/notes/moses-1):12-13). In the BYU–Idaho devotional notes Fulfilling the Measure of Our Creation - September 5, 2021, Kyle files this exchange under understanding *who we are*: the answer to Satan's lie is a knowledge of divine sonship, the same defense pointed to in [JSH 1](/notes/jsh-1) and [Alma 36](/notes/alma-36). Just as we can't have faith in God until we understand who He is, we can't fulfill the measure of our creation until we understand who *we* really are.

In Pattern of Prophetic Callings, Moses 1 appears as part of the recurring pattern in how God calls prophets — specifically Moses 1:8, where Moses sees "things unknown to the natural eye." Through an empowering cleansing the body is purified and prophets are given to see with more than the natural eye (paired with [Moses 6](/notes/moses-6):36, Enoch), and a vision of the premortal world tells a prophet who he was and what he can become.

**Context:** Moses 1 is a revelation given to Joseph Smith in June 1830, recording a vision of Moses in which God shows him the worlds He created, Moses physically collapses from the experience, withstands Satan's temptation to be worshipped, and is told "this is my work and my glory — to bring to pass the immortality and eternal life of man" (a later verse in the same chapter).

## Where this appears

- Fulfilling the Measure of Our Creation - September 5, 2021 — Moses 1:12-13 as the model of answering Satan's lie from a knowledge of divine identity ("I am a son of God").
- Pattern of Prophetic Callings — Moses 1:8 as a prophet seeing with more than the "natural eye" through divine cleansing.
