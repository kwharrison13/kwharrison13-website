# Mosiah 7

The Book of Mormon chapter in which Ammon finds Limhi's people in bondage to the Lamanites, and Limhi rehearses how their transgressions brought them into captivity and how turning to the Lord can deliver them. Across Kyle's gospel study the chapter recurs through three verses that frame a single arc — transgression brings bondage, and humble repentance brings deliverance.

Mosiah 7:29 is the verse Kyle returns to most: "I will not succor my people in the day of their transgression; but I will hedge up their ways that they prosper not." It appears in the August 24–30, 2020. Helaman 7–12: "Remember the Lord" study (under "God will give you strength" — the idea that God will not support us in our sins) and again in Born Again, where it figures God's response to covenant unfaithfulness. The counterweight is Mosiah 7:33, quoted in Diligence: "if ye will turn to the Lord with full purpose of heart... he will, according to his own will and pleasure, deliver you out of bondage." In Virtue, Mosiah 7:18 supplies the language of the adversary's "snare" that brings us "into subjection unto him" — the [Natural Man](/notes/natural-man) we must master before we can submit to God.

**Context:** Mosiah 7 begins the account of Limhi and the people of Zeniff, whom Mosiah's expedition under Ammon discovers in bondage. Limhi's speech in the following chapters frames their captivity as the consequence of rejecting prophets, and their hope of deliverance as conditioned on repentance.

## Where this appears

- August 24–30, 2020. Helaman 7–12: "Remember the Lord" — Mosiah 7:29 ("hedge up their ways") on God withholding support in the day of transgression.
- Diligence — Mosiah 7:33 ("turn to the Lord with full purpose of heart... deliver you out of bondage") as the promise after the furnace of affliction.
- Virtue — Mosiah 7:18 ("snare of the adversary... subjection") on the natural man and self-mastery.
