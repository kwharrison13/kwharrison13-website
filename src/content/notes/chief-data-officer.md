# Chief Data Officer

In Kyle's wiki the Chief Data Officer (CDO) role surfaces through [Tom Schenk](/notes/tom-schenk), the deputy IT director for the [City of Chicago](/notes/chicago) who oversaw the full municipal data lifecycle — database management, BI, data publication, and advanced analytics. Per the [Tom Schenk](/notes/tom-schenk) notes, Schenk co-founded a Harvard-based group of Chief Data Officers spanning 25 cities, split roughly evenly across budget office / IT / mayor's-office reporting lines. That split is itself the interesting data point: the CDO function had no settled home in city government, sitting variously under finance, technology, or the executive depending on the city.

The role is presented as a practical, service-delivery-oriented one rather than a buzzword job — Schenk's CDO work centered on expanding Chicago's Open Data Portal, building tools like Open Grid (public) and Windy-Grid (internal), and analytics for food-inspector routing and lead-paint prediction. It is a recurring [GovTech](/notes/govtech) theme in the wiki.

**Context:** "Chief Data Officer" is an executive role, in both companies and governments, responsible for an organization's data strategy, governance, and analytics. In US municipal government it spread in the 2010s alongside the open-data movement.

## Where this appears

- [Tom Schenk](/notes/tom-schenk) — Chicago's deputy IT director, who co-founded a 25-city Harvard-based group of CDOs and exemplifies the practical, open-data flavor of the role.
