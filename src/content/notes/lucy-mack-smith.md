# Lucy Mack Smith

Lucy Mack Smith is the mother of the prophet [Joseph Smith](/notes/joseph-smith), and across Kyle's gospel study she appears in two roles: as a **primitivist seeker** who shaped the religious household Joseph grew up in, and as a **firsthand source** for the years when Joseph was being prepared to receive the golden plates. In Christian Reformation, Zion, and Seekers she is the named example of a *primitivist* — someone trying to *find* the church that Christ had set up — as distinct from a pure *seeker* who only waits; the note adds that "not all primitivists are seekers, but seekers were usually primitivists." Both Joseph Sr. and Lucy Mack were "the first in their families for many generations NOT to join a specific church," and Lucy is described as "a fervent seeker because of the death of her two sisters" (Kyle flags her critical-edition autobiography).

In the First Vision — Topical Study, Lucy carries the family's geographic and spiritual dislocation — the 1816 move from Vermont to New York meant "leaving Lucy's mother behind, never to return again" — and she and three of Joseph's siblings are recorded as members of the Western Presbyterian church from which they were later "kicked out." In Joseph Smith — A Young Seer she becomes a documentary witness for the 1823–1824 plate attempts: she describes Moroni's words to the young Joseph as "I have shown you the distance between light and dark," and she labels his 1824 failure — when Satan put the thought of financial gain in his mind — as his "first failure," in contrast to Joseph Sr., who "saw this experience as simply a failed treasure attempt."

**Context:** Lucy Mack Smith (1775–1856) was the mother of Joseph Smith, founder of the Latter-day Saint movement. Her memoir, dictated in 1844–1845 and published as *Biographical Sketches of Joseph Smith the Prophet* (later issued in a scholarly "critical edition"), is one of the most important early narrative sources for Joseph Smith's youth and the origins of the Restoration.

## Where this appears

- Christian Reformation, Zion, and Seekers — named as the example of a *primitivist* (vs. seeker) and a "fervent seeker" after her sisters' deaths; first in her family for generations not to join a church.
- First Vision — Topical Study — carries the family's 1816 dislocation (leaving her mother behind) and her Western Presbyterian membership.
- Joseph Smith — A Young Seer — firsthand witness to the 1823–1824 plate attempts ("the distance between light and dark"; Joseph's "first failure").
