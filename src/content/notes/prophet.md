# Prophet

In the LDS context that frames Kyle's notes, the **prophet** is the President of the Church — the living authority who receives revelation for the whole body of members. Two of Kyle's book pages anchor the concept from opposite ends. In [Gordon B. Hinckley — Go Forward With Faith](/books/gordon-b-hinckley-go-forward-with-faith), the prophet is approached through loyalty and revelation: Hinckley's blunt formulation to a man lobbying for a policy change — "we either have a prophet, or we don't have a prophet. If we have a prophet, we have everything. If we do not have a prophet, then we have nothing" — and the 1978 priesthood-revelation account, where revelation comes to "the kneeling, pleading prophet of God" as a conduit "between the heavenly throne" and the Brethren. [Henry B. Eyring](/notes/henry-b-eyring)'s gift-of-a-prophet description ("he brings revelation to other people") reinforces the prophet as the channel of [Revelation](/notes/revelation).

[The Next Mormons](/books/the-next-mormons) supplies the sociological and historical frame: the title "the prophet" for the Church president became standard usage only under [David O. McKay](/notes/david-o-mckay) around 1955 (before that "prophet" referred to founding figures like [Joseph Smith](/notes/joseph-smith) and [Brigham Young](/notes/brigham-young)). Jana Riess's survey data sets the prophet at the center of a generational tension — between "rulebook" obedience (Ezra Taft Benson's "fourteen fundamentals," which held the living prophet above the standard works) and personal spiritual authority — with Millennials drifting from deference to general authorities. Together the two sources hold the devotional ideal ([Follow the Prophet](/notes/follow-the-prophet)) against the measured reality of how that ideal is transmitted and eroding across generations.

**Context:** In The Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-day Saints, the President of the Church is sustained as "prophet, seer, and revelator," believed to hold the keys to receive revelation for the entire Church.
