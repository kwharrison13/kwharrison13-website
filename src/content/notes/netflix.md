# Netflix

Netflix appears across Kyle's notes mainly as a benchmark for data-driven sophistication. In the November 2nd, 2021 — Rangan Anandan (BBC) reference call on ANTENNA, Netflix is repeatedly held up as the company "at the forefront" of using subscription data to market and build audiences — the model other streamers (Paramount+, BBC) are shifting toward as the market saturates and "growth is no longer a given." Anandan also flags Netflix's expansion beyond a single format (e.g. getting into gaming) as the reason subscription-analytics tools have to track multiple media formats. The throughline is Netflix as the data-and-algorithm exemplar that an external dataset like ANTENNA must plug into.

Separately, in [SignalRank](/notes/signalrank)'s pitch, the company's data-science team is described as being "led by the guy who helped build the Netflix recommendation engine" — Netflix here is shorthand for recommendation/ranking-engine pedigree applied to ranking venture investors.

**Context:** Netflix (NASDAQ: NFLX) is the largest subscription video-on-demand streaming service, founded 1997, widely cited as a pioneer of recommendation algorithms and data-driven content and marketing decisions.
