# Recurrency

Recurrency appears in the April 5th, 2022 — Frederik Gieschen & Eric Tarczynski (Contrary Research) conversation as an illustrative example in a discussion about how to find the compelling angle on a company story. Recurrency is offered as a case of a binary-outcome business: "either you replace this or you totally fail. What is the actual risk here?" The point being made by [Frederik Gieschen](/notes/frederik-gieschen) and [Eric Tarczynski](/notes/eric-tarczynski) was about [Contrary Research](/essays/contrary-research)'s editorial approach — that only some companies are worth going deep on, and the interesting ones often hinge on life-or-death stakes rather than well-funded incremental competition. Recurrency stands in as the example of such a high-stakes, replace-the-incumbent story.

**Context:** Recurrency is an AI-powered ERP and pricing/automation software company built for distributors and wholesalers, aimed at modernizing legacy systems like Epicor and SAP.
