# Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-Day Saints

Kyle's faith, and a recurring frame across both his personal and intellectual notes. In the September 13th, 2022 — Harry Stebbings Podcast he describes how membership shapes his priority on family, a sense of purpose ("eternal implications"), and a humility grounded in [Eternal Progression](/notes/eternal-progression) — the church's tenet that always improving means acknowledging failure and working to improve, which underpins what Kyle calls his "[Spirit of Humility](/notes/spirit-of-humility)."

The church is also the subject of much of Kyle's reading. [Gordon B. Hinckley](/notes/gordon-b-hinckley), its fifteenth President, is the subject of Sheri Dew's authorized biography [Gordon B. Hinckley — Go Forward With Faith](/books/gordon-b-hinckley-go-forward-with-faith), which Kyle has read. [An Insider's View of Mormon Origins](/books/an-insider-s-view-of-mormon-origins) — Grant H. Palmer's revisionist history of the church's founding — Kyle reads with active disagreement, flagging logical and historiographical gaps in Palmer's case against the [Joseph Smith](/notes/joseph-smith) origin story rather than accepting its conclusions. The faith thus functions in the wiki both as a lived commitment and as a corpus Kyle engages critically.

**Context:** The Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-day Saints is a restorationist Christian denomination founded by Joseph Smith in 1830, headquartered in Salt Lake City, Utah, and led by a President regarded as a prophet. Members are commonly called Latter-day Saints or, informally, Mormons.
