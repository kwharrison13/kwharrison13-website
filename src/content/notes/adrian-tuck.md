# Adrian Tuck

CEO of [Tendril](/notes/tendril), a vertical-software company serving the utilities space. In a July 2018 call with Kyle, Tuck laid out Tendril's two business lines — a mature customer-engagement and analytics product priced at ~$8/house/year, and a newer energy-management product at ~$50/house/year — both sold on multi-year contracts to utilities (he cited an in-progress $58M deal over 36 months). He framed the business as B2B2C with very low churn (under 2% cumulative over two years) because utilities are sticky, ~95% recurring revenue, ~$36M of 2018 revenue, and profitability with $6M EBITDA on the core business.

Tuck was contemplating a transaction at the time of the call: he wanted to look at a deal in September 2018, preferring a growth-equity partner who would buy out existing shareholders plus inject $15–20M of primary capital over a strategic sale (he had engaged a banker for the strategic path but didn't want to pursue it). He pegged the whole company's worth at $150M–$200M (roughly 3.5x LTM / 4x NTM revenue), with O-Power (acquired by Oracle) named as the biggest competitor Tendril was picking up churned customers from.
