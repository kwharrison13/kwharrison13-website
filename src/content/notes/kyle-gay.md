# Kyle Gay

Kyle Gay is an investor at [Kensington Permanent Capital](/notes/kensington-permanent-capital) (formerly at [Norwest](/notes/norwest)) who serves in Kyle Harrison's notes as a recurring sounding board on permanent-capital and buyout strategy. In the March 2020 reference call — taken as Kyle diligenced whether to leave Coatue for [Teamshares](/notes/teamshares) — Gay walked through SMB-buyout economics: search-fund economics that "don't really make sense" given the searcher's risk and opportunity cost, family offices wanting direct deal access but ill-suited to the operational grind of buyouts, fundless sponsors raising deal-by-deal SPVs, and the [Norwest](/notes/norwest) data point that a proprietary buyout (20% growth, $3M+ EBITDA) took ~2.7 years first-call-to-close. He was impressed Teamshares had raised from [USV](/notes/usv) and judged the ESOP-conglomerate model interesting but "a lot of moving parts," comparing it to platform plays like Cadre and Candid.

By the March 2022 call he was deeper into permanent capital and public equities — buying a minority stake in a hedge fund, leading a seed for a growth-buyout vehicle out of [Oak HC/FT](/notes/oak-hc-ft), and weighing a fund with [Peter McCoy](/notes/peter-mccoy) to institutionalize early-employee access into top rounds. His brother Dan does more [Family Office](/notes/family-office) work. [SageLink](/notes/sagelink) (where "Mike" was a notably successful operator) surfaces in passing through his calls.
