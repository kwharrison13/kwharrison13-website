# C.S. Lewis

The British Christian writer and apologist who recurs across Kyle's faith reading and study notes — almost always through *[Mere Christianity](/notes/mere-christianity)* and almost always on the theme of total surrender to God. The signature passage Kyle returns to is "I will give you Myself": Christ says "give me All… I want to have the whole tree down… I will give you a new self instead. In fact, I will give you Myself" — quoted in [Faith is Not Blind](/books/faith-is-not-blind) and echoed in [Future Mormon](/books/future-mormon) (where Kyle's own note reads "*I want the whole tree out*") and in his Puritans of Venture Capital research. The Humility — Topical Study uses Lewis (via [Tad R. Callister](/notes/tad-r-callister)) on the Savior who "will never rest nor let you rest until you are literally perfect" — God's refusal to settle for anything less than absolute perfection.

In [Why You're Christian](/notes/why-you-re-christian), Lewis supplies the "liar, lunatic, or Lord" trilemma — "either this man was, and is, the Son of God: or else a madman or something worse" — which Kyle pairs against Hitchens. He is also a fixture in [Chad's Big Quote Book](/notes/chad-s-big-quote-book), quoted there on there being "no neutral ground in the universe." Notably, Lewis (a non-Latter-day Saint Anglican) is folded into Kyle's LDS-heavy quote corpus alongside [G.K. Chesterton](/notes/g-k-chesterton) and [T.S. Eliot](/notes/t-s-eliot) as a Christian voice whose framing of discipleship and surrender resonates across denominational lines.

**Context:** C.S. Lewis (1898–1963) was an Oxford and Cambridge literary scholar and one of the 20th century's most influential Christian apologists, author of *Mere Christianity*, *The Screwtape Letters*, *The Problem of Pain*, and the *Chronicles of Narnia*.
