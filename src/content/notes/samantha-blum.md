# Samantha Blum

Customer Success Lead at App Annie (mobile analytics) and former Managing Director of Client Success at Meltwater, interviewed as expert call #10 in Kyle's Planhat - Diligence dossier. At the time she was actively evaluating customer-success tools and offered an unusually pro-[Planhat](/notes/planhat) view: she had **used Planhat for two years previously** and, on reflection, thought it "really great" despite growing pains — she would **recommend Planhat and would not recommend [Gainsight](/notes/gainsight)**, and saw the future evaluation as Planhat vs. [Catalyst](/notes/catalyst).

Her framework names three key evaluation areas — (1) user experience; (2) interface look and flexibility; (3) data ingestion and accuracy ("if the connections can't be 100%, it'll become useless") — and her stated ROI goals are mitigating churn, increasing renewal rate, and equipping CSMs to drive sales. She characterized [Gainsight](/notes/gainsight) as the original but "the oldest... not as flexible/sexy," built as a Salesforce connector that "stopped in terms of technology," while [Catalyst](/notes/catalyst), [ToTango](/notes/totango), and Planhat have invested heavily in UX. On Planhat's weakness: "a bit overwhelming — there is so much you can do," intimidating if data isn't optimized. She also noted Catalyst's marketing edge (hiring a CS community manager / social influencer) and its low (~1%) cited churn.
