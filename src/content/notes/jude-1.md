# Jude 1

The single chapter of the Epistle of Jude, cited in Kyle's Charity & Love study. The note draws on Jude 1:22 ("And of some have compassion, making a difference") as a self-examination prompt for charity — "Do you try to understand others' feelings and see their point of view?" — set among a list of questions on the pure love of Christ, intent, and forgiveness ([Ephesians 4](/notes/ephesians-4):32, [Mosiah 18](/notes/mosiah-18):9, [Moroni 7](/notes/moroni-7):47–48).

**Context:** Jude (the Epistle of Jude) is a short, single-chapter book near the end of the New Testament, traditionally attributed to Jude, brother of James. It warns against false teachers and urges believers to keep themselves in the love of God.
