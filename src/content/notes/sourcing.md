# Sourcing

Sourcing appears in Kyle's notes in two distinct senses. The dominant one is **deal/founder sourcing** in a venture context: the work of finding investable companies and entrepreneurs. [Inder Takhar](/notes/inder-takhar), an investor at [Village Capital](/notes/village-capital), is described as "sourcing entrepreneurs and executing investments" across sector-focused accelerator programs. Kyle's [Networking Calls](/notes/networking-calls) tag marks exploratory calls with founders, operators, and advisors that "double as deal/founder sourcing." [ClickUp](/notes/clickup) surfaced as a candidate growth deal during an [Index Ventures](/notes/index-ventures) sourcing/diligence discussion raised by [Mark Goldberg](/notes/mark-goldberg). The pattern links sourcing tightly to its downstream counterpart, Diligence.

The second sense is the contrasting one Munger draws in the [Berkshire Hathaway Annual Letters](/books/berkshire-hathaway-annual-letters): a critique of standardized, banker-driven acquisition *sourcing* — the auction "book" process where bankers prepare glossy projections. Buffett tells the Scott Fetzer story (a deal the bankers failed to source, which he closed in a week by simply writing the CEO), and Munger's tagged punchline — "I'll pay $2.5 million not to read it" — stands as the anti-pattern to Berkshire's relationship-driven deal flow.
