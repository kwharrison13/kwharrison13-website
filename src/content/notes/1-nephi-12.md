# 1 Nephi 12

A chapter in the Book of Mormon's first book of Nephi, read in Kyle's gospel study mainly as one panel of Lehi's [Tree of Life](/notes/tree-of-life) vision (the vision spanning [1 Nephi 8](/notes/1-nephi-8), [11](/notes/1-nephi-11)–[12](/notes/1-nephi-12), [15](/notes/1-nephi-15)). Plan of Salvation — Topical Study cites [1 Nephi 12](/notes/1-nephi-12):17 for the "mist of darkness" that stands as the "temptations of the devil" upon the hearts of fallen men — the mortal hazard mapped onto the large and spacious field of earth.

The chapter also surfaces in Excommunication — Topical Study, where Kyle flags [1 Nephi 12](/notes/1-nephi-12):9–10 (alongside [Mormon 3](/notes/mormon-3):19) as a thread to connect to the idea of bishops as common judges — who judges whom, and who holds that authority.

**Context:** 1 Nephi 12 records Nephi's vision of the future of his posterity in the promised land — wars, the appearance of Christ, the eventual destruction of the Nephites, and the twelve who would judge the house of Israel.

## Where this appears

- Plan of Salvation — Topical Study — cited for the "mist of darkness" / temptations of the devil within Lehi's Tree of Life vision
- Excommunication — Topical Study — verses 9–10 flagged to develop the doctrine of bishops as common judges in Israel
