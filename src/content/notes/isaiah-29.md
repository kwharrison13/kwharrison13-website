# Isaiah 29

A chapter of the Old Testament book of Isaiah that recurs across Kyle's [gospel-study](/notes/gospel-study) notes in two distinct uses. In Publication of the Book of Mormon, Isaiah 29 is read as a prophecy of the coming forth of the Book of Mormon — "the work given to the learned and the unlearned." The episode in which Martin Harris carried symbols from the plates to the scholar Charles Anthon is framed as its fulfillment: Anthon (the learned) could not truly read "reformed Egyptian," and neither could [Joseph Smith](/notes/joseph-smith) (the unlearned), but "it was the Lord who would provide a way."

In Diligence, the chapter appears as Isaiah 29:13–14 — the warning against "apparent busy-bodies seeking out with their lips as their hearts draw farther away from Christ." There it sharpens the study's distinction between carnal diligence ("attentive and busy") and the true, all-encompassing hunger of those who "diligently seek" God.

**Context:** Isaiah 29 is a chapter in the biblical book of Isaiah. In Latter-day Saint scripture it is widely cited (alongside its quotation in 2 Nephi 27) as foretelling a sealed book delivered to "the learned" and "him that is not learned" — an interpretation that figures heavily in LDS accounts of the Book of Mormon's translation and the Anthon transcript episode.

## Where this appears

- Publication of the Book of Mormon — read as the prophecy of the Book of Mormon coming forth to the learned and unlearned, fulfilled in the Charles Anthon / Martin Harris episode
- Diligence — cited (29:13–14) to contrast lip-service "busy-body" seeking with true diligent hunger after God
