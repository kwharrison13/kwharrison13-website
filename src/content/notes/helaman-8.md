# Helaman 8

Chapter 8 of the Book of Helaman in the Book of Mormon. In Kyle's [Come, Follow Me](/notes/come-follow-me) study of [Helaman 7](/notes/helaman-7)–[Helaman 12](/notes/helaman-12), this chapter grounds two recurring ideas. First, **[The Wicked Take the Truth to be Hard](/notes/the-wicked-take-the-truth-to-be-hard)**: the judges "were angry with him because he spake plainly unto them concerning their secret works of darkness" ([Helaman 8](/notes/helaman-8):4), which Kyle pairs with Nephi's words in [1 Nephi 16](/notes/1-nephi-16) that "the guilty taketh the truth to be hard, for it cutteth them to the very center." Second, **pride cometh before the fall** ([Pride](/notes/pride)): the people's boast in [Helaman 8](/notes/helaman-8):6 — "we are powerful, and our cities great, therefore our enemies can have no power over us" — as a case study in misplaced confidence.

**Context:** Helaman 8 records Nephi prophesying to a hostile crowd, defending his authority by appeal to prior prophets, and foretelling the murder of the chief judge as a sign.

## Where this appears

- Helaman 7–12 — Remember the Lord — cited for "the wicked take the truth to be hard" (with 1 Nephi 16) and for "pride cometh before the fall."
