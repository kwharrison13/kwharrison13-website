# D&C 130

Section 130 of the Doctrine and Covenants, a recurring scriptural anchor across Kyle's gospel study. Three verses do most of the work in his notes. **Verses 18–19** — that "whatever principle of intelligence we attain unto in this life, it will rise with us in the resurrection," and the person who gains more knowledge and intelligence "will have so much the advantage in the world to come" — ground the Knowledge — Topical Study, where they are paired with the Bible Dictionary's gloss that "no one can be saved in ignorance of the gospel" and that one "progresses only as fast as one gains knowledge." **Verses 20–21** — "There is a law, irrevocably decreed in heaven before the foundations of this world, upon which all blessings are predicated… And when we obtain any blessing from God, it is by obedience to that law" — appear in [Chad's Big Quote Book](/notes/chad-s-big-quote-book) via [Hugh B. Brown](/notes/hugh-b-brown) and again in the Faith — Topical Study as the basis for the claim that blessings follow obedience and faith offered first.

**Verse 22** is load-bearing for The Godhead: "The Father has a body of flesh and bones as tangible as man's; the Son also; but the Holy Ghost… is a personage of Spirit." Kyle's study uses it as the Latter-day Saint counter to the creedal Trinity born of the Nicene and Athanasian councils — the doctrine that the Father and Son have tangible bodies and are three distinct beings, "one in every way except being the same person."

**Context:** D&C 130 records instructions Joseph Smith gave on April 2, 1843, in Ramus, Illinois, canonized in the Doctrine and Covenants — a book of latter-day revelation in the Latter-day Saint scriptural canon. It is among the most frequently cited sections for its statements on the nature of God, the persistence of intelligence beyond death, and the law-and-blessing relationship.

## Where this appears

- Knowledge — Topical Study — vv. 18–19 ground the argument that knowledge gained in this life "rises with us" and confers advantage in the world to come.
- Faith — Topical Study — v. 21 cited for the principle that blessings come "by obedience," with faith offered to God first.
- The Godhead — v. 22 is the central proof-text that the Father and Son have tangible bodies and the Holy Ghost is a personage of spirit, against the creedal Trinity.
- [Chad's Big Quote Book](/notes/chad-s-big-quote-book) — vv. 20–21 quoted via [Hugh B. Brown](/notes/hugh-b-brown) on the law upon which every blessing is predicated.
