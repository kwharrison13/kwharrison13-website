# David Kadavy

Author and entrepreneur (@kadavy) who writes about creativity, design, and independent work. Kyle bookmarked his April 2020 Twitter thread — saved as Everything You Ever Need to Know... (tweet) — a screenplay analysis arguing that Napoleon Dynamite is, beneath its apparent randomness, a movie about following your dreams. Kadavy reads the film through "two kinds of optimism" (Napoleon's naive optimism and Kip's delusional optimism) and lands on a "fake it 'til you make it" middle way, closing with the idea that even if your dreams seem silly to others, they matter to you.

**Context:** David Kadavy is a writer and podcaster known for books on creativity and self-publishing, including *Design for Hackers*, *The Heart to Start*, and *Mind Management, Not Time Management*, and for his "Love Your Work" podcast.
