# Joseph F. Smith

Joseph F. Smith recurs across Kyle's reading as both a historical figure and a source of frequently-cited teachings. In [Saints — No Unhallowed Hand](/books/saints-no-unhallowed-hand) he appears as a young man (he "was only fifteen, after all," feeling "his own weakness and insignificance") and later as one of the leaders — alongside [John Taylor](/notes/john-taylor) and [George Q. Cannon](/notes/george-q-canon) — who led the church "through the crisis" of the polygamy/Manifesto era, writing to his sister that "I do not believe as a people we have lived as near to God as we should have done."

His most-quoted line in Kyle's notes is a counsel against leader-worship: "I want no man to lean upon me nor to follow me, only so far as I am a consistent follower in the footsteps of the Master" — cited in [Faith is Not Blind](/books/faith-is-not-blind) and echoed in Kyle's margin note in [Discipline Is Destiny](/books/discipline-is-destiny). In the book's argument the line sits alongside [Michael Polanyi](/notes/michael-polanyi) on learning by example and submission to tradition, and just before Kyle's note on [Camden Harrison](/notes/camden-harrison)'s MTC teaching — together making the case that authority and discipleship are pointed past the leader toward Christ, not toward the leader himself. [The Priesthood Power of Women](/books/the-priesthood-power-of-women) draws on him for the home as "a divine institution" and his charge that women are "to lead … the women of the world, in everything that is praise-worthy." [Working Toward Zion](/books/working-toward-zion) quotes his temporal-salvation doctrine: "a religion which has not the power to save people temporally … cannot be depended upon to save them spiritually." [Chad's Big Quote Book](/notes/chad-s-big-quote-book) lists him among its recurring quoted authorities.

**Context:** Joseph F. Smith (1838–1918) was the sixth president of The Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-day Saints, serving from 1901 to 1918. He was a son of Hyrum Smith and nephew of the church's founder, [Joseph Smith](/notes/joseph-smith); he led the church through the early-20th-century transition out of polygamy and is known for the vision of the redemption of the dead (now D&C 138).
