# Galatians 6

A New Testament chapter (Paul's letter to the Galatians) Kyle cites in his Alma 30–31 study for the "law of the harvest." He quotes [Galatians 6](/notes/galatians-6):7 — "Be not deceived; God is not mocked: for whatsoever a man soweth, that shall he also reap" — to underscore the teaching that obedience and personal integrity provide the "needed nutrients for fertile spiritual soil," while corrupting influences are "bitter poisons that kill the soil." There is no shortcut around the law of the harvest: we reap what we sow, and steady obedience yields the line-upon-line growth toward "the mind of Christ."

**Context:** Galatians 6 is the closing chapter of Paul's epistle to the Galatians, containing the "whatsoever a man soweth, that shall he also reap" sowing-and-reaping passage (Gal 6:7).
