# Greylock Partners

In Kyle's corpus, Greylock Partners appears in [VC: An American History](/books/vc) as part of the venture-capital industry's east-coast family tree. The book profiles Greylock as founded in 1965 by William Elfers after a long career at ARD (ARD), grouping it with Venrock Associates as a firm that "did much better" than some contemporaries and "provid[ed] impetus to expansion in the industry." It sits in the lineage the book traces from ARD — the first institutional venture firm — outward into the named partnerships that professionalized the asset class.

**Context:** Greylock Partners is one of the oldest U.S. venture-capital firms, founded in 1965 by William Elfers. Now headquartered in Silicon Valley, it has backed companies including LinkedIn, Facebook, Airbnb, and Workday.
