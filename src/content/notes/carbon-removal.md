# Carbon Removal

Carbon removal — taking CO2 already in the atmosphere via nature-based sequestration (trees, soils) or new technologies like direct air capture — is the "get to zero" step that sits at the end of the climate workflow in the Watershed Climate Diligence — 2021 dossier. The framing there: companies should "measure comprehensively, drive high-impact reductions, and only then think about offsets or carbon removal to get to zero." [Watershed Climate](/notes/watershed-climate) doesn't do removal itself; its marketplace module routes customers toward removal supply and takes ~10% on credits purchased — with [Stripe](/notes/stripe)'s carbon-removal program as a channel partner (one customer bought 83 tons of removal from [Charm Industrial](/notes/charm-industrial) through the platform).

The supply side is captured in Watershed's "Frontiers of Carbon Removal" panel, which paired [Running Tide](/notes/running-tide) (ocean-based, [Marty Odlin](/notes/marty-odlin)) with [Heirloom](/notes/heirloom) as "two cutting-edge carbon removal startups" — framed not as "speculative non-profits" but as "tech companies with customers, investors, deliverables—and competitors" that sell to climate leads at [Stripe](/notes/stripe) and [Shopify ($SHOP)](/notes/shopify) (collectively kickstarting the market with $10M+ of purchasing commitments). The diligence flags **energy efficiency — power burned per tonne removed — as the key metric**, and projects that the cost floor could drop from "a few thousand dollars per tonne" in 2021 to "perhaps under $100 this decade" (analogizing to solar's 2,000x cost decline). One expert call also voices skepticism, comparing premature carbon removal to "the CDOs in the financial crisis."

**Context:** Carbon dioxide removal (CDR) refers to methods that draw down CO2 already in the atmosphere — including afforestation/soil sequestration, ocean-based approaches, enhanced weathering, and engineered direct air capture — distinct from emissions *reduction* (avoiding new emissions) and from conventional offsets.
