# Wickedness Never Was Happiness

A scriptural theme Kyle traced through his Helaman 13–16 study. The phrase comes from Alma's words to his son Corianton — "wickedness never was happiness" (Alma 41:10) — and Samuel the Lamanite's parallel charge that the Nephites "have sought for happiness in doing iniquity, which thing is contrary to the nature of that righteousness which is in our great and Eternal Head" (Helaman 13:38). The core claim is that sin is structurally incapable of producing happiness, because it runs against the grain of the divine nature.

Kyle pairs the idea with the "sorrowing of the damned" from Mormon 2:13 — the grief of those whose sorrow is "not unto repentance," because "the Lord would not always suffer them to take happiness in sin." In his notes this connects to Godly Sorrow (people "glory in sin" and seek teachers who flatter them so they can dull the guilt in their souls) and to the violent reaction in Helaman 16:2, where unbelievers cast stones at Samuel rather than walk away — Kyle's question being why personal guilt drives people to try to destroy the source of their conviction rather than simply ignore it.
