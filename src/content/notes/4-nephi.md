# 4 Nephi

A short Book of Mormon book describing the roughly two-century-long Zion society that followed Christ's visit — a people with "all things common" and no contention. In Kyle's The Process of Zion topical study it serves as the working *model* of how [Zion](/notes/zion) is built: by a process, not a snap of God's fingers. Kyle reads the book as a people who allowed "all men the same privilege" — they did not judge, but neither did they *receive* wickedness, "because once we receive them, more people will eventually accept those things" (4 Nephi 1:27, 34). This becomes Kyle's evidence for [David A. Bednar](/notes/david-a-bednar)'s "tyranny of tolerance" balance: be careful around evil practices, yet tolerant and loving of people. The broader argument is that *we* make the world celestial by learning and living the celestial law, rather than waiting for God to descend and do it.

**Context:** Fourth Nephi is one of the shortest books in the Book of Mormon, narrating the unusually peaceful, communal society that endured for nearly 200 years after Christ's appearance in the Americas before pride and inequality eroded it.

## Where this appears

- The Process of Zion — used as the model Zion society (vv.27, 34) for the "tyranny of tolerance" and the doctrine that we build the celestial world rather than wait for it
