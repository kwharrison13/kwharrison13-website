# CallMiner

A ~20-year conversation-analytics incumbent in the contact-center space, surfaced throughout Kyle's Cresta.ai - Diligence as a benchmark competitor in the "agent assist / conversation intelligence" landscape (alongside [Observe.ai](/notes/observe-ai), [Balto.ai](/notes/balto-ai), [Gong](/notes/gong), and [Chorus](/notes/chorus)). Expert references in the diligence describe CallMiner as a base analytics platform whose strength is best-in-class speech-to-text driving best-in-class analytics, with two key modules: **Coach** (performance management) and **Alert** (real-time guidance). Its speech recognition is praised for handling context — e.g. the many meanings of "balance."

Across the calls, CallMiner is repeatedly contrasted with the real-time live-coaching vendors. CallMiner Alert resembles [Balto.ai](/notes/balto-ai)'s real-time function but references found it hard to integrate and use, and — like [Cresta.ai](/notes/cresta-ai) — it shows a next-best-action pop-up with 2–3 options "the agent may or may not use, and you never know whether they did," whereas Balto's in-line phrasing confirms execution. The more analytical framing is that CallMiner tells you "what are the issues you need to solve, and how," while Balto drives org-wide consistency. CallMiner also appeared in head-to-head evaluations against Calabrio and Observe (both close, established platforms).

**Context:** CallMiner is a Waltham, Massachusetts conversation-intelligence company founded in 2002, known for its speech analytics platform (Eureka) that mines recorded customer interactions for insights, sentiment, and compliance.
