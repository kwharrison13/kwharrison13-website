# The Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-day Saints

The institutional and doctrinal center of Kyle's gospel-study corpus — the church he belongs to and writes about. It is the entity behind the recurring cast of leaders in his notes: members of the Quorum of the Twelve Apostles like [D. Todd Christofferson](/notes/d-todd-christofferson), [Dale G. Renlund](/notes/dale-g-renlund), and [Gary E. Stevenson](/notes/gary-e-stevenson), and earlier leaders like [Marion G. Romney](/notes/marion-g-romney), whose General Conference talks and teachings he studies. In The First Principles of Faithful Pragmatism Kyle writes to defend his own belief as he watches people close to him leave the Church or struggle with serious doubt — locating the *first principles* underneath his faith rather than defending every individual claim, and arguing that the Church's organization and teachings are, "despite its flaws, reflective of the God I worship."

Kyle's interest in the institution is heavily about the problem of [Institution Building](/notes/institution-building) out of fallible people. [History of the Church](/notes/history-of-the-church) (a tag he applies to his reading of the Church's official narrative history) frames the pioneer era less as a trek story than as an interior case study: Brigham Young's fear that prosperity rather than persecution would ruin the Saints, the candid admission that "a prophet… may be fallible" while "the work… emanated from God," Wilford Woodruff's Manifesto, and the governance mechanic of the Quorum of the Twelve acting "unanimously or not at all." Through these sources the Church reads as an ongoing exercise in [Stewardship](/notes/stewardship) and [Living Revelation](/notes/living-revelation) — testing what is genuine revelation versus "the wisdom of men" (a recurring [Marion G. Romney](/notes/marion-g-romney) frame), and bridging gaps in personal testimony by triangulating from the fruits of the Church today.

**Context:** The Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-day Saints, headquartered in Salt Lake City, is the largest denomination in the Latter Day Saint (Mormon) movement, founded by Joseph Smith in 1830. It is led by a President (sustained as prophet) and a governing Quorum of the Twelve Apostles, and its members are encouraged in personal scripture study and weekly "Come, Follow Me" curriculum.
