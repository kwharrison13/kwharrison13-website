# Enduring to the End

The fifth and final principle of the Doctrine of Christ in Kyle's gospel-study notes — "staying on the strait and narrow path." In Follow the Doctrine and Gospel of Christ — M. Russell Ballard, it completes the five doctrinal principles [M. Russell Ballard](/notes/m-russell-ballard) draws from 2 Nephi 31: faith in Christ, [Repentance](/notes/repentance), [Baptism](/notes/baptism), the gift of the [Holy Ghost](/notes/holy-ghost), and enduring to the end.

The principle is anchored in the talk by the warning of Ballard's stake patriarch, who in his nineties prayed nightly "that God will see me safely dead with my testimony burning brightly," and cautioned the young Ballard that "no one is secure until the very end." Enduring to the end is thus framed not as a one-time event but as the lifelong continuation of faith, repentance, and covenant-keeping — the discipline that keeps a testimony "burning brightly" to the finish.

**Context:** "Enduring to the end" is a Latter-day Saint doctrinal term for remaining faithful and keeping one's covenants throughout life. In LDS teaching it is the culminating element of the "Doctrine of Christ" set out in 2 Nephi 31, following faith, repentance, baptism, and receiving the Holy Ghost.
