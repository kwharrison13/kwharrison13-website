# Plan of Salvation

The Plan of Salvation is a central organizing doctrine across Kyle's [gospel-study](/notes/gospel-study) notes — the framework that maps premortal life, mortality, and the world to come. His dedicated Plan of Salvation — Topical Study (Topical Journal 1.71–1.77) reads it as one plan with **two proposed implementations** — Christ's and Satan's — where Satan's was "exaltation without obedience," the doing-away of [Agency](/notes/agency). The purpose of birth, in these notes, is to "become Gods ourselves" ([D&C 76](/notes/dandc-76)); we kept our first estate by using agency to follow Christ ([Abraham 3](/notes/abraham-3):26) and keep our second estate by bringing celestial spirits to reign over telestial bodies. He treats [Lehi](/notes/lehi)'s [Tree of Life](/notes/tree-of-life) vision as a map of the whole journey — premortal waste, the rod of iron as the word of God, the mists of darkness as temptation — and quotes Joseph Smith's charge to "make the study of the plan of Salvation the center focus of our study."

The concept threads through many other gospel-study pages as a recurring "core doctrine" link: The King Follett Sermon (where denying "the plan of salvation with eyes open to its truth" is tied to the unpardonable sin), Christ Defines His Gospel, The Godhead, and The Process of Zion all list it among their core themes. It functions less as a single note and more as the doctrinal backbone connecting Kyle's studies of agency, [Exaltation](/notes/exaltation), the [Atonement](/notes/atonement), and [Eternal Progression](/notes/eternal-progression).

**Context:** In Latter-day Saint (LDS) theology, the Plan of Salvation (also called the plan of happiness or plan of redemption) is the doctrine describing God's purpose for humanity across premortal existence, mortal life, and the afterlife — spanning the Creation, the Fall, the Atonement of Jesus Christ, agency, and the degrees of glory.
