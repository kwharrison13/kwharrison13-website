# The Restoration of the Fulness of the Gospel of Jesus Christ

In Kyle's General Conference- October 2021 notes this phrase appears as the title heading of a conference talk grouped under teachings on following God's living prophet. The surrounding bullets carry the talk's emphasis: "Try a little harder to be a little better" ([Gordon B. Hinckley](/notes/gordon-b-hinckley)), the warning that to "cut the ties that bind yourself to the Prophet of God" is to "sink yourself to hell" ([Brigham Young](/notes/brigham-young)), the assurance that the prophet "will NEVER be led astray," and the caution against "picking and choosing what prophetic counsel you will follow." The phrase frames the restoration as ongoing and prophet-led rather than a single past event.

**Context:** "The Restoration of the Fulness of the Gospel of Jesus Christ" is a recurring formulation in The Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-day Saints (e.g., the 2020 proclamation "The Restoration") referring to the reestablishment, beginning with Joseph Smith, of priesthood authority, doctrine, and ordinances understood to have been lost in apostasy.
