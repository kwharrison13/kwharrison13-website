# Luke 7

A Gospel of Luke chapter Kyle cites for two distinct passages across his study. The first is the **centurion's faith** ([Luke 7](/notes/luke-7):7, 9) — Christ marveling, "I have not found so great faith, no, not in Israel." Kyle files this alongside the other moments where Christ ranks someone's faith above all others: in the [Brother of Jared](/notes/brother-of-jared) page it sits beside the centurion of [Matthew 8](/notes/matthew-8) and the Nephites of [3 Nephi 19](/notes/3-nephi-19), collected under [Never have I seen such great faith](/notes/never-have-i-seen-such-great-faith). The November 9–15, 2020. Ether 1–5: "Rend That Veil of Unbelief" week pairs the same centurion verses with the Brother of Jared's exceeding faith.

The second passage is the **parable of the two debtors** ([Luke 7](/notes/luke-7):41–43, 47) — "her sins, which are many, are forgiven; for she loved much: but to whom little is forgiven, the same loveth little." In the [Knowledge](/notes/knowledge) topical study this is the hinge of Kyle's quandary about gaining experience: the one forgiven much loves much, which complicates the idea that those who have sinned more partake more deeply of the [Atonement](/notes/atonement). The resolution Kyle reaches (via [Bruce C. Hafen](/notes/bruce-c-hafen)) is that we need not more sin but more understanding, so gratitude increases. The Charity & Love study cites the chapter (Luke 7:12–15, the widow of Nain's son raised) as scriptural backing for telling others, when appropriate, that you love and care about them.
