# Exodus 19

A scripture reference cited in Kyle's Abrahamic Covenant gospel study. Exodus 19:5–6 — Israel called to be "a kingdom of priests, and an holy nation" — is read alongside [1 Peter 2](/notes/1-peter-2):9, where [Martin Luther](/notes/martin-luther)'s "priesthood of all believers" reading is set against the note that early Israelites "had not yet accepted Christ and therefore cannot specifically fit the Christian idea." The pairing appears in the study's discussion of priesthood authority and who is meant to baptize.

**Context:** Exodus 19 is the chapter of the Hebrew Bible in which Israel arrives at Mount Sinai and God offers the covenant — that if they obey, they will be "a peculiar treasure," "a kingdom of priests, and an holy nation" — immediately preceding the giving of the Ten Commandments.
