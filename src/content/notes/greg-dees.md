# Greg Dees

Greg Dees appears in Kyle's wiki as a Duke CASE figure quoted in the [MBA Degrees](/notes/mba-degrees) note on whether [Social Entrepreneurship](/notes/social-entrepreneurship) can actually be taught. His position: the core can't be transmitted directly — "We're not teaching them to have the personal characteristics required to be a successful social entrepreneur any more than a music teacher teaches the personal characteristics to be a gifted musician." But good teachers still matter; they "coax, encourage, inspire, reward, and model," and can *inspire courage* by exposing students to people like themselves who have started social ventures. He sits within the note's "counter-current" of MBAs using the degree as a springboard into the social sector, alongside [Muhammad Yunus](/notes/muhammad-yunus), [Wendy Kopp](/notes/wendy-kopp), and [Jed Emerson](/notes/jed-emerson).

**Context:** J. Gregory Dees (1950–2013) was a business academic widely regarded as the "father of social entrepreneurship education." He taught at Harvard, Stanford, and Duke, where he co-founded the Center for the Advancement of Social Entrepreneurship (CASE) at the Fuqua School of Business.
