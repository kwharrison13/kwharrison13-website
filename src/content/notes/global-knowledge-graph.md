# Global Knowledge Graph

Kyle's shorthand for the cumulative, interlinked body of human knowledge — and specifically for how ideas, citations, and reputations propagate (and distort) as they pass through it. The tag surfaces in [The Black Swan](/books/the-black-swan) alongside [Memetics](/notes/memetics), on a passage where [Nassim Nicholas Taleb](/notes/nassim-taleb) describes book reviewers anchoring on prior reviews of William Gaddis's *The Recognitions*, "even in their wording" — a worked example of how the network of references amplifies an initial signal rather than independently re-evaluating it. The same cluster of highlights covers Robert K. Merton's Matthew effect (initial advantage compounding through a career) and Taleb's account of why a few hundred words dominate English usage: the more a node is referenced, the cheaper it becomes to reference again.

The throughline is preferential attachment — knowledge, like wealth or word-frequency, accrues to what is already prominent, so the graph's structure is path-dependent and self-reinforcing rather than meritocratic.
