# Self-Serve

In [Product Led Growth — The End User Era](/books/product-led-growth-the-end-user-era), self-serve is a core PLG mechanism: a purchase path for buyers with a simple decision process who don't require human interaction, letting them try a product and buy it without going through a salesperson. The book frames adding this path into an existing customer journey as the lever that "will drive incremental product led growth." Its anchor data point is [SendGrid](/notes/sendgrid), where 98% of customers come in through the self-service engine — which led the company to "view the website and signup flow as part of the product, not separate from it." Freeing spend from sales let SendGrid reinvest 25%+ of revenue into R&D (vs. 15% at [CA](/notes/ca)) and run a leaner 6:1 engineer-to-PM ratio, while elevating [Pricing](/notes/pricing) into its own dedicated function.

**Context:** "Self-serve" (self-service) describes a sales motion in software where users sign up, onboard, and pay through the product itself rather than via a sales rep — a defining trait of product-led growth and freemium businesses.
