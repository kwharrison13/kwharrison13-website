# Databricks

Databricks shows up across Kyle's notes as the dominant incumbent of the modern data stack — the platform other data companies position themselves around. Kyle's diligence on [Starburst](/notes/starburst) frames Starburst's ambition as doing "for the business user what [Databricks](/notes/databricks) did for the data scientist," and lists Databricks (valued at $6B in those notes, "data scientist buyer") alongside [Snowflake ($SNOW)](/notes/snowflake-snow) and Confluent ($CFLT) as the set of platforms customers see Starburst measured against. The Deepnote pre-brief casts Databricks as both a competitive threat and an exit risk: Databricks "have their own notebook product... tightly coupled with the Databricks ecosystem" and had "casually offered to buy Deepnote," with one bear scenario being Deepnote getting acquired "for a menial amount of upside" by a larger data platform like Databricks.

In the meeting notes, Databricks appears as a marquee logo and ecosystem node — [Steve Malouf](/notes/steve-malouf) (Datadog) cites helping get calls at [Atlassian](/notes/atlassian) and Databricks as a go-to-market asset, and the Will Szczerbiak (Greycroft) call describes Databricks as "a great partner" relative to Snowflake. The Cultivating Cults - Research notes feature Databricks through Ari Kaplan, its Head of Evangelism, as a case study in how companies build evangelist-led cultures around an "emerging moment."

**Context:** Databricks is a data and AI platform company founded in 2013 by the original creators of Apache Spark out of UC Berkeley. It popularized the "lakehouse" architecture (unifying data lakes and warehouses) and is among the most valuable private software companies, competing closely with Snowflake.
