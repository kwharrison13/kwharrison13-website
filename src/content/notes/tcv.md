# TCV

TCV is a growth-equity firm where [Kyle Harrison](/notes/kyle-harrison) worked earlier in his career, before [Coatue](/notes/coatue), [Index Ventures](/notes/index-ventures), and [Contrary](/notes/contrary). In the September 13th, 2022 — Harry Stebbings Podcast Kyle describes TCV during his tenure as a "very 'private equity' style of investing" — "we were looking for diamonds in the rough" — and credits it with shaping his investing identity: the people at TCV "were never afraid to get their hands dirty," which instilled a lasting "we do the work" attitude. He maps his career as sampling investing styles "like Goldilocks": TCV (PE-style), Coatue (hedge-fund-style), and Index ("venture classic"). A colleague's later reflection noted Kyle had to "unlearn some of the hierarchy from TCV and Coatue," where he was in more of a support than decision-making role.

TCV also appears as the firm behind Kyle's TCV-era deal work. The mParticle — Diligence (2018) page captures TCV's fall-2018 diligence into the emerging [Customer Data Platform](/notes/customer-data-platform) (CDP) category — anchored on [mParticle](/notes/mparticle), with a priority list (per [Scott Kirk](/notes/scott-kirk)) spanning [Redpoint](/notes/redpoint), Lytics, SessionM, and [Segment](/notes/segment) — illustrating the firm's hands-dirty, thesis-driven category mapping. TCV partners also recur in Kyle's later survey research as named contacts (Joe Blundo, Aaron Ford, David Liang).

**Context:** TCV (Technology Crossover Ventures), founded in 1995, is a Menlo Park-based growth-equity firm known for late-stage technology investments in companies like Netflix, Spotify, and Airbnb.
