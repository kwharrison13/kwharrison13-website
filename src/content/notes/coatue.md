# Coatue

Coatue is the crossover/hedge-fund-style firm founded by [Philippe Laffont](/notes/philippe-laffont) where Kyle worked, and it recurs across his notes as both a source of investing lessons and a network of contacts. Its laser focus on large markets is the origin of Kyle's [TAM Arbitrage](/notes/tam-arbitrage) framing: "if you understand a market is bigger than others appreciate, you can pay higher prices and tolerate more burn" — early-stage investors chase big ideas and personalities, while Coatue chased big markets (per September 13th, 2022 — Harry Stebbings Podcast). The same notes flag Coatue's hedge-fund culture and its hiring of operating partners like Caryn Marooney and Dave Schneider, while [Erik Kriessmann](/notes/erik-kriessmann) characterized its talent function as unprogrammatic (strong people like [Jess Oynick](/notes/jess-oynick) underused).

[Inside the Rise and Fall of Coatue's Quant Fund](/notes/inside-the-rise-and-fall-of-coatue-s-quant-fund) documents a cautionary chapter: Coatue raised >$350M for its first quant fund in early 2019 (out of a data-science group started in 2014), only to return capital and wind it down within 15 months. Business Insider framed it as a culture failure as much as a performance one — a "bait and switch" with credentialed scientists doing grunt work under a brutal regime, plus the core flaw of buy-in at the top: Laffont, "a stellar stock-picker with no formal quant training," second-guessed signals and "wanted to make it more and more like a hedge fund." The breadwinner was a dollar-neutral credit-card data strategy that worked in 2018 but got arbitraged away as larger funds caught on. A long run of Coatue contacts also appears in Kyle's meetings (Gordon Tsai, Jeff Tang, Nick Gagnet, Rahul Chitturi) as deal-intel and sourcing calls.

**Context:** Coatue Management is a technology-focused investment firm founded by Philippe Laffont in 1999, originally a "Tiger Cub" hedge fund that later expanded into venture and growth investing.
