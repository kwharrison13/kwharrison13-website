# HashiCorp

HashiCorp shows up in Kyle's diligence as the maker of the **infrastructure-as-code and infrastructure-automation tooling** that modern, command-line-oriented engineering teams adopt. In the Noname Security - Diligence notes, [Dave Thompson](/notes/dave-thompson) of [Kong](/notes/kong) — describing his Linux-background, "command-line geeks" platform team — says they've adopted [Terraform](/notes/terraform) from HashiCorp, while noting that [Nomad](/notes/nomad) (HashiCorp's workload orchestrator) "isn't a good fit for their needs." HashiCorp also appears in the Identity & Access Management Diligence — 2021 dossier via Hashi Vault, its secrets-management product, in the secrets/IAM landscape.

**Context:** HashiCorp is an infrastructure-software company whose open-source-origin tools include Terraform (infrastructure as code), Vault (secrets management), Consul (service networking), and Nomad (workload orchestration). It went public in 2021 and was acquired by IBM (announced 2024).
