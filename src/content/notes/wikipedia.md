# Wikipedia

**One-line:** *The free, collectively-maintained encyclopedia Kyle treats as the canonical example of open-source knowledge — and of its governance and notability tensions.*

## What we know

- A free encyclopedia with 6.3mn articles; for comparison Encyclopedia Britannica never had more than ~100,000, even digitally. ([Matt Clancy](/notes/matt-clancy), *Free Knowledge and Innovation*, via Open-Source Knowledge - Research)
- Evidence it boosts innovation: references in articles published to Wikipedia got 91% more downstream citations on average than a control group, and scientists read and are influenced by it even when they don't cite it. ([Matt Clancy](/notes/matt-clancy), Open-Source Knowledge - Research)
- Increasingly built by individuals, not just communities ("the man behind a third of Wikipedia"), illustrating [Nadia Eghbal](/notes/nadia-eghbal)'s "online worlds built by individuals" thesis. (Open-Source Knowledge - Research)
- Governance tension: notability is "inherently subjective," and editing is gatekept by a small (~90% male) group of top editors; Wikipedia and the media reinforce each other. ([Catherine Yeo](/notes/catherine-yeo), *Wikipedia's Struggle with Creators*, via Open-Source Knowledge - Research)
- Named as a candidate for [Unbundling](/notes/unbundling) into specialized niche "front pages" — part of the market landscape behind the [Contrary Research](/essays/contrary-research) launch. (Open-Source Knowledge - Research)
