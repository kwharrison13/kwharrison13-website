# Wikipedia

**One-line definition:** *The free, collectively-maintained encyclopedia Kyle treats as the canonical example of open-source knowledge — and of its governance and notability tensions.*

## How sources describe it

- A free encyclopedia with 6.3mn articles; Britannica never exceeded ~100,000 even digitally. ([Matt Clancy](/notes/matt-clancy), *Free Knowledge and Innovation*)
- Evidence it boosts innovation: references in articles published to Wikipedia got 91% more citations than a control group; scientists read and are influenced by it even when they don't cite it.
- Built increasingly by individuals, not just communities ("the man behind a third of Wikipedia").
- Governance tension: notability is "inherently subjective," and editing is gatekept by a small (~90% male) group of top editors. Wikipedia and the media reinforce each other. ([Catherine Yeo](/notes/catherine-yeo))
- Candidate for [Unbundling](/notes/unbundling) into specialized niche "front pages."
