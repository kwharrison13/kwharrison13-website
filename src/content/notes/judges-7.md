# Judges 7

Judges 7 — Gideon's army cut from thousands to 300 — is used in Kyle's notes for two distinct lessons. In Miscellaneous Scriptures it anchors a missionary-work chain: "Judges 7:2–12 – We would rather have 300 good missionaries than 10,000 average ones" and "Judges 7:17 – Look on me and do likewise," reading the winnowed band as a model of quality and leadership-by-example over raw numbers.

In The Lehonti Principle, Judges 7:2 appears in a cluster on "not trusting in the arm of flesh" — God deliberately shrinks Gideon's force "lest Israel vaunt themselves" and claim "Mine own hand hath saved me." It is grouped with Deuteronomy 8:17, [Alma 14](/notes/alma-14):11, and [D&C 59](/notes/dandc-59):2 to make the point that victory and provision come from God, not from human strength, reinforcing the habit of acknowledging God's hand in all things.

**Context:** Judges 7 recounts how God told Gideon his army of 32,000 was too large, reducing it to 300 men (selected by how they drank water) so that Israel could not boast the victory over Midian was its own — a classic Old Testament text on divine deliverance over human numbers.

## Where this appears

- Miscellaneous Scriptures — Gideon's 300 as a missionary-work model: quality over quantity, "look on me and do likewise"
- The Lehonti Principle — Judges 7:2 in the "not trusting in the arm of flesh" cluster on acknowledging God's hand
