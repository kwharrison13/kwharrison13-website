# Council of Fifty

In [Brigham Young — Pioneer Prophet](/books/brigham-young-pioneer-prophet), the Council of Fifty appears as the governing body [Brigham Young](/notes/brigham-young) resuscitated in response to the revocation of the Mormon charter. Its members sometimes called it the "living constitution" — a phrase the book reads as shorthand for governance of the church by *ongoing revelation* rather than by any written set of scriptures or laws. It sits alongside Young's valuation of the "living oracles" above fixed written scripture, making it a concrete institutional expression of the wider Mormon preference for continuing authority over codified text.

**Context:** The Council of Fifty was a secretive governing council organized by Joseph Smith in 1844 and revived under Brigham Young, intended as the political/temporal arm of the Latter-day Saint movement and envisioned as a theocratic body to administer a future Kingdom of God.

## Where this appears

- [Brigham Young — Pioneer Prophet](/books/brigham-young-pioneer-prophet) — Young revives the Council as a "living constitution," governance by ongoing revelation, after the charter's revocation; tied to his preference for "living oracles" over written scripture.
