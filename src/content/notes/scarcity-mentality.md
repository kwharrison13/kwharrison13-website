# Scarcity Mentality

**One-line:** *The behavioral-economics finding (Mullainathan & Shafir, cited in [Utopia For Realists](/books/utopia-for-realists)) that scarcity itself narrows cognitive bandwidth — distinct from the religious/abundance sense of [Scarcity](/notes/scarcity) elsewhere in the wiki.*

The claim reframes the "why do the poor make dumb decisions?" question: people aren't poor because they make bad decisions; they make bad decisions because scarcity consumes attention, collapsing the long-term perspective. It explains why financial-education programs barely move outcomes ("like teaching someone to swim and then throwing them in a stormy sea") and why an unconditional [Universal Basic Income](/notes/universal-basic-income) — which removes the scarcity itself — outperforms nudges.
