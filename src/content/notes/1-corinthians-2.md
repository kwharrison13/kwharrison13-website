# 1 Corinthians 2

A chapter of Paul's first epistle to the Corinthians, used across Kyle's gospel study as a proof-text for learning by the Spirit rather than by intellect alone. In the [Come, Follow Me](/notes/come-follow-me) week ["We Are Responsible for Our Own Learning"](December 31–January 6, 2019. "We Are Responsible for Our Own Learning"), Kyle cites verses 9–11 — "Eye hath not seen... the things which God hath prepared for them that love him. But God hath revealed them unto us by his Spirit; for the Spirit searcheth all things, yea, the deep things of God" — alongside [John 7](/notes/john-7):17 and [Acts 17](/notes/acts-17):11 to support the theme that spiritual knowledge comes through the Spirit and personal seeking, not just study.

In the Alma 30–31 week ("The Virtue of the Word of God"), Kyle pulls verse 16 — "For who hath known the mind of the Lord, that he may instruct him? But we have the mind of Christ" — as a cross-reference. In both passages 1 Corinthians 2 functions as the New Testament anchor for the idea that the natural man cannot grasp the things of God without the Spirit.

## Where this appears
