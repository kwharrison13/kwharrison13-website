# Jay Barney

**One-line:** *Strategy scholar known for the "costly-to-imitate resources and capabilities" theory of competitive advantage; co-author of [Invisible Companies](/notes/invisible-companies).*

## What we know

- Co-authored (with Haiyang Zhang and [Jerry Neumann](/notes/jerry-neumann)) "Invisible Firms, Competitive Neglect, and Sustained Profit Generation," the University of Utah / Harvard Business School working paper behind [Invisible Companies](/notes/invisible-companies), arguing some businesses earn outsized profits for decades not via a moat but because competitors never notice the opportunity ("competitive neglect"). (source: [Invisible Companies](/notes/invisible-companies))
- His own resource-based theory of competitive advantage is cited alongside [Michael Porter](/notes/michael-porter)'s barriers-to-entry framework as one of the two classic explanations the paper argues is insufficient to explain invisible companies. (source: [Invisible Companies](/notes/invisible-companies))
