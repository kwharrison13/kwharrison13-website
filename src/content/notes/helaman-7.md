# Helaman 7

A chapter of the Book of Mormon, the opening of Kyle's [Come Follow Me](/notes/come-follow-me) study of [Helaman 7](/notes/helaman-7)–[Helaman 12](/notes/helaman-12) under the theme "Remember the Lord." The chapter records Nephi returning from the north — rejected, unable to stay among the people (Helaman 7:3) — to find "Gadianton robbers filling the judgment-seats," having usurped power and "laying aside the commandments of God" (Helaman 7:4). His heart "swollen with sorrow," he laments from his garden tower (Helaman 7:6).

Kyle's central note on the chapter centers on Nephi's lament: "Oh, that I could have had my days in the days when my father Nephi first came out of the land of Jerusalem… then were his people easy to be entreated, firm to keep the commandments of God" (Helaman 7:7-9). Kyle reads this as a chain of longing — current Nephi wants to be like old Nephi, old Nephi wanted to be like Isaiah — and connects it to modern members wishing they could have lived in the days of [Joseph Smith](/notes/joseph-smith) and the [Restoration](/notes/restoration). His conclusion: "in every age and dispensation there is righteousness and there is wickedness… it is up to us to find our path in that time." Nephi diagnoses the people's deafness — "It is because you have hardened your hearts" (Helaman 7:18) — echoing [1 Nephi 17](/notes/1-nephi-17):45's "swift to do iniquity but slow to remember the Lord."

**Context:** Helaman 7 is the seventh chapter of the Book of Helaman in the Book of Mormon; it begins the account of the prophet Nephi's ministry and his tower-top lament over Nephite wickedness.
