# Boldness

In Teaching Repentance, boldness is the missionary virtue of opening your mouth and declaring repentance — but reframed away from "hellfire and brimstone." The charge to "say nothing but repentance unto this generation" (D&C 6:9) is read as a charge to *extend invitations and opportunities to repent*, not to drag or push. The key reframe is that **boldness flows from love**: "Never be afraid to be bold, because each invitation, each statement, each question is an expression of your love for them" ([The Flow](/notes/the-flow)), and "perfect love casteth out fear" (1 John 4:17–18). The note labels this "Sweet Boldness: The Inviting Spirit."

The page pairs boldness with restraint, quoting Alma 38:12 — "Use boldness, but not overbearance; and also see that you bridle all your passions, that ye may be filled with love" — tying it to [Self-Mastery](/notes/self-mastery). The master-teacher exemplars are [Ammon](/notes/ammon), who "with boldness" expounded the plan of redemption to King Lamoni (Alma 18:36, 39) through invitations and commitments rather than overbearance, and [Nephi](/notes/nephi) son of Helaman (3 Nephi 7:18). [Harold B. Lee](/notes/harold-b-lee) supplies the standard: teach so plainly and assuredly that "no one can misunderstand" the message.

## Where this appears

- Teaching Repentance — boldness as inviting, love-driven declaration of repentance (the "Sweet Boldness / Inviting Spirit" thread); exemplified by Ammon and Nephi, bounded by "not overbearance"
