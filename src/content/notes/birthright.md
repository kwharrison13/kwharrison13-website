# Birthright

In Kyle's topical study of the Abrahamic Covenant, the birthright is the double portion of inheritance and the patriarchal status of the family — the right to lead and bless the covenant lineage. The study traces how the birthright was lost by the unrighteous firstborn and passed instead to [Ephraim](/notes/ephraim), the younger son of Joseph (Genesis 48:17–20), while Judah became chief ruler (1 Chronicles 5:1–2). Inheritance flows from father to son through covenant, and all may enter the covenant by birth or by adoption, but only the repentant receive its blessings.

The birthright carries responsibility, not just privilege. The study identifies the LDS church as made up mostly of the house of [Ephraim](/notes/ephraim), and frames the holders of the birthright as charged to gather Israel, build the kingdom, and spread the gospel before the second coming — what the page calls "the birthright responsibility," tied to the larger [Gathering of Israel](/notes/gathering-of-israel).
