# Agriculture

Agriculture is a topic Kyle has tracked as a reading thread and as an investable sector, though it remains lightly synthesized in the wiki. It originated as a "Quick Capture" reading log from a June 29th, 2021 Roam export — a list of saved links on wheat genomics, the farmer trade-war crisis, vertical/hydroponic farming, the environmental footprint of food (especially beef), and agtech startups (Terviva, Siete, Indigo Ag, Renewal Mill) — which is mirrored on the sibling [Food](/notes/food) concept page. As a sector it shows up concretely in [Village Capital](/notes/village-capital)'s portfolio, which runs one accelerator cohort per sector across fintech, education, health, **agriculture/agtech**, and energy/cleantech, investing in ~2 companies per sector per year.

It also appears in a very different register in [What Is ChatGPT Doing and Why Does It Work?](/notes/what-is-chatgpt-doing-and-why-does-it-work): in [Stephen Wolfram](/notes/stephen-wolfram)'s decomposition of the sentence "I bought two pounds of apples for my children," agriculture is named as one of the deep human concepts ("apples") encoded in ordinary language — alongside [Capitalism](/notes/capitalism), [Family](/notes/family), and [Parenting](/notes/parenting) — illustrating how much conceptual structure sits beneath everyday speech.
