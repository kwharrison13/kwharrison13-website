# Matthew 13

The chapter Kyle treats as the master text on the **Parables**. In his topical study, [Matthew 13](/notes/matthew-13) supplies the worked examples for the LDS distinction between the Kingdom of God and the [Kingdom of Heaven](/notes/kingdom-of-heaven): the sower (different kinds of ground/people — "it is so much easier to care for the PLACEMENT of the seeds"), the pearl of great price versus treasure in a field (actively searching versus stumbling upon — the difference between seeking the gospel and meeting the missionaries by chance), the mustard seed versus leaven (external church growth versus internal member growth), and the net versus wheat-and-tares (letting good and bad show themselves before the final separation, which Christ performs). The closing principle Kyle pulls out is that parables give "formulas for personal application" — generalities to fortify personal worthiness, not always specifics.

The chapter recurs in two other places. The October 12–18, 2020. 3 Nephi 20–26: "Ye Are the Children of the Covenant" week uses the pearl-of-great-price verses ([Matthew 13](/notes/matthew-13):45–46) under the prompt "Are you building things that will last?" And in The Lehonti Principle, Kyle files [Matthew 13](/notes/matthew-13):57 — "a prophet is not without honour, save in his own country" — as the scriptural anchor for what he calls "The Nazarene Principle," alongside [Mark 6](/notes/mark-6), [Luke 4](/notes/luke-4), and [John 4](/notes/john-4).
