# Teaching by the Spirit

Teaching by the Spirit is the gospel-study principle that effective teaching is not a matter of technique or eloquence but of the Holy Ghost supplying the words and changing hearts. Its scriptural anchor is D&C 42:14 — "The Spirit shall be given unto you by the prayer of faith; and if ye receive not the Spirit, ye shall not teach" — which both of Kyle's sources cite as the load-bearing text.

In Find To Teach, Kyle frames this as the interior **Process** of teaching (be worthy, open your mouth, let the Spirit supply the words, teach what the person needs), running in parallel with the outward **Pattern** of a teaching visit (rapport → teach → testify → invite → promise → follow up). Alma 18's account of Ammon is the model of the process in action, with [Brigham Young](/notes/brigham-young) grounding the worker's confidence to act on best judgment when no specific revelation comes. In The Candle of the Lord — Boyd K. Packer, the same verse undergirds Packer's broader teaching that revelation is given "by the prayer of faith" and that "the ordinary member of the Church, sustained by the Spirit, can do the work of the Lord" — illustrated by his account of losing and regaining the gift of promptings once he did "as I was bidden to do by the Spirit."

## Where this appears

- Find To Teach — teaching by the Spirit as the interior Process (vs. the outward Pattern), anchored in D&C 42:14 and Alma 18
- The Candle of the Lord — Boyd K. Packer — Packer's teaching that revelation comes "by the prayer of faith"; "if ye receive not the Spirit, ye shall not teach"
