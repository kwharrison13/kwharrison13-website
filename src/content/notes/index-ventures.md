# Index Ventures

[Index Ventures](/notes/index-ventures) is the venture firm whose sourcing and diligence flow runs through a large slice of Kyle's late-2021 deal notes — it functions in the corpus as the institutional context around which a cluster of company pages, expert calls, and a co-investment vehicle are organized. The firm's 2021 attempt to pre-empt a Series C in [Pave](/notes/pave) (a ~$50M lead in a $100M round at a ~$1B valuation) is documented in detail in Pave Diligence — 2021, including the partnership pre-brief, the give-to-get compensation-data thesis, and the fact that 40+ Index portfolio companies (Instabase, Anrok, [Discord](/notes/discord), Newfront Insurance, [Scale AI](/notes/scale-ai), Boulevard, Applyboard) were already contributing to Pave's dataset.

Index's diligence machinery surfaces a recurring set of companies in the wiki: an Index [Behavioral Health](/notes/behavioral-health) expert-call screener brought up [Grow Therapy](/notes/grow-therapy) alongside [Headway](/notes/headway), SonderMind, Spring Health, and others; a separate sourcing call log raised [Adyen](/notes/adyen) as the canonical growth-thesis example and connected [CapitalG](/notes/capitalg)'s shut-down [Uptycs](/notes/uptycs) term sheet; and a [Spellbound](/notes/spellbound) intro to [Akshaya Dinesh](/notes/akshaya-dinesh) was routed through Index and Common Room. Kyle is an LP in the [Index Co-Invest](/notes/index-co-invest) vehicle, with a multi-year capital-call and loan-repayment schedule documented in the December 2021 notes. Individual Index relationships in the corpus include [Benjy Boateng](/notes/benjy-boateng) (BD / executive-relationships playbook) and [Erik Kriessmann](/notes/erik-kriessmann) (building a VC talent function).

**Context:** Index Ventures is a global venture capital firm founded in 1996, with offices in San Francisco, London, and Geneva, known for early and growth-stage investments in companies such as Adyen, Datadog, Figma, and Revolut.

### Growth Planning — "2020 Growth Plan: Stories vs. Snapshots" (Dec 2020)

- 2020 Index Growth Plan deck (PDF) — `wiki/attachments/index-ventures/growth-planning.pdf` (39 slides, Dec 2020)

The founding strategy deck for the **Index Growth** practice, built as Kyle was joining — the "ideal candidate profile" slide is literally [Kyle Harrison](/notes/kyle-harrison)'s LinkedIn (Growth Investor at [Coatue](/notes/coatue), "1 year Coatue Growth + 3 years TCV Growth"), and the plan schedules "Kyle joins in January." It's the blueprint that precedes his 2021 tenure documented in the working notes above.

**Central thesis — "The Movie vs. the Snapshot."** Index's edge is the deep relationship it forms with founders in their *early* days (the "movie"), which later-stage funds pricing off a "snapshot" can't replicate. The plan is to systematically convert that venture-fund *alpha* into growth-stage wins — provoked by a partner at Andreessen: *"Why are you guys letting other funds build 5x+ returns on the back of your hard work?"*

**Why now — an inflection point.** The growth market is professionalizing: more money chasing fewer deals, bigger teams and diligence budgets, a race to preemptions, and tech exits large enough that even $1B+ rounds can return the fund. The goal is to up-level rigor, process, and bandwidth while preserving Index's core strengths (teamwork, agility, deep relationships, early-stage success).

**Problems → proposed solutions:**

| Problem today | Proposed solution |
|---|---|
| Lack focus on growth (team whipsaws on fund pacing) | Associates specialize by stage; dedicated "Growth Pod" ("majors"); new monthly growth meeting |
| Not enough bandwidth for deep growth diligence | Hire 3–4 investors/geo; give investors "diligence superpowers" (vendor stable) |
| React to rounds, caught in quick decision loops | Quarterly portfolio reviews; off-cycle portco check-ins; Conviction List 2.0 with ownership |
| Conviction list is anemic | Growth Pods own a live, per-geo conviction list — fresh eyes on familiar names |
| Diligence is inconsistent / ad hoc | A consistent "goldilocks" process and memo template |
