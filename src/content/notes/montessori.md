# Montessori

The child-led education method developed by [Maria Montessori](/notes/maria-montessori) — and, across Kyle's education reading, the recurring exemplar of pedagogy that actually works yet stays stubbornly un-deployed. In [It's Time To Build](/notes/it-s-time-to-build), [Marc Andreessen](/notes/marc-andreessen) uses it as the indictment: the *last major K-12 innovation was Montessori (1960s)*, after which 50 years of education research never reached deployment — the gap between what we know works (one-to-one [Tutoring](/notes/tutoring), the [Bloom two-sigma effect](/notes/bloom-two-sigma-effect)) and what we've built.

[The Science of Learning](/notes/the-science-of-learning) supplies the evidence behind the reputation: *"Montessori students were more effective at directing their own learning, and by adolescence were much faster and more likely to correct their own mistakes,"* with different patterns of behavior and brain activity than traditionally-schooled kids. It places Montessori in the education-reform lineage alongside Dewey and Piaget, and clusters it with project-based approaches, a well-sequenced [Curriculum](/notes/curriculum), and a [Growth Mindset](/notes/growth-mindset) as environment-and-pedagogy factors that measurably improve learning. [Why Can't School Be Fun?](/notes/why-can-t-school-be-fun) makes it the prescriptive model — *"Montessori points the way"* — citing intellectual and physical freedom, growth through trial and error instead of instruction, mixed-age interaction, and no grades as *"a liberating system that nurtures imagination and an entrepreneurial spirit,"* grouped with [Project-Based Learning](/notes/project-based-learning), [Alternative Education](/notes/alternative-education), and [Sora Schools](/notes/sora-schools).

**Context:** The Montessori method, developed by Italian physician and educator Maria Montessori in the early 20th century, emphasizes self-directed activity, hands-on learning, mixed-age classrooms, and child-chosen work within a prepared environment. (General background.)
