# Hewlett-Packard

Hewlett-Packard surfaces across Kyle's notes in three distinct registers. As a career credential, it appears in [Atanu Dasgupta](/notes/atanu-dasgupta)'s background — he was "Head of Product Development at Hewlett-Packard" before his later product-engineering roles. As a case of [Financialization](/notes/financialization), [The Man Who Broke Capitalism](/books/the-man-who-broke-capitalism) lists HP among the industrials (with John Deere and Caterpillar) that copied GE's playbook, creating finance divisions and "making substantial profits not by selling their products, but by lending money."

Most pointedly, HP is [Charlie Munger](/notes/charlie-munger)'s standing example of a flawed hiring decision driven by psychological bias. In [Poor Charlie's Almanack](/books/poor-charlie-s-almanack) (and the [Charlie Munger — The Psychology of Human Misjudgment](/notes/charlie-munger-the-psychology-of-human-misjudgment) long-read), Munger argues that "man is likely to be overinfluenced by face-to-face impressions," making "a marvelous presenter" dangerous under modern executive search. He believes HP "made a bad decision when it chose Ms. [Carly] Fiorina" as CEO — and that the error would not have been made had HP "taken the methodological precautions it would have taken if it knew more psychology."

**Context:** Hewlett-Packard, founded in 1939 by Bill Hewlett and Dave Packard in a Palo Alto garage, is a foundational Silicon Valley technology company. Carly Fiorina served as CEO from 1999 to 2005; the company later split into HP Inc. and Hewlett Packard Enterprise in 2015.
