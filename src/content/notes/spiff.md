# Spiff

Spiff is a sales-commission / incentive-compensation software company that appears throughout Kyle's 2021 CaptivateIQ diligence as the closest mid-market competitor. In the category framing, Spiff and QuotaPath are the "drag-and-drop UI" mid-market players, distinct from the "heavily customized legacy" incumbents (Xactly, CallidusCloud) and CaptivateIQ's "Excel in the Cloud" approach. The diligence is consistently negative on Spiff's product depth: CaptivateIQ "won 8 Spiff customers" that year, Spiff ran ~98% NDR and ~30% gross margins (vs. CaptivateIQ's 136% NDR and 70%+ GM), and the November 8th, 2021 — Global Deal Flow notes say Spiff "clearly broke away"-from only to be passed, planning to go from $2M to $10M ARR. Expert and customer reference calls repeatedly describe Spiff demos as "very underwhelming" and "nascent," with implementation teams flagged as "unreliable" — though several note Spiff's interface is "vastly more aesthetically pleasing" than Xactly's and that it won deals on branding, word-of-mouth, and marketing (Armand Farrokh: "Spiff is known to have better branding / investment into marketing").

Spiff also appears outside the CaptivateIQ lens: in November 18th, 2021 — Cody Guymon (Qualtrics), a buyer reports having "just signed with Spiff" as their first enterprise client, and in Pave Diligence — 2021 Shilo Riddle at Spiff is logged as a reference contact. Mark Schopmeyer (CaptivateIQ) lists Spiff among the direct competitors in the November 2nd, 2021 — Mark Schopmeyer (CaptivateIQ) call.

**Context:** Spiff is a Utah-based sales-commission / incentive-compensation-management software company (founded 2017); it was acquired by Salesforce in 2024.

## Where this appears
