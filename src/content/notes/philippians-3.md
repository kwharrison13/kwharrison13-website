# Philippians 3

In The Reasons The Spirit Withdraws, Philippians 3:15 ("Be perfect. God will remind you if you are otherwise") is cited to make the point that the laws and one's behavior exist at one level, and the Spirit is lost when behavior sinks — i.e., when it falls into sin. The verse sits in a cluster with [D&C 66](/notes/dandc-66):3 (clean, but repent) and [Luke 2](/notes/luke-2):52 (Jesus increased in wisdom and stature), reinforcing the study's theme that ongoing repentance and continued learning are conditions for retaining the [Holy Ghost](/notes/holy-ghost). The note pairs it with [Brigham Young](/notes/brigham-young)'s simple repentance prayer: "Please forgive me and inform me as to anything else I have done."

## Where this appears

- The Reasons The Spirit Withdraws — verse 15 ("Be perfect") frames the idea that God reminds us where behavior falls short, and the Spirit withdraws when behavior sinks into sin.
