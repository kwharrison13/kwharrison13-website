# Ether 12

Ether 12 — Moroni's discourse on faith — is the chapter Kyle's gospel study leans on most for the **faith → hope → strength** sequence, and his notes cite a handful of its verses repeatedly. **Ether 12:4** ("hope cometh of faith, maketh an anchor to the souls of men") is his anchor text for hope: it opens Hope — Topical Study and is quoted in full in The Whole Armor of God. **Ether 12:6** ("ye receive no witness until after the trial of your faith") grounds Faith — Topical Study, paired with [Alma 32](/notes/alma-32):21 on faith as hope rather than knowledge.

The chapter's two best-known verses carry its other themes. **Ether 12:27** — "I give unto men weakness that they may be humble … then will I make weak things become strong" — appears in Christ Is Risen; Faith in Him Will Move Mountains — Russell M. Nelson alongside the claim that "lazy learners and lax disciples will always struggle to muster even a particle of faith." **Ether 12:30** (faith moving the mount Zerin — God "workest after men have faith") is the title-verse of [Russell M. Nelson](/notes/russell-m-nelson)'s "faith … will move mountains" talk. Finally, **Ether 12:21** ties the chapter to the [Brother of Jared](/notes/brother-of-jared), who through faith "could no longer be kept without the veil" — used in Eye single to the glory of God to argue it is "not beyond our capacity to see the face of God in this life."

**Context:** Ether 12 is a chapter in the Book of Mormon, part of Moroni's abridgment of the Jaredite record (the book of Ether). It is one of the most-cited LDS scriptural treatments of faith — including the principle that the witness comes only after the trial of faith, and that God turns acknowledged weakness into strength.
