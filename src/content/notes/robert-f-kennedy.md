# Robert F. Kennedy

Across Kyle's corpus Robert F. Kennedy appears as the source of two oft-quoted lines that anchor distinct arguments. In [The Righteous Mind](/books/the-righteous-mind), [Jonathan Haidt](/notes/jonathan-haidt) closes with Kennedy's "There are those that look at things the way they are, and ask why? I dream of things that never were, and ask why not?" — the aspirational note Haidt ends on after a book about moral tribalism.

The other line recurs as a critique of how progress is measured: "The gross national product measures everything except that which makes life worthwhile." [Utopia For Realists](/books/utopia-for-realists) quotes it as [Rutger Bregman](/notes/rutger-bregman) builds his case against [GDP](/notes/gdp) as the master metric, and Kyle carries the same quotation into his Toil We Must - Research notes, citing it (via Scientific American's "GDP is the wrong tool for measuring what matters") in service of an argument about work and what economies should optimize for.

**Context:** Robert F. Kennedy (1925–1968) was U.S. Attorney General under his brother John F. Kennedy and later a U.S. Senator from New York; he was assassinated during his 1968 presidential campaign. The "gross national product" passage is from his March 1968 address at the University of Kansas.
