# Goldman Sachs

Global investment bank that surfaces across Kyle's notes in three distinct roles. In the Watershed Climate Diligence — 2021 dossier it appears as both a sustainability customer and a partnership node: Goldman is "in pretty deep" with Watershed but hadn't yet been engaged on facilities/operational-footprint measurement (its sustainability work was being done through one of the big consultancies), with [Kara Mangone](/notes/kara-mangone) and Roy Joseph (Division Head of Facilities) named as contacts and Goldman's own ESG report cited. In a January 2022 call, Nikita Parekh was at Goldman Sachs; the conversation centered on pricing discipline — passing on a deal where a company wanted $1.5B on $5M ARR — and on the unusually high bar of raising a $5B fund "with outside capital for the first time," effectively as a first-time fund despite Goldman's scale.

Goldman also appears as a career credential: [Tyler Payne](/notes/tyler-payne) did oil & gas research at Goldman (and interned at JP Morgan) before the market turned when he finished.

**Context:** The Goldman Sachs Group is a New York–headquartered global investment bank and financial-services firm, active in investment banking, asset and wealth management, and markets.
