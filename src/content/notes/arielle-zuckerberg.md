# Arielle Zuckerberg

An early-stage investor at [Long Journey Ventures](/notes/long-journey-ventures), where she invests alongside [Cyan Banister](/notes/cyan-banister) and [Lee Jacobs](/notes/lee-jacobs) (a Shack 15 member in the Ferry Building). On a June 6th, 2022 call with Kyle — logged during his [Contrary Capital](/notes/contrary)-era sourcing — Zuckerberg laid out the firm's distinctive model: checks of $800K–$1M (smaller at pre-seed), deliberately *not* taking the whole round or "elbowing people out," and competing on **speed** rather than the standard "value add" pitch — willing to commit in the room and become a founder's first big believer despite having five venture partners.

The throughline she emphasized was **values over value-add**: Long Journey leads with a sharply articulated set of values ("Chase the magic weirdly," "Be a bobe" — Yiddish for grandma, "Not monopoly money"), reasoning that the right founders respond with "you're my people" while the wrong fit self-selects out — "such a 'hell yeah' for that identity group." She also described the firm's backing by a deep roster of [Super Angels](/notes/super-angels) (Alfred Lin via [Sequoia Capital](/notes/sequoia), Peter Thiel, Marc Andreessen, Chris Dixon, Keith Rabois, David Sacks, Lee Fixel, Lenny Rachitsky, Andrew Chen, and others), and offered candid asides on peers — surprise that [Sarah Cannon](/notes/sarah-cannon) was worried about Index's future, and on [Coatue](/notes/coatue): "You're still an employee. Not a partner."
