# Naval Ravikant

Naval Ravikant is an [AngelList](/notes/angellist) co-founder, investor, and aphorist whose framings recur across Kyle's corpus as canonical sources on contrarianism, conviction, and habit. The most load-bearing is his definition of the **optimistic contrarian**, drawn from [The Navalmanack](https://www.navalmanack.com/): "A contrarian isn't one who always objects—that's a conformist of a different sort. A contrarian reasons independently from the ground up and resists pressure to conform... Optimistic contrarians are the rarest breed." Kyle uses this in [Conviction-Led Contrarianism](/essays/conviction-led-contrarianism) to argue that reflexive anti-conformism is itself a kind of conformism — true [Contrarianism](/notes/contrarianism) is reasoning from [first principles](/notes/first-principles-thinking), not opposition for its own sake, and the independent reasoning that "resists pressure to conform" is the [Conviction](/notes/conviction) half of the frame.

His aphorisms also surface inside Kyle's book notes. [Atomic Habits](/books/atomic-habits) ([James Clear](/notes/james-clear)) quotes him twice: "To write a great book, you must first become the book" and "The trick to doing anything is first cultivating a desire for it" (Clear's point that desire, not intelligence, prompts behavior). The compiled collection of his thinking, [The Almanack of Naval Ravikant](/books/the-almanack-of-naval-ravikant) (by [Eric Jorgenson](/notes/eric-jorgenson)), sits in Kyle's read library, and the [Knowledge Project interview](/notes/naval-ravikant-the-angel-philosopher) with [Shane Parrish](/notes/shane-parrish) is an archived clipping in the corpus.

**Context:** Naval Ravikant is an Indian-American entrepreneur and angel investor — co-founder and former chairman of AngelList — known for early bets on companies like Uber and Twitter and for widely shared writing on wealth, judgment, and happiness (e.g. his "How to Get Rich" tweetstorm). He hosts a podcast and is one of the most-quoted figures in tech aphorism.
