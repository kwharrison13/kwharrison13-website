# Global Pride Cycle

The "global pride cycle" is Kyle's own phrase, coined while reading [Reinventing Knowledge — From Alexandria To The Internet](/books/reinventing-knowledge-from-alexandria-to-the-internet). He sketches it as a recurring civilizational arc: **globalization → prosperity → equality → suspicion → nationalism → tribalism → war.** Kyle's marginal note frames it through the lens of American power — "Crazy the global impact a nation can have. What happens if America falls?" — and the book's synthesis positions the United States as "Greek-philosophical-democratic and therefore inherently combative," the modern node in a talent-marketplace cycle that swings between multicultural competition (the Ptolemies, the Abbasid House of Wisdom) and violent unification (Qin, Rome, Alexander).

In the book's Key Takeaways, Kyle flags this as a Harari-style move — "the 'global pride cycle' sketch is a Harari-style move applied to knowledge institutions" — explicitly connecting it to the civilizational-arc framing of [Sapiens](/books/sapiens) and [Homo Deus](/books/homo-deus). The cycle is a lens on how knowledge institutions and prosperity eventually breed the suspicion and tribalism that unwind them, rather than a claim attested outside Kyle's reading of this one book.
