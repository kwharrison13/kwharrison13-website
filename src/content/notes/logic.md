# Logic

In Kyle's notes, logic appears as a tool whose power and limits sit in tension. [Will Durant](/notes/will-durant) is quoted to the effect that "logic is an invention of man and may be ignored by the universe" — a caution that human reasoning systems do not bind reality. The counterpoint is [Robert McNamara](/notes/robert-mcnamara), the Secretary of Defense who "viewed the world as a big math problem," wanted everything quantified, and built his career on the belief that any problem could be solved if you obeyed the cold truth of statistics and logic. The pairing frames logic as indispensable yet dangerous when treated as a complete model of the world.

**Context:** Logic is the formal study of valid inference — the principles by which conclusions follow from premises — foundational to philosophy, mathematics, and computer science.
