# 1 Peter 1

A New Testament chapter cited in Kyle's Faith — Topical Study notes. There, 1 Peter 1:8 anchors a discussion of belief as a power that "can wrought miracles" — Peter describing those who love and rejoice in Christ "whom having not seen, ye love; in whom, though now ye see him not, yet believing, ye rejoice with joy unspeakable and full of glory." The note pairs it with the centurion's faith in [Matthew 8](/notes/matthew-8):8, 13.

**Context:** 1 Peter 1 is the opening of Peter's first epistle, addressing scattered believers about the living hope of salvation, the trial of faith being more precious than gold, and loving Christ though they have not seen him.
