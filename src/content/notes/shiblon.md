# Shiblon

Shiblon is one of [Alma](/notes/alma)'s three sons addressed in Alma 36–Alma 38, the focus of Kyle's [Come, Follow Me](/notes/come-follow-me) study for July 20–26, 2020. Where Alma 36 records Alma's counsel to [Helaman](/notes/helaman) and Alma 39–42 corrects [Corianton](/notes/corianton), Alma 38 is Alma's brief, approving charge to Shiblon. The verse Kyle pulls is Alma 38:5 — *"as much as ye shall put your trust in God even so much ye shall be delivered out of your trials, and your troubles, and your afflictions, and ye shall be lifted up at the last day."* The chapter sits within the week's larger themes: the promise that keeping the commandments brings prosperity, "by small and simple things are great things brought to pass," and the charge to "bridle all your passions."

**Context:** In the Book of Mormon, Shiblon is a son of Alma the Younger, described as a faithful and steady servant; Alma 38 is the shortest of Alma's three sermons to his sons and the only one with no rebuke.
