# Operations

The hands-on running of a business — the operating proficiency an entrepreneur or CEO builds over time. In [On the Nature of Long-Term Holds](/notes/on-the-nature-of-long-term-holds), the early stages of operating a company are described as "awkward and embryonic," with a CEO needing roughly four to five years (the [10,000-hour](/notes/outliers-why-some-people-succeed-and-some-don-t) threshold) to reach a rhythm and full proficiency.

## Where this appears

- [On the Nature of Long-Term Holds](/notes/on-the-nature-of-long-term-holds) — the long ramp to operational proficiency as a new CEO is part of the case for holding businesses for the long term.
