# Don MacAskill

Don MacAskill is the founder and CEO of [SmugMug](/notes/smugmug), the image-sharing and hosting company, and in Kyle's wiki he is the canonical example of the [Alternative Company Buying](/notes/alternative-company-buying) / long-term-hold philosophy. Per *On the Nature of Long-Term Holds*, MacAskill built SmugMug (founded 2002, ~$66M revenue, ~210 employees, a 2020 *Forbes* Small Giant) **without raising external capital** — choosing a customer-paid business model over the venture ethos of the Silicon Valley he sits in. His views were shaped by his father and partner Chris MacAskill, whose company Fatbrain.com went public and was acquired by Barnes & Noble.com; watching that experience taught Don to liken being public to "the Wizard of Oz... everything is different behind the curtain."

His most-quoted line in Kyle's notes is the case against building for an exit: *"When an exit is always in your mind, it is harder to be operational and customer-centric, it becomes all about financial performance... There are many ripple effects when you are all about an exit. We are building an enduring company that I control and can determine where we are going."* MacAskill frames himself as "a very, very long-term holder" motivated by purpose, the people he works with, and the joy of building — not by a liquidity event. The [Alternative Company Buying](/notes/alternative-company-buying) concept page distills this as the SmugMug/MacAskill model: an owner-controlled, customer-centric company that deliberately avoids outside capital and is held to operate and grow indefinitely rather than sold.
