# Mosiah 5

A chapter in the Book of Mormon (Mosiah), drawn on in Kyle's General Conference- October 2021 notes. The cited verse is Mosiah 5:2, where King Benjamin's people respond with one voice that they believe his words and know their truth "because of the Spirit of the Lord Omnipotent, which has wrought a mighty change in us, or in our hearts, that we have no more disposition to do evil, but to do good continually." In Kyle's notes it sits alongside [Mosiah 3](/notes/mosiah-3):19 (the "natural man" / "putteth off the natural man" passage) as part of King Benjamin's discourse on the mighty change of heart.

**Context:** Mosiah 5 is the chapter of King Benjamin's address (in the Book of Mormon) in which his people enter into a covenant and are told they will be called "the children of Christ" — a foundational LDS text on taking upon oneself the name of Christ and on conversion as a change of heart.

## Where this appears

- General Conference- October 2021 — Mosiah 5:2 quoted as evidence of the "mighty change" of heart, paired with Mosiah 3:19 on putting off the natural man
