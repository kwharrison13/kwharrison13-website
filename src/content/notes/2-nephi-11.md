# 2 Nephi 11

Across Kyle's study, **2 Nephi 11** is read on two adjacent points from its opening verses. August 24–30, 2020. Helaman 7–12: "Remember the Lord" quotes verse 2 — Nephi writing more of the words of Isaiah because "my soul delighteth in his words," likening them to his people because Isaiah "verily saw my Redeemer, even as I have seen him." Anti-Christ — Topical Study and Miscellaneous Scriptures both cite verses 2–3 for the same fact — that Nephi and Jacob have *seen Christ* — which the anti-Christ study uses to explain why Jacob "could not be shaken" when Sherem tried to argue there was no Christ.

**Context:** 2 Nephi 11 is a short chapter in which Nephi explains his delight in the words of Isaiah and testifies, with his brother Jacob, that they have both seen their Redeemer — the basis for the Isaiah chapters that follow.
