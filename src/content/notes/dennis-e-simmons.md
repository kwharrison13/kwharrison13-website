# Dennis E. Simmons

Dennis E. Simmons appears in Kyle's Obedience — Topical Study as the author of the talk **"But If Not,"** which anchors the study's section on *true faith and trusting obedience*. The talk reframes faith not as "bravado, not just a wish, not just a hope," but as "confidence and trust in Jesus Christ that leads a person to follow him." Its central example is the story of Shadrach, Meshach, and Abed-nego, who tell King Nebuchadnezzar that God "is able to deliver us… **but if not**… we will not serve thy gods" — a declaration the talk holds up as the model of faith that endures even when outcomes don't match hope. In Kyle's notes this becomes the seed for the linked theme that "great challenges make us great."

**Context:** Dennis E. Simmons was a General Authority Seventy of The Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-day Saints. His April 2004 General Conference address "But If Not" is widely cited within LDS teaching on faith and obedience.
