# Selfishness

In Kyle's [Come, Follow Me](/notes/come-follow-me) study of [3 Nephi 1](/notes/3-nephi-1) (week of September 7–13, 2020), selfishness is named as the ultimate path to [Pride](/notes/pride). Reading [3 Nephi 1](/notes/3-nephi-1):29 — where [the rising generation](/essays/the-rising-generation) "became for themselves" — Kyle sets the phrase against the prodigal son who instead "came to himself," reflecting and turning inward ([Luke 15](/notes/luke-15):17; [Psalm 119](/notes/psalm-119):59). The contrast is the point: "becoming for yourself" is a turning toward self that hardens into pride, while "coming to yourself" is an honest turn inward that leads back. The note sits alongside related threads in the same week — that there is nothing Christlike about taking joy in another's sorrow, and that [Christlike Sorrow](/notes/christlike-sorrow) breaks the heart over others' suffering rather than the self's.

## Where this appears

- September 7–13, 2020. 3 Nephi 1–7: "Lift Up Your Head and Be of Good Cheer" — "became for themselves" read as the selfishness that is the ultimate path to [Pride](/notes/pride), contrasted with the prodigal who "came to himself."
