# Marriage

A recurring theme across Kyle's reading where marriage shows up less as romance than as **a renewable partnership that has to be actively maintained**. [Becoming](/books/becoming) supplies the load-bearing framing: even a happy marriage is "a contract best renewed and renewed again, even quietly and privately — even alone," and [Michelle Obama](/notes/michelle-obama) describes her own as "a full-on merger, a reconfiguring of two lives into one, with the well-being of a family taking precedence over any one agenda or goal." [Driven](/books/driven) is the cautionary mirror image — Larry Miller worked 6:30am-to-10pm for twenty years building his business while Gail was "basically a single parent," and the book's emotional core is his late recognition that the partnership in marriage was as real as the one in business and that "time apart takes its toll." [John Quincy Adams](/books/john-quincy-adams) adds the friction case: Adams could criticize his wife Louisa but could not accept criticism from her, never reaching "the sense of perfect trust and mutuality that allows couples to surmount whatever problems life puts in their way."

Two of Kyle's gospel-study pages treat marriage explicitly as a maintainable system. The Honeymoon and 8 Steps To Avoid Divorce both read the missionary companionship as a template for marriage — define not just *what* you want but *how*, then hold a weekly companionship-inventory "for the rest of your life," always striving to improve. The 8-steps page's sharpest line is that "you will find in a marriage exactly what you look for" (the Jewish leaders FOUND sin in the sinless Christ), an argument for choosing what to attend to in a spouse.

Institutionally, [The Next Mormons](/books/the-next-mormons) frames marriage as the strongest retention lever the LDS Church has — postwar Mormonism built its identity on the nuclear family, in-faith marriage best predicts keeping children in the tradition, yet the strain on never-married singles is growing as broader society shifts. [Faith is Not Blind](/books/faith-is-not-blind) surfaces marriage and motherhood/fatherhood as "sometimes-competing principles" to be applied prayerfully alongside a woman's education.
