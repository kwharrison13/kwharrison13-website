# UC Investments

UC Investments is the University of California's Office of the Chief Investment Officer, the entity managing the UC system's investment portfolio. In Kyle's notes it is the subject of [Assembly Bill 2041 — UC Office of the Chief Investment Officer](/notes/assembly-bill-2041-uc-office-of-the-chief-investment-officer), a California legislative factsheet describing it as a significant "fund of funds" limited partner ([LP](/notes/lp)) in venture capital and private equity, managing **$118 billion as of March 2018**. AB 2041 urged UC Investments to use best efforts to encourage diversity in its own staff hiring and among its investment-partner firms, to launch emerging-manager programs, and to encourage start-up firms to develop diverse executive teams. The bill surfaced in [Contrary Capital](/notes/contrary)'s 2022 sourcing/diligence log — the source export for the page — alongside companion reads on [CalPERS](/notes/calpers) and public-fund manager-diversity policy.

**Context:** UC Investments (the UC Office of the CIO) manages the University of California's pension, endowment, and working-capital pools across all UC campuses. As of the late 2010s/2020s its total assets under management have been on the order of $150B+.
