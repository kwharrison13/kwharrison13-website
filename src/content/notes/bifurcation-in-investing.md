# Bifurcation in Investing

One of Kyle's load-bearing investing tags: the thesis that investment firms — venture capital especially — are splitting into distinct camps that play *different games*, and that the central strategic question becomes a firm's value proposition. The seed idea comes from [Stephen A. Schwarzman](/notes/stephen-a-schwarzman) in [What It Takes](/books/what-it-takes): "adapt or die," refusing to define the firm too narrowly, and the open-ended "Why not?" — "if we could apply our strengths, our network, and our resources to make that business a success, why not? Other firms defined themselves too narrowly." Kyle quotes this Schwarzman/[Blackstone](/notes/blackstone) passage repeatedly across his venture research as the anchor for the concept.

Applied to venture, the tag tracks the industry's segmentation — from sector → stage → *value proposition* ([Chris Dixon](/notes/chris-dixon)'s "agglomerators vs. specialists"). The Productization of Venture Capital - Research frames venture as a *product* ("what job does a founder hire you to do?"), with Agglomerators broadening into multi-fund platforms with portfolio services while specialists differentiate. The Puritans of Venture Capital - Research sharpens it into the "different games" frame: a Cottage Keeper underwriting 5–10x and a Capital Agglomerator content with 2–3x can both be right and still pay wildly different prices — "the stupid game is not playing a different game, it's not realizing your competitor is." Kyle's own capture warns that venture is becoming a "loser's game" as more experts and more capital compete, making each fund's value proposition "increasingly more life or death." The Blackstone of Innovation - Research and the September 13th, 2022 — Harry Stebbings Podcast notes both reuse the same Schwarzman anchor.

## Where this appears

- [What It Takes](/books/what-it-takes) — Schwarzman's "adapt or die / Why not?" as the origin idea: refuse to define the firm too narrowly
- The Productization of Venture Capital - Research — venture's segmentation (sector → stage → value prop), agglomerators vs. specialists, venture-as-product
- The Puritans of Venture Capital - Research — the "different games" frame and venture as a loser's game
