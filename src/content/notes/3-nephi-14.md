# 3 Nephi 14

**3 Nephi 14** is the Book of Mormon parallel to [Matthew 7](/notes/matthew-7) in the Sermon on the Mount, and that is where Kyle works with it most. He reads it under "Judge Righteously," pairing it with [3 Nephi 13](/notes/3-nephi-13): before you judge, make sure you can see straight (14:2–4); judge based on the fruits of the people (14:15–16), remembering your own fruits are the standard by which you will be judged. Kyle's central point — following [Dallin H. Oaks](/notes/dallin-h-oaks)'s distinction between forbidden *final* judgments and directed *intermediate* judgments — is that "the point of Matthew 7 and 3 Nephi 14 is not 'do not judge' but use your agency" righteously. In August 31–September 6, 2020. Helaman 13–16: "Glad Tidings of Great Joy", 3 Nephi 14:20 is quoted: "by their fruits ye shall know them."

**Context:** 3 Nephi 14 is the chapter in which the resurrected Christ delivers, to the Nephites, the portion of the Sermon on the Mount that parallels Matthew 7 — "judge not," the mote and the beam, asking and receiving, the strait gate, and knowing false prophets by their fruits.

## Where this appears

- Sermon on the Mount — read as the Nephite parallel to Matthew 7 on righteous (not abstaining-from) judgment
- August 31–September 6, 2020. Helaman 13–16: "Glad Tidings of Great Joy" — quotes 3 Nephi 14:20, "by their fruits ye shall know them"
