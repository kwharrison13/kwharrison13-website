# David Dean

CEO of DGDean, a boutique firm providing complete technology services to well-funded start-ups (taking them from $0 to $100MM); concurrently CTO of Loeb Enterprises (a venture collective) and former CTO of ScriptRelief, a prescription-drug savings program. He served as a customer reference (October 5, 2021) in Kyle's Noname Security - Diligence dossier.

In that call, Dean described one of the companies DGDean was incubating — an automated employment and income-verification product for gig workers that relies heavily on internal and external APIs. He evaluated several API-security tools (Cequence, IMVision, [Salt Security](/notes/salt-security), and [Noname Security](/notes/noname-security)) and ran a proof-of-concept only with Noname, which made onboarding easy: a single 45-minute session with his head of DevOps, a week of running, and a dashboard that had identified every inbound and outbound API endpoint — including flagging APIs touching SSNs or credit card numbers. He favored Noname for its traffic-mirroring approach (which keeps traffic local rather than routing across the internet) over agent-based tools like Salt's older architecture, citing ease of use and effectiveness as his core criteria.
