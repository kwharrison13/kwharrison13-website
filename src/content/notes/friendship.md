# Friendship

Friendship appears in the corpus along two threads. In the [John Quincy Adams](/books/john-quincy-adams) biography, it surfaces as intellectual companionship: late in his political career, when his prospects seemed to be "drawing to an ignominious close," Adams found in his son Charles "an intellectual friend and partner," staying up past midnight writing letters about the great ethical writers — Cicero, Pascal, Socrates, Milton. The note pairs friendship with #[Education](/notes/education) and #[mentorship](/notes/mentorship), framing it as the comfort and continuity of a life lived among books and correspondents.

In the gospel-study thread (Alma 23–29, June 29–July 5, 2020. Alma 23–29: "They Never Did Fall Away"), friendship is one of the chapter's key ideas — the happiness "seekers" take in finding their friends on the same path to righteousness. Kyle records a [Dieter F. Uchtdorf](/notes/dieter-f-uchtdorf) line that captures the distinction he wants to keep: "Acquaintances are at church. Friends are in our homes."

## Where this appears

- [John Quincy Adams](/books/john-quincy-adams) — friendship as intellectual partnership between Adams and his son Charles, built through letters about books and ethics.
- June 29–July 5, 2020. Alma 23–29: "They Never Did Fall Away" — friendship as a key idea of the chapter, with the Uchtdorf "acquaintances vs. friends" distinction.
