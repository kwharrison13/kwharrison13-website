# Venture Studio

A model for *building* companies rather than just funding them — a team of operators/entrepreneurs that originates business ideas in-house and stands them up, as opposed to a traditional investor putting capital into existing businesses. Across Kyle's calls the model is repeatedly defined by contrast: [High Alpha Innovation](/notes/high-alpha-innovation) (via [Lars Hamilton](/notes/lars-hamilton)) described it as "somewhat similar to Touchdown Ventures but instead of putting capital in existing business they put it in ideas for new businesses" — "not just Think Tanks and not just an investor" (July 2nd, 2020 — Lars Hamilton (High Alpha Innovation)). [Atomic Ventures](/notes/atomic-ventures) (where [Tyler Payne](/notes/tyler-payne) was a principal) runs the same playbook — partnering with strong engineers to build startups to traction, then stepping back to advisor/passive-investor roles as the company matures.

The recurring tension in Kyle's notes is **shared services**. [Dave Linhardt](/notes/dave-linhardt) of [InsightStudios](/notes/insightstudios) argued the problem with shared services is that "when you're early on and searching for a business model, you don't know what services you need" — citing a Chicago studio (Ronan) that spent $800K of a $1M investment on its own services and thereby took decision power away from the entrepreneur. Linhardt also distinguished a studio's mindset from a VC's: he'd been involved with PSL but found "the way they make decisions is completely different… they're run by venture capitalists" used to looking at Series A companies "instead of companies from scratch" (July 10th, 2018 — Dave Linhardt (InsightStudios)). His defensibility pitch leaned on the [lean startup](/books/the-lean-startup) analogy — anyone can read the method in a textbook, but it takes "knowledge AND experienced judgment to make it work" — and on Marvel Studios as a metaphor for craft that compounds.

The 2020 [Lars Hamilton](/notes/lars-hamilton) call also flagged an effort to actually *define and quantify* the space — an Italian researcher trying to identify the variables that predict startup success, "slightly challenging [The Lean Startup](/books/the-lean-startup) hypothesis" — alongside a reading list (Startups and the Remaking of the Firm by Elliott Parker, The Acorn Method) and a skeptical note from Tom Peterson: "Why do we glorify these high risk, 1% success rate businesses that most people will fail at?"

**Context:** A venture studio (or startup studio / company builder) is an organization that systematically generates startup ideas internally, then assembles founding teams, capital, and shared operating resources to launch them — distinct from incubators/accelerators (which take in outside founders) and from traditional VC funds (which invest in already-formed companies). High Alpha and Atomic are well-known examples in the U.S.
