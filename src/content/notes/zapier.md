# Zapier

The incumbent no-code automation/integration tool that recurs in Kyle's no-code diligence as the baseline everyone is measured against — and, often, the thing customers want to escape. In a customer-reference call for [WhaleSync](/notes/whalesync), Rob of [Cartooli](/notes/cartooli) described stitching Zapier together to move data between [Airtable](/notes/airtable) and [Webflow](/notes/webflow) before [Airtable](/notes/airtable) built native automations, but flagged its drawbacks: the number of "zaps" run drives pricing, every added layer "introduces a breakage point," and doing Airtable formula logic inside Zapier required "so many Zap layers." His vision for WhaleSync was explicitly framed as a potential Zapier alternative — a dashboard of connections rather than brittle multi-step zaps — noting "Zapier is always deeply embedded in these tools but would love nothing more than a dashboard of the various connections" (August 18th, 2021 — Rob @ Cartooli on WhaleSync).

Zapier also appears as an acquirer in Kyle's notes: it acquired Makerpad, the no-code education platform run by Ben Tossell, whose post-acquisition build-out Kyle discussed with him directly (September 7th, 2021 — Ben Tossell (Makerpad)).

**Context:** Zapier is a web-automation platform that connects thousands of apps via trigger-action workflows ("Zaps"), a leading tool in the no-code/automation space; it acquired no-code education community Makerpad in 2021.
