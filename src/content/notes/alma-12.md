# Alma 12

Alma 12 is a Book of Mormon chapter that Kyle cross-references in several gospel studies, chiefly for two strands. In the Come Follow Me study of Alma 13–16 he notes Alma 12:24–27, where [Alma the Younger](/notes/alma-the-younger) teaches the [Plan of Salvation](/notes/plan-of-salvation) — mortality as a probationary state, a time given to prepare to meet God. Across The Lehonti Principle and Virtue he uses the chapter for its warning about Satan's tactics: Alma 12:6 describes "the snare of the adversary" that "bring[s] you into subjection unto him" — the basis for Kyle's point in Virtue that God never coerces agency but our enemies seek to subject us — and Alma 12:14 is chained into The Lehonti Principle (Mosiah 4:30, the discipline of thoughts and desires).

**Context:** Alma 12 records Alma the Younger's confrontation with Zeezrom in Ammonihah, teaching the chains of hell, the resurrection, and the plan of redemption; it is one of the Book of Mormon's fuller doctrinal expositions of the fall and the plan of salvation.

## Where this appears

- Alma 13–16 — Enter into the Rest of the Lord — cites Alma 12:24–27 as Alma teaching the [Plan of Salvation](/notes/plan-of-salvation).
- The Lehonti Principle — chains Alma 12:14 with Mosiah 4:30 on guarding thoughts and desires.
- Virtue — cites Alma 12:6 ("snare of the adversary") on agency and subjection to the enemy.
