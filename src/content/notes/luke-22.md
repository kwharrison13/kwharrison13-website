# Luke 22

Luke 22 is the New Testament chapter Kyle returns to for two distinct lessons in his gospel study. In Obedience — Topical Study, **Luke 22:42** — Christ in Gethsemane praying "Father, if thou be willing, remove this cup from me: nevertheless, not my will but thine be done" — is the anchor verse for the "**But If Not**" principle: the same trusting obedience shown by Shadrach, Meshach, and Abed-nego, who would not serve other gods even if God did not deliver them. Kyle pairs it with Christ suffering "pains and afflictions and temptations of every kind" ([Alma 7](/notes/alma-7):11) as the model of self-discipline unto obedience that is undertaken not because it is easiest but because it is necessary.

In the [Come, Follow Me](/notes/come-follow-me) study of 3 Nephi 20–26, Kyle draws on **Luke 22:32** — "when thou art converted, strengthen thy brethren" — as a pattern for how Christ teaches: conversion is meant to overflow into service to others.

**Context:** Luke 22 is the chapter of the Last Supper, Christ's agony in Gethsemane, His arrest, and Peter's denial.
