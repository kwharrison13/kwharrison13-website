# Permanent Beta

Permanent beta is the signature framing of [The Start-Up of You](/books/the-start-up-of-you): treat your career, like a start-up product, as something that is never finished but always in development. As the book puts it, "keeping your career in permanent beta forces you to acknowledge that you have bugs, that there's new development to do on yourself, that you will need to adapt." Its operating rule is **"for life in permanent beta, the trick is to never stop starting."** The posture is set up early by [Andy Grove](/notes/andy-grove)'s maxim "Only the paranoid survive" — permanent beta as a vigilance habit, the career counterpart of treating yourself as an [entrepreneurial](/notes/entrepreneurship) venture in continuous iteration rather than a fixed, completed thing.

**Context:** "The Start-Up of You" is a career book by Reid Hoffman and Ben Casnocha; "permanent beta" borrows the software term for a product kept perpetually unfinished and open to improvement, applying it to a person's professional life.
