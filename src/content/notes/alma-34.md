# Alma 34

Alma 34 is the chapter of [Amulek](/notes/amulek)'s sermon to the Zoramites, and across Kyle's gospel-study notes it surfaces chiefly as a key text on the [Atonement](/notes/atonement). In How To Apply The Atonement it anchors the doctrine that Christ's atonement is **infinite but not unconditional**: Alma 34:12–14 is cited alongside [2 Nephi 9](/notes/2-nephi-9) for the "great and last sacrifice" that is "infinite and eternal," with the corollary that "infinite does NOT mean unconditional" — the resurrection is unconditional, but eternal life is conditional and "cannot come to man through sin." The June 15–21, 2020. Alma 13–16: "Enter into the Rest of the Lord" study cross-references Alma 34:11 ("there is not any man that can sacrifice his own blood which will atone for the sins of another") to show why the atonement had to be the act of an infinite being, and Alma 34:8 as one source of "a true testimony of Jesus Christ" — tying the chapter to [Testimony](/notes/testimony).

The chapter also sits inside the Come Follow Me block July 13–19, 2020. Alma 32–35: "Plant This Word in Your Hearts", following [Alma 32](/notes/alma-32)'s teaching that affliction prepares the heart to receive the word (though Kyle's notes there are partial and cover Alma 32 most fully). It is further referenced in the Fasting - September 5, 2021 study among the related scriptures on humility, prayer, and offering.
