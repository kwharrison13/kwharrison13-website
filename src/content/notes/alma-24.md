# Alma 24

The chapter of the buried weapons of war. In June 29–July 5, 2020. Alma 23–29: "They Never Did Fall Away", Kyle reads Alma 24 as the covenant of the converted Anti-Nephi-Lehies: having had their stains taken away, they hide their swords deep in the earth rather than risk staining them again "through the blood of the Son of our great God, which shall be shed for the [Atonement](/notes/atonement) of our sins" (Alma 24:13). Kyle turns it into a personal challenge — what are *your* weapons of war, the habits and attitudes that led you to sin? Bury them.

The same chapter appears as evidence elsewhere in Kyle's gospel study. In Scripture Study — Topical Study, Alma 24:19 is cited for the principle that the Lamanites buried their weapons *because* of how fully converted they were to living the gospel — obedience flowing from conversion. And in Christ Defines His Gospel, Kyle connects [2 Nephi 25](/notes/2-nephi-25):25 to Alma 24:11 in working out the relationship between grace and works.

**Context:** Alma 24 (in the Book of Mormon) recounts the people of Anti-Nephi-Lehi covenanting never to shed blood again and burying their weapons deep in the earth, then submitting to slaughter rather than break that covenant.
