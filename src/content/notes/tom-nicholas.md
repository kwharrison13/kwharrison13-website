# Tom Nicholas

Author of [VC: An American History](/books/vc), the history of venture capital that Kyle read with the [Investing 101 2.0](/notes/investing-101-2-0) and [Bifurcation of Investing](/notes/bifurcation-of-investing) threads explicitly in view. Nicholas's book traces the asset class from nineteenth-century whaling syndicates and the early partnerships through the rise of the modern firm ([Kleiner Perkins](/notes/kleiner-perkins), [Sequoia](/notes/sequoia), [Bessemer Venture Partners](/notes/bessemer-venture-partners)) and the [Power Law](/notes/power-law) dynamics that govern returns. Kyle's book page treats Nicholas as the narrator of how VC's risk-and-reward structure developed over time.

**Context:** Tom Nicholas is a professor at Harvard Business School, where he holds the William J. Abernathy chair and teaches the history of entrepreneurship and finance. His 2019 book *VC: An American History* is a standard scholarly account of the venture-capital industry's origins.
