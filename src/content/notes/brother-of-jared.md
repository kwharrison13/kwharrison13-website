# Brother of Jared

The Jaredite prophet whose faith was so complete that the veil could not keep him from seeing the Lord. Across Kyle's gospel study he functions less as a historical figure than as a working model of how faith is built, tested, and finally made into knowledge. He recurs in the Come Follow Me study of the Book of Ether and in several topical studies, almost always as the case study for one idea: faith precedes the miracle.

## The narrative arc ([Ether 1](/notes/ether-1)–[Ether 5](/notes/ether-5))

He comes "from the great tower, at the time the Lord confounded the language of the people" ([Ether 1](/notes/ether-1):33). Described as "a large and mighty man, and a man highly favored of the Lord," he is the one [Jared](/notes/jared) repeatedly sends to inquire of the Lord — first to spare their language, then to learn where they should go ([Ether 1](/notes/ether-1):34–38). The party is led "continually by the hand of the Lord" across the wilderness to the great sea, then crosses it in eight sealed barges, arriving in a promised land under the covenant that whoever possesses it must "serve the true and only God" ([Ether 2](/notes/ether-2)).

The arc ends with the vision on Mount Shelem: having molten sixteen clear stones and asked the Lord to touch them, "the veil was taken from off the eyes of the brother of Jared, and he saw the finger of the Lord" — and then the Lord, marveling at his faith, shows Himself fully ([Ether 3](/notes/ether-3):6–20).

## The time to prepare is before the danger comes

Kyle's reading of [Ether 1](/notes/ether-1) turns on *why* the Brother of Jared was protected when the languages were confounded: he was *already* righteous and "highly favored of the Lord." The relationship with God that saved him was built before the crisis arrived, not in response to it. He frames this through Thomas S. Monson's line that "when the time for performance arrives, the time for preparation is past" — readiness is a position you have to already be standing in. Kyle pairs it with Russell M. Nelson's warning that "in coming days, it will not be possible to survive spiritually without the guiding, directing, comforting, and constant influence of the Holy Ghost" — i.e. the constant relationship, not the emergency appeal, is what carries you. See [Spiritual Complacency](/notes/spiritual-complacency).

## Spiritual complacency: chastened for three hours

The counterweight to that favored status is the warning Kyle keeps returning to. After being "directed continually by the hand of the Lord," the Brother of Jared spends four years on the seashore at Moriancumer, and the Lord "came again unto the brother of Jared... and chastened him because he remembered not to call upon the name of the Lord" — for the space of three hours ([Ether 2](/notes/ether-2):14). Kyle's gloss: we can become so *comfortably led* by God that faith goes slack, and it is in hardship that we feel our need of Him most acutely. He cross-references [Helaman 12](/notes/helaman-12):3 ("except the Lord doth chasten his people with many afflictions... they will not remember him") and [D&C 122](/notes/dandc-122):7 ("all these things shall give thee experience, and shall be for thy good"). In his Helaman 13–16 study the same three-hour chastening appears under the heading "God chastens because He loves." This is the anchor case for the [Spiritual Complacency](/notes/spiritual-complacency) concept.

## The barges: [3 Answers to Gospel Questions](/notes/3-answers-to-gospel-questions)

The Brother of Jared's three problems with the sealed barges are Kyle's central framework for how God answers prayer — developed both in the [Ether 1](/notes/ether-1)–[Ether 5](/notes/ether-5) study and in the standalone topical study 3 Answers To Every Prayer. The problems, named in [Ether 2](/notes/ether-2):19 ("in them there is no light... whither shall we steer... we cannot breathe"), map to three distinct answers:

1. **No Light → you must think for yourself.** The Lord answers the lighting problem with a question — "What will ye that I should do?" ([Ether 2](/notes/ether-2):23). The Brother of Jared molds sixteen clear stones himself and *only then* asks the Lord to touch them ([Ether 3](/notes/ether-3):1–5). Kyle ties this to the [D&C 9](/notes/dandc-9) pattern: study it out in your mind, then ask. The struggle is where faith grows most fervent.
2. **No Steering → God will take care of it.**
3. **No Air → God tells us what to do.** The Lord gives a direct, step-by-step commandment — holes top and bottom, unstopped for air ([Ether 2](/notes/ether-2):20) — "and it came to pass that the brother of Jared did so." In 3 Answers To Every Prayer Kyle reads the airholes as the necessary travails of life: the barge must be breached so its passengers can be "agents unto [themselves]" ([D&C 29](/notes/dandc-29):39).

A recurring sub-theme: when prayer meets silence, it may be because there is sin, or because there is already something we've been told to do first — the Brother of Jared *acts* on the commands he's given before more is revealed.

## Faith that pierces the veil ([Ether 3](/notes/ether-3))

This is the figure's defining moment in Kyle's notes. The Lord's reason for revealing Himself is the faith itself: "never has man come before me with such exceeding faith as thou hast; for were it not so ye could not have seen my finger" ([Ether 3](/notes/ether-3):9). Kyle files this alongside the other scriptural moments where Christ ranks someone's faith above all others — the Centurion ("I have not found so great faith, no, not in Israel," [Matthew 8](/notes/matthew-8) / [Luke 7](/notes/luke-7)) and the Nephites ([3 Nephi 19](/notes/3-nephi-19):35). This cluster is collected under [Never have I seen such great faith](/notes/never-have-i-seen-such-great-faith).

The mechanism matters to Kyle: the Brother of Jared's faith had become *perfect knowledge*. "He had faith no longer, for he knew, nothing doubting... having this perfect knowledge of God, he could not be kept from within the veil" ([Ether 3](/notes/ether-3):19–20) — read against [Alma 32](/notes/alma-32)'s definition of faith as hope for things not seen. Kyle connects the vision to the [First Vision](/notes/first-vision): every time God appears He teaches doctrine, and the truths the Brother of Jared learns (men "created after mine own image") echo the eight truths Joseph Smith learned in the grove. In the **Faith — Topical Study**, Kyle traces the full distance traveled — from being chastened for not praying to seeing the finger of Christ — as the model of *exercising the faith you already have*: "Behold, O Lord, Thou canst do this. We know that thou art able to show forth great power" ([Ether 3](/notes/ether-3):5).

## Faith precedes the miracle: moving Mount Zerin ([Ether 12](/notes/ether-12))

In the [Ether 12](/notes/ether-12)–[Ether 15](/notes/ether-15) study and in his notes on Russell M. Nelson's talk *"Christ Is Risen; Faith in Him Will Move Mountains,"* the Brother of Jared becomes the proof text for ordering: faith comes first, the miracle after. "For the brother of Jared said unto the mountain Zerin, Remove, and it was removed. And if he had not had faith it would not have moved; wherefore thou workest after men have faith" ([Ether 12](/notes/ether-12):30). This sits inside Moroni's wider discourse on weakness made strong through humility and faith ([Ether 12](/notes/ether-12):27) — the same logic Kyle pulls into [Humble Yourselves](/notes/humble-yourselves) and [The Pride Cycle](/notes/the-pride-cycle).

## The veil becomes unnecessary

In **Eye single to the glory of God**, the Brother of Jared illustrates a progression Kyle cares about: as faith grows, so does the capacity to bear spiritual truth, until the veil itself is no longer needed. "The Lord could not withhold anything from his sight; wherefore he showed him all things, for he could no longer be kept without the veil" ([Ether 12](/notes/ether-12):21). The redemption is tied to knowledge: "because thou knowest these things, ye are redeemed from the fall" ([Ether 3](/notes/ether-3):13).

## On the name

The Book of Ether never names him; the Book of Mormon refers to him only as "the brother of Jared." Latter-day Saint tradition holds his name to be **Mahonri Moriancumer** (recorded as given by Joseph Smith), which is why that form is kept here as an alias. Kyle's notes use only "the Brother of Jared."
