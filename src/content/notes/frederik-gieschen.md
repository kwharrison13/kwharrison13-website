# Frederik Gieschen

The writer behind [Neckar's Notes](/notes/neckar-s-notes) (also "Alchemy of Money"), the Substack on investing, creativity, and the inner game that recurs as a referenced body of work across Kyle's notes. Kyle read a cluster of Gieschen's essays in April 2022 — "Attempting the Impossible," "Divorce, Denial, Dissonance Reduction," "The Reading Obsession," and his conversation with [Jimmy Soni](/notes/jimmy-soni) about *The Founders* — and pulled durable lessons from them. His essay "Attempting the Impossible" anchors the [Build a Fund](/notes/build-a-fund) concept: it argues how hard it is to spin out and raise a first fund "unless you come from a prestigious shop and have initial backing," and that the real edge is your ability to "build a network, tell a story, and get an introduction." Gieschen's broader thesis, recorded in Kyle's notes, is that writers like Buffett and Howard Marks "used content to instill trust, trust gave them permanent capital, and permanent capital gave them a massive financial advantage" — and a related distinction between an investment *business* (gives the customer what they want) and an investment *practice* (serves the client in a way that reflects the practitioner's craft).

Gieschen also appears as a working interlocutor: an April 5th, 2022 call with him and [Eric Tarczynski](/notes/eric-tarczynski) about [Contrary Research](/essays/contrary-research) explored the role of a research fellow, differentiation, and finding the unique angle on a company story (using [Recurrency](/notes/recurrency) as an example of a binary-outcome, "replace this or totally fail" company worth going deep on). His Neckar piece "Cranking The Learning Machine Up To 11" is catalogued as a long read, and Kyle harvested the [Jesse Livermore](/notes/jesse-livermore) "nothing new in Wall Street" quote while reading his writing. He is cited (as "Neckar") in the The Hardening Of The Great Softening - Research notes for a tweet on knowledge supporting the distribution-vs-optimization-of-resources argument.
