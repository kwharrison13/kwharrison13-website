# Vaclav Smil

Vaclav Smil is the scientist-historian whose work on energy underpins the energy framing in [How To Avoid a Climate Disaster](/books/how-to-avoid-a-climate-disaster). [Bill Gates](/notes/bill-gates) credits Smil's books with helping him "understand just how critical energy is to modern civilization," and names *Energy Transitions* and *Energy Myths and Realities* as the best books he has read on how energy transitions actually happen — borrowing from them for the book's core claim that previous shifts from one energy source to another have "always taken decades upon decades."

Smil recurs in Kyle's reading as a source of grounding statistics about long-run economic transformation. In [The AppetiZIRP](/notes/the-appetizirp), the "most striking stat" is drawn from Smil's *How the World Really Works*: the share of the US population working as farmers fell from 83% in 1800 to about 1% today — used to make the point that the economy had to find new work for everyone displaced.

**Context:** Vaclav Smil is a Czech-Canadian scientist and prolific author (energy, food, materials, the environment), professor emeritus at the University of Manitoba, widely cited for empirical, numbers-first analysis of energy systems and a skepticism toward optimistic timelines for energy transitions. Bill Gates has repeatedly said he reads everything Smil writes.
