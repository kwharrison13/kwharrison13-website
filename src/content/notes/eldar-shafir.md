# Eldar Shafir

Eldar Shafir is, in the wiki, the co-author with [Sendhil Mullainathan](/notes/sendhil-mullainathan) of the behavioral-economics research underpinning the [Scarcity Mentality](/notes/scarcity-mentality) concept — the finding that scarcity itself narrows cognitive bandwidth. The Mullainathan & Shafir scarcity work is cited in [Rutger Bregman](/notes/rutger-bregman)'s [Utopia For Realists](/books/utopia-for-realists), which is the route by which it enters Kyle's notes. This is the empirical, cognitive sense of scarcity, distinct from the religious/abundance sense of [Scarcity](/notes/scarcity) elsewhere in the wiki.

**Context:** Eldar Shafir is a Princeton psychologist and behavioral scientist who, with economist Sendhil Mullainathan, co-authored *Scarcity: Why Having Too Little Means So Much* (2013), arguing that the experience of scarcity (of money, time, etc.) taxes mental bandwidth and impairs decision-making.
