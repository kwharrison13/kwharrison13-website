# 2 Corinthians 12

Paul's chapter describing a man "caught up to the third heaven." In Kyle's Come Follow Me study of 3 Nephi 27–4 Nephi, it is cited for verse 2 — "I knew a man in Christ above fourteen years ago, (whether in the body, I cannot tell; or whether out of the body, I cannot tell: God knoweth;) such an one caught up to the third heaven" — appearing alongside cross-references on heavenly visions and degrees of glory ([Moses 7](/notes/moses-7), [D&C 93](/notes/dandc-93), [D&C 131](/notes/dandc-131)).

**Context:** In 2 Corinthians 12, the Apostle [Paul the Apostle](/notes/paul-the-apostle) recounts a vision of a man (understood to be himself) caught up to "the third heaven" / paradise, a passage long read as scriptural support for the doctrine of multiple heavens or degrees of glory.
