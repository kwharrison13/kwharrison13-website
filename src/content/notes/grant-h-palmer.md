# Grant H. Palmer

Author of [An Insider's View of Mormon Origins](/books/an-insider-s-view-of-mormon-origins), a revisionist history of LDS origins. Kyle's notes describe Palmer as a fourth-generation Mormon and retired CES (Church Educational System) instructor writing a revisionist account of Mormon origins. In the book Palmer reconstructs [Joseph Smith](/notes/joseph-smith)'s translation method and shifting godhead theology, and weighs the testimony of Book of Mormon witnesses (such as David Whitmer) against a fabrication thesis.

Kyle read the book with active disagreement — most of his notes flag a logical gap or historiographical flaw rather than accept Palmer's conclusions. One thread he singles out is what he calls Palmer's "grand-conspiracy assumption": that all the witnesses and inner-circle figures held to a fabrication "despite unending suffering," a conspiracy theory at scale that, in Kyle's reading, requires more believing than the alternative.
