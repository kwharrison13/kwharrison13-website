# Jonathan Edwards

Colonial American theologian and preacher who appears in Kyle's corpus as the devotional counterweight to [Benjamin Franklin](/notes/benjamin-franklin). In [Chad's Big Quote Book](/notes/chad-s-big-quote-book), Kyle collects a passage from Edwards on the sweetness of coming to Christ "as a little child" — "to be united to Christ; to have him for my head… cut off entirely from my own root, in order to grow into, and out of Christ" — a meditation on humility, grace, and emptying of self.

That same Edwards anchors the central dichotomy of [Benjamin Franklin — An American Life](/books/benjamin-franklin-an-american-life): [Walter Isaacson](/notes/walter-isaacson) frames Edwards (with the Mather family) as the pole of an "anointed elect" and salvation through God's grace alone — religious fervor, social hierarchy, exalted over earthly values — set against the Franklins, who believed in salvation through good works, benevolent tolerance, and striving upward mobility. Edwards thus represents the grace-and-hierarchy side of the American character that Franklin's good-works strain runs against.

**Context:** Jonathan Edwards (1703–1758) was a leading Puritan theologian of the First Great Awakening, best known for the sermon "Sinners in the Hands of an Angry God" and for works defending Calvinist doctrine; he briefly served as president of the College of New Jersey (later Princeton).
