# Perfect Knowledge

In Kyle's Faith — Topical Study, **perfect knowledge** is the top rung of a ladder that runs belief → [Faith](/notes/faith) → perfect knowledge. The structure leans on [Alma 32](/notes/alma-32):21 — faith is "a hope for things which are not seen, which are true," and therefore *not* a perfect knowledge — so a perfect knowledge is recast as a knowledge of things which ARE seen and ARE true. Once that knowledge arrives, "your faith is dormant... and this because you know" ([Alma 32](/notes/alma-32):34): seeing is no longer believing but *knowing*, which makes belief and faith unnecessary.

The study's central provocation is "the responsibility of a perfect knowledge" — the idea that knowing carries an immediate obligation to act, and that to deny what one perfectly knows is to sin against light. This is the doctrinal mechanism behind denying the Holy Ghost (which requires a perfect knowledge first) and behind warnings like "if we sin willfully after that we have received the knowledge of the truth, there remaineth no more sacrifice for sins" ([Hebrews 10](/notes/hebrews-10):26). The study cites [Lorenzo Snow](/notes/lorenzo-snow) and [Bruce R. McConkie](/notes/bruce-r-mcconkie) as witnesses who describe a manifestation amounting to perfect knowledge, and notes the Holy Ghost as the only path to it.

## Where this appears

- Faith — Topical Study — the anchor source; treats perfect knowledge as the rung above faith, tied to Alma 32 (dormant faith), and develops "the responsibility of a perfect knowledge" — knowing obligates action, and denying known truth is sin against light.
