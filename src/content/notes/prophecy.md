# Prophecy

In Kyle's Come Follow Me study of [Alma 30](/notes/alma-30)–[Alma 31](/notes/alma-31) (["The Virtue of the Word of God"](July 6–12, 2020)), prophecy appears as one of the core truths the [Anti-Christ](/notes/anti-christ) Korihor attacks. The note catalogs Korihor's tactics: he "argues against prophecy, [Atonement](/notes/atonement), [Agency](/notes/agency), [resurrection](/notes/resurrection), [ordinances](/notes/ordinances), and [Priesthood](/notes/priesthood) leadership, mixing in half-truths and baseless accusations." Prophecy here stands as one of the foundational doctrines that anti-Christ arguments seek to undermine — set against the chapter's larger theme of "the virtue of the word of God."

**Context:** In Latter-day Saint usage, prophecy refers both to inspired foretelling and, more broadly, to revelation delivered through prophets — testimony given by the power of the Holy Ghost. The Alma 30 narrative dramatizes the clash between that prophetic claim and Korihor's rationalist skepticism.
