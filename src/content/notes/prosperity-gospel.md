# Prosperity Gospel

Across Kyle's notes, "prosperity gospel" is used as a foil — the belief that righteousness reliably yields material reward (or that faith reliably produces the outcome you prayed for) — which the LDS sources he draws on push *against*. In [Saints — No Unhallowed Hand](/books/saints-no-unhallowed-hand), Brigham Young's "worst fear" is not persecution but success: when gold is discovered in California, his response is "if we were to go to San Francisco and dig up chunks of gold, it would ruin us," fearing the Saints "will get rich… forget God… wax fat, and kick themselves out of the Church." Kyle's gloss: prosperity makes it *easy to think you don't need to call on God*, which makes the retrenchment, anti-extravagance, and cooperative economics of the [United Order](/notes/united-order) an "institutional immune response to wealth" — the inverse of a prosperity gospel.

The concept surfaces again, in its faith-and-outcome sense, in the General Conference — October 2021 notes, in the context of praying through a loved one's terminal illness ("first we prayed for a miracle… finally we prayed that he would pass quickly"). There the prosperity-gospel reflex shows up as the painful question — "Would he have answered my prayer if I was more faithful?" — immediately complicated by the thought "maybe he did," and by Kyle's interest in "the power of faith as a skill, not just a binary 'wondering' after God's will." In both places the notes resist the clean transaction between faith/righteousness and reward that the prosperity gospel promises.

**Context:** The "prosperity gospel" is a strain of (largely American Protestant) Christian teaching holding that faith, positive confession, and donations produce material wealth and physical health — widely critiqued as a transactional distortion of the gospel.

## Where this appears

- [Saints — No Unhallowed Hand](/books/saints-no-unhallowed-hand) — Brigham Young's fear that wealth will cause the Saints to "forget God"; the cooperative economy as an immune response to prosperity.
- General Conference — October 2021 — the prosperity-gospel reflex in praying through illness ("would he have answered if I were more faithful?") and faith as a skill rather than a transaction.
