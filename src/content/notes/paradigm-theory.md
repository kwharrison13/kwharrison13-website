# Paradigm Theory

In Kyle's gospel-study notes on Alma 36–38 (July 20–26, 2020. Alma 36–38: "Look to God and Live"), "Paradigm Theory" is the idea that a person's worldview is built up from the accumulated material they choose to consume and the activities they engage in: "Building up your faith is based on all of your activities. If you're consuming negative material that will become a part of your Paradigm Theory." Kyle pairs it with [Entropy](/notes/entropy) — the notes quote [Dieter F. Uchtdorf](/notes/dieter-f-uchtdorf) that "faith is never standing still; it is either growing or shrinking" — and with [The Value Way of Thinking](/notes/the-value-way-of-thinking), framing faith as something built deliberately through small daily inputs rather than left to drift. It sits alongside [Confirmation Bias](/notes/confirmation-bias) and [Leap of Faith](/books/leap-of-faith) in the same cluster of notes on how belief is constructed.

## Where this appears

- July 20–26, 2020. Alma 36–38: "Look to God and Live" — paradigm built from chosen inputs; faith as growing or shrinking (anti-entropy), tied to The Value Way of Thinking
