# Dunbar's Number

**One-line:** *The cognitive limit (~150, often a deeper ~100) on the number of stable relationships a person can actively maintain — a recurring constraint in how Index's relationship-builders manage their networks.*

## What we know

- [Benjy Boateng](/notes/benjy-boateng): ~150 (maybe 200 possible), but ~100 allows much deeper relationships; he knows 40-50 people really well plus a broader thin list, tracked in an Airtable with personal detail. (source: December 14th, 2021 daily-note)
- [Erik Kriessmann](/notes/erik-kriessmann): increasingly thoughtful about each incremental person to add — "what is the best possible outcome for this person?" If they lack momentum you have to be a snob; a personal favor is fine but doesn't move the needle. (source: December 14th, 2021 daily-note)
