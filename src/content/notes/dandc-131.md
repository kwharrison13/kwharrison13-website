# D&C 131

Section 131 of the Doctrine and Covenants appears across Kyle's study mostly through **verse 6**: "it is impossible for a man to be saved in ignorance." Christ Defines His Gospel cites it directly, and Knowledge — Topical Study pairs it with [D&C 130](/notes/dandc-130):18–19 to argue that "the person who gains knowledge will have 'the advantage in the world to come'" and progresses only as fast as he gains knowledge. Obedience — Topical Study invokes the same verse to frame baptism and ordinances as conscious, knowing acceptance of [Jesus Christ](/notes/jesus)'s [Atonement](/notes/atonement) rather than ignorant compliance. Kyle's October 19–25, 2020. 3 Nephi 27–4 Nephi: "There Could Not Be a Happier People" study references verses 7–8 (on spirit as refined matter, discerned by purer eyes).

**Context:** Doctrine and Covenants Section 131 records instructions [Joseph Smith](/notes/joseph-smith) gave in May 1843. It teaches the three degrees within the celestial glory and the necessity of the new and everlasting covenant of marriage for the highest degree; verse 6 declares that no one can be saved in ignorance, and verses 7–8 state that "all spirit is matter" — more fine and pure, discernible only by purer eyes.

## Where this appears

- Christ Defines His Gospel — verse 6: impossible to be saved in ignorance
- Knowledge — Topical Study — verse 6 paired with D&C 130 on knowledge as the "advantage in the world to come"
- Obedience — Topical Study — verse 6 framing ordinances as knowing, not ignorant, acceptance of the Atonement
- October 19–25, 2020. 3 Nephi 27–4 Nephi: "There Could Not Be a Happier People" — verses 7–8 on spirit as refined matter
