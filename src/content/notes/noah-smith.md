# Noah Smith

Economist and writer behind the **[Noahpinion](/notes/noah-smith)** Substack, and one of the steadier sources in Kyle's "build" and venture-economics reading. His essay [The Build-Nothing Country](/notes/the-build-nothing-country) is the mechanistic counterpart to [Marc Andreessen](/notes/marc-andreessen)'s [It's Time To Build](/notes/it-s-time-to-build): where Andreessen diagnoses a failure of *will*, Smith diagnoses a broken permitting / land-use / development system — "money is not physical stuff" — exploited by entrenched local interests (the Second Avenue Subway as the world's most expensive subway line, NIMBY opposition to housing and green energy, homeowners who raise their paper wealth by restricting local supply). Kyle reads the two essays as direct intellectual descendants in his [Techno-Optimism](/notes/techno-optimism) thread.

Smith also anchors part of [What Is An Investor](/essays/what-is-an-investor). Kyle leans on his "Will VC produce fewer home runs in the future?" piece — the "next Facebook" question — and on Smith's application of [Frank Knight](/notes/frank-knight)'s [Knightian Uncertainty](/notes/knightian-uncertainty) to explain why outsized [Venture Capital](/notes/venture-capital) returns are getting rarer as the market matures.

**Context:** Noah Smith is an economist and former Bloomberg Opinion columnist who now writes the widely read Noahpinion newsletter, covering economics, technology, and the political economy of growth and abundance.
