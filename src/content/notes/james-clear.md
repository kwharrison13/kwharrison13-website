# James Clear

Author of [Atomic Habits](/books/atomic-habits) (read by Kyle in 2020, rated 4/5), the book on building habits through small, compounding, identity-based changes — Kyle tags it under [Compounding](/notes/compounding), [Systems Thinking](/notes/systems-thinking), and [Identity Formation](/notes/identity-formation). Beyond the book, Clear recurs in Kyle's notes as the source of a frequently-saved quote on goals versus proxies, captured in the July 2nd, 2020 daily log and cross-tagged into [Goals](/notes/goals), [Investing](/notes/investing), and [Reading](/notes/reading):

> "What is the real goal? The real goal is not to 'beat the market.' The goal is to build wealth. The real goal is not to read more books. The goal is to understand what you read. **Don't let a proxy become the target. Don't optimize for the wrong outcome.**"

Kyle uses the line as a guard against optimizing the measurable stand-in instead of the thing itself — the same "don't let the metric become the mission" logic that runs through his thinking on habits and systems.

**Context:** James Clear is an American author and speaker best known for "Atomic Habits" (2018), a bestselling book on habit formation, and for his widely-read "3-2-1" email newsletter.
