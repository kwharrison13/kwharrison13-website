# UserTesting

**One-line:** *The heavyweight [User Research](/notes/user-research) platform — recruited user panels plus live and asynchronous interviews/recordings — repeatedly described as the sticky incumbent and "system of record" for in-depth research.*

## What we know

- Very sticky and "wins most of the time" vs. legacy competitors; [Joseph Ambeault](/notes/joseph-ambeault) said every evaluation he ran ([Codecademy](/notes/codecademy), Verizon, Discovery) it won, competing against agencies on simple ROI. (Sprig fka UserLeap - Diligence)
- Recruits non-customer prospects matching a persona for a few dollars each; added video in 2018 (faces + voices), enabling synchronous research where you can "pull on a thread." ([Joseph Ambeault](/notes/joseph-ambeault), Sprig fka UserLeap - Diligence)
- The system of record for 1x1 interviews (Zoom call → records → pushes into platform); [Chipper Cash](/notes/chipper-cash)'s best sign-up path is a [Sprig](/notes/sprig) survey that offers a UserTesting booking slot at the end. (Sprig fka UserLeap - Diligence)
- Pricing: several thousand per corporate seat ([Dropbox](/notes/dropbox)); [Chipper Cash](/notes/chipper-cash) estimates ~$45K/year plus panel-acquisition charges. (Sprig fka UserLeap - Diligence)
- Caveat: paid panel participants aren't always the average customer you serve, which can be a deterrent ([Square](/notes/square)); [Dropbox](/notes/dropbox) mitigates by setting stringent panel requirements. (Sprig fka UserLeap - Diligence)
- Already has a collaborative research function, which [Yue Wu](/notes/yue-wu) (Dropbox) said makes [Great Question](/notes/great-question) "not much different from UserTesting." (Sprig fka UserLeap - Diligence)
