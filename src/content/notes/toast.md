# Toast

Toast appears in Kyle's notes as a recurring exemplar in two threads. First, as a **"foundational data layer"**: per the [Pave](/notes/pave) notes (drawing on the September 13th, 2022 Harry Stebbings Podcast), Toast is the canonical case of a company that wins by owning the foundational data of its domain — restaurants — placed in parallel with [Ramp](/notes/ramp) (expenses), [Persona](/notes/persona) (identity), and Pave itself (compensation). Second, as a **public-market cautionary tale**: in [Semil Shah](/notes/semil-shah)'s [The Market Is The Greatest Critic](/notes/the-market-is-the-greatest-critic), Toast ($TOST) is named alongside [Robinhood](/notes/robinhood) as a high-flying tech company punished in the Nov '21–Jan '22 crash, an illustration of the piece's thesis that "the market delivers the harshest critique."

A more skeptical read shows up in the June 24th, 2021 — Gordon Tsai (Coatue) sourcing call: when pitching a "Toast thesis" for a deskless-worker company (Workstream), the critique was that the comparable doesn't have "nearly as good of ARR per location and payments on the upside" — i.e., Toast's economics (recurring software plus payments take rate, multiplied across locations) are the bar that copycat theses fail to clear.

**Context:** Toast is a Boston-based restaurant technology company providing an all-in-one point-of-sale, payments, and management platform for restaurants; it went public (NYSE: TOST) in 2021.
