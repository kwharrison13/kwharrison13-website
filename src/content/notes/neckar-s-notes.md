# Neckar's Notes

Neckar's Notes (also published as "Alchemy of Money") is the Substack written by [Frederik Gieschen](/notes/frederik-gieschen) — "the Neckar" / "NeckarValue" — on investing, creativity, and the inner game. In Kyle's notes it recurs as a referenced body of work whose individual pieces are cited across the wiki. Kyle read a cluster of Gieschen's essays in April 2022 — "Attempting the Impossible," "Divorce, Denial, Dissonance Reduction," and "The Reading Obsession" — and pulled durable lessons from them. The "Attempting the Impossible" piece anchors Kyle's [Build a Fund](/notes/build-a-fund) concept, arguing how hard it is to spin out and raise a first fund "unless you come from a prestigious shop and have initial backing," with the real edge being the ability to "build a network, tell a story, and get an introduction."

Gieschen's broader thesis, recorded from his Neckar writing, is that investor-writers like Buffett and Howard Marks "used content to instill trust, trust gave them permanent capital, and permanent capital gave them a massive financial advantage" — alongside a distinction between an investment *business* (gives customers what they want) and an investment *practice* (serves the client in a way that reflects the practitioner's craft). His Neckar piece "Cranking The Learning Machine Up To 11" is catalogued as one of Kyle's long reads, and Kyle harvested the [Jesse Livermore](/notes/jesse-livermore) "nothing new in Wall Street" quote while reading his work.

**Context:** Neckar's Notes / The Alchemy of Money is a real investing newsletter by Frederik Gieschen, known for essays blending market history, the psychology of great investors, and the writer's-craft angle on building trust and capital.
