# Contenda

Contenda is a content-tooling startup founded by [Lilly Chen](/notes/lilly-chen), tracked in Kyle's notes through its portfolio updates while at [Contrary Capital](/notes/contrary). The product repurposes existing content (e.g. turning blog posts and developer material into new content pieces), positioned explicitly against the manual content-agency workflow. In the May 2022 update Chen reported a pilot with [Segment](/notes/segment), where Segment's Head of Content Marketing was paying ~$10k/mo to an agency to repurpose six blogs with month-plus turnaround — a successful pilot there could open a path into Twilio, and Chen noted that "anchoring our product against an agency feels like strong positioning."

On go-to-market, Contenda learned its sale typically needs only one decision-maker at its price point: a head of content or marketing who can approve up to ~$30k/month, whereas Developer Relations are users but not the buyers. The company was hiring an ML/NLP specialist and counted investors including Acrew Capital and Scribble Ventures. The earlier March 2022 update was lighter — a demo for [Honeycomb](/notes/honeycomb)'s CTO aimed at securing a design partnership.
