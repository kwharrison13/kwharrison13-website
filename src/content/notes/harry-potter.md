# Harry Potter

In Kyle's wiki, Harry Potter appears as a worked example of franchise/[IP](/notes/intellectual-property) dependence in Hollywood, via the essay [The Hits Business](/essays/the-hits-business). [David Zaslav](/notes/david-zaslav), CEO of Warner Bros. Discovery, named it explicitly in a November 2022 message to investors: *"We're going to focus on franchises. We haven't had a Superman movie in 13 years. We haven't done a Harry Potter in 15 years."* The quote is cited as an executive "saying the quiet part out loud" — Harry Potter standing in for the dormant, high-value IP that public-company growth pressure pushes studios to revive.

In that framing, the franchise is less a story than an asset: the public-company profit motive ("there must be growth… there must be Shared Universes") is what drives the decision to reactivate it.

**Context:** Harry Potter is the J.K. Rowling book series and Warner Bros. film franchise; its Wizarding World is one of the highest-grossing media franchises ever, and a Max television adaptation was subsequently greenlit — the kind of IP revival Zaslav was signaling.
