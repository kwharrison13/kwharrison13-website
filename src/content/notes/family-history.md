# Family History

A theme that runs in two distinct senses across Kyle's notes. In its **genealogical / heritage** sense, it tags moments of honoring and recounting forebears: in [Gordon B. Hinckley — Go Forward With Faith](/books/gordon-b-hinckley-go-forward-with-faith), Kyle marks Hinckley stopping at "nearly every roadside historical marker" and repeating stories of Ira Hinckley and other ancestors at Cove Fort, and pairs the note with [Darwin Harrison](/notes/darwin-harrison)'s line "Pray as if everything depended on God, and then work as if everything depended on you." [Saints — No Unhallowed Hand](/books/saints-no-unhallowed-hand) is read under the tags `#Faith` #[History of the Church](/notes/history-of-the-church) #[Family History](/notes/family-history) — the interior story of building an institution out of fallible people. In the Alma 17–22 missionary study, "family history" is invoked in the sense of making teaching relevant by rehearsing a people's own lineage and record (Ammon recounting the rebellions of Laman and Lemuel down to the present).

In a second, looser sense it marks **a person's family background or lineage** as a way of understanding them. In the June 24th, 2021 — Molly Alter (Index Ventures) call, Kyle logs Molly Alter's family under this tag — a sister reporting at TIME, a father at Newsweek and presidential biographer, a mother who worked at the Colbert Report and The Daily Show. The same flavor appears in [Becoming](/books/becoming), tagged alongside [Marriage](/notes/marriage), [Parenting](/notes/parenting), and [Everyone Has a Story](/notes/everyone-has-a-story). Across both senses the through-line is Kyle's interest in how lineage, heritage, and the stories a family carries shape identity and example.

**Context:** In Latter-day Saint practice, "family history" specifically denotes genealogical research undertaken so that temple ordinances can be performed for deceased ancestors — a doctrinally significant activity, distinct from the general English sense of one's family background.
