# Observability

Observability is the software category Kyle's notes anchor to [Datadog ($DDOG)](/notes/datadog) — the monitoring/observability platform that serves as the reference company across his 2021 cloud-infrastructure diligence. Its strategic significance in the wiki comes from the analogy [Shardul Shah](/notes/shardul-shah) of [Index Ventures](/notes/index-ventures) used to frame [Wiz.io](/notes/wiz-io): "Wiz has the opportunity to do the same for Security" that "[Datadog ($DDOG)](/notes/datadog) did for Observability." Observability thus functions less as a standalone idea than as the *template* — a horizontal, practitioner-facing infrastructure layer that grew into a category-defining public company, and the pattern a new cloud-security platform might repeat.

The company-building lessons Kyle preserved from Shah's Datadog teardown are read as the observability playbook: price premium as "positive friction," market to the practitioner through educational content, expect a TAM that expands every six months, don't fear a competitive market (it signals demand), and be creative about building ownership by scooping up secondary.

**Context:** In software engineering, observability is the ability to understand a system's internal state from its external outputs — typically the "three pillars" of metrics, logs, and traces. Datadog, Splunk, New Relic, and Grafana are leading vendors in the category.
