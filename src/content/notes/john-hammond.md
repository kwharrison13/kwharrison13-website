# John Hammond

The legendary music talent scout held up in [Talent (Book)](/books/talent) (by [Tyler Cowen](/notes/tyler-cowen) and [Daniel Gross](/notes/daniel-gross)) as the archetypal scout. The book uses Hammond's record to make a core point about spotting talent: top talent is *rarely* identified by the establishment first, precisely because original creators are doing something new. Guitarist George Benson offered one clue to Hammond's method — "John Hammond didn't necessarily care about how many records I could sell; his approach was more along the lines of 'look at what this talent can do, and I hope you enjoy him as much as I do.'" The lesson Kyle draws from the passage: great scouting means betting on originality before the market validates it.

**Context:** John Hammond (1910–1987) was an American record producer and talent scout who discovered or championed Billie Holiday, Count Basie, Aretha Franklin, Bob Dylan, and Bruce Springsteen, among others.
