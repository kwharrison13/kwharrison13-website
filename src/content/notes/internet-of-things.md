# Internet of Things

The Internet of Things (IoT) runs through Kyle's 2018 smart-city and mobility notes as the instrumentation layer that turns physical assets into data. At [Innova](/notes/innova), IoT is the defining feature of the business: a first/last-mile electric mobility-as-a-service company whose electric vehicles are instrumented with an IoT data-analytics platform (sensors, cameras, air-quality sensing, ground radar) gathering data roughly every 10ms. The real thesis there isn't rides but data ([Data in Investing](/notes/data-in-investing)) — Innova aims to be the most-instrumented high-use mobility option and sell its aggregated data and dashboards to cities, utilities, transit agencies, and employers.

[Venture Smarter](/notes/venture-smarter) frames IoT at the city scale: a smart-city / IoT community and [Cisco](/notes/cisco) partner whose whole premise rests on a Cisco study finding ~75% of IoT projects fail, which Venture Smarter attributes to smart-city development being a deeply siloed problem — "how can we get these smart projects to completion in real life?" Its co-founder Zach started the [IEEE Smart City Standards Committee](/notes/ieee-smart-city-standards-committee), the standards body anchoring municipal IoT interoperability. On the public-sector side, [Doug McCullough](/notes/doug-mccullough) — CIO of Dublin, Ohio — names IoT as one of the technology domains he works across (alongside AI, autonomous driving, and AR), with Dublin's IoT and autonomous-mobility pilots tying into broader [City Building](/notes/city-building) themes.

> **Note:** This is the canonical page; "Internet of Things (IoT)" is an alias. A separate stub at `wiki/concepts/Internet of Things (IoT).md` (sourced from [Doug McCullough](/notes/doug-mccullough)) points here and can be merged/retired in cleanup.

**Context:** The Internet of Things refers to networks of physical objects — vehicles, sensors, appliances, infrastructure — embedded with connectivity that lets them collect and exchange data. It is foundational to "smart city" initiatives, which instrument municipal systems (transit, utilities, mobility) to gather real-time data for analytics and automation.
