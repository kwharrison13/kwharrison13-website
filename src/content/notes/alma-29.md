# Alma 29

A chapter in the Book of Mormon best known to Kyle's study notes for Alma's exclamation **"O that I were an angel, and could have the wish of mine heart, that I might go forth and speak with the trump of God... and cry repentance unto every people"** ([Alma 29](/notes/alma-29):1) — immediately tempered by **"But behold, I am a man, and do sin in my wish; for I ought to be content with the things which the Lord hath allotted unto me"** ([Alma 29](/notes/alma-29):3). Kyle's notes on the Alma 23–29 study read the chapter as a meditation on being **an instrument in God's hands** ([Alma 29](/notes/alma-29):9 — "I do not glory of myself, but I glory in that which the Lord hath commanded me... that perhaps I may be an instrument in the hands of God to bring some soul to repentance; and this is my joy"), pairing it with [D&C 18](/notes/dandc-18):14–16 and [Joseph Smith](/notes/joseph-smith)'s line that "a man filled with the love of God is not content with blessing his family alone."

The chapter also anchors Kyle's reading of [Envy](/notes/envy): Alma "do[es] not joy in my own success alone, but my joy is more full because of the success of my brethren" ([Alma 29](/notes/alma-29):14–15) — joying in others' missionary fruit rather than measuring himself against it. Across the topical studies, Alma 29 is cited as a paradigm of humility (a prophet who was a spiritual giant *because* he recognized his own inadequacy and that he "ought to be content") and of patience (contentment with "the things which the Lord hath allotted").

**Context:** Alma 29 is a short chapter in the Book of Mormon recording the missionary Alma the Younger's psalm-like reflection on his desire to preach repentance to all people.
