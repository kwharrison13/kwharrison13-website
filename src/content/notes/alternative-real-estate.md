# Alternative Real Estate

Kyle's term for treating under-used or distressed built space — dead malls, crumbling suburbs, parking lots, cheap small-metro land — as a reinvention and arbitrage opportunity rather than a write-off. The thesis crystallizes in his notes on [The New Urban Crisis](/books/the-new-urban-crisis): the same clustering force that prices people out of superstar cities opens "a huge buying opportunity… if you have a formula / strategy for increasing the value of that land," reading the gulf between $64,800-an-acre superstar land and $6,700-an-acre small-metro land as straightforward [Capital Allocation](/notes/capital-allocation) arbitrage. His margin note asks directly, "Can we seize on these as buying opportunities to reinvent?"

The concept bridges into Kyle's deal and city-building work. [Fifth Partners](/notes/fifth-partners) — surfaced in the July 2nd, 2020 — Lars Hamilton (High Alpha Innovation) meeting via investor Tom Peterson — is building modular homes for low-income families, tagged here. [The New City](/notes/the-new-city) gathers the co-living and land-acquisition material (Blueground, Brooklyn/Bay-Area co-living, Toll Brothers) under the same banner. The saved reading The Massive Farms Emerging Beneath Our Cities (BBC Future) extends it to repurposing under-used urban space for farming. The connective tissue runs through [Malls](/notes/malls) and [Parking Lots](/notes/parking-lots) as the canonical under-utilized assets to reimagine.
