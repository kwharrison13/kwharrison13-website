# Roll-up Strategy

A roll-up strategy acquires many small businesses in the same category and consolidates them under one owner. In Kyle's notes it appears as the buy-side counterpart to the [SMB](/notes/smb) asset class: the targets are small, owner-operated businesses with no real management bench, where the major risk is the ownership transition itself (per the March 5th, 2020 diligence calls cited on the [SMB](/notes/smb) page). [Teamshares](/notes/teamshares) and "related roll-up strategies" are named as the kind of buyers pursuing these assets, and the broader category for roll-ups buying up SMBs is [Private Equity](/notes/private-equity).

**Context:** A roll-up (or "buy-and-build") strategy consolidates a fragmented industry of small operators into one larger company to capture scale economies, multiple arbitrage (small companies trade at lower earnings multiples than the combined entity), and shared back-office infrastructure. It is a common private-equity playbook in services-heavy, locally fragmented industries.

## Where this appears

- [SMB](/notes/smb) — small owner-operated businesses are the target asset class for [Teamshares](/notes/teamshares) and related roll-up strategies, where ownership transition is the major risk.
