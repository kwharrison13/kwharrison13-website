# Character Skills

In the August 18th, 2021 conversation with [Conor White-Sullivan](/notes/conor-white-sullivan) and [Darwin Harrison](/notes/darwin-harrison), "Character Skills" is paired with [Technical Skills](/notes/technical-skills) as the two capability sets a person needs to actually *act* on a Holistic Mindset rather than just hold a holistic viewpoint. The distinction came out of Darwin Harrison's observation, from 15 years of working with people who shared a "Holistic Viewpoint but not a Holistic Mindset": when it came to action they weren't capable of doing certain things, because they lacked a clear understanding of what was required — and even once they understood, it never became a priority. The diagnosis in the notes is that "they lacked Character Skills and Technical Skills."

The term sits inside a broader framework in that meeting about how to "help a group of people who share values transform the world" — moving people from a siloed mindset to a holistic one, distinguishing doers from thinkers, and connecting to [Practical Knowledge](/notes/practical-knowledge), [Theoretical Knowledge](/notes/theoretical-knowledge), and [Growth Mindset](/notes/growth-mindset). Character Skills is the disposition/will side of that pair (priority, intentional action), where Technical Skills is the competence side.
