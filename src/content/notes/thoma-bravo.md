# Thoma Bravo

Thoma Bravo appears in Kyle's notes as a software-focused private equity firm distinguished by its bet quality. In the June 24th, 2021 — Gordon Tsai (Coatue) deal-intel call, Gordon Tsai sketches a taxonomy of crossover/PE firms and rates Thoma Bravo highly — "Thoma is really good and takes non-consensus bets" — in explicit contrast to Vista Equity Partners ("pretty stupid and taking consensus bets"). The same framework places [Coatue](/notes/coatue) and Dragoneer/Greenoaks Capital Partners elsewhere on the smart/consensus axis.

Thoma Bravo also surfaces concretely as the owner of [Instructure](/notes/instructure): in the March 30th, 2022 — Daniel Appel (Instructure) call, Appel notes he's been "100% dedicated" to corporate development / inorganic growth "since Thoma Bravo bought them" — Instructure being a ~$3B-revenue, ~2K-employee business under Thoma Bravo's ownership.

**Context:** Thoma Bravo is one of the largest technology-focused private equity firms, known for take-private buyouts of enterprise software companies (e.g. SolarWinds, Anaplan, Coupa, Instructure).
