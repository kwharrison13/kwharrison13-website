# Thinking

A broad bare-hashtag concept Kyle uses to tag passages about sustained mental application. On the [Thomas Edison](/notes/thomas-edison) page it anchors Edison's argument that most people spend their hours "walking, or [Reading](/notes/reading), or [Writing](/notes/writing), or [Thinking](/notes/thinking)" but scatter that effort across many things, whereas success comes from applying it "in one direction, to one object" — "the severest kind of mental and physical application." In [Poor Charlie's Almanack](/books/poor-charlie-s-almanack) the tag appears with `#listening`, attached to Charlie Munger's habit of deep, distraction-walling concentration; the book's broader treatment of cognition lives under the more specific [Multidisciplinary Thinking](/notes/multidisciplinary-thinking) concept.

## Where this appears

- [Thomas Edison](/notes/thomas-edison) — in Edison's quote that success is the product of severe, single-object mental application rather than scattered effort
- [Poor Charlie's Almanack](/books/poor-charlie-s-almanack) — tagged `#thinking` on a passage about Munger's deep, distraction-proof concentration
