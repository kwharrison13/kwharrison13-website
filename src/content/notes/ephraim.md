# Ephraim

In Kyle's Abrahamic Covenant study, Ephraim is the lineage through which the birthright of Israel passes. Though Manasseh was Joseph's firstborn, Israel (Jacob) gave the birthright — a double portion of inheritance and patriarchal status — to the younger son Ephraim, because he knew Ephraim's seed would grow (Genesis 48:17–20), while Judah became chief ruler ([1 Chronicles 5](/notes/1-chronicles-5):1–2). The study treats Ephraim as a load-bearing thread in the covenant story: [Joseph Smith](/notes/joseph-smith) was of Ephraim and held "the stick of Joseph (Ephraim)" when he held the plates, and in [Ezekiel 37](/notes/ezekiel-37)'s prophecy the stick of Ephraim/Joseph (Book of Mormon) joins the stick of Judah (Bible).

The practical upshot Kyle draws is responsibility: the LDS church is understood as made up mostly of the house of Ephraim, and as "holders of the birthright" its members carry the charge to gather Israel, build the kingdom, and spread the gospel before the Second Coming — the modern fulfillment of the [Gathering of Israel](/notes/gathering-of-israel).

**Context:** In the Hebrew Bible, Ephraim is the younger son of Joseph and grandson of Jacob; the tribe of Ephraim became one of the most prominent of the ten northern tribes of Israel. In Latter-day Saint theology, Ephraim is associated with the birthright lineage responsible for gathering Israel in the last days.
