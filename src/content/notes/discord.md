# Discord

Discord appears in Kyle's notes as a reference customer across two 2021 diligence dossiers. In the Pave Diligence — 2021 research it is listed among [Pave](/notes/pave)'s customers and, more specifically, among the "40+ [Index Ventures](/notes/index-ventures) portfolio companies" contributing to Pave's give-to-get compensation data network — grouped with [Instabase](/notes/instabase), [Anrok](/notes/anrok), [Newfront Insurance](/notes/newfront-insurance), [Scale AI](/notes/scale-ai), [Boulevard](/notes/boulevard), and [Applyboard](/notes/applyboard). That clustering is part of how Pave's proprietary, real-time comp dataset ("[Radford](/notes/radford) but real-time") is shown to have gained density inside Index's portfolio.

In the Watershed Climate Diligence — 2021 research, Discord is profiled as a different kind of customer archetype for climate-accounting software: a company that knows "this isn't going to be my thing" but wants the carbon-measurement box "done right" — Watershed as the "internal ESG team." So across the two dossiers Discord functions as a representative mid-market SaaS/consumer-platform buyer rather than a deal subject.

**Context:** Discord is a voice, video, and text chat platform founded in 2015, originally built for gaming communities and now used broadly for online communities and groups.

## Where this appears

- Pave Diligence — 2021 — listed as a Pave customer and one of the 40+ Index Ventures portfolio companies feeding Pave's comp-data network.
- Watershed Climate Diligence — 2021 — profiled as a "check-the-box, done right" climate-software customer archetype (Watershed as the internal ESG team).
