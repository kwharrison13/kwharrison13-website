# John 21

The final chapter of the Gospel of John, which Kyle cites in two studies for its closing exchange between the resurrected Christ and Peter. In Obedience — Topical Study, the key verse is John 21:22 — "What is that to thee? Follow thou me." — Kyle's image of obedience as a "leap in the dark," the willingness to follow Christ regardless of what we do or don't understand, the prerequisite to sanctification. In The Process of Zion, the relevant passage is John 21:15–18 (the "lovest thou me… feed my sheep" dialogue), used to anchor the claim that we must learn to live celestial law *because we love Christ* — celestial obedience is the "want to" of consecration, not the "have to" of compulsion.

**Context:** John 21 is the epilogue of the Gospel of John, recounting Jesus' post-resurrection appearance to disciples while fishing, the miraculous catch, and his threefold charge to Peter to "feed my sheep."

## Where this appears

- Obedience — Topical Study — 21:22 ("Follow thou me") as the leap-in-the-dark invitation that precedes sanctification
- The Process of Zion — 21:15–18 ("feed my sheep") grounding celestial obedience in love of Christ
