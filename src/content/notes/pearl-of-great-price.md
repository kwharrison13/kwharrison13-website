# Pearl of Great Price

One of the standard works of the LDS canon, referenced across Kyle's gospel study as both a source text and a point of scholarly contention. In First Vision — Topical Study it is the canonical published account of the [First Vision](/notes/first-vision) — it "declares the first vision occurred in the 'early spring of 1820,'" and Kyle weighs its reliability as Joseph's "3rd draft of a published work" against the earlier unpublished manuscript that seems to place the event in his 16th year (a memory/chronology question via [Joseph Smith History](/notes/joseph-smith-history)). In Worthiness, it appears in Brother Bott's recommended missionary reading order — [Book of Mormon](/notes/book-of-mormon) first, then the New Testament, [D&C](/notes/doctrine-and-covenants), the Pearl of Great Price, then the Old Testament.

[An Insider's View of Mormon Origins](/books/an-insider-s-view-of-mormon-origins) engages the Pearl of Great Price critically, reading the Book of Abraham (contained within it) as reflecting antebellum, Thomas-Dick cosmology rather than antiquity — Palmer's suspicion that both the Book of Mormon and the Pearl of Great Price "represent a nineteenth-century encounter with God rather than an ancient epic," enlightening spiritually but of no value for ancient history. Kyle's note there observes that ideas compatible with science would be expected to sound more modern than ancient over time.

**Context:** The Pearl of Great Price is one of the four standard works of the Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-day Saints, alongside the Bible, the Book of Mormon, and the Doctrine and Covenants. It contains the Book of Moses, the Book of Abraham, Joseph Smith—Matthew, Joseph Smith—History, and the Articles of Faith.
