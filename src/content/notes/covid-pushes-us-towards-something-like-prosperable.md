# COVID pushes us towards something like Prosperable

This is Kyle's own thesis, recorded against his read of [The New Urban Crisis](/books/the-new-urban-crisis): the pandemic accelerates the case for [Prosperable](/notes/prosperable) / [The New City](/notes/the-new-city) and the [micro cities](/notes/micro-cities) design. Florida's data says the affluent return to the dense urban core for three reasons — high-paying jobs, short commutes, and amenities. [Remote Work](/notes/remote-work) dissolves the first two by removing the proximity and commute constraints on a high-paying job, leaving amenities as the only remaining draw. And in a [COVID](/notes/covid) world the desired amenities have inverted: backyards, larger houses, and open space become more attractive than the density of the core — pointing people *away* from mega-cities and toward deliberately designed micro-cities. The idea ties Richard Florida's "micro cities over sprawl and mega-cities" prescription directly into Kyle's [Prosperable](/notes/prosperable) project and the [EPCOT](/notes/epcot) hub-and-spoke model from [Walt Disney and the Promise of Progress City](/books/walt-disney-and-the-promise-of-progress-city).
