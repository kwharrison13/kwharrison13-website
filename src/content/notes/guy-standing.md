# Guy Standing

Guy Standing is the British economist who named [The Precariat](/notes/the-precariat). He enters Kyle's notes through [Utopia For Realists](/books/utopia-for-realists), where [Rutger Bregman](/notes/rutger-bregman) quotes Standing's prediction of "the emergence of a new, dangerous 'precariat' — a surging social class of people in low-wage, temporary jobs and with no political voice." Standing supplies the human-cost concept that Bregman's case for [Universal Basic Income](/notes/universal-basic-income) and post-scarcity policy is built to address: the population stranded by precarious, low-status work as [Automation](/notes/automation) decouples productivity from jobs.

**Context:** Guy Standing is a development economist (a founder of the Basic Income Earth Network) whose 2011 book *The Precariat: The New Dangerous Class* popularized the term — a portmanteau of "precarious" and "proletariat" — for workers lacking stable employment, occupational identity, and labor protections. He has been a longtime advocate of unconditional basic income.
