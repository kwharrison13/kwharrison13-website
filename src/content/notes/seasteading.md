# Seasteading

Building permanent autonomous communities on the open ocean as a path to greenfield governance — one branch of the broader [City Building](/notes/city-building) thesis (charter cities, seasteads, network-state "exits") of founding new polities from scratch rather than reforming legacy ones. In the wiki, seasteading enters through the [City Building](/notes/city-building) page as part of the intellectual lineage that runs from [Paul Romer](/notes/paul-romer)'s charter-cities idea through [Patri Friedman](/notes/patri-friedman) and the seasteading movement to [Balaji Srinivasan](/notes/balaji-srinivasan)-style network-state exit.

It is a formative influence in the Bluebook Cities origin story recounted there: [Dryden Brown](/notes/dryden-brown) had "followed [Paul Romer](/notes/paul-romer), [Patri Friedman](/notes/patri-friedman), and the seasteading movement since high school" and was "obsessed with building a new city." Friedman himself later appears as an investor in the founders' seed round to develop charter-city legislation in Ghana — so on this page seasteading reads less as a literal ocean-platform project than as the ideological on-ramp to frontier city-building.

**Context:** Seasteading was popularized by Patri Friedman (grandson of Milton Friedman), who co-founded The Seasteading Institute in 2008 with early backing from [Peter Thiel](/notes/peter-thiel). The aim is to create floating ocean settlements outside existing national jurisdictions so that new systems of governance can be tried experimentally — "exit" rather than "voice."
