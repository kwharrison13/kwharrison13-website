# Names

In Kyle's Come Follow Me study of Helaman 1–Helaman 6 (August 17–23, 2020. Helaman 1–6: "The Rock of Our Redeemer"), "Names" is the theme drawn from Helaman's counsel to his sons Nephi and Lehi. Helaman tells them he has given them "the names of our first parents who came out of the land of Jerusalem… that when you remember your names ye may remember them; and when ye remember them ye may remember their works… that they were good" (Helaman 5:6). The study treats a name as a memory-device and an inheritance: remembering your name chains backward to remembering whose example you carry, which is why Helaman pairs it with building "on the rock of our Redeemer, who is Christ." Kyle juxtaposes the scriptural treatment with [John Proctor](/notes/john-proctor)'s cry from #[The Crucible](/notes/the-crucible) — "Because it is my name!… How may I live without my name?" — framing a name as identity, integrity, and the thing one will not surrender.

## Where this appears

- August 17–23, 2020. Helaman 1–6: "The Rock of Our Redeemer" — Helaman's counsel to remember your name (and thus your forebears' works) as a foundation built on Christ, set against John Proctor's defense of his name in *The Crucible*.
