# Matt Yglesias

American political and economic writer, author of [One Billion Americans](/notes/one-billion-americans) and proprietor of the [Slow Boring](/notes/slow-boring) newsletter. In Kyle's Great Softening research, Yglesias surfaces as the source of the "increase [immigration](/notes/immigration) to 1b Americans" idea — his argument that the U.S. should roughly triple its population, largely through immigration, to sustain its standing and dynamism. The note slots this into a thread weighing whether America is in decline, alongside the framing that immigrants may be the most aspirational people and that great powers (Romans → British) average roughly 200 years.

**Context:** Matthew Yglesias is a co-founder of Vox who now writes the Substack newsletter Slow Boring; his 2020 book *One Billion Americans: The Case for Thinking Bigger* argues for tripling the U.S. population through immigration and pro-family policy.
