# Moses 5

The Book of Moses chapter (Pearl of Great Price) covering the lives of Adam and Eve after the Fall and the story of Cain and Abel — the source for Kyle's The Curse of Cain topical study. There, Moses 5:36 ("cursed from the earth") is read as the loss of the birthright of an heir of the earth — the opposite of "if ye shall keep my commandments, ye shall prosper in the land" — and Moses 5:39 ("cut off from the presence of God") as comparable to losing access at the entrance of the temple. In committing murder against the covenant, Cain lost his birthright and his priesthood power, and the curse becomes generational: if a forefather loses his inheritance, his posterity loses theirs — "that's how [Satan](/notes/satan) can damage your posterity."

The chapter also surfaces in Teaching Repentance, where Moses 5:14, 58-59 ground the principle that "as long as there has been [Agency](/notes/agency) there has been repentance" — God allows all men to partake adequately of redemption, only as they choose. The gospel was taught to Adam and his posterity from the beginning, so accountability and the call to repent are as old as agency itself.

**Context:** Moses 5 retells Genesis 4–5 in Joseph Smith's revelatory expansion, adding that Adam and Eve received the gospel and the plan of redemption after leaving Eden, and detailing Cain's covenant with Satan, the murder of Abel, and the curse that followed.

## Where this appears

- The Curse of Cain — Moses 5:36 and 5:39 as Cain's loss of birthright, priesthood, and the presence of God, and the generational mechanism of a broken lineage.
- Teaching Repentance — Moses 5:14, 58-59 as evidence that redemption and repentance have accompanied agency from the beginning.
