# Mormon 6

The Book of Mormon chapter recording the **final destruction of the Nephites** and [Mormon](/notes/mormon)'s lamentation over them — [Mormon 6](/notes/mormon-6):17: "O ye fair ones, how could ye have departed from the ways of the Lord? O ye fair ones, how could ye have rejected that Jesus, who stood with open arms to receive you." In Charity & Love Kyle uses Mormon's lament as a case study in *charity as the prompting motive*: Mormon's grief was not itself charity but the pure love of Christ that *moved* the lament — paralleling [Lehi](/notes/lehi) desiring to share the fruit ([1 Nephi 8](/notes/1-nephi-8)) and [Alma](/notes/alma) nursing Amulek ([Alma 15](/notes/alma-15)). "Their acts were not charity — their charity was the love that moved the acts."

In the Come Follow Me study October 26–November 1, 2020. Mormon 1–6: "I Would That I Could Persuade All … to Repent", the chapter is the tragic close of [The Pride Cycle](/notes/the-pride-cycle) — a people who "fortify their cities in fear instead of faith" and are "left to ourselves" when they refuse to repent, tied to the study's themes of Godly Sorrow and [Repentance](/notes/repentance).

**Context:** Mormon 6 is the chapter in the Book of Mormon in which the prophet-historian Mormon describes the final battle at Cumorah where the Nephite nation is destroyed, and mourns over his fallen people.
