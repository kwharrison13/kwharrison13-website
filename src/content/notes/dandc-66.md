# D&C 66

Section 66 of the [Doctrine and Covenants](/notes/doctrine-and-covenants) appears in Kyle's The Reasons The Spirit Withdraws study, where verse 3 is cited for the principle that one can be "clean, but not all" — pure in most things yet still in need of repentance in the remainder. The reference sits among scriptures ([Philippians 3](/notes/philippians-3), [Isaiah 48](/notes/isaiah-48), [1 Nephi 20](/notes/1-nephi-20)) used to examine why the Spirit withdraws and how incomplete cleanliness leaves room for repentance.

**Context:** Doctrine and Covenants Section 66 is a revelation given to Joseph Smith in October 1831 for William E. McLellin, who had silently asked the Lord to reveal his standing through the prophet. It tells him he is "blessed... for the integrity of his heart" yet warns him of areas still requiring repentance.
