# Twitter

In Kyle's notes, Twitter is less a company than a *networking-and-learning engine* — the recurring frame is [Bill Gurley](/notes/bill-gurley)'s line, quoted in the [Perell/Kobach workshop](/notes/how-to-crush-it-on-twitter-david-perell-and-matthew-kobach-workshop): "Twitter is the most amazing networking and learning network ever built," where you can build a network "solely based on your clarity and quality of thought." That workshop treats Twitter as a place rewarding compression and novelty, and explains why VCs in particular "crush it" — their job is to have ideas others don't ([VC Twitter](/notes/vc-twitter)). [How to Maximize Serendipity](/notes/how-to-maximize-serendipity) adds the forward-looking version: "If Facebook connects you with people from your past, Twitter connects you with people in your future." It's also Kyle's own building surface — the July 18th, 2022 — Tidemark (Brand-Building Presentation) notes cite his own tweets (first blog post, angel investments) as brand-building artifacts and name Twitter as a core channel ("Newsletter + Twitter").

The platform also appears as a talent and signal map: [Dan McMurtrie](/notes/dan-mcmurtrie) is "quoted on identifying networks via [Twitter](/notes/twitter)" and describes it as the platform for mapping market networks and talent pools, and [The Everything Store](/books/the-everything-store) notes surface it under the [Talent Vortex](/notes/talent-vortex) thread ("Dropbox and Twitter for finance"). Its corporate identity surfaces sharply in the June 8th, 2023 — Amir Shevat (Darkmode Ventures) call, where Shevat recounts being "fired by [Elon Musk](/notes/elon-musk) at Twitter when he shut down the API."

**Context:** Twitter is the social-media platform founded in 2006; Elon Musk acquired it in 2022 and rebranded it to "X" in 2023.
