# John 6

The chapter of John's Gospel — the Bread of Life discourse — that Kyle draws on for two distinct teachings. For submission to God's will, Prayer — Topical Study cites [John 6](/notes/john-6):38 ("I came down from heaven, not to do mine own will, but the will of Him that sent me") as Christ's model of sanctification: "it is as we submit ourselves to God's will that we sanctify ourselves," and "our job is not to pull heaven down to our will but to align our will with God's." For laboring after the right things, the Helaman 13–16 study quotes [John 6](/notes/john-6):27 ("Labour not for the meat which perisheth, but for that meat which endureth unto everlasting life") as part of a scripture chain on enduring versus perishing goods. In Spiritual Gifts, the chapter is tagged simply as [John 6](/notes/john-6):35 — "bread of life."

**Context:** John 6 contains the feeding of the five thousand, Jesus walking on water, and the Bread of Life discourse in which He declares "I am the bread of life," teaching that He is the sustenance of eternal life.
