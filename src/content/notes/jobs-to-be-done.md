# Jobs to Be Done

[Clayton Christensen](/notes/clayton-christensen)'s framing — what *job* is a customer "hiring" a product to do — that recurs across Kyle's product and venture thinking. It travels with the [Innovator's Dilemma](/notes/innovator-s-dilemma) as the demand-side companion to disruption theory. The clearest worked example in Kyle's corpus is [Playing To Win](/books/playing-to-win), where Febreze is described as "a product technology in search of a job to be done" (Kyle's note: "Not always bad" — sometimes the technology arrives before the use case clarifies). In [How Will You Measure Your Life?](/books/how-will-you-measure-your-life) Christensen turns the same lens on personal life and on schools "structured to do the job of success."

Kyle's signature move is applying JTBD to **venture capital itself**. In the September 13th, 2022 — Harry Stebbings Podcast interview he argues that "founders will increasingly start to ask the question, 'what is the job-to-be-done that I'm hiring this firm for?' — and if firms can't answer those questions, then they're going to struggle." This connects to his thesis that venture is becoming productized: firms, individual investors, and even functional teams each carry a "brand" that signals what job a founder is hiring them to do (he frames this alongside [Naked Brands](/notes/naked-brands) and well-articulated value props). It is a deliberate borrowing from "a Clayton Christensen framework from [Innovator's Dilemma](/notes/innovator-s-dilemma)."

On the product side, JTBD shows up as a go-to-market and retention discipline. [Product Led Growth: The End User Era](/books/product-led-growth-the-end-user-era) notes that some [Churn](/notes/churn) comes not from unhappy customers but from people "successfully completing a project and not knowing what to do next" — so education teams create JTBD-based content "to inspire customers to do more." In the November 2nd, 2021 — Riley Hughes (Trinsic) call, Auth0 is cited as a company that took OAuth/OIDC and "focused on the 'Jobs To Be Done' around easy sign-in" rather than the underlying protocols. It links to [Product-Market Fit](/notes/product-market-fit), [Differentiation in Venture](/notes/differentiation-in-venture), and the customer-obsession threads in [A.G. Lafley](/notes/a-g-lafley)'s work.

**Context:** Jobs to Be Done is a theory of customer behavior popularized by Harvard Business School professor Clayton Christensen (often illustrated with the "milkshake" study — customers "hire" a morning milkshake for the job of a long, boring commute). It reframes product strategy around the progress a customer is trying to make rather than demographics or product features.
