# Evelyn Beatrice Hall

English writer (1868–1956), biographer of [Voltaire](/notes/voltaire), best known for coining the line **"I disapprove of what you say, but I will defend to the death your right to say it"** in *The Friends of Voltaire* (1906) — often misattributed to Voltaire himself as a summary of his stance. Kyle pairs the quote, as a marginal cross-reference, with [Thomas Jefferson](/notes/thomas-jefferson)'s "eternal hostility against every form of tyranny over the mind of man" in Where Is Wisdom? — Russell M. Nelson.
