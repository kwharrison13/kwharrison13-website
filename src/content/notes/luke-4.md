# Luke 4

A New Testament chapter cited in The Lehonti Principle under the "Topics For Further Study" list, specifically Luke 4:24 as a proof-text for what Kyle there calls **The Nazarene Principle** — the pattern that a prophet is not accepted in his own country. In that note it is grouped with the parallel accounts in [Matthew 13](/notes/matthew-13):57, [Mark 6](/notes/mark-6):4, and [John 4](/notes/john-4):44, the last connected to [Jeffrey R. Holland](/notes/jeffrey-r-holland)'s "Remember Lot's Wife."

**Context:** Luke 4 narrates the beginning of Christ's Galilean ministry — His temptation in the wilderness, His reading of Isaiah in the Nazareth synagogue, and His rejection by His hometown, where He declares (v. 24) that "no prophet is accepted in his own country."
