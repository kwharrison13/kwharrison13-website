# Cesar Hidalgo

Physicist and complexity researcher cited in the long read [Critical States](/notes/critical-states) for his framing of criticality and [Phase Transition](/notes/phase-transition) — the idea that water about to freeze is still liquid, so "the real cause of the transition is the criticality of the state." He is the source Kyle leans on for the nonlinear, tipping-point view of change in that piece.

**Context:** César Hidalgo is a complexity scientist known for his work on economic complexity and the growth of information, author of *Why Information Grows: The Evolution of Order, from Atoms to Economies*. His research connects physics, networks, and economics.
