# Edward Lloyd

Edward Lloyd is the founder of [Lloyd's](/notes/lloyd-s), the London insurance market. Per the [Berkshire Hathaway Annual Letters](/books/berkshire-hathaway-annual-letters) (as recounted on the Lloyd's page), around 1688 Edward Lloyd opened a small London coffee house whose clientele — shipowners, merchants, and venturesome capitalists — began writing contracts transferring the risk of disaster at sea. Those capitalists became the "underwriters at Lloyd's," and the institution [Warren Buffett](/notes/warren-buffett) cites as more than three centuries old grew, almost spontaneously, out of that coffeehouse community — a parallel to [Clark's Coffeehouse](/notes/clark-s-coffeehouse).

**Context:** Edward Lloyd (c. 1648–1713) ran a coffee house on Tower Street, later Lombard Street, in London that became a gathering place for those involved in shipping; it gave its name to the marine-insurance market Lloyd's of London and to *Lloyd's List*, the maritime shipping journal.
