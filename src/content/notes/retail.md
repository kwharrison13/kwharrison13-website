# Retail

In Kyle's notes, **retail** is most developed through [David Perell](/notes/david-perell)'s essay [Saving Retail](/notes/saving-retail), which frames brick-and-mortar's reinvention as a city-building and experience problem rather than a transactions problem. The argument: [Amazon](/notes/amazon) disrupted malls on convenience (price, selection, speed), so survival depends on competing where technology can't — human connection, live entertainment, community, and photographable, status-conferring [experiences](/notes/experiences-vs-possessions). Perell revives [Victor Gruen](/notes/victor-gruen)'s original vision of the mall as a European-style community town square, and reads latent value in mall parking lots as self-driving cars and ride-hail reshape transport. The thread Kyle pulls forward: "status is transitioning from a function of what you own to what you do."

The concept also collects a reading-list pointer from Kyle's June 29th, 2021 Quick Capture log — an Axios piece on the "future of retail" and the shopping divide/inequality angle.

**Context:** Retail — the sale of goods to end consumers — has been reshaped over the last two decades by e-commerce (especially Amazon), driving the "retail apocalypse" of mall and department-store closures and a pivot toward experiential and omnichannel formats.
