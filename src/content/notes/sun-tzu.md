# Sun Tzu

In [2034 — A Novel of the Next World War](/books/2034-a-novel-of-the-next-world-war), Sun Tzu's [The Art of War](/books/the-art-of-war) is the strategic frame for the Chinese admiral Lin Bao, who quotes it directly — "If you know the enemy and know yourself, you need not fear the result of a hundred battles." The novel uses the maxim as Lin Bao's operating philosophy, threading classical strategy through a near-future scenario of networked naval warfare and escalation.

**Context:** Sun Tzu was a Chinese military strategist and general traditionally dated to the 5th–6th century BCE, credited as the author of *The Art of War*, the foundational treatise on strategy, deception, and the conduct of conflict that remains widely read in military, business, and political circles.
