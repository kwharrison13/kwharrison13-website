# John A. Widtsoe

An LDS apostle and scientist whose writing on truth-seeking recurs across Kyle's gospel study, especially the Knowledge — Topical Study and Spiritual Gifts pages. He is quoted on epistemics — "Knowledge comes by comparisons and contrasts. By light, we know darkness, by good we know evil... A person does not need to practice evil to know good" — and on the disciplined truth-seeker who "holds his judgement in abeyance until he has accumulated sufficient evidence," respecting authority "only after full personal conviction that it is real authority." That blend of faith and evidence is why he sits in the same cluster as [Boyd K. Packer](/notes/boyd-k-packer), [Truman G. Madsen](/notes/truman-g-madsen), and [Joseph Smith](/notes/joseph-smith) in Kyle's notes.

Widtsoe is also one of the LDS thinkers [Henry Eyring](/notes/henry-eyring) turns to in [The Faith of a Scientist](/books/the-faith-of-a-scientist) (alongside [Orson Pratt](/notes/orson-pratt) and [James E. Talmage](/notes/james-e-talmage)) for "just the right slant on the Gospel for scientific friends." And in [The Priesthood Power of Women](/books/the-priesthood-power-of-women) he is quoted twice — on a Heavenly Mother "who possesses the attributes of Godhood," and (via M. Russell Ballard) that "men have no greater claim than women upon the blessings that issue from the Priesthood."

**Context:** John A. Widtsoe (1872–1952) was a Norwegian-American chemist, university president (Utah State Agricultural College, University of Utah), and an apostle of The Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-day Saints from 1921 until his death, known for reconciling science and faith.
