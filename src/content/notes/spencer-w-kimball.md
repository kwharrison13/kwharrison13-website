# Spencer W. Kimball

The twelfth President of the LDS Church, who recurs across Kyle's reading in two main registers. The first is his terse imperative on works over knowledge — "To know is not enough. One must do" — which Kyle pulls forward as a structural quote in [Faith is Not Blind](/books/faith-is-not-blind) (where it argues that becoming Christlike "is more about acquiring skills than it is about learning facts"), [Joseph Smith — Rough Stone Rolling](/books/joseph-smith-rough-stone-rolling) (paired with the Nehemiah "arise and build" frame, [Be Up and Doing](/notes/be-up-and-doing)), and [Saints — No Unhallowed Hand](/books/saints-no-unhallowed-hand). [Discipline Is Destiny](/books/discipline-is-destiny) reads Ryan Holiday's Stoicism with a Kimball overlay, including Kyle's note on "Kimball's struggle with God."

The second register is economic and prophetic. In [Working Toward Zion](/books/working-toward-zion), Kimball is the modern voice on consecration and materialism: he warned against "lusting to be rich" and "making compromises in order to accumulate," called for Saints to "keep your money clean" (fair profit where "all parties profit," against caveat emptor), named the united order "the full economic plan of Zion," and called Welfare Services "not a program, but the essence of the gospel… the gospel in action." [Stretching The Heavens](/books/stretching-the-heavens) documents his most consequential act: Kimball, who "had studied [Lester Bush's *Dialogue* article] carefully and marked it up extensively," spearheaded the 1978 revocation of the priesthood ban. The same book preserves the folk-wisdom Kimball line about three types of men — one who learns from books, one from observation, and "the third type who just has to piss on the electric fence himself."

**Context:** Spencer W. Kimball (1895–1985) served as President of The Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-day Saints from 1973 to 1985; his presidency is most remembered for the 1978 revelation extending priesthood and temple ordinances to all worthy male members regardless of race.
