# Breakthrough Energy

[Bill Gates](/notes/bill-gates)'s climate-investment vehicle, which recurs in Kyle's notes for one specific, quantified discipline: **"We fund only technologies that could remove at least 500 million tons a year if they're successful and fully implemented"** — roughly 1% of global emissions. Kyle flags this in his [How To Avoid a Climate Disaster](/books/how-to-avoid-a-climate-disaster) notes as an "interesting and quantified argument for why some things shouldn't be funded," tagging it #[Natural Selection Among Startups](/essays/natural-selection-among-startups) and reading it as a portable frame for VC sectoral discipline: don't fund what can't matter at scale. The same 500M-ton bar reappears in his Renegade Spotlight — Lowercarbon - Research doc, set against [Chris Sacca](/notes/chris-sacca)'s Lowercarbon Capital thesis that the winning climate companies will be the cheapest options (so mass adoption, gigaton-scale impact, and profit go hand in hand) — making Breakthrough Energy the "fund the gigaton-relevant bets" counterpart to Lowercarbon's market-driven framing.

**Context:** Breakthrough Energy is the climate initiative founded by Bill Gates in 2015, encompassing Breakthrough Energy Ventures (a multi-billion-dollar fund backing hard-tech climate startups) plus policy and catalyst programs aimed at decarbonizing the global economy.
