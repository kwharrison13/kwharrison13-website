# Alma 41

Book of Mormon chapter Kyle reads as Alma's counsel to his son Corianton on the doctrine of restoration — the source of two verses he leans on heavily. The first is 41:10: "wickedness never was happiness," which appears across his Publication of the Book of Mormon notes and his August 31–September 6, 2020. Helaman 13–16: "Glad Tidings of Great Joy" study (paired with [Helaman 13](/notes/helaman-13):38 on seeking happiness in iniquity). The second is 41:3, 5, on being judged "according to the desires of [our] heart" — "the one raised to happiness according to his desires of happiness."

That desire-judges-us verse does the real work in two topical studies. In Virtue, Kyle uses 41:3, 5 to argue the difference between putting off sin and putting on Christ is the *desire* — God judges us by what our hearts truly want, and works done without true desire "become as sounding brass." In Patience — Topical Study he cites 41:4 (mortality "raised to immortality, corruption to incorruption") on accepting what the Lord is willing to give and submitting "thy will be done." His own Come Follow Me week (July 27–August 2, 2020. Alma 39–42: "The Great Plan of Happiness") notes that his study of Alma 41 itself was partial — the chapter has no per-verse notes in that source.

**Context:** Alma chapter 41 is in the Book of Mormon (Latter-day Saint scripture). Part of Alma's instruction to his son Corianton, it teaches the doctrine of restoration and the principle that the good is restored to good and the evil to evil ("wickedness never was happiness").
