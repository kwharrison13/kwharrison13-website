# D&C 4

The short, famous "missionary" section of the Doctrine and Covenants, which Kyle's study returns to for both its call to consecrated service and its catalog of the disciple's characteristics. The opening verse — **"a marvelous work is about to come forth among the children of men"** (4:1) — anchors his notes on the Restoration and the Publication of the Book of Mormon (where D&C 4 and 6 "speak directly to their audience"). The section's most-quoted lines run through his topical journals: **4:2**, "forget yourself in the Lord's service," appears in Things To Know Before Your Mission (James E. Faust's mission-prep counsel), and **4:5** — "with an eye single to the glory of God" — is read in Faith — Topical Study as the set-purpose-no-matter-the-cost that makes true discipleship possible, and in Humility — Topical Study as "the consecration / concentration clause."

The closing verses' list of qualities — faith, hope, charity, love, an eye single to God's glory, and the call to "qualify him for the work" — is the source Kyle cites in Scripture Study — Topical Study under the "characteristics" required for service (alongside [D&C 12](/notes/dandc-12):8–10 and [D&C 123](/notes/dandc-123):13). Across these pages D&C 4 functions as a compact summary of what God asks of one who would serve: total consecration plus a defined cluster of Christlike attributes.

**Context:** Doctrine and Covenants Section 4 is a brief 1829 revelation given through Joseph Smith to his father, Joseph Smith Sr.; it is widely memorized by Latter-day Saint missionaries as a statement of the qualifications for the Lord's work.
