# Adams vs. Jefferson

A shorthand Kyle uses for the tension over majority rule, flagged while reading [Joseph Smith — Rough Stone Rolling](/books/joseph-smith-rough-stone-rolling). It appears beside [Voice of the People](/notes/voice-of-the-people) as the "church-vs-democracy through-line" in the book: when Joseph Smith is helpless to resolve the Kirtland bank crisis and says "in this Republic, the vox populi is the vox Dei" (the voice of the people is the voice of God), Kyle's margin note reads "Mob rule. #[Voice of the People](/notes/voice-of-the-people) #[Adams vs. Jefferson](/notes/adams-vs-jefferson)." The pairing connects to [Mosiah 29](/notes/mosiah-29) and to Bushman's recurring theme of Mormonism colliding with democratic society — where the majority ruled "even when it trampled the rights of a minority," as Tocqueville predicted, leaving the Mormons no redress.

**Context:** "Adams vs. Jefferson" evokes the founding-era split between Federalist and Republican visions of self-government — John Adams's wariness of unchecked majority passions versus Thomas Jefferson's greater faith in popular will. Kyle uses it as a label for the same dilemma surfacing in the Mormon experience: whether the voice of the people is genuinely the voice of God, or merely mob rule trampling a minority.
