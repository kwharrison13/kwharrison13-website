# Collective Impact

Collective impact appears in Kyle's notes as the operating model behind [Destination: Home](/notes/destination-home), the Santa Clara County public-private partnership working to end [Homelessness](/notes/homelessness). Per the May 2018 call with operations lead [Chad Bojorquez](/notes/chad-bojorquez), Destination: Home grew out of a San Jose mayor / county board "blue ribbon commission," and under leader Jennifer Loving it adopted the Stanford collective-impact model. In practice this means acting as a "backbone" and strategy builder that convenes public and private partners, deploys cheap or 0% forgivable loans to (often for-profit) developers, and incubates data-driven systems the county later institutionalizes — anchored by a $50M [Cisco](/notes/cisco) grant seeding the Supportive Housing and Innovation Fund, with goals of ~5,000–6,000 supportive-housing units and a $1B private-sector target.

In the wiki the concept connects to [Social Innovation](/notes/social-innovation), which is described as the cross-sector, public-private approach to entrenched social problems that Destination: Home exemplifies, and where corporate philanthropy (the Cisco grant) is framed as a community-partnership necessity rather than a tax on the corporate partner.

**Context:** "Collective impact" is a framework — popularized by John Kania and Mark Kramer in a 2011 Stanford Social Innovation Review article — for cross-sector coordination on complex social problems. Its hallmarks are a common agenda, shared measurement, mutually reinforcing activities, continuous communication, and a dedicated "backbone" organization to coordinate the participating groups.
