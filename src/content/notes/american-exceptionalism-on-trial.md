# American Exceptionalism on Trial

A recurring theme Kyle uses to tag the question "Is America in decline?" — the case for and against the idea that the United States is exceptional. It functions less as a single argument than as a shelf where Kyle gathers evidence on both sides: the hollowing-out of once-dominant institutions, the racial-economic resentment behind events like [January 6th](/notes/january-6th), and the counter-case of American aspiration and uncapped upside.

In The Hardening Of The Great Softening - Research, the theme anchors a long thread weighing decline against vitality — the demographic analysis of the [January 6th](/notes/january-6th) marchers (most predictive factor: coming from a county that shifted from predominantly white to more racially diverse), the postwar data showing uneducated whites as the one group that hasn't advanced, and the [hollowed-out middle class](/notes/the-hollowing-out-of-the-middle-class) — set against America's entrepreneurialism, immigrant aspiration, and the [veil-of-ignorance](/notes/veil-of-ignorance) question of which country you'd choose without knowing your race or gender. Kyle uses it to frame [Flying Blind](/books/flying-blind), reading Boeing's decay as a symptom of broader institutional decline. [One Billion Americans](/notes/one-billion-americans) is linked as a proposal that responds to the same decline question, and [Rob Henderson](/notes/rob-henderson) and [Jamie Raskin](/notes/jamie-raskin) both attach to the theme through that decline-framing thread.
