# Prosperable

Note: despite the `company` type carried over from auto-generation, "Prosperable" is Kyle's own **city-building thesis/project tag**, not an operating company. It is the through-line Kyle uses across his reading on the geography of opportunity: the idea that talent and prosperity no longer have to concentrate in superstar cities, and that smaller towns can be deliberately built or revived to capture them. In [Our Towns](/books/our-towns), Kyle pins the thesis to the line "Every city that is trendy or successful in some way attracts people from someplace else," and to the book's central open question — "Could younger, better-educated people really be lured that far away from the benefits increasingly concentrated in larger cities?" — which he reads as increasingly answerable yes (Bend, Greenville, Sioux Falls).

The thesis is tightly coupled to remote work and the post-COVID dispersion. In [The New Urban Crisis](/books/the-new-urban-crisis), Kyle ties Prosperable to the [micro cities](/notes/micro-cities) answer ("deliberately designed" small cities with a bustling main-street core) and the [COVID pushes us towards something like Prosperable](/notes/covid-pushes-us-towards-something-like-prosperable) note: [Remote Work](/notes/remote-work) removes the commute and job-proximity reasons the affluent return to the urban core, leaving amenities as the only question — and in a COVID world those amenities (backyards, space, the outdoors) point away from density. He tags the same idea in [Remote — Office Not Required](/books/remote-office-not-required) against the book's small-town list ("the luxury privilege of the next twenty years will be to leave the city"). In [Amazon Unbound](/books/amazon-unbound), Kyle flags Prosperable against the HQ2 city-selection dynamics and "social license to operate." The [Small Town Headhunter](/notes/small-town-headhunter) pattern — a handful of forceful people reshaping a region — recurs as the supply-side counterpart.
