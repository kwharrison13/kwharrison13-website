# Learning

Learning is one of Kyle's densest concept hubs, and the sources cluster around a counterintuitive throughline: **effective learning looks inefficient.** [Range](/books/range) states it plainly — "learning itself is best done slowly to accumulate lasting knowledge, even when that means performing poorly on tests of immediate progress"; "desirable difficulties," spacing, and interleaving make knowledge durable and transferable. [Forget More To Learn More](/notes/forget-more-to-learn-more) takes the same idea further: "the most important part of learning is forgetting, not remembering" — the brain, like a deep-learning algorithm, learns by compressing information through bottlenecks and discarding the inessential to lift signal over noise.

The mechanism Kyle keeps returning to is **elaboration**: [How To Take Smart Notes](/books/how-to-take-smart-notes) calls it "the best-researched learning method" — rephrasing an idea in your own words and connecting it to what you already know, because "familiarity is not understanding." That book also feeds the [Learning in Public](/notes/learning-in-public) thread ("an idea kept private is as good as one you never had"). [Why Books Don't Work](/notes/why-books-don-t-work) and [Metacognition](/notes/metacognition) supply the constraint: static books offload the self-monitoring work onto the reader, and many adults lack the metacognitive skills to do it. The corpus also frames learning as a **moral duty** — [Charlie Munger — USC Gould School of Law Commencement Address](/notes/charlie-munger-usc-gould-school-of-law-commencement-address) ("be a learning machine"; the acquisition of wisdom as a moral responsibility) — and as the skills-over-pedigree currency of [The Expertise Economy](/books/the-expertise-economy) ("if you could have a Harvard education OR a Harvard degree…").

**Context:** Learning, in the cognitive-science sense these sources draw on, is the durable change in knowledge or skill produced by experience. The research Kyle's notes lean on — desirable difficulties, spacing, interleaving, retrieval practice, and elaboration — comes largely from cognitive psychology (e.g. the work popularized in *Make It Stick*), which distinguishes durable learning from the easy fluency that fades.

- [Range](/books/range) — slow, effortful learning; "desirable difficulties," spacing, interleaving for durable transfer.
- [The Expertise Economy](/books/the-expertise-economy) — skills over pedigree; continuous learning built into work.
- [Forget More To Learn More](/notes/forget-more-to-learn-more) — forgetting as the engine of learning; compression over storage.
