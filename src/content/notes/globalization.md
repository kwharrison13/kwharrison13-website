# Globalization

In Kyle's notes globalization appears mostly as a structural force acting on other topics rather than a concept treated on its own. In [Unions](/notes/unions) it is named — alongside offshoring, [Automation](/notes/automation), and corruption scandals — as one of the drivers that eroded US union membership from its ~35% post-Wagner-Act peak down to roughly 10% today. It also threads through [Legendary Entertainment](/notes/legendary-entertainment), where it was flagged during the connection sweep as central to the studio's formula: Thomas Tull is described as weaponizing globalization (notably co-financing and the China market) to scale blockbuster outcomes.

A related but distinct treatment lives in [Global Pride Cycle](/notes/global-pride-cycle), Kyle's sketch of a civilizational arc that begins with globalization and runs through prosperity, suspicion, nationalism, and eventually war. This page remains thin and associative — globalization tags economic-headwind and scale passages across the corpus without receiving sustained synthesis on any single source.

**Context:** Globalization refers to the increasing integration of economies, supply chains, labor markets, and cultures across national borders — accelerated by trade liberalization, container shipping, and digital communication. Its labor-market effects (offshoring of manufacturing, wage competition) and its cultural reach (global film/media markets) are both reflected in how Kyle's sources invoke it.
