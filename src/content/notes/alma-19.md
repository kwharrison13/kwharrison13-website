# Alma 19

A chapter in the Book of Mormon (book of Alma), studied in Kyle's [Come, Follow Me](/notes/come-follow-me) week on Alma 17–22 — I Will Make an Instrument of Thee. Kyle's notes draw on the queen's exchange with [Ammon](/notes/ammon) (Alma 19:9), where she believes on the strength of his word alone before having her own witness, and on King Lamoni's conversion experience — his heart "swollen within him" as he testifies of the coming Redeemer before being overpowered by the Spirit (Alma 19:13). The chapter also introduces **[Abish](/notes/abish)** (Alma 19:16), a Lamanite servant woman already converted "for many years, on account of a remarkable vision of her father," whom Kyle flags as a notable figure of quiet, long-held faith. Verse 32 records that many would not hear and went their way.

**Context:** Alma chapter 19 narrates the conversion of King Lamoni, his queen, and members of his household after Ammon's teaching, and the role of Abish in gathering the people to witness it.
