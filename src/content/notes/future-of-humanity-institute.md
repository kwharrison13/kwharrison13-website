# Future of Humanity Institute

Surfaces in Historical Futurism - Research as one of the institutional anchors Kyle collects for the [Historical Futurism](/notes/historical-futurism) essay — listed alongside the [Institute For The Future](/notes/institute-for-the-future) and the [RAND Corporation](/notes/rand-corporation) as organizations that have formally tried to study and forecast the long-term future. In the research notes it sits in the cluster of [Forecasting](/notes/forecasting) / [Futurology](/notes/futurology) reference points (saved via its Wikipedia entry), tagged to the broader theme of treating the future as a serious object of study rather than mere speculation.

**Context:** The Future of Humanity Institute (FHI) was a multidisciplinary research center at the University of Oxford, founded in 2005 and directed by philosopher Nick Bostrom, focused on existential risk, the long-term trajectory of civilization, and the implications of advanced technologies including AI. It closed in April 2024.
