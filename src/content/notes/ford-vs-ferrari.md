# Ford vs. Ferrari

A film Kyle references in his annotations on [Charlie Munger — The Psychology of Human Misjudgment](/notes/charlie-munger-the-psychology-of-human-misjudgment). He cites the boardroom scene where, right after acknowledging that Ford could go under, the board reacts negatively to [Lee Iacocca](/notes/lee-iacocca) — a vivid example of the status-defending, inconsistency-avoiding behavior in Munger's catalog of misjudgment.

**Context:** *Ford v Ferrari* (2019), directed by James Mangold, dramatizes Ford's 1960s effort, led in part by Lee Iacocca, to build a car capable of beating Ferrari at Le Mans.
