# Korihor

The Book of Mormon anti-Christ (Alma 30) who appears in Kyle's gospel writing as the archetype of the sign-seeker — one who demands proof before he will believe. In Revelation — Topical Study, Korihor's challenge, "Show unto me that He hath power, and then will I be convinced of the truth of thy words" (Alma 30:43), is the negative example: "It is a sign seeker who requires witness before faith." The study's counterpoint is that "it is in trying your faith that you receive a witness" (Ether 12:6) — you must develop the intention to act before the answer comes.

In Kyle's essay-style reflection The First Principles of Faithful Pragmatism, the debate between [Alma](/notes/alma) and Korihor is foregrounded as logic Kyle "has always resonated with." Korihor demands a sign (Alma 30:43); Alma answers that "thou hast had signs enough" and that "all things denote there is a God" — the earth, its motion, and the planets in their regular form witnessing a Supreme Creator (Alma 30:44).

**Context:** In the Book of Mormon, Korihor is an "anti-Christ" who preached against belief in Christ and demanded a sign from the prophet Alma; he was struck dumb as the sign he requested, later confessing he had been deceived, and ultimately died destitute.
