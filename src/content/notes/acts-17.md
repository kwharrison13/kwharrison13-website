# Acts 17

A New Testament chapter Kyle draws on across three different gospel-study threads. In "We Are Responsible for Our Own Learning", he cites the Bereans of vv.10–12 — who "received the word with all readiness of mind, and searched the scriptures daily" — as a model for nurturing one's own testimony by searching the scriptures. In The Godhead, he uses v.29 ("we are the offspring of God") to argue that there is "one God the Father to me because I am the spiritual offspring of only one being," while holding Christ as a separate, distinct being. In Miscellaneous Scriptures, vv.26–27 — Paul teaching the Gentiles that God "made of one blood all nations" — appear in a chain about the gospel going first to Israel and then to the Gentiles.

**Context:** Acts 17 recounts the Apostle Paul's missionary journey through Thessalonica, Berea, and Athens, including his sermon at the Areopagus on the "unknown god" and humanity as the "offspring of God."
