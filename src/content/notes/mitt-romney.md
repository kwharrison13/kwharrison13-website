# Mitt Romney

Mitt Romney is one of the recurring anchor figures in Kyle's corpus — both as a political subject and as an author Kyle has read across three books. He is the subject of [McKay Coppins](/notes/mckay-coppins)'s biography [Romney: A Reckoning](/books/romney-a-reckoning), which Kyle reads with explicit admiration and a "politics-of-character" thesis. The sharpest insight in those notes is that Romney is the **counter-example to beliefs becoming a lagging indicator of group membership**: where the post-2016 GOP made beliefs downstream of tribe, Romney kept conscience upstream of party, posing "the weight of conscience" question on the Senate floor and casting the lone Republican impeachment vote. His son [Tagg Romney](/notes/tagg-romney)'s letter is the emotional center of the notes ("live your life so that your children will live their lives hoping to make you proud").

As an author, Romney supplies a through-line of **operational, problem-solving instinct applied to public life**. [Turnaround](/books/turnaround) (2018) recounts his fix-it formula across business, the 2002 Olympics, and public service — the same instinct that defined his Bain and Senate work, and where he quotes [Henry Kissinger](/notes/henry-kissinger) on heroes who "become myths when they ennoble the lives... of all of us." [No Apology](/books/no-apology) extends that turnaround logic to national policy: three rival global strategies (Western free-enterprise democracy, China's authoritarian free-enterprise, Russia's energy-based authoritarianism), a critique of media and elite [Group Think](/notes/group-think), and an "anecdotes are illustrative but data is compelling" stance on education policy.

Across the books, Romney's basic democratic premise — that an [educated and informed electorate](/notes/educated-electorate) "can be trusted with hard truths if they're presented as truths" — is the link Kyle draws to [The Death of Expertise](/books/the-death-of-expertise). The fact that this premise is now contested is, in the notes' framing, the diagnosis: [Donald Trump](/notes/donald-trump)'s rise "inverted the GOP's belief-to-tribe relationship," which Romney called even more devastating than losing his own 2012 election.

**Context:** Mitt Romney is an American businessman and Republican politician — co-founder of Bain Capital, 2002 Salt Lake Olympics organizer, Governor of Massachusetts (2003–2007), the 2012 Republican presidential nominee, and U.S. Senator from Utah (2019–2025). He was the only Republican senator to vote to convict Trump in the first impeachment trial. He is also a prominent member of the LDS Church.
