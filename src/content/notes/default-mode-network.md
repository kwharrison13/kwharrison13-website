# Default Mode Network

The Default Mode Network (DMN) is one of the central threads the wiki pulls from [The Self-Driven Child](/books/the-self-driven-child), where it anchors the principle "doing nothing is the most important something." Per the book, scientists around the turn of the twenty-first century found "a complex and highly integrated network in the brain that only activates when we are 'doing nothing'" — and it is far from idle, consuming 60 to 80 percent of the brain's energy. When you're not reading, watching, or on your phone, the DMN is "projecting the future and sorting out the past... processing your life": the system for self-reflection, identity formation, empathy, memory consolidation, and creativity.

The book's prescription is to protect this state. Stress impairs the DMN, and the authors worry that technology's ubiquity leaves young people too few opportunities to activate it — hence the call for "radical downtime": "doing nothing purposeful, nothing that requires highly focused thought." Kyle's notes on the page distill the thread into his own refrains — "doing nothing is the most important something," "Be up and doing," and "Define your downtime" — and the source links it to the Buffett line about "the ability to sit quietly in a room."

**Context:** The default mode network is a well-documented brain network (associated with regions like the medial prefrontal cortex and posterior cingulate) that is most active during rest and mind-wandering rather than goal-directed tasks; it's a standard topic in cognitive neuroscience.
