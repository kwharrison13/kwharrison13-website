# Moroni 1

The Book of Mormon chapter Kyle's notes use for **Moroni's integrity in the face of death** — [Moroni 1](/notes/moroni-1):2–3: "because of their hatred they put to death every Nephite that will not deny the Christ. And I, Moroni, will not deny the Christ." In the Come Follow Me study November 30–December 6, 2020. Moroni 1–6: "To Keep Them in the Right Way", Kyle reads this as the model of standing by what matters even when refusing means wandering for the safety of one's life. The chapter also carries the Written for Our Day theme: Moroni, writing alone after the destruction of his nation, keeps writing "contrary to that which I had supposed" for the Lamanites "in some future day" ([Moroni 1](/notes/moroni-1):4) — and Kyle marvels at the charity of a man who, having watched his entire nation slaughtered, still calls his killers "my brethren."

In Christ Is Risen; Faith in Him Will Move Mountains — Russell M. Nelson, [Moroni 1](/notes/moroni-1):1–3 appears in a cluster of martyr/faith witnesses alongside Abinadi ([Mosiah 17](/notes/mosiah-17)) and Ether ([Ether 13](/notes/ether-13)) — examples of those who would not deny their faith under threat of death.

**Context:** Moroni 1 is a short chapter in which Moroni, the last surviving Nephite, explains that he continues writing after the destruction of his people, refusing to deny Christ even at the cost of being hunted.
