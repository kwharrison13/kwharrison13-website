# René Girard

Invoked in Kyle's Cultivating Cults - Research notes — the research feeding his "Cultivating Cults" essay on how modern brands and products borrow the machinery of religion. Girard appears as the aside ("eat your heart out, Girard") alongside the observation that Peloton intentionally advertised itself as "for the rich and ripped" and was repeatedly compared to a megachurch. The link draws on Girard's theory of mimetic desire — that we want what others (especially aspirational others) want — to explain how status-coded brands manufacture belonging and rivalry the way religions cultivate community and emotional loyalty.

**Context:** René Girard (1923–2015) was a French-American literary critic and anthropologist best known for mimetic theory — the idea that human desire is imitative — and the scapegoat mechanism. His ideas have been widely adopted in Silicon Valley, notably by Peter Thiel.
