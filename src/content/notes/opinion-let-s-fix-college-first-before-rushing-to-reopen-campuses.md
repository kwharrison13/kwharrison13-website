# Opinion: Let's fix college first, before rushing to reopen campuses

**Author:** [Shaan Hathiramani](/notes/shaan-hathiramani) (CEO of [Flockjay](/notes/flockjay))
**URL:** https://www.fastcompany.com/90507449/opinion-lets-fix-college-first-before-rushing-to-reopen-campuses
**One-line:** *Rushing to reopen campuses shifts the pandemic's risk onto students; instead, use the moment to overhaul a broken, unaffordable higher-education system.* #[University Disruption](/notes/university-disruption)

## Key claims

- As colleges crafted nuanced optimism about reopening in the fall, **they shifted the burden entirely onto students to weigh highly uncertain disease risks for an on-campus experience that may not even happen** — on top of tens of thousands in debt amid Great Depression-level unemployment. At least one in six college students are abandoning plans to enroll.
- **Pushing to reopen college campuses seems tone-deaf at best, and catastrophically self-serving at worst.**
- Colleges have funded deficits by passing the bill to students. **Currently, 85% of students get some form of financial aid, and 70% of students graduate with average debt approaching $30,000.**
- As the pandemic ravages state budgets, colleges may face funding shortfalls larger than the 25% cuts of 2008. Moody's downgraded the entire sector, and [Bob Zemsky](/notes/bob-zemsky) (Univ. of Pennsylvania professor, coauthor of *The College Stress Test*) predicts **at least 20% of colleges (≈1,200 of the 6,600+ American colleges) now face severe market risk.**
  > **Kyle:** *Compare to The Innovative University and Disrupting Class by [Clayton M. Christensen](/notes/clayton-christensen).*
- It's hard to imagine students incurring crippling debt for "virtual social activities replacing parties" and stadium lectures.

### Nine ways to start fixing higher education

1. **Reset curriculums to improve employment opportunities.** 91% of students list improving employment opportunities as a top reason for attending college. ==**This means tuition resets, job-ready curriculum, shorter degrees, and partnerships to connect job seekers with growing industries — particularly ones that accommodate remote working.**==
2. **Bite the bullet and dramatically overhaul costs.** Tools such as financial exigency can address bloat and set a higher bar for costly administrators and disengaged staff (as much as 67% of tenured faculty).
3. **Liberate instructors to design new courses across platforms and collaborate on best practices** — the single biggest opportunity to scale personalized online learning. #[Alternative Education](/notes/alternative-education) #[Online Education](/notes/online-education)
4. **Unlock endowments to put cash directly into the pockets of students, families, and campus workers.**
5. **Invest in holistic anti-poverty student support** — connecting students with social services raises retention and revenue (one in four undergrads has children).
6. **Provide reskilling resources to essential on-campus workers** — subcontracted kitchen, janitorial, IT, and hourly staff who can't work from home.
7. **Make the internet a public utility.** Only two-thirds of rural homes have high-speed internet; end the digital divide.
8. **Embrace the internet's power of community building** — virtual concerts, commencements, campuses; incentivize students to build a new way of learning together.
9. **Partner with hotels to create safe learning environments and local meetups** — work toward in-person interaction among students in the same cities and states. #[Alternative Real Estate](/notes/alternative-real-estate)
