# Revelation 22

Revelation 22 is the final chapter of the New Testament book of Revelation. In Kyle's gospel-study note Eye single to the glory of God it appears as a supporting cross-reference (Revelation 22:3–4) for the theme of seeing the face of God — listed alongside [D&C 93](/notes/dandc-93):1, [D&C 88](/notes/dandc-88):67–68, [D&C 67](/notes/dandc-67):10, and [D&C 130](/notes/dandc-130):1 to support the idea that beholding God's glory is within mortal capacity as one's faith and spiritual preparation grow.

**Context:** Revelation 22 closes the Bible with the vision of the river of the water of life and the tree of life; verses 3–4 promise that the servants of God "shall see his face, and his name shall be in their foreheads."

## Where this appears

- Eye single to the glory of God — cited (22:3–4) as a cross-reference for the capacity to behold the face and glory of God.
