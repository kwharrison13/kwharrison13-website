# Decarbonization

**One-line definition:** *The economy-wide shift away from carbon-emitting processes; framed by Watershed as the defining economic transition of the decade.*

## How sources describe it

- The economy-wide shift away from carbon-emitting processes; framed by Watershed as the defining economic transition of the decade. (source: Watershed Climate Diligence — 2021)

## Where it shows up

- Watershed Climate Diligence — 2021 — Watershed Climate Series B diligence (Index Ventures, 2021).

## Related concepts

- [Climate Change](/notes/climate-change) — the broader problem decarbonization responds to; reducing carbon-emitting processes is the economy-wide mitigation lever.
- [Carbon Accounting](/notes/carbon-accounting) — the measurement layer (Watershed's product) that makes decarbonization tractable by quantifying emissions to be reduced.
- [Distributed Energy Model](/notes/distributed-energy-model) — a structural shift in how energy is produced and consumed that contributes to the economy-wide move away from carbon.
- [Index Ventures](/notes/index-ventures) — led Watershed's Series B, framing decarbonization as the defining economic transition of the decade.
