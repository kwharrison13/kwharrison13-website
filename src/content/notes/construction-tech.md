# Construction Tech

Construction Tech is the category Kyle uses to group the construction-site capture and digital-twin tools that surfaced in his 2021 sourcing and diligence work. The anchor examples are [Openspace.ai](/notes/openspace-ai) — a construction-site photo-documentation tool reviewed by [Nina Achadjian](/notes/nina-achadjian) ([Index Ventures](/notes/index-ventures)) as a vertical-SaaS deal — and [Buildots](/notes/buildots), an Israel-based company surfaced by [Molly Alter](/notes/molly-alter) that uses helmet-mounted cameras to build a constantly-updating digital twin of a site. Disperse.io appears alongside them as a third competitor in the same space.

The recurring diligence questions across these deals define the category's open problems: it is a crowded market (drones, cameras on cranes, walkie-talkie robots, helmet cameras), so the live questions are defensibility, margin and pricing power, greenfield-vs-switching sales, and whether a tool can graduate from an analytical layer into a system of record. Buildots is positioned as more granular and operational than OpenSpace and Disperse — more data driving higher engagement — which Kyle's notes frame as the differentiator that matters in this category.

**Context:** "ConTech" or construction technology refers to digital tools — reality-capture cameras, drones, photogrammetry, AI progress-tracking, and digital twins — applied to a historically low-productivity, slow-to-digitize industry, an area that drew significant venture investment in the early 2020s.
