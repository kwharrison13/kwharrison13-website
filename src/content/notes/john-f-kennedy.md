# John F. Kennedy

John F. Kennedy appears in [The Magic of Learning](/notes/the-magic-of-learning) via his "ask not what your country can do for you — ask what you can do for your country" line, which [David Perell](/notes/david-perell) weaves into a civic-service thread about contributing rather than consuming.

In [Speeches That Changed The World](/books/speeches-that-changed-the-world) Kyle highlights two passages from Kennedy's space-program oratory: the ["not because they are easy, but because they are hard"](/notes/hard-work) moon address, and the line that "space science, like nuclear science and all technology, has no conscience of its own." Together with the inaugural's "ask not" summons, they form Kyle's picture of JFK as the great civic-ambition orator.

**Context:** John F. Kennedy (1917–1963) was the 35th President of the United States. The "ask not" line is from his January 1961 inaugural address; the moon passages are from the September 1962 Rice University speech.
