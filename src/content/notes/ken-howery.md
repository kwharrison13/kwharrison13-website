# Ken Howery

In the wiki, Ken Howery surfaces through the [Peter Thiel](/notes/peter-thiel) page (drawn from [The Founders](/books/the-founders) by Jimmy Soni), where he is quoted on the magnetism that pulled the early [PayPal](/notes/paypal) circle together at Stanford: *"Peter might be the smartest person I've met in my four years at Stanford. I think I might work for him for the rest of my life."* That line frames Howery as one of the original believers in Thiel — the kind of friend-as-cofounder loyalty that the source presents as the trust-producing-speed instinct underpinning the [PayPal Mafia](/notes/paypal-mafia).

**Context:** Ken Howery is an American entrepreneur and investor who was one of PayPal's co-founders (its first CFO) and later a founding partner of Peter Thiel's Founders Fund. He subsequently served as U.S. Ambassador to Sweden. (General background, outside the wiki sources.)
