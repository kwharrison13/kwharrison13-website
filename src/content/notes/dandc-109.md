# D&C 109

Section 109 of the [Doctrine and Covenants](/notes/doctrine-and-covenants) — the Kirtland Temple dedicatory prayer — appears in Kyle's study across several threads. In Christian Reformation, Zion, and Seekers he cites D&C 109:73 (alongside [D&C 86](/notes/dandc-86):3, [D&C 5](/notes/dandc-5):14, and [D&C 33](/notes/dandc-33):4–5) under the theme of restoring Christianity "to a healthy condition, and restore truth!" In Spiritual Gifts he reads D&C 109:22–23 on being an "emissary of truth." And in Publication of the Book of Mormon he draws on D&C 109:31's language of "bearing record," connecting it to the law of witnesses and [Triangulation](/notes/triangulation).

**Context:** Doctrine and Covenants Section 109 (March 1836) is the dedicatory prayer of the Kirtland Temple, given by revelation to Joseph Smith — a petition that the Lord's servants go forth armed with power, that His name be upon them, and that the gospel "may roll forth unto the ends of the earth."
