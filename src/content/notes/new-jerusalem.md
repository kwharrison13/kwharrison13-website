# New Jerusalem

The New Jerusalem is the city of God that, per 3 Nephi 21:23–24, the remnant of Jacob and the gathered house of Israel are to build — a key idea in Kyle's Come Follow Me study of 3 Nephi 20–26, where the risen Christ unfolds the covenant of the [Gathering of Israel](/notes/gathering-of-israel). Kyle treats it as more than a prophecy to await: his note asks, "What is the New Jerusalem really? Why aren't we working harder to build up a city of God in Missouri?" and flags it under #[Gospel Topics to Explore](/notes/gospel-topics-to-explore). The framing connects scripture directly to Kyle's recurring [City Building](/notes/city-building) interest — the New Jerusalem read as an actual city to be built, not just a symbol.

**Context:** In Latter-day Saint theology the New Jerusalem (or City of Zion) is a prophesied gathering place, traditionally located in Independence, Missouri, to be established before or at Christ's Second Coming.
