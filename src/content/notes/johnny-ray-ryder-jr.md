# Johnny Ray Ryder Jr.

Credited author of "The Oak Tree," the inspirational poem that closes the short-quote section of [Chad's Big Quote Book](/notes/chad-s-big-quote-book) — Kyle's verbatim collection of gospel and motivational quotations given to him by his brother Chad. The poem casts an oak that survives a relentless wind by holding its ground: the wind strips its leaves, snaps its boughs, and pulls its bark, but cannot reach the roots ("the deepest part of me"), and the tree closes "I'm stronger than I ever knew." In the collection it sits among quotes on endurance and adversity, alongside passages Kyle linked to figures like [Richard G. Scott](/notes/richard-g-scott) on refinement through trials.

**Context:** "The Oak Tree" is a widely circulated inspirational poem about resilience, commonly attributed to Johnny Ray Ryder Jr. in motivational anthologies and devotional collections.
