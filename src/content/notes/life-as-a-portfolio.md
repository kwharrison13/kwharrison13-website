# Life as a Portfolio

[Kyle Harrison](/notes/kyle-harrison)'s framing of his career as portfolio construction across experiences rather than progression at any single firm. In his September 13th, 2022 — Harry Stebbings Podcast prep, Kyle uses it to answer the charge that he "jumps around firms too much": he says he's "sampling different styles of investing like Goldilocks" — private-equity style at [TCV](/notes/tcv), hedge-fund style at [Coatue](/notes/coatue), "venture classic" at [Index Ventures](/notes/index-ventures) — optimizing for "the best portfolio construction for my life" over "any one firm's definition of 'good.'"

The mental model rests on two ideas drawn from the same source. First, a friend's pyramid metaphor: most people prefer a hierarchical structure because its bounded bottom limits how far they can fall — but the same angles that bound the bottom also bound the top, so to reach your potential you have to "open up the top" *and* the bottom (an "unbounded top and bottom"). Second, an Amazon-vs-Shopify contrast: an Amazon-style life rides a big lumbering machine that rolls on with or without you, while a Shopify-style life is a tool where you live or die on your own capability but get infrastructure to build your own thing. Kyle says he prefers the unbounded, Shopify-style mode — which is also why he frames investing itself as "allocating finite resources to maximize an optimal outcome," with time as the most finite resource everyone allocates.
