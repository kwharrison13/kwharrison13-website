# Following Christ

A discipleship theme Kyle develops in his topical study of The Good Shepherd. The core move: we first acknowledge that we must be *followers*, and Christ then deepens our understanding of following by inviting us to be *leaders* — "what better way to become a better follower than to be placed in the position of a leader and see, first hand, how you wish people would follow." The application to daily life is decisional: "in every decision you must know who you follow," because "there is no neutral ground in the universe" ([C.S. Lewis](/notes/c-s-lewis)) — and if you are not the sheep of the good shepherd, "the devil is your shepherd" ([Alma 5](/notes/alma-5):37–39).

The study contrasts being *driven* with being *led*: God leads through love, "for if we were merely driven where they wish us to go, we would not be worthy to be there" ([Neal A. Maxwell](/notes/neal-a-maxwell)) — "They are shepherds, not sheepherders." Following Christ here is not passive sheep-ness but choosing one's shepherd: as the poem Kyle quotes puts it, "Do not exclaim 'I am no sheep!' / Only ask 'who is my shepherd?'"

## Where this appears

- The Good Shepherd — the central discipleship thread: acknowledge the need to follow, learn following by leading, and in every decision know who you follow.
