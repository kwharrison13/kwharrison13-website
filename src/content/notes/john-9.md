# John 9

The chapter of John's Gospel — the healing of the man born blind — that Kyle cites for two threads. On [Accountability](/notes/accountability) and knowledge, [John 9](/notes/john-9):41 ("if ye were blind, ye should have no sin; but now ye say we see; therefore your sins remain") is paired with [Luke 23](/notes/luke-23):34 to contrast "the ignorant Romans vs. the accountable Jews": those who acted in ignorance are "in part justified," while those who claimed to see remained in sin. This pairing appears in Eye single to the glory of God, Knowledge — Topical Study, and the General Conference — October 2021 notes (which cite 9:2-3 as well).

On discipleship and obedience, Obedience — Topical Study cites [John 9](/notes/john-9):4 ("I must work the works of Him that sent me, while it is day: the night cometh, when no man can work") to frame obedience "not as daily drudgery, but rather daily discipleship."

**Context:** John 9 narrates Jesus healing a man blind from birth, the Pharisees' interrogation of the healed man, and Jesus' closing teaching that He came "that they which see not might see; and that they which see might be made blind."

## Where this appears

- Eye single to the glory of God — John 9:41 paired with [Luke 23](/notes/luke-23):34: ignorant Romans vs. accountable Jews.
- Knowledge — Topical Study — same Luke 23:34 / John 9:41 pairing on ignorance partly justifying versus claimed sight condemning.
- General Conference — October 2021 — cites John 9:2-3 (and the man-born-blind passage).
- Obedience — Topical Study — John 9:4, obedience as daily discipleship, working while it is day.
