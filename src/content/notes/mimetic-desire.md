# Mimetic Desire

Mimetic desire is [René Girard](/notes/ren-girard)'s thesis — recurring across Kyle's corpus via [Peter Thiel](/notes/peter-thiel), Girard's former Stanford student who popularized it in Silicon Valley — that humans don't know what to want on their own and so copy each other's desires. As [The Founders](/books/the-founders) quotes Girard: "Man is the creature who does not know what to desire, and he turns to others in order to make up his mind. We desire what others desire because we imitate their desires." Thiel credited the framework with letting him see that Facebook would succeed, since people would use it to signal social rank.

[Talent](/books/talent) sharpens the contrarian edge of the idea: if everyone is copying everyone else, who is left to be an original thinker? The book argues that autistic and Asperger's individuals, sitting outside "the usual loops of social pressure and mimetic desire," may retain stronger capacities for non-conformist thought. [Boom — Bubbles & The End of Stagnation](/books/boom-bubbles-and-the-end-of-stagnation) extends mimetic desire to markets: bubbles channel the "destructive mimetic dynamic" into something productive — markets "collectivize contagious mimetic desires and suppress their violent discharge," giving the market a "quasi-sacred status." The same source notes Girard's warning that imitation breeds rivalry and conflict (see [Mimetic Rivalry](/notes/mimetic-rivalry)).

**Context:** Mimetic desire is the central concept of French literary theorist and anthropologist René Girard, who argued that human desire is fundamentally imitative rather than autonomous — we want things because others want them — and that this dynamic generates rivalry, scapegoating, and social conflict.

## Where this appears

- [The Founders](/books/the-founders) — Thiel's college immersion in Girard; the canonical "we imitate their desires" quote and the warning about rivalry.
- [Talent](/books/talent) — mimetic desire as the foil to original thought; why non-conformist minds may escape the imitation loop.
- [Boom — Bubbles & The End of Stagnation](/books/boom-bubbles-and-the-end-of-stagnation) — bubbles as a channel that redirects mimetic dynamics into productive coordination; markets as collectivized mimetic desire.
