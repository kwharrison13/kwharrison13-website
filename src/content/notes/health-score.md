# Health Score

A health score is a composite metric that scores a customer's health from usage and engagement signals to predict churn risk and upsell potential. It is the single most-cited battleground in Kyle's Planhat - Diligence notes on [Customer Success](/notes/customer-success) tooling: experts repeatedly called the **health score the most important aspect of any CS tool**, and "it all starts and ends with data integration" — native integrations plus a well-weighted health-score algorithm are the most-cited differentiators between vendors. Typical inputs flagged in the diligence are number of logins, NPS, adoption, and trend, which roll up into an overall health number used to predict upsell or churn. The hard part is *weighting* those inputs (more KPIs tends to produce more false positives), and most companies' data isn't structured well enough to feed a clear score — the pivot to a dedicated platform usually comes around 200–300 customers. Configurability and weighting of the score were named as genuine product differentiators for [Planhat](/notes/planhat) over rivals like [Gainsight](/notes/gainsight) and [Catalyst](/notes/catalyst).

**Context:** In customer-success software, a customer health score aggregates product-usage, support, sentiment, and relationship signals into a single indicator (often red/yellow/green) so CS teams can triage at-risk accounts and prioritize expansion. Vendors differentiate on how well the underlying data integrates and how the scoring model is weighted.
