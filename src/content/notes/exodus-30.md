# Exodus 30

A scripture reference cited in Kyle's Original 12 Apostles gospel study. Exodus 30:11–16 (the half-shekel temple tax) is paired with [Matthew 17](/notes/matthew-17):24–27 (the coin found in the fish's mouth, which paid the tax for two people) to support a conjecture in the study: that Peter may have been the only adult among the apostles, since the fish-coin covered the temple tax for only two — Jesus and Peter — implying the rest were not yet old enough to owe it.

**Context:** Exodus 30:11–16 institutes the half-shekel census/temple tax — "a ransom for his soul" — levied on every Israelite male twenty years and older, the basis later interpreted as the temple tax in the Gospels.
