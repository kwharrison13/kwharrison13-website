# Build a Better On Ramp For Life

A recurring tag Kyle uses for the question of how someone deliberately sets up the early, formative stretch of a life or career — the "on-ramp" you build before you accelerate. He pins it to the closing reframe of [Marc Andreessen](/notes/marc-andreessen)'s [It's Time To Build](/notes/it-s-time-to-build): instead of asking "what do you do for work?", ask **"what are you building?"** — directly building, or helping/teaching others to build, or caring for the people who build.

The tag's clearest concrete instance is [Julie Chen](/notes/julie-chen)'s career thesis, captured in the June 6th, 2022 — Julie Chen (Retool) call. Having left [Contrary](/notes/contrary) to join [Retool](/notes/retool) very early ("I knew I could start my own thing but I'm really young"), her logic was to **surround herself with super smart people to "learn by doing,"** find her "spike," not rush her learning, and "not start something just for the sake of starting something." Kyle files this under "build a better on-ramp for life": choosing the environment that compounds your learning fastest as the deliberate first stage, rather than optimizing prematurely for a title or a founding.
