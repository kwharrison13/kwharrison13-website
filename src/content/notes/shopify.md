# Shopify

E-commerce platform that recurs across Kyle's notes in three roles. As an **investing position**, Shopify is a recurring name in Kyle's [Berkshire Hathaway Annual Letters](/books/berkshire-hathaway-annual-letters) reading notes — which double as a private memo on live deals, with Shopify appearing alongside Costco, Amazon, Constellation Software, and others as part of an in-progress investment thesis. As a **career-strategy exemplar**, Shopify is one of Kyle's go-to present-day examples of a dominant [Platform](/notes/platform) to build on: his [College Students- Focus On This](/notes/college-students-focus-on-this) note advises "build where the people are... today it's [Webflow](/notes/webflow) or Shopify."

As a **diligence reference customer**, Shopify shows up as a marquee logo validating the companies Kyle evaluated. In the Watershed Climate Diligence — 2021, Shopify (via [Stacy Kauk](/notes/stacy-kauk)) is named among Watershed's forward-thinking, trend-setting early customers (Airbnb, Stripe, MongoDB, DoorDash) whose word-of-mouth drives the carbon-accounting purchasing decision. In the Pave Diligence — 2021, Shopify appears among Pave's compensation-platform customers (alongside Fivetran, Discord, Allbirds, Checkr). Its presence on a customer list functions as a quality signal in Kyle's deal evaluations. It is also cited in the The Storytelling of Investing - Research as one of the companies (with LinkedIn and Stripe) running a structured weekly process to summarize customer feedback.

**Context:** Shopify (NYSE/TSX: SHOP) is a Canadian commerce company founded in 2006 that provides software and infrastructure for merchants to build and run online stores — a flagship example of a platform whose value scales with the third-party merchants and developers building on top of it.
