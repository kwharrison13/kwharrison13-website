# Joshua 7

An Old Testament chapter (the sin of Achan at Ai) that Kyle's Born Again study uses to illustrate the weight of covenants. After Israel's victory at Jericho, they sent only three thousand against Ai and were routed; Joshua "fell to the earth upon his face," and the Lord rebuked him — "Get thee up; wherefore liest thou thus upon thy face?" (Joshua 7:4, 8, 10). The Lord revealed that one man among them (Achan/Zabdi) had "transgressed my covenant," and that He would not be with Israel until the accursed thing was destroyed: "Up, sanctify yourselves against tomorrow" (Joshua 7:11–13).

The study draws the principle that "you can't break a covenant you haven't made" — and so it is *our* transgressions that disrupt God's work, because "our sins are of greater damage because of our covenants" (Gene Hurley). Achan's hidden sin bringing destruction on the whole camp is the cautionary case for that idea.

**Context:** Joshua 7 in the Hebrew Bible recounts how Achan secretly took plunder ("the accursed thing") from Jericho against God's command, causing Israel's defeat at Ai; once exposed by lot, Achan and his household were destroyed in the Valley of Achor, restoring Israel's standing before God.
