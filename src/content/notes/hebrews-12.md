# Hebrews 12

A chapter in the New Testament epistle to the Hebrews, cited in Kyle's Patience — Topical Study under "Further Reading." Kyle pulls [Hebrews 12](/notes/hebrews-12):1 — the exhortation to "run with patience the race that is set before us" — as a scriptural anchor for the theme of enduring with patience. He files it alongside [Enos 1](/notes/enos-1):9,12 (prayers granted according to faith) and [James 1](/notes/james-1):19 (swift to hear, slow to wrath) in the same patience reading-chain.

**Context:** Hebrews 12 (New Testament) opens with the image of running a race "compassed about with so great a cloud of witnesses," then develops the theme of God's loving chastening of those He receives as sons, urging readers toward endurance and holiness.
