# Reciprocation Tendency

One of [Charlie Munger](/notes/charlie-munger)'s human-misjudgment tendencies in [Charlie Munger — The Psychology of Human Misjudgment](/notes/charlie-munger-the-psychology-of-human-misjudgment): the deep human reflex to repay favors, concessions, and gifts in kind — which "compliance practitioners" can trigger subconsciously to manipulate behavior. Munger cites Cialdini's campus experiment demonstrating its power.

**Context:** Reciprocation is one of Robert Cialdini's principles of influence: an unsolicited favor or concession creates a felt obligation to give something back, a lever salespeople and negotiators exploit (e.g. the "rejection-then-retreat" concession tactic).

## Where this appears

- [Charlie Munger — The Psychology of Human Misjudgment](/notes/charlie-munger-the-psychology-of-human-misjudgment) — Munger recounts Cialdini's experiment in which "compliance practitioners" wandered campus exploiting subjects' subconscious reciprocation tendency.
