# Blitzscaling

Across Kyle's notes, "blitzscaling" is [Reid Hoffman](/notes/reid-hoffman)'s neologism for Silicon Valley's prioritization of speed and fast growth over efficiency — and it draws as much critique as endorsement. [The Founders](/books/the-founders) traces the term and the Valley's growth obsession at least partially back to the PayPal/X.com cohort on University Avenue, noting one founder's reflection that this scale of growth colored his view of future ventures. [The Power Law — Venture Capital & the Making of the New Future](/books/the-power-law-venture-capital-and-the-making-of-the-new-future) is sharply ambivalent: in less thoughtful hands "blitzscaling" has come to mean little more than "get rich quick," filed alongside Masayoshi Son's "crazier, faster, bigger" and Mark Zuckerberg's "move fast and break things." Mike Maccoby's worry there is that blitzscaling aims to establish market power approaching monopoly, that pouring VC money into it can elbow aside more efficient incumbents, and that the right remedy is to regulate monopolies rather than subsidize them.

[We're Selling Entrepreneurship Short](/notes/we-re-selling-entrepreneurship-short) turns blitzscaling into the target of a reform argument: the VC model of blitzscaling "was not delivered from the mountain on stone tablets" and can be iterated or replaced, with founder-controlled, cash-flow-positive companies offered as the alternative to chasing outlier math. The thread connecting all three is [Capital Allocation](/notes/capital-allocation) — whether pouring capital into hypergrowth is the best use of society's savings.

**Context:** "Blitzscaling" was coined by LinkedIn co-founder Reid Hoffman (with Chris Yeh in their 2018 book of the same name) to describe prioritizing speed over efficiency in pursuit of market dominance under conditions of uncertainty.

## Where this appears

- [The Founders](/books/the-founders) — traces the term's roots to the PayPal/X.com cohort and Silicon Valley's growth obsession.
- [The Power Law — Venture Capital & the Making of the New Future](/books/the-power-law-venture-capital-and-the-making-of-the-new-future) — critiques blitzscaling as drifting toward "get rich quick" and monopoly-seeking, with society-level costs.
- [We're Selling Entrepreneurship Short](/notes/we-re-selling-entrepreneurship-short) — frames the blitzscaling VC model as a changeable convention, not gospel, advocating founder-controlled alternatives.
