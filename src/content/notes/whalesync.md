# WhaleSync

WhaleSync is a no-code data-sync startup Kyle sourced and diligenced in August 2021, capturing both a pitch deck and a customer reference with Rob at [Cartooli](/notes/cartooli). The product's wedge is two-way sync between a data layer ([Airtable](/notes/airtable)) and a front-end builder ([Webflow](/notes/webflow)) — keeping records continuously synchronized so non-technical builders can "take all this data in Airtable, still have that flexibility, but move it into a front end that's more attractive," without manual monitoring. Rob, a no-code builder with no technical background, contrasted WhaleSync favorably against the status quo of stitching [Zapier](/notes/zapier) together: Zapier's per-zap pricing and every added layer introducing "a breakage point" made building data products painful, whereas WhaleSync let him set up projects that push content "without any manual steps."

Beyond the immediate Airtable↔Webflow sync, Rob saw a larger vision: WhaleSync becoming "an alternative to [Zapier](/notes/zapier) that lets people run their business as a series of dashboards" — a single dashboard of connections feeding databases into front-end and back-end tools. The team (Rob worked with "Curtis") was praised as proactive about catching sync errors before customers noticed, and as "super professional, very warm and friendly."

**Context:** WhaleSync is a no-code tool for two-way data synchronization across apps like Airtable, Webflow, Notion, and Supabase, aimed at builders assembling sites and apps without writing integration code.
