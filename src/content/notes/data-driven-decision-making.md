# Data-Driven Decision Making

Tagged once in [The Expertise Economy](/books/the-expertise-economy), where it surfaces around the credibility of data in front of an audience: "Especially for data-driven leaders, if someone can point out errors in the data you show, you won't get to tell the story behind the data. It just won't be credible." In Kyle's notes the concept is less about analytics tooling than about the *integrity* of the numbers you present — flawed data undermines a leader's standing to interpret it. It sits within the book's broader argument that skills, learning agility, and evidence (not pedigree) should drive how organizations evaluate people.

## Where this appears

- [The Expertise Economy](/books/the-expertise-economy) — flagged on a quote about data-driven leaders losing credibility when errors are exposed in the data they present
