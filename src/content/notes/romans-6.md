# Romans 6

In Kyle's gospel study, Romans 6:3–6 is the anchor scripture for the idea of "crucifying the old man of sin" and becoming a new creature through the [Atonement](/notes/atonement). In How To Apply The Atonement it grounds the corrected object lesson: where the children's image of pulling nails from a board leaves holes, the truer picture (Romans 6:3–6, [Mosiah 27](/notes/mosiah-27):25–26) is that the Atonement throws away the worn board and gives you a new one — "you become a new creature; forget the old one." The note stresses that the Atonement is infinite but not unconditional: resurrection is unconditional, eternal life is conditional.

In Mighty Repentance, Romans 6:6 closes Enos's arc of expanding repentance: full repentance equals conversion — crucifying the old man, becoming a new creature ([2 Corinthians 5](/notes/2-corinthians-5):17), with joy "as exquisite as my pain" ([Alma 36](/notes/alma-36)).

**Context:** Romans 6 is Paul's chapter on baptism and new life, teaching that believers are buried with Christ in baptism and raised to "walk in newness of life," with the "old man" crucified so that sin no longer reigns.

## Where this appears

- Mighty Repentance — Romans 6:6, "crucify the old man of sin," as the capstone of Enos-style conversion.
