# Ulisses Soares

Latter-day Saint apostle whose October 2021 General Conference address on compassion Kyle logged in his General Conference- October 2021 notes, under the "love your neighbor" theme. Soares taught that "the Savior acted compassionately toward all who would come unto Him — without distinction — and most especially toward those who most needed His help," and that compassion is "a fundamental characteristic of those who strive for sanctification," intertwining with mourning with those that mourn, empathy, mercy, and kindness. Kyle captures his summary line that "as we strive to incorporate a compassionate attitude into our way of life we'll become more sensitive to people's needs," alongside "Jesus always looks on the heart and not on the appearance."

**Context:** Ulisses Soares (b. 1958), originally of São Paulo, Brazil, is a member of the Quorum of the Twelve Apostles of The Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-day Saints, ordained in 2018 — the first apostle from South America.
