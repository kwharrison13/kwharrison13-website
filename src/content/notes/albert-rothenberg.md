# Albert Rothenberg

Cited in [How To Take Smart Notes](/books/how-to-take-smart-notes) as a researcher on creativity and insight. The book invokes Rothenberg (Rothenberg 1971; 1996; 2015) for the claim that **the construction of oppositions is the most reliable way of generating new ideas**. This supports the smart-notes argument that comparing notes to surface contradictions, paradoxes, and oppositions is a key facilitator of insight — a paradox signals either incomplete thinking or an exhausted paradigm, and oppositions sharpen ideas by providing contrast.

**Context:** Albert Rothenberg is a psychiatrist and creativity researcher, long associated with Harvard, known for his studies of the cognitive processes behind creative thought (including the concept of "Janusian" thinking — holding opposing ideas simultaneously).
