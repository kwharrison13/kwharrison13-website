# Jessica Mah

CEO of [InDinero](/notes/indinero), an accounting-automation company. In a July 2018 call with Kyle, she described the product as observing accountants and augmenting/automating their service rather than replacing it — competing in some sense with [Xero](/notes/xero) and [Quickbooks](/notes/quickbooks) but "more than anything augmenting them," by pulling in the necessary financial data and working through customers' pain points. Her stance on the category: "If someone comes in and says they have a magic formula to automate accounting / tax, they're probably lying."

On financials, she reported InDinero was north of $10M in revenue (sounding closer to $30M), EBITDA-positive, and growing with or without capital since it wasn't burning. The company was raising $15–30M, preferred to work with insiders, wanted to cap dilution at ~10%, and expected to raise again in 18 months — but was guarded with Kyle, sharing few details and "only looking for someone who can write a $10M check."
