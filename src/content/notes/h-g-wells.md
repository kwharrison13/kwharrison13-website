# H. G. Wells

H. G. Wells is the author of [The Time Machine](/books/the-time-machine), which Kyle reads as a foundational text of [Science Fiction](/notes/science-fiction) / [Speculative Fiction](/notes/speculative-fiction) — framing time as a navigable fourth dimension and following the Time Traveller into a far future split between the comfortable Eloi and the subterranean Morlocks. Through a [Charles Darwin](/notes/charles-darwin)–inflected lens, Wells presents the Eloi/Morlock divergence as evolutionary degeneration: "intellectual versatility is the compensation for change, danger, and trouble," so the "too-perfect security" of a frictionless civilization breeds stagnation and decline. Kyle situates Wells's vision alongside [Brave New World](/books/brave-new-world) and [1984](/books/1984) as a stratified, comfort-and-decay dystopia, and reads the Eloi-Haves / Morlock-Have-nots split as the projected endpoint of the industrial [Capitalism](/notes/capitalism) of Wells's day, ending in [Entropy](/notes/entropy) and the heat-death of human activity.

**Context:** Herbert George Wells (1866–1946) was an English writer often called a "father of science fiction," author of *The Time Machine*, *The War of the Worlds*, *The Invisible Man*, and *The Island of Doctor Moreau*, as well as histories and works of social and political commentary.
