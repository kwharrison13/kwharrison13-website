# Ezra Klein

Journalist and NYT columnist who shows up across Kyle's notes in three distinct registers. In [Talent](/books/talent), [Tyler Cowen](/notes/tyler-cowen) holds him up as a case study in reading evolving hierarchies: an early blogger who "saw that the sector was evolving," helped found Vox aspiring to a higher station, and later moved to The New York Times — what set him apart from his "bloggy competitors" was the ability to figure out new ways to climb the totem pole of achievement (source: [Talent](/books/talent)).

In [Utopia For Realists](/books/utopia-for-realists), [Rutger Bregman](/notes/rutger-bregman) quotes him on motivated reasoning: "Smart people don't use their intellect to obtain the correct answer; they use it to obtain what they want to be the answer." That line is the connective tissue to the [Cognitive Dissonance](/notes/cognitive-dissonance) concept page, where it sits beside Festinger's psychology of belief-rigidity as evidence that intelligence makes convictions more unshakable, not less (sources: [Utopia For Realists](/books/utopia-for-realists), [Cognitive Dissonance](/notes/cognitive-dissonance)). Third, in the research for Kyle's essay [It's Time To Build... But Build What](/essays/its-time-to-build-but-build-what), Klein's NYT piece "Reactionary Futurism" is logged among the responses to [Marc Andreessen](/notes/marc-andreessen)'s techno-optimist manifesto (source: It's Time To Build... But Build What - Research).

**Context:** Ezra Klein co-founded Vox in 2014 after blogging at The Washington Post, became editor-at-large there, and since 2021 has been a New York Times opinion columnist and host of *The Ezra Klein Show*. He is the co-author of *Abundance* (2025, with Derek Thompson).
