# Stephen Covey

Stephen Covey surfaces across Kyle's reading as a recurring touchstone for two ideas in particular: the abundance-vs-scarcity frame and the listening discipline from [The 7 Habits of Highly Effective People](/books/the-7-habits-of-highly-effective-people). In [Our Towns](/books/our-towns), the "Women of the Commons" in Eastport, Maine are described as drawing explicitly on Covey: where others looked at a struggling town and thought "scarcity," these women "focused on 'abundance'" — and Kyle annotates the passage as abundance over a [Scarcity Mindset](/notes/scarcity-mindset). The book ties its whole "handful of forceful people make the difference" thesis to this Covey-style mindset shift.

The second thread is Covey's Habit #5, "seek first to understand, then to be understood." Kyle reads [Benjamin Franklin — An American Life](/books/benjamin-franklin-an-american-life) through it: Franklin's verdict that being "disputatious" was "a very bad habit" is treated as a precursor to Covey's listening maxim — winning the argument costs you the goodwill. The same line appears in [The Self-Driven Child](/books/the-self-driven-child), which quotes Covey (paraphrasing St. Francis) to argue parents should "ask your child questions in an effort to understand what's behind her resistance." Finally, [The Courage To Be Disliked](/books/the-courage-to-be-disliked) cites Covey from the other direction: the book notes that much of *7 Habits* "closely resembles Adler's ideas," positioning Covey as a popularizer of [Alfred Adler](/notes/alfred-adler)'s individual psychology.

**Context:** Stephen R. Covey (1932–2012) was an American educator and author whose 1989 book *The 7 Habits of Highly Effective People* became one of the best-selling business/self-help titles ever; he was also a prominent Latter-day Saint, which is part of why he recurs across Kyle's LDS-adjacent reading.

## Where this appears

- [Our Towns](/books/our-towns) — the "Women of the Commons" applying Covey's abundance-over-scarcity frame to revive Eastport, Maine.
- [Benjamin Franklin — An American Life](/books/benjamin-franklin-an-american-life) — Franklin's anti-disputation stance read as an early "seek first to understand, then to be understood" (Habit #5).
- [The Courage To Be Disliked](/books/the-courage-to-be-disliked) — the text names *7 Habits* as closely resembling Adler's ideas; Covey as Adler-popularizer.
- [The Self-Driven Child](/books/the-self-driven-child) — Covey's "seek first to understand" maxim applied to parenting and a child's resistance.
