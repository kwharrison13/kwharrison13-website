# Teletherapy

In the wiki's market map of [Behavioral Health](/notes/behavioral-health) (drawn from two expert calls on Sept 22, 2021), "D2C teletherapy" is one of the distinct business-model segments of the space. The page splits the category into employer-benefit platforms that measure outcomes (Spring, Lyra, Ginger), provider-infrastructure marketplaces that handle credentialing and billing for therapists ([Alma](/notes/alma), [SonderMind](/notes/sondermind), [Headway](/notes/headway)), and **direct-to-consumer teletherapy** — [Talkspace](/notes/talkspace) and BetterHelp — with platforms like [Grow Therapy](/notes/grow-therapy) alongside. [Talkspace](/notes/talkspace) is the named D2C teletherapy reference point. The segmentation matters because it sorts who the customer is (consumer vs. employer vs. provider) and therefore how each company acquires demand and monetizes.

**Context:** Teletherapy is the delivery of mental-health counseling remotely — via video, phone, or text/chat — rather than in person. The model expanded sharply after 2020; direct-to-consumer brands like Talkspace and BetterHelp acquire patients directly online, in contrast to employer-sponsored or insurance-routed behavioral-health offerings.
