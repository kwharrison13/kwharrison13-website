# Gartner

Gartner plays two distinct roles in Kyle's notes. As a **business-model comparable**, it anchors the bull case for [Tegus](/notes/tegus): Kyle's pre-brief frames Tegus as building "a massive data network of expert insights akin to what Gartner built for IT ($4B in revenue) or [ZoomInfo](/notes/zoominfo) built in sales ($26B market cap)," and it cites the billions enterprises already spend on market research from Gartner, Forrester, etc. as the TAM for Tegus's enterprise expansion. Gartner is the template for a compounding, hard-to-displace research-data franchise.

As a **research authority**, Gartner shows up throughout Kyle's API-security work. [Michael Isbitski](/notes/michael-isbitski) — Salt Security's Technical Evangelist — was Gartner's [DevSecOps](/notes/devsecops) analyst before joining Salt, and noted that "API security was one of those fundamental topics but it was too hard to focus on" as a pure analyst. The Salt Security Diligence — 2021 leans on Gartner's prediction that "by 2022, API abuses will move from an infrequent to the most-frequent attack vector," and cites Gartner's own API-security research as a market-validation source.

**Context:** Gartner, Inc. is a leading technology research and advisory firm (founded 1979, HQ Stamford, CT) known for its Magic Quadrant and Hype Cycle frameworks; it sells subscription research, advisory, and consulting to enterprise IT buyers and vendors.
