# Atlassian

Developer-and-collaboration software company that recurs in Kyle's diligence notes mostly as an incumbent ripe for disruption. In the Sprig fka UserLeap - Diligence notes, [Jira](/notes/jira) is described as the "center of gravity" in product — engineering, design, and product "completely dependent" on it — yet the framing is explicitly disruptive: the question is *"who disrupts Atlassian?"* Jira works for devs/designers/PMs but isn't built to collaborate with marketing, so the prediction is that "some company will do to Jira what [Figma](/notes/figma) did to [Adobe](/notes/adobe)" by putting collaboration first and connecting it to where people already do their work (Linear is floated as a candidate). Atlassian's [Confluence](/notes/confluence) is dismissed as "terrible" versus [Notion](/notes/notion) for knowledge management.

Atlassian also appears as a credential/network marker: in the CaptivateIQ Diligence — 2021 notes a CEO is flagged as a former Atlassian person, and in the September 17th, 2021 — Steve Malouf (Datadog) on Prelay call Atlassian (with Databricks) is named among companies where intro calls could be arranged.

**Context:** Atlassian (NASDAQ: TEAM) is an Australian enterprise-software company best known for Jira, Confluence, Trello, and Bitbucket, founded by Mike Cannon-Brookes and Scott Farquhar. (General background.)
