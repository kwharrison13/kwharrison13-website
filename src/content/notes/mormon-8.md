# Mormon 8

A chapter of the [Book of Mormon](/notes/book-of-mormon) Kyle reads chiefly through one verse — [Mormon 8](/notes/mormon-8):35, "I speak unto you as if ye were present, and yet ye are not. But behold, Jesus Christ hath shown you unto me, and I know your doing." It is the seed of the whole "written for our day" theme across his late-2020 [Come, Follow Me](/notes/come-follow-me) study: [Moroni](/notes/moroni) knows us not only because Christ showed him in vision, but because he watched the Nephites fall to the exact weaknesses he sees in us. Kyle returns to this verse in the Mormon 7–9, Moroni 1–6, and Moroni 10 weeks as the reason to read the Book of Mormon as addressed personally to the reader.

The chapter carries several other threads Kyle flags. [Mormon 8](/notes/mormon-8):8 — "one continual round of murder and bloodshed… no one knoweth the end of the war" — he reads as sin being an unquenchable fire that "never stops and never is enough" (echoed in the November 23–29, 2020. Ether 12–15: "By Faith All Things Are Fulfilled" week). [Mormon 8](/notes/mormon-8):16 ("blessed be he that shall bring this thing to light") prompts his wonder at how [Joseph Smith](/notes/joseph-smith), still a young man, must have felt reading those words about his own future work. And [Mormon 8](/notes/mormon-8):32 — churches that say "come unto me, and for your money you shall be forgiven of your sins" — he tags as [Indulgences](/notes/indulgences).
