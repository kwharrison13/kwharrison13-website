# Neiman Marcus

Luxury retailer that appears in Kyle's Cresta.ai - Diligence as a marquee Cresta pilot customer. Its 500+ seat Customer Care org — supporting the Neiman Marcus, Last Call, Horchow, and Bergdorf Goodman brands, ramping to ~900 agents at the holidays plus BPOs — is run by [Chris Purpura](/notes/chris-purpura), who joined from [Groupon](/notes/groupon) (where he was one of [Zendesk](/notes/zendesk)'s largest customers). Per Purpura, Neiman Marcus is **less than 5% of Cresta's annual revenue**, and the relationship is a low-six-figure live-chat contract negotiated **per-seat** (volume is too variable for interaction-based pricing), with a ceiling in the mid-to-high six figures.

Purpura's frame for adopting Cresta is cautious: as a cost center running customer service, he sees real-time agent coaching as a way to **get comfortable with AI/ML before letting bots loose** on customers. As of the November 2021 call the rollout was up and running but not fully operational — agents were largely told to ignore Cresta because they didn't yet trust its answers ("output just wasn't ready"). The Neiman Marcus stack he described — [Twilio Flex](/notes/twilio-flex) (chat/voice), Calabrio (WFM), Khoros Care, [StellaConnect](/notes/stellaconnect) (survey response, 35% rate) — illustrates the contact-center tooling landscape Cresta plugs into. Because luxury customers skew heavily to phone (75%→58% voice, still preferred), Cresta's real-time **voice** offering is the appealing expansion path.

**Context:** Neiman Marcus Group is an American luxury department-store chain headquartered in Dallas, whose banners include Neiman Marcus, Bergdorf Goodman, and (historically) Last Call and Horchow. It emerged from bankruptcy in 2020 and was acquired by Saks Global in 2024.
