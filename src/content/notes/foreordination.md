# Foreordination

An LDS doctrine Kyle studies most directly in the Alma 13–16 Come Follow Me block, where Alma's teaching on "the order of the Son of God" frames priesthood callings as prepared "from the foundation of the world according to the foreknowledge of God." Kyle's load-bearing distinction is **foreordination ≠ predestination**: callings were appointed in the premortal council, but they come to pass *on account of faith and good works freely chosen*, not by decreed destiny. Quoting the Gospel Topics entry — "Foreordination does not guarantee that individuals will receive certain callings" — and [Joseph Smith](/notes/joseph-smith) ("I suppose that I was ordained to this very office in that Grand Council"), the principle is that God offers blessings *if* we prepare to receive them, preserving [Agency](/notes/agency).

In Find To Teach, foreordination connects to missionary work: every minister "was ordained to that very purpose in the Grand Council of heaven before this world was," tying the calling to teach to the "spirit of promise" in 2 Timothy 1:7–9 — and reframing being a missionary at all as a statistically rare (1-in-323,000) circumstance one was prepared for.

**Context:** In Latter-day Saint theology, foreordination is the belief that God appointed certain spirits in the premortal existence to specific missions or callings in mortal life, contingent on their continued righteousness — explicitly contrasted with Calvinist predestination, which the Church rejects.
