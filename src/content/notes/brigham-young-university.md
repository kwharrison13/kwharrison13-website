# Brigham Young University

In Kyle's notes, Brigham Young University appears as the venue for [Truman G. Madsen](/notes/truman-g-madsen)'s celebrated lecture series on the life and character of the Prophet [Joseph Smith](/notes/joseph-smith). Madsen — an LDS philosopher and BYU professor — delivered the lectures there; the second, [Joseph Smith Lecture 2 — Joseph's Personality and Character](/notes/joseph-smith-lecture-2-joseph-s-personality-and-character), was published August 22, 1978, and is preserved on the BYU Speeches archive (speeches.byu.edu). The university is thus the institutional setting that anchors Madsen's best-known work in the corpus.

**Context:** Brigham Young University (BYU), in Provo, Utah, is the flagship university of The Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-day Saints. Its BYU Speeches archive publishes devotional and academic addresses given on campus.
