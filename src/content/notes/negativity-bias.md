# Negativity Bias

The tendency for negative events to dominate attention out of proportion to their frequency. In [News in the Age of Abundance](/notes/news-in-the-age-of-abundance), Perell argues that because creation is slow and destruction is fast, rare negative events fill headlines while steady gains (falling poverty, better health) go unreported — distorting our worldview ([Paradox of Abundance](/notes/paradox-of-abundance)).

**Context:** "Negativity bias" is a well-documented psychological finding that negative information tends to have a greater effect on attention, memory, and decision-making than equivalently intense positive information.
