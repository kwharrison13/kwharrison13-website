# Brigham Young

Second President of [The Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-day Saints](/notes/the-church-of-jesus-christ-of-latter-day-saints) and successor to [Joseph Smith](/notes/joseph-smith), read by Kyle as both an institutional builder and the source of durable problems. The fullest portrait is John Turner's [Brigham Young — Pioneer Prophet](/books/brigham-young-pioneer-prophet), which Kyle reads "with explicit willingness to register both the institutional accomplishments and the durable problems Young set in motion." Four threads anchor those notes: "no one is safe until the end" (Young's rebuke of those satisfied with progress already made); the honest acknowledgment that "Joseph's policy ≠ Brigham's policy" — Young himself conceded "much of Joseph's policy in temporal things was different from my ideas of the way to manage the kingdom"; the uncomfortable observation that "racial discomfort, ideological discrimination, social cohesion, and elitism — all negative realities of modern Mormonism seem to stem directly from Brigham Young"; and the handcart episode reframed from heroic suffering into "a poorly organized mass movement" born of "disregard for prudence."

[American Zion](/books/american-zion) situates his rise: after Smith left no clear succession plan and [Sidney Rigdon](/notes/sidney-rigdon)'s erratic claim failed, Young's leadership of the Quorum of the Twelve and his mission record placed him closest to the deceased prophet; he led the pioneer party to the Salt Lake Valley and declared it "the place." That same book flags the darker inheritance — Young ordering Lucy Mack Smith's memoir burned, and his 1852 anti-Black priesthood sermon. Separately, the studied chapter Temporal Wealth and the Kingdom of God — Brigham Young preserves the devotional Young: a practical man who built homes and farms but "did not set his heart on worldly things," taught that wealth is a stewardship ("it all belongs to Him… to see what they will do with it"), and warned "I am more afraid of covetousness in our Elders than I am of the hordes of hell."

**Context:** Brigham Young (1801–1877) led the Latter-day Saints west to the Salt Lake Valley after Joseph Smith's death, served as the church's second president and the first governor of Utah Territory, and oversaw the colonization of the Intermountain West.
