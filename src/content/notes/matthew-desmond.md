# Matthew Desmond

**One-line:** *Sociologist of poverty and housing whose ethnography Evicted reframed eviction as a cause of poverty, not just a symptom of it.*

## What we know

- Author of Evicted: Poverty and Profit in the American City, a year-in-the-field study of eight families and their landlords in Milwaukee (source: Evicted).
- Argues that poverty research has fixated on what the poor *lack* while ignoring how much money is *extracted* from them — "there is a lot of money to be made off the poor. The 'hood is good" (source: Evicted).
- Rejects the standard liberal "structural forces" vs. conservative "individual deficiency" framing of inequality; says both "treated low-income families as if they lived in quarantine" (source: Evicted).
- Proposes a universal housing voucher with mandatory landlord participation and rent regulation, arguing it could be funded without new spending if overcharging were curbed (source: Evicted).
- Released his Milwaukee survey data publicly through the Harvard Dataverse Network (source: Evicted).
