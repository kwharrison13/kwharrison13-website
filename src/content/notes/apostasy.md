# Apostasy

A recurring theme across Kyle's [Come, Follow Me](/notes/come-follow-me) and topical gospel study, treated in the Latter-day Saint sense of the "great apostasy" — the falling-away from the early church that, in this framing, created the need for a [Restoration](/notes/restoration). In The Godhead, Kyle pins the apostasy to a specific theological shift: the move from Apostles and Prophets who spoke "thus saith the Lord" to men who spoke opinion and philosophy, with the creedal Trinity born of the Nicene and Athanasian councils (the *homoousios* vs. *homoiousios* / [Arian](/notes/arius) dispute) rejected because "it is not in the scriptures." Olive Tree Allegory reads the apostasy as one movement in Zenos's chronicle of scattering and Restoration (Jacob 5): "the apostasy brought these things to pass… there was a need for a restoration of good fruit."

The theme also runs through Kyle's covenant and pride-cycle notes. The Curse of Cain uses the "great apostasy" as the large-scale parallel to Cain's broken lineage — all lineages broken, individuals robbed of a sealed inheritance unless they use temple ordinances, which is why Malachi turns the hearts of fathers and children. In the 3 Nephi 27–4 Nephi study, apostasy sits inside [The Pride Cycle](/notes/the-pride-cycle), with the observation that apostate Mormons caused greater early trouble for the church than non-Mormons — "often it was the apostates riling up the citizens." It is named among the core themes of the Anti-Christ — Topical Study as well.

**Context:** In Latter-day Saint theology, the "Great Apostasy" is the doctrine that the original Christian church lost priesthood authority and pure doctrine after the death of the apostles, necessitating a Restoration through Joseph Smith.
