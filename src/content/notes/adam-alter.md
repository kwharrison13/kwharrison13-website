# Adam Alter

Adam Alter appears in Kyle's corpus through [Digital Minimalism](/books/digital-minimalism), where [Cal Newport](/notes/cal-newport) leans on Alter's 2017 book *[Irresistible](/notes/irresistible)* for the mechanics of behavioral addiction. As the book's highlights record, "in his 2017 book, Irresistible, which details his study of this topic, Alter explores the many different 'ingredients' that make a given technology likely to hook our brain and cultivate unhealthy use." Newport uses Alter to name the two levers tech companies pull to engineer compulsive use: "intermittent positive reinforcement and the drive for social approval."

In Kyle's notes Alter functions as the supporting expert behind Newport's case that smartphone and social-media overuse is the product of deliberate design rather than personal weakness — the analytical scaffolding that sits beneath complementary admissions like Sean Parker's account of Facebook engineering "a little dopamine hit every once in a while."

**Context:** Adam Alter is a professor of marketing and psychology at NYU's Stern School of Business. His book *Irresistible: The Rise of Addictive Technology and the Business of Keeping Us Hooked* (2017) popularized the idea of "behavioral addiction" to screens, games, and apps. He is also the author of *Drunk Tank Pink* and *Anatomy of a Breakthrough*.
