# Books To Read

The `#books-to-read` tag — Kyle's marker for a book he wants to read but hasn't yet, the routing signal that feeds the wiki's anti-library. Across the corpus, when a book surfaces in a clipping, call, essay, or another book's recommendation, Kyle flags it with this hashtag so it can later become a `wiki/books-to-read/` entry. The intellectual case for the practice comes from [The Black Swan](/books/the-black-swan), where Kyle ties this tag to Taleb's "antilibrary" — the idea that unread books are more valuable than read ones, a private store of what you don't yet know.

In practice the tag attaches to specific recommendations: [Michael Nielsen](/notes/michael-nielsen)'s *Reinventing Discovery* (the lens for "why ideas are different than software today") in Open-Source Knowledge - Research; [Will Thorndike](/notes/will-thorndike)'s *[The Outsiders](/notes/the-outsiders-eight-unconventional-ceos-and-their-radically-rational-blueprint-for-success)* in [On the Nature of Long-Term Holds](/notes/on-the-nature-of-long-term-holds); and list articles like 20 Book Recommendations by Top Venture Capitalists, which Kyle tagged `#books-to-read` but which was filed as a reading-queue item (a list, not a single book) worth mining for individual titles. Note that the canonical anti-library now lives in the dedicated `wiki/books-to-read/` folder; this page is the legacy tag-hub that the hashtag resolves to.
