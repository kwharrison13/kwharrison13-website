# Marcel Proust

In Kyle's notes, Proust appears as one of the pillars of [Rene Girard](/notes/rene-girard)'s evidence base for [Mimetic Theory](/notes/mimetic-theory). Per raw/clippings/Peter Thiel's Religion, Girard's theory "rests on the assumption that all our cultural behaviors… are imitative" and "builds upon the kinds of books and people that modern people tend to ignore: The Bible, classic fiction writers such as Marcel Proust, and playwrights like [William Shakespeare](/notes/william-shakespeare)." Girard reads such fiction as a record of a "theatre of envy, where, like mimes, we imitate other people's desires." Proust is grouped with Shakespeare as a novelist whose close attention to desire and imitation modern readers have set aside — the same framing the [William Shakespeare](/notes/william-shakespeare) page makes explicit.

**Context:** Marcel Proust (1871–1922) was a French novelist, author of *In Search of Lost Time (À la recherche du temps perdu)*; Girard drew on Proust's depiction of jealousy and imitated desire in his early literary criticism (*Deceit, Desire, and the Novel*).
