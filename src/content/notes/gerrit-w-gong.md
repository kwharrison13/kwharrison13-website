# Gerrit W. Gong

An apostle of The Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-day Saints whose General Conference addresses recur in Kyle's gospel-study notes. In the General Conference — October 2021 notes his Sunday Afternoon talk on trust and forgiveness is captured — "When trust is broken we wonder how to trust again," a call to be "more forgiving of ourselves and each other," and "God is a God of truth and cannot lie" — along with the line "Condemnation focus[es] on the past. Forgiveness looks liberally into the future." In the General Conference — April 2020 notes his Saturday Evening talk is summarized around the Atonement ("We owed a debt we could not pay") and the "complimentary contrasts" of beginning and end, living and dead.

**Context:** Gerrit W. Gong (b. 1953) is a member of the Quorum of the Twelve Apostles of The Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-day Saints, the first of East Asian descent called to that body, ordained in 2018.

His son [Matthew Gong](/notes/matthew-gong), who is openly queer, reflects on Gerrit's calling and on being a queer member of the Church in Birthday Letters — Matthew Gong — drawing a careful line between "the Church" and "my father": "I have never seen him desire a position of power… My father is a good man."
