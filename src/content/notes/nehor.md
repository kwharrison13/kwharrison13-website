# Nehor

A figure from the Book of Mormon (Alma) whom Kyle studies in his [Alma 13](/notes/alma-13)–16 ("Enter into the Rest of the Lord") notes as the archetype of priestcraft and false doctrine. Drawing on [Alma 1](/notes/alma-1):3-6, Kyle summarizes "the order of Nehor" as three things: that priests and teachers ought to become *popular*; that they ought *not to labor* for themselves but be supported by the people; and that *all mankind should be saved* and have eternal life — which he tags as [Universalism](/notes/universalism). Nehor "began to be lifted up in the pride of his heart… and even began to establish a church after the manner of his preaching."

In the same study, the lawyers, judges, priests, and teachers who confront Alma and Amulek at the prison are described as being "of the profession of Nehor" ([Alma 14](/notes/alma-14):18-19). Kyle contrasts the order of Nehor against the true [Priesthood](/notes/priesthood) (via [D&C 107](/notes/dandc-107) and [D&C 121](/notes/dandc-121)) — using Nehor as the negative foil to the priesthood truths taught in [Alma 13](/notes/alma-13).

**Context:** In the Book of Mormon, Nehor is the man who introduces priestcraft among the Nephites and is later executed for killing Gideon; "the order of Nehor" becomes shorthand in the text for a popular, paid, doctrinally permissive religious movement opposed to the church.
