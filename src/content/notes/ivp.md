# IVP

Late-stage venture / growth firm that appears across Kyle's notes both as a market participant and as a source of partner commentary. In the Pave Diligence — 2021 dossier, IVP is listed (alongside [YC](/notes/y-combinator), [Madrona](/notes/madrona), and [Sequoia](/notes/sequoia)) among the 70+ funds Pave built strategic give-to-get data relationships with — part of the proprietary compensation data network underpinning the company's product.

IVP partners also feature prominently in Kyle's venture-structure essay research. In [The Death of a Venture Fund](/essays/death) research, [Michael Miao](/notes/michael-miao) at IVP names the failure mode as getting "too skittish or valuation-sensitive during this downturn and ced[ing] market share to other growth investors" — losing optimism — while Jake Storm (associated with Felicis Ventures / IVP) points to generational transition and the inability to stay competitive at a given fund size. In [The Productization of Venture Capital](/essays/productization) research, Miao offers a sharp self-assessment: "our true product SKU is our individual partners, which creates a lot of variance in terms of how founders perceive us and makes it hard for me to generalize IVP" — a data point in the essay's argument about partner-as-product versus firm-as-product.

**Context:** IVP (Institutional Venture Partners) is a Menlo Park–based later-stage venture and growth-equity firm founded in 1980, known for backing companies like Twitter, Slack, and Coinbase.
