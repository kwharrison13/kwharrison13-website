# 3 Nephi 10

A chapter in the Book of Mormon's third book of Nephi, studied in Kyle's Come Follow Me week on [3 Nephi 8](/notes/3-nephi-8)–[3 Nephi 11](/notes/3-nephi-11) (September 14–20, 2020. 3 Nephi 8–11: "Arise and Come Forth unto Me"). For Kyle this chapter carries the week's central idea that **Christ never gives up**. He reads verses 4–6 — "how oft have I gathered you… how oft would I have gathered you… how oft will I gather you" — as a never-ending circle of love: Christ has gathered us, would gather us now, and will yet gather us, with no point at which he stops caring.

Kyle ties the chapter's threefold "has / would / will" gathering to the same past-present-future structure in the [Articles of Faith](/notes/articles-of-faith) (God "has revealed," "does now reveal," and "will yet reveal"), and pairs it with the [His Grace is Sufficient](/notes/his-grace-is-sufficient) image of Christ pleading with the unrepentant to *choose to stay* rather than begging to be let in.

**Context:** In the Book of Mormon, 3 Nephi 10 follows the three hours of destruction at Christ's death with three days of darkness, during which the voice of the risen Christ calls the surviving Nephites to repentance and gathering before His personal ministry among them.
