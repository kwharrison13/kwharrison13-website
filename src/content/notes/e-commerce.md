# E-Commerce

E-commerce — online selling, marketplaces, and the tooling and services built around them — surfaces in Kyle's corpus mainly as the substrate for two investment angles he was tracking. In his April 30th, 2020 Roam "Quick Capture" log, the recurring observation is that marketplace friction spawns point-solution tooling: Victor Levitin created CrazyLister in 2013 in response to sellers' frustration over the complicated process of posting items on [eBay](/notes/ebay) — an example of software built to ease selling on a marketplace. That kind of seller tool is exactly what the [E-Commerce Tech Roll-Up](/notes/e-commerce-tech-roll-up) thesis (also captured in the same notes) aims to acquire and consolidate. Pattern, an e-commerce enablement business, surfaces in the same set of notes.

**Context:** E-commerce refers to the buying and selling of goods and services over the internet, spanning first-party online stores, third-party marketplaces (Amazon, eBay, Etsy, Shopify-powered storefronts), and the broad ecosystem of enablement tools — listing software, fulfillment, advertising, analytics, and brand aggregators — that has grown up around sellers operating on those platforms.
