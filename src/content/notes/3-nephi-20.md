# 3 Nephi 20

A Book of Mormon chapter in which the risen Christ ministers among the Nephites and opens his covenant teaching to them. In Kyle's Come Follow Me study, this is the chapter he annotated most heavily, drawing three threads from it. First, the standing command to "[Cease to Pray](/notes/cease-to-pray)" aloud but "not cease to pray in their hearts" (v.1) — which Kyle questions as a possible excuse to skip actual prayer. Second, that Christ is the source of the living bread: he gives the multitude bread and wine that neither they nor the disciples brought (vv.3–7), modeling [Servant Leadership](/notes/servant-leadership) by serving the apostles first and sending them to do likewise. Third, his testimony that all the prophets "from Samuel and those that follow after... have testified of me" (vv.23–24), which Kyle pairs with [Truman G. Madsen](/notes/truman-g-madsen) on Joseph Smith *reporting* rather than speculating about the character of God.

The same chapter anchors Kyle's Diligence topical study: "his soul shall never hunger nor thirst, but shall be filled" (vv.8–9) becomes the image of the [Sacrament](/notes/sacrament) as the fountain of living waters after which one must "seek with all diligence."

**Context:** 3 Nephi 20 is a chapter in the Book of Mormon's account of Christ's post-resurrection visit to the Americas, in which he administers the sacrament miraculously and begins expounding the covenant of the gathering of Israel.
