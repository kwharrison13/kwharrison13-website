# Sign Seeking

Sign Seeking is a recurring theme across Kyle's 2020 Come Follow Me studies, treated as **a symptom of weak faith and a road toward apostasy**. The governing principle, drawn from D&C 63:9 and quoted in the Helaman 7–12 week, is that "faith cometh not by signs, but signs follow those that believe" — and that "all things already denote there is a God" (Alma 30). In the Alma 30–31 week, Korihor the [Anti-Christ](/notes/anti-christ) demanding "if thou wilt show me a sign" is the archetypal sign-seeker, against Matthew 12:39: "an evil and adulterous generation seeketh after a sign."

Kyle's notes connect the theme to humility and to [Joseph Smith](/notes/joseph-smith)'s warning. In the Alma 32–35 week, sign seeking pairs with [Righteous in the Dark](/notes/righteous-in-the-dark) — "sometimes we ask for a sign because we're not humble enough to rely on our own faith," and must instead be left to trust humility and [Faith](/notes/faith). Joseph Smith's teaching is quoted directly: the man who "rises up to condemn others, finding fault with the Church… is in the high road to apostasy," and "whenever you see a man seeking after a sign, you may set it down that he is an adulterous man." The 3 Nephi 8–11 week adds the corrosive dynamic: sign seeking **hardens hearts over time** — even after "so many signs," people grow "less and less astonished at a sign," become hard, and let Satan blind their eyes.

**Context:** "Sign seeking" is a term in Latter-day Saint teaching for demanding a miraculous sign as a precondition for belief — consistently framed in LDS scripture and doctrine as evidence of spiritual weakness rather than faith.
