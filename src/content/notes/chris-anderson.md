# Chris Anderson

Head of TED and author of [TED Talks: The Official TED Guide to Public Speaking](/books/ted-talks-the-official-ted-guide-to-public-speaking) — the corpus's anchor text on the craft of [Public Speaking](/notes/public-speaking). Anderson's central claim is that a speaker's only real job is to take an idea that matters deeply to them and "rebuild it inside the minds of listeners"; everything else (style, charisma, stagecraft) is in service of that transfer, and "style without substance is awful."

**How he came to TED:** Anderson recounts that when [Ricky Wurman](/notes/richard-saul-wurman) looked to sell the TED conference, he was tantalized by it because his entrepreneurial mantra was to *follow other people's passion* — "passion was a proxy for potential" (the logic behind the dozens of hobbyist magazines he launched). What finally convinced him was a passage in [David Deutsch](/notes/david-deutsch)'s [The Fabric of Reality](/notes/the-fabric-of-reality): the argument that real understanding comes from the *unification* of knowledge rather than ever-narrower specialization. That spiderweb-of-knowledge idea became the backbone of his thesis that live human idea-sharing matters *more* in a machine age, not less — "humans are for being more human than we've ever been."

**Context:** Chris Anderson (b. 1957) is the British-American media entrepreneur who acquired TED through his nonprofit Sapling Foundation in 2001 and turned it from an invite-only conference into a global ideas platform, most famously by putting TED Talks online for free.
