# NCX

NCX (Natural Capital Exchange) is a forest-carbon marketplace Kyle diligenced at [Index Ventures](/notes/index-ventures) around its Series B. Per the NCX - Diligence dossier, NCX pays landowners to defer timber harvests **one year at a time**, generating carbon credits bought by corporate buyers (e.g. Microsoft). Its differentiators: annual contracts (vs. the 10–100-year lock-ins of traditional improved-forest-management programs), no minimum acreage (democratizing access for small private landowners), satellite imagery plus LiDAR to estimate timber volume without ground surveys, auction-based pricing, and "ton-year accounting" as the underlying methodology. It was founded by Zack Parisa (also lead author on the ton-year academic paper). The diligence drew on five expert calls via Guidepoint plus two NCX white papers.

The deal went to a December 13th, 2021 — NCX Partner Presentation, where the Index partnership reviewed it (convened by [Mark Goldberg](/notes/mark-goldberg)). The partners split on it: [Carlos Gonzalez-Cardenas](/notes/carlos-gonzalez-cardenas) was positive on the macro / "why now" but had pause about whether supply (the landowner land-grab) scales fast enough to matter; [Julia Andre](/notes/julia-andre) found the landowner-acquisition approach unique and the marketplace compelling, conditioned on offset quality and supply scale holding up.

**Context:** NCX (Natural Capital Exchange), founded by SilviaTerra's team, built a forest-carbon marketplace using remote sensing to measure forests and one-year harvest-deferral contracts to generate credits, aiming to open carbon markets to small landowners.
