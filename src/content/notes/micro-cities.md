# Micro Cities

"Micro cities" is the throughline answer Kyle draws out of [The New Urban Crisis](/books/the-new-urban-crisis): against both [suburban sprawl](/notes/suburban-sprawl) and [mega cities](/notes/mega-cities) of inequality, the alternative is deliberately designed micro cities. Even small towns, in this view, should have a bustling downtown / main-street core rather than diffusing into car-dependent sprawl or concentrating into a handful of unaffordable superstar metros. Kyle's notes tie the idea directly into the [The New City](/notes/the-new-city) / [Prosperable](/notes/prosperable) project and the [EPCOT](/notes/epcot) hub-and-spoke transit model from [Walt Disney and the Promise of Progress City](/books/walt-disney-and-the-promise-of-progress-city).

The concept is the constructive half of a recurring frame in his city-building reading: where [Richard Florida](/notes/richard-florida) diagnoses the clustering paradox (talent clustering produces both growth and inequality), Kyle reads it as an argument for intentionally designed, walkable, mixed-use, transit-connected small cities — denser than sprawl but deliberately kept human-scale rather than mega-scale. [Remote Work](/notes/remote-work) is what makes this newly viable, dissolving the proximity and commute constraints that once pulled the affluent back into the dense core.
