# Moses 7

Moses 7 — the Enoch vision in the Book of Moses — is the load-bearing scripture behind Kyle's The Process of Zion study. There he draws the core doctrine from [Moses 7](/notes/moses-7):18–19 (with [D&C 101](/notes/dandc-101)): the world will *not* see God descend to "snap his fingers" and make it celestial; rather, *we* make it celestial by learning and living the celestial law. Enoch's [Zion](/notes/zion) (like Melchizedek's) is read as "a celestial city existing in a telestial world" that "cannot abide" — so its residents are taken up while the residue cannot come. The chapter also feeds [Moses 7](/notes/moses-7):67, where the Lord shows Enoch "all things, even unto the end of the world … and [he] received a fulness of joy," cited in his 3 Nephi 27–4 Nephi study.

In Pattern of Prophetic Callings, Moses 7 supplies the temple imagery of the prophetic call: prophets "see God — a common thing in the temple" ([Moses 7](/notes/moses-7):2–3) and enter covenants "symbolized by being clothed in glory" (7:3). It also appears in Plan of Salvation — Topical Study via the "Lamb slain from the foundation of the world" ([Moses 7](/notes/moses-7):47), tied to the Atonement.

**Context:** Moses 7 is a chapter in the Book of Moses (part of the Pearl of Great Price in the Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-day Saints), recounting Enoch's vision and the establishment of the city of Zion, which was taken up to God.

## Where this appears

- The Process of Zion — 7:18–19 as the doctrine that *we* build Zion / make the world celestial; Enoch's Zion as a celestial city that "cannot abide" a telestial world.
- Pattern of Prophetic Callings — 7:2–3 (prophets see God in the temple; covenants symbolized by being clothed in glory).
- Plan of Salvation — Topical Study — 7:47, the "Lamb slain from the foundation of the world," tied to the Atonement.
- 3 Nephi 27–4 Nephi — There Could Not Be a Happier People — 7:67, Enoch shown all things and receiving a fulness of joy.
