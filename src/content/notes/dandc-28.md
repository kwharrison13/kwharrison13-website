# D&C 28

Both gospel-study pages cite [D&C 28](/notes/dandc-28):4, but for opposite-facing lessons about following the Spirit. In Spiritual Gifts, the verse appears in a list of the Spirit's functions as enabling action: "If thou art led by the Comforter… thou mayest do it" — the Comforter authorizes you to act. In Scripture Study — Topical Study, the same verse is filed under the danger that the still small voice "can easily be misunderstood" — mistaken for logic or "feelings of the flesh" — which is why recognizing the voice of the Spirit requires effort and forethought ([D&C 9](/notes/dandc-9):7–8) and quieting the inner noise ([Henry B. Eyring](/notes/henry-b-eyring)).

Together the two uses bracket a single point about revelation: being led by the Comforter both empowers action and demands careful discernment, since the prompting is subtle and easily confused.

**Context:** Doctrine and Covenants 28 is an 1830 revelation to Oliver Cowdery (addressing Hiram Page's false revelations); verse 4 instructs that one led by the Comforter "may speak or teach… by way of commandment unto the church."
