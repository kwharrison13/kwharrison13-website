# BlackRock

Across Kyle's notes BlackRock appears mostly as the **demand-side forcing function for corporate climate accounting**. In the Watershed Climate Diligence — 2021 dossier, BlackRock's expectation — announced in Larry Fink's CEO letter — that every company in its portfolio develop a plan to reach net-zero emissions is cited as a core piece of the bull case: regulatory and shareholder pressure is what creates the $100B+ pool of demand Watershed is built to serve. Kyle also listed a sustainability contact at BlackRock ([Christopher Weber](/notes/christopher-weber)) among the corporate climate leads he emailed as reference-call / outreach targets during that diligence. The [Sylvera](/notes/sylvera) call (Nov 2021) reinforces the theme: Sylvera had been talking to senior BlackRock leadership about measuring the carbon impact of green bonds, and "they just don't know" how — the visibility gap that carbon-measurement startups aim to fill.

Separately, BlackRock surfaces in [What It Takes](/books/what-it-takes) (Steve Schwarzman's memoir) as Larry Fink's firm, appearing on the roster of the president's Strategic and Policy Forum alongside other large-cap CEOs.

**Context:** BlackRock is the world's largest asset manager. Its CEO Larry Fink's annual letters to portfolio companies were influential in pushing corporate net-zero commitments and ESG disclosure.

## Where this appears
