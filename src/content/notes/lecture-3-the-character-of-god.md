# Lecture 3: The Character of God

The third of the [Lectures on Faith](/notes/lectures-on-faith), cited repeatedly across Kyle's gospel study for its statement of the **three things necessary to exercise faith in God unto life and salvation**: (1) the idea that He actually exists; (2) a correct idea of His character, perfections, and attributes; and (3) an actual knowledge that the course of life one is pursuing is according to His will. Without these three "the faith of every rational being must be imperfect and unproductive."

Kyle leans on this framework in several places. In notes on Christ Is Risen; Faith in Him Will Move Mountains — Russell M. Nelson he groups it with [Lecture 1: Faith Defined](/notes/lecture-1-faith-defined) and [Lecture 7: The Effects of Faith](/notes/lecture-7-the-effects-of-faith) to support [Russell M. Nelson](/notes/russell-m-nelson)'s definition of faith as power and "mental exertion." In the Alma 13–16 Come, Follow Me week the second requirement — a *correct idea of His character* — anchors a meditation on "but if not…" faith: people mistake God's inaction for inability because they lack a correct idea of His character and can't see the eternal picture. It also appears in his October 2021 General Conference notes and a September 2021 study on fulfilling the measure of our creation.

**Context:** The Lectures on Faith are a series of seven theological lectures on faith prepared for the School of the Prophets and published in the 1835 Doctrine and Covenants; Lecture 3 sets out the three prerequisites for saving faith and remains widely cited in Latter-day Saint teaching on faith.
