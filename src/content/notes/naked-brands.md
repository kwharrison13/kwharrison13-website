# Naked Brands

Despite living in the `companies/` folder, "Naked Brands" is not a company — it is a thesis and essay series by [David Perell](/notes/david-perell). The core claim: as the internet collapses distribution costs and removes geographic and informational gatekeepers, audiences increasingly "follow people, not brands." Naked Brands are defined "not by symbols, logos, or television advertisements, but by the authenticity of dynamic personalities" — they win on trust, attention, and transparency rather than scale or secrecy. Perell builds the argument on [Marshall McLuhan](/notes/marshall-mcluhan)'s "the medium is the message," arguing that electric/digital media decentralize influence and produce diversity rather than the conformity print bred.

Perell applies the thesis across domains in a series: **The Future of Fashion** (individual personalities replace fashion gatekeepers; Virgil Abloh as designer-influencer-brand in one), **The Future of Basketball** (LeBron's "The Decision" ushered in athletes circumventing media to connect with fans directly), **The Future of Music** (the internet unbundled the album, inverting the business so free streaming is the top of the funnel and artists monetize via touring, merch, and fan intimacy), and **The Future of Finance** (money managers are now a commodity, so firms compete on trust and personality, openly disclosing strategy). The [Morgan Housel: Naked Brands Interview Series](/notes/morgan-housel-naked-brands-interview-series) entry extends this: because information is now free-flowing, "you can't hide misbehavior anymore," and content becomes the "Chef's Table" of asset management — building subconscious trust a legacy brand like Merrill Lynch can't.

The thesis is load-bearing in Kyle's own thinking. His essay-research for [The Unbundling of Venture Capital](/essays/the-unbundling-of-venture-capital) is "most of it built on David Perell's Naked Brands thesis applied to venture" — the idea that "value props that resonate" come from people having relationships with specific investors and firms (knowing what job they're hiring them to do), per the September 13th, 2022 — Harry Stebbings Podcast notes. It also surfaces in [The Everything Store](/books/the-everything-store), where Kyle annotates Amazon's pricing transparency as "visibility necessitates transparency #Naked Brands — in a world where we can see most things we get nervous when we can't see something," and it underpins the [Differentiation in Venture](/notes/differentiation-in-venture) framing of brand/positioning as the surviving differentiator.

**Context:** David Perell is a writer and online-education entrepreneur (founder of the "Write of Passage" course). His "Naked Brands" essays, published on perell.com starting around 2017, are among his best-known early work on how the internet reshapes branding, distribution, and creator-audience relationships.
