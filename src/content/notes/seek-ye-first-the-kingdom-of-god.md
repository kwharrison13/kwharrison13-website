# Seek ye first the kingdom of God

This phrase anchors Kyle's reading of [Brigham Young](/notes/brigham-young)'s teaching on temporal wealth. Young — "a practical man who built homes, businesses, and farms, but who did not set his heart on worldly things" — taught that temporal wealth should be dedicated to building up the kingdom of God, and that patient labor (not hurry) brings both temporal and eternal riches: "Stop, do not be in a hurry. … you do not pray enough, you do not read the Scriptures enough, you do not meditate enough … This is not the way to get rich." Kyle's note on the chapter ties it directly to this idea: *"This chapter hit me harder than anything has in a while; it reinforced the idea that true happiness only comes when you seek ye first the kingdom of God."*

**Context:** "Seek ye first the kingdom of God" is from the Sermon on the Mount (Matthew 6:33): "But seek ye first the kingdom of God, and his righteousness; and all these things shall be added unto you" — the counsel that spiritual priorities, rightly ordered, take precedence over temporal anxiety.
