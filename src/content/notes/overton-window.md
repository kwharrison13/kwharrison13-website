# Overton Window

**One-line:** *[Joseph Overton](/notes/joseph-overton)'s model of the band of policy ideas politically "acceptable" at a given moment — and the lever by which today's radical becomes tomorrow's common sense.*

[Utopia For Realists](/books/utopia-for-realists) uses it as the mechanism of utopian politics: to make a radical idea reasonable, you stretch the bounds of the radical, shifting the whole window. It dovetails with [Milton Friedman](/notes/milton-friedman)'s claim that ideas "lying around" during a crisis become the ones adopted, and [Albert Hirschman](/notes/albert-hirschman)'s observation that utopias are first attacked as futile, dangerous, and perverse — then quickly seen as commonplace once enacted.

## Related concepts

- [Joseph Overton](/notes/joseph-overton) — originator of the model the band of "politically acceptable" policy ideas is named for.
- [Utopia For Realists](/books/utopia-for-realists) — uses the window as the mechanism of utopian politics: stretch the radical to shift the whole band.
- [Milton Friedman](/notes/milton-friedman) — his claim that ideas "lying around" during a crisis become the ones adopted is the supply side of a window shift.
- [Albert Hirschman](/notes/albert-hirschman) — his observation that utopias are first attacked as futile, dangerous, and perverse, then seen as commonplace once enacted, traces the same band moving.
