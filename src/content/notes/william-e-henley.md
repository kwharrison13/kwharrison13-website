# William E. Henley

Poet quoted in Kyle's Patience — Topical Study for the closing lines of "Invictus" — "I am the master of my fate, I am the captain of my soul." Kyle uses the line not as defiant self-sufficiency but folded into a gospel reading: that in patiently enduring the tribulations of mortality we may someday exclaim those words, knowing the purpose behind our trials.

**Context:** William Ernest Henley (1849–1903) was an English poet, critic, and editor, best known for his 1875 poem "Invictus," written while recovering from tuberculosis-related illness and amputation.
