# Stuart McClure

Former CEO of [Cylance](/notes/cylance) and a longtime McAfee veteran, brought into Kyle's Noname Security - Diligence dossier (Nov 11, 2021) as a security-industry expert giving a skeptical read on the emerging API-security category. McClure framed API-security tooling as "the same old security stuff, just applied into the API world" — diagnostics rather than prevention. His core argument: these products can only provide *visibility* into attacks and weaknesses, not stop them ("you have the agent, the network, and the log, but you can describe any other security solution the same way"), and the bigger players (AWS, Splunk) will likely build or buy, so a buyer has to scrutinize true differentiation against incumbents.

Much of his call concerned "Mike," a McAfee colleague McClure hired at [Cylance](/notes/cylance) before Mike departed for [Armis](/notes/armis). McClure described Mike as humble with a Marine background and a savvy security operator at McAfee, but said personal/family difficulties during the Cylance years left him "unstable, unpredictable," to the point McClure nearly had to let him go; growth decelerated after Mike took over a function. He was complimentary personally ("I love him personally, he's a great guy") but ambivalent on whether the challenges were foundational or temporary.

**Context:** Stuart McClure is a well-known cybersecurity figure — co-author of the *Hacking Exposed* book series, an early McAfee/Foundstone executive, founder and former CEO of AI-endpoint-security firm Cylance (acquired by BlackBerry in 2019), and later founder/CEO of Qwiet AI.
