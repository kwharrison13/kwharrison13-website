# Abigail Adams

Mother of [John Quincy Adams](/books/john-quincy-adams) and wife of [John Adams](/books/john-adams), appearing in Kyle's notes on the [John Quincy Adams](/books/john-quincy-adams) biography (by [James Traub](/notes/james-traub)) primarily as a co-architect of JQA's education. Kyle's notes flag that John and Abigail believed "[education](/notes/education) taught without morality was pointless" — the goal of education was "to produce both goodness and usefulness" — and that the classical curriculum they imposed on their son (classics, Scripture, ancient history as a *lens*) shaped his entire public philosophy. The recurring channel for this in the notes is John's 1775 letter to Abigail urging that it should be "your care, therefore, and mine to elevate the minds of our children... to excite in them a habitual contempt of meanness, abhorrence of injustice and inhumanity, and an ambition to excel in every capacity, faculty, and virtue" — Kyle tags this thread #[Parenting](/notes/parenting). She also surfaces as a touchstone in John's later correspondence comparing JQA's early productivity to his own.

**Context:** Abigail Adams (1744–1818) was the wife of the second U.S. president John Adams and mother of the sixth, John Quincy Adams; her extensive letters are a major primary source for the era and are remembered for her advocacy of women's education and her "remember the ladies" appeal.
