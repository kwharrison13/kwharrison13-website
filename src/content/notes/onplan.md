# OnPlan

**One-line definition:** *FP&A / financial-planning tool ([Dave Greenbaum](/notes/dave-greenbaum), founder) that sits on top of Google Sheets and BigQuery, aiming to be the "app store for financial planning."*

## How sources describe it

- Thesis: there's no "app store for spreadsheets" — GitHub exists for open-source engineering but nothing equivalent in finance, and entry products like [Adaptive Planning](/notes/adaptive-planning) are a big, expensive lift. OnPlan tries to offer a tool earlier in a company's life while preserving what people love about spreadsheets (formula, syntax, architecture) and filling the holes around integrations and data visualization. (September 26th, 2022 — Dave Greenbaum (OnPlan))
- Product: sits on top of Google Sheets with BigQuery as the dimensional database; pipes data from BigQuery into Sheets and daisy-chains models (cash flow, balance sheet, CapEx, revenue) like microservices. "You're not in a spreadsheet, you're in OnPlan as a builder." (September 26th, 2022 — Dave Greenbaum (OnPlan))
- App store: about to roll out the first app store for financial planning — industry-specific templates that get customers ~80% of the way there for zero-day value; content produced in-house first, later opened to the community. (September 26th, 2022 — Dave Greenbaum (OnPlan))
- Customers: mid-market (30–500 employees), ~60% SaaS. (September 26th, 2022 — Dave Greenbaum (OnPlan))
- Traction (Sept 2022): 100 customers; new ACVs ~$15K; $8–10K implementation. (September 26th, 2022 — Dave Greenbaum (OnPlan))
- Fundraising: raised from [Redpoint](/notes/redpoint) the prior year (spreadsheet layer only, no dimensional database yet); doing a $1.5M inside round to prove out onboarding, then planning a $10–20M round off sandbox metrics. (September 26th, 2022 — Dave Greenbaum (OnPlan))
