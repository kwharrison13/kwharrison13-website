# 3 Nephi 27

The chapter where, in Kyle's reading, the resurrected Christ "defines His own gospel" — the source text two of his most-built-out topical studies are organized around. Christ Defines His Gospel centers on [3 Nephi 27](/notes/3-nephi-27):13–21, drawing from it the distinction between saving grace (what Christ did *for* us) and enabling grace (what He allows us *to do*), and the "pendulum of grace" between pride and hopelessness. 8 Points of the Gospel extracts from the same chapter the eight elements of the gospel of Christ — Atonement, Resurrection, Judgment, Faith, Repentance, Baptism, Gift of the Holy Ghost, and Endure to the End.

The Come Follow Me study October 19–25, 2020. 3 Nephi 27–4 Nephi: "There Could Not Be a Happier People" treats verses 1–9 as Christ naming His church and defining what makes it *His* — "if it be in my name the Father will hear you." Obedience — Topical Study and Faith — Topical Study both lean on verse 27 ("what manner of men ought ye to be? Verily… even as I am") as the call to become emulatively like Christ, with [Neal A. Maxwell](/notes/neal-a-maxwell) cited on consecration carving out "the emulative character." 3 Nephi 27 thus serves Kyle as the canonical statement both of *what* the gospel is and of *whose* church it is.
