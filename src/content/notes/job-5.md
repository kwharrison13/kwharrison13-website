# Job 5

A chapter from the Old Testament book of Job. In Kyle's Helaman 13–16 study, Job 5:17 — "Behold, happy is the man whom God correcteth: therefore despise not thou the chastening of the Almighty" — appears in a scripture chain supporting the theme that **God [Chasten](/notes/chasten)s because He loves**. It is grouped with [Proverbs 3](/notes/proverbs-3):11, [Proverbs 19](/notes/proverbs-19):18, and [Revelation 3](/notes/revelation-3):19 (tagged #[Spirit of Humility](/notes/spirit-of-humility)) as cross-references echoing Helaman 15:3's teaching that the Lord chastens the people he loves.

**Context:** Job 5 is part of the first speech of Eliphaz the Temanite, counseling Job that affliction can be God's correction and that those reproved by God are ultimately blessed ("happy is the man whom God correcteth").
