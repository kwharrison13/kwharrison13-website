# Philippians 2

Philippians 2 is the chapter of Paul's epistle whose verse 12 — "work out your own salvation with fear and trembling" — anchors a recurring theme in Kyle's [gospel-study](/notes/gospel-study) notes: that salvation requires the individual's own agency and effort, not passive reliance. In Christ Defines His Gospel it sits in a scripture chain ([2 Nephi 31](/notes/2-nephi-31), [Helaman 10](/notes/helaman-10), [1 Thessalonians 5](/notes/1-thessalonians-5)) about following Christ by keeping His commandments and seeking His will over one's own. In Virtue, the same verse frames the argument that "you alone are the only one who may righteously demand something of yourself" — that bringing the [Natural Man](/notes/natural-man)'s appetites under subjection is the believer's own work, tied to [Lawrence E. Corbridge](/notes/lawrence-e-corbridge)'s teaching that the will is the one thing a person gives the Lord that the Lord has not already granted.

## Where this appears

- Christ Defines His Gospel — verse 12 ("work out your own salvation") in a chain on following Christ through obedience and submitting to His will.
- Virtue — verse 12 grounds the claim that only the self can righteously demand mastery over the natural man.
