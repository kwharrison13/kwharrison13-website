# Luke 15

Luke 15 appears in Kyle's [Come, Follow Me](/notes/come-follow-me) study on 3 Nephi 1–7, cited as the parable of the prodigal son and specifically Luke 15:17 — "and when he came to himself." In the note it provides the redemptive contrast to the wicked: where [the rising generation](/essays/the-rising-generation) "became for themselves" out of selfishness and [Pride](/notes/pride) ([3 Nephi 1](/notes/3-nephi-1):29), the prodigal son "came to himself," reflecting and turning inward toward repentance. It is paired with [Psalm 119](/notes/psalm-119):59 as a model of self-examination.

**Context:** Luke 15 is the New Testament chapter containing Jesus' three parables of lost-and-found — the lost sheep, the lost coin, and the prodigal son; verse 17 marks the prodigal's turning point of self-realization.
