# Max Gasner

Max Gasner is a co-founder (alongside [Zac Townsend](/notes/zac-townsend)) of [Standard.re](/notes/standard-re), an on-chain life-insurance startup pursuing a Bermuda-first regulatory path to issue crypto-denominated insurance products. He leads the build-out of the company's backend infrastructure and customer-facing product. Kyle's only logged contact is the June 6th, 2022 — Max Gasner & Zac Townsend (Standard.re) fundraise call, held just after "Luna Wednesday" — the day the Luna collapse blew up the market and Dragonfly pulled a signed term sheet, stalling the round.

On that call, Gasner and Townsend framed the capital as optional ("the capital isn't something we need; we have 2.5 years of runway either way"), emphasizing instead an 18-month regulatory and product roadmap: license in Bermuda (expected by end of summer 2022), then Singapore, an Ethereum company in Bermuda, the UAE, and a single-state US license — having retained the former governor of Wyoming to navigate state legislators. The product roadmap ran from offshore whole life ("estate tax coverage for whales") and variable universal life to onshore indexed universal life and annuities, launching first for ETH. They had built a ~10-person team drawn from Slack, [Ramp](/notes/ramp), Airbnb, Corelight, and the life-insurance industry, had just hired engineer [Glenn Ren](/notes/glenn-ren) (ex-[Ramp](/notes/ramp)), and still needed Solidity talent. They were targeting a ~$5M round on a $100M post and talking to strategics (Coinbase, Alchemy, DCG) and reinsurer venture arms.
