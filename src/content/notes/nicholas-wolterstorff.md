# Nicholas Wolterstorff

In [The Crucible of Doubt](/books/the-crucible-of-doubt), the philosopher Nicholas Wolterstorff grounds the book's "empathy leads to suffering" takeaway with the line: "We all suffer. For we all prize and love; and in this present existence of ours, prizing and loving yield suffering. Love in our world is suffering." The wiki page reads this as a moral-psychology frame that pushes back against the Stoic strategy of detachment and aligns with the Adlerian "be of use" thread from [The Courage To Be Disliked](/books/the-courage-to-be-disliked). The quote also anchors the cross-link to [Empathy](/notes/empathy) — empathy as the road to suffering, because to love is to be exposed to loss.

**Context:** Nicholas Wolterstorff is an American philosopher and emeritus professor at Yale; the quoted lines come from *Lament for a Son*, his reflection written after the death of his son.
