# Wallace Toronto

Wallace Toronto is the central figure of the signature doorstep story in [Feed My Sheep](/notes/feed-my-sheep), Elder Jeffrey R. Holland's MTC address to departing missionaries (the transcript also spells the name "Wallas Toronto"). As Holland tells it, around 1928 Toronto — a 21-year-old missionary proselyting in Czechoslovakia between the world wars, with only halting, broken Czech — knocked on the door of a grieving mother who had been told by her minister that her unbaptized dead child was in hell, and who had vowed never to speak to a minister again. Refusing to leave, Toronto asked, "Would you like us to tell you where your daughter is?" and on the doorstep read her from the 8th chapter of Moroni — Mormon's letter on infant baptism and the grace of Christ. The woman later said she felt the Spirit of the Lord for the first time in her life. Holland uses the episode to make his pedagogical point: no set of memorized, sequenced discussions could have scripted that moment — "that's why it's got to be in the hearts of the missionaries."

**Context:** Wallace F. Toronto (1907–1968) was a Latter-day Saint missionary and later the long-serving president of the Czechoslovak Mission, which he led across the disruptions of World War II and the postwar Communist takeover.
