# Michael Guirguis

Co-founder and CEO of [Seso](/notes/seso), the agricultural-labor startup evaluated at a December 2021 Series A investment committee. The pre-brief profiles Guirguis as a former Stanford, McKinsey, and Yup.com operator. The IC framed him as mission-driven — having "made all of his career decisions to date based on this filter" — and noted his motivation for the farm-labor space appeared genuine: he worked on labor laws while interning at the White House, has relatives who work in the space, and visited dozens of farms before launching Seso, earning the trust of farmers.

The diligence flagged one open question for the in-person meeting: why Guirguis stayed at Yup.com (which never scaled past Series A) for four years. He co-founded Seso with [Jordan Taylor](/notes/jordan-taylor) (Head of Product) and [William Dembinski](/notes/william-dembinski) (Head of Engineering), and the firm's biggest ask of the IC was to evaluate whether the founders were "the ones worth taking a bet on in this very hard market."
