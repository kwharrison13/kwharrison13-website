# Alma 27

A chapter in the Book of Mormon's Anti-Nephi-Lehi narrative, studied in Kyle's June 29–July 5, 2020. Alma 23–29: "They Never Did Fall Away" Come Follow Me notes. Kyle draws several threads from it. On [Anger](/notes/anger): the Amalekites, unable to take revenge on the Nephites, "stir up the people in anger against their brethren" (Alma 27:2) — Kyle's read is that when your anger is decoupled from what you're actually upset about, you're looking for an excuse to be angry. On trusting revelation: [Ammon](/notes/ammon) says "I will go and inquire of the Lord, and if he say unto us, go… will ye go?" (Alma 27:7–10), which Kyle frames as confidence in God expressed through trusting received revelation. On [Happiness](/notes/happiness): Alma 27:18 ("this is joy which none receiveth save it be the truly penitent and humble seeker of happiness") becomes Kyle's text for the joy "pure seekers" take in finding friends on the same path to righteousness — paired with [Dieter F. Uchtdorf](/notes/dieter-f-uchtdorf)'s line that friends are in our homes.

In the Patience — Topical Study, Alma 27:11–12 is cited as one of the reasons an answer to prayer may be withheld — "you have not yet asked the right question."

**Context:** Alma 27 is a chapter in the Book of Alma in the Book of Mormon, recounting Ammon leading the converted Anti-Nephi-Lehies to safety among the Nephites in the land of Jershon.
