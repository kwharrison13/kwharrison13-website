# M&A

M&A (mergers and acquisitions) enters Kyle's wiki as a topic he flagged for study rather than one he has synthesized — captured in his Roam "Quick Capture" reading logs (June 29th, 2021 from Index Ventures; March 30th, 2022 from Contrary Capital sourcing/diligence work) and filed into his [Playbooks](/notes/playbooks) reading cluster as [Playbook — M&A](/notes/playbook-manda). The links he saved center on the Harvard Business Review "new M&A playbook" framing — how to think systematically about when and how to acquire — alongside an M&A book reference. It sits beside his other operating playbooks (Finance, Growth, Sales, Operations, Product), reflecting Kyle's habit of building topic-level "playbook" stubs from saved reading.

**Context:** M&A — mergers and acquisitions — is the umbrella term for transactions that combine or transfer ownership of companies, ranging from strategic acquisitions and roll-ups to mergers of equals. In corporate strategy it is a primary lever for inorganic growth, consolidation, and capital allocation.
