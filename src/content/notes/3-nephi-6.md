# 3 Nephi 6

A chapter in the Book of Mormon, covered as part of Kyle's Come Follow Me study of 3 Nephi 1–7. In that week's notes it appears in the chapter roster for the block but draws no separate verse-level commentary — Kyle's substantive notes for the week stay anchored in [3 Nephi 1](/notes/3-nephi-1) and its themes of [Christlike Sorrow](/notes/christlike-sorrow), [Pride](/notes/pride), and the [rising generation](/essays/the-rising-generation).

**Context:** 3 Nephi 6 describes the rapid moral collapse of the Nephites after a period of peace and prosperity — class division ("distinguished by ranks"), the rise of [pride](/notes/pride), and the secret combinations of lawyers and judges who conspire against the prophets, setting up the breakdown of government that follows.
