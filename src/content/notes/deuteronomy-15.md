# Deuteronomy 15

An Old Testament chapter Kyle cites in his September 5, 2021 study of Fasting. He pulls [Deuteronomy 15](/notes/deuteronomy-15):11 — "For the poor shall never cease out of the land: therefore I command thee... Thou shalt open thine hand wide unto thy brother, to thy poor, and to thy needy" — as a scriptural anchor for the claim that caring for the poor and needy is "a fundamental gospel doctrine and an essential element in the eternal plan of salvation." In context it sits alongside [Isaiah 58](/notes/isaiah-58) and [Malachi 3](/notes/malachi-3) as support for the law of the fast and fast offerings as the Lord's mechanism for providing for the poor.

**Context:** Deuteronomy 15 is the Mosaic law of the sabbatical year — the cancellation of debts every seventh year, the release of bondservants, and the command to give generously to the poor "for the poor shall never cease out of the land."
