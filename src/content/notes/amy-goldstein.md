# Amy Goldstein

Author of [Janesville](/books/janesville), the book in Kyle's library chronicling a Wisconsin town after its [GM](/notes/general-motors) plant closure. Her ProPublica-rooted reporting documents the failed retraining experiment around Janesville: laid-off workers who went back to school at Blackhawk fared worse on employment and pay than those who didn't, despite the bipartisan "[Retraining](/notes/retraining) gospel" embraced by Paul Ryan and Obama alike. The book also tracks the town's [City Building](/notes/city-building) attempts to reinvent its economy (Forward Janesville, Rock County 5.0) and the middle-class GM families tumbling out of the middle class as $28/hour wages disappeared.

**Context:** Amy Goldstein is a Pulitzer Prize-winning staff writer at The Washington Post; *Janesville: An American Story* won the Financial Times/McKinsey Business Book of the Year.
