# Cloud Adoption

Cloud adoption is the secular shift Kyle treats as the "why now" beneath several investing theses. In [Todd McKinnon — Creating and Defining a New Market Category](/notes/todd-mckinnon-creating-and-defining-a-new-market-category), it's the whole-stack thesis that drove McKinnon to leave Salesforce in 2008: business apps (Salesforce), collaboration apps (Google Apps), and infrastructure ([AWS](/notes/aws), launched ~2006) were all migrating, so "the whole stack was going to move to the cloud." That conviction made identity the inevitable next problem — and McKinnon's lesson is that timing matters, since Okta sold the *later-adopted* security/identity layer of cloud and had to wait until ~2012 for the market to catch up. As he put it, "there's no more fertile environment to start new companies than when there's a ton of transition."

In the [Starburst](/notes/starburst) diligence, cloud adoption appears with the opposite valence — "at least partly, an obstacle." Starburst's federated-query thesis thrives in a #hybrid-cloud / #multi-cloud world where data stays spread across on-premise data lakes and cloud data warehouses, so a clean, total migration to a single cloud would erode part of its rationale. The note's counter-framing is that the move to cloud is precisely what made the Data Lake (S3) the primary store for analytical data, creating the silo-spanning problem Starburst exists to solve.

**Context:** "Cloud adoption" refers to enterprises migrating computing, storage, applications, and data from on-premise infrastructure to cloud platforms (AWS, Azure, Google Cloud) and SaaS. It accelerated through the 2010s and reshaped enterprise software economics — shifting costs from capital expenditure to subscription/usage and enabling new categories built natively on cloud infrastructure.

## Where this appears

- [Todd McKinnon — Creating and Defining a New Market Category](/notes/todd-mckinnon-creating-and-defining-a-new-market-category) — the whole-stack cloud-migration thesis (apps → collaboration → infrastructure) that birthed Okta, plus the timing lesson on selling cloud's later-adopted identity layer.
- [Starburst](/notes/starburst) (diligence) — cloud adoption as a partial *obstacle* to a multi-cloud/hybrid-cloud federated-query business, offset by the data-lake-as-primary-store dynamic the cloud created.
