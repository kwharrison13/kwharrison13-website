# Migration

The broader concept of population movement across geographies, tied in Kyle's notes to economic opportunity and cost of living. Its main grounding is the [California Diaspora](/notes/california-diaspora) page, which frames the outflow from California as one instance of a "larger diaspora from high-COL to low-COL places" — people leaving expensive metros for cheaper ones. That page surfaces a notable side effect: blue-state residents moving to red states turn them into swing states, with the asymmetry driven by highly-educated people moving *into* California while younger, lower-income people leave. Migration sits alongside [City Building](/notes/city-building) in this cluster, since housing costs and city affordability are treated as central drivers of where people move.

**Context:** Migration is the movement of people from one place to another, whether across borders (immigration/emigration) or within a country (internal migration); economists commonly model it as a response to differences in wages, housing costs, and opportunity between regions.

## Where this appears

- [California Diaspora](/notes/california-diaspora) — the outflow from California as one case of broader high-cost-to-low-cost migration, with downstream effects on state politics.
