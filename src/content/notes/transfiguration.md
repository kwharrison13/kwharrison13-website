# Transfiguration

In Kyle's Come Follow Me study of 3 Nephi 27–4 Nephi, transfiguration is one of the core themes drawn from the account of the [The Three Nephites](/notes/the-three-nephites). The anchor verse is 3 Nephi 28:15: "And whether they were in the body or out of the body, they could not tell; for it did seem unto them like a transfiguration of them, that they were changed from this body of flesh into an immortal state, that they could behold the things of God." The note groups transfiguration alongside related themes from the same chapters — the Godhead, the fulness of joy, matter, and Zion — treating the bodily change the Three Nephites undergo as a scriptural touchpoint for how flesh can be altered to perceive and endure the things of God.

**Context:** In Latter-day Saint and broader Christian usage, transfiguration refers to a temporary change in a mortal's physical state that allows them to behold or withstand the presence of God — distinct from resurrection or translation, though related to both. The Book of Mormon applies the term to the Three Nephites' altered state.
