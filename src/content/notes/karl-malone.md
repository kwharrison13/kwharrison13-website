# Karl Malone

Malone appears in [Driven](/books/driven), the autobiography of [Larry H. Miller](/notes/larry-h-miller) (owner of the Utah Jazz), as the voice of an extended reflection on saying what matters before it is too late. In the book's telling, Malone recounts wrestling for a year or two with the impulse to tell teammate Larry Bird how he felt — "In life, we always wait until it's too late to apologize or tell someone how we feel… What if something happened to Larry, and I hadn't said what I needed to say?" He also supplies one of the book's affectionate first-impression vignettes of Miller's unpretentious style: "I was looking for someone in a double-breasted suit and tie, and out walked this guy in a polo shirt, jeans, and sneakers… we went to Wendy's. I thought, I'm going to like this guy."

**Context:** Karl Malone is a Hall of Fame NBA power forward who spent most of his career (1985–2003) with the Utah Jazz — the franchise Larry H. Miller owned — forming the celebrated pick-and-roll duo with John Stockton.
