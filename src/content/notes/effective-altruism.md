# Effective Altruism

Effective Altruism (EA) — the "do good effectively" movement — appears in Kyle's notes in two registers. In It's Time To Build... But Build What - Research it is the counterpole to [Effective Accelerationism](/notes/effective-accelerationism) in the EA-vs-e/acc dichotomy that structures the essay. Kyle files [Molly White](/notes/molly-white)'s "Effective Obfuscation" as the critical read: both movements offer "an easy way to brand themselves as a deep-thinking do-gooder," and EA's flexible logic has "justified other not-so-altruistic things" — White cites ideological originator William MacAskill urging people *not* to boycott sweatshops, and notes that, taken to the extreme, the framework "could feasibly justify committing massive fraud… to obtain billions that they could, maybe someday, donate to worthy causes." Her sharper claim is that, like e/acc, *"effective altruism can be used to justify nearly any course of action an adherent wants to take."*

In the second register, EA supplies the *evidence base* Kyle finds genuinely persuasive, via [Utopia For Realists](/books/utopia-for-realists). Rutger Bregman leans on [Esther Duflo](/notes/esther-duflo)'s Hyderabad RCT, which showed microcredit's heralded anti-poverty effects don't hold while [unconditional cash transfers](/notes/cash-transfers) work better — *"cash handouts may be the most extensively studied anti-poverty method around."* The book pairs this with [Open Borders](/notes/open-borders) as the highest-leverage anti-poverty move (full labor mobility estimated to raise gross world product 67-147%). So Kyle's notes hold EA at arm's length as a justifying *ideology* while crediting the rigorous-giving / RCT *methodology* it popularized.

**Context:** Effective Altruism is a philosophical and social movement (associated with figures like Peter Singer and William MacAskill, and organizations like GiveWell) that applies evidence and reason to maximize the good done per dollar — historically championing cash transfers and global-health interventions, later expanding into longtermism and AI safety, and bruised reputationally by the FTX/Sam Bankman-Fried collapse.
