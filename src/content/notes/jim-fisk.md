# Jim Fisk

One of the six "select and representative few" whose lives [The Idea Factory](/books/the-idea-factory) uses to tell the story of [Bell Labs](/notes/bell-labs) — alongside [Mervin Kelly](/notes/mervin-kelly), [William Shockley](/notes/william-shockley), [Claude Shannon](/notes/claude-shannon), John Pierce, and William Baker. A physicist hired by Kelly toward the end of the Great Depression, Fisk arrived with the cohort the Labs' colleagues called the "Young Turks." The book casts him as the natural-manager foil to Shockley: whatever Shockley found impossible about leading people — "relaxing a roomful of scientists with some inspired slapstick" or "giving men freedom to do their work as they chose" — his friend Jim Fisk "found easy and natural." Fisk also embodies the institution's long technological bets; the Labs' leadership (Jewett, Kelly, Fisk, Pierce, Baker) chose to invest heavily and consistently in basic research and "the unknown" when they were not forced to.

**Context:** James Brown Fisk (1910–1981) was a physicist and longtime executive at Bell Telephone Laboratories, serving as its president from 1959 to 1973.
