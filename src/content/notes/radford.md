# Radford

Radford is the legacy compensation-benchmarking survey provider that Kyle's Pave Diligence — 2021 treats as the primary incumbent Pave was built to disrupt — Pave's internal framing was literally building "Radford in the cloud," a real-time give-to-get data network replacing Radford's annual spreadsheet surveys. The dossier records Radford as owned by [AON](/notes/aon) (a ~60K-employee financial-services firm), estimated at roughly $50M of annual revenue, with ~172 FTE and 1,656 participating companies in its technology compensation survey (Adobe, Amazon, Apple, Facebook, Microsoft, Nvidia, Tesla, Zoom, etc.). Pricing was modeled at ~$34K in the US, +$15K per additional country, +$5K per extra data cut — roughly $70K on average for a survey a company would buy and "still need to hire consultants to use."

Customer references repeatedly cast Radford as the high-friction, high-credibility status quo. The [Allbirds](/notes/allbirds) Total Rewards lead described it as "the legacy one, not super user-friendly or high velocity, particularly for startups," while [Mark Seidel](/notes/mark-seidel) at Relativity Space called it "very legacy" — a benchmarking and job-description consulting service used ad hoc (typically annually) rather than continuously. Seidel noted Radford-type firms have "done well even though it's effectively a consulting service," which is exactly the inefficiency Pave's diligence argued was vulnerable: Pave's pitch was that real-time, integrated, API-driven data could undercut a $70K annual survey gated behind manual spreadsheet upload.

**Context:** Radford is a compensation-survey and rewards-consulting business, part of Aon's human-capital practice, long considered an industry-standard source for technology and life-sciences pay benchmarking data.
