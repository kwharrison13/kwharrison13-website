# Temptation

In The Lehonti Principle, temptation is read through the Book of Mormon story of Amalickiah and Lehonti (Alma 47): Lehonti is "you" and Amalickiah is [Satan](/notes/satan). The core insight is that "resolution and desire are the first steps to success in overcoming the temptations of Satan" — but that refusing Satan does not end the matter, because he "will NEVER give up." Amalickiah's invitation to "come down" was sent again and again not because it grew more attractive, but because "the constant reminder of a temptation will keep it in your mind." As long as you are thinking about the sin — even thinking about avoiding it — you are still thinking of it.

The study escalates the threat: when Satan cannot move you to the line, he brings the line to you (Amalickiah went up "nearly to Lehonti's camp") and "administered poison by degrees." Satan's aim is not to ride shotgun but to drive, leading "by the neck with a flaxen cord" (2 Nephi 26:22). Against this, the page sets Moroni 7:16–17 as a tool to judge every invitation, and the choice of 2 Nephi 2:27 — yielding brings "captivity and death," obedience "liberty and eternal life." The named counter-themes are [Agency](/notes/agency) and [Self-Mastery](/notes/self-mastery), with protection coming by turning toward the Savior ([Henry B. Eyring](/notes/henry-b-eyring)).

## Where this appears

- The Lehonti Principle — listed as a core theme; the whole study is an allegory of how Satan administers temptation "by degrees" and how resolution, scripture, and turning to Christ overcome it.
