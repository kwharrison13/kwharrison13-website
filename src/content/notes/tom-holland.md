# Tom Holland

Historian quoted in [Why You're Christian](/notes/why-you-re-christian) for his framing of [Christianity](/notes/christianity) as the "most enduring and influential legacy of the ancient world," its emergence "the single most transformative development in Western history."

**Context:** Tom Holland (b. 1968) is a British historian and author known for popular histories of antiquity (*Rubicon*, *Dynasty*) and, most relevantly here, *Dominion: How the Christian Revolution Remade the World*, which argues that Western secular values are themselves downstream of Christian moral assumptions. (Not the actor of the same name.)

## Where this appears

- [Why You're Christian](/notes/why-you-re-christian) — supplies the "most transformative development in Western history" framing of Christianity's legacy that anchors the essay's argument.
