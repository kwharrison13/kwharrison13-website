# John 1

The first chapter of the Gospel of John, cited across Kyle's gospel-study notes for a handful of recurring passages. In the 2019 New Testament opening week, it anchors the theme of personal responsibility for learning through Christ's question "What seek ye?" ([John 1](/notes/john-1):38) — "what you find in the scriptures depends on what you seek," paired with "Seek, and ye shall find."

In The Godhead, John 1's opening — "In the beginning was the Word, and the Word was God… and the Word was made flesh" — is flagged as "a chief source of Christian confusion" about the nature of God, and the chapter also supplies the claim that Christ as Creator "can command the elements" ([John 1](/notes/john-1):3). In Original 12 Apostles, John 1:47 names Nathanael "an Israelite with no guile." In Christ Defines His Gospel, John 1:17 appears among the cross-references on grace and truth coming through Jesus Christ. The chapter also surfaces in Abrahamic Covenant (John 1:29–34 on baptism and the laying on of hands).

**Context:** John 1 is the prologue to the Gospel of John, opening with the "Word" (Logos) hymn that identifies Christ as the divine Word made flesh, then narrating John the Baptist's testimony and the calling of the first disciples.
