# 1 Nephi 18

1 Nephi 18 — Nephi building the ship and the family's voyage to the promised land — appears across several of Kyle's Book of Mormon study notes, mined for a few distinct lessons. In July 6–12, 2020. Alma 30–31: "The Virtue of the Word of God" he highlights 18:1: the Lord "did show me from time to time after what manner I should work the timbers of the ship," used as an example that revelation and capability come incrementally — Nephi was not shown how to build a ship all at once, just as he tried several approaches before obtaining the brass plates.

In August 24–30, 2020. Helaman 7–12: "Remember the Lord", 18:10 surfaces Nephi's fear that the Lord would smite the company "that we should be swallowed up in the depths of the sea," set against Laman and Lemuel's refusal to have their "younger brother... be a ruler over us." The chapter is also part of the broader study of 1 Nephi 16–22 — I Will Prepare the Way before You, where Kyle threads the contrast between Nephi's faithful initiative — including his being bound with cords on the ship — and Laman and Lemuel's murmuring.

**Context:** In 1 Nephi 18, the prophet Nephi constructs a ship under the Lord's instruction and the family sails to the promised land; during the voyage Laman and Lemuel bind Nephi, a storm threatens the ship, and they relent only when in danger of drowning.
