# Descartes

Descartes is invoked in [The Notetaking Cold War](/notes/the-notetaking-cold-war) as an example of an "essentialist" philosopher — grouped with [Plato](/notes/plato) and [Immanuel Kant](/notes/immanuel-kant) as thinkers who hold that things have an underlying essence, even though their philosophical stances differ widely.

**Context:** René Descartes (1596–1650) was a French philosopher and mathematician, the father of modern rationalist philosophy, known for "Cogito, ergo sum" ("I think, therefore I am").

## Where this appears

- [The Notetaking Cold War](/notes/the-notetaking-cold-war) — listed among essentialist philosophers alongside Plato and Kant.
