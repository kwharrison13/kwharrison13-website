# Helion

An Everett, Washington-based nuclear fusion startup, captured in Kyle's November 2021 sourcing brief as the subject of the largest-ever VC investment into nuclear fusion. Per the notes, Helion raised **$500M in Series E funding at a $3B post-money valuation**, led by former Y Combinator president [Sam Altman](/notes/sam-altman) — Altman's largest-ever personal investment — with up to a further $1.7B in milestone-based commitments. Other investors named include [Dustin Moskovitz](/notes/dustin-moskovitz), [Mithril Capital](/notes/mithril-capital), and Capricorn Investment Group.

Kyle's framing positions fusion as *"the holy grail of clean power tech"* — a technology that could eliminate both supply constraints and carbon emissions at once — placing Helion in the [Nuclear Energy](/notes/nuclear-energy) / climate-tech thesis cluster alongside his other late-2021 energy-transition sourcing work.

**Context:** Helion Energy pursues fusion via a pulsed, non-ignition magneto-inertial approach and direct electricity capture; in 2023 it signed a power-purchase agreement to supply Microsoft with fusion electricity, targeting a first commercial plant later in the decade.
