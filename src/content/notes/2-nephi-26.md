# 2 Nephi 26

A Book of Mormon chapter that recurs across [Kyle Harrison](/notes/kyle-harrison)'s gospel study above all through **verse 22** — "he leadeth them by the neck with a flaxen cord, until he bindeth them with his strong cords forever" — Kyle's favorite image of how Satan operates. It appears in his Diligence study (the flaxen cord pacifies souls into carnal security, "all is well in Zion," until the [Natural Man](/notes/natural-man) leads them down to hell) and is the doctrinal backbone of The Lehonti Principle, where the gentle, gradual pull of the flaxen cord illustrates that "Satan doesn't want to ride shotgun — he wants to drive." The same verse surfaces in [Kyle Harrison](/notes/kyle-harrison)'s annotated [Charlie Munger — Harvard School Commencement Speech](/notes/charlie-munger-harvard-school-commencement-speech), cross-referenced against Munger's warnings about the slow grip of bad incentives and habits.

The chapter's other half supplies a counterweight: Kyle cites **26:30** in Charity & Love (charity as the bond, "the Lord hath commanded that men should not murder... but that men should love one another") and notes **26:33** in Miscellaneous Scriptures ("all are alike unto God," black and white, bond and free, male and female).

**Context:** 2 Nephi 26 is a chapter of Nephi's prophecy and exhortation near the end of his record, foretelling Christ's ministry and the destruction of the Nephites, warning against secret combinations and the subtle ways the devil leads people to spiritual ruin, and declaring that God invites all to come unto Him and denies none — "all are alike unto God."

## Where this appears

- The Lehonti Principle — 26:22 (the flaxen cord) as the central illustration that Satan's pull is gradual and relentless, not sudden.
- Diligence — 26:22 on Satan pacifying souls into carnal security and complacency.
- [Charlie Munger — Harvard School Commencement Speech](/notes/charlie-munger-harvard-school-commencement-speech) — 26:22 annotated alongside Munger on the slow grip of bad habits/incentives.
- Charity & Love — 26:30 on the commandment to love one another.
- Miscellaneous Scriptures — 26:33, "all are alike unto God."
