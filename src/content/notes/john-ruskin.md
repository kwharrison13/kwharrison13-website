# John Ruskin

In [Gordon B. Hinckley — Go Forward With Faith](/books/gordon-b-hinckley-go-forward-with-faith), John Ruskin is cited for a single quotation on the value of work: "The greatest reward is not what we receive for our labor, but what we become by it." The line sits among the book's gathering of writers and thinkers — [Ralph Waldo Emerson](/notes/ralph-waldo-emerson), Robert Browning, [Charlie Munger](/notes/charlie-munger) — whom Hinckley (or biographer [Sheri L. Dew](/notes/sheri-l-dew)) draws on to frame a life of diligent effort and self-improvement.

**Context:** John Ruskin (1819–1900) was an influential English art critic, social thinker, and writer of the Victorian era, known for works such as *Modern Painters* and *Unto This Last* and for tying aesthetics to morality and the dignity of labor.
