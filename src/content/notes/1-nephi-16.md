# 1 Nephi 16

1 Nephi 16 is the Book of Mormon chapter covering the Lehite company's journey through the wilderness — the appearance of the [Liahona](/notes/liahona), Nephi's broken bow, the death of Ishmael at [Nahom](/notes/nahom), and the murmuring of Laman and Lemuel. In Kyle's Come Follow Me study it is the chapter he worked most deeply, and several recurring themes anchor to it. Its opening verses are the locus classicus for [The Wicked Take the Truth to be Hard](/notes/the-wicked-take-the-truth-to-be-hard) — "the guilty taketh the truth to be hard, for it cutteth them to the very center" (16:2) — a thread Kyle returns to repeatedly across his other studies. The chapter also grounds the principle of being [Anxiously Engaged](/notes/anxiously-engaged): when Nephi's bow loses its spring (a natural consequence), he builds a new one on his own initiative rather than waiting to be "compelled in all things," contrasted against Laman and Lemuel's murmuring. And verse 29's warning on the [Liahona](/notes/liahona) — that it worked "according to the faith and diligence and heed which we did give unto it" — is cited in the Scripture Study — Topical Study as the model of guidance dependent on faith and diligence.

The chapter recurs as a cross-reference well beyond its own week's study. Kyle's studies of Alma 13–16, Helaman 7–12, Alma 23–29, and the Brigham Young temporal-wealth study all reach back to 1 Nephi 16:1-2 for the "hard things / the guilty take the truth to be hard" framing — making this chapter one of the connective hubs of his scripture notes.

**Context:** 1 Nephi 16 is the sixteenth chapter of the First Book of Nephi in the Book of Mormon, recounting Lehi's family traveling through the Arabian wilderness guided by the Liahona, a brass compass-like ball that functioned by their faith and diligence.
