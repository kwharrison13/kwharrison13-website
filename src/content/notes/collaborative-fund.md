# Collaborative Fund

Mission-driven venture firm; [Taylor Greene](/notes/taylor-greene)'s firm at the time he led [Teamshares](/notes/teamshares)' Seed round. In Kyle's March 2020 reference call, Greene described leading that Seed round in October and introducing Teamshares' founders to all but one of their other investors (March 5th, 2020 — Taylor Greene (Collaborative Fund)). Greene framed his investing taste as oriented toward "mission driven entrepreneurs" and "weird B2B platform plays in markets that are under-appreciated" — a profile consistent with Collaborative Fund's positioning — and was an enthusiastic backer of Teamshares' employee-ownership ("participatory capitalism") thesis.

The call is significant in Kyle's notes less as company diligence than as a career-decision conversation: Greene argued the real risk in Teamshares was the fundraise (Kyle's own role), that the model was "storytelling not rocket science," and that Kyle's genuine alignment with the business would be an edge over a typical "placement agent banker."

**Context:** Collaborative Fund is a New York–based venture capital firm founded by Craig Shapiro, known for backing mission-driven and consumer companies and for partner Morgan Housel's writing on money and behavior.
