# Intellectual Property

**One-line:** *In the film context (IP), a movie whose origin story was a popular thing from another medium — and the franchise/reboot strategy that turns owning a property into the dominant Hollywood business model.*

## How sources describe it

- The "IP Era": "It's not about creating something, it's about owning something, forever." ([Paste Magazine](https://www.pastemagazine.com/movies/intellectual-property/ip-era-franchises-venture-capital-filmmaking))
- Counter-history: "Hollywood Has Always Banked on I.P." — traceable to 1930's *Tom Sawyer* ([Mark Twain](/notes/mark-twain)); since 2014 every year has been dominated by franchises and sequels.
- The pro-IP case ([The Ankler](https://theankler.com/p/ip-hollywood-box-office-streaming-theaters)): "IP isn't the problem. It's the solution for Hollywood" — because it makes money.
- In [The Hits Business](/essays/the-hits-business), IP exploitation is not condemned (Kyle "loved" *Deadpool & Wolverine*); the critique is reserved for when **the pursuit of big comes at the sacrifice of good.**
