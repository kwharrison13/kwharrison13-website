# Equal Partners

A theme Kyle tags in his [John Quincy Adams](/books/john-quincy-adams) book notes, applied to marriage as a partnership of intellectual and emotional equals. The triggering highlight describes how Louisa Catherine Adams "held a mirror to his foibles" — his excessive concern for dignity, his withdrawal into solitude and books — but how Adams, self-critical as he was, "could not accept even the loving, rallying form of advice… he could criticize her, but she could not criticize him." Kyle flags the passage under #[equal partners](/notes/equal-partners) (alongside [Marriage](/notes/marriage), [Self-Reflection](/notes/self-reflection), and "Be Observant") as a case study in the gap between an espoused ideal of partnership and the reality of a one-directional critique.

## Where this appears
