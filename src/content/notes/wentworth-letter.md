# Wentworth Letter

The source document of the thirteen [Articles of Faith](/notes/articles-of-faith), treated in Articles of Faith — Topical Study as a compact outline of the entire restored gospel. Kyle's note is that the Wentworth Letter "successfully outlined the entirety of our gospel, a fact that most forget because of their average appearance" — its plainness causes most readers to overlook how much doctrine it contains, so he urges searching it "for ANY doctrine." Read structurally, the thirteen articles drawn from the letter fall into five movements: the plan of salvation (1–3), church structure (4–6), the resources (7–9), the performance (10–12), and the goal (13). The source text is traced to [Joseph Smith History](/notes/joseph-smith-history).

**Context:** The Wentworth Letter is an 1842 letter written by Joseph Smith to newspaper editor John Wentworth, recounting the rise of the Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-day Saints and concluding with the statements of belief later canonized as the Articles of Faith.
