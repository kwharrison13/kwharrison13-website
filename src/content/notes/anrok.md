# Anrok

Anrok is an automated sales-tax compliance platform built for SaaS companies, founded and led by Michelle Valentine. It was an [Index Ventures](/notes/index-ventures) portfolio company in Kyle's deal flow: Index and [Sequoia](/notes/sequoia) co-led the seed round (at a ~$40m post, each taking ~5%) about a year before the November 2021 partner presentation. Kyle and [Mark Goldberg](/notes/mark-goldberg) then proposed Index lead a pre-emptive Series A — a $20m round ($15m Index check) at a $100m post, bringing ownership to ~19% (November 15th, 2021 — Anrok Partner Presentation).

The thesis: pandemic-era state tax laws (post *South Dakota v. Wayfair* economic nexus) made SaaS companies liable to collect and remit sales tax everywhere their customers sit, while the incumbent, Avalara (~$15B, founded ~2003), was built pre-SaaS and serves it poorly. Anrok began selling in summer 2021 and quickly reached 25+ customers, $250k+ ARR, and a ~60% close rate, with a team of 14 drawn from Stripe, Coinbase, Checkr, Airtable, and Dropbox. Kyle framed sales tax as a beachhead to become a company's financial source of truth, expanding into invoicing and billing. Risks he flagged: still early, low ACV (~$10K deals), and competition from Avalara and Stripe (which had acquired TaxJar). Anrok was first surfaced in a June 24th, 2021 — Hannah Seal (Index Ventures) call comparing it against Fenoa, and it also appears as a Pave reference customer in Pave Diligence — 2021.

**Context:** Anrok is a San Francisco-based sales-tax automation startup for software companies, founded in 2020 by Michelle Valentine and Kanthi Anand; it has since raised growth rounds from investors including Khosla Ventures and Index Ventures.
