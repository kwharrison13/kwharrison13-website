# Women and the Priesthood

A recurring question Kyle returns to across his LDS reading and scripture study. Reading [Joseph Smith — Rough Stone Rolling](/books/joseph-smith-rough-stone-rolling), he flags the tension directly in a margin note: "Then why do we excommunicate people who think women should get the Priesthood?" — the question prompted by the book's account of Joseph Smith's developing teachings on priesthood and women.

In his Alma 13–16 study, the theme threads through a meditation on what the priesthood actually is. Kyle quotes [Zina Peterson](/notes/zina-peterson) — "I don't understand the Priesthood well enough to say exactly what it is and isn't. But when people say 'we'd like to thank the priesthood for setting up the chairs'; I'm pretty sure it wasn't the priesthood that set up the chairs" — as a needle against conflating priesthood with the men who hold it. He sets that beside [Spencer W. Kimball](/notes/spencer-w-kimball)'s teaching that "faithful women were given certain assignments while faithful men were foreordained to certain priesthood tasks" in the premortal world, and references [Joseph Fielding McConkie](/notes/joseph-fielding-mcconkie)'s [Answers: Straightforward Answers to Tough Gospel Questions](/notes/answers-straightforward-answers-to-tough-gospel-questions). His own conclusion is that the priesthood "is a much bigger thing than we realize" and that he doesn't yet understand it well enough to know its eternal ramifications.
