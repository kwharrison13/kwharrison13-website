# Adam Chekroud

Co-Founder and Chief Product Officer of [Spring Health](/notes/spring-health), the outcomes-based employer mental-health benefit. Kyle's single grounded source is a September 22nd, 2021 expert/sourcing call. Chekroud started in academic research on treatment trial-and-error, built technology to identify symptoms and match the best treatment — claiming patients who take the recommended route are "2x more likely to be successful" — then published the research and brought it into the market, founding [Spring Health](/notes/spring-health) in March 2016. By the call he described a just-announced Series C at a $2B valuation, with employer partners including Adobe, Pfizer, Gap, and Pepsi, sometimes replacing or augmenting an EAP. He came up doing research at the FMRIB Centre, University of Oxford under Dr. [Holly Bridge](/notes/holly-bridge) at the Brain Imaging Unit, John Radcliffe Hospital.

The call lays out Chekroud's core thesis: [Behavioral Health](/notes/behavioral-health) is a [Vertical Integration](/notes/vertical-integration) play. He frames Spring's edge as *measuring outcomes* where the status quo (health plans, EAPs) only matches patients to providers with "no sight into clinical quality" — Spring locks in rates but can lose money if patients don't improve, enabling performance guarantees and shared-risk pricing ("we'll tell you who used it and who got better"). He maps the competitive field: [Lyra](/notes/lyra) as the closest peer (therapist-heavy, "can't even get a basic anti-depressant"), Ginger on speed/access, and Modern Health as more "wellness" / SMB; against infrastructure marketplaces — Alma, [SonderMind](/notes/sondermind), [Headway](/notes/headway) — that "captured one part of the value chain," face rising CAC, and lack the employer channel ([Talkspace](/notes/talkspace) bids higher on provider ads). On TAM he argues even modest penetration of EAP-covered lives clears $1B in ARR, with payers (e.g. Guardian Life) and government multiplying it.

**Context:** Adam Chekroud is a psychiatry/data-science researcher (Yale) and entrepreneur who co-founded Spring Health; his academic work focused on using machine learning to predict mental-health treatment response.
