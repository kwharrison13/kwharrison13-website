# Carbon Offsets

Credits that fund emissions reductions or removals elsewhere to compensate for one's own emissions — the connective concept running through Kyle's 2021 climate diligence at [Index Ventures](/notes/index-ventures). The thesis the diligence kept returning to: since "reducing or avoiding 100% of emissions is impossible," offsetting is critical for the net-zero journey, and the voluntary carbon market — growing ~60% YoY and on track to break $1B in 2021 — could be worth upward of $50B by 2030 (November 5th, 2021 — Sylvera Pre-Brief (Julia Andre)). The recurring problem across the space is **quality and fungibility**: as the November 5th, 2021 — Sylvera Call put it, "today there is literally nothing to effectively grade these offsets," which makes them non-fungible and caps what buyers can credibly accomplish — the gap [Sylvera](/notes/sylvera) is building ratings infrastructure to close.

The concept maps the supply-and-demand stack of Kyle's climate companies. [Watershed Climate](/notes/watershed-climate) sits on the demand side — an end-to-end platform whose third module is a marketplace to buy offsets for residual emissions, taking ~10% on purchases routed through it. The supply side is the offset-generators: [NCX](/notes/ncx) pays landowners to defer timber harvests one year at a time (ton-year accounting); [Running Tide](/notes/running-tide) and [Perennial fka Cloud Agronomics](/notes/perennial-fka-cloud-agronomics) generate ocean- and soil-carbon removal credits that a marketplace like Watershed routes customers toward. In the December 13th, 2021 — NCX Partner Presentation, offset *quality* and whether supply scales fast enough were the central partner debates — [Julia Andre](/notes/julia-andre) conditioning her enthusiasm on offset quality holding up, [Carlos Gonzalez-Cardenas](/notes/carlos-gonzalez-cardenas) questioning supply scale.

**Context:** A carbon offset is a tradable certificate representing the reduction, avoidance, or removal of one metric ton of CO2-equivalent. Offsets are issued by avoidance projects (e.g. avoided deforestation) or removal projects (e.g. reforestation, soil/ocean carbon, direct air capture), verified by standards bodies, and bought in voluntary or compliance markets to meet net-zero commitments.
