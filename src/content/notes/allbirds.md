# Allbirds

Allbirds appears in Kyle's notes mainly as a customer reference inside the Pave Diligence — 2021 dossier, where it is one of the headline case studies for [Pave](/notes/pave)'s compensation system-of-record. The diligence captures a long [Tegus](/notes/tegus) expert call with Allbirds' Associate Director of Global Talent and a Pave case study: Allbirds previously did all comp planning in Excel ("a very small company with low budgets"), routed employees across [Carta](/notes/carta) (equity), [ADP](/notes/adp) (HRIS), and assorted benefits vendors, and adopted Pave as "a one-stop-shop that creates a personalized view of all salary, equity and benefit details." The Head of Global Talent's verdict on legacy comp tools is quoted: "Their data is absolutely garbage… We bought it and immediately regretted it." In the November 2nd, 2021 Armand Farrokh (Pave) call, Allbirds is named as "a perfect example" of the ideal Pave customer profile, alongside [Ro](/notes/ro).

It also surfaces briefly in the Watershed Climate Diligence — 2021 research, where Allbirds and [Everlane](/notes/everlane) are flagged among sneaker/apparel brands "becoming customers" of climate-accounting software — i.e., as a sustainability prospect rather than a deal subject.

**Context:** Allbirds is a San Francisco–based sustainable-footwear and apparel brand founded in 2016, known for its wool and tree-fiber shoes; it went public on Nasdaq in 2021.
