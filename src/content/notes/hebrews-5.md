# Hebrews 5

A chapter in the New Testament epistle to the Hebrews, cited in Kyle's Abrahamic Covenant study. Kyle lists [Hebrews 5](/notes/hebrews-5):4 — "no man taketh this honour unto himself, but he that is called of God, as was Aaron" — within a cluster of New Testament proof-texts on priesthood authority and church organization (alongside [1 Corinthians 12](/notes/1-corinthians-12):27-29, [Ephesians 4](/notes/ephesians-4):11-13, [1 Timothy 3](/notes/1-timothy-3):1-13, [Titus 1](/notes/titus-1):5-9, and [Matthew 16](/notes/matthew-16):19). The verse supports the point that priesthood office is conferred by divine call rather than self-assumed, agreeable to the articles of faith on the need to be "called of God."

**Context:** Hebrews 5 (New Testament) presents Christ as the great high priest "after the order of Melchizedek," stressing that priestly office is received by God's calling, and rebukes its readers for remaining spiritual infants who still need milk rather than meat.
