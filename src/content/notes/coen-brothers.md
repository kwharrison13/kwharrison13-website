# Coen Brothers

Filmmakers Kyle names among the directors he **"voraciously consumed"** growing up — listed alongside [Christopher Nolan](/notes/christopher-nolan), [Quentin Tarantino](/notes/quentin-tarantino), [Martin Scorsese](/notes/martin-scorsese), and Rian Johnson. They recur as exemplars of [Auteur Theory](/notes/auteur-theory): the kind of artist who "owns so much of a creative experience," a quality Kyle is drawn to (a lens that runs through his essay [The Hits Business](/essays/the-hits-business)). On the [Quentin Tarantino](/notes/quentin-tarantino) page they're held up, with Tarantino and Scorsese, as artists whose ownership of a singular creative vision Kyle admires.

**Context:** The Coen Brothers, Joel and Ethan Coen, are American filmmaker siblings known for writing, directing, and editing their films together — *Fargo*, *No Country for Old Men*, *The Big Lebowski*, *True Grit*, *O Brother, Where Art Thou?* — and are canonical examples of the auteur in modern American cinema.
