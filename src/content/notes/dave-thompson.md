# Dave Thompson

Director of Engineering at [Kong](/notes/kong) and a reference expert in Kyle's 2021 API-security diligence on [Noname Security](/notes/noname-security) and [Salt Security](/notes/salt-security). He joined Kong because it had attracted a number of former [MuleSoft](/notes/mulesoft) employees, and leads platform engineering for site reliability and DevOps plus security and compliance engineering — describing the company as scaling fast ("typical company going from 100 to 1K," doubling engineering roughly every year). On the central diligence question — whether a gateway/middleware company would expand into API security — his read was that securing the API layer "could be an interesting play for service mesh" (e.g. access control), but he framed [Kong](/notes/kong) as a slow-compounding middleware business: years from open source to monetization to enterprise to SaaS, much like MuleSoft's ~15-year road to IPO (Noname Security - Diligence, call dated October 21, 2021).

His call also colored Kyle's read of the broader landscape — over-saturated [Kubernetes](/notes/kubernetes) security, WAF dominated by [Palo Alto Networks ($PANW)](/notes/palo-alto-networks) and [Imperva](/notes/imperva), the Ann Arbor security-research cluster ([Censys](/notes/censys)), and a tooling critique (Jira/Jenkins "ripe for disruption," enthusiasm for [Terraform](/notes/terraform), [PagerDuty](/notes/pagerduty), and [Blameless](/notes/blameless)). He is also listed among the Salt Security Diligence — 2021 reference experts.
