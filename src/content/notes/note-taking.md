# Note-taking

A central practice in Kyle's [Open Source Knowledge](/notes/open-source-knowledge) thinking and the engine behind this wiki itself. The anchoring book is [How To Take Smart Notes](/books/how-to-take-smart-notes) by [Sonke Ahrens](/notes/sonke-ahrens), whose [Zettelkasten](/notes/zettelkasten) slip-box reframes note-taking away from accumulation toward transformation: "the slip-box does not put the learner in the centre… Learning, thinking and writing should not be about accumulating knowledge, but about becoming a different person with a different way of thinking." Kyle folds this into a **Culture of Questions** in his [Open-Source Knowledge](/essays/open-source-knowledge) research, alongside [Andy Matuschak](/notes/andy-matuschak)'s "Why Books Don't Work."

The sharpest case for it comes from [The Collector's Fallacy](/notes/the-collector-s-fallacy) by [Christian Tietze](/notes/christian-tietze): reading without taking notes is "a waste of time in the long run," because the information stays in short-term memory and is forgotten, while taking notes "permanently integrates the text's information into your own knowledge system, making it available forever." Tietze's remedy — Research, Read, Assimilate; rinse and repeat — frames note-taking as the assimilation step that turns mere collection into actual knowing. [Umberto Eco](/notes/umberto-eco) supplies the negative version (in *How to Write a Thesis*): photocopying a text gives "the illusion of having read the text already."

[The Notetaking Cold War](/notes/the-notetaking-cold-war) by [Dan Shipper](/notes/dan-shipper) reframes the whole subject as "philosophy in action": the [Tiago Forte](/notes/tiago-forte)-vs-[Conor White-Sullivan](/notes/conor-white-sullivan) debate over how to organize notes (actionable PARA hierarchy vs. infinitely-flexible bidirectional links) maps onto a two-thousand-year-old split between [Essentialists](/notes/essentialists) (truth as a relationship between statement and reality) and [Pragmatists](/notes/pragmatists) (truth as what works). A note, Shipper argues, is a box you can stick anything into — information whose use is unpredictable — which is exactly why it resists organization. [David Perell](/notes/david-perell) adds the social dimension in his [Twitter workshop](/notes/how-to-crush-it-on-twitter-david-perell-and-matthew-kobach-workshop): "note-taking is a conversation with your past self." The practice also surfaces historically in [Saints — No Unhallowed Hand](/books/saints-no-unhallowed-hand), where Kyle flags the discipline of [Eliza R. Snow](/notes/eliza-snow)'s careful minute-keeping and "home literature." Kyle threads this whole cluster — note-taking, [Knowledge Management](/notes/knowledge-management), [Personal Knowledge Management](/notes/personal-knowledge-management) — into the conceptual ancestry of his own LLM wiki.

**Context:** Note-taking as a knowledge-work discipline saw a software-driven revival in the late 2010s — Roam Research, Obsidian, Notion — popularizing networked-thought tools and methods like Zettelkasten and "building a second brain."
