# Semil Shah

Semil Shah is a venture investor and writer whose January 2022 post [The Market Is The Greatest Critic](/notes/the-market-is-the-greatest-critic) Kyle annotated as a long-read. The piece is his central appearance in the corpus: writing from nine years of investing in an era where "the money has been easy," Shah argues that in a downturn it is the market — not journalists or ecosystem players — that delivers the harshest and truest critique of companies. Riffing off [Chris Dixon](/notes/chris-dixon)'s defense of startups, he frames the Nov '21–Jan '22 crash (Kyle's first crash as an investor) as a moment where public companies get punished in milliseconds while private investors can only cheer from the sidelines.

His forward-looking thesis is the one Kyle pulls out: the public markets will increasingly reward only companies that can [Bundle](/notes/bundle) product lines into diversified growth platforms — spared names like Facebook, Microsoft, Google, and Apple versus single-product casualties like [Robinhood](/notes/robinhood) and [Toast ($TOST)](/notes/toast). The piece ties into the wiki's [Venture Capital](/notes/venture-capital) and [Capital Allocation](/notes/capital-allocation) threads, predicting the best Series A/B and growth investors will demand "more than just top-line momentum."

**Context:** Semil Shah is the founder of the seed-stage venture firm Haystack and a longtime writer on venture capital and startups; he has also been a venture partner at firms including Lightspeed.
