# Philanthropy

Philanthropy enters the wiki through [Social Innovation](/notes/social-innovation), where it appears in two concrete, cross-sector forms drawn from the 2018 [Destination: Home](/notes/destination-home) conversation. The first is **collective-impact philanthropy**: convening public and private partners, deploying cheap or forgivable capital, and incubating data-driven systems that the county later institutionalizes — the model [Destination: Home](/notes/destination-home) used against [Homelessness](/notes/homelessness). The second is **corporate philanthropy**, framed not as charity or a tax on the company but as a *necessity of being a community partner* — illustrated by [Cisco](/notes/cisco)'s $50M grant. In both forms, philanthropy is positioned as an active instrument of [Social Innovation](/notes/social-innovation) for tackling entrenched social problems, rather than as passive giving.

**Context:** Philanthropy is the private giving of money, time, or resources toward the public good. "Collective impact" and "blended/forgivable capital" are contemporary structures through which foundations, corporations, and government coordinate on systemic social problems.
