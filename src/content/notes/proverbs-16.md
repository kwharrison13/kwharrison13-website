# Proverbs 16

A chapter from the book of Proverbs in the Old Testament, used in Kyle's scripture study as a cross-reference on [Pride](/notes/pride) and the moral consequences of a man's words. In the July 27–August 2, 2020. Alma 39–42: "The Great Plan of Happiness" study, two verses are cited verbatim: Proverbs 16:18 — "Pride goeth before destruction, and an haughty spirit before a fall" — and Proverbs 16:27 — "An ungodly man diggeth up evil: and in his lips there is as a burning fire." The chapter appears in that page's scripture cross-reference list alongside [Alma 38](/notes/alma-38) and [Alma 42](/notes/alma-42).

**Context:** Proverbs is a book of Hebrew wisdom literature, traditionally attributed largely to King Solomon, collecting short maxims on righteous living. Proverbs 16:18 is among its most widely quoted verses.
