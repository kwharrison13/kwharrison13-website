# Sherry Turkle

A key interlocutor in [Cal Newport](/notes/cal-newport)'s [Digital Minimalism](/books/digital-minimalism), cited repeatedly on the difference between conversation and connection. Newport quotes her premise that "face-to-face conversation is the most human — and humanizing — thing we do," the setting where we learn to listen and "develop the capacity for [Empathy](/notes/empathy)." He also cites her research that just five days at a camp without phones or internet was enough to induce major increases in campers' well-being and sense of connection. Newport agrees with Turkle's diagnosis but pushes past her prescription: he argues that her recommendation to simply make more space for quality conversation is likely to fail unless you first reform your relationship with the digital tools — like social media and texting — that force a trade-off between conversation and connection.

**Context:** Sherry Turkle (b. 1948) is an American sociologist and clinical psychologist, a professor at MIT studying the social and psychological effects of technology. Her books include *Alone Together* (2011) and *Reclaiming Conversation* (2015), the source of the conversation-vs-connection arguments Newport engages.
