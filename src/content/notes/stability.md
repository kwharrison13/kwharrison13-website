# Stability

In [Brave New World](/books/brave-new-world), stability is the supreme value of the World State and the justification Mustapha Mond offers for every design choice in the regime — the caste system, soma, conditioning, and the deliberate suppression of science and art. Mond frames stability as a direct trade-off against truth, beauty, and [Free Will](/notes/free-will): "We believe in happiness and stability. A society of Alphas couldn't fail to be unstable and miserable." Bokanovsky's Process — mass-producing identical humans — is called "one of the major instruments of social stability," because uniformity removes the friction of "separate and unrelated individuals" capable of free choice.

The book's central argument is that stability has a cost denominated in everything that makes life grand. "You can't make tragedies without social instability," Mond tells the Savage; the world is stable precisely because people "get what they want, and they never want what they can't get." Even progress is sacrificed to it — the Inventions Office is "stuffed with plans for labour-saving processes" that go unused because "every change is a menace to stability," and "even science must sometimes be treated as a possible enemy." In Kyle's reading, stability is the lens that exposes the dystopia: a perfectly engineered, contented order purchased by chucking liberty "out of the window."

## Where this appears

- [Brave New World](/books/brave-new-world) — Mustapha Mond's repeated justification for castes, soma, and suppressed science; stability framed as the trade-off against truth, liberty, and tragedy.
