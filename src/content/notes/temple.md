# Temple

In Kyle's gospel-study and LDS reading, the temple is the site of the highest ordinances and covenants — and several sources tie it specifically to priesthood power and to building Zion. [The Priesthood Power of Women](/books/the-priesthood-power-of-women) makes its sharpest doctrinal point through the temple: "women receive priesthood power and authority through temple ordinances rather than ordination," with [M. Russell Ballard](/notes/m-russell-ballard) quoted that "when men and women go to the temple, they are both endowed with the same power, which by definition is priesthood power." The temple is where the "patriarchal order of the priesthood" — the new and everlasting covenant of marriage — is entered into "as coequals."

The Process of Zion casts temples as instruments of preparation: "We are dotting the earth with temples (D&C 101:46) so that people can go and learn the laws of a celestial world" — temples "help us learn how to live in Zion," with the analogy that "the Temple is Mt. Sinai" on the road to the promised land. [Restoration — God's Call To The 21st Century](/books/restoration-god-s-call-to-the-21st-century) frames temple ordinances as where "national identities dissolve in the kingdom of God," and points to [Russell M. Nelson](/notes/russell-m-nelson)'s temple-building expansion as evidence of an "ongoing, accelerating Restoration" (96% of operating temples dedicated in his lifetime). The Alma 30–31 study traces the same "line upon line" growth — from the Kirtland Temple's restored keys to the Nauvoo Temple's ordinances to "more than 130 temples throughout the world today." [Gordon B. Hinckley — Go Forward With Faith](/books/gordon-b-hinckley-go-forward-with-faith) grounds it biographically, with the "smaller-temples idea maturing over years as iterative revelation."

**Context:** In The Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-day Saints, temples are dedicated buildings — distinct from ordinary meetinghouses — where members participate in ordinances such as the endowment and eternal (sealing) marriage; the church has rapidly expanded its number of temples worldwide, especially under Russell M. Nelson.
