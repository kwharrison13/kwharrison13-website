# Ursula K. Le Guin

Ursula K. Le Guin surfaces in Historical Futurism - Research as a science-fiction author whose work is part of the genre's imaginative-futures canon Kyle is mining. Her novel *The Dispossessed* appears on "The Good Society Reading List" (a syllabus of utopian/alternative-society texts) alongside works like *Gaviotas: A Village to Reinvent the World* and *The Long Haul* by [Myles Horton](/notes/myles-horton). She is also catalogued among the people in the historical-futurism corpus next to other speculative-fiction figures — [Arthur C. Clarke](/notes/arthur-c-clarke), [Isaac Asimov](/notes/isaac-asimov), [Kurt Vonnegut](/notes/kurt-vonnegut), [William Gibson](/notes/william-gibson), [Ray Bradbury](/notes/ray-bradbury), [Philip K. Dick](/notes/philip-k-dick), and [Ted Chiang](/notes/ted-chiang).

**Context:** Ursula K. Le Guin (1929–2018) was an American writer best known for the science-fiction and fantasy novels of the Hainish Cycle and Earthsea series, including *The Left Hand of Darkness* and *The Dispossessed*, works that explored anarchist, anthropological, and utopian themes.
