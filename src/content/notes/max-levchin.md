# Max Levchin

**One-line:** *Co-founder and CTO of Confinity (which became PayPal); the engineering counterpart to [Peter Thiel](/notes/peter-thiel)'s strategy work. Famous for high engineering-hiring bar and the all-nighter ethic that defined Confinity's early culture.*

## What we know

- Studied at University of Illinois at Urbana-Champaign (UIUC); inspired by Marc Andreessen's example that "students like us built these amazing tools."
- Watched Akira Kurosawa's *Seven Samurai* "at least twenty-five times" one summer. As of writing, has watched it 100+ times. Calls it his "sole source of management training."
- **"As hire As. Bs hire Cs. So the first B you hire takes the whole company down."** Hiring discipline that arguably defined PayPal's eventual talent density.
- Co-founded Confinity (later PayPal) with [Peter Thiel](/notes/peter-thiel).
- Observed the n-squared communication-cost law in teams: "In a five-person team, there are something like twenty-five pairwise relationships to manage."
- Heuristic: "Diversity of opinion is essential anytime you don't know anything about something important. But if there's a strong sense of what's right already, don't argue about it."
- During the X.com / Confinity merger, Musk's decision to rewind Levchin's code stack to Windows from Unix nearly drove him to quit: "This V2 thing is just destroying my will to live." Was core to the eventual boardroom coup that ousted Musk.
- **The all-nighter philosophy.** "There's definitely something about the nocturnal lifestyle for engineers specifically that really opens up the chakras of creativity or code-writing. People get slightly sillier, but also maybe a little more creative."
- With Thiel and Sacks, designed PayPal's fraud-vs-usability "dials" framework.
- Engineering-team hiring bar so high it created staffing delays the company accepted as a trade-off.
