# Roelof Botha

A member of the [PayPal Mafia](/notes/paypal-mafia) who appears in two distinct threads of Kyle's corpus: as a young PayPal operator in [The Founders](/books/the-founders), and as the [Sequoia](/notes/sequoia) steward whose succession discipline and pre-mortem thinking anchor The Death of a Venture Fund - Research.

In [The Founders](/books/the-founders), Botha is the fresh-out-of-business-school CFO whom [Peter Thiel](/notes/peter-thiel) promoted — an instance of Thiel's conviction that "bucking convention had nothing to do with math or political philosophy — it had to do with people." Botha turned Musk down twice on joining X.com but came away struck ("There are people you meet, and then two weeks later, you can't remember anything about them… Elon lingers"). He coined the company's enduring image of existential risk — the "sword of Damocles" — after hearing eBay CEO Meg Whitman say of PayPal, "We're going to crush them."

In The Death of a Venture Fund - Research, Botha is cited as the architect of a recurring [Pre-Mortem](/notes/pre-mortem) exercise: *"Imagine the venture business in a decade, and Sequoia is gone. We presided over the decline of Sequoia… What happened? What did we not do?"* Kyle holds up Sequoia's long succession runway — Botha was there 9 years before [Doug Leone](/notes/doug-leone) took over and 20 before he himself led — as evidence that surviving multiple regime changes requires building a long-term institution, not just a transition. Botha's thesis that **venture capital doesn't scale as an asset class** also surfaces in the ["Build What's Fundable" - Research](<“Build What's Fundable” - Research>) notes.

**Context:** Roelof Botha is a South African–born venture capitalist who joined Sequoia Capital after serving as PayPal's CFO; he led early investments including YouTube and Instagram and became Sequoia's senior steward (managing/senior partner) following Doug Leone.
