# Helaman 15

A chapter in the Book of Mormon — the close of Samuel the Lamanite's prophecy — studied in Kyle's Come Follow Me study of Helaman 13–16. Kyle reads it around three themes. [Sign Seeking](/notes/sign-seeking): "wo unto this people… except they shall repent, when they shall see all these signs and wonders" ([Helaman 15](/notes/helaman-15):3) — even people given signs still have to act on them rather than harden their hearts. God [Chasten](/notes/chasten)s because He loves: "the people of Nephi hath he loved, and also hath he chastened them… because he loveth them" ([Helaman 15](/notes/helaman-15):3), which Kyle treats as a hub for the many scriptural examples of chastening-as-love. And [Accountability](/notes/accountability): the chapter's promise that the Lord will be merciful to the scattered Lamanites in the latter days ([Helaman 15](/notes/helaman-15):12, 14-15).

Kyle also wrestles in his notes with the harder verse — "the Lamanites hath he hated because their deeds have been evil continually" ([Helaman 15](/notes/helaman-15):4) — against the backdrop of [Romans 3](/notes/romans-3):23 ("all have sinned"), reading the "hatred" as directed at evil deeds and the iniquitous traditions of the fathers rather than the people themselves, since salvation is then extended to them through the preaching of the Nephites.

**Context:** Helaman 15 (Book of Mormon) concludes Samuel the Lamanite's sermon, contrasting the steadfastness of the converted Lamanites with the hardness of the Nephites and prophesying the eventual mercy extended to the Lamanite remnant in the last days.
