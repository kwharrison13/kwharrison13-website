# When China’s Open-Source AI Is a Trap

**Author:** The Economist
**URL:** https://www.economist.com/international/2026/07/14/when-chinas-open-source-ai-is-a-trap
**One-line:** *Xi Jinping is promoting China's open-source AI models as a "shared asset for all humanity," but The Economist frames both America's AI-dominance push and China's open-source generosity as suspect — and argues China's open models are not a neutral solution to America's own AI ambitions.*

## Key claims

- Xi Jinping's planned remarks at the World Artificial Intelligence Conference (WAIC), opening in Shanghai on July 17th, were previewed by Communist Party media using language like "those who walk together go far" and "global AI for good."
- China's official framing is that its open-source and open-weight large language models are a "shared asset for all humanity," particularly benefiting users in less wealthy countries — open-weight meaning models that can be downloaded, run on a user's own servers, and customized.
- The piece's headline framing signals skepticism of that narrative: it treats China's open-source AI generosity as a potential "trap," positioned as a counterpoint to America's own AI-dominance ambitions — implying neither side's stated motives should be taken fully at face value.
