# Matthew 25

Matthew 25 is the New Testament chapter of three end-of-ministry parables — the Ten Virgins, the Talents, and the Sheep and the Goats — that recur as cross-references throughout Kyle's [gospel-study](/notes/gospel-study) notes. Its most-cited verse is the Parable of the Talents' "unto every one that hath shall be given... but from him that hath not shall be taken away even that which he hath" (Matthew 25:29), which several Come Follow Me studies apply to spiritual gifts: in the Helaman 13–16 study it grounds the warning that the Spirit grows duller if neglected, and it also appears in the Helaman 7–12 study and the Ether 12–15 study.

The chapter's other facets carry distinct themes. In Chosen Leaders the "well done, thou good and faithful servant" / "depart from me" judgment (Matthew 25:21) frames the called-vs-chosen distinction. In Spiritual Gifts the Parable of the Talents (Matthew 25:14–30) prompts the question of which talents are innate versus developed. In Fasting - September 5, 2021 the Sheep-and-Goats passage (Matthew 25:35–40) is used to teach children about fast offerings and caring for the poor and needy. The Parable of the Ten Virgins (Matthew 25:1-13) is cited in the Helaman 1–6 study.

**Context:** Matthew 25 is the twenty-fifth chapter of the Gospel of Matthew, the final stretch of Jesus' Olivet discourse before His arrest. It contains three parables about readiness, stewardship, and final judgment: the Ten Virgins, the Talents, and the Sheep and the Goats.
