# Helaman 2

Chapter 2 of the Book of Helaman in the Book of Mormon. In Kyle's [Come, Follow Me](/notes/come-follow-me) study of [Helaman 1](/notes/helaman-1)–[Helaman 6](/notes/helaman-6), this chapter anchors the lesson on how **the politics of dissent breeds secret combinations**: people who let their political opinions lead them into covenants of murder became "a plague among their society" — a warning to beware leaders driven by their ability to manipulate, even when they happen to agree with your cause (paired with [Helaman 1](/notes/helaman-1)). The chapter introduces Gadianton, with the foreshadowing in [Helaman 2](/notes/helaman-2):13 that "this Gadianton did prove the overthrow, yea, almost the entire destruction of the people of Nephi" — the seed of [The Pride Cycle](/notes/the-pride-cycle) running its full course across these chapters.

**Context:** Helaman 2 narrates the rise of the Gadianton robbers, a secret combination bound by oaths, which the Book of Mormon presents as the eventual ruin of Nephite civilization.
