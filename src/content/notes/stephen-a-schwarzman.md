# Stephen A. Schwarzman

Stephen A. Schwarzman is the co-founder of [Blackstone](/notes/blackstone) and the author of *[What It Takes](/books/what-it-takes)*, the memoir Kyle has catalogued in the wiki. The book traces his path from Lehman through founding Blackstone, and Kyle mines it for operating lessons: the [Theory of Masters](/notes/theory-of-masters) (Felix Rohatyn's "masters vs. slaves" advice to train before becoming a master), [Risk Management](/notes/risk-management) distilled to "Don't. Lose. Money.," depersonalizing the investment process into a team decision after the Edgcomb loss ([Build a Fund](/notes/build-a-fund)), and assessing [Talent](/books/talent) as the critical entrepreneurial skill — hiring 9s and 10s over 6s and 7s. Schwarzman frames [Failure](/notes/failure) (the botched deal book, the Edgcomb loss and the screaming investor) as the best teacher, and [Education](/notes/education) as a lifelong discipline whose object is learning how to think.

The book records his maxims on [Sales](/notes/sales) (selling a vision over and over, raising the funds that founded Sallie Mae and Blackstone), [Listening](/notes/listening) as his edge on Wall Street, and a [Culture](/notes/culture) of meritocracy and intellectual integrity. It also notes the CEOs he recruited to the president's Strategic and Policy Forum — including [Elon Musk](/notes/elon-musk), [Jack Welch](/notes/jack-welch), and [Bob Iger](/notes/bob-iger) — and his citation of [Malcolm Gladwell](/notes/malcolm-gladwell)'s *[Outliers: The Story of Success](/notes/outliers-the-story-of-success)* for the practice-before-mastery argument.

**Context:** Stephen A. Schwarzman is an American businessman who co-founded the private-equity and asset-management firm Blackstone with Pete Peterson in 1985, building it into one of the world's largest alternative-asset managers. He is also a prominent philanthropist.
