# Exodus 4

A scripture reference Kyle uses in Humility — Topical Study as evidence that meekness is not weakness. Citing **Exodus 4:10** — Moses' declaration, "I am not eloquent... I am slow of speech" — Kyle reads this as Moses feeling inadequate as a speaker, yet that felt inadequacy did not make him weak. He pairs it with [Numbers 12](/notes/numbers-12):3 ("Moses was very meek, above all the men which were upon the face of the earth") to argue that Moses' meekness — his honest recognition of his own limitation — was a source of spiritual strength rather than a deficit, summed up with [John Bytheway](/notes/john-bytheway)'s line, "if you think being meek is weak, try being meek for a week."

**Context:** Exodus 4 is the chapter in which God responds to Moses' objections at the burning bush, giving him signs (the staff turned to a serpent, the leprous hand) and appointing Aaron to speak for him after Moses protests that he is "slow of speech, and of a slow tongue."
