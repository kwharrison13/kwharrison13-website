# Alma 28

Alma 28 is the Book of Mormon chapter Kyle most associates with [Mormon's Commentary](/notes/mormon-s-commentary) and the reflection phrase "[And thus we see](/notes/and-thus-we-see)." In his Come Follow Me study of Alma 23–29, he reads Alma 28:8 as one of the rare moments Mormon-as-historian lets his own feeling show ("may the Lord, the Redeemer of all men, bless their souls forever"), and Alma 28:13–14 as the locus classicus of "and thus we see" — the editorial pivot from narrative to meaning that occurs 18 times in the Book of Mormon. He pairs it with [Boyd K. Packer](/notes/boyd-k-packer)'s "Therefore, what?" and [Jeffrey R. Holland](/notes/jeffrey-r-holland)'s commentary: exhortation is "to no avail if the actual lives of His disciples did not change." The chapter also surfaces in cross-references — Alma 28:13–14 in the Helaman 1–6 study, and Alma 28:4–6 in his fasting notes.

**Context:** Alma 28 narrates the return of the sons of Mosiah from their mission among the Lamanites and a great battle; it closes with Mormon's editorial reflection on the inequality of man wrought by sin and the contrast of sorrow and joy.

## Where this appears

- Alma 23–29 — They Never Did Fall Away — Alma 28:8 as [Mormon's Commentary](/notes/mormon-s-commentary) and 28:13–14 as the "[And thus we see](/notes/and-thus-we-see)" reflection phrase, with [Boyd K. Packer](/notes/boyd-k-packer)'s "Therefore, what?".
- Helaman 1–6 — The Rock of Our Redeemer — cross-reference to Alma 28:13–14.
- Fasting - September 5, 2021 — cites Alma 28:4–6.
