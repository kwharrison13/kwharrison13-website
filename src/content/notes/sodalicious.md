# Sodalicious

A specialty-soda chain that Kyle invokes in his notes on the [Berkshire Hathaway Annual Letters](/books/berkshire-hathaway-annual-letters) as a culture case study. Reacting to Buffett's observation that See's is "a one-of-a-kind performance" — practically no one besides See's has made significant profits operating candy shops — Kyle muses: "Pop n' Sweets? Maybe a one-off culture case? Soda is a Mormon vice," tagging both [Fifth Partners](/notes/fifth-partners) and Sodalicious. The book page's synthesis frames this as part of a "Mormon-investment framing," treating Sodalicious as a culture case study where religious and investing frameworks become substitutable vocabularies for questions of character, longevity, and trust. The link is associative — Kyle testing whether a brand built on a regional cultural quirk (soda as a Latter-day Saint indulgence) could be the kind of durable, one-off franchise Buffett prizes.

**Context:** Sodalicious is a Utah-based "dirty soda" chain serving customized soft-drink mixes, part of a regional category that grew around Latter-day Saint communities where alcohol and coffee are avoided.
