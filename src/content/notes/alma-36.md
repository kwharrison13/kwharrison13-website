# Alma 36

Alma's counsel to his son [Helaman](/notes/helaman), and in Kyle's notes the great chiasmus of the Book of Mormon — a chapter structured around Alma's own conversion. The Come Follow Me study July 20–26, 2020. Alma 36–38: "Look to God and Live" reads it alongside [Alma 37](/notes/alma-37)–38 for the central promise ("inasmuch as ye shall keep the commandments… ye shall prosper in the land"), the chiastic structure built around Alma's conversion, and the [Central Theme of the Book of Mormon](/notes/central-theme-of-the-book-of-mormon) as "one of perseverant obedience."

The chapter's emotional core — joy "as exquisite as my pain" — anchors Kyle's repentance and humility studies. Mighty Repentance cites it as the marker of full repentance-as-conversion: crucifying the old man, becoming a new creature, with joy as exquisite as the prior pain. Humility — Topical Study quotes verse 18 ("O Jesus, thou son of God, have mercy on me, who am in the gall of bitterness"), the cry from the depths that precedes deliverance. The Process of the Book of Mormon folds verse 30 into the recurring prosperity-and-obedience chain that runs from [2 Nephi 1](/notes/2-nephi-1) through Alma. The Fulfilling the Measure of Our Creation study points to verses 17–20 as the turning point of Alma's conversion narrative.
