# Doctrine of Christ

The Doctrine of Christ is the structural spine of [M. Russell Ballard](/notes/m-russell-ballard)'s talk Follow the Doctrine and Gospel of Christ — M. Russell Ballard, built on [Nephi](/notes/nephi)'s plain statement in 2 Nephi 31. Ballard names its basic, repeatable elements — "have faith in the Lord Jesus Christ, repent, honor the covenant of baptism, live worthy of the Holy Ghost as your companion, and endure to the very end" — and argues that staying on the gospel path "requires nothing exotic, only the basics lived daily," set against Satan as a "counterfeit fisher of men" casting artificial lures (addiction, pornography, misused technology). He urges young people to "know the doctrine of Christ and live and defend it all the days of your life."

In [Russell M. Nelson](/notes/russell-m-nelson)'s Christ Is Risen; Faith in Him Will Move Mountains — Russell M. Nelson, the doctrine of Christ is approached through study: "Know the doctrine of Christ so that you understand its power for your life," internalizing that the Atonement "applies to *you*." There the thread runs into [Repentance](/notes/repentance) — "true repentance begins with faith that Jesus Christ has the power to cleanse, heal, and strengthen us." The [Joseph Smith](/notes/joseph-smith) lecture on the First Vision ties the same doctrine to the witness of the [Holy Ghost](/notes/holy-ghost) — a confirmation "even greater than sight."

**Context:** In Latter-day Saint theology, the "doctrine of Christ" refers to the foundational gospel pattern taught in 2 Nephi 31 and 3 Nephi 27 — faith in Christ, repentance, baptism, receiving the gift of the Holy Ghost, and enduring to the end — presented as the simple, essential path to salvation.

## Where this appears

- Follow the Doctrine and Gospel of Christ — M. Russell Ballard — the talk's spine: the five basics lived daily, against Satan's "artificial lures."
- Christ Is Risen; Faith in Him Will Move Mountains — Russell M. Nelson — studying the doctrine to grasp the Atonement's personal power.
- Joseph Smith Lecture 1 — The First Vision and Its Aftermath — the witness of the Spirit as part of the same doctrine.
