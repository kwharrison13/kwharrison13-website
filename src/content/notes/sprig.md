# Sprig

**One-line:** *An in-app micro-survey and product-research platform (formerly **UserLeap**) whose wedge is contextual, SDK-native surveys served inside a product flow, plus the ability to take open-ended responses and categorize them automatically.*

## What we know

- Original differentiator (as UserLeap): taking in **open-ended data and categorizing it for you** — described as "a game changer in the surveying world." (Sprig fka UserLeap - Diligence)
- **Contextual in-app micro-surveys** drive dramatically higher response rates than email/out-of-context tools: ~60% on mobile at [Chipper Cash](/notes/chipper-cash) (vs. 3-5% on Zoom Survey / Google Docs / [Typeform](/notes/typeform)); 20-30% at [Codecademy](/notes/codecademy) vs. ~1% on [SurveyMonkey](/notes/surveymonkey). (Sprig fka UserLeap - Diligence)
- Lightweight SDK integration — [Dropbox](/notes/dropbox) integrated in **about a week**; engineers use the SDK once; non-disruptive to performance. Template library + suggested questions; supports asynch video answers and Loom-style recorded feedback surveys. (Sprig fka UserLeap - Diligence)
- No session replay — deliberately distinct from [FullStory](/notes/fullstory) (self-reported/subjective vs. "what did you actually do"). (Sprig fka UserLeap - Diligence)
- Named customers: Robinhood, Square, Nike, Figma; land-and-expand pattern ([Square](/notes/square) estimates ~100 potential seats across Square + Cash App; [Chipper Cash](/notes/chipper-cash) grew from one designer to a full team). (Sprig fka UserLeap - Diligence)
- Attributable ROI in places — Chipper Cash's Uganda crypto-trading survey drove +190% adoption within a month. (Sprig fka UserLeap - Diligence)
- Funding: [a16z](/notes/a16z) led the **$38M Series B** (June 2021); [Accel](/notes/accel) led the **$16M Series A** (Dec 2020); [Bill Trenchard](/notes/bill-trenchard) at [First Round Capital](/notes/first-round-capital) led the **$4M seed** (Sep 2019). (Sprig fka UserLeap - Diligence)
- Bear critiques from expert calls: shallow analytics output ("primitive page," users download and analyze themselves), no follow-ups/depth, doesn't reach non-customers, user-based pricing (cost rises with growth at flat usage), "easy in / easy out," and an ambiguous category (straddles qualitative research vs. A/B testing like LaunchDarkly / Optimizely). (Sprig fka UserLeap - Diligence)
