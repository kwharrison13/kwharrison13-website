# 1 John 4

A New Testament chapter Kyle returns to as the corpus's anchor text for **"God is love"** and for love as the source and test of true discipleship. Two verse-clusters do most of the work across his topical studies. The first, **1 John 4:7–8** ("everyone that loveth God is born of God, and KNOWETH GOD… for God is love"), grounds The Good Shepherd and the Missionary Work study: God leads "through love" as a shepherd rather than a sheepherder ([Neal A. Maxwell](/notes/neal-a-maxwell)), and in Charity & Love Kyle reads it as "we have no chance of loving anyone unless God loves us first." The second cluster, **1 John 4:16–18** ("God is love" / "perfect love casteth out fear"), anchors Radiating Your Example (a missionary must "exude love") and Teaching Repentance, where boldness flows from love — "each invitation is an expression of love… perfect love casteth out fear."

The chapter also carries a discernment function. In Anti-Christ — Topical Study, **1 John 4:3–6** is cited for how to detect the "spirit of antichrist": those who refuse to confess Jesus as the Christ are "not of God," while verse 6 names the Spirit's role in helping "us detect what is wrong (the spirit of error)" — a point Spiritual Gifts picks up directly.

**Context:** 1 John 4 is a chapter in the first epistle of John in the New Testament. It contains the much-quoted declaration "God is love," an exhortation to "test the spirits" to discern truth from error, and the teaching that "perfect love casteth out fear."

## Where this appears

- Charity & Love — "we love God because he first loved us"; love of God as prerequisite to loving others.
- The Good Shepherd — 4:7–8 grounds God leading by love, "shepherds, not sheepherders."
- Missionary Work — same shepherd-by-love pattern applied to missionary discipleship.
- Radiating Your Example — 4:16, "God is love," so a missionary must exude love.
- Teaching Repentance — 4:17–18, perfect love casteth out fear; boldness as an expression of love.
- Anti-Christ — Topical Study — 4:3–6, the "spirit of antichrist" and not confessing Christ.
- Spiritual Gifts — 4:6, the Spirit helps detect "the spirit of error."
