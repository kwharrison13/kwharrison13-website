# Getting Things Done

David Allen's workflow system (Allen, 2001), described in *How to Take Smart Notes* as collecting everything that needs attention into one place and processing it in a standardized way, producing a "mind like water" free of nagging open loops. The book argues GTD doesn't transfer cleanly to insight-oriented writing: GTD needs clearly defined objectives and concrete next-steps, while writing toward insight starts vague and can't always be broken into predictable steps.
