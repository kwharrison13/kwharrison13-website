# Quentin L. Cook

A member of the Quorum of the Twelve Apostles of The Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-day Saints, cited across Kyle's notes from the General Conference — October 2021. Cook's contributions cluster around two of the recurring threads Kyle traced that weekend: *seeking personal peace amid division* and *love God, then your neighbor, then yourself*.

On contention, Kyle recorded Cook observing that "in my lifetime, I have never seen a greater lack of civility… But personal peace can be achieved despite division. It has never been more important to seek personal peace." On the order of the commandments, Cook's distilled instruction was "Love God, live His commandments, and forgive everyone." A third passage ties D&C 121:7-8 to the idea that "adversity is not a witness of the Lord's displeasure, nor the withdrawal of His favor" — a counter to reading affliction as divine abandonment.

**Context:** Quentin L. Cook (b. 1940) is a senior LDS apostle, ordained to the Quorum of the Twelve in 2007. Before his Church service he was a healthcare-industry executive and attorney in the San Francisco Bay Area.
