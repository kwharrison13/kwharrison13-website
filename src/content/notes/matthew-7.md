# Matthew 7

[Matthew 7](/notes/matthew-7) is the closing chapter of the Sermon on the Mount, and in Kyle's gospel study it carries three recurring threads. First, **judging**: 7:1 ("judge not") is read through [Dallin H. Oaks](/notes/dallin-h-oaks)'s "Two Kinds of Judging," which distinguishes forbidden *final* judgments from directed *intermediate* judgments made on righteous principles (within one's stewardship, with adequate information, judging situations rather than people, with forgiveness as a companion). The Sermon on the Mount note insists the point of Matthew 7 (and its parallel [3 Nephi 14](/notes/3-nephi-14)) "is not 'do not judge' but use your agency" righteously. Second, **asking and seeking**: 7:7 ("Ask, and it shall be given you; seek, and ye shall find; knock, and it shall be opened") anchors the December 31–January 6, 2019. "We Are Responsible for Our Own Learning" study — what you find in the scriptures depends on what you seek — and the Prayer — Topical Study notes (7:7–11) on prayer becoming "instinctive and natural."

Third, **doing versus saying**: 7:21–23 ("Not everyone that saith unto me, Lord, Lord… but he that doeth the will of my Father") is central to Preach My Gospel Missionary, where the promise comes only to those who do the Father's will — "be a Christlike missionary, not just do missionary things." The chapter is also cross-referenced in the Abrahamic Covenant and Missionary Work — Topical Study scripture webs, and 7:6 ("neither cast ye your pearls before swine") appears in the June 15–21, 2020. Alma 13–16: "Enter into the Rest of the Lord" notes.

**Context:** Matthew 7 is the final chapter of Jesus's Sermon on the Mount in the New Testament, containing "judge not," "ask, seek, knock," the narrow gate, "by their fruits ye shall know them," and the house built on rock vs. sand.
