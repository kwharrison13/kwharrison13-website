# Hiring

Across Kyle's reading, hiring recurs as the load-bearing mechanism of company quality — the place where culture, talent density, and trust are either set or squandered. The dominant thread is the **bar-raiser** model from the Amazon and PayPal canon. In [The Everything Store](/books/the-everything-store), [Jeff Bezos](/notes/jeff-bezos) told early recruiters to "go hire the smartest people he knew" and treated every hire as something that must raise the bar — the [Talent Vortex](/notes/talent-vortex) effect where Jeff Wilke recruits "the ten smartest people I knew." [The Founders](/books/the-founders) sharpens this into [Max Levchin](/notes/max-levchin)'s rule: "As hire As. Bs hire Cs. So the first B you hire takes the whole company down." [Amazon Unbound](/books/amazon-unbound) frames hiring as active outreach over inbound, and hiring people who fill your gaps rather than mirror you.

The second thread is **hiring on trust and contrarian judgment**. [The Founders](/books/the-founders) presents [Peter Thiel](/notes/peter-thiel) less as a payments founder than as an organizational architect: he favored friends-turned-employees because they "came preinstalled with trust," ran an early no-firing rule, and overruled the team to hire [David Sacks](/notes/david-sacks) precisely because Sacks walked in "guns blazing" against the flagship product — "Peter said, 'I need people here I can scream at.'" Trust produced speed. A third thread comes from [Remote — Office Not Required](/books/remote-office-not-required), which reframes hiring for distributed work: "pre-hiring" mini-projects and contract work as a test-drive, judging on work-not-where, and rejecting riddle-based screens like Microsoft's 1990s "Cult of the Puzzle." [Managers Not MBAs](/books/managers-not-mbas) supplies the dissent — Mintzberg's critique of the "star system" (via Gladwell's "The Talent Myth" and Enron) argues selection by test scores and charisma produces narcissistic managers, and that hiring should reward demonstrated managerial performance instead.

## Where this appears

- [Amazon Unbound](/books/amazon-unbound) — hiring as active outreach over inbound; hiring people who fill your gaps
- [The Everything Store](/books/the-everything-store) — bar-raising mechanism; Bezos wanting high-IQ recruits, every hire raising the bar
- [The Founders](/books/the-founders) — Levchin's "As hire As" talent bar, Thiel's trust-based / no-firing hiring, the Sacks override
- [Remote — Office Not Required](/books/remote-office-not-required) — pre-hiring mini-projects, contract-as-test-drive, judging on work not location
- [Managers Not MBAs](/books/managers-not-mbas) — the "star system" critique; selection by performance, not test scores
- [Peter Thiel](/notes/peter-thiel) — contrarian, bet-on-people hiring that defined the [PayPal Mafia](/notes/paypal-mafia)
