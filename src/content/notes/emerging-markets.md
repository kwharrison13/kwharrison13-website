# Emerging Markets

In Kyle's notes, emerging markets is the geographic / impact lens running through his [Village Capital](/notes/village-capital) sourcing thread. [Village Capital](/notes/village-capital) is an impact-focused venture firm whose sector accelerators span "the US, LatAm, East Africa, and India, with one-off events in West Africa and Europe" — a deliberately global, developing-economy footprint across fintech, education, health, agriculture, and energy. Its investor [Inder Takhar](/notes/inder-takhar) embodies the orientation: his father was an entrepreneur, and "early in his career he worked with micro-entrepreneurs in Indonesia" before covering fintech and energy at Village Capital. The two pages together frame emerging markets as where early-stage, impact-minded venture capital meets developing-world [Entrepreneurship](/notes/entrepreneurship).

**Context:** "Emerging markets" denotes the economies of developing nations (across Latin America, Africa, South and Southeast Asia, and beyond) that are industrializing and integrating into global markets but carry higher growth and higher risk than developed economies. In venture/impact investing it often pairs with financial-inclusion and micro-entrepreneurship theses.
