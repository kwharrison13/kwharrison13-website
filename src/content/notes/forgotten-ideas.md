# Forgotten Ideas

A theme Kyle tagged across his notes on [The Man From The Future](/books/the-man-from-the-future) (the [John von Neumann](/notes/john-von-neumann) biography): rigorous, well-developed work that was dropped not because it was wrong but because it was *ahead of execution capability*. The load-bearing example is self-replicating systems — von Neumann's *Theory of Self-Reproducing Automata* and the 1980 NASA SRS workshop on lunar self-replicating factories — which Kyle marks as #[Forgotten Ideas](/notes/forgotten-ideas): serious science that lay fallow waiting for the rest of the world to catch up. The frame treats history's discard pile as a source of leverage, since an idea that was merely premature can become viable once the surrounding capability arrives.

## Where this appears

- [The Man From The Future](/books/the-man-from-the-future) — Kyle tags self-replicating automata and the NASA lunar-factory workshop as #[Forgotten Ideas](/notes/forgotten-ideas), rigorous work dropped ahead of its time rather than because it failed.
