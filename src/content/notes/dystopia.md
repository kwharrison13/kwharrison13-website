# Dystopia

In Kyle's notes, "dystopia" is the literary lineage he uses to place [Metamorphosis](/books/metamorphosis) — Franz Kafka's modernist short fiction about an individual erased by an inescapable system. The [Metamorphosis](/books/metamorphosis) page frames Kafka as a contemporary of [Aldous Huxley](/notes/aldous-huxley) and [George Orwell](/notes/george-orwell), "whose dystopian fiction shares the early-20th-century anxiety over the individual crushed by social and family systems," and connects the book out to [Brave New World](/books/brave-new-world) (the individual subordinated to a dehumanizing collective) and [1984](/books/1984) (selfhood erased by an inescapable system). The through-line is less about totalitarian state machinery than about the individual being ground down — in Kafka's case, by his own [Family](/notes/family).

**Context:** Dystopia is a literary and thought-experiment genre depicting a society organized around oppression, dehumanization, or systemic dysfunction — the inverse of utopia. The 20th-century canon Kyle cites (Huxley's *Brave New World*, Orwell's *1984*) is its defining strain.
