# First Principles Thinking

**One-line definition:** *Reasoning from physical or mathematical fundamentals rather than by analogy to existing solutions — the conscious refusal to inherit constraints from prior practice when the underlying physics, economics, or math don't require them.*

## How sources describe it

- **[Elon Musk](/notes/elon-musk) (via [JB Straubel](/notes/jb-straubel)):** "The way Elon talks about this is that you always need to start with the first principles of a problem. What are the physics of it? How much time will it take? How much will it cost? How much cheaper can I make it? There's this level of engineering and physics that you need to make judgments about what's possible and interesting."
- **[The Founders](/books/the-founders) (Bhargava's random deposit):** [Sanjay Bhargava](/notes/sanjay-bhargava) solved bank-account verification by reasoning from Shannon's signal/noise theory — generate two tiny random deposits and use the digits as a one-time code. Kyle tags this `#[First Principles Thinking](/notes/first-principles-thinking)`. Engineer [Santosh Janardhan](/notes/santosh-janardhan): "The people who ended up working on this were not domain experts. And that, frankly, is good. They had no preconceived notions; they approached it with very fresh eyes. And converted [fraud] to a tractable, mathematical thing."
- **[Amazon Unbound](/books/amazon-unbound) ([Dave Clark](/notes/dave-clark)):** "I'm a simplifier. I can take complicated stuff and figure out how to boil it down into what you need to do to actually make it big." Kyle tags this `#[First Principles](/notes/first-principles-thinking)`.
- **[The Founders](/books/the-founders) ([Elon Musk](/notes/elon-musk)):** "A lot of times the question is harder than the answer, and if you can properly phrase the question, then the answer is the easy part." A first-principles instinct on problem framing, not just solution finding.

## Where it shows up

- [Elon Musk](/notes/elon-musk) — Musk's hallmark engineering posture; underwrites both SpaceX cost analysis and Tesla architecture
- [The Founders](/books/the-founders) — operational discipline that produced multiple PayPal innovations from non-domain-experts
- [Amazon Unbound](/books/amazon-unbound) — Bezos's "the question is whether it can work tomorrow if the cash flow funds it" reasoning; Clark's "simplifier" identity

## Conflicts

No source-to-source conflicts found. Different operators apply first principles in different domains (Musk on physics; Bhargava on signal theory; Clark on operations) but the underlying move — refusing to inherit constraints from analogy — is consistent.
