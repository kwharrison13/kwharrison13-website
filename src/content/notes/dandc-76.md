# D&C 76

Doctrine and Covenants section 76 — "The Vision," the revelation to [Joseph Smith](/notes/joseph-smith) and Sidney Rigdon describing the three degrees of glory (celestial, terrestrial, telestial). It threads through Kyle's gospel-study notes on two fronts: as **doctrine of exaltation and salvation**, and as a **case study in receiving revelation**.

On the doctrine: Plan of Salvation — Topical Study uses 76 for "the purpose of our birth was to become Gods ourselves," for the war in heaven ([Satan](/notes/satan) as "a son of the morning" over whom we all wept), and for verse 24's "begotten sons and daughters of God." Christian Reformation, Zion, and Seekers reads 76 as a richer, more attractive form of [Universalism](/notes/universalism) than Calvinist or universalist alternatives. The **valiant testimony** verses (76:75, 79) anchor Obedience — Topical Study (the terrestrial kingdom for those "not valiant in the testimony of Jesus") and Faith — Topical Study ([Neal A. Maxwell](/notes/neal-a-maxwell) on becoming steadily more like Christ), while Testimony — Topical Study cites 76:22–24 (the witness "that he lives" added by Jacob, Amulek, Peter, Joseph Smith and Sidney Rigdon). On revelation: June 22–28, 2020. Alma 17–22: "I Will Make an Instrument of Thee" recounts that by the time Joseph received section 76 he was so accustomed to the Spirit that he did not collapse from exhaustion as Sidney did — "Brother Sidney is not as used to it as I am."

## Where this appears

- Plan of Salvation — Topical Study — becoming Gods, the war in heaven, "begotten sons and daughters of God" (76:24).
- Testimony — Topical Study — 76:22–24, the witnesses who add "that he lives."
- Christian Reformation, Zion, and Seekers — a richer form of Universalism than Calvinism or universalism.
- Obedience — Topical Study — 76:79, the terrestrial kingdom for those not valiant in the testimony of Jesus.
- Faith — Topical Study — 76:75, 79, Maxwell on emulative character.
- June 22–28, 2020. Alma 17–22: "I Will Make an Instrument of Thee" — Joseph's familiarity with the Spirit when receiving the vision.
