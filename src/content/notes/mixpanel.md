# Mixpanel

Mixpanel surfaces in Kyle's notes as a reference point for product analytics in the product-led-growth (PLG) era. In [Product Led Growth: The End User Era](/books/product-led-growth-the-end-user-era), it is cited as a product analytics company whose 2017 report analyzed 572 products and 50 billion events, finding that the top 10% of products outperformed the average by nearly 100% on retention — the kind of benchmarking data the PLG playbook leans on to argue that product usage, not sales touch, is the leading indicator that matters.

In the Sprig fka UserLeap - Diligence notes, Mixpanel appears alongside Amplitude as one of the common, established product-data tools teams already run — context for assessing where a newer product-research tool (Sprig/UserLeap) fits in a stack where accessing product data without going through data science (complex SQL/Python pipelines) is a recurring pain point.

**Context:** Mixpanel is a San Francisco-based product analytics platform (founded 2009) that lets companies track user behavior and events to measure engagement, conversion, and retention. It is frequently mentioned in the same breath as Amplitude as a category leader in self-serve product analytics.
