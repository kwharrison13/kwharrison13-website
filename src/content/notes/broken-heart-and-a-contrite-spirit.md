# Broken heart and a contrite spirit

A recurring anchor in Kyle's scripture study: the inward offering that replaced the [Law of Moses](/notes/law-of-moses)'s burnt sacrifices. When Christ appears to the Nephites He declares the old sacrifices "done away," and in their place asks "for a sacrifice unto me a broken heart and a contrite spirit" (3 Nephi 9:20) — promising to baptize with fire and the Holy Ghost whoever comes to Him that way (September 14–20, 2020. 3 Nephi 8–11: "Arise and Come Forth unto Me"). Kyle's notes gather a cross-reference cluster around this phrase — Moroni 6:2, D&C 20:37, D&C 59:8, Psalm 34:18, Psalm 51:17, Isaiah 57:15 — treating it as the standing scriptural definition of what God asks of the repentant.

[Ezra Taft Benson](/notes/ezra-taft-benson) frames the broken heart as "the absolute prerequisite for true repentance," distinguishing it from worldly regret: it grows out of [Godly Sorrow](/notes/godly-sorrow), the keen awareness that one's sins caused the sinless Savior to suffer, not mere remorse over consequences or getting caught (A Mighty Change of Heart — Ezra Taft Benson). [Jeffrey R. Holland](/notes/jeffrey-r-holland) casts it as the disciple's offering "in similitude of His offering" — Christ's own yielding in Gethsemane ("not my will, but thine, be done") is the pattern, and our broken heart is the lesser cup we accept in obedience (Therefore, What — Jeffrey R. Holland). Kyle's notes also tie it downstream to the [Spirit of Humility](/notes/spirit-of-humility) (humility being "essential before you can have a broken heart and a contrite spirit," per General Conference — October 2021) and connect it polemically to [Cancel Culture is the Anti-Repentance](/notes/cancel-culture-is-the-anti-repentance).

**Context:** "A broken heart and a contrite spirit" is a phrase from the Hebrew Bible (Psalm 51:17) carried into Latter-day Saint theology, where it names the inward sacrifice God requires after the law of animal sacrifice was fulfilled in Christ.

## Where this appears

- A Mighty Change of Heart — Ezra Taft Benson — Benson defines it as the prerequisite for true repentance, growing out of godly sorrow; Kyle's cross-reference cluster lives here
- Therefore, What — Jeffrey R. Holland — the student's offering "in similitude of His offering," patterned on Christ's submission in Gethsemane
- September 14–20, 2020. 3 Nephi 8–11: "Arise and Come Forth unto Me" — the 3 Nephi 9:20 source text where it replaces the Law of Moses sacrifices
- General Conference — October 2021 — humility presented as essential before one can have a broken heart; offered as a sacrifice
- Fulfilling the Measure of Our Creation - September 5, 2021 — pleading before God "with a broken and humble heart" to confirm one's divine inheritance
