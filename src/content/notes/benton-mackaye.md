# Benton MacKaye

Benton MacKaye originated the idea of the Appalachian Trail, as recounted in [A Walk in the Woods](/books/a-walk-in-the-woods). He pitched the concept to Charles Whitaker, who published the proposal in the *Journal of the American Institute of Architects* in October 1921. In Kyle's highlights, a hiking trail was only part of MacKaye's grand vision: he imagined the AT as a thread connecting a network of mountaintop work camps where "pale, depleted urban workers in the thousands" would engage in healthful, selfless toil and refresh themselves on nature — complete with hostels, inns, seasonal study centers, and eventually permanent "self-owning" woodland villages sustained by cooperative, non-industrial forestry, farming, and crafts. MacKaye described the whole as "a retreat from profit," a utopian, almost communitarian notion that one biographer noted others saw as "smacking of Bolshevism."

**Context:** Benton MacKaye (1879–1975) was an American forester, regional planner, and conservationist who proposed the Appalachian Trail in 1921 and later co-founded the Wilderness Society.
