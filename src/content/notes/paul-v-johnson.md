# Paul V. Johnson

An LDS author quoted repeatedly in Kyle's topical study The Reasons The Spirit Withdraws on the theme that trials are the furnace that purifies and no suffering patiently endured is wasted. The quoted lines include "The furnace of affliction helps purify even the very best of saints by burning away the dross in their lives and leaving behind pure gold" (tied to Isaiah 48:10 and 1 Nephi 20:10), "The only way to see the view is to make the climb," and "growth cannot come by taking the easy way." His teaching frames hard tests as preparation for "remarkable events and tremendous growth," sitting alongside [Orson F. Whitney](/notes/orson-f-whitney) and [Richard G. Scott](/notes/richard-g-scott) in the study's affliction-as-purification thread.

**Context:** Paul V. Johnson is a General Authority Seventy of The Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-day Saints; the lines Kyle quotes come from his April 2011 General Conference address "More Than Conquerors Through Him That Loved Us."

## Where this appears

- The Reasons The Spirit Withdraws — quoted multiple times on affliction as a purifying furnace and on growth requiring struggle, anchoring the study's "no suffering is wasted" section.
