# 3 Nephi 21

A Book of Mormon chapter, one of the most heavily annotated in the Come Follow Me study of 3 Nephi 20–26. Kyle traces several covenant threads through it: the Father gives a sign tied to the [Gathering of Israel](/notes/gathering-of-israel) and the establishing of Zion, with this land set up as a free people — the [Destiny of America](/notes/destiny-of-america) (21:1–4); a [Marvelous Work](/notes/marvelous-work) done "for my sake," tying to the [Intercession of Christ](/notes/intercession-of-christ) and the recurring "[For My Sake](/notes/for-my-sake)" phrasing (21:9); graven images cut off, read not as literal statues but as "anything you put before God" (21:17); the [Unity of the Faith](/notes/unity-of-the-faith) and the limits of imperfect language, pointing toward the [Adamic Language](/notes/adamic-language) (21:20); and the building of the [New Jerusalem](/notes/new-jerusalem) by the remnant of Jacob (21:23–24).

**Context:** 3 Nephi 21 records the risen Christ unfolding to the Nephites the latter-day sign of the covenant's fulfillment — the gathering of Israel, a marvelous work among the Gentiles, and the establishment of the New Jerusalem on the American continent.
