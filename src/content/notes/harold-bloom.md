# Harold Bloom

In [Joseph Smith — Rough Stone Rolling](/books/joseph-smith-rough-stone-rolling), the literary critic Harold Bloom is quoted calling [Joseph Smith](/notes/joseph-smith)'s The King Follett Sermon "one of the truly remarkable sermons ever preached in America." Bowman cites Bloom's assessment to underscore the stature of the sermon — delivered at the April conference, never canonized, and known only through the overlapping notes of four diarists — as the culminating statement of Joseph Smith's theology.

**Context:** Harold Bloom (1930–2019) was an American literary critic and Yale professor known for *The Anxiety of Influence* and *The Western Canon*; in *The American Religion* he wrote admiringly of Joseph Smith's religious imagination.
