# Land and Expand

"Land and expand" is the go-to-market motion at the heart of [Product Led Growth (PLG)](/notes/product-led-growth-plg), catalogued in [Product Led Growth: The End User Era](/books/product-led-growth-the-end-user-era) as part of the PLG vocabulary alongside PQLs, viral coefficient, and North Star metrics. The book frames it as one of the most appealing attributes of product-led companies: a "maniacal focus on selling directly to the end user of the product and a strong understanding of an entry point to **land** and then **expand** within an organization." Rather than a top-down enterprise sale, the product enters through a single user or team (the "land"), then grows seat-by-seat and team-by-team as adoption spreads (the "expand") — making the entry point and the expansion path critical information for any SaaS company.

The frame ties directly to [Net Revenue Retention](/notes/net-revenue-retention) and the [Consumerization of the Enterprise](/notes/consumerization-of-the-enterprise): a successful land-and-expand motion shows up as accounts that grow in value over time, the mechanism behind best-in-class NRR. Kyle flagged it among the book's useful taxonomy even while rating the book itself low (2/5) as a curated set of practitioner posts rather than a unifying argument.

**Context:** "Land and expand" is a standard B2B SaaS sales/growth strategy: win a small initial deployment cheaply (often via freemium or a single team), prove value, then grow revenue within the same customer through more seats, upsells, and cross-sells. It is closely associated with bottom-up, product-led companies like Slack, Datadog, and Atlassian.
