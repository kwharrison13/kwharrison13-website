# Atlas Shrugged

In Kyle's notes, *Atlas Shrugged* is referenced from [Anthem](/books/anthem) as "[Ayn Rand](/notes/ayn-rand)'s later, fuller statement of the individualist ethic" — the mature expression of the same philosophy that [Anthem](/books/anthem) sketches in miniature. The connection ties the two books through their shared ego-over-collective creed: the line "I owe nothing to my brothers, nor do I gather debts from them," which closes Anthem with the word EGO. Anthem is grouped in Kyle's notes with the dystopia cluster ([Brave New World](/books/brave-new-world), [Animal Farm](/books/animal-farm), [1984](/books/1984)) of engineered or coerced collectivism, with Atlas Shrugged positioned as Rand's full-length elaboration of the individualist counter-ethic.

**Context:** *Atlas Shrugged* (1957) is Ayn Rand's longest novel and the fullest fictional statement of her philosophy of Objectivism, dramatizing a "strike" by the world's productive innovators against a collectivist society. Kyle has not catalogued it as a read book; it is referenced as a cited/related work.
