# Tempted Above

In Kyle's Alma 13–16 — Enter into the Rest of the Lord study, "tempted above" tags the question of whether we truly "have the strength to overcome any temptation and nothing is ever 'too much' for us." The anchor scripture is [1 Corinthians 10](/notes/1-corinthians-10):13 — "There hath no temptation taken you but such as is common to man: but God is faithful, who will not suffer you to be tempted above that ye are able; but will with the temptation also make a way to escape, that ye may be able to bear it." The phrase is filed alongside [Gospel Topics to Explore](/notes/gospel-topics-to-explore), marking it as an open question Kyle wants to study further rather than a settled conclusion.

**Context:** "Tempted above that ye are able" is a much-quoted line from Paul's first epistle to the Corinthians, commonly read as a promise that God limits trials to what a person can bear and always provides a way of escape.

## Where this appears

- Alma 13–16 — Enter into the Rest of the Lord — raised as a gospel topic to explore, paired with 1 Corinthians 10:13 on whether any temptation can exceed our God-given capacity to bear it.
