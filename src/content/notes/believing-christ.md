# Believing Christ

A reference to Stephen E. Robinson's 1992 Ensign piece (and the book of the same name) on internalizing how the [Atonement](/notes/atonement) works, cited in Kyle's Christ Is Risen; Faith in Him Will Move Mountains — Russell M. Nelson study under the heading "Trust Him." The core distinction Kyle pulls forward is between believing *in* Christ and *believing* Christ — taking him at his word about what he can do for you. The quoted passage recounts Robinson and his wife Janet weeping as they grasped the difference: "I've always believed that Jesus suffered and died for me… But now I realize that he must save me from myself, from my sins and my weaknesses." The study pairs this with [2 Nephi 33](/notes/2-nephi-33):10–11 — "hearken unto these words and believe in Christ… for they are the words of Christ" — framing belief in Christ and belief in his words as inseparable.

**Context:** *Believing Christ: A Practical Approach to the Atonement* is a widely read 1992 book by Latter-day Saint scholar Stephen E. Robinson, expanding his earlier Ensign article; its central argument is that many Christians believe in Christ without fully trusting his promise to redeem them personally.
