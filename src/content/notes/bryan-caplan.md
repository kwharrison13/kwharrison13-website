# Bryan Caplan

Economist named among those [Talent](/books/talent) (by [Tyler Cowen](/notes/tyler-cowen) and Daniel Gross) thanks for "useful comments and discussion and assistance" — appearing in the acknowledgments alongside figures like [Patrick Collison](/notes/patrick-collison), [Peter Thiel](/notes/peter-thiel), Robin Hanson, [Marc Andreessen](/notes/marc-andreessen), and [Sam Altman](/notes/sam-altman). Within the wiki his only grounded reference so far is this acknowledgment, placing him in the book's intellectual orbit rather than as a subject of its argument.

**Context:** Bryan Caplan is an economist at George Mason University known for *The Myth of the Rational Voter*, *The Case Against Education*, and *Open Borders*. He works in the libertarian, public-choice tradition and is a prominent rationalist/EconLog blogger.
