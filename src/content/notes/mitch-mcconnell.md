# Mitch McConnell

In [Romney — A Reckoning](/books/romney-a-reckoning), McConnell is the Senate Majority Leader during [Mitt Romney](/notes/mitt-romney)'s impeachment-trial period, and the book uses him to expose how the proceeding was understood internally as politics rather than adjudication. In one scene, after Romney thanks him for sticking up against [Donald Trump](/notes/donald-trump), McConnell deflects — "It wasn't for you so much as for him... He's an idiot... How stupid do you have to be to not realize that you shouldn't attack your jurors?" In another, at a GOP caucus meeting, McConnell sneers at the thinness of the House's impeachment articles and tells the senators that whether or not they call themselves "jurors," the trial "was not really a trial at all": "This is a political process," and it was thus appropriate for them to behave like politicians.

**Context:** Mitch McConnell is a longtime U.S. Senator from Kentucky who served as Senate Majority/Minority Leader; he was Majority Leader during Trump's first impeachment trial in early 2020, the period this book covers.
