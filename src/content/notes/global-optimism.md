# Global Optimism

[Christiana Figueres](/notes/christiana-figueres)'s climate organization. It appears in Kyle's Watershed diligence notes as the affiliation of Figueres — described there as the **architect of the Paris Agreement** — who gave a keynote at a Watershed-adjacent climate event. The keynote recounts how, after the failed 2009 Copenhagen talks, Figueres's first move on taking over the UN climate negotiations was to commission a third-party analysis of everything that went wrong; her team pored over the resulting 300 pages for years, ultimately producing the unanimous 195-country Paris Agreement, agreed down to "every paragraph, word, and comma."

The notes quote her central message — *"Every company inherits the carbon footprint of their suppliers,"* and a call to move "from lamenting to action" — which dovetails with the Watershed thesis that every company will need to measure, reduce, and offset emissions across its supply chain. (Note: a separate mention of "Global Optimism" in Kyle's post-mortem research refers to the *concept* of optimism, not this organization.)

**Context:** Global Optimism is the climate-advocacy organization co-founded by Christiana Figueres (former UNFCCC Executive Secretary) and Tom Rivett-Carnac, known for the book and podcast *The Future We Choose* and its "stubborn optimism" framing of climate action.
