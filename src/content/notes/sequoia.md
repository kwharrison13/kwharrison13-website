# Sequoia

Sequoia is one of the most-referenced venture firms across Kyle's corpus, appearing on both the **history-of-VC** side and the **live-deal** side. As a historical node it is profiled in [VC — An American History](/books/vc) (Don Valentine founding Sequoia Capital in 1972, alongside Arthur Rock and Tom Perkins as the figures who compounded the Bay Area's regional advantage) and in [The Power Law](/books/the-power-law-venture-capital-and-the-making-of-the-new-future), where the firm anchors two of the book's recurring lessons: Michael Moritz's patience on Yahoo ("the secret was just learning to be a little patient" — Yahoo generated more gains than all of Sequoia's prior investments combined) and Roelof Botha's decision-postmortem discipline. The Power Law also captures Moritz's recruiting philosophy — no interest in "comfortable executives... not hungry enough... not prepared to become rookies again."

On the deal side Sequoia recurs as a co-investor in Kyle's diligence work. Watershed Climate Diligence — 2021 notes Sequoia co-led Watershed's $14.7M Series A (at $56.7M post, with [Kleiner Perkins](/notes/kleiner-perkins) and [Laurene Powell Jobs](/notes/laurene-powell-jobs), July 2020). Salt Security Diligence — 2021 records Sequoia's $30M Series B into [Salt Security](/notes/salt-security) (Dec 2020). And on [Instacart](/notes/instacart), Sequoia is cited as one of the early winners whose spectacular returns were *dependent* on later, largely unprofitable capital — the "capital dependency" point. [Doug Leone](/notes/doug-leone) surfaces as a key Sequoia figure.

**Context:** Sequoia Capital is a venture firm founded by Don Valentine in 1972, with early bets on Apple, Cisco, Google, Yahoo, and many others. It is among the canonical "power law" funds whose returns are concentrated in a small number of outlier outcomes.
