# Cresta.ai

Cresta.ai is a real-time AI agent-coaching platform for contact centers, and the subject of Kyle's detailed VC diligence in Cresta.ai - Diligence (six expert calls plus Tegus transcripts and a McKinsey piece). It uses ML/AI on unstructured conversation data to coach agents **mid-conversation** — suggesting next-best-actions and responses across chat and voice — learning from a company's best agents to lift the rest, with a supervisor-facing layer surfacing quality (not just quantity) metrics. It competes in a crowded "agent assist / conversation intelligence" field against [Observe.ai](/notes/observe-ai), [Balto.ai](/notes/balto-ai), [Gong](/notes/gong), [Chorus](/notes/chorus), and [CallMiner](/notes/callminer), under the looming question of whether [CCaaS](/notes/ccaas) platforms ([Five9](/notes/five9), [Genesys](/notes/genesys), [Amazon Connect](/notes/amazon-connect)) eventually absorb the category.

The diligence's consistent finding is that **product/AI quality is Cresta's edge** while its weaknesses are commercial and operational. [Apple ($AAPL)](/notes/apple) (a ~3-year customer covering ~35% of agents) summed it up: "Observe might give quicker responses but Cresta is better responses... all the metrics were better with Cresta across the board" — and judged Cresta could more easily build Observe's analytics than Observe could build Cresta's AI. Marquee logos describe clear land-and-expand arcs toward $1M+ contracts ([Samsung](/notes/samsung) reported CSAT +20% and support-to-sales conversion +50%; [Adobe](/notes/adobe) tied ROI to chat save-rates; [Neiman Marcus](/notes/neiman-marcus) launched on chat with voice as the expansion path). The differentiating axes surfaced were **real-time live coaching** (vs. post-call QA), product/AI quality, a service-and-sales orientation, and a historically interaction/volume-based pricing model.

The bear case is equally grounded: Cresta **lost a deal at Advantage Communications** ([Randy Clapp](/notes/randy-clapp)) purely over lack of self-serve admin control (buyers couldn't build/edit playbooks without a ticket), [Adobe](/notes/adobe) called the sales team "borderline toxic" and said replacing Cresta "would be quite easy right now," buyers repeatedly preferred competitors' per-seat pricing over interaction pricing, and multiple experts expect a [CCaaS](/notes/ccaas) platform to buy and fold the category in. Agent **trust/adoption** also recurs as the hard part — [Neiman Marcus](/notes/neiman-marcus) initially told agents to ignore it.

**Context:** Cresta is a San Francisco-based contact-center AI company founded in 2017 out of Stanford (co-founded by Zayd Enam, with AI pioneer Sebastian Thrun as a co-founder/chairman), backed by Greylock, Sequoia, and Andreessen Horowitz.
