# Habit #5

Shorthand for [Stephen Covey](/notes/stephen-covey)'s fifth habit from *The 7 Habits of Highly Effective People* — "seek first to understand, then to be understood." In Kyle's reading of [Benjamin Franklin — An American Life](/books/benjamin-franklin-an-american-life), it is the lens he applies to Franklin's conviction that being *"disputatious"* was *"a very bad habit"* because contradicting people produced "disgusts and perhaps enmities." Kyle's note on that passage reads simply: *"'Seek first to understand, then to be understood' — [Stephen Covey](/notes/stephen-covey), Habit #5."* The page's synthesis treats this anti-disputation, persuasion-through-humility stance as the load-bearing virtue that runs from the Junto straight to Franklin's [Compromise](/notes/compromise)-first posture at the Constitutional Convention.

**Context:** "Seek first to understand, then to be understood" is Habit 5 in Stephen Covey's *The 7 Habits of Highly Effective People* (1989), advocating empathic listening before advancing one's own position.
