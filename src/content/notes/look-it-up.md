# Look It Up

"Look It Up" is one of Kyle's reading-annotation conventions rather than a subject in its own right: he tags a highlight with `#look-it-up` (or a `> **Kyle:** Look it up` note) to flag a person, study, source, or claim he wants to chase down later. It recurs across three book pages. In [The Self-Driven Child](/books/the-self-driven-child) he applies it to specific sources worth tracking — Lori Gottlieb's Atlantic article on too-attuned parents, and a researcher's "test of adultness." In [Poor Charlie's Almanack](/books/poor-charlie-s-almanack) he attaches it to [Charlie Munger](/notes/charlie-munger)'s references to "a rule of psychology" and the mental-models repertoire he wants to pin down. In the [Berkshire Hathaway Annual Letters](/books/berkshire-hathaway-annual-letters) he flags John Medlin's "chain letter in reverse" line about value-destroying acquisitions. The common thread: a deliberate to-do marker for follow-up research, a personal counterpart to the wiki's `articles-to-read` / `books-to-read` queues embedded directly in the highlights.
