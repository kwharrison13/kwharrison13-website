# Chris Purpura

VP of Customer Service at [Neiman Marcus](/notes/neiman-marcus), where he interviewed as an expert/customer reference in Kyle's Cresta.ai - Diligence (call dated November 30, 2021). He had been running Customer Service at Neiman Marcus for ~5 years — a 500+ seat Customer Care org supporting the Neiman Marcus, Last Call, Horchow, and Bergdorf Goodman brands, ramping to ~900 agents at the holidays plus BPOs — and previously held the same VP of Customer Service role at [Groupon](/notes/groupon), which he uses as a scale reference ("one of [Zendesk](/notes/zendesk)'s largest customers").

Purpura's frame for adopting [Cresta.ai](/notes/cresta-ai) is cautious and cost-center-driven: he sees real-time agent coaching as a way to **get comfortable with AI/ML before letting bots loose** on customers, and is "not a fan of letting unproven tech loose." As of the call the rollout was up and running but not fully operational — agents were largely told to ignore Cresta because they didn't yet trust its answers ("output just wasn't ready"), partly because the deployment collided with a [Twilio Flex](/notes/twilio-flex) voice switch and the holidays. He negotiated a low-six-figure live-chat contract **per-seat** (volume too variable for interaction-based pricing), with a ceiling in the mid-to-high six figures, and saw Cresta's real-time **voice** offering as the appealing expansion path given that luxury customers skew to phone.
