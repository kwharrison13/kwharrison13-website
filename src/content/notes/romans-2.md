# Romans 2

A chapter of Paul's epistle to the Romans that Kyle returns to for the idea of an innate moral law. In his Knowledge — Topical Study, Romans 2:11–15 supplies "God's law written in every heart" — the Gentiles who, "having not the law, ARE A LAW UNTO THEMSELVES... show the works of the law written in their hearts, their conscience also bearing witness." Kyle threads this with the parables of the two debtors and the unmerciful servant to resolve his "Atonement: Revelation or Experimentation" quandary — concluding "we need not more sin but more understanding, that our gratitude might increase." In Miscellaneous Scriptures the chapter is logged more briefly for Romans 2:23 — "everyone falls short."

**Context:** Romans 2 is part of Paul's argument in the New Testament that both Jew and Gentile stand accountable before God — the Gentiles by the moral law "written in their hearts" (conscience), the Jews by the law of Moses — leading toward his thesis that all have sinned and need grace.

## Where this appears

- Knowledge — Topical Study — Romans 2:11–15 cited for the moral law "written in every heart," woven into Kyle's argument that gaining knowledge requires more understanding rather than more sin.
- Miscellaneous Scriptures — Romans 2:23 noted for "everyone falls short."
