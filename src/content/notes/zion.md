# Zion

Across Kyle's gospel-study and reading notes, Zion is the gathering ideal of a celestial society — a community with "no rich and no poor" — and the throughline is that it is *built by a process*, not handed down by a snap of God's fingers. The Process of Zion develops this directly from the book of [4 Nephi](/notes/4-nephi) as a model society: the world will not see God descend to make it celestial; rather, *we* make it celestial by learning and living the celestial law, dotting the earth with [temples](/notes/temple) so people can learn those laws. It ranks the kingdoms by motivation — celestial = *want to* (consecrated), terrestrial = *need to*, telestial = *have to* — and uses Enoch's and Melchizedek's Zions as celestial cities a telestial world "cannot abide." [Working Toward Zion](/books/working-toward-zion) carries the same ideal into modern economics: Zion as "a Saintly community with no rich and no poor," set against the homelessness and inequality the book documents along the Wasatch Front, with [Consecration](/notes/consecration) and the [United Order](/notes/united-order) as the mechanism — "financier for the poor… for the welfare of Zion."

The historical sources show Zion as a literal place and a source of conflict. Early Mormonism frames [Joseph Smith](/notes/joseph-smith)'s plan "to build [Zion](/notes/zion) into a new world order" as something that "guaranteed conflict" in 1830s Missouri — what enraged the Jacksonians was the Saints' obedience and their teaching "without respect of persons." [American Zion](/books/american-zion) takes its title from the Jackson County, Missouri original — the county "in which Zion was to be built," where the gathering ideal collided with frontier politics, vigilante groups, and the "tyranny of the majority."

## Where this appears

- The Process of Zion — Zion as a process built on celestial law, modeled on 4 Nephi; the celestial/terrestrial/telestial framework of desire
- [Working Toward Zion](/books/working-toward-zion) — Zion as a "no rich and no poor" economic ideal; consecration and the United Order against modern inequality
- [American Zion](/books/american-zion) — the Jackson County, Missouri original that gives the book its title; the gathering ideal in collision with frontier politics
- Early Mormonism — Joseph Smith's plan to build Zion into "a new world order" as a guaranteed source of conflict
- October 19–25, 2020. 3 Nephi 27–4 Nephi: "There Could Not Be a Happier People" — the 4 Nephi model society where Zion is realized
