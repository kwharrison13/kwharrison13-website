# J. Reuben Clark

A counselor in the First Presidency of the LDS Church whose aphorisms recur in Kyle's gospel reading. He is quoted in [An Insider's View of Mormon Origins](/books/an-insider-s-view-of-mormon-origins) for a provocative defense of inquiry: "If we have the truth, [it] cannot be harmed by investigation. If we have not the truth, it ought to be harmed" — a line Kyle files alongside his broader truth-seeking thread.

Clark's second recurring quote sits at the center of Godly Characteristics of the Master's [Knowledge](/notes/knowledge) section: from his April 1934 Conference address, "There is spiritual learning just as there is material learning, and the one without the other is not complete; yet... if I could have only one sort of learning, that which I would take would be the learning of the spirit." Kyle uses it (paired with a [Spencer W. Kimball](/notes/spencer-w-kimball) passage) to argue that secular learning must be balanced by — and sequenced behind — spiritual learning. He also appears in [Chad's Big Quote Book](/notes/chad-s-big-quote-book), the inherited compilation of attributed quotes.

**Context:** J. Reuben Clark, Jr. (1871–1961) was a U.S. diplomat (Under Secretary of State, Ambassador to Mexico) who served as a counselor in the First Presidency of The Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-day Saints from 1933 to 1961.
