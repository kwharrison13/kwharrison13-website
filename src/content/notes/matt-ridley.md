# Matt Ridley

Science writer and author whose work surfaces in Kyle's reading around optimism and human progress. He wrote [The Rational Optimist](/notes/the-rational-optimist), cited in [The Psychology of Money](/books/the-psychology-of-money) for its argument about the cultural status of pessimism: [Morgan Housel](/notes/morgan-housel) quotes Ridley's observation that "a constant drumbeat of pessimism usually drowns out any triumphalist song" — say the world is getting better and you're called naïve; predict imminent catastrophe and "you may expect a McArthur genius award or even the Nobel Peace Prize." Within Psychology of Money, the reference supports Housel's broader point about why frightening narratives feel smarter and spread faster than optimistic ones. Ridley's [Genome](/notes/genome) (The Autobiography of a Species in 23 Chapters) also sits in Kyle's anti-library reading queue.

**Context:** Matt Ridley is a British science writer and member of the House of Lords whose books — *Genome*, *The Rational Optimist*, *The Red Queen* — argue that human prosperity is driven by trade and the exchange of ideas, and that long-run trends justify optimism despite persistent cultural pessimism.
