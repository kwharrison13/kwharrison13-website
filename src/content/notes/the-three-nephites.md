# The Three Nephites

In Kyle's 3 Nephi 27–4 Nephi study, the three Nephites are listed among the chapter's focus themes as "the three Nephites who tarry" — the disciples Christ permits to remain on earth without tasting death. They appear in the study's framing of Christ naming his church and the nearly 200 years of [Zion](/notes/zion) peace and [Fulness of Joy](/notes/fulness-of-joy) that followed his visit, before the [pride cycle](/notes/the-pride-cycle) returns.

**Context:** In the Book of Mormon (3 Nephi 28), three of the twelve Nephite disciples ask Jesus that they might tarry on earth to bring souls to him until his second coming; he grants the request and they are transfigured so as not to taste death. They became a folk-tradition figure within Latter-day Saint culture as wandering ministers who appear in moments of need.
