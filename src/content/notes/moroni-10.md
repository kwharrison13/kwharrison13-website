# Moroni 10

The final chapter of the Book of Mormon, and the source of Moroni's Promise — the invitation in [Moroni 10](/notes/moroni-10):3–5 to remember how merciful the Lord has been, ponder it, and "ask God, the Eternal Father, in the name of Christ, if these things are not true," with a sincere heart, real intent, and faith in Christ, that "by the power of the Holy Ghost ye may know the truth of all things." Across Kyle's gospel study this chapter functions as the Book of Mormon's closing argument: in the December 14–20, 2020 Come Follow Me study he reads the promise phrase by phrase — remembering mercy humbles the heart, pondering lets facts "sink into your soul," and the promise is not a demand to *prove* the book true but an invitation to let the Lord *confirm* truth already in the heart.

In Moroni's Exhortations, Kyle frames the chapter as [Moroni](/notes/moroni)'s eight strong invitations to act ([Moroni 10](/notes/moroni-10):2): remember, ponder, ask ([Moroni 10](/notes/moroni-10):3–4), then deny not God's power or gifts, remember their source, and come unto Christ ([Moroni 10](/notes/moroni-10):7, 8, 18, 27–34) — most of them rooted in the word "remember," which [Boyd K. Packer](/notes/boyd-k-packer) called the most important word in the English language. The recurring thesis: establishing that the book is true is the *beginning* of the conversation, not the end; we do not become perfect and then come to Christ, but come unto Christ and are perfected *in Him*. The chapter is also a primary text on Spiritual Gifts — in that study Kyle points to [Moroni 10](/notes/moroni-10) (alongside [1 Corinthians 12](/notes/1-corinthians-12)) as the place to look for the gift that is yours and begin magnifying it.

**Context:** Moroni 10 is the concluding chapter of the Book of Mormon, written by the prophet Moroni as he sealed up the record. Verses 3–5 — often called "Moroni's Promise" — are widely cited in The Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-day Saints as the scriptural basis for gaining a testimony of the book through prayer.
