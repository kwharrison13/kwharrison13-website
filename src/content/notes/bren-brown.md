# Brené Brown

Brené Brown is the author of [Braving The Wilderness](/books/braving-the-wilderness), which Kyle reads through the lens of his nuance-and-honesty discipline. The book's threads — authenticity, vulnerability, and belonging — show up in passages like Brown's account of a friend catching her "floating above my life, watching it and studying it, rather than living it" instead of being present. The wiki connects her work to [The Death of Expertise](/books/the-death-of-expertise) (the "everyone has a fact-based opinion on everything" failure she names directly), [The Courage To Be Disliked](/books/the-courage-to-be-disliked) (internalization over external validation), and [Discipline Is Destiny](/books/discipline-is-destiny) (daily practice in the face of social cost).

Brown also appears as a source on the [Voice of the People](/notes/voice-of-the-people) concept page, cited for the note that group loyalty built on shared belief makes dissent risky — a bridge between her belonging-and-vulnerability framework and Kyle's recurring interest in [Mob Rule](/notes/mob-rule), [Democracy](/notes/democracy), and how social cohesion can suppress honest disagreement.

**Context:** Brené Brown is an American research professor at the University of Houston known for her work on shame, vulnerability, courage, and empathy. She is the author of best-selling books including *Daring Greatly*, *Braving the Wilderness*, and *Atlas of the Heart*, and her 2010 TEDx talk "The Power of Vulnerability" is among the most-viewed TED talks.
