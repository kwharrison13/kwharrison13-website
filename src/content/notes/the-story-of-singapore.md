# The Story of Singapore

**Author:** [David Perell](/notes/david-perell)
**URL:** https://www.perell.com/blog/story-of-singapore
**One-line:** *How [Singapore](/notes/singapore) leaped from third-world to first in 53 years under [Lee Kuan Yew](/notes/lee-kuan-yew) — by running clean systems, courting global capital, paying officials world-class salaries, and choosing discipline over the "exuberance of democracy."*

## Key claims

- **Singapore went third-world to first in 53 years**, despite having no natural resources, a diverse immigrant population, and hostile neighbors at independence in 1965.
- **Differentiation through reliability.** Lee's strategy: "They are not clean systems; we run clean systems… We become reliable and credible to investors." World-class infrastructure and English-educated staff made it a trusted trade hub.
- **[Scarcity](/notes/scarcity) was a blessing.** Lacking resources forced Singapore to "think in terms of a global network" and to be a stable, valuable trade partner; it bucked the third-world trend by inviting multinationals.
- **Discipline over democracy.** Lee: "what a country needs to develop is discipline more than democracy." Popular democracy incentivizes short-termism and never-ending auctions of giveaways paid for by the next generation; the presidential system rewards TV-ready marketers over governors.
- **No "all-you-can-eat" welfare state.** Lee critiqued the West's "Buffet Syndrome"; in Singapore, families — not government — are the safety net, and the government has run surpluses for decades.
- **Small, efficient, high-quality government.** Singapore spends ~5% of GDP on healthcare and ~3.3% on education yet gets world-class outcomes; America spends ~17% on healthcare alone. Top officials are among the world's highest paid — "one of the few countries where there is brain drain *into* the public sector" ([Tyler Cowen](/notes/tyler-cowen)).
- **Values reproduce themselves.** [Thrift](/notes/thrift), [hard work](/notes/hard-work), filial piety, and respect for learning, reinforced at home, school, and work, become "a self-reproducing, self-perpetuating cycle."

## Notable quotes

> "How did we differentiate ourselves from them? They are not clean systems; we run clean systems. Their rule of law is wonky; we stick to the law." — [Lee Kuan Yew](/notes/lee-kuan-yew)

> "Contrary to what American political commentators say, I do not believe that democracy necessarily leads to development. I believe that what a country needs to develop is discipline more than democracy." — [Lee Kuan Yew](/notes/lee-kuan-yew)

> "Presidents do not get reelected if they give a hard dose of medicine to their people. So, there is a tendency to procrastinate, to postpone unpopular policies in order to win elections." — [Lee Kuan Yew](/notes/lee-kuan-yew)

> "Singapore is one of the few countries where there is brain drain into the public sector." — [Tyler Cowen](/notes/tyler-cowen)
