# Galatians 5

A chapter of Paul's epistle to the Galatians that recurs across Kyle's topical studies for one passage: **the fruit of the Spirit** (Galatians 5:22–23 — "love, joy, peace, longsuffering, gentleness, goodness, faith, meekness, temperance"). Kyle uses it as a practical test in several contexts. In the Moroni 7–9 study it pairs with the [Light of Christ](/notes/light-of-christ): the Spirit of Christ given to every person lets them judge good from evil, and the Fruits of the Spirit are "the measure." In the Revelation — Topical Study and Scripture Study — Topical Study, the same verses become a discernment test — feelings of the heart that are true revelation "are ALWAYS uplifting and invite us to do good," consistent with the fruit Paul describes.

In the Self-Mastery — Topical Study, Kyle reads Galatians 5 as the contrast between the "works of the flesh" (v. 19) and the "fruit of the spirit" (v. 22): the fruits are not so much actions you perform as the *result* of "denying yourselves of all ungodliness," cross-referenced with D&C 93's "grace for grace" and Moroni 10:32.

**Context:** Galatians 5 is Paul's argument for Christian liberty over the law, contrasting "the works of the flesh" with "the fruit of the Spirit." Verses 22–23 are among the most quoted in Christian devotional writing as a checklist of Spirit-led character.

## Where this appears

- December 7–13, 2020. Moroni 7–9: "May Christ Lift Thee Up" — fruit of the Spirit as the measure that accompanies the Light of Christ
- Revelation — Topical Study — Galatians 5:22–23 as a test that true revelation is uplifting and invites good
- Self-Mastery — Topical Study — works of the flesh vs. fruit of the Spirit; the fruits as the result of self-denial
- Scripture Study — Topical Study — revelation given "for the benefit of the children of God," citing Galatians 5:22–23
