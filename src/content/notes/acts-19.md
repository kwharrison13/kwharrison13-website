# Acts 19

A New Testament chapter cited in Kyle's Abrahamic Covenant topical study. In the section on "The Authority to Gather Israel" (Topical Journal 1.169), [Acts 19](/notes/acts-19):1–7 is paired with [Matthew 3](/notes/matthew-3):11 and [John 1](/notes/john-1):29–34 to explain the relationship between baptism and the laying on of hands — a relationship the study reads as "agreeable to the 3rd–6th articles of faith." It sits in a larger scripture chain establishing the authority by which Abraham's seed administers the ordinances of salvation.

**Context:** Acts 19 opens with Paul at Ephesus finding disciples who had received only John's baptism; he baptizes them in the name of the Lord Jesus and lays his hands on them, after which they receive the Holy Ghost. The passage is a standard LDS proof-text for the distinction between baptism by water and the conferral of the gift of the Holy Ghost by the laying on of hands.
