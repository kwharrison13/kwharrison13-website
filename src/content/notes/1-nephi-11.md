# 1 Nephi 11

A Book of Mormon chapter — Nephi's vision of the [Tree of Life](/notes/tree-of-life) — that recurs across [Kyle Harrison](/notes/kyle-harrison)'s gospel study chiefly through verses 16–17, Nephi's answer to the angel: "**I know that [God] loveth his children; nevertheless, I do not know the meaning of all things**." Kyle returns to this couplet as a model of faith that holds knowledge of God's love together with comfort in not understanding everything: it anchors his Faith — Topical Study (our limited capacity for faith makes the Atonement necessary because we "do not know the meaning of all things"), his First Vision — Topical Study, his Teaching Repentance study (declaring the "condescension of God" as Nephi did), and his Spiritual Gifts notes (the chapter teaches of God's love for His children).

The chapter also feeds Kyle's Plan of Salvation — Topical Study, where Lehi's and Nephi's [Tree of Life](/notes/tree-of-life) vision (spanning [1 Nephi 8](/notes/1-nephi-8), 11–12, and [15](/notes/1-nephi-15)) maps the whole journey of existence — the rod of iron as the word of God, the mists of darkness as temptation, and the great and spacious building as "the pride of the world" (11:36). It appears once more in his General Conference — October 2021 notes (citing 11:21–23, the condescension of God).

**Context:** 1 Nephi 11 records the prophet Nephi being shown, by an angel, the vision his father Lehi had received of the Tree of Life — interpreting the tree as the love of God, witnessing the birth and ministry of Jesus Christ ("the condescension of God"), and seeing the great and spacious building as the pride and wisdom of the world.

## Where this appears

- Faith — Topical Study — 11:17 on faith amid not knowing "the meaning of all things," tied to the necessity of the Atonement.
- Plan of Salvation — Topical Study — the [Tree of Life](/notes/tree-of-life) vision as a map of premortal, mortal, and eternal life; 11:36 on the great and spacious building as worldly pride.
- First Vision — Topical Study — 11:16–17, "I know God loveth his children."
- Teaching Repentance — Nephi's declaration of the "condescension of God" as a model of testimony.
- Spiritual Gifts — 11:17 as a teaching of God's love for His children.
- General Conference — October 2021 — cites 11:21–23.
