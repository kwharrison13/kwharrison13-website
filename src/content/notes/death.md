# Death

In this corpus, death surfaces through [Becoming](/books/becoming) as grief held in the body rather than abstracted. The flagged passage is unsparing: "It hurts to live after someone has died... It can hurt to walk down a hallway or open the fridge... Food tastes like nothing. Colors go flat. Music hurts, and so do memories... Grief is so lonely this way." Kyle's reading frames it alongside one of the book's load-bearing ideas — that "grief and resilience live together," with loss, death, and [miscarriage](/notes/miscarriage) sitting *simultaneously* with the insistence to "control what you can" and keep moving, not sequentially.
