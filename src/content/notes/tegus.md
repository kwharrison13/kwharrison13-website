# Tegus

Tegus is a research-data platform for investors that Kyle diligenced closely at [Index Ventures](/notes/index-ventures) ahead of a $50M Series B. Per the Tegus pre-brief (written for an Index subset meeting with CEO Michael Elnick), the business runs a user-generated-content flywheel: investors pay a platform fee plus a per-call charge to access experts, Tegus takes no margin on the expert calls (passing 100% of a ~$300 call to the expert, vs. GLG or Guidepoint keeping ~70% on a $1K call), and the calls they generate become a compounding library that improves company coverage and pulls in new users. The thesis framed Tegus as building an expert-insight data network akin to what [Gartner](/notes/gartner) did in IT or [ZoomInfo](/notes/zoominfo) did in sales, with ZoomInfo ($26B market cap, ~$700M ARR) as the closest public comp; Index expected a $1–1.5B+ valuation on ~22–33x '21 ARR. The team: Michael Elnick (ex-AlphaSights manager, founded Tegus with his brother Thomas in 2017), CPO Tim Anderson (ex-Zapier, ex-Glassdoor), and ZoomInfo CEO Henry Schuck on the board.

Tegus also recurs as a *tool* Kyle and others used and as a competitive landmark. The Pave Diligence — 2021 dossier sourced an [Allbirds](/notes/allbirds) expert call through Tegus, and CaptivateIQ Diligence — 2021 and Cresta.ai - Diligence both cite Tegus guest transcripts as diligence inputs. As a competitor it frames adjacent companies: [George Sivulka](/notes/george-sivulka) of [Hebbia](/notes/hebbia) noted "Tegus clones," and the Sacra call positioned [Sacra](/notes/sacra) and [Contrary Research](/essays/contrary-research) relative to Tegus, flagging a cold-start problem where most Tegus call-takers already understand the market.

**Context:** Tegus is an investment-research platform offering expert-call transcripts, a content library, and financial data; it was acquired by AlphaSense in 2024.
