# Scattering of Israel

In Kyle's study of the Olive Tree Allegory (Jacob 5, Zenos's allegory of the tame and wild olive trees), the scattering of Israel is one of the load-bearing movements the allegory chronicles. The branches of the tame tree are scattered across the vineyard — mapped onto sacred history from the Exodus through the dispersion of the tribes, then "scattered Israel and the House of Lehi" (the promised land as a good spot, Nephites as the part-tame, Lamanites as the part-wild). The scattering sits alongside the [Apostasy](/notes/apostasy) and the [Restoration](/notes/restoration) as the arc the Lord labors against, the roots being the covenants that survived (Abrahamic Covenant) and the eventual gathering of the tribes being the natural fruit gathered back in.

**Context:** In Latter-day Saint and broader Judeo-Christian theology, the "scattering of Israel" refers to the dispersion of the twelve tribes — beginning with the Assyrian and Babylonian conquests and continuing through later diaspora — understood as a consequence of covenant unfaithfulness, to be reversed by a latter-day "gathering of Israel."
