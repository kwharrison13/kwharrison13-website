# Christian Tietze

Christian Tietze is the writer behind the zettelkasten.de project and the author of [The Collector's Fallacy](/notes/the-collector-s-fallacy), the essay Kyle reads as the negative case for the slip-box method. Tietze's argument is that "knowing about" something is not the same as "knowing" it: gathering books, bookmarks, and photocopies feels immediately rewarding (he likens it to Skinner's doves) but creates only an *illusion* of knowledge. He cites [Umberto Eco](/notes/umberto-eco)'s *How to Write a Thesis* on photocopying as an alibi for not reading, and prescribes a tight "Research, Read, Assimilate; rinse and repeat" cycle — reading without taking notes is "a waste of time in the long run" because the information never gets integrated into your own system.

Within Kyle's wiki, Tietze sits as the companion voice to [Zettelkasten](/notes/zettelkasten) and [How To Take Smart Notes](/books/how-to-take-smart-notes): where [Sonke Ahrens](/notes/sonke-ahrens) makes the positive case for note-taking-as-thinking, Tietze supplies the practical justification for actually running a slip-box — taking notes is what permanently converts collected material into knowledge, which is why hoarding references is the trap to avoid. This cluster — [Note Taking](/notes/note-taking), [Knowledge Management](/notes/knowledge-management) — is part of the conceptual ancestry of Kyle's own LLM wiki.

**Context:** Christian Tietze is a software developer and writer who runs Zettelkasten.de, one of the most influential English-language resources on the Zettelkasten note-taking method, and co-develops note-taking software in that tradition.
