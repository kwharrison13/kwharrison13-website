# Loom

Loom appears in Kyle's notes mainly as a reference product — the canonical example of bottoms-up, product-led growth driven by serving individual productivity, and the model for async recorded-video collaboration. In the September 22nd, 2021 — Sunil Patro (SignEasy) call, [Sunil Patro](/notes/sunil-patro) benchmarked [SignEasy](/notes/signeasy)'s go-to-market against [Calendly](/notes/calendly) and Loom — "products that attract a lot of decision-makers by serving their own productivity." Loom recurs as a PLG comparable across the [Calendly](/notes/calendly) and [SignEasy](/notes/signeasy) company pages, and the [Sprig](/notes/sprig) diligence cites "Loom-style recorded video surveys" as the model for asynchronous video feedback.

It also surfaces as a deal-flow target: in the December 13th, 2021 — US Deal Flow (2022 Goals) notes, an intro to the Loom founders came through Ivan at [Notion](/notes/notion). In [Remote — Office Not Required](/books/remote-office-not-required), Loom is cited as screen-sharing/recording tooling that makes async visual collaboration easy, with Kyle's own note that remote work is "even easier now with Loom, Tandem, etc."

**Context:** Loom is an async video messaging tool that lets users record their screen and camera to share quick walkthroughs instead of meetings; it grew via product-led adoption and was acquired by Atlassian in 2023.
