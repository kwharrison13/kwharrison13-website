# Self-Knowledge

In [The Expertise Economy](/books/the-expertise-economy), self-knowledge is the foundation employees draw on to build a personalized learning or skill plan. Palmer and Blake argue that "once employees have a good sense of what skills they want to focus on, they can create a personalized learning or skill plan based on self-knowledge and their learning and career goals" — distinct from a top-down development plan tied to a performance review, which "almost seems punitive." The point is motivational: when learning starts from the employee's own sense of where they stand and where they want to go, it becomes "an inspiring career vision" rather than a corrective. It sits in the book's web of concepts around measuring and directing capability — alongside the [Skills Quotient](/notes/skills-quotient) and [Self-Directed Learning](/notes/self-directed-learning).

## Where this appears
