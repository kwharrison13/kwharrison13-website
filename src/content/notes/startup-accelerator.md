# Startup Accelerator

A startup accelerator is the program model that anchors how Kyle's notes describe [Plug and Play](/notes/plug-and-play) — a global, stage-agnostic investor that runs an accelerator alongside writing $25K–$200K checks. Per the May 2018 intro call captured in those notes, Plug and Play pairs its accelerator with seed–Series B+ investing across offices in New York, the Bay Area, Munich, Singapore, and Beijing. In the wiki the concept is anchored to Plug and Play as its source example, and it sits adjacent to [Venture Capital](/notes/venture-capital) as a distinct but overlapping vehicle.

**Context:** A startup accelerator is a fixed-term, cohort-based program (typically a few months) that offers early-stage companies mentorship, resources, and often seed capital in exchange for equity, culminating in a demo day. Y Combinator and Techstars are the canonical examples; many corporate and regional accelerators followed.
