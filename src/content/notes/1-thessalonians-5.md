# 1 Thessalonians 5

A chapter of Paul's first epistle to the Thessalonians, cited in two of Kyle's gospel-study pages for two distinct verses. In the Come Follow Me study December 31–January 6, 2019. "We Are Responsible for Our Own Learning", [1 Thessalonians 5](/notes/1-thessalonians-5):21 ("Prove all things; hold fast that which is good") is gathered with [John 7](/notes/john-7):17 and [James 1](/notes/james-1) as scripture on taking personal responsibility for one's own gospel learning.

In Christ Defines His Gospel, the chapter supplies a sanctification proof-text: [1 Thessalonians 5](/notes/1-thessalonians-5):23 ("the very God of peace sanctify you wholly"), listed alongside [2 Nephi 31](/notes/2-nephi-31):19 (relying wholly upon the merits of Christ) and [Philippians 2](/notes/philippians-2):12 (work out your own salvation).

**Context:** 1 Thessalonians 5 is Paul's closing exhortation — watchfulness for the day of the Lord, and a chain of practical commands (pray without ceasing, rejoice evermore, prove all things, abstain from all appearance of evil) ending in the blessing that God sanctify the believer "wholly."

## Where this appears

- December 31–January 6, 2019. "We Are Responsible for Our Own Learning" — verse 21 ("Prove all things") cited on self-directed gospel learning
- Christ Defines His Gospel — verse 23 cited as a sanctification scripture on relying wholly on Christ
