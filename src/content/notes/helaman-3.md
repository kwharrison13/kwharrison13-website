# Helaman 3

Helaman 3 carries two distinct loads in Kyle's notes. The verse he cites most is **3:35** — the Nephites "did fast and pray oft, and did wax stronger and stronger in their humility, and firmer and firmer in the faith of Christ… even to the purifying and the sanctification of their hearts, which sanctification cometh because of their yielding their hearts unto God." This is his go-to proof-text for [Sanctification](/notes/sanctification) as a *yielding* rather than a *qualifying*. It anchors Humility (humility leading to sanctification by yielding the heart), appears in Fasting - September 5, 2021 as the pattern fasting produces, grounds Virtue's distinction between subjection and submission ("becoming a saint comes only through yielding to the enticings of the holy spirit"), and runs through Christian Reformation, Zion, and Seekers on wading through affliction toward a sanctified heart.

The chapter's second use is narrative and apologetic. In the Helaman 1–6 study Kyle reads 3:7,9,11 — the people becoming "exceedingly expert in the working of cement" — as a Book of Mormon detail once dismissed as anachronistic but now corroborated by archaeological evidence of sophisticated pre-Columbian Mesoamerican cement (#[Mormon Apologetics](/notes/mormon-apologetics)). The same study pulls a moral thread from 3:23: Gadianton's secret combinations were "well received in the more settled parts of the land" — comfort as a quick path to sin, "an idle mind is the devil's workshop."

**Context:** Helaman 3 is a chapter in the Book of Mormon recounting Nephite northward migration, large-scale building (including the "cement" passages), and a period in which the people grew in humility and were sanctified.

## Where this appears

- Fasting - September 5, 2021 — 3:35 as the fruit of fasting and prayer: humility, faith, and sanctification by yielding the heart
- Humility — Topical Study — 3:35 as the link from humility to sanctification (the C.S. Lewis "never let you rest until you are perfect" thread)
- Virtue — 3:35 grounding the subjection-vs-submission distinction; becoming a saint by yielding to the Spirit
- Helaman 1–6 — The Rock of Our Redeemer — the "cement" passages as apologetic evidence; comfort as a path to sin (3:23)
- Christian Reformation, Zion, and Seekers — 3:34–35 on affliction leading to a sanctified, yielded heart
