# 3 Nephi 9

The chapter recording the voice of Christ to the Nephites after the great destruction, which Kyle's gospel study returns to for two of its central verses. [3 Nephi 9](/notes/3-nephi-9):20 — "ye shall offer for a sacrifice unto me a broken heart and a contrite spirit" — is the key text for the [Broken heart and a contrite spirit](/notes/broken-heart-and-a-contrite-spirit) theme, cited in [Ezra Taft Benson](/notes/ezra-taft-benson)'s A Mighty Change of Heart — Ezra Taft Benson alongside [Moroni 6](/notes/moroni-6):2 and [D&C 20](/notes/dandc-20):37 as the new sacrifice that replaces burnt offerings. The same verse, with [3 Nephi 9](/notes/3-nephi-9):19 (sacrifices and burnt offerings "done away"), drives Kyle's September 14–20, 2020. 3 Nephi 8–11: "Arise and Come Forth unto Me" study of the [Law of Moses](/notes/law-of-moses) being fulfilled.

That weekly study also wrestles with [3 Nephi 9](/notes/3-nephi-9):5, 7, where cities are covered with earth and water "to hide their iniquities… from before my face" — which Kyle reads as a "complicated doctrine" to be understood as more than social ostracism, linking it to his note that [Cancel Culture is the Anti-Repentance](/notes/cancel-culture-is-the-anti-repentance). In The Little Ones, The Lost Ones, [3 Nephi 9](/notes/3-nephi-9):22 ("for such have I given my life") appears in the cluster on Christ's care for the lost.

**Context:** 3 Nephi 9 is the chapter of the Book of Mormon in which the resurrected Christ's voice speaks out of the darkness after the destruction at his crucifixion, calling survivors to repentance and declaring the law of sacrifice fulfilled in a broken heart and a contrite spirit.
