# Techno-Optimism

The belief that technology makes the world better — and, in Kyle's notes, a position with two distinct flavors. It's Time To Build... But Build What - Research frames the debate through [Marc Andreessen](/notes/marc-andreessen)'s [The Techno-Optimist Manifesto](https://a16z.com/the-techno-optimist-manifesto/), which argues technology drives productivity growth — "the main driver of economic growth, wage growth, and the creation of new industries and new jobs" — invokes [Ray Kurzweil](/notes/ray-kurzweil)'s Law of Accelerating Returns, and treats intelligence as "the birthright of humanity." This is the accelerationist register, continuous with [Effective Accelerationism](/notes/effective-accelerationism) and downstream of Andreessen's earlier "[It's Time To Build](https://a16z.com/its-time-to-build/)" (a16z, early COVID 2020), which diagnosed Western failure as both a failure of imagination and "our widespread inability to *build*."

Against that, the research note sets [Noah Smith](/notes/noah-smith)'s "[Thoughts on techno-optimism](https://www.noahpinion.blog/p/thoughts-on-techno-optimism)," which splits the concept into a **positive** variety (the opposite of stagnationism — there's still lots of low-hanging fruit to find) and a **normative** variety (more technology makes the world *better* for humanity). Smith calls normative techno-optimism "surprisingly rare," grounds it in humanism — "I see human beings wanting things, and I want to give them what they want" — and insists it is an *active* optimism: "I think that choosing the right policies is very important," worrying about R&D funding and IP theft. The stub's framing holds: Kyle distinguishes Andreessen's accelerationist version from Smith's normative/humanist one — active, policy-aware, whole-of-society — and lands closer to the latter. The cluster of names orbiting the topic in the wiki ([David Deutsch](/notes/david-deutsch), [Ezra Klein](/notes/ezra-klein), [Josh Wolfe](/notes/josh-wolfe), [Vitalik Buterin](/notes/vitalik-buterin), [Robin Hanson](/notes/robin-hanson), [James Pethokoukis](/notes/james-pethokoukis), [Joshua Gans](/notes/joshua-gans), [Dave Karpf](/notes/dave-karpf)) marks it as a contested ideological family rather than a single creed.

**Context:** Techno-optimism is the broad view that technological progress is, on balance, good for humanity and should be accelerated; it sits in tension with stagnationist, degrowth, and AI-doomer positions, and overlaps with — but is distinct from — the more confrontational "effective accelerationism" (e/acc) movement.
