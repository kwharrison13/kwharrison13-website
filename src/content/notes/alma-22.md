# Alma 22

Book of Mormon chapter Kyle reads as the conversion of King Lamoni's father — the Lamanite king who, taught by Aaron, declares "I will give up all that I possess, yea, I will forsake my kingdom, that I may receive this great joy" (22:15) and prays "I will give away all my sins to know thee" (22:18). In his June 22–28, 2020. Alma 17–22: "I Will Make an Instrument of Thee" study this chapter closes the arc of the sons of [Mosiah](/notes/mosiah)'s missions, contrasting Aaron's approach with [Ammon](/notes/ammon)'s.

The chapter does its heaviest lifting in Kyle's Patience — Topical Study, where 22:15–16 become the template for how desire works: "We must first state our desire" and then act on the knowledge we receive — God can educate our desires if we qualify them by the sacrifice we are willing to make. He pairs it with [Alma 5](/notes/alma-5):14 ("receive His image in your countenances") and [Neal A. Maxwell](/notes/neal-a-maxwell)'s line that "what we insistently desire, over time, is what we will eventually become." Alma 22:22 also surfaces in his Charity & Love study as a cross-reference.

**Context:** Alma chapter 22 is in the Book of Mormon (Latter-day Saint scripture). It records Aaron teaching the Lamanite king (Lamoni's father), the king's conversion, and the resulting opening of Lamanite lands to the gospel.
