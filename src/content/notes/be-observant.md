# Be Observant

"Be Observant" is a working-habit tag Kyle applies across biographies where careful, sustained observation is the engine of insight. In [Walt Disney and the Promise of Progress City](/books/walt-disney-and-the-promise-of-progress-city), Gennawey extracts it as one of Walt's core methods: "Walt's best solutions came after a great deal of observation" — a lesson Walt himself learned from [Jack Lindquist](/notes/jack-lindquist) in *[In Service to the Mouse](/notes/in-service-to-the-mouse)*. Walt studied how people move through museums to design around "museum feet," visited dozens of parks, and brought back ideas from every trip abroad, using travel to "observe, and to think."

The tag recurs as the through-line of genius in [Leonardo Da Vinci](/books/leonardo-da-vinci), where [Walter Isaacson](/notes/walter-isaacson) insists Leonardo's gift "was not some magical gift but a product of his own effort" — acute observation built deliberately, in steps ("begin with the details... do not go on to the second step until you have the first well fixed in memory"), and paired with the childlike wonder he shared with [Albert Einstein](/notes/albert-einstein). In [John Quincy Adams](/books/john-quincy-adams), Kyle tags Adams's compulsive habit of producing [character sketches](/notes/character-sketches) of all forty-six classmates and his unsparing self-observation in his journals — observation turned both outward and inward, overlapping with [Self-Reflection](/notes/self-reflection). And in [Our Towns](/books/our-towns), the tag marks the Fallows' on-the-ground reporting method and the embedded "community librarians" of Bend who learned a place by "listening, learning, sharing stories."

Across these sources the concept reads less as passive looking than as a discipline: gather the raw material first, attend closely, and let solutions emerge from the accumulated detail rather than from a flash of inspiration. It pairs naturally with [Curiosity](/notes/curious) and [Experimentation](/notes/experimentation) in Kyle's habit vocabulary.
