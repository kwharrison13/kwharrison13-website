# Talkspace

A D2C teletherapy company that recurs across Kyle's [Behavioral Health](/notes/behavioral-health) diligence as the cash-pay / consumer pole of the mental-health market, contrasted against insurance-network therapist platforms ([SonderMind](/notes/sondermind), [Headway](/notes/headway), [Alma](/notes/alma)) and employer/EAP-replacement plays ([Spring Health](/notes/spring-health), Lyra, [Ginger](/notes/ginger), Modern Health). In the Sept 22, 2021 call with [Adam Chekroud](/notes/adam-chekroud), Talkspace was flagged as a competitive threat in provider-acquisition economics: it "is going to bid higher for these ads because they're getting paid directly by the provider," squeezing CAC for marketplaces that pay therapists out — though it "doesn't have the employer channel" (September 22nd, 2021 — Adam Chekroud (Spring Health)).

Expert transcripts Kyle reviewed gave a sharper picture. A former Talkspace SVP of Health Plan Business Development described B2B revenue jumping from $2.74M (2019) to $14.6M (2020), but attributed most of it to adding Cigna (~20M new lives) rather than employer-side growth; Talkspace's core weakness was asynchronous, text-only therapy versus competitors offering live chat, coaching, and psychiatry at roughly the same PEPM price, and app reviews fell sharply in 2020 as Cigna onboarding overwhelmed provider supply (October 4th, 2021 — Behavioral Health Expert Transcripts). The same transcripts grouped Talkspace with [BetterHelp](/notes/betterhelp) in the "telehealth/cash" bucket — highest brand recognition, low therapist payout, "most hated by providers" — and noted several Teladoc executives later joined Talkspace as President/COO.

**Context:** Talkspace is a publicly traded (Nasdaq: TALK) online therapy and behavioral-health company offering text, audio, and video sessions to consumers, employers, and health plans; it went public via SPAC in 2021.
