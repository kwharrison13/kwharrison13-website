# Pulp Fiction

In Kyle's notes *Pulp Fiction* appears as an auteur-defining work by [Quentin Tarantino](/notes/quentin-tarantino) — one of the directors Kyle "voraciously consumed" growing up. It is cited (alongside *Reservoir Dogs*) as an example of [Auteur Theory](/notes/auteur-theory) in practice: a film where the director "truly becomes 'the author'" of the work, with a distinctive, recognizable signature. The reference is the anchor for treating Tarantino as a quintessential auteur in Kyle's corpus.

**Context:** *Pulp Fiction* (1994) is Quentin Tarantino's second feature, a nonlinear crime film that won the Palme d'Or at Cannes and the Academy Award for Best Original Screenplay. It is widely regarded as a landmark of 1990s American independent cinema and a defining auteur work.
