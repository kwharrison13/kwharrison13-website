# Ordinances

In Kyle's gospel-study notes on Alma 30 (July 6–12, 2020. Alma 30–31: "The Virtue of the Word of God"), ordinances appear as one of the doctrines the [Anti-Christ](/notes/anti-christ) Korihor attacks. Korihor's tactic is to argue against [prophecy](/notes/prophecy), [Atonement](/notes/atonement), [Agency](/notes/agency), [resurrection](/notes/resurrection), ordinances, and [Priesthood](/notes/priesthood) leadership — dismissing them as "the foolish ordinances and performances which are laid down by ancient priests, to usurp power and authority" and "keep them in ignorance" (Alma 30:23). The notes contrast this with the Nephites who "were strict in observing the ordinances of God, according to the law of Moses."

The notes also trace ordinances forward through the Restoration: the performance of "essential ordinances in the Nauvoo [Temple](/notes/temple)" and the spread of temples worldwide is cited as evidence of revelation given "line upon line and precept upon precept."

**Context:** In Latter-day Saint theology, ordinances are sacred, formal rites performed by priesthood authority (baptism, the sacrament, temple endowment, sealing) understood as essential, covenant-making acts.
