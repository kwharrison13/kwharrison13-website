# Patri Friedman

Patri Friedman recurs in Kyle's notes as one of the intellectual roots of the [City Building](/notes/city-building) / [Seasteading](/notes/seasteading) thread and as an early backer of Bluebook Cities. In the [City Building](/notes/city-building) case study, [Dryden Brown](/notes/dryden-brown) cites following [Paul Romer](/notes/paul-romer), Patri Friedman, and the [Seasteading](/notes/seasteading) movement "since high school" as the lineage behind his obsession with building a new city. Friedman shows up again in two concrete roles in that story: as one of the Silicon Valley new-cities figures Brown and [Charlie Callinan](/notes/charlie-callinan) consulted during their May–July 2019 research mode (alongside [Mark Lutter](/notes/mark-lutter), [Tamara Winter](/notes/tamara-winter), [Iyinoluwa Aboyeji](/notes/iyinoluwa-aboyeji), [Mike Solana](/notes/mike-solana), and [Jason Crawford](/notes/jason-crawford)), and as a participant in the Bluebook Cities seed round that funded charter-city legislation in Ghana (with [Tyler Cowen](/notes/tyler-cowen) and others). He also appears in [Tamara Winter](/notes/tamara-winter)'s page as part of that same new-cities circle.

**Context:** Patri Friedman is an American libertarian activist and the founder of the Seasteading Institute (2008), which promotes building autonomous floating communities in international waters. He is the grandson of economist [Milton Friedman](/notes/milton-friedman) and has since worked on charter-cities and startup-cities ventures, including the Pronomos Capital fund.
