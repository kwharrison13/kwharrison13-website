# Neuralink

Neuralink — [Elon Musk](/notes/elon-musk)'s brain-computer-interface company — recurs across Kyle's corpus as the leading frontier example of human-machine symbiosis. In [The Kill Chain](/books/the-kill-chain), Christian Brose flags Neuralink as a military-relevant frontier alongside hypersonics and quantum computing: brain-computer interface as "exactly what it sounds like — the ability to connect the human brain to machines and control them," with Musk's stated goal of "a full brain-machine interface where we can achieve a sort of symbiosis with [artificial intelligence]" and a near-term target of typing forty words per minute by thought. Kyle's note there reads "Biological warfare isn't just disease. Human augmentation is also a big part of it." Neuralink also anchors a softer intellectual thread: [Tim Urban](/notes/tim-urban)'s 2017 Wait But Why Neuralink piece is the origin of the line "Language made ideas composable... Knowledge, when shared, becomes like a grand, collective, inter-generational collaboration," which [Packy McCormick](/notes/packy-mccormick) quotes as the backbone of Kyle's [Open-Source Knowledge](/essays/open-source-knowledge) argument. It is also tagged from Kyle's [Neuroscience](/notes/neuroscience) capture (a Vox piece on Musk's and Facebook's brain-reading ambitions and the neuroethics).

**Context:** Neuralink is a neurotechnology company founded by Elon Musk in 2016 developing implantable brain-computer interfaces, with stated goals ranging from restoring function for people with paralysis to longer-term human-AI symbiosis. It began first-in-human implant trials in 2024.
