# Alma

In Kyle's gospel study, Alma is the Book of Mormon prophet whose debate with [Korihor](/notes/korihor) anchors the first of The First Principles of Faithful Pragmatism ("There is a God"). When Korihor demands a sign to be convinced God exists, Alma answers that he has "had signs enough" — "the scriptures are laid before thee, yea, and **all things denote there is a God**… even the earth, and all things that are upon the face of it… do witness that there is a Supreme Creator" (Alma 30:43–44). Kyle writes that he has "always resonated with the logic of Alma" here, pairing it with the design-from-organized-complexity intuition against [Atheism](/notes/atheism). Alma also recurs across Kyle's cross-reference scripture chains in Miscellaneous Scriptures — the "mind = knowledge AND heart = wisdom" reading at the end of Alma, the noble-and-great-ones thread in Alma 13, and the Alma 32 breakdown on faith and humility.

**Note — name collision:** "Alma" is also the name of a [Behavioral Health](/notes/behavioral-health) company in Kyle's diligence notes — a provider-infrastructure marketplace handling credentialing and billing for therapists (alongside [SonderMind](/notes/sondermind) and [Headway](/notes/headway)). That usage is unrelated to the prophet and is tracked on the [Behavioral Health](/notes/behavioral-health) page.
