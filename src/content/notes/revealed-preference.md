# Revealed Preference

In the wiki, revealed preference is the sharpest framing of the [Capital Allocator Mindset](/notes/capital-allocator-mindset): a budget constraint (dollars) acts as a forcing function for prioritization, because "stated 'interest' is cheap and feels unlimited; *allocated* interest is revealed preference." Asking where you would actually deploy capital collapses the gap between felt interest and real priority — the dollar question disciplines [Curiosity](/notes/curious) by separating cheap stated curiosity from revealed priority. The same logic ties directly to [Resource Allocation](/notes/resource-allocation) and [Capital Allocation](/notes/capital-allocation): what you actually fund, not what you claim to care about, is your true strategy.

**Context:** Revealed preference is an economics concept (formalized by Paul Samuelson) holding that consumers' true preferences are inferred from their actual purchasing choices under a budget constraint, rather than from stated intentions — what people *do* with scarce resources reveals what they value.

## Where this appears

- [Capital Allocator Mindset](/notes/capital-allocator-mindset) — the core mechanism: a dollar budget is a forcing function that turns cheap stated interest into revealed, allocated priority.
