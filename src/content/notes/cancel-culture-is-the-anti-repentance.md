# Cancel Culture is the Anti-Repentance

This is one of Kyle's gospel-study reflections, raised in his study of [3 Nephi 8](/notes/3-nephi-8)–11 (September 14–20, 2020. 3 Nephi 8–11: "Arise and Come Forth unto Me"). It hangs on the "complicated doctrine" in [3 Nephi 9](/notes/3-nephi-9):5, 7, where the Lord covers wicked cities "to hide their iniquities… from before my face." Kyle flags that this needs to be read as **more than social ostracism** — and the heading frames the contrast: where repentance reconciles a person to God through a [Broken heart and a contrite spirit](/notes/broken-heart-and-a-contrite-spirit) ([3 Nephi 9](/notes/3-nephi-9):20), "cancel culture" is its inverse — a permanent removal with no path back. The note sits among the chapter's themes of [Regret](/notes/regret) ("O that we had repented before this great and terrible day"), [Sign Seeking](/notes/sign-seeking), [Excommunication](/notes/excommunication), and the fulfilled [Law of Moses](/notes/law-of-moses), pushing back on a purely punitive reading of the destruction.
