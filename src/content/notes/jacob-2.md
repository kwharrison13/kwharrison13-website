# Jacob 2

A chapter from the Book of Jacob in the Book of Mormon. In Kyle's Come Follow Me study of [Alma 13](/notes/alma-13)–16, [Jacob 2](/notes/jacob-2):3 is grouped with [2 Nephi 6](/notes/2-nephi-6):3 and [Mosiah 28](/notes/mosiah-28):12 as an example of the godly [anxiety](/notes/anxiety) that prophets feel for the welfare of others' souls ("I this day am weighed down with much more desire and anxiety for the welfare of your souls"). It illustrates the study's "Like a Parent" theme — that those filled with the love of God feel great anxiety, even unto pain, for others' repentance.

**Context:** Jacob 2 records Jacob's temple sermon to the Nephites, opening with his concern for their souls and then condemning the love of riches, pride, and unauthorized polygamy.

## Where this appears

- Alma 13–16 — Enter into the Rest of the Lord — Jacob 2:3 cited as a scriptural example of the anxiety/love a prophet feels for the welfare of souls.
