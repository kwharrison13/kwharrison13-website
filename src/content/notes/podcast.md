# Podcast

Podcasting surfaces across Kyle's book notes in two distinct registers. In [Stop Asking Questions](/books/stop-asking-questions) it is the craft-and-audience playbook for online interviewing: hooking listeners in the first minute (Anchor's second-by-second drop-off analytics showed many shows lose over half their audience within a minute), the counterintuitive finding that lesser-known guests can drive bigger audiences than superstars, and the promotion-stopper move of asking a guest "how can you help me promote you?" In [The Expertise Economy](/books/the-expertise-economy), the podcast appears instead as a *learning* medium — one of the informal formats ([Videos](/notes/videos), [articles](/notes/articles), [Books](/notes/books), events) through which a culture of [Continuous Learning](/notes/continuous-learning) is built, and part of the 5–10 weekly hours of self-directed learning the book argues people need to avoid obsolescence.

**Context:** A podcast is an episodic, on-demand audio (sometimes video) program distributed over the internet, typically via RSS, that listeners subscribe to and consume across apps like Apple Podcasts and Spotify.

## Where this appears

- [Stop Asking Questions](/books/stop-asking-questions) — the online-interview playbook: first-minute hooks, drop-off analytics, landing newsmakers, and promotion-trade tactics.
- [The Expertise Economy](/books/the-expertise-economy) — listed as one of the informal media that feed a continuous-learning culture.
