# Alma 30

Alma 30 is the Book of Mormon chapter of **Korihor**, the anti-Christ, and across Kyle's gospel study it functions as the canonical case study of the sign-seeker who demands evidence before faith. In the Anti-Christ — Topical Study, Korihor is one of the three Book of Mormon anti-Christs (with Sherem and Nehor) who all attack the [Atonement](/notes/atonement): he "preached that prophecy was foolish tradition and every man prospers by his own genius, demanded a sign, and was struck dumb." Alma withstands him because he *knows* Christ, accusing Korihor of being "possessed with a lying spirit" and declaring "thou hast had signs enough" — the scriptures and the earth itself standing "as a witness of God" ([Alma 30](/notes/alma-30):44).

The chapter recurs wherever Kyle's study turns on the relationship between **faith and evidence**. In Publication of the Book of Mormon he sets the sign-seeker against the believer — "a sign-seeker is an adulterer at heart" — noting that Alma shifts the burden of proof to the unbeliever (Alma 30:43–44) while the believer rests on the witness of the Holy Ghost. The same Korihor line ("show unto me that He hath power, and then will I be convinced") anchors his Revelation — Topical Study on why one must act in faith before receiving a witness. The weekly study July 6–12, 2020. Alma 30–31 ("The Virtue of the Word of God") is the source reading.
