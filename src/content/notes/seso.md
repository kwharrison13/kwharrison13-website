# Seso

A vertical-SaaS company building software for agricultural workforce management, evaluated by Index for a Series A in December 2021 (see December 13th, 2021 — Seso (Series A)). Per the brief from [Nina Achadjian](/notes/nina-achadjian), Seso starts with the onboarding and compliance of H-2A migrant farm workers — a 50-year-old visa program buried in heavy documentation, where growers maintain dedicated administrators, teams of lawyers, and physical file cabinets to stay compliant for 3–7 years. Seso's product offers an applicant tracking system, H-2A visa application automation, compliance software, and digital onboarding, with a roadmap toward a worker-facing platform and payroll — "the biggest leap of faith but also the biggest opportunity." Index originally met Seso in the seed as a migrant-worker marketplace (and passed), then tracked it for a year as it pivoted to SaaS. Founded by [Michael Guirguis](/notes/michael-guirguis) (CEO), [Jordan Taylor](/notes/jordan-taylor) (Head of Product), and [William Dembinski](/notes/william-dembinski) (Head of Engineering), the company had grown to $1.2M SaaS ARR plus $1.4M logistics bookings. Index's recommendation: invest $15M of a $17M round at $120M post for 12.5%, competing against an insider Founders Fund term sheet.

**Context:** Seso (Seso Labor) is a U.S. agtech startup providing H-2A visa automation, recruiting, and HR/payroll software for agricultural employers and farmworkers.
