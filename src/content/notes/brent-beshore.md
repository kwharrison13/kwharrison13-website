# Brent Beshore

Brent Beshore is the author of [The Messy Marketplace](/books/the-messy-marketplace) (read 2019), the book that supplies Kyle's notes on buying and selling small private businesses — owner-earnings valuation, the perils of debt as an "amplifier," fundless sponsors, earnouts, and the buy-and-hold model contrasted with fixed-life [Private Equity](/notes/private-equity) funds. Kyle's wiki frames Beshore as the founder of [Permanent Equity](/notes/permanent-equity), a long-term small-business acquirer, and surfaces his VC Has Gotten More Efficient... (tweet) on VC efficiency and return spreads under [Capital Allocation](/notes/capital-allocation).

Beshore also appears in Kyle's Contrary-era sourcing notes as a node in the small-business / permanent-capital network. In the February 9th, 2022 — John Burke (Merit Holdings) call, Beshore is credited with introducing John Burke to Washington University (an LP that went on to invest). In the March 30th, 2022 quick-capture log he recommended two investing books that became anti-library entries: [The Fish That Ate the Whale](/notes/the-fish-that-ate-the-whale) and [Mistakes Were Made (But Not by Me)](/notes/mistakes-were-made-but-not-by-me).

**Context:** Brent Beshore is the founder and CEO of Permanent Equity, a Columbia, Missouri firm that acquires and holds profitable small-to-mid-size private businesses for the very long term using committed, no-fixed-life capital. He authored *The Messy Marketplace*, a practitioner's guide to lower-middle-market M&A.
