# Alma 13

Alma 13 is the Book of Mormon chapter Kyle reads as the central text on the high priesthood — "the order of the Son of God." In his Come Follow Me study of Alma 13–16, he traced its core doctrines: the priesthood is *His* — patterned on [Jesus Christ](/notes/jesus), given "to teach these things unto the people," and eternal, "without beginning of days or end of years"; men are ordained to it "on account of their exceeding faith and repentance," choosing to "work righteousness rather than to perish" ("many are called, but few are chosen"); and the sanctified, having "their garments made white," reach a state where they "could not look upon sin save it were with abhorrence" and so enter into [The Rest of the Lord](/notes/the-rest-of-the-lord).

Kyle pairs the chapter's high-doctrine sermon on [Priesthood](/notes/priesthood) with Alma's urgent altar call — "Now is the time to repent" (Alma 13:21), "I wish from the inmost part of my heart… that ye would hearken unto my words" (Alma 13:27) — and notes its caution that "the scriptures are before you; if ye will wrest them it shall be to your own destruction" (Alma 13:20). It is cited again in the General Conference — October 2021 notes (Alma 13:27, 29).
