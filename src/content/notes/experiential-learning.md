# Experiential Learning

Learning by doing — acquiring skills through real work and self-directed projects rather than through lectures or facts alone. In [The Expertise Economy](/books/the-expertise-economy), it appears as the "on the job" half of a learning culture: "the learning is 'event-driven,' meaning that people are taken out of their jobs to learn new skills, or a company will use a blended approach where they learn both through more structured learning and also on the job."

In [Your Passion, Your Solution (YPYS)](/notes/your-passion-your-solution-ypys) it is the program's core pedagogy. YPYS is designed as a year-long developmental program that teaches social innovation and social entrepreneurship "through experiential learning" — students take an issue they care about and work to alleviate it through self-directed projects, supported by workshops, mentors, investors, and a collaborative community. The YPYS thesis is that students want to contribute but lack know-how, resources, support, and social understanding; the experiential curriculum supplies all four. Its pilot is explicitly built to isolate how much workshops versus lectures actually drive outcomes — a test of experiential method against passive instruction.
