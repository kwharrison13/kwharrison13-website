# Alma 25

A chapter in the Book of Mormon's account of the Anti-Nephi-Lehies, studied in Kyle's June 29–July 5, 2020. Alma 23–29: "They Never Did Fall Away" Come Follow Me notes. Kyle reads it through two lenses. First, Godly Sorrow: the Lamanites who slew their brethren grow "more angry" rather than penitent (Alma 25:1) — an illustration of the two kinds of remorse, the sorrow that leads to [Self-Reflection](/notes/self-reflection) and change versus "the sorrowing of the damned" who are simply angry at being shown their own awfulness. Kyle pairs the verse with [2 Corinthians 7](/notes/2-corinthians-7):10 and Mormon's own lament in [Mormon 2](/notes/mormon-2):13.

Second, Kyle treats Alma 25:15 as a key proof-text for [Unapologetically Christian](/notes/unapologetically-christian) / Old Testament Christology: the converts "did keep the [Law of Moses](/notes/law-of-moses)… for it was not all fulfilled," yet looked forward to Christ, treating the law as "a type of his coming." Kyle ties this to the [Joseph Smith — Rough Stone Rolling](/books/joseph-smith-rough-stone-rolling) idea that "the ancient patriarchs were the equals of later Christians" — the problem of history was to hold on to the gospel, not to prepare for its coming. The same verse is cited again in his July 6–12, 2020. Alma 30–31: "The Virtue of the Word of God" notes.

**Context:** Alma 25 is a chapter in the Book of Alma in the Book of Mormon, narrating the aftermath of the Anti-Nephi-Lehies' conversion and their continued observance of the Law of Moses while awaiting Christ.
