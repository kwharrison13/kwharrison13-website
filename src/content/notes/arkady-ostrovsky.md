# Arkady Ostrovsky

**One-line:** *Russian-born journalist and author of [The Invention of Russia](/books/the-invention-of-russia), a history of how Russia's media manufactured the country's "meaning" from the late Soviet era through Putin.*

## What we know

- Author of [The Invention of Russia](/books/the-invention-of-russia). He frames the book around "those who generated the 'meaning' of the country, who composed the storyline, who produced and broadcast it and in the process led the country from freedom to war… ideologists, journalists, editors, television executives" (source: [The Invention of Russia](/books/the-invention-of-russia)).
- His central thesis is that in an "idea-centric country," the "means of mass information" mattered more than the means of production — media both brought down the Soviet system and built the new authoritarian one (source: [The Invention of Russia](/books/the-invention-of-russia)).
