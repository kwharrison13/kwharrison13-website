# Michael Faraday

Chemist and physicist invoked in Kyle's notes on [Charlie Munger — A Lesson on Elementary Worldly Wisdom, Revisited](/notes/charlie-munger-a-lesson-on-elementary-worldly-wisdom-revisited) as the source of the "Work. Finish. Publish." maxim. In that talk (Talk 3 of Chapter Four of *Poor Charlie's Almanack*), Munger relays Faraday's advice to the young [William Crookes](/notes/william-crookes) — later a famous chemist and physicist in his own right — as a compact rule for productive work: do the work, bring it to completion, and ship it. The line anchors one of the talk's recurring themes about discipline and follow-through.

**Context:** Michael Faraday (1791–1867) was an English scientist whose discoveries in electromagnetism and electrochemistry — including electromagnetic induction and the laws of electrolysis — laid foundations for much of modern physics and chemistry, despite his having little formal schooling.
