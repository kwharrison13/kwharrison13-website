# James 1

A chapter of the New Testament epistle of James, recurring across Kyle's [gospel-study](/notes/gospel-study) notes around two clusters of verses. The "doers, not hearers" charge (James 1:22) anchors the [Come, Follow Me](/notes/come-follow-me) week December 31–January 6, 2019. "We Are Responsible for Our Own Learning" — "be ye doers of the word, and not hearers only" — paired with 1:5–6 ("if any of you lack wisdom, let him ask of God… ask in faith, nothing wavering") to teach personal responsibility for one's own learning. The same 1:22 verse surfaces in Diligence to make the point that conversion requires diligent action, not passive belief.

The patience cluster (James 1:3–4) anchors Patience — Topical Study: "the trying of your faith worketh patience," and "let patience have her perfect work, that ye may be perfect and entire, wanting nothing." That study also draws on James 1:19 ("swift to hear, slow to wrath") as a further proof-text for patience.

**Context:** James 1 is the opening chapter of the Epistle of James in the New Testament. It is among the most-quoted chapters in LDS teaching — verse 5 ("if any of you lack wisdom, let him ask of God") is the passage Joseph Smith credited as prompting his First Vision, and verse 22 ("be ye doers of the word, and not hearers only") is a staple call to active discipleship.

## Where this appears

- December 31–January 6, 2019. "We Are Responsible for Our Own Learning" — 1:5–6, 22 ground the week's theme of taking responsibility for our own learning by asking in faith and being doers
- Patience — Topical Study — 1:3–4 (and 1:19) anchor patience as the trial of faith that works toward perfection
- Diligence — 1:22 cited for the diligence of being "doers of the word, and not hearers only"
