# Howard P. Segal

Historian whose 1985 book Technological Utopianism in American Culture is a touchstone in Kyle's historical futurism research. Per those notes, Segal's book "focused on futurist fiction between 1883 and 1933 and identified the major themes shared by the authors" — making it a primary source for cataloguing what early American futurists believed the technological future would look like.

The research notes connect Segal's work to [EPCOT](/notes/epcot) by way of Stephen Fjellman's *Vinyl Leaves*, which "benchmarked EPCOT against these themes and noted many were expressed in the theme park." [Walt](/notes/walt-disney)'s vision for EPCOT, the notes observe, "shared the same themes" Segal had identified in turn-of-the-century utopian fiction — tying the academic taxonomy of historical futurism directly to its physical expression in Disney's planned city.

**Context:** Howard P. Segal is an American historian of technology, long a professor at the University of Maine. *Technological Utopianism in American Culture* (1985, expanded 2005) is among his best-known works.
