# D&C 101

Doctrine and Covenants section 101 — a revelation given amid the Saints' persecution in Missouri, covering chastening, agency, [Zion](/notes/zion), and the building of temples. Kyle's gospel-study notes draw on several distinct verses.

The most-cited is **verse 38** — "in patience ye may possess your souls" — which anchors Patience — Topical Study as patience being the key to self-mastery and personal perfection ("the command of our fate," soul-control as freedom and peace). Diligence cites the chastening theme in verse 8: "in the day of their peace, they esteemed lightly my counsel; but in the day of their trouble... they feel after me." The Whole Armor of God uses verses 32–34 for the **shield of faith** — "covers wherever you're hit" — paired with [Alma 37](/notes/alma-37). Teaching Repentance cites verse 78 on **moral agency and accountability** ("every man may be accountable for his own sins in the day of judgment"). And The Process of Zion leans on the temple verses (101:46–51) — "we dot the earth with temples so people can learn the laws of a celestial world" — central to its argument that Zion is built by a process, not a snap of God's fingers.
