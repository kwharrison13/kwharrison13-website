# Sriram Krishnan

Sriram Krishnan appears in Kyle's Historical Futurism - Research as the source of a point about prediction and timing: under the "You'll get it wrong" theme (illustrated by *Back to the Future 2*), Kyle cites a Krishnan tweet for the idea that a prediction can fail not because it's wrong but because "it's not the right time." He functions as a supporting voice in the essay's argument about why forecasts of the future so often miss.

**Context:** Sriram Krishnan is a technology investor and writer who has held product and leadership roles at companies including Twitter, Facebook, Microsoft, and Snap, and was a general partner at Andreessen Horowitz; he is known for active commentary on tech, startups, and product strategy.

## Where this appears

- Historical Futurism - Research — cited (via a tweet) on why predictions fail for timing rather than substance ("not the right time").
