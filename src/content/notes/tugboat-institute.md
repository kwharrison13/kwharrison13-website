# Tugboat Institute

The Tugboat Institute is the organization founded and led by [Dave Whorton](/notes/dave-whorton) to serve businesses with long-term horizons, and it sits at the center of the long-horizon framing in [On the Nature of Long-Term Holds](/notes/on-the-nature-of-long-term-holds). Whorton — a former entrepreneur (Good Technologies, drugstore.com) and investor (Kleiner Perkins, [TPG](/notes/tpg) Ventures) who lived the Silicon Valley "get-big-and-exit" narrative — built Tugboat to promulgate an alternative. He calls these long-lived organizations "evergreen" companies and encourages leaders to adopt a [100 Year Business Plan](/notes/100-year-business-plan), setting building strategies of 100 years or more that encompass business transfer and consistent ownership. In Kyle's note, Tugboat anchors the "build or buy one good business and hold it forever" cluster alongside search funds and holding companies — an explicit counterweight to exit-driven venture orthodoxy.

**Context:** Tugboat Institute, founded by Dave Whorton in 2013, is a membership community for leaders of privately held, purpose-driven "Evergreen" companies; it is known for its Evergreen 7Ps framework and an annual Tugboat Institute Summit.

## Where this appears

- [On the Nature of Long-Term Holds](/notes/on-the-nature-of-long-term-holds) — Tugboat is presented as Whorton's vehicle for the "evergreen" / 100-year-business-plan philosophy of holding a business forever.
