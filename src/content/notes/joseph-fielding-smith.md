# Joseph Fielding Smith

LDS apostle, longtime Church Historian, and (from 1970) President of the Church — and in Kyle's corpus a figure with two distinct faces. As a **doctrinal voice** he appears in Kyle's quote collections for his teachings on continual progress and agency: "It is my duty, it is yours, to be better today than I was yesterday… because we are on that road… to perfection, and that can only come through obedience and the desire in our hearts" (Spiritual Quotes — Greatest Hits); "God can educate our desires" if we let Him (Patience — Topical Study); and "My standing in this church is worth more to me than this life — ten thousand times" ([Chad's Big Quote Book](/notes/chad-s-big-quote-book)). In Kyle's First Vision study he is cited (via [Truman G. Madsen](/notes/truman-g-madsen)) on the relationship of the Father and the Son — that Jesus doing "nothing but what he had seen the Father do" meant "it had been revealed to him what his Father had done."

The two LDS-history biographies in the corpus paint a more contested picture: Smith as the **orthodox literalist** the new generation of Mormon historians worked against. In [Stretching The Heavens](/books/stretching-the-heavens) he is "a powerful voice suspicious of 'dangers lurking in modern thought'" who effectively killed B. H. Roberts's systematic theology *The Truth, the Way, the Life* (objecting to "compromises with scientific theory"), opposed biological evolution "vehemently," and — per Eugene England — had been "fooled… into false belief" on the priesthood-and-race question. In [Leonard Arrington and the Writing of Mormon History](/books/leonard-arrington-and-the-writing-of-mormon-history) he anchors the orthodoxy that "refused to see as acceptable anything but a literal interpretation," lined up with [Bruce R. McConkie](/notes/bruce-r-mcconkie), [Boyd K. Packer](/notes/boyd-k-packer), and [Ezra Taft Benson](/notes/ezra-taft-benson) against [Leonard Arrington](/notes/leonard-arrington)'s "real history" — Benson explicitly framed by his "approach in which the Lord is responsible for all… growth of the Church and the devil… for all things that interfere." His *Essentials in Church History*, the book notes, drew its own sharp criticism when first published.

**Context:** Joseph Fielding Smith (1876–1972) was the tenth President of the Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-day Saints, serving 1970–1972 after six decades as an apostle and Church Historian. A son of President Joseph F. Smith, he was a prolific doctrinal writer (e.g. *Doctrines of Salvation*, *Essentials in Church History*) known for theological conservatism. Distinct from his grandfather [Joseph Smith](/notes/joseph-smith) (the founding prophet) and his father [Joseph F. Smith](/notes/joseph-f-smith).
