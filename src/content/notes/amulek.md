# Amulek

A missionary companion to [Alma the Younger](/notes/alma-the-younger) in the Book of Mormon, prominent across Alma 8–16 and 34. In Alma 13–16 — Enter into the Rest of the Lord, Kyle treats Amulek as the case study of discipleship's cost — what is given up, and what is gained, for the gospel. The notes trace the people of Ammonihah turning their anger on Amulek for "reviling against the law and the lawyers and judges" (Alma 14:2), Kyle's observation that people often rage at perceived offenses when they don't want to face the real substance of a message, and the harrowing scene where Amulek, watching the women and children consumed in the fire, pleads with Alma to stretch forth their hands and save them (14:10).

In Testimony — Topical Study, Amulek appears as a model witness: in Alma 34 he presents his testimony of the scriptures, expressing the surety with which he knows of the coming of Christ and the [Atonement](/notes/atonement).

**Context:** Amulek is a Nephite figure in the Book of Mormon — a once-prosperous man of Ammonihah who is visited by an angel, takes in Alma, and becomes his missionary companion, later delivering a major sermon on the Atonement.

## Where this appears

- Alma 13–16 — Enter into the Rest of the Lord — Amulek as the case study in the sacrifice of discipleship, and the persecution at Ammonihah
- Testimony — Topical Study — Amulek (Alma 34) as a witness who declares his surety of Christ's coming and the Atonement
