# Academia

Across Kyle's notes, "academia" is a recurring foil — a critique of institutional learning rather than a neutral descriptor. The strongest thread is [Charlie Munger](/notes/charlie-munger)'s. In [Charlie Munger — A Lesson on Elementary Worldly Wisdom, Revisited](/notes/charlie-munger-a-lesson-on-elementary-worldly-wisdom-revisited), Munger argues that academic structure discourages "minds jumping jurisdictional boundaries," so students are "mistaught," and he traces "present bad education" to the "balkanization of academia" — a psychology professor who doesn't know the adjacent disciplines can't do the [necessary integration](/notes/multidisciplinary-thinking). In [Charlie Munger — The Psychology of Human Misjudgment](/notes/charlie-munger-the-psychology-of-human-misjudgment) he says he was "lucky to avoid for so long the academic psychology" of the textbooks, prompting Kyle's note about the spectrum between "over-complex academia and worthless 'pop' psychology," and observes that truths obvious in life but not easily reproduced in tidy experiments get missed by "the truffle hounds of psychology."

The second thread is about timing and self-direction. In [College Students- Focus On This](/notes/college-students-focus-on-this), the advice "if your professors think you're crazy, you're doing something right" is paired with Kyle's note that academia is "by nature, reactionary and reflective of the world and therefore 'behind the times'" — so as the speed of change rises, self-directed learning becomes critical. That page links the critique to [University disruption](/notes/university-disruption): schools teaching for a world that no longer exists.

## Where this appears

- [Charlie Munger — The Psychology of Human Misjudgment](/notes/charlie-munger-the-psychology-of-human-misjudgment) — the impracticality of academic psychology; the spectrum from over-complex academia to worthless pop psychology.
