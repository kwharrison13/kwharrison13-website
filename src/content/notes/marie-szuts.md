# Marie Szuts

A people/HR leader at [Figma](/notes/figma) who served as a customer-perspective reference for Kyle's Pave Diligence — 2021 work at [Index Ventures](/notes/index-ventures). In a November 1, 2021 diligence call she walked through the messy state of compensation-planning tooling from the buyer's seat — Figma had cycled through [Compaas](/notes/compaas), [ChartHop](/notes/charthop), spreadsheets, and was waiting on [Workday](/notes/workday) to go live (still running a breaking Sapling in the interim). Her core read: the "draw to just do this in spreadsheets is really strong because of the key gaps," and "80% is great but 20% makes it impossible to use." She framed comp as a massive TAM that nobody has cracked, with the real opportunity in the "25 people to 1K people" mid-market and in adjacent leveling conversations — "comp doesn't exist in a vacuum … and there is just nothing out there for that." She spoke highly of [Bethanye Blount](/notes/bethanye-blount) of Compaas but found its comp-planning cycle tool failed in practice.
