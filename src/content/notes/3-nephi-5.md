# 3 Nephi 5

A chapter Kyle's gospel study leans on most heavily for missionary identity. [3 Nephi 5](/notes/3-nephi-5):13 — Mormon's declaration "Behold, I am a disciple of Jesus Christ, the Son of God. I have been called of him to declare HIS word among HIS people, that they might have everlasting life" — is the keystone verse for both his Missionary Work — Topical Study and his Preach My Gospel Missionary page, used to make the point (following [David A. Bednar](/notes/david-a-bednar)) that the missionary's purpose "has always been the same," doing the Lord's work in His way. The chapter also supplies [3 Nephi 5](/notes/3-nephi-5):22 to his July 20–26, 2020. Alma 36–38: "Look to God and Live" study, where the Lord blessing and prospering "the children of Lehi" insomuch as they kept his commandments illustrates the Book of Mormon's recurring obedience-and-prosperity theme.

Beyond missionary work, [3 Nephi 5](/notes/3-nephi-5):13 doubles as a "pick-me-up" in How to Overcome Depression — dive into the scriptures and into service, taking the focus off yourself. It is one of the chapters covered in the weekly September 7–13, 2020. 3 Nephi 1–7: "Lift Up Your Head and Be of Good Cheer" Come Follow Me study.

**Context:** 3 Nephi 5 is a chapter of the Book of Mormon in which the record's compiler Mormon interjects to declare his own discipleship and testify of Christ, set within the account of the Nephite–Gadianton conflict.
