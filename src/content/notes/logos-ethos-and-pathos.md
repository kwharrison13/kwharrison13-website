# Logos, Ethos, and Pathos

The classical triad of persuasion — appeal to logic (logos), credibility (ethos), and emotion (pathos) — surfaces in Kyle's August 3–9, 2020. Alma 43–52: "Stand Fast in the Faith of Christ" study as a lens on the difference between manipulation and motivation. Reading Alma 43, Kyle reads Zerahemnah's design to "stir up the Lamanites to anger" so as to usurp power as a description of Satan's method: stoking others' feelings and pushing them toward your own end. The tag flags pathos-driven manipulation as the dark mirror of legitimate persuasion, and Kyle marks it as a gospel topic to explore alongside [Small and Simple Things](/notes/small-and-simple-things).

**Context:** Logos, ethos, and pathos are the three modes of rhetorical appeal Aristotle named in his *Rhetoric* — reasoned argument, the speaker's character/authority, and the audience's emotions, respectively — and remain the standard framework for analyzing persuasion.
