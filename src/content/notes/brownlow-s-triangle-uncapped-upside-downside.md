# Brownlow's Triangle (uncapped upside/downside)

"Brownlow's Triangle" is a framing of the American opportunity structure attributed to [Jeff Brownlow](/notes/jeff-brownlow) in Kyle's The Hardening Of The Great Softening - Research notes. The single grounded reference reads: "America has seemingly uncapped upside but also uncapped downside ([Jeff Brownlow](/notes/jeff-brownlow)'s triangle)." The idea Kyle returns to throughout that research is that opening the ceiling on possible outcomes also opens the floor — the same forces that make extraordinary success possible make real destitution and failure possible too. The surrounding notes contrast this with countries that "may have low ceilings but a much higher floor." The precise geometry of the "triangle" model is not spelled out in the source.
