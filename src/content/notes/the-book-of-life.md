# The Book of Life

In Kyle's Revelation — Topical Study, "The Book of Life" appears as a pointer attached to a [Henry B. Eyring](/notes/henry-b-eyring) teaching — "Listening to Hear the Lord's Voice" — within a section on never thinking you have learned enough. The surrounding passage stresses that "as soon as you consider yourself content, thinking you've learned enough to get by, you express your pride in your capability and slowly remove your dependence on Christ," cutting off "the flow of life-giving water." Eyring is quoted urging readers to "put yourself where you can hear the words of testimony... and never think you have been taught enough or that you have listened long enough." The "(see [The Book of Life](/notes/the-book-of-life))" note marks it as a cross-reference for further study rather than developing it in place.

**Context:** In Christian scripture the "book of life" (e.g. Revelation 20:12–15, 21:27) is the register of those granted eternal life, opened at the final judgment.

## Where this appears

- Revelation — Topical Study — cross-referenced beside a Henry B. Eyring talk on continual learning and listening for revelation.
