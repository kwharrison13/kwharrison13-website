# Acts 3

A New Testament chapter cited in Kyle's Mighty Repentance topical study (Topical Journal 1.141). Under "Further Study," [Acts 3](/notes/acts-3):19 appears in the chain equating full repentance with conversion — alongside [Romans 6](/notes/romans-6):6 (crucify the old man of sin), [2 Corinthians 5](/notes/2-corinthians-5):17 (become a new creature), [Colossians 3](/notes/colossians-3):10, [Alma 36](/notes/alma-36), and [Mosiah 27](/notes/mosiah-27):25. The study attaches the Bible Dictionary entry on "Conversion" (pg. 650): continued faith in Christ makes conversion complete, changing the natural man into "a sanctified, born again, purified person — a new creature in Christ."

**Context:** Acts 3:19 — "Repent ye therefore, and be converted, that your sins may be blotted out" — is Peter's call to repentance after healing the lame man at the temple gate. It is a frequent LDS proof-text linking repentance directly to conversion.
