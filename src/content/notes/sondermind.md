# SonderMind

**One-line:** *Provider-infrastructure / group-practice enablement for therapists — credentialing, billing, and paneling under the org so a mom-and-pop provider can take insurance.*

## What we know

- Grouped with Alma and [Headway](/notes/headway) as behavioral-health "infrastructure providers": a two-sided marketplace that credentials providers under its organization, panels them to bill [Insurance](/notes/insurance), and pays them out — letting a small provider offload the business/billing side (September 22nd, 2021 — Adam Chekroud (Spring Health)).
- Captures only one part of the value chain; gets squeezed on both sides — rising consumer-acquisition cost (CAC) and lower margin on what it pays providers (September 22nd, 2021 — Adam Chekroud (Spring Health)).
- Per [Adam Chekroud](/notes/adam-chekroud), these players were heard to be fronting money to providers before insurance pays — a worse working-capital dynamic (September 22nd, 2021 — Adam Chekroud (Spring Health)).
- Lacks the employer channel that Spring/Lyra/Ginger compete in: "Everyone wants to do that but they're not built for that" (September 22nd, 2021 — Adam Chekroud (Spring Health)).
