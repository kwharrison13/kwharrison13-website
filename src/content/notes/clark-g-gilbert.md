# Clark G. Gilbert

Elder Clark G. Gilbert is the author of Come Home, his April 2026 General Conference address — his first as a newly called Apostle. In it he pledges his life as a witness of Jesus Christ as Redeemer and Repairer, and shares four ministry stories of people who found their way back to the gospel: Sister Anglesey (who parked in a church lot for years before finally receiving her endowment), Brother Vargas ("you don't have to be perfect — Christ makes up the difference"), Justin and Kenna Valdez (who resolved faith doubts by reanchoring on the Holy Ghost rather than answering every question), and John Raass (a former BYU football star who joined realizing his late parents would support it). His central thread: Christ is calling everyone home, and it is He — not us — who ultimately does the restoring.

**Context:** Clark G. Gilbert is a leader in The Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-day Saints, called to the Quorum of the Twelve Apostles in 2026. He previously served as Commissioner of the Church Educational System and as president of BYU–Pathway Worldwide and BYU–Idaho, and earlier was a Harvard Business School professor specializing in disruptive innovation.
