# Agency

Agency runs through Kyle's corpus on two tracks that he deliberately overlays. The theological track is dominant: in [Faith is Not Blind](/books/faith-is-not-blind), agency and discovery are "the point" — "we value what we discover more than we value what we are told," and God "refuses to compel belief" precisely so "we are not forced by circumstances to believe," with the Restoration placing "enormous value on the concepts of personal freedom, agency, and growth." The Come Follow Me studies sharpen this: the 2019 New Testament intro week (December 31–January 6, 2019. "We Are Responsible for Our Own Learning") builds on [David A. Bednar](/notes/david-a-bednar)'s charge to be [Agents Unto Themselves](/notes/agents-unto-themselves) who learn by faith rather than waiting to be acted upon, and the Alma 30–Alma 31 study shows Korihor the [Anti-Christ](/notes/anti-christ) arguing *against* agency (alongside [prophecy](/notes/prophecy), [Atonement](/notes/atonement), and resurrection), countered by "Choose ye this day whom ye will serve" and [Richard G. Scott](/notes/richard-g-scott)'s line that God's help comes "in response to faith, obedience, and the proper use of agency."

The secular track reads agency through developmental science and philosophy. [The Self-Driven Child](/books/the-self-driven-child) argues "agency takes practice" and maps an autonomy/self-determination thesis directly onto the LDS framing of agency and self-mastery — which is exactly how Kyle read it, "with constant overlay between developmental neuroscience and an LDS framework." [Stories of Your Life and Others](/books/story-of-your-life) (Ted Chiang) presses the hard case: the narrator concludes that knowledge of the future is incompatible with [Free Will](/notes/free-will), yet freedom remains "perfectly real in the context of sequential consciousness" — Chiang's free-but-bound paradox that volition is intrinsic to consciousness even where coercion becomes meaningless. The concept recurs across dozens of gospel-study pages and pairs with [Accountability](/notes/accountability), [Self-Mastery](/notes/self-mastery), and the [Plan of Salvation](/notes/plan-of-salvation).

**Context:** In Latter-day Saint theology, "agency" (or moral agency / free agency) is the divinely-given capacity to choose between good and evil and to be accountable for one's choices; in the broader philosophical sense it overlaps with debates over free will and determinism.
