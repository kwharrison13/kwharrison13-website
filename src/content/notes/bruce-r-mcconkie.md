# Bruce R. McConkie

An LDS apostle whose teachings recur throughout Kyle's gospel-study corpus, cited most often on **conversion, the priesthood, and the doctrinal structure of the gospel** — but also as a study in doctrinal overreach. [Chad's Big Quote Book](/notes/chad-s-big-quote-book) quotes him "repeatedly on conversion, the 'straight and narrow' path, and the natural man," including that "true conversion is not born of contention or debate. The things of God are known only by revelation from the spirit of God." [Future Mormon](/books/future-mormon) builds its grace-first reading on McConkie's 1981 BYU devotional "The Three Pillars of Eternity" — creation, fall, atonement — "the three events, preeminent and transcendent above all others… the foundations upon which all things rest." [The Priesthood Power of Women](/books/the-priesthood-power-of-women) quotes his teaching that "this doctrine of the priesthood… cannot be learned out of the scriptures alone… It is known only by personal revelation," and his argument that the Church's ecclesiastical structure exists because "you can't have civil and ecclesiastical authority combined" in the modern social setting.

The countervailing portrait comes from [Stretching The Heavens](/books/stretching-the-heavens), which casts McConkie as the author of *Mormon Doctrine* — a compendium for which two reviewing apostles recommended over 1,000 corrections and that President [David O. McKay](/notes/david-o-mckay) suspended from republication — and of the 1980 "Seven Deadly Heresies" talk that publicly censured Eugene England. The book also surfaces McConkie's later act of contrition on race ("Forget everything that I said… that is contrary to the present revelation. [I] spoke with a limited understanding"), while noting he continued to publish problematic views afterward.

**Context:** Bruce R. McConkie (1915–1985) was a member of the Quorum of the Twelve Apostles of The Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-day Saints and author of the influential reference work *Mormon Doctrine*.
