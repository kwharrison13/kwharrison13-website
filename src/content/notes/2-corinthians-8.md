# 2 Corinthians 8

Paul's chapter on liberal, equality-minded giving. In Kyle's 8 Points of the Gospel study, it is cited for verses 14–15 in a note contrasting communism with the gospel law of consecration (connected to an anti-Christ section and to [1 John](/notes/1-john)) — the passage about abundance supplying others' want so "there may be equality."

**Context:** In 2 Corinthians 8, the Apostle [Paul the Apostle](/notes/paul-the-apostle) urges the Corinthian believers toward generous giving for the relief of the Jerusalem saints, framing the goal as an "equality" in which one's abundance supplies another's lack (vv. 14–15), echoing the manna account where each had enough.

## Where this appears

- 8 Points of the Gospel — cited (8:14–15) in a note distinguishing communism from gospel consecration, alongside [1 John](/notes/1-john)
