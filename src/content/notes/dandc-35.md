# D&C 35

Across Kyle's gospel-study notes, Doctrine and Covenants section 35 is cited almost entirely for **verse 13: God calling "the weak things of the world."** In Chosen Leaders, "God calls 'the weak things of the world, those who are unlearned and despised' (D&C 35:13) because it is 'not by might, nor by power, but by the spirit'" — the unlearned are sent "to thrash the nations by the power of my spirit," making weakness the precondition for being chosen rather than a disqualification. Miscellaneous Scriptures distills it to "the unlearned shall preach the gospel."

The same verse underwrites the missionary-preparation notes: Things To Know Before Your Mission cites D&C 35:13–14 for the importance of hard work despite inadequacy ("I can't enjoy what I don't do"). And Born Again folds it into a reading of the Good Samaritan — it is "not the nations of the earth, but rather the 'weak things of the world' who lift us up," pointing ultimately to Christ "who was wounded for our transgressions." The unifying theme is that God deliberately works through the weak and unlearned so that the power is plainly His.

**Context:** Doctrine and Covenants 35 is a revelation through [Joseph Smith](/notes/joseph-smith) (1830) given to Sidney Rigdon; verse 13's promise that the Lord will call "the weak things of the world, those who are unlearned and despised… to thrash the nations by the power of my Spirit" is a frequently cited Latter-day Saint text on divine empowerment of the humble.

## Where this appears

- Chosen Leaders — D&C 35:13, God calling "the weak things of the world" to work by the Spirit, not by might
- Things To Know Before Your Mission — D&C 35:13–14 on the importance of hard work in missionary service
- Born Again — the "weak things of the world" who lift us up, read into the Good Samaritan and pointing to Christ
- Miscellaneous Scriptures — "the unlearned shall preach the gospel" (35:13)
