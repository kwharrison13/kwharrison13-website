# Letters to My Family

A recurring personal tag in Kyle's [Come, Follow Me](/notes/come-follow-me) study, marking the verses he reads as a model for writing to his own children. The thread runs strongest through the father-to-son chapters of [Alma the Younger](/notes/alma-the-younger)'s counsel and Mormon's epistles to Moroni. From Alma 37, Kyle pulls the simplest things worth teaching children — repentance, faith on Christ, meekness, and to "never be weary of good works" — and from Alma 38:3 he flags Alma's expression of "great joy in thee already" as the tone a parent should take. In the Alma 39–Alma 42 week he draws out that Alma spends only 14 verses on his son [Corianton](/notes/corianton)'s sins but 74 verses teaching about [Jesus Christ](/notes/jesus) before relating it back — the model of teaching *toward* Christ rather than merely against sin, paired with [Listening](/notes/listening) closely enough to know what burdens a child's mind ([Parenting](/notes/parenting)).

The tag's clearest statement comes in Moroni 7 and Moroni 8, which are literally Mormon's words written to his son Moroni. Kyle's note — "Your children will take comfort in your writing. Write to them." — reads Moroni's preservation of his father's epistle (Moroni 8:1) as proof that "these were the words that Moroni took comfort in during his final days." The takeaway Kyle carries forward is to value letters as something a child will hold onto when a parent is gone.
