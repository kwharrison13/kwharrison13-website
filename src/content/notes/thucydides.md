# Thucydides

In Kyle's [John Quincy Adams](/books/john-quincy-adams) notes, Thucydides appears as a pillar of the classical education that formed citizens of the early republic. A highlighted passage names him "the great historian of ancient Greece," listed alongside [Cicero](/notes/cicero), Tacitus, [Plutarch](/notes/plutarch), and Sallust as the "curricular core" of an emerging "education for citizenship." The note's argument is that "the classics" functioned not as a subject like geography but "as a lens through which to examine and understand the life around you" — Thucydides standing in for the ancient-history training that shaped Adams and his generation.

**Context:** Thucydides (c. 460–400 BC) was an Athenian general and historian whose *History of the Peloponnesian War* is regarded as a founding work of rigorous, cause-seeking historiography and a touchstone of realist thought about power and politics.
