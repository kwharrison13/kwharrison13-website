# A24

**One-line:** *Independent entertainment company founded in 2012 (David Fenkel & Daniel Katz) that became "less a film business and more an emerging cultural conglomerate" by backing auteurs and reading the cultural zeitgeist.*

## What we know

- The central case study of [The Hits Business](/essays/the-hits-business): proof that **scale and taste need not be mutually exclusive.** Behind *Everything Everywhere All At Once* (seven Oscars, $140M+ globally on a $25M investment), *Moonlight* ($4M budget → $65M+), *Uncut Gems*, *Lady Bird*, *Swiss Army Man*. (The Hits Business - Research, per [Mario Gabriele](/notes/mario-gabriele) / [The Generalist](/notes/the-generalist))
- "Rather than underwrite safe bets, founders David Fenkel and Daniel Katz look for true [auteurs](/notes/auteur-theory) — then back them to the hilt"; an "almost venture-like search for outliers," with **purposefully small investments** sized so they're "not massive swings." (The Hits Business - Research, per [Mario Gabriele](/notes/mario-gabriele) / [The Generalist](/notes/the-generalist))
- Built on internet-culture, fandom, and cheap social-media customer acquisition — "no studio has more efficiently inserted itself into the online zeitgeist." (The Hits Business - Research)
- Reached a **~$2.5B valuation**; described by critic Alison Willmore as "an incredible triumph of branding," and "less a film business and more an emerging cultural conglomerate." (The Hits Business - Research)
- Expanding beyond film into TV, documentaries, music, publishing, cosmetics, digital memberships, and physical experiences — "a redux of Walt's strategy designed for the 21st century." (The Hits Business - Research, cf. [Walt Disney](/notes/walt-disney))
- Culture described as "inspired by Silicon Valley rather than Hollywood" — "more akin to a tech company than a classic studio." (The Hits Business - Research)
