# Pondering

Pondering is the second of Kyle's 5 P's Never To Forget — five keys for studying the Book of Mormon so that reading becomes revelation rather than mere reading. The note frames pondering as distinct from reading: "The difference between reading and pondering is like night and day." It grounds the practice in [Moroni](/notes/moroni)'s own exhortation, who "suggested you do this" — i.e. to ponder the text rather than only read it. Alongside Prayer, Pencil, Purpose, and reading Pretty often, pondering is part of how scripture study is meant to slow down and deepen.

**Context:** In Latter-day Saint scripture study, "pondering" draws on Moroni 10:3–5, where readers are invited to ponder a passage and then ask God whether it is true — making reflection a precondition for receiving spiritual confirmation.

## Where this appears

- 5 P's Never To Forget — listed as the second "P," contrasted with mere reading and tied to [Moroni](/notes/moroni)'s exhortation to ponder
