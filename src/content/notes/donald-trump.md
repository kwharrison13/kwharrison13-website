# Donald Trump

Across Kyle's corpus, Donald Trump recurs as the figure whose rise reframes the books he appears in. In [Romney — A Reckoning](/books/romney-a-reckoning) he is "the antagonist whose rise inverted the GOP's belief-to-tribe relationship and made 'moral tests' the recurring frame" — watching Trump's conquest of the party was, for [Mitt Romney](/notes/mitt-romney), "even more devastating... than losing his own election in 2012," and it led Romney to conclude "a very large portion of my party really doesn't believe in the Constitution." [Shortest Way Home](/books/shortest-way-home) reads "Make America Great Again" as "a politics of loss-reversal, the 'core falsehood' that time itself can be rewound," with [Pete Buttigieg](/notes/pete-buttigieg) noting how opposing Trump tempted even his critics into "a loss of decency."

[The Man Who Broke Capitalism](/books/the-man-who-broke-capitalism) casts Trump's campaign as "a rebuttal of Welchism" — the [Jack Welch](/notes/jack-welch)-era doctrine of downsizing and financialization — even as Trump and Welch "bonded over social-media falsehoods" and Trump cosplayed the CEO on *The Apprentice*. [The Next Mormons](/books/the-next-mormons) supplies a narrower note: when then-candidate Trump called for a ban on Muslims entering the country, the LDS Church publicly defended religious freedom for everyone, part of its comparatively pro-immigration posture. On the [Bill Maher](/notes/bill-maher) page, Trump appears as a case study in tribal belief-flipping — the American Academy of Pediatrics reversing on school reopening "once Trump favored it," and the "Make America Healthy Again" rebrand.

**Context:** Donald Trump is the 45th and 47th President of the United States, a real-estate developer and former reality-television host whose 2016 takeover of the Republican Party reshaped American conservative politics.
