# Historical Futurists

"Historical Futurists" is the human lineage behind the discipline Kyle names [Historical Futurism](/essays/historical-futurism) — the people who treat the future as a craft by "looking back to see forward," reading and writing optimistic [Science Fiction](/notes/science-fiction) as blueprints, then building toward it. The Historical Futurism - Research doc lays out the thesis: most science fiction is dystopian, and that shortage of hopeful dreaming has a real cost, because "to invent the future, people first need to be inspired by hopeful visions of it" (the [David Perell](/notes/david-perell) thesis). The futurist's feedback loop runs from foundational futures → literature of dreaming → dreamers → artists' renditions → forecasting → building — a cousin of open-source knowledge that Kyle calls "open-source imagination."

The tag also marks specific exemplars across the corpus. In [Amazon Unbound](/books/amazon-unbound) Kyle tags Bezos "reading SF as blueprints for the future" with `#Historical Futurists` — a builder treating science fiction as an engineering spec. [Where Is My Flying Car](/notes/where-is-my-flying-car) (anti-library) "sits squarely in the lineage of thinkers asking why the predicted future of atoms (not just bits) never arrived" — the stagnation-diagnosing wing of the same family.
