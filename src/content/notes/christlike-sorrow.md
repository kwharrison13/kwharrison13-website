# Christlike Sorrow

"Christlike sorrow" is one of the core themes Kyle draws from his Come Follow Me study of 3 Nephi 1–3 Nephi 7 (week of September 7–13, 2020). The idea is that genuine, Christ-like grief breaks the heart over others' suffering rather than taking joy in it. Kyle anchors it in 3 Nephi 1:10, where [Nephi](/notes/nephi)'s "heart was exceedingly sorrowful" at the wickedness of his people. He pairs this with the [Joseph Smith](/notes/joseph-smith) teaching that true love of God ranges "through the whole world, anxious to bless the whole human race," and with [Brad Wilcox](/notes/brad-wilcox)'s image of Christ begging the unrepentant to *choose to stay*.

The concept is defined partly by its opposite: "There is nothing Christlike about taking joy in another's sorrow." When unbelievers in 3 Nephi 1:6 rejoice that Samuel's prophesied sign has supposedly failed, Kyle reads animosity toward one's fellow man as a sign you are "on the wrong end of that idea." In the week's study it sits alongside [Charity](/notes/charity), [Pride](/notes/pride), and the lesson of [The Rising Generation](/essays/the-rising-generation).
