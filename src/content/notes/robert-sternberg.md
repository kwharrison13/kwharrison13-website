# Robert Sternberg

Cornell psychology professor whose warning on generative AI anchors the cognitive-decline thread in [We Need a Renaissance of Rhetoric](/essays/we-need-a-renaissance-of-rhetoric). Quoted (via *The Guardian*) for the line: "The greatest worry in these times of generative AI is not that it *may* compromise human creativity or intelligence, but that it already has." In Kyle's research the quote sits directly beside the [Flynn Effect](/notes/flynn-effect) — the generation-over-generation rise in IQ scores since the 1930s that began to slow and in some cases reverse around 2006 — to argue that cognitive off-loading onto LLMs may already be eroding human intelligence rather than merely threatening to.

His role in [AI Cheating in Education](/notes/ai-cheating-in-education) is to supply the empirical edge of the humanist case: not just that students *might* lose something by ceding writing and thinking to machines, but that measurable cognitive capacity may already be in retreat.

**Context:** Robert J. Sternberg is a prominent American psychologist known for his triarchic theory of intelligence and theories of creativity and wisdom, and has held faculty positions at Yale, Tufts, and Cornell.
