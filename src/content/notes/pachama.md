# Pachama

A carbon-offset marketplace that recurs in Kyle's 2021 climate diligence as a competitive reference point — and, notably, a contested one. In the December 13th, 2021 — NCX Partner Presentation, NCX positions itself against Pachama by drawing a sharp distinction: NCX *originates* carbon projects and handles "the nitty-gritty of creating those carbon credits," whereas Pachama is characterized as "just buying and reselling credits without creating things." In the November 5th, 2021 — Sylvera Pre-Brief (Julia Andre), Pachama appears on the supply/sourcing side of the carbon stack — sourcing offset supply and offering "seamless access to vetted projects" — alongside players like Watershed, Patch, and Sweep. Sylvera's pre-brief frames Pachama's move into ratings skeptically: because Pachama also sells credits, its ratings "cannot be considered an unbiased market standard," and Sylvera judges Pachama's rating technology (satellite-data-based) less sophisticated than its own. The brief also notes Pachama reached out to Sylvera for a partnership in which Sylvera would rate Pachama's supply.

**Context:** Pachama is a San Francisco-based startup using satellite imagery and machine learning to verify and monitor forest carbon projects, helping companies purchase higher-confidence nature-based carbon offsets.

## Where this appears

- December 13th, 2021 — NCX Partner Presentation — cited as a competitor NCX differentiates against; framed as a credit reseller rather than a project originator.
- November 5th, 2021 — Sylvera Pre-Brief (Julia Andre) — positioned as an offset-sourcing marketplace whose conflicted rating efforts Sylvera contrasts with its own unbiased ratings.
