# Unity of the Faith

Across two consecutive Come Follow Me studies of the risen Christ's ministry among the Nephites, **Unity of the Faith** is a central theme. In the 3 Nephi 20–26 study, it appears alongside the [Gathering of Israel](/notes/gathering-of-israel) and the building of the [New Jerusalem](/notes/new-jerusalem): the ultimate sign of unity is when "every knee shall bow" and no one can deny Jesus Christ ([Every knee shall bow](/notes/every-knee-shall-bow)). Kyle ties this to **the limits of language** — imperfect human communication points back toward the [Adamic Language](/notes/adamic-language) that was "pure and undefiled" (3 Nephi 21:20), so that perfect unity and perfect communication are linked.

In the 3 Nephi 27–4 Nephi study, Unity of the Faith is paired with the name of Christ, [Fulness of Joy](/notes/fulness-of-joy), and [Zion](/notes/zion) — the nearly 200 years of peace after Christ's visit, when "there could not be a happier people." The unsettling counterpoint is the **speed with which pride reclaims a happy people** once that unity dissolves into the [pride cycle](/notes/the-pride-cycle). The two studies together frame unity as both the high-water mark of a covenant people and a fragile state that apostasy erodes.

## Where this appears

- October 12–18, 2020. 3 Nephi 20–26: "Ye Are the Children of the Covenant" — unity as covenant destiny, "every knee shall bow," and the limits of language pointing toward the Adamic Language
- October 19–25, 2020. 3 Nephi 27–4 Nephi: "There Could Not Be a Happier People" — unity of the faith as the foundation of the Zion society after Christ's visit, and how quickly the pride cycle undoes it
