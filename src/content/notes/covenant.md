# Covenant

In LDS doctrine as Kyle's notes use it, a covenant is a sacred, terms-set agreement between God and a person or people. The Process of the Book of Mormon cites the [Gospel Principles](/notes/gospel-principles) definition: "a covenant means a sacred agreement or mutual promise between God and a person or a group of people. In making a covenant, God promises a blessing for obedience… He sets the terms of His covenants… If we CHOOSE not to obey, He withholds the blessings, and in some instances a penalty also is given." This frames the Book of Mormon's recurring "obedience-and-prosperity chain" — keep the commandments and prosper, keep them not and be cut off ([D&C 82](/notes/dandc-82):10).

In [The Priesthood Power of Women](/books/the-priesthood-power-of-women), covenant-making is the entry point to priesthood power: "the moment a man begins making covenants, he starts the process of becoming a patriarch. The moment a woman begins making covenants, she starts the process of becoming a matriarch." Covenants are made in the [Temple](/notes/temple) but kept at home ("the home is the testing ground"), and the new and everlasting covenant of marriage seals husband and wife as coequal partners in the patriarchal order of the [Priesthood](/notes/priesthood). The book's argument is that God's power "is shared with those who make and keep covenants" — locating women's access to priesthood power in covenant-keeping rather than ordination.

## Where this appears

- [The Priesthood Power of Women](/books/the-priesthood-power-of-women) — covenant-making as the start of becoming patriarch/matriarch; God's power shared with covenant keepers; made in the temple, kept at home
- The Process of the Book of Mormon — the [Gospel Principles](/notes/gospel-principles) definition of covenant and the obedience-prosperity covenant chain running through the Book of Mormon's first 60 pages
