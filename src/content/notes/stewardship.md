# Stewardship

In Kyle's notes, **stewardship** is the doctrine that what we hold — wealth, talents, callings, even other people — is the Lord's, lent to us to manage rather than to own. [Brigham Young](/notes/brigham-young) states it most bluntly in Temporal Wealth and the Kingdom of God — Brigham Young: the Lord gives gold and silver "to this one and that one — the wicked as well as the righteous — to see what they will do with it, but **it all belongs to Him**." Our "legitimate business is to do what the Lord wants us to do with that which He bestows upon us … whether it is to give all, one-tenth, or the surplus." Stepping aside from that is "an illegitimate train of business." The corollary is escalating accountability: "the more we are blessed with means, the more we are blessed with responsibility," tied to the parable of the talents (Matthew 25:14–30).

In [Working Toward Zion](/books/working-toward-zion) (James W. Lucas and Warner Woodworth) the same doctrine becomes an organizing principle for economics. The **law of stewardship** demands local self-government — "a hand up, not a hand out" — over top-down, "president knows best" development (a Haiti cooperative that failed for lack of local management is the cautionary tale). It calls for private ownership and management of property, not "centrally controlled Church Communism": employee-owned enterprises run "by common consent," with allocation decided by "the voice and common consent of the order" rather than flowing to leaders. Kyle links this thread to [Capital Allocation](/notes/capital-allocation), [United Order](/notes/united-order), [Employee Ownership](/notes/employee-ownership), and modern analogues like [Teamshares](/notes/teamshares) and [Mondragon](/notes/mondragon).

A third, personal register shows up in Spiritual Gifts and [Saints — No Unhallowed Hand](/books/saints-no-unhallowed-hand): [David O. McKay](/notes/david-o-mckay)'s "Stewardship" lists the order the Lord interviews you at the last day — wife, children, your personal talents, service through your calling, honesty in your work, contribution to those around you — making stewardship a frame for ordering one's whole life. In Saints, it surfaces as the apostolic governance theme of "authority to decide vs. authority to execute" — the Quorum acting unanimously over "everyone within its stewardship."

**Context:** In Latter-day Saint theology, stewardship is the principle that all things belong to God and that humans are accountable agents (stewards) over what they are given — rooted in scriptural texts like Doctrine and Covenants 104 and the parable of the talents, and historically expressed through the United Order and law of consecration.

## Where this appears

- [Working Toward Zion](/books/working-toward-zion) — the "law of stewardship" as the basis for local self-government, employee ownership, and bottom-up allocation in a Zion economics.
- Temporal Wealth and the Kingdom of God — Brigham Young — "it all belongs to Him"; wealth is lent "to see what they will do with it," to be disposed of as the Lord dictates.
- Spiritual Gifts — David O. McKay's "Stewardship": the order the Lord interviews you at the last day (wife, children, talents, calling, work, contribution).
- [Saints — No Unhallowed Hand](/books/saints-no-unhallowed-hand) — apostolic governance: authority over "everyone within its stewardship," and the call to women to use "every capacity" in their stewardship.
