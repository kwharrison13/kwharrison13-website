# Genesys

Genesys appears throughout the Cresta.ai - Diligence notes as one of the major **[CCaaS](/notes/ccaas) (Contact-Center-as-a-Service) platforms** — grouped with [Five9](/notes/five9) and [Amazon Connect](/notes/amazon-connect) — and the central role it plays in Kyle's notes is as a **consolidation threat** to the agent-assist / real-time-coaching category Cresta competes in. Multiple experts expect a CCaaS platform like Genesys to buy one of Cresta / [Balto.ai](/notes/balto-ai) / [Observe.ai](/notes/observe-ai) and fold real-time agent coaching into its toolkit, since the platforms already own the agent's workflow. [Randy Clapp](/notes/randy-clapp) (Advantage Communications) specifically expects the PBX/CCaaS players — "NICE inContact, [Five9](/notes/five9), Genesys PureCloud" — to acquire one of the agent-assist vendors. The recurring [All-in-One vs. Best-in-Class](/notes/all-in-one-vs-best-in-class) tension runs through it: [Klasey Gage](/notes/klasey-gage) (Adobe), who spent 13 years at a contact-center solution built on / acquired by Genesys, notes that [Amazon Connect](/notes/amazon-connect) and Genesys are building toward this category and that it feels like "a matter of time until it lives in [CCaaS](/notes/ccaas)." Genesys also surfaces operationally — Samsung used Genesys for telephony in the [Observe.ai](/notes/observe-ai) rollout described by [Kostia Pertsovskyi](/notes/kostia-pertsovskyi).

**Context:** Genesys is a customer-experience and contact-center software company (its cloud platform is branded Genesys Cloud / formerly PureCloud), one of the leading CCaaS vendors alongside Five9, NICE, and Amazon Connect.
