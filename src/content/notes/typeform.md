# Typeform

Typeform, the conversational online-form and survey product, recurs across Kyle's notes as a product-led-growth reference and a GTM lineage marker. In [Product Led Growth — The End User Era](/books/product-led-growth-the-end-user-era) it is one of the named "Companies cited" (alongside [Calendly](/notes/calendly), [Stripe](/notes/stripe), [Mixpanel](/notes/mixpanel), and Expensify), and the book uses it twice as a case study: Barrett's customer conversations at Expensify are described as "the same as the kind Typeform's sales team was having," the difference being that Expensify let the product sell itself; and Typeform is the example for **tracking leading rather than lagging indicators** of customer health — features that make customers "sticky," like its teams feature or integrations, ahead of [Net Promoter Score](/notes/net-promoter-score) surveys.

Typeform also appears as a GTM-talent anchor and a competitive baseline. A saved Brianne Kimmel tweet (One Of The Most Challenging... (tweet)) shares an early-stage go-to-market playbook drawn from being the first Head of Sales & Customer Success at Typeform and [Notion](/notes/notion) — [Brianne Kimmel](/notes/brianne-kimmel) being the source of that playbook. And in the Sprig fka UserLeap - Diligence, Typeform is repeatedly cited as a generic, out-of-context survey tool that Sprig outperforms: customers reported ~3–5% response rates on Typeform/Zoom Survey/Google Docs versus ~60% on Sprig's in-app micro-surveys at [Chipper Cash](/notes/chipper-cash).

**Context:** Typeform is a Barcelona-based SaaS company (founded 2012) known for conversational, one-question-at-a-time online forms and surveys.
