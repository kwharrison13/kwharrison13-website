# Jim Bowe

Bishop quoted in Kyle's Obedience — Topical Study for an analogy about how obedience is actually learned. The teaching: "Obedience isn't always learned by doing something bad and then you get punished. It happens over time as you're desensitized to dangerous things. You cross the street without looking and nothing happens. One day, you cross and are hit by a car. If you live you've gained by experience a respect for obedience." Kyle pairs this with the idea that the point of *intelligent* obedience is to learn the blessings of obedience without having to be "hit by a car" — to know the reward without first seeing it, which is the substance of faith and valiant discipleship.
