# Mormonism

The religious tradition at the center of much of Kyle's reading and study. In the wiki it surfaces most directly through [Truman G. Madsen](/notes/truman-g-madsen) — the LDS philosopher and BYU professor best known for his lecture series on the Prophet [Joseph Smith](/notes/joseph-smith) — where "mormonism" serves as the tying tag for his place in Kyle's `religious-intellectualism` corpus. The tag connects a wider cluster of LDS-focused pages (people like [Jeffrey R. Holland](/notes/jeffrey-r-holland), concepts like the [First Vision](/notes/first-vision), and gospel-study material such as Early Mormonism and the Joseph Smith lectures), though those pages carry the theme rather than linking to this concept directly.

**Context:** Mormonism is the religious tradition originating with Joseph Smith's founding of the Church of Christ in 1830 (today principally The Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-day Saints), centered on the Book of Mormon, continuing revelation through living prophets, and temple ordinances. It is a recurring subject in Kyle's wiki across books, gospel-study notes, and people pages.
