# Dietrich Bonhoeffer

The German theologian whose "cheap grace" critique recurs in Kyle's gospel-study and faith-and-doubt sources. In [The Crucible of Doubt](/books/the-crucible-of-doubt) (Terryl and Fiona Givens), Bonhoeffer's line "cheap grace is the mortal enemy of the church" anchors the book's doubt-as-faith-building thread: an easy faith doesn't stick, sitting alongside Joseph Smith's claim that no church can produce salvation "that does not demand the sacrifice of its members."

The same idea is quoted more fully in Christ Defines His Gospel: "Cheap grace is grace without discipleship. Costly grace is the call of Jesus Christ at which the disciple leaves his nets and follows him. It is costly because it costs a man his life, and it is grace because it gives a man the only true life." There it reinforces the LDS framing — paired with [Dallin H. Oaks](/notes/dallin-h-oaks) on being saved "conditionally" but not "finally" until enduring to the end — that grace and discipleship (obedience, repentance, ongoing faithfulness) are inseparable rather than opposed.

**Context:** Dietrich Bonhoeffer (1906–1945) was a German Lutheran pastor and theologian executed by the Nazi regime for his role in the resistance. His concept of "cheap grace" versus "costly grace" comes from his book *The Cost of Discipleship* (1937).
