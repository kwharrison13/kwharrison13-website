# Onfido

Onfido is an identity-verification (KYC / IDV) company that recurs across Kyle's 2021 diligence-research as one of the document-and-selfie verification players. In the Identity & Access Management Diligence — 2021 notes it is listed among the Identity Verification vendors (alongside [Persona](/notes/persona) and Jumio) and raised as the kind of KYC vendor that tracks users during identity governance for consumer applications. In the November 2nd, 2021 — Riley Hughes (Trinsic) sourcing call, Onfido is named (with [Persona](/notes/persona)) as the comparison point for HooYou — the verification step NatWest uses to "take pictures of a doc or selfie and match that up" as part of authenticating users.

Onfido also appears as a person and a customer in the Watershed Climate Diligence — 2021 dossier: co-founder and COO [Eamon Jubbawy](/notes/eamon-jubbawy) gave an October 2021 expert call as a Watershed customer, walking through how Onfido evaluated carbon-accounting vendors. His most transferable insight came from Onfido's own product — that identity verification "need[s] the human component because there are always new versions of documents emerging over time," so human analysts must understand and feed them into the machines — which he used as an analogy for why carbon accounting will likewise stay a human-plus-automation hybrid.

**Context:** Onfido is a London-founded (2012) identity-verification company that uses document checks and facial-recognition/biometric matching to verify users, selling primarily into fintech and regulated industries; it was acquired by Entrust in 2024.
