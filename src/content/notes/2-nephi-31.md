# 2 Nephi 31

Nephi's discourse on "the doctrine of Christ" — the chapter Kyle's topical studies return to most often when defining the gate and the path of the gospel. In Christ Defines His Gospel it pairs with [3 Nephi 27](/notes/3-nephi-27) as a scriptural anchor for relying "wholly upon the merits of Him" (v. 19) and the truth that "we can't follow Christ without keeping HIS commandments" (v. 10). Obedience — Topical Study reads verses 17 and 19 as the pattern of agency exercised toward Christ — repentance and baptism by water are "the gate by which ye should enter," after which one presses forward "with unshaken faith in Him." Knowledge — Topical Study and Faith — Topical Study both quote verse 14's warning that to know Christ and then deny Him "would have been better for you that ye had not known me."

The chapter also surfaces in Kyle's notes on the [Holy Ghost](/notes/holy-ghost), plainness, and endurance. Spiritual Gifts cites verse 18 as "the only way we can know the Father and the Son"; Patience — Topical Study draws on verse 3 ("my soul delighteth in plainness") as the basis for glorying in the gospel's simplicity and not looking "beyond the mark"; and Miscellaneous Scriptures and Humility — Topical Study return to verse 20 ("press forward… endure to the end") as the capstone of enduring faith. Across these studies 2 Nephi 31 functions as the recurring scriptural definition of the strait-and-narrow path: gate (faith, repentance, baptism, the Holy Ghost), then endurance.

## Where this appears

- Christ Defines His Gospel — paired with [3 Nephi 27](/notes/3-nephi-27); vv. 10, 19 ground "relying wholly upon the merits of Him"
- Obedience — Topical Study — vv. 17, 19 as the gate and the strait, narrow path entered by agency
- Knowledge — Topical Study — v. 14 on the danger of knowing then denying Christ
- Faith — Topical Study — v. 14 quoted on the cost of denying Christ after knowing Him
- Patience — Topical Study — v. 3 on plainness; not looking "beyond the mark"
- Spiritual Gifts — v. 18 as the means of knowing the Father and the Son through the [Holy Ghost](/notes/holy-ghost)
- Miscellaneous Scriptures — v. 20 in the chain on enduring faith ("press forward… endure to the end")
- Humility — Topical Study — v. 12 on following Christ's example of sanctification
