# BizOps

In Kyle's notes, "BizOps" names the operations-and-business-operations role that sits at the center of [Retool](/notes/retool)'s addressable market. [Retool](/notes/retool) is framed as serving two audiences at once across the [Low Code / No Code](/notes/low-code-no-code) spectrum: self-upskilling BizOps/operations teams and technical teams automating drudge work. The product reshapes how BizOps people think about their roles — operators move from copy-paste workflows toward building [Automation](/notes/automation)s themselves, even though doing so requires a little technical skill (some SQL or JavaScript). This is the recurring self-[Upskilling](/notes/upskilling) theme: a non-engineering operator graduating from manual work into tool-building.

**Context:** "BizOps" (business operations) is a generalist function common in startups and scale-ups, responsible for cross-functional operational problems — process, tooling, analytics, and internal workflow — that fall between traditional departments. It is a frequent buyer/builder persona for low-code internal-tools products.

## Where this appears

- [Retool](/notes/retool) — frames BizOps/operations teams as a primary self-upskilling audience moving from copy-paste to building automations.
