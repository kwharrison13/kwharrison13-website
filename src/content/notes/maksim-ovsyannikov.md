# Maksim Ovsyannikov

A voice quoted in [The Expertise Economy](/books/the-expertise-economy) on the rise of skills as the unit of account for the modern workforce. The line Kyle captured: *"Skills are not only becoming the currency of learning, but also an economic indicator, and the very language of workforce analytics."* It sits alongside quotes from [Laszlo Bock](/notes/laszlo-bock) and [Peter Senge](/notes/peter-senge) in the book's argument that what you can actually *do* — your skills, however acquired — should drive opportunity, rather than the credential or institution behind them.
