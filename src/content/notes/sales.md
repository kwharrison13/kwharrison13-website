# Sales

Sales is a recurring [operational-skill](/notes/operational-skills) and [playbook](/notes/playbooks) theme across Kyle's corpus. In [Product Led Growth: The End User Era](/books/product-led-growth-the-end-user-era) it sits in tension with bottom-up adoption — the question of when a self-serve, product-led motion needs a sales layer, and how [Customer Success](/notes/customer-success), [Onboarding](/notes/onboarding), and expansion ([Land and Expand](/notes/land-and-expand)) relate to a traditional sales org. In the September 17th, 2021 — Steve Malouf (Datadog) on Prelay sourcing call, the focus is the *complexity* of enterprise selling: [Prelay](/notes/prelay)'s deal-management approach fits "longer, more complex sales cycles" (60+ days), and the more use cases and orgs that have to buy in across sales and implementation, the longer and more involved the cycle gets. In [What It Takes](/books/what-it-takes), [Stephen A. Schwarzman](/notes/stephen-a-schwarzman) supplies the founder/leadership register of selling — pitching, raising, and winning deals.

The page also collects sources Kyle saved to study sales as a discipline (his June 29th, 2021 Roam "Quick Capture" log and a later manual batch), captured below. These are unsynthesized reading pointers, kept verbatim. The topic pairs closely with [Growth](/notes/growth) and its [Playbook: Sales](/notes/playbook-sales) / [Playbook: Growth](/notes/playbook-growth) companions.
