# Dave Whorton

Dave Whorton anchors the evergreen-company thesis in [On the Nature of Long-Term Holds](/notes/on-the-nature-of-long-term-holds). A former entrepreneur and venture capitalist (Stanford GSB 1997), he came up through the "get-big-and-exit" Silicon Valley playbook — founding ventures including Good Technologies and drugstore.com, and investing in companies like Amazon and Google as an investor at Kleiner Perkins and TPG Ventures. He now promulgates an alternative as founder and head of the [Tugboat Institute](/notes/tugboat-institute), an organization built to serve businesses with long-term horizons. Whorton calls these "evergreen" companies and encourages leaders to adopt building strategies of 100 years or more — the [100 Year Business Plan](/notes/100-year-business-plan) mindset that sits at the center of the long-read's Search Funds / holding-companies cluster (build or buy one good business and hold it forever). On the [Jack Ma](/notes/jack-ma) page, Ma is tagged alongside Whorton's evergreen / 100-year philosophy of long-horizon company building.

**Context:** The Tugboat Institute is a membership community Whorton founded around the "Evergreen" philosophy — purpose-driven, privately held companies built to endure for generations rather than to be sold.
