# D&C 29

A Doctrine and Covenants section Kyle draws on across his gospel-study notes for a cluster of doctrines: **agency**, **accountability for knowledge**, and the **character of the Godhead**. In Eye single to the glory of God he leans on the warning that with greater understanding comes greater responsibility — "whoso having KNOWLEDGE, have I not commanded to REPENT?" (D&C 29:59) — the basis for his argument that God mercifully gives light only as fast as a person can bear it. In 3 Answers To Every Prayer he reads D&C 29:39 (the necessity of opposition so we can be "agents unto [ourselves]") through the [Brother of Jared](/notes/brother-of-jared)'s sealed barges, where the air-holes stand for the unavoidable travails of mortal life.

The section also anchors Kyle's note on The Godhead, which cites D&C 29:1–5 (Christ speaking of His Father and His gospel) and D&C 29:34–35 (the Father speaking without any change of character) as evidence that the Father and Son are so unified that either can speak on any topic and say the same thing — a contrast to the creedal Trinity. Other notes pick up narrower verses: the "robe of righteousness" (29:12) in Born Again, and the "first shall be last" reversal (29:30) in Chosen Leaders.

**Context:** Doctrine and Covenants Section 29 is a revelation recorded by Joseph Smith in September 1830, given in the presence of six elders before a Church conference. It addresses the gathering of the elect, the Fall, the role of agency and opposition, the consequences of knowledge, and the redemption of little children.
