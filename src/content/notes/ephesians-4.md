# Ephesians 4

Ephesians 4 is a New Testament chapter cited across Kyle's gospel study in two distinct ways. In Abrahamic Covenant it appears as proof-text support for priesthood organization and authority — verses 11–13 (apostles, prophets, evangelists, and the building up of the body of Christ) are grouped with [1 Corinthians 12](/notes/1-corinthians-12):27–29, [1 Timothy 3](/notes/1-timothy-3), and [Titus 1](/notes/titus-1) as scriptures showing that Christ established specific offices and called specific followers, agreeable to the Articles of Faith on Church organization.

In Charity & Love the chapter is cited at verse 32 ("forgiving one another, even as God for Christ's sake hath forgiven you") as part of a Preach My Gospel self-examination checklist on developing charity — paired with the practical question of whether one forgives those who have offended or wronged them.

**Context:** Ephesians 4 is a chapter of the Apostle Paul's epistle to the Ephesians, covering the unity of the body of Christ, spiritual gifts and Church offices given for ministry, and an exhortation to put off the "old man" in favor of kindness, tenderheartedness, and forgiveness.

## Where this appears

- Abrahamic Covenant — vv. 11–13 cited as scriptural support for priesthood offices and Church organization
- Charity & Love — v. 32 cited in a Preach My Gospel checklist on forgiveness and the pure love of Christ
