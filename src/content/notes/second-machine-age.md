# Second Machine Age

In [Utopia For Realists](/books/utopia-for-realists), the "Second Machine Age" is the frame for the automation-and-redistribution argument Kyle reads as the book's core unfinished syllogism. [Rutger Bregman](/notes/rutger-bregman) casts it as the successor to the First Machine Age: where the first industrial revolution was met "through a revolution in education and welfare," the Second Machine Age "calls for drastic measures" — specifically a shorter workweek and [Universal Basic Income](/notes/universal-basic-income). The load-bearing claim is that for 200 years the labor market was the mechanism that "ceaselessly churned out new jobs and, in so doing, distributed the fruits of progress," and that mechanism may be ending: "What if most of us are doomed, in the long run, to lose the race against the machine?"

The page sits at the center of a tight cluster in Kyle's reading. The [Automation](/notes/automation) note treats the Second Machine Age as the era of the "great decoupling" of productivity from jobs — the empirical backdrop for why labor stops distributing prosperity. The [Universal Basic Income](/notes/universal-basic-income) note then frames UBI as the redistribution mechanism the Second Machine Age requires, "venture capital for the people." So the three concepts form a chain: automation drives the decoupling, the Second Machine Age names the era, and UBI is the proposed answer to the redistribution question the optimists left dangling.

**Context:** "The Second Machine Age" is the title of a 2014 book by MIT economists Erik Brynjolfsson and Andrew McAfee, arguing that digital technologies are automating cognitive work the way the steam engine once automated physical work — with the "great decoupling" of productivity growth from median wages and employment as its signature economic symptom.

## Where this appears

- [Utopia For Realists](/books/utopia-for-realists) — Bregman's frame for why automation demands a new redistribution mechanism (shorter workweek + UBI) the way the First Machine Age demanded education and welfare.
- [Automation](/notes/automation) — the Second Machine Age as the era of the "great decoupling" of productivity from jobs.
- [Universal Basic Income](/notes/universal-basic-income) — UBI offered as the redistribution mechanism for the Second Machine Age.
