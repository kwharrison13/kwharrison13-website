# Leo Strauss

**One-line:** *Political philosopher whose tradition of esoteric writing — concealing controversial truths behind a curtain of mystery, addressed only to "trustworthy and intelligent readers" — is cited in [Peter Thiel's Religion](/notes/peter-thiel-s-religion) as a second influence on [Peter Thiel](/notes/peter-thiel), alongside [Rene Girard](/notes/rene-girard).*

## What we know

- Wrote obscurely on purpose, believing that even in open-minded societies many truths were too dangerous to state plainly; esoteric writing, per Strauss (quoted in the source), "has all the advantages of private communication without having its greatest disadvantage—that it reaches only the writer's acquaintances... Their literature is addressed, not to all readers, but to trustworthy and intelligent readers only."
- Thiel's attraction to "searching for secrets" (his job-interview question, "What very important truth do very few people agree with you on?") is traced in the source to Strauss's influence.
- In 2004, Thiel contributed an essay called *The Straussian Moment* to a conference he sponsored, "On Politics and Apocalypse," attempting to find common ground between [Rene Girard](/notes/rene-girard)'s Mimetic Theory and the work of two right-wing political philosophers: Leo Strauss and Carl Schmitt.

## Mentioned in

- [Peter Thiel's Religion](/notes/peter-thiel-s-religion) — Strauss's esoteric-writing tradition as a second philosophical influence on Thiel, alongside Girard; Thiel's *The Straussian Moment* essay explicitly tries to fuse the two.
