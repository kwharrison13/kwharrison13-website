# Bridgewater Associates

**One-line:** *Ray Dalio's hedge fund (world-knowledge identifier); in Kyle's writing it's a [Wordsmith](/notes/wordsmiths) exemplar — a culture that "shows, don't tells" via its published Daily Observations.*

## What we know

- Held up as a "show, don't tell" / [Wordsmith](/notes/wordsmiths) culture: *"A cardinal rule of [Storytelling](/notes/storytelling) is to 'show, don't tell.' … You think Bridgewater publishes their Daily Observations for fun? Find a way to show, not tell."* (The Storytelling of Investing - Research)
- Cited for its individual "baseball card" for everyone and ~70% attrition, paired against [Zappos](/notes/zappos)'s $1K bounty to get people to quit as a high-attrition, strong-culture comparison. (The Storytelling of Investing - Research)
- One of four cultures Kyle would write a "Wordsmith Spotlight" on — alongside [Amazon](/notes/amazon), [Stripe](/notes/stripe), and [Berkshire Hathaway](/notes/berkshire-hathaway) (plus [ARK Invest](/notes/ark-invest)). (The Storytelling of Investing - Research)
- Kyle's call with [Amit Shah](/notes/amit-shah) at Bridgewater is what seeded the essay [The State of Startup Media](/essays/the-state-of-startup-media). (The State of Startup Media - Research)
