# TAM Arbitrage

**One-line definition:** *Coatue lesson: if you understand a market is bigger than others appreciate, you can pay higher prices and tolerate more burn.*

## How sources describe it

- Doing the work to understand a market better than anyone lets you pay up and get aggressive on burn; early-stage investors chase big ideas/personalities while Coatue chased big markets (per September 13th, 2022 — Harry Stebbings Podcast).

## Related concepts

- [Coatue](/notes/coatue) — the investor whose practice of chasing big markets (rather than big ideas) the lesson is drawn from.
- [Venture Capital](/notes/venture-capital) — frames the contrast between early-stage investors chasing ideas/personalities and a market-sizing approach to paying up and tolerating burn.
- [Harry Stebbings](/notes/harry-stebbings) — host of the podcast where the lesson surfaced.
- September 13th, 2022 — Harry Stebbings Podcast — the source long-read recording this idea.
