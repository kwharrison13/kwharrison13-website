# Museumification

Museumification appears in [Saving Retail](/notes/saving-retail) as Baudrillard's term for how, with cameras always on us, we process experiences as potential posts — society treated as a museum to be photographed. A modern experience's value tracks its photographability, part of the broader shift in [Experiences vs. Possessions](/notes/experiences-vs-possessions).

**Context:** "Museumification" is a term associated with the French theorist Jean Baudrillard, describing the conversion of lived reality into displayable, image-ready spectacle.

## Where this appears

- [Saving Retail](/notes/saving-retail) — Baudrillard's "museumification" of society, where experiences are valued by their photographability, supporting the status shift toward [Experiences vs. Possessions](/notes/experiences-vs-possessions).
