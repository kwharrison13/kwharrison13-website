# I Have Never Been Working Class

**Author:** [Noah Smith](/notes/noah-smith)
**URL:** https://www.noahpinion.blog/p/i-have-never-been-working-class
**One-line:** *Class in America is really about future earning potential, not a present-day snapshot of income or lifestyle — which is why self-styled "working class" progressive champions and PMC socialists keep ringing false with actual low-income, low-education Americans.*

## Key claims

- **Class in America tracks *potential*, not a present-day snapshot.** Smith grew up materially modest but never considered himself working class, because his family was upwardly mobile (GI Bill-era grandparents, a father with a PhD, savings earmarked for college) — the household's *permanent income* (per Milton Friedman's Permanent Income Hypothesis) was always high even when current income wasn't.
- **Most low-income Americans call themselves middle class** because they expect to move up; the share calling themselves "lower class" rose after the 2008 financial crisis as the potential for downward mobility became more visible.
- **The Democratic Party has become the party of the well-educated professional-managerial class (PMC)**, not the working class — a dynamic Smith traces back to George Orwell's *The Road to Wigan Pier* critique of middle-class British socialists, and to Barbara and John Ehrenreich's 1977 essay on the "professional-managerial class." 80% of DSA members had college degrees in 2021, more than double the national average.
- **Socialists have responded by trying to cast themselves, or hand-picked champions, as authentically working class** — citing a UAW member (actually a Harvard grad-student instructor) wearing a union jacket, and the recruitment of Graham Platner, an oyster farmer and Marine veteran with a lawyer father and architect mother, as a Maine Senate candidate by "educated scions of rich families." Smith calls Platner "a rich person's idea of a working-class person."
- **Progressives have struggled to win over low-income, low-education voters**, who broke solidly for Trump in 2024 even as the Democratic coalition has become more educated and affluent; the Teamsters refused to endorse Biden in 2024.
- **The integrating institutions that once bridged class lines — church, the military, mixed-income schools — have waned**, replaced by geographic and media sorting (knowledge workers in coastal urban enclaves, blue-collar workers in small towns) that leaves Americans increasingly unable to imagine how the other half lives.

## Notable quotes

> "I have never once considered myself part of the working class... Instead, I always thought of class as being about someone's *potential*."

> "But on some level, everyone knows that class in America is really about the *future*."

> "As many people have said, if Trump is 'a poor person's idea of a rich person,' Platner is basically the opposite — a rich person's idea of a working-class person."

> "I read Tocqueville, and I miss the roiling, fluid, egalitarian young democracy that I never knew... Something happened between then and now. We let the Old World sneak up on us."
