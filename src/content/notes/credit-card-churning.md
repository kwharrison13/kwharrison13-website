# Credit Card Churning

Credit card churning surfaces in Kyle's March 30th, 2022 — 1x1 with Luke Piette as an example of an outside-of-work hobby project. In that conversation, John-Luca's "Knowledge Transfer" talks (likened to [Lightning Talks](/notes/lightning-talks)) covered churning "to game the point system" — he "ran a group on Reddit that talked about it," engineered transactions to "make transactions go through to get 3% cash back but the transaction comes back to you," and "got millions of air miles" doing it. The framing in the notes is that this kind of obsessive optimization "has to be outside of what you're working on" — a side-channel for the same restless ingenuity.

**Context:** Credit card churning is the practice of repeatedly opening rewards credit cards (and engineering qualifying spend) to harvest sign-up bonuses, points, and airline miles, then moving on — a well-documented hobbyist pursuit with active online communities.
