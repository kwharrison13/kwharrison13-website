# D&C 121

Section 121 of the Doctrine and Covenants is one of the most heavily cross-referenced scriptures in Kyle's gospel-study notes, and his studies pull on three distinct passages of it. The leadership passage (vv. 34–46) anchors his Chosen Leaders study: "Many are called, but few are chosen" (v. 34) frames the difference between leaders who merely hold a calling (the *called*) and those who lift and change lives (the *chosen*), with vv. 34–46 laying out "the strengths to develop versus the weaknesses to avoid, and the blessings of the priesthood versus the penalties of failure." That same block grounds his Virtue study, where the priesthood "cannot be controlled nor handled only upon the principles of righteousness" (vv. 36, 45), "amen to the priesthood of that man" (v. 37), and "let virtue garnish thy thoughts unceasingly" supplies the link between personal virtue and priesthood power.

The opening cry of the section — "O God, where art thou?" (v. 1) — anchors his Patience — Topical Study as the universal question of waiting on the Lord, paired with the promise that affliction is "but a small moment" (v. 7). And v. 34 reappears in Eye single to the glory of God, where Kyle reasons that some born in times of apostasy may have been mercifully spared a call they could not yet withstand, to receive the gospel later in the spirit world. The General Conference- October 2021 notes cite vv. 1–2, 6–8 in the same vein.

**Context:** Doctrine and Covenants Section 121 records a revelation given to Joseph Smith in 1839 while he was imprisoned in Liberty Jail, Missouri. It opens with his anguished plea ("O God, where art thou?") and includes the LDS Church's foundational teaching on righteous priesthood leadership — that authority operates "only upon the principles of righteousness," "by persuasion, by long-suffering, by gentleness and meekness."

## Where this appears
