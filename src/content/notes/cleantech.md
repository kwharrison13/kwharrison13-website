# Cleantech

In Kyle's notes cleantech surfaces as one of [Village Capital](/notes/village-capital)'s five program sectors — fintech, education, health, agriculture/agtech, and energy/cleantech — each running a 12-company accelerator/development program with ~2 investments per sector. It is one of the two sectors [Inder Takhar](/notes/inder-takhar) primarily covers at the firm (alongside fintech), per his June 2018 call. The reference is categorical rather than deeply developed: cleantech here denotes the energy/clean-energy slice of an impact-focused, sector-specialized venture portfolio spanning the US, LatAm, East Africa, and India.

**Context:** "Cleantech" (clean technology) refers to products and services that improve operational performance while reducing environmental impact — solar, wind, energy storage, efficiency, water, and related categories. The term was widely used in 2000s–2010s venture investing and has since largely been folded into or rebranded as [Climate Tech](/notes/climate-tech), which frames the same space around decarbonization rather than resource efficiency.

## Where this appears

- [Village Capital](/notes/village-capital) — energy/cleantech is one of the firm's five named accelerator sectors and one of [Inder Takhar](/notes/inder-takhar)'s primary coverage areas.
