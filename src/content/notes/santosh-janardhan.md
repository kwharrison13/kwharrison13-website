# Santosh Janardhan

Santosh Janardhan appears in Kyle's [First Principles Thinking](/notes/first-principles-thinking) note as an engineer quoted in connection with [Sanjay Bhargava](/notes/sanjay-bhargava)'s random-deposit solution to bank-account verification (a story drawn from *[The Founders](/books/the-founders)*). Janardhan credits the breakthrough to the team's lack of domain expertise: "The people who ended up working on this were not domain experts. And that, frankly, is good. They had no preconceived notions; they approached it with very fresh eyes. And converted [fraud] to a tractable, mathematical thing." Kyle uses the quote to illustrate how fresh, non-expert eyes can convert a fuzzy problem into a tractable mathematical one — a hallmark of first-principles reasoning.
