# Luke 23

A chapter of the Gospel of Luke that recurs in Kyle's gospel-study notes around a single verse — Luke 23:34, Christ on the cross saying "forgive them, for they know not what they do." Both Eye single to the glory of God and Knowledge — Topical Study pair it with [John 9](/notes/john-9):41 to draw a contrast between the ignorant Roman soldiers and the accountable Jewish leaders: the Romans' ignorance left them "in part justified," while the Pharisees, who claimed to see, bore the full weight of their sin ("now ye say we see; therefore your sins remain"). The verse anchors Kyle's recurring theme that knowledge raises accountability — sin committed in ignorance is judged differently from sin committed against the light one already has.

**Context:** Luke 23 in the New Testament narrates the trial of Jesus before Pilate and Herod, the crucifixion, and his burial; verse 34 is among the traditional "seven last words" from the cross.

## Where this appears

- Eye single to the glory of God — Luke 23:34 // John 9:41 cited as the ignorant Romans vs. the accountable Jews.
- Knowledge — Topical Study — the same pairing developed into the principle that ignorance can in part justify, while claimed sight removes that excuse.
