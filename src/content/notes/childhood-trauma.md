# Childhood Trauma

Childhood trauma is the core subject of [The Boy Who Was Raised as a Dog](/books/the-boy-who-was-raised-as-a-dog) by [Bruce D. Perry](/notes/bruce-d-perry) and [Maia Szalavitz](/notes/maia-szalavitz). The book's central neurodevelopmental claim is that "even seemingly minor stress during infancy could have a permanent impact on the architecture and the chemistry of the brain and, therefore, on behavior" — and that this holds for humans, not just the animal studies it draws on. By the book's conservative estimate, about 40 percent of American children will have at least one potentially traumatizing experience by age eighteen. It directly rejects the comforting folk view that "children are resilient": resilient children are *made, not born*, because the developing brain is most malleable and most sensitive to experience early in life, which makes the young rapidly transformable by trauma as well as by love.

The page's hopeful counterweight is the book's finding on healing: what determines how children survive trauma is "whether the people around them...stand by them with love, support, and encouragement," and the most effective treatment is "anything that increases the quality and number of relationships in the child's life." That ties childhood trauma to the wiki's [Relationships](/notes/relationships), [Memory](/notes/memory) (the brain as "an historical organ" storing template memories), and [Parenting](/notes/parenting) (since we tend to parent as we were parented) pages.

**Context:** Bruce D. Perry is a child psychiatrist known for the neurosequential model of therapeutics; this 2006 book of clinical cases is a foundational popular text on developmental trauma, alongside later work like *What Happened to You?* (co-authored with Oprah Winfrey).

## Where this appears

- [The Boy Who Was Raised as a Dog](/books/the-boy-who-was-raised-as-a-dog) — Perry and Szalavitz's argument that early trauma permanently shapes brain architecture, that resilience is built not innate, and that relationships are what heal it.
