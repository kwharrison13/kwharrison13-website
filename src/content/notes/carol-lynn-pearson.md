# Carol Lynn Pearson

Carol Lynn Pearson appears in [Stretching The Heavens](/books/stretching-the-heavens) within the passage documenting the suppression of Latter-day Saint intellectuals in the early 1980s. Drawing on Church Historian [Leonard Arrington](/notes/leonard-arrington)'s journal, the book lists Pearson among writers placed on an informal "blacklist" — in her case for giving a talk "mildly favorable to ERA [the Equal Rights Amendment]." The account notes that "through prayers and tears she finally got Elder [Boyd K.] Packer to make an exception for her" so she could continue publishing in Church magazines, after a period when she could not. She sits alongside [Eugene England](/notes/eugene-england), Jim Allen, Arrington himself, and others as evidence of the anti-intellectual climate the book describes.

**Context:** Carol Lynn Pearson (b. 1939) is a Latter-day Saint poet, playwright, and author known for works such as *Goodbye, I Love You* and writing on women's experience within Mormonism.

## Where this appears

- [Stretching The Heavens](/books/stretching-the-heavens) — named in the blacklist passage as a Mormon writer disciplined for a talk favorable to the ERA, later granted an exception to keep publishing.
