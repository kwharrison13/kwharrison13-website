# Clubhouse is a Unicorn(?); Clubhouse Versus Podcasts; Monetization, Moderation, and Monopoly

**Author:** [Ben Thompson](/notes/ben-thompson) ([Stratechery](/notes/stratechery))
**URL:** https://stratechery.com/2021/clubhouse-is-a-unicorn-clubhouse-versus-podcasts-monetization-moderation-and-monopoly/
**One-line:** *Why Clubhouse's audio-first format opens a much larger attention space than visual social networks — and why its ease-of-use means it will likely produce far more audio content than the podcast ecosystem.*

## Key claims

- Clubhouse has confirmed it will introduce creator-monetization products — subscriptions, tipping, and ticket sales. #[Digital Creators](/notes/digital-creators)
- The product's evolution is impressive, and the drive to establish a real [network effect](/books/network-effect) is both obvious and well-done.
- Being **audio-based** means Clubhouse's total addressable *attention* space is much larger than visual-based alternatives — and not really competitive with existing social networks.
- The biggest barrier to podcasting is acquisition/discovery (hard for podcasters to acquire users, hard for listeners to find shows) — which is why media organizations should **use text for user acquisition and podcasts for monetization**. #Podcast
- Thanks to its ease-of-use, **Clubhouse will likely produce far more audio content than the podcast ecosystem** — making discovery and recommendation especially important. #Podcast

## Notable quotes

> Clubhouse has confirmed it will be introducing products to help creators on the platform get paid, including subscriptions, tipping and ticket sales.

> The most obvious place to start when it comes to thinking about Clubhouse is the fact that it is audio-based. This has a few obvious implications, including the fact that it's total addressable attention space (TaaS, and yes, I just made that up) is both much larger than visual-based alternatives, and also not really competitive with existing social networks.

> Still, the biggest barrier to podcasting remains just how difficult it is, from a podcaster perspective, to acquire users, and, from a listener perspective, to find shows you are interested in; this is why I have advocated that **media organizations take a wholistic approach to mediums, using text for user acquisition, and podcasts for monetization.**

> Clubhouse, **thanks to its ease-of-use, will in the long run likely produce far more audio content than the podcast ecosystem.** That makes the discovery and recommendation bits noted above particularly important.
