# Patrick Q Mason

Patrick Q. Mason is the author of [Restoration: God's Call To The 21st Century](/books/restoration-god-s-call-to-the-21st-century). In Kyle's notes, Mason's central move is reframing the Restoration as forward-looking rather than primitivist: he argues [Joseph Smith](/notes/joseph-smith) never spoke of a "restored church," and recasts [Zion](/notes/zion) as "for the world" rather than a tribal Saints-only community. Mason critiques the "Restoration-as-reprise" instinct ([Primitivism](/notes/primitivism)) in favor of an ongoing, accelerating Restoration — citing [Russell M. Nelson](/notes/russell-m-nelson)'s temple-building expansion and [Jeffrey R. Holland](/notes/jeffrey-r-holland)'s claim that ending hunger, poverty, and injustice is "the work of the Restoration." He draws on [Brigham Young](/notes/brigham-young) ("gather up every item of truth" regardless of source), [Max Weber](/notes/max-weber)'s concept of disenchantment, and the church's "allergy to talking about inequality" to argue for a faith that engages the modern world rather than retreats from it.

**Context:** Patrick Q. Mason is a historian of religion and the Leonard J. Arrington Chair of Mormon History and Culture at Utah State University, known for scholarship on Latter-day Saint thought, faith, and doubt.
