# Twilio

Communications-API company that recurs across Kyle's notes as a canonical example of the [API Economy](/notes/api-economy) and the "developers are the new kingmakers" thesis. In the Pave Diligence — 2021 dossier (via [API Economy](/notes/api-economy)), Twilio is the communications-data exemplar in the pattern that wins by aggregating a previously-siloed category into a real-time, integration-driven system of record — financial data → [Plaid](/notes/plaid), communications → Twilio, payments → [Stripe](/notes/stripe), compensation → Pave. The Identity & Access Management Diligence — 2021 dossier groups Twilio with [SendGrid](/notes/sendgrid), [Stripe](/notes/stripe), and [PagerDuty](/notes/pagerduty) as proof developers are "the new kingmakers," and Riley Hughes of [Trinsic](/notes/trinsic-id) modeled a usage-throttled pricing tier "after Twilio or SendGrid." Twilio acquired [SendGrid](/notes/sendgrid) in 2019, which is why the two appear paired in Kyle's developer-distribution notes.

The notes also track Twilio as a *business model* and a *market signal*. The Mysteries of an Economic Engine - Research uses Twilio's revenue to illustrate the carrier-margin COGS drag ("must pay cell carriers to deliver texts") and lists it among usage-based pricing companies alongside Snowflake and AWS. In [Apple Earnings, An Interview with Jay Goldberg About Chips and Intel](/notes/apple-earnings-an-interview-with-jay-goldberg-about-chips-and-intel), Twilio appears as a daily-sentiment tracking name connected to "the coming explosion in mobile video." [Todd McKinnon — Creating and Defining a New Market Category](/notes/todd-mckinnon-creating-and-defining-a-new-market-category) invokes Twilio's founder/CEO Jeff Lawson and his "you get to see the future" framing — the idea that an infrastructure supplier glimpses what others build next. Kyle's own concept page [Twilio Flex](/notes/twilio-flex) tracks Twilio's contact-center product.

**Context:** Twilio is a cloud communications platform (CPaaS) founded in 2008 by Jeff Lawson, providing programmable APIs for SMS, voice, email, and other messaging; it went public in 2016 ($TWLO) and acquired email-API company SendGrid in 2019.
