# Amazon Process

A tag Kyle uses for the question of how [Amazon](/notes/amazon) systematizes value creation. In the August 18th, 2021 — Valued Empyrean (Conor White-Sullivan & Darwin Harrison) notes, it appears against the prompt "What is the 'PROCESS' for creating Value?" — sitting alongside ideas like intrinsic value, technical skills, and project management as a model for what people need to succeed.

The concept gets its substance in [What's In a Post-Mortem](/essays/whats-in-a-post-mortem) research, where Kyle ties it to Amazon's **"correction of error" (COE) reports**: after an incident, an engineer writes a COE that traces root causes via "the five whys," and the memo could go all the way to [Jeff Bezos](/notes/jeff-bezos). Kyle sources this through his own essay [Building a Product Engine](/essays/building-a-product-engine), and uses it as a worked example of the journaling/post-mortem discipline — measuring and reporting performance so the rate of improvement accelerates.

**Context:** Amazon is widely documented as running structured operational rituals — the six-page narrative memo, the "two-pizza team," and the correction-of-error process — that codify how the company learns from failure and pushes decisions toward customer value.
