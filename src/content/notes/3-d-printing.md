# 3-D Printing

In Kyle's notes, 3-D printing appears specifically as a construction method for housing — one strand of the [Alternative Housing](/notes/alternative-housing) cluster he tracks. The grounded references come from his June 29th, 2021 Roam "Quick Capture" reading log, where he saved articles on 3-D-printed houses, including ICON's reported $4,000 house printed in 24 hours, alongside related links to ICON's site and factory-built homebuilder Dvele. The framing throughout that scan is whether new construction techniques can deliver affordable, equity-building shelter where conventional site-built housing fails lower-income buyers.

**Context:** 3-D printing (additive manufacturing) builds objects layer by layer from a digital model. In construction, large-scale printers extrude concrete or other materials to form building walls on site; companies such as ICON have demonstrated rapidly printed, low-cost homes as a potential answer to housing affordability and construction-cost pressures.
