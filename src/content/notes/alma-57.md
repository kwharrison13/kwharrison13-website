# Alma 57

A chapter in the Book of Mormon recording the stripling-warrior war narrative under [Helaman](/notes/helaman). In Kyle's Come Follow Me study of Alma 53–63, this chapter anchors the theme of **[Faith](/notes/faith) unto Obedience**: the 2,060 young men "were firm and undaunted" and "did obey and observe to perform every word of command with exactness; yea, and even according to their faith it was done unto them" (Alma 57:20–21). Kyle's gloss: if you actually have faith, you'll be obedient, because you genuinely believe the blessings that obedience brings.

The chapter is also the source of the study's title phrase. None of the warriors were lost while "a thousand of our brethren … were slain," and the army "justly ascribe[d] it to the miraculous power of God … that there was a just God, and whosoever did not doubt, that they should be **preserved by his marvelous power**" (Alma 57:26). Kyle noted the tension with the [But If Not...](/notes/but-if-not) theme he traced elsewhere in these chapters — here the blessing is unambiguous, raising the question of whether the warriors' faith would have survived had hundreds of them died.
