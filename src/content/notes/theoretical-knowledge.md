# Theoretical Knowledge

In the August 18th, 2021 conversation with [Conor White-Sullivan](/notes/conor-white-sullivan) and [Darwin Harrison](/notes/darwin-harrison), "theoretical knowledge" appears as one half of a knowing/doing pair, set against [Practical Knowledge](/notes/practical-knowledge). The frame: theoretical knowledge is *knowing what a thing is* — for example, knowing what a Siloed Mindset or a Holistic Mindset is, or knowing how to change a person's viewpoint — while practical knowledge is the *skill of recognizing* when yourself or others are operating from one mindset versus the other. The pair sits alongside [Character Skills](/notes/character-skills) and a [Growth Mindset](/notes/growth-mindset) (the "Fixed → Value standpoint" shift) inside a larger model of how a group of like-minded people transforms movements to impact the world.

The discussion ties this back to Darwin Harrison's recurring observation that "we have a world full of people who are doers but not thinkers" — that holding a viewpoint without the underlying knowledge (and the character and technical skills to act on it) leaves people "acting when they are acted upon" rather than acting intentionally.

## Where this appears
