# Alma 47

The Book of Mormon chapter Kyle reads as the master text for The Lehonti Principle — Amalickiah's incremental seduction of Lehonti as a model of how [Satan](/notes/satan) works. In the story, Lehonti commands a body of Lamanites "fixed in their minds with a determined resolution" not to go to war (Alma 47:6); Amalickiah, scheming to place himself at their head, sends a secret embassy "desiring Lehonti that he should come down," and sends it again "the second time, and the third time" when Lehonti refuses (Alma 47:10–12). The repetition isn't because the offer grows more attractive — it's because Satan never gives up, and the constant reminder keeps the [Temptation](/notes/temptation) in mind.

When Amalickiah can't move Lehonti to the line, he brings the line to Lehonti: he goes up "nearly to Lehonti's camp," offers him command of the entire army in exchange for being made second-in-command, then "caused that one of his servants should administer poison by degrees to Lehonti, that he died" (Alma 47:12–18). Kyle's gloss, drawing on [Henry B. Eyring](/notes/henry-b-eyring): Satan doesn't want to ride shotgun, he wants to drive — "poison by degrees" is how the adversary takes someone who refused the obvious sin. The chapter also appears in Kyle's August 3–9, 2020. Alma 43–52: "Stand Fast in the Faith of Christ" Come Follow Me study, among the Alma 43–52 war chapters.
