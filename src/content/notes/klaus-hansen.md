# Klaus Hansen

LDS scholar quoted in [An Insider's View of Mormon Origins](/books/an-insider-s-view-of-mormon-origins) to support the book's argument that [Joseph Smith](/notes/joseph-smith)'s theology and cosmology reflect antebellum thought rather than ancient revelation. The book cites Hansen writing that "the progressive aspect of Joseph's theology, as well as its cosmology, while in a general way compatible with antebellum thought, bears some remarkable resemblances to Thomas Dick's *Philosophy of a Future State*," a work whose second edition appeared in 1830. The surrounding passage notes that Smith owned a copy of Dick's book and that [Oliver Cowdery](/notes/oliver-cowdery) quoted lengthy excerpts from it in the *Messenger and Advocate* in December 1836 — using Hansen as a credentialed witness for the Thomas Dick resemblance.

**Context:** Klaus J. Hansen (1931–2020) was a historian of Mormonism, longtime professor at Queen's University in Canada, and author of *Quest for Empire* (on the Mormon political Kingdom of God / Council of Fifty) and *Mormonism and the American Experience*.
