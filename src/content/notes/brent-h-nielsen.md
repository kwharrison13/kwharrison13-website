# Brent H. Nielsen

Brent H. Nielsen appears across two of Kyle's gospel-study pages. In the Diligence topical study he is quoted on how spiritual complacency creeps in: "As we become casual in our relationship with deity and as we minimize the importance of constant communication through prayer, we may begin to feel overwhelmed, become confused, and even be deceived, finding ourselves doing things that are not in accordance with God's will." In the General Conference — October 2021 notes Kyle records his talk (tagged #[But If Not...](/notes/but-if-not)) on praying through a loved one's terminal illness — "First we prayed for a miracle. Then we prayed that he wouldn't feel pain. Finally we prayed that he would pass quickly" — which Kyle links to the [Prosperity Gospel](/notes/prosperity-gospel) question of whether more faith would have changed the outcome, landing on "maybe he did" answer the prayer.

**Context:** Brent H. Nielson (the Church spells the surname "Nielson") is a general authority Seventy of The Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-day Saints, called in 2009 and later serving in the Presidency of the Seventy.

## Where this appears

- Diligence — quoted on how casualness in prayer and our relationship with deity opens the door to deception and acting against God's will.
- General Conference — October 2021 — speaker; his talk on praying through terminal illness, filed under faith and the limits of the prosperity gospel.
