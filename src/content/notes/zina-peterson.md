# Zina Peterson

A voice Kyle returns to for crisp, slightly contrarian framings of faith and conduct. In his Alma 13–16 study, he uses her line on the priesthood to puncture the habit of conflating priesthood power with the men who hold it: "I don't understand the Priesthood well enough to say exactly what it is and isn't. But when people say 'we'd like to thank the priesthood for setting up the chairs'; I'm pretty sure it wasn't the priesthood that set up the chairs" — tagged to [Women and the Priesthood](/notes/women-and-the-priesthood).

In Spiritual Quotes — Greatest Hits two more of her aphorisms are collected: "In a fight we don't say anything about the other person, only ownership of our role in the situation," and "I'm a Mormon. I don't do that, but I study it. I want to understand it." Together they sketch a posture of self-accountability and curious, study-driven engagement with what one doesn't practice — the throughline in why Kyle keeps her quotes.
