# eBay

eBay shows up across Kyle's corpus mainly as a competitive foil and a marketplace substrate. In [Alibaba — The House That Jack Ma Built](/books/alibaba-the-house-that-jack-ma-built), eBay is the central rival [Jack Ma](/notes/jack-ma) out-positions by choosing the home battlefield rather than confronting it head-on — captured in the two highlights Kyle pulled: "eBay wants to buy the Chinese market, but we want to create China's Internet trading market," and "**eBay may be a shark in the ocean, but I am a crocodile in the Yangtze River. If we fight in the ocean, we lose, but if we fight in the river, we win.**" The book uses this as its recurring [Competition](/notes/competition) lesson: beating a stronger opponent by changing where the fight happens.

In Kyle's [E-Commerce](/notes/e-commerce) notes, eBay is the marketplace whose seller friction spawns point-solution tooling — Victor Levitin built CrazyLister in 2013 in response to how complicated it was to post items on eBay, the kind of tool the [E-Commerce Tech Roll-Up](/notes/e-commerce-tech-roll-up) thesis aims to consolidate. eBay also appears as a competitive benchmark in Kyle's Cash—Kingmaker or Killer - Research, in a list of "why did the challenger win" matchups ("Why did GOAT beat eBay?").

**Context:** eBay (NASDAQ: EBAY) is a pioneering online marketplace founded in 1995, built on consumer-to-consumer auctions and later fixed-price listings. It was an early dominant force in global e-commerce before facing competition from Amazon, vertical marketplaces, and, in China, Alibaba's Taobao.
