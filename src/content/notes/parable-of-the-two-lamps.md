# Parable of the Two Lamps

The Parable of the Two Lamps is a teaching attributed in Kyle's Revelation — Topical Study to [James E. Talmage](/notes/james-e-talmage), used under the "Let your light shine" theme (3 Nephi 12:16). In Kyle's notes it is paired with the conviction that "the day obedience became a quest instead of an irritation was the day I gained my power" — and that "as long as you see obedience as an irritation you will be robbed of inspiration." The parable's lesson, as Kyle frames it: we do not "tear down the light of other religions," but "simply allow them to see the far-surpassing quality of our own religion" — letting one's light shine by teaching the gospel and helping others partake of saving ordinances rather than by diminishing others.

**Context:** James E. Talmage (1862–1933) was an LDS apostle and author of *Jesus the Christ* and *The Articles of Faith*. The "two lamps" image — contrasting how light is shared versus how it is competed over — circulates in Latter-day Saint teaching, though the source material here records the parable as a placeholder ("INCLUDE THE PARABLE HERE") rather than its full text.
