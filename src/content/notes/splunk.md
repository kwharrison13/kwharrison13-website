# Splunk

SIEM / log-aggregation platform that recurs across Kyle's 2021 security-deal notes, framed two ways: as incumbent and as integration target. In the September 17th, 2021 — Monad Pre-Brief (Max Rimpel) notes, Monad positions Splunk (with Panther and [Cribl](/notes/cribl)) as a traditional/cloud [SIEM](/notes/siem) that is only "mildly competitive" — closed systems that are difficult to manage, can't be accessed outside security teams, and "generally do little more than log aggregation and alerting." Elsewhere Splunk shows up as a standard integration endpoint: the November 2nd, 2021 — Oz Golan & Shay Levi (Noname Security) notes list it (with SumoLogic and Logz.io) as a destination you can pipe alerts into, and the Salt Security Diligence — 2021 dossier notes Salt's customers use Splunk as their SIEM and integrate with it.

**Context:** Splunk is a San Francisco–based data and security platform best known for log aggregation, observability, and SIEM; it was acquired by Cisco in 2024.
