# Instructure

Surfaced through a March 2022 call with [Daniel Appel](/notes/daniel-appel), who had spent five years at Instructure and was exploring his next move toward earlier-stage, mission-driven work. Per Daniel's account, Instructure carried roughly **$3B of revenue and ~2,000 employees** — a scale he contrasted against the much-earlier-stage companies he wanted to build with next. His roles there were mostly **strategic finance** (modeling new product builds in new markets, not FP&A or monthly/quarterly close), and after [Thoma Bravo](/notes/thoma-bravo) bought the company he shifted 100% to **corporate development**, looking at ways Instructure could grow inorganically.

**Context:** Instructure is the edtech company behind Canvas, the learning-management system widely used across K-12 schools, universities, and corporate training. It was taken private by Thoma Bravo in 2020 and later returned to public markets.
