# Running a Meeting

A concept Kyle threads through his notes on [Gordon B. Hinckley — Go Forward With Faith](/books/gordon-b-hinckley-go-forward-with-faith) to capture Hinckley's distinctive meeting discipline. The pattern: meetings begin and end on time; leaders come prepared and "speak their mind"; Hinckley "grasps the essence of the matter quickly"; and once a decision is made, "he didn't look back." Rather than calling a follow-up meeting to gather missing information, he "calls those individuals on the phone, asks the questions, and makes decisions on the spot." When colleagues tried to re-litigate settled questions, his stock reply was, "We have already covered that ground and given our word to others, now let us move forward." Kyle files this alongside [Operational Skills](/notes/operational-skills) and [efficiency](/notes/efficiency) as a decide-and-don't-relitigate, phone-don't-meet management style — Hinckley "held no more meetings than were absolutely essential."
