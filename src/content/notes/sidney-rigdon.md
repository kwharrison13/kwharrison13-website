# Sidney Rigdon

**Sidney Rigdon** appears across Kyle's Mormon-history reading as the early-church figure who was, as [American Zion](/books/american-zion) puts it, "as instrumental as anyone, save Smith, in building the faith" and "the last remaining member of the First Presidency" — yet whose succession claim after Joseph Smith's death failed when his "erratic behavior resulted in a fallen stature" and [Brigham Young](/notes/brigham-young), heading the Quorum of the Twelve, prevailed. (American Zion also notes Rigdon was "the first to use the 'extermination' rhetoric" later weaponized by Governor Boggs.) In [Joseph Smith — Rough Stone Rolling](/books/joseph-smith-rough-stone-rolling) he is one of the early associates, the recipient of one of Joseph's early character-blessings, and the author of the 1831 line that "there is and can be no ruler nor lawgiver in the Kingdom of God save it be God our Saviour."

He recurs as a *foil* to Joseph. In Joseph Smith Lecture 1 — The First Vision and Its Aftermath, the 1832 vision of the three degrees of glory (D&C 76) left Joseph strong while "Sidney went limp" — "Sidney is not as used to it as I am." In [Joseph Smith Lecture 2 — Joseph's Personality and Character](/notes/joseph-smith-lecture-2-joseph-s-personality-and-character), the attorney Peter Burnett measures Rigdon against Joseph's "native intellect": Rigdon was "a man of superior education, an eloquent speaker, of fine appearance," but "did not possess the native intellect of Smith, and lacked his determined will," and Joseph held "much greater influence" among the Mormons.

**Context:** Sidney Rigdon (1793–1876) was an early leader of the Latter Day Saint movement, a former Campbellite preacher who became Joseph Smith's counselor in the First Presidency and his spokesman. After Smith's 1844 death he contested the succession against Brigham Young, lost, and was excommunicated.
