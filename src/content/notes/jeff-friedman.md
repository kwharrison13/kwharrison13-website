# Jeff Friedman

**One-line:** *At [New York University](/notes/new-york-university)'s center for "smart government"; former Microsoft government-sales executive and turnaround consultant for small cities.*

## What we know

- Frames NYU's work as "smart government" over "smart cities," arguing "there's no such thing as a smart city because there's no such thing as a city; a city is a construct" — prefers organizing around the citizen and community.
- Cynical about cities deriving value from new sensor data when they haven't used existing data; believes the hard part is changing mindsets, not technology.
- Did turnaround consulting for 3rd-tier cities (<50K, e.g. Scranton and York, PA); skeptical that comparative city metrics or ISO standards will be widely adopted; floated a "request for startup" where governments help build businesses to serve problems they can't.
