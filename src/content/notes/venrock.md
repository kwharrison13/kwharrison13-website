# Venrock

In [VC: An American History](/books/vc), Venrock is used as a canonical example of how early American venture capital traced directly from concentrated family wealth. The book notes that Laurance Rockefeller — grandchild of oil baron John D. Rockefeller and "a prolific venture capitalist" — formalized his investing activities into Venrock Associates, founded in 1969. Venrock is grouped with the same family-wealth-to-VC lineage as Bessemer Venture Partners (a 1981 spinoff from Henry Phipps's family office, Phipps having partnered with Andrew Carnegie). The book also profiles Venrock alongside Greylock Partners as east-coast firms that "did much better" and provided impetus to the industry's expansion, in contrast to less successful early entities of the same chapter.

**Context:** Venrock is a U.S. venture capital firm originally created to manage the Rockefeller family's venture investments; it has backed companies including Apple, Intel, and Gilead Sciences.
