# Bessemer Venture Partners

Bessemer Venture Partners appears across Kyle's notes as one of the canonical American venture firms, both historically and as a live career and diligence node. [VC — An American History](/books/vc) places it in "the family tree of venture funds," noting it "was founded in 1981 as a spinoff from a family office created by Henry Phipps, who had partnered with the famous steel magnate [Andrew Carnegie](/notes/andrew-carnegie)" — the same direct-lineage-from-family-wealth pattern as Venrock from the Rockefellers. In Kyle's Pave Diligence — 2021 dossier it shows up as a credential marker (Pave's Nitin and others came out of Bessemer) and as a back-channel reference point (getting a read from Alex Sukin there).

On the career side, Kyle's call with Craig Tarnopol (intro'd via [David Selverian](/notes/david-selverian)) captured Bessemer's "apprenticeship model" and "eat what you kill" culture, including an associate offer to work with [Mike Droesch](/notes/mike-droesch) — with the caveat that the firm is "top-heavy," making it tough to reach the top. The firm is also the publisher behind two vertical-software pieces in Kyle's reading queue — "Ten Lessons from a Decade of Vertical Software Investing" and [Brian Feinstein](/notes/brian-feinstein)'s "Vertical Software: A Founder's Guide to Success" — anchoring Bessemer as a thought-leader on [Vertical Software](/notes/vertical-saas) / vertical SaaS investing.

**Context:** Bessemer Venture Partners (BVP) is one of the oldest U.S. venture capital firms, tracing its roots to the Bessemer Trust family office (Henry Phipps, a Carnegie Steel partner). It is known for early investments in companies like Pinterest, Shopify, LinkedIn, and Twilio, and for publishing widely read frameworks such as its cloud and vertical-software theses.
