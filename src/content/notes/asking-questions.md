# Asking Questions

In [Our Towns](/books/our-towns), "Asking Questions" is the craft of sizing someone up — or drawing them out — through the *kind* of question you lead with. The Fallows contrast the default Washington/New York opener ("What do you do?" / "Where do you work?"), which "reflects the currency of the town" and leaves respondents "feeling boxed in and, often, judged," against the gentler, open-ended alternative: "So, what's your story?" Kyle flags this as a recurring theme, pairing the concept with [Be Observant](/notes/be-observant) and [Everyone Has a Story](/notes/everyone-has-a-story) — the posture of inviting a person to take an answer in any direction rather than slotting them into a status hierarchy. It is framed in the book as one of the "traits of a town on the rise": a more curious, less transactional way of meeting people.
