# Alma 53

The chapter that opens the account of the 2,000 stripling warriors, and the first chapter Kyle annotates in his August 10–16, 2020. Alma 53–63: "Preserved by His Marvelous Power" study. Two threads come out of it. On who you surround yourself with: the young men are kept from breaking their oath "by the persuasions of [Helaman](/notes/helaman) and his brethren" (Alma 53:14) — Kyle pairs this with [Jim Rohn](/notes/jim-rohn)'s "we are the average of the five people we spend the most time with," and the takeaway to surround yourself with people who raise your expectations of yourself, not lower them. On parenting ("that I might have such sons"): the warriors "were true at all times in whatsoever thing they were entrusted… men of truth and soberness… taught to keep the commandments of God and to walk uprightly" (53:20–21), which Kyle ties to [3 Nephi 27](/notes/3-nephi-27):27 ("what manner of men ought ye to be?").

**Context:** Alma 53 is a chapter in the Book of Alma in the Book of Mormon, introducing Helaman and the 2,000 young Ammonite men ("stripling warriors") who take up arms to defend the Nephites without breaking their parents' oath of peace.
