# Mosiah 28

Mosiah 28 recurs across Kyle's gospel study as the proof-text for missionary love and charity. The verse he returns to most is [Mosiah 28](/notes/mosiah-28):3 — the Sons of Mosiah "were desirous that salvation should be declared to every creature, for they could not bear that any human soul should perish; yea, even the very thoughts that any soul should endure endless torment did cause them to quake and tremble." It appears as a measure of charity in his Charity & Love study ("Do you feel sincere desire for the eternal welfare and happiness of other people?") and as a cross-reference in the Alma 23–29, Alma 30–31, and 3 Nephi 1–7 weekly studies.

A second verse, [Mosiah 28](/notes/mosiah-28):12, surfaces in his Alma 13–16 notes on the "great *anxiety* of his people … desirous beyond measure to know concerning those people who had been destroyed" — the spiritual hunger that motivates translation and revelation.

**Context:** Mosiah 28 is a chapter in the Book of Mormon, recounting the sons of King Mosiah requesting permission to preach to the Lamanites and Mosiah translating the Jaredite records via interpreters.

## Where this appears

- Charity & Love — 28:3 as a test of sincere desire for others' eternal welfare.
- Alma 13–16 — Enter into the Rest of the Lord — 28:12, the people's "anxiety … to know" about those destroyed.
- Alma 30–31 — The Virtue of the Word of God — 28:3 cited as a cross-reference.
- Alma 23–29 — They Never Did Fall Away — 28:3 cited as a cross-reference.
- 3 Nephi 1–7 — Lift Up Your Head and Be of Good Cheer — 28:3 cited as a cross-reference.
