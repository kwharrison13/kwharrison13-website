# Randy Clapp

Randy Clapp is CRO at Advantage Communications, a call-center BPO (US, Canada, Mexico, Caribbean), responsible for top-line growth and the tech stack and the chief decision-maker for innovation/operations. He appears as a customer reference call (November 4, 2021) in the [Cresta.ai](/notes/cresta-ai) diligence, where he is a current [Balto.ai](/notes/balto-ai) customer who had also evaluated [Cresta.ai](/notes/cresta-ai), [Observe.ai](/notes/observe-ai), and [Gong](/notes/gong) — making him the diligence's most detailed hands-on comparison of the real-time-guidance field.

His stack ran [CallMiner](/notes/callminer) as the base analytics platform (Coach + Alert) atop Balto for org-wide consistency. His comparative judgments are sharp: [Observe.ai](/notes/observe-ai) was eliminated for being more post-call QA scoring than live guidance; Cresta was eliminated because it "didn't expose the admin portal to end users" — playbook changes required Cresta to build them through a ticketing system, and Clapp wanted to build playbooks himself ("what eliminated Cresta was that lack of control"). What made [Balto.ai](/notes/balto-ai) stand out was fast implementation (~1 hour of training), affordability, and especially its in-line phrasing — a scripted phrase gets checked off once the agent actually says it, so you know it was executed, versus Cresta's and CallMiner's "next-best-action pop-up with 2–3 options the agent may or may not use." He passed on [Gong](/notes/gong)/[Chorus](/notes/chorus) as too sales-oriented for a contact-center use case.

**Context:** Advantage Communications is a contact-center BPO; Clapp's title in the notes is Chief Revenue Officer. (Role context from the source.)
