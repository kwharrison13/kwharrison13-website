# Zappos

In Kyle's notes Zappos appears as a single, sharp data point about strong-culture hiring: the company famously offered new employees a roughly **$1,000 bounty to quit**, treating self-selection as a deliberate culture filter. It is never discussed as a retailer here — it functions as the paired comparison to [Bridgewater Associates](/notes/bridgewater-associates)'s ~70% attrition, the two together standing for the idea that a high-conviction culture is built by actively pushing out people who don't fit. This pairing surfaces in The Storytelling of Investing - Research (under the "show, don't tell" theme) and again in the June 22nd, 2021 — Conor White-Sullivan (Roam Research) call, where [Conor White-Sullivan](/notes/conor-white-sullivan) frames the willingness to be "an arch villain" in service of keeping "the right people."

**Context:** Zappos is an online shoe and clothing retailer founded in 1999 (led for years by the late Tony Hsieh) and acquired by Amazon in 2009; it is widely cited in management literature for its culture-first practices, including the "pay-to-quit" offer to new hires and its experiment with Holacracy.

## Where this appears

- The Storytelling of Investing - Research — Zappos' $1K "pay to quit" bounty cited as the foil to Bridgewater's ~70% attrition under the "show, don't tell" culture theme.
- [Bridgewater Associates](/notes/bridgewater-associates) — paired with Zappos as a high-attrition, strong-culture comparison.
- June 22nd, 2021 — Conor White-Sullivan (Roam Research) — Conor invokes both Bridgewater's attrition and Zappos' quit-bounty while describing wanting "the right people."
