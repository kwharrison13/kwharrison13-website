# Running Tide

An ocean-based [Carbon Removal](/notes/carbon-removal) startup founded by [Marty Odlin](/notes/marty-odlin), referenced in the Watershed Climate Diligence — 2021 dossier. Running Tide surfaces in the carbon-removal-supply context of [Index Ventures](/notes/index-ventures)' Watershed diligence: a panel write-up ("The Frontiers of Carbon Removal") pairs Running Tide with [Heirloom](/notes/heirloom) as "two cutting-edge carbon removal startups" that chatted with the climate leads at [Stripe](/notes/stripe) and [Shopify ($SHOP)](/notes/shopify) — buyers who collectively kickstarted the market with $10M+ of purchasing commitments. The note frames both not as "speculative non-profits" but as "tech companies with customers, investors, deliverables—and competitors," and flags energy efficiency (power burned per tonne removed) as the key metric, with the cost floor potentially dropping under $100/tonne this decade from the few-thousand-dollars-per-tonne of 2021.

The relevance to the Watershed thesis: companies like Running Tide are the supply side of [Carbon Offsets](/notes/carbon-offsets) / removal that [Watershed Climate](/notes/watershed-climate)'s marketplace module routes customers toward, the way Watershed takes a cut on credits purchased through its platform.

**Context:** Running Tide was a Maine-based ocean carbon-removal company (founded by Marty Odlin) using kelp and other ocean-based methods to sequester carbon; it wound down operations in 2024.
