# Shirley Jackson

Shirley Jackson appears in Kyle's Historical Futurism - Research as the author of "The Lottery," listed in his short-story reading set on speculative and cautionary fiction — alongside "The Machine Stops" ([E.M. Forster](/notes/e-m-forster)), "The Last Question" ([Isaac Asimov](/notes/isaac-asimov)), "A Sound of Thunder" ([Ray Bradbury](/notes/ray-bradbury)), "Story of Your Life" ([Ted Chiang](/notes/ted-chiang)), "The Jaunt" ([Stephen King](/notes/stephen-king)), and "Second Variety" ([Philip K. Dick](/notes/philip-k-dick)). The grouping frames these stories as source material for thinking about how the future is imagined and how visions of it can go dark.

**Context:** Shirley Jackson (1916–1965) was an American writer best known for the 1948 short story "The Lottery" and the novels *The Haunting of Hill House* and *We Have Always Lived in the Castle*, all hallmarks of mid-century gothic and psychological horror.
