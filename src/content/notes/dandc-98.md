# D&C 98

Section 98 of the Doctrine and Covenants recurs across Kyle's gospel study as a set of promises about being tried and proven. The Patience topical study leans hardest on **D&C 98:2** — the promise that prayers are heard and granted: "waiting patiently on the Lord, for your prayers have entered into the ears of the Lord — the Lord hath sworn and decreed that THEY SHALL BE GRANTED." That verse becomes the scriptural backbone for the study's argument that patience is "active waiting and enduring" ([Dieter F. Uchtdorf](/notes/dieter-f-uchtdorf)) and that an answer, though promised, may be withheld for reasons that test faith.

Two other verses extend the theme. **D&C 98:12** ("he will give unto the faithful line upon line, precept upon precept; and I will try you and prove you herewith") appears in the Alma 30–31 study on revelation given incrementally. **D&C 98:14** anchors Temporal Wealth and the Kingdom of God — Brigham Young: "I have decreed in my heart, saith the Lord, that I will prove you in all things... that you may be found worthy." Across all three sources, Section 98 reads as the Lord's covenant to hear, try, and prove the faithful.

**Context:** Doctrine and Covenants Section 98 is a revelation given to Joseph Smith in August 1833 at Kirtland, Ohio, during the persecution of the Saints in Jackson County, Missouri. It counsels patience under affliction and renounces war, promising that the faithful will be proven and their prayers granted.

## Where this appears

- Patience — builds on 98:2, prayers sworn to be granted to those who wait patiently
- Alma 30–31 — "The Virtue of the Word of God" — cites 98:12, the faithful given line upon line and tried
- Temporal Wealth and the Kingdom of God — Brigham Young — quotes 98:14 on being proved in all things to be found worthy
