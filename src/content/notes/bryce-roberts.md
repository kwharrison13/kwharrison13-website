# Bryce Roberts

Bryce Roberts is the author and central voice of [We're Selling Entrepreneurship Short](/notes/we-re-selling-entrepreneurship-short), the essay that anchors his critique of the [Venture Capital](/notes/venture-capital) model. His argument: the industry's obsession with $50B+ public outcomes — where roughly 15 companies a year drive 95% of all venture value — has crowded out a much larger population of profitable, founder-controlled businesses that represent a better path for most entrepreneurs. He frames the blitzscaling model as something that "was not delivered from the mountain on stone tablets" and can be iterated on, and points to the optionality "indie" companies retain by staying profitable (repurchasing equity, merging, getting acquired, or later raising growth capital on their own terms). In Kyle's notes he is a recurring figure in the VC-alternatives line of thinking.

**Context:** Bryce Roberts is a venture investor who co-founded O'Reilly AlphaTech Ventures (OATV) and launched Indie.vc, an early experiment in funding profitable, founder-controlled companies with revenue-based / non-traditional structures rather than the standard equity-and-exit venture model.
