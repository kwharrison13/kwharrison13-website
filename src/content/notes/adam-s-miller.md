# Adam S. Miller

Adam S. Miller is the Mormon philosopher-theologian who wrote [Future Mormon: Essays for a Different Christianity](/books/future-mormon), one of the books in Kyle's read library. In Kyle's reading, Miller's signature move is **grace-first**: he inverts [Bruce R. McConkie](/notes/bruce-r-mcconkie)'s "three pillars" (creation, fall, atonement) to argue that creation is grace, the fall is a *response* to grace, and sin "at root is the rejection or suppression of what God has already freely given" — "we don't have to work our way into grace; we have to stop working so hard to pretend we aren't already in it." His chief sparring partner there is [Terryl Givens](/notes/terryl-givens)'s *[The God Who Weeps](/notes/the-god-who-weeps)*.

Miller is also visible as a *conduit* to other works in Kyle's anti-library — books Miller cites that Kyle flagged `#books-to-read` while reading Future Mormon. These include [The Book of Mormon — The Earliest Text](/notes/the-book-of-mormon-the-earliest-text) (Royal Skousen's critical edition, referenced in Miller's Book of Mormon essays), [The Time That Remains](/notes/the-time-that-remains) (Giorgio Agamben, whose account of "messianic time" Miller leans on to read repentance and grace), and [Way Below the Angels](/notes/way-below-the-angels) (Craig Harline, the "just be yourself"/emptiness-before-God passage). None of these are Miller's own writing — they are sources he draws on — so Future Mormon is currently the one Miller-authored work in the corpus.

**Context:** Adam S. Miller is an American philosophy professor (Collin College) and a prominent voice in contemporary Latter-day Saint thought, known for works like *Letters to a Young Mormon* and *Future Mormon* and for bringing continental philosophy to bear on LDS theology.
