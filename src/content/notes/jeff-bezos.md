# Jeff Bezos

**One-line:** *Founder and former CEO of Amazon; founder of Blue Origin and current owner of the Washington Post — known less for any single product than for institutionalizing an operating philosophy (mechanisms, narratives, two-pizza teams) that propagated across Amazon's many divisions.*

## What we know

- Founded [Amazon](/notes/amazon) in 1994 after quitting D. E. Shaw, driving cross-country from NYC to Fort Worth in an '88 Chevy Blazer with [MacKenzie](/notes/mackenzie-scott) at the wheel ([Amazon Unbound](/books/amazon-unbound)).
- Operating philosophy encoded in his **fourteen leadership principles** (customer obsession, high bar for talent, frugality, "think big") — employees are trained to apply them daily, even to trivial decisions ([Amazon Unbound](/books/amazon-unbound)).
- **Banned PowerPoint** company-wide; replaced with silent reading of **six-page narratives** at meeting start ([Amazon Unbound](/books/amazon-unbound)). See [Six-Page Narratives](/notes/six-page-narratives).
- Famously confrontational ("Why are you wasting my life?", "Did I take my stupid pills today?") — pairs this with what some colleagues describe as patient generosity for those who "practice the challenging art of invention" ([Amazon Unbound](/books/amazon-unbound)).
- **"Good intentions don't work, but mechanisms do."** Translated his beliefs into process rituals — OP1 planning, weekly business reviews, "correction of error" memos, "S-team goals" ([Amazon Unbound](/books/amazon-unbound)).
- Reorganized Alexa into **two-pizza teams with single-threaded leaders**, unified by a "north star" strategy document ([Amazon Unbound](/books/amazon-unbound)). See [Two-Pizza Teams](/notes/two-pizza-teams).
- **Stubborn on vision, flexible on details.** Multiple teams allowed to compete on parallel tracks toward the same goal (the cashierless store; Alexa's natural-language approach) ([Amazon Unbound](/books/amazon-unbound)).
- **"Day 1" obsession.** "Day two is stasis, followed by irrelevance, followed by excruciating, painful decline, followed by death. That is why it is always Day one" ([Amazon Unbound](/books/amazon-unbound)).
- Reads aggressively — known to lead executives in book discussions (e.g. [Christensen](/notes/clayton-christensen)'s *[Innovator's Dilemma](/books/the-innovator-s-dilemma)*). Owns a personal library of hundreds of [sci-fi](/notes/science-fiction) volumes ([Asimov](/notes/isaac-asimov), Clarke, Heinlein) — treats them as "blueprints for an exciting future" ([Amazon Unbound](/books/amazon-unbound)).
- Founded **[Blue Origin](/notes/blue-origin)** in 2000. The Welcome Letter (2004): "Don't just build a space vehicle, build a company that builds space vehicles." Latin motto: *Gradatim Ferociter* — "Step by Step, Ferociously" ([Amazon Unbound](/books/amazon-unbound)).
- **Sustained involvement at the detail level.** Met with the Alexa team every other day for years; meets with Tyto team every few days for three years; reads every Friday-night two-pizza-team update.
- **The "scorpion and the frog" debate.** Allowed advertising to take over search rankings inside Amazon despite leadership's recognition that it would undermine the customer-experience flywheel.
- Cited as comparable founder-archetype to [Elon Musk](/notes/elon-musk) in [The Founders](/books/the-founders) ("Both Bezos and Musk knew that one site offering everything trumped five sites offering one thing apiece").

## Connections

- [Amazon](/notes/amazon) — the company he founded in 1994 and the institution his operating philosophy was built to propagate across.
- [Blue Origin](/notes/blue-origin) — his 2000 space venture, governed by the *Gradatim Ferociter* ethos and the "build a company that builds space vehicles" mandate.
- [Six-Page Narratives](/notes/six-page-narratives) — the PowerPoint-banning meeting ritual he imposed company-wide.
- [Two-Pizza Teams](/notes/two-pizza-teams) — his small-team, single-threaded-leader org structure, applied to the Alexa reorganization.
- [Customer Obsession](/notes/customer-obsession) — the lead principle in his fourteen leadership principles and the "flywheel" he debated cannibalizing with ad-driven search rankings.
- [The Innovator's Dilemma](/books/the-innovator-s-dilemma) — Clayton Christensen's book he led executives through in discussion.
- [Science Fiction](/notes/science-fiction) — his personal sci-fi library treated as "blueprints for an exciting future."
- [Elon Musk](/notes/elon-musk) — cited as a comparable founder-archetype in [The Founders](/books/the-founders).
- [MacKenzie Bezos](/notes/mackenzie-scott) — drove the cross-country relocation when Amazon was founded.
