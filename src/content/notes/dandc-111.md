# D&C 111

Both gospel-study notes that cite this section lean on the same verse, [D&C 111](/notes/dandc-111):8 — the assurance that the Lord will tell you whether you are on the right path. In 8 Points of the Gospel, it grounds the idea of being a "reforming perfectionist": "It is Heavenly Father who tells you if you're on your way ([D&C 111](/notes/dandc-111):8). Peace and power of the spirit shall flow into you." The verse reframes perfectionism as a relationship in which God confirms direction, rather than a standard one self-judges against.

In Using Satan Against Satan, [D&C 111](/notes/dandc-111):8 is grouped with [Philippians](/notes/philippians-4) and [D&C 11](/notes/dandc-11):12–14 as scriptures on discerning right direction, and listed among the page's core scripture references alongside [D&C 64](/notes/dandc-64), [Isaiah 59](/notes/isaiah-59), and [D&C 11](/notes/dandc-11).

**Context:** Doctrine and Covenants 111 is an 1836 revelation given at Salem, Massachusetts; verse 8 reads, "And it shall come to pass, in due time, that I will give this people a knowledge of all things which pertain to this place," with the assurance that the Lord will make known the path as one proceeds.
