# Daniel 3

In Kyle's [gospel-study](/notes/gospel-study) notes, **Daniel 3** is shorthand for the **"But If Not…" pattern of faith** — Shadrach, Meshach, and Abed-nego before Nebuchadnezzar (Daniel 3:16–18): "our God whom we serve is able to deliver us from the burning fiery furnace… **but if not**, be it known unto thee, O king, that we will not serve thy gods." Kyle reads this as the crucial distinction that runs through his Faith — Topical Study: faith that something *can* happen (belief, trust) becomes saving faith only when it resolves to obey *whether or not* God delivers the outcome we want. The three men knew God *could* deliver them but resolved to serve Him regardless — the same posture as [Alma 14](/notes/alma-14)'s Alma and Amulek, who accepted that it might be the Lord's will to burn or to save.

The motif recurs across the corpus: the Alma 53–63 study links the stripling-warrior faith to Daniel 3; the Alma 13–16 study uses "but if not" faith to explain why people mistake God's inaction for inability; Christ Is Risen; Faith in Him Will Move Mountains — Russell M. Nelson reads the Tonga rain that did *not* stop as the Daniel 3 pattern ("faith to persevere when the rain did not stop"); and 10 Gifts To Take Home From Your Mission cites it as the faith to act, trusting God blesses obedience. Kyle's Topical Study notes this came from a district meeting where the lesson was "what can happen and what will happen."

**Context:** Daniel chapter 3 is the Old Testament account of Shadrach, Meshach, and Abednego, three Hebrew exiles thrown into a fiery furnace for refusing to worship King Nebuchadnezzar's golden image, and delivered unharmed.
