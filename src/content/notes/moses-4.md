# Moses 4

The Book of Moses chapter (Pearl of Great Price) covering the war in heaven and the Fall of Adam and Eve — two scenes Kyle's gospel study returns to repeatedly. The early verses (Moses 4:1-4) record [Satan](/notes/satan)'s rebellion: he sought to redeem all mankind "that one soul shall not be lost," demanded God's honor, and was cast down. [The King Follett Sermon](/notes/the-king-follett-sermon) cites these verses ([Moses 4:1–4](/notes/moses-4) with [Abr. 3:23–28](/notes/abraham-3)) as the scriptural account of the contention in heaven, where the devil claimed he would save everyone and was rejected. In 8 Points of the Gospel, Kyle reads Moses 4:1 as Lucifer teaching "exaltation without obedience" — reward for no effort, the doing-away of [Agency](/notes/agency) — and Moses 4:6 as evidence that Satan "does not know the mind of God" because he can't feel the spirit.

The chapter's later verses figure in Born Again's covenant theme: Moses 4:13, 27 (Adam and Eve's fig-leaf covering after the Fall) become a type — when we seek to cover our own sins, "our nakedness is left fairly uncovered," but the covenants of God stand as a coat of skins; we take off the figs of the natural man and put on Christ.

**Context:** Moses 4 is part of Joseph Smith's revelatory translation/expansion of Genesis. It opens with Satan's premortal proposal and fall, then retells the temptation of Eve, the eating of the forbidden fruit, and God's pronouncements after the Fall — a foundational Latter-day Saint text on agency and the Plan of Salvation.

## Where this appears

- [The King Follett Sermon](/notes/the-king-follett-sermon) — Moses 4:1–4 cited as the scriptural account of the war in heaven and Satan's rejected plan to save all.
- 8 Points of the Gospel — Moses 4:1 as Lucifer's "exaltation without obedience"; Moses 4:6 as Satan not knowing the mind of God.
- Born Again — Moses 4:13, 27 (the fig-leaf covering) as a type of covering our own sins versus the covenants that clothe us in Christ.
