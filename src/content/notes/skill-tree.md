# Skill Tree

"Skill Tree" is Kyle's own question, raised in his notes on [How To Read a Book](/books/how-to-read-a-book): *"Is reading something together a Skill Tree? Picture a class all collectively staring at a google doc. The ability to read things through, comment, highlight, bold, reference, take notes. What is that skill?"* The thought extends Adler's [Active Reading](/notes/active-reading) thesis (reading as demanding more of both self and text) into a collective, collaborative mode — the composite, named skill of a group reading a shared document together: annotating, cross-referencing, and building meaning in real time. It's framed as an open question rather than a settled concept: what to call that bundle of group-reading abilities.

- [How To Read a Book](/books/how-to-read-a-book) — Kyle's question about whether collective reading of a shared doc (comment, highlight, reference, note) constitutes a distinct, namable "skill tree," extending Adler's [Active Reading](/notes/active-reading) frame.
