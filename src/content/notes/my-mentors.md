# My Mentors

`My Mentors` is Kyle's personal Roam thread for collecting passages about mentorship — who shapes him and how he gives it back. The recurring idea is that mentors come in two flavors: personal and impersonal. [A Message to Every Entrepreneur](/notes/a-message-to-every-entrepreneur) frames it through David Perell's line that "your professors are the writers you read," arguing you can and should have both personal and impersonal mentors (a thread Kyle ties to [Live the Library](/notes/live-the-library)). [The Expertise Economy](/books/the-expertise-economy) reinforces it with [Jim Rohn](/notes/jim-rohn)'s "you are the average of the five people you spend the most time with" and [Dorothy Leonard](/notes/dorothy-leonard)'s questions for navigating mentorship.

From [Becoming](/books/becoming), Kyle tags the "relay" model of mentorship — "we should always have three friends … one who walks ahead who we look up to and follow; one who walks beside us … and one who we reach back for" — pairing it with his [Pay it forward](/notes/pay-it-forward) thread. And from the [Berkshire Hathaway Annual Letters](/books/berkshire-hathaway-annual-letters) he flags the simple discipline of "give credit where credit is due." Taken together the thread treats mentorship as bidirectional: study and follow those ahead of you, and deliberately reach back for those behind you.
