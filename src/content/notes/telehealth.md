# Telehealth

**One-line definition:** *Delivering medical care (consults, prescriptions) over digital channels — infrastructure matured and de-regulated through COVID, now the substrate for grey-market [GLP-1](/notes/glp-1) businesses like [MEDVI](/notes/medvi).*

## How sources describe it

- The actual business underneath the [MEDVI](/notes/medvi) "AI" headline: telehealth subscriptions. (source: The $1B Rorschach Test - Research)
- A precondition that had to exist independent of AI: telehealth infrastructure mature enough that a random person could plug into a licensed physician network and compliant pharmacy supply chain with no healthcare expertise — built by [OpenLoop Health](/notes/openloop-health) and [CareValidate](/notes/carevalidate). (source: The $1B Rorschach Test - Research)
- Critiqued as "just [Regulatory Arbitrage](/notes/regulatory-arbitrage)" — "a 60 second video 'appointment' with an MD in his living room asking everyone the same three questions." (source: The $1B Rorschach Test - Research)
- The legitimate comparable [Hims & Hers](/notes/hims-and-hers) (and Ro, Noom) run the identical model under real compliance spend. (source: The $1B Rorschach Test - Research)
