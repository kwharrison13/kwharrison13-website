# Roone Arledge

The formative early mentor in [Bob Iger](/notes/bob-iger)'s [The Ride of a Lifetime](/books/the-ride-of-a-lifetime), and the figure Iger credits with shaping him most. As the head of ABC Sports (and later News), Arledge approached "the human drama of athletic competition" as narrative — athletes were characters in unfolding stories, and the events were a window into different cultures and geopolitical dramas. Iger says he absorbed lessons about structure, pacing, and clarity from watching Arledge tell stories for years, and later applied that storytelling instinct to brand and content across Disney ([Storytelling](/notes/storytelling)).

Arledge's most enduring lesson for Iger was a mindset rather than a rulebook: his mantra "Do what you need to do to make it better" — refusing to accept mediocrity, pushing back against the urge to say "there's not enough time" or "good enough is good enough." Iger pairs Arledge with Tom Murphy and Dan Burke as his formative mentors ([Mentorship](/notes/mentorship)), and credits Arledge with teaching him that success or failure so often comes down to the details. That early grounding is why Iger never needed convincing of Michael Eisner's later "micromanaging is underrated" view. Arledge also reinforced Iger's instinct that "long shots aren't usually as long as they seem" — the belief in big swings and organizational power to execute bold ideas ([Risk Management](/notes/risk-management)).

**Context:** Roone Arledge (1931–2002) was a pioneering American television executive who ran ABC Sports and ABC News. He created *Wide World of Sports*, *Monday Night Football*, and *Nightline*, and is widely credited with transforming sports broadcasting through innovations like instant replay and intimate athlete storytelling.
