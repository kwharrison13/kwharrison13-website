# Mormon 9

The Book of Mormon chapter Kyle's notes use for **a God of miracles and the role of unbelief** — [Mormon 9](/notes/mormon-9):19–20: "And the reason why he ceaseth to do miracles among the children of men is because that they dwindle in unbelief, and depart from the right way, and know not the God in whom they should trust." In Christ Is Risen; Faith in Him Will Move Mountains — Russell M. Nelson it anchors the argument that God is unchanging — "the same yesterday, today, and forever" ([Mormon 9](/notes/mormon-9):9) — and "a God of miracles" who works "after men have faith," paired with the [Ether 12](/notes/ether-12):30 mountain-moving example.

In the Come Follow Me study November 2–8, 2020. Mormon 7–9: "I Speak unto You As If Ye Were Present", Kyle reads [Mormon 9](/notes/mormon-9):1–30 as **[Moroni](/notes/moroni)'s answer to [Atheism](/notes/atheism)** — Moroni speaking across the centuries to a future generation of unbelief, asking "Then will ye say that there is no God?" and warning that miracles require [Faith](/notes/faith). The chapter also supplies the [Celestial Kingdom](/notes/celestial-kingdom) meditation ([Mormon 9](/notes/mormon-9):3–4): the unrepentant "would be more miserable to dwell with a holy and just God… than… with the damned souls in hell" — which Kyle pairs with [Brad Wilcox](/notes/brad-wilcox)'s "heaven will not be heaven for those who have not chosen to be heavenly."

**Context:** Mormon 9 is the closing chapter of Moroni's record of his father Mormon's writings, in which Moroni addresses future unbelievers, testifies that God is a God of miracles, and exhorts readers to faith and repentance.
