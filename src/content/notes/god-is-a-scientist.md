# God is a Scientist

**God is a Scientist** is one of Kyle's bare-hashtag motifs, marking passages where the divine and the empirical are described in the same language. It runs across two otherwise distant sources. In [Charlie Munger — Practical Thought About Practical Thought](/notes/charlie-munger-practical-thought-about-practical-thought), Kyle tags Munger's "Second Notion" — which mimics [Galileo](/notes/galileo)'s conclusion that "scientific reality is often revealed only by math, as if math was the language of God" — arguing that numerical fluency is indispensable even in messy practical life. In [Faith is Not Blind](/books/faith-is-not-blind), the Hafens make the religious counterpart explicit through [Francis Collins](/notes/francis-collins), who led the year-2000 mapping of the human DNA code and called that code "the language in which God created life," concluding that "the principles of faith are complementary with the principles of science."

The two anchors converge on the same proposition: that the order discoverable by science — math as God's language, the fine-tuned physical constants, the genome as a written code — points toward rather than against a designing intelligence. Within [Faith and Reason](/notes/faith-and-reason), this is the argument that reason can *point toward* faith without replacing it; Collins and the improbable fifteen physical constants are offered as reason gesturing at the divine. The motif sits alongside Henry Eyring's [The Faith of a Scientist](/books/the-faith-of-a-scientist) in the same harmonizing tradition.
