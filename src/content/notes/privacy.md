# Privacy

**Privacy** in Kyle's wiki is the human-stakes pole of a three-way cluster with [Data Privacy](/notes/data-privacy) and [Personal User Data](/notes/personal-user-data) — built from a June 29th, 2021 Roam "Quick Capture" reading log on surveillance, data ownership, and trust scoring. The most synthesized claim comes from [Homo Deus](/books/homo-deus), where [Yuval Noah Harari](/notes/yuval-noah-harari) frames personal data as "the most valuable resource most humans still have to offer," surrendered to tech giants in exchange for services — and warns that people "just want to be part of the data flow, even if that means giving up their privacy, their autonomy and their individuality." The book also flags a geopolitical edge: US biotech worries that "strict privacy laws in the USA combined with Chinese disregard for individual privacy may hand China the genetic market on a plate." [Amazon ($AMZN)](/notes/amazon) appears as a node in the same thread, tagged off the "constant consumer" reading.

The saved links below — Zuboff's *Age of Surveillance Capitalism*, the WSJ "secret trust scores" piece, "another tax on the poor: surrendering privacy for survival," the data-as-a-human-right Economist piece, and the Xinjiang DNA story — map the debate Kyle was tracking: data as an asset to be owned and compensated for vs. a vector of surveillance and discrimination. These are unsynthesized reading pointers, kept verbatim.

**Context:** Shoshana Zuboff's *The Age of Surveillance Capitalism* (2019) named the practice of converting personal data into behavioral-prediction products as a distinct economic logic; it became a touchstone for the data-privacy debate the saved links here trace.
