# Matthew 20

In Kyle's gospel study, Matthew 20 is anchored by the **parable of the laborers in the vineyard** (20:1–16). In Chosen Leaders he uses it to teach that whether we enter the vineyard first — "agreed with the laborers for a penny a day" (20:2), with all the Lord's blessings available — or last, "standing idle... because no man hath hired us" (20:6–7), the difference is only what we *become*. He pairs the closing line "the last shall be first, and the first last: for many be called, but few chosen" (20:16) with his broader reversal cross-reference web ([Matthew 19](/notes/matthew-19):30, [Mark 10](/notes/mark-10):31).

The chapter's other thread is **leadership as service**. In Spiritual Gifts he cites Matt 20:27 ("whosoever will be chief among you, let him be your servant") in his list of personal callings, and in The Godhead he flags Matt 20:25–28 ("not so... but whosoever will be great among you, let him be your minister") as an area of further study on Christ's titles and ministry.

**Context:** Matthew 20 contains the parable of the laborers in the vineyard, Christ's third prophecy of his death and resurrection, the request of the mother of Zebedee's sons, the teaching that "whosoever will be great among you, let him be your minister," and the healing of two blind men near Jericho.

## Where this appears

- Chosen Leaders — Matt 20:2, 6–7, 16, the parable of the laborers and "the last shall be first."
- Spiritual Gifts — Matt 20:27 ("let him be your servant") in the list of personal callings.
- The Godhead — Matt 20:25–28, leadership as ministry, flagged for further study.
