# Thomas Laffont

Co-founder of [Coatue](/notes/coatue) and its private-strategies lead (brother of [Philippe Laffont](/notes/philippe-laffont)). He shows up across Kyle's notes mostly as a source of growth-investing theses. In [The Puritans of Venture Capital](/essays/the-puritans-of-venture-capital) research, a Thomas Laffont conversation supplies the load-bearing line that "you can't keep scaling a capital agglomerator indefinitely — there aren't enough generational companies," and that "proper growth investing is likely more like PE": good businesses, not necessarily generational ones. Kyle treats this as a key open question in that essay — whether the Cottage Keeper vs. Capital Agglomerator split is stable or just a transitional state on the way to a venture industry resembling the PE megafund-vs-boutique structure.

In a June 24th, 2021 call with Gordon Tsai (Coatue), Kyle notes that a deal he'd pitched as a pass got Thomas Laffont excited because he "likes the idea of rebuilding [Workday](/notes/workday) for deskless workers" — a thesis Kyle judged "not bad, but not this company." And in [Inside the Rise and Fall of Coatue's Quant Fund](/notes/inside-the-rise-and-fall-of-coatue-s-quant-fund), Thomas appears at the Domino Data Lab panel stressing that "leadership from the top is really important," citing the Astros' Moneyball data-vs-scouts transition — unaware the firm's own quant fund was about to collapse.
