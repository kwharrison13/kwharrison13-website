# D&C 95:1

A verse from the Doctrine and Covenants collected in [Chad's Big Quote Book](/notes/chad-s-big-quote-book), where it appears (page 21) attributed as scripture: "Verily, thus saith the Lord unto you whom I love, and whom I love I also chasten that their sins may be forgiven." In that quote book it sits among entries from [Henry B. Eyring](/notes/henry-b-eyring), [Wilford Woodruff](/notes/wilford-woodruff), and [David O. McKay](/notes/david-o-mckay) on agency, love, and divine correction — framing chastening as an expression of God's love.

**Context:** Doctrine and Covenants Section 95 is an 1833 revelation given through Joseph Smith at Kirtland, Ohio, chastening the Saints for delaying construction of the Kirtland Temple. Verse 1 establishes the principle, echoing the New Testament, that the Lord chastens those whom he loves.

## Where this appears

- [Chad's Big Quote Book](/notes/chad-s-big-quote-book) — the verse is collected verbatim (p. 21) as a quote on chastening and divine love
