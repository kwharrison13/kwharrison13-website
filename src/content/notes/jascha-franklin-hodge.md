# Jascha Franklin-Hodge

Former Chief Information Officer of the City of [Boston](/notes/boston), serving from summer 2014 to January 2018 (the start of the mayor's second term). In a July 2018 call with Kyle, he described the playbook he ran on municipal digital services and data — a case study Kyle filed under [The New City](/notes/the-new-city) (city-building) themes.

His two big investment areas were **digital services** — improving every constituent touchpoint with government (mobile apps, the city website, paying parking meters and property-tax bills) — and **data & analytics**, extracting value from transactional data via dashboarding, accountability reporting, and predictive modeling (e.g. flagging high-risk restaurants for health inspections and dangerous intersections for road-safety interventions). A recurring theme was building **in-house capacity**: he inherited essentially two content editors, a social-media person, and a part-time developer, and built out an in-house digital product studio plus a consolidated data team, bringing on external help (Acquia for development, IDEO for design, on a Drupal stack) only for large projects like rebuilding the city website. He emphasized that digital services must be treated as continuously evolving tools rather than one-off capital projects, and that the hardest work was cultural — getting proud, siloed departments to share data. By 2018 he was focused on the wave of transformation in transportation and mobility and how companies and governments can jointly shape built environments for social good.
