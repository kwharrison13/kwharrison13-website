# David Perell

Writer, essayist, and writing teacher whose ideas recur as connective tissue across Kyle's reading on [Open Source Knowledge](/notes/open-source-knowledge), [Historical Futurism](/notes/historical-futurism), and [Digital Creators](/notes/digital-creators). Perell shows up most often not as a subject but as a *lens Kyle applies to other texts*. In [The Death of Expertise](/books/the-death-of-expertise), Kyle ties Nichols's argument — that experts who can't translate findings into plain English leave "a wide open space… for amateurs, hucksters, charlatans" — directly to "a David Perell tweet about the opportunity to translate [Academic Research](/notes/academic-research) for everyday people." In [The Great Mental Models Volume 1 — General Thinking Concepts](/books/the-great-mental-models-volume-1-general-thinking-concepts), a Kyle note connects the material to "David Perell talking about how knowledge workers should train like athletes." In [Poor Charlie's Almanack](/books/poor-charlie-s-almanack), Perell surfaces via a [Sam Walton](/notes/sam-walton) quote (from a Perell article) about being willing to change one's mind.

He is also the named author of the [Historical Futurism](/notes/historical-futurism) thesis Kyle works from in [Historical Futurists](/notes/historical-futurists): "to invent the future, people first need to be inspired by hopeful visions of it." The [Digital Creators](/notes/digital-creators) concept page frames Kyle's "David Perell-era long reads" as a body of work about the internet collapsing gatekeepers and letting individual personalities build founder-led audiences. And in [Twitter](/notes/twitter), Perell co-anchors the workshop ([How to Crush It on Twitter: David Perell and Matthew Kobach Workshop](/notes/how-to-crush-it-on-twitter-david-perell-and-matthew-kobach-workshop)) that treats Twitter as "the most amazing networking and learning network ever built," rewarding compression and novelty. His fellowship essay on Walt Disney as a city architect (the EPCOT planned-city vision) is one of the reading-list links saved under [The New City](/notes/the-new-city).

**Context:** David Perell is an American writer and entrepreneur best known for founding Write of Passage, an online writing course, and for his essays and podcast (The Portal / How I Write) on writing, the internet, and learning.
