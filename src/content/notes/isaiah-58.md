# Isaiah 58

A chapter of the Old Testament book of Isaiah. In Kyle's Fasting - September 5, 2021 gospel-study note, [Isaiah 58](/notes/isaiah-58):6–12 is the central scriptural definition of the "fast that the Lord has chosen" — not mere abstention from food but loosing the bands of wickedness, dealing bread to the hungry, and bringing the poor into one's house. The passage frames Kyle's recurring theme that Fasting and caring for the poor are inextricably linked, with the promise that those who draw out their soul to the hungry are "called The repairer of the breach."

**Context:** Isaiah 58 contrasts hollow, ritual fasting with the fast God desires — one expressed through justice, mercy, and care for the needy — promising light, healing, and guidance to those who keep it.
