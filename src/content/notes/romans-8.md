# Romans 8

A chapter of Paul's epistle to the Romans cited in Fulfilling the Measure of Our Creation - September 5, 2021 to ground the doctrine of divine identity. The passage used is Romans 8:16–17 — "The Spirit itself beareth witness with our spirit, that we are the children of God: And if children, then heirs; heirs of God, and joint-heirs with Christ." In Kyle's note this verse anchors the argument that, just as we cannot exercise faith in God until we understand His character (drawing on [Lecture 3: The Character of God](/notes/lecture-3-the-character-of-god)), we cannot "fulfill the measure of our creation" until we understand who *we* really are — celestial material, heirs of God.

**Context:** Romans 8 is one of the most quoted chapters of the New Testament, treating life in the Spirit, adoption as children of God and joint-heirs with Christ, and the assurance that nothing can separate believers from the love of God.
