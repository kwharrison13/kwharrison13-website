# E-Signature

E-signature is the SaaS category Kyle's notes anchor around [SignEasy](/notes/signeasy) — bootstrapped, mobile-first electronic signing for SMBs — and its competitive set. Per the September 22nd, 2021 — Sunil Patro (SignEasy) sourcing call, [DocuSign](/notes/docusign) is the public market leader and the benchmark incumbent; [SignEasy](/notes/signeasy) is the only other e-signature app Apple promotes, and competes against [HelloSign](/notes/hellosign) and an [Adobe](/notes/adobe) product on the SMB end. The strategic read from that call is a market structure split between "clunky enterprise tools with overwhelming feature sets" and "narrow point solutions that aren't cross-platform" — with SignEasy positioning in the blue-ocean middle as a cross-device, low-setup workflow tool. Founder Sunil Patro pegged the market at $20–30B growing 30%+ after COVID, with a product-led, app-store-distributed GTM closer to [Calendly](/notes/calendly) or [Loom](/notes/loom) than to enterprise sales.

**Context:** An electronic signature (e-signature) is a legally recognized digital method of signing documents, given enforceability in the US by the 2000 ESIGN Act. DocuSign and Adobe Sign dominate enterprise; tools like SignEasy and HelloSign (Dropbox Sign) target SMBs and developers.
