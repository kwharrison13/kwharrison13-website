# Michael Mauboussin

Michael Mauboussin is Kyle's go-to reference for the luck-versus-skill question in investing. In [What Is An Investor](/essays/what-is-an-investor) research, his work supplies two moves: find performance measures that reflect *skill* ([Process Over Outcome](/notes/process-over-outcome) — "we have a strong tendency to dwell on outcomes without considering the role of process… we need to find performance measures that reflect skill, or elements of the outcome that we can control"), and locate an activity on a continuum "from pure skill/no luck on one extreme to no skill/pure luck on the other" ([Skill vs. Luck](/notes/skill-vs-luck)). Kyle uses this to argue a public stock-picker and a card counter sit at the skill end while a day trader and a craps shooter sit at the luck end — but that strategy and outcomes shouldn't be conflated. The framing comes from his book [The Success Equation: Untangling Skill and Luck in Business, Sports, and Investing](/notes/the-success-equation) and his HBR piece "Untangling Skill and Luck," and underwrites the [Paradox of Skill](/notes/paradox-of-skill) concept.

He also anchors a structural argument in [The Death of a Venture Fund](/essays/death) research. There Kyle cites Mauboussin's Morgan Stanley white paper, "Public to Private Equity in the United States: A Long-Term Look," for the persistence finding — "Funds that have done well continue to do well" — i.e. strength follows strength, a flywheel. Kyle reads this as a caution on the "death of venture" thesis: it implies the brand-name top of the market is *more* durable than the death framing suggests, with mortality concentrated in the long tail of sub-scale firms.

**Context:** Michael Mauboussin is an investment strategist and author (long at Credit Suisse and Morgan Stanley, later Counterpoint Global), known for *More Than You Know*, *The Success Equation*, and a body of research on decision-making, skill versus luck, and the dynamics of investing.
