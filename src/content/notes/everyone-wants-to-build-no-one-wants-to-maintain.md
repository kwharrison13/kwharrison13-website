# Everyone wants to build; no one wants to maintain

A tag Kyle attached to a remark in the August 18th, 2021 — Valued Empyrean (Conor White-Sullivan & Darwin Harrison) conversation: *"We have a world full of people who are doers but not thinkers."* The note frames a recurring distinction in that conversation between a **Siloed Mindset** and a **Holistic Mindset** — between people who can execute a task but never step back to understand the whole system they're operating in, versus those who do. The "build vs. maintain" framing extends that: building is the visible, celebrated, doer's work; maintenance is the unglamorous, thinking-and-tending work that keeps a system whole, and it's chronically under-supplied.

The remark sits alongside [Darwin Harrison](/notes/darwin-harrison)'s observation in the same notes that he spent 15 years working with people who held a holistic *viewpoint* but lacked a holistic *mindset* — they couldn't act on it because they lacked the Character Skills and Technical Skills, and even once they understood what was required, maintaining that standard never became a priority for them.
