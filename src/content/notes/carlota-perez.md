# Carlota Perez

Carlota Perez is the scholar behind the [Perez Technological Surge Cycle](/notes/perez-technological-surge-cycle), the framework Kyle's [Technological Innovation](/notes/technological-innovation) concept page leans on for its long-wave view of progress. In that framing, successive techno-economic paradigms unfold through two periods: an **installation phase** (frenzy, bubble, crash) followed by a **deployment phase** (the "golden age" of broad diffusion). In the wiki this supplies the macro counterpart to [Bilal Zuberi](/notes/bilal-zuberi)'s more practitioner-level argument (the six hurdles facing deep tech) — Perez answers *where in the cycle we are*, while Zuberi answers *what gates the next build-out*. Her page was captured from an image-only inbox note, so the grounded detail here is the surge-cycle structure as the concept pages describe it.

**Context:** Carlota Perez is a Venezuelan-British scholar of technology and socio-economic development, best known for *Technological Revolutions and Financial Capital* (2002), which formalized the installation→deployment "great surge" model of how technological revolutions and financial capital interact across roughly 50-year cycles.
