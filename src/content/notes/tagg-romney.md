# Tagg Romney

Tagg Romney is [Mitt Romney](/notes/mitt-romney)'s eldest son, and the emotional center of Kyle's [Romney: A Reckoning](/books/romney-a-reckoning) notes. The single most-quoted line in the notes is treated as Mitt's actual life rubric and traces to Tagg: "Live your life so that your children will live their lives hoping to make you proud." Tagg's letter to his father is what Kyle flags as the emotional core. Tagg also figures in the family's decision-making: when the Romneys voted on whether to run again, the tally was 9–2, with only Ann and Tagg in favor. The notes recall the family's tradition of nighttime living-room conversations in the dark where the boys could share doubts without feeling judged — Tagg's recollection of finding it easier to talk openly that way.

**Context:** Tagg Romney (Taggart Romney, b. 1970) is the oldest of Mitt and Ann Romney's five sons, a private-equity investor (co-founder of Solamere Capital) who took an active advisory role in his father's presidential campaigns.
