# Ether 14

Ether 14 is one of the chapters in Kyle's November 23–29, 2020. Ether 12–15: "By Faith All Things Are Fulfilled" Come Follow Me study, where it carries the dark culmination of the Jaredite collapse. Kyle draws three readings from it. First, **"Are we our own curse?"** — Ether 14:1 describes a curse so great that "if a man should lay his tool or his sword upon his shelf… upon the morrow, he could not find it." Kyle reframes what he had always read as a petty magical curse: "What if it's more that the people are so wicked that no one is honest… And in a way we 'become our own curse.'" Second, **being weak vs. wicked** — Ether 14:7 has Coriantumr's army receiving "great strength," and Kyle's gloss is that "being wicked doesn't automatically mean being weak"; you may be frustrated by less-righteous people who prosper, but that is a short-term view. He pairs this with [Dieter F. Uchtdorf](/notes/dieter-f-uchtdorf)'s [Word of Wisdom](/notes/word-of-wisdom) story that the Lord's promises are "if perhaps not always swift… always certain." Third, **wickedness is all-encompassing and unquenchable** — Ether 14:21–22 shows the land "covered with the bodies of the dead," the war marching "from the shedding of blood to the shedding of blood."

In Scripture Study — Topical Study, Ether 14:25 appears as a case study in reading scripture for spiritual insight: "In the fullness of wickedness we see the course of destruction," set alongside [Henry B. Eyring](/notes/henry-b-eyring)'s point that scripture states truth through description rather than argument.

**Context:** Ether 14 is a chapter in the Book of Ether (Book of Mormon) recounting the final, total war of the Jaredite civilization under Coriantumr and Shiz — a spiral of revenge and bloodshed that, with Ether 15, ends in the people's complete self-destruction.
