# Sallust

Sallust appears in Kyle's notes on [John Quincy Adams](/books/john-quincy-adams) as one of the ancient historians at the "curricular core" of the era's education for citizenship. The highlight names him alongside [Cicero](/notes/cicero), [Tacitus](/notes/tacitus), [Plutarch](/notes/plutarch), and [Thucydides](/notes/thucydides), describing "the classics" not as a discrete subject like geography or history "but rather a lens through which to examine and understand the life around you." In this frame, Sallust is part of the classical canon that shaped Adams's republican intellect.

**Context:** Sallust (Gaius Sallustius Crispus, 86–35 BC) was a Roman historian whose surviving works — *The Conspiracy of Catiline* and *The Jugurthine War* — were prized for their terse moralizing style and their analysis of political corruption and the decline of the Roman Republic.
