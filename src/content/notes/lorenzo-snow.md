# Lorenzo Snow

Lorenzo Snow is doubly important in Kyle's corpus — as an economic model and as a doctrinal source. In [Working Toward Zion](/books/working-toward-zion) and [Brigham Young — Pioneer Prophet](/books/brigham-young-pioneer-prophet) he is the apostle whose Brigham City cooperative prospered through the Panic of 1873 and became the prototype for the [United Order](/notes/united-order): he organized all of the community's businesses into cooperatives, with stock deliberately "held throughout the community" rather than only by participants in each business — an early instance of the [Employee Ownership](/notes/employee-ownership) thread Kyle traces forward to [Teamshares](/notes/teamshares) and [Mondragon](/notes/mondragon). *Working Toward Zion* preserves his teaching that "men on whom God has bestowed financeering ability are the men that are wanted," that economic freedom should be used to "banish pride, poverty, and iniquity," and his frustration with laborers "unwilling to be put to the test." (The book notes Edward Bellamy's long 1883 visit with Snow at Brigham City as an unacknowledged influence on *Looking Backward*.)

Doctrinally, Snow is the source of the couplet that frames divine progression — "as man now is, God once was; as God is, man may become" — cited in The First Principles of Faithful Pragmatism and traced through the King Follett lineage in [Stretching The Heavens](/books/stretching-the-heavens). Faith — Topical Study quotes his testimony of "the strongest revelation concerning the truth" of the Restoration, and [Chad's Big Quote Book](/notes/chad-s-big-quote-book) preserves additional quotes on happiness and friendship over temporal means.

**Context:** Lorenzo Snow (1814–1901) was the fifth President of The Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-day Saints, an apostle who led the Brigham City cooperative experiment, authored the well-known couplet on divine progression, and as Church president emphasized tithing to relieve Church debt.

## Where this appears

- [Working Toward Zion](/books/working-toward-zion) — organized Brigham City's businesses into community-wide cooperatives; an early [United Order](/notes/united-order) and [Employee Ownership](/notes/employee-ownership) model; quoted on "financeering ability" and economic freedom.
- [Brigham Young — Pioneer Prophet](/books/brigham-young-pioneer-prophet) — the apostle whose Brigham City cooperative survived the Panic of 1873 and modeled the United Order.
- The First Principles of Faithful Pragmatism — source of the "as man is, God once was" couplet on divine progression.
- Faith — Topical Study — quoted on his powerful revelation confirming the Restoration.
- [Chad's Big Quote Book](/notes/chad-s-big-quote-book) — quotes on happiness, friendship, and temporal vs. spiritual wealth.
