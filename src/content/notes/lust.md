# Lust

In Kyle's gospel-study notes, lust appears not as an abstract topic but as one of four personal sticking points. During the July 6–12, 2020. Alma 30–31: "The Virtue of the Word of God" study — tied to a mission recording where Kyle examined what was holding him back — he records that when he "asked what was holding me back," four words came to mind: [Anger](/notes/anger), [Pride](/notes/pride), [Envy](/notes/envy), and lust. The reflection is framed around "Choose ye this day, whom ye will serve" (Alma 30:8 · Joshua 24:15) and the two-wolves story attributed to [John Bytheway](/notes/john-bytheway), placing lust within a self-mastery / agency framework rather than as a doctrinal exposition.

## Where this appears

- July 6–12, 2020. Alma 30–31: "The Virtue of the Word of God" — one of four personal weaknesses (with [Anger](/notes/anger), [Pride](/notes/pride), [Envy](/notes/envy)) Kyle named when asking what was holding him back, set against "choose whom ye will serve."
