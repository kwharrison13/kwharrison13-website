# Six-Page Narratives

**One-line definition:** *Amazon's institutional alternative to PowerPoint — a structured, data-rich, six-page written memo read silently at the start of every meeting before any discussion happens. Forces both the writer to think clearly and the reader to actually engage with the argument.*

## How sources describe it

- **[Amazon Unbound](/books/amazon-unbound) (the ban and the substitute):** "PowerPoint presentations, with their litany of bullet points and incomplete thoughts, were banned inside the company despite being popular in the rest of corporate America. Instead, all meetings started with almost meditative readings of data-rich, six-page documents, called 'narratives.'"
- **[Amazon Unbound](/books/amazon-unbound) (the press-release variant):** "the six-page narrative Amazonians craft in the form of a press release at the start of a new initiative to envision the product's market impact. The paper, a hallowed part of Amazon's rituals around innovation, forces them to begin any conversation about a new product in terms of the benefit it creates for customers."
- **[Amazon Unbound](/books/amazon-unbound) (Bezos's reading discipline):** "Bezos is usually one of the slowest readers in these sessions; he seems to carefully consider every sentence." Kyle's marginalia: "A culture of [Writing](/notes/writing) has to go hand in hand with a culture of [Reading](/notes/reading)."
- **[Amazon Unbound](/books/amazon-unbound) (the Onetto / air-conditioning failure mode):** When Bezos rejected the air-conditioning proposal and later the issue exploded into media coverage, his defense was that the proposal had been "so poorly written and ambiguous that no one had understood what course of action [Onetto] was recommending." Kyle annotated this `#[[Roam Brainstorm]] - culture of reading vs writing`. The narrative format protects against silent ambiguity *only* if the reading culture catches what the writing produces.
- **[Amazon Unbound](/books/amazon-unbound) (the OP1 cycle):** Every year, every Amazon business unit presents a written OP1 plan to the S-team. "S-team goals" — about 500 across the company — emerge from this and become the operating metrics for the year.

## Where it shows up

- [Amazon Unbound](/books/amazon-unbound) — the central mechanism of Amazon's meeting culture
- [Jeff Bezos](/notes/jeff-bezos) — author and primary enforcer
- [Two-Pizza Teams](/notes/two-pizza-teams) — the team structure that produces and consumes these narratives at scale

## Conflicts

The Onetto incident reveals a known failure mode: a poorly written narrative can fail silently, then be retroactively blamed on the writer when the issue surfaces — a one-sided contract that biases against the proposing party. Kyle's marginalia explicitly flags this as the failure mode worth thinking about more.
