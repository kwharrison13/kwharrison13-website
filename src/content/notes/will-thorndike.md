# Will Thorndike

Investor, author, and search-fund thought leader, and the source of one of the load-bearing quotes in [On the Nature of Long-Term Holds](/notes/on-the-nature-of-long-term-holds) — the Yale case Kyle annotated on why holding a business for decades beats flipping it. Per that case, Thorndike has invested in over 200 small businesses and founded the Boston private-equity firm Housatonic Partners; he is the author of [The Outsiders: Eight Unconventional CEOs and Their Radically Rational Blueprint for Success](/notes/the-outsiders-eight-unconventional-ceos-and-their-radically-rational-blueprint-for-success), the study of eight CEOs who excelled at [Capital Allocation](/notes/capital-allocation) and produced market-trouncing public-equity returns over multi-decade tenures.

In the case he is quoted directly making the long-hold argument central to Kyle's thinking: that the best investor outcomes come from businesses with "an extremely long-term horizon" that can excel "without the friction (and tax inefficiency) of worrying about exits," and that he has watched many great businesses get sold for portfolio/fund-life reasons rather than because their prospects had dimmed. *The Outsiders* lives in Kyle's anti-library ([The Outsiders: Eight Unconventional CEOs and Their Radically Rational Blueprint for Success](/notes/the-outsiders-eight-unconventional-ceos-and-their-radically-rational-blueprint-for-success), `books-to-read`), surfaced through this citation and tagged via the legacy [Books To Read](/notes/books-to-read) hub.

**Context:** William N. Thorndike Jr. is the founder of Housatonic Partners and author of *The Outsiders* (2012), a widely cited book on capital allocation profiling CEOs such as Henry Singleton, John Malone, and Katharine Graham.
