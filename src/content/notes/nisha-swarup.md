# Nisha Swarup

A founder/operator Kyle spoke with on September 13th, 2022 (a [Contrary](/notes/contrary) sourcing call) about a vendor **discovery + diligence** product for [Procurement](/notes/procurement). As captured in September 13th, 2022 — Nisha Swarup, the product lets a buyer ask for information about a company, figure out whether they need a vendor, surface relevant vendors weighted by relevance to the buyer's business, and drill into each vendor's security and compliance details — the things procurement teams care about.

Her framing supplies the core distinction recorded in Kyle's [System of Record](/notes/system-of-record) concept page: for early/SMB customers the value is mostly *discovery*, but moving up-market into formal *diligence* "will require more system of record." She cited [Valon](/notes/valon) and [Netlify](/notes/netlify) as discovery-focused early conversations, and pointed to the [Sales Intelligence](/notes/sales-intelligence) category — [6sense](/notes/6sense), [Bombora](/notes/bombora), [ZoomInfo](/notes/zoominfo), [Crayon](/notes/crayon), [Klue](/notes/klue), and [Slintel](/notes/slintel) — as comparable case studies for how a discovery/diligence layer gets built and positioned.
