# 2 Corinthians 13

Paul's closing chapter of his second epistle to the Corinthians. In Kyle's Publication of the Book of Mormon study, it is cited for verse 1 as a statement of the law of witnesses — "In the mouth of two or three witnesses shall every word be established" — connected to the ideas of "bearing record" ([3 Nephi 17](/notes/3-nephi-17), [D&C 109](/notes/dandc-109)) and triangulation, alongside [Deuteronomy 19](/notes/deuteronomy-19):15.

**Context:** 2 Corinthians 13:1 restates the Old Testament principle (also in Deuteronomy 19:15) that a matter is to be confirmed by two or three witnesses — a "law of witnesses" that recurs across scripture and is central in Latter-day Saint thought to the testimony of multiple records and observers.
