# City of Dublin

**One-line definition:** *Dublin, Ohio (~45K people) — an early-adopting small city in the smart-city / autonomous-mobility space, where [Doug McCullough](/notes/doug-mccullough) is CIO.*

## How sources describe it

- Has "jumped in with both feet" on smart-city technology: part of the 33 Smart Mobility Corridor council of cities, with a USDOT grant for fiber/wireless to enable autonomous driving along the corridor. (June 1st, 2018 — Doug McCullough (City of Dublin))
- Runs an IBM Watson instance and is building data infrastructure around rich police/911 and traffic data; partners with Ohio State on environmental sensors and vendors like TTS and NoTraffic on traffic signal timing.
- Created the "Dublin Digital Experience Lab," a cross-functional group that vets immature ideas (Alexa skills, AR, chatbots, RFID at the Dublin Irish Festival) and has vendors pitch it.
