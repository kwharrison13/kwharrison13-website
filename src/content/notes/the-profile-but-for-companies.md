# The Profile but for companies

Kyle's own idea-tag, attached to passages in the [Berkshire Hathaway Annual Letters](/books/berkshire-hathaway-annual-letters) where Warren Buffett tells the story of a *company* the way [Polina Marinova](/notes/polina-marinova)'s newsletter *The Profile* tells the story of a *person*. He tags Buffett's narrative of State Farm — the semi-retired Illinois farmer who launched it in 1922 to beat the "haughty institutions in New York, Philadelphia and Hartford," eventually amassing one of the largest tangible net worths in American business — as the kind of deep, narrative company portrait he'd want a "Profile but for companies" to produce. A second tagged note reframes investing itself: "Think of it as building a relationship with a company." The thread treats company-building stories as biography, worth studying as counter-evidence to business-school dogma.
