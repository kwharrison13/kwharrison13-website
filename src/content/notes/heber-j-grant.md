# Heber J. Grant

A seventh-president-of-the-Church figure whose words recur across Kyle's gospel-study and quote collections, most heavily in [Chad's Big Quote Book](/notes/chad-s-big-quote-book), where he is "quoted on self-criticism, music and doctrine, self-reliance, and forgiveness." The standout self-reliance passage Kyle preserved frames charity as a gospel principle — "It is an easy thing to throw a dollar at a man, but it requires sympathy and a heart… to help every man to help himself" — invoking [Brigham Young](/notes/brigham-young)'s view that giving "out and out" to the able-bodied "would ruin any community."

A second, recurring Grant story appears in both [Chad's Big Quote Book](/notes/chad-s-big-quote-book) and Miscellaneous Scriptures: spotting his inactive brother in the tabernacle, Grant prayed to "speak beyond his natural ability," bore simple testimony of Christ and [Joseph Smith](/notes/joseph-smith), and [George Q. Cannon](/notes/george-q-canon) declared the meeting should be dismissed without further remarks — after which the brother became active. Kyle distills it as Grant saying "a prayer before he spoke, that he would be able to speak beyond his natural ability." He is also quoted in Fulfilling the Measure of Our Creation - September 5, 2021 on a mother's role in instilling a love of the gospel in her children. In [Leonard Arrington and the Writing of Mormon History](/books/leonard-arrington-and-the-writing-of-mormon-history), Grant appears institutionally as the church president to whom [B.H. Roberts](/notes/b-h-roberts) addressed his sobering Book of Mormon study in 1921.

**Context:** Heber J. Grant (1856–1945) was the seventh president of The Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-day Saints (1918–1945), known for guiding the Church through the Great Depression and launching the Church welfare program emphasizing self-reliance.
