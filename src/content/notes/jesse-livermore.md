# Jesse Livermore

Early-20th-century stock speculator surfacing in Kyle's notes via a single quote pulled while reading [Frederik Gieschen](/notes/frederik-gieschen)'s writing on April 5th, 2022: "There is nothing new in Wall Street. There can't be because speculation is as old as the hills. Whatever happens in the stock market today has happened before and will happen again." Kyle collected it alongside market-and-temperament lines from [Ben Horowitz](/notes/ben-horowitz), [Paul Tudor Jones](/notes/paul-tudor-jones), and [Warren Buffett](/notes/warren-buffett) — the through-line being the durable, cyclical psychology of markets.

**Context:** Jesse Livermore (1877–1940) was a famed American trader known for making and losing several fortunes by shorting market crashes (notably 1907 and 1929); his career was the basis for *Reminiscences of a Stock Operator*.
