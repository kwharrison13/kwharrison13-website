# Tiny Capital

Tiny Capital is the modern holding-company model Kyle keeps measuring against [Warren Buffett](/notes/warren-buffett)'s Berkshire as he reads the [Berkshire Hathaway Annual Letters](/books/berkshire-hathaway-annual-letters). On the [Autonomy](/notes/autonomy) concept page, his margin note — "Autonomy — is this how [Tiny](/notes/tiny-capital) does it?" — connects Buffett's hands-off ownership ("operate autonomously… no second-guessing by Charlie and me," winning as "buyer of choice" by granting unusual autonomy) directly to the Tiny model of acquiring good businesses and leaving operators alone. Elsewhere in the same notes Kyle asks "What would Buffett build his business on today? Still insurance float? Software margins? (e.g. Tiny Capital)" — explicitly framing Tiny as a possible contemporary answer to the Berkshire question. Tiny also appears in the live-portfolio list that runs through the reading notes, alongside Shopify, Costco, Constellation Software, and [Scalefactor](/notes/scalefactor).

**Context:** Tiny (Tiny Capital) is a Canadian holding company co-founded by Andrew Wilkinson and Chris Sparling that acquires and holds internet and software businesses long-term, often described as a "Berkshire Hathaway for internet businesses."
