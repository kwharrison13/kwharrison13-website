# Jake Estes

Jake Estes is cited in Kyle's Patience — Topical Study (drawn from his mission-era Topical Journal) as the source of a line about why acting on truth matters: "It's a defense mechanism of the spirit that if we are taught something and don't act on it, we quickly forget it." Kyle uses the quote to make the point that refusing to live the "beautifully simple truths" of the gospel is not a physical loss of knowledge but "a hibernation of accountability" — a forgetting that follows from not acting on what we've been given.

Beyond this single attribution, the sources give no further detail about who Jake Estes is — most likely a mission companion, leader, or teacher Kyle recorded during his mission journaling.
