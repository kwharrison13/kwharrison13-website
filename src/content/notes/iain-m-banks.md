# Iain M. Banks

Science-fiction author included in Kyle's historical futurism research among the canon of speculative-fiction writers used to map how humans have imagined the technological future. In those notes he appears alongside [Arthur C. Clarke](/notes/arthur-c-clarke), [Isaac Asimov](/notes/isaac-asimov), [Ursula K. Le Guin](/notes/ursula-k-le-guin), [William Gibson](/notes/william-gibson), [Ray Bradbury](/notes/ray-bradbury), [Philip K. Dick](/notes/philip-k-dick), and [Ted Chiang](/notes/ted-chiang) as part of the futurist-author roster the research draws on — though the notes do not yet attach a specific argument or quotation to him.

**Context:** Iain M. Banks (1954–2013) was a Scottish writer best known for the "Culture" novels — a sequence of space-opera books depicting a post-scarcity, AI-governed utopian civilization, which made him a touchstone for optimistic, technologically abundant futures.
