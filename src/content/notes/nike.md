# Nike

Nike appears in Kyle's notes as a canonical example of a brand that sells a feeling rather than a feature. In [David Perell](/notes/david-perell)'s [The Greatest Thing Since Sliced Bread](/notes/the-greatest-thing-since-sliced-bread), Nike is one of three brands — alongside [Apple ($AAPL)](/notes/apple) and [Disney](/notes/disney) — invoked to make the point that "we buy a company's story as much as the products themselves": Apple makes us feel artistic and limitless, Nike "athletic and unstoppable," Disney happy and like a kid again. The same trio recurs on the [Disney](/notes/disney) page, where Disney, Apple, and Nike are grouped as the brands that win on emotional resonance rather than invention.

**Context:** Nike, Inc. (NYSE: NKE) is the world's largest athletic footwear and apparel company, founded in 1964 (as Blue Ribbon Sports) by Phil Knight and Bill Bowerman, and is a frequent textbook example of brand-and-storytelling-driven marketing ("Just Do It").
