# Mechanisms

"Mechanisms" is the load-bearing term in the operating philosophy the wiki attributes to [Jeff Bezos](/notes/jeff-bezos). His core dictum — "Good intentions don't work, but mechanisms do" — captures the conviction that durable organizational behavior comes not from exhorting people to do the right thing but from building processes that *force* the right thing to happen. On the [Jeff Bezos](/notes/jeff-bezos) page he is described as "known less for any single product than for institutionalizing an operating philosophy (mechanisms, narratives, two-pizza teams) that propagated across Amazon's many divisions."

The concrete mechanisms cited (via *Amazon Unbound*) include OP1 planning, weekly business reviews, "correction of error" memos, and "S-team goals" — process rituals that translate belief into repeatable practice. A mechanism, in this sense, is a self-reinforcing loop that makes the desired outcome the default rather than depending on individual discipline or memory.

**Context:** At Amazon, Bezos formalized the idea that a "mechanism" is a complete process — tool plus adoption plus inspection — designed to close the loop between good intentions and consistent results, a concept widely cited in writing on Amazon's management culture.
