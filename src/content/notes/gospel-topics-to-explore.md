# Gospel Topics to Explore

A running tag Kyle uses across his gospel-study notes to flag open questions and topics he wants to study further — a personal "to-research" queue inside his scripture work rather than a doctrine in its own right. He attaches #[Gospel Topics to Explore](/notes/gospel-topics-to-explore) wherever a reading raises a thread he doesn't yet have an answer for, and the resulting list spans both close textual puzzles and larger doctrinal questions.

The flagged items run a wide range: narrative observations (do [Laman](/notes/laman) and [Lemuel](/notes/lemuel) only start complaining "he seeks to be a ruler over us" *after* a particular rebellion?), rhetorical lenses ([Logos, Ethos, and Pathos](/notes/logos-ethos-and-pathos) in the war chapters of Alma), big abstractions ([Entropy](/notes/entropy), [The Spirit of Prophecy](/notes/the-spirit-of-prophecy), "Silence in the Scriptures"), and worldview questions (does [Morality](/notes/morality) exist without an eternal construct? — raised reading [Why You're Christian](/notes/why-you-re-christian); is it true nothing is ever "too much" for us to overcome?). The tag is how Kyle keeps these questions visible so a future study session can pick them up.
