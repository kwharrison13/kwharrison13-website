# Disruptive Innovation

The signature framework of [Clayton Christensen](/notes/clayton-christensen), the author of [How Will You Measure Your Life](/books/how-will-you-measure-your-life). That book is Christensen's applied-to-life work rather than his innovation theory — it takes business-strategy frameworks (resource allocation, jobs-to-be-done, the trap of short-term metrics) and turns them on personal direction — so the wiki's grounding for "disruptive innovation" itself is currently the Christensen authorship link rather than a passage in the source. This page is a stub pending a source where Christensen's disruption theory is directly discussed (e.g. _The Innovator's Dilemma_).

**Context:** Disruptive innovation, the theory Christensen introduced in _The Innovator's Dilemma_ (1997), describes how a new entrant initially serves overlooked or low-end customers with a simpler, cheaper product, then improves until it displaces established incumbents — who rationally ignore the threat because it appears unprofitable in their core market. It is one of the most cited frameworks in modern business strategy.
