# The Rest of the Lord

The Rest of the Lord is the central theme of Kyle's Come Follow Me study of Alma 13–Alma 16. In Alma 13 the phrase describes those "sanctified by the Holy Ghost, having their garments made white, being pure and spotless before God" who "could not look upon sin save it were with abhorrence" and so "entered into the rest of the Lord their God" (Alma 13:12) — a rest tied to the high priesthood "after the holy order of God" and to looking forward to the Son of God for a remission of sins (Alma 13:6, 16).

Kyle's own gloss makes it personal and active: he reads "the Rest of the Lord" as "a feeling when we are washed clean ourselves and [have] done all we can to share the gospel with everyone we can." It is not idleness but the peace that follows sanctification plus diligent labor — a theme he pairs in this study with the warning to [Be Up and Doing](/notes/be-up-and-doing) (the angel chastening Joseph Smith for not being "engaged enough in the work of the Lord"). The concept sits among the study's core themes alongside [Priesthood](/notes/priesthood), [Faith](/notes/faith), [Repentance](/notes/repentance), and [Atonement](/notes/atonement).

**Context:** "Enter into my rest" is a recurring scriptural phrase (e.g., Hebrews 3–4, Psalm 95, Alma 12–13) referring to entering into the fulness and peace of God's presence.
