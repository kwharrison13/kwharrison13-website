# Napoleon

In [Founders' Son](/books/founders-son) Napoleon is the archetype of the "towering genius" / restless political adventurer that the young [Abraham Lincoln](/notes/abraham-lincoln) warned against in his 1838 Lyceum Address — the figure who "fixes a broken system by overturning it and imposing a new one, controlled by himself." Lincoln's republican objection: Napoleon destroyed the French republic and replaced it with his own personal empire. The contrast anchors the [Build vs. Maintain](/notes/build-vs-maintain) theme.
