# Airtable

Airtable recurs across Kyle's sourcing calls as the reference no-code/low-code database that founders build on, hire from, and sell to. In the August 18th, 2021 — Rob @ Cartooli on WhaleSync call, Rob describes building data products in Airtable and "falling in love" with it, then hitting its limits — collecting and leveraging data required jumping through [Zapier](/notes/zapier) hoops (pre-Airtable automations), and its presentation layer wasn't attractive enough, so he wanted to keep Airtable's flexibility but push data into a nicer front end ([Webflow](/notes/webflow)). This is the gap [WhaleSync](/notes/whalesync) / Cartooli targets. In the January 31st, 2022 — Shuli Song (Plaid) notes, Airtable is grouped with [Notion](/notes/notion) as a productivity tool Shuli is open to.

Airtable also shows up as a talent pool and a customer in Kyle's diligence. The November 15th, 2021 — Anrok Partner Presentation notes list Airtable among the companies ([Stripe](/notes/stripe), Coinbase, Checkr, Dropbox) that [Anrok](/notes/anrok)'s 14-person team was recruited from, and recount the CEO hearing the SaaS sales-tax problem articulated by Airtable's Head of Finance on a run in 2019. In the November 15th, 2021 — David Bromberg (Lantern) call, Airtable is named as one of Lantern's design partners, with its CRO on Lantern's cap table.

**Context:** Airtable is a low-code platform (founded 2012, San Francisco) that blends a spreadsheet interface with relational-database capabilities, used for building lightweight apps, workflows, and data products without traditional coding.
