# Philippians 4

A New Testament chapter Kyle treats as a recurring scripture chain in his [Come, Follow Me](/notes/come-follow-me) study, cited for two verses. **Philippians 4:13 — "I can do all things through Christ which strengtheneth me"** — appears as a cross-reference in both the Helaman 7–12 and Alma 23–29 studies, paired with Book of Mormon passages about Christ as the source of strength. **Philippians 4:7** is invoked in the Prayer — Topical Study for the peace "which passeth all understanding," supporting the note that Christ is the Prince of Peace. The chapter also lands as a cross-reference in Using Satan Against Satan.

**Context:** Philippians 4 is the closing chapter of Paul's epistle to the Philippians, containing some of the New Testament's most-quoted lines on contentment, peace, and reliance on Christ — including "be careful for nothing… and the peace of God, which passeth all understanding" (v. 7) and "I can do all things through Christ" (v. 13).
