# Chris Power

**One-line:** *Australian-born founder and CEO of [Hadrian](/notes/hadrian), an automated-manufacturing company for aerospace and defense parts; identified in the mid-2010s that America's aging, family-run machine shops were on the verge of a generational skills collapse and built a company to automate around it.*

## What we know

- Grew up in Melbourne, Australia; trained as an accountant and lawyer, then worked in product/sales at an Australian software company doing blue-collar workforce scheduling before moving to San Francisco around 2020 (per [He Came From Oz To Save American Manufacturing — EP 80 Chris Power](/notes/he-came-from-oz-to-save-american-manufacturing-ep-80-chris-power)).
- Landed in the Bay Area's "Embassy" communal house network (tied to Planet Labs founders Will Marshall, Robbie Schwengler, Jesse Schwengler) by accident, through a journalist friend's introduction.
- Spent roughly a year reading history and finance before moving, concluding manufacturing had been hollowed out of the US and that a reindustrialization wave was coming.
- Founded a private equity fund first (shut down after about a year), then founded [Hadrian](/notes/hadrian) to automate small-supplier aerospace/defense manufacturing.
- 35 years old as of the July 2026 podcast interview.
- Frames Hadrian's mission through a "fourth turning" lens: great powers get complacent, offshore heavy industry, and pay for it generations later in conflict.
