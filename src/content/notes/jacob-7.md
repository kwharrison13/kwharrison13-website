# Jacob 7

The Book of Mormon chapter recounting Sherem, the first of the three Book of Mormon anti-Christs. In Anti-Christ — Topical Study, Jacob 7 anchors the Sherem case study: Sherem "denied Christ and that any man can know the future, demanded a sign, and gave up the ghost." He is grouped with Nehor ([Alma 1](/notes/alma-1)) and Korihor ([Alma 30](/notes/alma-30)) as the three figures who each attack the [Atonement](/notes/atonement) — and Jacob withstands him because he *knows* Christ ([Alma 13](/notes/alma-13)). The study notes [Boyd K. Packer](/notes/boyd-k-packer)'s warning that the types of Sherem, Nehor, and Korihor "live among us today."

In Testimony — Topical Study, the chapter is cited at [Jacob 7](/notes/jacob-7):7–12 as a witness of how a true testimony confounds an anti-Christ — Jacob's settled knowledge of Christ holding firm against Sherem's challenge. The chapter is cross-listed in that study alongside [Alma 5](/notes/alma-5), [Alma 34](/notes/alma-34), [Moroni 10](/notes/moroni-10), and [2 Nephi 33](/notes/2-nephi-33) as testimony texts.

**Context:** Jacob 7 is the final chapter of the Book of Jacob in the Book of Mormon, narrating Jacob's confrontation with Sherem, who accuses Jacob of blasphemy, demands a sign, is smitten, and confesses Christ before dying.
