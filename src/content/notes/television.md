# Television

Across Kyle's reading, television recurs as a medium that degrades public reason and concentrates political power. [The Invention of Russia](/books/the-invention-of-russia) makes the sharpest version of the claim: television is treated as a "literal weapon" — "to take the Kremlin you must take television" — and as a "psychoactive agent" pumped into a nation's bloodstream. The book tells [Vladimir Putin](/notes/vladimir-putin)'s rise as a story of television, with Putin keeping a TV remote on his desk as "the scepter of his power" and moving to capture the oligarchs' channels; in the Ukraine war the metaphor "lost its metaphorical sense" as state-TV disinformation became central to the conflict.

[The Assault on Reason](/books/the-assault-on-reason) supplies the democratic-epistemology register, though Kyle reads it with active pushback. Al Gore argues television's "quasi-hypnotic effect" (drawing on McLuhan's "cool medium") displaced the print-based "rule of reason," giving American democracy "an immune system disorder" so it overreacts to illusory threats and underreacts to real ones. Kyle's marginalia resists the nostalgia — "the pre-television public wasn't actually that reasonable" — but the throughline to [Amusing Ourselves To Death](/books/amusing-ourselves-to-death) is explicit: both books argue television converts public discourse into entertainment and emotion rather than fact. The religious register comes from [Gordon B. Hinckley — Go Forward With Faith](/books/gordon-b-hinckley-go-forward-with-faith), where Hinckley worries television viewing could "seriously damage a child's ability to think" and decries the "filth, the rot, the violence and profanity" entering the home, urging people to "turn off the TV and open a good book."

**Context:** Television's role as a mass medium reshaping politics and cognition is a long-running theme in media-ecology criticism — Marshall McLuhan's hot/cool-medium distinction and Neil Postman's *Amusing Ourselves to Death* are the canonical reference points, both invoked in these sources.
