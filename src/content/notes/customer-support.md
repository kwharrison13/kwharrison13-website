# Customer Support

Customer support shows up in Kyle's notes from two complementary angles: cost-to-serve and the automation market it creates. On the cost side, [Aryeo](/notes/aryeo)'s Q1 2022 update from [Branick Weix](/notes/branick-weix) supplies a sharp data point — after a deep dive into their stats, the company found its **lowest subscription tier drove less than 10% of revenue but almost 40% of support tickets and questions** — the classic SaaS pattern where the cheapest customers are the most expensive to serve. On the market side, [Ada](/notes/ada) (a customer-support automation company valued ~$2B) is cited as an on-thesis growth opportunity precisely because "the market for support is so big, moves so fast" — making support a prime target for [Automation](/notes/automation).

**Context:** The tension across these two notes is the standard SaaS support economics: support volume scales with low-margin customers, which is why support automation/deflection (the category Ada plays in) is a large and fast-moving software market.

## Where this appears

- April 5th, 2022 — Aryeo Q1 Update (Branick Weix) — Aryeo's low tier as <10% of revenue but ~40% of support tickets, the cost-to-serve problem.
- [Ada](/notes/ada) — support-automation company (~$2B) cited as a growth deal; "the market for support is so big, moves so fast."
