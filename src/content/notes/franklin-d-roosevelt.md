# Franklin D. Roosevelt

Quoted in [Evicted](/books/evicted) ([Matthew Desmond](/notes/matthew-desmond)) in support of the book's argument that housing should be protected from the profit motive. Desmond invokes Roosevelt's framing that such protections come "not to destroy individualism, but to protect it" — placing him in a "long line of American visionaries" who called for a more balanced relationship that shields people from excessive profit, alongside the lineage of child-labor laws, the minimum wage, and workplace-safety regulations.

**Context:** Franklin D. Roosevelt (1882–1945) was the 32nd President of the United States, architect of the New Deal, and the figure most associated with the 20th-century expansion of American social and economic protections.
