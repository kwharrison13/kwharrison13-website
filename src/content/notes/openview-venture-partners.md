# OpenView Venture Partners

OpenView Venture Partners is the expansion-stage SaaS firm whose collected essays became [Product Led Growth: The End User Era](/books/product-led-growth-the-end-user-era) — the book is authored by OpenView Partners and argues that software buying has inverted toward end users, drawing heavily on OpenView's SaaS benchmarking data (e.g. the fastest-growing SaaS companies hitting 89% logo and 109% net-dollar retention vs. 82%/90% for slower-growers, and NPS being only weakly predictive of retention). [Blake Bartlett](/notes/blake-bartlett), the OpenView partner who popularized the term "PLG," is credited as a recommender of the book.

Bartlett at OpenView also appears in two of Kyle's essay-research files as one of the ~30 VCs Kyle surveyed. In The Death of a Venture Fund - Research, Bartlett's answer to "why would your firm go out of business?" was the generic one — not being able to raise a new fund because returns weren't strong enough (noting that usually takes more than five years to play out). In The Productization of Venture Capital - Research, he listed "talent" as OpenView's value-add to portfolio companies.

**Context:** OpenView (founded 2006, Boston) is a venture firm focused on expansion-stage B2B SaaS, widely associated with evangelizing the product-led-growth strategy and publishing influential SaaS benchmarking research. The firm announced it was winding down / not raising a new fund in 2023.
