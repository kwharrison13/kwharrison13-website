# Quantum Country

Quantum Country is the prototype "mnemonic medium" that [Andy Matuschak](/notes/andy-matuschak) built with [Michael Nielsen](/notes/michael-nielsen) — a "book" on quantum computation that interleaves prose with [Spaced Repetition](/notes/spaced-repetition) review sessions so that "reading" becomes the same act as "understanding." In Kyle's notes it is the concrete answer to the problem diagnosed in [Why Books Don't Work](/notes/why-books-don-t-work): that books and lectures fail because they carry no functioning cognitive model and silently offload all the self-monitoring and feedback-loop work onto the reader. Quantum Country embodies Matuschak's prescription to design mediums "made out of" how people actually learn rather than inheriting [transmissionist](/notes/transmissionism) assumptions — weaving in [Spaced Repetition](/notes/spaced-repetition) and [Memory](/notes/memory) (re-testing prerequisites over expanding intervals until they are "not just familiar but fluent, encoded in long-term memory").

Across the wiki it serves as the canonical exhibit in the [Tools For Thought](/notes/tools-for-thought) strand — the recurring example Kyle reaches for whenever he thinks about designing new mediums for understanding, and the concrete "Books 2.0" referenced in the [Andy Matuschak](/notes/andy-matuschak) and [Michael Nielsen](/notes/michael-nielsen) pages.

**Context:** Quantum Country (quantum.country) is a free online primer on quantum computing and quantum mechanics, published ~2019 by Andy Matuschak and Michael Nielsen, notable for embedding spaced-repetition flashcard reviews directly into the reading experience — an early demonstration of the "mnemonic medium."
