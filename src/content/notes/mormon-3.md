# Mormon 3

A chapter in the Book of Mormon where [Mormon](/notes/mormon) reaches his breaking point with the Nephites. In Kyle's Come Follow Me study, three threads run through it. First, an instance of [The Pride Cycle](/notes/the-pride-cycle): after the Lord spares them and grants "a chance for repentance," the people "did harden their hearts against the Lord their God" (3:3). Second, *exhaustion* — Kyle notes that Mormon no longer bothers to narrate why they are fighting; the king of the Lamanites' renewed attack is recorded as flat, matter-of-fact (3:4), in contrast to the detailed war chapters earlier. Third, a *stand of desperation*: when the Nephites begin to "boast in their own strength" and swear by the heavens to avenge their dead (3:9–10), Mormon "did utterly refuse from this time forth to be a commander and a leader of this people, because of their wickedness" (3:11).

The chapter also surfaces in Kyle's Excommunication — Topical Study, where he connects Mormon 3:19 (with [1 Nephi 12](/notes/1-nephi-12):9–10) to the idea of bishops as common judges — who judges whom, and who holds that authority. He reads Mormon's righteous frustration as a model: loving a people, urging them to be better, and eventually, in good conscience, being unable to keep supporting them.

## Where this appears

- Come Follow Me — Mormon 1–6 — Mormon's exhaustion, the pride-cycle hardening (3:3), and his refusal to lead a people who boast in their own strength (3:9–11).
- Excommunication — Topical Study — Mormon 3:19 cited on bishops as common judges and the question of who has authority to judge.
