# Accelerator

The structured, cohort-based startup-development program model that anchors [Village Capital](/notes/village-capital)'s investment approach. Per the June 1st, 2018 call with [Inder Takhar](/notes/inder-takhar), Village Capital runs one accelerator/development program per sector — fintech, education, health, agriculture/agtech, and energy/cleantech — taking roughly 12 companies into each, then investing in ~2 per sector (about 20–25 investments a year total). The accelerator is both a sourcing funnel and a screening mechanism: it is where Takhar sources entrepreneurs and executes investments, sitting within the broader [Venture Capital](/notes/venture-capital) discipline. Takhar was also working on a one-off AI-focused event/program on top of the standard sector accelerators.

**Context:** A startup accelerator is a fixed-term, cohort-based program that supports early-stage companies with mentorship, education, and often a small investment, typically culminating in a demo day — Y Combinator and Techstars are the canonical examples.
