# Alma 55

A chapter of the Book of Mormon (Alma 53–63 — Preserved by His Marvelous Power reading block) in which [Moroni](/notes/moroni) retakes the city of Gid by getting the Lamanite guards drunk rather than killing them, then fortifies his strongholds. In Kyle's Come Follow Me study, he pulls two threads from it.

**What are your desires?** From Alma 55:19 — Moroni "did not delight in murder or bloodshed, but he delighted in the saving of his people from destruction." Kyle's note turns this into a question of motive: "What are your motivations for doing things?"

**Fortify your strongholds.** From Alma 55:25–31, where the Nephites strengthen the fortifications of Gid and Bountiful, refuse the Lamanites' wine, and "were not slow to remember the Lord their God in this their time of affliction." Kyle's takeaway: "Victory comes when we begin to recognize our weak spots and defend them appropriately."
