# Trinsic

Trinsic is a verifiable-credentials / decentralized-identity infrastructure company that Kyle sourced and tracked via founder Riley Hughes. In the November 2nd, 2021 — Riley Hughes (Trinsic) sourcing call, Hughes lays out the thesis: customers build identity products on the Trinsic platform (NatWest ID, FarmerConnect, a BMW in-car identity wallet), and Apple's announcement of W3C verifiable-credential support in Apple Wallet was a strong validating signal for the standard. Trinsic's wedge is a developer motion modeled on [Twilio ($TWLO)](/notes/twilio) / SendGrid — 32 developers self-serving on credit cards at ~$22/month rising to ~$95 — converting into enterprise deals (NatWest ~$300K, FarmerConnect ~$90K; BMW, Bosch, and a UNICEF project in the next tranche). Hughes notes all enterprise deals were in Europe, attributing it to GDPR-driven data-privacy culture, and explicitly analogizes Trinsic's role to Auth0 making OAuth/OIDC accessible to developers. At the time he was raising a $5–8M round (parting with ~15–20%), with Dan Goldsmith and [Dalton Wright](/notes/dalton-wright) helping.

Beyond diligence, Kyle engaged with Trinsic's culture: the August 29th, 2022 — Trinsic Work-Life Balance Memo captures Trinsic's "we don't pay for your time, we pay for your mind / ingenuity and impact" work-expectations memo (owners, Vitality Plans, a 15-day minimum vacation floor, "maximize positive impact") and Kyle's detailed feedback — pushing for concrete "what does enough look like?" rituals (e.g. Contrary's weekly HQ Sync) and a role-specific "measurement toolkit" so impact-based pay feels aligned rather than ambiguous.

**Context:** Trinsic (trinsic.id) is a developer platform for decentralized digital identity and W3C verifiable credentials, founded by Riley Hughes (an early member of the Sovrin Foundation) and Michael Boyd.
