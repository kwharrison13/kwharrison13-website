# Zechariah 3

In Kyle's Pattern of Prophetic Callings study, Zechariah 3 supplies a scriptural anchor for the idea that prophets enter into covenants symbolized by being clothed in glory and garments. The notes cite Zechariah 3:5 — the vision in which the high priest Joshua is given clean garments and a fair mitre — as part of the recurring prophetic-call pattern in which the body is purified, the prophet is clothed, and is sent out endowed with power. It is paired with Moses 7:3 in the same point about prophets being "clothed in glory… and garments," linking the temple endowment to ancient prophetic vesting.

**Context:** Zechariah 3 is a chapter of the Old Testament book of Zechariah recounting the prophet's vision of the high priest Joshua standing before the angel of the Lord, having his filthy garments removed and being clothed in clean priestly vestments — read in Latter-day Saint study as a type of temple covenant and cleansing.
