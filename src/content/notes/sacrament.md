# Sacrament

In Kyle's gospel study the sacrament is read as a two-part covenant of physical and spiritual sacrifice. In 3 Answers To Every Prayer the bread (Moroni 4) is where we witness we are *willing* to take Christ's name, and the water (Moroni 5) is where we affirm we *do* always remember Him — a symbolism of death, sacrifice, and [Atonement](/notes/atonement). That page draws the worthiness lesson from 1 Corinthians 10: the unworthy take damnation when they partake, but "we don't demand to know who is and isn't worthy" — we examine our own souls, and being within the repentance process is itself a sign of worthiness.

In Diligence the sacrament is the ordinance through which we remember baptism and the spiritual rebirth Christ described to Nicodemus — "the fountain of living waters after which we must seek with all the diligence we can muster" (3 Nephi 20:8–9). It surfaces again among the core themes of the June 15–21, 2020. Alma 13–16: "Enter into the Rest of the Lord" study, connecting weekly worship to the rest of the Lord.

**Context:** In The Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-day Saints, the sacrament is the weekly ordinance of bread and water through which members renew their baptismal covenants and remember the body and blood of Jesus Christ.

## Where this appears

- 3 Answers To Every Prayer — the bread/water as willing-to-take vs. do-always-remember; worthiness as self-examination (1 Corinthians 10).
- Diligence — the sacrament as remembering baptism and the "fountain of living waters."
- June 15–21, 2020. Alma 13–16: "Enter into the Rest of the Lord" — listed among the week's core themes.
