# Ayn Rand

Ayn Rand is the author of [Anthem](/books/anthem), the early novella Kyle read in 2025 that prefigures the Objectivist themes of her later work. In the wiki's reading, Anthem's argument is an inversion of collectivism: the evil it indicts is "there is no will save the will of all men together," and it climaxes with the protagonist carving the word EGO over his fort and declaring "I owe nothing to my brothers, nor do I gather debts from them." The page treats this as Rand's individualist ethic in seed form, pointing forward to [Atlas Shrugged](/notes/atlas-shrugged) as its fuller statement.

Rand sits inside the wiki's dystopia cluster alongside [George Orwell](/notes/george-orwell) ([Animal Farm](/books/animal-farm), [1984](/books/1984)) and [Aldous Huxley](/notes/aldous-huxley) ([Brave New World](/books/brave-new-world)). The [Animal Farm](/books/animal-farm) page draws the connection explicitly: Rand's inversion of collective "equality" echoes the farm's slogan "ALL ANIMALS ARE EQUAL BUT SOME ANIMALS ARE MORE EQUAL THAN OTHERS." Where Orwell and Huxley dramatize coerced or engineered collectivism from the outside, Anthem inverts it from the standpoint of the suppressed individual.

**Context:** Ayn Rand (1905–1982) was a Russian-American writer and the founder of Objectivism, best known for the novels *The Fountainhead* and *Atlas Shrugged* and for a philosophy championing rational self-interest and laissez-faire capitalism. *Anthem* (1938) is one of her earliest works.
