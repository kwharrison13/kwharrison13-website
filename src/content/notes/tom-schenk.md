# Tom Schenk

**One-line:** *Deputy IT director for the City of [Chicago](/notes/chicago), overseeing the full data lifecycle — database management, BI, data publication, and advanced analytics.*

## What we know

- Controls the entire digital lifespan of data in Chicago, from database to publication; co-founded a Harvard-based group of Chief Data Officers spanning 25 cities (split roughly evenly across budget office / IT / mayor's office).
- Expanded Chicago's Open Data Portal and built tools like Open Grid (public) / Windy-Grid (internal); co-wrote the legislation legalizing ride shares in Chicago that required per-trip data.
- Skeptical of technology-for-its-own-sake (e.g. blockchain); most excited about practical service-delivery analytics (food-inspector routing, lead-paint prediction). Avoids procurement via resellers like CDW/CDWG.
