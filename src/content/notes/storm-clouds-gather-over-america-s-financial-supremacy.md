# Storm Clouds Gather Over America's Financial Supremacy

**Author:** [The Economist](/notes/the-economist)
**URL:** https://www.economist.com/finance-and-economics/2026/07/12/storm-clouds-gather-over-americas-financial-supremacy
**One-line:** *America's trade threats over Brazil's Pix instant-payments system are an early skirmish in a broader splintering of the global financial system into sovereign, national payment infrastructure independent of American dominance.*

_Note: the saved clipping is a paywalled excerpt — only the opening section (headline, standfirst, and first two paragraphs) was captured. Confidence and completeness are limited accordingly; the full argument beyond the intro is not in the source._

## Key claims

- **The U.S. objected to Brazil's Pix instant-payments system**, with trade official Jamieson Greer complaining it unfairly disadvantages American firms like [Visa](/notes/visakan-veerasamy) and Mastercard; America proposed an extra 25% tariff on Brazil in response.
- **Brazil, across its political spectrum, refused to back down** — President Lula da Silva called Pix "a Brazilian achievement," and his right-wing rival Flávio Bolsonaro also declined to give it up, instead offering only not to link Pix to cross-border payment infrastructure that rivals America's.
- **Treasury Secretary Scott Bessent has framed U.S. dollar/economy access as conditional** — "economic statecraft in the 21st century" in which access is "no longer unconditional" — and other countries are responding by building sovereign financial infrastructure.
- **The global financial system is beginning to splinter into regional/national systems, starting with payments** — a dynamic the piece frames as a direct headache for the American Visa/Mastercard payments duopoly.

## Notable quotes

> "Pix is a Brazilian achievement and we will not give it up." — Luiz Inácio Lula da Silva

> "Economic statecraft in the 21st century," in which global access to the dollar and the American economy is "no longer unconditional." — Scott Bessent

> "The global financial system is splintering into regional and national systems. This is happening first in payments. It means a headache for Visa and Mastercard, the industry's American duopoly."

## How it connects

- [Visa](/notes/visakan-veerasamy) — one of the two American payments companies (with Mastercard) the piece frames as exposed to sovereign-payments-system fragmentation, starting with Brazil's Pix.
