# The Square Deal

**One-line:** *[George F. Johnson](/notes/george-f-johnson)'s name for the Endicott-Johnson Shoe Company's brand of "welfare capitalism" — provide enough for workers' welfare (free healthcare, cheap housing, parks, an 8-hour day) that they feel like partners and don't need a union, while the company stays consistently profitable.*

## What we know

- A near-utopian company town: free or near-free libraries, parks, hospitals, golf courses, pools, theaters, 6 carousels that could never be charged for, $10 bank books for newborns, houses financed/sold at a loss via its own housing corporation, free healthcare, an 8-hour day, and the most extensive medical plan in the country. (source: NPR [The Square Deal](https://www.npr.org/sections/money/2015/04/17/400360768/the-square-deal); Unions, Unions Everywhere - Research)
- Built by the Endicott-Johnson Shoe Company, which made boots for the US military in both World Wars (~52M shoes/year). From the EJ pamphlet "A Workers First Lesson in The Square Deal": *"If you are faithful, loyal, and reliable you will earn a good living under fair conditions. You are, indeed, a part of the company. You are cared for when sick."* (Unions, Unions Everywhere - Research)
- The wager: *"if you took care of your workers, you could be more competitive"* — EJ stayed profitable "despite paying some of the highest wages in the industry." In the 1932 downturn Johnson cut wages rather than lay anyone off. (Unions, Unions Everywhere - Research)
- Engineered to defuse unionization: in a ~15K-worker vote, 80% voted against the union, and Johnson said labor and capital could "live in harmony." Critics noted the benefits were designed to make workers feel **grateful** rather than fairly compensated — *"We're making money for them."* (Unions, Unions Everywhere - Research)
- [Gerald Zahavi](/notes/gerald-zahavi) (author of *Workers, Managers, and Welfare Capitalism*) gave the later verdict: *"the Square Deal is an anachronism today. People don't expect much from corporations."* The essay Unions, Unions Everywhere - Research argues against accepting that as inevitable. (Unions, Unions Everywhere - Research)
