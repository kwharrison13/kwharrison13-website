# All-in-One vs. Best-in-Class

Kyle's label for the recurring platform-strategy tension in his software diligence: whether a buyer is better served by a single integrated suite ("all-in-one") or by a best-of-breed point solution that does one thing better than any platform can. The framing does its heaviest work in the Cresta.ai - Diligence, where Kyle argues the answer is segment-dependent — *"the larger you are the more you want customization"* (which makes an integrated platform like [Amazon Connect](/notes/amazon-connect) attractive), the mid-market favors a point solution like [Cresta.ai](/notes/cresta-ai), and the smallest buyers favor all-in-one because they want "one throat to choke." The same diligence ties this directly to consolidation risk: multiple experts expect [CCaaS](/notes/ccaas) platforms ([Five9](/notes/five9) / [Genesys](/notes/genesys) / [Amazon Connect](/notes/amazon-connect)) to buy a real-time agent-assist vendor (Cresta / Balto / Observe) and fold it into the suite, with the all-in-one-vs-best-in-class dynamics favoring the platforms at the high end. It recurs in the Noname Security - Diligence, where a buyer was "actively evaluating standalone products vs. an all-in-one."

The [Company Building In The Curiosity Phase of AI](/notes/company-building-in-the-curiosity-phase-of-ai) long read pushes the tension a step further: it predicts AI will drive *"a collapse of features from disparate products into a single product,"* upending incumbent "professional" workflows — i.e. AI itself becomes the force that tilts the balance from best-in-class point tools back toward all-in-one consolidation.

**Context:** "All-in-one vs. best-of-breed" is a standard enterprise-software purchasing debate — integrated suites win on simplicity, vendor consolidation, and data continuity, while point solutions win on depth, speed of innovation, and fit — and the equilibrium shifts with buyer size, market maturity, and (here) the arrival of AI.

## Where this appears

- Noname Security - Diligence — a buyer evaluating standalone products vs. an all-in-one
