# ELGL

**One-line definition:** *A cross-functional professional membership organization for people working in and around local government, co-founded by [Kirsten Wyatt](/notes/kirsten-wyatt).*

## How sources describe it

- Founded to fill the gap left by siloed legacy associations (Government Finance Officers Association, City Management Association, Public Works Association) organized by job title and seniority; ELGL is cross-functional and egalitarian — startup founders and city managers join on equal terms. (June 1st, 2018 — Kirsten Wyatt (ELGL))
- Grew from a 16-person Portland meetup to ~4,000 members nationwide (~85% government employees); a 501(c)(6) with low membership fees (~$300/yr all-in) and event sponsorships, deliberately avoiding paywalls.
- Treats vendor companies as necessary partners (not just sponsorship dollars), makes introductions to cities, and gives companies a platform (webinars, podcast, blog). Started a partnership with What Works Cities (Bloomberg).
