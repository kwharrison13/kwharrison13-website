# Hustle Porn

Hustle porn appears in the research behind Kyle's essay [The Renaissance of Rise and Grind](/essays/the-renaissance-of-rise-and-grind) (subtitle *Working Hard, Or Hardly Working?*) as a real danger Kyle is careful to acknowledge — not something the essay dismisses. In the "Caveats" section of The Renaissance of Rise and Grind - Research, Kyle writes: "There IS danger in hustle porn, just the same as there is danger in endlessly optimizing a note-taking system without doing anything noteworthy, or establishing vanity metrics that act as proxy for actual output." The frame he draws is the key one: hustle porn is the **performance** of work substituting for the **output** of work — the same category error as over-engineering a productivity system or chasing [Vanity Metrics](/notes/vanity-metrics) instead of shipping something noteworthy.

The concept is the counterweight that keeps the essay's pro-hard-work argument honest: Kyle pairs it with caveats that employers can mistreat people, that mental health matters, and that self-worth shouldn't be assigned to work — distinguishing genuine, productive effort from its glorified, identity-signaling imitation.

**Context:** "Hustle porn" is a term (popularized around 2018, often attributed to Reddit/Stripe co-founder Alexis Ohanian) for the performative glorification of relentless overwork — the grind-culture content that treats sleeplessness and burnout as status symbols. Critics argue it valorizes the appearance of effort over actual results and contributes to burnout.
