# Self-motivated

In [The Expertise Economy](/books/the-expertise-economy), "self-motivated" marks the learner who takes development into their own hands without managerial support or personalized technology. The book concedes the point but pushes past it: even self-motivated employees rarely track what they actually learned in a year, which is the argument for personalized learning records or profiles — *"to help people keep track of all the valuable learning they do every day"* so that learning "count[s] for something towards your goals." Self-motivation is also cast as a precondition for learning itself: *"It is impossible for any meaningful learning to take place without that initial self-drive and self-motivation, and there is no better way to learn than applying new skills directly to real work."*

## Where this appears

- [The Expertise Economy](/books/the-expertise-economy) — the self-driven learner as both a precondition for meaningful learning and the case for personalized learning records to capture it.
