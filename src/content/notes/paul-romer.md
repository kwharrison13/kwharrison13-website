# Paul Romer

In the wiki, Paul Romer anchors the intellectual lineage of [City Building](/notes/city-building) as the originator of the charter-cities idea. The concept page traces the practice of founding new cities from scratch — master-planned communities, charter cities, seasteads, and network-state "exits" — through a lineage running from Romer's charter-cities idea to [Patri Friedman](/notes/patri-friedman) and the [Seasteading](/notes/seasteading) movement and on to [Balaji Srinivasan](/notes/balaji-srinivasan)-style network-state "exit." Romer is also named as one of the thinkers a founder profiled on the page had followed since high school while becoming obsessed with building a new city.

**Context:** Paul Romer is an economist who won the 2018 Nobel Prize for his work on endogenous growth theory (ideas and knowledge as drivers of long-run growth). He separately championed "charter cities" — new cities governed under reformed rules, often hosted by a partnering jurisdiction — as a development tool.
