# Charter Cities

Charter cities are the governance model at the core of Kyle's [City Building](/notes/city-building) thesis: founding new cities from scratch under a fresh legal charter rather than reforming legacy ones, on the bet that frontier governance can be built greenfield. The [City Building](/notes/city-building) page traces the lineage through [Paul Romer](/notes/paul-romer)'s charter-cities idea, [Patri Friedman](/notes/patri-friedman) and the [Seasteading](/notes/seasteading) movement, and [Balaji Srinivasan](/notes/balaji-srinivasan)-style network-state "exit." Its central narrative is [Dryden Brown](/notes/dryden-brown) and [Charlie Callinan](/notes/charlie-callinan)'s attempt to build a charter city in West Africa — researching every book on ancient/modern/future cities, consulting the Silicon Valley new-cities crowd ([Mark Lutter](/notes/mark-lutter) and [Tamara Winter](/notes/tamara-winter) at the [Charter Cities Institute](/notes/charter-cities-institute), [Iyinoluwa Aboyeji](/notes/iyinoluwa-aboyeji), [Patri Friedman](/notes/patri-friedman), [Mike Solana](/notes/mike-solana), [Jason Crawford](/notes/jason-crawford)), and ultimately pitching Ghana's Vice President [Mahamudu Bawumia](/notes/mahamudu-bawumia) before raising a seed round to develop enabling legislation.

The concept also appears as a policy prescription. In [To Save America, Restore Our Frontier](/notes/to-save-america-restore-our-frontier), Joe Lonsdale's program for rebuilding the American "Frontier" explicitly calls for "charter cities and bold governance experiments (even on Mars or ocean)," sitting alongside funding new science and universities and breaking up regulatory monopolies. [Mark Lutter](/notes/mark-lutter), founder of the [Charter Cities Institute](/notes/charter-cities-institute), is Kyle's primary node into this world (the saved SoundCloud talk pairs him with [Progress Studies](/notes/progress-studies)), and [Tamara Winter](/notes/tamara-winter) connects it to the [Stripe](/notes/stripe) / Stripe Press orbit.

**Context:** "Charter city" refers to a new city granted a distinct set of governing rules — popularized by economist Paul Romer — as a way to import better institutions into a region; the Charter Cities Institute, founded by Mark Lutter, is a nonprofit advancing the idea.
