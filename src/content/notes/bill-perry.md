# Bill Perry

Bill (William) Perry recurs across three of Kyle's reads as a pivotal Pentagon figure in the transition to information-based warfare — a key reference point for Kyle's [Anduril](/notes/anduril) reading lens. In [Chip War](/books/chip-war), as Pentagon R&E chief he commissions the DARPA "Assault Breaker" program, integrating sensors, guided weapons, and communications into "automated war" — the early vision Kyle tags as [Sensor Fusion](/notes/sensor-fusion). Perry's 1981 prophecy that chip density would rise "ten to a hundredfold," fielding "'smart' weapons at all levels," anchors the book's account of the offset strategy he pursued alongside [Andrew Marshall](/notes/andrew-marshall) and Harold Brown.

In [Skunk Works](/books/skunk-works), Ben Rich recounts Perry (as the Carter administration's research-and-engineering chief from January 1977) immediately backing stealth on seeing the low-observability results — standing up a counter-stealth office, pushing the Have Blue / stealth-bomber decision ("we can't sit around and play the usual development games"), and ordering Navy R&D funding for the Sea Shadow stealth ship. Kyle's notes frame him as a customer who trusts the customer to know the problem but not the solution, and whose conviction "paid off and brought about government R&D spending." In [Flying Blind](/books/flying-blind), Perry plays a different role: as Defense Secretary, his 1993 "Last Supper" urged America's military contractors to consolidate, setting up the McDonnell Douglas–Boeing merger the book treats as the seed of Boeing's cultural decay.

**Context:** William J. Perry (b. 1927) was U.S. Secretary of Defense from 1994 to 1997 under President Clinton, and earlier served as Under Secretary of Defense for Research and Engineering (1977–1981), where he championed stealth technology and the precision-strike "offset strategy."

## Where this appears

- [Chip War](/books/chip-war) — commissions DARPA "Assault Breaker," the sensor-fusion/precision-strike root of the offset strategy
- [Skunk Works](/books/skunk-works) — Pentagon R&E chief who backed stealth (Have Blue, stealth bomber, Sea Shadow) and funded government R&D
- [Flying Blind](/books/flying-blind) — Defense Secretary whose "Last Supper" pushed the defense-contractor consolidation behind the McDonnell merger
