# Arthur Henry King

Arthur Henry King appears in Kyle's notes as a convert to the Church and a renowned English professor whose literary judgment of [Joseph Smith](/notes/joseph-smith) is a recurring touchstone in [Joseph Smith Lecture 2 — Joseph's Personality and Character](/notes/joseph-smith-lecture-2-joseph-s-personality-and-character). King placed Joseph Smith's account in Joseph Smith—History (the First Vision and the visits of Moroni) "among the sublime prose in world literature," contrasting it favorably with Oliver Cowdery's more ornate but shallower "flowery journalese." In a longer passage King argues that supreme literary figures — Homer, Vergil, Dante, Shakespeare, Goethe — seem to have stopped, "but I tell you there was one: Joseph Smith." His essay "Joseph Smith As a Writer" appears in *The Abundance of the Heart*. In the lecture's Connections, King's verdict sits alongside [Hugh Nibley](/notes/hugh-nibley)'s and Truman G. Madsen's defenses of the Book of Mormon as canonical-grade literature.

**Context:** Arthur Henry King (1910–2000) was a British linguist, literary scholar, and convert to the Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-day Saints who taught English at Brigham Young University.
