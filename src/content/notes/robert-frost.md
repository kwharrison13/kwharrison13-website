# Robert Frost

Poet whose poem "[The Pasture](/notes/the-pasture)" Kyle invokes as his model for fatherhood. In his prep for the [Harry Stebbings](/notes/harry-stebbings) podcast (September 13th, 2022 — Harry Stebbings Podcast), Kyle planned to "drop some Robert Frost" when asked about great fatherhood, quoting the poem's refrain — "I shan't be gone long — You come too" — as advice he received from a father-figure years earlier. He reads the poem as the heart of parenting: bringing your kids along for the journey, playing different roles over time (caretaker, then coach, then counselor), and living a life that *includes* your kids rather than one lived in spite of them.

**Context:** Robert Frost (1874–1963) was an American poet known for plainspoken depictions of rural New England life; "The Pasture" opens his first collection, *A Boy's Will* (1913).
