# Corianton

The wayward son of [Alma the Younger](/notes/alma-the-younger) in the Book of Mormon, the subject of Alma's fatherly counsel in Alma 39–Alma 42. In Kyle's Come Follow Me studies, Corianton is the occasion for one of the most striking insights Kyle drew out of the chapters: Alma spends only **14 verses on Corianton's sins but 74 verses teaching about [Jesus Christ](/notes/jesus)** before relating it back to his son (Alma 39:15, Alma 42:29). The lesson Kyle traced is to **teach toward Christ, not just against sin** — a model of correction that leads with the Atonement rather than condemnation.

The Corianton chapters carry two further threads in Kyle's notes. First, **idleness as the devil's playground**: Corianton "shouldst have tended to the ministry," and Kyle connects "the idle brain is the devil's playground" ([Meredith Willson](/notes/meredith-willson), [The Music Man (musical)](/notes/the-music-man-musical)) and "idleness is the root of mischief" ([Geoffrey Chaucer](/notes/geoffrey-chaucer)). Second, the chapters are a study in [Parenting](/notes/parenting) and [Self-Mastery](/notes/self-mastery) — the call to "cross yourself," and the work of knowing the mind of your children well enough to speak to what stirs their hearts. Corianton also appears alongside his brothers [Shiblon](/notes/shiblon) and [Helaman](/notes/helaman) in the Alma 36–38 study as one of the three sons Alma counsels.

**Context:** Corianton is a figure in the Book of Mormon, the youngest of Alma the Younger's three sons; the book records Alma's counsel to him after Corianton forsook his ministry and committed sexual sin.
