# D&C 3

Section 3 of the Doctrine and Covenants — the Lord's rebuke of [Joseph Smith](/notes/joseph-smith) after the loss of the 116 manuscript pages — runs through three of Kyle's studies. Its central line, **verse 3** ("remember that it is not the work of God that is frustrated, but the work of men"), answers the Lord's anguished question "What could I have done more for my vineyard?" in Kyle's Olive Tree Allegory study of [Jacob 5](/notes/jacob-5). His Publication of the Book of Mormon study treats the whole section as evidence of authenticity: D&C 3 "is basically a rebuking record of the 116 pages," and it seems unlikely a man concocting a religion would begin by rebuking his own faults — the lost pages read as Joseph being *allowed to make a mistake*, not God changing His mind. Chosen Leaders cites verse 10 for the principle that even after a serious error, the Lord's servant is "still chosen and again called."

**Context:** Doctrine and Covenants Section 3 is a revelation given to [Joseph Smith](/notes/joseph-smith) in July 1828, after Martin Harris lost the first 116 pages of the Book of Mormon translation manuscript. It rebukes Joseph for fearing man more than God, while affirming that God's work cannot be frustrated.

## Where this appears

- Olive Tree Allegory — verse 3 ("not the work of God that is frustrated, but the work of men") answering Jacob 5:41
- Publication of the Book of Mormon — the section as a self-rebuke that argues for the Book of Mormon's authenticity
- Chosen Leaders — verse 10 on being "still chosen and again called" after error
