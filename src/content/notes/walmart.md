# Walmart

In Kyle's corpus, Walmart shows up in two registers. As an **operating-philosophy case study**, it is the subject of [David Perell](/notes/david-perell)'s [Wisdom From Walmart](/notes/wisdom-from-walmart) (drawn from [Sam Walton](/notes/sam-walton)'s [Sam Walton: Made in America](/notes/sam-walton-made-in-america)): the math of [Discounting](/notes/discounting) ("by cutting your price, you can boost your sales to a point where you earn far more… than you would have by selling the item at the higher price"), frugality reframed as [Customer Obsession](/notes/customer-obsession) ("every time Wal-Mart spends one dollar foolishly, it comes right out of our customers' pockets"), [Vertical Integration](/notes/vertical-integration) of distribution and logistics for a durable cost-per-unit edge, and pushing authority to the front lines. Walton's stance of relentless [Self-Criticism](/notes/self-criticism) — "less afraid of being wrong than anyone" — is the trait Kyle keeps returning to.

The same scale shows up on the other side of the ledger. In [What I Found in a Thousand Towns](/books/what-i-found-in-a-thousand-towns), Walmart is invoked as the "grim reaper" big-box strategy targeting Main Streets with weak political will — complicated by towns (Lowell) that needed the jobs. And in the Watershed Climate Diligence — 2021 dossier, Walmart appears as enterprise emissions scale: [Elizabeth Willett Murchison](/notes/elizabeth-willett-murchison) at Walmart was on the outreach list for sustainability reference calls, a marker of the kind of high-footprint enterprise customer Watershed must reach for gigaton-level impact.

**Context:** Walmart is the world's largest retailer by revenue, founded by Sam Walton in 1962 in Rogers, Arkansas, and built on an everyday-low-prices, high-volume, logistics-heavy model.

- [What I Found in a Thousand Towns](/books/what-i-found-in-a-thousand-towns) — Walmart as the big-box "grim reaper" pressuring small-town Main Streets.

- [Sam Walton](/notes/sam-walton) — founder; the operating philosophy traces to his autobiography.
