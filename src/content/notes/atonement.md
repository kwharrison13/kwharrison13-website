# Atonement

The Atonement is one of the most heavily cross-linked theological concepts in Kyle's gospel-study corpus, and the sources converge on a single emphasis: the Atonement is rehabilitative and enabling, not merely retributive or a fallback. [The Broken Heart](/books/the-broken-heart) (Bruce C. Hafen) makes the central argument that the Atonement is "a rich source of strength to deal productively with the disappointments and heartbreaks that form the deliberate fabric of mortal life" — the gospel "was given us to heal our pain, not to prevent it" — and distinguishes the unconditional gifts (resurrection, paying for Adam's transgression) from the conditional part requiring [Repentance](/notes/repentance). [The Crucible of Doubt](/books/the-crucible-of-doubt) frames it the same way: "not a backup plan in case we happen to fall short... it is the ordained means whereby we gradually become complete and whole." [Future Mormon](/books/future-mormon) pushes the grace-first reading even further via McConkie's "three pillars" (creation, fall, atonement), arguing grace is primal and sin is the suppression of grace.

Kyle's own How To Apply The Atonement topical study supplies the corpus's signature image: the Atonement does not pull nails from a board (leaving holes) but throws away the worn board and gives you a new one — "you become a new creature in Christ" ([Mosiah 27](/notes/mosiah-27):25–26). It is **infinite** (unbounded by space, time, or mortal limits) but infinite does **not** mean unconditional: resurrection is unconditional, eternal life conditional. Christ Defines His Gospel adds the "pendulum of grace" — [Satan](/notes/satan) pushes people toward either pride ("I'll save myself") or hopelessness ("save me, doing nothing") — and warns against "prepentance" and "cheap grace." The concept threads through dozens of weekly Come Follow Me studies and General Conference notes (General Conference — October 2021).

**Context:** In Latter-day Saint theology, the Atonement refers to Jesus Christ's suffering, death, and resurrection, understood as the means by which humanity can be redeemed from sin and physical death and enabled to repent and become sanctified.

## Where this appears

- [The Broken Heart](/books/the-broken-heart) — the Atonement as rehabilitative strength for mortal heartbreak; conditional vs. unconditional gifts.
- [The Crucible of Doubt](/books/the-crucible-of-doubt) — the Atonement as the ordained means of sanctification, not a backup plan.
- [Future Mormon](/books/future-mormon) — McConkie's three pillars and the grace-first reading where sin is the suppression of grace.
- How To Apply The Atonement — Kyle's topical study; the "new board" image; infinite but not unconditional.
- Christ Defines His Gospel — the "pendulum of grace," saving vs. enabling grace, "prepentance" and "cheap grace."
- General Conference — October 2021 — Conference talks invoking the Atonement.
