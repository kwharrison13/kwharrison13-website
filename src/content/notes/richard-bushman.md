# Richard Bushman

**Richard Lyman Bushman** is the believing historian whose landmark biography [Joseph Smith — Rough Stone Rolling](/books/joseph-smith-rough-stone-rolling) anchors much of Kyle's Mormon-history reading — the work that, per [Leonard Arrington and the Writing of Mormon History](/books/leonard-arrington-and-the-writing-of-mormon-history), "[Leonard Arrington](/notes/leonard-arrington)'s institutional setup made possible," and one of only eight books Bushman himself named as essential on Mormonism. He appears as a recurring analytical voice: in [Stretching The Heavens](/books/stretching-the-heavens) he is quoted on Joseph Smith's restructuring of leadership — that by 1835 "Smith not only shifted the responsibility for revelation from himself to his councils, he moved the locus of revelation from the individual prophet to the church's administrative bureaucracy."

Bushman also models the posture of faith-amid-questions that runs through Kyle's notes. [Faith is Not Blind](/books/faith-is-not-blind) cites him spending "a lifetime learning to communicate about religion 'in a way that can be understood' by a secular audience" rather than forcing them "to learn our language in order to understand us" — a model for translating faith across the secular/believing divide. The book quotes his candid testimony at length: "I know the arguments against the [Book of Mormon's] historicity, but I can't help feeling that the words are true and the events happened… Unanswerable as some questions are, we need not lament the questions they bring. The strain of believing in unbelieving times is not a handicap or a burden. It is a stimulus and a prod… And… we are in this together." That sustained faith — neither denying hard questions nor defeated by them — is exactly the "faith is not blind" stance the Hafens argue for. In Early Mormonism he supplies the "stereotype of fanaticism" — the airtight logic that anyone who claims to speak for God is assumed to possess absolute truth and a commission to impose it, which is why claiming revelation is the surest way to be thought fanatical.

**Context:** Richard Lyman Bushman (b. 1931) is an American historian, Gouverneur Morris Professor Emeritus of History at Columbia University and a practicing Latter-day Saint. His *Joseph Smith: Rough Stone Rolling* (2005) is widely regarded as the definitive modern biography of the church's founder.
