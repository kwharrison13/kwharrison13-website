# James 2

The New Testament chapter Kyle returns to across his gospel study as the canonical "faith without works is dead" passage. The most-cited verses are James 2:17 ("Even so faith, if it hath not works, is dead, being alone"), 2:20, and 2:22 ("By works was faith made perfect"). In Faith — Topical Study and Miscellaneous Scriptures he pairs it with [Ether 12](/notes/ether-12):6 (no witness until after the trial of faith) and [Alma 32](/notes/alma-32):21 (faith as hope, not knowledge) to frame faith as something proven and completed through action.

In the December 31–January 6, 2019 [Come, Follow Me](/notes/come-follow-me) week ("We Are Responsible for Our Own Learning") it grounds the charge to be *doers* and not merely hearers, alongside [James 1](/notes/james-1). In Christ Defines His Gospel it appears within a cluster on grace and works — [Galatians 2](/notes/galatians-2), [Ephesians 2](/notes/ephesians-2), [D&C 20](/notes/dandc-20) — where the law represents the demands of justice that cannot save us and it is Christ's grace that does, with works as the evidence of living faith rather than the means of earning salvation. In That All Men May Come Unto Him the same chapter supports a study on [Agency](/notes/agency) and self-mastery.

**Context:** James 2 is a chapter in the Epistle of James in the New Testament, best known for its argument that genuine faith necessarily produces works ("faith without works is dead") — a passage central to longstanding Christian debates about the relationship between faith and works.
