# Starting a Junto

A tag Kyle uses for the idea of running a recurring peer knowledge-transfer circle — modeled on Benjamin Franklin's "Junto," a club of mutual improvement. It surfaces in a [Luke Piette](/notes/luke-piette) 1x1 (March 30th, 2022 — 1x1 with Luke Piette), where Luke describes how a community member ("John-Luca") runs informal Knowledge Transfer sessions, framed by Kyle as similar to [Lightning Talks](/notes/lightning-talks). The defining rule that came up: the topic "has to be outside of what you're working on" — the example given was a deep talk on [Credit Card Churning](/notes/credit-card-churning) to game points systems, a subject far from the speaker's day job. The Junto-style session is thus a structured way to surface cross-domain expertise within a tight community.

**Context:** A Junto was the club Benjamin Franklin founded in 1727 in Philadelphia — a small group of tradesmen and artisans who met weekly to debate morals, politics, and natural philosophy with the explicit goal of mutual self-improvement. The format has become a recurring template for modern peer-learning and salon-style groups.
