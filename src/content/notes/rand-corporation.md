# RAND Corporation

The RAND Corporation runs through two of Kyle's research threads. In [The Man From The Future](/books/the-man-from-the-future) (Bhattacharya's [John von Neumann](/notes/john-von-neumann) biography), RAND is framed as a **proto-[Anduril](/notes/anduril)** — the defense think-tank-as-prototype, built on [Operations Research](/notes/operations-research) and embedded academics who self-organized around national-security problems before the bureaucracy could catch up, a core piece of the book's [Civil-Military Fusion](/notes/civil-military-fusion) thread. The book traces RAND's founding (named for "Research ANd Development," seeded by Hap Arnold and the wartime *Toward New Horizons* report, with von Neumann consulting for $200/month and famously solving problems RAND scientists thought intractable), and its role as the home of nuclear-strategy [Game Theory](/notes/game-theory) — "a veritable Who's Who of game theorists charged with thinking about the unthinkable" — plus the "systems analysis" method that fed ICBM development and McNamara's Pentagon "Whizz Kids."

In Historical Futurism - Research, RAND appears as the origin of the futures-studies movement: the [Institute For The Future](/notes/institute-for-the-future) was a spin-off from RAND set up in 1968 to help organizations with "future studies" — Kyle's first exposure to futurology and futurism, and a seed for one of his portfolio ideas.

**Context:** RAND Corporation is a nonprofit global policy think tank founded in 1948 in Santa Monica, California, originally a project under Douglas Aircraft for the U.S. Air Force. It pioneered systems analysis and operations research and became foundational to Cold War nuclear strategy and game theory.

## Where this appears

- [The Man From The Future](/books/the-man-from-the-future) — RAND as proto-[Anduril](/notes/anduril): operations research, embedded academics, civil-military fusion, the home of nuclear-strategy game theory and systems analysis; von Neumann consulted there.
- Historical Futurism - Research — RAND as the parent of the [Institute For The Future](/notes/institute-for-the-future) (1968 spin-off), the seed of Kyle's interest in futurology.
