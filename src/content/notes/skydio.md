# Skydio

Skydio shows up in Kyle's corpus as an autonomous drone company in the defense / [American Dynamism](/notes/american-dynamism) thread. In [Katherine Boyle](/notes/katherine-boyle)'s [Building American Dynamism](/notes/building-american-dynamism), it is named alongside Anduril and Shield as one of the defense companies "entrenched in the work and mission of government" — part of Boyle's list of technologists building for the national interest (aerospace: SpaceX/Blue Origin; intelligence: [Palantir](/notes/palantir); defense: Anduril, Shield, Skydio). Skydio also appears in Kyle's own essay [Chasing The Rush of Creation](/essays/chasing-the-rush-of-creation): a 17-year-old applicant, Alexa, studied Kyle's memo format and replicated it to write a memo about Skydio, "an autonomous drone company," unpacking its defense use cases — an example Kyle uses to praise curiosity-driven research on a hardware company.

**Context:** Skydio is a U.S.-based maker of autonomous drones, originally known for consumer self-flying camera drones and now focused on defense, public-safety, and enterprise/infrastructure inspection markets.
