# Soko

Soko is a compliance-software startup building "the largest database of compliant independent workers" to make it easier for companies to engage 1099 contractors, per its pre-seed memo in the August 18th, 2021 — Soko Data Room notes. Its wedge is a compliance engine that ingests a company's contractor data, documents, and credentials, audits them, and produces an ongoing compliance report plus a downloadable audit trail — pitched as a way for large organizations to get near-instant visibility into the misclassification and legal risk of their independent workforce. The longer-term vision is a portable "compliance profile" that contractors own and travel with, reducing onboarding time across companies (the memo frames Soko as "TrueWork for independent contractors"). Initial target verticals were logistics, healthcare, caregiving, home services, and retail, monetized via a per-contractor-per-month subscription.

The team Kyle reviewed comprised [Farhaj Mayan](/notes/farhaj-mayan) (CEO), [Eve Epstein](/notes/eve-epstein) (CLO, a former U.S. Department of Labor trial attorney who founded SoleVenture), [Vu Francois](/notes/vu-francois) (CPO), and [Bradley Faught](/notes/bradley-faught) (CTO). Soko grew out of the founders' prior labor-marketplace ventures — OKO (a cannabis labor marketplace), Fade (a barber-booking marketplace), and Craterland — with SoleVenture's IP and compliance process folded in via asset purchase to seed the company. At the time of Kyle's data-room review the company had raised a $1M angel round (Operator Partners, Strada Holdings, Jon Oringer) and was raising a $2.5M round to generate 500,000 contractor profiles. Kyle reviewed this as a sourcing/diligence look during his time at [Index Ventures](/notes/index-ventures).
