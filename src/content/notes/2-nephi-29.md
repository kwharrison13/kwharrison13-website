# 2 Nephi 29

A chapter in the Book of Mormon's second book of Nephi, cited in Kyle's gospel study chiefly for its "A Bible! A Bible!" passage — the Lord's rebuke of those who deny continuing revelation. The verses recur as a proof-text on the open canon: "many of the Gentiles shall say: A Bible! A Bible! We have got a Bible, and there cannot be any more Bible" ([2 Nephi 29](/notes/2-nephi-29):3), answered with "Thou fool, that shall say: A Bible, we have got a Bible, and we need no more Bible" (v. 6). The Come Follow Me study October 19–25, 2020. 3 Nephi 27–4 Nephi: "There Could Not Be a Happier People" quotes these verses alongside [3 Nephi 29](/notes/3-nephi-29):6 under the heading "A Bible, We Have a Bible."

In Diligence, Kyle turns the same verse into a lesson on spiritual hunger: a person who is content ("we have a Bible, and we need no more Bible") is full and so will not hunger after more, contrasted against the disciple who seeks "not bread alone, but every word that proceedeth out of the mouth of God" ([Matthew 4](/notes/matthew-4):4).

**Context:** 2 Nephi 29 is the Lord's prophecy, given through Nephi, that He will bring forth additional scripture (the Book of Mormon) and that He speaks the same words to all nations — a chapter Latter-day Saints read as a defense of continuing revelation and an open canon.
