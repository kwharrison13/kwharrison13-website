# Oxaliplatin

Oxaliplatin appears in two of Kyle's essays as a prop in the same thought experiment about networked knowledge. In both [Building the Global Knowledge Graph](/essays/building-the-global-knowledge-graph) and [I Wish I Knew How To Quit Roam Research](/essays/i-wish-i-knew-how-to-quit-roam-research), the reader is asked to imagine a cancer researcher in Chicago "poring over a query showing every time `carboplatin` has come up in relation to `oxaliplatin` without references to `melatonin`" — and from that query stumbling onto an unlinked note by a marine biologist in Oslo, then a nuclear physicist in Moscow, and unknowingly curing cancer. The chemical name is incidental; it stands in as a concrete-sounding term to dramatize how a shared, queryable global knowledge graph (the [Roam Research](/notes/roam-research) vision) could surface non-obvious connections across distant fields.

**Context:** Oxaliplatin is a real platinum-based chemotherapy drug used to treat colorectal and other cancers, part of the same drug family as carboplatin and cisplatin. Kyle's essays use it only as illustrative vocabulary, not as a medical claim.
