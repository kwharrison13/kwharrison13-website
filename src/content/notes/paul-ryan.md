# Paul Ryan

In [Janesville](/books/janesville), Ryan is the local thread tying the town's fate to national policy. The book's central empirical finding — that laid-off workers who went back to school at Blackhawk fared *worse* on employment and pay than those who didn't — cuts against the bipartisan "retraining gospel" embraced by Ryan and [Barack Obama](/notes/barack-obama) alike. Retraining, the book notes, "may be the only economic idea on which Republicans such as Paul Ryan and Democrats such as President Obama agree," anchored in an "abiding cultural myth... of this as a land that offers its people a chance at personal reinvention" — a myth the evidence in Janesville undercuts.

In [Romney — A Reckoning](/books/romney-a-reckoning), Ryan appears as a fiscal hawk who abandoned his signature cause. The book observes that "even Paul Ryan, whose 2016 surrender to Trump Romney had treated more generously than others', seemed to have given up on the issue to which he'd devoted most of his political career": during his three years as Speaker the deficit rose nearly 80 percent — including two years of full GOP control — with the era's signature legislation being a tax overhaul that slashed rates with few offsetting mechanisms.

**Context:** Paul Ryan is an American politician from Janesville, Wisconsin, who served as Speaker of the U.S. House of Representatives (2015–2019) and was Mitt Romney's running mate in the 2012 presidential election.
