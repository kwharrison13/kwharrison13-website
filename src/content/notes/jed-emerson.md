# Jed Emerson

Jed Emerson appears in Kyle's [MBA Degrees](/notes/mba-degrees) notes as the figure behind the **Haas / REDF** program — described as "the program that became a template for social entrepreneurship." In that source, a first-cohort Haas/REDF student argues social entrepreneurship is a *culture* permeating the whole program rather than a course track — "the way Haas MBAs think and approach business." Emerson is also listed there alongside [Muhammad Yunus](/notes/muhammad-yunus), [Wendy Kopp](/notes/wendy-kopp), and Greg Dees as part of the social-entrepreneurship "counter-current" within business education.

**Context:** Jed Emerson is widely credited with coining the term "blended value," the idea that social and financial returns are inseparable rather than a trade-off. He was an early leader of REDF (the Roberts Enterprise Development Fund) and a pioneer of impact-investing and social-enterprise thinking.
