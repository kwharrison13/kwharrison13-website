# Arik Bronshtein

**One-line:** *Founder/operator at [UrbanLeap](/notes/urbanleap), a platform that helps cities structure and manage pilot tests of new solutions.*

## What we know

- Worked in government for 5 years and then served as a public-private partnership leader submitting requests to government, before [UrbanLeap](/notes/urbanleap).
- Believes the only way for governments to grow and innovate is to do more testing of new products, and that cities lack the capacity/knowledge to do as much testing as they should.
- Had been talking with [Mariel Reed](/notes/mariel-reed) (who may join the company) about the transition from a successful pilot (UrbanLeap) to RFP ([CoProcure](/notes/coprocure)).

## Connections

- [UrbanLeap](/notes/urbanleap) — the pilot-management platform Bronshtein founded/operates, built to help cities structure and run tests of new solutions.
- [Mariel Reed](/notes/mariel-reed) — discussed potentially joining the company; conversations covered the move from a successful pilot to RFP.
- [CoProcure](/notes/coprocure) — referenced as the RFP-side counterpart Bronshtein and Reed discussed when mapping the pilot-to-procurement transition.
- [Procurement](/notes/procurement) — central to his thesis: governments innovate by testing more, but lack the capacity to procure and pilot new products.
- [City Building](/notes/city-building) — his work targets how cities adopt and trial new solutions.
- [GovTech](/notes/govtech) — his five years in government and public-private partnership work sit squarely in this space.
