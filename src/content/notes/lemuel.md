# Lemuel

A figure from the Book of Mormon who appears across Kyle's gospel-study notes, almost always paired with his brother [Laman](/notes/laman) as the rebellious sons of [Lehi](/notes/lehi) who fail to hearken to their father. In the Alma 39–42 study, Kyle connects to the Book of Mormon video of the tree of life, where [Lehi](/notes/lehi) tells [Laman](/notes/laman) and Lemuel that they don't hearken to his words. In The Process of the Book of Mormon, Lemuel recurs in the early-Nephi narrative — the angel appearing to [Laman](/notes/laman) and Lemuel, and the running contrast of [Nephi](/notes/nephi) against his older brothers.

**Context:** Lemuel is one of the sons of Lehi in the Book of Mormon (1 Nephi), the second-eldest after Laman. The two brothers are portrayed as murmuring, faithless, and resistant to their father's and Nephi's spiritual leadership, and their descendants become the Lamanites.
