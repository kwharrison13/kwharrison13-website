# Mormon 1

Mormon 1 is the opening chapter of the Book of Mormon's namesake record, where the prophet-historian [Mormon](/notes/mormon) introduces himself. Across Kyle's gospel study the chapter is anchored by two verses. The recurring hook is [Mormon 1](/notes/mormon-1):2 — Mormon, "about ten years of age," is told by Ammaron, "I perceive that thou art a *sober* child, and art *quick to observe*." Kyle threads this into his Scripture Study — Topical Study as a model for attentive study and prayer: when the mind wanders, "be sober and quick to observe" — stop, pay attention, and take what you wandered to before the Lord. The chapter also supplies [Mormon 1](/notes/mormon-1):15 ("tasted of the goodness of Jesus"), filed under his miscellaneous-scriptures notes.

The verses sit inside the broader Mormon 1–6 — I Would That I Could Persuade All to Repent weekly study, which frames the young Mormon as a sober, quick-to-observe historian called to record the final Nephite wars — set against [The Pride Cycle](/notes/the-pride-cycle), Godly Sorrow, and the tragedy of a people "left to ourselves" when they refuse to repent.

**Context:** Mormon 1 is a chapter in the Book of Mormon (a volume of scripture in the Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-day Saints), narrating the calling of the prophet Mormon as a young record-keeper amid the Nephite–Lamanite wars.
