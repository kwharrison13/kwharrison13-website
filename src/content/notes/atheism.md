# Atheism

A recurring theme across Kyle's 2020 Come Follow Me studies, where unbelief in God is examined through several Book of Mormon confrontations. In July 6–12, 2020. Alma 30–31: "The Virtue of the Word of God", Korihor the [Anti-Christ](/notes/anti-christ) argues against prophecy, the [Atonement](/notes/atonement), [Agency](/notes/agency), and resurrection — and Kyle's note is that atheism "cuts both ways": "What evidence have ye that there is no God…? ye have none, save it be your word only" (Alma 30:40). In August 31–September 6, 2020. Helaman 13–16: "Glad Tidings of Great Joy", the theme reappears as "atheism as imagined slavery" — skeptics frame revelation as a scheme "to keep us in ignorance," missing that following [Jesus Christ](/notes/jesus) is freeing ("we are obedient because we can see," per [Boyd K. Packer](/notes/boyd-k-packer)).

In November 2–8, 2020. Mormon 7–9: "I Speak unto You As If Ye Were Present", Moroni's message to a future generation of unbelief becomes "Moroni's answer to atheism": miracles require [Faith](/notes/faith), and a God of revelation does not cease to work miracles "except as men dwindle in unbelief." Kyle ties this to [Pascal's Wager](/notes/pascal-s-wager) — "Then will ye say that there is no God?… what will you do when you have to face him if he does turn out to exist?"

## Where this appears

- July 6–12, 2020. Alma 30–31: "The Virtue of the Word of God" — Korihor the Anti-Christ; "atheism cuts both ways" (no evidence there is no God either)
- August 31–September 6, 2020. Helaman 13–16: "Glad Tidings of Great Joy" — atheism as imagined slavery; obedience as freedom
- November 2–8, 2020. Mormon 7–9: "I Speak unto You As If Ye Were Present" — Moroni's answer to atheism, paired with Pascal's Wager
