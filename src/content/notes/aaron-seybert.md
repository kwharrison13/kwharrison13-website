# Aaron Seybert

A social-investment practitioner at the [Kresge Foundation](/notes/kresge-foundation) whom Kyle spoke with on July 10th, 2018 (call notes). His background was in portfolio management and underwriting for affordable-housing work — tax-equity buy-side and project finance around real estate, always in the low-income area — and his last major effort before Kresge was investing $100M into Detroit. He joined Kresge to build its central investment practice, covering the Detroit program and the American Cities practice.

In the conversation he described Kresge's model of putting the endowment to work through charitable investments that still return capital: aligning tightly with program sections, direct investing across equity and debt, and using balance-sheet capacity to de-risk transactions, aiming for roughly 20-to-1 leverage on their dollars. About 80% of the work sits in debt-like structures (subordinate/equity-like positions managed through intermediaries) and 20% in higher-touch equity and guarantee work. He highlighted a deliberate tension in the approval process — a program officer must vouch that a deal "fits the strategy" while he confirms it is "financially structured correctly" — and noted the screening rigor: of 100+ impact funds they review, maybe one clears the bar, with most being "interesting, but not really impactful enough" or "too watered down" for either the endowment or the charitable side.
