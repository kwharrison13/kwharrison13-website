# Micron

**One-line:** *US semiconductor company (~$1.3 trillion valuation) and major domestic player in memory chips, building what could become a $100 billion memory-chip manufacturing campus in Syracuse, NY as part of the post-CHIPS-Act reindustrialization push.*

## What we know

- Before the CHIPS Act, Micron produced the majority of its memory offshore in Taiwan and Japan; a few months after the Act's 2022 passage, it chose the Syracuse, NY area for a planned $100 billion memory-chip manufacturing campus (per [Inside Micron's Attempts to Reindustrialize the Dying American Heartland](/notes/inside-micron-s-attempts-to-reindustrialize-the-dying-american-heartland)).
- Memory chip shortages date to 2018 and have worsened with AI demand: data centers are expected to consume 70% of all high-end memory produced in 2026, RAM prices jumped 50% in a single quarter, while production capacity is only expanding by 16%.
- Local Syracuse-area officials (led by Ryan McMahon) spent roughly two years assembling land and utility capacity to make the site investment-ready, offering tax incentives plus a workforce pipeline including a $20 million semiconductor manufacturing training center (Onondaga County/Syracuse University); Micron reciprocated with a $15 million investment in the nation's first community-college cleanroom.
- Commerce Secretary Howard Lutnick renegotiated Micron's CHIPS grant agreements, reducing additional commitments and securing an extra $30 billion in fab investment from the company.
