# Helaman 16

The closing chapter of the book of Helaman, covering the aftermath of Samuel the Lamanite's prophecy from the wall of Zarahemla. In Kyle's [Helaman 13](/notes/helaman-13)–16 study it anchors several recurring themes: those who believed Samuel's word "went forth and sought for Nephi" and confessed their sins (16:1) — the Fruits of the Spirit — while the unbelievers grew angry and cast stones and arrows at him, unable to hit him because the Spirit of the Lord was with him (16:2). Kyle pulls a question out of this: why didn't the angry simply walk away? — the pattern that when people feel personal guilt, they feel obligated to destroy the thing that made them guilty, a thread he ties to [The Wicked Take the Truth to be Hard](/notes/the-wicked-take-the-truth-to-be-hard) and to apostates riling up citizens against the early Church.

The chapter also supplies one of Kyle's standing [Atheism](/notes/atheism) proof texts: the skeptics dismiss Samuel's warning as "a wicked tradition" designed to "keep us in ignorance" and "keep us down to be servants to their words" (16:20-22) — framing revelation as intellectual and emotional slavery. Kyle answers it with [Boyd K. Packer](/notes/boyd-k-packer)'s line, "We are not obedient because we are blind, we are obedient because we can see" ([Blind Obedience](/notes/blind-obedience)). A further note ("Save the most believing part of them," 16:15) leans on [Russell M. Nelson](/notes/russell-m-nelson): in times of great contention it becomes harder to remain righteous, and survival depends on the [Holy Ghost](/notes/holy-ghost).
