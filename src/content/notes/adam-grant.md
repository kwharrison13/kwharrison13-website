# Adam Grant

A reference point in Kyle's notes for a recurring idea: that we should value leaders as good *learners* rather than know-it-alls. In [The Death of Expertise](/books/the-death-of-expertise), Kyle ties Grant to "the Armchair Expert / imposter-syndrome thread Camden flagged — politicians as good learners rather than know-it-alls," with the connecting note: "Why do we expect our politicians to have the answers to everything rather than being really good learners who we trust to do the work and figure things out?" The same Adam Grant / Armchair Expert thread resurfaces in [The Self-Driven Child](/books/the-self-driven-child), where Kyle's note pairs it with [Steve Jobs](/notes/steve-jobs) ("Coatue vs. Index - Adam Grant re: Steve Jobs on Armchair Expert"). In both cases the citation is to a podcast conversation (the *Armchair Expert* episode) rather than to Grant's own books.

**Context:** Adam Grant is an organizational psychologist at the Wharton School and bestselling author (*Give and Take*, *Originals*, *Think Again*). *Think Again* in particular argues for the value of intellectual humility and "rethinking" — directly aligned with the learner-over-expert framing in Kyle's notes.
