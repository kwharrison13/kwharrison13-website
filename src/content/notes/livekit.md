# LiveKit

A real-time audio/video infrastructure startup Kyle diligenced in August 2021, working through questions surfaced by [Kelly Toole](/notes/kelly-toole) (whose notes came from a meeting with founder [David Zhao](/notes/david-zhao)). LiveKit positions itself as **"open source infrastructure for building and scaling realtime audio and video experiences."** Per Kelly's competitive map of the [WebRTC](/notes/webrtc) space, developers' options range from building everything in-house (hard, flexible, scales) through other open-source projects (Janus, Jitsi) to **CPaaS** providers (Twilio, Daily.co, Agora — easy but inflexible with scalability limits). LiveKit's wedge was to **fill the gap with an easy, flexible, *and* scalable solution**, with a managed cloud product on the roadmap. The diligence questions probed how the cloud version would balance simplicity vs. flexibility, what technically set it apart on scalability, pricing strategy to counter cost fears, and the target customer profile (e.g. apps scaling past 200 participants in a single room).

The notes captured strong early traction roughly one month post-launch: **~1.6k GitHub stars** (1.4k in the first week), 188 Slack members, 4.5k Docker pulls, 3 integrations in production with dozens more in development, 6 larger partnerships in discussion, and even an acquisition offer from a public company — with at least one term sheet in hand and more VC meetings that week.

**Context:** LiveKit is an open-source WebRTC platform widely adopted for low-latency voice/video, including as real-time infrastructure powering AI voice agents.
