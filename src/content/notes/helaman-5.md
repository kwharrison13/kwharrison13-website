# Helaman 5

Helaman 5 is one of the most-cited chapters in Kyle's Book of Mormon study, and the title of the week it anchors — "The Rock of Our Redeemer" — comes from it. The central image is Helaman counseling his sons Nephi and Lehi to "build upon the rock of our Redeemer, who is Christ, the Son of God, … a foundation whereon if men build they cannot fall" (Helaman 5:12), the way any of us stay strong in the "mighty storm" the devil sends to beat upon us. Kyle's August 17–23, 2020. Helaman 1–6: "The Rock of Our Redeemer" study pulls three threads from it: the call to remember your [Names](/notes/names) and the works of your forebears (5:4–12); the still, small "voice of perfect mildness" that pierces the cloud of darkness — "power and volume are not the same thing" (5:29–30); and "do-your-best parenting," where Aminadab, a less-active member, still holds "a vault of truth," so the teaching remains even when children go astray (5:35–41).

The chapter recurs as a cross-reference elsewhere: the Anti-Christ — Topical Study cites 5:12 ("upon the rock of redeemer is our foundation"), the December 14–20, 2020. Moroni 10: "Come unto Christ, and Be Perfected in Him" study uses 5:29–30 for the still-small-voice spirit of revelation, and the Spiritual Gifts note cites 5:18 — the Spirit "will tell us what to say."

**Context:** Helaman 5 is a chapter in the Book of Mormon recounting Nephi and Lehi's missionary work, their imprisonment, and a dramatic deliverance in which a cloud of darkness and a still voice lead to the conversion of their captors — a passage Latter-day Saints frequently cite for the "rock of the Redeemer" foundation metaphor and the "still small voice" of revelation.
