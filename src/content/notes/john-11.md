# John 11

A New Testament chapter (the raising of Lazarus) that Kyle's gospel study cites for two distinct points. In Original 12 Apostles, it is the proof-text that rehabilitates the apostle Thomas: though everyone remembers him as "Doubting Thomas," John 11:16 records him saying of Christ, "Let us also go that we may die with him" — the study uses this as a lesson against defining a person by their worst moment rather than their best, paralleled to marriage ("an eternity with someone at their best is worth a few times of seeing them at their worst," per [S. Michael Wilcox](/notes/s-michael-wilcox)).

In Prayer — Topical Study, John 11:41 appears in the catalog of *how* to pray — specifically as the model for praying with a grateful heart, alongside praying humbly, with feeling, with faith, and joined with fasting.

**Context:** John 11 in the Gospel of John narrates Jesus raising Lazarus of Bethany from the dead, four days after his death — one of the climactic signs in John's Gospel. It contains the verse "Jesus wept" (11:35) and Jesus's declaration "I am the resurrection, and the life" (11:25).

## Where this appears

- Original 12 Apostles — Thomas's "Let us also go that we may die with him" (John 11:16) cited to recover his faith and loyalty, against the "Doubting Thomas" caricature.
- Prayer — Topical Study — John 11:41 listed as the scriptural model for praying with a grateful heart.
