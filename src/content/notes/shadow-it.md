# Shadow IT

In [Product Led Growth — The End User Era](/books/product-led-growth-the-end-user-era), "Shadow IT" is the negative-framed name for the same power shift that [Consumerization of the Enterprise](/notes/consumerization-of-the-enterprise) frames positively: *"Power is shifting away from executive buyers and we're seeing the rise of the individual user. You've heard this trend called a number of things, both positive (Consumerization of the Enterprise) and negative ('Shadow IT')."* The term reflects the IT-department's anxiety about employees adopting their own tools bottom-up, outside official procurement — but the book treats that same individual-user adoption as the engine of [Product Led Growth (PLG)](/notes/product-led-growth-plg) rather than a threat to be controlled.

**Context:** "Shadow IT" is the standard enterprise-IT term for software, devices, and SaaS that employees use without explicit approval from the IT department — historically viewed as a security and governance risk, and reframed by the PLG movement as evidence of genuine end-user demand.

## Where this appears

- [Product Led Growth — The End User Era](/books/product-led-growth-the-end-user-era) — the pejorative label for individual-user-led adoption, paired against [Consumerization of the Enterprise](/notes/consumerization-of-the-enterprise) as two names for one power shift.
