# Wrapper Companies

**One-line definition:** *Businesses that are a marketing/distribution layer on top of someone else's underlying infrastructure — the structure of [MEDVI](/notes/medvi) (a marketing layer on rented [Telehealth](/notes/telehealth)).*

## How sources describe it

- "[MEDVI](/notes/medvi) is, functionally, a marketing layer on top of rented telehealth infrastructure." Kyle notes this isn't inherently an insult — "we've all matured past the point of criticizing 'wrapper companies'"; many businesses are marketing layers and "[Distribution](/notes/distribution) is all that matters" is a common claim. (source: The $1B Rorschach Test - Research)
- The Shopify-dropshipper analogy ([Sam Altman](/notes/sam-altman)): calling MEDVI an AI/manufacturing company because it has a nice storefront misses that "the storefront isn't the business. The supply chain is the business" — and the supply chain is 100% human and regulated. (source: The $1B Rorschach Test - Research)
- The critique lands not on the wrapper structure itself but on (a) calling it an AI story and (b) the [Fraud](/notes/fraud) / lack of guardrails underneath. (source: The $1B Rorschach Test - Research)
