# Judith Rich Harris

A psychology writer who appears in Kyle's wiki as the author of the anti-library entry [No Two Alike](/notes/no-two-alike) and as a figure [Charlie Munger](/notes/charlie-munger) credits — in [Charlie Munger — The Psychology of Human Misjudgment](/notes/charlie-munger-the-psychology-of-human-misjudgment) — with demonstrating that young people's "superrespect" for their peers (rather than parents) drives much of adolescent behavior, tied to [Social-Proof Tendency](/notes/social-proof-tendency).

**Context:** Judith Rich Harris (1938–2018) was an independent American psychology researcher and author best known for *The Nurture Assumption*, which argued that peer groups, not parents, are the dominant environmental influence on a child's development.

## Where this appears

- [No Two Alike](/notes/no-two-alike) — listed as author.
- [Charlie Munger — The Psychology of Human Misjudgment](/notes/charlie-munger-the-psychology-of-human-misjudgment) — cited by Munger for her work on peer influence and [Social-Proof Tendency](/notes/social-proof-tendency).
