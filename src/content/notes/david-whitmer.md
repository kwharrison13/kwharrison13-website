# David Whitmer

One of the Three Witnesses to the Book of Mormon, who appears across Kyle's early-Mormonism sources both as a testimony-bearer and as an internal critic. In [An Insider's View of Mormon Origins](/books/an-insider-s-view-of-mormon-origins), Grant Palmer weighs Whitmer's witness against the fabrication thesis; the page records that Whitmer told an RLDS member in 1882, "These hands handled the plates, these eyes saw the angel, and these ears heard his voice" — a testimony he maintained even after leaving the church.

In Early Mormonism and [The Life of John Taylor](/books/the-life-of-john-taylor), Whitmer is invoked for a more self-critical claim: that the Saints were "largely to blame for being run out of Jackson [County]" for declaring the land consecrated by God for the New Jerusalem and talking about taking over the county. Kyle's marginal note in the John Taylor biography flags exactly this — "Find the David Whitmer quote about the saints being at fault for talking about taking over the county and being given the land by God" — using Whitmer's hindsight as a counterweight to a purely persecution-framed account of the Missouri expulsions, since even revelation (D&C 103) directed the Saints to act within the law.

**Context:** David Whitmer (1805–1888) was one of the Three Witnesses whose joint testimony appears in every edition of the Book of Mormon. He left the Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-day Saints in 1838 but never recanted his witness, later publishing *An Address to All Believers in Christ* (1887).
