# Henry Kissinger

Henry Kissinger surfaces in Kyle's reading as a touchstone in two distinct registers. In the [John Quincy Adams](/books/john-quincy-adams) biography (by James Traub), Kissinger is cited as the modern echo of Adams's [Realism](/notes/realism) — Traub names Adams the "fountainhead" of realist foreign policy, skeptical of America's capacity to do good abroad, and quotes Kissinger's observation "that the American people will not accept a 'realist' president." The book frames Adams's warning against reckless adventure abroad as having "important adherents" and offering "more to the American people today than it ever has."

In [Turnaround](/books/turnaround), [Mitt Romney](/notes/mitt-romney)'s account of his turnaround formula, Kissinger appears in a wholly different vein — quoted on leadership and heroism: "Heroes walk alone, but they become myths when they ennoble the lives and touch the hearts of all of us."

**Context:** Henry Kissinger (1923–2023) was a German-American diplomat and scholar who served as U.S. National Security Advisor and Secretary of State under Presidents Nixon and Ford, and a leading exponent of realist (realpolitik) foreign policy.
