# Solopreneurs

In [Our Towns](/books/our-towns), Kyle ties "solopreneurs" to the [Maker Movement](/notes/maker-movement) thread — the rise of small, close-to-the-customer, fast-turnaround manufacturing enabled by 3-D printers, laser cutters, and low-cost multi-axis machine tools. The book describes microfactories pursuing "low-volume manufacturing" ("What can we make that could be successful in units of ten?"), combining cloud-centric techniques (crowdsourcing, crowdfunding, online collaboration, open-source design) with new production hardware to let people in "smaller, less formal, much lower-cost workspaces" design, test, and manufacture items that previously required full production lines. Kyle's note on that passage links the maker movement directly to solopreneurs and [Entity Ideas](/notes/entity-ideas) — reading it as an enabler for individual and very-small-team builders.
