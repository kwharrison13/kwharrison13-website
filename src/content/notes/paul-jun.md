# Paul Jun

A contributor to Kyle's The Talent Vortex - Research, drawing on his time at [Dropbox](/notes/dropbox). His account of why Dropbox concentrated talent early combines three factors: (a) strong founder/exec focus on talent — the Day-1 CEO talk was about hiring, and a cofounder sat on every interview panel until 300+ employees; (b) differentiated initial traction and branding — "most valuable startup," with a who's-who of investors (Sequoia, Benchmark, Accel, Index); and (c) a business model strong enough to support aggressive talent investment (>$1M revenue/employee until 500+, >10x LTV/CAC). His framing supports the research's thesis that talent concentration is downstream of focus, signaling, and economics — not luck.
