# FanCoin

The crypto-native creator-economy startup founded by [Bradley Miles](/notes/bradley-miles) (author of [Breaking into VC](/notes/breaking-into-vc) and former [CoinDesk](/notes/coindesk) research lead). It grew out of stealth work Miles began as a [Techstars](/notes/techstars) EIR — initially framed as a Goldman-Sachs-style bank advising businesses on blockchain protocols (ICOs, security, game theory, mechanism design) — which converged on the "engagement economy": the relationship between creators/artists and their fans (source: [Bradley Miles](/notes/bradley-miles), May 2018 conversation).

The thesis: fans crave access that artists find hard to monetize, so a network where creators issue their own fan currencies lets an artist "be the network and unlock the value of their own engagement" rather than using Facebook to engage fans for free. After cycling through coin designs, the play settled on an underlying base currency that can issue creator-based currencies plus provide an exchange, launching with a first cohort of 3–5 creators ([Crypto](/notes/crypto), [Creator Economy](/notes/creator-economy)). FanCoin reflects Miles's recurring instinct for "turning complicated things — VC, crypto — into something understandable" (source: [Bradley Miles](/notes/bradley-miles)).
