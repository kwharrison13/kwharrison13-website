# InVision

InVision recurs in Kyle's diligence notes as one of the design-tooling incumbents that [Figma](/notes/figma) displaced — the load-bearing example of the "collaboration-first disruption" thesis. In the Sprig fka UserLeap - Diligence, [Joseph Ambeault](/notes/joseph-ambeault) argues that Figma "blew up Sketch, [Invision](/notes/invision), and eventually [Adobe](/notes/adobe) (worth $10B+) by putting collaboration first but connecting it tightly to the creation workflow." The same expert call cites InVision again in the product-development-lifecycle pain mapping, where design tooling is described as "constantly changing (Sketch → [Invision](/notes/invision) → [Figma](/notes/figma))" — InVision sits in the middle of that lineage as a now-superseded step. Ambeault, a former VP of Product at [Codecademy](/notes/codecademy) with 25 years in product, lists InVision among the design/prototyping tools he can speak to firsthand.

**Context:** InVision was a digital product-design and prototyping platform, founded in 2011, known for clickable prototypes and design collaboration; it lost ground to Figma's real-time browser-based editor and wound down its core product in 2024.
