# Jack Raines

Jack Raines is a writer whose work runs through [Young Money](/notes/young-money), the publication of two pieces Kyle saved. In [Infinite Games](/notes/infinite-games), Raines applies [James Carse](/notes/james-carse)'s finite-vs-infinite-games framework to how we live — arguing that optimizing for achievements (finite play) is a trap, since "the more we are recognized as winners, the more we know ourselves to be losers," and prescribing instead that you "spend your 4,680 weeks in pursuit of the things that you actually enjoy pursuing." In "Ask Good Questions" (research for the essay [Embrace The Nuance](/essays/embrace-the-nuance)), Raines argues that as the internet and generative AI drive the cost of finding and creating information to zero, the antidote to information overload is distillation downstream of interrogation: "if you ask the right questions, the information filters itself."

**Context:** Jack Raines is the author of the Young Money newsletter, a personal-finance and life-philosophy blog that grew a large following; he has also written for institutional finance outlets.
