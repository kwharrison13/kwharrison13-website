# Data Labeling

In Kyle's notes, data labeling (or data tagging) appears as [Mark Cuban](/notes/mark-cuban)'s answer to AI-driven labor disruption. Cuban argues that as AI and machine learning displace low-skilled work, a rising demand for tagging and labeling data can absorb much of that displaced labor — he calls it "the 21st century version of an assembly line." It is the first half of his two-part fix for automation's fallout (the second being a broad expansion of AmeriCorps-style national-service programs), and notably his alternative to [UBI](/notes/universal-basic-income).

**Context:** Data labeling is the human annotation of raw data (images, text, audio) into the structured, tagged examples that supervised machine-learning models train on — historically a large source of contract and gig labor in the AI supply chain.

## Where this appears

- [Mark Cuban](/notes/mark-cuban) — frames data tagging/labeling as the assembly-line-style job category that can absorb workers displaced by automation.
