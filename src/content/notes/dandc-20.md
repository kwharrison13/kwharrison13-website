# D&C 20

Doctrine and Covenants section 20 — often called the "Articles and Covenants" of the Church. Kyle's gospel-study notes draw on several distinct verses rather than the section as a whole. The cluster he returns to most is **verses 30–33** on sanctification and justification: "sanctification is just and true" and the warning that one may "fall from grace" (Christ Defines His Gospel cites 20:32–33; Humility — Topical Study pairs 20:30–31 with [Mosiah 4](/notes/mosiah-4) on what is "just and true"). Miscellaneous Scriptures reads 20:31 as **sanctification = repentance for the forgiveness of sins**.

Two other verses recur. **Verse 37** — baptism for those who "come forth with broken hearts and contrite spirits" and "witness before the church that they have truly repented" — is cited in A Mighty Change of Heart — Ezra Taft Benson (the baptismal covenant) and, in Things To Know Before Your Mission, as the basis for [James E. Faust](/notes/james-e-faust)'s charge that "your testimony is your strongest arrow." Scripture Study — Topical Study uses 20:31 for "sanctify yourselves through prayer and fasting," and Miscellaneous Scriptures also draws on 20:8–12 (Joseph Smith given power as a witness of the Book of Mormon).

## Where this appears

- Christ Defines His Gospel — 20:32–33 on falling from grace.
- Humility — Topical Study — 20:30–31, sanctification as "just and true" (with Mosiah 4).
- Scripture Study — Topical Study — 20:31, sanctify yourselves through prayer and fasting.
- Things To Know Before Your Mission — 20:37, "your testimony is your strongest arrow."
- Miscellaneous Scriptures — 20:31 (sanctification = repentance) and 20:8–12 (Joseph Smith's witness).
- A Mighty Change of Heart — Ezra Taft Benson — 20:37, baptism for the broken-hearted and truly repentant.
