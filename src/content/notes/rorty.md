# Rorty

Rorty appears in [The Notetaking Cold War](/notes/the-notetaking-cold-war) as one of the philosophers grouped under the [Pragmatists](/notes/pragmatists) label — alongside William James and [Wittgenstein](/notes/wittgenstein) — used to anchor the pragmatist side of the essay's note-organization-as-philosophy framing.

**Context:** Richard Rorty (1931–2007) was an American philosopher and a leading figure of neopragmatism, best known for *Philosophy and the Mirror of Nature*, who argued against the idea that knowledge "mirrors" a fixed external reality.

## Where this appears

- [The Notetaking Cold War](/notes/the-notetaking-cold-war) — cited as an exemplar pragmatist thinker, illustrating the view that categories are useful tools rather than reflections of a fixed essence.
