# Lollapalooza Effect

The lollapalooza effect is [Charlie Munger](/notes/charlie-munger)'s term for the way **really big effects come only from large combinations of factors acting in the same direction.** The canonical write-up lives at [Lollapalooza](/notes/lollapalooza) — this page is the longer-name variant for the same idea, kept so wikilinks under either form resolve.

In the wiki it surfaces in [Charlie Munger — Harvard Law School Fiftieth Reunion Address](/notes/charlie-munger-harvard-law-school-fiftieth-reunion-address), where Munger lists it among his "ultra-simple, general problem-solving notions": after deciding the big no-brainer questions, applying numerical fluency, inverting the problem, and thinking in a [multidisciplinary](/notes/multidisciplinary-thinking) way, the final notion is to "watch for combinations of factors — the Lollapalooza effect." The point is that the biggest outcomes are typically multi-causal, so single-discipline thinking systematically under-predicts them.

**Context:** Munger coined "lollapalooza effect" to describe the confluence of multiple psychological tendencies or causal factors producing outsized, often nonlinear results — a signature idea of his mental-models framework collected in *Poor Charlie's Almanack*.
