# 1 Chronicles 5

In Kyle's Abrahamic Covenant gospel-study notes, 1 Chronicles 5 (specifically 5:1–2) is cited as the scriptural anchor for how the birthright was redistributed within the house of Israel. The note reads the passage as teaching that the birthright — the double portion of inheritance and the patriarchal status of the family — was lost by the unrighteous firstborn and passed instead to [Ephraim](/notes/ephraim) (the younger son of Joseph), while Judah became the chief ruler. Kyle's outline summarizes it as "Judah prevails above the others, 11.5 tribes are lost and Judah becomes the chief ruler ([1 Chronicles 5](/notes/1-chronicles-5):1–2)," paired with Genesis 48:17–20 on the transfer of the birthright to Ephraim.

It appears in the study's running scripture chain alongside Genesis 48, [1 Kings 12](/notes/1-kings-12), [Isaiah 51](/notes/isaiah-51), [Ezekiel 37](/notes/ezekiel-37), and [Daniel 2](/notes/daniel-2) as part of tracing the covenant lineage from the birthright through Israel's division and scattering.

**Context:** 1 Chronicles 5 is an Old Testament chapter giving the genealogies of the trans-Jordan tribes (Reuben, Gad, half of Manasseh); verses 1–2 note that Reuben, the firstborn, forfeited the birthright (which went to the sons of Joseph) while the chief ruler came from Judah.

## Where this appears
