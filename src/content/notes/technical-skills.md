# Technical Skills

In the August 18th, 2021 — Valued Empyrean (Conor White-Sullivan & Darwin Harrison) conversation, "Technical Skills" is one of the things people need to "succeed" and "create Value" — paired throughout with [Character Skills](/notes/character-skills) (and, more broadly, with a Holistic vs. Siloed Mindset). The notes break technical skills into individual productivity ([Self-Reflection](/notes/self-reflection) / self-assessment), integration, project management, and interdependent skills. The anchoring resource is [Douglas Engelbart](/notes/douglas-engelbart) and "The Engelbart Hypothesis" — the model that continuous improvement requires Group A (direct work), Group B (improvement groups leveling people up), and Group C (improving the improvement processes), plus "bootstrapping."

The page's load-bearing observation, attributed to [Darwin Harrison](/notes/darwin-harrison), is that technical skills and character skills are both prerequisites for action: he spent 15 years working with people who had a "Holistic Viewpoint but not a Holistic Mindset," and "when it came to action they weren't capable of doing certain things because they didn't have a clear understanding of what was required… They lacked Character Skills and Technical Skills." In the Valued Empyrean framing, technical skills are the "can-do" half of capability, distinct from the "will-do" / disposition half.
