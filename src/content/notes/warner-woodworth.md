# Warner Woodworth

Warner P. Woodworth is a professor emeritus of Organizational Leadership and Strategy in BYU's Marriott School and a longtime advocate of [microcredit](/notes/microfinance) as a tool for fighting [poverty](/notes/poverty). He graduated from South High School in Salt Lake City (1960), holds bachelor's and master's degrees from BYU and a master's and PhD from the University of Michigan, and founded the *Journal of Microfinance*. He helped found the NGOs **Enterprise Mentors International**, **Unitus**, and **HELP International**, and co-authored *[Working Toward Zion](/books/working-toward-zion)* (with James W. Lucas).

Woodworth's through-line is that microfinance is "a hand up, not a hand out" — a present-tense, practical way for Latter-day Saints to live the [law of consecration](/notes/law-of-consecration) and build [Zion](/notes/zion) by empowering the poorest of the poor. He collaborated with [Muhammad Yunus](/notes/muhammad-yunus) and brought him to BYU (the first Muslim to receive an honorary doctorate there), and he seeds student- and professional-led ventures — from the hurricane-Mitch students who founded HELP International to LDS "microfinance action groups."
