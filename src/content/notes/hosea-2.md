# Hosea 2

A chapter from the Old Testament book of Hosea that Kyle uses, in his Born Again gospel study, as a centerpiece for the covenant-as-marriage theme. The Lord's people are figured as an adulterous wife — "I will go after my lovers, that give me my bread and my water… put away her whoredoms" (2:2, 5). When the bride strays, the Lord declares "I will hedge up thy way with thorns and make a wall" (2:6), which Kyle reads alongside [Mosiah 7](/notes/mosiah-7):29 ("I will not succor my people in the day of their transgression") as the consequence of serving other masters. But the chapter turns toward mercy: "I will allure her, and bring her into the wilderness, and speak comfortably unto her" (2:14).

Kyle's takeaway, written as a **Never Forget**, is that the Lord will never break the covenant — "only we can do that" — and that He never gives up on us, always inviting us back into the fold. He places Hosea 2 inside a wider cluster of covenant-bride imagery: the spreading of the skirt as covenant-making in [Ruth 3](/notes/ruth-3):9 and [Ezekiel 16](/notes/ezekiel-16):8, garments as the "coat of skins" that covers the natural man's nakedness where fig-leaves fail, and the [Spencer W. Kimball](/notes/spencer-w-kimball) "two-part rebirth" frame of being born again as a child of Christ.
