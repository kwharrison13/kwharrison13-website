# David Deutsch

David Deutsch is the physicist-philosopher whose case for optimism Kyle threads through two of his essays. In It's Time To Build... But Build What - Research, Deutsch supplies the closing line of [Marc Andreessen](/notes/marc-andreessen)'s Techno-Optimist Manifesto: "We have a duty to be optimistic. Because the future is open, not predetermined and therefore cannot just be accepted: we are all responsible for what it holds. Thus it is our duty to fight for a better world." He anchors the e/acc / [Techno-Optimism](/notes/techno-optimism) section of that research as the moral grounding for progress-positivity.

In What's In a Post-Mortem - Research, Deutsch returns via The Beginning of Infinity and his **Principle of Optimism** — "all evils are caused by insufficient knowledge" — which Kyle, by way of [Packy McCormick](/notes/packy-mccormick)'s "Optimism" piece, uses to distinguish real optimism from "happy ears." Deutsch's framing is that optimism is "a way of explaining failure, not prophesying success," and that "there is only one way of making progress: conjecture and criticism." That conjecture-and-criticism loop maps directly onto the post-mortem discipline the essay argues for.

**Context:** David Deutsch is a British physicist at Oxford, a pioneer of quantum computation, and the author of *The Fabric of Reality* and *The Beginning of Infinity*. His philosophy of knowledge — drawing on Karl Popper — holds that problems are soluble and that progress comes from creating good explanations.
