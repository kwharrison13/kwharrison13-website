# Martin O'Malley

In [Shortest Way Home](/books/shortest-way-home), Pete Buttigieg credits O'Malley — as mayor of Baltimore — with the "CitiStat" model that "brought modern performance management to Baltimore" and "became a template for data-driven local government everywhere." Buttigieg explicitly modeled his own South Bend "SBStat" process on it: a sequence of intensive meetings where city staff bring rigorous analysis to vet new ideas and surface issues. O'Malley is thus the originating reference point for the book's data-driven-government theme, which the wiki links to [Data in Investing](/notes/data-in-investing) alongside the book's cautions about data's limits and "data-obsessed" government that never turns analysis into action.

**Context:** Martin O'Malley is an American politician who served as mayor of Baltimore and governor of Maryland; his CitiStat program built on the NYPD's CompStat to apply performance metrics to city services.
