# Creation

The first of the three pillars of the [Plan of Salvation](/notes/plan-of-salvation) — [Creation](/notes/creation), [the Fall](/notes/the-fall-of-adam-of-eve), and [Atonement](/notes/atonement). In Kyle's Plan of Salvation — Topical Study, Creation answers the question "What is the purpose of the earth?" and frames the two commandments tied to the two estates: to multiply and replenish the earth, and not to eat the fruit of the tree of knowledge. The study reads Lehi's [Tree of Life](/notes/tree-of-life) vision as a map of creation itself — we are "invited into creation by a 'man in a white robe'" ([1 Nephi 8](/notes/1-nephi-8):5) who invites us to "follow Him" — and asks the pointed question of whether "Adam and Eve [were] created to fail."

In [The Faith of a Scientist](/books/the-faith-of-a-scientist), Creation is approached from the other direction — through natural theology and the reconciliation of scientific and scriptural accounts. The book offers the "watch-found-running" and improbability-of-a-hot-sun arguments for a Creator, and treats the scriptural "six creative periods followed by a period of rest" as compatible with deep geological time: the King James "days" of creation admit interpretations ranging from 24-hour days to thousand-year periods to "creative periods" of unspecified length, with "a study of the earth itself" giving added meaning to the brief scriptural accounts. Man is framed as "in the image of God... set apart from the rest of creation."

**Context:** In Latter-day Saint theology, the Creation, the Fall, and the Atonement are the three foundational events ("the three pillars of eternity") of the plan of salvation.

## Where this appears

- Plan of Salvation — Topical Study — Creation as one of the three pillars; the purpose of the earth, the two estates' commandments, and Lehi's Tree of Life vision
- [The Faith of a Scientist](/books/the-faith-of-a-scientist) — Creation through natural theology: arguments for a Creator and the reconciliation of scriptural "creative periods" with the age of the earth
- Genesis 1 — the six days of Creation: God creating heaven and earth by his word and making man in his own image
