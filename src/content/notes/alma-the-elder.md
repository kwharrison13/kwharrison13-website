# Alma the Elder

Book of Mormon prophet whom Kyle cites for one specific episode: in [Mosiah 26](/notes/mosiah-26):13–32, Alma the Elder receives counsel from God about how to govern the Church he founded. The detail Kyle pulls out is that the word "my" appears 18 times in that passage — "blessed is this people who are willing to bear my name… and they are mine" (v. 18) — which he reads as the reminder that *it is God's work*, not the leader's.

That reading is the heart of his [What I Want My Son to Know before He Leaves on His Mission](/notes/what-i-want-my-son-to-know-before-he-leaves-on-his-mission) notes, where Alma the Elder and [Ammon](/notes/ammon) are held up as Book of Mormon exemplars of the same lesson ("I will be thy servant"; the work belongs to God). The Mosiah 26 episode also surfaces in his Come Follow Me weeks June 15–21, 2020. Alma 13–16: "Enter into the Rest of the Lord" and June 29–July 5, 2020. Alma 23–29: "They Never Did Fall Away", where Alma the Elder is listed among the people referenced. He is distinct from his son [Alma the Younger](/notes/alma-the-younger), the high priest of the surrounding Alma chapters.

**Context:** Alma the Elder is a prophet in the Book of Mormon (Latter-day Saint scripture). A former priest of King Noah converted by the prophet Abinadi, he established and led the Church among the Nephites and was the father of Alma the Younger.
