# 1 Corinthians 7

Paul's chapter on marriage and the believing/unbelieving spouse. In Kyle's Abrahamic Covenant study it is cited (7:12–13) alongside a [Wilford Woodruff](/notes/wilford-woodruff) paraphrase about working on one's own lineage and inheritance: the believing spouse may "not be safe in your inheritance if your spouse is unbelieving," but should pray for the family — linked in turn to [D&C 128](/notes/dandc-128)'s "we without them cannot be made perfect."

**Context:** 1 Corinthians 7 is the New Testament chapter where the Apostle Paul gives counsel on marriage, singleness, and the situation of a believer married to an unbelieving spouse — advising (7:12–14) that the believer remain with the unbeliever, who may be sanctified through the marriage.

## Where this appears

- Abrahamic Covenant — cited (7:12–13) on the believing spouse, inheritance, and praying for an unbelieving family member.
