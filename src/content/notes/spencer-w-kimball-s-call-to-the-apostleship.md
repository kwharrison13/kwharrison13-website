# Spencer W. Kimball’s Call to the Apostleship

This is a touchstone Kyle invokes — not a passage from the book itself but a parallel he draws in the margins of [Gordon B. Hinckley — Go Forward With Faith](/books/gordon-b-hinckley-go-forward-with-faith). Reading about [Gordon B. Hinckley](/notes/gordon-b-hinckley) trying "desperately to avoid thinking about becoming President of the Church" — telling himself "you are an old man, and anything can happen" — Kyle annotates with the theme "When the Church calls, we obey" and the famous account of overwhelming inadequacy on receiving a sacred call: "the predominant thought was my own limitations and incapacities and weaknesses and I was overcome… I wept and wept… in convulsions of sobbing," with a spouse stroking the called man's hair to quiet him.

The note links that reaction to [Spencer W. Kimball](/notes/spencer-w-kimball)'s call to the apostleship — the well-known story of Kimball's tearful, sleepless distress at feeling unworthy of the office. Kyle uses it as a cross-reference for the recurring LDS pattern of profound humility and felt inadequacy when a leader is called to high responsibility, mapping Hinckley's reticence about the presidency onto Kimball's earlier experience.

**Context:** Spencer W. Kimball (1895–1985) was called to the Quorum of the Twelve Apostles in 1943 and later served as the twelfth President of the Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-day Saints; accounts of his call describe an extended period of feeling overwhelmed and unworthy.
