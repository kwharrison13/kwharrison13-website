# Walkable

In [The New Urban Crisis](/books/the-new-urban-crisis), walkability is one of the "smaller things" that make cities great places to live and work in the new people-driven, place-based economy. The book argues that enduring urban success turns less on a competitive *business* climate and more on a great *people* climate — "making sure there were walkable, pedestrian-friendly streets, bike lanes, parks, exciting art and music scenes, and vibrant areas where people could gather in cafes and restaurants." Walkability appears in Kyle's [City Building](/notes/city-building) concept cluster for the book, alongside ideas like micro cities, the new city, and remote work — pedestrian-scale design as a precondition for the kind of dense, livable urban fabric that attracts individuals and families of all types.

**Context:** "Walkable" describes neighborhoods and streets designed so that daily destinations are reachable comfortably on foot — short blocks, mixed uses, pedestrian-priority streets. In urban planning it is a widely used proxy for livability, health, and economic vibrancy.
