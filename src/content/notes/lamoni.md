# Lamoni

Lamoni is the Lamanite king at the center of Kyle's Come Follow Me study of Alma 17–22 — the convert whose household Ammon's missionary service opens up. The narrative hinge of the whole study is Ammon's refusal of Lamoni's offered daughter ("Nay, but I will be thy servant"), choosing instead to watch the king's flocks. That service, plus the defense of the flocks at the waters of Sebus, "wins the hearts" of the king and his servants where argument could not — the case study Kyle uses for "service IS missionary work."

In Kyle's reading, Lamoni is also the model of [Real Intent](/notes/real-intent) and "innocent faith": he begins without even the vocabulary of belief ("I do not know what that meaneth"), yet is willing to act on any answer he receives, moving from offering "half my kingdom" to "all that I possess" as his mind is opened to eternity. Lamoni serves as the foil to his father, the over-king, whose anger at Lamoni's conversion (and near-slaying of his own son) Ammon interrupts — a parallel Kyle draws to converts whose families resist their joining the Church. Ammon's patient, question-led approach with Lamoni is contrasted throughout with [Aaron](/notes/aaron)'s direct call to repentance, the study's "two approaches, not good vs. bad teacher" thread.

**Context:** In the Book of Mormon (Alma 17–22), Lamoni is a Lamanite king in the land of Ishmael, a descendant of Ishmael, who is converted through the missionary Ammon, one of the sons of Mosiah.
