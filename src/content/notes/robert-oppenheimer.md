# Robert Oppenheimer

In [Nuclear War — a Scenario](/books/nuclear-war-a-scenario), Robert Oppenheimer is quoted for his image of the U.S.–Soviet arms race as "two scorpions in a bottle, each capable of killing the other, but only at the risk of his own life." The line distills the book's logic of mutual annihilation: two superpowers locked in a confined space where any decisive strike is also self-destructive. He serves as a foundational voice in the nuclear origin story the book builds on.

**Context:** J. Robert Oppenheimer (1904–1967) was the American physicist who directed the Los Alamos Laboratory during the Manhattan Project, earning the label "father of the atomic bomb." His later warnings about the arms race, and his 1954 security-clearance revocation, made him an enduring symbol of the scientist confronting the consequences of his own work.
