# Todd McKinnon

Co-founder and CEO of [Okta ($OKTA)](/notes/okta), and the central subject of [Todd McKinnon — Creating and Defining a New Market Category](/notes/todd-mckinnon-creating-and-defining-a-new-market-category) — Patrick O'Shaughnessy's *Invest Like the Best* interview that Kyle annotated heavily. McKinnon ran engineering at [Salesforce](/notes/salesforce) before leaving in 2008 (into the financial crisis) on the thesis that the whole stack — apps, collaboration, infrastructure — was moving to the cloud, and that the shift would create demand for new tools. Okta began as a cloud-app monitoring idea ("SaaSure") but pivoted early to identity management when IT buyers kept surfacing one acute pain: they couldn't log in to their new cloud apps.

The interview is Kyle's reference text for several company-building themes: threading the timing needle (building before the market obviously needs it, but not so early it "falls on a world that doesn't need it"), enduring the doubt required to *create* a category and become the default ("cognitive referent," like Kleenex or Stripe), and the second act — customer identity (CIAM) — emerging from customers (Allergan's doctors, MLB.com) rather than a plan, eventually ~24% of revenue. McKinnon's early mistake was importing Salesforce's small-company go-to-market wholesale; correcting it via first-principles thinking ("the more users you have, the bigger the problem is," so sell to the biggest customer you can) is a clean case study Kyle flags. He also articulates leadership as clarity plus admitting you don't have the answers, and the software economics of high gross margins + big TAM rewarding growth.

**Context:** Todd McKinnon is the co-founder and CEO of Okta, the publicly traded (Nasdaq: OKTA) enterprise identity and access management company he started in 2009 after running engineering at Salesforce.
