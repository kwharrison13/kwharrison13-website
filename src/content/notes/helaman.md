# Helaman

A figure from the Book of Mormon who appears across two of Kyle's Come Follow Me study weeks. In August 10–16, 2020. Alma 53–63: "Preserved by His Marvelous Power", Helaman leads the 2,000 stripling warriors — the company of young men who "never did fall away" — and Kyle treats him as the human anchor for the war narrative's lessons on who you surround yourself with ("we are the average of the five people we spend the most time with," per [Jim Rohn](/notes/jim-rohn)), on [But If Not...](/notes/but-if-not) faith that is not contingent on the outcome, and on [Faith](/notes/faith) unto Obedience. It is to Helaman that the warriors testify, "we do not doubt our mothers knew it." The chapters describe how Helaman's persuasion kept the stripling warriors from breaking their oath (Alma 53:14).

In July 20–26, 2020. Alma 36–38: "Look to God and Live", Helaman appears earlier in his life as the son to whom Alma gives his farewell counsel — the recipient of the great chiasmus of Alma 36, structured around Alma's own conversion, and of the charge that "by small and simple things are great things brought to pass" (Alma 37:6-7). Alma entrusts Helaman with the sacred records and reminds him of the [Central Theme of the Book of Mormon](/notes/central-theme-of-the-book-of-mormon): "inasmuch as ye shall keep the commandments of God ye shall prosper in the land."

**Context:** Helaman (son of Alma the Younger) is a Nephite prophet and record-keeper in the Book of Mormon, best known for commanding the 2,000 young Lamanite-convert soldiers ("stripling warriors") during the wars recounted in Alma 53–63.

## Where this appears

- August 10–16, 2020. Alma 53–63: "Preserved by His Marvelous Power" — Helaman leads the 2,000 stripling warriors; the focus of lessons on faith, obedience, and the company you keep.
- July 20–26, 2020. Alma 36–38: "Look to God and Live" — Helaman is the son receiving Alma's farewell counsel and the chiastic conversion account of Alma 36.
