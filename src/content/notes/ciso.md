# CISO

In Kyle's security-tooling diligence, the CISO is the buyer persona whose adoption signals product-market fit. The [Wiz.io](/notes/wiz-io) pre-brief from [Shardul Shah](/notes/shardul-shah) uses early CISO traction as the central evidence of momentum: a [Jim Routh](/notes/jim-routh) (former [MassMutual](/notes/massmutual)) $1.3M three-year deal called "exceptional" for a CISO of his stature, an [Andrew Stanley](/notes/andrew-stanley) $500K ACV deal closed in under six weeks, a [Steve Ward](/notes/steve-ward) / [Home Depot](/notes/home-depot) 500K+ deal in discussion, and the [Cisco](/notes/cisco) CISO calling it "the best vendor pitch he has heard in 2 years," plus Barclays and Datadog/Iterable security leaders evaluating. The pattern in the notes: in cybersecurity sales, the named CISO reference is the unit of credibility — who bought, how fast, and how senior.

**Context:** A Chief Information Security Officer (CISO) is the senior executive accountable for an organization's information and cybersecurity strategy. For security vendors, the CISO is the economic buyer, so CISO references and reference-driven enterprise deals are the standard proxy for traction.
