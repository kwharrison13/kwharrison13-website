# Innovation

Across Kyle's reading, "innovation" is treated less as invention than as the full chain from idea to a thing that ships and sells. [The Idea Factory](/books/the-idea-factory) supplies the sharpest definition via [Bell Labs](/notes/bell-labs)' [Jack Morton](/notes/jack-morton): "if you haven't sold anything you haven't innovated" — the idea → discovery → invention → innovation sequence, where manufacture-and-sell is the load-bearing step. [What It Takes](/books/what-it-takes) echoes the commercial register from finance: [Stephen Schwarzman](/notes/stephen-schwarzman)'s "there are no patents in finance," innovation as the discipline of evolving and changing "before we are forced to."

A second thread frames innovation as a collaborative, multidisciplinary act rather than solo genius. [Leonardo Da Vinci](/books/leonardo-da-vinci) insists "innovation is a team sport. Creativity is a collaborative endeavor," with the Florence studio and the Last Supper as collective productions, and locates the source of innovation in [Multidisciplinary Thinking](/notes/multidisciplinary-thinking) — making connections across arts and sciences. [Range](/books/range) reinforces this with "serial innovators" and birds-vs-frogs polymaths who integrate across peripheral domains, and the finding that breadth (not R&D spend) seeds durable innovation — a PwC study found "no statistically significant relationship between R&D spending and performance" except at the bottom.

A third thread, from [The Kill Chain](/books/the-kill-chain), is institutional: the founder-led innovation era in U.S. defense ended with McNamara-style cost-cutting and "Last Supper" consolidation, because the Eisenhower-era patrons who protected risk-taking (the [Skunk Works](/books/skunk-works) model) are gone. The book treats innovation as requiring both military mavericks and civilian champions, plus protection for the risk they take.

**Context:** "Innovation" in the economics/management sense (after Schumpeter) is the successful commercialization of an invention — distinct from invention itself. The convergent message across these sources is that it depends on cross-domain breadth, collaboration, and institutional patronage of risk far more than on lone-genius insight or raw R&D budget.
