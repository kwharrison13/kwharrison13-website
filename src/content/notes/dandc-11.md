# D&C 11

A section of the Doctrine and Covenants that Kyle's scripture study mines repeatedly for a handful of recurring verses on revelation, the word, and divine power. The most-cited passage is **D&C 11:21** — "obtain my word, and then shall your tongue be loosed; then, if you desire, you shall have my Spirit and my word" — which appears across Miscellaneous Scriptures (his shorthand: "obtain, then declare, then desire the spirit"), Scripture Study — Topical Study, Revelation — Topical Study (paired with the idea that "no one can ever learn enough about the gospel"), and Virtue (the spirit "unto the convincing of men" follows submission to God's will). The verse functions in his notes as a sequence: seek the word first, and the power to teach and the Spirit follow.

Two other verse-clusters recur. **D&C 11:12–14** — "put your trust in that spirit which leadeth to do good… which shall enlighten your mind, which shall fill your soul with joy" — is Kyle's test for distinguishing true revelation, cited in Knowledge — Topical Study and used in Using Satan Against Satan as a discernment standard. And **D&C 11:30** — "as many as receive me, to them will I give power to become the sons of God" — anchors the covenant/Born Again theme (paired with [Moses 6](/notes/moses-6):68): we are endowed with the power to become children of God through covenants, and that section ties into priesthood power in Scripture Study — Topical Study.

**Context:** Doctrine and Covenants Section 11 is a revelation given through Joseph Smith in 1830 to his brother Hyrum Smith, urging him to "seek not to declare my word, but first seek to obtain my word."

## Where this appears

- Born Again — D&C 11:30 ("power to become the sons of God") in the covenant/rebirth cluster
- Knowledge — Topical Study — 11:12–13 as the "spirit which leadeth to do good," enlightening the mind
- Miscellaneous Scriptures — 11:21 as "obtain, then declare, then desire the spirit"
- Revelation — Topical Study — 11:21 on continuous learning to overcome challenges
- Scripture Study — Topical Study — 11:21 and 11:30 (priesthood power)
- Using Satan Against Satan — 11:12–14 as a discernment standard
- Virtue — 11:21, the Spirit "unto the convincing of men" following submission
