# Eric Drexler

In [The Man From The Future](/books/the-man-from-the-future), Eric Drexler is the American engineer credited with first articulating the vision of molecular manufacturing and popularizing the term "nanotechnology." The book traces his thinking to a transcript of [Richard Feynman](/notes/richard-feynman)'s 1959 talk "There's Plenty of Room at the Bottom," in which Feynman asked what would happen "if we could arrange the atoms one by one the way we want them?" Fired up by an idea consistent with his own, Drexler set out to realize Feynman's vision. In his influential book *Engines of Creation*, he proposes an "assembler" a few billionths of a metre long that can both replicate itself and build other machines atom by atom — placing him in the book's lineage of self-replication ideas running through Feynman and von Neumann.

**Context:** K. Eric Drexler (b. 1955) is an American engineer often called the founding figure of molecular Nanotechnology; his 1986 book *Engines of Creation* laid out the concept of molecular assemblers.
