# Jean Piaget

Jean Piaget appears in Kyle's wiki as part of the lineage of education reform traced in [The Science of Learning](/notes/the-science-of-learning) (TIME's special edition on how the brain learns), which lines him up with [Maria Montessori](/notes/maria-montessori), [John Dewey](/notes/john-dewey), and Carol Dweck as the figures who reshaped how educators think about development. Within that piece he is specifically associated with [Multiple Intelligences](/notes/multiple-intelligences) in its developmental framing of how children build understanding — the [Multiple Intelligences](/notes/multiple-intelligences) page notes that across its sources the through-line is skepticism toward any one-size measure of ability, favoring matching the mode of learning to the individual.

**Context:** Jean Piaget (1896–1980) was a Swiss psychologist whose theory of cognitive development — children progressing through distinct stages as they construct knowledge — became foundational to developmental psychology and education.

## Where this appears

- [The Science of Learning](/notes/the-science-of-learning) — placed in the Hall→Dewey→Montessori→Piaget→Dweck lineage of education reform; tied to the developmental framing of learning.
- [Multiple Intelligences](/notes/multiple-intelligences) — associated with Piaget in the piece's developmental account of how children build understanding.
