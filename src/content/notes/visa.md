# Visa

In Kyle's wiki, Visa shows up less as a payments network and more as the institutional design case study of its founder Dee Hock, who built it as a "chaordic" (chaos + order) — decentralized, self-organizing — organization. The thread originates in Kyle's April 30th, 2020 Roam "Quick Capture" reading log, tagged `#Leadership` and `#Visa`, where he saved both the Fast Company profile The Trillion-Dollar Vision of Dee Hock (M. Mitchell Waldrop, 1996) and Hock's own book [One from Many](/notes/one-from-many) (*One from Many: VISA and the Rise of Chaordic Organization*), recommended to him by [Tim Riser](/notes/tim-riser). [Peter Senge](/notes/peter-senge), in [Fifth Discipline](/notes/fifth-discipline), called Hock "one of the most original thinkers on the subject of organization that I've come across" — the blurb Kyle records on the [Leadership](/notes/leadership) page as the draw of the whole Visa/Hock thread.

**Context:** Visa Inc. ($V) is a global payments-technology network founded by Dee Hock in 1958 (as BankAmericard, reorganized into Visa in 1970); it remains one of the largest electronic-payments companies in the world.
