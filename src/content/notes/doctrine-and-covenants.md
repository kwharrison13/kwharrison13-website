# Doctrine and Covenants

One of the standard works of scripture in The Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-day Saints, abbreviated D&C. In Kyle's topical study Worthiness, the [Doctrine and Covenants](/notes/doctrine-and-covenants) appears in Brother Bott's recommended reading order for missionary preparation: the [Book of Mormon](/notes/book-of-mormon) first, then the New Testament, then the Doctrine and Covenants, then the [Pearl of Great Price](/notes/pearl-of-great-price), then the Old Testament. Individual D&C sections are cited heavily throughout Kyle's gospel-study notes (e.g. [D&C 3](/notes/dandc-3), [D&C 4](/notes/dandc-4), [D&C 6](/notes/dandc-6), [D&C 84](/notes/dandc-84)) as primary scriptural source material.

**Context:** The Doctrine and Covenants is a collection of revelations, mostly given through [Joseph Smith](/notes/joseph-smith), that documents the founding and governance of the Church; together with the Bible, the [Book of Mormon](/notes/book-of-mormon), and the [Pearl of Great Price](/notes/pearl-of-great-price) it forms the LDS canon of "standard works."

## Where this appears

- Worthiness — listed in the recommended missionary-preparation reading order after the Book of Mormon and New Testament
