# Product Led Growth (PLG)

**Product-led growth** is the go-to-market model where, as Kyle's notes on [Product Led Growth — The End User Era](/books/product-led-growth-the-end-user-era) put it, "the first interaction with your company or product is typically through the product itself" — end users find, evaluate, and adopt software on their own, then tell their bosses what to buy, and sales/marketing only enter afterward. Every function reorganizes around the product team; the website and signup flow become *part of the product*. The model is partly a response to a harder, more expensive market — [CAC](/notes/customer-acquisition-cost) up 50–65% over five years, median growth halving — except for [freemium](/notes/freemium) businesses where the cost rose far less. Crucially, PLG is "not anti-sales": sales survives but turns consultative, looking and feeling "more like [Customer Success](/notes/customer-success)" (the [Stripe](/notes/stripe) developer-sales model).

In Kyle's investing diligence the term is a recurring sorting axis between sales-led and product-led organizations. David Bromberg (Lantern) frames it as a spectrum — "[ServiceNow ($NOW)](/notes/servicenow) is very sales led; Slack is very product led." [Sharad Verma](/notes/sharad-verma) of [Boostup.ai](/notes/boostup-ai) sees PLG as a future motion "for Ops, not for reps," consistent with Boostup's ~50% self-serve, top-down GTM. The model also surfaces as a *risk* in diligence: [Marie Szuts](/notes/marie-szuts) (Figma) noted it's "really hard to sell a PLG product because it can inflate performance," and Anshul Gupta (Actively) framed a CRM play around replacing PLG-era tooling.

**Context:** "Product-led growth" was coined and popularized by the VC firm OpenView Venture Partners (notably partner [Blake Bartlett](/notes/blake-bartlett)) around 2016 to describe SaaS companies — Slack, Figma, Zoom, Calendly — that grow primarily through the product itself rather than through traditional sales and marketing.
