# Mosiah 17

Mosiah 17 is the Book of Mormon chapter recounting the martyrdom of the prophet Abinadi, who is put to death by fire for refusing to recant his testimony before King Noah. In Kyle's gospel study it functions primarily as a witness of faith under persecution. [Russell M. Nelson](/notes/russell-m-nelson)'s talk "Christ Is Risen; Faith in Him Will Move Mountains" cites Mosiah 17:7–20 (Abinadi) alongside [Ether 13](/notes/ether-13):13–14 (Ether) and [Moroni 1](/notes/moroni-1):1–3 (Moroni) as evidence that faith is "the power that enables the unlikely to accomplish the impossible" — asking whether, without that power, Abinadi "would have suffered death by fire for refusing to deny what he knew to be true."

The chapter also surfaces in the Alma 13–16 study, which quotes Mosiah 17:8 — the charge against Abinadi for testifying "that God himself should come down among the children of men" — connecting his martyrdom to the broader theme of prophets bearing witness at the cost of their lives.

**Context:** In the Book of Mormon, Mosiah 17 narrates Abinadi's final testimony and death; the convert Alma, formerly one of King Noah's priests, escapes after being persuaded by Abinadi's words.
