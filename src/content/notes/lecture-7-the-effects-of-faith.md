# Lecture 7: The Effects of Faith

The seventh of the *Lectures on Faith*, cited in Kyle's study of Christ Is Risen; Faith in Him Will Move Mountains — Russell M. Nelson for its account of how faith operates. Verse 3 holds that "when a man works by faith he works by mental exertion instead of physical force: it is by words instead of exerting his physical powers" — God said "Let there be light," and there was light; the Savior teaches that with faith as a grain of mustard seed one may say to a mountain "remove" and it will remove. The lecture concludes that "Faith, then, works by words; and with these its mightiest works have been, and will be performed."

This passage supplies the talk's core mechanism — faith as mental exertion expressed through words — building on the definition of faith as power in [Lecture 1: Faith Defined](/notes/lecture-1-faith-defined) and the conditions for exercising faith laid out in Lecture 3.

**Context:** The *Lectures on Faith* are a series of seven theological lectures prepared for the School of the Prophets in Kirtland, Ohio, in 1834–35, associated with [Joseph Smith](/notes/joseph-smith) and printed in early editions of the Doctrine and Covenants of the Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-day Saints.
