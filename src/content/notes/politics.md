# Politics

A cross-cutting theme in Kyle's reading, recurring most strongly around the question of whether politics degrades or distributes truth. [Romney — A Reckoning](/books/romney-a-reckoning) is the anchor: a "politics-of-character" reading in which "politics will always fetter language" (reduced to signal, not content — the Newt Gingrich pattern from [The Death of Expertise](/books/the-death-of-expertise)), where the post-2016 GOP made beliefs a *lagging* indicator of group membership rather than a leading one, and where the alternative is "data over dogma" and an [Educated Electorate](/notes/educated-electorate) that "can be trusted with hard truths." It is notably the most approving read in the library relative to Kyle's usual skepticism.

The two [David Perell](/notes/david-perell) long-reads supply the media and economic angles. [Do You #FeelTheBern- Behind Bernie Sanders' Quest To Take Over The White House](/notes/do-you-feelthebern-behind-bernie-sanders-quest-to-take-over-the-white-house) reads modern politics through [Marshall McLuhan](/notes/marshall-mcluhan) — "societies have always been shaped more by the nature of the media by which men communicate than by the content of the communication" — arguing social platforms, not the debate stage, are now the arena, and that direct, unfiltered access (Snapchat, Reddit, YouTube) is what builds youth trust. [Where Do We Go Next?](/notes/where-do-we-go-next) reads Trump's election as a symptom of rising global populism and inequality bred by class and racial segregation, invoking [Naval Ravikant](/notes/naval-ravikant)'s "history's classic solution is either politics distributing prosperity or revolution distributing poverty," and prescribing participation over nostalgia.

## Where this appears

- [Romney — A Reckoning](/books/romney-a-reckoning) — politics fettering language; beliefs as lagging vs. leading indicators of tribe; data over dogma and the educated electorate
- [Do You #FeelTheBern- Behind Bernie Sanders' Quest To Take Over The White House](/notes/do-you-feelthebern-behind-bernie-sanders-quest-to-take-over-the-white-house) — politics through McLuhan's "the medium is the message"; social platforms as the new arena
- [Where Do We Go Next?](/notes/where-do-we-go-next) — Trump's election as a symptom of populism and inequality; politics vs. revolution
