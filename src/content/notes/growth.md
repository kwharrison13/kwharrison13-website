# Growth

Growth runs across two registers in Kyle's notes. In the **company/startup register** it is the operating discipline of compounding a business metric. [Product Led Growth — The End User Era](/books/product-led-growth-the-end-user-era) cites Brian Balfour's 2014 definition — growth as "the blending of marketing, product and engineering into one tight knit team," whose mission is "the growth rate of a metric that tells a more complete picture of the business" rather than any single function's output. This is the [Playbooks](/notes/playbooks) / [Playbook — Growth](/notes/playbook-growth) / [Playbook — Sales](/notes/playbook-sales) / [Sales](/notes/sales) cluster: growth as a holistic funnel discipline, distinct from sales as a function but tightly coupled to it.

In the **civilizational register**, [Homo Deus](/books/homo-deus) frames perpetual economic growth as the engine of modernity's bargain — "modernity's deal of giving up meaning for power," with knowledge as the one non-exhaustible resource that keeps the engine running. This pairs with the [Brave New World](/books/brave-new-world) / [Brave New World Revisited](/books/brave-new-world-revisited) material on what unchecked growth in scale and technique does to the individual, and with [Berkshire Hathaway Annual Letters](/books/berkshire-hathaway-annual-letters) on the durability and persistence of growth rates as a question for capital allocation. Kyle's June 29th, 2021 reading log gathered the strands together: an LSV research paper on the *persistence of growth rates*, the Andreessen–Barksdale HBR conversation on how to make money, and a sales-leadership podcast — growth read simultaneously as an empirical question (do high rates persist?) and an operating one (how do you produce them?).
