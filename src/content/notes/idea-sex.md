# Idea Sex

The idea that good ideas come from the *intersection* of different things you've been reading or seeing — connection, not generation, is where insight lives. In Kyle's [How To Take Smart Notes](/books/how-to-take-smart-notes) notes the frame is attributed to [Conor White-Sullivan](/notes/conor-white-sullivan): "Good ideas come from when ideas have sex: the intersection of different things… You can have better ideas faster if you are actually reviewing the old things and you are building up. You're not throwing away work." It pairs with [Niklas Luhmann](/notes/niklas-luhmann)'s slip-box insight that "one idea, one note was only as valuable as its context" and Steve Jobs's "creativity is just connecting things," making it the [Zettelkasten](/notes/zettelkasten) system's animating premise: the slip-box's value grows exponentially because it surfaces unsought connections.

The same frame recurs in Kyle's [Open Source Knowledge](/notes/open-source-knowledge) research. There it explains how [Walt Disney](/notes/walt-disney) "would synthesize the best of other people's ideas and create something not only better, but wholly unique" (→ [Great Artists Steal](/notes/great-artists-steal)), and how language and then technology — the printing press, the telegram, the internet, Twitter — made ideas composable so they "composed and compounded more quickly" (via [Packy McCormick](/notes/packy-mccormick) quoting [Tim Urban](/notes/tim-urban)). On the [Tim Urban](/notes/tim-urban) page it is tagged as the composability-of-ideas theme his quote illustrates, linking it to [Compounding](/notes/compounding).

**Context:** "Ideas having sex" is a phrase popularized by Matt Ridley in *The Rational Optimist* to describe how human progress accelerates when ideas meet and recombine; the "idea sex" shorthand is common in note-taking and creativity circles.
