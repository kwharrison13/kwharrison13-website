# Imagination

In Kyle's wiki, imagination surfaces through the afterword to [Wicked](/books/wicked), where Gregory Maguire makes the case that fiction is a survival tool rather than an escape. The argument runs through the "World Next Door" — exile from "the World at Hand" is what "liberated [Dante, Malory, Milton, and Bunyan] into being able to conceive of a map to the World Next Door." Maguire's claim is that even after "the Enlightenment, then… Darwin" folded up the old maps of "otherness," we still need them: "The World Next Door is crucial to our ability to survive the World at Hand. We need a Middle-earth when there is an Al Qaeda." The pivot quote is Roger Scruton's: "The consolation of the imaginary is not imaginary consolation."

This connects to the wiki's [Storytelling](/notes/storytelling) thread — fiction as "a map to the World Next Door we need to navigate the World at Hand" — and to the afterword's note that, after real catastrophe, "Fiction couldn't hold a metaphoric candle to the literal firestorm of reality," yet the imaginative faculty is exactly what lets us "see more clearly where we really stand, and what to do next."

## Where this appears

- [Wicked](/books/wicked) — Maguire's afterword treats imagination and fiction as a "map to the World Next Door," a survival tool for navigating reality rather than an escape from it.
