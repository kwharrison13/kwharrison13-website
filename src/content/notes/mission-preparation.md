# Mission Preparation

Mission Preparation is the theme tying together Kyle's topical study Things To Know Before Your Mission, built on [James E. Faust](/notes/james-e-faust)'s talk framing "10 things I would like my son or my grandson to know before he leaves on his mission." The ten points form a working checklist for getting ready to serve: your call comes from the all-knowing Lord (D&C 50:26–28); respect and obey your mission president without criticizing or demeaning him; hard work matters (D&C 35:13–14); forget yourself in the Lord's service (Matthew 10:39, D&C 4:2); never permit contention, since a companionship pulls the load only through teamwork; keep clean, healthy, and neat as a representative of the Lord; love and serve the people; study and teach from the scriptures (you cannot convert people beyond your own conversion); be prepared for opposition and discouragement; and let your testimony be your strongest arrow (D&C 20:37). Kyle pairs it with the companion study 10 Gifts To Take Home From Your Mission.

**Context:** In the Latter-day Saint tradition, "mission preparation" refers to the spiritual, practical, and doctrinal readiness expected of young members before serving a full-time proselytizing mission, supported by Church resources like *Preach My Gospel*.
