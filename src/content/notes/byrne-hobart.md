# Byrne Hobart

Financial writer (author of the newsletter The Diff) and co-author, with [Tobias Huber](/notes/tobias-huber), of [Boom — Bubbles & The End of Stagnation](/books/boom-bubbles-and-the-end-of-stagnation) — the pro-bubble book arguing that financial bubbles, far from pure pathologies, can be the coordinating mechanism that funds ambitious, definite-optimist projects and breaks technological stagnation. In Kyle's notes the book is read through threads like [Progress Studies](/notes/progress-studies), [Historical Futurism](/notes/historical-futurism), [Mimetics](/notes/mimetics), [Nuclear Energy](/notes/nuclear-energy) (nuclear as the "purest apocalyptic-technology case study"), and [Thymos](/notes/thymos). That last one carries into Kyle's [We Need a Renaissance of Rhetoric](/essays/we-need-a-renaissance-of-rhetoric) research, where Hobart is cited for the argument that technological innovation is driven more by [thymos](/notes/thymos) — "spiritedness," a relentless drive to transcend the listless present — than by rational cost-benefit analysis.

Hobart's other recurring role in Kyle's corpus is as a sharp financial detective. In VC Contagion - Research, Kyle leans on Hobart's *The Diff* reporting on Palantir's "roundtripping" — booking revenue from non-government SPAC customers that Palantir had itself invested in (money sent out so it could come back and be booked as revenue) — as the cleanest example of the circular-capital dynamic the essay tracks ("turtles all the way down").

**Context:** Byrne Hobart writes *The Diff*, a widely read newsletter on finance, technology, and markets. *Boom: Bubbles and the End of Stagnation* (2024), co-written with Tobias Huber, sits in the progress-studies / definite-optimism orbit associated with thinkers like [Peter Thiel](/notes/peter-thiel).
