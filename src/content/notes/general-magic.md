# General Magic

In Kyle's notes, General Magic appears as one of Jimmy Soni's "Bell-like constellations of talent." In [The Founders](/books/the-founders), Soni writes that PayPal's founding cohort prompted him "to wonder about other Bell-like constellations of talent — including tech companies like [PayPal](/notes/paypal), General Magic, and Fairchild Semiconductor, but also non-technological cohorts like the Fugitive Poets, the Bloomsbury Group, and the Soulquarians." It is cited as a textbook [Talent Vortex](/notes/talent-vortex): a company whose dispersed alumni went on to outsized influence, the same pattern the [Talent Vortex](/notes/talent-vortex) concept page draws from PayPal and Fairchild.

**Context:** General Magic was a 1990s Silicon Valley startup, spun out of Apple, that pursued an early vision of the handheld personal communicator. It commercially failed but its alumni went on to help create the iPhone, eBay, Android, and other defining products — making it a frequently cited "company that failed but seeded an industry."
