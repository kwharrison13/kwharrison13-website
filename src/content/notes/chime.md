# Chime

Consumer-fintech/neobank that recurs in Kyle's sourcing notes as a reference point for other companies. In the [Point Card](/notes/point-card) pre-brief from [Mark Fiorentino](/notes/mark-fiorentino), Chime is used as the positioning anchor — Point pitched itself as "the spend platform for Millennials… think Chime, but for a younger demographic," and the competitive map placed Chime/Current as the players "tackling subprime and paycheck-to-paycheck users," leaving a gap of 80M+ middle-to-upper-middle income Gen Z and Millennials underserved by legacy providers (August 18th, 2021 — Point Card Pre-Brief (Mark Fiorentino)).

Chime also surfaces as a real customer in Miles Beckstead's UnitQ pricing/packaging call: because Chime is a transactional app product without Discord or social-feedback surfaces, UnitQ's value to them is concentrated on reducing support tickets — identifying a spiking issue sooner so support volume drops (July 18th, 2022 — Miles Beckstead (UnitQ)).

**Context:** Chime is a US neobank (founded 2013) offering fee-light mobile banking — debit cards, early direct deposit, and no-overdraft features — targeted largely at consumers underserved by traditional banks. It became one of the highest-valued US fintech startups before its 2025 IPO.
