# Martin Luther

The Protestant reformer appears in two of Kyle's sources, both placing him as a forerunner whose work was incomplete. In [The Life of John Taylor](/books/the-life-of-john-taylor), Luther is named (with [John Calvin](/notes/john-calvin), Melancthon, Wesley, Whitfield) among the reformers who "tried to bring about the ancient order of things" but, per Taylor's sermon from Jude, "failed to accomplish it" — the restored gospel through [Joseph Smith](/notes/joseph-smith) being framed as the completion of what they could only reach toward. In Abrahamic Covenant, Luther is the reference point for the doctrine that all believers are made priests through baptism (the "priesthood of all believers"), which the note examines against [1 Peter 2](/notes/1-peter-2):9 and Exodus 19:5–6.

**Context:** Martin Luther (1483–1546) was the German monk and theologian whose 1517 Ninety-Five Theses sparked the Protestant Reformation. His doctrine of the "priesthood of all believers" rejected a separate priestly caste, holding that all baptized Christians share priestly standing before God.

## Where this appears

- [The Life of John Taylor](/books/the-life-of-john-taylor) — named among reformers who "tried to bring about the ancient order of things" but failed, in Taylor's sermon framing the Restoration
- Abrahamic Covenant — cited as the source of the "priesthood of all believers" doctrine, tested against 1 Peter 2:9 and Exodus 19:5–6
