# His Grace is Sufficient

A talk by [Brad Wilcox](/notes/brad-wilcox) that recurs across Kyle's gospel study as a touchstone for understanding [Grace](/notes/grace) and the [Atonement](/notes/atonement). Its central reframe — drawn out in Kyle's notes — is that the Atonement is not just to cleanse us but to *change* us: in the final judgment "it will not be the unrepentant sinner begging Jesus, 'Let me stay'… it would probably be Jesus begging the unrepentant sinner, 'Please, choose to stay. Please, use my Atonement—not just to be cleansed but to be changed so that you want to stay.'" (cited in the 3 Nephi 8–11 study).

The talk anchors a thread Kyle traces through scripture about weakness made strength. In the [Ezra Taft Benson](/notes/ezra-taft-benson) study ("A Mighty Change of Heart"), the same promise appears via Ether 12:27 — "if they humble themselves before me, and have faith in me, then will I make weak things become strong unto them" — with the point that the very source of our troubles can be changed into a strength, and that true [Repentance](/notes/repentance) is more than reformation of behavior. In the Alma 13–16 study, Kyle ties the talk to sanctification changing one's relationship with sin (the sanctified "could not look upon sin save it were with abhorrence") and uses it to argue against [Universalism](/notes/universalism) as an [Anti-Christ](/notes/anti-christ) idea: grace does not mean we change without wanting to. In 3 Nephi 10:4-6 it illustrates that "Christ never gives up" — a never-ending circle of gathering love.

**Context:** "His Grace Is Sufficient" is a widely circulated devotional address given by Brad Wilcox at Brigham Young University in 2011, arguing that grace is not a final reward for those who have done their best but an enabling power available throughout the process of becoming.

**Full talk:** the complete devotional text (and Kyle's emphasis) lives at His Grace Is Sufficient — Brad Wilcox in gospel-study.
