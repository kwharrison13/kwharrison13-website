# Gerald Zahavi

**One-line:** *Historian and author of "Workers, Managers, and Welfare Capitalism," the book on [George F. Johnson](/notes/george-f-johnson) and the Endicott-Johnson [Square Deal](/notes/the-square-deal).*

## What we know

- Wrote *"Workers, Managers, and Welfare Capitalism"* about [George F. Johnson](/notes/george-f-johnson) and the Endicott-Johnson Shoe Company. (source: NPR Planet Money [Episode 618: The Square Deal](https://www.npr.org/sections/money/2015/04/17/400360768/the-square-deal))
- His verdict in the episode: *"I think the [Square Deal](/notes/the-square-deal) is an anachronism today. People don't expect much from corporations, period."* — the line Kyle pushes back against in the essay's close.

## Connections

- [George F. Johnson](/notes/george-f-johnson) — subject of Zahavi's book; the Endicott-Johnson founder whose paternalist program he chronicles.
- [The Square Deal](/notes/the-square-deal) — the Endicott-Johnson labor compact Zahavi judges "an anachronism today."
- [Welfare Capitalism](/notes/the-square-deal) — the framework named in his book title, "Workers, Managers, and Welfare Capitalism."

- Unions, Unions Everywhere - Research — supplies the "anachronism" framing the essay argues with.
