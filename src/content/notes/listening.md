# Listening

A recurring craft across Kyle's reading, where listening is consistently framed as edge rather than passivity. In [Stop Asking Questions](/books/stop-asking-questions) it's the core move behind good interviewing — "the magic of listening" is that "people prefer to be heard than to be helped"; the host's job is mostly "mhmm" and "why did you say that?", and a strong opening hook matters because podcast analytics show listeners drop off within the first minute. In [What It Takes](/books/what-it-takes), [Stephen A. Schwarzman](/notes/stephen-a-schwarzman) names listening as the skill that marked him out on Wall Street: he "listens hard to what people say, believing that things that are said are said for a reason," rarely takes notes, waits to hear what people *want* even when unspoken, and uses that to find a point of personal connection. He treats it as an unusually high "gift of recall" — common-sense, yet rare in practice.

The same idea threads through Kyle's other notes. In his [Cultivating Cults](/essays/cultivating-cults) essay research he flags "people whose job is [Listening](/notes/listening)" and the pursuit of "shared understanding" as a binding mechanism. And in gospel study he ties it to [Parenting](/notes/parenting): "you can't know what is burdening your children's minds if you don't do a good job listening to them" — where listening "comes from empathy and empathy comes from respect" (July 27–August 2, 2020. Alma 39–42 — The Great Plan of Happiness).
