# Brianne Kimmel

An angel investor / early-stage VC who appears in Kyle's notes as a co-investor and as a source of go-to-market thinking. She participated in [Candor](/notes/candor)'s seed round (which [Contrary](/notes/contrary) led), alongside Ellen DeSilva, Joseph Quan, Andrew Farah, and Anamitra Banerji, per [Kelsey Bishop](/notes/kelsey-bishop)'s February 2022 portfolio update.

Kyle also saved a Kimmel tweet on early-stage [Go-To-Market](/notes/gtm) strategy, in which she frames defining a startup's initial GTM motion as one of the most challenging exercises and references a playbook drawn from being the first Head of Sales & Customer Success at [Typeform](/notes/typeform) and [Notion](/notes/notion). The tweet is tagged to the `company-building` portfolio idea.

**Context:** Brianne Kimmel is the founder of Worklife Ventures, an early-stage venture firm focused on software for the future of work; she was previously at Zendesk and is known for SaaS School and her go-to-market expertise.
