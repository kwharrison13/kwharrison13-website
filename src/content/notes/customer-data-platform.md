# Customer Data Platform

**One-line definition:** *Infrastructure layer that ingests every customer touchpoint, resolves identity across systems/devices, builds a unified customer profile, and syndicates it (push + pull) to downstream marketing/product/analytics tools — the "silo-buster" for first-party data.*

## How sources describe it

- An emerging category in "peak hype cycle" as of 2018; real-data CDPs ([Segment](/notes/segment), [mParticle](/notes/mparticle), Treasure Data) collect primary data in real time, vs. front-end players that only ingest 3rd-party tools.
- Core jobs: identity resolution (the hardest part — 30–40% of dev resources), unified single customer view, orchestration/syndication to "final-mile" tools. Stickiness compounds — each n+1 integration deepens lock-in.
- Tightly intertwined with product-analytics tools (Amplitude, Mixpanel, Heap). Buyers ~70% technical (IT/analytics/engineering), shifting toward marketing.
- Full mapping of buying signals, tablestakes, and differentiation in mParticle — Diligence (2018).
