# Joe Lonsdale

Joe Lonsdale is, in Kyle's notes, the author of [To Save America, Restore Our Frontier](/notes/to-save-america-restore-our-frontier) — an essay arguing that America's national greatness was always tied to "Frontier conditions": falsifiable, accountable environments that harden ideas and people. Lonsdale's diagnosis is that bureaucratic capture, not ideology, is the real source of decline, and that restoring the Frontier mindset is the antidote rather than fighting cultural sideshows. His prescriptions include funding new science and universities, breaking the FDA into competing pieces, abolishing healthcare monopolies, creating innovation zones, and pursuing "[charter cities](/notes/city-building) and bold governance experiments (even on Mars or ocean)." His notes credit him with founding the [Cicero Institute](/notes/cicero-institute), which pursues non-partisan legislative solutions with "dozens of laws now on the books."

The page [It's Time To Build](/notes/it-s-time-to-build) frames Lonsdale's essay as a parallel argument to [Marc Andreessen](/notes/marc-andreessen)'s — that bureaucratic capture, not ideology, is the real story behind America's failure to build.

**Context:** Joe Lonsdale is a venture capitalist and entrepreneur who co-founded Palantir Technologies and the venture firm 8VC, and founded the Cicero Institute, a policy organization focused on government reform.
