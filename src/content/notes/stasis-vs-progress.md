# Stasis vs. Progress

A framing in Kyle's Historical Futurism - Research notes that pits a civilization that keeps advancing against one that stalls out. The seed quote is [Jeff Bezos](/notes/jeff-bezos) on [Blue Origin](/notes/blue-origin): *"I'm pursuing this work because I believe if we don't, we will eventually end up with a civilization of stasis, which I find very demoralizing. I don't want my great-grandchildren's great-grandchildren to live in a civilization of stasis"* (May 2018, onstage with Mathias Döpfner). Bezos frames space less as a business than as long-term philanthropy aimed at keeping the trajectory of human progress open.

The research pairs this with the essay's opening device — the idea of "50-year news": a publication issued only every fifty years would skip the gossip and tragedy of the 24-hour cycle and reveal that "the curve of history bends toward progress." Stasis-vs-progress is the underlying tension that historical futurism resolves in favor of progress, by zooming out far enough to see the long arc.
