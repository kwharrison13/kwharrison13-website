# 3 Nephi 28

A Book of Mormon chapter — the account of the Three Nephites who desire to tarry until Christ's coming — annotated in the Come Follow Me study of 3 Nephi 27–4 Nephi. Kyle reads it across three themes: [Fulness of Joy](/notes/fulness-of-joy) (28:10, the state of being that Heavenly Father and Christ are in, achieved after resurrection or great vision), The Godhead (28:10–11, the Father, Son, and Holy Ghost as one and bearing record), and [Transfiguration](/notes/transfiguration) (28:15, the disciples "changed from this body of flesh into an immortal state," cross-linked to [2 Corinthians 12](/notes/2-corinthians-12):2 and [D&C 131](/notes/dandc-131):7–8 on the materiality of spirit). The chapter also appears in Helaman 7–12, where verses 34–35 join a [Gene Hurley's Scripture Chain](/notes/gene-hurley-s-scripture-chain) on knowledge and [Accountability](/notes/accountability) — woe to those who will not hearken to the words of Jesus and those he has sent.

**Context:** 3 Nephi 28 records Christ granting nine of his twelve Nephite disciples their desire to die quickly, while three ask to tarry on earth until he comes again, and are transfigured so they cannot feel pain or death.
