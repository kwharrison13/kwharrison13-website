# Equity Crowdfunding

Equity crowdfunding appears in Kyle's wiki as one item on the [Investing 101 2.0](/notes/investing-101-2-0) agenda — the "2.0" list of ways the investing world is being reinvented "through data in investing, bifurcation of investment firms, solo capitalists, equity crowdfunding, diversity & inclusion, democratized secondary transactions, or triple-bottom-line investing." It sits in that note alongside other access-broadening, democratizing forces in venture and private markets, but the reference is a single line-item; the wiki does not yet carry a developed treatment.

**Context:** Equity crowdfunding lets a large number of ordinary (often non-accredited) investors buy small equity stakes in early-stage private companies via online platforms — e.g. Republic, Wefunder, SeedInvest — typically enabled in the U.S. by the JOBS Act's Regulation Crowdfunding. It is part of the broader "democratized access to private markets" theme rather than a fully separate asset class.
