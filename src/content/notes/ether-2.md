# Ether 2

The chapter in the Book of Ether where the [Brother of Jared](/notes/brother-of-jared) builds the barges and brings the Lord three problems with them — they couldn't breathe, had no light, and couldn't steer ([Ether 2](/notes/ether-2):19). It is one of the most-studied chapters in Kyle's gospel notes. The topical study 3 Answers To Every Prayer reads those three problems as a pattern for the three answers God gives to prayer: a step-by-step solution (breathing, where God commands the holes be cut), a call to use one's own agency and study it out (light), and simply taking the problem off the petitioner's hands (steering). The barge itself becomes a figure for mortal life — "tight like unto a dish" ([Ether 2](/notes/ether-2):17), with air holes that admit the necessary travails of mortality while keeping out the filth of the world, and with the promise that "when ye are swallowed up in the depths of the sea" (2:25) the Lord "will bring you up again" (2:24).

The chapter also carries Kyle's study of [Spiritual Complacency](/notes/spiritual-complacency). In November 9–15, 2020. Ether 1–5: "Rend That Veil of Unbelief" and the Faith — Topical Study, he marks Ether 2:14 — the Lord talked with the Brother of Jared "for the space of three hours" and **chastened him because he remembered not to call upon the name of the Lord** after years of being "directed continually by the hand of the Lord" (2:6). The lesson: being comfortably led can dull our reliance on God; it is in hardship that we are most deeply in need of Him. That chastening is the prelude to the Brother of Jared's later faith, when he sees the finger of Christ in [Ether 3](/notes/ether-3). The Helaman 13–16 study cross-references Ether 2 as an instance of God chastening because He loves.

**Context:** Ether 2 is a chapter in the Book of Ether, near the end of the Book of Mormon, recounting the Jaredites' journey and the construction of the barges that carry them across the ocean to the promised land.
