# Decentralized Brain

Kyle's term for treating knowledge as a network you both draw on and keep expanding — relying on outside "nodes" (other people, references, external memory) while continuously enlarging your own territory of understanding. The tension is the whole point: it's fine to lean on nodes, but you must "constantly work to expand your own sphere of knowledge (e.g. a bigger territory)," as Kyle notes against [Charlie Munger: Practical Thought About Practical Thought?](/notes/charlie-munger-practical-thought-about-practical-thought).

In [The Death of Expertise](/books/the-death-of-expertise), the idea connects to a "latticework of memory" story (tied to [Pat Grady](/notes/pat-grady)) and Zach's "Theory of Masters," and is set against Malcolm Gladwell's observation that networks can't set goals — applied to [David Vargas](/notes/david-vargas)'s vision of a [Decentralized Community](/notes/decentralized-community) built on [Roam Research](/notes/roam-research). So the concept sits at the intersection of personal mastery and networked/external cognition: distributed knowledge is powerful, but only an individual mind can hold direction and keep growing its own range.
