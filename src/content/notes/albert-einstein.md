# Albert Einstein

Across Kyle's library Einstein recurs as the patron saint of [Curiosity](/notes/curious) and as a touchstone for the science-and-faith question. In [Leonardo Da Vinci](/books/leonardo-da-vinci) he is Isaacson's "recurring foil" — both men retained a childlike wonder ("I am just passionately curious") about phenomena most people abandon by age ten (why the sky is blue, how clouds form), Einstein saying he marveled at such questions because he was slow to learn to talk as a child. The line "I have no special talents… I am just passionately curious" anchors the book's argument that observation and wonder are products of effort, not magic. In [Range](/books/range) he is invoked (with [Mark Zuckerberg](/notes/mark-zuckerberg)) as an icon of precocity the book pushes back against — including Laszlo Polgar prepping for fatherhood by poring over biographies "from Socrates to Einstein."

In [The Faith of a Scientist](/books/the-faith-of-a-scientist), [Henry Eyring](/notes/henry-eyring) quotes Einstein on "the most incomprehensible thing about the world is that it is comprehensible" and on belief in nature's regularity belonging to religion — "science without religion is lame, religion without science is blind" — making Einstein a witness for the compatibility of scientific rigor and faith. [The Man From The Future](/books/the-man-from-the-future) casts him as [John von Neumann](/notes/john-von-neumann)'s IAS colleague and quantum-foundations interlocutor, the man who coined "spooky action at a distance" and refused to accept that the moon exists only when observed. [Nuclear War — a Scenario](/books/nuclear-war-a-scenario) closes its civilization-reset arc on his "I know not with what weapons World War III will be fought, but World War IV will be fought with sticks and stones." [Homo Deus](/books/homo-deus) uses him as the evolutionary-humanist example that "the experience of an Einstein… is far more valuable than that of a drunken good-for-nothing" — a claim Harari surfaces critically.

**Context:** Albert Einstein (1879–1955) was the German-born theoretical physicist who developed special and general relativity and won the 1921 Nobel Prize in Physics for his work on the photoelectric effect.
