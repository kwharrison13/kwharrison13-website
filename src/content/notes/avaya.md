# Avaya

Legacy telephony / contact-center vendor that appears in Kyle's notes as a *displaced* incumbent. In the Cresta.ai - Diligence tech-stack review, the customer being evaluated (Neiman Marcus) "had been using Avaya" for voice and was in the process of converting that voice traffic off Avaya onto [Twilio Flex](/notes/twilio-flex) — part of a broader migration off legacy contact-center stack (also moving off [NICE](/notes/nice) IEX for workforce management). Avaya functions in the note as the kind of entrenched, on-prem-era contact-center platform that modern cloud players like Twilio and AI layers like Cresta are eating into.

**Context:** Avaya is an American enterprise communications company spun out of Lucent (itself ex-AT&T) in 2000, historically a major vendor of business telephony, PBX systems, and contact-center software; it has gone through bankruptcy restructuring as the market shifted to cloud-native competitors.

## Where this appears

- Cresta.ai - Diligence — Neiman Marcus migrating its voice contact center off Avaya onto [Twilio Flex](/notes/twilio-flex).
- [Twilio Flex](/notes/twilio-flex) — the cloud platform Avaya's voice traffic was being converted to.
