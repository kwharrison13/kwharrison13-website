# Spencer Christensen

Spencer Christensen is a [Stripe](/notes/stripe) alum whose verbatim survey response feeds Kyle's The Talent Vortex - Research — the source material behind the "Talent Vortex" essay. His central claim is that Stripe didn't so much *make* great talent as *select* and *unleash* it: he doesn't feel Stripe had particularly talented salespeople, and "to this day doesn't have a great system for making them better." Instead the company hired "independent folks keen to 'write the book' on how things were done," and promoted not the top earners but those who, on top of clearing the bar, "built systematic solutions for problems" — better training, new frameworks, industry-specific applications. The Stripe ethos he names: "if you see a problem, solve it for yourself and those that follow."

Christensen also supplies the research's sharpest critique of Stripe's go-to-market: its "organizational muscle around sales is generally underdeveloped" (three CROs in three years; enterprise-readiness only starting once [Mike Clayville](/notes/mike-clayville) joined in late 2020), and because the comp system rarely let a salesperson clear $500k, true heavyweights "historically steered clear." The salespeople it did attract were "mental athletes" without traditional sales backgrounds — which he likens to Facebook circa 2012: "super sexy, able to take high talent for granted, growing dangerously fast." A telling quirk he flags: at Stripe, the reward for good work "was generally more important work, not more money" — even IC-to-manager moves came without a pay raise.
