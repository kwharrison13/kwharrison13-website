# Matt Cohler

Venture investor whose framing of the VC job recurs in Kyle's writing on [historical futurism](/essays/historical-futurism) and markets. Kyle describes him as "a partner at Benchmark" and quotes him repeatedly: *"Our job is not to see the future, it's to see the present very clearly."* In a Quora AMA, Cohler elaborated that "noticing the present first" applies to surfacing new investment opportunities — but once an investor is actually working with a company, the critical skill becomes "to be able to see around corners… not to predict the future many years out, but to have the experience, pattern-recognition and judgment to know what's coming next."

Kyle uses this two-step — see the present clearly, then see around corners — as a launchpad in [Historical Futurism (Essay)](/essays/historical-futurism), arguing that turning the next corner is "something ants can do too," and that meaningful influence on the future requires the ability to *zoom out*. The same Cohler quote anchors an earlier essay, [Markets, Markets, and Markets](/essays/markets-markets-and-markets).

**Context:** Matt Cohler is a longtime general partner at the venture firm Benchmark, and was an early executive at LinkedIn and Facebook before joining the firm. The "notice the present" line dates to a 2012 interview about his investing philosophy.
