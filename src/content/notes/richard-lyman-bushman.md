# Richard Lyman Bushman

Author of [Joseph Smith: Rough Stone Rolling](/books/joseph-smith-rough-stone-rolling), the landmark biography Kyle read in 2020 and rated 5/5. In Kyle's notes, Bushman is characterized as "the biographer; a believing historian who engages the messiness of Church history" — a believing Latter-day Saint scholar who nonetheless evaluates the prophet and the institution he built as fallible humans in fallen systems, rather than against a perfect ideal. That posture is precisely what makes the book valuable to Kyle: it asks hard questions about Church history without surrendering the underlying claim.

Bushman's authorial choices surface in the notes as historiographical contrasts — for example, his treatment of Joseph Smith's expansive Bible revision is set against [Thomas Jefferson](/notes/thomas-jefferson)'s pared-down New Testament. Kyle pairs the book with [Leonard Arrington and the Writing of Mormon History](/books/leonard-arrington-and-the-writing-of-mormon-history) as a historiographical companion, framing Bushman's candid scholarship as part of how serious, faith-engaged Mormon history became possible.

**Context:** Richard Lyman Bushman (b. 1931) is an American historian, emeritus Gouverneur Morris Professor of History at Columbia University, and a practicing Latter-day Saint. *Rough Stone Rolling* (2005) is widely regarded as the definitive modern biography of Joseph Smith.
