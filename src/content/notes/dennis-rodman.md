# Dennis Rodman

In [Discipline Is Destiny](/books/discipline-is-destiny), Dennis Rodman is the case study for the "lift-the-supporting-players" leadership frame: [Michael Jordan](/notes/michael-jordan) learned to elevate teammates like Rodman *despite* their flaws and weaknesses rather than trying to force them to become Jordans — "Help them be their best." Kyle flagged this directly: "Michael Jordan learned to lift his supporting players, like Dennis Rodman, DESPITE their flaws and weaknesses. He didn't try and force them to be Michael Jordan. Just to be their best." The book sets this against [Kobe Bryant](/notes/kobe-bryant) as a cautionary foil — Bryant "couldn't accept that teammates couldn't all be Kobes."

**Context:** Dennis Rodman is a Hall of Fame NBA forward known as one of the greatest rebounders and defenders in league history, famous for his eccentric persona and for winning three championships with the Chicago Bulls alongside Michael Jordan.

## Where this appears

- [Discipline Is Destiny](/books/discipline-is-destiny) — the supporting player Michael Jordan learned to lift despite his flaws; the positive example of the "help them be their best" leadership frame (vs. the Kobe Bryant foil)
