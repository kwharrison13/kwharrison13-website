# Google

Google recurs across Kyle's corpus in several distinct registers. In his **VC diligence work** it is most often an expert-network and reference target: the Watershed Climate Diligence — 2021 notes flag Google contacts ([Teesha Chowdhury](/notes/teesha-chowdhury), [Kate Brandt](/notes/kate-brandt)) for outreach and cite it as a cloud + sustainability leader (100% renewable data centers); the Salt Security Diligence — 2021 notes list [Joel D'sa](/notes/joel-d-sa) at Google as an API-security reference; and the Identity & Access Management Diligence — 2021 page treats Google (alongside [Apple](/notes/apple) and [Facebook](/notes/meta-company)) as the canonical consumer-era identity provider whose social-login OAuth flows defined the consumer side of the IAM market. In the [Salt Security](/notes/salt-security) cluster, [Apigee](/notes/apigee) surfaces as Google's owned API-management platform — the incumbent gateway world [Kong](/notes/kong) grew out of.

In Kyle's **book notes**, Google is a recurring symbol of technological power. [Homo Deus](/books/homo-deus) ([Yuval Noah Harari](/notes/yuval-noah-harari)) makes it the concrete face of "Dataism" — the Baseline Study and decision-outsourcing ("Listen, Google…") as post-liberal authority, ending on Harari's provocation about whether the century's big changes come "from the Islamic State, or Google." [The Kill Chain](/books/the-kill-chain) ([Christian Brose](/notes/christian-brose)) names Google among the top-five AI R&D spenders that dwarf the defense industry, and dwells on its **2018 withdrawal from Project Maven** as a marker of the Silicon-Valley-vs-Pentagon rift (with Eric Schmidt quoted on China's AI trajectory). [The Power Law](/books/the-power-law-venture-capital-and-the-making-of-the-new-future) uses Google's founders as the emblem of the **founder-control turn** in venture capital — citing Bezos, Gates, and Dell as the retained-control precedents the Googlers leaned on — and Kyle's own margin-note there reads "Compare to Google being scared of AI."

**Context:** Google (a subsidiary of Alphabet Inc. since 2015) is one of the world's largest technology companies, spanning search and advertising, Android, Google Cloud, YouTube, and AI research (DeepMind, Gemini). It is a leading corporate buyer of renewable energy and an early claimant of "quantum supremacy."
