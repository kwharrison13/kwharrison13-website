# A Short Guide to Spectacles

**Author:** [David Perell](/notes/david-perell)
**URL:** https://www.perell.com/blog/2017/1/14/a-short-guide-to-spectacles-
**One-line:** *A short how-to on [Snap](/notes/snapchat)'s Spectacles — that the device's edge is enabling content only a hands-free camera can capture, letting viewers embody the creator's authentic, first-person experience.*

## Key claims

- **Positive, shareable content wins on the internet.** Inspiring, uplifting, beautiful content gets shared most; sharing is how people connect, so we naturally pass along positive, enjoyable experiences ([Consumer Products](/notes/consumer-products)).
- **Spectacles is a chance to invent new conventions.** The best videos will be the ones *only* a hands-free camera can record — formats that didn't exist before the device ([Consumer Investing](/notes/consumer-investing), [Snapchat](/notes/snapchat)).
- **First-person embodiment is the differentiator.** "Let viewers embody your authentic experience. Let them walk in your shoes and feel what you feel."

## Notable quotes

> "Recording with Spectacles is a unique opportunity to create new conventions."

> "Let them walk in your shoes and feel what you feel."

## How it connects

- [David Perell](/notes/david-perell) — an early product-strategy note on a buzzy consumer-hardware launch.
- [Snapchat](/notes/snapchat) — the parent product; Spectacles as its hardware extension.
- [Consumer Investing](/notes/consumer-investing) / [Consumer Products](/notes/consumer-products) — the thesis that new form factors win by enabling content that couldn't exist before.
