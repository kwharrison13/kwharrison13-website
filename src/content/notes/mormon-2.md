# Mormon 2

Mormon 2 is the Book of Mormon chapter Kyle's notes return to most as the scriptural anchor for **the difference between worldly sorrow and godly sorrow**. The load-bearing verse, [Mormon 2](/notes/mormon-2):13, is quoted across multiple study weeks: "their sorrowing was not unto repentance, because of the goodness of God; but it was rather the sorrowing of the damned, because the Lord would not always suffer them to take happiness in sin." In the Godly Sorrow — Topical Study, Kyle reads Mormon 2:10–14 as modeling "the worst loss of godly sorrow" — the Nephites curse God and can never be satisfied — while [Mormon 2](/notes/mormon-2):23 warns that losing sight of God costs "our religion, freedom, peace, families, lives, and even our eternal souls."

In the Mormon 1–6 study week, the chapter marks **[The Pride Cycle](/notes/the-pride-cycle)'s true bottom** — "the cycle's real failure point is when even destruction can no longer bring you to humility" (Mormon 2:8, 12–13) — and contrasts Mormon's people fortifying cities "with our might" yet still being driven out, against [Captain Moroni](/notes/captain-moroni)'s defenses built in faith (Mormon 2:4 vs. [Alma 49](/notes/alma-49)). It recurs as a cross-reference in the Alma and Helaman study weeks wherever godly sorrow and repentance come up.

**Context:** Mormon 2 is a chapter in the Book of Mormon, narrated by the prophet-historian Mormon, recounting the Nephites' wars against the Lamanites and their failure to repent despite suffering — a key proof text in Latter-day Saint teaching on repentance versus mere remorse.
