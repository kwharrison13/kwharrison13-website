# CapitalOne

Surfaces once in September 17th, 2021 — Wiz.io Pre-Brief (Shardul Shah), cited as the canonical cautionary tale for cloud-security sprawl: [Shardul Shah's](/notes/shardul-shah) [Wiz.io](/notes/wiz-io) pre-brief argues that adding multiple teams and multiple clouds creates a paralyzing, compounding challenge that "leads to incidents like CapitalOne's open s3 bucket fiasco." The breach is invoked to motivate Wiz's agentless, context-rich approach to cloud-infrastructure security — Capital One itself is not a subject of the note, only the reference example of what goes wrong without that visibility.

**Context:** Capital One is a large US consumer and commercial bank (and one of the bigger enterprise adopters of public cloud). In 2019 it disclosed a major data breach in which a misconfigured AWS resource exposed the records of roughly 100 million customers — the case that made "misconfigured S3 bucket" shorthand for cloud-security risk.
