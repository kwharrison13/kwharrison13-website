# Cylance

AI-driven endpoint-security company that appears in Kyle's [Noname Security](/notes/noname-security) diligence as the prior employer of a reference-call source. The Noname Security - Diligence dossier records a November 2021 expert call with [Stuart McClure](/notes/stuart-mcclure), described as the former CEO of Cylance, who spoke to the character and reliability of a Noname leader he had hired at Cylance from his McAfee days. McClure's account — that the executive was "humble" and "savvy" but became "unstable, unpredictable" during a difficult personal stretch, to the point McClure "almost had to let him go" — was one of eight reference inputs gathered while Kyle evaluated Noname's $60M Series B in the API-security category.

**Context:** Cylance was a Irvine-based cybersecurity company that pioneered machine-learning-based endpoint malware prevention; Stuart McClure co-founded it in 2012, and it was acquired by BlackBerry in 2019 for roughly $1.4B.
