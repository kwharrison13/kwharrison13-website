# D&C 123

Section 123 of the Doctrine and Covenants — part of [Joseph Smith](/notes/joseph-smith)'s Liberty Jail epistle — recurs in Kyle's study chiefly through its **small-helm metaphor**. In Self-Mastery — Topical Study, verse 16 ("a very large ship is benefited very much by a very small helm in the time of a storm") becomes the image for how self-mastery is won not by one heroic effort but by a few good decisions at a time — the small helm that turns a great ship in a storm. The Scripture Study — Topical Study cites verse 13 among the **characteristics** to cultivate (alongside [D&C 4](/notes/dandc-4) and [D&C 12](/notes/dandc-12)), and Spiritual Gifts lists the section among texts on learning together and peacemaking in the priesthood.

**Context:** Doctrine and Covenants Section 123 is drawn from a March 1839 letter [Joseph Smith](/notes/joseph-smith) wrote while imprisoned in Liberty Jail, Missouri. It urges the Saints to gather and document the wrongs done to them, and includes the counsel that small, faithful efforts — like a small helm steering a great ship — accomplish much over time.
