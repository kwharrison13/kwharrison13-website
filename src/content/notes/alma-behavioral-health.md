# Alma (behavioral health)

Alma is one of the three insurance-network therapist platforms — alongside [SonderMind](/notes/sondermind) and [Headway](/notes/headway) — that anchor the "provider-infrastructure / paneling" cluster in Kyle's October 4th, 2021 — Behavioral Health Expert Transcripts (a batch of 15 Tegus expert calls done for [Behavioral Health](/notes/behavioral-health) diligence at Index Ventures). Across multiple calls, experts describe Alma, Headway, and SonderMind as sharing "essentially identical business models" — they help solo therapists accept insurance by absorbing billing, credentialing, and admin while negotiating better payer rates as a large contracting body, then take a cut (~20%) of each session fee.

The differentiator experts repeatedly draw is **team DNA / tech-stack depth**: Headway was built tech-first from day one; Alma started as a **co-working space** and pivoted; SonderMind started as a clinic. Because of that origin, Alma (like SonderMind) still leans more on human matchers and billers than Headway's product-for-scale approach. On unit economics, one ex-Headway co-founder cites Alma in New York reimbursing ~$140/session, paying the therapist ~$120, and the platform keeping ~$20 — thin enough that paid patient acquisition is uneconomical. Alma's early edge was rate access (good Optum/United rates because its CEO is ex-Oscar) and geographic footprint (NY/NJ/CT). Therapists multi-home across Alma and Headway simultaneously, and no platform has built meaningful lock-in. Magellan Health's corp-dev lead rated familiarity with Alma a 4/5 but declined vendor-specific commentary given active business conversations.

**Context:** Alma is a New York–based company that provides solo and small-group mental-health clinicians with the infrastructure to accept insurance (credentialing, billing, a member-facing directory), monetizing via a membership/subscription and take-rate model. It is distinct from the Book of Mormon prophet "Alma" — hence the "(behavioral health)" disambiguator.
