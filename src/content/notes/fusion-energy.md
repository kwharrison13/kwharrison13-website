**Context:** Fusion energy aims to generate power by fusing light atomic nuclei (as in the sun), in contrast to fission (splitting heavy nuclei) used in today's nuclear plants. It promises abundant, low-carbon baseload power with no long-lived radioactive waste and no meltdown risk, but sustained net-energy-positive commercial fusion has historically remained "decades away." The Oxford-linked startup referenced is likely First Light Fusion or Tokamak Energy, two UK firms pursuing unconventional approaches.
