# The Pasture

"The Pasture" is the [Robert Frost](/notes/robert-frost) poem Kyle uses as his model for fatherhood. In his prep for the September 13th, 2022 — Harry Stebbings Podcast, Kyle planned, when asked about great fatherhood, to "drop some Robert Frost" and quote the poem — advice he received from a father-figure years earlier. The line he returns to is the refrain: **"I shan't be gone long — You come too."**

Kyle reads the poem as the heart of parenting: bringing your kids *along* for the journey rather than living your life in spite of them. He pairs it with the idea that a parent plays different roles over time — first caretaker, then coach, then counselor — and that, against an "incredibly me-centric world," his life *includes* his kids, it isn't lived around them.

**Context:** "The Pasture" by Robert Frost (1874–1963) opens his first collection, *A Boy's Will* (1913). Its two stanzas invite a companion to "come too" while the speaker tends the farm — a short, plainspoken lyric of invitation.
