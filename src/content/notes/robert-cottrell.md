# Robert Cottrell

**One-line:** *Editor of The Browser; reads ~1,000 articles a day to curate a handful of pieces of lasting value — profiled in [The Man Who Reads 1,000 Articles a Day](/notes/the-man-who-reads-1-000-articles-a-day).*

## What we know

- "I'm able to read a thousand pieces of journalism a day, which I think makes me the most read person in the world. Not the best read, mind you, but the most read." (source: [The Man Who Reads 1,000 Articles a Day](/notes/the-man-who-reads-1-000-articles-a-day)).
- Looks for pieces of *lasting value* — ones that share interesting ideas, deploy strong arguments, and have particularly fine writing (typically first person).
- Puts heavy trust in headlines as a signal; if a piece doesn't start well, he abandons it. Saves the best pieces in Bear Notes and selects ~5 for his newsletter.
