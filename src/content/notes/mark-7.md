# Mark 7

Mark 7 is Kyle's key reference for the **law vs. tradition** distinction in Jewish Leaders During The Time of Christ. The framing question — keeping the Sabbath holy is *law*; counting steps is *tradition* — turns on **Mark 7:9**: "full well ye reject the commandments of God, that ye may keep your own tradition." Kyle reads the "fences around the law" (like an alcoholic avoiding a bar) as sensible in spirit but as condemnation when made universally binding. This grounds the note's broader map of the Pharisees, Sadducees, and other groups contending around Christ's ministry.

In Miscellaneous Scriptures, **Mark 7:27–28** appears in a chain of cross-references on the gospel going first to Israel and then to the Gentiles ([2 Nephi 26](/notes/2-nephi-26):33 → [Matthew 10](/notes/matthew-10):6 → [Matthew 15](/notes/matthew-15):28 → Mark 7:27–28 → [D&C 50](/notes/dandc-50):30).

**Context:** Mark 7 records Christ's confrontation with the Pharisees over ceremonial washing and tradition, His teaching on what truly defiles a person, and the healing of the Syrophoenician woman's daughter.
