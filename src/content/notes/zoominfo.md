# ZoomInfo

The dominant B2B contact/company-data vendor that recurs in Kyle's notes as the canonical [Sales Intelligence](/notes/sales-intelligence) comp — listed alongside [6sense](/notes/6sense), [Bombora](/notes/bombora), [Crayon](/notes/crayon), [Klue](/notes/klue), and [Slintel](/notes/slintel) (September 13th, 2022 — Nisha Swarup). In Kyle's [Tegus](/notes/tegus) pre-brief, ZoomInfo is the central public comparable for the bull case: at a ~$26B market cap on ~$700M ARR, it's the proof point that "Tegus built... in sales" the way [Gartner](/notes/gartner) built a $4B data business in IT. Kyle modeled Tegus's path partly off ZoomInfo's shape — a long tail of five-figure deals, ~$30K ACVs, 500+ customers at $100K+, and roughly 20K logos — and noted that Henry Schuck, ZoomInfo's CEO (and DiscoverOrg co-founder), had recently joined the Tegus board (Tegus - Pre-Brief — September 27th, 2021).

ZoomInfo also surfaces as a real-world tool whose limits create openings: in the CaptivateIQ Diligence — 2021 notes, an operator using ZoomInfo for lead/contact data "has really disliked them more and more" as the company grew, and ZoomInfo's $1K+ pricing is cited as the high end against which lower-ARPU sales tools are benchmarked (CaptivateIQ Diligence — 2021).

**Context:** ZoomInfo (Nasdaq: ZI) is a go-to-market intelligence platform providing B2B contact and company data, intent signals, and sales/marketing workflow tools; it went public in 2020.
