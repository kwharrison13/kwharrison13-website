# Mosiah

In Kyle's Come Follow Me study of [Alma 17](/notes/alma-17)–[Alma 22](/notes/alma-22) (Alma 17–22 — I Will Make an Instrument of Thee), Mosiah is the Nephite king whose sons — [Ammon](/notes/ammon), [Aaron](/notes/aaron), and their brethren — give up their right to the throne to undertake missionary work among the Lamanites. The study notes that none of the sons of Mosiah were willing to take the kingdom; Aaron, who was next in line, had gone to the land of Nephi instead (Mosiah 29:2-3). Their motive, recorded in the study, was that "salvation should be declared to every creature, for they could not bear that any human soul should perish" (Mosiah 28:3). His record is also the source of the much-cited service teaching: "when ye are in the service of your fellow beings ye are only in the service of your God" (Mosiah 2:17).

**Context:** Mosiah is a name carried by two kings in the Book of Mormon (Mosiah I and Mosiah II) and is also the title of one of its books. Mosiah II, the king referenced here, abolished the Nephite monarchy in favor of a system of judges; his four sons became the missionaries chronicled in Alma 17–22.

## Where this appears

- Alma 17–22 — I Will Make an Instrument of Thee — the missionary sons of Mosiah renounce the throne to preach to the Lamanites; his record supplies the "service of your God" passage.
