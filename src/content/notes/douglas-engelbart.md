# Douglas Engelbart

Engelbart surfaces in Kyle's August 18th, 2021 — Valued Empyrean (Conor White-Sullivan & Darwin Harrison) meeting notes as a touchstone for the [Tools For Thought](/notes/tools-for-thought) thread that [Conor White-Sullivan](/notes/conor-white-sullivan) brought to the conversation. The notes summarize "the Engelbart Hypothesis": his model of continuous improvement structured as three nested groups — **Group A** doing direct-level work (building bridges, teaching math), **Group B** improvement groups leveling people up, and **Group C** improving the improvement processes themselves. Kyle's notes capture the framing that the real leverage comes from "frameworks for taking the improvement processes to the working groups" — i.e. bootstrapping.

The notes also trace Engelbart's arc: originally DoD-funded, he created the mouse and hypertext and gave the "Mother of all demos" that "created personal computing," but lost funding, sold to an aircraft group, and saw the work fall apart — a lineage that "led to [Xerox PARC](/notes/xerox-parc)." Conor's shared resources included [Ted Nelson](/notes/ted-nelson)'s eulogy for Engelbart and material from dougengelbart.org.

**Context:** Douglas Engelbart (1925–2013) was an American engineer and inventor who pioneered human–computer interaction. His 1968 "Mother of All Demos" introduced the computer mouse, hypertext, video conferencing, and collaborative real-time editing, foundational to modern personal computing.
