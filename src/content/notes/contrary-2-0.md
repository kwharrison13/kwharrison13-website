# Contrary 2.0

[Eric Tarczynski](/notes/eric-tarczynski)'s March 2022 internal strategy memo marking Contrary's inflection point heading into its most transformative year. The thesis: the firm had reached a position where it had the resources and infrastructure to "scale and do nearly everything we want to do" — going from a team of seven managing ~$100M across three core funds (with top-decile performance and a recognized emerging brand) toward a projected ~30–40 people. Tarczynski's frame was that the foundation laid "right now — with the first 10 hires — is going to define who we are in 3/5/10+ years from now."

The memo defines three pillars for the next generation of venture firms: **Technology** (a world-class internal engineering and data science team to identify the best people and companies first, then applied to Fellowship and investment teams), **Talent/Community** (community as Contrary's "secret sauce" and most powerful moat — VPs, Fellows, Alums — paired with the lagging Talent/Startup Search effort and the "Talent Terminal" database vision), and **Distribution** (content/media, explicitly modeling Andreessen Horowitz's "maniacal focus on distribution" and the insight that "the firm that combines loud and sharp thinkers is the firm that wins"). It then lays out concrete operational changes for scaling from a seven-person team to an enduring institution: clearer ownership and single-decision-maker decision-making, weekly team-lead syncs, quarterly performance reviews scored on culture-fit and performance, structured onboarding, and upgraded HR practices. The recurring theme is candor about past failures (Startup Search "largely a failure over the past 12 months," a tendency to keep "good but not great" people) and a "relentless focus on speed and execution."
