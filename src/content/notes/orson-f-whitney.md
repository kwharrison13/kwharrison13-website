# Orson F. Whitney

An early LDS apostle whose lines recur across Kyle's gospel study as quotable distillations of doctrine. In [Chad's Big Quote Book](/notes/chad-s-big-quote-book) he is the source of a forceful passage on living revelation over scripture as the church's foundation: "No book presides over this church and no book lies at its foundation… You cannot pile books enough to take the place of God's priesthood inspired by the power of the Holy Ghost." In The Reasons The Spirit Withdraws he anchors the affliction-as-purification theme: "No pain that we suffer, no trial that we experience is wasted… It is through sorrow and suffering, toil and tribulation, that we gain the education that we came here to acquire." In the 1 Nephi 16–22 study he is quoted (via [Jeffrey R. Holland](/notes/jeffrey-r-holland)) on optimism: "The spirit of the gospel is optimistic; it trusts in God and looks on the bright side of things." He also surfaces in General Conference — October 2021 with the counsel "Pray for your children. Hold onto them with your faith."

**Context:** Orson F. Whitney (1855–1931) was a poet, historian, and member of the Quorum of the Twelve Apostles of the Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-day Saints, often cited for his sermons on suffering, faith, and the redemption of family members.
