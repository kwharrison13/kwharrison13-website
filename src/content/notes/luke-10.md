# Luke 10

A chapter of the Gospel of Luke that Kyle's gospel-study notes cite in two distinct registers. In Abrahamic Covenant, Luke 10 appears alongside [Mark 3](/notes/mark-3):13–15, [Luke 6](/notes/luke-6):13, and [John 15](/notes/john-15):16 as scriptural evidence that specific followers of Christ were chosen as Apostles — chosen by Christ himself, not self-appointed. In Born Again, the chapter's Parable of the Good Samaritan (Luke 10:25–37) is read typologically: the traveler stripped of his raiment on the road from Jerusalem stands for each person ravished by mortality, and the true Good Samaritan is Jesus Christ, "wounded for our transgressions," through whom one's garments and covenants are purified.

**Context:** Luke 10 in the New Testament includes Jesus sending out the seventy disciples, the parable of the Good Samaritan, and the account of Mary and Martha.

## Where this appears

- Abrahamic Covenant — cited (with Mark 3, Luke 6, John 15) as evidence that Apostles were specifically chosen by Christ.
- Born Again — the Good Samaritan parable (10:25–37) read as a type of Christ purifying the covenant-keeper.
