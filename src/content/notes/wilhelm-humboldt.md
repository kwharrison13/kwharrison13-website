# Wilhelm Humboldt

In [Reinventing Knowledge — From Alexandria To The Internet](/books/reinventing-knowledge-from-alexandria-to-the-internet), Wilhelm Humboldt is presented as **the architect of the modern research university**. The book contrasts the two Humboldt brothers: Wilhelm "spent his life at a writing desk, drawing up the blueprints for Prussia's educational system," writing treatises on comparative linguistics and serving as Prussian ambassador in Rome, Vienna, and London — while his younger brother [Alexander Humboldt](/notes/alexander-humboldt) became the world-traveling naturalist. The book treats Wilhelm's "comprehensive, integrated, tiered system of teaching and research" as the institutional template that united teaching with research, and frames the modern disciplines' retreat from pedagogy as their "most consequential departure" from the system Wilhelm von Humboldt established.

**Context:** Wilhelm von Humboldt (1767–1835) was a Prussian philosopher, linguist, and statesman who founded the University of Berlin (now Humboldt University) in 1810. His model — uniting research and teaching under principles of academic freedom — became the template for the modern research university worldwide.
