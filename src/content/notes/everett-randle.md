# Everett Randle

Investor and writer — at [Founders Fund](/notes/founders-fund) per Kyle's productization survey — whose essay "Playing Different Games" became a recurring reference in Kyle's venture-structure thinking. Randle coined "The Dead Zone": the bind facing firms that get stuck in the medium-fund, medium-team middle and fail to differentiate, a framing Kyle invokes in his Stebbings podcast notes as the future trouble spot for undifferentiated venture firms.

Randle's "Playing Different Games" middle-squeeze thesis is load-bearing for Kyle's [The Puritans of Venture Capital](/essays/the-puritans-of-venture-capital) argument that venture is "(at least) three" games, where the [Benchmark](/notes/benchmark) "Puritans" and the AUM-maximizing capital agglomerators play by different return thresholds and the middle gets spewed out. Kyle's research flags one specific call as wrong — Randle named [Tiger](/notes/tiger-global-management) as the archetypal low-cost vendor, but Tiger turned out not to be Walmart — while holding that the underlying phenomenon (a hollowing middle) still holds. Kyle captured that nuance in [Playing Different (Stupider) Games](/essays/player-different-stupider-games).

**Context:** Everett Randle is a venture investor known for his Substack essay "Playing Different Games" (2021), which argued that venture firms compete in distinct strategic "games" and that the undifferentiated middle is most exposed.

In October 2022 Kyle noted Randle **moving from [Founders Fund](/notes/founders-fund) to [Kleiner Perkins](/notes/kleiner-perkins)** (source: October 17th, 2022 — Everett Randle to Kleiner Perkins).
