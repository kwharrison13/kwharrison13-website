# Robert Greene

Greene is cited twice in [Discipline Is Destiny](/books/discipline-is-destiny). First on restraint as power: *"Powerful people impress and intimidate by saying less"* — Holiday's gloss being that with power comes license to say whatever you want, and that it's the discipline *not* to that creates a powerful person's presence. Second, the book invokes Greene's idea of *"formlessness"* — describing him as a *"great strategist"* — when asking whether a figure would become a prisoner of his method or break through into the adaptive, unfixed posture Greene later named.

**Context:** Robert Greene is an American author of strategy and power books, including *The 48 Laws of Power*, *The 33 Strategies of War* (the source of "formlessness"), *Mastery*, and *The Laws of Human Nature*.

## Where this appears

- [Discipline Is Destiny](/books/discipline-is-destiny) — cited on "powerful people impress by saying less" and on the strategic ideal of "formlessness."
