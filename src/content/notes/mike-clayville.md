# Mike Clayville

Sales executive cited in The Talent Vortex - Research as the inflection point when [Stripe](/notes/stripe) began building toward an enterprise-ready sales organization. The survey note observes that Stripe's "organizational muscle around sales is generally underdeveloped" — they "went through 3 CROs in as many years" and "didn't really start building toward enterprise-ready until Mike Clayville joined in late 2020." The same passage frames Stripe's historical sales-talent gap: top salespeople "steered clear because the comp system didn't offer the outperformance > comp that heavyweights get elsewhere," and Stripe's salespeople were described as "mental athletes" who may or may not have had a sales background.

**Context:** Mike Clayville joined Stripe as Chief Revenue Officer in late 2020, coming from AWS where he had led worldwide commercial sales; he was brought in specifically to scale Stripe's enterprise go-to-market.
