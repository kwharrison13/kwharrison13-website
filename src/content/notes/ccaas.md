# CCaaS

CCaaS (Contact-Center-as-a-Service) is the cloud platform layer that runs a contact center — [Five9](/notes/five9), [Genesys](/notes/genesys), [Amazon Connect](/notes/amazon-connect), [NICE](/notes/nice) inContact — and in Kyle's notes it is mostly the **consolidation threat** hanging over the agent-assist category. The Cresta.ai - Diligence returns to this question repeatedly: multiple experts expect a CCaaS platform to buy one of Cresta / [Balto.ai](/notes/balto-ai) / [Observe.ai](/notes/observe-ai) and fold real-time agent coaching into the toolkit, since the platforms already own the agent's workflow and several have coaching layers that merely lack the real-time component. ([Cresta.ai](/notes/cresta-ai) itself runs on Five9 as its CCaaS.) The recurring framing is the [All-in-One vs. Best-in-Class](/notes/all-in-one-vs-best-in-class) tension: the larger and more customization-hungry the buyer, the more the integrated platform (e.g. Amazon Connect) appeals; mid-market favors a best-of-breed point solution like Cresta; the smallest buyers want "one throat to choke."

The grounded counterweight in the notes is timing and quality: less than 5% of the market was adopting real-time agent assist at the time, the platforms hadn't shipped native versions, and experts note acquisitions in this space "typically aren't executed well" — leaving an open window for standalone vendors before the CCaaS layer absorbs the category. The open diligence question is explicitly: how does Cresta fare if a CCaaS platform ships native agent-assist?

**Context:** CCaaS denotes cloud-delivered contact-center software (routing, IVR, omnichannel, workforce management, analytics) sold as a subscription rather than on-prem PBX hardware. Major vendors include Five9, Genesys, Amazon Connect, NICE/inContact, and RingCentral.
