# Carl Braun

An engineer Charlie Munger praises in [Charlie Munger — The Psychology of Human Misjudgment](/notes/charlie-munger-the-psychology-of-human-misjudgment) as designing oil refineries "with spectacular skill and integrity." Munger holds up Braun's communication rule — that every message specify the who, what, where, when, and why — as a model of clear, accountable practice in his Teutonic company.

**Context:** Carl F. Braun (1884–1954) was the founder of the C.F. Braun engineering and construction company, known for its rigorous standards of clarity and communication, which Munger frequently cited.
