# Indulgences

In Kyle's November 2–8, 2020. Mormon 7–9: "I Speak unto You As If Ye Were Present" study, "Indulgences" is the heading he attaches to Moroni's prophecy in Mormon 8:32 — "it shall come in a day when there shall be churches built up that shall say: Come unto me, and for your money you shall be forgiven of your sins." Kyle reads this as Moroni, looking forward through vision to "our day," warning against the buying and selling of forgiveness.

**Context:** Indulgences were grants offered by the medieval Catholic Church purporting to remit punishment for sin; their commercialized sale was a central grievance of Martin Luther's 1517 Ninety-Five Theses and a catalyst of the Protestant Reformation. The label here maps that historical practice onto the scripture's image of churches that promise forgiveness in exchange for money.
