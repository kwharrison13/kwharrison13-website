# Science and Religion

In Kyle's wiki, "Science and Religion" names the harmonization thread that runs through [Faith is Not Blind](/books/faith-is-not-blind) (Bruce C. and Marie K. Hafen) and its companion arguments: the claim that empirical science and revealed religion are not adversaries but different instruments, and that science — used honestly — can model the right *posture* for faith rather than displace it. The book offers science writer [Christie Aschwanden](/notes/christie-aschwanden) as the key text: science "can increase or decrease our confidence in some propositions, but it can't produce 'absolute certainty.' Instead, 'it's a process of uncertainty reduction.'" That framing maps onto belief that reduces uncertainty without demanding proof. The Hafens pair it with [Francis Collins](/notes/francis-collins), who led the human-genome mapping and called the code "the language in which God created life," arguing that "the principles of faith are…complementary with the principles of science" — and with the fine-tuned "physical constants" argument ([God is a Scientist](/notes/god-is-a-scientist)) that reads cosmic order as reason *pointing toward* the divine.

The concept sits adjacent to but is distinct from [Faith and Reason](/notes/faith-and-reason): where Faith and Reason is about reason's ceiling (faith "ultimately takes us beyond the boundaries of reason," so conditioning belief on rationality risks "shrink[ing] back from a sanctifying experience"), Science and Religion is the narrower claim that the scientific method itself — provisional, evidence-welcoming, uncertainty-reducing — is a *complement* to honest faith rather than a rival to it. [Faith is Not Blind](/books/faith-is-not-blind) also names the failure mode: the "merchants of doubt" who "amplify uncertainty…and undermine scientific discoveries that threaten their interests" (the tobacco-industry playbook) — manufactured doubt masquerading as the genuine article.

**Context:** "Science and religion" is a long-running theme in religious intellectualism, with figures like Francis Collins (geneticist, author of *The Language of God*) and, in a Latter-day Saint frame, chemist Henry Eyring frequently cited as models of reconciling empirical science with faith.

## Where this appears

- [Faith is Not Blind](/books/faith-is-not-blind) — the Hafens' argument that faith and the principles of science are complementary; Christie Aschwanden on "uncertainty reduction," Francis Collins on the genome as "the language in which God created life," and the "merchants of doubt" failure mode.
- [Faith and Reason](/notes/faith-and-reason) — the sibling concept: reason has a ceiling, and faith eventually moves beyond it; science-as-honest-belief is one resolution of that tension.
- [Christie Aschwanden](/notes/christie-aschwanden) — the science writer quoted on science as "a process of uncertainty reduction."
