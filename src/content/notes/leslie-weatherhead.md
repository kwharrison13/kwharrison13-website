# Leslie Weatherhead

Leslie Weatherhead appears in the wiki as the attributed author of one passage in [Chad's Big Quote Book](/notes/chad-s-big-quote-book) (page 85–86, "The Blessed and Happy State"). The quote argues that sin makes life harder than virtue — describing the physical, restless agony of doing wrong versus the "sense of well-being," peace, and "triumphant sense of victory" that follows when "you deliberately chose good and smashed it in the face of evil... it is easy to do right and hard to do wrong if only you look far enough ahead." It sits among the collection's recurring themes of repentance, integrity, and choosing the harder right.

**Context:** Leslie Weatherhead (1893–1976) was an English Methodist minister and author known for preaching that bridged Christian theology and psychology; his best-known book is *The Will of God*. (General background, outside the wiki sources.)
