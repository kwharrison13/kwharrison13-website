# Book in a Box

The "done-for-you" book-writing company Tucker Max launched in 2015 (later renamed Scribe Writing / Scribe Media). In Kyle's notes it is the canonical example of a productized-service "pickax" play on the publishing gold rush: rather than write more bestsellers himself, Max sold the shovel. The [Publishing](/notes/publishing) and [Self-Publishing](/notes/self-publishing) concept pages both cite Book in a Box as their illustrating case — a service that turns a client's interview answers into a published book in their voice, productizing the desire to be a published author (for thought-leadership and higher consulting/speaking fees) for clients who lack the time or expertise to do it themselves.

**Context:** Book in a Box was co-founded by author Tucker Max and Zach Obront in 2014–2015; rebranded to Scribe Media, it built a ghostwriting/publishing service model around guided author interviews. The company drew attention (and later turbulence) as a productized professional-services business.

## Quick Capture note — April 30th, 2020

_Saved in Kyle's April 30th, 2020 Roam "Quick Capture" log (tagged #[Publishing](/notes/publishing) #[Self-Publishing](/notes/self-publishing) #[Book in a Box](/notes/book-in-a-box))._

> Tucker Max decoded a pickax to book publishing when he became an author. He realized there's a huge market of people (gold miners) who want to establish themselves as thought leaders so they can charge higher consulting and speaking fees (gold mine). Publishing a book is a silver bullet to accomplishing that. The problem: most people don't have the time or expertise required to become an author. Max immediately spotted his pickax cash cow and got to work launching Book in a Box (now Scribe Writing) in 2015. Since then the company has worked with more than five hundred authors and passed $11.3M in revenue as of September 2017. They charge clients a minimum of $25K to write their book for them, in their voice. All the client has to do is answer a few interview questions. Max realized that Book in a Box had the potential to be more lucrative than his own best-selling books, which is why he spends more time on the business these days than on his own writing.
