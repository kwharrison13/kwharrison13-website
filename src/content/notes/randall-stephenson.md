# Randall Stephenson

In [The Expertise Economy](/books/the-expertise-economy), Stephenson is quoted (via the New York Times) as a load-bearing voice on continuous learning and the risk of obsolescence: *"There is a need to retool yourself, and you should not expect to stop."* He added that people who dedicate less than an ideal 5–10 hours a week to learning *"will obsolete themselves with the technology."* The book uses the line to argue that ongoing skill development — across articles, books, podcasts, and videos — is non-optional in a fast-moving economy, and that people often learn more than they realize without categorizing it as learning.

**Context:** Randall Stephenson is the former chairman and CEO of AT&T, where he led the company from 2007 to 2020 through acquisitions including DirecTV and Time Warner.

## Where this appears

- [The Expertise Economy](/books/the-expertise-economy) — quoted on the need to "retool yourself" and dedicate 5–10 hours/week to learning to avoid obsolescence.
