# Hollywood

In Kyle's wiki, Hollywood is the setting for the [Intellectual Property](/notes/intellectual-property) / "IP Era" debate. The framing on that page is that the dominant Hollywood business model has shifted from *creating* something to *owning* something forever — "It's not about creating something, it's about owning something, forever." Since 2014, every year has been dominated by franchises and sequels, with a counter-history (via the 1930 *Tom Sawyer* adaptation of [Mark Twain](/notes/mark-twain)) arguing "Hollywood Has Always Banked on I.P." The pro-IP case holds that "IP isn't the problem. It's the solution for Hollywood" because it reliably makes money; the critique links it to [Monoculture](/notes/monoculture) and the homogenization of cultural output.

**Context:** Hollywood is the Los Angeles district and global metonym for the American commercial film and television industry — the major studios and the franchise/blockbuster economics that dominate theatrical box office.
