# Confluence

Confluence is [Atlassian](/notes/atlassian)'s team-wiki / documentation product, and in Kyle's diligence notes it shows up almost entirely as the **incumbent that everyone wants to replace**. In the Noname Security - Diligence notes it is named alongside [Jira](/notes/jira) and [Jenkins](/notes/jenkins) as part of the entrenched CI/collaboration ecosystem "ripe for disruption" — they'll "do anything you need them to do at pretty significant cost." In the Sprig fka UserLeap - Diligence expert call, [Joseph Ambeault](/notes/joseph-ambeault) is blunter: for knowledge management he favors [Notion](/notes/notion) ("think Sharepoint but that doesn't suck") and calls Atlassian's Confluence "terrible." Both the [Atlassian](/notes/atlassian) and [Notion](/notes/notion) company pages carry the same verdict — Confluence dismissed as the weak knowledge-management incumbent against which Notion is positioned.

The recurring thesis behind these mentions is the [Figma](/notes/figma)-vs-[Adobe](/notes/adobe) pattern: the question "who disrupts Atlassian?" assumes that a collaboration-first tool, tightly connected to where people already do their work, eventually unseats the incumbent — and Confluence is the part of the Atlassian stack most exposed to that.

**Context:** Confluence is a wiki-style collaboration and documentation tool from Atlassian (the company behind Jira, Trello, and Bitbucket), used by teams for internal knowledge bases, project documentation, and meeting notes.
