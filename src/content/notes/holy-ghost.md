# Holy Ghost

The richest treatment in Kyle's notes is [Boyd K. Packer](/notes/boyd-k-packer)'s The Candle of the Lord — Boyd K. Packer, which describes how the Spirit communicates and why it operates by a different epistemology than ordinary learning. Packer's throughline: spiritual things are real and knowable, but "the Spirit speaks in a feeling, not a sound" — "a voice that one feels, more than one hears" — and the Still Small Voice whispers rather than shouts. You "can no more force the Spirit to respond than you can force a bean to sprout," and counterfeit spirits are a real danger because emotion and the spiritual are so closely linked that "the devil may come disguised as an angel of light."

A recurring theme is that the Spirit must be earned and can be lost. The Reasons The Spirit Withdraws frames it as a gap between the law's level and your behavior's level: when behavior sinks, that's sin, and the Spirit leaves — the remedy being continual repentance and continual learning. Reception is also active, not passive: per [David A. Bednar](/notes/david-a-bednar), "a learner exercising agency by acting in accordance with correct principles opens his or her heart to the Holy Ghost," and "he that diligently seeketh shall find" the mysteries of God "by the power of the Holy Ghost" (1 Nephi 10). The Spirit is thus the channel of personal [Revelation](/notes/revelation) — "spiritual confirmation in our heart and our mind."

**Context:** In Latter-day Saint theology the Holy Ghost is the third member of the Godhead, a personage of spirit whose role is to testify of truth, comfort, and sanctify; "the gift of the Holy Ghost" is conferred by ordinance after baptism.

## Where this appears

- The Candle of the Lord — Boyd K. Packer — how the Spirit is recognized and received: a feeling not a sound, the still small voice, guarding against counterfeit spirits
- The Reasons The Spirit Withdraws — sin as the gap that costs you the Spirit; repentance and learning as the remedy
- Follow the Doctrine and Gospel of Christ — M. Russell Ballard — the Spirit in the doctrine of Christ
