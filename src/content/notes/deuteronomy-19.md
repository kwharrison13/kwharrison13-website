# Deuteronomy 19

An Old Testament chapter Kyle cites in his topical study Publication of the Book of Mormon for the **law of witnesses**. He references [Deuteronomy 19](/notes/deuteronomy-19):15 (alongside [2 Corinthians 13](/notes/2-corinthians-13):1) when asking what the idea of "bearing record" has to do with the law of witnesses and "triangulation" — the principle that a matter is established at the mouth of two or three witnesses. Kyle uses this as a frame for how the testimonies of Oliver Cowdery, Martin Harris, and Emma corroborate the historical fact of the Book of Mormon's translation.

**Context:** Deuteronomy 19 sets out Mosaic law on cities of refuge, boundary markers, and judicial witnesses; verse 15 establishes that "at the mouth of two witnesses, or at the mouth of three witnesses, shall the matter be established," the basis for the scriptural "law of witnesses."
