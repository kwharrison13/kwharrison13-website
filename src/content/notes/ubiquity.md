# Ubiquity

In Kyle's Historical Futurism - Research notes, **ubiquity** is one of the two properties that determine whether an invention persists — the other being [Incentives](/notes/incentives). These are "the only two concepts Kyle keeps coming back to for why an invention persists." The test is a thought experiment: what would it take for Coca-Cola to disappear? Something massive, because it is ubiquitous — and the same goes for Google, Apple, Band-Aids, and Kleenex. Their pervasiveness makes them durable and therefore predictable.

The flip side is that **technologies that have not yet achieved ubiquity are hard to forecast**. Google Glass, Pelotons, and VR are named as examples that resist prediction precisely because they have not become woven into everyday life. The concept thus serves as a filter for [Forecasting](/notes/forecasting): what is ubiquitous is safe to extrapolate; what is not is speculative.
