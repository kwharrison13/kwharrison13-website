# Financial Literacy

In Kyle's corpus, financial literacy is a theme he associates with [John Hoffmire](/notes/john-hoffmire), who is candidly pessimistic about how fast it can spread. In their August 2nd, 2014 Oxford conversation, Hoffmire framed it with a memorable analogy: financial literacy is "like weight loss" — you're "selling discipline people don't enjoy, for a long time," so "it'll be a long time before financial literacy is something everyone wants." He added that the financial crisis was a missed teaching moment: "I think we've wasted a perfectly good recession." The takeaway is that adoption is gated less by curriculum than by demand — employers won't push something employees dislike, and people opt out of unglamorous discipline.

**Context:** Financial literacy refers to the practical knowledge and skills needed to manage money — budgeting, saving, debt, investing, and understanding financial products — and is a recurring policy and education concern given widespread evidence of low household financial knowledge.
