# Karl Marx

Marx appears in [Double Entry](/books/double-entry) only as part of one of the book's signature ironies: that Venetian double-entry bookkeeping, by guiding the British factories of the Industrial Revolution, "directly funded two of the great revolutionary thinkers of the nineteenth century." The first is [Charles Darwin](/notes/charles-darwin), funded through his grandfather [Josiah Wedgwood](/notes/josiah-wedgwood)'s Etruria pottery works; the second is Marx, funded through the Engels family's cotton mills of Manchester — i.e., the capital that supported Marx's work was itself produced by the account-driven capitalist machinery he set out to critique.

**Context:** Karl Marx (1818–1883) was a German philosopher, economist, and revolutionary socialist, author of *The Communist Manifesto* and *Das Kapital*. He was financially supported for much of his life by Friedrich Engels, whose family owned textile mills in Manchester.
