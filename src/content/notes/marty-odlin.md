# Marty Odlin

Founder of [Running Tide](/notes/running-tide), the ocean-based [Carbon Removal](/notes/carbon-removal) startup, who surfaces in Kyle's Watershed Climate Diligence — 2021 dossier. Odlin spoke at Watershed's 2021 Climate Week event in the "Frontiers of Carbon Removal" breakout, where Running Tide was paired with [Heirloom](/notes/heirloom) (represented by [Shashank Samala](/notes/shashank-samala) and [Max Scholten](/notes/max-scholten)) as one of "two cutting-edge carbon removal startups" whose founders chatted with the climate leads at [Stripe](/notes/stripe) and [Shopify ($SHOP)](/notes/shopify) — buyers who had collectively kickstarted the market with $10M+ of purchasing commitments. The diligence note frames Running Tide not as a "speculative non-profit" but as a tech company "with customers, investors, deliverables—and competitors," with energy efficiency (power burned per tonne removed) as the key metric.

**Context:** Marty Odlin is the founder of Running Tide, a Maine-based ocean carbon-removal company that used kelp and other ocean-based methods to sequester carbon; the company wound down operations in 2024.
