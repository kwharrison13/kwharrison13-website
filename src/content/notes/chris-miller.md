# Chris Miller

Author of [Chip War](/books/chip-war), the history of the semiconductor industry that frames the US-China rivalry as a contest decided by computing power rather than steel or atomic weapons. In Kyle's notes the book functions less as a standalone semiconductor history than as evidence collection for a defense-tech ([Anduril](/notes/anduril)) investment thesis — Miller's accounts of [Morris Chang](/notes/morris-chang) leaving [Texas Instruments](/notes/texas-instruments) to found TSMC, of monopolies (Bell Labs, TSMC) as innovation engines, and of China's civil-military fusion doctrine are mined as supporting facts.

**Context:** Chris Miller is an economic historian at Tufts University's Fletcher School; *Chip War: The Fight for the World's Most Critical Technology* (2022) won the Financial Times Business Book of the Year.
