# Helaman 10

A chapter in the Book of Mormon that, across Kyle's study notes, anchors two linked themes: [Faith](/notes/faith) proven by obedience, and revelation that comes through pondering. Its pivotal verses are Helaman 10:4–5, where the Lord blesses Nephi: "thou hast not feared them, and hast not sought thine own life, but hast sought my will… I will make thee mighty in word and in deed, in faith and in works; yea, even that all things shall be done unto thee according to thy word, for thou shalt not ask that which is contrary to my will." In Faith — Topical Study Kyle reads this as the mechanism of faith maturing into power: "the power comes in knowing God's will and then following it," with great things beginning only after near-perfect obedience.

The chapter also feeds Christ Defines His Gospel, cited (10:3–5) for "seek not your own life but His will," and it underwrites the August 24–30, 2020. Helaman 7–12: "Remember the Lord" notes' point that "pondering invites revelation" — the voice comes to Nephi (10:2–4) as he is "pondering upon the things which the Lord had shown unto him." It recurs as a cross-reference in June 22–28, 2020. Alma 17–22: "I Will Make an Instrument of Thee", tying Nephi's instrument-of-God role to Ammon's.

**Context:** Helaman 10 records the prophet Nephi (son of Helaman) receiving the sealing power from God after faithfully declaring repentance to a wicked Nephite people — a chapter Latter-day Saints frequently cite for the relationship between submission to God's will and spiritual power.
