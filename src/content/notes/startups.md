# Startups

In Kyle's wiki, "startups" is the culture and category most tightly bound to [Paul Graham](/notes/paul-graham) — the [Y Combinator](/notes/y-combinator) co-founder cast as the defining voice of modern startup culture. The arc traced through [The Power Law — Venture Capital & the Making of the New Future](/books/the-power-law-venture-capital-and-the-making-of-the-new-future) frames the early-2000s "hacker-founder youth revolt against traditional VC," where young entrepreneurs stopped deferring to experienced investors. Graham's quoted defense of startup hubs in [The New Urban Crisis](/books/the-new-urban-crisis) makes the political stakes explicit: "you can't prevent great variations in wealth without preventing people from getting rich, and you can't do that without preventing them from starting startups" — startup hubs as "manufacturers of inequality" that are nonetheless the price of progress.

The concept thus carries a normative charge in Kyle's reading: starting startups is treated as the engine of variation, wealth creation, and progress, even where that engine produces inequality.

**Context:** A startup is a young company built to grow fast, typically pursuing a scalable, repeatable business model — distinct from a small business by its growth ambition and (often) reliance on venture capital. Paul Graham's essays at paulgraham.com are among the most influential writing on the form.
