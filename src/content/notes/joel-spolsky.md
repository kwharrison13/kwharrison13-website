# Joel Spolsky

Software writer cited in [Remote — Office Not Required](/books/remote-office-not-required) for the hiring heuristic the authors lean on to make their case for remote work. In his "Guerrilla Guide to Interviewing," Spolsky distilled what to look for in a hire down to two qualities: "Smart, and Gets Things Done." [Remote — Office Not Required](/books/remote-office-not-required) argues that remote work simply exposes what was always true — "great remote workers are simply great workers" who exhibit exactly those two Spolsky qualities — so the framework becomes the book's lens for evaluating distributed talent.

**Context:** Joel Spolsky is a software developer and writer, author of the influential "Joel on Software" blog, and co-founder of Fog Creek Software, Trello, and Stack Overflow. His interviewing essays, including the "Smart, and Gets Things Done" formulation, were widely read in the early-2000s software-management world.
