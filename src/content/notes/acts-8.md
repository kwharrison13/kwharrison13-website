# Acts 8

A New Testament chapter cited in Kyle's Abrahamic Covenant topical study, in the section on "The Authority to Gather Israel" (Topical Journal 1.169). [Acts 8](/notes/acts-8):14–20 is read as the apostles laying on hands "for POWER" — part of the scripture chain establishing that the priesthood authority and power to administer the ordinances of salvation comes by ordained ministry, not by general belief. It sits alongside the study's broader argument (drawing on [Luke 10](/notes/luke-10), [Mark 3](/notes/mark-3), [John 15](/notes/john-15), and others) that Christ chose *specific* followers as Apostles rather than empowering everyone.

**Context:** Acts 8:14–20 recounts Peter and John traveling to Samaria to lay hands on the newly baptized so they might receive the Holy Ghost; Simon the sorcerer offers money for the power and is rebuked. It is a common LDS proof-text that conferral of the Holy Ghost requires authorized priesthood hands, and that this power cannot be bought.
