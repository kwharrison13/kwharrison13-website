# Forestry

Forestry surfaces in the corpus through Kyle's diligence on [NCX](/notes/ncx) (Natural Capital Exchange), a forest-carbon marketplace that pays landowners to defer timber harvests one year at a time to generate carbon credits. The diligence frames forestry as both NCX's market and its moat: roughly 60% of US forestry acreage by count is privately owned family land, but only ~45–50% of the players who actively move the timber market — and reaching forestry buyers means winning over an old-school, word-of-mouth community slow to trust newcomers. Expert calls came from career foresters: Paul Davis (40-year industrial forester, ex-Weyerhaeuser VP) and Jayk Graham (procurement forester, ex-Rayonier). The real moat, per the experts, is **distribution** — consultant relationships and brand trust in the forestry community — not the carbon science.

The December 13th, 2021 — NCX Partner Presentation reinforces that forestry is not a uniform commodity: mature pine next to a mill commands a very different price than remote timber, and the notion of permanence in forests "just isn't a reality" since "people gave up on steady-state ecologies in the 70s." NCX positioned itself as the only tech-enabled forestry partner in early forest-carbon deals (e.g. with Microsoft), with forestry-type credits representing ~50% of all issued voluntary carbon credits.

**Context:** Forestry is the science and practice of managing forests and woodlands, including timber harvesting, conservation, and — increasingly — forest-carbon markets that monetize standing trees as carbon sinks.
