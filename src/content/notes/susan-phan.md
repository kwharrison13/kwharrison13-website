# Susan Phan

Susan Phan is a [Dropbox](/notes/dropbox) alum (Strategic Finance) whose survey response in The Talent Vortex - Research gives one of the bluntest accounts of *why* the company concentrated talent. She attributes roughly 80% of it to momentum and prospects: "for a few years Dropbox was *the* fastest-growing startup and had a lot of buzz," and "the 'best' people gravitate to companies with the best prospects." The secondary factor, she says, was "a pretty elitist hiring culture" — snobbery over hiring engineers from certain schools (MIT, Stanford) or companies (Facebook); for the first year or two of StratFin, "the finance leader *only* wanted people who'd done IBD at Goldman and Morgan Stanley." Phan is careful to add she isn't endorsing the elitist strategy, but concludes that "a hyper-selective approach plus bonkers growth rates resulted in a healthy concentration of talent at Dropbox early on."

Her response is one of several Dropbox accounts (alongside [Ross Freiman-Mendel](/notes/ross-freiman-mendel), [Alex Yen](/notes/alex-yen), [Rachel Hansen](/notes/rachel-hansen), and [Paul Jun](/notes/paul-jun)) that fed the essay's recurring synthesis: a high bar plus runaway growth creates a self-reinforcing talent flywheel.
