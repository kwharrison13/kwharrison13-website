# Sir William Osler

Sir William Osler surfaces in Kyle's notes as a recurring source of aphorism inside [Charlie Munger — A Lesson on Elementary Worldly Wisdom, Revisited](/notes/charlie-munger-a-lesson-on-elementary-worldly-wisdom-revisited) (Talk 3 of [Poor Charlie's Almanack](/books/poor-charlie-s-almanack)). Munger quotes him twice. The first — *"The incessant concentration of thought upon one subject, however interesting, tethers a man's mind in a narrow field"* — Munger marshals in support of [Multidisciplinary Thinking](/notes/multidisciplinary-thinking), the warning against over-specialization. The second — *"The best that is known and taught in the world—nothing less can satisfy a teacher worthy of the name"* — anchors Munger's closing argument that one should master the best ideas other people have already figured out rather than try to dream everything up alone.

**Context:** Sir William Osler (1849–1919) was a Canadian physician, one of the founders of Johns Hopkins Hospital, and a pioneer of modern medical education; he is widely quoted for his essays and aphorisms on the practice of medicine and the life of the mind.
