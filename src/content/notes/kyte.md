# Kyte

Kyte is a car-rental / mobility startup and [Contrary](/notes/contrary) portfolio company that Kyle tracked from 2022 through its 2025 acquisition. Co-founder [Ludwig Schoenack](/notes/ludwig-schoenack) wrote its investor updates: the May 2022 update (read June 2022) reported revenue +23%, gross profit +33%, and net profit +270% across markets, with the company targeting free cash flow within ~12 months in a capital-efficient way. Kyle continued reading the Sep 2022 update from Schoenack while clearing his inbox that fall.

By the April 2025 update call, Kyte was being acquired by Vay (a remote-driving / driverless mobility company led by Thomas von der Ohe, ex-Zoox). Kyte's business at that point: ~$30M revenue, EBITDA breakeven as of Q2 2025, ~60% gross margins / ~30% net margins, and 8–9x LTV/CAC — but carrying a "defective balance sheet" with ~$33M in liabilities (Barclays, WAM, aged taxes/AP, Avidbank term loan), no cash on hand, and a borrowing-base deficiency. The deal had Vay acquiring 100% of Kyte's shares for a $3M convertible note (30% discount/cap) into Vay's Series C; no employee or founder options would receive proceeds. The strategic logic was a rental-duration ladder — Uber (0–1 hr trips), Vay (1–12 hr), Kyte (12 hr–multiday) — with Kyte's fleet (cycling ~1/3 per year) gradually adopting Vay's remote-driving technology.

**Context:** Kyte was a U.S. car-rental startup offering on-demand car delivery and pickup ("cars delivered to your door"); it was acquired by the German remote-driving company Vay.
