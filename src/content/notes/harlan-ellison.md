# Harlan Ellison

Harlan Ellison appears in Kyle's Historical Futurism - Research as the author of the short story "I Have No Mouth, and I Must Scream," listed among the canonical science-fiction works Kyle catalogs for the historical-futurism essay thread — alongside short stories by [Isaac Asimov](/notes/isaac-asimov), [Arthur C. Clarke](/notes/arthur-c-clarke), [Ray Bradbury](/notes/ray-bradbury), [Ted Chiang](/notes/ted-chiang), and [Philip K. Dick](/notes/philip-k-dick).

**Context:** Harlan Ellison (1934–2018) was an American writer best known for prolific, acerbic speculative fiction and screenwriting; "I Have No Mouth, and I Must Scream" (1967) is a widely anthologized dystopian story about a hostile supercomputer torturing the last humans.
