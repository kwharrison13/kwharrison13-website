# Figma

The collaborative design tool that functions as the canonical "collaboration-first disruption" archetype across Kyle's diligence work. The Sprig fka UserLeap - Diligence uses Figma as the load-bearing example of a thesis: it "blew up Sketch, [Invision](/notes/invision), and eventually [Adobe](/notes/adobe) (worth $10B+) by putting collaboration first but connecting it tightly to the creation workflow," and the open question becomes "who disrupts [Atlassian](/notes/atlassian)?" — "some company will do to Jira what Figma did to Adobe." The same pattern drives the Deepnote pre-brief, which pitches Deepnote as "the Figma for Data Scientists" and explicitly analogizes pre-Figma notebook sharing to "sharing photoshop files before Figma." Design-first products (Notion, Figma, Linear) are held up as the template for winning in collaborative software.

Figma also recurs as a **customer/reference account and a talent origin**. [Marie Szuts](/notes/marie-szuts) at Figma gave the customer-perspective deep dive for the Pave diligence on the messy state of comp-planning tooling (Compaas, ChartHop, Workday, spreadsheets). Merlinda Barrington at Figma was a reference for the CaptivateIQ Diligence — 2021. Figma is named as a marquee customer of [Sprig](/notes/sprig), and surfaces in product-tooling stack-rankings via [Joseph Ambeault](/notes/joseph-ambeault). As a talent thread, [Joel Kwartler](/notes/joel-kwartler) "found Figma when he was interning at [Microsoft](/notes/microsoft)" and contrasts Figma's lack of a [GitLab](/notes/gitlab)-style handbook (which "led to bumps in how different teams interact") in the Sourcegraph 1x1.

**Context:** Figma is a browser-based collaborative interface-design tool founded in 2012 by Dylan Field and Evan Wallace; its real-time multiplayer editing reshaped the design-tooling market and led to a (later-abandoned) $20B acquisition agreement with Adobe.
