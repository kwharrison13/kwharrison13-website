# Proverbs 14

Proverbs 14 is cited in Kyle's Patience — Topical Study for its verse on patience as a marker of understanding: "He that is slow to wrath is of great understanding" (Proverbs 14:29). The study uses it to argue that patience signals great understanding precisely because it has been *learned* — set against the natural-man impulse, "especially in our world today," to want everything and want it now. The contrast Kyle draws is the tower built in days that stands no longer than one built patiently.

**Context:** Proverbs 14 is a chapter of the biblical book of Proverbs, part of Hebrew wisdom literature traditionally ascribed to Solomon. It collects short, antithetical maxims contrasting the wise and the foolish; verse 29 links patience ("slow to wrath") with understanding.
