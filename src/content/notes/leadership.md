# Leadership

Leadership is a connective theme threaded through Kyle's reading, and the books that anchor it converge on a few claims. From [The Ride of a Lifetime](/books/the-ride-of-a-lifetime): "True authority and true leadership come from knowing who you are and not pretending to be anything else" — Bob Iger pairs humility with the refusal to fake knowledge, and Kyle tags [Intellectual Honesty](/notes/intellectual-honesty) ("they willfully avoided asking the hard questions") as the rare leadership virtue. [Turnaround](/books/turnaround) reduces it to two jobs: "the two most critical jobs of leadership are building a good team and keeping the teammates highly motivated." [Managers Not MBAs](/books/managers-not-mbas) supplies the contrarian edge — Mintzberg argues leadership *without* management is "disconnected and breeds hubris," that both are "life itself" learned in context rather than conferred by a degree, and warns against the "heroic" leader.

The corpus also pulls leadership toward conviction over consensus. [John Quincy Adams](/books/john-quincy-adams) embodies the tension between leading constituents and following them: Adams "took as a first principle that a leader's duty is to defend constituents' interests against their inclinations when necessary" — and paid for it with his presidency. [The Art of War](/books/the-art-of-war) locates accountability in the commander ("the general bears the blame for unclear orders") and catalogs the five dangerous faults. The Roam reading thread connects to Dee Hock, founder of [Visa](/notes/visakan-veerasamy), and his theory of "chaordic" (chaos + order) organizations — The Trillion-Dollar Vision of Dee Hock, which Kyle filed here.

**Context:** Leadership is the broad practice of directing, motivating, and organizing people toward shared goals. The management-literature debate Kyle's notes engage runs between "great man" / heroic framings and more situational, service-oriented models (servant leadership, the manager-as-craftsperson view), with intellectual honesty and team-building recurring as durable virtues.
