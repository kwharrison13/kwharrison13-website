# Minimum Viable Product

The minimum viable product (MVP) is the practice of shipping the smallest releasable version of a product and then iterating, rather than perfecting before launch. It recurs across three of Kyle's book notes. In [Product Led Growth — The End User Era](/books/product-led-growth-the-end-user-era) it is the core spine of PLG via [The Lean Startup](/books/the-lean-startup): "release an MVP and then focus on evolving it" to meet as many customer needs as possible, treating data and an experimentation-driven mindset as essential ingredients — one of two SaaS paths to becoming "remarkable." In [Amazon Unbound](/books/amazon-unbound), Amazon's Jeff Wilke reframes the term as "**minimum lovable product**" (MLP), asking "What would we be proud to take to the market?" — raising the bar past mere viability. In [Essentialism](/books/essentialism), [Greg McKeown](/notes/greg-mckeown) adapts it as "minimal viable progress," and models it himself by testing ideas on Twitter before writing the book.

**Context:** The MVP concept was popularized by Eric Ries in *[The Lean Startup](/books/the-lean-startup)*: a build-measure-learn loop where you ship a minimal product to validate (or falsify) hypotheses with real users before investing further.
