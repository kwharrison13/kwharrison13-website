# Replit

Replit is a collaborative, browser-based coding platform led by CEO [Amjad Masad](/notes/amjad-masad), and a company Kyle dug into closely in early 2022. His self-directed deep dive — built from a [Saanya Ojha](/notes/saanya-ojha) debrief plus close reads of [Packy McCormick](/notes/packy-mccormick)'s Not Boring memo and Masad's vision essay — captures the thesis: Replit is "in the primitives business," aiming to "make the internet read-write-remix, spread code literacy, and give a billion people the chance to build businesses online." The strategic core is that Replit wants to be "the first place that anyone writes a line of code," deliberately *not* targeting experienced developers (who definitionally can't write their first line of code there), and instead building for the underserved with something that "starts out looking like a toy." Masad's framing, quoted in Kyle's notes: "To discover the future, you need a population that will grow up with the technology and partner with you in figuring out where software is heading." The endgame Packy describes is Replit as "the world's first massively distributed and collaborative operating system."

On the September 13th, 2022 — Harry Stebbings Podcast, Kyle named Masad as the **most underrated angel in the ecosystem** — "an absolute oracle" who supplies the ambition founders are short on, such that "having Amjad on your cap table will give you that in spades." Replit also surfaces in Kyle's hype-cycle research as a developer-tools reference point.

**Context:** Replit is a San Francisco-based company offering an online integrated development environment (IDE) for collaborative coding in the browser; it later became known for AI-assisted "agent" coding tools. Amjad Masad co-founded it in 2016.
