# Fulness of Joy

"Fulness of joy" is a doctrinal concept Kyle worked out in his Come Follow Me study of 3 Nephi 27–4 Nephi 1 (October 19–25, 2020. 3 Nephi 27–4 Nephi: "There Could Not Be a Happier People"). It anchors on 3 Nephi 28:10, where Christ promises the three Nephites "your joy shall be full, even as the Father hath given me fulness of joy; and ye shall be even as I am." From the scripture chain Kyle assembled, he reads the fulness of joy as: the state of being that Heavenly Father and Jesus Christ are in; a state often reached after an overwhelming vision (e.g. Enoch in Moses 7:67); and the state of being after resurrection — citing D&C 93:33–34, where spirit and element "inseparably connected" receive a fulness of joy, and separated they cannot.

The concept sits inside the chapter's larger meditation on The Godhead and the near-300-year [Zion](/notes/zion) peace ("there could not be a happier people") that followed Christ's visit before the [pride cycle](/notes/the-pride-cycle) returned.

## Where this appears
