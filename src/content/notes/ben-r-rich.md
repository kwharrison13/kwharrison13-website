# Ben R. Rich

Ben R. Rich is the author and narrator of [Skunk Works](/books/skunk-works), the memoir at the center of Kyle's notes on Lockheed's legendary advanced-projects shop. He was [Kelly Johnson](/notes/kelly-johnson)'s vice president for advanced projects and Johnson's hand-picked successor when Johnson was forced to retire at sixty-five, running the Skunk Works through its stealth era (the F-117 Nighthawk). The book portrays Rich as a Skunk Worker "through and through" — an extrovert, high-intensity, no-B.S. character whom Johnson described as raised "in my own image": loving the cutting edge but knowing "the value of a buck" and "as practical as a goddam screwdriver." In Kyle's reading, Rich carries the book's founder-led innovation thesis — the contrast between missionary, founder-mode engineering (Johnson's "only build what you believe in") and the "build for the buck rather than the mission" managerial drift exemplified by Boeing — a frame Kyle links forward to [Anduril](/notes/anduril), [Palmer Luckey](/notes/palmer-luckey), and [Founders Mentality](/notes/founders-mentality).

**Context:** Ben R. Rich (1925–1995) was an American aerospace engineer who succeeded Kelly Johnson as head of Lockheed's Skunk Works and is regarded as the "father of stealth" for his work on the F-117. His 1994 memoir *Skunk Works* (co-written with Leo Janos) is a classic of defense and engineering management.
