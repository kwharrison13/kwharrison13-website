# Concentrated Poverty

Concentrated poverty in the wiki is the racially concentrated, geographically clustered form of poverty studied by [Patrick Sharkey](/notes/patrick-sharkey) in [Stuck in Place](/notes/stuck-in-place). It is more specific than the general [Poverty](/notes/poverty) page: Sharkey's argument, cited via [The New Urban Crisis](/books/the-new-urban-crisis), is that "neighborhood inequality is multi-generational, something that is passed down from parents to children in the same way that genetic background and financial wealth are transmitted across generations." The disadvantage is treated as an *inherited* attribute of place — a multigenerational inheritance of neighborhood disadvantage rather than a transient income gap. (source: [Patrick Sharkey](/notes/patrick-sharkey))

The concept recurs across the wiki's city-building and urban sources, where Sharkey is enlisted alongside [Richard Florida](/notes/richard-florida)'s [The New Urban Crisis](/books/the-new-urban-crisis) on the inheritance of neighborhood poverty. (source: [Patrick Sharkey](/notes/patrick-sharkey))

**Context:** "Concentrated poverty" is a term in urban sociology for neighborhoods where a high share of residents live below the poverty line, compounding individual hardship with disinvestment, weak schools, and limited opportunity. Patrick Sharkey's *Stuck in Place* (2013) is a landmark study arguing that the *persistence of place* — families remaining in the same disadvantaged neighborhoods across generations — drives durable racial inequality in the U.S.
