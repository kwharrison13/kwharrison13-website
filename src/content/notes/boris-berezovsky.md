# Boris Berezovsky

Russian oligarch and media owner who appears in [The Invention of Russia](/books/the-invention-of-russia) (and the [Vladimir Putin](/notes/vladimir-putin) page) as the foil in the pivotal scene of Putin's media capture. After reading Berezovsky a list of accusations, Putin told him flatly, "I want to run ORT... I personally am going to run ORT" — the television channel that, Putin noted, covered 98% of Russian households. Berezovsky retorted that this was "ridiculous, at a minimum" and "unrealizable," but Putin simply restated the channel's reach and left the room. The book uses the exchange to mark the moment the state moved to seize the media power the oligarchs had built. Berezovsky also figures in the book's account of 1990s elite politics: his conflict with Chubais is framed as a fight over "political power rather than democracy," with neither side believing ordinary Russians should be trusted with important decisions.

**Context:** Boris Berezovsky (1946–2013) was a mathematician-turned-oligarch who amassed a fortune in the chaotic privatizations of 1990s Russia, gained control of the ORT television network, helped engineer Putin's rise, then fell out with him and fled to exile in Britain, where he died.
