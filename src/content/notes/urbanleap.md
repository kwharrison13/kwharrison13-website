# UrbanLeap

**One-line:** *[Arik Bronshtein](/notes/arik-bronshtein)'s govtech platform that helps cities structure, run, and manage pilot tests of new solutions before they reach procurement.*

## What we know

- Helps cities structure the goals and contract of a pilot, since vendors and cities often fail to define the outcomes they hope to see; founder [Arik Bronshtein](/notes/arik-bronshtein) describes the platform as "like a CRM with Excel, Trello, and email all in there." (June 1st, 2018 — Arik Bronshtein (UrbanLeap))
- Captures structured pilot data (cost, duration, success/failure, who managed it, accuracy); had ~23 pilots on the platform and had just closed its fourth paying customer. (June 1st, 2018 — Arik Bronshtein (UrbanLeap))
- Pilots range from $500 to $1M and can compress a typical 6-month process toward 6 weeks; pricing is tiered by city size (~$1,000/month per 50K people). (June 1st, 2018 — Arik Bronshtein (UrbanLeap))
- Positioned as the "pilot" complement to the "RFP" stage — Bronshtein discussed with [Mariel Reed](/notes/mariel-reed) (who may join the company) the transition from a successful pilot (UrbanLeap) to RFP ([CoProcure](/notes/coprocure)). (June 1st, 2018 — Arik Bronshtein (UrbanLeap))
- Running a pilot with [Cisco](/notes/cisco), where Cisco could pay and provide the platform free to cities, betting on higher pilot-to-project conversion. (June 1st, 2018 — Arik Bronshtein (UrbanLeap))
- [Doug McCullough](/notes/doug-mccullough), CIO of the [City of Dublin](/notes/city-of-dublin), OH, cited UrbanLeap's testing model — they know cities are overwhelmed with pilots and lack the resources to test everything. (June 1st, 2018 — Doug McCullough (City of Dublin))
