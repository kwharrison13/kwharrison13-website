# Robinhood

Robinhood, the commission-free trading app, recurs across Kyle's 2021 notes from several angles. In the Watershed Climate Diligence — 2021, Robinhood appears as a freshly-public company "quite serious" about its new ESG program — Kyle logs [Erika Balbuena](/notes/erika-balbuena) (8–9 years in tech, previously at [TaskRabbit](/notes/taskrabbit), [Twilio ($TWLO)](/notes/twilio), and [AWS](/notes/aws)) as running Robinhood's "ESG year," and Robinhood as one of the customer intros Index Ventures made on Watershed's behalf (alongside Peloton, Okta, Confluent, Deliveroo, and Sonos). In the November 2nd, 2021 — Rick Song (Persona) call, Robinhood is named as an anchor [Persona](/notes/persona) customer whose own struggles with user growth flow through to Persona's volumes. It's also a named customer of [Sprig](/notes/sprig) (UserLeap) in that diligence's reference set.

Robinhood doubles as a market signal in Kyle's notes. In the Koyfin call, Rob Koyfman positions his product against it — *"the average Robinhood user isn't going to open up Koyfin"* — using Robinhood as shorthand for the casual retail trader Koyfin doesn't serve. And in the long-read [The Market Is The Greatest Critic](/notes/the-market-is-the-greatest-critic), Robinhood is named alongside [Toast ($TOST)](/notes/toast) as a high-flying tech company punished in the late-2021/early-2022 crash.

**Context:** Robinhood Markets is a US commission-free brokerage app (founded 2013) that popularized fractional and zero-commission retail trading; it IPO'd on the Nasdaq in July 2021.
