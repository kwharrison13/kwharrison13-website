# Data Security Posture Management

Data security posture management (DSPM) is the category Kyle's notes attach to [Laminar](/notes/laminar), surfaced during the [Salt Security](/notes/salt-security) diligence. The notes describe it as complementary to [API Security](/notes/api-security): customers evaluating Laminar were often also buying API-security products and saw the two as coexisting, willing to pay for both. The strategic angle DSPM raises — voiced by analyst [Alexei Balaganski](/notes/alexei-balaganski) — is *"Why would you want to protect individual components of your infrastructure when you can just protect your data at the point of creation?"* (see [Data-at-point-of-creation protection](/notes/data-at-point-of-creation-protection)). Laminar's stated strategy was to coexist with API-security vendors for now and absorb that functionality over time.

**Context:** DSPM is a cloud-security category focused on discovering, classifying, and protecting sensitive data wherever it lives across an organization's cloud estate — a data-centric posture, as opposed to securing infrastructure components individually.

## Where this appears

- [Laminar](/notes/laminar) — the DSPM platform whose diligence introduced the category as complementary to API security.
- [Alexei Balaganski](/notes/alexei-balaganski) — analyst who framed DSPM's "protect data at the point of creation" thesis against component-level security.
