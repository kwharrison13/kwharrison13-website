# Mahamudu Bawumia

Mahamudu Bawumia, the Vice President of Ghana, appears as the dramatic climax of the Bluebook Cities case study told within the [City Building](/notes/city-building) page (drawn from [Dryden Brown](/notes/dryden-brown)'s 2020 tweet thread). After [Dryden Brown](/notes/dryden-brown) and Charlie Callinan had spent months in West Africa cold-pitching a [charter city](/notes/city-building), a finance-minister meeting in Accra fell through; then they got a call to be in front of the Parliament building in 20 minutes, were picked up by the Presidential Motorcade, and taken to Jubilee House (Ghana's "White House"). There Bawumia was described as enthusiastic about the benefits charter cities could bring to Ghana — the high-water mark of the founders' effort before COVID closed Ghana's borders in early 2020.

**Context:** Mahamudu Bawumia is a Ghanaian economist and politician who served as Vice President of Ghana under President Nana Akufo-Addo (2017–2025) and was the New Patriotic Party's presidential candidate in 2024.
