# Mark 10

Across Kyle's gospel study, Mark 10 is the chapter he draws on for two of Christ's hardest teachings about wealth and reward. In Temporal Wealth and the Kingdom of God — Brigham Young he reads Mark's account of the **rich young man** (Mark 10:22 — "And he was sad at that saying, and went away grieved: for he had great possessions"), noting that he prefers this telling because, like Matthew's, it says the man "went away grieved" or sorrowful — making the point that "it's okay to be sad and to be asked to do hard things."

In Chosen Leaders he uses two other passages: Christ's promise that "there is no man that hath left house, or brethren for my sake, and the gospels, but he shall receive an hundredfold" (Mark 10:29–30), tied to the theme that we are rewarded for perseverance by who we become; and Mark 10:31 ("the first shall be last") in his cross-reference web on reversal ([Matthew 19](/notes/matthew-19):30, [Matthew 20](/notes/matthew-20):16, [Jacob 5](/notes/jacob-5):63, [Ether 13](/notes/ether-13):12).

**Context:** Mark 10 is a chapter of the Gospel of Mark covering teachings on marriage and divorce, blessing little children, the rich young man, the disciples on greatness ("whosoever will be chief... shall be servant of all"), and the healing of blind Bartimaeus.

## Where this appears

- Temporal Wealth and the Kingdom of God — Brigham Young — Mark 10:22, the rich young man who "went away grieved"; Kyle's preferred telling.
- Chosen Leaders — Mark 10:29–30 ("hundredfold" for those who leave all) and Mark 10:31 ("first shall be last") in the reversal cross-reference web.
