# The Notetaking Wars Are Real

A tag Kyle attaches to a thread from [How To Read a Book](/books/how-to-read-a-book) about how genuine disagreement should be resolved. Against Adler and Van Doren's claim that "the great majority of disagreements ... can be resolved by the removal of misunderstanding or of ignorance," Kyle notes: "Misunderstanding and ignorance are the real villains to fight against." The wiki's framing of the book ties this to a possible essay-in-progress ([Learning, No Greater Responsibility](/notes/learning-no-greater-responsibility)) and treats misunderstanding/ignorance — not the opposing party — as the thing worth fighting in a conversation. The surrounding passage reframes conversation as learning rather than winning, with the reader as ready to be taught as to teach.

## Where this appears

- [How To Read a Book](/books/how-to-read-a-book) — Kyle's note on the passage that disagreements are usually resolvable by removing misunderstanding or ignorance; the tag flags those two as "the real villains."
