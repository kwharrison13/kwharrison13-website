# Super Angels

A sourcing/diligence shorthand Kyle uses across his [Contrary Capital](/notes/contrary)-era call notes for the roster of high-profile angel investors backing a company — a proxy for both credibility and access to capital. The label clusters tightly around the June 6th, 2022 daily log. For [Maximus](/notes/maximus), the super-angel cap table reads as a roster of operator-founders: Dave Asprey (Bulletproof), Fred Ehrsam (co-founder of [Coinbase](/notes/coinbase)), Brian Lee (Honest Company), Brian Norgard (CPO of Tinder), Jeff Weiser (ex-CMO of [Shopify](/notes/shopify)), and Ryan Holiday (Daily Stoic). For [Long Journey Ventures](/notes/long-journey-ventures), [Arielle Zuckerberg](/notes/arielle-zuckerberg) listed a values-led angel base spanning Alfred Lin (via [Sequoia Capital](/notes/sequoia)), Peter Thiel, Marc Andreessen, Chris Dixon, Keith Rabois, David Sacks, Lee Fixel, Lenny Rachitsky, and Andrew Chen.

The concept is load-bearing in the [SignalRank](/notes/signalrank) thesis: [Rob Hodgkinson](/notes/rob-hodgkinson) told Kyle the company "raised a seed round last year from a bunch of super angels with deep pockets and access to capital," the kind of backers who can help build toward a $1B permanent-capital balance sheet to fund productized pro-rata rights. It also surfaces in the December 2021 [Cloudeagle.ai](/notes/cloudeagle-ai) intro, where notable operators (ex-CIO of Zoom, GM of Medallia, ex-CPO of Uber, F7 Ventures) are flagged as super-angel backing on an otherwise early company.

**Context:** "Super angel" is a venture term for a prolific, often operator-turned-investor angel who writes many checks at meaningful size — straddling the line between individual angel and small institutional fund, and frequently sought on cap tables as a signal of credibility.
