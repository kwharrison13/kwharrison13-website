# Kostia Pertsovskyi

Kostia Pertsovskyi is Director of Go-to-Market (Strategy & Digital Transformation) at [Samsung](/notes/samsung) Electronics America (ex-McKinsey; prior Samsung global-HQ roles in B2B demand gen and AI/5G/IoT marketing). He appears as a customer reference call (November 11, 2021) in the [Cresta.ai](/notes/cresta-ai) diligence, where he is the marquee enterprise voice: Samsung runs [Cresta.ai](/notes/cresta-ai) across 1,000+ support agents globally. His story is that COVID-era ecommerce growth and a D2C push turned customer support into a revenue driver (e.g., selling Samsung Care+), forcing the company to move store-floor staff into call-center roles that required heavy coaching — and the "Great Resignation" added a continuous-onboarding need.

His account is the clearest competitive comparison in the diligence. Samsung first chose [Observe.ai](/notes/observe-ai) for a Europe test (strong scoring, more flexible, priced per-agent vs. Cresta's per-interaction model), but Observe "struggled to scale" through COVID volume swings and had integration weaknesses as Samsung shifted systems for WFH — prompting a re-evaluation that moved them to Cresta (live ~June 2020, ~70% rolled out). He also evaluated [Balto.ai](/notes/balto-ai) (cheapest, but weak telecom/consumer-electronics depth and too B2B-sales-focused for Samsung's B2C support need) and named [Gong](/notes/gong) and [Chorus](/notes/chorus). His criteria included a notable one: preferring to avoid vendors that serve their largest competitors (leak risk). On ROI he cited CSAT up 20%+, support-call-to-sales conversion +50%, an 8/10 NPS, and handle time returning from 7 back to 5 minutes — figures the diligence uses as evidence for the Cresta thesis.

**Context:** Samsung Electronics America is the US arm of Samsung; the title and prior roles above are as recorded in the diligence call. (Role context from the source; not independently corroborated.)
