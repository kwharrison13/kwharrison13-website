# Alma 56

A chapter of the Book of Mormon (Alma 53–63 — Preserved by His Marvelous Power reading block): [Helaman](/notes/helaman)'s epistle recounting the 2,000 stripling warriors going into their first battle. In Kyle's Come Follow Me study, this is the chapter where the [But If Not...](/notes/but-if-not) theme lands hardest.

**[But If Not...](/notes/but-if-not) faith.** From Alma 56:17, "they were determined to conquer in this place or die." Kyle's note: "We believe that our God can deliver us… But even if he doesn't, and even if we die, our [Faith](/notes/faith) is not contingent on God doing what we want." He cross-references [Daniel 3](/notes/daniel-3):16–18 (Shadrach, Meshach, and Abednego — "but if not, be it known unto thee, O king, that we will not serve thy gods"). The chapter then shows the faith rewarded: Alma 56:56, not one of the young men fell, having "fought as if with the strength of God."

**"We do not doubt our mothers knew it."** From Alma 56:44–48, where the young men — who "never had fought, yet they did not fear death" — rehearse the words of their mothers, that if they did not doubt, God would deliver them. Kyle ties it back to the "that I might have such sons" thread and the maxim "doubt not, fear not."
