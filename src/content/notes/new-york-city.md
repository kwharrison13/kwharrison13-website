# New York City

New York City appears in the wiki so far only as a comparison point in the [Chicago](/notes/chicago) note: alongside Chicago, it is named as one of the few cities whose legislation legalizing ride-shares required per-trip data (driver, vehicle), the kind of municipal data infrastructure that lets a city understand mobility. That is the single grounded reference to NYC in the corpus to date; the page exists mainly as a resolvable target for that and future links.

**Context:** New York City is the most populous city in the United States, comprising five boroughs (Manhattan, Brooklyn, Queens, the Bronx, Staten Island), and a global hub of finance, media, and culture.

## Where this appears

- [Chicago](/notes/chicago) — cited as one of the few cities (with Chicago) whose ride-share legislation mandated per-trip data access, a marker of municipal data capability.
