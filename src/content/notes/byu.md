# BYU

Brigham Young University surfaces across Kyle's corpus at the intersection of his religious and entrepreneurial threads. In [Working Toward Zion](/books/working-toward-zion), BYU is offered as the answer to the book's central obstacle to [Consecration](/notes/consecration): that the hardest part of building [Zion](/notes/zion) is finding "men who know what to do with property." Kyle's note makes the connection explicit — **"This should be why BYU has so many business students"** — the school's business program *exists* to produce capable stewards who can put consecrated capital to productive use ([Working Toward Zion](/books/working-toward-zion): "BYU business students should be the answer to this problem"). BYU is also the launch base and pilot cohort for [Your Passion, Your Solution (YPYS)](/notes/your-passion-your-solution-ypys), the social-venture education program whose model starts with "12 BYU students" organized into control and test groups, operating out of Provo, UT.

BYU is likewise the venue for two of the gospel-study talks Kyle has ingested — both delivered as BYU devotional/speeches addresses: [M. Russell Ballard](/notes/m-russell-ballard)'s "Follow the Doctrine and Gospel of Christ" and [Dallin H. Oaks](/notes/dallin-h-oaks)'s "Truth and Tolerance" (Sept 11, 2011). And it appears in [Robert Pottroff](/notes/robert-pottroff)'s profile as where the Nvidia deep-research engineer earned his Masters in Computer Science.

**Context:** Brigham Young University is a private research university in Provo, Utah, owned by The Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-day Saints; its Marriott School of Business is a notable feeder of LDS entrepreneurs and executives.
