# John Calvin

Across Kyle's notes, Calvin appears chiefly as a doctrinal foil. In the July 6–12, 2020. Alma 30–31: "The Virtue of the Word of God" study, the Zoramites' Rameumptom prayer — the boast of an elect-and-damned, a people sure they alone are saved — is read as echoing Calvinism/Calvinist [Predestination](/notes/predestination), set against [Alma 31](/notes/alma-31)'s [Prayer](/notes/prayer) for strength and for one's fellow laborers. The framing makes Calvin shorthand for a theology of fixed election that Kyle's reading of the [Book of Mormon](/notes/book-of-mormon) pushes back on in favor of [Agency](/notes/agency).

In [The Life of John Taylor](/books/the-life-of-john-taylor), Calvin shows up in [John Taylor](/notes/john-taylor)'s sermon on Jude ("earnestly contend for the faith") among the Reformers — Luther, Melancthon, Calvin, Wesley, Whitfield — who "tried to bring about the ancient order of things" but, per Taylor, failed to fully restore the New Testament gospel. The point of the catalog is that the Reformation prepared the ground for, but did not accomplish, the Restoration through [Joseph Smith](/notes/joseph-smith).

**Context:** John Calvin (1509–1564) was a French theologian of the Protestant Reformation, author of the *Institutes of the Christian Religion*, and the central figure of Reformed (Calvinist) Christianity, best known for the doctrine of predestination — that God has already elected who is saved.
