# Spiritual Complacency

In Kyle's gospel study, spiritual complacency is the danger of being *comfortably led* — of becoming so accustomed to God's continual guidance that faith goes slack. The anchor case is the [Brother of Jared](/notes/brother-of-jared): after being "directed continually by the hand of the Lord," he spends four years on the seashore at Moriancumer, and the Lord chastens him for the space of three hours "because he remembered not to call upon the name of the Lord" ([Ether 2](/notes/ether-2):14). Kyle's gloss is that it is precisely in hardship that we feel our need of God most acutely, and that ease can dull the relationship.

He cross-references this against [Helaman 12](/notes/helaman-12):3 ("except the Lord doth chasten his people with many afflictions... they will not remember him") and [D&C 122](/notes/dandc-122):7 ("all these things shall give thee experience, and shall be for thy good"), and elsewhere files the same three-hour chastening under the heading "God chastens because He loves." The counterweight is preparation-in-advance: the relationship with God that carries you through crisis is the one built *before* the crisis arrives — "when the time for performance arrives, the time for preparation is past" (Thomas S. Monson), and [Russell M. Nelson](/notes/russell-m-nelson)'s warning that survival in coming days requires the "constant influence of the Holy Ghost," not the emergency appeal.

## Where this appears
