# Bilal Zuberi

Bilal Zuberi is a partner at [Lux Capital](/notes/lux-capital) and the author of [How To Build If… It's Time To Build?](/notes/how-to-build-if-it-s-time-to-build) (April 2020), the practitioner's reply to [Marc Andreessen](/notes/marc-andreessen)'s [It's Time To Build](/notes/it-s-time-to-build). Zuberi agrees with Andreessen's imperative — he calls the original essay "likely to be remembered, discussed, and cited as much as Marc's last seminal essay 'Software is Eating the World'" — but argues the harder question is *how* to build, laying out six concrete hurdles that keep deep tech / hardware from attracting mainstream investment (lower margins, long sales cycles, fragile supply chains, regulation, capital intensity, fewer acquirers). The piece doubles as a Lux thesis statement and portfolio showcase, citing the firm's hardware bets (DesktopMetal, Saildrone, Zoox, AirMap, [Anduril Industries](/notes/anduril), Recursion) as proof that deep tech is investable, and closes "I am ready. Lux is ready. Are you?"

In the wiki's concept graph Zuberi anchors the deep-tech / "are we still building?" strand of [Technological Innovation](/notes/technological-innovation), where his six hurdles are paired with [Carlota Perez](/notes/carlota-perez)'s long-wave surge cycle as complementary frames on what gates real-world progress.

**Context:** Bilal Zuberi is a general/managing partner at Lux Capital focused on deep tech, hardware, robotics, defense, and physical infrastructure; he holds a PhD in physical chemistry from MIT and was previously a founder and operator before moving into venture.
