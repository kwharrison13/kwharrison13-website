# Stop Screeching About Immigration, And Get Smart About It

**Author:** [Noah Smith](/notes/noah-smith)
**URL:** https://www.noahpinion.blog/p/stop-screeching-about-immigration
**One-line:** *The culture war has made the anti-immigration fringe act like an invasion is underway even though most Americans favor keeping birthright citizenship and think immigration is good — but America's real problem is a fertility crisis, and the smart response is more skilled, legal, college-educated immigration, not zero.*

## Key claims

- **Birthright citizenship remains the law after the Supreme Court upheld it**, over furious reactions from the online right (Sean Davis of *The Federalist* floated dissolving the Union or sterilizing foreign visitors); polling (Quinnipiac) shows nearly 70% of Americans want to keep birthright citizenship in place.
- **The "immigration as invasion" worldview is a small minority position.** 79% of Americans say immigration is good for the country overall, even while a strong majority also wants more border security and opposes illegal immigration specifically.
- **America is in a fertility crisis** — U.S. fertility has fallen below Japan's 1980s level — while Trump's restrictions on both illegal and *legal* immigration have sharply cut the immigrant inflow, with some estimates showing the U.S. population actually shrinking as a result.
- **Fewer immigrants means fewer workers per retiree** — the ratio of working-age Americans to retirees is projected to fall from 3-to-1 to 2-to-1 over the next 25 years with zero immigration, meaning more toil for the young/middle-aged and higher elder-care burdens (which further suppress fertility).
- **Immigrant education level matters enormously for fiscal impact.** College-educated immigrants reduce the national debt; immigrants without college degrees are a net fiscal drain, both directly and even accounting for upward mobility in later generations.
- **The smart policy is more legal, skilled immigration plus more border security and less quasi-legal asylum** — which Smith argues is *exactly* the mix of policies polling shows Americans actually want, contra both MAGA's zero-immigration instinct and any Democratic drift toward open borders as pure reaction to Trump.
- **Neither party is executing a pragmatic strategy**: the right treats maximalist anti-immigration rhetoric as a loyalty signal to the MAGA movement rather than pursuing an achievable Constitutional carve-out, while Democrats mostly just react against whatever Trump does.

## Notable quotes

> "The notion that immigration is an invasion bent on destroying the country by replacing its founding population has become a bedrock belief on the Right... nativism has become a self-contained, hermetically sealed worldview not subject to reasoned argumentation, logic, or data."

> "Most of us grew up thinking of Japan as a place with an aging, shrinking population. America is now headed for exactly that same fate, unless we take in immigrants."

> "In the age of low fertility, America desperately needs to be *smart* about its immigration strategy. We can't let a fringe of culture-warriors dictate our national policy."
