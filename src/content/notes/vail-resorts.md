# Vail Resorts

Mountain-resort operator (NYSE: MTN) that appears in Kyle's Noname Security - Diligence as a prospective enterprise customer evaluating API-security tooling. The expert source is [Ian Buxton](/notes/ian-buxton), Senior Director of Information Security, Risk, Compliance & Privacy — a 21-year security veteran who has spent 16+ years at Vail building its enterprise security, compliance, and data-privacy programs with multi-million-dollar budget authority. He was evaluating [Noname Security](/notes/noname-security) against Imperva (with existing Akamai/F5 relationships).

Buxton's account is used in the diligence as a window into how a large, resource-constrained enterprise actually buys: an "initiative lifecycle" that gathers data before selecting a vendor, a desire for both **preventative and detective** ("defense in depth") [Application Security](/notes/application-security), and a need to **risk-prioritize** which issues to fix first. A key differentiator he credits to Noname is its willingness to run a professional-services **risk assessment** to build a business case — where competitors offered a "take it or leave it" POC. His commentary also anchors the diligence's maturity benchmark: API security "feels like Endpoint Security 5–6 years ago," with [CrowdStrike](/notes/crowdstrike) as the model of packaging signal into actionable reports.

**Context:** Vail Resorts (NYSE: MTN) is a Colorado-based mountain-resort and hospitality company that operates ski resorts including Vail, Breckenridge, Whistler Blackcomb, and Park City, and is known for its Epic Pass season-pass product.
