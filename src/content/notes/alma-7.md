# Alma 7

Alma 7 is the Book of Mormon chapter Kyle returns to as the scriptural anchor for *how* the [Atonement](/notes/atonement) reaches a person individually. Alma 7:11–13 is the verse on Christ taking "upon him their infirmities… that he may know according to the flesh how to succor his people according to their infirmities." Across How To Apply The Atonement Kyle uses it to make the point that "Christ chose to learn how to heal us by His own personal experience rather than by revelation alone" — succor learned by living it, per [Henry B. Eyring](/notes/henry-b-eyring) and [Kent F. Richards](/notes/kent-f-richards).

In his notes on Russell M. Nelson's talk, Alma 7:12–13 grounds the claim that "the [Atonement](/notes/atonement) applies personally to *you*" — every trial requires faith in Christ as Redeemer, paired with [Ether 12](/notes/ether-12):27 on weakness made strong. It is cited again in the General Conference — October 2021 notes.
