# Lilly Chen

Lilly Chen is the founder of [Contenda](/notes/contenda), a content-tooling startup that repurposes existing material (e.g. turning developer blogs into new content), tracked in Kyle's notes through its [Contrary Capital](/notes/contrary) portfolio updates. The March 2022 update was light — a demo for [Honeycomb](/notes/honeycomb)'s CTO aimed at a design partnership. The richer May 2022 update showed Chen working out go-to-market: she learned the sale typically needs only one decision-maker at Contenda's price point — a head of content or marketing who can approve up to ~$30k/month — while Developer Relations are users but not buyers. She reported a pilot with [Segment](/notes/segment) (whose Head of Content Marketing was paying ~$10k/mo to an agency to repurpose six blogs with month-plus turnaround), noting "anchoring our product against an agency feels like strong positioning," with a successful pilot potentially opening Twilio. She was hiring an ML/NLP specialist; investors named in the update include Acrew Capital and Scribble Ventures.
