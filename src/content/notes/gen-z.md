# Gen Z

Gen Z appears in Kyle's notes mainly as the subject of a generational shift in attitudes toward earnest, relentless work. The recurring datum is [Rex Woodbury](/notes/rex-woodbury)'s tidbit that **"Gen Z hates [Leslie Knope](/notes/leslie-knope)"** — the *Parks and Recreation* civil servant who embodies cheerful civic overwork. The move from celebrating Knope's earnest diligence to recoiling from it is read as a barometer for how the rising generation re-evaluates [Ambition](/notes/ambition), [Work Ethic](/notes/work-ethic), and [Hard Work](/notes/hard-work) — possibly an instance of the "great softening," possibly a hardening against it. The same observation threads through The Hardening Of The Great Softening - Research and Toil We Must - Research, where Gen Z's recoil from the workaholic archetype is treated as evidence of a generational mood shift Kyle is tracking.

**Context:** Generation Z generally refers to people born roughly between the late 1990s and early 2010s — the cohort after Millennials, the first to grow up fully online, frequently discussed for distinctive attitudes toward work, institutions, and ambition.
