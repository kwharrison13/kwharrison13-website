# Essentialists

In the philosophy-of-knowledge framing from [The Notetaking Cold War](/notes/the-notetaking-cold-war), **essentialists** believe the world has a fixed, underlying structure that our categories should mirror — so the "right" note system is one that captures the true nesting of things. The piece groups [Plato](/notes/plato), [Immanuel Kant](/notes/immanuel-kant), and [Descartes](/notes/descartes) under this label (despite their differing stances) and contrasts them with the [Pragmatists](/notes/pragmatists), who treat categories as useful tools rather than discoveries of essence.

## Where this appears

- [The Notetaking Cold War](/notes/the-notetaking-cold-war) — the essentialist/pragmatist split is offered as the deep frame behind the Tiago Forte vs. Conor White-Sullivan debate over how to organize notes, mapping note systems onto rival theories of truth.
