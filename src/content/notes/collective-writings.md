# Collective Writings

**The Collective Writings of Kyle William Harrison** is Kyle's master gospel-study anthology — a running notebook of poems, quotes, talks, journals, and recorded reflections he has gathered over time and returns to when studying. He began the record at age nineteen, having just started his mission to the Pennsylvania Pittsburgh Mission, and frames it as an attempt to chronicle the gospel topics that have become personally meaningful to and partially understood by him — explicitly acknowledging that a written record is "wholly inadequate," since "the great berth of knowledge is acquired in experience."

## Epigraph

> "While walking in a rapid stream we cannot tread twice in the same water. Neither can we spend twice the same time. When we pass out of that door, the work of this meeting will be closed to us forever. We shall never spend the time of this evening again. **Then should we not keep a record of our work, teachings, and counsel that we give? We should.**"
> — [Wilford Woodruff](/notes/wilford-woodruff) #[Journals](/notes/journals)

## Statement of purpose

> **Kyle:** *A collective record of gospel study in an individual's life is wholly inadequate as the great berth of knowledge is acquired in experience. This collection is an attempt to chronicle the gospel topics that have become personally meaningful to me and partially understood. I began this record at the age of nineteen, having just begun my mission to the Pennsylvania Pittsburgh Mission.*

## Contents

The anthology gathers several component volumes:

- Notes on the Scriptures — an interconnected record of the notes and impressions Kyle had reading two sets of physical scriptures: one "quad" held since May 12th, 2000 (the day of his baptism) and another set bought on his mission after receiving revelation.
- Topical Study — a culmination of his gospel studies organized by topic, drawn largely from the two impersonal mission journals in which he avoided personal experiences and stuck deliberately to study and knowledge.
- Notes & Records — an abridgement of the most meaningful notes taken in meetings (direct quotations and inspiration), plus inspirations and reflections from personal scripture study.
- My Book of Life — the large body of material Kyle kept in his affectionately termed "book of life": talks, articles, and sayings, each personalized with an entry on how he came across it and why it is special to him.
- My Mini Book of Talks — the talks he wanted to carry around with him; his own personal "Gospel Library app."
- Spiritual Quotes: Greatest Hits — a collection of quotations that aided his gospel understanding, taken from both his scholarly and poetic collections.
- Personal Journal: Mission — a digitized rendition of his personal day-to-day mission journals.
- Conference Report Journal — including notes taken from non–General Conference talks.
- Spiritual Book Log — a record of his reading within the spiritual and religious genre.
- Mission Counsel Journal — written offerings from members and companions on his mission.

## How it gets used

In the [Alma 30](/notes/alma-30)–31 gospel-study, Kyle tags the anthology against "Mission Recording 33," a recording he made on his mission about feeling guided "from idea to idea and finally leading to a conclusion" — God offering "not a definitive answer but a path to more understanding." He was struck to rediscover that the recording was about Alma 30, which he hadn't remembered when re-encountering the chapter.

In [The Life of John Taylor](/books/the-life-of-john-taylor), Kyle connects a Taylor passage on liberty and refusing to be a slave to "a poem from Collective Writings" — "when you see an outstretched hand you exclaim that is an enslaving band" — showing the anthology functioning as a cross-reference store: a place where a line he has collected illuminates something he reads later.
