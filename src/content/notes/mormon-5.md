# Mormon 5

A Book of Mormon chapter recording [Mormon](/notes/mormon)'s grief as the Nephite nation falls. Kyle's notes pair it with [Helaman 12](/notes/helaman-12): he reads Helaman 12 as Mormon reacting to what he has been compiling — recognizing "the same unsteadiness of heart in his own day." The key verse he marks is Mormon 5:2: *"But behold, I was without hope, for I knew the judgments of the Lord which should come upon them; for they repented not of their iniquities, but did struggle for their lives without calling upon that Being who created them."* The chapter is the abbreviator's own hopeless vantage point on a people who war for survival without turning to God.

It also sits in the chapter sequence of Kyle's Come Follow Me study of Mormon 1–6, between [Mormon 4](/notes/mormon-4) and the final destruction at Cumorah.

## Where this appears

- Come Follow Me — Helaman 7–12 — Mormon 5:2 ("I was without hope") read alongside [Helaman 12](/notes/helaman-12) as Mormon recognizing his own people's unsteadiness in what he reads.
- Come Follow Me — Mormon 1–6 — part of the studied chapter span on the Nephite collapse.
