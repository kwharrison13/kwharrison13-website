# Objectivism

In Kyle's corpus, Objectivism is [Ayn Rand](/notes/ayn-rand)'s individualist ethic, encountered in seed form through her early novella [Anthem](/books/anthem) (read 2025). The wiki reads Anthem as "an inversion of collectivism": the evil it indicts is "there is no will save the will of all men together," and it climaxes with the protagonist carving the word **EGO** over his fort and declaring, "I owe nothing to my brothers, nor do I gather debts from them." The highlights carry the philosophy's core moves verbatim — the self as its own justification ("I wished to find a warrant for being. I need no warrant for being… I am the warrant and the sanction") and happiness as an end in itself ("My happiness is not the means to any end. It is the end… It is its own purpose").

Anthem also dramatizes the *epistemic* claim underneath Objectivism: that truth is not decided by consensus. The Council suppresses Equality 7-2521's rediscovered electric light to protect the Department of Candles, ruling that "What is not thought by all men cannot be true" — collectivist agreement standing in for reality. The wiki frames this as Rand's individualist ethic "in seed form, pointing forward to [Atlas Shrugged](/notes/atlas-shrugged) as its fuller statement," and situates Rand inside its dystopia cluster alongside [George Orwell](/notes/george-orwell) and [Aldous Huxley](/notes/aldous-huxley) — though where they dramatize collectivism from the outside, Anthem inverts it from the standpoint of the suppressed individual.

**Context:** Objectivism is the philosophical system Ayn Rand (1905–1982) founded, holding that reality exists independent of consciousness, that reason is the only means of knowledge, that the proper moral purpose of life is rational self-interest, and that laissez-faire capitalism is the only just social system. Its fullest fictional statement is *Atlas Shrugged* (1957).

## Where this appears

- [Anthem](/books/anthem) — Rand's early dramatization of the Objectivist ego/self-as-warrant ethic, climaxing on the word EGO; suppression of invention shows the anti-consensus epistemology.
- [Ayn Rand](/notes/ayn-rand) — author and founder of Objectivism; the wiki treats Anthem as prefiguring her later canon and the dystopia cluster.
