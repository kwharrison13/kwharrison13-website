# Trough of Feedback

A hiring-and-judgment tag Kyle attaches in [Talent](/books/talent) to the warning about the highly credentialed, fairly talented candidate who keeps job-hopping — "restless, never happy, and never able to put down any roots." Cowen and Gross frame this as a version of the **Groucho Marx effect** (Groucho wouldn't belong to any club that would have him as a member): if a strong-looking candidate from above your tier wants to work with you, you should get *nervous*, not flattered. Why aren't they already somewhere better? Maybe they lack self-confidence, maybe their personality is poison, maybe they plan to leave after a year and are just using you. They stay employable enough to keep getting hired, but the signal you should read in their availability is a kind of negative feedback the market keeps giving them.

The phrase captures that information loop: a candidate's persistent availability and restlessness is itself feedback about them, and the interviewer's job is to hear it rather than override it with the candidate's polished interview performance.
