# Light of Christ

In Kyle's gospel framework the Light of Christ is the universal spiritual endowment that lets every person tell good from evil — "the Spirit of Christ is given to every man, that he may know good from evil," with the Fruits of the Spirit as the measure (December 7–13, 2020. Moroni 7–9 — May Christ Lift Thee Up, drawing on Moroni 7:13–16). Kyle reads it as the mechanism by which truth attracts: "when God speaks the Light of Christ recognizes truth and truth attracts," and by opening to that feeling we "lay hold upon every good thing." It also anchors his account of [Faith](/notes/faith) as judging good from evil (Faith — Topical Study).

The concept does double duty in his theology of [Agency](/notes/agency) and fairness. In Plan of Salvation — Topical Study he frames it as the reason "we are not expected to wander aimlessly through a field of doubt" — the Light of Christ gives everyone a fair chance because spiritual influence begins there. In The First Principles of Faithful Pragmatism it sits inside the God-as-Parent model as the *universal* form of guidance: some guidance "is universal (e.g. the Light of Christ) and guide[s] everyone in seeking out 'that which is good,'" while other forms are made opt-in.

**Context:** In Latter-day Saint theology the Light of Christ is described as a divine influence or power that proceeds from God to fill the immensity of space, giving every person a conscience to discern good from evil — distinct from (and preliminary to) the gift of the Holy Ghost. Kyle's notes cite Moroni 7:16 and Preach My Gospel for this teaching.
