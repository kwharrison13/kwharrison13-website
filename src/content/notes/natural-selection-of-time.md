# Natural Selection of Time

One of Kyle's personal career frames, used as an explicit reading lens for [The Ride of a Lifetime](/books/the-ride-of-a-lifetime) (Bob Iger's Disney memoir), where it appears paired with [Lily Pads of Your Career](/notes/lily-pads-of-your-career). Kyle attaches the "natural selection of time" note to Iger's passages about saying yes to every opportunity — "My instinct throughout my career has always been to say yes to every opportunity… I wasn't going to forgo any chance to do that" — and to Iger's counsel about making yourself indispensable: "be patient; look for opportunities to pitch in and expand and grow; and make yourself one of the people… that your bosses feel they have to turn to when an opportunity arises."

The frame seems to capture the idea that over a long enough horizon, consistently saying yes, proving yourself indispensable, and accumulating varied experience lets time itself "select" you into the right inflection points — a compounding-of-opportunity view of a career, closely linked to [Interest Discovery](/notes/interest-discovery) (recognizing quiet inflection points as a learnable skill) and the lily-pad metaphor of hopping deliberately from one career platform to the next.
