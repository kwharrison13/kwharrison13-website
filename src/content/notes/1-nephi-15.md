# 1 Nephi 15

A chapter in the Book of Mormon's first book of Nephi — Nephi's interpretation of Lehi's [Tree of Life](/notes/tree-of-life) vision for his brothers — and a recurring proof-text in Kyle's gospel study. Plan of Salvation — Topical Study leans on it for the vision's machinery: the rod of iron as the word of God ([1 Nephi 15](/notes/1-nephi-15):24, "whoso would hearken unto the word of God, and would hold fast unto it, they would never perish"), and the "awful gulf" of justice that divides the wicked from the tree of life and sweeps the unclean down the river of filthy waters ([1 Nephi 15](/notes/1-nephi-15):28, 30).

In Charity & Love, Kyle reads the same river through Lehi's parental love: the visions of God cause Lehi to dwell only on the salvation of his sons, his worry so consuming that he does not even notice the river of filthy waters ([1 Nephi 15](/notes/1-nephi-15):27).

**Context:** 1 Nephi 15 is Nephi's chapter-long explanation, given to Laman and Lemuel, of the symbols in his father Lehi's dream — the tree, the rod of iron, the river, the great and spacious building — and a call to keep the commandments and not perish.

## Where this appears

- Plan of Salvation — Topical Study — rod of iron as the word of God (v. 24) and the gulf of divine justice dividing wicked from righteous (vv. 28, 30)
- Charity & Love — Lehi's consuming concern for his sons' salvation, blind to the filthy river (v. 27)
