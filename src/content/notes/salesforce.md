# Salesforce

Salesforce runs through Kyle's notes as the **incumbent platform and category-creation archetype** of the modern SaaS era. The clearest case study is [Okta](/notes/okta) founder [Todd McKinnon](/notes/todd-mckinnon), who ran engineering at Salesforce before leaving in 2008 to bet on the cloud — and whose early mistake was "copying Salesforce's small-company go-to-market" before correcting via first-principles to chase the largest customers ([Todd McKinnon — Creating and Defining a New Market Category](/notes/todd-mckinnon-creating-and-defining-a-new-market-category)). Salesforce is also Kyle's standing example of a **talent vortex**: reading [The Everything Store](/books/the-everything-store) he notes "Mulesoft and Salesforce for product marketing" as among "the greatest talent training grounds."

In diligence, Salesforce appears as the **integration substrate and competitive gravity well**. In the Planhat diligence, [Gainsight](/notes/gainsight) is repeatedly described as having been built originally as a Salesforce add-on (~90% of its CRM connections to Salesforce), which both defined the customer-success category and constrains it. The CaptivateIQ diligence treats Salesforce as the core data source sales-comp tooling must read from. It also surfaces as a strategic investor: in the Uptycs call, Salesforce and [ServiceNow ($NOW)](/notes/servicenow) were "eager to invest" but were blocked by [Tanium](/notes/tanium) ("thou shalt not invest"), and Salesforce renewed a $3M [MuleSoft](/notes/mulesoft) check. [Salesforce Ventures](/notes/salesforce-ventures) and Salesforce employees appear in the Watershed diligence, and Steve Shillingford pitched Deepsee.ai as "Salesforce for AI digital transformation," following the Salesforce land-and-expand blueprint (September 7th, 2021 — Steve Shillingford (Deepsee.ai)).

**Context:** Salesforce (NYSE: CRM) is the pioneering cloud CRM company, founded 1999 by Marc Benioff; its enterprise-software platform and venture arm (Salesforce Ventures) make it both a category-defining incumbent and an active strategic investor.
