# Alma 43

The chapter that opens the Book of Mormon war narrative, and the one Kyle's August 3–9, 2020. Alma 43–52: "Stand Fast in the Faith of Christ" study engages most fully (his notes for that week cover Alma 43 only). Three threads run through it. First, "former Mormons" as the most dangerous opponents: the Amalekites, "of a more wicked and murderous disposition than the Lamanites" (Alma 43:6), are the apostate chief captains — Kyle's gloss is that the most vocal opponents of the gospel are those who turned away from it, not those who never knew it. Second, manipulation versus motivation: Zerahemnah's design to "stir up the Lamanites to anger" to usurp power (43:8) is, Kyle notes, a description of Satan's plans — stoking others' feelings toward your own end — contrasted with the Nephites' design to defend "their lands… wives… children… rights… and liberty… to worship God" (43:9). He connects this to [Logos, Ethos, and Pathos](/notes/logos-ethos-and-pathos) and to [Simon Sinek](/notes/simon-sinek)'s "Why vs. What."

Third, [Small and Simple Things](/notes/small-and-simple-things): [Moroni](/notes/moroni) is made chief captain "only twenty and five years old" (43:17), which Kyle ties to [Alma 37](/notes/alma-37):6–7 ("by small and simple things are great things brought to pass") and [Joseph Smith](/notes/joseph-smith)'s reply about "illiterate fishermen."

**Context:** Alma 43 is a chapter in the Book of Alma in the Book of Mormon, beginning the extended war chapters in which Captain Moroni leads the Nephite armies against Zerahemnah and the Lamanites.
