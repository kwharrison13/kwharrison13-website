# Paul Graham

Paul Graham — programmer, essayist, and co-founder of [Y Combinator](/notes/y-combinator) — recurs across Kyle's reading as the defining voice of modern startup culture and as a frequently-quoted aphorist. [The Power Law — Venture Capital & the Making of the New Future](/books/the-power-law-venture-capital-and-the-making-of-the-new-future) tells his origin: a "self-described hacker" who founded Viaweb in 1995, sold it to Yahoo in 1998 for $45M, then turned to writing essays "which celebrated coders and disparaged business types" — crystallizing the early-2000s "hacker-founder youth revolt against traditional VC," where young entrepreneurs no longer deferred to experienced investors. That same essayist voice shows up as a source of compressed wisdom: [Atomic Habits](/books/atomic-habits) quotes his "keep your identity small" as a guard against the brittle, over-attached identity that "wrecks you" when a single facet of life is lost.

Graham also surfaces in Kyle's essay research as a recurring authority. [The New Urban Crisis](/books/the-new-urban-crisis) ([Richard Florida](/notes/richard-florida)) quotes his defense of startup hubs as "manufacturers of inequality" that are nonetheless the price of progress — "you can't prevent great variations in wealth without preventing people from getting rich, and you can't do that without preventing them from starting startups." His tweets anchor arguments in Toil We Must - Research (why humans overlook great ideas) and VC Contagion - Research (his 2013 "fundraising isn't what defines a startup" as one prophetic bookend), and [Your Passion, Your Solution (YPYS)](/notes/your-passion-your-solution-ypys) maps its early-engagement thesis to what Graham has written about durable, intrinsic motivation.

**Context:** Paul Graham (b. 1964) co-founded Viaweb (acquired by Yahoo, became Yahoo Store) and, in 2005, the startup accelerator Y Combinator. His widely read essays (paulgraham.com) on startups, founders, and ideas have made him one of the most influential writers in Silicon Valley.
