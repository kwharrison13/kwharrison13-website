# Meritech

Meritech is a growth-stage venture firm that recurs across Kyle's sourcing and VC-research notes. In the Uptycs sourcing call, it appears as inbound interest: Dave Zimbelman reached out to [Ganesh Pai](/notes/ganesh-pai) from Meritech, knowing him from Comcast, while Uptycs was fielding interest from multiple growth investors. In the Dallin Bills (Battery Ventures) call, Meritech is described as having recently done a round after losing a prior one to [Tiger Global Management](/notes/tiger-global-management) and Dragoneer (its last round valued the company at ~$3B); the note records that Meritech "did an insane amount of work" on the Dragoneer/Tiger round and was "super bummed" when they lost it.

Meritech also shows up as a high-conviction, low-volume model in Kyle's VC-research. In The Productization of Venture Capital - Research, Ashley Paston (at Bain Capital Ventures and Meritech) frames the Meritech pitch as "much more like a true partner" — *"each partner only does 1-2 deals a year and so their companies receive so much attention"* — citing a case where Meritech immediately warned a portfolio company that an expert call threatened to lose them a customer and then "called in all favors" to prevent it. The same Paston/Meritech relationship recurs in the The Death of a Venture Fund - Research investor roster.

**Context:** Meritech Capital Partners is a Palo Alto-based growth-equity firm specializing in late-stage technology investments, known for a concentrated, partner-led model.
