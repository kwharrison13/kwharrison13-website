# Patrick Collison

Co-founder and CEO of [Stripe](/notes/stripe), cited across Kyle's corpus as an exemplar of cross-disciplinary curiosity, writing culture, and talent evaluation. In [Talent (Book)](/books/talent) (Tyler Cowen & Daniel Gross), Collison appears twice: as a model of curiosity "about models, frameworks, cultural understandings, disciplines, and methods of thought," whose writing draws on economics, science, history, and Irish culture; and as the source of three reference-check questions he posed to [Reid Hoffman](/notes/reid-hoffman) in a 2019 public interview — "Is this person so good that you would happily work for them? Can this person get you where you need to be way faster than any reasonable person could? When this person disagrees with you, do you think it will be as likely you are wrong as they are wrong?"

He recurs as an emblem of [Stripe](/notes/stripe)'s deliberate culture: in The Storytelling of Investing - Research he anchors the company's writing discipline ("The first emails I saw from Patrick Collison literally had footnotes"), and in The Talent Vortex - Research he's credited with building "this culture of learning, a lot of it by Patrick who gets involved in issues outside of payments." In Watershed Climate Diligence — 2021, he and his brother [John Collison](/notes/john-collison) are listed among the investors in [Watershed](/notes/watershed-climate).

**Context:** Patrick Collison is the Irish-born co-founder and CEO of the payments company Stripe (founded with his brother John in 2010) and a co-founder of the Arc Institute and the Fast Grants / Progress Studies efforts.
