# Okta

The cloud identity company that anchors Kyle's [Identity & Access Management](/notes/identity-and-access-management) (IAM) investing research. The [Todd McKinnon — Creating and Defining a New Market Category](/notes/todd-mckinnon-creating-and-defining-a-new-market-category) long-read (a Patrick O'Shaughnessy interview with Okta's co-founder/CEO) is Kyle's deepest source: it traces how Okta left [Salesforce](/notes/salesforce), pivoted from a cloud-monitoring idea (SaaSure) to identity management within months after IT buyers kept surfacing the pain of "couldn't log in to their new cloud apps," and then "threaded the needle" on timing — selling cloud security/identity, the later-adopted part of cloud, so the market took until ~2012 to catch up. The episode frames Okta as a case study in *creating a category* and becoming the default "cognitive referent," and in launching a second act (customer identity, ~24% of revenue before the 2017 IPO) that came from customers rather than a plan.

Okta is also the pressure-test case in Kyle's Identity & Access Management Diligence — 2021: the stickiness puzzle ("Why is Okta so easy to rip out? Why isn't Single-Sign On (SSO) more sticky?"), captured in a SailPoint CEO's line — "My customers tell me they could replace Okta in a weekend if they had to" — versus the bull case where Okta could displace Microsoft Active Directory. Around the McKinnon read, Kyle asks whether Okta will compete with or acquire into the data-privacy/governance category alongside [OneTrust](/notes/onetrust) and [BigID](/notes/bigid). Separately, Okta surfaces in the Watershed Climate Diligence — 2021 notes as a Watershed prospect/customer (Alison Colwell), and as an example company page in the Networked Conviction Roam-as-investing-CRM workflow.

**Context:** Okta (NASDAQ: OKTA) is a publicly traded identity-and-access-management company founded in 2009 by Todd McKinnon and Frederic Kerrest; it acquired Auth0 in 2021 and is a leading independent SSO/identity provider competing with Microsoft Entra.
