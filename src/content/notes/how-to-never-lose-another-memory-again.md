# How to Never Lose Another Memory Again

**Author:** [Dan Shipper](/notes/dan-shipper) and Kira Deutch (Superorganizers)
**URL:** https://superorganizers.substack.com/p/how-to-never-lose-another-memory
**One-line:** *A profile of "MC," who logs everything significant he does every day in Workflowy — a low-overhead, un-opinionated Daily Log as an external memory, in the lineage of Buckminster Fuller's Dymaxion Chronofile.*

## Highlights

- Story of MC who keeps a Daily Log
- He wants to remember everything so he can push himself to keep getting better.
- [Buckminster Fuller](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Buckminster_Fuller) was a genius inventor, Mensa president, and an architect, best known for inventing the geodesic dome. Like MC, he also chronicled everything he did, starting at the age of four. Using a scrapbook Fuller nicknamed the Dymaxion Chronofile, **he wrote down what he did every 15 minutes.** Inside his journal, he stuffed everything from newspaper clippings to dry cleaning bills.
- **It was his life, in 140,000 pieces of paper. And here is MC's life, in 50,000 bullet points.**
- As MC scans the page, there's one line about Fuller's scrapbook that catches his eye: "This is said to be the most documented human life in history." MC laughs. "Challenge accepted."
- **I started using it to plan everything day by day: who I was meeting up with, what restaurant we ate at, what podcast I was listening to on the way. Eventually it evolved to the point where I was just recording everything significant I did every day.** Once I got a job, I was doing more things, and I ended up using Workflowy for that too.
- As the music writer Ted Gioia [said](https://austinkleon.com/2019/12/19/your-output-depends-on-your-input/), "**If you don't have good input, you cannot maintain good output.**" One thing I try to do to help "manage my inputs," to borrow Gioia's phrase, is each morning I try to pick one of each of these to do that day.
- Workflowy isn't opinionated, to borrow a term from the programming world. **There's not many guardrails or templates—it's just bullet points, allowing me to log and file things away quickly and not think about it in the moment. And then I can turn to the app whenever I need to check on something or spontaneously reflect.** So many other tools have a lot of mental overhead, where you have to learn a new system full of complex features you don't actually need. You don't need that when what you want to do is fairly simple.
- If I don't remember to write it down, there's a great chance it's getting lost. It's gotten to the point that [Socrates](/notes/socrates) predicted where [Writing](/notes/writing) **replaces our memories**. It's kinda bad but mostly good, I think...

## Connections

- Daily Log — MC's everything-logging practice is exactly the bullet-timeline habit Kyle keeps (this very file was split from one).
- Workflowy — the low-overhead, un-opinionated bullet tool that makes the habit sustainable.
- [Writing](/notes/writing) — the Socratic worry that writing replaces memory, reframed as a feature.
- [Forget Forgetting. Build a Zettelkasten.](/notes/forget-forgetting-build-a-zettelkasten) — the complementary "don't trust your memory, externalize it" argument Kyle read the same day.
