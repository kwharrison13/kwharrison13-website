# Sanctification

Across Kyle's topical gospel study, sanctification is treated as the ongoing process of being purified and made holy — the journey that begins at [Baptism](/notes/baptism) ("the first fruits of repentance") and continues as one yields one's heart to God. The Humility — Topical Study notes frame [Humility](/notes/humility) and meekness as the first step into that process: "humility is not thinking less of yourself, but less ABOUT yourself" ([Dieter F. Uchtdorf](/notes/dieter-f-uchtdorf)), and from there one is to "deny [oneself] of all ungodliness" (Moroni 10:32) and seek "the purifying and the sanctification of [our] hearts, which sanctification cometh because of [our] yielding [our] hearts to God" (Helaman 3:35). C.S. Lewis (via Tad R. Callister) describes the corresponding Savior who "will never rest nor let you rest until you are literally perfect."

The Revelation — Topical Study notes locate the engine of sanctification in the [Holy Ghost](/notes/holy-ghost): "Sanctification comes through the Holy Ghost because the Holy Ghost teaches us all things, namely those things we need to repent of, which leads to our sanctification." The danger flagged is complacency — "as soon as you consider yourself content… you slowly remove your dependence on Christ" and cut off the flow of living water. The supporting scriptural chain Kyle gathers includes 2 Nephi 31:12 (Christ was sanctified, we follow His example), Alma 13:10–12, D&C 20:30–31, D&C 43:16 (sanctification yields an endowment of power), and D&C 88:68.

**Context:** In Christian theology generally, and Latter-day Saint usage specifically, sanctification is the process of being cleansed from sin and made holy, distinguished from (but following) justification — here attributed especially to the influence of the Holy Ghost.

## Where this appears

- Humility — Topical Study — sanctification as the destination of the humility/meekness journey that begins at baptism, reached by yielding one's heart to God.
- Revelation — Topical Study — sanctification as the work of the Holy Ghost, who teaches what we must repent of; complacency cuts off the process.
