# Jake Smith

Elder Jake Smith is a figure quoted in Kyle's [gospel-study](/notes/gospel-study) notes for a single line about the shepherd metaphor that recurs across two related studies. In both The Good Shepherd and Missionary Work — Topical Study, Kyle attributes to "Elder Jake Smith" the observation: *"A shepherd knows every sheep by name, but the sheep all know him by name as well. That's the important part."* The quote reinforces the studies' core distinction between a *shepherd* who leads and a *sheepherder* who merely drives — the mutual, name-by-name knowing of the Good Shepherd as a model for both missionary work and daily discipleship.

The notes give no biographical detail beyond the "Elder" honorific, which places him as a quoted source in Kyle's missionary-era topical journals rather than a public author.

## Where this appears

- The Good Shepherd — quoted on the shepherd knowing each sheep by name (and being known in return)
- Missionary Work — Topical Study — same quote reused in the "fishers and hunters" section on leading vs. driving
