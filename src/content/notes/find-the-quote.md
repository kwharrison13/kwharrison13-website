# Find the Quote

A personal workflow tag Kyle attaches to "open loops" in his notes — moments where he half-remembers a line and wants to track down the exact wording later. The #[Find the Quote](/notes/find-the-quote) flag is not a topic but a to-do marker that recurs across very different bodies of work: investing marginalia, gospel study, and notes on knowledge work.

In [How To Take Smart Notes](/books/how-to-take-smart-notes) it tags open loops to source an [Andy Matuschak](/notes/andy-matuschak) line about there being little deliberate "practice" in knowledge work, plus a missionary-journal line ("He is wise who knows what has been written and where to find it"). In the [Berkshire Hathaway Annual Letters](/books/berkshire-hathaway-annual-letters) it marks half-recalled investing aphorisms Kyle wants verbatim — "the ability for someone to sit in a room and do nothing," and the leverage maxim "if you're smart you don't need it and if you're dumb you shouldn't touch it." In gospel-study (e.g. August 24–30, 2020. Helaman 7–12: "Remember the Lord") it flags a mission poem and a [Joseph Smith](/notes/joseph-smith) quote about God working "by the power of words." In the Networked Conviction — Roam + Investing - Research notes it pairs with [Tiago Forte](/notes/tiago-forte) to flag a quote about the exponential explosion of content to read.

**Context:** This entity is idiosyncratic to Kyle's note-taking practice — a private tag, not a public concept. It functions like a GTD-style "open loop": a deliberately incomplete note left as a breadcrumb to finish later, consistent with the [How To Take Smart Notes](/books/how-to-take-smart-notes) / Zettelkasten habit of capturing first and refining on review.
