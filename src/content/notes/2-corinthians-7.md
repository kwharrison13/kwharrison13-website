# 2 Corinthians 7

A chapter of Paul's second epistle to the Corinthians, used in Kyle's gospel study as the anchor text for Godly Sorrow. The key verse — [2 Corinthians 7](/notes/2-corinthians-7):10, "for godly sorrow worketh repentance to salvation not to be repented of: but the sorrow of the world worketh death" — frames Kyle's distinction between two kinds of remorse: one that leads to [Self-Reflection](/notes/self-reflection) and the desire to change, and one that only makes a person angry at being shown their own awfulness (the "sorrowing of the damned").

In the Come Follow Me study June 29–July 5, 2020. Alma 23–29: "They Never Did Fall Away", this verse is paired with [Alma 25](/notes/alma-25):1 — the Lamanites who grew "more angry" at their guilt rather than repenting — as the negative example of worldly sorrow. The page Using Satan Against Satan applies the same passage practically: the heavy-duty sins enact a heavy-duty sorrow, so rather than wallow in self-pity one should simply accept the [Atonement](/notes/atonement) of Christ and move on.

**Context:** 2 Corinthians 7 is Paul rejoicing that his earlier, painful letter produced "godly sorrow" in the Corinthian saints — sorrow that led to repentance rather than mere regret — the source of the much-cited verse 10.
