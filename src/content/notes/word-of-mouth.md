# Word of Mouth

In [Product Led Growth — The End User Era](/books/product-led-growth-the-end-user-era), word of mouth is one of the core mechanisms by which a product-led company grows without a heavy sales motion — the product itself "drives end user conversion and expansion through increased usage, viral loops, collaboration and word-of-mouth referrals." The book frames it as the output of product quality rather than a marketing tactic: "The key to word of mouth is simple: Have a product that is so awesome people just can't stop talking about it." Its second formulation is even more direct — "the 'secret' is to deliver value... You need to create such a great experience during [Onboarding](/notes/onboarding) that people feel compelled to tell other people about it." In other words, word of mouth is not a channel you crack; it's a downstream effect of onboarding and value delivery, sitting in the book's "viral growth, word of mouth & community" cluster alongside [Community](/notes/community).

**Context:** Word-of-mouth growth — users recommending a product to others organically — is a foundational concept in product-led growth and viral marketing, prized because it lowers customer acquisition cost and signals genuine product-market fit.
