# CoProcure

**One-line:** *[Mariel Reed](/notes/mariel-reed)'s govtech startup improving city procurement and enabling cooperative purchasing across municipalities.*

## What we know

- Aims to build a product (explicitly *not* be a consulting company): three goals are (1) improve the procurement marketplace, (2) enable cooperative purchasing across cities, and (3) turn one-off contracts into recurring ones — positioning itself as the "regulatory and legal gearbox" for procurement. (May 16th, 2018 — Mariel Reed (CoProcure))
- Early "do things that don't scale" work is ride-along consulting with cities to understand procurement beyond the San Francisco context and to map the "can we do it this way?" regulatory questions. (May 16th, 2018 — Mariel Reed (CoProcure))
- Market framing: ~300 cities of 100K+ people have experimental budgets, but a huge long tail of ~20K cities/towns can't experiment yet would buy proven solutions; compared to "the Massdrop model for cities." (May 16th, 2018 — Mariel Reed (CoProcure))
- As of the call, Mariel had been full-time ~8 months, was finalizing a technical co-founder, and planned to start productizing in the fall after running initial experiments. (May 16th, 2018 — Mariel Reed (CoProcure))
- [Arik Bronshtein](/notes/arik-bronshtein) of [UrbanLeap](/notes/urbanleap) positioned CoProcure's "RFP" stage as downstream of a successful UrbanLeap pilot, and said Mariel may be joining his company. (June 1st, 2018 — Arik Bronshtein (UrbanLeap))
