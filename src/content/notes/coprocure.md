# CoProcure

**One-line definition:** *[Mariel Reed](/notes/mariel-reed)'s govtech startup improving city procurement and enabling cooperative purchasing across municipalities.*

## How sources describe it

- Aims to build a product (not be a consulting company): improve the procurement marketplace, enable cooperative purchasing, and turn one-off contracts into recurring ones — positioned as the "regulatory and legal gearbox" for procurement. (May 16th, 2018 — Mariel Reed (CoProcure))
- Early work is "ride-along" consulting with cities to understand procurement beyond the SF context; sees a large long-tail of ~20K cities/towns that would buy proven solutions but can't experiment.

## Related concepts

- [Procurement](/notes/procurement) — CoProcure's core domain: improving the city procurement marketplace and turning one-off contracts into recurring ones.
- [GovTech](/notes/govtech) — the broader category of the company; a product (not consulting) sold to municipal governments.
- [City Building](/notes/city-building) — addresses the long tail of ~20K cities/towns seeking proven solutions, framed as a "regulatory and legal gearbox."
- [Mariel Reed](/notes/mariel-reed) — founder of CoProcure.
- [UrbanLeap](/notes/urbanleap) — positions CoProcure's "RFP" stage downstream of an UrbanLeap pilot.
