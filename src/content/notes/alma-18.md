# Alma 18

Book of Mormon chapter Kyle treats as the master case study of [Ammon](/notes/ammon) teaching King Lamoni — his go-to model for missionary teaching by the Spirit. In his June 22–28, 2020. Alma 17–22: "I Will Make an Instrument of Thee" study he walks the chapter verse by verse: Lamoni astonished at Ammon's faithfulness (18:10), Ammon perceiving the king's thoughts because he was "filled with the Spirit of God" (18:16), the "wise, yet harmless" exchange (18:22), and Ammon beginning "at the creation of the world" to expound the plan of redemption (18:36, 39) until "the king believed all his words" (18:40).

Kyle builds two topical studies on this chapter. In Find To Teach, Alma 18 is named explicitly as the model of teaching's interior **Process** — be worthy, open your mouth, let the Spirit supply the words, and teach what the person needs — paired with [D&C 42](/notes/dandc-42):14. In Teaching Repentance, he holds up Ammon's "sweet, inviting boldness" (18:36) as the pattern for declaring repentance through invitation rather than hellfire, noting pointedly that "nowhere in Alma 18 does Ammon extend an invitation," yet his teaching was so plain "no one can misunderstand" (echoing [Harold B. Lee](/notes/harold-b-lee)).

**Context:** Alma chapter 18 is in the Book of Mormon (Latter-day Saint scripture). It records Ammon's service to and teaching of the Lamanite king Lamoni, leading to the king's conversion.
