# James Carse

Carse is the originator of the finite-vs-infinite-games distinction that anchors the [Infinite Games](/notes/infinite-games) long read (where [Jack Raines](/notes/jack-raines) applies the framework to how we live). The page quotes Carse directly: "There are at least two kinds of games: finite and infinite. A finite game is played for the purpose of winning, an infinite game for the purpose of continuing the play." It also quotes his reframing of work — an infinite player works "not… for the purpose of filling up a period of time with work, but for the purpose of filling work with time… moving toward a future which itself has a future." Raines uses these ideas to argue that optimizing for achievements (finite play) is a trap.

**Context:** James P. Carse (1932–2020) was an American academic and religious scholar, longtime professor at New York University, best known for his 1986 book *Finite and Infinite Games*.
