# Auteur Theory

**One-line:** *The idea that a single creative author imprints a personal vision across a film (often filling multiple roles — director, writer, producer, editor) — and, by analogy, that the most passionate "author" of a company beats the most credentialed.*

## How sources describe it

- A recurring lens in [The Hits Business](/essays/the-hits-business): Kyle loves films where one person "owns so much of a creative experience," and admires auteurs like [Christopher Nolan](/notes/christopher-nolan), [Quentin Tarantino](/notes/quentin-tarantino), the Coen Brothers, and [Martin Scorsese](/notes/martin-scorsese).
- [A24](/notes/a24)'s strategy: "look for true auteurs — then back them to the hilt." ([Mario Gabriele](/notes/mario-gabriele))
- The venture parallel: back the **most passionate**, not the most qualified, person — "the author" (e.g. [Mark Zuckerberg](/notes/mark-zuckerberg) as the author of Facebook; the failed "popcorn-movie" handling of [Ready Player One](/books/ready-player-one) by [Steven Spielberg](/notes/steven-spielberg)). [Ryan Reynolds](/notes/ryan-reynolds) cited as a real auteur "in everything that he does, from film to business."
