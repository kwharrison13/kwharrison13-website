# Ruth 3

A chapter of the Old Testament book of Ruth cited in Kyle's Born Again gospel study as part of the covenant-bride theme. The verse used is Ruth 3:9 — "I am Ruth, thine handmaid: spread therefore thy skirt over thine handmaid." In the note, the offering of clothing or garments reads as a covenant gesture, and Ruth's appeal to Boaz is grouped with [Ezekiel 16](/notes/ezekiel-16):8 ("I spread my skirt over thee… and entered into a covenant with thee") to develop the idea that God's people are a covenant bride — taken in lovingly even when they "play the harlot," and ever invited back ([Hosea 2](/notes/hosea-2)).

**Context:** Ruth 3 is the chapter in which Ruth, at Naomi's direction, approaches Boaz at the threshing floor and asks him to act as her kinsman-redeemer by spreading his garment over her — a key step in the redemption-and-marriage narrative of the book.

## Where this appears

- Born Again — cited at Ruth 3:9 (with [Ezekiel 16](/notes/ezekiel-16):8) to frame the covenant as a marriage and God's people as a covenant bride whose garment-covering signifies the binding promise.
