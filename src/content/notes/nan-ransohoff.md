# Nan Ransohoff

Nan Ransohoff leads [Stripe](/notes/stripe)'s climate / [Carbon Removal](/notes/carbon-removal) work (Stripe Climate). She surfaces in the [Watershed Climate](/notes/watershed-climate) Series B diligence (Watershed Climate Diligence — 2021) via an expert call on October 28th, 2021, where she described both the [Stripe](/notes/stripe)–Watershed relationship and her view of the carbon-removal market. Stripe is a Watershed customer, and Stripe Climate acts as Watershed's carbon-removal provider — making Stripe a channel partner. She noted that when she started at Stripe, Watershed co-founder [Christian Anderson](/notes/christian-anderson) (a few years ahead of her at Harvard undergrad) was a mentor to her.

In that call she framed Stripe Climate's org as a product team split into two halves: (1) raising money for carbon removal and making it seamless with Stripe's existing distribution, and (2) spending that money as effectively as possible by purchasing removal (rather than making equity investments). The bulk of Stripe Climate's ~10K users are startups without internal climate teams who want carbon removal to be "super easy" — distinct from the massive enterprises with complex programs that Watershed targets. Her sharpest diligence-relevant point: the north star is a gigaton of addressable emissions, which means going after "the [Walmart](/notes/walmart)s of the world"; carbon removal is maybe 10% of the problem, with the rest coming from reduction, and net-zero ultimately requires "an entire reimagining of the infrastructure." She judged the market to be at a tipping point, with hundreds or thousands of companies having made net-zero commitments.

**Context:** Nan Ransohoff is the head of Stripe Climate and Frontier, Stripe's advance-market-commitment program for carbon removal. She has been a prominent public voice on building demand for permanent carbon removal.
