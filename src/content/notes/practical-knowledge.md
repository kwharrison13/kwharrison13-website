# Practical Knowledge

In the August 2021 [Valued Empyrean](/notes/valued-empyrean) conversation with [Conor White-Sullivan](/notes/conor-white-sullivan) and [Darwin Harrison](/notes/darwin-harrison), **practical knowledge** is paired with [Theoretical Knowledge](/notes/theoretical-knowledge) as one of the things people need to succeed in creating "Value" — alongside [Technical Skills](/notes/technical-skills) and [Character Skills](/notes/character-skills). The distinction drawn in those notes is between *knowing what something is* and *being able to act on it*: theoretical knowledge is knowing what a Siloed Mindset or a Holistic Mindset are; practical knowledge is "the skill of recognizing when yourself, another person, or people are operating from a Siloed Mindset or Holistic Mindset," and the harder, downstream skill of "motivating someone to desire a Holistic Mindset."

The notes treat practical knowledge as the action-bearing companion to theory — the conversation observes that shifting someone's viewpoint is most workable only once they want to make the shift ("they have to be ready to, they have to have the desire — and they have to make the choice"), so the practical skill of motivating that desire is flagged as "a harder thing to do." It sits inside the broader framing that "we have a world full of people who are doers but not thinkers," with the implied goal of pairing both.

## Where this appears

- August 18th, 2021 — Valued Empyrean (Conor White-Sullivan & Darwin Harrison) — listed among the things people need to succeed; defined as the skill of *recognizing* and *motivating* mindset shifts, distinct from the theoretical knowledge of merely knowing the categories.
