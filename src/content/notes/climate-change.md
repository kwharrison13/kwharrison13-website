# Climate Change

In Kyle's corpus climate change shows up mostly as **an investing and "build" thesis** rather than as a standalone science topic. [It's Time To Build](/notes/it-s-time-to-build) ([Marc Andreessen](/notes/marc-andreessen)) frames it as a buildable problem: solve climate by building "a few thousand zero-emission [nuclear reactors](/notes/nuclear-energy)" — start with 10, then 100, then the rest. [How To Avoid a Climate Disaster](/books/how-to-avoid-a-climate-disaster) ([Bill Gates](/notes/bill-gates)) is the book-length version of that engineering-and-deployment lens (nuclear, [TerraPower](/notes/terrapower), [Breakthrough Energy](/notes/breakthrough-energy), the path from emissions to zero). The connective tissue across these is [Decarbonization](/notes/decarbonization) — the economy-wide mitigation lever that climate change is the problem behind.

On the deal side, climate is the thesis underneath several companies Kyle tracked: [Watershed Climate](/notes/watershed-climate) (carbon accounting / [Carbon Offsets](/notes/carbon-offsets)) and [CleanCapital](/notes/cleancapital) (solar and clean-energy financing) both serve the broader sustainable-energy thesis. The topic also threads through the reading logs below — the June 29th, 2021 Quick Capture batch is dense with climate links (AI for energy efficiency, carbon-removal startups, green-finance critiques), and Kyle keeps adding to it (the June 2026 batch on climate-and-mortgages). [Berkshire Hathaway Annual Letters](/books/berkshire-hathaway-annual-letters) references the topic from the capital-allocation/insurance angle.

**Context:** Climate change refers to long-term shifts in global temperatures and weather driven largely by greenhouse-gas emissions from human activity. In tech/VC circles it is treated both as a mitigation challenge (decarbonization, carbon accounting, clean energy) and as a large new market category ("climate tech").
