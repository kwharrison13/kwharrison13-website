# Walt Disney

In Kyle's corpus Walt Disney shows up far less as the cartoon mogul than as a **city builder and historical futurist**. The center of gravity is [Walt Disney and the Promise of Progress City](/books/walt-disney-and-the-promise-of-progress-city) (Sam Gennawey), which reconstructs his most ambitious and least-understood project: the original EPCOT — the Experimental Prototype Community of Tomorrow — not a theme park but a real city of ~20,000 residents Walt intended to build to "solve the problems of our cities." Gennawey reads Disney as an urban planner whose Main Street, hub-and-spoke plan, monorail, and PeopleMover were live prototypes for a transit-oriented, pedestrian-first city. The vision threads through [Christopher Alexander](/notes/christopher-alexander)'s "timeless way of building" and "quality without a name," and [Victor Gruen](/notes/victor-gruen)'s cellular theory of the city (Gruen's *[The Heart of Our Cities](/notes/the-heart-of-our-cities)* was the only urban-planning book in Walt's office). The unresolved tension at the heart of it: Walt's [Utopia](/notes/utopia) required near-total corporate control — no landowners, no voting residents — because "you can't experiment with people's lives," which is exactly what made it problematic. Walt died before ground broke, and a team uninterested in city-building quietly collapsed the dream into a vacation resort.

The page is also where Kyle's "How to Build a City" framework lives: be observant, build what people actually want, experiment using what you already run as the testbed, make transportation the backbone, design for the shared 90% of human feeling, and "think beyond your lifetime." Walt is grouped with the same voracious, multidisciplinary-learning visionary archetype Gennawey's corpus attributes to [Sam Walton](/notes/sam-walton) and [Elon Musk](/notes/elon-musk). Kyle's most pointed note ties Disney to his investing lens: "[Disney ($DIS)](/notes/disney) needs a [Walt Disney](/notes/walt-disney)-caliber CEO, and then they need to partner with [Tesla ($TSLA)](/notes/tesla)… maybe Tesla should buy Disney."

The second anchor is [The Ride of a Lifetime](/books/the-ride-of-a-lifetime) ([Bob Iger](/notes/bob-iger)'s memoir), where Disney is the founder whose brand and "technology as storytelling tool" ethos Iger inherits and extends — the same company a generation later. Disney also surfaces as a touchstone in Kyle's media/hits-business and creative-studio thinking (e.g. [A24](/notes/a24)).

**Context:** Walt Disney (1901–1966) co-founded The Walt Disney Company, pioneered feature animation and the theme park, and at his death was planning EPCOT as a real, experimental city in Florida — a plan abandoned by his successors and reborn as a theme park.
