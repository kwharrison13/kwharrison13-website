# The Rational Optimist

[Matt Ridley](/notes/matt-ridley)'s book, cited in [The Psychology of Money](/books/the-psychology-of-money) for its argument about the cultural status of pessimism. [Morgan Housel](/notes/morgan-housel) quotes Ridley's observation that "a constant drumbeat of pessimism usually drowns out any triumphalist song": say the world has been getting better and you're called naïve and insensitive; say it will keep getting better and you're "embarrassingly mad"; but predict imminent catastrophe and "you may expect a McArthur genius award or even the Nobel Peace Prize." Within Psychology of Money, the reference supports Housel's broader point about why frightening narratives feel smarter and spread faster than optimistic ones.

**Context:** *The Rational Optimist: How Prosperity Evolves* (2010) is Matt Ridley's book arguing that human progress is driven by trade and the "exchange of ideas," and that long-run trends justify optimism despite persistent cultural pessimism.
