# Ohana Capital

Ohana Capital is the firm of [Tim Ludwig](/notes/tim-ludwig), whom Kyle spoke with on March 5th, 2020 as a reference call for [Teamshares](/notes/teamshares) diligence (per March 5th, 2020 — Tim Ludwig (Ohana Capital)). Ludwig's frame of reference was debt-financed SMB roll-ups and ESOP-style employee-ownership models, drawing comparison to Canada's Link Equity — which won't buy more than ~4x EBITDA, raises debt from Canadian retirees at an 8% coupon, cross-collateralizes the businesses to offer income-producing exposure, and pays the debt down over 8–10 years. Through that lens Ludwig gave Kyle detailed written feedback on the Teamshares deck: skepticism about management-bench risk (small companies lack a leadership bench if a CEO fails), questions about how returns are underwritten when you "constantly sell your winners," surprise at the 50% seller debt, and doubt about the deck's claim of 10–15-year money at 8–10% (most acquisition bank debt runs ~5 years).
