# Spring Health

**One-line:** *Comprehensive, outcomes-based mental-health benefit for employers; vertically integrated and differentiated by measuring quality of care.*

## What we know

- Co-founded ~2016 by [Adam Chekroud](/notes/adam-chekroud) (Co-Founder & Chief Product Officer); grew out of his academic research building technology to identify symptoms and match patients to the best treatment — patients on the recommended route are reportedly 2x more likely to succeed (September 22nd, 2021 — Adam Chekroud (Spring Health)).
- A [Vertical Integration](/notes/vertical-integration) play: started by selling its software, then began delivering the care itself, accelerating outcomes (September 22nd, 2021 — Adam Chekroud (Spring Health)).
- Sells to large employers (Adobe, Pfizer, Gap, Pepsi), often replacing or augmenting EAPs, delivering care in the US and globally (September 22nd, 2021 — Adam Chekroud (Spring Health)).
- Unlike status-quo EAPs that just match patient to provider, Spring locks in rates AND takes risk on outcomes — performance guarantees and shared risk (e.g. 20% of a deal tied to time-to-recovery / recovery rates), and reports "who used it and who got better" (September 22nd, 2021 — Adam Chekroud (Spring Health)).
- Differentiation framed as (1) personalization (algorithms/IP matching symptoms to treatment, "looks like IP") and (2) comprehensiveness — therapy + medication + substance-abuse (launched with General Mills) + residential care, vs. [Lyra](/notes/lyra)'s therapy-heavy, medication-light model (September 22nd, 2021 — Adam Chekroud (Spring Health)).
- Announced a Series C at a $2B valuation; the big new customer launching next year is Guardian Life — its first payer, opening a TAM beyond employers (September 22nd, 2021 — Adam Chekroud (Spring Health)).
- Competitive set: in big employer RFPs it's "always Spring, [Lyra](/notes/lyra), and Ginger"; Ginger competes on immediate access/coaches/speed; Modern Health framed as more SMB "wellness" (September 22nd, 2021 — Adam Chekroud (Spring Health)).
- Uses [Kareo](/notes/kareo) as its EHR; not planning to build its own EHR soon (September 22nd, 2021 — Adam Chekroud (Spring Health)).
