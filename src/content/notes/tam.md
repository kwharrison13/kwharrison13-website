# TAM

TAM (total addressable market) is the market-sizing concept Kyle tags across his company-building and investing notes. The recurring theme is that the size of a market is not fixed but a function of imagination and execution. In [The Everything Store](/books/the-everything-store), Kyle tags the [Jeff Bezos](/notes/jeff-bezos)/Amazon line *"Sometimes there are small markets. And sometimes there are small minds. #TAM"* — the idea that an apparently small TAM often reflects a failure of vision rather than a real ceiling.

[Datadog](/notes/datadog) is the worked example of TAM *expansion*: in [Shardul Shah](/notes/shardul-shah)'s company-building case study, the lesson is that Datadog's "TAM expanded every six months" as the observability platform added products and reached more practitioners — pairing TAM growth with premium pricing and practitioner-led [Content Marketing](/notes/content-marketing). The related [Software TAM](/notes/software-tam) page tracks Kyle's references for sizing software markets specifically (e.g. the list of the largest software companies).

**Context:** Total Addressable Market (TAM) is the standard venture/strategy metric for the total revenue opportunity available for a product or service if it captured 100% of its market — often paired with SAM (serviceable addressable market) and SOM (serviceable obtainable market).
