# Universal Basic Income

**One-line:** *An unconditional, regular cash payment to everyone — framed in [Utopia For Realists](/books/utopia-for-realists) as "venture capital for the people" and the redistribution mechanism for the [Second Machine Age](/notes/second-machine-age).*

The core argument across Kyle's sources: the biggest reason poor people are poor is that they don't have money, and the no-strings cash transfer — not means-tested aid, job-training, or microcredit — is the most extensively-studied and effective anti-poverty tool ([Esther Duflo](/notes/esther-duflo)'s RCTs, the Manitoba "Mincome" experiment, the cash-vs-microcredit verdict). Bregman ties it to the [Bullshit Jobs](/notes/bullshit-jobs) and [Automation](/notes/automation) threads: as the labor market stops reliably distributing the fruits of progress, an unconditional income becomes the candidate replacement.

- [Utopia For Realists](/books/utopia-for-realists) — Bregman's book that frames UBI as "venture capital for the people" and the primary source for this page.
- [Rutger Bregman](/notes/rutger-bregman) — author who makes the case that no-strings cash, not means-tested aid, is the most-studied effective anti-poverty tool.
- [Second Machine Age](/notes/second-machine-age) — the era of automation-driven abundance for which UBI is offered as the redistribution mechanism.
- [Automation](/notes/automation) — as the labor market stops reliably distributing the fruits of progress, an unconditional income becomes the candidate replacement.
- [Bullshit Jobs](/notes/bullshit-jobs) — the parallel thread Bregman ties to UBI: decoupling income from increasingly meaningless work.
- [Esther Duflo](/notes/esther-duflo) — her RCTs are cited as evidence that the binding constraint on the poor is simply a lack of money.
- [Scarcity Mentality](/notes/scarcity-mentality) — the psychological cost of poverty that an unconditional cash floor is meant to relieve.
