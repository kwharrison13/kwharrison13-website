# Universal Basic Income

**One-line:** *An unconditional, regular cash payment to everyone — framed in [Utopia For Realists](/books/utopia-for-realists) as "venture capital for the people" and the redistribution mechanism for the [Second Machine Age](/notes/second-machine-age).*

The core argument across Kyle's sources: the biggest reason poor people are poor is that they don't have money, and the no-strings cash transfer — not means-tested aid, job-training, or microcredit — is the most extensively-studied and effective anti-poverty tool ([Esther Duflo](/notes/esther-duflo)'s RCTs, the Manitoba "Mincome" experiment, the cash-vs-microcredit verdict). Bregman ties it to the [Bullshit Jobs](/notes/bullshit-jobs) and [Automation](/notes/automation) threads: as the labor market stops reliably distributing the fruits of progress, an unconditional income becomes the candidate replacement.
