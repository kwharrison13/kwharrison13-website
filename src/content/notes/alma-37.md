# Alma 37

A chapter of Alma's counsel to his son Helaman that Kyle's scripture study returns to for several recurring teachings. Most prominent is the [Small and Simple Things](/notes/small-and-simple-things) principle: "by small and simple things are great things brought to pass; and small means in many instances doth confound the wise" (Alma 37:6-7) — read alongside Alma 36 and 38 as carrying the [Central Theme of the Book of Mormon](/notes/central-theme-of-the-book-of-mormon), "one of perseverant obedience" (July 20–26, 2020. Alma 36–38: "Look to God and Live"). The chapter also anchors the **Liahona** image of heeding the words of Christ: "DO NOT LET US BE SLOTHFUL BECAUSE OF THE EASINESS OF THE WAY… the way is prepared, and if we will look we may live forever" (Alma 37:45–46), which Missionary Work — Topical Study draws on directly.

Two further uses recur. Life's Gentle Helm takes Alma 37:35 ("learn in thy youth to keep the commandments of God" / "bridle ALL your passions, that ye may be filled with love") as a controlling image — life's control comes from the gradual, repetitive strengthening of faith through obedience; the point is to *bridle* (guide) the passions, not to imprison them. And Spiritual Gifts leans on Alma 37:33 ("humble yourselves") in its argument that "there is nothing you cannot overcome that is not ordained of God." The verse most often quoted whole is 37:13: "O remember, remember… how strict are the commandments of God… If ye will keep my commandments ye shall prosper in the land."

**Context:** Alma 37 is a chapter in the Book of Mormon, part of Alma the Younger's parting counsel to his son Helaman as he entrusts him with the sacred records, including instructions about the Liahona and the brass plates.
