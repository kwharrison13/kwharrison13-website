# David Blake

Co-author (with [Kelly Palmer](/notes/kelly-palmer)) of [The Expertise Economy](/books/the-expertise-economy), the book on workforce capability that Kyle read January 8–25, 2019 — and a personal recommender of it (Kyle's notes credit the read to Blake and [Signal Peak Ventures](/notes/signal-peak-ventures)). Within the book, Palmer and Blake propose the [Skills Quotient](/notes/skills-quotient) (SQ = skills you have ÷ skills you need × 100) as a way to quantify how well-equipped a workforce is, surface threats and opportunities, and flag flight risks — anyone scoring above 90 gets flagged because they'll seek growth elsewhere if it isn't offered. Their answer to the question of what expertise *is* — "anyone who can demonstrate the skill" — is what makes the book a deliberate title-rhyme counterpoint to [The Death of Expertise](/books/the-death-of-expertise).

**Context:** David Blake is an education-technology entrepreneur, co-founder of Degreed (and later Learn In), and co-author of *The Expertise Economy: How the Smartest Companies Use Learning to Engage, Compete, and Succeed* (2018) with Kelly Palmer.
