# Jony Ive

In Kyle's notes, Jony Ive figures mainly as [Apple](/notes/apple)'s longtime chief designer and as the other voice in a [Steve Jobs](/notes/steve-jobs) anecdote about vanity versus "the work." Per *Jony Ive: The Genius Behind Apple's Greatest Products* (recounted in [Conviction-Led Contrarianism](/essays/conviction-led-contrarianism) research): after Jobs mistreated the team, Ive said Jobs should be kinder "because I care about the team." Jobs replied: "No Jony, you're just really vain... you just want people to like you. And I'm surprised at you because I thought you really held the work up as the most important, not how you believed you were perceived by other people." Kyle uses the exchange to argue that the antidote to caring what people think is caring about *the work* — the [Likability](/notes/likability) tension at the heart of the page.

He also appears as part of the design lineage behind Apple's hardware decisions — the [Airpods](/notes/airpods) page situates the removal of the headphone jack within the Ive-era industrial-design philosophy of wireless, port-minimal products.

**Context:** Jony Ive (b. 1967) is the British industrial designer who led design at Apple for roughly two decades, shaping the iMac, iPod, iPhone, and MacBook lines, before departing in 2019 to found the design firm LoveFrom.
