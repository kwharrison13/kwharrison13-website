# Pave

Pave (formerly **Trove**) is a real-time compensation system-of-record — what the Pave Diligence — 2021 dossier calls "Radford in the cloud" — building the largest database of compensation data for tech through a **give-to-get data network**: companies share their comp data to access aggregated benchmarks. Founded by [Matt Schulman](/notes/matt-schulman), it was the subject of intensive diligence by Kyle at [Index Ventures](/notes/index-ventures) in late 2021, when Index attempted to pre-empt a Series C at a ~$1B valuation (a $50M lead in a $100M round) on the back of a $300K→$5M ARR year. The benchmarking product had reached 1,300+ companies, most onboarded through ~40 VC partnerships (November 5th, 2021 — Matt Schulman (Pave) Diligence). [a16z](/notes/a16z) led the earlier $16M Series A (Aug 2020, $75M post) and had 50%+ Pave penetration across its portfolio. The diligence flagged a [Carta](/notes/carta) dependency risk and mapped Pave against a crowded landscape — Carta, [Assemble](/notes/assemble), Compaas, PayScale, Radford, ChartHop.

Beyond the deal, Pave functions in Kyle's notes as a canonical example of the **"foundational data layer"** thesis: per the September 13th, 2022 — Harry Stebbings Podcast notes, Pave aspires to be the comp-data layer that lets companies read the labor market in real time, parallel to [Toast](/notes/toast) (restaurants), [Ramp](/notes/ramp) (expenses), and [Persona](/notes/persona) (identity) — companies that win by owning the foundational data of their domain. Sourcing and diligence calls with Armand Farrokh and others probed where the product wins relative to Workday and the European comp landscape.

**Context:** Pave is a San Francisco-based compensation-management and benchmarking startup founded by Matt Schulman in 2019 (originally as Trove). It provides tools for comp planning, benchmarking, and total-rewards communication, monetizing a crowdsourced real-time compensation dataset.
