# Endicott-Johnson

The shorthand name for the [Endicott-Johnson Shoe Company](/notes/endicott-johnson-shoe-company) — the shoe manufacturer at the heart of [George F. Johnson](/notes/george-f-johnson)'s [Square Deal](/notes/the-square-deal) and his experiment in [Welfare Capitalism](/notes/the-square-deal). The company was "neither an open shop nor a closed shop": a steady-work, premium-pay, profit-sharing model in which "every improvement and every saving" was divided three ways among workers, consumers, and the company ([George F. Johnson and His Industrial Democracy](/books/george-f-johnson-and-his-industrial-democracy)). It is the subject of historian [Gerald Zahavi](/notes/gerald-zahavi)'s scholarly study *Workers, Managers, and Welfare Capitalism*.

See **[Endicott-Johnson Shoe Company](/notes/endicott-johnson-shoe-company)** for the full entity page — scale (~52M shoes/year, ~19,000 workers), the company town, the union vote, and the later decline. This page exists only as the short-form alias that recurs across the corpus.
