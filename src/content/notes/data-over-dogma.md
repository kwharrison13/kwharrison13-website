# Data Over Dogma

Kyle's phrase for evaluating policy through the lens of human outcomes rather than partisan signaling — "policy and partisanship over practicality" inverted. It is tagged explicitly in [Romney — A Reckoning](/books/romney-a-reckoning), where it captures [Mitt Romney](/notes/mitt-romney)'s instinct to judge legislation by whether it actually helps people, against what the book calls "the rank commitment to dogma over practical outcomes." The grounding anecdote: Romney was embittered that critics on healthcare "had their facts wrong" but more so by the ideological reflex — "It was a little ironic," he grumbled, "that saving human lives was seen by some as being disqualifying in a Republican primary." The book frames the failure mode as "truth, obscured by nuance and buried under trifles," and notes Romney and [John McCain](/notes/john-mccain) shared a pragmatism "skeptical of ideological dogmas."

In [Voice of the People](/notes/voice-of-the-people), Data Over Dogma appears alongside [Spirit of Humility](/notes/spirit-of-humility) and [Living Revelation](/notes/living-revelation) as one of the recurring **counterweights to crowd-driven certainty** running through Kyle's reading — a brake on mob conviction and reflexive partisanship.
