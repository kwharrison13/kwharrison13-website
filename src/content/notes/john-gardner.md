# John Gardner

Cited in [The Pursuit of Meaning](/notes/the-pursuit-of-meaning) for the idea that "meaning is something you build into your life… out of your own past, out of your affections and loyalties" — that meaning is built, not bought, and that "you are the only one who can put them together into that unique pattern that will be your life."

**Context:** John W. Gardner (1912–2002) was an American writer and public official — Secretary of Health, Education, and Welfare under Lyndon Johnson and founder of Common Cause — known for his writing on self-renewal, leadership, and meaning.
