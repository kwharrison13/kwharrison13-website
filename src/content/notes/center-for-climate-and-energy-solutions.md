# Center for Climate & Energy Solutions

A U.S. climate-policy nonprofit, surfaced in the [Watershed Climate](/notes/watershed-climate) Series B diligence (Index Ventures, 2021) through its president [Nat Keohane](/notes/nat-keohane), a former White House climate advisor. At Watershed's COP26 customer/ecosystem event, co-founder [Taylor Francis](/notes/taylor-francis) interviewed Keohane in a "Policy: COP26 and Beyond" breakout, where his framing was that climate-policy advocacy should "set the stage by talking about how the policy is going to make people's lives better, in concrete ways they can see in the real world" before getting into technical detail. The organization sits alongside other climate institutions in Watershed's orbit — [Global Optimism](/notes/global-optimism) (Christiana Figueres) and [ClimateWorks](/notes/climateworks) (Rebecca Dell) — as part of the policy-and-ecosystem layer around Watershed's carbon-measurement platform.

**Context:** The Center for Climate and Energy Solutions (C2ES) is a Washington, D.C.–based nonprofit working on bipartisan climate and energy policy; it is the successor to the Pew Center on Global Climate Change. Nat Keohane became its president in 2020.
