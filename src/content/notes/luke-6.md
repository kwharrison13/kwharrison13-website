# Luke 6

A chapter of the Gospel of Luke that Kyle's gospel-study notes cite in two threads. In Abrahamic Covenant, Luke 6:13 appears with [Luke 10](/notes/luke-10), [Mark 3](/notes/mark-3):13–15, and [John 15](/notes/john-15):16 as evidence that Christ specifically chose his Apostles. In Diligence, Kyle quotes the chapter's beatitudes-and-woes (Luke 6:21, 25) — "Blessed are ye that hunger now; for ye shall be filled. Woe unto you that are full! For ye shall hunger" — using the reversal of fullness and hunger to frame spiritual appetite and the danger of complacency.

**Context:** Luke 6 in the New Testament includes the calling of the twelve Apostles and Luke's version of the Sermon on the Plain, with its beatitudes and corresponding woes.

## Where this appears

- Abrahamic Covenant — Luke 6:13 cited (with Luke 10, Mark 3, John 15) as evidence the Apostles were chosen by Christ.
- Diligence — the "hunger now / full now" beatitude-and-woe (6:21, 25) used to frame spiritual appetite vs. complacency.
