# “If Ye Had Known Me”

A talk flagged under "Talks to Read" in Kyle's 3 Nephi 27 – 4 Nephi study, listed alongside [The Correct Name of the Church](/notes/the-correct-name-of-the-church) and [The Importance of a Name](/notes/the-importance-of-a-name). It is gathered in the part of the study working through 3 Nephi 27 on taking Christ's name and being "built upon my gospel" — the distinction between calling something in Christ's name "in name only" versus being built on his doctrine. The thematic anchor is Matthew 7:21–23, where Christ says of those who prophesied in his name yet worked iniquity, "I never knew you," expanded in the [Joseph Smith Translation](/notes/joseph-smith-translation) to "Ye never knew me" (JST Matt. 7:33). The talk's title plays on that inversion — being truly *known by* and *knowing* Christ, rather than invoking his name without relationship.
