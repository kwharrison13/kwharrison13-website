# Poverty Stoplight

In [The New Urban Crisis](/books/the-new-urban-crisis), Poverty Stoplight is paired with [Fundacion Paraguaya](/notes/fundacion-paraguaya) as an example of bottom-up, entrepreneurship-driven anti-poverty work. Kyle's note attaches it to the book's observation that the poor often generate creative business ideas but lack the financial resources to refine those plans or the time to wait for markets to mature — making self-employment and entrepreneurship important alternatives where conventional jobs are scarce and the poor face discrimination in getting them. The reference frames Poverty Stoplight as a self-help, agency-centered approach to measuring and escaping poverty rather than a top-down transfer program.

**Context:** Poverty Stoplight is a self-assessment tool and methodology developed by Fundación Paraguaya that lets families rate their own situation across dozens of poverty indicators using a red/yellow/green color scale, then build a plan to move each indicator toward "green."

## Where this appears

- [The New Urban Crisis](/books/the-new-urban-crisis) — cited with [Fundacion Paraguaya](/notes/fundacion-paraguaya) as bottom-up, entrepreneurial anti-poverty work, against the finding that the poor have creative business ideas but lack capital and time.
