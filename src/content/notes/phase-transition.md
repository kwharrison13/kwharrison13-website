# Phase Transition

A system absorbing or emitting energy to change states (ice → water), used in [Critical States](/notes/critical-states) as the governing metaphor for nonlinear, tipping-point change. The dynamics of criticality are counterintuitive: cold water and about-to-freeze water look identical, but microscopically the latter is full of tiny ice crystals — one degree from a solid mesh.

**Context:** In physics, a phase transition is a change between states of matter (solid, liquid, gas) driven by energy and temperature. The concept is borrowed widely as a metaphor for sudden, system-wide shifts that follow long periods of apparent stability.
