# D&C 6

**D&C 6** is one of the most-cited Doctrine and Covenants sections across Kyle's [gospel-study](/notes/gospel-study) notes, with several verses doing distinct work. **6:9** — "say nothing but repentance unto this generation; keep my commandments; and assist to bring forth my work" — anchors Teaching Repentance, where Kyle reads it not as a charge to preach hellfire but to extend invitations and opportunities to repent. **6:4** ("whosoever desireth to reap, the same is called of God") anchors Chosen Leaders, grounding the distinction between the *called* (every leader) and the *chosen* (the few who truly lift and change lives). **6:14** ("God doesn't answer unasked prayers") appears in Prayer — Topical Study tied to Oliver Cowdery's worry, "what if I'm answering my own prayers?"

Other verses round out the section's range: **6:36** ("Doubt not, fear not") shows up in Born Again and Prayer — Topical Study; **6:8** (the Lord using Oliver's divining rod as a learning tool) appears in Joseph Smith — A Young Seer as evidence that God works through mundane things so people can exercise agency; and **6:38** appears in Worthiness explaining why missionary work goes slowly when people serve unworthily. Publication of the Book of Mormon notes that D&C 4 and 6 "speak directly to their audience."

**Context:** Doctrine and Covenants section 6 is an April 1829 revelation given through Joseph Smith for Oliver Cowdery, who had just become Joseph's scribe for the translation of the Book of Mormon; it includes counsel on revelation, repentance, and reassurance ("Doubt not, fear not").

## Where this appears

- Teaching Repentance — 6:9 as the charge to invite (not coerce) repentance
- Chosen Leaders — 6:4 grounding the called-vs-chosen distinction
- Prayer — Topical Study — 6:14 (unasked prayers) tied to Oliver Cowdery's question
- Born Again — 6:36 "Doubt not, fear not"
- Joseph Smith — A Young Seer — 6:8 (Oliver's divining rod) as God using mundane tools
- Publication of the Book of Mormon — D&C 4 & 6 "speak directly to their audience"
- Worthiness — 6:38 on unworthy service invalidating the work
