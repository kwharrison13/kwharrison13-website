# Emma Smith

Emma Smith is the wife of [Joseph Smith](/notes/joseph-smith), and across Kyle's wiki she sits at the center of two threads: Joseph's domestic life and the early Latter-day Saint debates over women's authority and polygamy. In Truman Madsen's *Joseph Smith Lecture 2*, she is the "elect lady" whom Joseph "loved with his whole soul" — the portrait of their home shows nine children (four lost at birth, one in infancy), three-times-daily family prayer, hymn-singing she led, a hymnal she compiled (some of it still in the modern hymnbook), and Joseph helping with chores and hospitality even toward enemies ("If ye will not embrace our religion, accept our hospitality"). Mother Smith's tribute, quoted there, praises her "unflinching courage, zeal, and patience" through persecution.

In *American Zion*, Emma is the institutional driving force behind the Relief Society: though the idea originated with Sarah Granger Kimball, "the prophet's wife quickly became its driving force," insisting the society not operate like "other Societies in the world." [American Zion](/books/american-zion) traces the women's-autonomy lineage she founded — "established by Emma Smith, expanded by [Eliza R. Snow](/notes/eliza-snow), defended by Emmeline B. Wells" — down to its later erosion under a streamlined priesthood. The book also surfaces her conflict with polygamy: unaware of the full details of Joseph's 1842 unions, she and Hyrum promised to banish those practicing it, and her protest is tied to the dictation of Doctrine & Covenants 132. A later biography depicting her as a "reform-minded activist" got its authors forbidden from speaking in church settings — a measure of how contested her memory remained.
