# Experiences vs. Possessions

A status-shift theme across Kyle's long reads: status is migrating from what people own to what they do and stand for. Millennials signal success through lifestyle rather than possessions, following friends and influencers to see what they do ([Equinox Is More Than A Gym. It's A Church](/notes/equinox-is-more-than-a-gym-it-s-a-church)); with cameras always on, experiences are processed as potential posts and an experience's value tracks its photographability ([Saving Retail](/notes/saving-retail)).

## Where this appears

- [Equinox Is More Than A Gym. It's A Church](/notes/equinox-is-more-than-a-gym-it-s-a-church) — the status shift from owning to doing that powers the whole thesis.
- [Saving Retail](/notes/saving-retail) — status migrating from ownership to activity and social capital (Baudrillard's "museumification").
