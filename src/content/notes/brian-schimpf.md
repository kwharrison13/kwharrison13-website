# Brian Schimpf

Brian Schimpf appears in Kyle's wiki as a co-founder and CEO of [Anduril](/notes/anduril), surfacing across two defense-and-innovation books. In [Skunk Works](/books/skunk-works), Kyle flags him on the "importance of being first" thread, tying his name to a Stratechery framing about punitive contract terms — his note reads "Brian Schimpf; Stratechery -> importance of being first (mention punitive contract terms)." Schimpf is positioned there alongside [Palmer Luckey](/notes/palmer-luckey) as the modern counterpart to [Kelly Johnson](/notes/kelly-johnson)'s founder-led Skunk Works playbook.

In [The Kill Chain](/books/the-kill-chain), Schimpf and Luckey are named together as the Anduril founders whose company the book treats as the obvious endpoint of its argument — the shift from a few exquisite platforms toward many cheap, autonomous, networked nodes ([Anti-Platform](/notes/anti-platform) warfare, the [Lattice](/notes/lattice) battle-network). The wiki also captures Anduril's hiring preference for veterans "because they have lived experience 'from the ground up,'" reinforcing the founder-led, mission-built model both books contrast against bureaucratized procurement.

**Context:** Brian Schimpf is the co-founder and CEO of Anduril Industries, the defense-technology company he started in 2017 with Palmer Luckey and others. He previously worked at Palantir Technologies.
