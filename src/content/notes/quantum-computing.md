# Quantum Computing

Quantum computing recurs across Kyle's notes mostly as a frontier surface area for deep-tech investing and national-security strategy. In the April 5th, 2022 — Francesco Corea (Balderton Capital) conversation it is named as an example of a "basic facts around the technology" deep-dive for Balderton's research deck, and Corea cites quantum (alongside robotics and remote communications) as deep-tech the firm has actually funded. [The Kill Chain](/books/the-kill-chain) flags quantum computing as one of several future surface areas (with BCIs, hypersonics, directed-energy weapons) where defense innovation has to go; it recounts [Google](/notes/google)'s 2019 "quantum supremacy" demonstration (and IBM's pushback), and frames the real near-term race as quantum-cracking-encryption versus quantum-resistant cryptography — with Kyle's own notes registering Palmer Luckey's apparent skepticism ("Palmer sort of dismisses it?").

The historical and speculative bookends come from two other reads. [The Man From The Future](/books/the-man-from-the-future) traces qubits and entanglement back to [John von Neumann](/notes/john-von-neumann), the first to prove wave and matrix mechanics were the same theory and to formalize quantum mechanics — situating today's machines in a century-old foundations debate that "never goes away." In the near-future novel [2054](/books/2054), quantum computing turns load-bearing: "bio-integrated quantum computing" is imagined as the substrate fusing biological and technological evolution, the path to [Ray Kurzweil](/notes/ray-kurzweil)'s hard-takeoff Singularity.

**Context:** Quantum computing uses quantum-mechanical effects (superposition and entanglement of qubits) to perform certain computations infeasible for classical machines. Its most-cited near-term implications are breaking current public-key cryptography (motivating post-quantum cryptography) and simulating quantum systems for chemistry and materials science.
