# David Allen

Author of "Getting Things Done" (2001), discussed in *How to Take Smart Notes* as the source of the "great insight" that collecting everything needing attention into one place and processing it in a standardized way produces a "mind like water" — the state of focusing on the task in front of you without competing thoughts nagging at working memory. The book also argues GTD can't be directly transferred to insight-driven writing, since GTD relies on clearly defined objectives while writing that aims at insight starts vague and can't be broken into fixed next-steps the way GTD requires.
