# Publishing

In Kyle's wiki, "Publishing" covers the business of producing and distributing books and the service/tooling layer growing around authorship. The load-bearing note is a "pickax" thesis from his April 30th, 2020 Quick Capture log: people want to become published authors to establish thought leadership (and command higher consulting/speaking fees), but most lack the time or expertise — so a done-for-you service captures the value. Tucker Max's [Book in a Box](/notes/book-in-a-box) (now Scribe Writing) is the canonical example, charging a $25K+ minimum to ghostwrite a client's book in their voice; Kyle's note records it passing $11.3M in revenue by 2017 and becoming more lucrative than Max's own best-sellers. This connects publishing to [Self-Publishing](/notes/self-publishing) (the author-controlled model the productized services build on).

The Books 2.0 - Research notes track a second front: the *distribution* fight. Kyle flags four big publishers suing the Internet Archive over its scanning of more than a million books and lending them for free — a tag pointing to the broader tension between legacy publishing's gatekeeping/copyright posture and the internet's push toward abundance and open access.

**Context:** Book in a Box was founded by Tucker Max in 2015 and later rebranded Scribe Writing / Scribe Media; it is widely cited as an example of a productized "done-for-you" professional service. The Internet Archive lawsuit (*Hachette v. Internet Archive*) was filed in 2020 by four major trade publishers over the Archive's "Controlled Digital Lending" program.
