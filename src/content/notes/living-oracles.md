# Living Oracles

"Living oracles" is the doctrine of present, ongoing revelation through living prophets — that the word of God spoken by a current prophet outweighs even the standing scriptural canon. It appears in Kyle's Alma 13–16 gospel-study note via the well-known Kirtland anecdote: when a leading man argued the Church should "confine ourselves" to the written word of God in the Bible, Book of Mormon, and Doctrine and Covenants, Joseph Smith asked Brigham Young to respond. Brigham laid the three books down and declared, "when compared with the living oracles those books are nothing to me… I would rather have the living oracles than all the writing in the books." Joseph affirmed that Brigham had "told you the truth." The note uses the story to elevate present revelation over a closed canon.

**Context:** In Latter-day Saint usage, the "living oracles" are the current prophets and apostles, whose counsel is held to take precedence over past written revelation precisely because it speaks "direct to us now."
