# 1 Corinthians 10

A New Testament chapter (Paul's first epistle to the Corinthians) that recurs across [Kyle Harrison](/notes/kyle-harrison)'s gospel study as a touchstone for two distinct ideas. The most-cited verse is **10:13** — "God is faithful, who will not suffer you to be tempted above that ye are able; but will with the temptation also make a way to escape" — which Kyle pairs with [1 Nephi 16](/notes/1-nephi-16):2 in his 1 Nephi 16–22 — I Will Prepare the Way before You study: the guilty take the truth to be hard, but the hard things are bearable because God caps temptation at what we can bear. It also surfaces in his Alma 13–16 study as a companion text on enduring trial.

Elsewhere Kyle draws on the chapter's teaching on the [Sacrament](/notes/sacrament) and self-examination — "when in doubt, check it out" (10:11) — in 3 Answers To Every Prayer, emphasizing that we examine our own souls rather than demand to know who is or isn't worthy. In Physical Appearance of Missionaries he leans on **10:21–33** (alongside [Romans 14](/notes/romans-14)) for the principle that a disciple should never let a "personal word of wisdom" become a stumbling block to those they hope to help — standing above the standard rather than offending the watching world.

**Context:** 1 Corinthians 10 is a chapter in which Paul warns the Corinthian church against idolatry using Israel's wilderness wanderings as a cautionary example, teaches that no temptation is beyond God-given endurance, and counsels believers to forgo their personal liberty when exercising it would harm another's conscience ("all things are lawful... but not all things edify").

## Where this appears

- 1 Nephi 16–22 — I Will Prepare the Way before You — 10:13 paired with [1 Nephi 16](/notes/1-nephi-16):2: hard truths are bearable because God will not tempt us beyond our ability.
- Alma 13–16 — Enter into the Rest of the Lord — 10:13 quoted as a companion text on enduring temptation and trial.
- 3 Answers To Every Prayer — 10:11 ("when in doubt, check it out") on sacrament worthiness and self-examination.
- Physical Appearance of Missionaries — 10:21–33 (with [Romans 14](/notes/romans-14)) on not letting personal conduct offend or distract from the message.
