# Galileo

Invoked by [Charlie Munger](/notes/charlie-munger) as the anchor of the **Second Notion** in [Charlie Munger — Practical Thought About Practical Thought](/notes/charlie-munger-practical-thought-about-practical-thought) (Talk 4 of [Poor Charlie's Almanack](/books/poor-charlie-s-almanack)). Munger leans on Galileo's conclusion that "scientific reality is often revealed only by math as if math was the language of God," and argues that the same attitude "works well in messy, practical life" — without numerical fluency, "you are like a one-legged man in an ass-kicking contest." In Kyle's reading layer the line is tagged #[God is a Scientist](/notes/god-is-a-scientist), using Galileo to make the case for numeracy as a core practical-thinking skill rather than a purely academic one.

**Context:** Galileo Galilei (1564–1642) was an Italian astronomer, physicist, and mathematician, a central figure of the Scientific Revolution, often associated with the view that the book of nature "is written in the language of mathematics."
