# Consent of the Governed

In Kyle's corpus this concept anchors the central warning of [The Assault on Reason](/books/the-assault-on-reason): [Al Gore](/notes/al-gore)'s claim that the "consent of the governed" — which the book calls "the very source of legitimate political power in a democracy" — is degrading into "a commodity to be purchased by the highest bidder." Gore frames this against a [Democracy](/notes/democracy) premised on a well-informed citizenry and the rule of reason, arguing that the founders "could never have imagined" that the marketplace of ideas would change so profoundly that legitimacy itself could become something bought.

The book ties the corruption of consent directly to medium and money. Where [Propaganda](/notes/propaganda) and [Television](/notes/television) displace print reason, the public is fed a "counterfeit version of the consent of the governed" — "American Democracy: The Movie," a semblance of participatory democracy that produces endorsement without the ability to test the propositions presented. With access to the public forum monetized, manufactured consent looks real but is, in Gore's telling, a hollowed-out simulation of the genuine article.

**Context:** "Consent of the governed" is a foundational principle of liberal democratic theory — traceable to Enlightenment social-contract thinkers and enshrined in the U.S. Declaration of Independence ("governments… deriving their just powers from the consent of the governed") — holding that a government's legitimacy rests on the agreement of the people it governs.
