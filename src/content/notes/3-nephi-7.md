# 3 Nephi 7

A Book of Mormon chapter recounting the breakdown of Nephite government into tribes and the bold ministry of [Nephi](/notes/nephi), son of Helaman, in a wicked generation. It falls within Kyle's Come Follow Me week on 3 Nephi 1–7 (whose substantive notes concentrate on 3 Nephi 1). In Kyle's Teaching Repentance study it supplies a model of inviting, faith-driven boldness: Nephi's hearers "were angry with him, even because he had greater power than they, for it were NOT POSSIBLE THAT THEY COULD DISBELIEVE his words, for so great was his faith on the Lord Jesus Christ" (v.18). Kyle pairs this with [Ammon](/notes/ammon) as an example of the "sweet, inviting boldness of master teachers" — calling people to repentance as an expression of love rather than condemnation.

**Context:** 3 Nephi 7 describes the dissolution of the central Nephite government into tribal factions and Nephi the son of Helaman ministering with great power in the years just before Christ's appearance in the Americas.
