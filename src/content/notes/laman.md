# Laman

Laman appears in Kyle's gospel-study notes paired almost always with his brother [Lemuel](/notes/lemuel) as the [Book of Mormon](/notes/book-of-mormon)'s archetypal hardened, unhearkening sons of [Lehi](/notes/lehi). In the July 27–August 2, 2020. Alma 39–42: "The Great Plan of Happiness" study, Kyle references the Church's tree-of-life video in which Lehi pleads with Laman and Lemuel, who "don't hearken to his words" — the contrast between an invitation extended and a heart unwilling to receive it.

In The Process of the Book of Mormon, Laman figures in the early-narrative beats: the angel appearing to Laman and Lemuel, and the recurring [Nephi](/notes/nephi)-vs-Laman-and-Lemuel tension that drives the opening books. Together the references make Laman shorthand for the unbelieving sibling whose refusal to hearken sets up the spiritual fault line of the record.

**Context:** Laman is the eldest son of the prophet Lehi in the Book of Mormon; with his brother Lemuel he repeatedly rebels against his father and younger brother Nephi, and their descendants become the Lamanites.
