# Zeitgeist

A recurring framing word across Kyle's essays for "the moment" — the prevailing cultural mood a company or product can ride or fail to. Its central use is [The Hits Business](/essays/the-hits-business)'s "At the Mercy of the Zeitgeist": [A24](/notes/a24)'s critical advantage is "its focus on embracing and understanding culture; the zeitgeist, the general vibes." Kyle generalizes this into a company-building law: "When you look at companies that capture 'the moment,' they so often revolve around a particular cultural vein" — Anduril captures the zeitgeist of Western values, Ramp of commitment to quality, Canva of the democratization of creation, Deel of a global workforce. The AI boom, he argues, "awoke something in people… because the capability of ChatGPT made them feel something." This is the curatorial pole of [Tastemakers](/notes/tastemakers) (A24 as taste connoisseur curating the zeitgeist rather than chasing scale).

[The Meme Economy](/essays/the-meme-economy) elevates the same idea to a thesis — "the ability to capture the human zeitgeist is proving to be one of the most powerful business capabilities anyone can have" — distinguishing it from the flatter "attention economy." In [Cultivating Cults](/essays/cultivating-cults), the zeitgeist is an economic force on the cost side: a "macro tailwind or zeitgeist" can "outsource" the cost of educating a customer, and when the zeitgeist dies (as with Roam Research) that educational cost becomes unbearable. [The Man Who Broke Capitalism](/books/the-man-who-broke-capitalism) uses the word historically — Jack Welch "tapping into profound changes in the zeitgeist," the postwar free-market intellectual revolution (Hayek, then Friedman) that made shareholder primacy feel inevitable.

**Context:** *Zeitgeist* (German, "spirit of the time") denotes the dominant set of ideas, beliefs, and cultural mood characteristic of a particular era.

## Where this appears

- [The Hits Business](/essays/the-hits-business) — "At the Mercy of the Zeitgeist"; A24 and the company-building examples (Anduril, Ramp, Canva, Deel) that capture a cultural vein.
- [The Meme Economy](/essays/the-meme-economy) — "Harnessing The Human Zeitgeist" as a core business capability, distinct from the attention economy.
- [Cultivating Cults](/essays/cultivating-cults) — the zeitgeist as a macro tailwind that "outsources" customer-education cost (and what happens when it dies, e.g. Roam).
- [The Man Who Broke Capitalism](/books/the-man-who-broke-capitalism) — Welch tapping the free-market zeitgeist of the postwar intellectual revolution.
- [Tastemakers](/notes/tastemakers) — the curatorial advantage of capturing rather than chasing the zeitgeist.
