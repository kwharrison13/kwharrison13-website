# Britannica

In Kyle's notes, Encyclopedia Britannica functions as the legacy, closed-production foil that sets off the scale of [Wikipedia](/notes/wikipedia). The contrast is quantitative: Wikipedia carries roughly 6.3 million articles, while Britannica "never had more than ~100,000, even digitally" ([Matt Clancy](/notes/matt-clancy), *Free Knowledge and Innovation*). That gap is the entry point for the broader open-source-knowledge argument Kyle is building — that collectively, openly maintained reference works outrun expert-gatekept ones not just on access but on raw coverage.

**Context:** Encyclopædia Britannica is the oldest continuously published English-language general encyclopedia, founded in Edinburgh in 1768. It discontinued its print edition in 2012 to focus on its online and educational products, after its closed, professionally authored model was largely displaced by Wikipedia.
