# Stephen Schwarzman

Stephen Schwarzman is the founder of [Blackstone](/notes/blackstone) and the source of a quote that became load-bearing in Kyle's venture-industry writing. Kyle read his memoir, [What It Takes](/books/what-it-takes), and repeatedly cites the "Why not?" passage on how Blackstone was built: *"If we came across the right person to scale a business in a great investment class, why not? … Other firms, we felt, defined themselves too narrowly, limiting their ability to innovate."* Kyle reads Blackstone as a "holding company" for financial asset classes (PE, real estate, hedge funds, credit, $800B+ AUM) that identified the ~80% of any business it could supply — network, resources, brand — and then found the person with the magic 20% to scale it.

This becomes the seed for Kyle's "[The Blackstone of Innovation](/essays/the-blackstone-of-innovation)" thesis, raised in his September 13th, 2022 — Harry Stebbings Podcast prep and developed across The Blackstone of Innovation - Research, The Puritans of Venture Capital - Research, and Competitive Moats - Research — the idea that large venture firms are consolidating into Agglomerators that look like Blackstone. Kyle also cites Schwarzman's conviction that "it's as hard to start a small business as a big one," so one should "choose one with the potential to be huge."

**Context:** Stephen A. Schwarzman (b. 1947) co-founded the private-equity firm Blackstone in 1985 and serves as its chairman and CEO. His 2019 memoir is titled *What It Takes: Lessons in the Pursuit of Excellence*.
