# Literature

Literature as a recurring theme across Kyle's reading notes — the written art and the "mysterious power" it carries to forge connection across distance. In [China in Ten Words](/books/china-in-ten-words), [Yu Hua](/notes/yu-hua) names that power directly: that "one can read a book by a writer of a different time, a different country, a different race, a different language, and a different culture and there encounter a sensation that is one's very own" — set against the "reading famine" of the Cultural Revolution and the question "how does one become a writer?" answered "by writing." It sits in that book alongside [Reading](/notes/reading) and [Writing](/notes/writing) as a cluster.

In [Metamorphosis](/books/metamorphosis), the note tags Kafka's novella as "a canonical work of modernist short fiction," placing it in a lineage of literature about selfhood erased by an inescapable system (linked to [1984](/books/1984) and [Brave New World](/books/brave-new-world)). In [John Quincy Adams](/books/john-quincy-adams), the term attaches to Adams's journals — written for himself alone, late at night by candlelight — repeatedly called "one of the great works of American political literature," even as Adams dismissed his own mind as pedestrian.

## Where this appears

- [Metamorphosis](/books/metamorphosis) — Kafka's novella tagged as a canonical work of modernist short fiction
