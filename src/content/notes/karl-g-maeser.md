# Karl G. Maeser

Karl G. Maeser surfaces in Kyle's notes as a 19th-century Latter-day Saint educator and a source of recurring teaching-and-faith aphorisms. In [Saints — No Unhallowed Hand](/books/saints-no-unhallowed-hand) he appears on the education thread: as a classroom teacher he made "books, especially the scriptures, came alive," encouraged the Young children (including Susa Young Gates) to "ask questions and puzzle out solutions," and told a frustrated student that "only those who have the courage to make mistakes ever learn worthwhile lessons and truths." The same book records [Brigham Young](/notes/brigham-young)'s charge to him — "I want you to remember that you ought not to teach even the alphabet or the multiplication table without the Spirit of God" — fusing pedagogy with devotion.

In [Chad's Big Quote Book](/notes/chad-s-big-quote-book) Maeser is quoted with his well-known Alps parable: pointing at sticks marking safe passage through the snow, he likened the priesthood to "just common sticks… Some of them may even seem to be a little crooked. But the position they hold makes them what they are. If we step aside from the path they mark, we are lost" — a metaphor for following imperfect leaders.

**Context:** Karl G. Maeser (1828–1901) was a German-born convert to The Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-day Saints who became a foundational Mormon educator, serving as the first principal of Brigham Young Academy (the precursor to Brigham Young University).
