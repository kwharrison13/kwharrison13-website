# Proverbs 3

A chapter from the Old Testament book of Proverbs, used in Kyle's scripture study as a cross-reference on divine chastening. In the August 31–September 6, 2020. Helaman 13–16: "Glad Tidings of Great Joy" study, Proverbs 3:11 is cited verbatim — "My son, despise not the chastening of the LORD; neither be weary of his correction" — tagged to the [Spirit of Humility](/notes/spirit-of-humility). The study uses it as Old Testament support for the principle that God [Chasten](/notes/chasten)s those He loves, echoing the same idea across [Ether 2](/notes/ether-2), [Helaman 12](/notes/helaman-12), [Job 5](/notes/job-5), and [Revelation 3](/notes/revelation-3):19. It is paired with [Proverbs 19](/notes/proverbs-19):18 in the page's cross-references.

**Context:** Proverbs is a book of Hebrew wisdom literature in the Old Testament, traditionally associated with King Solomon. Proverbs 3:11–12 ("for whom the LORD loveth he correcteth") is quoted in the New Testament in Hebrews 12.
