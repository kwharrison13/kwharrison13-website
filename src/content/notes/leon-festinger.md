# Leon Festinger

Social psychologist who coined the term [Cognitive Dissonance](/notes/cognitive-dissonance) and whose 1956 book *When Prophecy Fails* is its seminal account. He surfaces in Kyle's notes through [Utopia For Realists](/books/utopia-for-realists), which quotes him directly on why people defend convictions against disconfirming evidence: "A man with conviction is a hard man to change. Tell him you disagree and he turns away. Show him facts or figures and he questions your sources. Appeal to logic and he fails to see your point." The dissonance mechanism Festinger names — "when reality clashes with our deepest convictions, we'd rather recalibrate reality than amend our worldview… we become even more rigid in our beliefs than before" — anchors the [Cognitive Dissonance](/notes/cognitive-dissonance) concept page, where it sits alongside [J.D. Vance](/notes/j-d-vance)'s more social rendering of the same idea in [Hillbilly Elegy](/books/hillbilly-elegy).

**Context:** Leon Festinger (1919–1989) was an American social psychologist whose theory of cognitive dissonance (1957) holds that people experience discomfort from conflicting beliefs and resolve it by rationalizing rather than updating; *When Prophecy Fails* was his field study of a doomsday cult that intensified its beliefs after the predicted apocalypse failed to arrive.
