# Oil Man in Heaven

A parable Charlie Munger uses in [Charlie Munger — A Lesson on Elementary Worldly Wisdom](/notes/charlie-munger-a-lesson-on-elementary-worldly-wisdom), grouped with his "I don't sell to fish" fishing-tackle story, to illustrate how incentives and self-interest distort behavior in investment management. The tag marks a recurring Munger teaching device: tales that expose the gap between what is sold and what actually serves the customer.

## Where this appears

- [Charlie Munger — A Lesson on Elementary Worldly Wisdom](/notes/charlie-munger-a-lesson-on-elementary-worldly-wisdom) — tagged alongside the fishing-tackle ("Mister, I don't sell to fish") anecdote on misaligned incentives in money management.
