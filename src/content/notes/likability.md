# Likability

**One-line definition:** *The pursuit of others' approval — "a jail" when it becomes the primary thing you optimize for, crowding out creative output and conviction.*

## How sources describe it

- [John Mulaney](/notes/john-mulaney): "likability is a jail" — borrowed by [Alex Karp](/notes/alex-karp) for his essay on agreeableness vs. creative output.
- [Alex Karp](/notes/alex-karp): a "culture of agreeableness... can move institutions away, not toward, creative output."
- Lao Tzu (via Twitter): "care about what other people think and you will always be their prisoner."
- [Steve Jobs](/notes/steve-jobs) to [Jony Ive](/notes/jony-ive): wanting people to like you is vanity that competes with caring about "the work."
- Kyle's nuance: there's "plenty of room for likability" in low-stakes social life; the danger is letting it govern conviction. Credibility + conviction can overpower likability ([Brie Wolfson](/notes/brie-wolfson)'s caveat: don't weaponize this into being difficult for its own sake).
