# Remnant Theology

In Kyle's Abrahamic Covenant study, remnant theology is the idea that God has always preserved a righteous remnant of the chosen lineage — that no matter how far Israel scatters or apostatizes, a more-righteous core is kept. The note grounds it in [Isaiah 51](/notes/isaiah-51):1–2 and [Isaiah 1](/notes/isaiah-1):9 ("there is always a remnant of the more righteous preserved"), and in [Abraham 1](/notes/abraham-1):4, read as the promise to Enoch that a remnant of his seed would ALWAYS remain on the earth. It also surfaces in the divided-kingdom history, where kingdoms "became remnant" when they returned to the Southern Kingdom ([2 Chronicles 11](/notes/2-chronicles-11):14–17).

This sits inside the larger covenant logic of the study: inheritance flowing father-to-son through covenant, the birthright passing to [Ephraim](/notes/ephraim), and the last-days [Gathering of Israel](/notes/gathering-of-israel). The preserved remnant is what makes the gathering possible — the through-line of covenant lineage that the Restoration re-collects.

**Context:** "Remnant" theology is a broad biblical and Latter-day Saint motif — the recurring scriptural pattern (Isaiah, the prophets) that God preserves a faithful minority through judgment and scattering as the seed of future restoration.

## Where this appears

- Abrahamic Covenant — remnant theology as the "always a remnant of the more righteous preserved" principle (Isaiah 1:9, 51:1–2; Abraham 1:4), tied to lineage, covenant, and the gathering of Israel.
