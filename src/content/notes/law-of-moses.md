# Law of Moses

Across Kyle's [Come, Follow Me](/notes/come-follow-me) studies, the Law of Moses appears chiefly as a *type* — an outward, preparatory law the Nephites kept while looking past it to the coming of Christ. In the Alma 30–31 study, [Alma 25](/notes/alma-25):15 and [Alma 30](/notes/alma-30):3 are read together: the people were "strict in observing the ordinances of God, according to the law of Moses; for they were taught to keep the law of Moses until it should be fulfilled," yet "notwithstanding the law of Moses, they did look forward to the coming of Christ, considering that the law of Moses was a type of his coming." Kyle's gloss: the ability to see past the law to Christ "was just a matter of preparation."

The Helaman 7–12 study turns this into a comparative question — why the Nephites kept the Law of Moses without developing the same intense focus on "tradition" the Jews did in the Old World, possibly because they knew early on of Christ and how he would fulfill the law. The 3 Nephi 8–11 study marks the fulfillment itself: with Christ's coming, "sacrifice and burnt offerings are done away" ([3 Nephi 9](/notes/3-nephi-9):19), and in their place he asks for a [Broken heart and a contrite spirit](/notes/broken-heart-and-a-contrite-spirit) ([3 Nephi 9](/notes/3-nephi-9):20). [3 Nephi 1](/notes/3-nephi-1):24 flags the error of those who preached it was "no more expedient to observe the law of Moses" before its fulfillment, "having not understood the scriptures."

**Context:** The Law of Moses is the body of commandments and ritual ordinances given to ancient Israel through Moses (the Mosaic covenant). In Book of Mormon and Latter-day Saint theology it is understood as a preparatory law, fulfilled by the atonement and ministry of Jesus Christ.

## Where this appears

- Alma 30–31 — the Nephites keep the law "until it should be fulfilled," looking past it to Christ as a type of his coming
- Helaman 7–12 — Kyle's question on why the Nephites kept the law without the Old-World focus on tradition
- 3 Nephi 8–11 — the law fulfilled at Christ's coming; burnt offerings replaced by a broken heart and contrite spirit
