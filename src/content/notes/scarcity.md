# Scarcity

Scarcity is a hinge concept that runs in two registers across Kyle's wiki, and the pages are careful to keep them distinct. The first is the **religious/abundance** sense: in [Gordon B. Hinckley — Go Forward With Faith](/books/gordon-b-hinckley-go-forward-with-faith), Hinckley's "things will work out" motto is read as an "attitude of abundance rather than scarcity" — expecting the best and working to make it happen rather than dwelling on the dark side. [Scarcity Mentality](/notes/scarcity-mentality) explicitly flags itself as the *behavioral-economics* sense (Mullainathan & Shafir, via [Utopia For Realists](/books/utopia-for-realists)) as opposed to this religious/abundance meaning.

The second is the **material/economic** sense. In [Evicted](/books/evicted), land scarcity is named as a deliberate moneymaker — concentrated poverty as "social design, not accident" — and the book is read as a direct study in how scarcity makes "irrational" choices rational ("small pleasures to season the suffering"). [Capital Allocator Mindset](/notes/capital-allocator-mindset) borrows the word metaphorically: the premise the mindset imposes is that attention and interest are scarce capital rather than something endless, to be allocated deliberately.
