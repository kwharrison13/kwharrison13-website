# Carta

In Kyle's [Pave](/notes/pave) diligence (2021), [Carta](/notes/carta) is treated as the single most important competitive variable — both the chief threat to Pave and the closest analog for its playbook. The memo's framing: there are some legacy comp players ([PayScale](/notes/payscale), [Shareworks](/notes/shareworks)) and early-stage startups ([Assemble](/notes/assemble), [Welcome (Comp Planning)](/notes/welcome-comp-planning)), but "the only viable threat is Carta," which launched a Total Compensation Management tool a few months prior. The bull case discounted that threat on three grounds — comp isn't a priority for Carta, Carta isn't known for product execution, and the PM running the project referenced poorly. The deeper analogy runs the other way: just as Carta moved equity from law-firm spreadsheets into software and built "the largest database of equity in the world" (selling 409A valuations, fund admin, and secondaries on top of that data asset), Pave is said to be running a parallel playbook against the larger compensation-data opportunity — and has hired a significant number of GTM people away from Carta (Armand, Bonnie, Kilian, Morgan, Caroline).

A recurring risk thread is **platform dependency**: Pave reads salary/equity data out of systems like Carta, so [Dominic Jacquesson](/notes/dominic-jacquesson) pressed [Matt Schulman](/notes/matt-schulman) on the "Carta dependency risk" — the scenario where Carta pulls API access. Schulman felt Pave was near "escape velocity," where Carta would face too much customer pushback to cut them off, but acknowledged an "uneasy relationship" with Carta CEO [Henry Ward](/notes/henry-ward) (notably, [a16z](/notes/a16z) is invested in both companies). Customer-reference calls reinforce Carta's gravity: [Mark Seidel](/notes/mark-seidel) named Carta (Pave's founders are ex-Carta) as the biggest competitive threat and as one of the few "essential" comp tools alongside [Zenefits](/notes/zenefits) and [Workday](/notes/workday); [Allbirds](/notes/allbirds) and Ro both use Carta for equity and integrate it with Pave. Kyle noted Carta was valued at $7B+ with 17K customers, used as a strong comp for Pave's own valuation.

**Context:** Carta is a San Francisco equity-management and cap-table software company (founded 2012 as eShares), known for digitizing startup cap tables, 409A valuations, fund administration, and a private-market data business.
