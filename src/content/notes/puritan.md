# Puritan

In Kyle's notes the Puritans appear through [Walter Isaacson](/notes/walter-isaacson)'s biography [Benjamin Franklin — An American Life](/books/benjamin-franklin-an-american-life), where the Puritan migration is cast as the cultural soil that shaped both [Benjamin Franklin](/notes/benjamin-franklin) and the early American character. Isaacson writes that "the Puritan migration established the foundation for some characteristics of Benjamin Franklin, and of America itself: a belief that spiritual salvation and secular success need not be at odds, that industriousness is next to godliness, and that free thought and free enterprise are integrally related." The thread Kyle pulls forward is this fusion of religious devotion with worldly enterprise — a worldview in which diligence and commerce are themselves moral.

**Context:** The Puritans were 16th–17th-century English Protestants seeking to "purify" the Church of England of perceived Catholic remnants; many emigrated to New England beginning in the 1620s–30s, where their theology of vocation, thrift, and industriousness became a durable strand of American culture (the root of Max Weber's later "Protestant work ethic" thesis).

## Where this appears

- [Benjamin Franklin — An American Life](/books/benjamin-franklin-an-american-life) — the Puritan migration framed as the foundation of Franklin's (and America's) belief that spiritual salvation and secular success need not be at odds.
