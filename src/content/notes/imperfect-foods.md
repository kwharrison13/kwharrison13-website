# Imperfect Foods

Imperfect Foods is one of the most substantive customer references in the Watershed Climate Diligence — 2021 dossier — a grocery/DTC company whose Head of Sustainability, [Madeline Rotman](/notes/madeline-rotman), gave Index Ventures two reference calls (May and October 2021) on her use of [Watershed Climate](/notes/watershed-climate). Imperfect had already hired Watershed and *re-chose* them after re-bidding its climate accounting, citing Watershed's ability to "grow and scale with us" and its three-in-one combination of carbon accounting, a reduction-planning tool, and an offsets marketplace (versus point solutions like [Patch](/notes/patch), Pachama, or Climate Care). Rotman described Imperfect as "one of the more high-touch customers," moving from annual to quarterly footprints and meeting a dedicated Watershed rep weekly, and praised Watershed's team of PhDs as a better alternative to expensive consultants.

For Kyle's diligence, Imperfect served as evidence on several theses: that the product is "low floor, high ceiling," that Watershed could expand beyond tech into food/consumer businesses, and that the binding constraint is data accessibility — Rotman's data lived across utility bills and tools like [Expensify](/notes/expensify) that "aren't ready" to expose clean APIs. She also flagged the company's strategic-engine potential: "this could move Watershed away from just 'sustainability' to become a decision-making engine."

**Context:** Imperfect Foods was a San Francisco-based online grocery delivery company (founded 2015) that sold "imperfect" or surplus produce and groceries to reduce food waste. It was acquired by Misfits Market in 2022.
