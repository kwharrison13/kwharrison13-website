# Jeff Immelt

Immelt shows up in Kyle's notes twice with different emphases. In January 11th, 2022, Kyle reads [Morgan Housel](/notes/morgan-housel)'s "I Have A Few Questions" and pulls an Immelt line — "Every job looks easy when you're not the one doing it" — tagged under #Empathy, as a prompt for recognizing how naive one is to work one hasn't done firsthand. Separately, his page is tied to [The Man Who Broke Capitalism](/books/the-man-who-broke-capitalism) as Jack Welch's successor at GE and a central figure in that book's narrative of the company's decline.

**Context:** Jeffrey Immelt was CEO of General Electric from 2001 to 2017, succeeding Jack Welch. His tenure spanned the 2008 financial crisis and is frequently cited in critiques of GE's long unwinding from its peak.
