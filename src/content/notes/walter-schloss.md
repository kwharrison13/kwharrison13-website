# Walter Schloss

Walter Schloss is a value investor quoted in [On the Nature of Long-Term Holds](/notes/on-the-nature-of-long-term-holds) on [Compounding](/notes/compounding): "Remember the power of compounding. You don't need to stretch for returns to grow your capital over the course of your life."

**Context:** Walter Schloss (1916–2012) was a renowned value investor and a disciple of Benjamin Graham, cited by Warren Buffett in his "Superinvestors of Graham-and-Doddsville" essay for his decades-long market-beating record.

## Where this appears

- [On the Nature of Long-Term Holds](/notes/on-the-nature-of-long-term-holds) — quoted on patient compounding over a lifetime rather than stretching for returns.
