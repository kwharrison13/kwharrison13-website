# Character Sketches

In James Traub's biography of [John Quincy Adams](/books/john-quincy-adams), "character sketches" names a self-imposed exercise from Adams' college years: as a Harvard student he set himself the task of producing written portraits of all forty-six of his classmates, in alphabetical order over a period of months, listing each one's birth date and home. The biography frames this as a window into Adams' temperament — he "deeply admired several of his classmates, liked most, and despised a few," and "few of them escaped unscathed from Adams' harsh judgment." Kyle tags the passage with [Be Observant](/notes/be-observant), reading the sketches as an early instance of disciplined, exacting observation of people.
