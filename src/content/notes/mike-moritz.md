# Mike Moritz

Legendary [Sequoia](/notes/sequoia) partner who appears throughout [The Founders](/books/the-founders) (Jimmy Soni's PayPal history) as the institutional adult in the room during PayPal's near-death years. Moritz arrived at PayPal's June 2000 board meeting and told the team, "you guys have seven months of runway left… You're not going to raise money again. The market is gone!" — a warning [Roelof Botha](/notes/roelof-botha) recalled as "bracing but clarifying," drumming into the founders that they should not assume more capital would come. He pitched the X.com/Confinity merger to [Max Levchin](/notes/max-levchin) as "a merger for the ages… the most important merger in the history of Silicon Valley."

Two of his moments recur in Kyle's notes. First, as a study in [Storytelling as Leadership](/notes/storytelling-as-leadership): on [Elon Musk](/notes/elon-musk), Moritz observed, "Elon, as the world knows today, is a very gifted storyteller. And some of the stories even come true" — a line Kyle pulls into The Storytelling of Investing - Research and tags `#Storytelling`. Second, as governance: when the board pushed Musk out, Moritz insisted that [Peter Thiel](/notes/peter-thiel) could be interim CEO but PayPal would have to run a proper CEO search. The mandated search left Thiel bitter and created a rift with Sequoia's Moritz that, per Soni, "never fully healed," reinforcing PayPal's allergy toward "executive experience."

**Context:** Michael Moritz is a Welsh-born venture capitalist and longtime partner (and former chairman) of Sequoia Capital, known for early investments in Yahoo, Google, PayPal, and many other companies; he began his career as a journalist at Time.
