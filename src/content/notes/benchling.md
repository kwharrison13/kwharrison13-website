# Benchling

Life-sciences R&D software company that appears across Kyle's notes as a marker of pedigree and as a category exemplar, rather than as a diligence subject in its own right. In the January 31st, 2022 — Ravi Parikh (Airplane.dev) call, Benchling shows up as a credential — Airplane was "working with Josh who was previously the CTO of Benchling." In the September 22nd, 2021 — Mark Goldberg (Career Feedback) notes it is listed among the high-conviction growth deals Goldberg cited (alongside [Ramp](/notes/ramp), [Chainalysis](/notes/chainalysis), and [Ada](/notes/ada)) as the kind of company he'd argue hard to invest in. And in the Pave Diligence — 2021 dossier it appears in a roster of notable vertical/modern SaaS companies ([GitLab](/notes/gitlab), [Reddit](/notes/reddit), Alation, Algolia).

**Context:** Benchling is a San Francisco cloud-platform company providing R&D software for biotech and life-sciences teams (lab notebooks, sample tracking, registries); it raised large late-stage rounds and is often cited as a leading vertical-SaaS success story for an unusually technical end market. (General background.)
