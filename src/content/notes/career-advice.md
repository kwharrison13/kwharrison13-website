# Career Advice

A tag Kyle attaches to the question of how to build a career well, anchored mostly in [Charlie Munger](/notes/charlie-munger)'s commencement-talk advice. The core injunction is **lean hard into intense interest and into your strengths**: "intense interest in any subject is indispensable if you're really going to excel," so "if at all feasible, you want to maneuver yourself into doing something in which you have an intense interest" ([Charlie Munger — USC Gould School of Law Commencement Address](/notes/charlie-munger-usc-gould-school-of-law-commencement-address)). The inverse is just as blunt — "if you try to succeed in what you're worst at, you're going to have a very lousy career" — where Kyle's own marginal note flags that this "requires intense [Self-Reflection](/notes/self-reflection)" and that "early in your career it's hard to discern what you're bad or good at vs. what you just haven't learned yet" ([Charlie Munger — A Lesson on Elementary Worldly Wisdom, Revisited](/notes/charlie-munger-a-lesson-on-elementary-worldly-wisdom-revisited)). The same talk's "the very first step towards success in any occupation is to become interested in it" reinforces the interest-first thesis.

The concept pairs closely with [Lilly Pads of Your Career](/notes/lilly-pads-of-your-career) — the stepping-stone framing of "what should a young person look for in a career?" — and surfaces in [Becoming](/books/becoming), where [Michelle Obama](/notes/michelle-obama)'s "could I have everything? Would I have everything?" passage about work life vs. home life is tagged as a career-advice moment.
