# AI Superpowers

AI Superpowers appears in the wiki as a cited book, not one Kyle has read: a highlight in [Chip War](/books/chip-war) references it as "a widely discussed book by Kai-Fu Lee, former head of Google China." It surfaces inside Chip War's argument about computing power as the decisive arena of US–China rivalry — the same thesis Kyle is mining the book for. As a book mentioned-but-unread, it sits as an anti-library candidate (the kind of cited reference that belongs in `books-to-read` with Chip War as its "how I heard about it" provenance) rather than a developed concept.

**Context:** *AI Superpowers: China, Silicon Valley, and the New World Order* (2018) is a book by Kai-Fu Lee, a venture capitalist and former president of Google China, arguing that China and the United States are the dominant powers in the global AI race.
