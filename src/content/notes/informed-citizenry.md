# Informed Citizenry

Informed citizenry is the precondition that, in Kyle's notes, keeps [Voice of the People](/notes/voice-of-the-people) from collapsing into mob rule. The idea is that legitimacy flows from the collective will of the people only when that people is equipped with knowledge — captured in the Madison line quoted in [The Death of Expertise](/books/the-death-of-expertise): "a people who mean to be their own Governors must arm themselves with the power which knowledge gives." [The Assault on Reason](/books/the-assault-on-reason) sharpens the same point from the other side: legitimate self-rule requires reason and deliberation, not mere preference or choice. An informed citizenry is the [Expertise](/notes/expertise) a self-governing people must arm itself with; its erosion is what makes the "wisdom of crowds" curdle into the false claim that "the crowds are wise."

The concept overlaps closely with [Educated Electorate](/notes/educated-electorate), which the [Voice of the People](/notes/voice-of-the-people) page frames as the structural precondition keeping popular sovereignty from degrading into the tyranny of an uninformed majority. Where "educated electorate" emphasizes the structural/institutional guardrail, "informed citizenry" emphasizes the deliberative, knowledge-bearing quality of the citizens themselves.

**Context:** "Informed citizenry" is a long-standing idea in democratic and republican political theory — most associated with the American founding (Jefferson, Madison) — holding that self-government depends on citizens who are educated and informed enough to deliberate and hold power accountable.
