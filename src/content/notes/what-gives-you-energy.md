# What gives you energy?

A recurring personal tag Kyle applies across his reading to passages about what energizes versus drains a person — covering both the people and the kind of work worth choosing. From [Gordon B. Hinckley — Go Forward With Faith](/books/gordon-b-hinckley-go-forward-with-faith), he flags [Henry B. Eyring](/notes/henry-b-eyring)'s account of Hinckley: "When I'm with him, I'm wiser… he brings down the power of heaven into my life," paired with Kyle's own takeaway — "don't work for anyone you don't respect and admire" (linked to [Charlie Munger](/notes/charlie-munger)). From [Becoming](/books/becoming), he tags Michelle Obama's counsel to surround yourself with people who don't weigh you down: "good relationships feel good… they don't hurt," and to choose friends and partners by that test.

In [How To Take Smart Notes](/books/how-to-take-smart-notes), the same tag marks Ahrens's distinction between exergonic and endergonic work: some work constantly drains energy and only moves if you keep adding more, while a good workflow "gains momentum, pulling us along and sometimes even energizing us." Ahrens ties this to [Mihaly Csikszentmihalyi](/notes/mihaly-csikszentmihalyi)'s [Flow](/notes/flow) and a "virtuous circle" where positive experience motivates the next task — versus a "vicious circle of failure." Across all three, the tag is a lens for designing a life and a working process around what generates energy rather than depletes it.
