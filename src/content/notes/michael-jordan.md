# Michael Jordan

Michael Jordan appears in Kyle's notes on [Discipline Is Destiny](/books/discipline-is-destiny) as the model for the "lift-the-supporting-players" frame: Jordan learned to elevate teammates like [Dennis Rodman](/notes/dennis-rodman) despite their flaws and weaknesses, rather than trying to force them to be Michael Jordan — "Help them be their best." Kyle reads this as the leadership analog to the book's refrain "whatever you are, be a good one," applied to the people you work with rather than to yourself.

The book sets Jordan as the positive contrast to [Kobe Bryant](/notes/kobe-bryant), who struggled his entire career to accept that his teammates "couldn't all be Kobes" — driving them so hard he sometimes drove them away (as with Shaquille O'Neal), depriving himself of a supporting cast. Jordan is the example of discipline directed outward as patient leadership; Kobe is the cautionary foil.

**Context:** Michael Jordan (b. 1963) is a former American professional basketball player, widely regarded as the greatest of all time, who won six NBA championships with the Chicago Bulls — three of them alongside rebounding specialist Dennis Rodman.
