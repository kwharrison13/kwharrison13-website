# 2 Nephi 4

The Book of Mormon chapter containing **"the Psalm of Nephi,"** and the source Kyle returns to for Nephi's model of humility, scripture-delight, and trust amid weakness. Several distinct verse-threads run through his studies. On **delighting in the scriptures**, 2 Nephi 4:15–16 ("my soul delighteth in the scriptures… my heart pondereth continually") anchors both December 31–January 6, 2019. "We Are Responsible for Our Own Learning" and the General Conference — October 2021 notes ("What do you ponder in your soul?").

The chapter's heaviest use is in Humility — Topical Study, where Nephi (2 Nephi 4:17, "O wretched man that I am") joins Ammon and Alma as a spiritual giant *because* he recognized his inadequacy, and where 4:19, 28 ("NEVERTHELESS, I know in whom I have trusted… Awake, my soul!") frames the [Atonement](/notes/atonement) as a two-part learning process. The verse **4:31** ("wilt thou make me that I may shake at the appearance of sin?") recurs as Kyle's working definition of sanctification — "abandon sin and become comfortable with God" — in the June 15–21, 2020. Alma 13–16: "Enter into the Rest of the Lord" and June 29–July 5, 2020. Alma 23–29: "They Never Did Fall Away" studies. Finally, in Obedience — Topical Study, 2 Nephi 4:6, 10, 12–13 supplies the grey-area case of Nephi being "constrained by the spirit" to slay Laban — Kyle's prime example of obedience "adapted to circumstances" by revelation.

**Context:** 2 Nephi 4 is a chapter in the Book of Mormon. Its latter portion, often called "the Psalm of Nephi," is a poetic lament in which the prophet Nephi mourns his own sinfulness yet reaffirms his trust in God — a passage frequently cited in LDS teaching on humility, repentance, and faith.
