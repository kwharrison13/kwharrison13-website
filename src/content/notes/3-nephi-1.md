# 3 Nephi 1

**3 Nephi 1** opens the Come Follow Me week September 7–13, 2020. 3 Nephi 1–7: "Lift Up Your Head and Be of Good Cheer", whose focus Kyle frames as the night without darkness fulfilling Samuel's sign, [Nephi](/notes/nephi)'s sorrow over the wickedness of his people, Satan's lies sent to harden hearts against the signs they had seen, and the [rising generation](/essays/the-rising-generation) who "became for themselves." In the following week's study, September 14–20, 2020. 3 Nephi 8–11: "Arise and Come Forth unto Me", Kyle cites 3 Nephi 1:24 on those "few that began to preach, endeavoring to prove by the scriptures that it was no more expedient to observe the law of Moses" — who "did err, having not understood the scriptures."

**Context:** 3 Nephi 1 is the chapter in the Book of Mormon where the sign of Christ's birth is fulfilled — a day, a night, and a day without darkness — as prophesied by Samuel the Lamanite, vindicating the believers who had been threatened with death.
