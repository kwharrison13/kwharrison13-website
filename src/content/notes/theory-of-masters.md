# Theory of Masters

"Theory of Masters" is Kyle's tag for a cluster of ideas about mastery, training grounds, and getting access to the people at the top. Its origin is a scene in [What It Takes](/books/what-it-takes): Felix Rohatyn tells a young Stephen Schwarzman that at Lazard "there are two types of people: masters like me and slaves like you would be," and advises him to "go to Lehman to be trained" before returning to Lazard "as a master." Kyle reads this as the rule to *know whether you're in a training ground or an arena* — go somewhere that will train you before you try to be a master — and pairs it directly with [Outliers: The Story of Success](/notes/outliers-the-story-of-success) (the Beatles in Hamburg, Gates on the Washington computers): mastery comes from repetitive practice before it yields its greatest rewards.

The concept takes on a second, complementary sense in [Zach Edwards](/notes/zach-edwards)'s investing notes. In the July 2020 call, Edwards offers an operator-first, access-the-master version: "Theory of Masters — I'd rather call the author than read their book." Here mastery is about going straight to the source — getting "a great, smart person to run something" rather than working secondhand. Together the two readings frame "Theory of Masters" as a personal heuristic about how expertise is acquired (train first) and how it's leveraged (go to the master directly).
