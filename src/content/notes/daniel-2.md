# Daniel 2

The Old Testament chapter containing Daniel's interpretation of King Nebuchadnezzar's dream of a great image — used in Kyle's Abrahamic Covenant topical study as one of the two scripture chains (alongside [Ezekiel 37](/notes/ezekiel-37)'s stick prophecy) establishing the [Gathering of Israel](/notes/gathering-of-israel) in the last days. In the study's reading, Daniel tells Nebuchadnezzar his dream and interprets it ([Daniel 2](/notes/daniel-2):24–25, 31–33): the image's metals map to the succession of empires of history down to the European nations, and the stone "cut without hands" that strikes the image is read as the restored church — "the rock that shall roll forth, organized in 1830" ([Daniel 2](/notes/daniel-2):44).

The chapter appears in the study's "Daniel's image — metals, empires & the Ten European Nations" section (Topical Journal 1.55) and recurs in the final scripture-chain list, placing it in the LDS framework where the empires of prophecy terminate in the Restoration.

**Context:** Daniel 2 is a chapter of the biblical Book of Daniel. Nebuchadnezzar dreams of a statue with a head of gold, chest of silver, belly of bronze, legs of iron, and feet of iron and clay; a stone cut without hands destroys it and becomes a mountain. The standard reading interprets the metals as a sequence of kingdoms succeeding Babylon, with the stone as a final, divinely established kingdom.
