# ChartHop

ChartHop appears throughout Kyle's Pave Diligence — 2021 work as an adjacent player and competitor in the people-data and compensation-planning market. Its product centralizes people data — salary, equity, demographics, performance — synced from the HR tech stack, then layers on org charts, smart guides, and benchmarks ("everything you can do in Excel you do within ChartHop"); the diligence notes cite [MongoDB](/notes/mongodb)'s [Alex Koykova](/notes/alex-koykova) using it to consolidate over a hundred headcount-planning spreadsheets into one platform. ChartHop is listed in the competitive landscape Kyle mapped for [Pave](/notes/pave) alongside [Carta](/notes/carta), [Assemble](/notes/assemble), [Compaas](/notes/compaas), [Welcome](/notes/welcome-comp-planning), [Radford](/notes/radford), and others.

Across the customer diligence calls, the picture is of a tool people stretch beyond its intended use. In the November 1st, 2021 — Marie Szuts (Figma) — Pave Diligence call, [Figma](/notes/figma)'s Marie Szuts describes "hacking ChartHop to be an interface" for comp planning, having cycled through Compaas, ChartHop, and Google Sheets — each affecting the internal HR brand. In the November 2nd, 2021 — Lisa Wallace (Assemble) call, ChartHop is positioned as co-embedded with Carta, plugging into the HRIS and visualizing it, raising the question of why you'd manage headcount in ChartHop when you could model costs and headcount in the same place as merit cycles and comp bands. It also surfaces briefly in the June 24th, 2021 — Brainstorm with Gokul Rajaram, Eric Tarczynski & Saanya Ojha notes as a company that "just got done" (raised).

**Context:** ChartHop is an HR-tech company (founded 2019, New York) that built a "people analytics" platform centered on dynamic org charts, headcount planning, and compensation data pulled from a company's HRIS.
