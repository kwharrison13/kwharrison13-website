# Sora Schools

Online, project-based middle/high school that Kyle tracks as a portfolio company (via [Contrary - Portfolio Updates (Feb 2022)](/notes/contrary-portfolio-updates-feb-2022)) and as an exemplar of the [Alternative Education](/notes/alternative-education) thesis. Its February 2022 update flagged two things: that "over half of our prospective families still mention the lack of an in-person space being a significant concern" — families fall for the model/pedagogy first, then decide whether online school will work for them — and that a new [Head of Product](/notes/head-of-product), [Risto Lähdesmäki](/notes/risto-l-hdesm-ki), was bringing sophistication and quality to the software team. (March 30th, 2022 — Sora Schools February Update)

Across Kyle's reading notes, Sora is cited as a concrete instance of the self-directed, long-term-group-project model the evidence favors: [Why Can't School Be Fun?](/notes/why-can-t-school-be-fun) names it under "long-term group projects with real responsibilities," and [The Science of Learning](/notes/the-science-of-learning) lists it among project-based and self-directed models. It sits alongside [AltSchool](/notes/altschool) (tracked as a cautionary rebrand case) under the [Alternative Education](/notes/alternative-education) theme. ([Why Can't School Be Fun?](/notes/why-can-t-school-be-fun), [The Science of Learning](/notes/the-science-of-learning), [Alternative Education](/notes/alternative-education))

**Context:** Sora Schools is a U.S.-based accredited virtual school for middle and high school students built around project-based, interest-driven learning rather than a conventional grade-and-subject structure.
