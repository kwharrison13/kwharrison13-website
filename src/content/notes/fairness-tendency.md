# Fairness Tendency

One of the psychological tendencies in Charlie Munger's framework, discussed in [Charlie Munger — The Psychology of Human Misjudgment](/notes/charlie-munger-the-psychology-of-human-misjudgment). Munger frames it as a Kantian impulse toward reciprocal fairness, and speculates that this "Kantian Fairness Tendency" was a major contributor to the abolition of slavery over the last three centuries after it had coexisted with the world's major religions for far longer.

## Where this appears

- [Charlie Munger — The Psychology of Human Misjudgment](/notes/charlie-munger-the-psychology-of-human-misjudgment) — Munger's account of fairness as a deep human disposition, used to explain large-scale moral shifts.
