# Lecture 1: Faith Defined

The first of the *Lectures on Faith*, cited in Kyle's study of Christ Is Risen; Faith in Him Will Move Mountains — Russell M. Nelson for its definition of faith as power. The lecture (verse 24) states that "Faith, then, is the first great governing principle which has power, dominion, and authority over all things: by it they exist, by it they are upheld, by it they are changed, or by it they remain, agreeably to the will of God. Without it, there is no power, and without power there could be no creation, nor existence."

In the gospel-study note it anchors the talk's claim that faith is not passive belief but an active, governing power — a foundation later extended by [Lecture 7: The Effects of Faith](/notes/lecture-7-the-effects-of-faith) (faith as "mental exertion") and Lecture 3 (the three things necessary to exercise faith in God).

**Context:** The *Lectures on Faith* are a series of seven theological lectures on faith prepared for the School of the Prophets in Kirtland, Ohio, in 1834–35, associated with [Joseph Smith](/notes/joseph-smith) and included in early editions of the Doctrine and Covenants of the Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-day Saints.
