# Excommunication

In Kyle's gospel study, excommunication is framed less as punishment than as **mercy**. The Excommunication — Topical Study (Topical Journal 1.81–1.82) reads the [Atonement](/notes/atonement) as an umbrella: sins under it are forgivable (D&C 93:25), and Satan lies when he says we cannot be forgiven, because the Atonement shifts the punishment from us to Christ. Sins that fall *outside* the umbrella are unforgivable in the sense that "we have to pay our own price," and for these there is excommunication. The study situates this against the three worst sins — denying the Holy Ghost (unpardonable), murder (the unauthorized taking of life), and sexual sin (the unauthorized giving of life) — each of which touches "the essence of the power of God," and notes the Church's revealed positions that abortion is not the same as murder (forgivable without excommunication) and that those who die by suicide are judged by God, not by man.

The weekly study July 13–19, 2020. Alma 32–35: "Plant This Word in Your Hearts" sharpens the mercy framing: excommunication is "not an act of punishment, but an act of mercy releasing you from your covenants and removing the weight of condemnation that comes from breaking them" (tied to the accountability-of-knowledge theme in Alma 32:19). The September 14–20, 2020. 3 Nephi 8–11: "Arise and Come Forth unto Me" study carries the same thread alongside [Regret](/notes/regret) and [Broken heart and a contrite spirit](/notes/broken-heart-and-a-contrite-spirit).

**Context:** Excommunication in the Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-day Saints (now termed "withdrawal of membership") removes a person from formal Church membership and releases them from associated covenants; it is taught as a step that can open the way back through repentance rather than a permanent severance.
