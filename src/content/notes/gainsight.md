# Gainsight

Gainsight is the **category-creating [Customer Success](/notes/customer-success) incumbent** across Kyle's diligence notes — the benchmark every other CS tool is measured against. The Planhat - Diligence expert calls return to it constantly: it was built originally as a [Salesforce](/notes/salesforce) add-on (~90% of its CRM connections are to Salesforce) and, through early entry, helped *define* the category. With [ToTango](/notes/totango) it is one of the two omnipresent enterprise incumbents (often #1/#2 in the US), praised for the deepest feature set — AI key-driver analysis, scorecards, best multinational reach (~41 native integrations vs. Planhat's ~17). But experts repeatedly flag the downsides: **clunky/legacy UI, high cost, complex integrations, occasionally troublesome support**, and a PE-ownership red flag that "innovation may have slowed." Several describe it as ripe for disintermediation — "the Gainsight way is not going to work" for some segments — while others remain committed precisely because it's the category leader.

It also surfaces as the cautionary "shelfware" case in the November 15th, 2021 — David Bromberg (Lantern) meeting, where a customer had paid for Gainsight for a year and no one had ever touched it — and where a key buyer persona is "sales-led people who tried Gainsight and hated it." In [Salesforce](/notes/salesforce)'s page it's the recurring example of a tool built atop the Salesforce substrate.

**Context:** Gainsight is a customer-success software platform founded in 2009; it is credited with popularizing "customer success" as a SaaS discipline. It was taken private by Vista Equity Partners in 2020.
