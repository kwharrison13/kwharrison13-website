# Deception

Deception is the signature claim the wiki draws from [The Art of War](/books/the-art-of-war): "All warfare is based on deception." Sun Tzu makes it operational — "when able to attack, we must seem unable; when using our forces, we must seem inactive; when we are near, we must make the enemy believe we are far away; when far away, we must make him believe we are near." The source page links this to [Game Theory](/notes/game-theory), citing Wellington's habit of framing his plans entirely on the opponent's expected moves, "distinguished by the extraordinary skill with which he concealed his movements and deceived both friend and foe."

**Context:** "All warfare is based on deception" is among the most-quoted lines of Sun Tzu's *The Art of War*, an ancient Chinese military treatise; the idea has been widely adapted into modern strategy, business, and game-theoretic thinking about signaling and misdirection.
