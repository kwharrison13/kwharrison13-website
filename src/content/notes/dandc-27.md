# D&C 27

Section 27 of the [Doctrine and Covenants](/notes/doctrine-and-covenants) appears in Kyle's The Whole Armor of God topical study as one of the scriptural homes for the "whole armor of God" analogy, cited alongside [Ephesians 6](/notes/ephesians-6) and [2 Nephi 1](/notes/2-nephi-1):23. It anchors his study (Topical Journal 1.2–1.4) of how each piece of armor maps to a part of the body and the spiritual defense it provides — truth, the breastplate of righteousness, feet shod with the gospel of peace, the helmet of the [Plan of Salvation](/notes/plan-of-salvation), the shield of faith, and the sword of the word of God.

**Context:** Doctrine and Covenants Section 27 (August 1830) is a revelation on the sacrament and the gathering of past dispensations; its closing verses (D&C 27:15–18) restate Paul's exhortation in Ephesians 6 to "take upon you my whole armor" — the loins girt with truth, the breastplate of righteousness, the shield of faith, the helmet of salvation, and the sword of the Spirit.
