# Goldilocks Rule

The Goldilocks Rule is a named framework from [Atomic Habits](/books/atomic-habits): "humans experience peak motivation when working on tasks that are right on the edge of their current abilities. Not too hard. Not too easy. Just right." In the book's habit-formation logic, it explains how to sustain motivation over the long arc of mastery — keep the difficulty calibrated to the just-manageable edge so a habit stays engaging rather than boring (too easy) or discouraging (too hard). It sits near the book's related point that "the mark of whether you are made for a task is not whether you love it but whether you can handle the difficulty."

**Context:** The Goldilocks Rule, popularized by James Clear in *Atomic Habits*, draws on the psychology of flow and optimal-challenge research — the finding that engagement peaks when a challenge is matched to skill, neither trivially easy nor overwhelmingly hard.
