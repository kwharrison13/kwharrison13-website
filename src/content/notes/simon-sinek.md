# Simon Sinek

Kyle returns to [Simon Sinek](/notes/simon-sinek)'s "Start With Why" framing as a lens for motivation across very different contexts. In his scripture study of Alma 43–52, he reads the Nephites' cause — preserving "their lands, houses, wives, children, rights, privileges, and liberty to worship God" — through Sinek's "Why vs. What" distinction: they were defending a *why*, not merely a *what*. In the Cultivating Cults - Research notes, Sinek's "Start With Why" talk appears among the source material for thinking about shared understanding and how a group coheres around a "why."

**Context:** Simon Sinek is a British-American author and speaker best known for the 2009 book and TED talk "Start With Why," which argues that effective leaders and organizations communicate purpose ("why") before method or product ("how"/"what") — the "Golden Circle" model.
