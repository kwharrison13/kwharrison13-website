# Roy O. Disney

_Stub — created on ingest of The Coward's Conviction - Research. Walt Disney's older brother, the financial and business mind whose pragmatic skepticism kept the company solvent through near-bankruptcies. In [The Coward's Conviction](/essays/the-cowards-conviction) Kyle uses the Walt/Roy relationship as the archetype of the optimist–skeptic balance: Roy's caution made the empire possible, but his lack of vision ("Marvin, Walt's dead… and so was [EPCOT](/notes/epcot)") also cut Walt's most ambitious project short. Alongside Gwynne Shotwell/Elon Musk and Tim Cook/Steve Jobs as a "dedicated operator who makes the visionary possible." Drawn from [Walt Disney — The Triumph of The American Imagination](/books/walt-disney-the-triumph-of-the-american-imagination)._
