# Galatians 2

A New Testament chapter (Paul's letter to the Galatians) that appears in Kyle's Christ Defines His Gospel study among the scriptures on faith, works, and grace. Kyle cites [Galatians 2](/notes/galatians-2):17–21 for the reading that the law equals "the demands of justice" — and that justice cannot save anyone, because all would fall short. It is Christ's grace, not the law, that saves us. The chapter sits alongside [Ephesians 2](/notes/ephesians-2), [James 2](/notes/james-2), and [3 Nephi 27](/notes/3-nephi-27) in his cross-reference set on how the gospel reconciles works with salvation by grace.

**Context:** Galatians 2 is where Paul recounts confronting Peter at Antioch and argues that a person is justified by faith in Christ rather than by the works of the law (Galatians 2:16–21).
