# Workday

Workday is the incumbent enterprise HRIS / human-capital-management suite that recurs across Kyle's comp-tech diligence as the "big-boy" tool startups eventually graduate into — and the question of whether it's a moat or a ceiling. In the Pave Diligence — 2021 notes it is described as where customers go once they hit 500+ employees, the system [Pave](/notes/pave) sits *beneath* ("tech growth pre-Workday"), even though customers like Figma's Marie Szuts said she "likes them, 85% favorable, **but would never use them for comp planning**" — the recurring wedge being that Workday owns the system of record but is weak at compensation planning specifically. Lisa Wallace of [Assemble](/notes/assemble) sharpened this in her call: "Workday is totally focused on IT, they're not focused on compensation," and she saw a "Workday killer at the top-end" if a product could be as exhaustive as Workday but modular.

Workday also functions as an integration target and acquirer in these notes: CaptivateIQ Diligence — 2021 tracks CaptivateIQ working to integrate with Workday so commission data can push directly rather than via spreadsheet, and notes Workday acquired Peakon for ~$700M (per the Workbounce pre-brief) and offers Adaptive Planning. In Kyle's essay research, Competitive Moats - Research flags Workday as a **counter-example** — a company that "hasn't successfully extended," putting its moat longevity in question — and it appears in the source set for [Cash—Kingmaker or Killer?](/essays/cashkingmaker-or-killer).

**Context:** Workday is a publicly traded (NASDAQ: WDAY) cloud-based enterprise software company founded in 2005 by Dave Duffield and Aneel Bhusri, best known for its HCM and financial-management suites used by large enterprises.
