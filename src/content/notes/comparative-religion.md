# Comparative Religion

Kyle tags "Comparative Religion" (alongside [Gospel Topics to Explore](/notes/gospel-topics-to-explore)) in his 3 Nephi 20–26 gospel-study, under the theme of [Unity of the Faith](/notes/unity-of-the-faith). The prompt arises from 3 Nephi 21:20 — whoever will not repent and come unto the Beloved Son will be cut off — and Kyle's open question: "Even in the millennium, will we have alternative religions?" He pairs this with a meditation on the limits of language: that we never communicate perfectly, always carrying different takeaways and perspectives, which leads him toward the idea of a perfectly-expressive [Adamic Language](/notes/adamic-language).

The tag marks comparative religion as an open area Kyle wants to explore further rather than a settled position — a question about how the gathering and unity of God's people relates to the persistence of other faiths.

**Context:** Comparative religion is the academic study of the world's religious traditions side by side — their beliefs, practices, and histories — examining similarities and differences to understand religion as a human phenomenon.
