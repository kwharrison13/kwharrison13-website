# 2 Corinthians 5

In Kyle's gospel study, 2 Corinthians 5 is read primarily for its **"new creature" image of conversion**. Mighty Repentance cites 2 Corinthians 5:17 — "become a new creature" — as the third beat in a chain on full repentance as conversion: crucifying the old man of sin ([Romans 6](/notes/romans-6):6), becoming a new creature (2 Corinthians 5:17), and putting on a new man ([Colossians 3](/notes/colossians-3):10), with the joy of that conversion "as exquisite as my pain" ([Alma 36](/notes/alma-36)).

The chapter also supplies a second, distinct line in the Plan of Salvation — Topical Study, which cites 2 Corinthians 5:7 — "walk by faith, not by sight" — to frame mortal life as a field of doubt and temptation we are invited to cross by faith rather than certainty.

**Context:** 2 Corinthians 5 is Paul's chapter contrasting the mortal "earthly house" with the eternal one, urging believers to "walk by faith, not by sight," and declaring that "if any man be in Christ, he is a new creature" — the verses underlying the ministry of reconciliation.

## Where this appears

- Mighty Repentance — 2 Corinthians 5:17 as the "new creature" step in the crucify-old-man / put-on-new-man chain of conversion
- Plan of Salvation — Topical Study — 2 Corinthians 5:7, "walk by faith, not by sight," framing mortality as a field crossed by faith
