# Chipper Cash

In Kyle's corpus Chipper Cash is described as the largest P2P payments company in Africa — a natively-mobile app — and surfaces almost entirely as a customer reference inside the Sprig fka UserLeap - Diligence. [Hasan Luongo](/notes/hasan-luongo), Chipper's VP of Global Marketing, was one of the four expert calls in that diligence. Through him Chipper became the strongest single data point for Sprig's core thesis on in-app micro-surveys: contextual surveys hit a ~60% mobile response rate at Chipper versus 3–5% on email/Zoom/Google-Docs tools, and a Uganda crypto-trading survey drove a +190% adoption lift within a month. It also illustrated Sprig's land-and-expand pattern, growing from one designer to a full team.

The call doubled as a real-world stack reference: Chipper is ~99% Android (Africa), which forced Sprig to bolster its Android SDK; Chipper used [UserTesting](/notes/usertesting) as its system of record for 1x1 interviews (estimated ~$45K/year), and its best sign-up path was a Sprig survey that offered a UserTesting booking slot at the end. Luongo's framing of the goal of any user-research tool — "can we get closer to our customers?" — anchors Kyle's [User Research](/notes/user-research) concept page.

**Context:** Chipper Cash is a fintech founded in 2018 (Ham Serunjogi and Maijid Moujaled) offering cross-border peer-to-peer money transfers across several African countries; it became one of the most prominent African fintech startups, backed by investors including FTX/SBF before that backer's collapse.
