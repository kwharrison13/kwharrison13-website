# Jack of All Trades, Master of None

A proverb about the tradeoff between breadth and depth of expertise. In [Charlie Munger — A Lesson on Elementary Worldly Wisdom](/notes/charlie-munger-a-lesson-on-elementary-worldly-wisdom), Munger inverts the usual lesson: he argues that, like animals flourishing in ecological niches, people who narrowly specialize can get "terribly good" at occupying a little niche and capture economics that generalists never reach.

## Where this appears

- [Charlie Munger — A Lesson on Elementary Worldly Wisdom](/notes/charlie-munger-a-lesson-on-elementary-worldly-wisdom) — Munger's niche/specialization argument, framed against the conventional warning of being a generalist.
