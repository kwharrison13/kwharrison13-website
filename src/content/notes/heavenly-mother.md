# Heavenly Mother

In Kyle's notes, Heavenly Mother is a load-bearing theme of the theology of womanhood and a piece of what he calls the "God-as-Parent model." In [The Priesthood Power of Women](/books/the-priesthood-power-of-women) she is invoked as the divine destiny of women: [John A. Widtsoe](/notes/john-widtsoe) is quoted that "we shall find a mother who possesses the attributes of Godhood," and Elder Glenn L. Pace testified that when a woman stands before her heavenly parents and looks "into Her eyes… any question you ever had about the role of women in the kingdom will evaporate," because she will see "your divine nature and destiny." The book repeatedly pairs Heavenly Mother and Heavenly Father — drawing closer to "becoming like our Heavenly Mother and Father" through ordinances, and asking how different things would be if members focused on "the order entered into by… Heavenly Mother and Heavenly Father."

In The First Principles of Faithful Pragmatism, Kyle places Heavenly Mother explicitly inside the theological scaffolding of the God-as-Parent model — "God, in my mind, is a unit. I have a Heavenly Father and a Heavenly Mother, just like I have an earthly father and an earthly mother" — alongside [Exaltation](/notes/exaltation), the Nature of God, and the [Atonement](/notes/atonement). The [Exaltation](/notes/exaltation) note carries the same grouping forward.

**Context:** In the theology of the Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-day Saints, "Heavenly Mother" (or "Mother in Heaven") is the belief in a divine female parent, the wife of God the Father. The doctrine is most familiarly expressed in Eliza R. Snow's hymn "O My Father" and was affirmed in the Church's 2015 gospel-topics essay "Mother in Heaven."
