# Mike Speiser Playbook

The "Mike Speiser Playbook" is a tag Kyle attached while reading [Product Led Growth — The End User Era](/books/product-led-growth-the-end-user-era), flagging the book's data on how much harder it has become for new SaaS startups to reach escape velocity. The anchoring passage notes that "benchmarking data shows that it's harder than ever for new SaaS companies to gain initial traction and reach the expansion stage" — more startups now compete against extremely well-capitalized SaaS powerhouses, and the median growth rate of startups with $1–2.5M in ARR fell from 100% to 64% in a single year, raising the question of how much room is left for emerging startups. Kyle reads this against the investing/company-building lens of Mike Speiser, pairing the PLG thesis with a venture playbook for getting a young SaaS company through the traction-to-expansion gap.

**Context:** Mike Speiser is a managing director at Sutter Hill Ventures known for an incubation-style, founder-as-investor approach — most famously co-founding and seeding Snowflake and Pure Storage from inside the firm. His "playbook" is widely associated with disciplined, capital-efficient early-stage company building in infrastructure and enterprise software.
