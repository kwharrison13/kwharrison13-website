# Salesforce Ventures

Salesforce Ventures is [Salesforce](/notes/salesforce)'s corporate venture arm, surfacing in Kyle's 2021 diligence-research mainly through its climate-investing practice. In the November 1st, 2021 — Claudine Emeott (Salesforce Ventures) sourcing call, [Claudine Emeott](/notes/claudine-emeott) (who leads impact/climate there) describes spending 50–75% of her time on climate investments and gives a landscape read on [Carbon Accounting](/notes/carbon-accounting): Salesforce Ventures is a paying customer of [Sylvera](/notes/sylvera) (and likes its data-quality-only approach, staying out of accounting/offsets), and she frames [Watershed Climate](/notes/watershed-climate) as "the most directly competitive solution" with Persefoni (fresh off a $101M round) as another competitor — while noting it all blends together against Salesforce's own Sustainability Cloud, which [Marc Benioff](/notes/marc-benioff) had made a much bigger priority. The call also notes that the Slack Fund became part of Salesforce Ventures.

Salesforce Ventures appears again as a contact in the Watershed Climate Diligence — 2021 dossier — Emeott was on Kyle's outreach/scheduling list during the Watershed Series B work — and the parent [Salesforce](/notes/salesforce) page tracks Salesforce Ventures as the strategic-investor expression of Salesforce's broader role as integration substrate and category-defining incumbent.

**Context:** Salesforce Ventures is the strategic investment arm of Salesforce (NYSE: CRM), launched in 2009, which invests in enterprise-software and adjacent startups, including a dedicated climate/impact investing focus.
