# Alma 17

A Book of Mormon chapter — the start of the sons of Mosiah's missions to the Lamanites, including Ammon's service to king Lamoni — that recurs across [Kyle Harrison](/notes/kyle-harrison)'s gospel study as the model of **the prepared instrument and the leader who serves**. It is the opening chapter of his Alma 17–22 — I Will Make an Instrument of Thee Come Follow Me study, where Kyle's takeaway is that "spiritual preparation precedes spiritual power": the missionaries "had searched the scriptures diligently," "had given themselves to much prayer, and fasting," and so "taught with power and authority of God" (17:2–3). The same verse 3 anchors his Fasting - September 5, 2021 study as evidence that fasting yields the spirit of prophecy and revelation. The chapter's narrative arc — Ammon refusing rule and choosing to be a servant ("Nay, but I will be thy servant," 17:25), then winning the king's heart by defending the flocks — runs through the verse-by-verse notes.

Kyle also leans on 17:18 in his Chosen Leaders study: the goal of leadership is to "administer unto them, after having blessed them according to their several stations" — and, above administering, to *minister*. The chapter is cross-referenced again in his Alma 23–29 study.

**Context:** Alma 17 introduces the missions of the sons of Mosiah among the Lamanites after fourteen years of preaching — emphasizing their diligent scripture study, prayer, and fasting — and narrates Ammon arriving in the land of Ishmael, becoming a servant to king Lamoni, and defending the king's flocks at the waters of Sebus to win the people's trust.

## Where this appears

- Alma 17–22 — I Will Make an Instrument of Thee — the lead chapter; 17:2–3 on prepared instruments; Ammon choosing servanthood (17:25) and defending the flocks.
- Chosen Leaders — 17:18 on administering and ministering to those in one's stewardship.
- Fasting - September 5, 2021 — 17:3 on fasting producing the spirit of prophecy and revelation.
- Alma 23–29 — They Never Did Fall Away — 17:2–3 cross-referenced.
