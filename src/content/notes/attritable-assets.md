# Attritable Assets

"Attritable assets" is one of the core threads Kyle mines out of [Chip War](/books/chip-war) as evidence for a verticalized defense-tech thesis centered on [Anduril](/notes/anduril). In his reading, the concept is tied to William Word's mandate to build weapons "cheap and familiar," priced "like an inexpensive family sedan" — affordable enough that losing them in combat (attrition) is acceptable rather than catastrophic. Kyle's own annotation captures the link directly: "#Anduril - attritable assets." It sits alongside [Sensor Fusion](/notes/sensor-fusion) and [Civil-Military Fusion](/notes/civil-military-fusion) in his cluster of facts supporting the case for low-cost, mass-producible defense hardware over a few exquisite, expensive platforms.

**Context:** "Attritable" is a defense-acquisition term for systems cheap enough to be expendable — designed to be lost in numbers without crippling cost — a concept central to drone-swarm and autonomous-systems doctrine and to companies like Anduril.
