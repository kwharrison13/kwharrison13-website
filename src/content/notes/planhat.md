# Planhat

Planhat is a Stockholm-based [Customer Success](/notes/customer-success) platform and the subject of Kyle's Planhat - Diligence (fourteen expert calls with CS leaders and CTOs across the US and EMEA). It aggregates data from a company's tech stack to produce [customer health scores](/notes/health-score), drives onboarding/renewal workflows via [playbooks](/notes/playbook), and increasingly positions across the broader post-sales surface (customer success + product experience + revenue management — closer to HubSpot-style breadth than a pure [Gainsight](/notes/gainsight) competitor). The diligence's recurring picture: founded ~2014 and built quietly product-first (2014–2018) before going to market, backed by one of the founders of Meltwater, and grown heavily by word-of-mouth ("the product sells itself"). It is repeatedly described as the leading European-origin CS tool — strong in the EMEA startup/scaleup segment, less known in the US at the time.

The praised-and-questioned axes are consistent: a delightful UI and responsive 24/7 support, with "it all starts and ends with data integration" health-score quality as the battleground. Enterprise buyers flag limited native integrations (~17 vs. Gainsight's ~41; no SAP, no Google Analytics) and relatively basic [playbook](/notes/playbook) automation ("basic triggers, no smart rules… we can already hack this together with Intercom & Salesforce") — with playbook/smart-rule automation surfacing as the clearest product gap versus [Catalyst](/notes/catalyst), [ToTango](/notes/totango), ChurnZero, and Vitally.

**Context:** Planhat is a customer-success software company headquartered in Stockholm, Sweden, founded in 2014, that unifies customer data into health scores and workflows for post-sales teams.
