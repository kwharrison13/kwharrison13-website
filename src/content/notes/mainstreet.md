# MainStreet

MainStreet shows up in Kyle's notes in two registers. As a **tax-credit / "found money" company**, it appears in the November 15th, 2021 — Anrok Partner Presentation, where the landscape discussion groups MainStreet with Column Tax as players focused on "found money" (recovering credits a company is owed) — distinct from Anrok's sales-tax focus, where there's "not as much opportunity." Separately, Kyle saved MainStreet against his [Small Towns](/notes/small-towns) / [Prosperable](/notes/prosperable) city-building thesis: a CNN piece describes it as bringing "Silicon-Valley-style tax-credit help to small towns," which is why the [Small Towns](/notes/small-towns) page calls it "the company he saved against that thesis." Both threads describe the same business — a startup that automates discovery and capture of tax credits (notably R&D credits) for companies.

**Context:** MainStreet was a U.S. fintech startup (founded 2019) that automated R&D and other tax-credit claims for startups and small businesses, taking a cut of the credits it recovered. After rapid early growth and a high-profile raise, it later pivoted and wound down much of its original tax-credit business.
