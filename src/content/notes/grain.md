# Grain

Grain shows up in Kyle's notes as a **call-recording / interview-snippet tool**, mostly as a comparison point in the [User Research](/notes/user-research) landscape. In the Sprig fka UserLeap - Diligence notes, an expert critiquing [Lookback](/notes/lookback) says it "probably isn't much better than Grain" — both being live, one-at-a-time recorded-interview tools that become a repository of videos with no organized insights, where the missed opportunity is taking snippets and building something digestible (a contrast drawn with [Great Question](/notes/great-question)). Grain is listed among the adjacent tools surfaced across those calls.

It also makes a cameo in the June 22nd, 2021 — Conor White-Sullivan (Roam Research) call, where Conor describes sharing "some Grain videos" with someone late at night — i.e., Grain used to clip and pass along recorded moments.

**Context:** Grain is a meeting/conversation-recording tool that captures video calls, transcribes them, and lets users clip and share highlight moments; it has increasingly added AI note-taking and CRM-sync features for sales and customer-facing teams.
