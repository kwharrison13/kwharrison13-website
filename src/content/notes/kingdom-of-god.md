# Kingdom of God

In Kyle's Parables study, the **Kingdom of God** is distinguished from the [Kingdom of Heaven](/notes/kingdom-of-heaven) under an LDS reading: while the Bible usually treats the two phrases as synonymous (Matthew favors "Kingdom of Heaven," Mark "Kingdom of God"), the study draws a difference. The **Kingdom of God** connotes the church established by divine authority on earth — with no claim to temporal rule — whereas the **Kingdom of Heaven** refers to the divinely ordained government in all matters, established only when Jesus reigns ("Thy kingdom come… in earth, as it is in Heaven").

The parables of [Matthew 13](/notes/matthew-13) each illuminate an aspect of these kingdoms — the mustard seed as the *external* growth of the church versus the leaven as the *internal* growth of individual members — teaching how to "better prepare the Kingdom of God for when Christ comes again."

**Context:** "Kingdom of God" is a central concept in Jesus's teaching across the Synoptic Gospels, especially the parables; theological traditions vary on whether it denotes a present spiritual reality, a future eschatological reign, or both ("already and not yet").

## Where this appears

- Parables — defined against the [Kingdom of Heaven](/notes/kingdom-of-heaven) in the LDS reading: the church on earth established by divine authority, without temporal rule.
