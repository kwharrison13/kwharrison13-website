# Abraham 3

A chapter of the Book of Abraham (Pearl of Great Price) that Kyle's gospel study treats as a foundational text on premortal existence and the purpose of mortality. In his Plan of Salvation — Topical Study, [Abraham 3](/notes/abraham-3):25 ("we will prove them herewith, to see if they will do all things whatsoever the Lord their God shall command them") frames mortality as a test — "you can't expect to do good things and think you'll have smooth sailing" — and [Abraham 3](/notes/abraham-3):26 grounds the doctrine of the two estates: "we kept our first estate by using our agency to follow Christ in our spiritual bodies."

The same proving verse, [Abraham 3](/notes/abraham-3):25, anchors [Brigham Young](/notes/brigham-young)'s Temporal Wealth and the Kingdom of God — Brigham Young discourse on mortality as a test of obedience. And [The King Follett Sermon](/notes/the-king-follett-sermon) cites [Abraham 3](/notes/abraham-3):23–28 (with [Moses 4](/notes/moses-4):1–4) for the premortal council in heaven and the contention over how souls would be saved — the rebellion of the devil and his casting down.

**Context:** Abraham 3 is a chapter of the Book of Abraham in the Latter-day Saint Pearl of Great Price, containing the vision of the cosmos and the premortal council in heaven, including the verses on "noble and great" spirits, the two estates, and the proving of mortal life.
