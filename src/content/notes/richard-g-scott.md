# Richard G. Scott

A recurring voice in Kyle's LDS quote collections, cited most often on **adversity, the refinement that comes through trials, and forgiveness**. In [Chad's Big Quote Book](/notes/chad-s-big-quote-book) he is quoted that "trials, disappointments, sadness, and heartache come to us from two basically different sources" — transgression of God's laws, or the Lord's purpose "that we may receive the refinement that comes from testing" — and that some trials "are evidence that the Lord feels you are prepared to grow more." A companion quote frames adversity as the forge of character: "the conquering of adversity produces strength of character, forges self-confidence, engenders self-respect." On forgiveness, he is cited that it "allows the love of God to purge your heart and mind of the poison of hate."

His most load-bearing appearance for Kyle is the line anchoring Diligence and The Reasons The Spirit Withdraws: "Profound spiritual truth cannot simply be poured from one mind and heart to another. It takes faith and diligent effort. Precious truth comes a small piece at a time through faith, with great exertion, and at times wrenching struggles. The Lord intends it to be that way so we can mature and progress." In Spiritual Quotes — Greatest Hits he is grouped at the center of gravity with other LDS leaders ([Neal A. Maxwell](/notes/neal-a-maxwell), [Henry B. Eyring](/notes/henry-b-eyring)), quoted there on staying on "the main track to happiness" and not being led "carefully" onto a sidetrack.

**Context:** Richard G. Scott (1928–2015) was an American nuclear engineer who served as an apostle in The Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-day Saints (Quorum of the Twelve, 1988–2015). He was widely known for sermons on prayer, adversity, and the patient, effortful acquisition of spiritual truth.
