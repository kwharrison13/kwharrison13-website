# Mimicry

The trap of copying a winning format or position without the lived experience that made it work. In Kyle's notes the concept runs through two paired arguments. [Manas Saloi](/notes/manas-saloi)'s essay "Sorry, you can't copy [TBPN](/notes/tbpn)" makes the format-specific version: "most formats aren't plug and play. They're built around people's lived experience, tone, chemistry, and voice. And when that's missing, the audience feels it instantly." Saloi's prescription is to "do something where you can bring a unique point of view and play to your strengths" rather than imitate.

Kyle pairs this with [Alex Karp](/notes/alex-karp)'s broader claim that much of Silicon Valley "innovation" is derivative imitation, and extends the logic from podcasts to conviction itself: a borrowed position lacks the chemistry that makes it land. This anti-mimicry beat is how Kyle closes [Conviction-Led Contrarianism](/essays/conviction-led-contrarianism) — the argument that genuine conviction can't be copied off someone else's sheet.
