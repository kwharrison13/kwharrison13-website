# Matthew 4

Matthew 4 appears in three of Kyle's gospel-study notes. In Diligence, **Matthew 4:4** — "not bread alone, but every word that proceedeth out of the mouth of God" — is read as a rebuke of contentment: a person who believes they already have all of God's guidance ("a Bible, we have a Bible, and we need no more Bible," [2 Nephi 29](/notes/2-nephi-29):6) hungers after nothing more, while a diligent disciple continues to seek continuing revelation. In Fasting - September 5, 2021, **Matthew 4:1–4** — Christ's forty-day fast and temptation in the wilderness — is studied as a pattern for fasting. In Missionary Work — Topical Study, **Matthew 4:6** ("he shall give his angels charge concerning thee... lest at any time thou dash thy foot against a stone") frames the struggles of missionary work as growth God permits because the Teacher thinks you are ready, with angels sent to bear you up.

**Context:** Matthew 4 records Christ's forty-day fast and threefold temptation by Satan, the beginning of His Galilean ministry, and the calling of His first disciples.

## Where this appears

- Diligence — Matthew 4:4 ("every word... out of the mouth of God") on rejecting contentment and seeking continuing revelation
- Fasting - September 5, 2021 — Matthew 4:1–4 (the wilderness fast) as a fasting pattern
- Missionary Work — Topical Study — Matthew 4:6 (angels bearing us up) on the struggles of missionary work
