# 100 Year Business Plan

The "100 Year Business Plan" is Kyle's shorthand for the evergreen, multi-generation hold mindset — building a company to be owned and operated for a century rather than flipped on a five-year cycle. The phrasing comes from [On the Nature of Long-Term Holds](/notes/on-the-nature-of-long-term-holds), where [Dave Whorton](/notes/dave-whorton) (former Kleiner Perkins / TPG investor, founder of the [Tugboat Institute](/notes/tugboat-institute)) calls such firms "evergreen" companies and "encourages leaders to set building strategies of 100 years or more, encompassing business transfer and consistent possession." Kyle tags this idea against the PE/VC/MBA habit of "thinking and modeling in five-year increments," and cites century-plus exemplars like the Zildjian Company (continuous since 1623, now in its 15th generation). It sits at the center of the long-hold cluster — [Compounding](/notes/compounding), Search Funds, Holding Companies, [Investing 101 2.0](/notes/investing-101-2-0) — and Kyle's notes link it to [Jack Ma](/notes/jack-ma), whose Alibaba is tagged alongside Whorton's 100-year philosophy as a long-horizon company-building example.

**Context:** The "100-year" framing of corporate longevity is associated with Dave Whorton's Tugboat Institute and its "evergreen" companies; Jack Ma separately stated an ambition for Alibaba to last 102 years (spanning three centuries), a widely-quoted long-termism slogan.
