# Mark 2

A chapter of the Gospel of Mark referenced in two of Kyle's gospel-study notes. In his notes on General Conference — October 2021, Mark 2:3–5 — the four men who uncovered a roof to lower a paralytic to Jesus, who, "when Jesus saw their faith," forgave and healed him — is cited under [Brent H. Nielson](/notes/brent-h-nielson)'s talk on the theme [But If Not...](/notes/but-if-not), next to Kyle's own reflection on "the power of faith as a skill, not just a binary." In Original 12 Apostles, Mark 2:14 is the call of Matthew (also Levi), the former publican, with Kyle noting the possible priesthood-lineage implication of the Levi name.

**Context:** Mark 2 in the New Testament recounts several early episodes of Jesus's ministry, including the healing of the paralytic lowered through a roof and the calling of Levi the tax collector (Matthew).

## Where this appears

- General Conference — October 2021 — Mark 2:3–5 (the paralytic lowered through the roof) cited under the "But If Not" theme on faith
- Original 12 Apostles — Mark 2:14 noted as the call of Matthew/Levi, the former publican
