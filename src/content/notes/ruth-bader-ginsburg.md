# Ruth Bader Ginsburg

Appears in Kyle's General Conference — October 2021 notes as an example of "great contenders" — held up alongside [Antonin Scalia](/notes/anton-scalia) as a pair who could disagree deeply yet still respect each other, surfaced in the conference thread that "the spirit of contention is not of Christ" (via Elder [Dale G. Renlund](/notes/dale-g-renlund)'s talk on disagreement). She also appears in Kyle's reading of [Discipline Is Destiny](/books/discipline-is-destiny) by [Ryan Holiday](/notes/ryan-holiday).

**Context:** Ruth Bader Ginsburg (1933–2020) was an Associate Justice of the U.S. Supreme Court from 1993 until her death, a pioneering advocate for gender equality, and famously a close personal friend of her ideological opposite, Justice Antonin Scalia — the friendship Kyle's notes invoke as a model of civil disagreement.

## Where this appears

- General Conference — October 2021 — cited (with Scalia) as an example of people who disagree well and still respect each other.
- [Discipline Is Destiny](/books/discipline-is-destiny) — referenced in Kyle's read of Ryan Holiday's Stoic discipline book.
