# Helaman 14

A chapter in the Book of Mormon — Samuel the Lamanite's prophecy of the signs of Christ's birth and death — studied in Kyle's Come Follow Me study of Helaman 13–16. Kyle reads it around three linked themes. [The Wicked Take the Truth to be Hard](/notes/the-wicked-take-the-truth-to-be-hard): Samuel notes that because his words were "hard against you, ye are angry with me and do seek to destroy me" ([Helaman 14](/notes/helaman-14):10). [Accountability](/notes/accountability): "repent ye… lest by knowing these things and not doing them ye shall suffer yourselves to come under condemnation" ([Helaman 14](/notes/helaman-14):19) — the recurring chain that we are held responsible for the knowledge we receive. And [Agency](/notes/agency): "whosoever perisheth, perisheth unto himself… ye are free; ye are permitted to act for yourselves; for behold, God hath given unto you a knowledge and he hath made you free" ([Helaman 14](/notes/helaman-14):30-31).

In Kyle's weekly synthesis the chapter carries the point that all a prophet can do is warn and share the mind of the Lord; once we know, we are personally accountable for what we choose, a theme he cross-links to [2 Nephi 2](/notes/2-nephi-2):25-27.

**Context:** Helaman 14 (Book of Mormon) records the Lamanite prophet Samuel, preaching from the walls of Zarahemla, foretelling the specific signs that would accompany Christ's birth (a night without darkness, a new star) and death (three days of darkness, earthquakes), and emphasizing human freedom and accountability.
