# Assemble

Compensation-management software company (assemble.inc) founded by Lisa Wallace. It enters Kyle's notes in two ways. In the November 2nd, 2021 — Lisa Wallace (Assemble) call, Wallace gave her read on the comp market while Kyle was diligencing [Pave fka Trove](/notes/pave): she argued the real prize is a "Workday killer at the top end" — a product as exhaustive as Workday but modular — and was critical of the field (Pave, Welcome, Figure) for being unclear on which market they're chasing and for products co-embedded with [ChartHop](/notes/charthop)/[Carta](/notes/carta) where "the product isn't good and the data isn't accurate." Her customer profile skewed to fast-growing 200-1,000-person public/C-corp companies that care about fair, equitable pay, with [ServiceTitan](/notes/servicetitan) cited as an early adopter and the high end of what the product could handle given its large surface area.

In the Pave Diligence — 2021 dossier, Assemble appears from the other side — listed as one of Pave's early-stage competitors (alongside Welcome) and noted as the comp tool [ServiceTitan](/notes/servicetitan) uses, with Pave already breaking away from the earlier-stage entrants.

**Context:** Assemble was a compensation-planning startup founded by Lisa Wallace and Lily Liu; the team was later acquired by Deel. (General background; Kyle's notes attest the 2021 competitive positioning, not the later outcome.)
