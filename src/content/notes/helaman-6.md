# Helaman 6

A chapter of the Book of Mormon, studied in Kyle's [Come Follow Me](/notes/come-follow-me) reading of [Helaman 1](/notes/helaman-1)–[Helaman 6](/notes/helaman-6) under the theme "The Rock of Our Redeemer." In Kyle's notes the chapter sits at the hinge of the book's pride cycle: Mormon abridges these chapters to show pride and wickedness leading "again and again" to destruction (Helaman 6:34-36), making it a key text for [The Pride Cycle](/notes/the-pride-cycle) and [Pride](/notes/pride).

Two threads Kyle pulled from the chapter. First, **the power of the word over the sword**: the Lamanites "did preach the word of God among the more wicked part of them," utterly destroying the band of Gadianton robbers — the word having "more powerful effect upon the minds of the people than the sword" (Helaman 6:37), tied to [Voice of the People](/notes/voice-of-the-people). Second, **the inversion of righteousness between Nephites and Lamanites** as the Gadianton Robbers and their secret combinations obtained "the sole management of the government" and turned on "the poor and the meek, and the humble followers of God" (Helaman 6:39). The chapter is cross-referenced again in the Helaman 13–16 study.

**Context:** Helaman 6 is the sixth chapter of the Book of Helaman in the Book of Mormon, recounting a period when the Lamanites grew more righteous than the Nephites and the secret society of the Gadianton robbers spread through Nephite government.
