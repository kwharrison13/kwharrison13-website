# Johannes Scheitel

Johannes Scheitel is a customer-success executive interviewed as an expert in Kyle's Planhat - Diligence research. At the time, he was VP of Customer Success & Services at Leanplum — SF-based, 6+ years in CS leadership up to 200-FTE companies, with prior roles as Director of CS at Gigster and Head of CS at Lystable, plus LinkedIn consulting. His view: a company outgrows a spreadsheet health model and pivots to a dedicated CS platform around **200–300 customers**, and what matters most is usability, ease of integration with the existing CRM stack, and ease of internal change.

On [Planhat](/notes/planhat) specifically, Scheitel was skeptical of its US prospects — "I never encountered it in the US... I'm getting hammered by [ChurnZero](/notes/churnzero) & [Gainsight](/notes/gainsight) & [ToTango](/notes/totango)" — and flagged a "trying to do everything" risk, since Planhat bundles CS, product experience, and revenue management ("when you try to do everything, it is tough"). He ranked the "omnipresent three" as [Gainsight](/notes/gainsight), [ChurnZero](/notes/churnzero), and [ToTango](/notes/totango), noting Planhat's customer stories skewed toward startups. His commentary is one data point in the diligence's broader finding that Planhat has low US brand awareness.
