# Dale G. Renlund

A member of the Quorum of the Twelve Apostles of [The Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-day Saints](/notes/the-church-of-jesus-christ-of-latter-day-saints) who recurs across Kyle's gospel study, most notably for his teaching on the nature of priesthood. In [The Priesthood Power of Women](/books/the-priesthood-power-of-women), Renlund (writing with Sister Ruth Lybbert Renlund) supplies the central "priesthood as earth/dirt" analogy: the word *priesthood* refers both to the total power and authority of God (the whole globe) and to the portion delegated to ordained holders on earth (the dirt one can pick up) — narrowing it to only the latter under-defines it. He also teaches there what it means to *preside* in the home, framing the priesthood holder as serving "with his wife as an equal partner." Elsewhere he is quoted that "the closer we are to Jesus Christ in the thoughts and intents of our hearts… the more we want to repent and become like Him."

He appears in Kyle's notes from General Conference (October 2021), and his talk "The Priesthood and the Savior's Atoning Power" (General Conference, October 2017) is cited in the Alma 13–16 Come Follow Me study.

**Context:** Dale G. Renlund has served as an apostle in The Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-day Saints since 2015.
