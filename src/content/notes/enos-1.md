# Enos 1

In Kyle's [gospel-study](/notes/gospel-study) notes, **Enos 1** is the model of **mighty prayer born of a hungering soul**, and the dedicated study Mighty Repentance reads the chapter as repentance unfolding in three expanding phases: a desire for **personal** remission (Enos 1:4–6), then, once forgiven, a desire for his **family's** remission (1:9, with revelation coming "in his mind," 1:10), and finally a desire for his **enemies'** remission — the reward being to look forward to seeing the face of God with pleasure (1:27). Kyle equates this full repentance with conversion.

The same chapter threads through Kyle's topical studies. Diligence cites Enos's hungering soul (1:4) as the starting humility from which true diligence — "diligently seeking" God — grows. Prayer — Topical Study uses 1:5 as the model of praying "with feeling and great desire." Patience — Topical Study cites 1:8–9, 12 for desires and prayers granted *according to* one's faith, after God has educated those desires in humility. Scripture Study — Topical Study draws on 1:15 for trust, and the Moroni 10 study pairs Enos 1:3–4 with [Alma 32](/notes/alma-32):27 on letting truth "sink into your soul" through pondering.

**Context:** Enos chapter 1 is a short single-chapter book in the Book of Mormon recounting Enos's "wrestle before God" — a day-and-night prayer in the forest that brought remission of his sins and then widening intercession for his people and his enemies.
