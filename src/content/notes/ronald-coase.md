# Ronald Coase

In [Charlie Munger — Academic Economics](/notes/charlie-munger-academic-economics), Ronald Coase appears as Munger's stand-in for the kind of canned, professor-supplied answer that economics students reflexively produce without understanding. Munger predicts that if he asked economists across the country to synthesize the field's big ideas, "they'd give me Ronald Coase. They'd talk about transaction costs" — clicking off "a little something that their professors gave them and spit it back" rather than grasping how the ideas actually fit together. The reference is a critique not of Coase's work but of how rote its invocation has become in economics teaching.

**Context:** Ronald Coase (1910–2013) was a British economist who won the 1991 Nobel Memorial Prize. His foundational papers — "The Nature of the Firm" and "The Problem of Social Cost" — introduced transaction-cost reasoning and what is now called the Coase theorem, ideas that became staples of the economics curriculum.
