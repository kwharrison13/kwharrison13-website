# Seeking

In [The Life of John Taylor](/books/the-life-of-john-taylor), "Seeking" names the truth-seeking disposition Kyle reads against its opposite — defensiveness — as the central tension running through the apologetic-vs-truth tradition in Mormon historiography. It is captured in the line Kyle pairs with the concept: *"Bring all the truth you have and let us see if we can add to it."* The frame treats genuine seeking (open, additive, unafraid of new truth) as the healthy pole, with [Apologetics](/notes/apologetics) (defending a fixed position) as its shadow. The same disposition shows up in Taylor's own biography as the "seeker arc" — his path from Methodist preacher into Mormonism via [Conversion](/notes/conversion) — and in his retort to the philosopher Krolokoski that the Mormons "have not been seeking the influence of the world, nor the power of government, but they have obtained both."
