# Truth and Tolerance

The pairing of truth and tolerance as inseparable "twin ideas." The fullest treatment in Kyle's notes is [Dallin H. Oaks](/notes/dallin-h-oaks)'s BYU devotional Truth and Tolerance — Dallin H. Oaks (September 11, 2011), which argues that belief in [Absolute Truth](/notes/absolute-truth) makes [Tolerance](/notes/tolerance) *harder*, not easier — whereas [Moral Relativism](/notes/moral-relativism) makes tolerance cheap ("almost anything goes"). For believers, tolerance is "a two-sided coin": respect on one side, truth always on the other, modeled on Christ with the woman taken in adultery — "Neither do I condemn thee" (tolerance) *and* "Go, and sin no more" (truth). Oaks distinguishes tolerating people and *beliefs* from being "required to respect and tolerate wrong *behavior*," and holds there is "no middle ground" on abandoning truth.

The same titular tension surfaces in a secular register in Kyle's venture work: during the September 17th, 2021 — Zach Edwards (Clearstone Associates) call, a discussion of secularism and free speech raised "Is it a violation of free speech when Twitter blocks people from spreading falsehoods?" — and in the same conversation [Zach Edwards](/notes/zach-edwards) recommended [Charles Taylor](/notes/charles-taylor)'s [A Secular Age](/notes/a-secular-age), which Kyle filed as the philosophical backdrop to how the West moved from axiomatic belief to "one option among many." The concept thus bridges Kyle's gospel study and his reading on secularism.
