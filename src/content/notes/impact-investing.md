# Impact Investing

Impact investing — deploying capital for measurable social or environmental return alongside financial return — runs through several of Kyle's notes, most pointedly in his 2014 conversation with John Hoffmire at Oxford. [John Hoffmire](/notes/john-hoffmire) (Saïd Business School faculty, ESOP investment banker, chair of [Progress Through Business](/notes/progress-through-business)) is sharply critical of the movement's leadership: "If you look at the leaders of the impact investment world, a lot of them are just really bad people" — figures with great reputations from books and programs who treat others poorly. His prediction: "If that's the leadership of that movement, it's bound to go nowhere and we'll just find a new name and move towards that." He extends the diagnosis to microfinance, which he says has largely "pushed out the people who made money from it."

The concept also tags entity pages elsewhere in the wiki — [Village Capital](/notes/village-capital) (an impact-focused investor) and [Inder Takhar](/notes/inder-takhar) — situating it alongside Kyle's adjacent interests in [Employee Ownership](/notes/employee-ownership) and the question of what business education and capital can actually do for social good rather than case-study homogeneity.

**Context:** Impact investing refers to investments made into companies, organizations, or funds with the intention of generating a measurable social or environmental impact alongside a financial return — a category that overlaps with ESG investing, microfinance, and social enterprise.
