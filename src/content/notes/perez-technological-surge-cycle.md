# Perez Technological Surge Cycle

[Carlota Perez](/notes/carlota-perez)'s framework for how successive technological revolutions unfold, captured by Kyle from an inbox image. In the corpus it supplies the long-wave frame for [Technological Innovation](/notes/technological-innovation): techno-economic paradigms move through an **installation phase** — frenzy, bubble, and crash — into a **deployment phase**, the "golden age." On the [Technological Innovation](/notes/technological-innovation) page this surge cycle sits alongside [Bilal Zuberi](/notes/bilal-zuberi)'s deep-tech "time to build" argument as the structural backdrop for whether real-world progress gets gated or unblocked.

**Context:** Carlota Perez, a Venezuelan-British scholar, laid out this model in *Technological Revolutions and Financial Capital* (2002). Each great surge (the Industrial Revolution, steam/railways, steel/electricity, oil/autos/mass production, and information/telecom) passes through installation — when financial capital drives speculative over-investment, ending in a crash — and deployment, when production capital spreads the technology's benefits across the economy. A turning point of institutional recomposition separates the two halves.
