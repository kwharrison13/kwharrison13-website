# Small and Simple Things

"Small and Simple Things" is Kyle's recurring gospel theme drawn from Alma 37:6-7: "by small and simple things are great things brought to pass; and small means in many instances doth confound the wise… and by very small means the Lord doth confound the wise and bringeth about the salvation of many souls." It appears in two consecutive 2020 Come Follow Me studies. In the Alma 36–38 study it is paired with its inverse warning — "the easiness of the way" — the danger of being "slothful because of the easiness of the way," like the Israelites who would not look upon the brass serpent "because of the simpleness of the labor" (Numbers 21 · 1 Nephi 17). Kyle's note ties it to entropy: "we're constantly descending deeper into chaos and the only way we can fight against [it] is by continuous 'small and simple things.'"

In the Alma 43–52 study the theme is applied to Moroni being made chief captain "at only twenty-five" — a concrete instance of great things from small means, which Kyle echoes against [Joseph Smith](/notes/joseph-smith)'s reply about "illiterate fishermen." Both studies also connect the simplest things to teach — repentance, faith on Christ, meekness — under Alma's counsel to his sons.

**Context:** "By small and simple things are great things brought to pass" (Alma 37:6) is one of the most frequently cited verses in Latter-day Saint teaching, used to emphasize the cumulative power of consistent small efforts.

## Where this appears
