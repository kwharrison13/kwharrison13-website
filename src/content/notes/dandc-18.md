# D&C 18

In Kyle's gospel-study notes, Doctrine and Covenants section 18 is cited most often for **the worth of souls and the joy of bringing even one to repentance**. The recurring verse is D&C 18:15, quoted in both General Conference, October 2021 and the Alma 13–16 study: "if it so be that you should labor all your days… and bring, save it be one soul unto me, how great shall be your joy with him in the kingdom of my Father!" The Alma 23–29 notes reference the surrounding verses (D&C 18:14–16) in the same "worth of souls" register.

A second use of the section appears in the Come, Follow Me opening week of 2019, which cites D&C 18:18 on asking the Father "in faith believing that you shall receive," and you shall have the [Holy Ghost](/notes/holy-ghost) which "manifesteth all things which are expedient." The Scripture Study — Topical Study notes likewise cross-reference D&C 18:35–36 alongside D&C 88 and 97. Across the sources the section functions as a touchstone for missionary purpose (the value of each soul) and for receiving revelation through faithful asking.

**Context:** Doctrine and Covenants 18 is an early revelation to [Joseph Smith](/notes/joseph-smith), Oliver Cowdery, and David Whitmer (1829) concerning the calling of the Twelve Apostles and the work of preaching repentance; its verses on "the worth of souls is great in the sight of God" (18:10) are among the most-quoted in Latter-day Saint missionary teaching.
