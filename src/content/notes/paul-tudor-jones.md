# Paul Tudor Jones

Hedge-fund manager surfacing in Kyle's notes via a single quote pulled on April 5th, 2022 while reading [Frederik Gieschen](/notes/frederik-gieschen)'s writing. The line is a rule about emotional distraction and investing discipline: "One of my no. 1 rules as an investor is as soon as I find out that a manager is going through divorce, I redeem immediately. Because the emotional distraction that comes from divorce is so overwhelming… You can automatically subtract 10 to 20% from any manager if he is going through divorce." Kyle collected it alongside market-and-temperament lines from [Ben Horowitz](/notes/ben-horowitz), [Jesse Livermore](/notes/jesse-livermore), and [Warren Buffett](/notes/warren-buffett) — the through-line being how psychology and durable patterns, more than novelty, govern market behavior.

**Context:** Paul Tudor Jones (b. 1954) is an American hedge-fund manager and founder of Tudor Investment Corporation, best known for correctly predicting and profiting from the 1987 stock-market crash.
