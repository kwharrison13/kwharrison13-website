# Margaret Heffernan

Writer cited in [MBA Degrees](/notes/mba-degrees) as a named critic of business-school culture. Via CBS MoneyWatch, the note credits her with the observation that her students worldwide are "too eager to please" — the pleasers "act like undergrads," proffering the answers they think the teacher wants. Her sharp line: "Such students will make excellent corporate foot soldiers but they won't make great leaders." This people-pleasing critique is a recurring thread in the [MBA Degrees](/notes/mba-degrees) page, echoed where Aine Donovan's HBR piece notes that "when authority figures ask people to act against their own values, most comply" — the same compliance Heffernan names.

**Context:** Margaret Heffernan is an Irish-American businesswoman and author (*Willful Blindness*, *A Bigger Prize*), and a former CEO; she writes and speaks on organizational culture, leadership, and the costs of conformity.
