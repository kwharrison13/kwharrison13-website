# Pyn

**Pyn** is an employee-communications platform that automates HR/people-team messaging on top of an HRIS integration. People teams use it to schedule and personalize comms — onboarding sequences for new hires, benefit reminders (e.g. nudging someone about a benefit three weeks after they start), performance-review-season messages, and other tenure-based touch points — delivered over email or Slack and pulling fields like name, role, start date, and benefits straight from the HRIS. Founded around 2020; [a16z](/notes/a16z) and [Accel](/notes/accel) led the Series A.

In Kyle's diligence (Pyn - Diligence, November 2021), the picture from customer/expert calls is consistently positive-but-modest: cheap and sticky (BlueVine went from a near-free first year to ~$20K, priced per-employee-per-month; Carta under $50K), strong onboarding/benefits use cases, an easy HRIS integration that removes manual list-building, and a receptive team. Recurring knocks: recipient-facing output can look like "junk email" (limited branding/imagery), analytics can't yet tie to outcomes, and most employees don't even know what Pyn is versus a tool like Slack. The strategic question raised repeatedly is **TAM and bundling** — reference users saw it as a "nice little business" whose natural path is to expand into adjacencies (intranet/home-page, sentiment) or get acquired into a broader HR suite like [Lattice](/notes/lattice) or CultureAmp (#[Bundling](/notes/unbundling)).

**Competitive / adjacent landscape surfaced in diligence:** Mailchimp (can't integrate HRIS or send to Slack), [Guru](/notes/guru) and Simpplr and [Notion](/notes/notion) (knowledge management / intranet), and emerging employee-sentiment tools (All Voices, Performetry, Donut, Textio). Integrations cited: BambooHR and Workday (HRIS), Greenhouse and Lever (ATS), with Modern Health surfaced as a benefit Pyn helps pitch.
