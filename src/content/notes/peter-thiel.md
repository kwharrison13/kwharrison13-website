# Peter Thiel

**One-line:** *Co-founder of Confinity (which became PayPal via the X.com merger); subsequent co-founder of Palantir and Founders Fund — in [The Founders](/books/the-founders) he is presented less as a payments founder and more as a contrarian organizational architect whose hiring and bet-on-people choices defined the "PayPal Mafia."*

## What we know

- At Stanford, ran a publication where he wrote essays with titles like "Open or Empty Mind?", "Institutionalized Liberalism," "Western Culture and Its Failures." [Ken Howery](/notes/ken-howery): "Peter might be the smartest person I've met in my four years at Stanford. I think I might work for him for the rest of my life."
- Ran the **"billionaires' breakfast club"** with [Luke Nosek](/notes/luke-nosek) and others — informal early-stage start-up philosophizing.
- Cut his investing teeth on Smart Calendar — a failure he treated as a paid education. Lessons: choose co-founders wisely, minimize competition.
- Co-founded **Confinity** (later PayPal) with [Max Levchin](/notes/max-levchin). Recruited [Reid Hoffman](/notes/reid-hoffman) as COO, [David Sacks](/notes/david-sacks) as VP strategy ("I need people here I can scream at"), [Roelof Botha](/notes/roelof-botha) as CFO (fresh out of business school). "Thiel's tendency to buck convention had nothing to do with math or political philosophy — it had to do with people."
- **No-firing rule** in early days: "Firing people is like war, and war is bad, so you should try not to do it." Consequence: underperformers got shuffled rather than dismissed — a known cost the team accepted.
- **"Show me a good loser, and I'll show you a loser."** Competitive intensity that drove the X.com price war.
- Sought a $100M buyout for Confinity from BeFree; BeFree's board balked. BeFree later sold for $128M; PayPal IPO'd at ~$1B.
- Predicted the dot-com crash early. Proposed PayPal transfer $100M of its raised capital to his hedge fund (Thiel Capital) to short the public markets. Board member [Tim Hurd](/notes/tim-hurd): "It was beautiful logic. One of the elements of PayPal was that they were untethered from how people did stuff in the real world."
- After Musk's ouster, ran the company through the IPO. The CEO-search process Moritz forced left him bitter and created a lasting rift with Sequoia's [Mike Moritz](/notes/mike-moritz).
- Operating instinct: **trust producing speed** — friends-as-cofounders bypass trust-building cost. "We had to recruit our friends because no one else would work for us" — [David Sacks](/notes/david-sacks).
- **"The dials" framing.** With Levchin and Sacks: fraud control vs. usability vs. revenue — three knobs to balance, not optimize independently.
