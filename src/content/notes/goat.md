# GOAT

GOAT is a sneaker resale marketplace that appears twice in Kyle's notes. In Watershed Climate Diligence — 2021 it shows up as a potential Watershed customer in the sneakers/apparel vertical (alongside [Everlane](/notes/everlane) and [Allbirds](/notes/allbirds)), but flagged "Aren't talking to them" — a prospect, not yet engaged. More substantively, in the Cash—Kingmaker or Killer - Research essay notes, GOAT is one of Kyle's marketplace-competition case studies: he lists "Why did GOAT beat [eBay](/notes/ebay)?" alongside "Why did [Uber ($UBER)](/notes/uber) win? Why did [DoorDash](/notes/doordash) beat Postmates?" as worked examples for his thesis that a startup's advantage comes less from its cash pile than from underlying market dynamics, maturity, and user economics. GOAT-vs-eBay is the case where a purpose-built, trust-and-authentication-driven marketplace displaced an incumbent generalist.

**Context:** GOAT is an online marketplace for sneakers, apparel, and accessories — founded in 2015 — known for its authentication process (verifying that high-value sneakers are not counterfeit), which differentiated it from open marketplaces like eBay in the resale category.
