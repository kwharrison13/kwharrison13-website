# Secondaries

Secondaries appear in Kyle's notes as an ownership-building tactic. The [Datadog](/notes/datadog) page records [Shardul Shah](/notes/shardul-shah)'s lesson from the deal: a good decision was to scoop up as much secondary as possible — to be creative about building ownership in a winner (September 22nd, 2021 — Shardul Shah on Datadog). The point is that when an investor identifies a likely winner, buying existing shares from earlier holders (secondary) is a way to increase position size and ownership beyond what primary rounds alone allow.

**Context:** In venture and private-equity terms, a "secondary" is the purchase of already-issued shares from an existing shareholder (a founder, employee, or earlier investor), as opposed to a "primary" investment where new shares are issued and capital flows into the company. Secondaries let investors build ownership in companies they're already conviction-high on, and give early stakeholders liquidity before an exit.
