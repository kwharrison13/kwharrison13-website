# Lincoln (Movie)

Kyle invokes the film *Lincoln* in his [John Quincy Adams](/books/john-quincy-adams) notes as the contrasting model on a [Leadership](/notes/leadership) dilemma: whether a leader should lead his constituents or follow them. Adams embodies the tension — the biography's recurring fear of being "palsied by the will of our constituents" — while Lincoln represents acting ahead of public opinion and trusting voters to ratify it. Kyle quotes the movie's Lincoln on the [Emancipation Proclamation](/notes/emancipation-proclamation): "I signed the Emancipation Proclamation a year and half before my second election. I felt I was within my power to do it; however I also felt that I might be wrong about that; I knew the people would tell me. I gave 'em a year and half to think about it. And they re-elected me." The Proclamation passage is set beside Adams' more cautious, constituent-bound posture as the two opposing answers to the same question.

**Context:** *Lincoln* (2012) is Steven Spielberg's historical drama, with Daniel Day-Lewis as Abraham Lincoln, centered on the political maneuvering to pass the Thirteenth Amendment abolishing slavery in early 1865.
