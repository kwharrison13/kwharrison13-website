# Titus 1

Scripture reference (the first chapter of Paul's epistle to Titus). In Kyle's Abrahamic Covenant gospel-study notes, **Titus 1:5–9** appears in a chain of New Testament passages assembled to establish the pattern of priesthood authority and church office — grouped alongside [1 Corinthians 12](/notes/1-corinthians-12):27–29, [Ephesians 4](/notes/ephesians-4):11–13, [1 Timothy 3](/notes/1-timothy-3):1–13, [Acts 14](/notes/acts-14):23, and [Hebrews 5](/notes/hebrews-5):4 as evidence that specific followers were ordained and called to lead.

**Context:** Titus 1 is a Pauline pastoral epistle chapter in which Paul instructs Titus to "ordain elders in every city" and lays out the qualifications for a bishop/elder (blameless, holding fast the faithful word, able to teach and refute). It is one of the standard proof-texts for the structure of church leadership and the laying on of hands.
