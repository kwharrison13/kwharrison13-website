# Religious Internalization

A central religious-intellectualism frame across Kyle's reading: faith owned from within rather than borrowed. In [The Crucible of Doubt](/books/the-crucible-of-doubt) (Terryl and Fiona Givens) it is **the** governing idea — faith held as one's own conviction instead of inherited or scaffolded, anchored to [Joseph Smith](/notes/joseph-smith)'s anti-creedal preaching and the warning against "when our leaders speak, the thinking has been done." [Stretching The Heavens](/books/stretching-the-heavens) gives it [Eugene England](/notes/eugene-england)'s sharp formulation: the instinct that constant, scaffolded divine contact "moves towards the devil's plan" rather than the saint's — righteousness must be learned and internalized, the [Righteous in the Dark](/notes/righteous-in-the-dark) frame, internalization over scaffolding.

The concept also has a sociological edge. In [High-Demand Religion](/notes/high-demand-religion) (drawn from [Law of the Harvest](/books/law-of-the-harvest)) and [The Righteous Mind](/books/the-righteous-mind), internalization is what makes strictness work: demands for sacrifice extended the life of religious — but not secular — communes, and "a religion that does not require the sacrifice of all things never has power sufficient to produce the faith necessary to lead unto life and salvation." [Feed My Sheep](/notes/feed-my-sheep) states it as program design — "convert the missionary first" — meaning doctrine has to be lived in the heart before it can be transmitted, connecting to [Gospel Study](/notes/gospel-study) and the broader religious-intellectualism cluster. It recurs in [The Faith of a Scientist](/books/the-faith-of-a-scientist) as the science-and-faith register of the same idea.

- [The Righteous Mind](/books/the-righteous-mind) — Sosis's finding that sacrifice extended religious but not secular communes
- [Feed My Sheep](/notes/feed-my-sheep) — "convert the missionary first": doctrine lived before transmitted
