# Day 1

"Day 1" is the [Jeff Bezos](/notes/jeff-bezos) framework Kyle's notes flag as a signature of his operating philosophy. The line, drawn from [Amazon Unbound](/books/amazon-unbound), is: *"Day two is stasis, followed by irrelevance, followed by excruciating, painful decline, followed by death. That is why it is always Day one."* It frames a company's vitality as a permanent founding-day posture — the antidote to the institutional drift that sets in once an organization stops behaving like a startup. It sits alongside the rest of the Bezos toolkit on the [Jeff Bezos](/notes/jeff-bezos) page (six-page narratives, two-pizza teams, "mechanisms not good intentions," customer obsession) as part of how he institutionalized a renewable [founder's mentality](/notes/founders-mentality) across Amazon's many divisions.

**Context:** "Day 1" is a long-running Bezos motif at Amazon — the headquarters building is named Day 1, and his shareholder letters repeatedly warned against the complacency of "Day 2."
