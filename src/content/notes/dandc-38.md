# D&C 38

Section 38 of the Doctrine and Covenants surfaces across Kyle's gospel study almost entirely through a single verse: **D&C 38:30**, "if ye are prepared, ye shall not fear." In General Conference — October 2021 and the Alma 17–22 study, the full verse is cited — "treasure up wisdom in your bosoms, lest the wickedness of men reveal these things unto you by their wickedness... but if ye are prepared ye shall not fear" — framing preparation as the antidote to fear of a wicked world.

The same verse anchors the [Plan of Salvation](/notes/plan-of-salvation) topical study ("If ye are prepared, ye shall not fear") and appears in the [Anti-Christ](/notes/anti-christ) topical study, where [Boyd K. Packer](/notes/boyd-k-packer) applies it as a warning against mockery: "Be careful that you do not fall into the category of mocking. The Lord promised, 'if ye are prepared, ye shall not fear.'" Across these sources the section functions as a preparedness-and-courage proof text rather than a study object in its own right.

**Context:** Doctrine and Covenants Section 38 is a revelation given to Joseph Smith in January 1831 near Fayette, New York, directing the early Latter-day Saints to gather to Ohio. Verse 30 — "if ye are prepared ye shall not fear" — is one of the most widely quoted lines in LDS discourse on temporal and spiritual preparedness.

## Where this appears

- General Conference — October 2021 — quotes D&C 38:30 in full on preparation against the wickedness of men
- Alma 17–22 — "I Will Make an Instrument of Thee" — cross-references 38:30 in a scripture chain on courage and preparation
- Plan of Salvation — cites "If ye are prepared, ye shall not fear"
- Anti-Christ — [Boyd K. Packer](/notes/boyd-k-packer) applies 38:30 against the temptation to mock
