# Growth Thesis

A "growth thesis" is the analytical product Kyle's notes treat as the craft at the center of [Growth Equity](/notes/growth-equity) investing — a structured, defensible argument for why a scaling company will become durably large. The canonical example in the wiki is [Adyen](/notes/adyen): a payments-platform pitch framed in an "investment-review style" is cited (in career feedback from [Mark Goldberg](/notes/mark-goldberg)) as the model of "what good growth thesis forming looks like" — i.e., strong growth-thesis *formation*, not just a deal pick.

The concept pairs with [Growth Equity](/notes/growth-equity): the latter is the capital strategy (backing scaling companies), while growth-thesis formation is the underwriting discipline — building the case for a company's end-state value — that the strategy depends on.
