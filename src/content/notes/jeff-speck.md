# Jeff Speck

In [What I Found in a Thousand Towns](/books/what-i-found-in-a-thousand-towns), Speck is cited as the urban planner who frames the idea of main-street "fabric" — the mix of architecture and storefronts along a main street. The book applies his concept to Beacon, observing that it "is starting to have a discernible fabric with many interesting textures… unified without uniformity and therefore incorporates many lifestyles." His vocabulary anchors the book's [City Building](/notes/city-building) thread about how bottom-up town revitalization works through main-street texture rather than uniformity.

**Context:** Jeff Speck is an American city planner and urban designer, author of *Walkable City* and *Walkable City Rules*, and an advocate of walkability and traditional main-street urbanism.

## Where this appears

- [What I Found in a Thousand Towns](/books/what-i-found-in-a-thousand-towns) — cited for the concept of main-street "fabric" (mixed architecture and storefronts), applied to Beacon as an example of being "unified without uniformity."
