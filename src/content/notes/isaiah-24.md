# Isaiah 24

A chapter of Isaiah that recurs across Kyle's gospel study as the proof-text for the doctrine of broken covenants. The anchor verse is [Isaiah 24](/notes/isaiah-24):5 — "The earth also is defiled under the inhabitants thereof; because they have transgressed the laws, changed the ordinance, broken the everlasting covenant." Kyle reads this not as a condemnation of outsiders but of the covenant people themselves: "you can't break a covenant you haven't made" (Born Again). The verse therefore applies most sharply to members of the Church, echoing Alma's warning that it is often the members who remain unconverted — "our sins are of greater damage because of our covenants" (Gene Hurley, quoted in Born Again).

The same verse threads through the Helaman 7–12 — Remember the Lord and Helaman 13–16 studies under the theme of Knowledge and [Accountability](/notes/accountability): we are held accountable for the knowledge we receive, and the "transgressed the laws… broken the everlasting covenant" charge falls on those who break covenants they actually made. In Christian Reformation, Zion, and Seekers it grounds the definition of apostasy — an apostate being "one who has been removed or has removed themselves from a true course," whose covenant garments are "spotted with iniquity" (cross-referenced with [Alma 5](/notes/alma-5):22). It is tagged in one source as part of #[Gene Hurley's Scripture Chain](/notes/gene-hurley-s-scripture-chain).

**Context:** Isaiah 24 is the opening chapter of the so-called "Isaiah Apocalypse" (Isaiah 24–27), a poetic oracle describing the LORD laying the earth waste as judgment for humanity breaking the "everlasting covenant," followed by the eventual reign of God in Zion.

## Where this appears

- Helaman 7–12 — Remember the Lord — Isaiah 24:5 as the covenant-accountability text: those held to account are those who broke covenants they made.
- August 31–September 6, 2020. Helaman 13–16: "Glad Tidings of Great Joy" — quotes 24:5 in full as part of a scripture chain on transgression and the everlasting covenant.
- Born Again — uses 24:5 to argue you can't break a covenant you haven't made; the warning targets covenant members.
- Christian Reformation, Zion, and Seekers — 24:5 paired with [Alma 5](/notes/alma-5):22 to define apostasy as covenant garments spotted with iniquity.
