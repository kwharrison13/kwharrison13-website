# 1 John 3

John's chapter on the children of God and purity. In Kyle's Humility — Topical Study it appears as further reading (3:3) for the principle that "when you hope to be like Christ you can be made pure" — set alongside [2 Corinthians 5](/notes/2-corinthians-5), [2 Nephi 31](/notes/2-nephi-31), and [Mosiah 2](/notes/mosiah-2) in a cluster about yielding the heart to God and laboring to be accepted of Him.

**Context:** 1 John 3 is the New Testament chapter where the Apostle John declares that believers are "the sons of God" and that everyone who has the hope of being like Christ "purifieth himself, even as he is pure" (3:3), going on to contrast the children of God with the children of the devil by their love and righteousness.
