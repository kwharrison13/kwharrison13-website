# What I Want My Son to Know before He Leaves on His Mission

A talk by [James E. Faust](/notes/james-e-faust) that Kyle drew on while studying [Alma 17](/notes/alma-17) in his Come Follow Me study of Alma 17–22. Kyle pulled it into the "Brethren in the Lord" section alongside Faust's (paraphrased) line, "I would give anything for one more day in the mission field with my brother companions."

The key takeaway Kyle recorded: "Missionary work is not easy. In fact, it is often quite difficult, but the Lord is the greatest paymaster in the world. Dedicated missionary service is one of life's most fulfilling experiences… because of the divine agency which flows so richly from the Lord to His humble and obedient servants to bless the lives of others." The talk's deeper through-line is that Faust's parents "didn't teach me how to tract, or how to teach, or how to study. They taught me to be '[Righteous in the Dark](/notes/righteous-in-the-dark)'" — and that this capacity to find what you need in any circumstance is the real preparation for a mission. Kyle connects that directly to [Brigham Young](/notes/brigham-young)'s teaching that man "must learn to be [Righteous in the Dark](/notes/righteous-in-the-dark)" because he is destined to act independently. The page sits inside Kyle's broader study of [Missionary Work](/notes/missionary-work) and what it means to be made "an instrument in the hands of God."
