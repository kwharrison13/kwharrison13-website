# Scalefactor

Scalefactor functions in Kyle's notes as his standing personal case study in hindsight error — the company he keeps returning to when thinking about [Post-Mortem](/notes/post-mortems) discipline. Reading the [Berkshire Hathaway Annual Letters](/books/berkshire-hathaway-annual-letters), where [Warren Buffett](/notes/warren-buffett) confesses to "unforced errors" and falling victim to "this time it will be different," Kyle's margin note asks "Why do investors so rarely think about hindsight error (e.g. [Post-Mortem](/notes/post-mortems) on [Scalefactor](/notes/scalefactor))?" — using Buffett's own admissions as a mirror for his own portfolio decisions. The book page flags Scalefactor as "a recurring example" of the post-mortem-on-past-decisions theme, and lists it among the live portfolio bets that populate the reading notes (alongside Shopify, Costco, Constellation Software, [Tiny Capital](/notes/tiny-capital), Marqeta, and others) — the notes doing "double duty as a private memo on live deals."

**Context:** ScaleFactor was an Austin-based accounting-automation startup that raised over $100M (led by Bessemer and Coatue) before shutting down in 2020; it became a widely-cited cautionary tale about software-vs-services overpromising in fintech.
