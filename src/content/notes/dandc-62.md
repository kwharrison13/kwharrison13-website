# D&C 62

A section of the Doctrine and Covenants that Kyle cites in his Publication of the Book of Mormon study on bearing testimony. The page quotes D&C 62:3 — "Ye are blessed, for the testimony which ye have borne is recorded in heaven for the angels to look upon" — to argue that a personal witness received by the Holy Ghost carries exquisite weight and is recorded in heaven, reinforcing that the bearer is personally responsible for building and declaring that testimony.

**Context:** Doctrine and Covenants Section 62 is an 1831 revelation given through Joseph Smith on the bank of the Missouri River to elders traveling to Zion. Verse 3 commends them for the testimonies they had borne, which are "recorded in heaven for the angels to look upon."

## Where this appears

- Publication of the Book of Mormon — quotes D&C 62:3 on testimony being "recorded in heaven," supporting the study's theme of personal witness and responsibility for one's testimony
