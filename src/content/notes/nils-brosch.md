# Nils Brosch

Nils Brosch is the founder of SAASCollective, a B2B SaaS consultancy (marketing, sales, customer success), and a former CCO/COO of Recruitee (a Dutch ATS) and co-founder/Head of Operations at Travelcircus. He appears as the first expert call in Kyle's Planhat - Diligence research, consulting scaleups on [Customer Success](/notes/customer-success) and building a CS tool of his own after seeing the demand.

His diligence input was among the most quotable. He framed CRM as a "blood bath" and CS as still a young space, with [Gainsight](/notes/gainsight) as the enterprise product (originally a Salesforce add-on, ~90% of its connections to Salesforce) that's wrong for the startups/scaleups he works with. His core thesis — "it all starts and ends with data integration" — held that the cleanliness and volume of native integrations is everything, citing a Dutch client that chose [Vitally](/notes/vitally) *solely* for native coverage across its stack. On the scaleup segment he was emphatic: "On the tech scaleup side, [Planhat](/notes/planhat) is by far the most successful," attributing it to a high integration barrier (2014→2018 spent just building), word-of-mouth buzz, a strong product, and Meltwater-founder backing — noting the product "sells itself" even before a real sales culture existed. He pegged willingness-to-pay at roughly €10K–€50K/yr (more than CRM tools) and ranked Planhat (tied with Vitally) for ~100-FTE companies, with [ToTango](/notes/totango) or [ChurnZero](/notes/churnzero) taking over at larger scale.
