# Mobility-as-a-Service

Mobility-as-a-service (MaaS) is a recurring framing in Kyle's 2018 smart-city / [The New City](/notes/the-new-city) networking calls. In the Innova call, the company describes itself "at their core" as a **first- and last-mile mobility-as-a-service** — electric low/medium on-highway vehicles instrumented with an IoT data platform, monetized across rides, on-vehicle advertising, in-vehicle media, and (the real prize) a data-analytics platform sold to cities, utilities, and employers.

[David Pickeral](/notes/david-pickeral), in his "New Mobility" call, casts MaaS as the necessary structural model for the sector: "You have to have the 'mobility-as-a-service' model where you better integrate the transportation options across public and private lines." For Pickeral the hard part is institutional — designing the entity or joint venture that lets transportation providers collaborate across public/private lines — and the value proposition is less about profiting on rides than about taking cost out of inefficient government transit.

**Context:** Mobility-as-a-Service is the concept of bundling multiple modes of transport (transit, ride-hail, bike/scooter share, etc.) into a single, on-demand, integrated digital service — shifting consumers from owning vehicles to consuming mobility as a subscription or pay-per-trip product.
