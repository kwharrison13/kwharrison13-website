# Ammaron

A Book of Mormon record-keeper who appears in Kyle's study of Alma 17–22 under the "Quick to Observe" theme. Ammaron, after hiding up the sacred records, sought out the boy [Mormon](/notes/mormon) — then about ten years old — and entrusted him with the future of the records because he discerned his character: "I perceive that thou art a sober child, and art quick to observe" (Mormon 1:2). Kyle draws the missionary-work lesson that life gets easier when you can be wise and observant, aware of people's behaviors and biases — cross-referencing the charge to be "wise as serpents, and harmless as doves" (Matthew 10:16), a quality he sees [Ammon](/notes/ammon) modeling when he reads the room before approaching [Lamoni](/notes/lamoni).

**Context:** In the Book of Mormon, Ammaron is a Nephite custodian of the records who, around AD 320, hid the plates and commissioned the young Mormon to retrieve and continue them when he came of age — making Ammaron the link in the chain of record-keepers that leads to Mormon's abridgment.
