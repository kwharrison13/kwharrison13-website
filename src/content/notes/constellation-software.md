# Constellation Software

A serial-acquirer software holding company that recurs across Kyle's notes as a model for compounding through acquisition. In Kyle's [Berkshire Hathaway Annual Letters](/books/berkshire-hathaway-annual-letters) reading notes it shows up as a live portfolio reference (alongside [Shopify](/notes/shopify), [Costco](/notes/costco), [Amazon](/notes/amazon), and [Tiny Capital](/notes/tiny-capital)), and Kyle flags a specific signal: Constellation Software's decision to stop publishing its annual letters and deal details — a Berkshire-adjacent move toward operating quietly at scale.

In Surviving The Death of Venture Capital - Research, Constellation is the archetype for **holding-company / roll-up exits**: drawing on Scott Belsky's "Great Sobriety," the research floats Constellation-style holding companies as a graceful exit for products trapped in poorly-structured companies — orphaned products rolled up rather than wound down — with the open question of whether that pattern is genuinely emerging in venture or still aspirational. It also surfaces in Networked Conviction — Roam + Investing - Research through Malcolm Gillespie, who described working at Constellation Software doing M&A across aerospace, oil & gas, renewables, and environmental verticals, focused on software companies within them.

**Context:** Constellation Software Inc. is a Toronto-listed (TSX: CSU) holding company founded by Mark Leonard in 1995. It acquires and operates hundreds of vertical-market software businesses and is widely studied as a disciplined serial-acquirer and capital-allocation compounder.
