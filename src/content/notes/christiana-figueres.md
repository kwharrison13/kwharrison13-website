# Christiana Figueres

Architect of the Paris Agreement and founder of [Global Optimism](/notes/global-optimism). She appears in Kyle's Watershed Climate Diligence — 2021 notes as the keynote speaker at a Watershed-adjacent climate event. The notes recount how, after taking over the UN climate negotiations following the failed 2009 Copenhagen talks, her first move was to commission a third-party analysis of everything that went wrong; her team pored over the resulting 300 pages obsessively for years, ultimately producing the unanimous 195-country Paris Agreement — agreed down to "every paragraph, word, and comma."

Her keynote message dovetails with the Watershed thesis that every company must measure, reduce, and remove its supply-chain emissions. She argued the world is "walking in the right direction, but not walking fast enough," that at minimum every company should have a plan to decarbonize its supply chain plus a public net-zero goal, and that *"every company inherits the carbon footprint of their suppliers."* She framed the planet as "a sick loved one" and called to move "from lamenting to action."

**Context:** Christiana Figueres is a Costa Rican diplomat who served as Executive Secretary of the UN Framework Convention on Climate Change (UNFCCC) from 2010 to 2016, presiding over the negotiations that produced the 2015 Paris Agreement. She co-founded Global Optimism and co-authored *The Future We Choose*, which popularized "stubborn optimism" as a climate stance.
