# Workbounce

Workbounce is a search-first knowledge hub for sales, described in the November 8th, 2021 — Workbounce (Pre-Brief from Hannah Seal) investment memo Kyle reviewed. Rather than building yet another content repository, the product integrates with the tools salespeople already use (Slack, Google Drive, Salesforce, Notion, etc.) and, via a Chrome plugin and Slack integration, lets sales reps ask specific questions and surface the most relevant answers and content — addressing the problem that sales knowledge and collateral are scattered across many systems, slowing sales cycles and ramp. The roadmap extended toward outcome-linked recommendations (connecting actions to closed-won deals) and collaborative, personalized customer micro-sites for each prospect.

The memo, written by [Hannah Seal](/notes/hannah-seal), framed the bet on a compelling two-person founding team — Adam Smith (CEO, ex-Commercial Growth Director at Peakon) and Rowan Bailey (COO, ex-Head of Revenue Ops at Peakon) — who met at Peakon (acquired by Workday for $700M) and personally felt the problem leading sales teams. At the time Workbounce had two paying customers (Apex and Perkbox) plus pilots. Named key risks included existing solutions being "good enough" (with [Guru](/notes/guru) and [Shelf.io](/notes/shelf-io) cited in that context), limited usage data this early, and the absence of technical leadership pending a CTO hire. Kyle and his firm were introduced via David Pevec, head of BD at [Fonoa](/notes/fonoa), and moved quickly to get ahead of competing top-tier funds.
