# 1 Peter 2

A chapter of Peter's first epistle that Kyle's gospel study mines for three different verses. In Abrahamic Covenant, [1 Peter 2](/notes/1-peter-2):9 ("a chosen generation, a royal priesthood") is set against Exodus 19:5–6 to argue against the claim — Kyle attributes the "all are made priests through baptism" reading to [Martin Luther](/notes/martin-luther) — that the verse universalizes the priesthood; he notes the early Israelites had not yet accepted Christ and that baptism is not even mentioned in the verse.

In Virtue, [1 Peter 2](/notes/1-peter-2):18 ("servants, be subject to your masters with all fear") anchors Kyle's distinction between subjection and submission: a demand requires subjection (being brought under control), while a command allows for submission (something freely offered) — leading to the question "who is our master?", the temptations of the devil or the enticings of Christ. In Radiating Your Example, [1 Peter 2](/notes/1-peter-2):12 is flagged alongside Peter's self-description as an Elder and witness of Christ's suffering, supporting [David O. McKay](/notes/david-o-mckay)'s teaching that every person radiates what he or she is.

**Context:** 1 Peter 2 is a chapter of the First Epistle of Peter in the New Testament, known for the "royal priesthood / chosen generation" passage and its counsel on submission and Christlike example under suffering.
