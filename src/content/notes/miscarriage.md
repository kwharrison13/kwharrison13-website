# miscarriage

In [Becoming](/books/becoming), miscarriage is one of the book's clearest illustrations of the theme that grief and resilience live together — that loss and "control what you can" are not sequential but simultaneous. Michelle Obama writes about it as the kind of thing "nobody tells you about until you're right in the thick of them": "A miscarriage is lonely, painful, and demoralizing almost on a cellular level. When you have one, you will likely mistake it for a personal failure, which it is not." She describes how naming her own miscarriage to friends unlocked their stories in return, and how that shared silence-breaking "steadied me during mine" — reframing the experience not as tragedy or failure but as "a normal biological hiccup." The passage is part of the book's larger insistence on speaking openly about hidden struggles.
