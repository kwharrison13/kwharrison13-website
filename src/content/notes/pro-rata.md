# Pro Rata

A venture investor's contractual right to invest enough in a later round to maintain their existing ownership percentage as a company raises more capital. In Kyle's notes pro rata is the central thesis of [SignalRank](/notes/signalrank): [Rob Hodgkinson](/notes/rob-hodgkinson) pitched the idea as "What if we productized pro rata?" The grounding insight is that seed managers "aren't setup to do more than 1.6 rounds of pro rata, even into their unicorns, because they don't have the capital to do it" — so the rights they hold in their best companies go unexercised. Running the math on just 2021 unicorns, Hodgkinson estimated "$14B of pro rata from pre-Series B unicorns" left on the table.

The structural alternative investors use today is to spin up an [SPV](/notes/spv) for each opportunity, which Hodgkinson described as "herding cats." SignalRank's plan was to put $100M (toward a larger permanent-capital balance sheet) to access the ~$200M of pro rata rights it had identified — funded in part by [Super Angels](/notes/super-angels) with deep pockets. Pro rata thus sits at the intersection of capital structure (you need a balance sheet, not just a fund) and access (you need the rights in the first place).

**Context:** "Pro rata" is Latin for "in proportion." In venture finance, a pro rata right lets an early investor buy more shares in subsequent financing rounds to avoid dilution; exercising it across a portfolio's winners is capital-intensive, which is why later-stage and dedicated vehicles often step in.
