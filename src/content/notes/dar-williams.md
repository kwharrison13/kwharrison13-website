# Dar Williams

Author of [What I Found in a Thousand Towns](/books/what-i-found-in-a-thousand-towns), described in Kyle's notes as a touring musician-author whose visits to small towns supply the book's "positive proximity" case studies. Her travels across America's small towns frame the book's argument about bottom-up town revitalization — main-street fabric, waterfront wayfinding, local cultural hubs — and the displacement risk of [Gentrification](/notes/gentrification) (see [City Building](/notes/city-building), [Small Towns](/notes/small-towns), [Community](/notes/community)).

**Context:** Dar Williams is an American singer-songwriter active since the 1990s; *What I Found in a Thousand Towns* (2017) draws on decades of touring to argue what makes small towns thrive.
