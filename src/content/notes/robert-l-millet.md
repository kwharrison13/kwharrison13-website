# Robert L. Millet

An attributed source in [Chad's Big Quote Book](/notes/chad-s-big-quote-book), the LDS quotation collection from Kyle's brother Chad. Two Millet passages are recorded there. The first is an epistemological line — "We don't see things as they really are; we see things as we really are" — on how the observer's character shapes perception. The second is on spiritual maturation: "It was never intended that we remain children forever… The gospel plan has been given that we might mature spiritually to the point where we become joint-heirs or co-inheritors, with Christ, and thus become sons and daughters of God."

**Context:** Robert L. Millet is an American Latter-day Saint scholar, emeritus professor of religious education at Brigham Young University and a former dean of religious education there, known for accessible writing on LDS doctrine and grace.
