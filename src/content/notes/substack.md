# Substack

The newsletter-publishing platform, which recurs in Kyle's notes as both a distribution tool he uses and a recurring object of debate about independent writing. In the Networked Conviction — Roam + Investing - Research notes, Kyle's own investing newsletter lives on Substack (`investing1012dot0.substack.com`), and his outreach scripts route prospects toward subscribing. That research also treats Substack as the leading trend in paid content while flagging its limits — "Substack is too much to read," and "there's no Amazon for content" — framing a discovery/curation gap on top of the publishing layer.

In [News in the Age of Abundance](/notes/news-in-the-age-of-abundance), Substack is invoked as the **counterpoint** to Andrew Potter's critique that subscription media fragments society (people pay for what they already agree with, pushing readerships toward the extremes) — a tension between Perell's pro-independent-writer stance and the priors-reinforcing risk of paywalled content. In the November 15th, 2021 — Rob Koyfman (Koyfin) call, Koyfin discusses providing its data tools to financial Substack writers (alongside a Getty Images license), treating the Substack creator base as a distribution channel. [Ben Thompson](/notes/ben-thompson) and Jake Singer come up as examples of people taking their mental models online via the platform.

**Context:** Substack is a US-based platform (founded 2017) that lets independent writers publish email newsletters and run paid subscriptions, taking a percentage of revenue. It became emblematic of the "passion economy" / creator-economy shift toward direct writer-to-reader monetization.
