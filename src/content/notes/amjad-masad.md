# Amjad Masad

Amjad Masad is the co-founder and CEO of [Replit](/notes/replit), the collaborative browser-based coding platform Kyle studied closely in early 2022. Across Kyle's deep dive (built from a [Saanya Ojha](/notes/saanya-ojha) debrief and close reads of [Packy McCormick](/notes/packy-mccormick)'s Not Boring memo plus Masad's own vision essays), Masad emerges as a founder with an unusually expansive thesis: that **code literacy is "the new literacy,"** analogous to literacy before the printing press, and that "when there is widespread code literacy… it will transform humanity again." His framing of the company's strategy — "to discover the future, you need a population that will grow up with the technology and partner with you in figuring out where software is heading" — drives Replit's deliberate choice to build for beginners and the underserved rather than experienced developers. Kyle's notes trace his arc: Lebanese-American hacker, soul-crushing stints at Yahoo and Facebook (drawn there by internet.org), a Kierkegaard-inflected reckoning that "entrepreneurship was a means of reconciling the finite… and the infinite," before building Replit with Haya Odeh and Faris Masad.

Beyond Replit, Kyle holds Masad up as a model angel investor. On the September 13th, 2022 — Harry Stebbings Podcast, asked for the **most underrated angel in the ecosystem**, Kyle named Masad — "an absolute oracle." His reasoning: "There is a shortage of ambition in the world, and every founder could benefit from having more ambitious perspective. Having Amjad on your cap table will give you that in spades." Masad also surfaces in Kyle's Historical Futurism - Research for the idea that "the core primitive of intelligence is prediction," which Kyle connects to reading science fiction as "training on how to dream."

**Context:** Amjad Masad is a Jordanian-American software engineer who co-founded Replit in 2016. He previously worked at Facebook (on the JavaScript bundler infrastructure behind React Native) and helped build the early interactive coding environment that Codecademy adopted.
