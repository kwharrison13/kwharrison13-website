# Christopher Hitchens

In [Why You're Christian](/notes/why-you-re-christian), Hitchens appears as the case's most striking witness *against* his own side. As "one of Christianity's fiercest critics," he is quoted conceding that "if you don't believe that Jesus of Nazareth was the Christ and Messiah, and that he rose again from the dead and by his sacrifice our sins are forgiven, you're really not in any meaningful sense a Christian." Kyle uses this concession to ground the argument that Christianity has a built-in "self-destruct mechanism" — per 1 Corinthians 15:17, its truth hinges on the literal historical reality of the Resurrection. The note pairs Hitchens, the atheist who marks the dividing line, with [C.S. Lewis](/notes/c-s-lewis)'s "liar, lunatic, or Lord" trilemma from [Mere Christianity](/notes/mere-christianity).

He is also flagged in [Animal Farm](/books/animal-farm), where the framing of reading Orwell as preventive medicine against servility evokes the Orwell-as-anti-totalitarian argument associated with Hitchens.

**Context:** Christopher Hitchens (1949–2011) was a British-American author and journalist, a leading figure of the "New Atheism" movement and author of *God Is Not Great*, known for his polemical style across politics and religion.
