# Steven Spielberg

**One-line:** *Generational filmmaker who, per [The Hits Business](/essays/the-hits-business), doesn't always fit the [auteur](/notes/auteur-theory) mold — and whose "popcorn-movie" handling of [Ready Player One](/books/ready-player-one) illustrates qualified-but-not-passionate.*

## What we know

- Kyle was frustrated by Spielberg's [treatment](https://www.reddit.com/r/TrueFilm/comments/8cto5b/read_player_one_was_spielberg_the_wrong_director/) of [Ready Player One](/books/ready-player-one) as a "popcorn movie" — a film he thinks could have been generational in more passionate hands.
- Also cited via the *Lincoln* story (in [The Existential Dread of Cognitive Dissonance](https://investing1012dot0.substack.com/p/the-existential-dread-of-cognitive)): even Spielberg, with Daniel Day-Lewis attached, nearly couldn't get the film funded as a feature — evidence the blockbuster model pushes unique stories to the periphery.
