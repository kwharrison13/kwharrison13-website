# Oath and Covenant of the Priesthood

In Kyle's gospel-study notes, the Oath and Covenant of the Priesthood is treated as the covenant frame that unlocks priesthood power. The Abrahamic Covenant study lays out the priesthood's three orders of power — Melchizedek, Abrahamic/Patriarchal, and Levitical (Aaronic), from [Joseph Smith](/notes/joseph-smith)'s August 27, 1843 sermon — and stresses that "power is unlocked by making and keeping covenants," connecting the authority of the [Priesthood](/notes/priesthood) to the same covenant lineage running from Abraham. In Godly Characteristics of the Master, the link is drawn to [Ezra Taft Benson](/notes/ezra-taft-benson)'s enumeration of Christlike attributes, framed as "delivered to priesthood holders as the standard for bearing the authority of Jesus Christ" — i.e. the Oath and Covenant sets the moral standard expected of those who bear the priesthood.

**Context:** In Latter-day Saint theology, the Oath and Covenant of the Priesthood is the formulation in Doctrine and Covenants 84:33–44 describing what God promises those who faithfully magnify their priesthood callings, and what is required of them in return.

## Where this appears

- Abrahamic Covenant — priesthood power "unlocked by making and keeping covenants," tied to the three orders of priesthood and the Abrahamic lineage.
- Godly Characteristics of the Master — the Christlike-attributes standard delivered to priesthood holders for bearing the authority of Jesus Christ.
