# 2 Timothy 4

A chapter of the New Testament cited in Kyle's study Christian Reformation, Zion, and Seekers. There, 2 Timothy 4:3 — people who will not "endure sound doctrine; but after their own lusts shall they heap to themselves teachers, having itching ears" — is invoked to describe the dynamic of apostasy: individual falling-away cascades into mass apostasy as people seek out teachers who flatter rather than correct, and "thus darkness descends upon the land."

**Context:** 2 Timothy 4 is the closing chapter of Paul's last epistle, charging Timothy to "preach the word" and warning of a coming time when people will reject sound doctrine in favor of teachers who tell them what they want to hear.
