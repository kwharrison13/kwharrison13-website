# Adam Keesling

Writer behind Every's Napkin Math newsletter. Kyle's Open-Source Knowledge - Research cites his piece "[Why Software is More Profitable Than Content](https://every.to/napkin-math/why-software-is-more-profitable-than-582107)" for the contrast that anchors the content-decays thread of the [Open Source Knowledge](/notes/open-source-knowledge) essay: software compounds because it's maintained and extended, while content decays as the ideas around it move on. In Kyle's Roam graph the source was tagged alongside [Open Source](/notes/open-source), [Wikipedia](/notes/wikipedia), and [Open Source Knowledge](/notes/open-source-knowledge).

Keesling also turns up in Kyle's Networked Conviction research as a December 9th, 2020 conversation about [Roam](/notes/roam-research) and public knowledge graphs. He had met [Conor White-Sullivan](/notes/conor-white-sullivan) at a [Tiago Forte](/notes/tiago-forte) meetup in SF in 2019, and the notes touch on templates as an on-ramp, the viability of courses/education as a low-touch business built on a tool ecosystem, and the idea of a content play bundled with a public Roam graph.

**Context:** Adam Keesling is a writer for Every, where his Napkin Math column applies back-of-the-envelope financial analysis to tech and media business models.
