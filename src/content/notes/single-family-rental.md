# Single-Family Rental

Single-family rental (SFR) enters Kyle's corpus as the asset class behind [Opendoor](/notes/opendoor), the real-estate iBuyer where [JD Rasmussen](/notes/jd-rasmussen) built the capital-markets function (per the March 5th, 2020 — JD Rasmussen (Opendoor) diligence call). The notes describe Opendoor as a single-family-rental / iBuyer business that funded home purchases with roughly $1B of credit-hedge-fund debt at a 10–15% spread across a portfolio of homes, later transitioning to lower-cost debt as it scaled. The interesting mechanic Kyle captured is the financing structure: warehouse-style debt against a pool of single-family homes, which ties SFR closely to [Debt](/notes/debt) and capital-markets execution rather than to property operations alone.

**Context:** Single-family rental is the asset class of detached houses owned as income-producing rentals (as opposed to multifamily apartments). It institutionalized after the 2008 housing crisis, when firms like Invitation Homes and American Homes 4 Rent bought distressed homes at scale and pioneered SFR-backed securitizations — the financing innovation that made the asset class investable for large pools of capital.
