# CRM

In [The Notetaking Cold War](/notes/the-notetaking-cold-war), the CRM is the worked example for the essay's central claim that all software is fundamentally the same thing — "ways to record, store, transform, and manipulate information." Spreadsheets, CRMs, Facebook, and Mario Kart are all cast as the same underlying machinery; the labels just describe what slice of the world the data points at. The CRM is singled out because its core abstraction maps so cleanly onto reality: "the 'customer' is a direct representation of something in the real world that you're trying to record information about. When you use a 'customer' record in a CRM it's because you're recording something about a real-life customer." That tight object-to-reality correspondence is what the piece uses to demystify software as nothing more than structured information about the world.

**Context:** A CRM (Customer Relationship Management system) is software for tracking a company's interactions with current and prospective customers — contacts, deals, communications, and pipeline. Salesforce, HubSpot, and Pipedrive are common examples.
