# Darwin Harrison

The driving figure behind [Valued Empyrean](/notes/valued-empyrean), an impact-oriented holding-company project Kyle discussed across two 2021 conversations. In the August 18th call — joined by [Conor White-Sullivan](/notes/conor-white-sullivan) — Harrison laid out the philosophy and structure of "the Value Way": a tiered set of entities (family-member subsidiaries vs. adjacent capital/sweat-equity/sponsorship relationships) organized around the principle of **impact first, income second**, leaning on [Douglas Engelbart](/notes/douglas-engelbart)'s hypothesis and [Tools For Thought](/notes/tools-for-thought) for its theory of continuous improvement. His own framing surfaces in that note: he describes having "spent 15 years working with folks with [a] Holistic Viewpoint but not a Holistic Mindset" who lacked the [Character Skills](/notes/character-skills) and [Technical Skills](/notes/technical-skills) to act, and the recurring lament that "we have a world full of people who are doers but not thinkers."

The September 17th call grounds Valued Empyrean in a concrete vertical: a [Behavioral Health](/notes/behavioral-health) opportunity, where Harrison walks Kyle through provider/clinic economics (group-therapy margins, the ~40% a therapist nets per session), the front/mid/back-office breakdown of a clinic, and the [Business-in-a-Box](/notes/business-in-a-box) question of which functions a practice can outsource and to whom.
