# James Baldwin

Baldwin appears in [Restoration — God's Call To The 21st Century](/books/restoration-god-s-call-to-the-21st-century) via a single quoted line used to make the book's argument about history and continuity: "the past is all that makes the present coherent." The citation frames the past not as something to escape but as the precondition for a coherent present — a thread that fits the book's restoration-of-the-past theme.

**Context:** James Baldwin (1924–1987) was an American novelist, essayist, and social critic whose work on race, identity, and American history includes *The Fire Next Time*, *Notes of a Native Son*, and *Go Tell It on the Mountain*.

## Where this appears

- [Restoration — God's Call To The 21st Century](/books/restoration-god-s-call-to-the-21st-century) — quoted for the line "the past is all that makes the present coherent," supporting the book's argument about the present depending on the past.
