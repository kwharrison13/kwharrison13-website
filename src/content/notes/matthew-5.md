# Matthew 5

Matthew 5 is the opening chapter of the Sermon on the Mount, and across Kyle's gospel study it functions as the textual home for several of his recurring themes. In the Sermon on the Mount topical study, the load-bearing passages are Christ's "But I say unto you…" antithesis (5:43–44), read not as a destruction of the law of Moses but its fulfillment — a progression from the natural man (love yourself) through the law of Moses (love your friends) to the law of Christ (love your enemies) — and the call to perfection (5:48), where "perfection" is glossed as *complete, finished, fully developed*, meaning the purpose of life is progression rather than instantaneous change.

The chapter recurs as a proof-text in Kyle's character studies. In Diligence, Matthew 5:6 ("blessed are they which do hunger and thirst after righteousness") anchors the definition of true diligence as the all-encompassing hunger of those who "diligently seek" God — and Kyle notes the contrast with 3 Nephi 12:6, where we are additionally told what fills us ("even the Holy Ghost"). In Virtue, the "salt of the earth" and "city... set on a hill" imagery (5:13–14) carries the argument that a disciplined conscience can only be lost as "vulgar additions" taint it — salt doesn't fade in savour, it can only be tainted. In Christ Defines His Gospel, 5:16 ("let your light so shine") is cited. The chapter's 3 Nephi parallel (3 Nephi 12) ties it to the Book of Mormon "Sermon at the Temple," which is why it surfaces in the 3 Nephi weekly Come Follow Me studies.

**Context:** Matthew 5 opens the Sermon on the Mount with the Beatitudes ("Blessed are the poor in spirit...") and proceeds through the salt-and-light metaphors and Christ's six "ye have heard... but I say unto you" antitheses, ending with the charge to "be ye therefore perfect."

## Where this appears

- Sermon on the Mount — the chapter's home; the 5:43–44 antithesis and 5:48 "perfection as progression" are the study's core.
- Diligence — 5:6 ("hunger and thirst after righteousness") as the definition of true diligence; contrasted with 3 Nephi 12:6.
- Virtue — 5:13–14 "salt of the earth" / "city on a hill" as the metaphor for a conscience that can only be tainted, never faded.
- Christ Defines His Gospel — 5:16 ("let your light so shine") cited.
- October 19–25, 2020. 3 Nephi 27–4 Nephi: "There Could Not Be a Happier People" — via the 3 Nephi Sermon-at-the-Temple parallel.
- September 14–20, 2020. 3 Nephi 8–11: "Arise and Come Forth unto Me" — same 3 Nephi study arc.
