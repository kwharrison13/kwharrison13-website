# Educated Citizenry

A concept Kyle tags via #[educated citizenry](/notes/educated-citizenry) in his notes on the [John Quincy Adams](/books/john-quincy-adams) biography. He attaches it to a highlight on the founders' theory of [Education](/notes/education): "The men who were to found the United States understood that while a nation of masters and servants needed only to elevate the one and abase the other, a nation of free men needed to cultivate the gifts of all its citizens." The idea is that self-government depends on broadly cultivated, educated citizens — distinguishing a republic of free men from a hierarchical society that need only train an elite.

**Context:** "An educated citizenry" is a recurring theme in early American political thought (Jefferson, the Adamses, and others) holding that a functioning republic requires widespread public education, since free citizens must be capable of governing themselves and judging their leaders.
