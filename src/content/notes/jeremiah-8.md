# Jeremiah 8

Old Testament chapter Kyle cites for two verses. In his notes on General Conference- October 2021, Jeremiah 8:22 ("Is there no balm in Gilead; is there no physician there? why then is not the health of the daughter of my people recovered?") appears in [Brent H. Nielson](/notes/brent-h-nielson)'s talk "[But If Not...](/notes/but-if-not)," woven into the account of praying first for a miracle, then for the absence of pain, then for a quick passing. In the Obedience — Topical Study, Jeremiah 8:20 ("the harvest is past, the summer is ended, and we are not saved") is the lament Kyle attaches to those who are "not valiant in the testimony of Jesus" ([D&C 76](/notes/dandc-76):79) — the regret of the unprepared looking back on their days of probation.

**Context:** Jeremiah 8 is a chapter of the book of Jeremiah in the Old Testament, part of the prophet's warnings to Judah; "balm in Gilead" (8:22) became a proverbial image of healing and consolation.
