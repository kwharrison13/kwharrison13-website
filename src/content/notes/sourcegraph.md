# Sourcegraph

Sourcegraph is a code-search / developer-tools company that appears in Kyle's notes primarily through [Joel Kwartler](/notes/joel-kwartler), who worked there. In the June 6th, 2022 — Joel Kwartler (Sourcegraph) call, Kwartler frames Sourcegraph's defining cultural trait as having "modeled their culture after [GitLab](/notes/gitlab) where they have a ton of visibility" — even copying GitLab's early handbook — which let him see "a bunch of things" as he plans to start his own company. He cites two early-stage lessons from Sourcegraph: hiring in a remote world (the company "eventually had a lot of former Helpscout and GitLab people," reflecting the "hard startups are easiest to hire for" pattern) and the handbook discipline of "delineating the APIs between departments" — which he contrasts with [Figma](/notes/figma), where the absence of that structure "led to bumps in how different teams interact." Sourcegraph also surfaces in Pave Diligence — 2021 as a named Pave customer (alongside [ServiceTitan](/notes/servicetitan) and Verkada).

**Context:** Sourcegraph is a developer-tools company building universal code search and code-intelligence tooling for large engineering organizations; it is well known as a remote-first company that, like GitLab, publishes much of its internal handbook openly.
