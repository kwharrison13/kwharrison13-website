# Todd Combs

Todd Combs surfaces in Kyle's notes through a single recurring quote on the compounding nature of knowledge, attached to the value-investing reading material Kyle was working through. The line — "That's how knowledge works. It builds up, like compound interest. All of you can do it, but I guarantee not many of you will do it." — appears both in Kyle's April 5th, 2022 — Reading Frederik Gieschen's Writing daily log and in Books 2.0 - Research, in each case nested under a [Reading](/notes/reading) cluster alongside [Warren Buffett](/notes/warren-buffett) material on the value-investor habit of reading constantly ("I just sit in my office and read all day"). Kyle uses Combs as a witness for the same thesis his Books 2.0 research circles: that reading compounds, but only for the few who actually do the work.

**Context:** Todd Combs is an American investment manager at [Berkshire Hathaway](/notes/berkshire-hathaway), hired by Warren Buffett in 2010 as one of two investment deputies; he also serves as CEO of GEICO. The quoted line echoes Buffett and Charlie Munger's frequent framing of knowledge as a compounding asset.

## Where this appears

- April 5th, 2022 — Reading Frederik Gieschen's Writing — the "knowledge compounds like interest" quote in Kyle's reading log.
- Books 2.0 - Research — the same quote under the [Reading](/notes/reading) section, beside Buffett-on-reading material.
