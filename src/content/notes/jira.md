# Jira

[Atlassian](/notes/atlassian)'s issue-tracking and project-management tool. Across Kyle's diligence notes it plays two recurring roles: the **product "center of gravity"** and the **incumbent everyone hates but can't leave** — making it a standing "who disrupts Atlassian?" question. In the Sprig fka UserLeap - Diligence notes, [Joseph Ambeault](/notes/joseph-ambeault) called Jira the center of gravity in product, with engineering, design, and product *"all completely dependent"* on it — yet *"everyone hates it but feels obligated; so entrenched (advanced features/customizations) that no one moves away."* His sharper read: *"People hate it when they use it for things beyond what it's for."* The disruption thesis that follows is the load-bearing idea — Jira works for devs/designers/PMs but isn't built to collaborate with marketing, so *"some company will do to Jira what [Figma](/notes/figma) did to [Adobe](/notes/adobe)"* by putting collaboration first and connecting it to where people already work (Linear floated as a candidate).

In the security dossiers, Jira shows up as the **default output destination** for the new generation of tools — the place alerts and tickets land. Across the Noname Security - Diligence and Salt Security Diligence — 2021 calls, vendors generate "Jira tickets or whatever system you want to use" (Slack, PagerDuty, SIEM) so IT teams can triage and tackle threats inside the workflow they already run; one expert noted this output is "pretty standard for all these vendors... not unique to Noname." In the CaptivateIQ Diligence — 2021 notes Jira is cited as historically hard to use for capturing the voice of the customer/prospect to justify product features.

**Context:** Jira is Atlassian's flagship issue-tracking and agile project-management software, widely used by software engineering and product teams; Atlassian (NASDAQ: TEAM) also makes Confluence, Trello, and Bitbucket. (General background.)
