# Determinism

Determinism shows up in two distinct registers across Kyle's corpus. The **metaphysical** sense runs through [Story of Your Life](/books/story-of-your-life): time-symmetric physical laws and the Laplace's-demon argument that the future is "already there," with our sense of openness a limit of perception. There it sits alongside [Block Universe](/notes/block-universe) and [Eternalism](/notes/eternalism), and the novella's resolution is that knowing the future and freely living it need not be opposed — a life can be a "completed shape" rather than a chain of contingencies, so freedom stays "perfectly real in the context of sequential consciousness."

The **technological** sense appears in The Value Chain of Capital - Research, drawn from [L.M. Sacasas](/notes/l-m-sacasas)'s "rhetoric of technological determinism" — narratives of inevitability that "frame technological development as a deterministic process to which human beings have no choice but to adapt." Sacasas's argument (with historian [Thomas Misa](/notes/thomas-misa)) is that this appearance of inevitability is a trick: "we see only the results and assume, understandably but in error, that there was no other path to the present," because the victors write the history and the "paths not taken" are suppressed. Lauren Collee's line — that techno-determinist futures "are used to habituate us to the present" — anchors the critique. This thread supplies the closing move of the Value Chain essay: progress is not a guaranteed output, so what we measure (the drivers of creation, not just capital in/out) is what we get.

**Context:** Determinism is the philosophical thesis that every event is fixed by prior causes and conditions, leaving no genuinely open alternatives. "Technological determinism" is a specific application of the idea to history — the (contested) claim that technology develops along an inevitable path and drives social change, rather than being shaped by economics, law, and politics.

## Where this appears

- [Story of Your Life](/books/story-of-your-life) — the metaphysical strand: time-symmetric laws, the block universe, and free will reconciled with a known future.
- The Value Chain of Capital - Research — the technological strand: Sacasas's "rhetoric of technological determinism" and the anti-inevitability argument.
- [L.M. Sacasas](/notes/l-m-sacasas) — source of the "rhetoric of technological determinism" framing.
- [Thomas Misa](/notes/thomas-misa) — the "paths not taken / victors write the history" line.
