# Anxiously Engaged

A scriptural principle of self-directed initiative, drawn from D&C 58:26-27 — "men should be anxiously engaged in a good cause, and do many things of their own free will, and bring to pass much righteousness" — and contrasted with being "compelled in all things." In Kyle's 1 Nephi 16–22 — I Will Prepare the Way before You study it is the interpretive key to Nephi's broken bow: the bow losing its spring is a natural consequence, but Nephi's response — building a new bow on his own initiative rather than waiting to be told — is the deliberate choice that being "anxiously engaged" describes. The Lord then blesses the effort with a successful hunt. The principle is set against [Laman and Lemuel's](/notes/lessons-from-laman-and-lemuel) murmuring, the opposite posture of passive complaint, and pairs with [Jeffrey R. Holland](/notes/jeffrey-r-holland)'s line that "no misfortune is so bad that whining about it won't make it worse." The same study connects it to [Hope](/notes/hope) and to the [Liahona](/notes/liahona), which works "according to the faith and diligence and heed which we did give unto it" — agency as the activating condition.

**Context:** "Anxiously engaged" is a widely cited phrase in Latter-day Saint teaching, taken from Doctrine and Covenants 58:27, used to encourage proactive, self-motivated good works rather than waiting for explicit command.
