# Matthew 17

Across Kyle's gospel study, Matthew 17 appears under three of its episodes. The most-cited is the **faith-as-a-grain-of-mustard-seed** teaching (17:20) — "if ye have faith as a grain of mustard seed... nothing shall be impossible unto you" — which he quotes in Christ Is Risen; Faith in Him Will Move Mountains — Russell M. Nelson (the title itself echoing the "move mountains" promise). The same passage, read in its fuller context (17:18–21, the cured child and "this kind goeth not out but by prayer and fasting"), anchors his Fasting - September 5, 2021 study on fasting with purpose.

The third use is the **temple-tax coin found in the fish** (17:24–27): in Original 12 Apostles Kyle offers it as a closing conjecture that Peter may have been the only *adult* apostle, since the coin paid the tax for only two — Christ and Peter.

**Context:** Matthew 17 records the Transfiguration of Christ (with Moses and Elias), the healing of the boy with an unclean spirit, the second prophecy of Christ's death and resurrection, and the temple-tax coin drawn from the fish's mouth.
