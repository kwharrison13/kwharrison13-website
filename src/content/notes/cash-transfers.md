# Cash Transfers

Cash transfers are the empirically-grounded anti-poverty intervention at the center of [Utopia For Realists](/books/utopia-for-realists) and the methodology Kyle credits within [Effective Altruism](/notes/effective-altruism). [Rutger Bregman](/notes/rutger-bregman) leans on [Esther Duflo](/notes/esther-duflo)'s Hyderabad RCT, which showed that microcredit's heralded anti-poverty effects don't hold, while unconditional cash works better — "cash handouts may be the most extensively studied anti-poverty method around... cash transfers are an extremely successful and efficient tool." The supporting findings the book cites: recipients put the money to good use, poverty declines, there are long-term gains in income and health, and the programs cost less than the alternatives; contrary to the "lazy poor people" stereotype, the Lancet found people who receive no-strings cash tend to work harder. The thread connects to [Universal Basic Income](/notes/universal-basic-income) (the same logic applied universally) and sits beside [Open Borders](/notes/open-borders) as the other high-leverage anti-poverty move.

In Kyle's notes this is the wedge that lets him hold [Effective Altruism](/notes/effective-altruism) "at arm's length as a justifying ideology while crediting the rigorous-giving / RCT methodology it popularized" — the evidence base he finds genuinely persuasive.

**Context:** Cash transfers are direct payments of money to poor households, either unconditional or conditional on behaviors like school attendance. Organizations like GiveDirectly and researchers including 2019 Nobel laureates Esther Duflo and Abhijit Banerjee have made them a benchmark anti-poverty intervention through randomized controlled trials.

## Where this appears

- [Utopia For Realists](/books/utopia-for-realists) — Bregman's core empirical claim, built on Duflo's Hyderabad RCT, that unconditional cash beats microcredit and most aid.
- [Effective Altruism](/notes/effective-altruism) — the RCT / rigorous-giving methodology Kyle credits, with cash transfers as the elevated high-leverage intervention.
