# D&C 103

Section 103 of the [Doctrine and Covenants](/notes/doctrine-and-covenants) surfaces in Kyle's Early Mormonism study as evidence that revelation directed the Saints "to do EVERYTHING by the law." Reading [Richard Bushman](/notes/richard-bushman), Kyle situates the section against the Missouri conflict: [Joseph Smith](/notes/joseph-smith)'s plan to build [Zion](/notes/zion) into "a new world order" guaranteed friction, the Saints grew fast, and what enraged the Jacksonians was the Saints' lawful obedience and their teaching "without respect of persons." D&C 103 is the revelation showing that even amid persecution they were commanded to proceed within the law. Kyle groups it with [D&C 52](/notes/dandc-52) and [D&C 132](/notes/dandc-132) in that study's scripture set. It also appears in Pattern of Prophetic Callings (D&C 103:16) as part of the recurring template of how the Lord raises up and commissions leaders.

**Context:** Doctrine and Covenants Section 103 (February 1834) is a revelation concerning the redemption of Zion after the Saints were driven from Jackson County, Missouri — calling for the organization of Zion's Camp and promising that "I will raise up unto my people a man, who shall lead them like as Moses led the children of Israel" (D&C 103:16).

## Where this appears

- Early Mormonism — revelation that the Saints should do "everything by the law," set against the Missouri/Zion conflict (grouped with [D&C 52](/notes/dandc-52), [D&C 132](/notes/dandc-132))
- Pattern of Prophetic Callings — D&C 103:16 cited within the pattern of how the Lord raises up and leads His people
