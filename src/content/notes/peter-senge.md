# Peter Senge

Organizational-learning theorist whose signature claim recurs across Kyle's notes as the case for [Learning Agility](/notes/learning-agility): **"the only sustainable competitive advantage is an organization's ability to learn faster than the competition."** [The Expertise Economy](/books/the-expertise-economy) (by [Kelly Palmer](/notes/kelly-palmer) and [David Blake](/notes/david-blake)) cites him for exactly this line, pairing it with [Hoffer's](/notes/eric-hoffer) "in times of change learners inherit the earth" as the book's argument that the durable edge in a world where ~65% of today's under-fives will hold jobs that don't yet exist is learning velocity.

Senge also surfaces as a credentialing voice for Dee Hock, the founder of [Visa](/notes/visakan-veerasamy). In his book [Fifth Discipline](/notes/fifth-discipline), Senge called Hock "one of the most original thinkers on the subject of organization that I've come across" — a blurb Kyle records both on the [Leadership](/notes/leadership) page and in the Fast Company profile queued in articles-to-read, where Hock's "chaordic" (chaos + order) theory of decentralized, self-organizing institutions is the draw.

**Context:** Peter Senge (b. 1947) is an MIT Sloan systems scientist and author of *The Fifth Discipline* (1990), the book that popularized the idea of the "learning organization" and brought systems thinking into mainstream management practice.
