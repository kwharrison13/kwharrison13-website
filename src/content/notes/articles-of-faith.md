# Articles of Faith

The thirteen statements of LDS belief authored by [Joseph Smith](/notes/joseph-smith) (the Wentworth Letter). In [Joseph Smith — Rough Stone Rolling](/books/joseph-smith-rough-stone-rolling), Bushman situates their adoption against early Saints' aversion to creeds — Elder Almon Babbitt was charged with saying "we have no articles of faith except the Bible," and the First Presidency acknowledged "there may be an aversion in the minds of some… against receiving any thing purporting to be articles of religious faith," answering that "it does not make a principle untrue to print it." Kyle's note from the biography flags the eleventh article on liberty of conscience — "We claim the privilege of worshiping Almighty God according to the dictates of our own conscience, and allow all men the same privilege" — tied to Joseph inviting other faiths to preach in Nauvoo.

The articles recur as a doctrinal index across Kyle's gospel study. Christian Reformation, Zion, and Seekers reads Articles 5, 6, and 9 through the lens of the Reformers and Seekers who recognized aspects of the apostasy — man must be called of God, the same organization as the primitive church, and belief in continuing revelation. Teachings of Presidents of the Church: Joseph Smith cites Article 10 on the literal gathering of Israel and Zion (the New Jerusalem) built on the American continent. The dedicated Articles of Faith — Topical Study works through them as a unit.

**Context:** The Articles of Faith are thirteen concise statements of Latter-day Saint doctrine that Joseph Smith composed in 1842 in a letter to newspaper editor John Wentworth; they were later canonized in the Pearl of Great Price.
