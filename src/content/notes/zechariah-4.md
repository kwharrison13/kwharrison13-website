# Zechariah 4

In Kyle's Chosen Leaders study, Zechariah 4 anchors the argument that God works through the weak rather than the worldly-strong. The notes lean on Zechariah 4:6 — "not by might, nor by power, but by the spirit, saith the Lord of hosts" — to explain why God calls "the weak things of the world, those who are unlearned and despised" ([D&C 35](/notes/dandc-35):13). The verse reframes leadership: "the weak of the world are the strong in the faith," and divine work advances by the Spirit rather than human power. It sits alongside [D&C 121](/notes/dandc-121), [Matthew 20](/notes/matthew-20), and [Isaiah 48](/notes/isaiah-48) in the study's scripture list on what it means to be *called* versus *chosen*.

**Context:** Zechariah 4 is an Old Testament chapter containing the vision of the golden lampstand and two olive trees; its best-known line, "not by might, nor by power, but by my spirit" (4:6), is a frequently cited statement that God's work proceeds through divine spirit rather than human strength.

## Where this appears

- Chosen Leaders — Zechariah 4:6 ("not by might, nor by power, but by the spirit") grounds the theme that God calls the weak and works through the Spirit.
