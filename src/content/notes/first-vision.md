# First Vision

The First Vision is [Joseph Smith](/notes/joseph-smith)'s account of a theophany in the [Sacred Grove](/notes/sacred-grove) near Palmyra, in which — driven to prayer by James 1:5 ("If any of you lack wisdom, let him ask of God") amid the religious confusion of his family and town — he saw the Father and the Son and asked *which church is true*. In [Truman G. Madsen](/notes/truman-g-madsen)'s reading (Joseph Smith Lecture 1 — The First Vision and Its Aftermath), the question was *which*, not *whether*: it had never occurred to Joseph that *all* the churches were in error, which made the answer ("join none of them") all the more startling. Madsen draws the theological extension Joseph later made from that morning — that to know the [Character of God](/notes/character-of-god) is the first principle of the gospel, and that even "the least Saint" may have the same glorifying certainty by the [Holy Ghost](/notes/holy-ghost). The vision also anchors the Godhead doctrine: it showed the Father and the Son as distinct, embodied beings of "exact resemblance," against the creeds Joseph was told to reject.

Kyle's own topical study (First Vision — Topical Study) frames it historically: a vision account was *not* unusual in the religious fervor of 1816–1820 Palmyra (Charles Finney prayed in the woods and saw Christ too); what was unusual was Joseph's later concreteness and the *theological content* — his vision named the creeds an abomination where Finney's did not, which is what drew persecution. Four firsthand accounts exist (1832, 1835, 1838, 1842), and Kyle distinguishes interpretive from factual memory, warning against [Fawn Brodie](/notes/fawn-brodie)'s and Wesley Walters' arguments from the *absence* of evidence: "Never set a historical argument on the LACK of evidence." Joseph Smith — A Young Seer adds that the vision was recognized as a foundational doctrine mostly only *after* Joseph's death — early missionaries taught the five points of the gospel and the [Book of Mormon](/notes/book-of-mormon) first.

**Context:** In the theology of The Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-day Saints, the First Vision (traditionally dated to spring 1820) is the founding event of the Restoration, recounted canonically in Joseph Smith—History 1 in the Pearl of Great Price.
