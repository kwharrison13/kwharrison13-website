# Charter Cities Institute

The Charter Cities Institute (CCI) appears in Kyle's notes as part of the Silicon Valley new-cities community that a pair of founders consulted during their May–July 2019 [City Building](/notes/city-building) research push. Per the [City Building](/notes/city-building) page, [Mark Lutter](/notes/mark-lutter) (CCI's founder) and [Tamara Winter](/notes/tamara-winter) — then at CCI — were among the [new-cities](/notes/city-building) crowd the founders met, alongside [Iyinoluwa Aboyeji](/notes/iyinoluwa-aboyeji), [Patri Friedman](/notes/patri-friedman), [Mike Solana](/notes/mike-solana), and [Jason Crawford](/notes/jason-crawford). CCI's role was material: when the founders couldn't afford flights to Nigeria to pursue their new-city thesis, CCI gave them a small grant that — combined with $2,000 Callinan won at a golf tournament — funded the tickets. The institute is distinct from the [Charter Cities](/notes/city-building) concept itself.

**Context:** The Charter Cities Institute is a Washington, DC–based nonprofit founded by economist Mark Lutter that researches and advocates for charter cities — new cities with their own governing frameworks, often aimed at economic development in the global south.
