# Dieter F. Uchtdorf

Dieter F. Uchtdorf recurs across Kyle's gospel-study corpus as the source of several of its most-quoted formulations, clustered around **doubt, faith, and humility**. The signature line — "first doubt your doubts before you doubt your faith" — anchors the posture in both [Faith is Not Blind](/books/faith-is-not-blind) (where, with Tolkien's "not all those who wander are lost," it frames faithful questioning as valuable rather than dangerous: the Church "does not self-destruct under questioning or scrutiny") and in the concept page [Doubt](/notes/doubt) (a single unresolved question doesn't overturn a mountain of resolved answers).

He is also the defining voice on **humility** in Humility — Topical Study: "humility is not thinking less of yourself, but less ABOUT yourself," and humility as "not a sign of weakness; it is a sign of spiritual strength." In [Chad's Big Quote Book](/notes/chad-s-big-quote-book) he supplies the "Forget Me Not" reflections, "move the work, not just mind it," and the optimistic agency line "you are only constrained by the limits you place upon yourself." He recurs through Godly Sorrow — Topical Study, Testimony — Topical Study, Patience — Topical Study, and many Come Follow Me pages, as well as 10 Gifts To Take Home From Your Mission and Christ Defines His Gospel.

**Context:** Dieter F. Uchtdorf (b. 1940), a German-born former Lufthansa airline captain, has been a member of the Quorum of the Twelve Apostles of The Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-day Saints since 2004, and previously served in the First Presidency (2008–2018). He is widely known for accessible, aviation-flavored sermons on grace, hope, and faith.
