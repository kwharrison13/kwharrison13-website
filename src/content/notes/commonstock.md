# CommonStock

CommonStock surfaces across Kyle's notes as a social-investing platform that doubled as a reference point in his "networked conviction" thinking — the idea of sharing and discovering investment ideas as a network rather than alone. In his [The Everything Store](/books/the-everything-store) notes, Kyle flags "the difference between Conor and David at CommonStock" as a portfolio-company operating-philosophy comparison, using the company as a lens on leadership contrast. In the Networked Conviction — Roam + Investing - Research research, CommonStock recurs as a tool he and the people he interviewed actually tried: it was "trying to get there" on socially-sourced conviction (perhaps needing a higher emphasis on memos), and Kyle floated using it "to hire analysts" by measuring people on the merit of their writing/performance.

The candid feedback in those interviews is notably mixed: one investor played with CommonStock but found it "just doesn't work" for how he trades — missing options/hedge integrations and feeling like "an echo chamber of people buying the same stuff." So in Kyle's corpus the company functions less as a deal and more as a recurring case study in whether networked, performance-attributed investing communities can actually drive genuine conviction rather than herd behavior.

**Context:** Commonstock was a social network for investors that let users link brokerage accounts to share verified, performance-attributed trades and investment theses; it was acquired by Yahoo in 2023.
