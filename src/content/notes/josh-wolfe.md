# Josh Wolfe

**Co-founder of [Lux Capital](/notes/lux-capital)**, the deep-tech venture firm, and a recurring reference point in Kyle's writing on venture and investing. In the September 13th, 2022 — Harry Stebbings Podcast prep, Kyle anchors the whole "[Differentiation in Venture](/notes/differentiation-in-venture)" thesis on a Wolfe prediction: that "between 50% and 75% of the active investors in private markets today will disappear" within a few years. Kyle uses that forecast as the setup for his argument that differentiation — not capital — will decide which firms survive, and that founders will increasingly ask "what is the [job-to-be-done](/notes/jobs-to-be-done) that I'm hiring this firm for?"

Wolfe also closes out Kyle's [Skill vs. Luck](/notes/skill-vs-luck) argument in [What Is An Investor](/essays/what-is-an-investor). Kyle cites his line — "Too often, we mistake luck for success… many extraordinarily successful people… are just downright lucky… So the key is to position yourself in a way to maximize your luck" — alongside [Michael Mauboussin](/notes/michael-mauboussin)'s skill/luck continuum, and weaves it through the [Kevin Gao](/notes/kevin-gao) conversation that fed the essay. Separately, Wolfe's "science fact vs. science fiction" framing recurs in Kyle's [Techno-Optimism](/notes/techno-optimism) and [Science Fiction](/notes/science-fiction) notes, and Kyle saved Wolfe's [Lux Ventures VII Q4 2022 Report](/notes/lux-ventures-vii-q4-2022-report) (Wolfe is its author) to his long-reads queue.

**Context:** Josh Wolfe is the co-founder and managing partner of Lux Capital, a venture firm focused on emerging science and frontier technology. He is a prolific commentator on markets and deep tech and a frequent CNBC contributor.
