# Self-Publishing

In Kyle's wiki, "Self-Publishing" is the author-controlled alternative to traditional [Publishing](/notes/publishing) — and, more specifically, the productized services that grow up around it. The load-bearing note comes from his April 30th, 2020 Roam "Quick Capture" log: Tucker Max's [Book in a Box](/notes/book-in-a-box) (later Scribe Writing / Scribe Media), a "done-for-you" service that turns a client's interview answers into a published book *in their voice* for a $25K+ minimum. Kyle frames it as a "pickax" play on a publishing gold rush — people want to be published authors to establish thought leadership and command higher consulting/speaking fees, but most lack the time or expertise, so a service captures the value. His note records the company passing $11.3M in revenue by 2017 and becoming more lucrative than Max's own best-sellers.

The concept pairs with [Publishing](/notes/publishing) (the broader industry) and [Book in a Box](/notes/book-in-a-box) (the canonical example): self-publishing is the model the productized service builds on — bypassing the traditional gatekeepers so the author controls the process — and Book in a Box productizes that desire into a packaged, ghostwritten offering.

**Context:** Self-publishing is the practice of an author publishing a book without a traditional publisher, typically via print-on-demand and platforms like Amazon KDP; the 2010s saw a wave of "done-for-you" services (ghostwriting, editing, distribution) built around it for non-writer professionals.
