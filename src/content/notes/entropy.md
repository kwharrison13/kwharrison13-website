# Entropy

Entropy — the inevitable decay of systems over time — runs through Kyle's notes as a unifying lens across software, physics, science fiction, and faith. In [Working in Public](/books/working-in-public), [Nadia Eghbal](/notes/nadia-eghbal) frames it as the *temporal* maintenance cost of software: costs that are "a function of entropy, or costs associated with decay over time" rather than of use — "the inevitable decay of systems over time." This makes entropy a core argument for why [Maintenance](/notes/maintenance), not creation, is the hard problem in [Open Source](/notes/open-source).

[The Time Machine](/books/the-time-machine) gives entropy its cosmic register: the far-future vision of "dwindling stars and the heat-death of activity," where "even the mere memory of Man... had been swept out of existence" — the endpoint of a civilization that traded struggle for security. [Story of Your Life](/books/story-of-your-life) treats entropy as the source of time's arrow: "the arrow of time is a statistical feature of entropy and initial conditions, not of the laws themselves," pointing toward an [eternalist](/notes/eternalism) [Block Universe](/notes/block-universe) where the laws of physics are time-symmetric.

The Alma 36–38 study applies the same idea to faith: "Faith is never standing still. It is either growing or shrinking" ([Dieter F. Uchtdorf](/notes/dieter-f-uchtdorf)) — a spiritual entropy where we "fight constant descent into chaos only by continuous small actions." Across all four, entropy is the default pull toward decay that only deliberate, ongoing effort resists.

**Context:** Entropy, in thermodynamics, is a measure of disorder in a system; the second law of thermodynamics holds that the entropy of an isolated system tends to increase over time, which is widely invoked as the physical basis for time's irreversibility and the eventual "heat death" of the universe.

## Where this appears

- [Working in Public](/books/working-in-public) — temporal maintenance costs as the inevitable decay of software systems over time
- [The Time Machine](/books/the-time-machine) — the heat-death vision of a far future where activity and memory dwindle
- [Story of Your Life](/books/story-of-your-life) — entropy as the statistical origin of time's arrow, pointing to the block universe
- Alma 36–38 — "Look to God and Live" — faith as growing or shrinking, never standing still (spiritual entropy)
