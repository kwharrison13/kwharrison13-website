# Nassim Nicholas Taleb

Taleb is the author of [The Black Swan](/books/the-black-swan), which Kyle reads as a foundational text on tail risk and epistemic humility — the trader-turned-flâneur who built the argument from the Lebanese war and the Chicago trading pits. Kyle pulls several threads from him: that "our inability to predict in environments subjected to the Black Swan" makes supposed experts no better than the general population (tagged directly to the [The Death of Expertise](/books/the-death-of-expertise) thread); that categorization becomes "pathological when the category is seen as definitive" ([Confirmation Bias](/notes/confirmation-bias) and the "Platonicity" critique); that "you need a story to displace a story" (the narrative fallacy); and the turkey problem, which Kyle pairs as the inverse of the [Lindy Principle](/notes/lindy-principle) — a micro data set looks safest at the exact moment of maximum risk.

Taleb threads outward into Kyle's reading on company-building and contrarianism. In [The Everything Store](/books/the-everything-store), *The Black Swan* is the book "all Amazon senior executives had to read," and [Jeff Bezos](/notes/jeff-bezos) invoked Taleb's "narrative fallacy" to favor experimentation and clinical knowledge over storytelling. In [The Death of Expertise](/books/the-death-of-expertise), [Tom Nichols](/notes/tom-nichols) cites Taleb on the "epistemic arrogance" of the whole enterprise of prediction. In Kyle's essay research, Taleb appears as a model contrarian: Conviction-Led Contrarianism - Research quotes his advice that trying to gratify haters only makes things worse ("drive them to hate you even more"), while Surviving The Death of Venture Capital - Research borrows his "ill-dressed rent-seeking oligarchic vampires" line accusing VCs of "injecting neomania."

**Context:** Nassim Nicholas Taleb (b. 1960) is a Lebanese-American essayist, statistician, and former options trader, best known for his *Incerto* series — *Fooled by Randomness*, *The Black Swan*, *Antifragile*, and *Skin in the Game* — on uncertainty, probability, and decision-making under extreme events.
