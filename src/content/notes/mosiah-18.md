# Mosiah 18

The Book of Mormon chapter recording Alma at the waters of Mormon — his teaching, the baptism of his people, and the covenant they enter. Across Kyle's gospel study, the chapter is referenced almost entirely through one verse, Mosiah 18:9, the description of what those entering the [Baptism](/notes/baptism)al covenant are "willing" to do: "mourn with those that mourn; comfort those that stand in need of comfort, and to stand as witnesses of God at all times and in all things, and in all places."

In the Come Follow Me study November 30–December 6, 2020. Moroni 1–6: "To Keep Them in the Right Way", Kyle pairs Mosiah 18:9 with Moroni 3:3 to show the "clear and consistent purpose" running through the priesthood, the Missionary Purpose, and the baptismal covenant — all aimed at bringing others unto Christ. In Charity & Love, the same verse appears on the checklist of charity ("Do you try to help others when they're discouraged or struggling?"). In Born Again, Mosiah 18:9 is grouped with [D&C 6](/notes/dandc-6):36 and [Alma 37](/notes/alma-37):36 in the discussion of how the Holy Ghost is the "earnest of our inheritance" — God's binding promise to those who keep their covenants.

**Context:** Mosiah 18 is one of the Book of Mormon's most-quoted chapters on baptism. The covenant language of verses 8–10 — bearing one another's burdens, mourning with those who mourn, standing as witnesses of God — is widely used in Latter-day Saint teaching about what baptism commits a person to.
