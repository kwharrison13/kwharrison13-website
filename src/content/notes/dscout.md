# Dscout

Dscout appears in Kyle's notes as one of the **recruited-panel / interview vendors** in the [User Research](/notes/user-research) landscape, surfaced during the Sprig fka UserLeap - Diligence expert calls. The User Research page places it in the "recruited panels + interviews" bucket alongside [UserTesting](/notes/usertesting), [Ethn.io](/notes/ethn-io), and [Userinterviews.com](/notes/userinterviews-com) — distinct from the contextual in-app micro-survey approach of [Sprig](/notes/sprig) and the live-recorded-interview tools like [Lookback](/notes/lookback). In the diligence notes, one expert's tech-stack description characterized Dscout as a "more engaged UserTesting," where the team continues engagement with participants after roughly four weeks.

It was also named by [Yue Wu](/notes/yue-wu) (a Group Product Manager at [Dropbox](/notes/dropbox) and Sprig customer) as one of the other research/testing vendors she uses alongside Sprig.

**Context:** dscout is a user-research platform known for "in-context" longitudinal research via mobile diary studies — participants capture moments and respond to prompts over time on their phones — plus live interviews and a participant recruiting panel.
