# D&C 1

Section 1 of the Doctrine and Covenants — the Lord's preface to the revelations — anchors two threads across Kyle's scripture study. The first is **verse 24**, the workhorse verse on how revelation works: "these commandments are of me, and were given unto my servants in their weakness, after the manner of their language, that they might come to understanding." Kyle's Revelation — Topical Study builds its whole framework around it — "God is a perfect encoder; the prophet (and we) are imperfect decoders," so revelation comes in our weakness as a gradual sunrise rather than a light switch ([David A. Bednar](/notes/david-a-bednar)). The same verse grounds Teaching Repentance (commandments given in our language "that they might come to understand") and The Lehonti Principle (the Spirit speaks to our understanding, to what we are already pondering).

The second thread is **verse 38** — "my word shall not pass away, but shall all be fulfilled, whether by mine own voice or by the voice of my servants, it is the same" — which Kyle cites in his study of [Alma 36](/notes/alma-36)–38 ("Look to God and Live") as the assurance that prophetic word carries the Lord's own authority.

**Context:** Doctrine and Covenants Section 1 is a revelation given to [Joseph Smith](/notes/joseph-smith) in November 1831, which the Lord designated as the preface to the published book of revelations. It addresses the whole world, declaring the voice of warning and the divine sanction behind the latter-day revelations.
