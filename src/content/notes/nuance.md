# Nuance

Note: despite the `company` type carried over from auto-generation, "Nuance" here is a recurring **concept** in Kyle's corpus, not an organization. It names the discipline of holding both/and against false either/or dichotomies — refusing to flatten complex reality into binaries. In [Braving The Wilderness](/books/braving-the-wilderness), [Brené Brown](/notes/bren-brown)'s "support both the police and the activists" stance is the canonical example: "most of the criticism comes from people who are intent on forcing these false either/or dichotomies and shaming us for not hating the right people. It's definitely messier taking a nuanced stance, but it's also critically important to true belonging." Kyle tags that passage #[Nuance](/notes/nuance).

The concept anchors two of Kyle's essay-research threads. In [Embrace The Nuance](/essays/embrace-the-nuance) (subtitle: *A Struggle At The Extremes*), nuance is the through-line — sources there observe that "seeing things as black or white takes less effort than accepting nuance," that the speed of the internet "leaves very little time for nuanced discussions," and Fred Rogers' reminder that "the very same people who are good sometimes are the very same people who are bad sometimes," culminating in the charge to "refuse to live a binary life." In [We Need a Renaissance of Rhetoric](/essays/we-need-a-renaissance-of-rhetoric), nuance pairs with [Rhetoric](/notes/rhetoric) — where the [Rhetoric](/notes/rhetoric) concept page frames it directly: "rhetoric as the craft of distinguishing and shading meaning rather than flattening it."
