# Law of Accelerating Returns

[Ray Kurzweil](/notes/ray-kurzweil)'s central predictive framework: the claim that technological advances feed on themselves, so the *rate* of further advance keeps increasing — progress compounds along exponential rather than linear curves. Across Kyle's corpus it is the canonical case of exponential-curve futurism. In [The Enchanted Notebook](/notes/the-enchanted-notebook), Kyle frames Kurzweil's superpower as "believing harder than anyone else in the power of exponential curves," and points to Kurzweil's 2001 essay on the law as worth reading "both for the ideas themselves and for how well many of his 21-year-old predictions have held up." The idea is also cited as a key claim of the [Techno-Optimist Manifesto](/notes/techno-optimist-manifesto), where accelerating returns underwrite the case for relentless technological progress.

**Context:** The Law of Accelerating Returns is the thesis Ray Kurzweil laid out in his 2001 essay of that name (and developed in *The Singularity Is Near*): that evolutionary and technological systems improve at an exponentially accelerating pace, which he uses to forecast a technological singularity around 2045.
