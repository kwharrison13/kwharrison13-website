# Cognizant

Cognizant appears in Kyle's notes as the subcontractor in the YouTube Music / Cognizant joint-employer fight, cited in the [Fissured Workplace](/notes/fissured-workplace) concept page. It serves as a concrete example of cross-pressure on the arm's-length subcontracting model — where a tech platform (here YouTube Music, via Google) routes specialized work through a contractor like Cognizant, and a joint-employer challenge tests whether the platform can hold those workers at arm's length. It anchors the same "fissured workplace" dynamic that the Unions, Unions Everywhere - Research note explores around tech's relationship with organized labor.

**Context:** Cognizant is a US-headquartered, India-centric IT services and business-process-outsourcing firm; its staffing-services arm supplies large contractor workforces to clients like Google. The 2019 YouTube Music workers' union drive at Cognizant became a notable test of joint-employer liability between a staffing vendor and the tech platform it served.
