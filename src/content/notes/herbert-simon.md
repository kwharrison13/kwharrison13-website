# Herbert Simon

A Nobel-laureate polymath whose autobiography, [Models of My Life](/notes/models-of-my-life), sits in Kyle's anti-library — picked up off [Charlie Munger](/notes/charlie-munger)'s perennial reading list in [Poor Charlie's Almanack](/books/poor-charlie-s-almanack) as one of the books that build "a latticework of multidisciplinary mental models." The anti-library entry frames Simon by exactly that quality: his interdisciplinary work spanning economics, psychology, artificial intelligence, and bounded rationality. He also recurs in Kyle's read management literature, surfacing in [Managers Not MBAs](/books/managers-not-mbas) and [The Messy Marketplace](/books/the-messy-marketplace).

**Context:** Herbert A. Simon (1916–2001) was an American economist, cognitive scientist, and political scientist who won the 1978 Nobel Memorial Prize in Economics for his work on decision-making within organizations; he coined "bounded rationality" and "satisficing" and was a founding figure of artificial intelligence. He spent most of his career at Carnegie Mellon.
