# Brex

Brex appears across Kyle's corpus as a recurring reference case in two threads: the corporate-card/fintech category and the "startups selling to startups" critique of late-cycle venture. In the Pave Diligence — 2021 notes, Brex is the strategic template — Pave is described as running "a [Brex](/notes/brex) playbook to own startups," with [Y Combinator](/notes/y-combinator) access as the meaningful go-to-market advantage. In the September 22nd, 2021 — Mark Goldberg (Career Feedback) meeting, Brex is the anchor for Kyle's [Ramp](/notes/ramp) thesis: he frames a counterfactual where, "during Brex," he had already done the category work and could argue Ramp (with the better product, and an [Altimeter](/notes/altimeter) principal leading) deserved an immediate markup. [Altimeter](/notes/altimeter) itself is catalogued via this Brex/Ramp comparison.

In Kyle's essay research Brex becomes the cleanest live example of the "turtles all the way down" venture-contagion concern: VC Contagion - Research cites the figure that ~80% of YC companies were on Brex as of November 2022, illustrating startups whose revenue comes largely from other (VC-subsidized) startups. The Mysteries of an Economic Engine - Research picks up the same thread via Shai Goldman's public concerns at Brex. Brex also surfaces operationally as a paying customer in the November 7th, 2022 "Cleared Email" note.

**Context:** Brex is a fintech company founded in 2017 by Henrique Dubugras and Pedro Franceschi, originally offering corporate charge cards aimed at startups (famously via a Y Combinator distribution motion) before expanding into broader spend management and banking. Ramp is its most-cited competitor.
