# Reid Hoffman

Reid Hoffman recurs across Kyle's reading as both author and PayPal-era operator. As author, he co-wrote *[The Start-Up of You](/books/the-start-up-of-you)* (with [Ben Casnocha](/notes/ben-casnocha), read by Kyle in 2017), the book arguing you should manage your career like a start-up — network, adapt, take intelligent risks — and the source of Kyle's [Networking](/notes/networking) note on "network intelligence" and the blend of strong and weak ties. As operator, he is a primary subject of *[The Founders](/books/the-founders)* (the PayPal story), described there as "COO-by-trust" who "coined 'blitzscaling'" and a central member of the [PayPal Mafia](/notes/paypal-mafia) alongside [Peter Thiel](/notes/peter-thiel), [Max Levchin](/notes/max-levchin), [Elon Musk](/notes/elon-musk), and [Roelof Botha](/notes/roelof-botha).

His sharpest appearance is in Kyle's [Platform Risk](/notes/platform-risk) note: facing PayPal's total dependence on eBay, Hoffman captured the precariousness with a line Kyle flags — "Just because someone shoots five bullets at you and misses… does not preclude the sixth one from killing you" (Keith Rabois later quotes this as "Reid Hoffman's sixth bullet"). He also surfaces in *[Talent (Book)](/books/talent)* (Cowen & Gross) as the 2019 interview interlocutor to whom [Patrick Collison](/notes/patrick-collison) presented the three reference-check questions Kyle finds useful, and in the April 5th, 2022 meeting notes ("You're hosed") on the early-PayPal underdog story.

**Context:** Reid Hoffman is the co-founder of LinkedIn, an early PayPal executive, a partner at Greylock, and co-author of *The Start-Up of You* and *Blitzscaling*. He is one of the most prominent members of the so-called "PayPal Mafia."
