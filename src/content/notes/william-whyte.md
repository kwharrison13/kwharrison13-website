# William Whyte

Author of [The Organization Man](/notes/the-organization-man), cited in [The Future of American Sports](/notes/the-future-of-american-sports). Whyte's argument — that mid-century American values shifted from individualism toward collectivist corporate loyalty — is used as the frame for how football, the sport of the corporation, mirrored the era of the collectivist "organization man."

**Context:** William H. Whyte (1917–1999) was an American journalist and urbanist whose 1956 book *The Organization Man* analyzed the conformist corporate culture of postwar America. He later became known for his observational studies of urban public spaces.

## Where this appears

- [The Future of American Sports](/notes/the-future-of-american-sports) — cited via [The Organization Man](/notes/the-organization-man): football as the sport that reflected the collectivist corporate-man era.
