# Outliers: The Story of Success

[Malcolm Gladwell](/notes/malcolm-gladwell)'s *Outliers* is cited directly in [What It Takes](/books/what-it-takes), where [Stephen A. Schwarzman](/notes/stephen-a-schwarzman) invokes it for the practice-before-mastery argument. Schwarzman uses Gladwell's examples — the Beatles transforming themselves through their 1960–1962 Hamburg residency, and Bill Gates logging hours on the University of Washington computers as a teenager — to argue that "people who succeed in finance must start with repetitive practice before they can ever hope to achieve mastery." In Kyle's notes this anchors the [Theory of Masters](/notes/theory-of-masters): know whether you're in a training ground or an arena, and go somewhere that will train you before you try to be a master.

**Context:** *Outliers: The Story of Success* (2008) is Malcolm Gladwell's book popularizing the "10,000-hour rule" — the idea that elite performance comes from accumulated deliberate practice plus advantages of timing, culture, and circumstance.
