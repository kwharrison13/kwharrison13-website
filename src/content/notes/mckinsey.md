# McKinsey

The management-consulting firm at the center of [Bonfire of the Consultancies](/notes/bonfire-of-the-consultancies), Lionel Barber's review of *When McKinsey Comes to Town* and *The Big Con*. The piece traces McKinsey's arc from prestige to scandal: by 1968 its name was "becoming as synonymous with managerial reform as Hoover is with vacuum cleaning," and a journalist could write that "if God were to remake the world, he would call upon McKinsey for assistance." The review then documents the firm's collapse in standards — an excessive focus on fees, partners chasing dubious government mandates, and failures of oversight that forced leadership departures. Its most damning example is McKinsey's Saudi Arabia work consulting on Vision 2030, where (alongside Bain and Cambridge Analytica) it appears to have promoted "sentiment analysis" whose mined social-media output was subsequently used by the Saudi regime to track political opponents. Barber's verdict: "the disconnect between the firm's high-minded public image and the grubby pursuit of profit was unsustainable."

McKinsey also surfaces in a very different register through its research arm. [Rekindling US Productivity for a New Era](/notes/rekindling-us-productivity-for-a-new-era) is a McKinsey Global Institute report arguing that regaining historical US productivity-growth rates (2.2% annually) would add $10 trillion to GDP — making the firm a source of the productivity-slowdown thesis as well as a target of the consulting critique.

**Context:** McKinsey & Company is the oldest and most prestigious of the "big three" strategy consultancies (with Bain and BCG), founded in 1926. The McKinsey Global Institute (MGI) is its in-house economics and business-research think tank.
