# Shane Parrish

Parrish is the author of [The Great Mental Models Volume 1 — General Thinking Concepts](/books/the-great-mental-models-volume-1-general-thinking-concepts) and the host of The Knowledge Project podcast at Farnam Street (fs.blog). In Kyle's corpus he functions as the modern systematizer of [Charlie Munger](/notes/charlie-munger)'s "latticework of mental models" thesis: his mental-models book is read as "Shane Parrish's explicit attempt to organize the latticework Munger argues for," a direct progenitor-to-curriculum line from [Poor Charlie's Almanack](/books/poor-charlie-s-almanack). While annotating Munger's "Elementary Worldly Wisdom" talk, Kyle's own margin note asks "How does Shane Parrish think about this with his 'mental models' books?" — making Parrish the explicit bridge between Munger and the practitioner's curriculum.

His ideas show up as load-bearing citations elsewhere. In [The Great Mental Models](/books/the-great-mental-models-volume-1-general-thinking-concepts) he uses [John Muir](/notes/john-muir)'s line ("When we try to pick out anything by itself, we find it hitched to everything else in the Universe") to argue for second-order/systems thinking, and his journaling passage on self-feedback ("Keeping a journal of your own performance is the easiest and most private way to give self-feedback... painful for the ego, which is also why it helps build a [Circle of Competence](/notes/circle-of-competence)") anchors Kyle's What's In a Post-Mortem - Research notes on reflection and post-mortems. He is also the interviewer of [Naval Ravikant](/notes/naval-ravikant) on The Knowledge Project Ep. #18 ([Naval Ravikant — The Angel Philosopher](/notes/naval-ravikant-the-angel-philosopher)).

**Context:** Shane Parrish is a Canadian writer and former intelligence-agency employee who founded Farnam Street (fs.blog), an influential blog and brand focused on mental models, decision-making, and learning, and hosts The Knowledge Project podcast.
