# William McLellin

In Kyle's Early Mormonism study notes, [William McLellin](/notes/william-mclellin) appears as a figure in the early Latter-day Saint community who interviews [Hiram Page](/notes/hiram-page) regarding the beatings Page suffered at the hands of the mob — a vignette set within the Jackson County, Missouri persecutions of 1832–1833. He is listed among the people of that period alongside [David Whitmer](/notes/david-whitmer), [Hiram Page](/notes/hiram-page), [Alexander Majors](/notes/alexander-majors), and [Joseph Smith](/notes/joseph-smith).

**Context:** William E. McLellin (1806–1883) was an early Latter-day Saint, an original member of the Quorum of the Twelve Apostles (1835), who later left the church and became a critic, but who is also remembered for documenting and interviewing other early members about their experiences.

## Where this appears

- Early Mormonism — recorded as interviewing [Hiram Page](/notes/hiram-page) about the mob beatings during the Jackson County persecutions.
