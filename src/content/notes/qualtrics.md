# Qualtrics

Qualtrics is the experience-management platform that recurs across Kyle's notes in three distinct roles. In customer-support / CX diligence it is grouped with [Medallia](/notes/medallia) as an experience-management adjacency and cited for its **Clarabridge acquisition** — feeding customer feedback into a central repository and using NLP to understand top issues, the kind of "post-contact scoring" and big-data heat-map opportunity discussed in the Cresta.ai - Diligence. In product-survey diligence, a generic Qualtrics form is invoked as the commoditization yardstick — "why is this better than a white-labeled Qualtrics form in a [Pendo](/notes/pendo)?" — the central self-build objection raised against survey startups like [Sprig](/notes/sprig).

As a [Utah Tech Ecosystem](/notes/utah-tech-ecosystem) anchor, Qualtrics functions as a talent-genealogy signal in Kyle's sourcing notes: operators coming out of sales-led companies like Qualtrics tend to over-index on go-to-market, arriving with the assumption that "they're going to hire 30 sales reps in the first year" — a cautionary pattern flagged by [Serene Papenfuss](/notes/serene-papenfuss). Kyle also took an angel-investing call with Cody Guymon, COO for Global Sales at Qualtrics, who owns the forecasting methodology there (implemented [Clari](/notes/clari) across the org, signed [Spiff](/notes/spiff)) and frames his investing around backing companies he'd be a customer of.

**Context:** Qualtrics is a Provo, Utah-based experience-management software company (surveys, customer/employee/product/brand experience). Founded 2002, it IPO'd in 2021 after SAP's 2018 acquisition, and acquired conversational-analytics firm Clarabridge in 2021.
