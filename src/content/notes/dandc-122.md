# D&C 122

Section 122 of the [Doctrine and Covenants](/notes/doctrine-and-covenants) is, for Kyle, the anchor scripture for the idea that adversity is given "for thy good." He repeatedly returns to D&C 122:7 — *"if the very jaws of hell shall gape open the mouth wide after thee, know thou, my son, that all these things shall give thee experience, and shall be for thy good."* In the Ether 1–5 study he quotes the verse in full. In the Plan of Salvation — Topical Study he applies it to the pre-mortal life: trusting the Lord's promise that the horrors and tribulations of mortality would ultimately be for our good. On the [Brother of Jared](/notes/brother-of-jared) page it sits alongside [Helaman 12](/notes/helaman-12):3 as support for the [Spiritual Complacency](/notes/spiritual-complacency) theme — that hardship is what makes us feel our need of God.

**Context:** Doctrine and Covenants Section 122 (March 1839) is part of a revelation received by Joseph Smith while imprisoned in Liberty Jail, in which the Lord enumerates every possible affliction and assures him that "all these things shall give thee experience, and shall be for thy good" and that "the Son of Man hath descended below them all."
