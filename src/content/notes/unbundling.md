# Unbundling

Unbundling recurs in Kyle's notes as a structural lens on how broad, monolithic platforms get broken into specialized, niche-focused pieces. In the Open-Source Knowledge - Research notes it appears as a market thesis behind the [Contrary Research](/essays/contrary-research) launch: monolithic knowledge communities like [Wikipedia](/notes/wikipedia) and [Reddit](/notes/reddit) get unbundled into specialized niche "front pages." Both [Reddit](/notes/reddit) and [Wikipedia](/notes/wikipedia) carry this thesis on their own pages — Reddit "cited as an example of a community ripe for unbundling," Wikipedia "named as a candidate for unbundling into specialized niche front pages."

The concept also anchors one of Kyle's published essays, [The Unbundling of Venture Capital](/essays/the-unbundling-of-venture-capital) (subtitle *Power of the People*), whose research notes apply [David Perell](/notes/david-perell)'s [Naked Brands](/notes/naked-brands) thesis to venture: as influencers and individuals build loyal followings and then companies, the bundled institution gives way to people-led, transparent brands. In tension with all of this sits [The Market Is The Greatest Critic](/notes/the-market-is-the-greatest-critic), framed in the wiki as "the conceptual counterpart" — the piece argues public markets *reward* firms that bundle product lines into diversified platforms, the opposite pull from unbundling.

**Context:** "Bundling and unbundling" is a long-running tech-strategy framing (popularized in venture circles by Jim Barksdale's "there are two ways to make money in business: bundle and unbundle") describing how incumbents aggregate many features into one product, while challengers attack by peeling off and improving a single piece.

## Where this appears

- Open-Source Knowledge - Research — the niche-front-pages thesis (unbundling Wikipedia/Reddit) behind the Contrary Research launch.
- The Unbundling of Venture Capital - Research — Perell's Naked Brands thesis applied to venture; people unbundling the institution.
- [Reddit](/notes/reddit) — cited as a community ripe for unbundling into niche front pages.
- [Wikipedia](/notes/wikipedia) — named as a candidate for unbundling into specialized front pages.
- [The Market Is The Greatest Critic](/notes/the-market-is-the-greatest-critic) — the conceptual counterpart: markets reward *bundling* into diversified platforms.
