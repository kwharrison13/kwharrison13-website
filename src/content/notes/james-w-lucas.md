# James W. Lucas

Co-author (with Warren Woodworth) of [Working Toward Zion](/books/working-toward-zion), the LDS-economics book Kyle reads as a practical blueprint for "what business done differently might actually look like" rather than as theology. Lucas and Woodworth set the [United Order](/notes/united-order) against [Capitalism](/notes/capitalism) and socialism as a genuinely distinct organizing principle for economic life — not "capitalism with charity added on" — and present [Consecration](/notes/consecration) as a still-live, unfinished framework that the modern Church has yet to build the institutional infrastructure for (cooperative banks, the [Mondragon](/notes/mondragon) model, Value Added Ventures). The book's recurring argument that Kyle flags: "maximizing shareholder wealth" cannot be a terminal goal, and the binding constraint on consecration is finding "men who know what to do with it" — capable stewards, which is what business education at [BYU](/notes/brigham-young-university) should exist to produce.
