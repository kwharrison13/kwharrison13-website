# Matt Levine

Financial commentator and author of Bloomberg's "Money Stuff" column, cited across Kyle's notes as a model for making complex financial subjects legible. On the [Adam Neumann](/notes/adam-neumann) page, Kyle quotes Levine's summary of Neumann: he "figured out maybe the best and funniest way anyone has ever made money in the history of capitalism, which is 'act crazy around Masayoshi Son until money spews out of him.'" In Kyle's call with Anna-Sofia Lesiv (recorded in the August 29th, 2022 — Anna-Sofia Lesiv meeting note), Levine is held up as the standard for technological/financial literacy in writing — "the way that [Matt Levine](/notes/matt-levine) can talk about really complex financial concepts in a way that feels obvious once you've read it," catering to laymen and experts at once.

**Context:** Matt Levine is a former Goldman Sachs banker and lawyer turned financial columnist who writes Bloomberg Opinion's daily "Money Stuff" newsletter, known for explaining finance and markets with humor and clarity.
