# 2 Nephi 28

In Kyle's gospel study, 2 Nephi 28 is mined for two distinct verse-clusters. The first, **2 Nephi 28:21–22**, is his recurring text on **complacency and apostasy**: the devil "will pacify, and lull them away into carnal security, that they will say: All is well in Zion … and thus the devil cheateth their souls, and leadeth them away carefully down to hell." Diligence uses it to describe how "the grinding, repetitive nature of daily righteousness" lets an open heart soften into a "slow-leaking cancer" — Satan leading "by the neck with a flaxen cord." Christian Reformation, Zion, and Seekers reads it as the mechanism of the Great Apostasy: an apostasy "begins in the hearts of individuals" ("all is well in Zion") until it becomes general. Strikingly, Kyle cross-references the same verses against [Charlie Munger — The Psychology of Human Misjudgment](/notes/charlie-munger-the-psychology-of-human-misjudgment) and Temporal Wealth and the Kingdom of God — Brigham Young, reading "carnal security" as a scriptural cousin of Munger's psychological complacency.

The second cluster, **2 Nephi 28:30** — "line upon line, precept upon precept; here a little and there a little" — anchors his theology of **incremental revelation and teaching**. Patience — Topical Study quotes [Robert D. Hales](/notes/robert-d-hales) that answers come "here a little and there a little" because that is all we can bear; Revelation — Topical Study uses it to justify teaching each person at their own level; and That All Men May Come Unto Him applies it to self-mastery — "the submission of one's will" comes not at once but "line upon line." The chapter thus pairs a warning (don't be lulled) with a method (grow incrementally).

**Context:** 2 Nephi 28 is a chapter in the Book of Mormon recording Nephi's prophecy of the spiritual conditions of the last days — false churches, pride, and the lulling of the comfortable into "carnal security" — and is among the most-cited LDS texts on both apostasy and the "line upon line" pattern of revelation.
