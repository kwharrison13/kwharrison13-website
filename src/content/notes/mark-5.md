# Mark 5

A chapter in the New Testament Gospel of Mark, cited in Kyle's gospel study. In Faith — Topical Study, Mark 5:34 anchors a study point on faith and forgiveness: Christ declares the woman's faith made her whole. Kyle's note reasons that Christ would often say "your sins are forgiven thee" after an expression of faith — sins don't necessarily cause ailments, but if a person has enough faith to seek his power, they have enough faith to be forgiven and to accept his role as Savior.

**Context:** Mark 5 records Jesus healing the Gadarene demoniac, raising Jairus's daughter, and healing the woman with the issue of blood, to whom he says "Daughter, thy faith hath made thee whole" (Mark 5:34).
