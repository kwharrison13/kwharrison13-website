# Psalm 121

Psalm 121 enters Kyle's notes through [Russell M. Nelson](/notes/russell-m-nelson)'s talk Christ Is Risen; Faith in Him Will Move Mountains — Russell M. Nelson, where verse 4 — "he that keepeth Israel shall neither slumber nor sleep" — anchors Nelson's assurance that "if everything and everyone else in the world whom you trust should fail, Jesus Christ and His Church will never fail you." It sits in that talk among a cluster of faith proof-texts (alongside [Matthew 17](/notes/matthew-17):20, [Mark 9](/notes/mark-9):23, [Daniel 3](/notes/daniel-3):16–18 and others) marshaled to the theme that faith increases our access to godly power. Kyle pairs the verse with [Mormon 9](/notes/mormon-9):9 ("God is the same yesterday, today, and forever") to underline the constancy of God as the ground of trust.

**Context:** Psalm 121 is one of the biblical "Songs of Ascents" (Psalms 120–134), opening "I will lift up mine eyes unto the hills, from whence cometh my help" — a pilgrimage psalm about God's unsleeping protection of those who trust him.

## Where this appears

- Christ Is Risen; Faith in Him Will Move Mountains — Russell M. Nelson — verse 4 ("neither slumber nor sleep") cited as the assurance that God never fails those who trust him, within the talk's larger argument that faith moves mountains.
