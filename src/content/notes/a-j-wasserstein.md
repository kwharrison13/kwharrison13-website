# A.J. Wasserstein

A.J. Wasserstein is a co-author (with [Mark Agnew](/notes/mark-agnew) and [Brian O'Connor](/notes/brian-o-connor)) of the Yale case study "On the Nature of Long-Term Holds" (May 28, 2020) that Kyle annotated in [On the Nature of Long-Term Holds](/notes/on-the-nature-of-long-term-holds).

**Context:** A.J. Wasserstein is a lecturer at the Yale School of Management focused on search funds and entrepreneurship through acquisition, and a frequent author of practitioner-oriented case studies and notes on small-company investing and long-term ownership.

## Where this appears

- [On the Nature of Long-Term Holds](/notes/on-the-nature-of-long-term-holds) — credited as a co-author of the Yale case study Kyle annotated on the merits of long-horizon, exit-free ownership.
