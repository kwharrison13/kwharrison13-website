# How Rare a Possession

A film Kyle watched on his mission, which he recalls in his Mormon 7–9 study as an illustration that the Book of Mormon's witness "can stand on its own." Where the usual argument treats the Book of Mormon as valuable because it demonstrates [Joseph Smith](/notes/joseph-smith)'s prophetic claims, Kyle notes that the film's protagonist, Vincenzo, knew nothing about Joseph Smith — he simply heard the prophets speaking in the Book of Mormon, took Moroni's promise, asked God, and received a witness that it was true (Moroni 10:3-5). The point sits next to the study's [Atheism](/notes/atheism) and [Pascal's Wager](/notes/pascal-s-wager) threads on belief and personal testimony.

**Context:** *How Rare a Possession: The Book of Mormon* is a 1987 dramatic film produced by The Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-day Saints, telling two stories of conversion to the Book of Mormon — that of Vincenzo di Francesca, an Italian minister who found torn pages of the book and was converted decades before joining the Church, and that of Parley P. Pratt.
