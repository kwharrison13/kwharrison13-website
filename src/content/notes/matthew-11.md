# Matthew 11

In Kyle's gospel study, Matthew 11 is the textual home of one passage above all: Christ's invitation, **"Come unto me, all ye that labour and are heavy laden, and I will give you rest... for my yoke is easy, and my burden is light"** (Matt 11:28–30). He quotes it in full in his notes on Christ Is Risen; Faith in Him Will Move Mountains — Russell M. Nelson, and uses the "rest unto our souls" phrase (11:29) in How To Apply The Atonement.

In Self-Mastery — Topical Study he sets Matthew 11:29–30 in deliberate tension with "deny himself and take up his cross" ([Matthew 16](/notes/matthew-16):24) — the gospel paradox between the cross that is hard and the yoke that is easy. The resolution he draws is that self-mastery is won not by one heroic effort but by yielding to a burden Christ makes light.

**Context:** Matthew 11 records Jesus's response to John the Baptist's disciples, his rebuke of the unrepentant cities (Chorazin, Bethsaida, Capernaum), and closes with the "Come unto me" invitation — one of the most quoted comfort passages in the New Testament.
