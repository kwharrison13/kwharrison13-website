# Patrick Kearon

A leader of The Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-day Saints whose April 2026 General Conference address About His Business Kyle studied. In that talk Kearon opens with his own experience of being called as an assistant ward clerk shortly after his baptism — a calling that felt entirely wrong for him but proved foundational — and uses it to teach that callings are divinely tailored for our growth. His central claim: serving in any calling with a humble heart is not merely filling an assignment but partnering with the Saviour in His redeeming work ("His business," echoing the twelve-year-old Jesus in the temple). He frames the sustaining vote as **common consent** — a voluntary commitment to support the Lord's called servants. The talk is delivered in the context of the solemn assembly sustaining [Dallin H. Oaks](/notes/dallin-h-oaks) as President of the Church.

**Context:** Patrick Kearon is a member of the First Presidency of The Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-day Saints. British-born, he joined the Church as a young adult, served in the Quorum of the Twelve Apostles, and was called to the First Presidency in 2025.
