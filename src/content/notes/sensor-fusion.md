# Sensor Fusion

Sensor fusion is one of the dominant threads Kyle mines from his defense and semiconductor reading as evidence for a verticalized defense-tech investment thesis. In [Chip War](/books/chip-war), its origin is [Bill Perry](/notes/bill-perry)'s DARPA "Assault Breaker" program: an aerial radar identifies enemy targets and feeds a ground-based processing center that "fuses the radar details with information from other sensors," with ground missiles communicating back to the radar — "a vision of automated war, with computing power distributed to individual systems." Kyle's annotation on that passage reads simply *"#Anduril - sensor fusion."* The concept threads directly into [Anduril](/notes/anduril), where Kyle's notes pair sensor fusion with [Civil-Military Fusion](/notes/civil-military-fusion) and the treatment of platforms as attritable/expendable — he reads semiconductor history as due-diligence material for the defense-tech thesis rather than for its own sake.

**Context:** Sensor fusion is the engineering practice of combining data from multiple sensors (radar, optical, infrared, signals) into a single, more accurate picture than any one sensor provides — foundational to modern autonomous systems, missile guidance, and battlefield targeting.
