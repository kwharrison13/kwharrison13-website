# Spirit of Humility

One of Kyle's most-tagged personal disciplines — the through-line where secular epistemic humility and the LDS doctrine of humility meet. It appears most densely in [Poor Charlie's Almanack](/books/poor-charlie-s-almanack), where Kyle repeatedly annotates Munger's material with "spirit of humility": [Charlie Munger](/notes/charlie-munger)'s pairing of [Inversion](/notes/inversion) and humility as core disciplines, Darwin's habit of writing down disconfirming evidence, Mark Twain's "what he knows for certain that just ain't so," and Epictetus's "it is impossible to begin to learn that which one thinks one already knows." The operative definition Kyle keeps returning to is treated as a *measurable skill, not a disposition*: "you are not suited to strongly hold an opinion until you can make the counter-argument more effectively than the people who want to argue with you." One Kyle note ties it to the Ford v. Ferrari clip ("you can't ask for ideas and then act like you know everything") and the [Sam Walton](/notes/sam-walton) story about learning from everyone, even associates — Munger's "acknowledging what you don't know is the dawning of wisdom."

The same posture recurs across Kyle's shelf. [Braving The Wilderness](/books/braving-the-wilderness) frames it as the "strong-back, soft-front" stance of acknowledging pain without weaponizing it. [The Death of Expertise](/books/the-death-of-expertise) supplies the inverse — the collapse of expert humility and the [Dunning-Kruger Effect](/notes/dunning-kruger-effect). [Discipline Is Destiny](/books/discipline-is-destiny) supplies a sharp warning that *false* humility is often narcissism in disguise: "we tell ourselves it's self-discipline when in fact it's self-consciousness." Read against scripture (the [Beware Of Pride](/notes/beware-of-pride) thread, the broken-heart-and-contrite-spirit verses), the concept is Kyle's bridge between Munger's cognitive-bias catalog and the religious virtue of being teachable.
