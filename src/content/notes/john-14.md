# John 14

A New Testament chapter Kyle cites for two distinct doctrines across his gospel study. The first is **the promise of the Comforter** — John 14:16, 26 ("I will pray the Father, and he shall give you another Comforter, that he may abide with you for ever"). He pulls this into the [Come, Follow Me](/notes/come-follow-me) weeks on Alma 17–22 and 3 Nephi 20–26, and in Spiritual Gifts he notes verse 26 specifically for the Holy Ghost's role in helping us recall what we've learned when we need it.

The second is **the unity of the Father and the Son**. In The Godhead he cites John 14:10 ("I am in the Father, and the Father in me") and reads it through the lens of *divine investiture* — God empowering the premortal Jesus to represent Him in all earthly affairs — grouping it with [John 17](/notes/john-17), [D&C 130](/notes/dandc-130), and teachings from [Jeffrey R. Holland](/notes/jeffrey-r-holland) and [Gerald N. Lund](/notes/gerald-n-lund).

**Context:** John 14 is part of Jesus's farewell discourse to his disciples at the Last Supper, containing his promise of the Holy Spirit as a "Comforter" and the well-known statements "I am the way, the truth, and the life" and "I am in the Father, and the Father in me."
