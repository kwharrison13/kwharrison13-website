# Rachel Hansen

A [Dropbox](/notes/dropbox) alum quoted in the research for Kyle's essay The Talent Vortex, where she's used as a first-person account of how exceptional recruiting created a talent flywheel. In her words, "they did an incredible job on recruiting, so you really felt like you were joining an exceptional team — that then lived up to the hype," and the early momentum "kept the flywheel going." She describes the culture as "almost cult-y but in the absolute best way, because we all felt like we were on this rocketship," recalling the CFO Sujay's onboarding line — "Google sells ads so they can build self-driving cars. Dropbox does sync-and-share so we can also do epic things" — and how people's lives "really centered around it."
