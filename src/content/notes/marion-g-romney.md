# Marion G. Romney

A leader of [The Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-day Saints](/notes/the-church-of-jesus-christ-of-latter-day-saints) whose counsel recurs across Kyle's gospel-study corpus, generally on the discipline of receiving and testing revelation. In [Boyd K. Packer](/notes/boyd-k-packer)'s talk The Candle of the Lord, Packer recounts hearing "President Marion G. Romney" counsel mission presidents in Geneva on guarding sacred things: "I do not tell all I know… for I found out that if I talked too lightly of sacred things, thereafter the Lord would not trust me." In [The Priesthood Power of Women](/books/the-priesthood-power-of-women) he is cited for four tests of whether a teaching is genuine revelation — whether it originates "in the wisdom of men" or "was revealed from heaven," whether it bears "the proper label," whether it conforms "to the other teaching of the Gospel," and whether it comes "through the proper Church channel."

He is also among the many General Authorities quoted in [Chad's Big Quote Book](/notes/chad-s-big-quote-book), Kyle's compiled quote collection.

**Context:** Marion G. Romney (1897–1988) was a member of the Quorum of the Twelve Apostles and later a counselor in the First Presidency of the LDS Church. He was known for his teachings on welfare, self-reliance, and personal revelation.
