# Lehi

Lehi is the founding prophet-patriarch of the Book of Mormon narrative as it surfaces across Kyle's gospel studies. In The Process of the Book of Mormon, Lehi's first vision opens the six-step pattern Kyle traces through the book's first 60 pages: he prays, sees a vision, sees Christ, reads from a book, praises God, desires to teach, teaches — and is then threatened for it. The study follows him through the next steps as he is commanded by revelation to get the plates and to take his journey, against the opposition of [Laman](/notes/laman), [Lemuel](/notes/lemuel), and Laban, with [Nephi](/notes/nephi) as the obedient son.

In Kyle's Alma 39–42 study, Lehi recurs as a parenting figure: Kyle cites the Book of Mormon video of Lehi inviting his family to the tree of life and telling Laman and Lemuel that they do not hearken to his words — part of his broader thread on parenting siblings without breeding comparison or competition. Across both studies Lehi is treated less as a doctrinal abstraction than as the model of a father and prophet whose obedience precedes guidance and whose children respond to him in opposite ways.

**Context:** In the Book of Mormon, Lehi is a prophet who leads his family out of Jerusalem (around 600 BC) to the promised land; he is the father of Nephi, Laman, Lemuel, and Sam.
