# Christlike Attributes

The set of character traits a disciple is charged to develop in becoming "even as I am" ([3 Nephi 27:27]). In Godly Characteristics of the Master, [Ezra Taft Benson](/notes/ezra-taft-benson) enumerates them through the Apostle Peter's progression by which one becomes "a partaker of the divine nature" (2 Peter 1:4) — each rung of the ladder a Christlike attribute the priesthood holder must emulate: [Faith](/notes/faith), Virtue, [Knowledge](/notes/knowledge), [Temperance](/notes/temperance), [Patience](/notes/patience), godliness, brotherly Kindness, and [Charity](/notes/charity) ("the pure love of Christ," the crowning virtue). Peter's promise is that if these "be in you, and abound," they make us "neither barren nor unfruitful in the knowledge of our Lord Jesus Christ" (2 Peter 1:8).

In 10 Gifts To Take Home From Your Mission, Christlike attributes appear as one of the core "gifts" — character traits developed in the field (alongside Virtue, Humility, and [Gratitude](/notes/gratitude)) that a returning missionary carries home as durable spiritual capital. The same 2 Peter 1 divine-nature ladder is the progression *Preach My Gospel* and Self-Mastery — Topical Study draw on.

## Where this appears

- Godly Characteristics of the Master — [Ezra Taft Benson](/notes/ezra-taft-benson) enumerates them via the 2 Peter 1 divine-nature ladder: faith, virtue, knowledge, temperance, patience, kindness, charity.
- 10 Gifts To Take Home From Your Mission — Christlike attributes as one of the durable "gifts" a missionary develops and carries home.
