# John Steinbeck

Steinbeck enters Kyle's notes as the originator of a single, recurring idea: the "[Temporarily Embarrassed Millionaires](/notes/temporarily-embarrassed-millionaires)" framing. In the research behind the essay What Is An Extraordinary Man - Research, under "The Hollowing Out of The Middle Class," Kyle records the attributed line — "Socialism never took root in America because the poor see themselves not as an exploited proletariat but as temporarily embarrassed millionaires" — using it to explain American workers' identification with wealth rather than class grievance. The same attribution anchors the [Temporarily Embarrassed Millionaires](/notes/temporarily-embarrassed-millionaires) concept page, where Steinbeck is named as the source of the phrase.

**Context:** John Steinbeck (1902–1968) was an American novelist and Nobel laureate known for *The Grapes of Wrath*, *Of Mice and Men*, and *East of Eden*, much of it focused on Depression-era working people. The popular "temporarily embarrassed millionaires" line is widely attributed to him, though it paraphrases rather than quotes his published work.
