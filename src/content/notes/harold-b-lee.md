# Harold B. Lee

A Latter-day Saint apostle and later church president who recurs across Kyle's Mormon-history reading as the chief architect of **Correlation** — the mid-century centralization of church programs under priesthood (and specifically apostolic) control. In [American Zion](/books/american-zion), Lee is the figure pressing [David O. McKay](/notes/david-o-mckay) to "formalize a system of correlation," against McKay's preference for "a loose governing structure based on agency rather than control"; though "Lee's vision of a completely correlated church was not to be," correlation still "enabled the church to continue its rapid growth through the 1960s." The same book records his conservative role in church politics — blocking Hugh B. Brown's effort to soften the priesthood ban, insisting "it remain in place." [Leonard Arrington and the Writing of Mormon History](/books/leonard-arrington-and-the-writing-of-mormon-history) sharpens the institutional consequence: after McKay's death, "Lee had used the Correlation movement to bring church departments under the direct supervision of the Quorum of the Twelve," so that "the Priesthood thus controlled everything—even the telling of church history," and his ascent to the First Presidency gave him the power to release Brown from that body — "the first time since the death of Brigham Young that a counselor in the First Presidency was not retained."

Lee also appears as a source of pedagogical and missionary counsel in [Chad's Big Quote Book](/notes/chad-s-big-quote-book): "May you stay with the context of the scripture and reduce it to understandable terms," and his charge to teachers — "you're to teach the old doctrines… so plainly that no one can misunderstand." A Mighty Change of Heart — Ezra Taft Benson paraphrases Lee's image that disciples "set fire in others because they are on fire."

**Context:** Harold B. Lee (1899–1973) was the 11th president of The Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-day Saints, serving briefly from 1972 until his death in 1973. He is widely credited as the driving force behind the church's Correlation program, which standardized curriculum, publications, and organizational reporting lines under priesthood leadership.

## Where this appears

- [Chad's Big Quote Book](/notes/chad-s-big-quote-book) — quoted on teaching scripture "so plainly that no one can misunderstand" and missionary work's everlasting consequences.
