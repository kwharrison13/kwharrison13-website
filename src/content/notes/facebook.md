# Facebook

Meta/Facebook recurs across Kyle's notes in three registers. **As a credential and talent source:** [Chris Cox](/notes/chris-cox), a longtime Facebook/Meta executive (Chief Product Officer), is flagged as an early [Watershed Climate](/notes/watershed-climate) investor and a credibility signal in the Series B diligence; [Matt Schulman](/notes/matt-schulman) spent a couple of years at Facebook before founding [Pave](/notes/pave); and Stephen Case was the Facebook contact behind Kyle's November 2nd, 2021 — Stephen Case (Facebook) call on Facebook's business-messaging strategy (FB Messenger / WhatsApp, the Kustomer acquisition, and where [Ada Support](/notes/ada-support) fits as an automation partner).

**As technical/competitive backdrop in diligence:** in Starburst - Diligence, Facebook is the origin of Presto — Starburst's edge being cost-optimized performance versus Facebook's internal Presto branch, which it could scale by "just adding nodes" rather than optimizing. In Identity & Access Management Diligence — 2021, Facebook is one of the consumer giants ([Google](/notes/google), [Apple](/notes/apple)) whose social-login OAuth flows define the consumer-era identity standard, and whose network-effect user bases contrast with Okta's enabler role. **As a platform exemplar:** [The Market Is The Greatest Critic](/notes/the-market-is-the-greatest-critic) lists Facebook among [Microsoft](/notes/microsoft), [Google](/notes/google), and [Apple](/notes/apple) as the diversified, bundled platforms spared in the downturn (Instagram/WhatsApp), and [Internalizing Negative Externalities](/notes/internalizing-negative-externalities) cites it as a product with large gross value but fuzzier net value once anti-socialization and addiction externalities are weighed. [The Power Law — Venture Capital & the Making of the New Future](/books/the-power-law-venture-capital-and-the-making-of-the-new-future) uses it as a portfolio case for VC founder-friendliness.

**Context:** Facebook, rebranded Meta Platforms in 2021, is the social-media and technology company founded by Mark Zuckerberg in 2004. Its products include Facebook, Instagram, WhatsApp, and Messenger, and it trades under the ticker $META.
