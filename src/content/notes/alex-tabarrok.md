# Alex Tabarrok

Economist who appears in Kyle's notes alongside [Tyler Cowen](/notes/tyler-cowen), with whom he co-blogs at Marginal Revolution. He is co-author of The End of Asymmetric Information (Cato Unbound, 2015), saved to Kyle's reading queue — the essay argues that the classic Akerlof "market for lemons" information asymmetries are being dissolved by reputation systems, sensors, and data, with sweeping implications for regulation. Tabarrok is also named among the acknowledged contributors thanked in Cowen and Daniel Gross's [Talent](/books/talent), the connection through which he was flagged as warranting his own page.

**Context:** Alex Tabarrok is a professor of economics at George Mason University, co-author (with Tyler Cowen) of the Marginal Revolution blog and the *Modern Principles of Economics* textbook, and a director at the Mercatus Center. He writes frequently on innovation policy, FDA reform, and prize/patent mechanisms.
