# Anti-Christ

A recurring theme across Kyle's 2020 Come Follow Me studies. Drawing on John's definition — one who "would assume the guise of Christ, but in reality would be opposed to Christ" — and the broader Book of Mormon sense of "anyone openly or secretly set up in opposition to Christ," the concept centers on the three Book of Mormon anti-Christs and what unites them: **all three attack the [Atonement](/notes/atonement) and [Agency](/notes/agency)**. Sherem (Jacob 7) and Korihor (Alma 30–31) both deny Christ and the possibility of knowing things to come, then **demand a sign** — making the anti-Christ the archetypal case in [Sign Seeking](/notes/sign-seeking) ("an evil and adulterous generation seeketh after a sign"). Nehor (Alma 1) preaches that all will be saved regardless, which Kyle's notes read as an anti-Christ form of [Universalism](/notes/universalism) precisely because it "comes close to the truth but perverts it" by erasing agency and the work of repentance.

The theme also anchors Kyle's [Atheism](/notes/atheism) thread: Korihor's denial of God is met with the note that atheism "cuts both ways" — "what evidence have ye that there is no God…? ye have none, save it be your word only." The deep-dive lives in the gospel-study entry Anti-Christ — Topical Study (Topical Journal 1.144–1.147, 1.176), which includes [Boyd K. Packer](/notes/boyd-k-packer)'s warning that the types of Sherem, Nehor, and Korihor live among us today.
