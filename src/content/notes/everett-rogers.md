# Everett Rogers

Author of *Diffusion of Innovations* (1962), referenced in [Remote — Office Not Required](/books/remote-office-not-required) by [Jason Fried](/notes/jason-fried) and [David Heinemeier Hansson](/notes/david-heinemeier-hansson) to frame remote work's "adoption curve." The 37signals authors invoke Rogers's model to situate distributed work as an innovation still moving through the population — early adopters ahead of a skeptical mainstream — rather than a settled norm, supporting their argument that resistance to remote work is a predictable stage of diffusion rather than evidence against it.

**Context:** Everett Rogers (1931–2004) was an American communication theorist and sociologist whose *Diffusion of Innovations* introduced the now-canonical framework of how new ideas and technologies spread through a society — and the adopter categories (innovators, early adopters, early/late majority, laggards) that go with it.
