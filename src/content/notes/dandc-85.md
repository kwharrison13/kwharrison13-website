# D&C 85

A section of the Doctrine and Covenants that Kyle's gospel-study notes cite specifically for verses 6–7, the "still, small voice." Across three study pages it serves as the proof-text for the *nature* of revelation: that the voice of the Lord is rarely heard in the ear but is "still, small, whispering." In Revelation — Topical Study it is grouped with [D&C 88](/notes/dandc-88):90, [1 Kings 19](/notes/1-kings-19):9–13, and [D&C 88](/notes/dandc-88):66 as scriptures describing what the voice of the Lord actually feels like — the takeaway being to "be modest in what you expect revelation to feel like."

In the Scripture Study — Topical Study notes the D&C 85:6 phrase ("the voice of the Lord is a 'still small voice'") is paired with [Boyd K. Packer](/notes/boyd-k-packer)'s teaching that the Spirit "does not get our attention by shouting or shaking us with a heavy hand. Rather it whispers… so gently that if we are preoccupied we may not feel it at all" — and withdraws if we don't heed it. The shared thread across all three references is that recognizing revelation requires quiet attention and effort ([D&C 9](/notes/dandc-9):7–8), not a dramatic sign.

**Context:** Doctrine and Covenants section 85 is a Latter-day Saint scripture, recorded as a letter from Joseph Smith in 1832. Its phrase "still, small voice" echoes 1 Kings 19, where Elijah hears the Lord not in wind, earthquake, or fire but in a "still small voice."

## Where this appears

- Miscellaneous Scriptures — listed as D&C 85:6–7, "Still, small voice," among scriptures on recognizing revelation
- Revelation — Topical Study — cited (85:6) alongside D&C 88:90 and 1 Kings 19 on the still, small, whispering nature of the Lord's voice
- Scripture Study — Topical Study — "the voice of the Lord is a 'still small voice'" (85:6), paired with [Boyd K. Packer](/notes/boyd-k-packer) on how the Spirit whispers rather than shouts
