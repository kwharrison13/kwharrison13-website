# Proverbs 25

A chapter from the Old Testament book of Proverbs, used in Kyle's scripture study as a cross-reference on self-mastery and self-government. In the That All Men May Come Unto Him topical study, Proverbs 25:28 is cited as "No rule over his own spirit" — the proverb that a man without self-control is "like a city that is broken down, and without walls." It appears alongside [D&C 8](/notes/dandc-8):10 and [James 2](/notes/james-2):22 in that study's treatment of how disciplined use can "change the stamp of nature."

**Context:** Proverbs is a book of Hebrew wisdom literature in the Old Testament, traditionally associated with King Solomon, collecting short maxims on prudent and righteous living. Chapters 25–29 are introduced as proverbs "which the men of Hezekiah king of Judah copied out."
