# Jim Rohn

A motivational speaker and personal-development author whom Kyle encounters mainly as a source of frequently-quoted aphorisms across books and gospel study. The most recurrent is **"You are the average of the five people you spend the most time with"** — cited from [The Expertise Economy](/books/the-expertise-economy) and reused in the Alma 53–63 Come, Follow Me week, where it underlines the lesson of the stripling warriors and the charge to surround yourself with people who raise your expectations of you.

In [Law of the Harvest](/books/law-of-the-harvest) he supplies two more lines in service of the book's argument about habit and missionary effectiveness: "Success is the natural consequence of consistently applying basic fundamentals," and "Education must precede motivation… If someone is going down the wrong road, he doesn't need motivation to speed him up. What he needs is education to turn him around." There he sits alongside Aristotle ("we are what we repeatedly do") as part of the engine behind the book's habits-over-deadlines thesis.

**Context:** Jim Rohn (1930–2009) was an American entrepreneur, author, and motivational speaker, widely regarded as a foundational figure in the modern personal-development industry and a mentor to speakers such as Tony Robbins.
