# Joshua 1

The opening chapter of the Book of Joshua, cited in Kyle's Faith — Topical Study as a scriptural example of how the power of the Lord removes fear. The note points to Joshua 1:5 — God's promise to be with Joshua and not fail or forsake him — as faith displacing fear, gathered alongside other passages (such as [Mark 4](/notes/mark-4):40 and [Mark 5](/notes/mark-5):34) that illustrate the relationship between faith, the attributes of God, and courage.

**Context:** Joshua 1 is the first chapter of the Book of Joshua in the Hebrew Bible / Old Testament. In it, the Lord commissions Joshua to lead Israel into the promised land after Moses's death, repeatedly charging him to "be strong and of a good courage."
