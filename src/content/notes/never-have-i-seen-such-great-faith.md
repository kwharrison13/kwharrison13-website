# Never have I seen such great faith

A theme Kyle collects across scripture: the moments where Christ ranks an individual's faith above all others. The phrase anchors the [Brother of Jared](/notes/brother-of-jared)'s vision in [Ether 3](/notes/ether-3):9 — "never has man come before me with such exceeding faith as thou hast; for were it not so ye could not have seen my finger" — the reason the Lord gives for rending the veil and showing Himself. In the Come Follow Me study November 9–15, 2020. Ether 1–5: "Rend That Veil of Unbelief", Kyle files this alongside the parallel New Testament and Book of Mormon moments where Christ marvels at faith: the Centurion ("I have not found so great faith, no, not in Israel," [Matthew 8](/notes/matthew-8):10 / [Luke 7](/notes/luke-7):9) and the Nephites ("So great faith have I never seen among all the Jews," [3 Nephi 19](/notes/3-nephi-19):35).

What ties the cluster together in Kyle's reading is the mechanism: the Brother of Jared's faith had become *perfect knowledge* — "he had faith no longer, for he knew, nothing doubting... having this perfect knowledge of God, he could not be kept from within the veil" ([Ether 3](/notes/ether-3):19-20), read against [Alma 32](/notes/alma-32)'s definition of faith as hope for things not seen. The recurring lesson is that great faith precedes the miracle and earns the privilege of seeing God face to face.

## Where this appears
