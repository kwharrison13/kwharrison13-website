# Dryden Brown

Co-founder of Bluebook Cities and the protagonist of the central case study on Kyle's [City Building](/notes/city-building) page — drawn from a 2020-09-23 tweet thread (@drydenwtbrown) recounting how he and [Charlie Callinan](/notes/charlie-callinan) tried to build a charter city in West Africa. In March 2019 the two were working at a NYC hedge fund, having left athletics and school to learn investing; they loved public-markets work but wanted to "do something heroic" on the frontier. Brown had followed [Paul Romer](/notes/paul-romer), [Patri Friedman](/notes/patri-friedman), and the [Seasteading](/notes/seasteading) movement since high school and was obsessed with building a new city.

They quit in April 2019, spent the summer in research mode (reading every book on cities, meeting master-planned-community developers and the Silicon Valley [new-cities](/notes/city-building) crowd), then settled on a Nigeria thesis. Funded by a golf-tournament prize and a small grant from the Charter Cities Institute, they flew to Lagos in August 2019, did relentless cold outreach, and built a thesis for a city near Callinan's university. A party introduction opened a thread into the Ghanaian government: Brown flew to Accra with their last ~$1,000, and after [Byrne Hobart](/notes/byrne-hobart) wrote Bluebook its first check off a cold DM, he ultimately landed a meeting with Vice President [Mahamudu Bawumia](/notes/mahamudu-bawumia) at Jubilee House. They raised a small seed round from [Patri Friedman](/notes/patri-friedman), [Tyler Cowen](/notes/tyler-cowen), and others to develop charter-city legislation in Ghana, before COVID border closures in February 2020 stalled the effort. The story anchors the page's "frontier > reform" thesis: bootstrapped, networked-from-zero greenfield city-building.
