# Business Incubators

A business incubator is the organizational structure that gives early-stage ventures shared infrastructure, services, and a network so they can get off the ground. In [Your Passion, Your Solution (YPYS)](/notes/your-passion-your-solution-ypys) — Kyle's 2014 business plan for a not-for-profit connecting college students to social ventures — the incubator is the core operating model. YPYS proposes a mixed **"HUB/Venture" incubator**: primarily virtual, with a small physical facility for conferences, workshops, and a few management-team operations. The model is explicitly drawn from InfoDev's *Mixed-use Incubator Handbook* (cited in Appendix F), specifically its "HUB/Venture Incubator" (mixed BAM + virtual) template.

The plan's distinguishing argument is *where* in the lifecycle the incubator engages. Most traditional incubators focus on post-launch scaling (startup through expansion); YPYS deliberately targets the earlier **ideation-through-startup gap**, where motivated students have no infrastructure at all. Incubator services are free for the first two years per incubatee, then move to tiered monthly service fees. [Mentorship](/notes/mentorship) is one of the named services — each social venture is paired with a mentor experienced in social ventures or the relevant issue, and the pilot explicitly tests whether mentorship is essential to success.

**Context:** A business incubator is a program (often a shared physical space, virtual network, or both) that supports founders with mentorship, services, funding access, and community during a company's earliest stages; "accelerators" are a related, typically cohort-based and time-boxed variant.
