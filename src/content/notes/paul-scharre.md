# Paul Scharre

In [The Kill Chain](/books/the-kill-chain), Scharre is cited as one of the leading defense and technology experts — alongside Heather Roff and Joe Chapa — who "have written extensively on the serious ethical and technical challenges involved in the military use of intelligent machines." The book invokes him while arguing that ethical debates about military technology are too often fixated on "killer robots" and platforms rather than on effective kill chains, and that the deepening U.S.–China strategic competition only exacerbates the difficulty of relying on AI that can be "brittle, opaque, unreliable, unpredictable, and prone to error."

**Context:** Paul Scharre is an American defense analyst and former Army Ranger, vice president at the Center for a New American Security, and author of *Army of None* (2018) and *Four Battlegrounds* (2023) on autonomous weapons and military AI.
