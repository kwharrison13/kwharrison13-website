# Aaron

Aaron is one of the sons of Mosiah, a missionary to the Lamanites alongside his brother [Ammon](/notes/ammon). In Kyle's Come Follow Me study of Alma 17–22, Aaron is the foil to Ammon in a study of *two approaches to teaching* — not good teacher vs. bad teacher. Where Ammon serves his way in (winning King Lamoni's trust through service), Aaron calls people to repentance immediately, sometimes alienating his hearers before they are ready. Kyle is careful to note the different outcomes owed as much to Aaron facing "a more hardened and a more stiffnecked people" as to method. Aaron later teaches Lamoni's father, who, struck down and converted, prays "O God, Aaron hath told me that there is a God... I will give away all my sins to know thee" (Alma 22:18). Kyle also flags that Aaron — not Ammon — was actually next in line for the throne, yet refused the kingdom to go preach.

**Context:** In the Book of Mormon, Aaron is a son of King Mosiah who, with his brothers, gives up the throne to serve a 14-year mission among the Lamanites; he is best known for teaching and converting the over-king (Lamoni's father).
