# 1 Timothy 2

A chapter of Paul's first epistle to Timothy, cited for two different verses across Kyle's gospel study. In Abrahamic Covenant, [1 Timothy 2](/notes/1-timothy-2):7 appears within a cluster of New Testament proof-texts ([1 Corinthians 12](/notes/1-corinthians-12), [Ephesians 4](/notes/ephesians-4), [Titus 1](/notes/titus-1), [Hebrews 5](/notes/hebrews-5), etc.) marshaled to establish the pattern of priesthood offices and the necessity of being called and ordained of God.

In Faith — Topical Study, [1 Timothy 2](/notes/1-timothy-2):4 is read as faith reaching toward knowledge — God's desire that all men "come to a knowledge of truth" — grouped with passages ([D&C 93](/notes/dandc-93):24, [Alma 32](/notes/alma-32):34, [2 Nephi 9](/notes/2-nephi-9):12–14) developing Kyle's theme that faith matures into perfect knowledge.

**Context:** 1 Timothy 2 is Paul's instruction on prayer and order in the church, declaring that God "will have all men to be saved, and to come unto the knowledge of the truth," and naming himself "a preacher, and an apostle... a teacher of the Gentiles" — the two verses Kyle draws on.

## Where this appears

- Abrahamic Covenant — verse 7 cited among proof-texts for priesthood calling and ordination
- Faith — Topical Study — verse 4 cited for faith maturing into a knowledge of truth
