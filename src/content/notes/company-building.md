# Company Building

**Company Building** is the umbrella tag Kyle uses for the craft of actually constructing an organization — team, culture, go-to-market, and the operating machinery beneath a product. It is also a Portfolio Idea: the tweets Kyle files under it are framed as operating experiments rather than commentary. Joel Gascoigne's Buffer tweet treats a four-day workweek at full pay as an explicit move to protect the org from the "burnout debt building in companies," i.e. company-building as a deliberate experiment in protecting people. [Brianne Kimmel](/notes/brianne-kimmel)'s tweet frames the hardest early-stage exercise as defining your initial [Go-To-Market](/notes/gtm) motion — drawing on her playbooks as first Head of Sales & Customer Success at Typeform and Notion. Zane Hengsperger's thread reframes Lockheed Martin's hidden network of 13,000+ suppliers (93% American, half small businesses) as the real organizational machine behind a prime contractor — company-building as the distributed industrial base, not just the headquarters.

The same tag collects Kyle's vertical-software reading. Both Bessemer pieces — Vertical Software— A Founder's Guide to Success (2017, [Brian Feinstein](/notes/brian-feinstein)) and its 2022 update Ten Lessons from a Decade of Vertical Software Investing ([Christine Deakers](/notes/christine-deakers)) — are catalogued as frameworks for building *enduring* companies: how founders choose markets wisely, why winner-take-most dynamics let vertical software outperform horizontal, and how to maintain durable growth. In Kyle's notes the source book [Alibaba — The House That Jack Ma Built](/books/alibaba-the-house-that-jack-ma-built) carries `company-building` as its sole tag, its highlights dense with team-building, hiring, and culture maxims.
