# Isaiah 51

A chapter of the Old Testament book of Isaiah. In Kyle's Abrahamic Covenant topical study, [Isaiah 51](/notes/isaiah-51):1–2 ("Look unto the rock whence ye are hewn… look unto Abraham your father") anchors the discussion of the **remnant of lineage and covenants** — the "promised seed, chosen lineage, literal seed of your body, heirs according to the flesh." It is cited as evidence that God has always preserved a righteous remnant of the chosen lineage and that inheritance under the covenant flows from father to son, an idea Kyle connects to [Remnant Theology](/notes/remnant-theology) and [Isaiah 1](/notes/isaiah-1):9.

**Context:** Isaiah 51 is a chapter of comfort to Zion, calling Israel to remember Abraham and Sarah as the rock from which they were hewn, and promising redemption and the awakening of the Lord's arm.

## Where this appears

- Abrahamic Covenant — Isaiah 51:1–2 grounds the "remnant of lineage and covenants" section (Topical Journal 1.164); used to argue God always preserves a righteous remnant of the chosen seed.
