# RFK Jr.

RFK Jr. appears in the [Bill Maher](/notes/bill-maher) notes as one example in Maher's argument about partisan tribalism — how a policy's perceived politics flips with who champions it. The case: conservatives mocked Michelle Obama's health focus until RFK Jr. rebranded it "Make America Healthy Again," at which point it became palatable on the right. He sits alongside parallel examples in the same note — liberals souring on EVs once [Elon Musk](/notes/elon-musk) turned conservative, and the American Academy of Pediatrics reversing on school reopening once [Donald Trump](/notes/donald-trump) favored it — to make the point that allegiance, not substance, often drives which side embraces an idea.

**Context:** Robert F. Kennedy Jr. is an environmental lawyer and vaccine-skeptic activist, son of Robert F. Kennedy. He ran for U.S. president in 2024 and became U.S. Secretary of Health and Human Services in 2025, where "Make America Healthy Again" (MAHA) became his signature public-health banner.
