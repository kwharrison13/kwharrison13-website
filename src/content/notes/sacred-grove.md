# Sacred Grove

The Sacred Grove is the wooded site where the [First Vision](/notes/first-vision) occurred — the spot to which the boy [Joseph Smith](/notes/joseph-smith) withdrew to pray his question, "which church is true?", and where, after being assaulted by the adversary, he saw the descending glory and the Father and the Son in "exact resemblance" of each other. In [Truman G. Madsen](/notes/truman-g-madsen)'s reading (the first lecture of his classic series), the grove is treated as the foundational theophany of the [Restoration](/notes/restoration) — and the adversary's attack there is presented as one instance of a recurring pattern in which "Beelzebub… has made his power felt" at "crucial turning points in the Restoration." It was a "natural point of attack" precisely because the devil "knew Joseph Smith" from premortal life.

Two doctrinal extensions Joseph later drew from that morning are emphasized: that to know the [Character of God](/notes/character-of-god) is the first principle of the gospel ("to see the Father is to see the Son, and vice versa"), and that even "the least Saint" may have the same glorifying certainty by the [Holy Ghost](/notes/holy-ghost).

**Context:** The Sacred Grove is a stand of trees near the Smith family farm in Palmyra, New York, traditionally identified as the location of Joseph Smith's 1820 First Vision in Latter-day Saint history.
