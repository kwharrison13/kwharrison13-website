# Martin Harris

Early supporter of [Joseph Smith](/notes/joseph-smith) and one of the Three Witnesses to the Book of Mormon, Martin Harris appears in Kyle's gospel study mainly around the lost-manuscript episode. In Publication of the Book of Mormon, Kyle reads [D&C 5](/notes/dandc-5) and [D&C 10](/notes/dandc-10) together as the revelations in which Harris is called a "wicked man" and rebuked harshly for the ultimatum he gave the Lord — pressing for permission to take the 116 manuscript pages, which were then lost — and commanded to repent before he could ever be permitted to see the plates as a witness. [D&C 5](/notes/dandc-5) (March 1829) was given through Harris himself after he sought to view the plates; it rebukes him, foreshadows the Three Witnesses arrangement, and conditions the privilege of witnessing on humility and repentance.

He is also a recurring figure in [An Insider's View of Mormon Origins](/books/an-insider-s-view-of-mormon-origins), where his 1838 and 1870 testimonies about the translation and the plates are quoted at length as primary-source evidence in the book's critical examination of Mormon origins.

**Context:** Martin Harris (1783–1875) was a prosperous farmer in Palmyra, New York, who mortgaged his farm to finance the first printing of the Book of Mormon and is named as one of its Three Witnesses. After losing the 116-page manuscript he had borrowed from Joseph Smith, he was estranged and reconciled with the church several times before dying a believing member in Utah.
