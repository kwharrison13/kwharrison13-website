# OpenSea

The largest NFT marketplace on Ethereum, referenced throughout the December 13th, 2021 — FormFn (Seed IC) diligence as the incumbent benchmark for the space. At the time of the [Index Ventures](/notes/index-ventures) seed look at [FormFn](/notes/formfn) (Dec 2021), the note described OpenSea as a four-year-old company on track for ~$10B in 2021 sales volume and ~$250M in fee revenue, taking a 2.5% fee — making it the giant any new NFT marketplace would be measured against.

The FormFn brief positioned OpenSea analogically: "FormFn is Etsy; OpenSea is Amazon; [SuperRare](/notes/superrare) and [Foundation](/books/foundation) are Sotheby's and Christie's." The argument was that OpenSea's revenue comes predominantly from large generative collections, so it is "extremely difficult and unlikely" for them to divert resources to independent mom-and-pop artists — leaving a creator-first wedge open. The note also flagged OpenSea's "data moat" as something that might not extend to lower-cost, more-open chains like [Solana](/notes/solana), and cited OpenSea as the cautionary lesson that a smooth user experience is key to drawing in NFT buyers.

**Context:** OpenSea, founded in 2017 by Devin Finzer and Alex Atallah, was the dominant Ethereum-based NFT marketplace through the 2021 NFT boom, later raising at a ~$13.3B valuation in early 2022 before NFT trading volumes collapsed.
