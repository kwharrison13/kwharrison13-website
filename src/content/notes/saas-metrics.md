# SaaS Metrics

In Kyle's notes, SaaS metrics surface chiefly as something to be *distrusted* — the subject of [How to Trick Investors and VCs](/notes/how-to-trick-investors-and-vcs), OnlyCFO's walkthrough of how SaaS efficiency and financial metrics get constructed and manipulated. The article shows how the **Burn Multiple** (Cash Burn / Net New ARR) is easy to game by varying how each component is defined, how capitalizing 90%+ of sales commissions and minimizing internal-use software capitalization flatters short-run efficiency and gross-margin optics, and why these distortions are hardest to catch before a company has a real controller and audited financials (often before ~$15M ARR). The takeaway Kyle draws out is that inflated efficiency [Metrics](/notes/metrics) distort how investors price and fund startups — a [Capital Allocation](/notes/capital-allocation) problem, not just an accounting one.

**Context:** "SaaS metrics" is the standard toolkit for evaluating subscription software businesses — ARR, net revenue retention, CAC payback, the Rule of 40, gross margin, magic number, and burn multiple. Because many are non-GAAP and definitionally loose, they are sensitive to accounting choices and reporting discretion, especially in private companies.
