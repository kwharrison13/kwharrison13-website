# Douglas Malloch

Appears once in [Chad's Big Quote Book](/notes/chad-s-big-quote-book), credited with the poem "The Best of You" ("If you can't put a pine on top of a hill / Be a scrub in the valley… / Be the best of whatever you are"). In the collection it sits among entries on character and quiet excellence — the theme that not everyone can be a captain, but each person should be the best at whatever role they fill.

**Context:** Douglas Malloch (1877–1938) was an American poet and writer, sometimes called "the Lumberman's Poet." The verse quoted here is his well-known poem commonly titled "Be the Best of Whatever You Are."
