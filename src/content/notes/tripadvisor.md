# TripAdvisor

Named in the [DigitalTown](/notes/digitaltown) pitch (June 2018 call with Faris) as one of the large platforms — alongside [Google](/notes/google) and [Amazon](/notes/amazon) — that drains digital-commerce revenue away from local communities. DigitalTown's whole thesis is keeping search and commerce local (e.g. a city running its own austin.city platform) rather than ceding that value and its monetization to national aggregators like TripAdvisor. It appears here purely as a foil for the local-commerce argument.

**Context:** TripAdvisor is a travel-information and reviews platform whose listings and bookings for hotels, restaurants, and attractions function as an aggregator layer between travelers and local businesses.

## Where this appears

- [DigitalTown](/notes/digitaltown) — listed alongside [Google](/notes/google) and [Amazon](/notes/amazon) as a platform that captures local commerce revenue, the dynamic DigitalTown positions itself against.
