# Philippians 1

A scripture cross-reference in Kyle's Sermon on the Mount study, invoked in the discussion of righteous judgment. Where [Matthew 7](/notes/matthew-7):1 ("Judge not") and [3 Nephi 14](/notes/3-nephi-14) are commonly read as a blanket prohibition, the study — following [Dallin H. Oaks](/notes/dallin-h-oaks)'s "Two Kinds of Judging" — argues the real point is to *use agency* righteously rather than to avoid judging entirely. **Philippians 1:9–10** ("that ye may approve things that are excellent") is cited as evidence for this discernment-positive reading: believers are expected to weigh and approve what is excellent, which requires the kind of intermediate, principle-guided judgment Oaks describes.

## Where this appears

- Sermon on the Mount — cited (with [Romans 14](/notes/romans-14)) to support the "use your agency" reading of [Matthew 7](/notes/matthew-7); "approve things that are excellent" as warrant for righteous discernment
