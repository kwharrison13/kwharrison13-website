# Robert Smith

Surfaces in Kyle's [Berkshire Hathaway Annual Letters](/books/berkshire-hathaway-annual-letters) notes as a reference point on working-capital and business-quality dynamics. In a margin note on which businesses have the best working-capital characteristics, Kyle connects the thread to "Robert Smith talking about software contracts as 2nd lien debt" — i.e. the idea that recurring, prepaid software contracts function like a durable, low-cost source of financing within a business. He is listed among the people threaded through the Berkshire highlights.

**Context:** Robert F. Smith is the founder, chairman, and CEO of Vista Equity Partners, a private-equity firm specializing in enterprise software, and is known for his analyses of software businesses' contractual and financial structures.

## Where this appears
