# Thoughtcrime

In Kyle's [Historical Futurism](/essays/historical-futurism) research, Thoughtcrime appears under the "Eery Reality of Fiction" heading — the strand on science fiction as cautionary tale. It surfaces via *Nineteen Eighty-Four* in the [The Openness of AI](/essays/the-openness-of-ai) deep dive, grouped with [Newspeak](/notes/newspeak) and the line "[malinformation](/notes/malinformation) = the wrong truth." The framing treats Orwell's coinage as a live lens on the present: a way of naming how language control and the policing of permitted belief recur in contemporary debates over AI, speech, and information.

**Context:** "Thoughtcrime" is a term from George Orwell's *Nineteen Eighty-Four* (1949) for the illegal act of holding unapproved beliefs — thoughts the totalitarian state forbids — alongside companion coinages like Newspeak and doublethink.
