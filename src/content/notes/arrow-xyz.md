# Arrow XYZ

**One-line definition:** *Vertical SaaS for car dealerships that can sit on top of the CRM as a "Trojan horse"; ~40% of dealerships already use something.*

## How sources describe it

- [Nina Achadjian](/notes/nina-achadjian)'s diligence questions — ~40% of dealerships already use something; the product can sit on top of the CRM as a Trojan horse; she wouldn't go above $100M post; open questions on monthly contracts and tech-team quality; reconciling why a dealership would buy a second CRM unless switching. (September 22nd, 2021 — Nina Achadjian (Index Ventures))
- By September 2022, founder [Charles Anderson](/notes/charles-anderson) framed it as building the "Amazon for [Heavy Equipment](/notes/heavy-equipment)" to compete with incumbent [Sandhills Publishing](/notes/sandhills-publishing) — a two-sided marketplace pairing dealer software (supply, built over ~2 years of CRM sales) with social-media demand generation. Drove lead cost from $75 → $3 and generated ~10K inbound leads/month (mostly Facebook); ~30% of leads from Sandhills, closing 8%. Deliberately raised less to avoid building a massive SaaS company. (September 26th, 2022 — Charles Anderson (Arrow XYZ))
