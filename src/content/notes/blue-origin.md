# Blue Origin

Blue Origin is the space company [Jeff Bezos](/notes/jeff-bezos) founded in 2000, which [Amazon Unbound](/books/amazon-unbound) frames as his "most important work." Bezos governs it by the *Gradatim Ferociter* ("step by step, ferociously") ethos laid out in a 2004 "Welcome Letter" given to new employees, with the founding mandate to "build a company that builds space vehicles" rather than just a single vehicle. Brad Stone casts it as the "tortoise" against [SpaceX](/books/elon-musk-tesla-spacex-and-the-quest-for-a-fantastic-future)'s better-funded "hare," with [Elon Musk](/notes/elon-musk) as the contrasting founder archetype — and notes the jibe SpaceX émigrés carried over: "Blue was the country club you go to after toiling at SpaceX." Its sci-fi intellectual lineage runs through [Neal Stephenson](/notes/neal-stephenson) and [Gerard K. O'Neill](/notes/gerard-k-o-neill).

Kyle uses Blue Origin as a case study in Historical Futurism - Research: Bezos reframes it "less as a hobby/business and more as long-term philanthropy," driven by a fear of civilizational [stasis](/notes/stasis-vs-progress) — "I don't want my great-grandchildren's great-grandchildren to live in a civilization of stasis" (May 2018, onstage with Mathias Döpfner). It also appears in [Building American Dynamism](/notes/building-american-dynamism), where it is cited alongside SpaceX, [Anduril Industries](/notes/anduril), and Palantir as a company entrenched in the work and mission of government (here, aerospace).

**Context:** Blue Origin is a private aerospace manufacturer founded by Jeff Bezos in 2000, headquartered in Kent, Washington. Its products include the suborbital New Shepard rocket and the orbital New Glenn launch vehicle, and it competes directly with SpaceX in commercial and government spaceflight.
