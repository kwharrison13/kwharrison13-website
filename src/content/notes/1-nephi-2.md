# 1 Nephi 2

In The Process of the Book of Mormon, Kyle maps 1 Nephi 2 to "Step 2: Revelation" in the gospel pattern he sees enacted across the book's opening chapters: [Lehi](/notes/lehi) is told to take the plates and leave Jerusalem, [Nephi](/notes/nephi) recognizes he needs to listen, and an angel appears to [Laman](/notes/laman) and [Lemuel](/notes/lemuel). It is the revelation hinge between Lehi's first vision ([1 Nephi 1](/notes/1-nephi-1)) and the opposition that follows.

The chapter also surfaces in 1 Nephi 16–22 — I Will Prepare the Way before You, where Kyle cites 1 Nephi 2:11 on "the stiffneckedness of Laman and Lemuel" — their murmuring against their "visionary" father for leading them out of Jerusalem to "perish in the wilderness" — as the founding instance of the rebellion pattern that recurs through First Nephi.

**Context:** In 1 Nephi 2, the prophet Lehi obeys a commandment to flee Jerusalem into the wilderness; his elder sons Laman and Lemuel murmur against him, while Nephi prays and has his heart softened to believe his father's words.
