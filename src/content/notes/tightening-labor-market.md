# Tightening Labor Market

A tailwind cited in the 2021 diligence on [Pave](/notes/pave). The thesis: a tightening labor market — especially the intensely competitive tech-talent market — "requires HR tools that are intelligent and personalized." Pave's wedge was real-time compensation data, and the diligence argued that the harder it is to hire and retain, the more employers need accurate, current pay benchmarking to stay competitive. The note frames this alongside Pave's data network effects: having "broken out in collecting data around the most competitive labor market (tech)," the value accrues to whoever owns the best data.

**Context:** A tightening labor market is one where unemployment is low and the supply of available workers is scarce relative to demand, which pushes up wages and intensifies competition among employers for talent. The early-2020s US tech sector was a notable example before the 2022–2023 hiring pullback.
