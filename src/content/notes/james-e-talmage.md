# James E. Talmage

James E. Talmage is one of the LDS scientist-theologians Kyle returns to across his gospel study. In [The Faith of a Scientist](/books/the-faith-of-a-scientist), [Henry Eyring](/notes/henry-eyring) names Talmage — alongside [Orson Pratt](/notes/orson-pratt) and [John A. Widtsoe](/notes/john-widtsoe) — as the writers he turns to "for just the right slant on the Gospel for scientific friends," casting Talmage as a model for reconciling rigorous science with faith.

Talmage recurs in Kyle's topical journals chiefly as a source of doctrinal quotes. In [Chad's Big Quote Book](/notes/chad-s-big-quote-book) he is quoted on agency: "Man is no less free than are the angels except as he has fettered himself with the bonds of sin and forfeited his power of will and force of soul." His *Jesus the Christ* supplies the "Come and See" passage Kyle cites in Spiritual Gifts on conversion as an individual response. A "Parable of the Two Lamps" reading appears in Revelation — Topical Study. In Christian Reformation, Zion, and Seekers he is quoted indicting the apostate church's persecutions by "fire and sword," "torture and death," as un-Christlike, and a Talmage quote from the "Little Blue Quote Book" anchors a Faith — Topical Study entry.

**Context:** James E. Talmage (1862–1933) was a British-born geologist and an apostle of The Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-day Saints. He authored the doctrinal works *Jesus the Christ* and *The Articles of Faith*, and is remembered for bridging scientific training with LDS theology.
