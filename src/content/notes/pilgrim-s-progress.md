# Pilgrim's Progress

**Why it's on the list:** *[Charlie Munger](/notes/charlie-munger) names the protagonist of [John Bunyan](/notes/john-bunyan)'s allegory among the characters whose "bequest practices" he wants to imitate before he dies — a touchstone Munger reaches for when summing up a life's accumulated wisdom.*

**Where I saw it:** Cited in [Charlie Munger — The Psychology of Human Misjudgment](/notes/charlie-munger-the-psychology-of-human-misjudgment), where Munger says he wants to imitate the bequest practices of three characters, the first being the protagonist in John Bunyan's *Pilgrim's Progress*.
