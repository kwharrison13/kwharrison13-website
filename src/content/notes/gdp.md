# GDP

In Kyle's library GDP appears far more often as a *flawed scoreboard* than as a neutral statistic. [Double Entry](/books/double-entry) supplies the origin story: there were no comprehensive measures of national income before the 1930s — GDP was devised by Simon Kuznets and applied via [John Maynard Keynes](/notes/john-maynard-keynes)'s framework in response to the Depression, and the book frames national-income accounting as itself a form of double-entry bookkeeping, while indicting GDP as "a deeply flawed measure of national wellbeing" that ignores unpriced externalities and ecological debt. [Utopia For Realists](/books/utopia-for-realists) presses the same critique hardest — quoting [Robert F. Kennedy](/notes/robert-f-kennedy) ("measures everything except that which makes life worthwhile"), noting it counts mental illness, obesity, pollution, and crime as gains, scarcely registers cheaper-and-smarter technology, and is "not a clearly defined object… To measure GDP is to seek to measure an idea." [Kevin Kelly](/notes/kevin-kelly)'s line that "productivity is for robots" rides along the same thread.

[The War on Normal People](/books/the-war-on-normal-people) reuses the RFK quote to argue for "Human Capitalism" — re-founding the economy on people rather than dollars — and [China in Ten Words](/books/china-in-ten-words) gives the cynical operational version: a China "where money is king," with growth measured by "spending 100 yuan to gain 10 in GDP." The one place Kyle treats GDP as a straightforward stake rather than a target of critique is [Rekindling US Productivity for a New Era](/notes/rekindling-us-productivity-for-a-new-era), where McKinsey quantifies the upside — regaining historical productivity growth (2.2% annually) would add **$10 trillion** to US GDP.

**Context:** Gross Domestic Product is the total monetary value of goods and services produced within a country over a period; it became the standard measure of national output after Simon Kuznets's work for the U.S. in the 1930s, and has long drawn criticism for excluding welfare, distribution, and non-market value.

## Where this appears

- [Double Entry](/books/double-entry) — GDP's 1930s origin in national accounting (Kuznets, Keynes) and its framing as a flawed wellbeing measure.
- [Utopia For Realists](/books/utopia-for-realists) — the central critique: RFK's quote, counting "bads" as gains, and GDP as "an idea," not an object.
- [The War on Normal People](/books/the-war-on-normal-people) — GDP as "the wrong scoreboard," motivating Human Capitalism.
- [China in Ten Words](/books/china-in-ten-words) — growth as "spending 100 yuan to gain 10 in GDP."
- [Rekindling US Productivity for a New Era](/notes/rekindling-us-productivity-for-a-new-era) — GDP as the stake: $10T from regained productivity growth.
