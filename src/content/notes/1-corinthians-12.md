# 1 Corinthians 12

In Kyle's gospel study, 1 Corinthians 12 is one of the three core **Spiritual Gifts** chapters — read in parallel with [Moroni 10](/notes/moroni-10) and [D&C 46](/notes/dandc-46) as the canonical lists of the gifts of the Spirit. The Spiritual Gifts study treats it as a place to "start reading… and look for the gift that is yours," pairing it with the counsel to look in your patriarchal blessing, and citing 1 Corinthians 12:4 for the "diversity of gifts, same Spirit." It anchors the study's claim — attributed to [Joseph Smith](/notes/joseph-smith) — that "a man who has none of these gifts has no faith."

The chapter also appears in two cross-reference webs. In Miscellaneous Scriptures, 1 Corinthians 12 is chained with [D&C 46](/notes/dandc-46):12–26 as a thread on revelation and spiritual gifts. In Abrahamic Covenant, it is cited (12:27–29) alongside [Ephesians 4](/notes/ephesians-4):11–13 and other passages on Church offices and the laying on of hands.

**Context:** 1 Corinthians 12 is Paul's New Testament chapter on the diversity of spiritual gifts within one body of Christ — "there are diversities of gifts, but the same Spirit" — using the metaphor that the many members form a single body, no part able to say it has no need of the others.

- Miscellaneous Scriptures — chained with [D&C 46](/notes/dandc-46) in the working web of revelation/spiritual-gifts cross-references
