# PayPal

Payments company whose early team became the canonical [Talent Vortex](/notes/talent-vortex) and seeded the [PayPal Mafia](/notes/paypal-mafia) — the network of early employees ([Peter Thiel](/notes/peter-thiel), [Max Levchin](/notes/max-levchin), [Elon Musk](/notes/elon-musk), [Reid Hoffman](/notes/reid-hoffman), [Roelof Botha](/notes/roelof-botha) and others) who went on to found or fund a large share of major tech companies. The definitive treatment in Kyle's library is [The Founders](/books/the-founders) by [Jimmy Soni](/notes/jimmy-soni), read as "a granular history of PayPal as a constellation of subsequent Silicon Valley founders." Kyle's [Elon Musk — Tesla, SpaceX, and the Quest for a Fantastic Future](/books/elon-musk-tesla-spacex-and-the-quest-for-a-fantastic-future) notes explicitly defer to it: Vance covers the SpaceX/Tesla years, while *The Founders* covers "the PayPal/X.com years that immediately precede them," where Musk "felt comfortable standing up for his positions and directing teams of coders."

PayPal alumni recur as framework-givers in Kyle's notes. [Keith Rabois](/notes/keith-rabois) (ex-PayPal and ex-[Square](/notes/square), now at [Founders Fund](/notes/founders-fund)) originated the "barrels vs. ammunition" and "scope expansion" hiring frameworks Kyle draws on. The company also surfaces in present-tense deal-flow: the February 9th, 2022 — Kevin Gao (PayPal Ventures) call is a catch-up with a contact at PayPal's venture arm.

**Context:** PayPal is an online payments company formed in 2000 from the merger of Confinity (Thiel/Levchin) and Elon Musk's X.com; after eBay acquired it in 2002, its alumni famously dispersed to found and fund companies including Tesla, SpaceX, LinkedIn, YouTube, Yelp, Palantir, and Founders Fund — the cohort dubbed the "PayPal Mafia."
