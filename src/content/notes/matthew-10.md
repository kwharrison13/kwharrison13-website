# Matthew 10

A New Testament chapter Kyle returns to across his missionary and discipleship study — Christ's instructions to the Twelve as He sends them out. Two verses recur in his notes. Matthew 10:16, "Behold, I send you forth as sheep in the midst of wolves: be ye therefore wise as serpents, and harmless as doves," anchors his thinking on declaring repentance: it's "rarely a popular business and the Savior knew that," so zeal must be tempered with wisdom that doesn't encroach on agency (Teaching Repentance, June 22–28, 2020. Alma 17–22 — I Will Make an Instrument of Thee). Matthew 10:39 — losing your life to find it — appears in Things To Know Before Your Mission under "forget yourself in the Lord's service" so that nothing distracts from the message God needs delivered.

The chapter also surfaces in his cross-reference webs: Matthew 10:22 for enduring to the end (Christ Defines His Gospel), and Matthew 10:6 in the gentiles-vs-house-of-Israel teaching thread (Miscellaneous Scriptures).

**Context:** Matthew 10 records Jesus calling and instructing the twelve apostles before sending them to preach — including the "sheep among wolves / wise as serpents, harmless as doves" charge (v. 16) and "he that loseth his life for my sake shall find it" (v. 39).
