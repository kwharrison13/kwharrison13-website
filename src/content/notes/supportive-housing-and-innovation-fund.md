# Supportive Housing and Innovation Fund

The Supportive Housing and Innovation Fund is the central financing vehicle in [Destination: Home](/notes/destination-home)'s effort to end homelessness in Santa Clara County. It was seeded by a $50 million grant from [Cisco](/notes/cisco), which [Chad Bojorquez](/notes/chad-bojorquez) — Destination: Home's operations lead — spent roughly a year securing. The fund anchors the broader program goals Bojorquez describes: about 6,000 new housing units, aligned service interventions, and a $1 billion private-sector fundraising target.

As a "supportive housing" vehicle, the fund is built to pair housing units with wraparound services rather than fund shelter alone, fitting Destination: Home's collective-impact model. It recurs across the 2018 call notes as the concrete mechanism through which the initiative's housing and service goals are financed.

**Context:** Supportive housing combines affordable housing with on-site or coordinated social services (case management, behavioral health, employment support) and is widely regarded as the leading evidence-based response to chronic homelessness. Innovation funds in this space typically blend philanthropic, corporate, and public capital to finance new units and pilot interventions.
