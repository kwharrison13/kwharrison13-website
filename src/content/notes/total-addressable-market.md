# Total Addressable Market

Total Addressable Market is the full-name form of the concept Kyle tags as [TAM](/notes/tam) across his company-building and investing notes — see that page for the synthesis. The recurring theme there is that a market's size is not fixed but a function of imagination and execution: in [The Everything Store](/books/the-everything-store), Kyle tags the [Jeff Bezos](/notes/jeff-bezos) line *"Sometimes there are small markets. And sometimes there are small minds,"* and [Datadog](/notes/datadog) is the worked example of TAM *expansion* ("TAM expanded every six months").

The related [TAM Arbitrage](/notes/tam-arbitrage) page captures the investing corollary drawn from [Coatue](/notes/coatue): doing the work to understand a market is bigger than others appreciate lets you pay up and tolerate more burn. This page exists mainly to resolve the long-form spelling of the abbreviation to the canonical [TAM](/notes/tam) concept.

**Context:** Total Addressable Market (TAM) is the standard venture/strategy metric for the total revenue opportunity available for a product or service if it captured 100% of its market — often paired with SAM (serviceable addressable market) and SOM (serviceable obtainable market).
