# Kelly Palmer

Co-author, with [David Blake](/notes/david-blake), of [The Expertise Economy](/books/the-expertise-economy). In Kyle's notes on the book, Palmer and Blake propose the [Skills Quotient](/notes/skills-quotient) (SQ = skills you have ÷ skills you need × 100) as a way to quantify how well-equipped a workforce is and to flag flight risks — anyone scoring above 90 is flagged because they will seek growth elsewhere if it isn't offered. This metric is the operational core of the book's argument for treating [human capital as something you measure](/notes/quantifying-human-capital) rather than assume.

**Context:** Kelly Palmer is a learning and workforce-development executive who has served as chief learning officer at companies including LinkedIn and Degreed. With David Blake she co-wrote *The Expertise Economy: How the Smartest Companies Use Learning to Engage, Compete, and Succeed* (2018).
