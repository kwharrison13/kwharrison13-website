# Inasmuch as ye will keep my commandments ye shall prosper in the land

One phrasing of the recurring conditional promise that Kyle's [Central Theme of the Book of Mormon](/notes/central-theme-of-the-book-of-mormon) treats as the spine of the entire Book of Mormon — what that theme calls "one of perseverant obedience." In the July 20–26, 2020. Alma 36–38: "Look to God and Live" study, Alma repeats it to each of his sons ([Helaman](/notes/helaman), [Shiblon](/notes/shiblon), and [Corianton](/notes/corianton)): "inasmuch as ye shall keep the commandments of God ye shall prosper in the land; and inasmuch as ye will not keep the commandments of God ye shall be cut off from his presence" ([Alma 36](/notes/alma-36):30, [Alma 38](/notes/alma-38):1).

That study traces the same covenant formula across the record — [2 Nephi 4](/notes/2-nephi-4):4, [Jarom 1](/notes/jarom-1):9, [Omni 1](/notes/omni-1):6, [Mosiah 1](/notes/mosiah-1):7 — the wording shifting slightly each time ("ye shall keep my commandments," "ye shall keep the commandments of God," "ye will keep my commandments") while the conditional structure holds. This particular "ye will keep my commandments" variant is the form quoted at [Jarom 1](/notes/jarom-1):9, where prospering against the Lamanites is framed as the verified word of the Lord made "unto our fathers." Kyle's note flags the whole family of phrasings (alongside its sibling variants) as worth indexing precisely because the promise threads the records of so many Book-of-Mormon writers.
