# John Bytheway

A popular LDS speaker and author whose memorable one-liners and stories recur across Kyle's gospel study. In Humility — Topical Study he supplies the meekness aphorism "if you think being meek is weak, try being meek for a week," set alongside [Dieter F. Uchtdorf](/notes/dieter-f-uchtdorf)'s "humility is not thinking less of yourself, but less ABOUT yourself." In the Alma 30–31 study, Kyle ties a mission recording (examining what was holding him back — [Anger](/notes/anger), [Pride](/notes/pride), [Envy](/notes/envy), and [lust](/notes/lust)) to "the two-wolves story" he attributes to Bytheway.

In the Helaman 13–16 and 3 Nephi 27–4 Nephi studies he appears beside [Truman G. Madsen](/notes/truman-g-madsen) as a source on how apostate Mormons caused greater early problems for the Church than non-Mormons did (a #[Gospel Topics to Explore](/notes/gospel-topics-to-explore) thread).

**Context:** John Bytheway is a well-known Latter-day Saint author and speaker, popular especially with LDS youth, known for clean humor, mnemonic phrases, and books and talks on living the gospel.
