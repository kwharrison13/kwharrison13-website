# Hebrews 11

Hebrews 11 is the New Testament's "faith chapter," and in Kyle's gospel-study notes it functions as the **definitional anchor for Faith**. The two verses he returns to most are 11:1 — "Now faith is the substance of things hoped for, the evidence of things not seen" — and 11:6 — "without faith it is impossible to please him; for he that cometh to God must believe that he is, and that he is a rewarder of them that diligently seek him." His Faith — Topical Study page opens on 11:1, pairing it with [Alma 32](/notes/alma-32):21 to draw the distinction that faith is "not a perfect knowledge but a hope for things unseen which are true." His Spiritual Gifts notes read 11:6 as the charter for being a seeker: to seek God you must first believe in Him as a rewarder.

The chapter recurs as cross-reference scaffolding across the corpus. It appears in the Helaman 7–12 Come Follow Me study and in Christ Is Risen; Faith in Him Will Move Mountains — Russell M. Nelson, where 11:6 grounds the talk's claim that faith is what moves mountains. In The Process of Zion Kyle pulls the chapter's account of Enoch (11:5, 13) to describe [Zion](/notes/zion) as a celestial city that, like Enoch's, "cannot abide" in a telestial world.

**Context:** Hebrews 11 is a chapter of the New Testament epistle to the Hebrews, often called the "roll call of faith" or "faith chapter," cataloguing Old Testament figures — Abel, Enoch, Noah, Abraham, Moses — who acted on faith in things unseen.
