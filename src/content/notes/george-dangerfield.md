# George Dangerfield

The British historian whose verdict on [John Quincy Adams](/books/john-quincy-adams)'s presidency is quoted in Kyle's book notes. Dangerfield summed up Adams as "a rather conspicuous example of a great man in the wrong place, at the wrong time, with the right motives and a tragic inability to make himself understood." The book uses the line to frame Adams as a leader whose bold, forward-looking ideas about an active national government came at a moment when most Americans were "seeking liberty rather than power" — making him a man both behind his time and ahead of it.

**Context:** George Dangerfield (1904–1986) was an Anglo-American historian best known for *The Era of Good Feelings*, a study of early-nineteenth-century American politics.
