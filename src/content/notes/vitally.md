# Vitally

Vitally appears in Kyle's notes as a **challenger in the [Customer Success](/notes/customer-success) tooling landscape**, repeatedly grouped with [Catalyst](/notes/catalyst) and [Planhat](/notes/planhat) as the modern "front-runners" newer buyers evaluate against the legacy incumbents ([Gainsight](/notes/gainsight)/[ToTango](/notes/totango)). In the Planhat diligence it is described as EMEA-leaning and Andreessen-backed ([a16z](/notes/a16z)), with strong usability and a slightly higher price point — a tool that would "directly rival Planhat in Europe if it pushes there." Concrete data points from the calls: one buyer self-selected Vitally "solely for native integrations across their entire stack"; another's price quotes ran Vitally $40K vs. Planhat $30K vs. Catalyst $20K, with Vitally seen as "slightly nicer on usability and slightly higher-end" but with reporting that "falls short of Planhat and Catalyst." It also surfaces in the November 15th, 2021 — David Bromberg (Lantern) sourcing call, where Lantern lists Gainsight, Catalyst, and Vitally as the existing CS tools its "engagement-led growth" suite would compete against.

**Context:** Vitally is a customer-success platform that aggregates product-usage and account data into health scores and workflows to help B2B SaaS teams reduce churn and drive expansion.

## Where this appears

- Planhat - Diligence — named as an a16z-backed, EMEA-leaning Planhat rival; strong usability, higher-priced ($40K quote), reporting weaker than Planhat/Catalyst.
- November 15th, 2021 — David Bromberg (Lantern) — listed (with Gainsight and Catalyst) as a CS tool Lantern's post-sale suite differentiates against.
