# Unapologetically Christian

This is a recurring theme in Kyle's 2020 [Come, Follow Me](/notes/come-follow-me) Book of Mormon study, where it pairs almost every time with #[Old Testament Christology](/notes/old-testament-christology). The throughline is that the Nephite prophets were explicitly, openly Christian *before* Christ's coming — they didn't merely anticipate a vague Messiah, they testified of Christ by name and "rejoiced in his day which is to come" ([Helaman 8](/notes/helaman-8):22). Kyle's note in the Helaman 7–12 study extends this: the Nephites kept the [Law of Moses](/notes/law-of-moses) but "didn't develop the same intense focus on 'tradition' as the Jews did in the Old World," wondering whether that's because "early on the Nephites knew about Christ and how he would eventually fulfill that law."

The Alma 30–31 study sharpens the doctrinal point with a quote (from [Joseph Smith — Rough Stone Rolling](/books/joseph-smith-rough-stone-rolling)): "There is no sharp drop after the Fall, followed by gradual spiritual enlightenment. Theologically, the ancient patriarchs were the equals of later Christians. The problem of history was to hold on to the Gospel, not to prepare for its coming." The Helaman 1–6 study grounds the theme in Helaman 5:9–12 — "no other way nor means whereby man can be saved, only through the atoning blood of Jesus Christ" — naming Christ as "the rock of our Redeemer." So "unapologetically Christian" captures Kyle's reading that the Book of Mormon presents a fully-formed, explicit Christianity throughout, not a developing or merely preparatory one.

## Where this appears

- August 17–23, 2020. Helaman 1–6: "The Rock of Our Redeemer" — Helaman 5:9–12: salvation only through "the atoning blood of Jesus Christ," building on the rock of the Redeemer.
- August 24–30, 2020. Helaman 7–12: "Remember the Lord" — Helaman 8:22: Lehi, Nephi, and "almost all of our fathers" testified explicitly of the coming Christ.
- July 6–12, 2020. Alma 30–31: "The Virtue of the Word of God" — the patriarchs as "equals of later Christians"; the Law of Moses as a type kept while looking forward to Christ.
- June 29–July 5, 2020. Alma 23–29: "They Never Did Fall Away" — same Old Testament Christology framing, paired with the theme.
