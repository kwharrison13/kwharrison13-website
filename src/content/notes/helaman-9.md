# Helaman 9

Chapter 9 of the Book of Helaman in the Book of Mormon. In Kyle's [Come, Follow Me](/notes/come-follow-me) study of [Helaman 7](/notes/helaman-7)–[Helaman 12](/notes/helaman-12), this chapter drives the lesson's question on **sign-seeking and "seeing is believing"**. When the people verify Nephi's prophecy by finding the chief judge dead, "now, when they saw they believed" ([Helaman 9](/notes/helaman-9):5) — they had not believed Nephi's words until the evidence was in front of them. Kyle reads this against [Hebrews 11](/notes/hebrews-11):1 (faith as "the evidence of things not seen") and [Alma 32](/notes/alma-32) (knowledge as perfect in that thing while "faith is dormant"), and flags it as "an interesting example of people not believing, seeing, and then believing," asking whether there are other examples of seeing pushing people to belief — pointing toward [Alma the Younger](/notes/alma-the-younger) and [Paul the Apostle](/notes/paul-the-apostle).

**Context:** Helaman 9 narrates the discovery of the murdered chief judge, the brief belief and fear of the people, and the accusation and vindication of Nephi as a true prophet.
