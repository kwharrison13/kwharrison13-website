# Roger Lowenstein

Author of [Buffett: The Biography](/books/buffett-the-biography), which Kyle read in 2018. The book traces [Warren Buffett](/notes/warren-buffett)'s career arc — from Graham-style "cigar butt" bargains to buying great businesses at fair prices — and Kyle's notes credit Lowenstein with framing Buffett's edge as "a genius of character — of patience, discipline, and rationality," and with drawing out the influence of [Benjamin Graham](/notes/ben-graham), [Charlie Munger](/notes/charlie-munger), and [Philip Fisher](/notes/phillip-fisher) on Buffett's evolution as a [Value Investing](/notes/value-investing) practitioner.

**Context:** Roger Lowenstein is an American financial journalist and author. *Buffett: The Making of an American Capitalist* (1995) is among his best-known works; he also wrote *When Genius Failed* on the collapse of Long-Term Capital Management.
