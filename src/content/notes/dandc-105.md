# D&C 105

Doctrine and Covenants section 105 anchors The Process of Zion's argument that [Zion](/notes/zion) is built by a *process*, not a snap of God's fingers. The note leans on [D&C 105](/notes/dandc-105):5 — "the laws of Zion" — paired with [D&C 101](/notes/dandc-101):46: we are "dotting the earth with [temples](/notes/temple)" so that people can "go and learn the laws of a celestial world." In this reading, temples exist as the training ground where Saints prepare to live celestial law before Zion can be realized.

The verse fits the page's larger claim that the world will *not* see God descend to make it celestial — "*we* will make it celestial" — by learning to live celestial law out of love for Christ. [D&C 105](/notes/dandc-105):5 is cited alongside [D&C 101](/notes/dandc-101):46–51 and [D&C 104](/notes/dandc-104):59 in the "we are preparing to live in Zion" study chain.

**Context:** Doctrine and Covenants 105 is the 1834 revelation given at Fishing River concerning Zion's Camp and the redemption of Zion; verse 5 states that Zion can be built up "only by the principles of the law of the celestial kingdom."

## Where this appears

- The Process of Zion — [D&C 105](/notes/dandc-105):5 ("the laws of Zion") cited as the reason temples exist: to teach people the laws of a celestial world in preparation for building Zion
