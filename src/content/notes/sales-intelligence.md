# Sales Intelligence

The category of tools that supply go-to-market teams with data on accounts and competitors — split in Kyle's notes between **intent / contact-data** vendors and **competitive-intelligence** vendors. It surfaces as a case-study analogy in the September 13th, 2022 — Nisha Swarup call about a vendor [Discovery + Diligence](/notes/discovery-diligence) product for [Procurement](/notes/procurement). Under that call's "Case Studies" heading, sales intelligence is the reference category whose vendor landscape Nisha's product would help buyers navigate, with two clusters of comps: the intent / contact-data side — [6sense](/notes/6sense), [Bombora](/notes/bombora), [ZoomInfo](/notes/zoominfo) — and the competitive-intelligence side — [Crayon](/notes/crayon), [Klue](/notes/klue), [Slintel](/notes/slintel).

The shape of the analogy is that sales intelligence already solved "help a seller understand the landscape and surface the right accounts/competitors," and a procurement discovery-and-diligence tool would do the mirror-image job for the buyer (understand a company, surface relevant vendors, assess security/compliance). [ZoomInfo](/notes/zoominfo) anchors the contact-data end as the dominant public comp; [6sense](/notes/6sense) and [Bombora](/notes/bombora) supply intent signals; [Crayon](/notes/crayon), [Klue](/notes/klue), and [Slintel](/notes/slintel) supply competitor battlecards and technographic/intent data.

**Context:** "Sales intelligence" broadly covers B2B data tools for go-to-market — contact/company databases (ZoomInfo), buyer-intent data (Bombora, 6sense), and competitive enablement (Crayon, Klue). Slintel was acquired by 6sense in 2021. (General background.)
