# Leslie Knope

Leslie Knope — the [Amy Poehler](/notes/amy-poehler) character at the center of NBC's [Parks and Recreation](/notes/parks-and-recreation) — serves in Kyle's notes as a *cultural barometer* for generational attitudes toward earnest, relentless work. In the Great Softening research she is invoked via [Rex Woodbury](/notes/rex-woodbury)'s tidbit that "Gen Z hates Leslie Knope": the shift from celebrating Knope's earnest civic overwork to recoiling from it is read as a sign that the rising generation has absorbed a lesson — possibly an instance of the "great softening" itself, or a hardening against it. The same observation feeds Toil We Must - Research.

She therefore sits at the intersection of [Ambition](/notes/ambition) (the earnest, relentless civic drive she embodies), [Work Ethic](/notes/work-ethic), and [Hard Work](/notes/hard-work) — the traits whose generational re-evaluation Kyle is tracking.

**Context:** Leslie Knope is the protagonist of *Parks and Recreation* (NBC, 2009–2015), played by Amy Poehler — a mid-level government employee in fictional Pawnee, Indiana, written as an exuberant, hyper-diligent civil servant.
