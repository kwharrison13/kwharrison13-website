# Matthew 12

A Gospel of Matthew chapter Kyle returns to for two recurring threads. The dominant one is **[Sign Seeking](/notes/sign-seeking)** — Christ's rebuke that "an evil and adulterous generation seeketh after a sign" ([Matthew 12](/notes/matthew-12):39). Kyle pairs this verse repeatedly with [Alma 30](/notes/alma-30)–[Alma 32](/notes/alma-32), where Korihor demands a sign, and with [Joseph Smith](/notes/joseph-smith)'s teaching that "whenever you see a man seeking after a sign, you may set it down that he is an adulterous man" — extended into the warning that the man who rises up to condemn the Church while calling himself righteous is on "the high road to apostasy." It surfaces across the Alma 30–31, Alma 32–35, Helaman 7–12, and 3 Nephi 20–26 study weeks, with Kyle wrestling with when people *do* deserve signs versus when seeking one is the fault.

The second thread comes from the How To Apply The Atonement topical study, which cites [Matthew 12](/notes/matthew-12):31 for the sobering point that there are things "that will NOT be covered by the [Atonement](/notes/atonement)" — the unpardonable sin — set against the otherwise infinite reach of Christ's sacrifice.
