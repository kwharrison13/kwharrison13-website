# Will Durant

Will Durant is the author of [The Greatest Minds and Ideas of All Time](/books/the-greatest-minds-and-ideas-of-all-time), the compressed intellectual history Kyle read for two specific frames. Durant frames the history of thought as "The Country of the Mind" — a realm where the heroes of the species dwell — and argues that the real history of man lives "in the lasting contributions made by geniuses to the sum of human civilization," not in prices, elections, or battles ([The Greatest Minds and Ideas of All Time](/books/the-greatest-minds-and-ideas-of-all-time)). His list of ten greatest minds runs from [Confucius](/notes/confucius) to [Charles Darwin](/notes/charles-darwin), and his prescription is to treat education as "ennobling intimacy with great men" — seven hours a week to make a self-taught scholar — rather than job training.

Across Kyle's notes Durant anchors several recurring threads. He supplies the [Educated Electorate](/notes/educated-electorate) frame (via Confucius: keep "ignorant persons from public office, and secure their wisest men to rule") and the [Progress Studies](/notes/progress-studies) frame (his refusal to define progress by one nation/religion/morality, settling on "increasing control of the environment by life"). He also surfaces in the [Stoicism](/notes/stoicism) research cluster via the shorthand "Control your mind first — Durant?", and his program of building one's own pantheon across statesmen, scientists, poets, and artists makes him a touchstone for [Polymath](/notes/polymath).

**Context:** Will Durant (1885–1981) was an American historian and philosopher best known for the eleven-volume *The Story of Civilization*, co-authored with his wife Ariel Durant, and for *The Story of Philosophy*. The Durants won the Pulitzer Prize for *Rousseau and Revolution* (1968) and were awarded the Presidential Medal of Freedom in 1977.
