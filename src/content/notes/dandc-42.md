# D&C 42

Across Kyle's [gospel-study](/notes/gospel-study) notes, **D&C 42** (the "Law of the Church") functions as the anchor scripture for **teaching by the Spirit**. The verse Kyle returns to most is **D&C 42:14** — "by the power of faith ye shall teach," paraphrased as the principle that *only teaching which comes through the Spirit can change lives, and the Spirit is available only through prayer*. In Find To Teach it frames the inner **Process** of teaching (be worthy, open your mouth, let the Spirit supply the words) against the outer **Pattern** of a teaching visit, paired with [Alma 18](/notes/alma-18)'s account of Ammon as the model in action. In How to Give a Talk the same verse becomes the decisive principle: a talk is not about the speaker, and the only teaching that changes people comes through the Spirit accessed by prayer.

Kyle's Miscellaneous Scriptures page cites the section across several verses for missionary work — D&C 42:6 (preaching two-by-two out of the Bible and the Book of Mormon), 42:11 (no preaching without ordination), and 42:12–14 (guidelines for missionaries) — making it a working hub for how and by what authority the gospel is to be taught.

**Context:** Doctrine and Covenants section 42 is a revelation Joseph Smith received in February 1831, often called "the Law of the Church"; it covers missionary work, moral commandments, consecration of property, and the care of the poor.
