# Computer Vision

Computer vision surfaces across the wiki as the underlying technology behind several tools and themes. In [Construction Tech](/notes/construction-tech) it is the engine of [Openspace.ai](/notes/openspace-ai) (construction-site photo-documentation) and its competitor [Buildots](/notes/buildots) — which [Molly Alter](/notes/molly-alter) flagged to Kyle as a more granular, operational rival building "helmet-camera digital twins of construction sites." (source: [Construction Tech](/notes/construction-tech), [Openspace.ai](/notes/openspace-ai))

The book sources tie computer vision to two larger arguments. In [Amazon Unbound](/books/amazon-unbound), Jeff Bezos circulates a New York Times article about a Google supercomputer that "pored over ten million images and taught itself to recognize cats," and a CTO recalls that "just as he got really enthusiastic about computer voice recognition, he was also really excited about computer vision" — an early bet on the trend. In [The Kill Chain](/books/the-kill-chain), computer vision is one of the software/autonomy capabilities where "the commercial sector outpaced government," illustrating the book's thesis that military procurement can't access the best of [Consumerization of the Enterprise](/notes/consumerization-of-the-enterprise). (source: [Amazon Unbound](/books/amazon-unbound), [The Kill Chain](/books/the-kill-chain))

**Context:** Computer vision is the field of AI concerned with deriving meaning from images and video — object recognition, classification, scene reconstruction. The 2012 deep-learning breakthrough (and the "neural net that learned to recognize cats" moment referenced in Amazon Unbound) made it commercially practical, powering applications from construction-site monitoring to defense autonomy.

## Where this appears

- [Openspace.ai](/notes/openspace-ai) — construction-site photo-documentation tool built on computer vision; tagged with this concept.
- [Amazon Unbound](/books/amazon-unbound) — Bezos's early enthusiasm for computer vision alongside speech recognition (the "ten million images / recognize cats" anecdote).
- [The Kill Chain](/books/the-kill-chain) — computer vision as a capability where the commercial sector outpaced government software and autonomy.
