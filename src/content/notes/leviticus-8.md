# Leviticus 8

Leviticus chapter 8 appears in Kyle's Born Again gospel-study note as a "Further Study" cross-reference — specifically [Leviticus 8](/notes/leviticus-8):7, cited for the "Clothing of the robes and aprons." It sits alongside [D&C 29](/notes/dandc-29):12 (robe of righteousness), [Alma 5](/notes/alma-5):21–22 (garments washed white and pure), and [2 Nephi 9](/notes/2-nephi-9):14 (knowledge of guilt and nakedness) as scriptural support for the study's clothing/covenant imagery — being clothed in righteousness as a figure for repentance and being made clean.

**Context:** Leviticus 8 is the Old Testament account of the consecration of Aaron and his sons to the priesthood, in which Moses washes them, clothes them in the priestly garments (coat, girdle, robe, ephod, and breastplate), and anoints them with oil.
