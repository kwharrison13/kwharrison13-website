# Alma 5

Alma 5 — Alma the Younger's sermon to the church at Zarahemla — recurs across Kyle's gospel study as a sequence of pointed examining questions, with several verse-clusters doing distinct work. **Alma 5:14** ("have ye spiritually been born of God? Have ye received his image in your countenances? Have ye experienced this mighty change in your hearts?") is his central text on the **born-again** experience: in Born Again it grounds the distinction that "baptism makes us members but not necessarily converted," and General Conference — October 2021 pairs the verse with the call to a "higher and holier life."

The chapter also supplies Kyle's material on discipleship, testimony, and apostasy. **Alma 5:37–39** — the Good Shepherd's voice that the sheep must know and follow — anchors The Good Shepherd and Missionary Work — Topical Study, where God "leads by love … not [as] a sheepherder" and "in every decision you must know who you follow" ([C.S. Lewis](/notes/c-s-lewis)). **Alma 5:45–48** records that Alma's testimony came not by his angelic visitation but "by prayer and fasting and the witness of the Holy Ghost" — used in Testimony — Topical Study and quoted at length in Fasting - September 5, 2021 ("how do ye suppose that I know of their surety?"). And **Alma 5:22** ("garments … spotted") supplies the definition of apostasy in Christian Reformation, Zion, and Seekers — covenant garments spotted with iniquity.

**Context:** Alma 5 is a chapter in the Book of Mormon containing Alma the Younger's sermon at Zarahemla, famous in LDS teaching for its searching rhetorical questions — most of all the threefold "have ye experienced this mighty change in your hearts?" — and a frequent text for self-examination and the doctrine of being spiritually born again.
