# Philip Wylie

In [Charlie Munger — Harvard School Commencement Speech](/notes/charlie-munger-harvard-school-commencement-speech), [Charlie Munger](/notes/charlie-munger) quotes Philip Wylie to characterize people who never revise their views: "You couldn't squeeze a dime between what they already know and what they will never learn." Munger deploys the line as a foil to [Charles Darwin](/notes/charles-darwin), whose working method he praises for giving priority attention to evidence tending to disconfirm his own cherished, hard-won theories — the opposite of the closed-mindedness Wylie's quip skewers. In the wiki the reference sits under the theme of [Confirmation Bias](/notes/confirmation-bias).

**Context:** Philip Wylie (1902–1971) was an American author best known for the 1942 polemic *Generation of Vipers* and for science-fiction and adventure works such as *Gladiator* and *When Worlds Collide*.
