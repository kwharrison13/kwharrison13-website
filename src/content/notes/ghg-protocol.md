# GHG Protocol

The GHG Protocol surfaces in [Index Ventures](/notes/index-ventures)' 2021 Watershed Climate diligence as the reporting standard [Watershed Climate](/notes/watershed-climate)'s carbon-accounting output is aligned to — Watershed's reporting is described as "Aligned with the GHG Protocol." It also appears on the diligence reading list (ghgprotocol.org) alongside CDP, TCFD, SASB, and GRI as the foundational frameworks for corporate carbon disclosure. In a related call, [Rini Banerjee](/notes/rini-banerjee) notes that "Greenhouse Gas Emission Protocol guides how we do things" when describing how footprint data gets plugged in and calculated. Within Kyle's notes it functions as the canonical accounting standard the whole [Carbon Accounting](/notes/carbon-accounting) category measures against — the "GAAP of climate."

**Context:** The Greenhouse Gas Protocol is the most widely used international standard for measuring and reporting corporate greenhouse-gas emissions, developed by the World Resources Institute (WRI) and the World Business Council for Sustainable Development (WBCSD); it defines the Scope 1, 2, and 3 emissions framework.
