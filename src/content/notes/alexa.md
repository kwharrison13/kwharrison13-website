# Alexa

In the [Jeff Bezos](/notes/jeff-bezos) page (drawing on [Amazon Unbound](/books/amazon-unbound)), Alexa is the recurring case study for two of Bezos's operating mechanisms. First, it shows his reorganization style: Bezos restructured Alexa into "two-pizza teams with single-threaded leaders, unified by a 'north star' strategy document" — a concrete application of [Two-Pizza Teams](/notes/two-pizza-teams). Second, it exemplifies his "stubborn on vision, flexible on details" management, in which "multiple teams [are] allowed to compete on parallel tracks toward the same goal" — Alexa's natural-language approach is cited alongside the cashierless store as an instance.

Alexa is also the headline example of Bezos's sustained, detail-level involvement: the page notes he "met with the Alexa team every other day for years," reading every Friday-night two-pizza-team update. The voice assistant thus functions less as a product in Kyle's notes than as a vehicle for documenting how Bezos institutionalized process and personally drove a major bet.

**Context:** Alexa is Amazon's cloud-based voice assistant, launched in 2014 alongside the Echo smart speaker. Its development is a central thread in Brad Stone's *Amazon Unbound*, the source behind these references.
