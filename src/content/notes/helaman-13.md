# Helaman 13

The opening chapter of Samuel the Lamanite's prophecy from the wall of Zarahemla, studied by Kyle in his August 31–September 6, 2020. Helaman 13–16: "Glad Tidings of Great Joy" Come Follow Me week. Kyle drew several threads from it. First, that prophets speak "whatsoever things should come into [their] heart" (Hel. 13:3-4) — the spirit of revelation that tells you "in your mind and in your heart." Second, the warning that the Spirit is withdrawn when neglected: "except they repent I will take away my word from them, and I will withdraw my Spirit from them" (Hel. 13:8). Third, that present-day prophets rarely speak of God's anger and the wicked being "utterly destroyed" the way Samuel did (Hel. 13:10-11).

The chapter is also Kyle's anchor for the doctrine that [Wickedness Never Was Happiness](/notes/wickedness-never-was-happiness): "ye have sought for happiness in doing iniquity, which thing is contrary to the nature of that righteousness which is in our great and Eternal Head" (Hel. 13:38). He pairs it with the chapter's rebuke of those who imagine they would have been righteous in a different age — "if our days had been in the days of our fathers of old, we would not have slain the prophets" (Hel. 13:25-26) — arriving at the idea that "it is not the time in which we live, but the nature we have crafted in our hearts" that determines our response to the word of God.

**Context:** Helaman 13 is a chapter in the Book of Mormon, part of the Latter-day Saint scriptural canon, recording the start of the Lamanite prophet Samuel's call to repentance to the Nephites at Zarahemla.
