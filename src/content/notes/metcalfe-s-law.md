# Metcalfe's Law

Metcalfe's Law — the value of a network grows by the square of its size — is the recurring lens Kyle uses to reason about [Network Effects](/notes/network-effects) across his corpus. Its sharpest statement comes in [The Founders](/books/the-founders), where Bill Harris invokes it to justify the X.com/Confinity merger: devised by Robert Metcalfe (inventor of Ethernet), the law means a network with 1,000 machines isn't 200x more valuable than one with 5 machines but 40,000x more valuable. Harris's gloss: "Volume wins. Nobody wants to be on a payment system where there's no one to pay" — so for payments "it's all about getting size," making the merger strategic offense, not just defense. The book also uses Metcalfe to argue that "true networks are a naturally monopolistic business."

The law cuts both ways in Kyle's reading. In [Oh Snap, It's Here](/notes/oh-snap-it-s-here), it explains why Snapchat is "near-irrelevant to anyone born before 1990" — the network is composed entirely of the cohort using it, so a generationally-bounded network is effectively invisible to those outside it. Through the [Network Effects](/notes/network-effects) page, the same lens recurs in [The Idea Factory](/books/the-idea-factory) (the Picturephone's failure: a minuscule network had vanishingly small per-user value) and in [The Power Law — Venture Capital & the Making of the New Future](/books/the-power-law-venture-capital-and-the-making-of-the-new-future) (applied to eBay and Yahoo, and used to puncture WeWork's "weak at best" alleged network effects).

**Context:** Metcalfe's Law, formulated by Ethernet co-inventor Robert Metcalfe, states that a communications network's value is proportional to the square of the number of connected users (n²). It is the canonical formal argument for network effects, though later analyses (e.g. by Briscoe, Odlyzko, and Tilly) argued the true scaling is closer to n·log(n).

## Where this appears

- [The Founders](/books/the-founders) — Bill Harris invokes Metcalfe's Law ("value grows by the square of the network's size," "volume wins") to justify the X.com/Confinity merger and the monopolistic nature of true networks.
- [Oh Snap, It's Here](/notes/oh-snap-it-s-here) — Metcalfe's Law as the reason a generationally-bounded network (Snapchat) is invisible to those outside the cohort.
- [Network Effects](/notes/network-effects) — the parent moat concept Metcalfe's Law formalizes across The Idea Factory, The Founders, and The Power Law.
