# Alex Osborn

Advertising executive (the "O" in BBDO) who coined the term **brainstorming** and wrote *Applied Imagination* (Charles Scribner's Sons, 1957), the book [Scott Berkun](/notes/scott-berkun) calls the "excellent" origin of the business-creativity industry — since "abused and bastardized." Osborn's actual method, recovered in [The Myths of Innovation](/books/the-myths-of-innovation), has two halves: three sequential stages of work — **fact finding, idea finding, solution finding** (see [Disney's Three Rooms](/notes/disney-s-three-rooms)) — and four idea-finding rules: produce as many ideas as possible, produce ideas as wild as possible, build on each other's ideas, and avoid passing judgment. See [Brainstorming](/notes/brainstorming).
