# Alma 31

Book of Mormon chapter Kyle reads as "the virtue of the word of God" — Alma's mission to the apostate Zoramites. The verse he returns to is 31:5: the preaching of the word "had had more powerful effect upon the minds of the people than the sword," so Alma tried "the virtue of the word of God." In his July 6–12, 2020. Alma 30–31: "The Virtue of the Word of God" study he frames the chapter around two threads: the power of the word over the sword (citing [Boyd K. Packer](/notes/boyd-k-packer)), and the Zoramites' Rameumptom — their prayer of the elect-and-damned that he reads as an echo of [John Calvin](/notes/john-calvin) and [Predestination](/notes/predestination), set against Alma's own prayer for strength and for his fellow laborers (31:26, 30, 32, 35–36).

The same 31:5 "word over the sword" verse recurs in his August 17–23, 2020. Helaman 1–6: "The Rock of Our Redeemer" notes and his General Conference- October 2021 notes. Separately, in How to Overcome Depression he cites Alma 31:31 — "comfort my soul in Christ" — as a scriptural anchor for finding relief.

**Context:** Alma chapter 31 is in the Book of Mormon (Latter-day Saint scripture). It records Alma's mission to the Zoramites, their set-prayer atop the Rameumptom tower, and Alma's prayer for his missionary companions.
