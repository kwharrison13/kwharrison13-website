# SecOps

SecOps (security operations) — the people and workflows that triage, investigate, and respond to security alerts. The term appears once in Kyle's wiki, in the Noname Security - Diligence dossier, where [Ian Buxton](/notes/ian-buxton) of [Vail Resorts](/notes/vail-resorts) uses it to describe the experience he wants from an API-security tool. Comparing the legacy EDR world to [CrowdStrike ($CRWD)](/notes/crowdstrike), he recalls that the legacy tool dumped raw indicators his team had to "piece together," whereas CrowdStrike pieced it together for them — "the SecOps guy had a nice report and a 'click here if you want us to stop X.'" The reference makes SecOps shorthand for the analyst on the receiving end of security tooling: the thing a good product should make *easier*, by delivering actionable, pre-correlated alerts rather than a pile of signals to interpret.

**Context:** "SecOps" is the standard industry shorthand for security operations — typically a SOC (security operations center) team running alert triage, threat detection, and incident response, increasingly automated through SIEM/SOAR tooling and managed detection-and-response services.
