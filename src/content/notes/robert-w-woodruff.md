# Robert W. Woodruff

Appears in Kyle's notes on [Charlie Munger — Investment Practices of Leading Charitable Foundations](/notes/charlie-munger-investment-practices-of-leading-charitable-foundations) as the source of the quote: "There is no limit to what a man can do or where he can go if he doesn't mind who gets the credit." In Kyle's reading layer the line anchors a small cluster on credit, ego, and teams — set against [Chamath Palihapitiya](/notes/chamath-palihapitiya)'s contrast that "investing is an individual sport" while company building is a team sport. (Note: distinct from [Wilford Woodruff](/notes/wilford-woodruff), the LDS Church president — Robert W. Woodruff is the Coca-Cola leader.)

**Context:** Robert W. Woodruff (1889–1985) ran The Coca-Cola Company for decades as president and later as its dominant board figure, building it into a global brand, and was a major Atlanta philanthropist. The "doesn't mind who gets the credit" maxim is widely attributed to him.
