# Moses 8

A chapter in the Book of Moses (Pearl of Great Price), covering Noah's day before the Flood. Kyle mines it heavily in his Abrahamic Covenant study for the "sons of God" / "sons of men" distinction: the "sons of God" are Noah's covenant sons (8:13; cf. [Moses 6](/notes/moses-6):68 — those who entered the covenant "became God's sons"), while the "sons of men" are the non-covenant people. Kyle reads 8:14–15 as a teaching on being *equally yoked*: a covenant daughter marrying a non-covenant husband (or vice versa) is "selling yourself," forfeiting the inheritance that flows through the patriarchal order. He also flags 8:30 to push against a narrow reading of the Flood: the destruction came "not particularly from immorality alone but VIOLENCE."

The chapter surfaces once more in Kyle's Diligence study, cross-referenced with [2 Nephi 28](/notes/2-nephi-28):21 on being "lulled away into carnal security" — the devil pacifying people into believing "all is well in Zion" (Moses 8:20–21).

## Where this appears

- Abrahamic Covenant — the "sons of God" (covenant) vs. "sons of men" (non-covenant) distinction (8:13–15), equally-yoked / inheritance themes, and the Flood as judgment on violence (8:30).
- Diligence — Moses 8:20–21 cross-referenced on being "lulled away into carnal security."
