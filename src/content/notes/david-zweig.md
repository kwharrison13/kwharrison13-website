# David Zweig

David Zweig is cited in the [Bill Maher](/notes/bill-maher) page as the author of *[An Abundance of Caution](/notes/an-abundance-of-caution)*, the source for one of Maher's examples of partisan tribalism flipping on a dime: the American Academy of Pediatrics reversing its position on school reopening once Trump came out in favor of it. The reference frames Zweig as a chronicler of how political identity, rather than evidence, drove pandemic-era institutional decisions.

**Context:** David Zweig is an American journalist and author known for his reporting on COVID-19 school-closure policy; *An Abundance of Caution* (2025) examines how U.S. schools handled the pandemic and the gap between the policy response and the underlying evidence.
