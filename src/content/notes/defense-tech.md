# Defense Tech

In Kyle's reading, "defense tech" names a single recurring investment thesis: that the future of military power belongs to autonomous, networked, attritable systems built at startup speed, rather than to a handful of exquisite legacy platforms produced by incumbent primes. [Anduril](/notes/anduril) is the lens through which this thesis recurs — a company explicitly read as reviving the founder-led [Skunk Works](/books/skunk-works) model of [Kelly Johnson](/notes/kelly-johnson), where speed of delivery is the competitive edge today's primes cannot match, and whose failure mode is drift into building "for the buck rather than the mission."

The frame pulls together several adjacent reads as one corpus. [Chip War](/books/chip-war) is read as evidence that semiconductors are the substrate of a [Civil-Military Fusion](/notes/civil-military-fusion) / defense-tech thesis; [The Kill Chain](/books/the-kill-chain) supplies the anti-platform argument (cheaper, more autonomous nodes networked into a lattice instead of exquisite carriers and bombers); [The Man From The Future](/books/the-man-from-the-future) traces the historical roots of civil-military fusion and applied research in the von Neumann / RAND lineage; and [Nuclear War — a Scenario](/books/nuclear-war-a-scenario) is tagged as adjacent within the broader frame. [Boeing](/notes/boeing) (via [Flying Blind](/books/flying-blind)) recurs as the contrast company — the anti-pattern of a once-great manufacturer hollowed out.

**Context:** "Defense tech" is the venture-capital term for startups building military hardware and software outside the traditional prime contractors (Lockheed, Boeing, Raytheon, Northrop, General Dynamics). The category gained prominence in the 2020s alongside firms like Anduril and Palantir and the "American Dynamism" investing thesis.

## Where this appears

- [Anduril](/notes/anduril) — the dominant annotation across Kyle's defense/industrial reading; framed as the lens for the entire defense-tech investment thesis (civil-military fusion, sensor fusion, treating platforms as expendable).
