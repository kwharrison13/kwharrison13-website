# Reputation

Reputation runs through Kyle's reading as a dynamic, maintained asset rather than a fixed possession. [The Righteous Mind](/books/the-righteous-mind) supplies the evolutionary floor: [Jonathan Haidt](/notes/jonathan-haidt) argues moral reasoning evolved to guard reputation, not find truth (Glaucon's wager that virtue is reputational, the "sociometer" gauging relational value), and that "the most important principle for designing an ethical society is to make sure that everyone's reputation is on the line all the time." [Working in Public](/books/working-in-public) makes it explicitly dynamic — [Nadia Eghbal](/notes/nadia-eghbal) calls reputation, "like software," something that "requires ongoing maintenance," a property of past-and-expected-future value "viewed through the eyes of others" that lets open-source maintainers raise sponsorship and that even YouTubers become "maintainers" of.

In the business sources reputation is currency. [What It Takes](/books/what-it-takes) frames [Stephen Schwarzman](/notes/stephen-schwarzman)'s edge as earning "a reputation for doing what others cannot" by tackling the hardest problems, and never doing hostile deals because they'd damage reputation and scare off decent investors. In [Driven](/books/driven), [Larry H. Miller](/notes/larry-h-miller) negotiated so every seller "felt treated fairly" and "never had to avoid the previous owner," with reputation eventually making financing "as easy as a phone call." Kyle's The Death of a Venture Fund - Research elevates this to the master frame for venture: "your reputation is your currency; brand is the proxy" — VCs are "borrowed credibility," and if your brand isn't credibility founders want to borrow, "your money's no good here." Reputation destruction (PR crises, founder mistreatment, losing market position) is listed as a recurring cause of a fund's death — though Bryan Offutt's counter-note observes that established brands tend toward "zombie persistence" rather than outright collapse.

## Where this appears

- [Working in Public](/books/working-in-public) — reputation as a dynamic, maintained property "viewed through the eyes of others," the basis for open-source sponsorship
- [What It Takes](/books/what-it-takes) — Schwarzman earning a reputation for solving problems no one else will; refusing hostile deals to protect it
- [Driven](/books/driven) — Larry H. Miller's fair-dealing reputation that eventually made financing trivial
- The Death of a Venture Fund - Research — "reputation is your currency"; VCs as borrowed credibility, and reputation destruction as a cause of fund death
