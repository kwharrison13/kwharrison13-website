# Walt Peregoy

Walt Peregoy appears in Historical Futurism - Research within the EPCOT lineage: where [Walt Disney](/notes/walt-disney) is cast as the dreamer (inspired by the World's Fair) of [EPCOT](/notes/epcot), Peregoy is named as the artist who "captured" that vision — "the drawing that inspired a generation" — connecting to [Disney Imagineering](/notes/disney-imagineering). He sits in the historical-futurism corpus as the renderer who translated Disney's planned-city idea into compelling imagery.

**Context:** Walt Peregoy (1925–2015) was an American artist and color stylist at Walt Disney Productions, known for his background and color design work on films such as *One Hundred and One Dalmatians* and *The Jungle Book*.
