# Mark 16

The final chapter of the Gospel of Mark, cited across two of Kyle's gospel-study notes. In Spiritual Gifts, Mark 16:17–18 anchors the principle that **"gifts follow belief"** — the signs that accompany those who believe. In Abrahamic Covenant, Mark 16:14–16 is read alongside [Matthew 28](/notes/matthew-28):19 and [Revelation 1](/notes/revelation-1):5–6 in a careful parsing of who the commission to baptize was addressed to: the note argues Mark clarifies Christ was speaking to the eleven apostles specifically, complicating the common Christian reading that all believers are commanded to baptize.

**Context:** Mark 16 closes the Gospel of Mark with the resurrection of Jesus, his appearance to the disciples, and the Great Commission to "go ye into all the world, and preach the gospel." Verses 17–18 list signs that "shall follow them that believe."

## Where this appears

- Spiritual Gifts — Mark 16:17–18 cited for "gifts follow belief"
- Abrahamic Covenant — Mark 16:14–16 read as addressed to the eleven apostles, in a study of the baptismal commission
