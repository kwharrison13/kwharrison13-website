# Sarah Blaffer Hrdy

Cited in [The Boy Who Was Raised as a Dog](/books/the-boy-who-was-raised-as-a-dog) for her book *Mother Nature: Maternal Instincts and How They Shape the Human Species*, which stresses the importance of the extended family — caregivers she calls "alloparents." Bruce Perry (and co-author Maia Szalavitz) quote her observation that "for children at risk of neglect, it is amazing how much difference alloparental interventions, say, from a grandparent, can make." Her alloparent concept underpins one of the book's central arguments: that the dissolution of the extended family is a driver of modern developmental stress, and that what most effectively heals child trauma is increasing the quality and number of relationships in a child's life ([Family](/notes/family)).

**Context:** Sarah Blaffer Hrdy (b. 1946) is an American anthropologist and primatologist, professor emerita at UC Davis, known for her work on evolutionary biology, maternal behavior, and cooperative breeding. *Mother Nature* (1999) and *Mothers and Others* (2009) developed her influential alloparenting theory of human social evolution.
