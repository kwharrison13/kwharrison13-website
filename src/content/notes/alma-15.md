# Alma 15

Book of Mormon chapter that Kyle reads as the conversion of [Zeezrom](/notes/zeezrom) and a key proof-text on Charity & Love. In his June 15–21, 2020. Alma 13–16: "Enter into the Rest of the Lord" study, Kyle tracks the sweep from Ammonihah's violent rejection ([Alma 14](/notes/alma-14)) into the healing of Zeezrom — the former accuser of [Alma the Younger](/notes/alma-the-younger) and [Amulek](/notes/amulek) — and the chapter's portrait of what [Amulek](/notes/amulek) gave up for the gospel: his friends and wealth (Alma 15:16, "rejected by those who were once his friends and also his father and his kindred").

The verse Kyle returns to most is Alma 15:18, where Alma "took Amulek and came over to the land of Zarahemla, and took him to his own house, and did administer unto him in his tribulations, and strengthened him in the Lord." In his Charity & Love topical study he uses this as evidence that charity is the *motive* behind the act, not the act itself: Alma pausing his ministry to nurse a friend models the pure love of Christ in practice, and the personal conversion of a fellow laborer can "rest squarely on the shoulders of their brethren." The same verse recurs in his June 29–July 5, 2020. Alma 23–29: "They Never Did Fall Away" notes as a cross-reference on Christlike service.

**Context:** Alma chapter 15 is in the Book of Mormon (Latter-day Saint scripture). It narrates the healing and baptism of Zeezrom after Alma and Amulek's imprisonment at Ammonihah, and Alma taking Amulek into his own home.
