# D&C 41

A section of the [Doctrine and Covenants](/notes/doctrine-and-covenants) that Kyle cites in his Original 12 Apostles study. **D&C 41:11** is paired with [John 1](/notes/john-1):47 in the note on the apostle **Nathanael**, of whom Christ said "here is an Israelite with no guile" — D&C 41:11 echoes that same description of a man without guile.

**Context:** Doctrine and Covenants section 41 is an 1831 revelation given through [Joseph Smith](/notes/joseph-smith). Verse 11 describes Edward Partridge — the first presiding bishop of the Church — as a man "in whom there is no guile," language that consciously parallels Christ's praise of Nathanael in the Gospel of John.

## Where this appears

- Original 12 Apostles — D&C 41:11 cited alongside [John 1](/notes/john-1):47 on Nathanael as "an Israelite with no guile"
