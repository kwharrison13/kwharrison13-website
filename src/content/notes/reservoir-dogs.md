# Reservoir Dogs

Within the wiki, *Reservoir Dogs* appears as one of the auteur-defining works of [Quentin Tarantino](/notes/quentin-tarantino), a director Kyle "voraciously consumed" growing up. The research notes pair it with *Pulp Fiction* as the films that establish Tarantino's voice — cited specifically as a debut that announced an auteur rather than as a subject of its own analysis.

**Context:** *Reservoir Dogs* (1992) is Quentin Tarantino's first feature film — a low-budget heist thriller built almost entirely around the aftermath of a botched diamond robbery, told out of chronological order with the dialogue-driven, ultraviolent style that became his signature. It launched his career and is widely treated as a landmark of 1990s American independent cinema.
