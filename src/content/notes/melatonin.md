# Melatonin

In Kyle's essays, melatonin is not a topic in its own right — it appears as an illustrative chemical inside a recurring hypothetical about the power of a shared, interconnected knowledge graph. In both [Building the Global Knowledge Graph](/essays/building-the-global-knowledge-graph) and [I Wish I Knew How To Quit Roam Research](/essays/i-wish-i-knew-how-to-quit-roam-research) he uses the same vignette: a cancer researcher in Chicago is studying a query showing every time `carboplatin` has come up in relation to `oxaliplatin` *without* references to melatonin, and through one small unlinked reference in a marine biologist's notes in Oslo and a nuclear physicist's work in Moscow, "you just cured cancer." Melatonin stands in as one of the linked-but-disconnected nodes — a concrete example of how interlinking the conceptual ancestors of human knowledge could surface non-obvious connections no single researcher would see.

The reference is therefore an example, not a claim about melatonin's pharmacology — it illustrates the [Global Knowledge Graph](/notes/global-knowledge-graph) thesis that "interconnecting the conceptual ancestors of human knowledge will unlock the maximized progeny of the human family."

**Context:** Melatonin is a hormone produced by the pineal gland that regulates the sleep–wake cycle; it is also studied for various other physiological effects, including some oncology research, which is likely why it appears in the cancer-research vignette.
