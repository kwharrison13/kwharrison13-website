# 1 Nephi 7

A chapter in [First Nephi](/books/the-book-of-mormon) that Kyle's gospel study returns to for two distinct threads. The narrative thread ([1 Nephi 7](/notes/1-nephi-7):6–7) is the rebellion in the wilderness: Laman, Lemuel, and members of Ishmael's family rebel against Nephi and Sam and become "desirous to return unto the land of Jerusalem" — a moment Kyle's 1 Nephi 16–22 — I Will Prepare the Way before You study uses to track Laman and Lemuel's recurring wish to go back rather than press forward.

The more frequently cited verse is [1 Nephi 7](/notes/1-nephi-7):12, which Kyle treats as a core proof-text on [Faith](/notes/faith): "the Lord is able to do all things according to his will, for the children of men, if it so be that they exercise faith in him." His Faith — Topical Study glosses it as a charge to never forget the Lord's ability, and [Russell M. Nelson](/notes/russell-m-nelson)'s Easter address Christ Is Risen; Faith in Him Will Move Mountains — Russell M. Nelson anchors its mountain-moving theme on the same verse — faith as the condition on which the Lord's power operates. A separate verse, [1 Nephi 7](/notes/1-nephi-7):21, appears in Miscellaneous Scriptures as one of the steps of repentance: asking for forgiveness.

**Context:** 1 Nephi 7 is the seventh chapter of First Nephi in the Book of Mormon, recounting Lehi's sons returning to Jerusalem to bring Ishmael's family into the wilderness and the rebellion that follows on the journey back.

## Where this appears

- 1 Nephi 16–22 — I Will Prepare the Way before You — cites verses 6–7 for Laman and Lemuel's rebellion and desire to return to Jerusalem
- Christ Is Risen; Faith in Him Will Move Mountains — Russell M. Nelson — anchors the mountain-moving-faith theme on verse 12
- Faith — Topical Study — verse 12 as a reminder not to forget the Lord's ability
- Miscellaneous Scriptures — verse 21 listed among the steps of repentance (asking for forgiveness)
