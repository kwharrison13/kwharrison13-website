# Self-Talk

In [Discipline Is Destiny](/books/discipline-is-destiny), Kyle tags self-talk twice as a discipline in its own right: negative self-talk isn't a fixed personality trait but a system that can be observed, interrupted, and rewritten — the adult-self version of the autonomy arguments in [The Self-Driven Child](/books/the-self-driven-child). He flags it against two passages from the book's Stoic material. The first is Cleanthes intervening with a stranger berating himself in the streets of Athens — *"Remember, you're not talking to a bad man."* The second is Seneca's measure of his own growth after a lifetime of philosophy: *"What progress have I made? I have begun to be a friend to myself."* The frame treats how you speak to yourself as a trainable habit and a marker of maturity, not a given.
