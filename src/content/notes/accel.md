# Accel

Accel is a venture-capital firm that recurs across Kyle's diligence notes as a co-investor and lead, and across his reading as the canonical example of disciplined VC strategy. In [The Power Law — Venture Capital & the Making of the New Future](/books/the-power-law-venture-capital-and-the-making-of-the-new-future), Accel is the originator of **"the prepared mind"** — rather than looking everywhere for the next big thing, the partnership ran management-consultant-style studies on promising technologies and business models, a watchword Jim Patterson borrowed from Louis Pasteur ("Chance favors only the prepared mind"). Accel's partners aimed to be "methodical explorers" rather than "wildcatters," internalizing a "90 percent rule" — knowing 90 percent of what founders would say before they opened their mouths. Kyle's notes pair this with the [Contrary Research](/essays/contrary-research) thread (preparation-as-strategy beats reactivity), and flag the book's counter-cautionary tale of Accel's missed early bet on Skype.

In the deal-flow record, Accel shows up repeatedly as an active lead and co-investor: it led the **$16M Series A** in [Sprig](/notes/sprig) (Dec 2020); co-led Deepnote's YC-stage round (with partner "Vas"), each of Index and Accel taking ~4.5%, and was looking to co-lead a later Deepnote round alongside Index; and held a Series B position in CaptivateIQ ("$46M Series B at $311M post"). Individual Accel people surface too — [Ben Fletcher](/notes/ben-fletcher) (board member co-leading a round in the Nov. 8, 2021 global deal-flow notes) and Katie Brown (in the Death of a Venture Fund outreach list).

**Context:** Accel is a global venture-capital firm founded in 1983, with offices in Palo Alto, London, and Bangalore; it is known for early investments in Facebook, Slack, Atlassian, and many other technology companies.
