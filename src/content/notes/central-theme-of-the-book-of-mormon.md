# Central Theme of the Book of Mormon

For Kyle, the central theme of the Book of Mormon is the recurring covenant promise: "Inasmuch as ye shall keep the commandments of God ye shall prosper in the land." In his study of Alma 36–38 (July 20–26, 2020. Alma 36–38: "Look to God and Live"), he frames it as "one of perseverant obedience" — an IF/THEN structure (**IF** you keep the commandments **THEN** you shall prosper) that recurs across the whole record and that he reads as itself an example of [The Spirit of Prophecy](/notes/the-spirit-of-prophecy). The theme anchors Alma's counsel to his sons [Helaman](/notes/helaman), [Shiblon](/notes/shiblon), and [Corianton](/notes/corianton), and ties to the surrounding ideas of [Repentance](/notes/repentance), [Faith](/notes/faith), [Self-Mastery](/notes/self-mastery), and the great [Chiasmus](/notes/chiasmus) of Alma 36. Kyle records several wordings of the promise as aliases of the same idea ("Inasmuch as ye will keep my commandments ye shall prosper in the land").
