# Supportive Housing

Supportive housing is the central production goal of the [Destination Home](/notes/destination-home) partnership in Santa Clara County — permanent housing paired with on-site case management and services for formerly homeless residents. In [Chad Bojorquez](/notes/chad-bojorquez)'s account, the county committed to funding 5,000 units of supportive housing, and "the primary goal is to increase the speed and decrease the cost of supportive housing development." Roughly 80% of Bojorquez's day-to-day work was building out the funding strategies for the [Supportive Housing and Innovation Fund](/notes/supportive-housing-and-innovation-fund), the vehicle (seeded by a $50M [Cisco](/notes/cisco) grant) created to accelerate this development.

The model is delivered through a backbone-and-subcontractor structure: the county's Office of Supportive Housing (created around 2015) acts as the backbone for federal and county funding, then subcontracts with non-profits like Abode to do the on-the-ground work. For every unit, each individual is matched with a case manager who handles services, while a landlord organization maintains relationships with property owners. Bojorquez also flagged a financing wrinkle — government funds can't pay for the commercial space some mixed-use developments require, creating an opening to fund the retail component privately and thereby make the supportive-housing units feasible. An earlier cost study of homelessness (2005–2012) helped build voter support for a $950M county bond for supportive housing.

**Context:** "Permanent supportive housing" is a widely-adopted U.S. approach to chronic homelessness that combines long-term subsidized housing with wraparound services (mental health, addiction, case management), often associated with the "Housing First" philosophy.
