# D&C 93

A section of the Doctrine and Covenants that anchors two recurring threads in Kyle's [gospel-study](/notes/gospel-study) notes: the **definition of truth/knowledge** and the **possibility of seeing Christ in this life**. The Knowledge — Topical Study takes verse 24 ("truth is a knowledge of things as they are, and as they were, and as they are to come") as its working definition of knowledge — the foundation on which one can "state with assured clarity, 'I KNOW that God lives'" — pairing it with [D&C 130](/notes/dandc-130):18–19 on the eternal advantage of knowledge gained here.

The Faith — Topical Study and 3 Answers To Every Prayer both cite verse 1 — that it is possible, through righteous living, to "see my face and know that I am" — as evidence that "it's not impossible for us in this day to see Christ," reduced to a short list of conditions a mortal must meet ([D&C 93](/notes/dandc-93):1 with [D&C 88](/notes/dandc-88):67–68). The same verse anchors the Eye single to the glory of God study's cluster on beholding God's face. Verse 13 — Christ "received not of the fulness at first, but continued from grace to grace, until he received a fulness" — surfaces in the General Conference — October 2021 notes as the pattern of incremental progression, the same "grace to grace" language that runs through [Intelligence](/notes/intelligence) and the Plan of Salvation — Topical Study.

## Where this appears

- Knowledge — Topical Study — v.24 as the definition of truth/knowledge; "knowledge of things as they are, and as they were, and as they are to come"
- Faith — Topical Study — v.1: through righteous living one may "see my face and know that I am" (with [D&C 88](/notes/dandc-88):67–68)
- 3 Answers To Every Prayer — v.1: it is not impossible in our day to see Christ; the conditions a mortal must meet
- General Conference — October 2021 — v.13: Christ "continued from grace to grace until he received a fulness"
- Eye single to the glory of God — v.1 in the cluster of verses on beholding God's face
