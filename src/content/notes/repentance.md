# Repentance

Kyle's sources frame repentance as a rehabilitative process, not a transaction. [The Broken Heart](/books/the-broken-heart) (Bruce C. Hafen) is the anchor: its central argument is that the [Atonement](/notes/atonement) is "rehabilitative, not merely retributive," and that repentance combined with divine assistance "permanently changes the heart." Hafen distinguishes the unconditional grace (resurrection) from the conditional part requiring repentance, and warns that "constant emphasis on the availability of forgiveness can be counterproductive" — it can wrongly suggest one can "live it up now and repent easily later." His most pastoral note is that the person most in need of understanding the Savior's mercy "is probably one who has worked himself to exhaustion in a sincere effort to repent."

The mission-era topical studies add structure. Mighty Repentance reads Enos as an expanding desire — personal remission, then family, then enemies — culminating in looking forward to "seeing the face of God with pleasure," and equates full repentance with conversion ("becoming a new creature," joy "as exquisite as my pain"). Teaching Repentance reframes the missionary charge to "say nothing but repentance unto this generation" not as hellfire preaching but as extending invitations and commitments to come unto Christ — boldness flowing from love, because "commitment is an essential part of repentance."

**Context:** In Latter-day Saint theology, repentance is one of the first principles of the gospel — a turning to God that, enabled by Christ's Atonement, involves recognition, godly sorrow, confession, restitution, and forsaking of sin.

## Where this appears

- [The Broken Heart](/books/the-broken-heart) — the Atonement as rehabilitative; the danger of cheapening forgiveness; mercy for the exhausted penitent
- Mighty Repentance — Enos's three expanding phases (personal, family, enemies); repentance as conversion
- Teaching Repentance — repentance as invitation-and-commitment, not hellfire preaching; boldness from love
