# Articles

In [The Expertise Economy](/books/the-expertise-economy), "articles" appears as one item in a list of informal learning channels — alongside [Videos](/notes/videos), [Podcast](/notes/podcast), and [Books](/notes/books) — that make up a culture of [Continuous Learning](/notes/continuous-learning). The book's argument is that learning is "part of everyday work" and "more than compliance or required training": employees can learn "in their own time and their own way" through all of these formats. It also cites AT&T's Randall Stephenson warning that people who dedicate less than "an ideal 5–10 hours a week to learning 'will obsolete themselves with the technology'" — counting articles and books among the materials people consume but "don't always categorize as learning."

## Where this appears

- [The Expertise Economy](/books/the-expertise-economy) — listed among informal learning channels (videos, articles, podcasts, books) that constitute a continuous-learning culture.
