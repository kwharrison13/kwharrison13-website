# Bill Gates

Bill Gates recurs across Kyle's reading as several distinct archetypes. As an **author**, he wrote [How To Avoid a Climate Disaster](/books/how-to-avoid-a-climate-disaster) — a climate framework Kyle reads "with a venture-investor's eye on what gets funded and why," heavily tagged to [Nuclear Energy](/notes/nuclear-energy) and tied to Gates's own ventures [TerraPower](/notes/terrapower) and [Breakthrough Energy](/notes/breakthrough-energy). As a **habits-and-focus exemplar**, [Essentialism](/books/essentialism) holds him up as the "Think Week" model — the CEO who "regularly and famously takes a week off... simply to think and read" — while [Atomic Habits](/books/atomic-habits) quotes his early career indirectly through the disciplined-systems lens.

He also appears as a **concentrated-wealth datapoint**: [The Man Who Broke Capitalism](/books/the-man-who-broke-capitalism) names Gates alongside [Jeff Bezos](/notes/jeff-bezos) and [Mark Zuckerberg](/notes/mark-zuckerberg) as the three men who in 2020 held ~1.35% of total U.S. wealth ("as much as the entire bottom half of the United States combined"), and as a failed would-be education reformer. As a **software-era reference**, [Working in Public](/books/working-in-public) cites his 1976 "Open Letter to Hobbyists" on how BASIC, once unbundled from the Altair, "wasn't worth very much at all," and [Poor Charlie's Almanack](/books/poor-charlie-s-almanack) quotes Gates praising [Charlie Munger](/notes/charlie-munger)'s gift for "capturing knowledge with simple descriptions." [The Idea Factory](/books/the-idea-factory) and [Buffett — The Biography](/books/buffett-the-biography) both invoke him as the modern "bigger than life" exemplar — the software founder whose fortune, unlike Buffett's, traces to a single product.

**Context:** Bill Gates co-founded Microsoft in 1975 with Paul Allen, built it into the dominant PC-era software company, and later became one of the world's most prominent philanthropists through the Bill & Melinda Gates Foundation. He founded TerraPower (nuclear) and Breakthrough Energy (climate) and writes the Gates Notes blog.
