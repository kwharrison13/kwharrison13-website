# Periodicals

A small figure-of-speech in Kyle's Alma 23–29 study, used to make a point about learning by synthesis. Reflecting on Mormon's editorial work as a historian in [Alma 28](/notes/alma-28), the note recalls a comment from a "Brother Merrill" in Kyle's freshman year: "Imagine BYU offered to give you a Bachelor's, Masters, and PhD degrees and you didn't have to take any classes. You just had to go down to the periodicals section in the library and summarize it into a few hundred pages." The image of the library periodicals stacks stands in for the idea that deep mastery comes from reading widely and distilling it down — much as Mormon condensed centuries of records into the Book of Mormon.
