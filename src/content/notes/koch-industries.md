# Koch Industries

Koch Industries surfaces in Kyle's notes as a reference point for capacity/capability development. In the August 2021 [Valued Empyrean](/notes/valued-empyrean) conversation with [Conor White-Sullivan](/notes/conor-white-sullivan) and [Darwin Harrison](/notes/darwin-harrison) — a wide-ranging discussion of how to build the skills and processes people need to "create Value" — Koch Industries is flagged (under "Random" notes) as an example of a thought process about capacity development, sitting alongside the Joseph Smith line "I teach them correct principles, and they govern themselves." The reference is a passing nod to Koch's management philosophy in a conversation about organizational design and self-governing skill-building, not a diligence subject.

The page also carries a June 2026 batch of links Kyle saved on Koch (CNN profile, the NYT review of Christopher Leonard's *Kochland*, and Bloomberg on Koch's tech bet) — captured for later reading rather than synthesized from a source.
