# Live the Library

"Live the Library" is one of Kyle's recurring reading-and-learning frames: **read what others have read, and learn from the people you choose to learn from** — "live the library of your librarians." It grows out of [How To Read a Book](/books/how-to-read-a-book), where Kyle attaches the note *"Reading what others have read — live the library of your librarians,"* and sits alongside companion concepts there: [Active Reading](/notes/active-reading) (demanding more of self and text), [Triangulation](/notes/triangulation) (reading several books on one subject against each other), and [Open Source Knowledge](/notes/open-source-knowledge). The idea treats your sources — the writers you read, the investors you study — as your real curriculum.

The clearest articulation is the Descartes line Kyle tags to this concept in [News in the Age of Abundance](/notes/news-in-the-age-of-abundance): *"Reading good books is like having a conversation with the finest minds of past centuries,"* paired with Perell's claim that "the people you follow online is a leading indicator for your success, your health, and your happiness." In [A Message to Every Entrepreneur](/notes/a-message-to-every-entrepreneur) the same tag carries [David Perell](/notes/david-perell)'s framing that "your professors are the writers you read" — you can and should have both personal and impersonal mentors (linked to [My Mentors](/notes/my-mentors)). [Conor White-Sullivan](/notes/conor-white-sullivan) supplies the operational version in the June 22nd, 2021 — Conor White-Sullivan (Roam Research) notes: he asks everyone he interviews "(1) what do you want to be great at? and (2) who are the three people that are better than you?"

In an investing register, Kyle applies "Live the Library" to learning through others' written records. In [On the Nature of Long-Term Holds](/notes/on-the-nature-of-long-term-holds) he tags it to the practice of going back and reading [Warren Buffett](/notes/warren-buffett)'s shareholder letters — Buffett and [Howard Marks](/notes/howard-marks) used communication "as a bridge towards trust" — and the same idea recurs in [The Storytelling of Investing](/essays/the-storytelling-of-investing) research and in Kyle's tag on [The Everything Store](/books/the-everything-store). It also attaches to [Charlie Munger](/notes/charlie-munger)'s account (in *Elementary Worldly Wisdom, Revisited*) of seeking to "learn from my betters a few practical mental tricks."
