# James Fallows

James Fallows is the co-author (with [Deborah Fallows](/notes/deborah-fallows)) of [Our Towns](/books/our-towns), the report of their multi-year project flying a single-engine prop plane town-to-town across America. The low-altitude vantage is the book's method and metaphor: from 1,500 feet you see patterns invisible at ground level but detail invisible to airline passengers. In the book, James and Deb Fallows quote [Henry Ford](/notes/henry-ford)'s "whether you think you can or think you can't, you're right" line to describe a town's willed optimism, and they invite readers to tweet about their own cities to @JamesFallows or @FallowsDeb with the tag #ThisIsMyTown — an emblem of the participatory, on-the-ground reporting that defines the project.

**Context:** James Fallows is an American journalist, longtime national correspondent for *The Atlantic*, and former chief White House speechwriter for Jimmy Carter. *Our Towns* (2018), co-written with his wife Deborah Fallows, grew out of their reporting flights across small and mid-sized American cities.
