# Paleofuture

Paleofuture appears in Kyle's Historical Futurism - Research notes as a source/site in the [Historical Futurists](/notes/historical-futurists) cluster, captured with its own tagline: "Looking back to see forward." It sits alongside [Futurology](/notes/futurology), [Forecasting](/notes/forecasting), and [paleofuture.com](/notes/paleofuture) among the references Kyle is gathering on how past generations imagined the future — the core preoccupation of his historical-futurism essay research.

**Context:** Paleofuture (paleofuture.com) is a long-running project, originally a blog by Matt Novak, that documents "the future that never was" — historical predictions, retro-futurist art, and obsolete visions of tomorrow. The name combines "paleo" (old) and "future," capturing its premise of studying yesterday's forecasts.
