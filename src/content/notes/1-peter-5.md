# 1 Peter 5

Kyle draws on two different verses of **1 Peter 5**. In 3 Answers To Every Prayer, verses 7–8 are read on the theme of opposition: "Satan can't help himself" — God uses the adversary to teach us the difference between bad and good (if Satan could restrain himself, he wouldn't have tempted men to crucify Christ or bind [Joseph Smith](/notes/joseph-smith)). In Radiating Your Example, verse 1 is used to define the title "Elder": Peter calls himself an elder and a witness of the sufferings of Christ, which Kyle ties to [Harold B. Lee](/notes/harold-b-lee)'s definition of an elder as a defender of the faith and to the two offices (missionaries and General Authorities) that bear the title.

**Context:** 1 Peter 5 is the closing chapter of Peter's first epistle, addressing the elders of the church, calling on the faithful to cast their care upon God ("casting all your care upon him; for he careth for you"), and warning that the adversary "walketh about, seeking whom he may devour."

## Where this appears

- 3 Answers To Every Prayer — cites 1 Peter 5:7–8 on opposition and how God uses Satan to teach
- Radiating Your Example — cites 1 Peter 5:1 to define the title "Elder" as a witness and defender of the faith
