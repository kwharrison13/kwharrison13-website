# Quickbooks

QuickBooks surfaces in Kyle's notes as a competitive/landscape reference, not a deep dive. In the July 10th, 2018 — Jessica Mah (InDinero) call, [Jessica Mah](/notes/jessica-mah) framed [InDinero](/notes/indinero)'s accounting-automation product as competing with [Xero](/notes/xero) and QuickBooks "but more than anything... augmenting them" — InDinero observes and augments accountants and pulls in customer data rather than trying to replace the bookkeeping ledger outright.

**Context:** QuickBooks is Intuit's small-business accounting software — the dominant SMB bookkeeping/ledger platform in the US — frequently named alongside Xero as the incumbent layer that newer fintech/accounting startups integrate with or build on top of.

## Where this appears

- July 10th, 2018 — Jessica Mah (InDinero) — named (with [Xero](/notes/xero)) as a tool InDinero competes with but mostly augments, illustrating InDinero's position relative to incumbent accounting software.
