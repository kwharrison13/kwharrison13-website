# 1 Kings 12

The chapter recording the division of Israel into two kingdoms. In Kyle's Abrahamic Covenant study it marks the split of Israel into two kingdoms in 952 B.C. (1 Kings 12) — a pivotal moment in the scattering of Israel that the study traces through successive empires down to the Roman period. Kyle also notes that Jeroboam instituted idol worship (12:26–31), tying the division to apostasy. It sits among the chapter's scripture spine alongside Genesis 48, [1 Chronicles 5](/notes/1-chronicles-5), [Isaiah 51](/notes/isaiah-51), and [Ezekiel 37](/notes/ezekiel-37).

**Context:** 1 Kings 12 is the Old Testament chapter in which, after Solomon's death, Rehoboam's harsh answer to the people's plea for relief provokes the ten northern tribes to revolt under Jeroboam, splitting the united monarchy into the northern kingdom of Israel and the southern kingdom of Judah. Jeroboam then sets up golden calves at Bethel and Dan to keep his people from worshiping in Jerusalem.
