# Joshua 6

The chapter of the Book of Joshua recounting Israel's victory at Jericho, cited in Kyle's Born Again study. The note quotes Joshua 6:27 — after the conquest, Joshua's "fame was noised throughout all the country" — to mark Israel becoming a force to be reckoned with under his leadership. In the study this victory sets up the contrasting failure that follows at Ai ([Joshua 7](/notes/joshua-7)), where Achan's hidden transgression of the covenant brings defeat, illustrating the study's theme that "our sins are of greater damage because of our covenants."

**Context:** Joshua 6 narrates the fall of Jericho, in which the Israelites march around the city for seven days and its walls collapse, giving them their first major victory in the conquest of Canaan.

## Where this appears

- Born Again — Joshua 6:27 cited for Israel's rising power under Joshua, preceding the covenant-failure lesson of Joshua 7
