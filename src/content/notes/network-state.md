# Network State

In the wiki's [City Building](/notes/city-building) page, the network state is the [Balaji Srinivasan](/notes/balaji-srinivasan)-style "exit" — the idea of building a new political community greenfield rather than reforming a legacy one. It sits alongside charter cities and seasteads in the lineage of founding-from-scratch governance, and the page frames it as one expression of the broader thesis: as the opportunity cost of living in legacy hubs like SF and NYC falls (lost community, poor city management, broken infrastructure, the rise of the cloud labor market), the timing improves for building governance and infrastructure on the frontier.

The City Building note also records a cautionary read on timing: founders who had followed [Paul Romer](/notes/paul-romer), [Patri Friedman](/notes/patri-friedman), and the [Seasteading](/notes/seasteading) movement judged the market for a Srinivasan-style "exit" still too small, and so chose to learn the existing new-city industry first rather than attempt a network-state from a standing start.

**Context:** "The Network State" is a 2022 book by Balaji Srinivasan arguing that a new country can be founded by first building an online community with a shared moral cause, then crowdfunding physical territory, then seeking diplomatic recognition — "cloud first, land last."
