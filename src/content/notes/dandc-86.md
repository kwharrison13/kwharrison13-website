# D&C 86

A section of the Doctrine and Covenants that Kyle cites in his Christian Reformation, Zion, and Seekers study, grouped with D&C 5:14, D&C 33:4–5, and D&C 109:73 under the theme of restoration. The page reads D&C 86:3 as supporting the idea of restoring Christianity "to a healthy condition" and restoring truth — contrasting [Joseph Smith](/notes/joseph-smith)'s vision of restoring the fullness of the gospel against the "primitivist" reformers who stopped at what God had already revealed.

**Context:** Doctrine and Covenants Section 86 is an 1832 revelation given through Joseph Smith, expounding the parable of the wheat and the tares. Verse 3 describes how, after the apostles "fell asleep," the great persecutor and apostate church corrupted the truth — language Joseph used to frame the apostasy and the need for restoration.
