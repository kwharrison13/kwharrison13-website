# Communication

In Kyle's Interviewing topical study (drawn from Topical Journal 1.156), communication is broken into **verbal** and **non-verbal** channels, with the non-verbal often more revealing than the words themselves. Verbal communication covers statements, questions, and intonations. Non-verbal communication is read as positive (open posture, leaning in, eye contact) or negative (closed posture, arms crossed, no eye contact).

The study extends communication into a taxonomy of **questioning** — restrictive/closed questions ("to be / to do / to have," yielding yes/no), non-restrictive/open questions ("tell me," "show me," "explain"), and probing questions ("give me an example," "your best guess") — and into **listening** as a "high-energy experience" that attends to what is said *and* left unsaid, where revelation and discernment come once internal and external distractions are eliminated. In context, communication is the foundational skill underneath conducting a baptismal interview, and it sits alongside [Leadership](/notes/leadership) and [Baptism](/notes/baptism) as a core theme of that study.
