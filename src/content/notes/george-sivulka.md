# George Sivulka

George Sivulka is the founder of [Hebbia](/notes/hebbia), whom Kyle met for a product deep-dive sourcing call on November 8th, 2021 (November 8th, 2021 — George Sivulka (Hebbia)). In that conversation he framed Hebbia around [Neural Search](/notes/neural-search) over unstructured documents — letting a user ask "what are the risks to the business?" of a pile of SEC filings, data rooms, or legal contracts and get an answer, rather than keyword-matching. He described early traction among data analysts and people synthesizing large document sets (1M LP agreements to work out a waterfall; PE firms, debt providers, and insurers spending 3+ weeks in data rooms). Sivulka noted the system already generalized across use cases without retraining — friends spinning up medical-school textbooks, others reviewing legal agreements — a generality he tied to his PhD work on making neural search general; the near-term plan was to make it *less* general and productize it (potentially modeling [Elastic](/notes/elastic) and selling an API).

**Context:** George Sivulka is the founder and CEO of Hebbia, an AI document-search and analysis company; he left a Stanford PhD program to build it. Hebbia later became known for enterprise AI tools used heavily by financial-services firms.
