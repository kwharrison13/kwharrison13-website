# Luke 21

Luke 21 appears in Kyle's [Come, Follow Me](/notes/come-follow-me) study on Helaman 7–12, cited as Luke 21:26 — "men's hearts failing them for fear, and for looking after those things which are coming on the earth: for the powers of heaven shall be shaken." In the note it sits among the chapter's cross-references on the last days and remembering the Lord, alongside passages such as [Matthew 25](/notes/matthew-25) and [3 Nephi 28](/notes/3-nephi-28).

**Context:** Luke 21 is the New Testament chapter recording Jesus' Olivet discourse in Luke's account — his prophecy of the destruction of Jerusalem and the signs preceding his Second Coming; verse 26 describes "men's hearts failing them for fear" as the powers of heaven are shaken.
