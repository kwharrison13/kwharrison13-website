# George Romney

George Romney is the father of [Mitt Romney](/notes/mitt-romney) and the moral lodestar of [Romney — A Reckoning](/books/romney-a-reckoning). The book frames his 1968 presidential campaign as "noble, idealistic, maybe a little naive" — and felled by "his most admirable and self-destructive quality: a stubborn insistence on telling the truth." His memory functions as both heroic parable and cautionary tale for Mitt: when George died, the ~1,200 who gathered for his funeral in Michigan "had not turned out to mourn a savvy businessman who excelled at outperforming quarterly profit expectations — they were mourning a public servant," a contrast that pushed Mitt to question whether dealmaking and turnarounds were enough for his own life. In [Becoming](/books/becoming), Michelle Obama's collection of quotes attributes the line **"If not us, who? If not now, when?"** to George Romney.

**Context:** George W. Romney (1907–1995) was CEO of American Motors, a three-term Republican governor of Michigan, a 1968 presidential candidate, and U.S. Secretary of Housing and Urban Development under Nixon. He was known for his civil-rights advocacy and his blunt independence — including a famous break with the party's conservative wing.
