# Tiger Global Management

The investment firm led by Chase Coleman, cited in Kyle's [Investment Frameworks](/notes/investment-frameworks) learning page for its approach to managing an investment portfolio. In [The Power Law — Venture Capital & the Making of the New Future](/books/the-power-law-venture-capital-and-the-making-of-the-new-future), [Mallaby](/notes/sebastian-mallaby) holds up Tiger Global as one of the rare cases where a new venture firm's success rests on a genuinely novel *approach* rather than the founders' pre-existing network — it "improvised the hedge fund/venture hybrid model," landing it alongside Yuri Milner and Y Combinator as an exception to the rule that VC success owes most to connections and status.

**Context:** Tiger Global Management is a New York investment firm founded by Chase Coleman in 2001, one of the original "Tiger Cubs" to spin out of Julian Robertson's Tiger Management. It runs both public-equity hedge funds and a large venture/growth-equity practice known for aggressive, fast-moving private-tech investing.
