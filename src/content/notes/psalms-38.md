# Psalms 38

A psalm cited in Kyle's Miscellaneous Scriptures gospel-study notes as a scriptural anchor for the first step of repentance. In a six-step repentance outline, Psalms 38:18 is the proof text for **godly sorrow** ("Godly sorrow ([Psalms 38](/notes/psalms-38):18)"), the opening step before confessing to God, asking forgiveness, rectifying problems, forsaking sin, and receiving forgiveness.

**Context:** Psalm 38 is a penitential psalm attributed to David, a lament confessing sin and the suffering it brings. Verse 18 reads, "For I will declare mine iniquity; I will be sorry for my sin" — a classic scriptural statement of contrition.
