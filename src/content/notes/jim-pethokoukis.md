# Jim Pethokoukis

Writer and commentator on technological progress and optimism who surfaces in Kyle's historical futurism research as the conduit for an [Arthur C. Clarke](/notes/arthur-c-clarke) artifact. The notes credit Pethokoukis with surfacing Clarke's 21st-century timeline (written in the 1960s) — a forecast that "optimistically projected a rapid path from the lunar landing to colonizing planets by 2000, fusion power in the 80s/90s." The Clarke timeline is one of the research's case studies in how mid-century futurists over-projected the pace of technological change.

**Context:** James Pethokoukis is an American economic-policy and technology writer — an AEI fellow and author of the "Faster, Please!" newsletter and the book *The Conservative Futurist* — known for advocating a more optimistic, growth- and progress-oriented vision of the future.
