# Jay Goldberg

**One-line:** *Semiconductor industry analyst interviewed by Ben Thompson on Stratechery about chips, fabs, and the integrate-vs-modularize cycle.*

## What we know

- Self-described "finance person" who argues a chip company like [Intel](/notes/intel) should be run by engineers, not finance leaders (source: [Apple Earnings, An Interview with Jay Goldberg About Chips and Intel](/notes/apple-earnings-an-interview-with-jay-goldberg-about-chips-and-intel)).
- Frames the [fabless](/notes/fabless) revolution around the ~$15B leading-edge fab cost that forced specialization between design and manufacturing.
- Reads [Apple](/notes/apple) as "going backwards" — re-integrating vertically down to the chip — against the industry's modularization trend, via the [Clayton M. Christensen](/notes/clayton-christensen) integrate-then-modularize model.
