# Decentralized Community

A model of community organization — distributed rather than centrally controlled — that recurs in Kyle's notes around [Roam Research](/notes/roam-research). In [Conor White-Sullivan](/notes/conor-white-sullivan)'s June 22nd, 2021 call, Conor describes the design challenge of combining "extreme power" that is "both centralized and decentralized," tagged #[Decentralized Community](/notes/decentralized-community), alongside a layered growth model: company → company + community → community leaders → bringing in their communities. The vision is an org whose latent knowledge lives in a pro-social network of contributors that the company can tap into.

In [The Death of Expertise](/books/the-death-of-expertise), Kyle attaches the same concept to [David Vargas](/notes/david-vargas)'s "decentralized community with [Roam Research](/notes/roam-research)" discussion, surfacing it via [Malcolm Gladwell](/notes/malcolm-gladwell)'s "networks can't set goals" highlight and connecting it to his own [Decentralized Brain](/notes/decentralized-brain) thread. The tension Kyle is tracking: decentralized communities are powerful reservoirs of distributed knowledge, but (per Gladwell) networks struggle to set and pursue shared goals — so the question is how to harness that latent capacity without losing direction.
