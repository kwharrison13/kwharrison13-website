# Mosiah 2

Mosiah 2 — King Benjamin's address — is the scripture chapter Kyle's study notes lean on for the linked ideas of **service, humility, and obedience**. The most-cited verse is [Mosiah 2](/notes/mosiah-2):17, "when ye are in the service of your fellow beings ye are only in the service of your God," which appears in the Alma 17–22 study week as a cross-reference on becoming an instrument. The Obedience — Topical Study builds on Mosiah 2:21–22 — the "unprofitable servants" passage — to argue that obedience "is not a price paid but the appreciation we show a magnanimous God," the means by which God asks us to become like Him. The Humility — Topical Study cites Mosiah 2:34 ("render all that you have and are") as a model of self-surrender.

The chapter also surfaces in Kyle's notes on staying on the right path: in Everyone Is Human, Mosiah 2:36 grounds the idea that "the SPIRIT is with you" when you're walking rightly, and in the Christ Is Risen; Faith in Him Will Move Mountains — Russell M. Nelson study, Mosiah 2:41 anchors "the blessed and happy state of those that keep the commandments of God."

**Context:** Mosiah 2 is a chapter in the Book of Mormon recording King Benjamin's farewell sermon to the Nephites from a tower by the temple — one of the most quoted discourses in Latter-day Saint teaching on service, humility before God, and obedience.
