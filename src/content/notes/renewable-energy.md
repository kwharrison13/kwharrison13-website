# Renewable Energy

Renewable energy surfaces in Kyle's notes as both an investing theme and a tell about how the best operators talk. On the company side it runs through [Jon Powers](/notes/jon-powers) — President Obama's former Chief Sustainability Officer and co-founder of [CleanCapital](/notes/cleancapital) — who applies third-party finance to clean-energy (largely solar) assets and frames his work as "energy as a service," extending beyond solar into broader energy efficiency. On the framing side, June 6th, 2022 — Katherine Boyle (a16z) captures a sharp observation: "the OGs in ClimateTech don't use the word 'climate.' They talk about energy or the actual technology they're building (e.g. [Carbon Capture](/notes/carbon-capture) or [Renewable Energy](/notes/renewable-energy))" — so the vocabulary itself lets you "pick up on a 'true believer.'"

The concept also has a long Quick Capture tail from Kyle's June 29th, 2021 reading log — saved links on 100% clean-energy policy support, next-generation ("retro") nuclear, and clean-energy operators — sources he flagged to explore but has not yet synthesized into page-level claims.

**Context:** "Renewable energy" refers to power from naturally replenished sources — solar, wind, hydro, geothermal — and the financing, deployment, and policy infrastructure around them. The "energy as a service" and third-party-finance models Powers describes let asset owners adopt clean energy without large upfront capital outlays.
