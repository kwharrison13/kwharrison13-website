# Altos Ventures

Altos Ventures is the firm co-founded and run by [Ho Nam](/notes/ho-nam), who recurs in Kyle's notes as a model of a particular venture philosophy. In [Crafting Your Average](/essays/crafting-your-average), Kyle recounts a "borderline spiritual" thirty-minute chat with Ho, describing Altos as a "very bottom-up focused investor, in contrast to others who may be more top-down thesis driven" — the work being to find exceptional people and become participants with them as they discover Doug Leone's "black magic." Kyle lists Altos investments including Roblox, Coupang, Krafton, Dunamu, and Toss.

Altos also appears in [Markets, Markets, and Markets](/essays/markets-markets-and-markets), where Ho Nam (at Altos) is named among the investors who pushed back on Kyle's "TAM arbitrage" concept — part of a comment thread that, Kyle notes, kept returning to the firms' shared focus on the founder over the market. Ho's broader "what is an investor? Not a VC" reframing — venture as value-creation alongside founders rather than capital allocation for LP returns — is the through-line connecting Altos to Kyle's thinking on what venture actually is.

**Context:** Altos Ventures is a Menlo Park venture firm known for a long-term, capital-efficient, founder-aligned style, with notable early bets on Roblox, Coupang, and a strong Korean-startup portfolio. This is general background.
