# Václav Havel

Václav Havel (1936–2011) was a Czech dissident playwright who became the first president of post-communist Czechoslovakia after the 1989 Velvet Revolution. In [Speeches That Changed The World](/books/speeches-that-changed-the-world) Kyle's highlight is from Havel's 1990 New Year's address, where he names the lasting wound of the totalitarian years as moral rather than material — "a contaminated moral environment," the habit of "saying something different from what we thought." It reads the [Totalitarianism](/notes/totalitarianism) entry in the anthology as a story about conscience and complicity.

## Where this appears

- [Speeches That Changed The World](/books/speeches-that-changed-the-world) — "We live in a contaminated moral environment… we became used to saying something different from what we thought."
