# Anthony Trollope

Trollope surfaces in Kyle's reading layer on [Charlie Munger](/notes/charlie-munger)'s talk [Charlie Munger — A Lesson on Elementary Worldly Wisdom](/notes/charlie-munger-a-lesson-on-elementary-worldly-wisdom) as the source of a single quoted line: "There is no royal road to learning; no short cut to the acquirement of any art." The quote sits in the talk's argument that a broad general education and patient, models-based study precede any real expertise — Munger's framing that you need general education "before you're going to be a great stock picker." Trollope is invoked purely as an aphorist on the cost of mastery; the source says nothing else about him.

**Context:** Anthony Trollope (1815–1882) was an English novelist of the Victorian era, best known for the Chronicles of Barsetshire and Palliser series. He was famously disciplined, writing a fixed word quota each morning before his day job at the Post Office — a work ethic that fits the "no short cut" line attributed to him.
