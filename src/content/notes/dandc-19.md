# D&C 19

In Kyle's gospel-study notes, Doctrine and Covenants section 19 supplies two distinct doctrines. The first, drawn from D&C 19:22, anchors the **"milk before meat" mercy of limited light**: in Eye single to the glory of God, "God gives us the light we need… but in his greatest act of mercy, he allows us only as much light and knowledge as we can handle — 'they cannot bear meat now, but milk they must receive… lest they perish.'" This frames the veil as "a medium of mercy" and our spiritual infancy as parental protection.

The second, from D&C 19:6–7, 11–12, reframes **"eternal" and "endless" punishment** in the Plan of Salvation — Topical Study: "Hotel Hell is a place prepared for the teaching and disciplining that people should have learned. We receive eternal punishments from God because He is eternal. But it will not last forever — only until we learn what is necessary." Miscellaneous Scriptures adds D&C 19:17 alongside [Moroni 10](/notes/moroni-10):33 on being "perfected in Christ" and casting off all ungodliness. Together the section appears in Kyle's notes as the scripture that softens the doctrine of punishment (eternal in source, not endless in duration) and that justifies God's measured revelation of truth.

**Context:** Doctrine and Covenants 19 is a revelation to [Joseph Smith](/notes/joseph-smith) (c. 1830) directed to Martin Harris; it is best known for redefining "eternal punishment" and "endless punishment" as God's punishment (because "Eternal" and "Endless" are His names) rather than punishment without end, and for verse 22's counsel that people receive truth "milk" before "meat."

## Where this appears

- Eye single to the glory of God — D&C 19:22 on receiving only as much light as we can bear ("milk before meat"); the veil as a medium of mercy
- Plan of Salvation — Topical Study — D&C 19:6–7, 11–12 reframing eternal/endless punishment as temporary teaching and discipline
- Miscellaneous Scriptures — D&C 19:17 paired with Moroni 10:33 on being perfected in Christ
