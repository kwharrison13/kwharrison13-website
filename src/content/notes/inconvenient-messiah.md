# Inconvenient Messiah

A tag Kyle attaches in his General Conference — October 2021 notes to the idea of following Christ even when discipleship is costly. He pins it to a closing exhortation: "May we take up the cross of Christ regardless of how inconvenient it may be." The surrounding notes frame the Savior's love as unfailing "even when we fail," and call for a "higher and holier life" in which shallow labels and "-ites" pass away into "one transcendent identity" as the children of Christ ([4 Nephi 1](/notes/4-nephi-1)). The "inconvenient Messiah" is the counterpoint: love and identity are freely given, but taking up the cross still asks something hard of the disciple.

**Context:** *The Inconvenient Messiah* is also the title of a well-known address by Jeffrey R. Holland on the cost and demands of true discipleship — the same theme Kyle's note invokes — though the source page cites the phrase rather than the talk directly.
