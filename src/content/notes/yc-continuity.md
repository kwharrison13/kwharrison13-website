# YC Continuity

YC Continuity is Y Combinator's later-stage / growth investment arm, appearing throughout the Pave Diligence — 2021 dossier as the incumbent investor [Index Ventures](/notes/index-ventures) was trying to "fast follow." Per the dossier, YC Continuity led [Pave](/notes/pave)'s $46M Series B in August 2021 at a $400M post-money valuation (Pave's earlier $16M Series A had been led by [a16z](/notes/a16z) at $75M post). Index's contemplated move was to pre-empt a Series C at roughly a $1B valuation — a $50M lead in a $100M round — on the back of Pave's $300K→$5M ARR year, which the memo explicitly framed as the discomfort of "fast follow[ing] YC Continuity into a company that raised at an already full price just over the summer." The dossier also flagged YC Continuity board contacts (Anu Hariharan, Matt Bogrand) as back-channel diligence targets.

**Context:** YC Continuity Fund was Y Combinator's growth-stage fund, created to invest in later rounds of YC alumni companies; YC wound down the standalone Continuity vehicle in the early 2020s.

## Where this appears

- Pave Diligence — 2021 — the existing growth investor in Pave (led the $46M Series B at $400M post, Aug 2021) that Index Ventures was weighing whether to fast-follow with a pre-emptive Series C.
