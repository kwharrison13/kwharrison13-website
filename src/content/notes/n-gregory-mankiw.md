# N. Gregory Mankiw

In [Charlie Munger — Academic Economics](/notes/charlie-munger-academic-economics), [Charlie Munger](/notes/charlie-munger) holds up Mankiw's freshman economics textbook as evidence that economics has always been more multidisciplinary than the rest of soft science — that it "just reached out and grabbed things as it needed to." Munger recounts buying the textbook immediately when it came out, partly because Mankiw had reportedly gotten such a large advance that Munger "wanted to figure out what the guy was doing." Riffling through it, he found two ideas laid out as principles of economics that he endorses as "superpowers": [opportunity cost](/notes/opportunity-cost) (to be used by anyone hoping to get the right answer) and incentives. The reference anchors Mankiw, in the wiki, to Munger's theme of [Multidisciplinary Thinking](/notes/multidisciplinary-thinking).

**Context:** N. Gregory Mankiw is a Harvard economist whose introductory textbook *Principles of Economics* is one of the most widely used in the field; he also chaired the Council of Economic Advisers under President George W. Bush.
