# Amos 3

A chapter in the Old Testament book of Amos, cited by Kyle for its verse on prophetic revelation: "Surely the Lord GOD will do nothing, **but he revealeth his secret unto his servants the prophets**" (Amos 3:7). In his notes on General Conference October 2021, the verse sits alongside the theme that true doctrine — not the mere study of behavior — changes attitudes and conduct.

In his Pattern of Prophetic Callings study, Amos 3:7 is one of the structural pillars: prophets are "called *with* God and given His counsel" (paired with [Jeremiah 23](/notes/jeremiah-23):18), foreordained and set apart by ordinance and calling. The verse undergirds Kyle's larger argument about the recurring shape of how God calls and equips His prophets.

## Where this appears

- General Conference- October 2021 — Amos 3:7 on God revealing His secret to the prophets, in the cluster on true doctrine and revelation
- Pattern of Prophetic Callings — Amos 3:7 as evidence that prophets are called with God and given His counsel
