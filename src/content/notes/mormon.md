# Mormon

Mormon is the Book of Mormon prophet-historian who abridged the Nephite records — father of [Moroni](/notes/moroni) — and he appears across Kyle's notes both as a person and as a model of the disciplined reader/recorder. In Scripture Study — Topical Study, Mormon's "and thus we see" passages are held up (citing [Ezra Taft Benson](/notes/ezra-taft-benson)) as the pattern for increasing testimony and faith: a description followed by a statement of truth that the spiritually sighted recognize. The same study draws on Mormon 1:2 — Ammaron's commendation that the young Mormon was "a sober child, and... quick to observe" — as the model for attentive scripture study (a detail also noted in the Alma 17–22 study, where Mormon is about ten years old).

In [How To Read a Book](/books/how-to-read-a-book), Mormon surfaces in connection with the condensation problem of reading: Kyle's note connects Adler's argument to "Mormon talking about including a hundredth of what he could have" written — i.e., the editor's burden of compressing a vast record into what is essential. Mormon is also listed among the people of the Moroni 1–6 study. (Distinct from the numbered chapter pages like Mormon 1 and Mormon 2, and from the religion/people sense of "Mormon," which appears separately in sources like [Romney — A Reckoning](/books/romney-a-reckoning).)

**Context:** In Latter-day Saint scripture, Mormon (c. 4th century AD) is the prophet, general, and historian who compiled and abridged the centuries of Nephite records onto the golden plates from which Joseph Smith translated the Book of Mormon — which bears his name. His son Moroni finished and buried the record.
