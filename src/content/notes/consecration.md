# Consecration

The LDS law of giving oneself fully to God — "the yielding up of oneself to God" ([Neal A. Maxwell](/notes/neal-a-maxwell)). In Kyle's 8 Points of the Gospel topical study, consecration is the process by which a "Reforming Perfectionist" makes progress: the celestial promise that "they SHALL overcome all things" is a future state, and the role in the meantime is one of progress through consecration. The study frames full consecration scripturally (2 Nephi 32:9 — pray and He will consecrate thy performance; John 3:30 — John the Baptist's consecration for Christ's elevation) and quotes Maxwell's consolation that "in pondering and pursuing consecration, understandably we tremble inwardly at what may be required. Yet the Lord has said... 'my grace is sufficient for you.'"

In [Working Toward Zion](/books/working-toward-zion), consecration is reframed economically and practically. Two threads stand out in Kyle's notes: consecration is "line upon line" — practical commitment over heroic gestures, "you don't start by donating all your property" — and its biggest obstacle is finding "men who know what to do with it." [Brigham Young](/notes/brigham-young)'s "I am laboring under a certain embarrassment" line surfaces twice: consecration without capable stewards just transfers wealth without transferring productive capability, which is why BYU business students are framed as part of the answer. Maxwell again sharpens it: "we tend to think of consecration only in terms of property and money. But there are so many ways of keeping back part... Consecration is the only surrender which is also a victory." It connects directly to [Stewardship](/notes/stewardship), [Zion](/notes/zion), and the [United Order](/notes/united-order).

**Context:** In Latter-day Saint theology, the law of consecration is a covenant to dedicate one's time, talents, and material resources to building the kingdom of God; it was institutionalized in the 19th century as the United Order.

## Where this appears

- 8 Points of the Gospel — consecration as the "process" of the Reforming Perfectionist; defined as "the yielding up of oneself to God"
- [Working Toward Zion](/books/working-toward-zion) — consecration as "line upon line" practical commitment, whose biggest obstacle is finding capable stewards
