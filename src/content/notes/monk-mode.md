# Monk Mode

"Monk mode" is the practice of carving out zero-interruption time to protect focus and do deep work. In [Essentialism](/books/essentialism), [Greg McKeown](/notes/greg-mckeown) coins the term to describe how he wrote the book: blocking off eight hours a day (5:00 A.M. to 1:00 P.M., five days a week) with "no e-mail, no calls, no appointments, and no interruptions," even setting his email bounceback to explain he was in "monk mode" until the book was complete. Kyle's note on the passage simply reads "Zero interruption writing. 'Monk mode.'"

In Kyle's reading the concept sits at the center of a cluster of focus-protection ideas: McKeown's eight-hour no-interruption writing blocks, the first-Monday-unplugged practice, and Jeff Weiner's blank-calendar discipline. It maps directly onto [Deep Work](/books/deep-work) — the operating-system version of the same idea of guarding undistracted time as the scarce input to meaningful output.
