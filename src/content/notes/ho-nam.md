# Ho Nam

**One-line:** *Venture capitalist ([Altos Ventures](/notes/altos-ventures)); his "what is an investor? Not a VC" reply frames venture as value-creation alongside founders rather than capital allocation for LP returns.*

**Context:** Ho Nam is co-founder and managing director of Altos Ventures, a Menlo Park venture firm known for a long-term, capital-efficient, founder-aligned style (notable early backer of Roblox, Coupang, and Korean startups).

## What we know

- In [a tweet response](https://twitter.com/honam/status/1526364080922980354) to "what is an investor? Not a VC," he objected to comparing venture capital to investing — because company-building is so human, and an LP/returns frame can be detrimental to long-term value creation. (source: What Is An Investor - Research)
- Kyle reads his point as being about a *frame of mind*: measure success by value created, not dollars returned — and the dollars tend to follow.
- Echoes [Tom Perkins](/notes/tom-perkins)' "we are not investors" stance in [The Power Law — Venture Capital & the Making of the New Future](/books/the-power-law-venture-capital-and-the-making-of-the-new-future).
