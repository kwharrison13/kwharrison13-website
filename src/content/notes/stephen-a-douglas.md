# Stephen A. Douglas

Stephen A. Douglas appears in [Joseph Smith Lecture 2 — Joseph's Personality and Character](/notes/joseph-smith-lecture-2-joseph-s-personality-and-character) as a non-Mormon character witness for [Joseph Smith](/notes/joseph-smith). The lecture lists Douglas among contemporaries — alongside Peter Burnett, Alexander Doniphan, James W. Woods, Daniel H. Wells, and [Brigham Young](/notes/brigham-young) — who attest to Smith's leadership and independence of mind. The notes record that Douglas's nickname "the Little Giant" was, by one source's claim, applied to him by Joseph Smith, and that Douglas had "many admiring things to say of Joseph during the Illinois period," specifically that he had independence of mind. The most-quoted line, attributed to Douglas via Anson Call, is: *"Joseph Smith is the only independent man I ever saw. We are always wondering what effect our actions will have upon our constituents or friends, but he does what he thinks is right regardless of what people think or say of him."* The lecture also notes Smith's prediction that Douglas would aspire to the Presidency.

**Context:** Stephen A. Douglas (1813–1861) was a U.S. senator from Illinois, author of the Kansas–Nebraska Act, Lincoln's opponent in the 1858 Lincoln–Douglas debates, and the Northern Democratic candidate for president in 1860.
