# Newspeak

In Kyle's Historical Futurism - Research notes, Newspeak surfaces as one of the Nineteen Eighty-Four threads pulled into the [The Openness of AI](/essays/the-openness-of-ai) deep dive — paired with [Thoughtcrime](/notes/thoughtcrime) and the formulation "[malinformation](/notes/malinformation) = the wrong truth." It belongs to a cluster of Orwellian concepts about language and information control that Kyle connects to present-day debates over AI, openness, and who gets to define acceptable speech, routed through [Contrary Research](/essays/contrary-research).

**Context:** Newspeak is the controlled, deliberately impoverished language of the totalitarian state in George Orwell's *Nineteen Eighty-Four* (1949). By systematically reducing vocabulary, the regime aims to make dissenting thought ("thoughtcrime") literally unthinkable — the premise being that you cannot conceive of what you have no words for.
