# Thomas B. Marsh

Invoked in Kyle's gospel-study note "Christ Defines His Gospel" as a cautionary example of letting imperfect people drive one away from the faith. The note — quoting [Jeffrey R. Holland](/notes/jeffrey-r-holland) — argues that "the church is operated for AND by imperfect people. We cannot let them offend us," and points to Thomas B. Marsh "for example," reframing the question from "is the church true" to "am I true?"

**Context:** Thomas B. Marsh was the first President of the Quorum of the Twelve Apostles in The Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-day Saints; he is widely cited in LDS teaching as someone who left the Church over a minor grievance and later returned, a stock example of taking offense.

## Where this appears

- Christ Defines His Gospel — cited as an example of being offended by imperfect people in the Church, in a passage on becoming "true."
