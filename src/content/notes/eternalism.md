# Eternalism

Eternalism enters Kyle's wiki through [Ted Chiang](/notes/ted-chiang)'s [Story of Your Life](/books/story-of-your-life), where it names the philosophical view paired with the [Block Universe](/notes/block-universe): past, present, and future all exist equally, and our sense of an "open" future is a feature of our limited access rather than of reality itself. In Kyle's notes this is grounded in the time-symmetry of the fundamental laws of physics — the arrow of time being a statistical feature of [entropy](/notes/entropy) and initial conditions, not of the laws — and quoted via Einstein: "the distinction between past, present and future is only a stubbornly persistent illusion."

The concept sits in a cluster with [Determinism](/notes/determinism), [Free Will](/notes/free-will), and [Predestination](/notes/predestination): the story's heptapods perceive time all at once, and its protagonist Louise comes to read her life as a "completed shape" she lives out fully despite knowing its ending — knowledge of the future and living it become complementary rather than opposed.

**Context:** Eternalism is a position in the philosophy of time holding that all points in time are equally real (the "block universe"), in contrast to presentism (only the present is real) and the growing block view (the past and present are real but the future is not). It is often associated with the relativity of simultaneity in special relativity.
