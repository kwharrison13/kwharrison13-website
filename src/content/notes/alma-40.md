# Alma 40

Alma 40 is one of [Alma the Younger](/notes/alma-the-younger)'s chapters of counsel to his son [Corianton](/notes/corianton) (part of the Alma 39–42 "Great Plan of Happiness" study), and in Kyle's notes it is cited chiefly for one verse used across two later studies. Alma 40:26 — "no unclean thing can inherit the kingdom of God; but they are cast out... and they drink the dregs of a bitter cup" — anchors his "Fulfilling the Measure of Our Creation" notes (the Father knew none save One would obey perfectly, which is why repentance and the Atonement are required) and his October 2021 General Conference notes (the Savior "cannot take any of us into His kingdom 'just as we are'" — sins must first be resolved, paired with [Hugh Nibley](/notes/hugh-nibley) and [Jeffrey R. Holland](/notes/jeffrey-r-holland) on the difference between forgiving sin and condoning it).

**Context:** Alma 40 is Alma the Younger's teaching to Corianton on the state of the soul between death and resurrection — the spirit world of paradise and prison — and on the doctrine and timing of the resurrection itself.

## Where this appears

- Alma 39–42 — The Great Plan of Happiness — part of the chapter set in Alma's counsel to [Corianton](/notes/corianton) (Kyle's notes for this chapter are partial).
- Fulfilling the Measure of Our Creation - September 5, 2021 — cites Alma 40:26 on no unclean thing inheriting the kingdom and the necessity of the Atonement.
- General Conference- October 2021 — cites Alma 40:26 that the Savior cannot bring us in "just as we are."
