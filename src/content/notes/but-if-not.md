# But If Not...

"But if not…" is Kyle's recurring name for faith that is **not contingent on the outcome** — drawn from Daniel 3:17–18, where Shadrach, Meshach, and Abednego tell the king "our God… is able to deliver us… *But if not*, be it known unto thee… that we will not serve thy gods." In his Alma 13–16 study he develops it as the question "Can God deliver us? Yes. Will He? If it is His will" — and notes people mistake God's inaction for inability because they cannot see the bigger, eternal picture (a correct idea of God's character, per [Lectures on Faith](/notes/lectures-on-faith)). The fiery furnace of Daniel parallels the women and children of Ammonihah in [Alma 14](/notes/alma-14).

The thread runs through several Come Follow Me weeks: in Alma 17–22, "sometimes God lets bad things happen to create an opportunity to show his power" (Ammon, Alma 17:29); in Alma 53–63, the stripling warriors' faith "not contingent on the outcome"; and in 3 Nephi 20–26 (3 Nephi 21:10). In the General Conference — October 2021 notes, [Brent H. Nielson](/notes/brent-h-nielson)'s talk on the death of a loved one — "First we prayed for a miracle… Finally we prayed that he would pass quickly" — is tagged here, set against the [Prosperity Gospel](/notes/prosperity-gospel) error and the idea of faith "as a skill, not just a binary."
