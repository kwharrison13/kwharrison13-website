# Focus

A recurring theme in Kyle's reading: focus as the discipline of applying effort in one direction, to one object, rather than scattering it. The [Thomas Edison](/notes/thomas-edison) quote he saved frames it sharply — everyone works all day, but most "do it about a great many things"; success comes from sticking to "one thing… letting all else go." [The Magic of Elon Musk](/notes/the-magic-of-elon-musk) supplies the causal chain Kyle returns to: "Goals breed commitment. Commitment breeds focus. Focus breeds achievement" — the engine behind Musk's ability to out-work and out-think. It connects naturally to [Goals](/notes/goals) (effort in one direction toward a single object) and to [Capital Allocator Mindset](/notes/capital-allocator-mindset), where the budget constraint *forces* focus by making you ask the dollar question and radically prioritize.

The concept threads through Kyle's two Cal Newport books — [Digital Minimalism](/books/digital-minimalism) (reclaiming attention from engineered distraction) and [Essentialism](/books/essentialism) (building thinking and quiet-reflection time into the schedule; "multifocus" as the real enemy) — and shows up in his more applied notes. In [Todd McKinnon — Creating and Defining a New Market Category](/notes/todd-mckinnon-creating-and-defining-a-new-market-category), focus is examined as a spectrum: "extreme singular focus" tends to be rewarded, yet a company that stays a "one-trick pony" with a narrow product focus can hit a wall — so focus has to be balanced against deliberate expansion. Kyle even files focus alongside intellectual character, tagging [Joseph Smith Lecture 2 — Joseph's Personality and Character](/notes/joseph-smith-lecture-2-joseph-s-personality-and-character) under it via Madsen's "four casts of mind."
