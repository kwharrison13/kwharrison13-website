# Destiny of America

A theme Kyle traces through his Come Follow Me study of 3 Nephi 20–3 Nephi 26: the idea that this land (America) is set apart in the covenant for a providential purpose. In 3 Nephi 21:4, the Father's wisdom is that the remnant "should be established in this land, and be set up as a free people by the power of the Father, that these things might come forth from them" — Kyle reads the establishing of a free people on this land as bound up with the [Gathering of Israel](/notes/gathering-of-israel) and the eventual building of the [New Jerusalem](/notes/new-jerusalem). Kyle annotates the verse line by line, parsing "they" (the Gentiles), "these things" (the signs given to the Nephites), and "remnant of your seed" (the descendants of the Nephites), so that America's freedom is framed as the staging ground for the covenant's fulfillment rather than an end in itself.

**Context:** Within Latter-day Saint thought, the "promised land" / destiny-of-America motif holds that the American continent was prepared as a place of liberty so the restored gospel could come forth and Zion (a New Jerusalem) be built — a reading drawn from Book of Mormon passages such as 1 Nephi, Ether, and 3 Nephi.
