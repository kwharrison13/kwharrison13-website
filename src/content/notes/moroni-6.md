# Moroni 6

Moroni 6 is the Book of Mormon chapter — part of [Moroni](/notes/moroni)'s closing record — that catalogs the simple, structural workings of the Church: requirements for [Baptism](/notes/baptism), record-keeping, meeting together to fast and pray, and watchfulness. In the Come Follow Me study of Moroni 1–6 it grounds the theme that "the plain things matter" — Moroni records ordinations, the sacrament prayers, baptism, and record-keeping because these are what "keep them in the right way" (Moroni 6:4). The same verse names Christ as "the author and the finisher of their faith," cited in Christ Defines His Gospel as Christ being the *finisher* of faith. Moroni 6:3 establishes that "none were received unto baptism save they took upon them the name of Christ, having a determination to serve him to the end."

The chapter recurs across topical studies. Faith — Topical Study draws on Moroni 6:8 to distinguish true repentance (repenting and seeking forgiveness) from "prepentance" (merely seeking to alleviate guilt, which "profiteth nothing"), and on Moroni 6:9 for being led by the Spirit. A Mighty Change of Heart — Ezra Taft Benson quotes Moroni 6:2 on receiving none to baptism "save they came forth with a broken heart and a contrite spirit." Fasting - September 5, 2021 cites Moroni 6:5 on the church meeting "together oft, to fast and to pray... concerning the welfare of their souls."

**Context:** Moroni 6 is the sixth chapter of the Book of Moroni, the final book of the Book of Mormon, written by the prophet Moroni after the destruction of the Nephite nation. The chapter describes the conditions of baptism and church membership and the early Nephite Christian church's practices of fasting, prayer, and mutual care.
