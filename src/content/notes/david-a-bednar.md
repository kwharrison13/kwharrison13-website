# David A. Bednar

An LDS apostle whose teaching recurs across Kyle's scripture study, most often on two linked ideas: **learning by faith** and **revelation that comes incrementally**. In Bednar's framing, learners are "to act and be doers of the word and not simply hearers who are only acted upon" — agents who open their hearts to the Holy Ghost rather than waiting to be taught (December 31–January 6, 2019. "We Are Responsible for Our Own Learning"). This makes him the anchor reference for the [Agency](/notes/agency) / [Learning, No Greater Responsibility](/notes/learning-no-greater-responsibility) thread.

His second recurring theme is that spiritual knowledge rarely arrives as a single "light-switch" or Sacred-Grove vision; it accrues "line upon line, precept upon precept, here a little and there a little" — a pattern Kyle's notes credit Bednar with drawing out at length from 2 Nephi 28:30 (July 6–12, 2020. Alma 30–31: "The Virtue of the Word of God" · [Line upon line, precept upon precept](/notes/line-upon-line-precept-upon-precept)). Moroni's Exhortations uses the same Bednar distinction — a testimony comes through serious, line-upon-line study that grows "from a trickle into a river," not as an instant manifestation. His April 2026 General Conference address (All Who Have Endured Valiantly) reframes "enduring to the end" as becoming possessed of charity, the pure love of Christ — spiritual transformation rather than mere persistence.

**Context:** David A. Bednar (b. 1952) is a member of the Quorum of the Twelve Apostles of The Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-day Saints, called in 2004, and a former president of BYU–Idaho.
