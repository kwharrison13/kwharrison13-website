# Ministering

Ministering surfaces in Kyle's Come Follow Me study of [Alma 13](/notes/alma-13)–[Alma 16](/notes/alma-16) as one of the core themes of "Enter into the Rest of the Lord." The text Kyle ties it to is [Alma 15](/notes/alma-15):18 — after the deliverance at Ammonihah, "Alma having seen all these things, therefore he took Amulek and came over to the land of Zarahemla, and took him to his own house, and did administer unto him in his tribulations, and strengthened him in the Lord." For Kyle, this is ministering in its plainest form: a personal, one-to-one act of taking someone into your own house and strengthening them through their trials, set within the week's larger study of priesthood, the rest of the Lord, and faith.

**Context:** In Latter-day Saint usage, "ministering" denotes individualized spiritual and temporal care of fellow members — a practice the Church formalized in 2018 to replace the older home/visiting teaching programs, emphasizing personalized service over routine visits.
