# Brain Interfaces

Brain Interfaces surfaces once, in Kyle's March 30th, 2022 — 1x1 with Luke Piette notes, as a topic that came up while discussing [Ideaflow](/notes/ideaflow) (a [Tools For Thought](/notes/tools-for-thought) product similar to [Roam Research](/notes/roam-research)). The note records that in the conversation "talked a lot about [Brain Interfaces](/notes/brain-interfaces)," immediately alongside a chat about [Davey Morse](/notes/davey-morse) and [Plexus](/notes/plexus). The mention is brief and sits in the tools-for-thought / knowledge-augmentation cluster of that 1x1 rather than being defined on its own — so this page is a thin placeholder for that thread.

**Context:** "Brain interfaces" generally refers to brain-computer interface (BCI) technology — systems that read from or write to neural activity to augment or externalize cognition. In a tools-for-thought conversation, the phrase is often used loosely for software that aims to be a more direct extension of how the mind captures and connects ideas.
