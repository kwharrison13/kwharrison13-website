# The Music Man (musical)

[Meredith Willson](/notes/meredith-willson)'s musical, which Kyle quotes in his Alma 39 study under the thread "Idleness is the devil's playground." Reading Alma's counsel that [Corianton](/notes/corianton) "shouldst have tended to the ministry wherewith thou wast entrusted" (Alma 39:4), Kyle pairs the line "Friends, the idle brain is the devil's playground" (from *The Music Man*) with [Geoffrey Chaucer](/notes/geoffrey-chaucer)'s "idleness is the root of mischief" (Tale of Melibee, c. 1386) — using both to gloss the scriptural point that idleness, not just overt sin, opens the door to temptation.

**Context:** *The Music Man* (1957) is a musical by Meredith Willson about con man Harold Hill posing as a boys' band leader in River City, Iowa; the "idle brain is the devil's playground" line is part of Hill's "Ya Got Trouble" patter.
