# GovTech

GovTech is the lens behind a cluster of 2018 sourcing/networking calls in which Kyle mapped the government-technology and smart-cities landscape — companies selling to, and ecosystems built around, local government. The recurring problem across these calls is the public-sector go-to-market: long municipal sales cycles, sub-$10K bid thresholds, and a procurement process that startups struggle to navigate. [eRepublic](/notes/erepublic) is the media/research backbone of the space, producing the GovTech 100 and operating like an ad agency where "companies pay; governments never do," with near-universal U.S. coverage (90K local agencies, 19K small cities). [Smart Cities Council](/notes/smart-cities-council) is the vendor-and-university coalition pushing "smart-city readiness" through content, coaching, competitions, and cohorts across a 15-vertical framework. [CoProcure](/notes/coprocure) ([Mariel Reed](/notes/mariel-reed)) and [UrbanLeap](/notes/urbanleap) ([Arik Bronshtein](/notes/arik-bronshtein)) attack [Procurement](/notes/procurement) directly — cooperative purchasing and city pilot-testing — while [CivicLift](/notes/civiclift) ([Evan Dobos](/notes/evan-dobos)) aggregates civic events/content and [ELGL](/notes/elgl) ([Kirsten Wyatt](/notes/kirsten-wyatt)) is the cross-functional professional org for people working in and around local government.

The GovTech thread feeds into Kyle's broader [The New City](/notes/the-new-city) / [City Building](/notes/city-building) interest — "how cities modernize, procure, use data, and partner with the private sector" — and to the [Playbooks](/notes/playbooks) work (a [Playbook: Government Regulation](/notes/playbook-government-regulation)). The June 29th, 2021 Quick Capture log carries a saved GovTech reading link.

**Context:** "GovTech" (government technology) is the sector of software and services sold to public-sector buyers — federal, state, and especially local government — spanning procurement, civic engagement, permitting, and smart-city infrastructure. It is tracked by outlets like Government Technology magazine and the annual GovTech 100 list.
