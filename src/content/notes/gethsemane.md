# Gethsemane

The garden where Christ's atoning suffering began, central to two of Kyle's gospel studies. In [Jeffrey R. Holland](/notes/jeffrey-r-holland)'s address Therefore, What?, Gethsemane is the heart of "Atonement lesson #1 — Christ remained true": in the garden's "nature-shattering pain," Jesus pleaded "Abba… take away this cup from me" and then yielded, "Not my will, but thine, be done" — the submission that, per Abinadi, made Jesus the Son of God (3 Nephi 11:11). The disciple's offering "in similitude" is [a broken heart and a contrite spirit](/notes/broken-heart-and-a-contrite-spirit). Holland reads it alongside Alma 7:11–12: because Christ suffered "pains and afflictions of every kind," He knows how to *succor* ("run to") the stumbling student even now.

In the Alma 13–16 study, Gethsemane appears in connection with the [Sacrament](/notes/sacrament) — both the suffering in the garden and the death on the cross being "parts of the [Atonement](/notes/atonement)."

**Context:** Gethsemane is a garden at the foot of the Mount of Olives outside Jerusalem where, in the Gospel accounts (Matthew 26, Mark 14, Luke 22), Jesus prayed in agony before His arrest. In Latter-day Saint theology the suffering in Gethsemane — not only the crucifixion — is understood as a central part of the Atonement.

## Where this appears
