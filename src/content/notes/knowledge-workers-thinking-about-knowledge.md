# Knowledge Workers Thinking About Knowledge

A recurring theme in Kyle's notes: the observation that the people whose job *is* thinking — writers, researchers, students, knowledge workers — rarely examine the practice of knowledge work itself. The framing quote comes from [Andy Matuschak](/notes/andy-matuschak) in [Andy Matuschak — Designing Education](/notes/andy-matuschak-designing-education): *"I spend a lot of time thinking about why knowledge workers don't spend very much time thinking about knowledge work."*

The idea threads through [How To Take Smart Notes](/books/how-to-take-smart-notes), which Kyle organizes under this heading. Sönke Ahrens argues that writing and note-taking "fly under the radar" because bad note-taking produces no immediate negative feedback, so there's little demand for help; that there's almost no *deliberate practice* in knowledge work; and that genuine understanding only appears when you try to rephrase an idea in your own [Writing](/notes/writing) — "we have to choose between feeling smarter or becoming smarter." Kyle's own note ties this directly to Matuschak: you don't realize how bad you are at knowledge work because you're rarely called upon to do it or reflect on it. It connects to [Learn Like An Athlete](/notes/learn-like-an-athlete) ("the one who does the work does the learning") and the [Dunning-Kruger Effect](/notes/dunning-kruger-effect). In the Conor White-Sullivan meeting, the same idea surfaces as the motivation behind Roam — "people don't take knowledge work seriously," and the goal is to help people deliberately level up at the skill.

## Where this appears

- [How To Take Smart Notes](/books/how-to-take-smart-notes) — the section header for Ahrens's argument that note-taking and writing get no deliberate practice and no feedback loop
- [Andy Matuschak — Designing Education](/notes/andy-matuschak-designing-education) — the source of the framing quote about knowledge workers not thinking about knowledge work
- June 22nd, 2021 — Conor White-Sullivan (Roam Research) — Roam framed as a tool for taking knowledge work seriously and leveling up at it
