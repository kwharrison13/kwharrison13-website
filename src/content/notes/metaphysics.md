# Metaphysics

In Kyle's notes, "Metaphysics" tags the way [Joseph Smith — Rough Stone Rolling](/books/joseph-smith-rough-stone-rolling) frames Joseph Smith's most radical theological revisions — the nature of reality, spirit, and creation. The book records that one of Joseph's most famed statements was a casual aside to a Methodist preacher: "There is no such thing as immaterial matter. All spirit is matter but is more fine or pure and can only be discerned by purer eyes." Bushman treats this as a quiet but radical revision of Christian metaphysics — and Kyle notes the striking detail that no one at the time made anything of it (the preacher "seemed pleased").

The concept clusters with [Matter](/notes/matter) ("there is no such thing as immaterial matter"), the doctrine of eternal uncreated intelligences, and the rejection of [Creation Ex Nihilo](/notes/creation-ex-nihilo) in favor of God ordering pre-existing chaos rather than creating from nothing. Together these mark Joseph Smith's pattern of "revealing new things, not rehashing old ones."

**Context:** Metaphysics is the branch of philosophy concerned with the fundamental nature of reality — being, substance, space, time, and the relation between mind and matter.
