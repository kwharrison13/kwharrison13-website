# Alex Yen

A [Dropbox](/notes/dropbox) alum Kyle surveyed for his Talent Vortex essay research. Yen contributed a view of **interviewing as a practiced skill** and is associated with a **"false negatives, no false positives"** hiring stance — the principle that it is better to wrongly pass on a good candidate (a false negative) than to wrongly hire a bad one (a false positive), so the bar should be set to never let in a mistake even at the cost of missing some good people.

He appears alongside other Dropbox alumni and contacts Kyle drew on for the same work — [Alex Allain](/notes/alex-allain) (a former Director of Engineering who wrote a hiring guide from 500+ technical interviews), [Karim Anchassi](/notes/karim-anchassi), and [Kyle Parrish](/notes/kyle-parrish) — making Dropbox a notable node in the talent-vortex research network.
