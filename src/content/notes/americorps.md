# AmeriCorps

AmeriCorps appears in Kyle's notes as the centerpiece of [Mark Cuban](/notes/mark-cuban)'s proposed answer to AI-driven labor disruption. Cited in the Unions, Unions Everywhere - Research thread, Cuban — who believes in the "earthquake power" of AI and machine learning and that massive labor disruptions are coming sooner than later — proposes a two-part fix that is explicitly **not** [Universal Basic Income](/notes/universal-basic-income): (1) absorbing displaced low-skilled workers via rising demand for data tagging and labeling ("the 21st century version of an assembly line"), and (2) a broad expansion of programs like AmeriCorps, reallocating the macroeconomic gains from automation into classrooms and domestic infrastructure. AmeriCorps thus stands in for a national-service model of absorbing labor dislocation rather than simply paying people not to work.

**Context:** AmeriCorps is a U.S. federal agency for national service and volunteerism, placing participants in education, environmental, disaster-relief, and community-development roles in exchange for stipends and education awards. This is general background.
