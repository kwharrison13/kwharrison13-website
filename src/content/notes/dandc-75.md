# D&C 75

Section 75 of the [Doctrine and Covenants](/notes/doctrine-and-covenants) recurs in Kyle's missionary-themed gospel study. In Teaching Repentance, verses 2–5 ("talk with the sound of a trump") and 10–11 ("pray always, call on the Lord") are cited for the charge to open one's mouth boldly and declare repentance — boldness understood as an expression of love rather than hellfire preaching. In Spiritual Gifts, verse 2 is listed among the functions of the Holy Ghost: the Spirit "will direct you to do those things that are necessary for you to gain exaltation and happiness."

**Context:** Doctrine and Covenants Section 75 is a revelation given to Joseph Smith in January 1832 at a conference in Amherst, Ohio. It calls a number of elders to missionary labor, instructing them to "lift up their voices as with the sound of a trump" and promising that the laborer is worthy of his hire.

## Where this appears

- Teaching Repentance — verses 2–5 and 10–11 cited for bold missionary proclamation and constant prayer.
- Spiritual Gifts — verse 2 listed as a function of the Holy Ghost: directing us toward exaltation and happiness.
