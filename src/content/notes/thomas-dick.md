# Thomas Dick

In [An Insider's View of Mormon Origins](/books/an-insider-s-view-of-mormon-origins), Thomas Dick is load-bearing to the book's thesis that [Joseph Smith](/notes/joseph-smith)'s theology reflects antebellum thought rather than ancient revelation. The book quotes LDS scholar Klaus Hansen: Joseph's progressive theology and cosmology, "while in a general way compatible with antebellum thought, bears some remarkable resemblances to Thomas Dick's *Philosophy of a Future State*," a second edition of which appeared in 1830. The book notes that Joseph Smith owned a copy of this work, and that [Oliver Cowdery](/notes/oliver-cowdery) quoted lengthy excerpts from it in the *Messenger and Advocate* in December 1836. The same Thomas Dick cosmology is invoked in the book's reading of the [Pearl of Great Price](/notes/pearl-of-great-price) / Book of Abraham as reflecting nineteenth-century rather than antique sources.

**Context:** Thomas Dick (1774–1857) was a Scottish minister, science writer, and Christian philosopher whose popular works — including *The Philosophy of a Future State* — blended astronomy, natural theology, and speculation about an eternal, progressive cosmos populated across many worlds.
