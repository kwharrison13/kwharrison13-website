# Mike Pence

In [Shortest Way Home](/books/shortest-way-home), [Pete Buttigieg](/notes/pete-buttigieg)'s memoir, Pence appears as the Indiana governor behind the state's Religious Freedom Restoration Act (RFRA), the bill that drives the book's chapter on discrimination and LGBT rights. Buttigieg recounts how, after a national backlash, the Republican state assembly "hastily composed a clarification" specifying the law could not justify discrimination — and how the bill's original backers complained that the clarification defeated its whole purpose. Buttigieg reads that objection as the revealing moment: "contrary to Pence's protestations, discrimination had been at the heart of their project all along."

**Context:** Mike Pence is a former Vice President of the United States (2017–2021) under Donald Trump and was Governor of Indiana (2013–2017) when the RFRA controversy unfolded in 2015.
