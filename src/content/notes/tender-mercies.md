# Tender Mercies

In Moroni's Exhortations, "tender mercies" is tied to Moroni 10:3–4, where Moroni charges us to **remember** how merciful the Lord has been and to **ponder** God's goodness before asking whether "these things" are true. The study notes this echoes [Nephi](/notes/nephi)'s stated purpose in writing — to show God's "tender mercies" — so Moroni is "affirming that fact": God is merciful, and the invitation to ask Him follows from first recognizing that mercy. It sits within the broader pattern of Moroni's exhortations, several of which begin with **remember**, the word [Boyd K. Packer](/notes/boyd-k-packer) called the most important in the English language.

**Context:** "The tender mercies of the Lord" comes from 1 Nephi 1:20 and was the subject of a well-known 2005 general conference address by [David A. Bednar](/notes/david-a-bednar), which frames tender mercies as the very personal, individualized blessings and assurances God extends to the faithful.

## Where this appears

- Moroni's Exhortations — listed as a core theme; connected to Nephi's purpose in writing and to Moroni 10:3–4's call to remember and ponder God's mercy before asking for a testimony.
