# And thus we see

A recurring editorial phrase Kyle tracks as "the ultimate reflection phrase" of the [Book of Mormon](/notes/book-of-mormon) — by his count it occurs 18 times, functioning as [Mormon's editorial aside](/notes/mormon-s-commentary) that pauses the narrative to draw a moral. Kyle pairs it with a parallel phrase, "therefore, what?", and connects both to President [Boyd K. Packer](/notes/boyd-k-packer)'s habit of asking "Therefore, what?" after a presentation (related by [Jeffrey R. Holland](/notes/jeffrey-r-holland)) — the point being that sermons and exhortations are worthless unless they actually change how disciples live.

In his study of Alma 23–29 he traces the phrase to [Alma 28](/notes/alma-28):13–14, where Mormon reflects on the inequality of man because of sin and the great call to labor in the Lord's vineyard. In his study of Helaman 1–6 it appears in [Helaman 3](/notes/helaman-3):27–28 (God's mercy and the open gate of heaven) and [Helaman 6](/notes/helaman-6):34–36, where the threefold "and thus we see" drives home the reversal of Nephite and Lamanite righteousness — Kyle reads it as Mormon "desperately trying to get this point across" about [Pride](/notes/pride) and [The Pride Cycle](/notes/the-pride-cycle).
