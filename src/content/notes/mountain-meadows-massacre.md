# Mountain Meadows Massacre

An 1857 atrocity that both [American Zion](/books/american-zion) and [Brigham Young — Pioneer Prophet](/books/brigham-young-pioneer-prophet) treat as a defining episode of the federal-conflict era in Utah. [Brigham Young — Pioneer Prophet](/books/brigham-young-pioneer-prophet) frames it as a rare "white-on-white massacre" in U.S. history (alongside the 1838 Haun's Mill Massacre against the Mormons) — "a heinous crime executed after careful deliberation and subterfuge rather than in the heat of any battle," testifying to "the extreme levels of anxiety, hatred, and avarice present in 1857 Utah." [American Zion](/books/american-zion) records the cover-up: when John D. Lee reported to [Brigham Young](/notes/brigham-young) and apostle Wilford Woodruff, Lee claimed the murdered migrants "belonged to the mob in Missouri & Illinois," that there "was not a drop of innocent blood in their Camp," and that the Indians had done the killing — and "Massacre leaders and perpetrators continued for years to lie to Young that Indians were solely responsible," a story "Young chose to believe . . . despite contrary evidence."

[American Zion](/books/american-zion) also traces the massacre's long historiographical afterlife: Juanita Brooks (the "little school teacher" who learned of a participant's secret) spent a decade producing her "magisterial history," declaring she would accept excommunication "if that is the price . . . for intellectual honesty"; and decades later the Church authorized "a new and unflinching account" around the tragedy's 2007 sesquicentennial, going beyond Brooks's book in "disrupting traditional and apologetic myths."

**Context:** The Mountain Meadows Massacre (September 11, 1857) was the killing of roughly 120 members of the Baker–Fancher emigrant wagon train in southern Utah by a local Latter-day Saint militia, with some Paiute participation, during the tensions of the Utah War. John D. Lee was eventually tried and executed for the crime in 1877.

## Where this appears

- [American Zion](/books/american-zion) — the cover-up (Lee, Woodruff, Young believing the "Indians did it" story) and the later historiography (Juanita Brooks, the 2007 unflinching account).
