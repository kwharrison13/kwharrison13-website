# Exaltation

In Latter-day Saint doctrine, exaltation is the destiny of becoming as God is — the highest end of the [Plan of Salvation](/notes/plan-of-salvation). Its fullest expression in Kyle's notes comes from [The King Follett Sermon](/notes/the-king-follett-sermon), [Joseph Smith](/notes/joseph-smith)'s April 1844 Nauvoo discourse: "you have got to learn how to be gods yourselves… by going from one small degree to another… from grace to grace, from exaltation to exaltation." The sermon frames this as continuous and reciprocal — God "is glorified and exalted in the salvation and exaltation of all His children," and Jesus "treads in the tracks of His Father," doing "the things I saw my Father do." It is paired tightly with the [Character of God](/notes/character-of-god) (God "was once a man… an exalted man") and with [Eternal Progression](/notes/eternal-progression) — exaltation is climbing a ladder "step by step," learning "all the principles of exaltation." The sermon also ties exaltation to knowledge: "in the world of spirits no man can be exalted but by knowledge."

The [Plan of Salvation](/notes/plan-of-salvation) notes sharpen the moral structure: "the purpose of our birth was to become Gods ourselves," and Satan's counterfeit was "exaltation without obedience" — entitlement without the doing-away of agency. Kyle's working summary, flagged for expansion, is "Saved by grace, exalted by works." In The First Principles of Faithful Pragmatism, exaltation sits among "the theological scaffolding of the God-as-Parent model" alongside [Heavenly Mother](/notes/heavenly-mother) and the [Atonement](/notes/atonement).

**Context:** Exaltation is the central concept in the Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-day Saints' doctrine of eternal progression — the belief that the faithful may inherit "all that the Father hath" (Doctrine and Covenants 84:38) and become like God, often summarized in the couplet attributed to Lorenzo Snow: "As man now is, God once was; as God now is, man may be."

## Where this appears

- [The King Follett Sermon](/notes/the-king-follett-sermon) — the doctrinal locus: "from exaltation to exaltation," God glorified in His children's exaltation, knowledge as prerequisite
- Plan of Salvation — birth's purpose as becoming Gods; Satan's plan as "exaltation without obedience"; "saved by grace, exalted by works"
- The First Principles of Faithful Pragmatism — listed in the theological scaffolding of the God-as-Parent model
