# Contrarianism

**One-line definition:** *Reasoning independently from the ground up and resisting pressure to conform — distinct from reflexive objection, which is just conformism of a different sort.*

## How sources describe it

- [Naval Ravikant](/notes/naval-ravikant) ([Navalmanack](https://www.navalmanack.com/)): "A contrarian isn't one who always objects—that's a conformist of a different sort. A contrarian reasons independently from the ground up and resists pressure to conform... Optimistic contrarians are the rarest breed."
- [Peter Thiel](/notes/peter-thiel): the standard is to be "contrarian *and right*" — Kyle's complaint is that many fixate on the "contrarian" half and drop the qualifier.
- Anti-conformism = conformism: being deviant for deviance's sake is one of the "wrong reasons" [Brie Wolfson](/notes/brie-wolfson) warns about.
- Kyle's synthesis — **conviction-led contrarianism**: contrarianism born of first-principles conviction and truth-seeking, typically countercyclical to hype, and rarely short-term.
