# Joanna Gaines

Joanna Gaines is the designer-narrator and co-author (with [Chip Gaines](/notes/chip-gaines)) of [The Magnolia Story](/books/the-magnolia-story). In the memoir she is the voice who leaves a career to follow intuition and faith, building Magnolia from a single shop run on instinct and word of mouth. Her arc carries the book's themes of [Frugality](/notes/frugality) ("having a tight budget doesn't have to mean watering down the design") and [Faith](/notes/faith) (trusting God's voice to walk away from a career and find peace). She credits Chip's belief in her — "He believed I could do it, and he was confident that what I didn't know, I could learn" — and frames her own growth as learning to "increase my capacity," manage disappointment, and make the most of the "opportunities" Chip kept finding, all while juggling four kids and recurring debt.

**Context:** Joanna Gaines is an American home-design entrepreneur and television personality who, with her husband Chip Gaines, built the Magnolia brand in Waco, Texas, and starred in HGTV's *Fixer Upper*.
