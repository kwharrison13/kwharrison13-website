# Maximizing Shareholder Value

In Kyle's notes, "maximizing shareholder value" appears as the foil against which the [Valued Empyrean](/notes/valued-empyrean) model is defined. In the August 18th, 2021 call with [Conor White-Sullivan](/notes/conor-white-sullivan) and Darwin Harrison, the old corporate model is framed as "maximizing shareholder value and then hoping to have some left over for impact" — profit first, impact as an afterthought. The Value model inverts this: "Impact first; income second" — designing income-generating relationships with long-term impact in mind, so that the producers, providers, and facilitators engage in activities that generate expertise and capital flowing directly into the ventures.

The phrase functions less as a concept Kyle endorses than as the conventional default he and his interlocutors are arguing against — the standard shorthand for shareholder-primacy capitalism that impact-driven and stakeholder-oriented structures position themselves in contrast to.

**Context:** "Maximizing shareholder value" is the doctrine — associated with Milton Friedman and popularized in 1980s–90s American corporate governance — that a company's primary obligation is to maximize returns to its shareholders. It has been widely critiqued by stakeholder-capitalism advocates for crowding out broader social and long-term considerations.
