# Andy Jassy

Across the two [Brad Stone](/notes/brad-stone) Amazon books, Andy Jassy is the co-architect of [AWS](/notes/aws) and an exemplar of [Jeff Bezos](/notes/jeff-bezos)'s mechanism-driven management. [The Everything Store](/books/the-everything-store) traces his rise: after engineers in the personalization group objected to being led by someone they viewed as "nontechnical," Bezos instead made Jassy his **first official "shadow"** — following Bezos into every meeting for ~18 months to build mutual trust, a role that became the coveted "technical adviser" position. Jassy then helped author the AWS primitives memo — listing the storage, compute, database, payments, and messaging building blocks Amazon would expose as web services — with the explicit goal of giving "a student in a dorm room… the same infrastructure as the largest companies in the world" as "a great playing-field leveler for startups."

[Amazon Unbound](/books/amazon-unbound) shows him running AWS by Bezos's maxim that "good intentions don't work, but mechanisms do," which "Jassy applied ferociously" through formal weekly rituals. In Kyle's marginalia he stands among the people Bezos's "system of invention" was built to scale.

**Context:** Andy Jassy joined Amazon in 1997, founded and led Amazon Web Services, and succeeded Jeff Bezos as Amazon's CEO in July 2021.
