# Mobility

Mobility is a central theme of Kyle's 2018 smart-city / [The New City](/notes/the-new-city) sourcing work, surfacing across the cluster of [Networking Calls](/notes/networking-calls) he logged with founders, advisors, and city officials in the "smart-city / mobility / govtech space." The strand runs through several distinct lenses: [David Pickeral](/notes/david-pickeral)'s "New Mobility" advisory work (arguing the sector struggles on monetization and needs deep public-private collaboration and a [Mobility-as-a-Service](/notes/mobility-as-a-service) model); [Innova](/notes/innova)'s first/last-mile electric mobility built around IoT data capture; and [Adam Cohen](/notes/adam-cohen)'s [UC Berkeley](/notes/uc-berkeley) research on [Mobility-on-Demand](/notes/mobility-on-demand), autonomous vehicles, land use, and the future of parking.

A recurring tension across these conversations: the transformative narrative around new mobility (autonomous vehicles, hyperloop, smart transit) keeps colliding with hard institutional realities — local politics, the difficulty of writing new street/land-use models into city codes, weak monetization, and the trillions in sunk transportation infrastructure that any new entrant has to supplement rather than replace. Counterparts in the call log carry the theme in their very names — "New Mobility" (Pickeral) and "Y-Mobility" (David Fidalgo).

**Context:** "Mobility" is the umbrella term used in urban-planning and transportation-tech circles for how people and goods move through cities — encompassing public transit, ride-hail, micromobility, autonomous vehicles, and integrated service models like Mobility-as-a-Service.
