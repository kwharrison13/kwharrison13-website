# Mark 6

Mark 6 appears in two of Kyle's gospel-study notes. In How to Overcome Depression, **Mark 6:31–32** — Christ withdrawing to a desert place to rest — grounds the "**Sacred Grove**" step in his 10-step plan for non-clinical depression: a place, physical or mental, to retreat and rest, on the logic that even Christ had a time to retreat ("if you don't have time, you don't have time NOT to take it"). In The Lehonti Principle, **Mark 6:4** is cited — Christ's teaching that "a prophet is not without honour, but in his own country, and among his own kin, and in his own house" — in the context of how those closest to a person can be the least receptive.

**Context:** Mark 6 includes Christ being rejected at Nazareth, the sending of the Twelve, the death of John the Baptist, the feeding of the five thousand, and Jesus walking on water.

## Where this appears

- How to Overcome Depression — Mark 6:31–32 (Christ retreats to rest) grounds the "Sacred Grove" step
- The Lehonti Principle — Mark 6:4 ("a prophet is not without honour... in his own country")
