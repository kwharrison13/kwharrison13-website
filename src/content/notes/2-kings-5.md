# 2 Kings 5

The Old Testament account of Naaman the leper, commanded by Elisha to wash seven times in the river Jordan. In Kyle's Obedience — Topical Study, verses 9–14 illustrate "Intelligent Obedience": there was nothing magic in the seventh washing versus the sixth — the point is that obedience is learned through "the accumulation of repeated simple acts" ([S. Michael Wilcox](/notes/s-michael-wilcox)), the same way a husband saying "I love you" repeatedly builds toward a celestial marriage.

**Context:** In 2 Kings 5, Naaman, a Syrian army commander afflicted with leprosy, is told by the prophet Elisha to wash seven times in the Jordan. He initially balks at so humble an instruction but, on washing, is healed — a story long used to teach humble, repeated obedience.
