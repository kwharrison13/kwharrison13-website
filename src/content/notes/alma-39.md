# Alma 39

[Alma the Younger](/notes/alma-the-younger)'s counsel to his son [Corianton](/notes/corianton) after Corianton's sin. In July 27–August 2, 2020. Alma 39–42: "The Great Plan of Happiness", Kyle draws out several threads from this chapter. Alma reproves Corianton for boasting in his own strength and wisdom (39:2) and for not heeding instruction as his brother did; he calls him to "cross yourself in all these things" (39:9) — which Kyle reads as [Self-Mastery](/notes/self-mastery), the rule of the self by the self. The chapter also grounds Kyle's reflections on idleness ("the idle brain is the devil's playground") and [Parenting](/notes/parenting): "command thy children to do good" (39:12), and the charge to turn to the Lord with might, mind, and strength (39:13). A structural insight Kyle highlights across the whole week: Alma spends only 14 verses on Corianton's sins but 74 verses teaching about [Jesus Christ](/notes/jesus) (39:15) before relating it back to his son — teaching *toward* Christ, not just *against* sin.

Alma 39 is also flagged for connection in Christ Defines His Gospel, where Kyle threads it into his broader study of grace, works, and repentance.

**Context:** Alma 39 (in the Book of Mormon) is the opening of Alma's fatherly counsel to Corianton, addressing the seriousness of sexual sin and pointing his son toward the coming of Christ and repentance.
