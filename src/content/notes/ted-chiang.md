# Ted Chiang

Ted Chiang is the author of [Story of Your Life](/books/story-of-your-life) (the novella behind the film *Arrival*), which Kyle reads as the canonical entry of the collection *Stories of Your Life and Others*. In Kyle's notes his work is the exemplar of idea-driven [Science Fiction](/notes/science-fiction) — a single linguistic/physical premise (the heptapods' simultaneous mode of consciousness, Heptapod B, [Fermat's Principle](/notes/fermat-s-principle)) reshaping how a mind experiences time. The story advances Chiang's recurring frame that [Free Will](/notes/free-will) and [Predestination](/notes/predestination) are not mutually exclusive: the heptapods are "neither free nor bound," acting to enact a chronology they already know. Chiang also appears in Historical Futurism - Research, where "Story of Your Life" is catalogued among the speculative short stories feeding Kyle's historical-futurism thesis.

**Context:** Ted Chiang is an American science-fiction writer celebrated for a small, meticulous body of short fiction — collected in *Stories of Your Life and Others* and *Exhalation* — that won multiple Hugo and Nebula awards.
