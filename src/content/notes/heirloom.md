# Heirloom

Heirloom is a direct-air-capture carbon-removal startup that surfaces in Kyle's [Watershed Climate](/notes/watershed-climate) Series B diligence (2021). In the diligence notes it appears alongside [Running Tide](/notes/running-tide) as one of "two cutting-edge carbon removal startups" whose founders spoke at Watershed's 2021 Climate Week event with the climate leads at [Stripe](/notes/stripe) and [Shopify ($SHOP)](/notes/shopify) — buyers who had "collectively kickstarted the market with $10m+ of purchasing commitments." [Shashank Samala](/notes/shashank-samala) and [Max Scholten](/notes/max-scholten) are listed as Heirloom's participants.

The notes frame Heirloom and Running Tide not as "speculative non-profits" but as "tech companies with customers, investors, deliverables—and competitors." The key metric cited is energy efficiency — how much power they burn removing each tonne of carbon — with the expectation that, like solar's ~2,000x cost decline since the late 1960s, carbon-removal costs (then up to a few thousand dollars per tonne) could fall below $100 within the decade. Heirloom is relevant to the Watershed thesis as part of the carbon-removal demand-and-supply ecosystem Watershed's enterprise customers buy into.

**Context:** Heirloom Carbon Technologies is a US direct-air-capture company that uses accelerated mineralization — exposing limestone to ambient air so it reabsorbs CO₂ — as a low-energy approach to atmospheric carbon removal, and was an early supplier to the Frontier advance-market-commitment buyers.
