# Malinformation

Malinformation is a tag Kyle uses for **"the wrong truth"** — true information whose framing or medium distorts its meaning. He attaches the `#Malinformation` note to a passage in [Amusing Ourselves To Death](/books/amusing-ourselves-to-death) where Postman argues that "variations in the structures of languages will result in variations in what may be called 'world view'" — that the grammar of a medium shapes how people think about time, space, things, and processes. On the page's concept index, Kyle pairs [Misinformation](/notes/misinformation) and [Malinformation](/notes/malinformation) under the idea of television "altering the meaning of being informed" by creating disinformation, glossing it as a note on the language-and-worldview highlight.

The same term recurs in his Historical Futurism - Research notes, where it sits in a *Nineteen Eighty-Four* thread alongside [Newspeak](/notes/newspeak) and [Thoughtcrime](/notes/thoughtcrime) — "malinformation = the wrong truth" — framing sci-fi as a cautionary lens on how truth itself can be weaponized or hollowed out.

**Context:** "Malinformation" is a term used in contemporary information-disorder taxonomies (alongside *misinformation* and *disinformation*) for genuine information shared — often deliberately — in a misleading context or to cause harm. Kyle's usage extends it conceptually to the Postman/Orwell theme of how the medium and framing of true information can corrupt its meaning.
