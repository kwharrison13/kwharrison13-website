# Calvin and Hobbes

Cited by Naval Ravikant in "Escape competition through authenticity" as the go-to example of a creative work so bound up with its creator's identity that no one could out-compete it: "Is someone going to compete with Bill Watterson and create a better Calvin and Hobbes? No. They're being authentic." A one-line illustration, not further discussed.
