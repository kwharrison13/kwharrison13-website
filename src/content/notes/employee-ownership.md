# Employee Ownership

Employee ownership is a recurring thread in Kyle's notes connecting equity, [Human Capital](/notes/human-capital), and the moral architecture of capitalism. Two registers run side by side. The **historical / structural** register comes through [Working Toward Zion](/books/working-toward-zion), where employee ownership is "the worker-cooperative thread" — instantiated by Lorenzo Snow's Brigham City model (stock held throughout the community rather than only by participants) and by Ernest Bader's 1951 decision to place 90% of his firm's stock in workers' hands, capping top-to-bottom pay at seven-to-one. The book frames worker ownership as consistent with free-market principles and the founders' "no taxation without representation" logic applied to absentee owners. [George F. Johnson and His Industrial Democracy](/books/george-f-johnson-and-his-industrial-democracy) supplies a parallel case study — Endicott-Johnson's "industrial democracy" experiment, read for echoes in modern owner-mentality and [Welfare Capitalism](/notes/the-square-deal) debates.

The **practitioner / operational** register comes through Kyle's own conversations. [John Hoffmire](/notes/john-hoffmire) — Saïd Business School (Oxford) faculty and former ESOP investment banker who helped employees buy ~$2.2B of [ESOP](/notes/esop) stock — discussed it with Kyle at Oxford in 2014. [Zabie Elmgren](/notes/zabie-elmgren) of [Index Ventures](/notes/index-ventures) approached it from the startup-equity angle in 2021, sharing founder-facing resources (an equity spreadsheet and a beginner's guide to equity) built to help founders communicate the value of equity to early employees, creating transparency around strike prices, vesting, dilution, and liquidation events.

**Context:** Employee ownership refers to arrangements in which a company's workers hold equity in the firm, ranging from broad-based Employee Stock Ownership Plans (ESOPs) and worker cooperatives to startup stock-option pools. Advocates argue it aligns incentives, builds wealth for workers, and shares the gains of enterprise more broadly than absentee shareholding.
