# Jeremy Stoppelman

An early X.com engineer who later co-founded [Yelp](/notes/yelp), appearing in [The Founders](/books/the-founders) as a firsthand witness to PayPal's early decision-making culture. Stoppelman recalled that the founders "sacrificed solidarity for speed and made decisions by fiat when necessary" — "It wasn't an open democracy of ideas." The book also uses him to illustrate how [Peter Thiel](/notes/peter-thiel) and [Max Levchin](/notes/max-levchin) managed dissent: when Stoppelman pushed back, they heard him out and took time to explain themselves, winning him over. "They didn't just say, 'Oh we don't care about you. You're just some junior engineer,'" he remembered. "They made me feel the love and it definitely de-fanged me."

**Context:** Jeremy Stoppelman co-founded Yelp in 2004 with fellow PayPal alum Russel Simmons and has served as its CEO since, making him one of the better-known members of the so-called "PayPal Mafia."

## Where this appears

- [The Founders](/books/the-founders) — quoted on PayPal's decision-by-fiat culture and on how Thiel and Levchin won over dissenting junior engineers.
