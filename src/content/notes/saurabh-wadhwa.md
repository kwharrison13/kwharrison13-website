# Saurabh Wadhwa

Senior Customer Success Manager at Delphix and former VP Customer Success at Altus Group (with a finance background, years at UBS), interviewed as expert call #4 in Kyle's Planhat - Diligence dossier. A multi-year [Gainsight](/notes/gainsight) user, he offered some of the most detailed product commentary in the diligence.

His first evaluation question is **company size**: large bases that want usage-based segmentation point to Gainsight (best in market, but higher cost, complex integrations, occasionally troublesome support); SMBs lean to [ToTango](/notes/totango). He flagged [Planhat](/notes/planhat) as having "come up really fast," especially in EMEA — "breaking the rules — based out of Sweden" — light-weight and extending beyond CS into account planning. On why Planhat isn't used in enterprise: excellent UI, but only **~17 native integrations vs. Gainsight's ~41** (Salesforce yes, but no SAP, no Google Analytics). He argued the **health score is the most important aspect of any CS tool**, breaking it into four weighted areas — customer adoption, product usage, customer engagement, and CSM touch — and warned against unweighted KPIs ("Why are customers turning green but about to churn?"); he praised Planhat for doing the configuration/weighting "very well." On Planhat vs. [Catalyst](/notes/catalyst) he rated Planhat slightly higher ("every user will give Planhat 10/10... it is a delight to work with"), with the caveat that its user base is smaller and Catalyst's marketing could win it a "winning spree." He noted Planhat's three editions — professional, enterprise, startup.
