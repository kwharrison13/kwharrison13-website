# **Thesis**

Creators and advertisers are constantly in need of image content, and supply has grown to meet that demand; the volume of image content has steadily climbed for more than a decade. The number of photos shared over social media has [tripled](https://photutorial.com/photos-statistics/) since 2013, with [14.1 billion](https://photutorial.com/photos-statistics/) images shared per day across WhatsApp, Snapchat, Facebook, and Instagram as of October 2023. Even on video-first platforms like YouTube and TikTok, creators and advertisers rely on [thumbnail images](https://databox.com/improve-youtube-ctr) to get users to click on the video.

Stock photography is one commonly used solution for creators and advertisers looking for images to help attract traffic or views. The stock photography market was valued at [$3.3 billion](https://www.coherentmarketinsights.com/market-insight/stock-photography-market-4549) in 2020. Free stock image sites host millions of photos — for example, Unsplash hosted [3 million](https://unsplash.com/about) photos as of October 2023. Meanwhile, Shutterstock, one of the largest paid stock photography sites, hosted over [1 billion](https://expertphotography.com/best-stock-photo-sites/) images as of October 2023. With the average cost of a stock photo reaching [$1.11](https://photutorial.com/how-much-do-stock-photos-cost/) as of April 2023 and the risk of the same photos being [overused](https://www.snappr.com/blog/10-most-overused-stock-photos), generative AI for commercial use becomes increasingly attractive.

Not everyone can take breathtaking photos or draw photorealistic art, but anyone can type an idea for an image that AI can then generate. In its early days, this ability quickly attracted the interest of social media users; for example, Hank Green’[“cat”](https://twitter.com/hankgreen/status/1599254476720066560?s=20) thread on Twitter attracted over 100K likes. Outside of social media, “AI art generator” received an average of [864K](https://www.semrush.com/analytics/keywordoverview/?q=ai+art+generator&db=us) searches per month globally from August 2022 to August 2023, while “stock photo” only achieved an average of [94K](https://www.semrush.com/analytics/keywordoverview/?q=stock+photo&db=us) searches per month globally in the same period. Generative image AI is already being used to quickly produce [concept art](https://80.lv/articles/using-midjourney-for-concept-and-asset-creation/), make [movie trailers](https://twitter.com/iamneubert/status/1684262309340774407?s=20), and even [win](https://www.nytimes.com/2022/09/02/technology/ai-artificial-intelligence-artists.html) art competitions.

Midjourney [describes](https://www.midjourney.com/) itself as an “independent research lab” known for producing a generative AI program that creates images based on text prompts. As the demand for online imagery continues to increase, Midjourney could offer a cheaper and more customized alternative to stock photography and reduce time spent on the brainstorming phase for contractors, game designers, and other artists alike. As of October 2023, Midjourney is a [self-funded](https://www.midjourney.com/) team with 11 full-time employees and is an [open beta](https://www.pcworld.com/article/820518/midjourneys-ai-art-goes-live-for-everyone.html) which it entered into in July 2022.

# **Founding Story**

Midjourney was founded in [2022](https://www.crunchbase.com/organization/midjourney) by [David Holz](https://www.linkedin.com/in/dsholtz/) (CEO). Prior to founding Midjourney, Holz [described himself](https://www.theverge.com/2022/8/2/23287173/ai-image-generation-art-midjourney-multiverse-interview-david-holz) as having been a serial entrepreneur since high school, when he ran a design business while still a student. He [studied](https://www.theverge.com/2022/8/2/23287173/ai-image-generation-art-midjourney-multiverse-interview-david-holz) physics and math in college and before going on to pursue a PhD in applied math while working [simultaneously](https://www.linkedin.com/in/dsholtz/) at NASA and the Max Planck Institute from [2009](https://www.linkedin.com/in/dsholtz/) through [2011](https://www.linkedin.com/in/dsholtz/).

Holz eventually became [overwhelmed](https://www.theverge.com/2022/8/2/23287173/ai-image-generation-art-midjourney-multiverse-interview-david-holz) and took a leave of absence from his Ph.D. program to move to San Francisco and start his first startup, Leap Motion, [around 2011](https://www.theverge.com/2022/8/2/23287173/ai-image-generation-art-midjourney-multiverse-interview-david-holz). The company’s hardware was utilized by over [300K developers](https://www.ultraleap.com/company/news/press-release/ultrahaptics-leap-motion/) during its twelve years of operating under Holz’s leadership. In [May 2019](https://venturebeat.com/games/ultrahaptics-acquires-ar-vr-pioneer-leap-motion-for-only-30-million/), the company was acquired by Ultrahaptics, now Ultraleap, for roughly [$30 million](https://www.theverge.com/2019/5/30/18645604/leap-motion-vr-hand-tracking-ultrahaptics-acquisition-rumor) or 10% of Leap Motion’s [$306 million](https://www.theverge.com/2019/5/30/18645604/leap-motion-vr-hand-tracking-ultrahaptics-acquisition-rumor) peak valuation in [2013](https://www.theverge.com/2019/5/30/18645604/leap-motion-vr-hand-tracking-ultrahaptics-acquisition-rumor).

In [August 2021](https://www.linkedin.com/in/dsholtz/), Holz left Leap Motion to start Midjourney, [seeking](https://www.theverge.com/2022/8/2/23287173/ai-image-generation-art-midjourney-multiverse-interview-david-holz) a change from large venture-backed companies. After recruiting a team of [ten](https://www.theverge.com/2022/8/2/23287173/ai-image-generation-art-midjourney-multiverse-interview-david-holz) engineers, the independent lab launched its first private demo in [September 2021](https://www.theverge.com/2022/8/2/23287173/ai-image-generation-art-midjourney-multiverse-interview-david-holz). As Holz watched the first users test Midjourney, he began to realize the tool’s potential, [wondering](https://www.theverge.com/2022/8/2/23287173/ai-image-generation-art-midjourney-multiverse-interview-david-holz):

> *“What does it mean when computers are better at visual imagination than 99 percent of humans?”*

By [February 2022](https://docs.midjourney.com/docs/model-versions), Midjourney was released publicly in the form of a [Discord](https://research.contrary.com/reports/discord) bot within the Midjourney community. Hosting the product in Discord gave people the space to imagine together while also giving newcomers examples to explore. As Holz described in a November 2022 [conversation](https://stratechery.com/2022/an-interview-with-midjourney-founder-david-holz-about-generative-ai-vr-and-silicon-valley/?access_token=eyJhbGciOiJSUzI1NiIsImtpZCI6InN0cmF0ZWNoZXJ5LnBhc3Nwb3J0Lm9ubGluZSIsInR5cCI6IkpXVCJ9.eyJhdWQiOiJzdHJhdGVjaGVyeS5wYXNzcG9ydC5vbmxpbmUiLCJlbnQiOnsidXJpIjpbImh0dHBzOi8vc3RyYXRlY2hlcnkuY29tLzIwMjIvYW4taW50ZXJ2aWV3LXdpdGgtbWlkam91cm5leS1mb3VuZGVyLWRhdmlkLWhvbHotYWJvdXQtZ2VuZXJhdGl2ZS1haS12ci1hbmQtc2lsaWNvbi12YWxsZXkvIl19LCJleHAiOjE2NzA2NzQ3NDAsImlhdCI6MTY2ODA4Mjc0MCwiaXNzIjoiaHR0cHM6Ly9zdHJhdGVjaGVyeS5wYXNzcG9ydC5vbmxpbmUvb2F1dGgiLCJzY29wZSI6ImZlZWQ6cmVhZCBhcnRpY2xlOnJlYWQgYXNzZXQ6cmVhZCBjYXRlZ29yeTpyZWFkIiwic3ViIjoiNDdiaXZVVExYREs3c3FHY2RuTGIyNyIsInVzZSI6ImFjY2VzcyJ9.vAGly-F2sOejdnNUMYdrX34m5yrv1dRnJ9bAacQTFLuoegTSL9Wn-2T-MrLG6ux9l0ZIW67tZeh1aEPSrtAe1mRZShMGsvkgBPVyAmTTtdc7unIQ3v-9ItAP7L9a1J1qKA_rdoaGrScRVD4ibf5c7Iz674zqznUmHQNcV-CtapHQld1NT31E63vifTC2-WPis5UKyZ3Hsk4f7wPn5ipHpB7oFktdNIQaAuB9yYx0kW2s5q8rX8PzaaMb7GGMNuc32G_JWT2qHmI8_naEHQ6qT7yC0p4A977DmohoLnM_hUVTUnR4qoWHvrWNpaSoLvksgpUQMyW_RKBgdofnrt-5sA) with Stratechery’s Ben Thompson:
