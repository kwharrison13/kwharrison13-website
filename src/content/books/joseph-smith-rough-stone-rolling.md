## Key Takeaways

<!-- key-takeaways -->
Bushman's landmark biography, read with a specific tension in view: prophets are fallible humans operating in fallen systems, and the institution they build has to be evaluated on that basis rather than against a perfect ideal. Kyle's reading organizes around three pillars — **Profile of a Prophet**, **City Building**, and **Gods in Embryo** — with the recurring threads folded into each.

### 1. [Profile of a Prophet](/notes/profile-of-a-prophet)

- **The ability to see what others cannot, and drive forward with unbelievable conviction.** "For years, the kingdom existed primarily in Joseph's mind. He was one of those unlettered men who could have built a railroad or governed a state." His upbringing "seems so inadequate to his ambitions" — where the power came from is a mystery.
- **A character that points toward the Savior, not toward himself.** "I do not wish to be a great deal better than any body else"; "I want no man to lean upon me… only so far as I am a consistent follower in the footsteps of the Master."
- **Stating fact — "thus saith the Lord" — rather than arguing.** "His storytelling was oracular rather than argumentative." You can't find one argument in Joseph Smith for the existence of God ([Truman G. Madsen](/notes/truman-g-madsen)); he made pronouncements on the authority of his own inspiration, heedless of current opinion.
- **Revealing new things, not rehashing old ones.** "There is no such thing as immaterial matter. All spirit is matter…" — a radical revision of Christian [Metaphysics](/notes/metaphysics) dropped as a casual aside.
- **Does not demand blind obedience — equips followers to find their own revelation.** He "gave tips on how to receive revelation"; "even the least Saint may know all things as fast as he is able to bear them."
- *Prophets are not protected from failure.* By June 1837 "not twenty men in Kirtland believed Joseph was a prophet." "There are no promises that he's protected from failure, especially when people's greed are more supportive than their faith."
- *Tension on creeds.* Joseph's resistance to creeds was both principled ("thus far and no further") and pragmatic — yet the contemporary Church has drifted toward an enforcement Joseph himself might have resisted ("Are we evolving?").

### 2. [City Building](/notes/city-building)

- **Requires vision; great cities aren't built casually.** "Zion became a city… He worked all his life to organize communities, and in the end he succeeded." Far West was his first chance to construct a city from the ground up — "we are a city-building people."
- **Governed by the [Voice of the People](/notes/voice-of-the-people) and the best ideas.** Joseph used his authority "to return power to illiterate men," yet warned that when the people's voice is unjust, "Aristarchy should be the governing principle… the wisest & best Laws should be made" ([Mosiah 29](/notes/mosiah-29):26-27). The prophet-vs-democracy tension is the book's political through-line — and the Missouri persecution was the formative encounter with mob rule and vox populi: "if a majority may crush any religious sect with impunity, any religion could suffer."
- **Build after "the order of heaven."** "Instead of creating parishes, he built cities… a new social order patterned after the 'order of heaven.'"
- **Understand what cities are built around, for longevity.** Nauvoo "had no natural economic advantages," as its decline after the Mormons left proved. Self-governed, distributed leadership doesn't appear spontaneously — it must be taught and honed ([Mitt Romney](/notes/mitt-romney)).
- **Scale of commitment.** Joseph lived only 38 years and devoted at least ~2.5% of his life — and a full year — almost entirely to city-building.

### 3. [Gods in Embryo](/notes/gods-in-embryo)

- **Understand your divine potential.** "Every man a god and a king fulfilled democratic aspirations to a degree unknown in any other religion." Gods in embryo; gods in training — subjects subordinate themselves "in preparation for assuming that power themselves."
- **You become more like God by gaining [Knowledge](/notes/knowledge).** "Knowledge is power… Whatever principle of intelligence we obtain in this life will rise with us in the resurrection" ([D&C 130](/notes/dandc-130):18). A man is saved no faster than he gets knowledge.
- **It is not enough to know — you must DO.** "To know is not enough. One must do" ([Spencer W. Kimball](/notes/spencer-w-kimball)). Through obedience and following the light, intelligence goes on growing through eternity. (The Nehemiah frame: "arise and build" — [Be Up and Doing](/notes/be-up-and-doing).)

Throughout, the notes register an unusual willingness to ask hard questions about Church history without surrendering the underlying claim — the "house of order" frame, taken seriously, demands engagement with the messiness of how that order was actually built.
