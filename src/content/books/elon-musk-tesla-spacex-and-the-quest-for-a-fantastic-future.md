## Connections

<!-- interconnections -->
**People:** [Elon Musk](/notes/elon-musk), the biography's subject, whose persona and operating style this book renders in detail; [Ashlee Vance](/notes/ashlee-vance), the author and reporter; [JB Straubel](/notes/jb-straubel), the Tesla co-founder and CTO repeatedly quoted on Musk's work ethic, conviction, and the all-or-nothing Supercharger/energy-network play; [Antonio Gracias](/notes/antonio-gracias), the investor who frames Musk's unconventional crisis management ("existential threat against the business") and quick, enormous decision-making.

**Concepts:** [First Principles Thinking](/notes/first-principles-thinking) — Vance's sources describe Musk starting every problem from "the physics of it," the framework the book most explicitly attributes to him; [Founders Mentality](/notes/founders-mentality) — Tesla "willed into existence by Musk and reflects his personality" the way Intel, Microsoft, and Apple reflect their founders'; [Vertical Integration](/notes/vertical-integration) — Tesla revamping the automobile and building an energy network (Superchargers) simultaneously as one all-or-nothing play; [Conviction](/notes/conviction) — Musk's "intense and exceptional" certainty, doing what he wants relentlessly even when others think it crazy; [Work Ethic](/notes/work-ethic) — "you should be working as hard as you can" because "life is short"; [Talent](/books/talent) / [Hiring](/notes/hiring) — both companies "designed with this vision of motivating a critical mass of talented people," with Musk grilling SpaceX engineers to tap their stores of knowledge; [Innovation](/notes/innovation) — Musk's lament that "too many smart people pursuing Internet stuff, finance, and law" is "part of the reason why we haven't seen as much innovation"; [Techno-Optimism](/notes/techno-optimism) — the book's wary portrait of "techno-utopians" and their view that "our humanity is an annoying burden."

**Books:** [The Founders](/books/the-founders) — the Musk content here picks up where Soni's PayPal coverage ends. Vance covers the SpaceX/Tesla doubling-down years; [The Founders](/books/the-founders) covers the [PayPal](/notes/paypal)/X.com years that immediately precede them.
