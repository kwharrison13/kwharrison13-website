Sam Battistone — the Jazz seller Miller raced to commit to before a loan deadline.

**Concepts:** [Entrepreneurship](/notes/entrepreneurship) — Miller's ten-point definition of an entrepreneur (risk/reward, supply and demand, overcoming fear of failure) anchors the book. [Hard Work](/notes/hard-work) — the central thesis: 90-hour weeks for 20 years, "overpower problems with work," outworking everyone to be undeniable. [Capital Allocation](/notes/capital-allocation) — buying ~90 businesses (40+ dealerships, the Jazz, the arena) while leveraged 40:1 and refusing to overpay. [Delegation](/notes/delegation) — splitting the company into sports/entertainment and automotive divisions under COOs to free Miller for expansion and acquisition. [Reputation](/notes/reputation) — negotiating so every seller felt treated fairly ("never had to avoid the previous owner"). [Compounding](/notes/compounding) — the grandfather's promise that working hard for an unfair employer "will pay off many times over." [Family](/notes/family) and [Marriage](/notes/marriage) — the cautionary core: working himself away from his children, with Gail as the unacknowledged 50/50 partner. [Faith](/notes/faith) — tithing as the inflection point ("how many coincidences before they're not coincidences") and living by the Spirit.
