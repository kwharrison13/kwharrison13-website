## Connections

<!-- interconnections -->
**People:** [Charles Darwin](/notes/charles-darwin) — the Time Traveller reads the Eloi/Morlock divergence through evolutionary theory ("unless biological science is a mass of errors"), framing degeneration as adaptation to security and ease. [Aldous Huxley](/notes/aldous-huxley) — author of the dystopian comfort-and-decay parallel below. [George Orwell](/notes/george-orwell) — author of the canonical political dystopia paired below.

**Concepts:** [Science Fiction](/notes/science-fiction) / [Speculative Fiction](/notes/speculative-fiction) — the novel is a foundational text of the genre, framing time as a navigable fourth dimension ("there are really four dimensions... a fourth, Time"). [Utopia](/notes/utopia) — the Eloi's "too-perfect security" inverts the utopian promise into civilizational decay ("the dream of the human intellect... had committed suicide"). [Entropy](/notes/entropy) — the far-future vision of dwindling stars and the heat-death of activity, where "even the mere memory of Man... had been swept out of existence." [Capitalism](/notes/capitalism) — the Eloi (Haves, above ground) and Morlocks (Have-nots, the underground workers) as the endpoint of "the industrial system of to-day," a "triumph over Nature and the fellow-man." [Historical Futurism](/essays/historical-futurism) / [Progress Studies](/notes/progress-studies) — the book's central thesis that hardship and necessity drive intelligence ("intellectual versatility is the compensation for change, danger, and trouble"), so security breeds stagnation and decline.

**Books:** [Brave New World](/books/brave-new-world) — dystopia parallel; comfort, pleasure, and the engineered abolition of struggle producing a degenerate species. [1984](/books/1984) — companion political dystopia of a stratified, controlled future. [2034 — A Novel of the Next World War](/books/2034-a-novel-of-the-next-world-war) _(stub)_, [2054](/books/2054) _(stub)_ — speculative-fiction frame.
